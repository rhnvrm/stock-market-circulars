## Summary

BSE's Surveillance Department (DOSS) issued Notice No. 20260420-28 on 20 April 2026, informing that significant price and/or volume movements were observed in 43 listed scrips. The Exchange had written to these companies to ensure trading members and investors have access to the latest relevant information. Clarifications have been received from 27 companies, while replies are still awaited from 16 companies.

## Key Points

- BSE observed significant price/volume movement in 43 scrips and sought clarifications from the respective companies
- Replies received from 27 companies have been attached as four separate annexures (Replies1, Replies2, Replies3, Replies4)
- 16 companies have not yet responded and their replies are awaited
- The notice was issued by the Surveillance department under Deputy Vice President Sanjay Jain and Manager Shreya Ghose

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard surveillance action under BSE's existing framework for monitoring unusual price and volume movements in listed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- The 16 companies that have not responded must submit their clarifications to BSE promptly
- All listed companies are expected to respond to BSE surveillance queries in a timely manner to ensure market transparency
- Investors and trading members should review the attached replies (Replies1–Replies4) for updated information on the responding companies

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 20 April 2026
- Replies received as of: 20 April 2026
- Deadline for pending replies: Not specified; responses are awaited

## Impact Assessment

This notice covers a broad range of 43 companies spanning diverse sectors including food processing, logistics, chemicals, steel, finance, pharmaceuticals, and electronics. The unusual price/volume movements in these scrips may indicate heightened investor interest or speculative activity. Investors holding or considering positions in these scrips should review the company clarifications before making trading decisions. The 16 companies yet to respond pose an information gap risk for their investors. BSE's proactive surveillance action helps maintain market integrity and investor confidence.
