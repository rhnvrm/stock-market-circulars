## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260522-18) dated 22 May 2026 regarding the review of guidelines applicable to market makers operating in the SME (Small and Medium Enterprises) segment. This notice is issued by the Membership Compliance department and falls under the 'Others' category for the SME segment.

## Key Points

- BSE is reviewing the existing guidelines for market makers in the SME segment.
- The notice is issued by the Membership Compliance department, indicating a regulatory/compliance-driven update.
- Market makers in the SME segment play a critical role in providing liquidity for listed SME securities.
- No attachments are included with this notice; members should refer to the notice number 20260522-18 for full details via the BSE portal.

## Regulatory Changes

The circular pertains to a review of the guidelines governing market maker obligations and operations in the BSE SME segment. Specific changes would be detailed in the associated notice document. Market makers are typically required to maintain continuous two-way quotes and meet minimum liquidity thresholds.

## Compliance Requirements

- Registered market makers in the SME segment should review the updated guidelines.
- Member firms with market-making obligations must ensure adherence to any revised norms as outlined by BSE Membership Compliance.
- Members are advised to monitor BSE circulars and notices for the full text of revised guidelines.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 22 May 2026
- Effective dates for revised guidelines, if any, would be specified in the detailed notice document.

## Impact Assessment

This review directly impacts brokers and trading members who are registered as market makers in the BSE SME segment. Any revision to market-making guidelines can affect quoting obligations, capital requirements, and operational procedures. SME-listed companies may also be indirectly affected through changes in liquidity support mechanisms. The impact is considered medium, as it applies to a specific subset of market participants rather than the broader market.
