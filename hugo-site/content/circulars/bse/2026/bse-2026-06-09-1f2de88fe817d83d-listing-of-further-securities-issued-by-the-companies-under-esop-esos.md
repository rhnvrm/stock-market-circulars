## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that further securities issued under Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP) and Employee Stock Option Schemes (ESOS) by nine companies are listed and admitted for trading on the exchange effective Wednesday, June 10, 2026. None of the newly listed shares carry any lock-in restrictions.

## Key Points

- Nine companies are listing additional shares arising from ESOP/ESOS exercises
- All shares are eligible for trading from June 10, 2026
- No lock-in period applies to any of the listed securities
- Vardhman Textiles has the largest allotment at 194,000 shares (face value ₹2)
- One 97 Communications (Paytm) lists 156,826 shares (face value ₹1)
- Rishabh Instruments lists 71,251 shares (face value ₹10)
- Tata Consumer Products lists 73,469 shares (face value ₹1)
- Tejas Networks lists 70,614 shares (face value ₹10)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing admission notice under BSE's Listing Operations process.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are required to take note of the updated share distribution ranges for each company and ensure trading systems reflect the newly admitted securities from June 10, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 9, 2026
- **Effective Trading Date:** June 10, 2026 (Wednesday)

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact. These are routine ESOP/ESOS allotments representing incremental dilution for each company. The share counts are small relative to existing outstanding shares for all nine issuers. No lock-in restrictions means shares are freely tradable from the listing date, but volumes are unlikely to be significant. The largest allotment (Vardhman Textiles at 194,000 shares) remains modest relative to its existing share capital of approximately 289 million shares.
