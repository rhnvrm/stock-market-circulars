## Summary

BSE Index Services Private Limited has announced the addition of two newly listed SME companies — Safety Controls & Devices Limited and EMIAC Technologies Limited — to the BSE SME IPO Index. Both companies were listed on BSE on April 13, 2026, and will be included in the index effective at the open of April 15, 2026.

## Key Points

- Safety Controls & Devices Limited (Exchange ticker: 544746) listed on BSE on April 13, 2026
- EMIAC Technologies Limited (Exchange ticker: 544747) listed on BSE on April 13, 2026
- Both stocks will be added to the BSE SME IPO Index effective at market open on April 15, 2026
- Corporate action included in the end-of-day corporate action file (*.SDE) on April 13, 2026
- Reference notices: 20260410-44 (Safety Controls) and 20260410-43 (EMIAC Technologies)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard index reconstitution notice following the listing of new SME IPO securities on BSE.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance requirements for market participants. Index-linked fund managers and ETF providers tracking the BSE SME IPO Index should update their portfolios to include the two new constituents effective April 15, 2026. Queries can be directed to bseindex@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **April 13, 2026**: Listing date for both Safety Controls & Devices Limited (544746) and EMIAC Technologies Limited (544747) on BSE
- **April 13, 2026**: Corporate action file (*.SDE) updated with these additions
- **April 15, 2026 (market open)**: Effective date for inclusion of both stocks in the BSE SME IPO Index

## Impact Assessment

The addition of these two SME IPO stocks to the BSE SME IPO Index is a routine reconstitution event with moderate market impact limited to index-tracking products. Passive funds, ETFs, and portfolios benchmarked against the BSE SME IPO Index will need to incorporate these constituents at the open of April 15, 2026. There is no broader market-wide or regulatory impact. The two-day lag between listing (April 13) and index inclusion (April 15) is standard practice, giving market participants adequate time to adjust their holdings.
