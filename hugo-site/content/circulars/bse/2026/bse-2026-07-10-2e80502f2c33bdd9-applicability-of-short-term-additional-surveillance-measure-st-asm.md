## Summary

BSE has notified that certain securities have met the criteria for inclusion under the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework, under both Stage I and Stage II, as per the attached Annexure I. These measures impose additional margin requirements on the identified securities effective July 14, 2026.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I have satisfied criteria for ST-ASM Stage I inclusion.
- Securities listed in Annexure I have also satisfied criteria for ST-ASM Stage II inclusion.
- ST-ASM Stage I: applicable margin rate is 50% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- ST-ASM Stage II: applicable margin rate is 100% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- The ASM framework operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures.
- Inclusion under ASM is purely a market surveillance measure and should not be construed as an adverse action against the concerned company/entity.
- Price bands for scrips moving out of the framework will be reinstated to pre-shortlisting levels, subject to no other surveillance measure applying.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice implements the ST-ASM framework as established under earlier BSE notices (20240920-71, 20181027-1, 20190719-33, 20201204-56, 20230925-49, and 20240920-63) regarding revisions to the ASM framework. No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is an application of existing rules to newly identified securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the specified higher margin rates (50% for Stage I, 100% for Stage II) on all open positions as on July 13, 2026, and on new positions created from July 14, 2026, for the securities listed in Annexure I.
- Members should ensure margin collection systems are updated to reflect these rates before the effective date.
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 10, 2026
- Effective Date: July 14, 2026 (applicable to open positions as of July 13, 2026, and new positions from July 14, 2026)

## Impact Assessment

The increased margin requirements (50%-100%) will raise the cost of trading in the affected securities, likely reducing speculative activity and liquidity in these stocks. Investors and traders holding or planning positions in the securities named in Annexure I should account for higher capital requirements. The measure is a standard surveillance tool aimed at curbing excessive volatility and does not reflect any fundamental issue with the underlying companies.
