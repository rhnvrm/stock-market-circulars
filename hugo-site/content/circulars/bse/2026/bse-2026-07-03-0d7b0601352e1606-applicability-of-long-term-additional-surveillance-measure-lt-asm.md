## Summary

BSE has issued an update on the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework, referencing earlier notices dating back to 2018. The circular outlines securities newly placed under LT-ASM, those moving to higher LT-ASM stages, those transferring to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups, and those exiting the framework, along with associated margin and price band changes.

## Key Points

- 100% margins will apply on all open positions as of July 07, 2026 and new positions from July 08, 2026 for securities listed in Annexure I (Part A).
- Securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages are listed in Annexure I (Part B), effective July 06, 2026.
- Lower price band applicable w.e.f. July 06, 2026 for relevant securities.
- Securities in Annexure I (Part D) will continue under LT-ASM but be transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group w.e.f. July 08, 2026.
- Securities moving out of the LT-ASM framework are listed in Annexure II, effective July 06, 2026.
- A consolidated list of all securities under the framework is provided in Annexure III.
- Shortlisting is based on surveillance criteria (XBRL submissions by listed companies) and does not indicate any adverse view of the company.
- The framework operates alongside other prevailing surveillance measures.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice implements the revised LT-ASM framework per prior BSE notices (20180321-46, 20181027-1, 20201204-56, 20240809-46, 20240920-63, and 20210604-41), applying periodic review-based updates to the list of securities under enhanced surveillance.

## Compliance Requirements

Market participants and trading members must apply 100% margin requirements on affected securities from the specified dates and observe revised price bands. Members with queries can contact bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 06, 2026: Lower price band applicable; securities move to higher LT-ASM stages; securities exit LT-ASM framework.
- July 07, 2026: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin.
- July 08, 2026: 100% margin applies to new positions; securities transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group.

## Impact Assessment

The measure increases margin requirements and trading restrictions on specific securities identified through surveillance criteria, which may reduce liquidity and trading activity in those names. The price band reinstatement provision offers relief once a scrip exits the framework, provided it isn't caught under another surveillance measure. Overall market impact is limited to the specific securities named in the annexures rather than broad market segments.
