## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that five new commercial papers (CPs), issued on a private placement basis, have been listed and admitted to trading on the BSE Debt Segment effective April 21, 2026. All securities trade exclusively in dematerialised form in standard denominations of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof, with a tick size of 1 paise.

## Key Points

- Five commercial papers freshly listed on BSE Debt Segment under Notice No. 20260421-25
- All CPs trade in demat form only; standard lot size Rs. 5 lakhs; tick size 1 paise
- Issuers span NBFC, infrastructure, cement, financial services, and securities sectors
- Rates range from 6.05% p.a. (JK Cement) to 9.70% p.a. (Muthoot MCred)
- Maturities range from approximately 2 months (KEC International, Jun 2026) to ~11 months (Credila Financial Services, Mar 2027)
- All interest payments are cumulative, paid at redemption

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational listing notice under existing BSE Debt Segment framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note that all listed CPs are tradeable only in dematerialised form
- Trades must conform to the standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- For clarifications, members may contact BSE Debt Department at 22728352 / 8597 / 8995 / 5753 / 8915
