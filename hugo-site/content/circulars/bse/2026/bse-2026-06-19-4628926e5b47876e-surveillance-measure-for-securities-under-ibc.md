## Summary

BSE has updated the list of securities subject to Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) for companies undergoing Insolvency Resolution Process (IRP) under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). The changes are effective June 22, 2026, covering new additions (Annexure I), removals (Annexure II), and a consolidated list (Annexure III).

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I have satisfied the ASM criteria for IRP under IBC and are placed under the framework effective June 22, 2026.
- Securities listed in Annexure II are moving out of the IBC Framework effective June 22, 2026.
- Annexure III provides a consolidated list of all securities currently under the IBC surveillance framework.
- The framework will be reviewed on a periodic basis.
- The IBC surveillance framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures.
- Shortlisting under this framework is purely for market surveillance and should not be construed as adverse action against the concerned company.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the IBC surveillance framework as established under earlier exchange notices: 20180608-34 (June 8, 2018), 20220520-52 (May 20, 2022), 20230210-55 (February 10, 2023), and 20250905-47 (September 5, 2025).

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants and members must note the updated list of securities under the IBC framework.
- For clarifications, members may write to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **June 19, 2026**: Notice issued.
- **June 22, 2026**: Effective date for securities entering and exiting the IBC surveillance framework.

## Impact Assessment

Securities newly placed under the IBC framework will be subject to additional surveillance measures including potential price band restrictions. Securities exiting the framework will have their price bands reinstated to the levels applicable prior to their inclusion, provided they are not subject to any other surveillance measure. If a security is under another surveillance framework, the price band of that framework will prevail. This update primarily affects investors and traders holding positions in companies undergoing insolvency proceedings.
