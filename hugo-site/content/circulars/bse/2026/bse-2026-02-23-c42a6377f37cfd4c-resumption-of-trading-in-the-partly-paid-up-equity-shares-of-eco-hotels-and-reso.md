## Summary

Eco Hotels and Resorts Limited (Scrip Code: 890225) has applied to BSE for resumption of trading in 88,54,060 partly paid-up equity shares upon receipt of first call money of Rs. 3.80 per share (Rs. 2.50 against face value and Rs. 1.30 against premium). The shares carry ISIN IN9638N01028 and are held across NSDL and CDSL depositories.

## Key Points

- Total partly paid shares issued as on September 26, 2025: 1,28,76,808
- Total partly paid shares on which first call money was received: 88,68,167
- Total partly paid shares available for trading on resumption: 88,54,060
- First call money received: Rs. 3.80 per share (Rs. 2.50 face value + Rs. 1.30 premium)
- Paid-up value per share available for trading: Rs. 5/-
- ISIN for shares available for trading: IN9638N01028
- No lock-in period applicable
- NSDL distinctive numbers: 51751136 to 55346877 (35,95,742 shares)
- CDSL distinctive numbers: 55352250 to 60610567 (52,58,318 shares)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular is an application under existing BSE listing and trading regulations governing the resumption of partly paid-up share trading after call money collection.

## Compliance Requirements

- BSE Listing Operations to process the application and enable trading in the specified partly paid-up shares
- The company (Eco Hotels and Resorts Limited) has submitted required documentation including distinctive numbers and depository-wise share breakup

## Important Dates

- Partly paid shares originally issued: September 26, 2025
- Application date for trading resumption: February 23, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The resumption of trading affects 88,54,060 partly paid-up equity shares, allowing holders who have paid the first call money to trade their shares on BSE. The impact is company-specific and moderate, benefiting investors who participated in the partly paid share issuance and have fulfilled call money obligations. No lock-in restrictions apply, enabling immediate market participation upon resumption approval.
