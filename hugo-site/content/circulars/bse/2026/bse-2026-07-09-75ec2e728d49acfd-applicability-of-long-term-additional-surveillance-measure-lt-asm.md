## Summary

BSE has issued an update to the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework, referencing earlier notices dated March 21, 2018, October 27, 2018, December 04, 2020, August 09, 2024, September 20, 2024, and June 04, 2021. The notice specifies securities newly placed under LT-ASM, securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages, securities exiting the framework, and securities being transferred to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups while remaining under LT-ASM. A consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework is provided as Annexure III.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I (Part A) will be subject to LT-ASM measures including 100% margin on all open positions.
- Securities in Annexure I (Part B) will move to their respective higher LT-ASM stages effective July 10, 2026.
- Securities in Annexure I (Part D), per the June 04, 2021 notice, will continue in the LT-ASM framework but be transferred to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups effective July 14, 2026.
- Securities listed in Annexure II will move out of the LT-ASM framework effective July 10, 2026.
- Annexure III provides the consolidated list of securities currently under the LT-ASM framework.
- Shortlisting under this framework is based on XBRL submissions by listed companies and is purely a surveillance measure, not an adverse action against any company.
- The framework operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchange.
- Price bands for securities exiting the framework revert to pre-shortlisting levels, unless the scrip remains subject to another surveillance framework.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice implements periodic revisions under the existing LT-ASM framework established via prior BSE notices (20180321-46, 20181027-1, 20201204-56, 20240809-46, 20240920-63, and 20210604-41). No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a scheduled review and update applying the existing rules to a revised set of securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and clients holding positions in securities newly placed under LT-ASM (Annexure I Part A) must arrange for 100% margin on all open positions as on July 13, 2026, and on new positions created from July 14, 2026 onwards.
- Market participants trading securities in Annexure I (Part D) should note the transfer of these securities to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups effective July 14, 2026.
- Members should monitor Annexure I (Part B) for securities moving to higher surveillance stages and adjust risk management accordingly.
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 10, 2026: Lower price band applicable to newly added LT-ASM securities; securities in Annexure II move out of the LT-ASM framework; securities in Annexure I (Part B) move to higher LT-ASM stages.
- July 13, 2026: Reference date for existing open positions subject to 100% margin requirement.
- July 14, 2026: 100% margin becomes applicable on open and new positions for Annexure I (Part A) securities; Annexure I (Part D) securities transferred to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups.

## Impact Assessment

The notice has a high operational impact for trading members and investors holding positions in the affected securities, as 100% margin requirements significantly increase the capital needed to maintain or initiate positions, effectively curbing speculative trading. Securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages face intensified restrictions, while transfer to T/XT/MT/TS groups affects settlement and trading mechanics. Conversely, securities exiting the framework see reduced restrictions and reinstated price bands, which may improve liquidity. As the specific security list resides in annexures not included in the notice text, market participants must consult the linked annexures directly to determine applicability to their holdings.
