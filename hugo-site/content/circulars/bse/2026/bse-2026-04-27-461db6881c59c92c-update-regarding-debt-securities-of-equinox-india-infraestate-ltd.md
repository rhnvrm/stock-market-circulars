## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260427-16) informing trading members that two debt securities of Equinox India Infraestate Ltd will be suspended from trading effective Thursday, April 30, 2026. This notice is a continuation of earlier notices dated October 1, 2025 (20251001-54) and March 25, 2026 (20260325-45), indicating a prolonged regulatory action against these securities.

## Key Points

- Two debt securities of Equinox India Infraestate Ltd are to be suspended from trading effective April 30, 2026.
- Scrip Code 977168 with ISIN INE741X07077 is suspended.
- Scrip Code 977672 with ISIN INE741X07085 is suspended.
- This notice continues a series of regulatory actions starting from October 2025.
- Trading members are advised to take note and communicate this to their clients.

## Regulatory Changes

The suspension is an extension of regulatory actions previously notified via:
- Notice No. 20251001-54 dated October 01, 2025
- Notice No. 20260325-45 dated March 25, 2026

The recurring notices suggest ongoing compliance or default-related issues with Equinox India Infraestate Ltd's debt obligations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must halt trading in Scrip Codes 977168 and 977672 from April 30, 2026.
- Members should inform their clients holding or intending to trade these securities about the suspension.
- For queries, members may contact Ms. Rutuja Mhasade at +91 22 2272 8844 or debt.listing@bseindia.com, or Hardik Bhuta (Assistant Vice President).

## Important Dates

- **April 27, 2026**: Notice issued by BSE.
- **April 30, 2026 (Thursday)**: Effective date of trading suspension for both Scrip Codes.

## Impact Assessment

Investors holding these debt securities will be unable to exit their positions on the exchange from April 30, 2026 onward. The suspension signals continued distress or non-compliance by Equinox India Infraestate Ltd with debt listing obligations. Market liquidity for these instruments will cease, potentially trapping investors. The repeated extension of notices over approximately seven months (October 2025 to April 2026) suggests the issuer has not resolved the underlying issues, elevating credit and liquidity risk for holders of these instruments.
