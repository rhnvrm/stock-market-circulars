## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure) will move to their respective higher stages under the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, effective from July 20, 2026. This action follows the GSM framework established via earlier exchange notices dated February 23, 2017, March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023.

## Key Points

- Securities specified in the Annexure will be moved to higher GSM stages effective July 20, 2026.
- GSM stages range from Stage I to Stage IV, each carrying progressively stricter surveillance actions.
- Stage I: 100% margin and price band of 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, plus 50% Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) from buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade, 5% or lower price band, trading permitted only once a week (Monday/first trading day), plus 100% ASD from buyers.
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III but with no upward price movement permitted.
- All trades in these securities settle on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this circular applies the existing GSM Framework (published February 23, 2017) to update the surveillance stage classification of specific securities identified in the Annexure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin rates, price bands, ASD requirements, and trading frequency restrictions corresponding to each security's new GSM stage.
- Members must ensure trade-to-trade settlement compliance with no netting off for the affected securities.
- Members should refer to the GSM FAQ or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications.

## Important Dates

- Effective date of GSM stage movement: July 20, 2026.
- Notice issued: July 17, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

Securities moved to higher GSM stages will face reduced liquidity due to trade-for-trade settlement, restricted trading frequency (in Stages III/IV), and higher capital requirements from ASD deposits. This is a routine, recurring surveillance action intended to curb speculative trading in securities exhibiting unusual price/volume behavior, with impact concentrated among holders and traders of the specific securities listed in the Annexure rather than the broader market.
