## Summary

BSE has issued a notice under its Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, informing trading members that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure_GSM) will move to their respective higher GSM stages effective July 02, 2026. This action is taken pursuant to the GSM Framework published on February 23, 2017, and subsequent related exchange notices, and triggers a defined set of escalating surveillance actions depending on the stage assigned.

## Key Points

- Securities identified in the Annexure_GSM attachment will move to higher GSM stages effective July 02, 2026.
- The GSM framework has four stages (I to IV), each carrying progressively stricter surveillance actions.
- Stage I: 100% margin applicable and price band capped at 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement with 5% or lower price band, plus Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band, trading permitted only once a week (Monday/first trading day), and ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers.
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III but with no upward price movement permitted.
- All settlements for these securities will be done on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice implements the existing GSM Framework (established via BSE notices dated February 23, 2017, March 03, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023) by moving specific securities to higher surveillance stages.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin, price band, ASD, and trade-for-trade restrictions corresponding to each security's new GSM stage.
- Members must ensure Additional Surveillance Deposits are collected from buyers as required at Stage II, III, or IV.
- Members should exercise adequate precaution while trading in the identified securities given the settlement restrictions.
- Queries or clarifications should be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com or the published GSM FAQ page.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 02, 2026 — securities move to their respective higher GSM stages.
- Notice Date: July 01, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a high impact on liquidity and tradability for the specific securities named in the Annexure, as it imposes trade-for-trade settlement, higher margin/ASD requirements, and in some cases weekly-only trading with no upward price movement. This can materially affect price discovery and investor exit options for affected stocks, though the broader market and unrelated securities are unaffected.
