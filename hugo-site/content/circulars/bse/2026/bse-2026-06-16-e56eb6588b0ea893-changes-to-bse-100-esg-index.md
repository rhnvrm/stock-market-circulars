## Summary

BSE Index Services Private Ltd. has announced changes to the BSE 100 ESG Index, effective at the open of Monday, June 22, 2026. Five companies will be removed from the index with no new additions.

## Key Points

- Five stocks are being dropped from the BSE 100 ESG Index; no stocks are being added
- Changes take effect at market open on June 22, 2026
- Affected companies span diverse sectors: energy, aerospace & defence, building materials, FMCG, and industrial manufacturing
- The corporate action has been included in the end-of-day corporate action file (*.SDE) dated June 16, 2026
- Queries can be directed to bseindex@bseindia.com

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory rule changes. This is a scheduled index rebalancing action by BSE Index Services Private Ltd., a wholly owned subsidiary of BSE Ltd.

## Compliance Requirements

- Index fund managers, ETF issuers, and portfolio managers tracking the BSE 100 ESG Index must rebalance their portfolios to reflect the updated constituents by the open of June 22, 2026
- No direct compliance obligations for the removed companies arising from this circular

## Important Dates

- **June 16, 2026**: Notice date; end-of-day corporate action file updated
- **June 22, 2026 (Monday)**: Effective date for index constituent changes (market open)

## Impact Assessment

**Removed Companies:**
| Company | BSE Ticker |
|---|---|
| Coal India Ltd. | 533278 |
| Hindustan Aeronautics Limited | 541154 |
| Ambuja Cements Limited | 500425 |
| Colgate-Palmolive (India) Limited | 500830 |
| Tube Investments of India Limited | 540762 |

The removal of five constituents with zero additions represents a net reduction in index membership. Passive funds and ETFs benchmarked to the BSE 100 ESG Index will need to sell the removed stocks ahead of or at the effective date, potentially creating short-term selling pressure on these securities. The absence of any additions is notable and may reflect tightened ESG screening criteria or eligibility constraints within the BSE 100 universe.
