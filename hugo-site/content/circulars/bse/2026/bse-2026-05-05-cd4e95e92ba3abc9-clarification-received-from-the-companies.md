## Summary

BSE's Surveillance department (Notice No. 20260505-26) has written to 12 companies that exhibited significant price/volume movement. Replies have been received from Hindustan Appliances Ltd (532918) and Spectrum Electrical Industries Ltd (544386), and are attached to the notice. Replies remain awaited from the remaining 10 companies.

## Key Points

- BSE observed significant price/volume movement in 12 listed scrips and sought clarifications from each company.
- Hindustan Appliances Ltd (Security Code: 531918) has submitted its reply.
- Spectrum Electrical Industries Ltd (Security Code: 544386) has submitted its reply.
- 10 companies have not yet responded to BSE's query as of 05 May 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This notice is part of BSE's ongoing surveillance process to ensure trading members and investors have access to the latest relevant information when unusual market activity is detected.

## Compliance Requirements

- Companies that have not yet responded — Damodar Industries Ltd (521220), Dhillon Freight Carrier Ltd (544556), Gconnect Logitech and Supply Chain Ltd (544156), IFB Agro Industries Ltd (507438), Milgrey Finance & Investments Ltd (511018), Prashant India Ltd (519014), Resourceful Automobile Ltd (544236), Solvex Edibles Ltd (544539), Spice Lounge Food Works Ltd (539895), and ZR2 Bioenergy Ltd (506640) — are required to provide clarifications to BSE on the observed price/volume movement.
- Investors and trading members should review the attached replies from Hindustan Appliances Ltd and Spectrum Electrical Industries Ltd for relevant information.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** 05 May 2026
- Replies from pending companies are expected as part of ongoing surveillance follow-up (no specific deadline mentioned in the notice).

## Impact Assessment

This is a standard BSE surveillance notice with moderate market relevance. The 12 scrips involved are small/mid-cap companies. The two replies already received may contain material disclosures that could influence short-term trading in those scrips. The 10 non-responding companies remain under surveillance scrutiny, and further escalation (such as trading restrictions) is possible if replies are not received. Investors holding positions in any of the listed scrips should monitor subsequent BSE communications.
