## Summary

BSE has notified that trading in the securities of three companies — Advance Syntex Ltd (539982), Padmalaya Telefilms Ltd (532350), and Shiva Granito Export Ltd (540072) — will be suspended with effect from August 14, 2026. The action follows non-compliance with Regulation 76 of the SEBI (Depositories & Participants) Regulations, 2018, which mandates submission of the Reconciliation of Share Capital Audit Report, for two consecutive quarters (December 2025 and March 2026). The action is being taken pursuant to SEBI Master Circular No. HO/49/14/14(7)2025-CFD-POD2/I/3762/2026 dated July 11, 2023 (last updated January 30, 2026).

## Key Points

- Three companies identified for non-compliance with Regulation 76 reconciliation of share capital audit report filing for Q3 FY2025-26 (December 2025) and Q4 FY2025-26 (March 2026).
- Trading suspension effective August 14, 2026 unless companies comply to the Exchange's satisfaction by August 11, 2026.
- Entire promoter shareholding and all other securities held in promoter demat accounts will be frozen during the suspension period.
- If suspension is effected, trading will resume 15 days later only on a Trade-for-Trade basis in Z group, restricted to the first trading day of each week, for six months.
- Revocation of suspension is subject to companies complying with the prescribed procedure and extant norms.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an enforcement action under the existing SEBI Master Circular framework governing penal actions for non-compliance with SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015 and the Standard Operating Procedure for suspension and revocation of trading of specified securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Advance Syntex Ltd, Padmalaya Telefilms Ltd, and Shiva Granito Export Ltd must submit the Reconciliation of Share Capital Audit Report under Regulation 76 of the SEBI (Depositories & Participants) Regulations, 2018, to the satisfaction of the Exchange on or before August 11, 2026 to avoid suspension.
- Market participants and depositories must freeze promoter demat accounts of non-compliant entities during the suspension period.

## Important Dates

- December 2025 and March 2026 quarters: periods of non-compliance.
- August 11, 2026: deadline for companies to comply and avoid suspension.
- August 14, 2026: effective date of trading suspension if non-compliance continues.
- 15 days post-suspension: trading resumes on Trade-for-Trade basis in Z group, first trading day of each week, for six months.

## Impact Assessment

Shareholders of Advance Syntex Ltd, Padmalaya Telefilms Ltd, and Shiva Granito Export Ltd face a near-term liquidity freeze if the companies do not rectify their compliance lapse by August 11, 2026. Promoters will have their entire shareholding and demat holdings frozen, limiting their ability to trade or pledge shares. Post-suspension trading restrictions (Trade-for-Trade, weekly window, Z group) will further reduce liquidity for six months even after any resumption. The broader market impact is limited given the small-cap nature of these companies, but it signals continued SEBI/BSE enforcement rigor on depository compliance requirements.
