## Summary

Bombay Stock Exchange Ltd. has published the Demat Auction notice for Settlement No. 829, dated 27 February 2026. The circular lists approximately 90 scrips across diverse sectors that are scheduled for auction due to non-delivery of shares in the respective settlement. Quantities range from as few as 1 share (e.g., AAA Technologies, State Bank of India) to as many as 372,400 shares (SpiceJet Ltd.).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice under existing BSE auction settlement procedures for handling short/non-delivery of securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members with outstanding short positions in the listed scrips must participate in or be aware of the auction process
- Buyers whose deliveries have failed will receive settlement through the auction mechanism
- Auction participants must comply with BSE's standard auction bidding procedures and timelines

## Important Dates

- Auction Date / Settlement Date: 27 February 2026
- Settlement No.: 829

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine demat auction notice with low systemic market impact. It signals short delivery failures across a broad range of scrips in settlement 829. The most notable entry is SpiceJet (372,400 shares), which may reflect ongoing liquidity or delivery concerns for that scrip. For most other scrips the quantities are small and represent isolated delivery failures. Participants holding positions in listed scrips should monitor auction outcomes as prices in auction may differ from market prices, potentially affecting their net settlement obligations.
