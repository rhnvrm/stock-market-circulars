## Summary

BSE has issued a notice to Research Analysts regarding a communication received from SEBI on integrating and utilizing the Department of Telecommunications' (DoT) Digital Intelligence Platform (DIP). The platform is aimed at strengthening cyber fraud prevention through intelligence sharing, and Research Analysts are directed to onboard and actively use the platform.

## Key Points

- SEBI has communicated to BSE regarding the DoT's Digital Intelligence Platform (DIP) for cyber fraud prevention.
- Research Analysts are specifically advised to onboard the DIP and actively utilize it for intelligence sharing.
- The notice is issued by BSE's Membership Compliance department.
- Contact details for support and guidance on Financial Fraud Risk Indicator (FRI) integration are provided.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulations are introduced. This notice operationalizes an existing SEBI directive to integrate the DoT's DIP into the compliance framework for Research Analysts, specifically around cyber fraud intelligence sharing via the Financial Fraud Risk Indicator (FRI).

## Compliance Requirements

- Research Analysts must onboard the DoT Digital Intelligence Platform (DIP).
- Research Analysts must actively utilize DIP for intelligence sharing.
- For technical or API-related issues with FRI integration, contact: diu@cdot.in and diu.ne1@cdot.in
- For administrative or onboarding queries, contact: dirdiu-dot@gov.in and adg1.diu-dot@gov.in

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 12 June 2026
- No specific compliance deadline is mentioned; Research Analysts are advised to act promptly.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has low immediate market impact but carries medium operational importance for Research Analysts registered with BSE. It requires them to onboard a new government platform (DoT DIP) and integrate with the Financial Fraud Risk Indicator (FRI) system. Non-compliance could carry regulatory risk. The directive is limited to the Research Analyst segment under BSE's Membership Compliance purview.
