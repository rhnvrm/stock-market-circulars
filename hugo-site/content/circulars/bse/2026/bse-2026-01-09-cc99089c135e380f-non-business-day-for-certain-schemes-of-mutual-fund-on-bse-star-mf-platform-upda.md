## Summary

BSE has issued an update regarding non-business days for certain mutual fund schemes on the BSE StAR MF Platform during January 2026. The circular specifically lists non-business days for international equity funds from Nippon India Mutual Fund and ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund. These non-business days are scheduled due to holidays in the respective international markets where these funds invest.

## Key Points

- Nippon India Japan Equity Fund will observe a non-business day on January 12, 2026
- Nippon India US Equity Opportunities Fund will observe a non-business day on January 19, 2026
- ICICI Prudential Global Advantage Fund will observe a non-business day on January 12, 2026
- These non-business days apply only to the specified schemes on BSE StAR MF Platform
- Transactions in these schemes will not be processed on the respective non-business days

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an operational notification regarding schedule adjustments for specific mutual fund schemes.

## Compliance Requirements

- Mutual fund distributors and investors should note the non-business days for the affected schemes
- No transactions (purchases, redemptions, switches) will be processed for these schemes on their respective non-business days
- Market participants should plan their investment activities accordingly

## Important Dates

- **January 12, 2026**: Non-business day for Nippon India Japan Equity Fund and ICICI Prudential Global Advantage Fund
- **January 19, 2026**: Non-business day for Nippon India US Equity Opportunities Fund

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This affects only specific international equity mutual fund schemes.

**Operational Impact**: Investors planning transactions in these schemes must account for the non-business days. Any transaction requests received for these schemes on non-business days will be processed on the next business day.

**Investor Impact**: Limited to investors holding or planning to transact in the three specified international equity funds. The non-business days align with holidays in the respective overseas markets (Japan and United States) where these funds invest.
