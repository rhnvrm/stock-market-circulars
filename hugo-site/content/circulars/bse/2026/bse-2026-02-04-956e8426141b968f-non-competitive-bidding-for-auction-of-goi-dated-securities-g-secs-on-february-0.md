## Summary

BSE has announced the non-competitive bidding facility for participation in the RBI auction of Government of India dated securities scheduled for February 06, 2026. Two G-secs totaling Rs. 29,000 crores will be available for re-issue: 6.68% GS 2040 (Rs. 16,000 crores) and 6.90% GS 2065 (Rs. 13,000 crores). Trading members can submit bids through BSE's iBBS web-based NCB-GSec module.

## Key Points

- Two G-secs available for auction: 6.68% GS 2040 and 6.90% GS 2065
- Total issue size: Rs. 29,000 crores (Rs. 16,000 crores + Rs. 13,000 crores)
- Bidding through BSE's iBBS NCB-GSec module at https://ibbs.bseindia.com
- Minimum subscription: 100 units (Rs. 10,000)
- Maximum bid amount: Rs. 2 crores per security
- Auction settlement date: February 09, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This circular operationalizes the existing non-competitive bidding facility established under BSE circular no. 20180423-42 dated April 23, 2018.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use the NCB-GSec module of BSE's iBBS system for bidding
- Bids must be in minimum subscription units of 100 and multiples thereof (Rs. 10,000 minimum)
- Maximum bid amount per security is Rs. 2 crores
- Members must submit bids by February 06, 2026, 8:00 AM
- Direct investors must submit bids by February 05, 2026, 5:00 PM

## Important Dates

- **Bid Collection Start**: February 03, 2026, 10:00 AM onwards (24-hour availability)
- **Bid Collection End (Direct Investors)**: February 05, 2026, 5:00 PM
- **Bid Collection End (Members)**: February 06, 2026, 8:00 AM
- **Auction Date**: February 06, 2026
- **Settlement Date**: February 09, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Low - This is a routine G-sec auction process that occurs regularly. The facility provides retail and institutional investors access to government securities through the non-competitive route.

**Operational Impact**: Limited to debt market participants and trading members who wish to participate in government securities auctions. The process is standardized with established parameters and settlement procedures through ICCL.
