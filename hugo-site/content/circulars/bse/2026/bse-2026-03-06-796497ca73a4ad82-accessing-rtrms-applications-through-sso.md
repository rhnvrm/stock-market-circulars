## Summary

ICCL (Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd.) has issued a reminder regarding the transition to accessing RTRMS applications through Single Sign-On (SSO), originally communicated via ICCL Circular no. 20260212-15 dated February 12, 2026. The implementation deadline has been postponed from its original date to **March 14, 2026**, to allow members a smoother transition.

## Key Points

- This circular is a reminder of ICCL Circular no. 20260212-15 (dated February 12, 2026) on SSO-based RTRMS access.
- The implementation date for mandatory SSO login to RTRMS has been postponed to **March 14, 2026**.
- All Members and Participants of BSE/ICCL are required to transition to SSO for RTRMS access.
- Support contacts are provided for SSO login issues and user mapping queries.

## Regulatory Changes

- RTRMS (Real-Time Risk Management System) applications will no longer be accessible via direct login after March 14, 2026.
- Access must be routed through Single Sign-On (SSO) at the member portal (https://membersso.bseindia.com or equivalent).

## Compliance Requirements

- All Members and Participants must ensure their RTRMS access is configured via SSO before March 14, 2026.
- Members must complete user mapping in the RTRMS application prior to the deadline.
- For SSO login issues: contact BSE.SSOsupport@bseindia.com or call 022-2272 5033.
- For user mapping issues: contact bsehelp@bseindia.com or call 022-6915 8500 / 022-4572 0400 / 022-4572 0600.

## Important Dates

- **February 12, 2026**: Original ICCL Circular no. 20260212-15 issued.
- **March 6, 2026**: This reminder circular issued.
- **March 14, 2026**: Revised implementation deadline for mandatory SSO-based RTRMS access.

## Impact Assessment

This change affects all BSE Members and Participants who use RTRMS for risk management and settlement activities. The transition to SSO is an operational/IT change requiring members to update their login workflows. The postponement provides additional time to avoid disruption. Members who do not complete the SSO setup and user mapping by March 14, 2026 may lose access to RTRMS applications, impacting their ability to manage risk and settlement operations.
