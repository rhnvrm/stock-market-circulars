## Summary

BSE has revised the price bands for 25 equity scrips effective June 10, 2026, as part of its surveillance function under the Department of Surveillance & Supervision (DOSS). The revised bands range from 2% to 10% depending on the scrip.

## Key Points

- 25 equity scrips have their price bands revised effective June 10, 2026
- New price bands are set at 2%, 5%, or 10% of the previous closing price
- The revision applies from the opening of trading on June 10, 2026
- Members may contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications
- Notice issued by Sanjay Jain (Deputy Vice President, Surveillance) and Arpita Joshi (Manager, Surveillance)

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must ensure that orders placed in the affected scrips do not exceed the revised price band limits. No additional action is required from members beyond adhering to the new trading range limits.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 9, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 10, 2026 (applicable from market open)

## Impact Assessment

The price band revision limits the maximum intraday price movement for 25 scrips, restricting volatility. Scrips assigned a 2% band (DCM Shriram International, Hypersoft Technologies, Source Industries, United Van Der Horst) face the tightest constraints, indicating heightened surveillance concern. Scrips with 10% bands face moderate restrictions compared to the standard 20% band. Traders and investors holding positions in these scrips should note the narrowed trading range, which may affect order execution and liquidity.
