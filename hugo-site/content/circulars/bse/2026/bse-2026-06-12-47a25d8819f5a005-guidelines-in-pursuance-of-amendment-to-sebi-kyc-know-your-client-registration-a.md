## Summary

BSE has issued a monthly compliance notice to all members regarding clients whose KYC records uploaded between May 1–31, 2026 remain in 'On Hold' status at KRA (both Aadhaar and Non-Aadhaar based OVD). Such clients will be barred from trading and from squaring off open positions effective June 26, 2026, until they complete KRA validation. The Exchange will flag non-compliant PANs as 'Not Permitted to Trade' and restore trading access on T+1 basis once KRA compliance is confirmed.

## Key Points

- Clients with KYC 'On Hold' status for any reason (Aadhaar or Non-Aadhaar OVD), uploaded May 1–31, 2026, will be blocked from trading and square-off from June 26, 2026.
- Open positions of non-compliant clients will naturally expire on the contract expiry date; forced closure is not indicated.
- BSE will receive daily demise data from KRAs; trading members must block debit transactions and suspend trading accounts for deceased investors.
- Non-compliant PAN list is available on the BSE system at path: `EQ\TRANSACTION\JUN-2026\12-06-2026`, file: `Non_Validated_Clients_by_KRAs_Clgno_xxxx.TXT`.
- Once a client becomes KRA-compliant, trading access is restored on T+1 after the Exchange receives confirmation from KRA on T Day.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular continues the ongoing implementation of SEBI Circular No. SEBI/HO/MIRSD/FATF/P/CIR/2023/0144 dated August 11, 2023, and references the centralized demise-reporting mechanism under SEBI/HO/OIAE/OIAE_IAD-1/P/CIR/2023/0000000163 dated October 3, 2023. It updates the monthly cycle for KYC non-compliance enforcement, succeeding Notice No. 20260512-16 dated May 12, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must identify clients with KYC uploaded in May 2026 that are flagged 'On Hold' by reviewing the provided non-compliant PAN list.
- Members must ensure such clients complete KYC validation with KRAs before June 26, 2026 to avoid trading restrictions.
- For deceased investors, members must block debit transactions, suspend all trading account transactions, and inactivate/close UCC across all stock exchanges upon receiving demise data from KRA.

## Important Dates

- **May 1–31, 2026**: KYC upload window for which 'On Hold' records are subject to restriction.
- **June 26, 2026**: Effective date from which non-compliant clients are barred from trading and square-off.
- **T+1**: Trading access restored for clients who achieve KRA compliance after restriction.

## Impact Assessment

Trading members face operational risk if their clients with KYC uploaded in May 2026 remain unvalidated by KRAs. Affected clients will lose the ability to trade or close open derivative positions, which may result in client complaints and liability exposure for brokers. The scale depends on the volume of 'On Hold' KYCs from the May 2026 upload cycle. Brokers should proactively review the non-compliant PAN list and prompt clients to resolve KYC issues well ahead of June 26, 2026. Contact: 022-2272 8435/5785 or ucc@bseindia.com.
