## Summary

BSE has announced the applicability of the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework effective March 04, 2026. Eleven securities have been newly shortlisted under the framework (Part A), while one security — Jai Mata Glass Ltd — is being moved out. No securities are being shifted to higher or lower ASM stages in this update.

## Key Points

- 11 securities newly added to LT-ASM Framework w.e.f. March 04, 2026
- 1 security (Jai Mata Glass Ltd, scrip code 523467) removed from LT-ASM Framework
- No securities moved to higher ASM stage (Part B: Nil)
- No securities moved to lower ASM stage (Part C: Nil)
- No securities directly shortlisted into Stage IV LT-ASM (Part D: Nil)
- Mahamaya Steel Industries Ltd (513554) added as per NSE data

## Regulatory Changes

The LT-ASM framework imposes enhanced surveillance conditions on listed securities that exhibit certain risk indicators over a longer time horizon. Securities placed under LT-ASM are subject to additional margin requirements and trade-to-trade settlement obligations, restricting intraday trading and increasing the cost of taking positions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and members must apply revised margin and settlement rules for the newly added scrips from March 04, 2026
- Clients holding positions in newly added scrips should be informed of the changed trading conditions
- Jai Mata Glass Ltd (523467) reverts to normal trading conditions upon exit from LT-ASM, subject to standard applicable frameworks

## Important Dates

- **March 02, 2026**: Circular issued by BSE
- **March 04, 2026**: Effective date for all additions, removals, and stage changes under this circular
