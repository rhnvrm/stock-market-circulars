## Summary

BSE has issued a notice, in continuation of its earlier notice dated July 06, 2026, confirming that the Seller (President of India, acting through the Ministry of Ports, Shipping and Waterways) has exercised the Oversubscription Option in the Offer for Sale (OFS) of Cochin Shipyard Limited equity shares. This increases the total Offer Shares from the Base Offer Size of 66,29,636 shares (2.52% of paid-up capital) to a combined total of 1,32,59,272 shares (5.04% of paid-up capital).

## Key Points

- Base Offer Size: up to 66,29,636 equity shares (face value Rs. 5 each), representing up to 2.52% of Cochin Shipyard's total paid-up equity capital.
- Oversubscription Option exercised in full: an additional 66,29,636 equity shares (up to 2.52%), bringing the total Offer Shares to 1,32,59,272 shares (5.04% of paid-up capital).
- Retail Investor reservation: 13,25,928 equity shares (10% of the offer) reserved for allocation to Retail Investors on T+1 Day (July 08, 2026), subject to valid bids.
- Employee reservation: up to 26,308 equity shares (0.20% of Offer Shares) reserved for eligible employees per the OFS Guidelines.
- The OFS is conducted through a separate, designated window on both BSE and NSE.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; this is an operational update under existing SEBI OFS Guidelines confirming exercise of the pre-disclosed Oversubscription Option.

## Compliance Requirements

Stock exchanges (BSE and NSE) and trading members must process the revised total offer size of 1,32,59,272 shares, honor the retail and employee reservation quotas, and facilitate bid carry-forward from Non-Retail Investors from T Day to T+1 Day as applicable.

## Important Dates

- T Day (Non-Retail Investors): July 07, 2026
- T+1 Day (Retail Investors, Employees, and carried-forward Non-Retail bids): July 08, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The exercise of the Oversubscription Option doubles the stake being divested by the Government in Cochin Shipyard Limited, from 2.52% to 5.04% of paid-up equity capital. This increases the available float in the near term and provides greater participation opportunity for retail investors and employees on T+1 Day. Market impact on Cochin Shipyard's share price and liquidity is likely to be moderate, given the sale size relative to total outstanding shares, with potential short-term supply-side pressure during the offer window.
