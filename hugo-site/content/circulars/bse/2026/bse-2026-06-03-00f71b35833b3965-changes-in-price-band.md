## Summary

BSE's Surveillance Department (DOSS) has revised price bands for 19 equity scrips with effect from **June 4, 2026**. The revisions span bands of 2%, 5%, 10%, and 20%, reflecting varying levels of surveillance scrutiny applied to each scrip.

## Key Points

- 19 scrips affected across the equity segment
- Effective date: **June 4, 2026**
- Price bands revised to 2% (most restrictive) for DCM Financial Services, Jayatma Enterprises, New Markets Avenue, and Ranjit Securities
- AVG Logistics Limited assigned the widest revised band of 20%
- Issued by Deputy Vice President Sanjay Jain and Manager Arpita Joshi, Surveillance
- Members may seek clarification at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Regulatory Changes

Price bands are being revised downward or adjusted for the listed scrips as part of ongoing surveillance. Lower bands (2%) restrict daily price movement significantly, signalling elevated surveillance concern for those counters.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must ensure that orders in the affected scrips comply with the revised price band limits from the opening of trade on June 4, 2026. No additional member action is required beyond adherence to the new band limits.

## Important Dates

- **June 3, 2026** — Notice issued
- **June 4, 2026** — Revised price bands come into effect

## Impact Assessment

The revision primarily impacts traders and investors holding or tracking positions in the 19 listed scrips. Scrips placed under 2% bands (DCM Financial Services, Jayatma Enterprises, New Markets Avenue, Ranjit Securities) face highly restricted intraday movement, limiting liquidity and increasing impact cost for participants. Scrips at 5%–10% bands face moderate restrictions. AVG Logistics at 20% retains relatively wider movement. Overall market impact is limited given the small-cap and SME nature of most affected companies.
