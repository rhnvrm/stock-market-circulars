## Summary

BSE has listed and admitted to trading five commercial paper (CP) issuances on its Debt Segment effective April 22, 2026. The issuers are Tata Realty and Infrastructure Limited, Nuvama Wealth Finance Limited, APL Apollo Tubes Limited (two tranches), and ONGC Petro additions Limited. All securities are issued on a private placement basis with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, tradeable only in dematerialised form in standard denominations of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof.

## Key Points

- Five CP issuances listed across four companies on BSE Debt Segment
- All securities trade in demat form only with a tick size of 1 paise
- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Credit ratings across issuances range from ICRA A1+ to CARE A1+/CRISIL A1+ — all top-tier short-term ratings
- Trading members with clarifications may contact the debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice under existing BSE debt market listing framework for privately placed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form under the specified ISIN numbers
- Trading must occur in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size of 1 paise must be observed for all listed CPs

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact, routine listing circular affecting institutional participants in the BSE Debt Segment. The commercial papers are short-term instruments (maturing within 2–3 months) with high credit quality (A1+ ratings). APL Apollo Tubes has two tranches listed simultaneously, indicating active short-term borrowing activity. Total issuance quantum includes 6,200 units (Tata Realty), 2,000 units (Nuvama), 6,000 units combined (APL Apollo), and 8,000 units (ONGC Petro additions), representing significant short-term fundraising by reputed corporates. No material impact on equity markets is expected.
