## Summary

BSE has notified trading members of the non-competitive bidding facility for the auction of two Government of India (GoI) dated securities on May 29, 2026. The auction follows an RBI press release dated May 25, 2026, and is conducted via BSE's NCB-GSec module on the iBBS platform. Both securities are re-issues, with a combined issue size of ₹28,000 crore.

## Key Points

- Two GoI G-Secs are available for non-competitive bidding: 6.68% GS 2040 (₹17,000 Cr) and 7.43% GS 2076 (₹11,000 Cr)
- Bidding is conducted through the NCB-GSec module at https://ibbs.bseindia.com
- Bid collection opens from May 26, 2026 at 10:00 am (24-hour availability)
- Members must submit bids by May 29, 2026 at 8:00 am; direct investors by May 28, 2026 at 5:00 pm
- Minimum subscription is 100 units with a minimum amount of ₹10,000 per security
- Settlement is scheduled for June 01, 2026
- This is in continuation of Exchange circular no. 20180423-42 dated April 23, 2018

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This notice operates under the existing non-competitive bidding framework established via BSE circular 20180423-42 and the RBI press release of May 25, 2026 announcing the auction.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members wishing to participate must use the NCB-GSec module on BSE's iBBS platform
- Members must submit bids before May 29, 2026 at 8:00 am
- Direct investors must submit bids before May 28, 2026 at 5:00 pm
- For new user ID creation or password resets, contact Trading Operations at user.trdops@bseindia.com or 022-2272-8005/8215
- For bidding queries, contact trading.ops@bseindia.com or 022-2272-8376/5626/8226
- For obligation and pay-in queries, contact ICCL Operations at Collaterals@icclindia.com / settlements@icclindia.com or 022-2272-8468/8704/8264

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine periodic notice for government securities auction participation. The two securities — a 2040-maturity bond at 6.68% coupon and a 2076-maturity bond at 7.43% coupon — are re-issues, indicating established demand. With a combined notified size of ₹28,000 crore, the auction is of moderate scale. Impact is primarily on debt market participants, institutional investors, and trading members active in the government securities segment. No equity market impact is expected.
