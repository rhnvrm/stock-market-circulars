## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260609-50 informing trading members that nine companies have fixed record dates for part redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs). Following the redemption, trading in these debentures will occur at reduced face values with effect from the respective ex-dates under Settlement DR 650/2026-2027.

## Key Points

- Nine companies have set record dates of 12 June 2026 or 14 June 2026 for part redemption of NCDs
- Trading in the affected debentures will shift to reduced face values from the ex-dates
- All settlements fall under Settlement DR 650/2026-2027
- Lodha Developers Limited has three separate NCD series undergoing part redemption simultaneously
- Companies span infrastructure, finance, and real estate sectors

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is a standard corporate action notification under existing BSE listing and debt segment operational rules requiring disclosure of record dates and reduced face values for part-redeemed NCDs.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the reduced face values effective from the ex-dates for each debenture series
- Settlement of trades in these debentures must account for the revised face values from the stated dates
- Members should update their systems to reflect the new face values under Settlement DR 650/2026-2027

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt segment corporate action. Debenture holders in these nine NCD series will receive partial principal repayment, and subsequent trading will reflect the lower outstanding face value. The reduced face values vary significantly across issuers (from Rs. 12,499.93 for Samunnati Finance to Rs. 406,574.99 for Western Transmission Gujarat), reflecting differing redemption schedules. Lodha Developers has three NCD series undergoing concurrent part redemption, signalling active deleveraging. Adani Transmission Step-Two and the transmission infrastructure companies represent regulated utility debt with predictable redemption profiles. No adverse market impact is anticipated; this is a scheduled amortisation event.
