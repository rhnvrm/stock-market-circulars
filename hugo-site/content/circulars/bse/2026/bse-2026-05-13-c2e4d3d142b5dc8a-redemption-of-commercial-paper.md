## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260513-33 (dated 13 May 2026) informs trading members that 16 companies in the debt segment have fixed record dates for the redemption of their commercial papers. Trading members are advised not to deal in these commercial papers from the respective ex-dates onwards.

## Key Points

- 16 companies across various sectors have scheduled commercial paper redemptions between 16 May 2026 and 26 May 2026.
- Trading members must cease dealing in each company's commercial paper from its ex-date.
- The notice covers settlement references in the DR series 631/2026-2027 through 638/2026-2027.
- Both Tata Steel Limited CPs appear twice under different scrip codes (731159 and 731439) with different ISINs and record dates.
- Motilal Oswal group has two CPs listed — Motilal Oswal Financial Services Limited and Motilal Oswal Finvest Limited — both maturing on 26 May 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice under BSE's listing operations for the debt segment, advising members of upcoming CP redemptions per existing exchange procedures.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must **not deal** in the commercial papers of the listed companies on or after their respective ex-dates.
- Members holding or trading these instruments should update their systems to block trading from the applicable ex-dates.

## Impact Assessment

This notice is of **direct relevance to debt market participants** holding any of the 16 listed commercial papers. Holders must note that these instruments will be redeemed at face value on the respective record dates and trading is prohibited from the ex-date. The earliest action point is **15 May 2026** (ex-date for L&T Metro Rail CP). For equity market participants, the impact is negligible as these are short-term debt instruments. The inclusion of large-cap names (Tata Steel, BHEL, Aarti Industries) alongside financial sector issuers reflects routine CP maturity cycles and does not signal any credit stress.
