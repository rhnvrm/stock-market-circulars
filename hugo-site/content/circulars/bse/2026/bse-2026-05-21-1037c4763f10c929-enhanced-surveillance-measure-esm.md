## Summary

BSE has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, adding new securities that will attract a minimum 100% margin requirement from May 25, 2026. Certain securities are being moved to a lower ESM stage (Stage I), while others are being removed from the framework entirely, effective May 22, 2026. Securities under ESM will be shifted from the Rolling Settlement segment to the Trade-for-Trade segment.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I have met the ESM inclusion criteria and will attract a **minimum 100% margin** on all open positions as of May 22, 2026 and all new positions from May 25, 2026
- Some securities in Annexure I will be moved to a **lower Stage (Stage I)** under the ESM framework effective May 22, 2026
- All securities qualifying under ESM will be shifted from **Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment** effective May 25, 2026
- Securities moving to **Stage II** will be placed under Trade-for-Trade with a **2% price band** and **Periodic Call Auction** effective May 22, 2026
- Securities exiting the ESM framework (listed in Annexure II) will be removed effective May 22, 2026
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the ESM framework is provided in Annexure III
- ESM operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the exchanges

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the ESM framework in reference to prior notices: 20230602-44 (June 2, 2023), 20230718-46 (July 18, 2023), 20240809-42 (August 9, 2024), 20240920-63 (September 20, 2024), 20241004-65 (October 4, 2024), and 20250725-61 (July 25, 2025). The framework continues to classify securities into stages with progressively restrictive trading conditions.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members**: Must ensure 100% margin is collected on all open and new positions in newly added ESM securities from the applicable effective dates
- **Market Participants**: Must note the transition of affected securities from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade and adjust trading strategies accordingly
- **Stage II Securities**: Must be traded under Trade-for-Trade rules with a strict 2% price band and through the Periodic Call Auction mechanism
- Members may contact BSE Surveillance at **bse.surv@bseindia.com** for clarifications

## Important Dates

| Event | Effective Date |
|---|---|
| Securities moved to Stage I (downgrade) | May 22, 2026 |
| Stage II price band (2%) + Periodic Call Auction begins | May 22, 2026 |
| Securities removed from ESM framework | May 22, 2026 |
| 100% margin requirement on open positions (as of May 22) | May 25, 2026 |
| Shift to Trade-for-Trade segment | May 25, 2026 |
| 100% margin on new positions | May 25, 2026 |

## Impact Assessment

**High impact** on affected securities and their investors:

- **Liquidity Reduction**: Transition to Trade-for-Trade eliminates intraday trading and netting, forcing delivery-based settlement and significantly reducing liquidity
- **Capital Requirement**: 100% margin requirement substantially increases the capital needed to hold or create positions, deterring speculative activity
- **Price Discovery**: Stage II securities face a 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction, which can slow price discovery and limit price movement
- **Investor Note**: ESM classification is purely a market surveillance action and should not be interpreted as an adverse finding against the listed company
- **Price Band Restoration**: Upon exit from ESM, a security's price band reverts to its pre-ESM level, unless it is under another surveillance framework, in which case that framework's band prevails
- Securities are reviewed periodically, allowing for stage transitions and eventual removal from the ESM framework based on market behavior
