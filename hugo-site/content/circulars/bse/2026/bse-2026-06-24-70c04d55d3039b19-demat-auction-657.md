## Summary

BSE Limited, through Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd., has announced Demat Auction 657 for Rolling Settlement No. DR-657/2026-27. Trading members are invited to submit offers for purchase of securities via their trading terminals on June 24, 2026, within specified session timings.

## Key Points

- Auction offer entry window: 2:00 PM to 2:45 PM on June 24, 2026
- Auction matching session: 2:45 PM to 3:00 PM on June 24, 2026
- Delivery statement and accepted offer reports available: 3:00 PM to 3:30 PM on June 24, 2026
- Money statement and final reports: 3:35 PM to 5:30 PM on June 24, 2026
- Auction pay-in (securities): June 25, 2026 at 8:00 AM
- No corrections permitted after punching errors in securities code or rate
- Exchange reserves the right to accept or reject any offer without reason

## Regulatory Changes

Following implementation of the Uniform Membership structure in the Equity Cash Segment, settlement is now processed through the Clearing Member of the Trading Member. Additionally, the earlier restriction on trading members offering securities in auction for settlements in which they had short-delivered has been removed.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and custodians must check their auction pay-in obligations for securities.
- Securities pay-in must be executed in depositories within depository cut-off timings on June 25, 2026.
- Members must refer to the cutoff rate on the "Auction Offer Entry Screen" before submitting offers.
- Members should review notices related to Corporate Actions (ex-rights, ex-bonus, ex-split, etc.) before entering offers.

## Important Dates

- **June 24, 2026**: Auction offer entry (2:00 PM–2:45 PM), matching, and report generation
- **June 25, 2026**: Auction securities pay-in at 8:00 AM

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice applicable to trading members and custodians participating in Demat Auction 657. Impact is limited to members with short delivery obligations in Rolling Settlement DR-657/2026-27. The removal of the restriction on offering in the same settlement where short delivery occurred may slightly increase auction liquidity. No broad market or systemic impact expected.
