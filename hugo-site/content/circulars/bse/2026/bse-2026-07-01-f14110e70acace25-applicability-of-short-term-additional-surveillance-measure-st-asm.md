## Summary

BSE has notified that certain securities, as listed in the attached Annexure I, have satisfied the criteria for inclusion under the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) Stage I and Stage II frameworks. This action follows the Exchange's ongoing surveillance framework established through prior notices dated September 20, 2024, October 27, 2018, July 19, 2019, December 4, 2020, September 25, 2023, and September 20, 2024.

## Key Points

- Securities meeting ST-ASM Stage I criteria will attract a margin of 50% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- Securities meeting ST-ASM Stage II criteria will attract a margin of 100% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- The ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchange.
- Inclusion under ASM is a market surveillance measure and should not be construed as an adverse action against the concerned company or entity.
- When a scrip moves out of the ASM framework, its price band will be reinstated to the level applicable before shortlisting, unless it remains subject to another surveillance framework.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing ST-ASM framework (as revised via prior circulars) to a fresh set of securities identified as meeting the relevant criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must apply the specified margin rates (50% for Stage I, 100% for Stage II securities) on all open positions as of July 2, 2026, and on new positions created from July 3, 2026 onward, for the securities listed in Annexure I. Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 1, 2026
- Margin applicability: Effective July 3, 2026, applicable to open positions as on July 2, 2026 and new positions created from July 3, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a targeted impact on trading in the specific securities listed in Annexure I, as it significantly increases margin requirements (50-100%) which will raise the cost of trading and may reduce liquidity and speculative activity in these names. Since the specific list of affected securities is contained only in the attached annexure and not in the notice text, the exact market impact cannot be fully assessed without that document. The measure is a standard surveillance mechanism rather than an indicator of company-specific fundamental issues.
