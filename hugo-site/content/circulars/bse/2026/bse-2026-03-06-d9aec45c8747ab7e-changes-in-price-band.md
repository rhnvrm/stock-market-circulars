## Summary

BSE's Surveillance department has revised price bands for 40 equity scrips, effective March 9, 2026. The revised bands range from 1% to 20%, replacing the existing price band levels for each affected scrip. This is a routine surveillance measure to manage price volatility.

## Key Points

- Price bands revised for 40 scrips across the equity segment
- Effective date: March 9, 2026
- Revised bands range from 1% (Seshachal Technologies) to 20% (Coastal Corporation, Jet Freight Logistics, Manomay Tex India, Rita Finance and Leasing, South West Pinnacle Exploration, TVS Electronics)
- Two scrips (KSR Footwear Ltd – 544615, PFL Infotech Ltd – 531769) assigned the most restrictive 2% band
- Issued by BSE Surveillance department (Sanjay Jain, Deputy Vice President; Arpita Joshi, Manager)

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must observe the revised price bands for the listed scrips from March 9, 2026 onwards
- Orders placed outside the revised price bands will be rejected by the exchange system
- Members with queries may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** March 6, 2026
- **Effective Date:** March 9, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular directly impacts trading activity in the 40 affected scrips. Stocks assigned tighter bands (1%–5%) will face greater intraday price movement restrictions, reducing liquidity and potentially widening bid-ask spreads. Stocks moved to wider bands (20%) may see increased price discovery flexibility. These changes are typically triggered by BSE Surveillance monitoring for unusual price movements, suggesting some of these scrips may have exhibited abnormal volatility. Traders and investors holding positions in these scrips should be aware of the new circuit limits before the market opens on March 9, 2026.
