## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that Life Insurance Corporation of India (Scrip Code: 543526, NSE: LICI) has fixed 29 May 2026 as the record date for a 1:1 bonus issue. Shares will trade on an ex-bonus basis from 29 May 2026 (Settlement No. DR-640/2026-2027), and 6,324,997,701 new equity shares of Rs. 10/- each will be allotted on 1 June 2026.

## Key Points

- **Bonus ratio:** 1 bonus equity share (Rs. 10/- face value) for every 1 existing equity share held.
- **Record date:** 29 May 2026.
- **Ex-bonus effective date:** 29 May 2026 (Settlement No. DR-640/2026-2027).
- **Total bonus shares to be allotted:** 6,324,997,701 equity shares.
- **Allotment date:** 1 June 2026, as communicated by the company vide its letter dated 25 May 2026.
- Notice issued by Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President – Listing Compliance and Operations, BSE.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This notice implements existing SEBI and BSE guidelines on corporate action processing for bonus issues, including mandatory ex-date and record-date disclosure to trading members.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must update client positions and margin calculations to reflect the ex-bonus price adjustment effective 29 May 2026.
- Risk systems should account for the theoretical post-bonus share price (approximately 50% of pre-bonus price) from the ex-date.
- Depository participants and back-office systems should process the credit of bonus shares post allotment on 1 June 2026.

## Impact Assessment

LIC is among the largest companies by market capitalisation on Indian exchanges. A 1:1 bonus issue doubles the free-float share count, which will: (1) proportionally reduce the share price on the ex-date, triggering automatic adjustments in index weights, derivatives contracts, and margin parameters; (2) improve share liquidity and retail accessibility due to the lower post-bonus price; (3) require F&O contract adjustments (lot sizes, strike prices) as per SEBI/exchange norms. Investors holding LIC shares as of the record date will receive one additional share for each share held, with no cash outflow required.
