## Summary

BSE has issued a notice regarding the applicability of Long Term Additional Surveillance Measures (LT-ASM) on select securities under the revised LT-ASM framework. The notice covers new inclusions with 100% margin requirements, securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages, lower price band restrictions, and securities being transferred out of the LT-ASM framework to standard trading groups.

## Key Points

- 100% margins will be applicable from June 22, 2026 on all open positions as of June 19, 2026 and on new positions created from June 22, 2026 onwards (Annexure I, Part A)
- Securities have been moved to higher stages of the LT-ASM framework effective June 18, 2026 (Annexure I, Part B)
- Lower Price Band shall be applicable from June 18, 2026 for securities in elevated LT-ASM stages
- Certain securities will continue in LT-ASM but be transferred to T / XT / MT / TS Group effective June 22, 2026 (Annexure I, Part D)
- Securities moving out of the LT-ASM framework effective June 18, 2026 are listed in Annexure II
- A consolidated list of all securities under the LT-ASM framework is provided in Annexure III
- Shortlisting of securities is based on XBRL submissions by respective listed companies and is purely for market surveillance purposes

## Regulatory Changes

This notice is issued in continuation of Exchange notice no. 20180321-46 dated March 21, 2018 and subsequent notices, including revisions to the LT-ASM framework via notices dated October 27, 2018, December 04, 2020, August 09, 2024, and September 20, 2024. The LT-ASM framework is being applied as per these revised guidelines, with periodic reviews of securities placed under the measure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must ensure 100% margin is collected/deposited for all open positions in affected securities as of June 19, 2026, and for all new positions from June 22, 2026
- The LT-ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by exchanges
- Price bands for securities exiting the framework revert to the band applicable before shortlisting, unless the security remains under another surveillance measure
- Members with queries may contact BSE at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **June 18, 2026**: Effective date for movement to higher LT-ASM stages (Annexure I Part B), lower price band applicability, and securities moving out of LT-ASM (Annexure II)
- **June 19, 2026**: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin requirement
- **June 22, 2026**: Effective date for 100% margin on open positions and new positions (Annexure I Part A); transfer of certain securities to T/XT/MT/TS group (Annexure I Part D)

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a high market impact. The imposition of 100% margins significantly increases the cost of holding or taking new positions in affected securities, likely reducing liquidity and trading volumes in those scrips. The transfer to T/XT/MT/TS groups further restricts trading to a trade-for-trade settlement basis, eliminating netting benefits. Lower price bands limit downside price movement. Collectively, these measures are designed to deter speculative activity in securities identified as surveillance concerns, and participants holding affected positions must arrange additional margin by June 22, 2026 to avoid forced liquidation.
