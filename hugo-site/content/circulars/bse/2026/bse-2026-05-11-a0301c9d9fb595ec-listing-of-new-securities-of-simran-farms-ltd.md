## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that 10,18,300 new equity shares of Simran Farms Ltd. (Scrip Code: 519566, ISIN: INE354D01017) are listed and permitted to trade on the Exchange effective Tuesday, May 12, 2026. The shares were issued on a preferential basis to Promoter and Non-Promoter allottees pursuant to the conversion of warrants at an issue price of Rs. 155/- per share (face value Rs. 10/- + premium Rs. 145/-).

## Key Points

- 10,18,300 equity shares of Rs. 10/- each issued at a premium of Rs. 145/- (issue price Rs. 155/-)
- Allotment made to Promoter and Non-Promoter on a preferential basis via conversion of warrants
- New shares rank pari-passu with existing equity shares of the company
- Distribution Numbers: 3791701 to 4810000
- Date of Allotment: 30 March 2026
- ISIN: INE354D01017
- Effective trading date: May 12, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notification under BSE's Listing Operations for securities issued on a preferential basis following warrant conversion.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are informed to note the new securities and their lock-in restrictions before executing trades
- 6,18,300 shares (Dist. Nos. 3791701 to 4410000) are subject to lock-in until 21 November 2027
- 4,00,000 shares (Dist. Nos. 4410001 to 4810000) are subject to lock-in until 21 November 2026
- Lock-in shares are not freely tradable until respective lock-in expiry dates

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment:** 30 March 2026
- **Effective Trading Date:** 12 May 2026
- **Lock-in Expiry (4,00,000 shares):** 21 November 2026
- **Lock-in Expiry (6,18,300 shares):** 21 November 2027

## Impact Assessment

The listing adds 10,18,300 shares to the tradable float of Simran Farms Ltd., representing a preferential allotment at Rs. 155/- per share. However, the effective free-float addition is limited in the near term due to lock-in restrictions covering all newly listed shares until at least November 2026. Market impact is expected to be minimal given the lock-in constraints and the routine nature of this warrant-conversion listing.
