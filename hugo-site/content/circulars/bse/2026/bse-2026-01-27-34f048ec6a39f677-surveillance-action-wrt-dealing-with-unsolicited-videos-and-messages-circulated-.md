## Summary

BSE has issued an update regarding surveillance actions taken against securities where unsolicited videos and messages have been circulated on social media platforms. Effective January 28, 2026, Silverline Technologies Ltd (Scrip Code: 500389, ISIN: INE368A01021) is being removed from the surveillance list. No new securities are being added to the list, and the consolidated list of securities under this surveillance measure is now empty.

## Key Points

- No new securities added to the unsolicited videos surveillance list
- Silverline Technologies Ltd removed from surveillance effective January 28, 2026
- Scrip details: Code 500389, ISIN INE368A01021
- Consolidated list of securities under surveillance is now nil
- This is a routine update to the ongoing surveillance mechanism

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular represents a routine update to the existing surveillance framework for dealing with unsolicited videos and promotional content circulated through social media platforms.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. Market participants should continue to:
- Remain vigilant about unsolicited promotional content on social media
- Report suspicious promotional activities to the exchange
- Avoid engaging with or acting on unsolicited investment advice from social media sources

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: January 28, 2026 - Silverline Technologies Ltd removed from surveillance list

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. The removal of Silverline Technologies from the surveillance list is positive for that specific security as it indicates cessation of problematic social media activity. With no securities currently on the consolidated surveillance list, there are no trading restrictions or enhanced surveillance measures in place related to unsolicited social media videos.

**Operational Impact**: No operational changes for market participants. This update demonstrates BSE's continued monitoring of social media channels for market manipulation attempts and willingness to remove stocks from surveillance once issues are resolved.
