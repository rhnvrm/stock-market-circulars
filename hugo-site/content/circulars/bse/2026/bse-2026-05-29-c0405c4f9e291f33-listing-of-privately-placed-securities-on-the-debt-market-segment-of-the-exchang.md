## Summary

BSE has notified trading members of the listing of five Commercial Papers (CPs) issued on a private placement basis on the BSE Debt Market Segment, effective 29 May 2026. All securities are traded in dematerialised form in standard denominations of Rs.5 Lakhs and multiples, with a tick size of 1 paise.

## Key Points

- Five companies have listed Commercial Papers on BSE Debt Segment via private placement
- Trading is permitted only in demat form in standard denomination of Rs.5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size for all listed securities is 1 paise
- Credit ratings for all issuances are in the highest short-term category (A1+)
- Issuing and paying agents include HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, and Axis Bank

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification under existing BSE Debt Segment rules for privately placed Commercial Papers.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form
- Transactions must be in standard denomination of Rs.5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Members requiring clarification may contact the BSE Debt Department at 22728352 / 8597 / 8995 / 5753 / 8915

## Important Dates

- **Listing/Trading Effective Date:** 29 May 2026
- **Allotment Dates:** 27 May 2026 (GIC Housing Finance, Bajaj Finance, Nuvama Wealth); 29 May 2026 (IGH Holdings, Birla Group Holdings)
- **Redemption Dates:** 24 Aug 2026 (Nuvama Wealth), 25 Aug 2026 (GIC Housing Finance), 28 Aug 2026 (IGH Holdings, Birla Group Holdings), 27 May 2027 (Bajaj Finance)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing notice with low market-wide impact. The five CPs represent short-to-medium term corporate borrowings totalling approximately Rs.11,750 crore face value across all issuers. All carry the highest short-term credit ratings (CRISIL A1+ / ICRA A1+ / CARE A1+), indicating minimal credit risk. Bajaj Finance's issuance has a longer tenor (approximately 1 year), while the remaining four mature within three months. Impact is limited to debt market participants and institutional investors tracking these specific instruments.
