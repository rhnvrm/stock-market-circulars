## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that 10 Non-Convertible Debenture (NCD) series across 8 companies have fixed record dates for interest payment or redemption. Trading in these debentures is suspended around the respective ex-dates and record dates falling between 27 May and 6 June 2026.

## Key Points

- Trading members are advised not to deal in the specified NCD scrips on or around their respective ex-dates.
- The suspension applies to debentures of 8 companies across 10 scrip codes.
- Record dates range from 28 May 2026 to 6 June 2026.
- Settlement DR references are under the 639/2026-2027 to 645/2026-2027 series.
- The notice is issued under Category: Corporate Actions, Segment: Debt, by the Listing Operations department.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is a standard operational notice pursuant to record date fixation by the respective companies for interest payment or redemption of NCDs listed on BSE.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must refrain from executing trades in the listed NCD scrips on and around their respective ex-dates.
- Members should refer to individual settlement DR references for each scrip for settlement obligations.

## Impact Assessment

The trading suspension is limited in scope and affects only holders of the specific NCD series listed. This is a routine action to facilitate smooth interest payment or redemption processing. There is no impact on equity markets. NCD holders in these instruments should note the ex-dates to ensure correct entitlement to interest or redemption proceeds. Market-wide impact is low; the action is procedurally standard for debt segment operations.
