## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260413-5) informing trading members that two T-bills/Government Securities are set to mature on their respective Redemption Dates. Trading in both securities is suspended with effect from April 15, 2026.

## Key Points

- Two government debt instruments are being suspended from trading due to impending maturity/redemption.
- Suspension is effective from **April 15, 2026** (DR- 610/2026-2027).
- Trading members are explicitly advised not to deal in these securities from the effective date.
- Both securities are listed on the Debt Segment of BSE.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This notice is a standard operational action under BSE's listing compliance framework, triggered by the scheduled redemption of the listed debt instruments.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must cease all transactions in the following securities from April 15, 2026:
  - **Scrip Code 800578** | 699GOI26 | ISIN: IN0020230028
  - **Scrip Code 805166** | 91TB170426 | ISIN: IN002025X414
- Members are required to take note of the suspension and ensure no trades are executed in these instruments on or after the effective date.

## Important Dates

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Notice Date | April 13, 2026 |
| Trading Suspension Effective | April 15, 2026 |
| Redemption Date | April 17, 2026 (implied by 91TB170426 naming convention) |

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, low-disruption action. The suspension affects only two specific government securities nearing maturity. Market participants holding these instruments should note the final trading deadline of April 14, 2026 (last trading day before suspension). The 91-day T-bill (91TB170426) and the Government of India bond (699GOI26) will be redeemed at par, so there is no credit risk. Traders and debt fund managers with positions in these scrips should plan accordingly to avoid settlement issues.
