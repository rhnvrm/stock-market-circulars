## Summary

The QSIF Active Asset Allocator Long-Short Fund by Quant AMC will reopen for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions on the BSE StAR MF Platform effective April 24, 2026, following Notice No. 20260401-45 dated April 01, 2026. Six scheme variants across Regular and Direct plans are available. A key restriction applies: redemption, switch-out, SWP, and STP-out transactions are accepted only twice a week.

## Key Points

- Fund reopens for ongoing transactions (subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, STP, SWP) from April 24, 2026
- All six scheme variants are classified under the HYBRID category
- Redemption, switch-out, SWP out, and STP out are restricted to **Tuesdays and Wednesdays only**
- If Tuesday or Wednesday is a non-business day, transactions are processed on the next working/business day
- Redemption payouts released by AMC/RTA per the payout TAT in the scheme master
- Issued by Arunkumar Ganesan (VP - Mutual Fund) and Mahesh Tare (AVP - Mutual Fund)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This notice implements the reopening of a Specialized Investment Fund (SIF) scheme for ongoing transactions as per SEBI's SIF framework, which permits limited liquidity windows for such funds.

## Compliance Requirements

- BSE StAR MF platform members must note the restricted transaction window for exit transactions
- Redemption, switch-out, SWP, and STP-out requests must be submitted on Tuesdays or Wednesdays (or the next business day if these fall on non-business days)
- Subscription and SIP/STP-in transactions follow standard platform timelines
- Members should communicate the liquidity restriction to their clients investing in this SIF scheme

## Important Dates

- **April 24, 2026**: Fund reopens for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions on BSE StAR MF Platform
- **Reference Notice**: No. 20260401-45 dated April 01, 2026 (original closure/NFO notice)
- **Ongoing**: Redemptions/switch-outs accepted every Tuesday and Wednesday

## Impact Assessment

This notice has moderate operational impact for investors and distributors using the BSE StAR MF platform. The twice-weekly redemption restriction is a notable liquidity constraint specific to SIF-category schemes and differs from standard open-ended mutual fund liquidity. Investors must plan redemptions and switches well in advance. For subscriptions and SIP registrations, no unusual restrictions apply. The fund's hybrid long-short strategy is targeted at sophisticated investors, and the limited redemption window is consistent with SEBI's SIF framework design.
