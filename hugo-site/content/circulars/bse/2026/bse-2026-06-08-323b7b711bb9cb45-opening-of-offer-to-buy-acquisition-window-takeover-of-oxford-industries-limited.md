## Summary

BSE has notified trading members and custodians of the opening of an Offer to Buy (Acquisition Window) under the Takeover mechanism for Oxford Industries Limited (OIL). The acquirer, Mr. Saroj Kumar Choudhury, has made an open offer to public shareholders at Rs. 5.00 per equity share in cash, seeking to acquire up to 26% of the company's fully paid-up equity and voting share capital.

## Key Points

- **Acquirer:** Mr. Saroj Kumar Choudhury
- **Target Company:** Oxford Industries Limited (BSE-listed)
- **Offer Price:** Rs. 5.00 per equity share (payable in cash)
- **Shares Targeted:** Up to 15,45,271 fully paid-up equity shares of face value Rs. 10.00 each
- **Stake Sought:** 26.00% of fully paid-up equity and voting share capital
- **Regulatory Basis:** SEBI (SAST) Regulations, 2011 — Regulation 3(1) and Regulation 4 (substantial acquisition with change in control)
- **Offer Mechanism:** BSE Acquisition Window as per SEBI circular CIR/CFD/POLICYCELL/1/2015 and CFD/DCR2/CIR/P/2016/131

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. The offer follows the existing framework established by SEBI circular no. CIR/CFD/POLICYCELL/1/2015 (April 13, 2015) and CFD/DCR2/CIR/P/2016/131 (December 9, 2016), along with BSE's revised guidelines issued via notices in 2017, 2019, 2020, and 2021.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and custodians must facilitate the tender-offer acquisition through the BSE Acquisition Window.
- All market participants must adhere to the Revised Guidelines of the SEBI mechanism for share acquisition through stock exchanges under Takeover regulations.
- Letter of Offer is attached and available on the BSE website for review by market participants.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 8, 2026
- **Offer Opening Date:** Friday, June 12, 2026
- **Offer Closing Date:** Thursday, June 25, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This open offer is limited in scope to shareholders of Oxford Industries Limited and does not have broad market impact. The offer price of Rs. 5.00 per share — below the face value of Rs. 10.00 — indicates the stock trades at a significant discount, reflecting the small/micro-cap nature of the company. The acquisition of 26% under SEBI SAST Regulations 3(1) and 4 implies a change in control is accompanied by this offer. Eligible public shareholders should review the Letter of Offer carefully to evaluate tendering their shares.
