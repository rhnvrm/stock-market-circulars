## Summary

BSE has announced updates to the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective January 21, 2026. Four securities are being newly shortlisted in the ST-ASM framework, while eight securities are moving out of the framework. The circular includes a consolidated list of securities currently under ST-ASM across different stages.

## Key Points

- 4 securities newly shortlisted in Short Term 5/15/30 Days ASM Framework
- 8 securities moving out of ST-ASM Framework
- No securities moving to higher or lower ASM stages within the framework
- Consolidated list includes 13+ securities under ST-ASM Stage I
- Changes effective from January 21, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

The Short Term ASM framework continues to operate with 5/15/30 Days stages to monitor securities showing unusual price movements or excessive volatility. Securities are evaluated periodically and moved in or out based on surveillance criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must note the revised surveillance categorization for affected securities
- Trading members should update their systems to reflect new ST-ASM classifications
- Enhanced due diligence may be required for transactions in ST-ASM securities
- Applicable price bands and trading restrictions for ST-ASM securities must be observed

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** January 21, 2026
- All changes to ST-ASM framework applicable from market opening on this date

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:**
- Trading in newly added securities may experience reduced liquidity due to surveillance measures
- Securities moving out of ST-ASM may see normalized trading conditions
- Investors should exercise caution when trading in ST-ASM securities due to enhanced surveillance

**Operational Impact:**
- Brokers and trading members need to update risk management systems
- Additional margin requirements may apply to ST-ASM securities
- Enhanced monitoring and reporting obligations for transactions in these securities
