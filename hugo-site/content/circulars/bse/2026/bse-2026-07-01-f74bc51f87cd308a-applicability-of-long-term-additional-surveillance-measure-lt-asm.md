## Summary

BSE has issued an update to the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework, referencing multiple prior notices dating back to 2018. The notice announces securities newly placed under LT-ASM, securities escalated to higher LT-ASM stages, securities moving out of the framework, and securities being transferred to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups while remaining under LT-ASM. A consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework is provided as Annexure III.

## Key Points

- 100% margin requirement applicable from July 06, 2026 on all open positions as of July 03, 2026 and on new positions created from July 06, 2026 onwards, for securities listed in Annexure I (Part A)
- Securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages effective July 02, 2026 are listed in Annexure I (Part B)
- Lower Price Band applicable from July 02, 2026 for the securities in Part A/B
- Additional LT-ASM measures per Annexure I (Part D), referencing the June 04, 2021 notice
- Securities continuing in the LT-ASM framework will be transferred to T / XT / MT / TS Group effective July 06, 2026
- Securities exiting the LT-ASM framework effective July 02, 2026 are listed in Annexure II
- Consolidated list of all securities currently under the LT-ASM framework is attached as Annexure III
- Shortlisting is based on periodic review and XBRL submissions by listed companies, and is not to be construed as adverse action against any company

## Regulatory Changes

This notice applies the revised LT-ASM framework established via prior BSE notices (20180321-46, 20181027-1, 20201204-56, 20240809-46, 20240920-63) and the June 2021 notice (20210604-41). No new framework rules are introduced; this is an implementation update listing specific securities affected by additions, escalations, transfers, and exits.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants trading in the affected securities must apply 100% margin on open and new positions per the effective dates
- Brokers/members must account for the lower price bands and group transfers (T/XT/MT/TS) when handling orders in the listed securities
- Members with queries should contact bse.surv@bseindia.com
- Refer to the attached Annexures (I-III) for the specific list of affected ISINs/securities

## Important Dates

- July 02, 2026: Lower price band effective; securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages effective; securities exiting LT-ASM framework effective
- July 03, 2026: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin
- July 06, 2026: 100% margin applicable on open/new positions; securities transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group

## Impact Assessment

The measures increase trading costs and reduce price movement flexibility for securities newly added to or escalated within the LT-ASM framework, likely dampening liquidity and speculative activity in those names. Conversely, securities exiting the framework should see reinstated price bands and reduced margin burden, potentially improving trading conditions. Since specific tickers are only identified in the PDF annexures (not extracted here), the direct stock-level impact is not determinable from the notice text alone; overall market impact is limited to designated surveillance-listed securities rather than the broader market.
