## Summary

BSE has issued a notice informing trading members that certain securities, as listed in the attached Annexure, will be moved to their respective higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework with effect from July 15, 2026. This action is taken under the GSM Framework published on February 23, 2017, and follows a series of earlier exchange notices refining the framework. Securities placed in higher GSM stages face escalating surveillance actions, including trade-for-trade settlement, tighter price bands, higher margin requirements, and additional surveillance deposits.

## Key Points

- Securities specified in the Annexure_GSM attachment will move to higher GSM stages effective July 15, 2026.
- The GSM framework has four stages (I to IV), each carrying progressively stricter surveillance actions.
- Stage I: 100% applicable margin rate and price band of 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, and 50% Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of trade value from buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, trading permitted only once a week (Monday or first trading day), and 100% ASD from buyers.
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III but with no upward price movement permitted.
- All trades in these securities will settle on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing GSM Framework (established via notices dated February 23, 2017, March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023) to a fresh set of securities being escalated to higher GSM stages.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin rates, price bands, ASD requirements, and trading frequency restrictions to the specified securities from the effective date.
- Members must ensure trade-to-trade settlement is followed for securities in Stage II and above, with no netting off permitted.
- Members should refer to the GSM FAQ or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 15, 2026 — securities move to their respective higher GSM stages.
- Notice Date: July 14, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

Securities moved to higher GSM stages will experience significantly reduced liquidity due to trade-for-trade settlement, restricted price movement, and higher capital requirements (via ASD) for buyers. Stage III and IV restrictions, limiting trading to once a week, can severely impact price discovery and shareholder ability to exit positions. Market participants holding or trading the affected securities (listed in the Annexure, not detailed in the notice text) should expect increased volatility risk and reduced trading flexibility until the stocks potentially move back to lower stages or exit the GSM framework.
