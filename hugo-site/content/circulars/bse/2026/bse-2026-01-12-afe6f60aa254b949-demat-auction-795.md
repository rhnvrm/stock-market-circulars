## Summary

BSE has published the list of securities to be auctioned under demat auction for Settlement No. 795 dated January 12, 2026. The auction includes 103 different securities from various companies with specified quantities to be auctioned. This is a routine settlement mechanism to close out pending delivery obligations.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 795
- Auction Date: January 12, 2026
- Total securities listed: 103 scrips
- Quantities range from as low as 1 share to as high as 9,999 shares
- Notable large quantities include JAIPRAKASH POWER (9,999 shares), VODAFONE IDEA (5,800 shares), and JONJUA OVERSEAS (4,286 shares)
- Includes securities across various sectors and market capitalizations

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational circular for demat auction settlement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and participants with obligations in the listed securities must participate in the auction
- Settlement must be completed as per BSE's standard auction settlement procedures
- Participants should ensure adequate funds/securities availability for auction settlement

## Important Dates

- **Settlement Date**: January 12, 2026 (Settlement No. 795)
- Auction timing and specific settlement schedule to follow BSE's standard auction procedures

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - Affects specific securities where delivery obligations remain pending. Creates short-term price discovery mechanism for these securities during auction.

**Operational Impact**: Medium - Brokers with positions in listed securities must ensure participation and settlement. Failure to participate may result in penalties and closeout at auction prices.

**Investor Impact**: Medium - Investors whose brokers have pending obligations in these securities may see their positions closed through auction mechanism. Auction prices may differ from market prices.

## Scrip Details

The auction includes securities from diverse sectors including banking (DCB Bank, Karnataka Bank, PNB, Yes Bank), metals (Hindalco, Tata Steel, Hindustan Zinc), technology (HCL Tech, Infronics, MTAR Tech), FMCG (ITC), and various small and mid-cap companies. The wide range indicates varied delivery failures across market segments.
