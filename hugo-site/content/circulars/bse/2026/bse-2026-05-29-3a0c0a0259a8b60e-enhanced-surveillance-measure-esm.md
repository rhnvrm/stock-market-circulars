## Summary

BSE has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework effective June 2026. Newly shortlisted securities will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open and new positions from June 2, 2026, and will be shifted from the Rolling Settlement segment to Trade-for-Trade settlement. Securities moving to Stage II will additionally face a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction. Certain securities are being downgraded to Stage I, and others are being removed from the framework entirely, as detailed in the attached annexures.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I are newly included under ESM and will attract **minimum 100% margin** w.e.f. **June 2, 2026** on all open positions as on June 1, 2026 and new positions created thereafter.
- Newly included securities will be shifted from **Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade (T2T)** segment w.e.f. June 2, 2026.
- Securities moving to **Stage II** will be subject to Trade-for-Trade with a **2% price band** and **Periodic Call Auction** w.e.f. June 1, 2026.
- Securities meeting criteria for downgrade will be moved to **Stage I** under ESM w.e.f. June 1, 2026 (details in Annexure I).
- Securities moving **out of the ESM framework** w.e.f. June 1, 2026 are listed in Annexure II.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under ESM is provided in **Annexure III**.
- ESM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by exchanges.
- Shortlisting under ESM is purely for market surveillance purposes and does not constitute adverse action against the concerned company.
- Price bands for securities exiting ESM revert to their pre-ESM bands, unless those scrips are under another surveillance measure.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the ESM framework last revised via notice no. 20250725-61 dated July 25, 2025. It references the original framework established via notice no. 20230602-44 dated June 2, 2023, with subsequent amendments in July 2023, August 2024, September 2024, and October 2024.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Members/brokers** must ensure that minimum 100% margin is collected and maintained on positions in newly included ESM securities from June 2, 2026.
- Members must comply with Trade-for-Trade settlement rules for all shortlisted securities; delivery-based settlement is mandatory—no netting of positions.
- For Stage II securities, members must adhere to the 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism.
- Members with clarifications may contact BSE Surveillance at **bse.surv@bseindia.com**.

## Important Dates

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| May 29, 2026 | Notice issued |
| June 1, 2026 | Stage I downgrades effective; Stage II (2% band + Periodic Call Auction) effective; ESM exits effective |
| June 2, 2026 | 100% margin requirement effective on new positions; Trade-for-Trade shift effective |

## Impact Assessment

This circular has **high market impact** for traders and investors holding or considering positions in the affected securities:

- **Liquidity**: Trade-for-Trade settlement eliminates netting, significantly reducing liquidity and increasing transaction costs for affected scrips.
- **Margins**: The 100% margin requirement substantially raises the capital requirement for taking positions, likely suppressing speculative activity.
- **Stage II constraints**: The 2% daily price band combined with Periodic Call Auction mechanics sharply restricts intraday price movement and trading flexibility.
- **Portfolio management**: Investors holding ESM securities may face restricted exit options and higher costs; institutional participants may be required to reassess exposure.
- **Positive note**: Securities exiting the framework (Annexure II) regain normal settlement and pre-ESM price bands, improving their tradability.
