## Summary

BSE Bulletin No. 198/2025-2026 dated January 16, 2026, announces book closure dates, record dates, and corporate actions for 21 securities. Key highlights include dividend declarations by Angel One Limited (Rs. 23 per share), Central Bank of India (Rs. 0.20 per share), D.B. Corp Limited (Rs. 2 per share), and Clean Science and Technology Limited. The bulletin also covers interest payments and redemptions for various privately placed debentures and commercial papers.

## Key Points

- Angel One Limited: Record date January 21, 2026 for first interim dividend of Rs. 23 per share (230%), payment on February 13, 2026
- Central Bank of India: Record date January 23, 2026 for third interim dividend of Rs. 0.20 per share (2%)
- D.B. Corp Limited: Record date January 22, 2026 for interim dividend of Rs. 2 per share (20%)
- Clean Science and Technology Limited: Record date February 6, 2026 for interim dividend
- Multiple commercial paper redemptions scheduled between January 19-February 4, 2026 by ICICI Securities, JSW Energy, KEC International, and Kotak Mahindra Investments
- Interest payment dates announced for privately placed debentures of Akara Capital, Ashiana Housing, Bank of Baroda, Inland Waterways Authority, and Lendingkart Finance
- Damji Shamji Realty: Part redemption of NCDs with record date January 22, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced in this bulletin.

## Compliance Requirements

- Investors must hold securities on the respective record dates to be eligible for dividends, interest payments, or redemptions
- Trading flag 0 indicates no trading restrictions; flag 1 indicates normal trading continues
- Securities will trade ex-dividend/ex-interest from the day following the record date

## Impact Assessment

This bulletin has medium impact for investors and market participants:

- **Equity Investors**: Shareholders of Angel One, Central Bank of India, D.B. Corp, and Clean Science must hold shares on record dates to receive dividends
- **Debt Investors**: Holders of NCDs and commercial papers need to track interest payment and redemption dates for cash flow planning
- **Market Liquidity**: Ex-dividend trading may cause price adjustments in affected stocks
- **Corporate Actions**: Multiple securities undergoing corporate actions may see increased trading activity around record dates
- **Income Planning**: Investors can plan for dividend income and debt redemptions based on announced dates
