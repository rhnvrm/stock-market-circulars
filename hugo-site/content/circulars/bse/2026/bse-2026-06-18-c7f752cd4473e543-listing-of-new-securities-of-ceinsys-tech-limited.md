## Summary

BSE has listed 30,96,515 new equity shares of CEINSYS TECH LIMITED (Scrip Code: 538734, ISIN: INE016Q01014) with effect from Friday, June 19, 2026. The shares were issued at a price of Rs. 559.90/- each (face value Rs. 10/- + premium Rs. 549.90/-) to Promoter and Non-Promoter on a preferential basis pursuant to conversion of warrants. These new shares rank pari-passu with existing equity shares.

## Key Points

- **Scrip Code:** 538734
- **ISIN:** INE016Q01014
- **Number of new shares:** 30,96,515 equity shares
- **Issue price:** Rs. 559.90/- per share (Rs. 10/- face value + Rs. 549.90/- premium)
- **Date of allotment:** March 18, 2026
- **Effective trading date:** June 19, 2026
- **Distribution numbers:** 17841147 to 20937661
- Shares rank pari-passu with existing equity shares
- Issued to both Promoter and Non-Promoter categories

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification following preferential allotment via warrant conversion under applicable SEBI regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are informed to update their systems to reflect the new securities eligible for trading from June 19, 2026.
- Lock-in restrictions must be observed for the respective tranches as detailed below.

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment:** March 18, 2026
- **Effective date for trading:** June 19, 2026 (Friday)
- **Lock-in expiry (Tranche 1):** December 30, 2026 — 14,89,086 shares (Dist. Nos. 17841147 to 19330232)
- **Lock-in expiry (Tranche 2):** December 31, 2027 — 16,07,429 shares (Dist. Nos. 19330233 to 20937661)

## Impact Assessment

The listing adds approximately 30.97 lakh new equity shares to the tradable universe for CEINSYS TECH LIMITED. However, the majority of shares (all 30,96,515) are subject to lock-in restrictions, with one tranche locked until end of 2026 and the larger tranche until end of 2027, limiting immediate float impact. The preferential allotment at Rs. 559.90/- signals capital raising activity likely for business expansion or debt reduction. Market impact is expected to be moderate given the lock-in constraints.
