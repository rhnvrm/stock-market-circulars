## Summary

360 ONE Mutual Fund is launching the DYNASIF Equity Ex-Top 100 Long-Short Fund, a Specialized Investment Fund (SIF), on the BSE StAR MF Platform. The NFO is open from June 5, 2026 to June 19, 2026, with SIP facility available. Six scheme variants are offered across Regular and Direct Plans with Growth, IDCW Payout, and IDCW Reinvestment options. Tentative allotment is scheduled for June 24, 2026.

## Key Points

- NFO period: June 5, 2026 to June 19, 2026 on BSE StAR MF Platform
- Six scheme variants available under Regular and Direct Plans (Growth, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment)
- ISINs assigned: INF579M30133, INF579M30141, INF579M30158, INF579M30166, INF579M30174, INF579M30182
- Tentative allotment date: June 24, 2026
- Clear funds must be available in the ICCL account by 6:00 PM on June 24, 2026
- NFO schemes extended till midnight on June 19, 2026 for Lumpsum purchases only; SIP/XSIP registrations have an earlier cutoff
- Cheque and OTM payments accepted during NFO period subject to clear fund receipt by the allotment deadline

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard NFO launch notice under the Specialized Investment Fund (SIF) category, a product framework introduced by SEBI.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure transactions for which clients do not initiate funds by 9:00 AM on T+1 day are cancelled, and a CC with 'N' flag is reported to the RTA/AMC.
- Transactions with client-initiated payment remain pending until 3:00 PM on the last day of funds pay-out to the AMC for the NFO, or T+5 day, whichever is earlier.
- Switch-out funds received on or after the allotment date will not be considered for allotment at the AMC/RTA end.
- Clear funds must be in the ICCL account by 6:00 PM on June 24, 2026, for allotment eligibility.

## Impact Assessment

This notice is relevant to BSE StAR MF platform members, distributors, and investors seeking exposure to a long-short equity SIF strategy from 360 ONE AMC. The fund targets equity securities outside the top 100 companies by market capitalisation using a long-short approach, which may appeal to sophisticated/accredited investors eligible for SIF products. There is no systemic market impact; operational compliance requirements apply to member firms handling NFO transactions on the platform.
