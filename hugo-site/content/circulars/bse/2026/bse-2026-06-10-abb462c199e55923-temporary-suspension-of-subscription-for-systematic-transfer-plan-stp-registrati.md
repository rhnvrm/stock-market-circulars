## Summary

BSE has notified members of a temporary suspension of fresh Systematic Transfer Plan (STP-In) registrations for select overseas fund-of-fund schemes under PGIM India Mutual Fund, effective June 11, 2026. This follows an earlier notice (20260605-41) restricting SIP registrations under the same fund house. The restrictions have been implemented on the BSE StAR MF Platform.

## Key Points

- Fresh STP-In registrations will not be accepted after the cut-off time on June 11, 2026
- Daily and Weekly frequency STP registrations will cease after the cut-off on June 10, 2026 (one day earlier)
- Existing Monthly and Quarterly STP registrations made prior to the effective date will continue to be processed at the AMC/RTA end without changes
- Redemption, Switch-out, and STP-out transactions remain fully permitted without any amount restriction
- Changes apply to three specific overseas/global fund-of-fund schemes only

## Regulatory Changes

PGIM AMC issued a Notice-cum-Addendum restricting new STP-In subscriptions for the affected schemes, which BSE has implemented on its StAR MF Platform. This extends the earlier SIP restriction notice (20260605-41 dated June 5, 2026) to cover STP registrations as well.

## Compliance Requirements

- Exchange members must cease accepting fresh STP-In registrations for the three specified PGIM India schemes from their respective cut-off times
- Daily/weekly STP registrations must stop from June 10, 2026 cut-off
- Monthly/quarterly STP registrations must stop from June 11, 2026 cut-off
- Members should continue processing existing eligible STP registrations and permit all exit transactions (redemption, switch-out, STP-out) without restriction

## Important Dates

- **June 10, 2026**: Deadline for daily and weekly frequency STP-In registrations (cut-off time applies)
- **June 11, 2026**: Deadline for all fresh STP-In registrations including monthly and quarterly frequency (cut-off time applies)
- **June 5, 2026**: Earlier related notice (20260605-41) on SIP restrictions for PGIM India

## Impact Assessment

The three affected schemes are overseas fund-of-fund products investing in global equities and real estate securities:
1. PGIM India Global Equity Opportunities Fund of Fund
2. PGIM India Emerging Markets Equity Fund of Fund
3. PGIM India Global Select Real Estate Securities Fund of Fund

The restriction is likely linked to regulatory caps on overseas investments by Indian mutual funds. Impact is moderate — existing STP investors in monthly/quarterly frequency are unaffected, and all exit options remain open. New investors and those wishing to set up fresh systematic transfer arrangements into these schemes are directly impacted. The restriction mirrors the pattern seen with SIP restrictions on the same fund house notified earlier.
