## Summary

Reserve Bank of India, in consultation with the Government of India, has released the auction calendar for Treasury Bills for the quarter ending March 2026 (January-March 2026). The calendar specifies auction dates, issuance dates, and notified amounts for 91-day, 182-day, and 364-day T-Bills totaling ₹3,84,000 crore for the quarter.

## Key Points

- Total T-Bill issuance planned: ₹3,84,000 crore for Q4 FY2025-26
- 91-day T-Bills: ₹1,44,000 crore across 12 auctions
- 182-day T-Bills: ₹1,44,000 crore across 12 auctions
- 364-day T-Bills: ₹96,000 crore across 12 auctions
- Auctions scheduled weekly, typically on Tuesdays/Wednesdays
- RBI retains flexibility to modify amounts and timing with due notice
- Calendar subject to change for holidays or other circumstances

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine quarterly auction calendar announcement governed by existing General Notification No. F.No.4(2)-B(W&M)/2018 dated March 26, 2025.

## Compliance Requirements

- Auctions subject to terms and conditions specified in General Notification No. F.No.4(2)-B(W&M)/2018 dated March 26, 2025 (as amended)
- Market participants to note that RBI may modify indicated amounts and timing after giving due notice
- Changes will be communicated through press releases

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - The quarterly T-Bill calendar provides transparency for short-term government borrowing and helps institutional investors, banks, and primary dealers plan their liquidity management and investment strategies.

**Operational Impact:** Standard operational process for debt market participants. The calendar shows increased weekly issuance from February onwards (from ₹29,000 crore to ₹34,000 crore), reflecting higher government borrowing requirements in the latter part of the quarter.

**Investor Impact:** Provides clarity on investment opportunities in risk-free government securities across three tenors, enabling better portfolio allocation and cash management for institutional and retail investors in the debt market segment.
