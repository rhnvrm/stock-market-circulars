## Summary

BSE has informed members of subscription restrictions on TATA GOLD ETF FUND OF FUND implemented on the BSE StAR MF Platform, pursuant to a Notice-cum-Addendum issued by Tata Mutual Fund. Fresh and additional purchases via lump sum mode and switch-ins are capped at ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month (at first holder level), effective after the 3:00 PM cut-off on June 8, 2026.

## Key Points

- Fresh/additional purchases through lump sum mode and switch-in are restricted to ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month (first holder level)
- The restriction applies to transactions received after the 3:00 PM cut-off on June 8, 2026
- Redemption, switch-out, and STP-out transactions are permitted without any amount restriction
- Subscription orders above ₹10,00,000 triggered to the RTA on or after 3:00 PM on June 8, 2026 will be rejected and refunded to investors after funds are received from AMC/RTA

## Regulatory Changes

Tata Mutual Fund issued a Notice-cum-Addendum introducing a monthly subscription cap of ₹10,00,000 per PAN (at first holder level) for lump sum purchases and switch-ins into TATA GOLD ETF FUND OF FUND. BSE has implemented these changes on the BSE StAR MF Platform accordingly.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members/distributors must ensure subscription orders for TATA GOLD ETF FUND OF FUND do not exceed ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month via lump sum or switch-in modes
- Orders above the limit submitted after the cut-off will be rejected; members should inform investors accordingly
- Refunds for rejected orders will be processed after funds are received from AMC/RTA

## Important Dates

- **June 8, 2026, 3:00 PM**: Effective cut-off time for the subscription limit restriction
- Subscription orders triggered to RTA on or after 3:00 PM on June 8, 2026 above ₹10,00,000 will be rejected

## Impact Assessment

This restriction primarily affects high-value investors seeking large lump sum exposures to gold through the fund-of-fund route on BSE StAR MF. Investors with monthly investment requirements above ₹10,00,000 will need to use alternative investment channels or split investments across family members (separate PANs). Redemption and exit transactions remain unrestricted, so existing investors are not impacted in their ability to exit positions.
