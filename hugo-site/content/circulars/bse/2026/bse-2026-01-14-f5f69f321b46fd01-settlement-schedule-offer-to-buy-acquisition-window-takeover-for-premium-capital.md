## Summary

BSE has announced the settlement calendar for the Offer to Buy via Acquisition Window (Takeover) for PREMIUM CAPITAL MARKET AND INVESTMENTS LIMITED. The bidding window will be open from January 16, 2026 to January 30, 2026, with settlement completion in mid-February 2026. Members and custodians must use the early pay-in mechanism for demat shares through the depository system.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 2026008
- Bid Start Date: January 16, 2026
- Bid End Date: January 30, 2026
- Obligation Download: February 12, 2026
- Settlement Date: February 13, 2026
- Demat shares must be delivered through Early Pay-in of Shares mechanism available in both depositories
- Market Type "(Takeover)" and Settlement No. 2026008 to be used in depository system
- CDSL & NSDL early pay-in securities will be earmarked/blocked in clients' accounts
- No EPN file upload required by members and custodians

## Regulatory Changes

This circular follows the Revised Guidelines of Mechanism for acquisition of shares through Stock Exchange as referenced in Notice No.20200528-32 dated May 28, 2020.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members/Custodians must ensure early pay-in of securities is reflected in BSE/ICCL systems before prescribed timelines
- Complete early pay-in process in Depository system sufficiently in advance before BSE OTB Bidding window closure
- Securities must be earmarked/blocked in requisite market type and settlement number in client accounts
- Members should refer to MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo file for client-wise stamp duty charges
- For clarifications, contact ICCL Help Desk at 022-69158590/45720490/45720690 or iccl.helpdesk@icclindia.com

## Impact Assessment

This takeover settlement affects shareholders of PREMIUM CAPITAL MARKET AND INVESTMENTS LIMITED who wish to participate in the acquisition window. Trading members and custodians need to ensure proper coordination with depositories for timely early pay-in of securities. The 15-day bidding window provides adequate time for participants to tender their shares. The impact is specific to this company's shareholders and does not affect broader market operations.
