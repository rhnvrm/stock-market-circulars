## Summary

BSE has issued a Master Circular consolidating all circulars issued by its Listing Compliance Department as on March 31, 2026. This single comprehensive document covers policy and operational matters related to listing compliance across equity, debt, and standard operating procedures (SOP) segments. All listed entities are required to take note and comply accordingly.

## Key Points

- Consolidation of all previous circulars issued by BSE's Listing Compliance Department up to March 31, 2026
- Three major attachments: MasterCircularEquity2026, MasterCircularDebt2026, and MasterCircularSOP2026
- Applicable to all listed entities across segments
- Serves as the single reference document for listing compliance policy and operational matters
- Contact for clarifications: bse.regulation30@bseindia.com

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is not introducing new regulations but rather consolidating all existing listing compliance circulars into three unified master documents covering:
- Equity listing compliance (MasterCrcularEquity2026)
- Debt listing compliance (MasterCircularDebt2026)
- Standard Operating Procedures (MasterCircularSOP2026)

## Compliance Requirements

- All listed entities must review and comply with the consolidated master circular
- Entities should use this document as the primary reference for all listing compliance obligations
- Any clarifications should be directed to bse.regulation30@bseindia.com
- Previous individual circulars are superseded by this consolidated document

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: April 30, 2026
- Consolidation Reference Date: March 31, 2026 (all circulars issued up to this date are included)

## Impact Assessment

This master circular has broad and significant impact on all BSE-listed entities. By consolidating multiple circulars into a single reference document, it simplifies compliance navigation for listed companies. Compliance officers and legal teams at listed entities must review the three attached documents (equity, debt, and SOP) to ensure alignment with all current listing obligations. Failure to comply with requirements outlined in the master circular may attract regulatory action from BSE's Listing Compliance-Monitoring department.
