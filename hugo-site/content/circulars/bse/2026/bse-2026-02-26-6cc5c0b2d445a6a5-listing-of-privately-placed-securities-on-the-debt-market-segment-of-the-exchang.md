## Summary

BSE has admitted five new commercial paper (CP) issuances to trading on its Debt Segment effective February 26, 2026. All securities were issued on a private placement basis and are available exclusively in dematerialised form, with a standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof, and a tick size of 1 paise.

## Key Points

- Five commercial papers from five companies (six total issuances, with Axis Finance having two) are now listed on the BSE Debt Segment
- All CPs trade only in demat form at standard lot size of Rs. 5 Lakhs
- Tick size: 1 paise for all listed instruments
- Credit ratings range from CARE A1 to CRISIL A1+ / ICRA A1+, indicating high short-term credit quality
- Interest payment for all CPs is cumulative, paid at redemption
- For clarifications, trading members may contact the BSE Debt Department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine admission-to-trading notice under existing BSE debt segment listing norms.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form
- Trades must be in standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Members should use the ISIN numbers provided for each instrument
