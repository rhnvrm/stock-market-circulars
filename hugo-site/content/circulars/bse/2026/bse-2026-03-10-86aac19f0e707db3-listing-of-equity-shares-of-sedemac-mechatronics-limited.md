## Summary

SEDEMAC MECHATRONICS LIMITED has submitted its post-issue shareholding pattern as of 10 March 2026 under Regulation 31(1)(a) of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015. The filing covers the equity share class and discloses the holding structure across promoter & promoter group, public, and non-promoter non-public categories.

## Key Points

- Filing type: Shareholding Pattern under Reg. 31(1)(a) — Post Issue
- Security class disclosed: Equity
- Record date: 10 March 2026
- The company has issued partly paid-up shares (YES)
- The company has issued convertible securities (YES)
- The company has issued warrants (YES)
- No ESOPs outstanding, no depository receipts, no differential voting rights shares
- No promoter shares are pledged, under NDU, or otherwise encumbered
- No locked-in shares reported
- No significant beneficial owner declared

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced by this circular. It is a standard disclosure filing mandated under SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015.

## Compliance Requirements

- SEDEMAC MECHATRONICS LIMITED is required to submit this shareholding pattern disclosure to BSE as a listed entity.
- Where 'NO' is declared for any specific instrument category (e.g., partly paid shares, warrants, ESOPs), those columns are not displayed on the exchange website and the values are treated as zero by default.
- The tabular format (Table I — Summary Statement) must cover all categories: Promoter & Promoter Group (A), Public (B), and Non-Promoter Non-Public (C/C1).

## Important Dates

- Post-issue shareholding pattern as on: **10 March 2026**
- Filed with BSE on: **10 March 2026**

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine regulatory disclosure with no direct market or operational impact. The filing confirms the post-issue capital structure of SEDEMAC MECHATRONICS LIMITED following its listing on BSE. Investors and analysts can use this data to assess promoter holding levels, public float, and any encumbrances on promoter shares. The presence of convertible securities and warrants (declared YES) may have a modest dilution implication that investors should factor into fully diluted share capital calculations.
