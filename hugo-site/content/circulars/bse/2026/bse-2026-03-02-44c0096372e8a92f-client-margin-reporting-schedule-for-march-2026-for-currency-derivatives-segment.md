## Summary

Indian Clearing Corporation Limited (ICCL) has issued the client margin reporting schedule for the Currency Derivatives Segment for March 2026. The circular outlines trade dates, the last date by which margin reporting must be completed, and the corresponding penalty dates for late or non-compliance.

## Key Points

- The schedule covers trade dates from 02-03-2026 through 30-03-2026.
- Each trade date has a corresponding last date for margin reporting (typically 5–7 trading days later) and a penalty date (the following trading day).
- Members of BSE trading in the Currency Derivatives Segment are required to comply with these deadlines.
- The circular is issued by Manoj Panchal and Omkar Hatkar, both Deputy Managers at ICCL.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is a routine periodic schedule notification consistent with existing margin reporting obligations under SEBI's risk management framework for derivatives segments.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must submit client margin reports for each Currency Derivatives trade date by the specified last date of margin reporting.
- Failure to report within the stipulated deadline will result in penalties being levied on the penalty date.
- Members should update their internal compliance and back-office systems to reflect this schedule.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on BSE members active in the Currency Derivatives Segment. Compliance and risk management teams must ensure timely client margin reporting for each trade date listed. Delays beyond the last reporting date will trigger penalties on the penalty date. The schedule extends into early April 2026, so members should account for this in their end-of-month and start-of-April workflows.
