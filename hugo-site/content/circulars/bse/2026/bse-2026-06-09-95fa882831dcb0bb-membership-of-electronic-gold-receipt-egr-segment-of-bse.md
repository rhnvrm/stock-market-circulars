## Summary

BSE has issued updated membership guidelines for the Electronic Gold Receipt (EGR) segment, referencing the earlier notice no. 20221003-22 dated October 03, 2022. The circular details the complete procedure, fee structure, networth requirements, and Base Minimum Capital (BMC) norms for both new applicants and existing trading members of the Exchange.

## Key Points

- New applicants must obtain SEBI registration under the SEBI (Stockbrokers) Regulations, 2026 to become an Exclusive Trading Member of the EGR segment.
- Networth requirement for EGR segment is Rs. 1 crore.
- Admission fee for new members is Rs. 50,000 plus applicable GST; existing trading members are exempt from this fee.
- Annual Membership Subscription fee is Rs. 1,00,000 plus applicable GST.
- SEBI one-time registration fee is Rs. 50,000 plus applicable GST, payable directly to SEBI via NEFT/RTGS.
- Existing trading members of the Exchange can apply via the BEFS portal with zero Membership Deposit, Admission Fees, and Processing Fees.

## Regulatory Changes

The notice updates the membership procedure for the EGR segment in line with SEBI (Stockbrokers) Regulations, 2026, superseding the earlier October 2022 notice.

## Compliance Requirements

- New applicants (non-members of any recognized Stock Exchange/Clearing Corporation) must download and submit the application form from the BSE website.
- New applicants who are already registered members of a recognized Stock Exchange/Clearing Corporation must use the separate application form provided.
- Existing BSE trading members must submit their EGR segment admission application through the BEFS portal using the provided user manual and checklist documents.
- All new trading members must maintain the applicable Base Minimum Capital (BMC) deposit based on their trading category:
  - Proprietary trading only (no Algo): Rs. 10 lakhs
  - Client trading only (no Algo): Rs. 15 lakhs
  - Proprietary + Client trading (no Algo): Rs. 25 lakhs
  - Proprietary trading only (with Algo): Rs. 50 lakhs
  - Client trading only (with Algo): Rs. 50 lakhs
  - Proprietary + Client trading (with Algo): Rs. 50 lakhs

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: June 09, 2026
- Reference Notice: 20221003-22 dated October 03, 2022

## Impact Assessment

This circular is relevant primarily to brokers, financial intermediaries, and entities looking to participate in gold-backed digital instruments via the EGR segment. The zero-cost entry for existing BSE members lowers the barrier to EGR participation, potentially increasing liquidity in the segment. New entrants face a combined upfront cost of Rs. 1,00,000 plus GST (exchange admission + SEBI registration) along with networth and BMC requirements.
