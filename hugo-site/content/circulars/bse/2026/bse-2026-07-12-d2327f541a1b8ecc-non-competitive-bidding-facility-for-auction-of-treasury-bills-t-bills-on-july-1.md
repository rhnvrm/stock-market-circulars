## Summary

BSE has issued a notice informing trading members and investors about the Non-Competitive Bidding (NCB) facility available for the auction of three Treasury Bills (T-Bills) to be conducted by RBI on July 15, 2026. Bidding will be facilitated through the NCB-GSec module of the Exchange's iBBS web-based system, in continuation of the facility launched vide Exchange circular no. 20180423-42 dated April 26, 2018.

## Key Points

- RBI press release dated July 10, 2026 announced the sale of three Treasury Bills through auction on July 15, 2026.
- Bidding is available via the NCB-GSec module at https://ibbs.bseindia.com.
- Three securities are on offer: 91-Day T-Bill (091T141026), 182-Day T-Bill (182T130127), and 364-Day T-Bill (364T150727).
- Issue sizes: 91-Day – Rs. 9,000 Cr; 182-Day – Rs. 8,000 Cr; 364-Day – Rs. 7,000 Cr.
- Minimum lot and multiples: Rs. 100; Minimum bid amount: Rs. 10,000; Maximum amount per bid: Rs. 450 Cr (91-Day), Rs. 400 Cr (182-Day), Rs. 350 Cr (364-Day).
- Queries can be directed to Trading Operations (user ID/password issues and bidding queries) or ICCL Operations (obligation and pay-in related queries).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; this is an operational notice implementing the existing NCB-GSec facility (introduced via circular 20180423-42) for the specific RBI auction scheduled on July 15, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and direct investors intending to participate in the non-competitive bidding must submit bids within the specified windows via the NCB-GSec module.
- Members must ensure timely obligation and pay-in coordination with ICCL Operations for settlement.

## Important Dates

- Bid Collection Start Date: July 13, 2026, from 10:00 am onwards (24-hour availability)
- Bid Collection End Date (Direct Investors): July 14, 2026, till 5:00 pm
- Bid Collection End Date (Members): July 15, 2026, till 8:00 am
- Auction Date: July 15, 2026
- Settlement Date: July 16, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, procedural notice with low market impact. It primarily affects debt segment trading members and direct investors participating in the non-competitive bidding facility for government T-Bills. No equity stocks are impacted, and there is no change to existing regulatory framework or trading mechanisms beyond standard auction logistics.
