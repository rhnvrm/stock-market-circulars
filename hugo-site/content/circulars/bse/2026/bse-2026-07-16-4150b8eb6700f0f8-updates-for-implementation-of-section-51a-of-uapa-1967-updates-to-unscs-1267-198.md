## Summary

BSE has issued a notice conveying a communication from SEBI regarding updates to the United Nations Security Council's (UNSC) 1267/1989 ISIL (Da'esh) & Al-Qaida Sanctions List. The update reflects an amendment to one entry per UNSC Note SC/16407 dated July 08, 2026. Members are required to implement Section 51A of the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act (UAPA), 1967, including screening existing and future accounts against the updated sanctions list.

## Key Points

- SEBI forwarded UNSC Note SC/16407 dated July 08, 2026 regarding amendment of one entry in the 1267 (1999) Sanctions List.
- Annexure-A contains the amendment details; Annexure-B contains the related UNSC press release.
- Updated sanctions lists are accessible on the UN Security Council website for both the ISIL (Da'esh)/Al-Qaida list (1267/1989) and the Taliban list (1988).
- Members must scan all existing and future accounts to ensure no linkage to listed entities or individuals, per SEBI's AML/CFT guidelines dated June 06, 2024.
- De-listing requests received from listed parties must be forwarded to the Joint Secretary (CTCR), MHA and copied to the Joint Secretary (UNP), MEA.
- Individuals/entities seeking removal from the ISIL (Da'esh) and Al-Qaida Sanctions List can apply to the UN's independent Ombudsperson.
- Updates on UNSC Sanctions List amendments are also published on the SEBI website under "Media and Notifications."

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulation is introduced; this is an update to the existing UNSC Sanctions List under the Central Government Order dated February 02, 2021, which implements Section 51A of UAPA, 1967. One entry in the 1267 (1999) list has been amended.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must screen all existing and prospective client accounts against the amended sanctions list.
- Any accounts found linked to listed individuals/entities must be handled per the Central Government Order and SEBI AML/CFT guidelines.
- Forward any de-listing requests received to the designated MHA and MEA contacts.
- Regularly monitor the SEBI website for further updates to the UNSC Sanctions List.

## Important Dates

- UNSC Note SC/16407: July 08, 2026
- Notice issued by BSE: July 16, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine regulatory compliance notice with no direct impact on trading, listing, or specific stocks. It primarily affects the compliance and AML/KYC functions of trading members, who must ensure sanctions screening processes are updated. No market-wide or stock-specific disruption is expected.
