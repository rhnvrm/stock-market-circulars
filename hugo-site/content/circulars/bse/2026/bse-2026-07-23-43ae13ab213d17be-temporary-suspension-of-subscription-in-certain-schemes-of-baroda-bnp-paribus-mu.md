## Summary

BSE has informed members that BARODA BNP Paribas Mutual Fund has temporarily suspended lumpsum investments (fresh/additional purchases), switch-in transactions, and new Systematic Investment Plan (SIP/XSIP) and Systematic Transfer Plan (STP-IN) registrations under the BARODA BNP Paribas Funds Aqua Fund of Fund scheme on the BSE StAR MF Platform, effective from July 22, 2026.

## Key Points

- The suspension applies to BARODA BNP PARIBAS FUNDS AQUA FUND OF FUND on the BSE StAR MF Platform.
- Lumpsum investments (fresh and additional purchases), switch-in transactions, and new systematic registrations (SIP/XSIP and STP-in) are disabled for the scheme.
- Applications received after the applicable cut-off time on July 22, 2026 will not be accepted or processed.
- Existing SIP/STP-IN registrations made prior to the effective date will continue to be processed by the AMC/RTA without change.
- Redemption, Switch-out, STP-out, and SWP transactions remain allowed.
- The notice-cum-addendum issued by BARODA BNP Paribas AMC is attached for reference.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes; this is an operational update reflecting a temporary suspension of new investment inflows into a specific scheme, communicated by the AMC to BSE.

## Compliance Requirements

Mutual fund distributors and members using the BSE StAR MF Platform must not process fresh lumpsum purchases, switch-ins, or new SIP/XSIP/STP-in registrations for the affected scheme. Existing systematic registrations should continue to be honored, and redemption/switch-out/STP-out/SWP transactions can proceed as usual.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date of Suspension: July 22, 2026
- Notice Date: July 23, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to investors and distributors dealing in the specific BARODA BNP Paribas Funds Aqua Fund of Fund scheme via BSE StAR MF. There is no impact on listed equity trading or broader market operations. Investors with existing SIPs/STPs are unaffected, while those seeking fresh investments or switch-ins into this scheme will need to route transactions directly through the AMC/RTA if required.
