## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260615-47 directing trading members to refrain from dealing in Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) and bonds of 13 companies. These suspensions are triggered by the companies' fixed record dates for the purpose of payment of interest or redemption of debentures, effective between 17 June 2026 and 22 June 2026.

## Key Points

- Trading members must not deal in the listed debentures on or around their respective ex-dates.
- A total of 13 scrips across 11 issuers are covered under this notice.
- Suspensions relate to debt settlement cycles (DR) under Settlement No. 653/2026-2027 through 656/2026-2027.
- All instruments are Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) or bonds listed on BSE's Debt Segment.
- Companies affected include large financial institutions (ICICI Bank, Aditya Birla Housing Finance) and smaller private issuers (Ambium Finserve, Hedge Finance).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This notice is issued in accordance with existing BSE procedures governing suspension of trading in debt securities ahead of record dates for corporate actions (interest payment or principal redemption).

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members**: Must not execute trades in the specified NCDs/bonds from their respective ex-dates onwards until further notice.
- Members should update their systems to block or flag these ISINs from the applicable ex-dates.

## Impact Assessment

Impact is confined to participants in BSE's Debt Segment holding or trading in these specific NCD/bond scrips. Retail equity investors are unaffected. Debt fund managers and bond desk traders must ensure their order management systems reflect the ex-date restrictions. The clustering of multiple suspensions under Settlement 655/2026-2027 (ex-date 19/06/2026) requires particular attention as it covers six separate scrips from five issuers on the same date.
