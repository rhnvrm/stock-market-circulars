## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260527-38) informing trading members that certain securities will be moved to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective May 29, 2026. The affected securities are listed in the attached Annexure and will be subject to enhanced surveillance actions based on their respective GSM stage.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in the attached Annexure will move to higher GSM stages from May 29, 2026
- Settlement for affected securities will be on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed
- Trading members must take adequate precautions while trading in these securities
- The GSM framework was originally published on February 23, 2017 and has been updated through subsequent notices
- Four stages of GSM apply with progressively stricter surveillance actions

## Regulatory Changes

The circular references the existing GSM framework established through multiple prior notices:
- Exchange notice no. 20170223-44 dated February 23, 2017 (original GSM framework)
- Exchange notice no. 20170303-29 dated March 03, 2017
- Exchange notice no. 20180720-55 dated July 20, 2018
- Exchange notice no. 20191129-23 dated November 29, 2019
- Exchange notice no. 20231117-63 dated November 17, 2023

No new regulatory framework is introduced; securities are being escalated within the existing GSM structure.

## Compliance Requirements

**Stage I:** Applicable margin rate of 100% and price band of 5% or lower.

**Stage II:** Trade-for-trade settlement with price band of 5% or lower, plus Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value to be deposited by buyers.

**Stage III:** Trade-for-trade settlement with price band of 5% or lower, trading permitted once a week (every Monday or first trading day of the week), and ASD of 100% of trade value to be deposited by buyers.

**Stage IV:** Trade-for-trade settlement with price band of 5% or lower, trading permitted once a week (every Monday or first trading day of the week), ASD of 100% of trade value to be deposited by buyers, with no upward price movement permitted.

Trading members must:
- Note the GSM stage applicable to each affected security
- Collect and deposit required ASD from buying clients where applicable
- Ensure no netting off in settlement for affected securities
- Refer to the BSE GSM FAQ page or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** May 27, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 29, 2026 — securities moved to respective higher GSM stages

## Impact Assessment

Securities moved to higher GSM stages face significantly reduced liquidity and tighter trading conditions. Stage III and IV securities are effectively limited to once-weekly trading, which can cause sharp price dislocations and make it difficult for investors to exit positions. The 100% ASD requirement in Stages III and IV substantially increases the cost of buying these securities, further suppressing demand. Retail investors holding these securities are most affected due to reduced ability to liquidate. The specific securities impacted are detailed in the attached Annexure and should be reviewed by trading members to update their risk systems accordingly.
