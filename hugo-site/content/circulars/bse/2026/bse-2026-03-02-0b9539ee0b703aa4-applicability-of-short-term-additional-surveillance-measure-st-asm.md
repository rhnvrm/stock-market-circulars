## Summary

BSE has issued an update on the applicability of the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework. Effective March 04, 2026, 6 securities have been newly shortlisted under the Short Term 5/15/30 Days ASM framework, while 10 securities have been moved out of the framework. No securities were moved to a higher or lower ASM stage in this update.

## Key Points

- 6 securities added to the ST-ASM (Short Term 5/15/30 Days) framework effective March 04, 2026
- 10 securities removed from the ST-ASM framework effective March 04, 2026
- No securities were moved to a higher ASM stage
- No securities were moved to a lower ASM stage
- Securities marked * are shortlisted/exiting as per NSE data
- Securities marked @ are SME scrips
- Some exits are due to inclusion in the LT-ASM framework (marked *)

## Regulatory Changes

The ST-ASM framework applies enhanced surveillance conditions (5/15/30-day variants) to shortlisted securities. Securities entering the framework are subject to additional margin requirements and trading restrictions. Securities marked with * that are exiting ST-ASM are doing so due to migration into the Long Term ASM (LT-ASM) framework, meaning they remain under surveillance.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must update their systems to reflect the revised ST-ASM list effective March 04, 2026
- Applicable margin and trading restrictions per the ST-ASM framework must be enforced for the 6 newly added securities
- Restrictions on newly exiting securities should be lifted unless those securities are migrating to another surveillance framework (LT-ASM, Trade for Trade, GSM, Pledge, or ESM)

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** March 02, 2026
- **Effective Date:** March 04, 2026 (for all additions and removals)
