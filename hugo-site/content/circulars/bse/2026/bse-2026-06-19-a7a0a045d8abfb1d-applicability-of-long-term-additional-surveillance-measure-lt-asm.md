## Summary

BSE has issued a notice regarding the applicability of Long Term Additional Surveillance Measures (LT-ASM) on select equity securities. The notice mandates 100% margins on open and new positions effective June 24, 2026, introduces lower price bands effective June 22, 2026, and updates the list of securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages or exiting the framework entirely.

## Key Points

- 100% margins will be applicable w.e.f. June 24, 2026 on all open positions as of June 23, 2026 and all new positions created from June 24, 2026 onwards (Annexure I, Part A)
- Securities moving to higher stages of the LT-ASM framework effective June 22, 2026 are listed in Annexure I (Part B)
- Lower Price Band is applicable w.e.f. June 22, 2026 for securities in higher LT-ASM stages
- Certain securities under LT-ASM will be transferred to T / XT / MT / TS Group w.e.f. June 24, 2026 (Annexure I, Part D)
- Securities moving out of the LT-ASM framework w.e.f. June 22, 2026 are listed in Annexure II
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the LT-ASM framework is provided in Annexure III
- Shortlisting is based on XBRL submissions by listed companies and is purely a market surveillance action

## Regulatory Changes

This notice references and operates within the revised LT-ASM framework established through a series of prior Exchange notices:
- Exchange notice no. 20180321-46 dated March 21, 2018 (original framework)
- Exchange notice no. 20181027-1 dated October 27, 2018
- Exchange notice no. 20201204-56 dated December 04, 2020
- Exchange notice no. 20240809-46 dated August 09, 2024
- Exchange notice no. 20240920-63 dated September 20, 2024 (latest revision)
- Exchange notice no. 20210604-41 dated June 04, 2021 (group transfer provisions)

Upon exit from the LT-ASM framework, a scrip's price band is reinstated to its pre-shortlisting level, unless it is subject to another surveillance measure, in which case that measure's price band will prevail.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must maintain 100% margins on open positions in LT-ASM-listed securities from June 24, 2026
- Members and participants should review the updated annexures to identify affected securities in their portfolios
- The LT-ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchanges
- For clarifications, members may contact bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **June 22, 2026**: Lower Price Band applicable for securities moved to higher LT-ASM stages; securities moving out of LT-ASM framework exit effective this date
- **June 23, 2026**: Reference date for existing open positions subject to 100% margin requirement
- **June 24, 2026**: 100% margins effective on all open positions (as of June 23, 2026) and all new positions; selected securities transferred to T / XT / MT / TS Group

## Impact Assessment

The imposition of 100% margins significantly increases the cost of holding or creating positions in affected securities, reducing leverage and likely dampening speculative activity. The lower price band limits downside volatility but may also restrict price discovery. Securities transferred to the T/XT/MT/TS group face additional trade-for-trade settlement restrictions, further reducing liquidity. Overall, these measures are designed to protect investors from high-risk or manipulated scrips, but directly impact trading volumes and market depth for the securities listed in the annexures.
