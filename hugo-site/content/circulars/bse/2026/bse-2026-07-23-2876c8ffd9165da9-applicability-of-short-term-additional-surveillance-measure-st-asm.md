## Summary

BSE has notified that certain securities (listed in Annexure I, attached separately) have met the criteria for inclusion under the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework. Securities have been classified into ST-ASM Stage I and Stage II, each carrying escalating margin requirements. This action follows the ASM framework established under earlier BSE notices dated September 20, 2024, October 27, 2018, July 19, 2019, December 4, 2020, and September 25, 2023.

## Key Points

- Securities satisfying ST-ASM Stage I criteria will be subject to a margin rate of 50% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- Securities satisfying ST-ASM Stage II criteria will be subject to a margin rate of 100% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- The specific list of affected securities is provided in the attached Annexure I (Annexure_STASM), not included in the notice text.
- The ASM framework operates alongside other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchange.
- Inclusion under ASM is purely a market surveillance action and should not be construed as an adverse view on the company or entity.
- When a scrip exits the ASM framework, its price band will revert to the level applicable before ASM inclusion, unless it remains subject to another surveillance framework.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing ST-ASM framework (as revised via prior circulars) to a fresh set of securities identified as meeting the relevant surveillance criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must apply the specified higher margin rates (50% for Stage I, 100% for Stage II) on all open positions in the identified securities as on July 24, 2026, and on new positions created from July 27, 2026 onward. Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 24, 2026: Reference date for open positions subject to the new margin requirement.
- July 27, 2026: Effective date from which the revised margin rates apply to open and new positions.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a targeted impact limited to the specific securities named in Annexure I, increasing trading costs and margin burden for market participants holding or trading these stocks. It does not affect the broader market but is significant for investors and traders in the affected scrips, who face substantially higher margin requirements that may reduce liquidity and trading activity in these names.
