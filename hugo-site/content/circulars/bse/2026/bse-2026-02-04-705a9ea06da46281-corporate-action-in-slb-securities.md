## Summary

BSE has announced shut periods for Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) activities due to corporate actions in 9 securities. Eight companies will undergo dividend distributions and one mutual fund will execute a unit split. During the shut periods, SLB transactions will not be permitted for these securities.

## Key Points

- 8 companies declaring dividends with ex-dates and record dates between February 5-20, 2026
- 1 mutual fund (Quantum Asset Management) executing unit split on February 13, 2026
- SLB shut periods range from 1-4 days for each security
- All corporate actions use record date methodology (no foreclosure dates specified)
- Shut periods commence 1-2 days before the ex-date

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine operational circular informing market participants of temporary restrictions on SLB activities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must not initiate new SLB transactions for the affected securities during the specified shut periods
- Existing SLB positions may be impacted and should be managed according to the corporate action schedules
- Brokers and clearing members should inform their clients engaged in SLB activities about these restrictions

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - Affects liquidity in the SLB segment for 9 securities during their respective shut periods. Investors who rely on securities lending/borrowing for hedging or leveraged strategies will need to plan around these restrictions.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - SLB participants must adjust their trading strategies and position management for the affected securities. The staggered dates across February provide some planning flexibility.

**Investor Impact:** Low to Medium - Only affects participants in the SLB segment. Cash market trading continues normally. Dividend-eligible shareholders benefit from these corporate actions.
