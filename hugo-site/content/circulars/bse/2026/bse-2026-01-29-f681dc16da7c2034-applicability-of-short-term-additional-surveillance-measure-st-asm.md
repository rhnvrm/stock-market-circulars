## Summary

BSE has updated the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective January 30, 2026. Ten securities have been newly shortlisted for ST-ASM, while seven securities are moving out of the framework. The circular provides consolidated lists showing securities under different stages (Stage I and Stage II) of the ST-ASM framework, which applies enhanced surveillance on securities exhibiting abnormal price movements.

## Key Points

- 10 securities newly shortlisted in Short Term 5/15/30 Days ASM Framework
- 7 securities moving out of ST-ASM Framework
- No securities moving to higher or lower ASM stages within the framework
- Includes both regular and SME scrips in the surveillance measures
- Changes effective from January 30, 2026

## Regulatory Framework

The ST-ASM framework is a surveillance measure applied to securities showing unusual price movements or volatility. Securities may exit ST-ASM due to:
- Inclusion in LT-ASM (Long Term ASM) Framework
- Inclusion in Trade for Trade Framework
- Inclusion in GSM (Graded Surveillance Measure) Framework
- Inclusion in Pledge Framework
- Inclusion in ESM (Enhanced Surveillance Measure) Framework

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the changes in surveillance categories
- Enhanced margin requirements and trading restrictions apply to ST-ASM securities
- Securities in ST-ASM may have reduced liquidity and higher transaction costs
- Investors should exercise caution when trading these securities

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: January 30, 2026
- All changes to ST-ASM classifications become applicable from market opening on this date

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - The surveillance measures affect 17 securities with potential impact on trading volumes and liquidity. Investors in these stocks will face enhanced surveillance conditions.

**Trading Impact**: Securities under ST-ASM face restrictions including higher margins, reduced position limits, and potential price bands, which may reduce speculative activity and volatility in these stocks.

**Investor Impact**: Traders and investors holding or planning to trade these securities must be aware of the enhanced surveillance measures and associated trading restrictions that become effective January 30, 2026.
