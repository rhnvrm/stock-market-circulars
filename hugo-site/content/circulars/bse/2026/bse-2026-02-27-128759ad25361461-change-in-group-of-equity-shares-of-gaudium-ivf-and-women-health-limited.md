## Summary

BSE has announced, in continuation of Notice No. 20260226-41 dated February 26, 2026, that the equity shares of Gaudium IVF and Women Health Limited (Scrip Code: 544709) will be transferred from the Trade for Trade segment (T Group) to the Rolling segment and placed under B Group, effective Monday, March 16, 2026.

## Key Points

- Equity shares of Gaudium IVF and Women Health Limited (Scrip Code: 544709) are currently traded under the Trade for Trade (T Group) segment
- The scrip will move to the Rolling settlement segment under B Group effective March 16, 2026
- This notice is a continuation of BSE Notice No. 20260226-41 dated February 26, 2026
- Contact person for queries: Mr. Parag Jain, Tel. No. 022 2272 8685

## Regulatory Changes

The scrip is being reclassified from the Trade for Trade (T Group) to the Rolling segment (B Group). Under Trade for Trade, each trade must result in delivery and cannot be netted; under Rolling settlement (B Group), normal netting and settlement rules apply.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must update their systems and processes to reflect the new segment classification for Scrip Code 544709 effective March 16, 2026
- Members should cease treating the scrip as a T Group security from the effective date
- Members with positions or pending settlements in the T Group for this scrip should ensure timely resolution before the transition date

## Important Dates

- **February 26, 2026**: Original notice issued (Notice No. 20260226-41)
- **February 27, 2026**: Continuation notice issued (Notice No. 20260227-16)
- **March 16, 2026 (Monday)**: Effective date of transfer from T Group to Rolling segment (B Group)

## Impact Assessment

This change will affect trading members and investors holding or intending to trade in Gaudium IVF and Women Health Limited shares. Moving from Trade for Trade to Rolling settlement (B Group) allows for intraday netting of trades, potentially improving liquidity and reducing settlement obligations. Investors should note the change in settlement mechanics. The impact is limited to this single scrip and represents a positive development for tradability.
