## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that nine companies have fixed record dates for payment of interest and/or redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) and Bonds. Trading members are advised not to deal in the debentures of these companies during the specified suspension windows.

## Key Points

- Nine companies have fixed record dates for NCD interest payment or redemption between 15 June 2026 and 18 June 2026
- Trading members are directed not to trade in the listed debentures around the respective ex-dates
- All instruments are privately placed NCDs/Bonds
- Settlement series covered: 651/2026-2027, 653/2026-2027, and 654/2026-2027
- ISINs affected span across housing finance, NBFC, microfinance, and real estate sectors

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational notice under existing exchange rules requiring suspension of NCD trading around record dates for corporate actions (interest payment/redemption).

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the debentures listed below on or around the specified ex-dates
- Members should update their systems to block trading in the affected ISINs during suspension periods
- Applicable from the respective ex-dates as listed

## Impact Assessment

Impact is limited to debt market participants holding or intending to trade in these specific NCD instruments. The suspension is temporary and confined to individual ISIN-level trading restrictions around record dates. Equity trading in these companies is unaffected. Bajaj Finance, Godrej Properties, and Poonawalla Fincorp are notable large-cap names; however, the affected instruments are privately placed NCDs with limited secondary market activity. Routine compliance action with no broader market implications.
