## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that 35,18,36,870 new equity shares of PC Jeweller Limited (Scrip Code: 534809, ISIN: INE785M01021) are listed and permitted to trade on the Exchange with effect from Monday, June 29, 2026. These shares were issued on a preferential basis to Promoter and Non-Promoter entities pursuant to conversion of warrants, at an issue price of Rs. 5.62/- per share (face value Re. 1/- + premium of Rs. 4.62/-).

## Key Points

- **New shares listed:** 35,18,36,870 equity shares of Re. 1/- each
- **Issue price:** Rs. 5.62/- per share (Re. 1/- face value + Rs. 4.62/- premium)
- **Basis of allotment:** Preferential issue to Promoter and Non-Promoter via conversion of warrants
- **Date of allotment:** March 23, 2026
- **Trading effective from:** Monday, June 29, 2026
- **Distribution numbers:** 8016750456 to 8368587324
- **Ranking:** Pari-passu with existing equity shares

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification pursuant to an earlier preferential allotment approved by the company.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members of BSE are informed to note the listing and permit trading of these new shares from June 29, 2026. Lock-in restrictions apply to certain tranches and must be observed accordingly.

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment:** March 23, 2026
- **Trading commencement:** June 29, 2026
- **Lock-in expiry (94,900,000 shares):** December 29, 2027 (Dist. Nos. 8016750456 to 8111650455)
- **Lock-in expiry (25,69,36,870 shares):** December 29, 2026 (Dist. Nos. 8111650456 to 8368587325)

## Impact Assessment

The listing of approximately 35.18 crore new shares represents a substantial addition to PC Jeweller's equity capital base. Since these were allotted via preferential warrant conversion, the dilutive effect on existing shareholders is significant. However, lock-in provisions covering all newly listed shares (until December 2026 or December 2027 depending on tranche) will restrict immediate secondary market supply, potentially limiting near-term price pressure. Market participants should note the two distinct lock-in tranches and their respective expiry dates when assessing future liquidity.
