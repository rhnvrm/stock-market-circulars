## Summary

BSE has issued this notice pursuant to the LT-ASM framework (originally established via notice no. 20180321-46 dated March 21, 2018 and subsequently revised) to announce the applicability of Long Term Additional Surveillance Measures on a list of securities. Key actions include imposition of 100% margins, movement of securities to higher LT-ASM stages, lower price band restrictions, and transfer of certain securities out of the LT-ASM framework.

## Key Points

- 100% margins will be applicable from June 11, 2026 on all open positions as on June 10, 2026 and all new positions created from June 11, 2026 onwards (Annexure I, Part A)
- Securities placed under the framework will be reviewed on a periodic basis
- Securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages effective June 09, 2026 are listed in Annexure I (Part B)
- Lower Price Band shall be applicable w.e.f. June 09, 2026 for securities in higher stages
- Certain securities will continue in the LT-ASM framework but will be transferred to T / XT / MT / TS Group effective June 11, 2026 (Annexure I, Part D)
- Securities moving out of the LT-ASM framework effective June 09, 2026 are listed in Annexure II
- A consolidated list of all securities under the framework is provided in Annexure III
- The LT-ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures
- Shortlisting of securities is purely for market surveillance purposes and should not be construed as adverse action against the concerned company
- Shortlisting is based on XBRL submissions by the respective listed companies

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued under the revised LT-ASM framework as per Exchange notice no. 20240920-63 dated September 20, 2024. The framework has been updated through multiple notices since its inception in March 2018, with key revisions in October 2018, December 2020, August 2024, and September 2024. The current circular implements periodic review outcomes within this framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must ensure 100% margins are maintained on open positions in affected securities from June 11, 2026
- Members dealing in affected securities must adhere to the lower price band restrictions effective June 09, 2026
- For securities transferred to T / XT / MT / TS Group, members must follow the applicable group-level trading norms effective June 11, 2026
- For clarifications, members may write to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **June 08, 2026**: Notice issued
- **June 09, 2026**: Securities move to higher LT-ASM stages; lower price band applicable; securities exit LT-ASM framework (Annexure II)
- **June 10, 2026**: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin
- **June 11, 2026**: 100% margins effective on all open positions and new positions; securities transferred to T / XT / MT / TS Group

## Impact Assessment

The imposition of 100% margins significantly increases the cost of holding positions in affected securities, likely reducing liquidity and trading volumes. The lower price band limits downside price movement but may create one-sided markets. Transfer to T / XT / MT / TS groups changes the settlement mechanism, adding settlement risk for traders. Securities exiting the framework may see restored price bands, potentially increasing volatility as suppressed price discovery resumes. The framework is intended to protect investors from potential price manipulation or abnormal trading activity in these securities.
