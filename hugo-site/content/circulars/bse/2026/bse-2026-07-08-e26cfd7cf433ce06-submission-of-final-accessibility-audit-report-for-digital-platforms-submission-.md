## Summary

BSE has issued a reminder to all Investment Advisers regarding the mandatory submission of the final accessibility audit report for their digital platforms. This is in line with SEBI Circular No. SEBI/HO/ITD-1/ITD_VIAP/P/CIR/2025/131 dated September 25, 2025, and follows a series of earlier Exchange circulars issued between August 2025 and April 2026 on compliance with the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 and rules made thereunder.

## Key Points

- Investment Advisers must submit the final accessibility audit report for their digital platforms.
- Remediation of findings identified in the audit must also be completed and reported.
- Submission must confirm overall compliance with the accessibility circular for digital platforms.
- Submissions are to be made through the IARA portal using existing login credentials.
- A User Manual (User_Manual_IA) has been attached with detailed navigation and step-by-step submission instructions.
- Support contact for queries: 022 22725854 / 5680 / 5359.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory requirement is introduced; this notice reiterates and sets a final deadline for compliance already mandated under SEBI Circular No. SEBI/HO/ITD-1/ITD_VIAP/P/CIR/2025/131 dated September 25, 2025, and referenced Exchange circulars dated April 28, 2026, March 25, 2026, December 9, 2025, August 29, 2025, August 26, 2025, and August 7, 2025.

## Compliance Requirements

All Investment Advisers registered with BSE must:
- Complete the final accessibility audit of their digital platforms.
- Remediate findings identified during the audit.
- Submit the final audit report and confirmation of compliance through the IARA portal.
- Refer to the attached User Manual for the submission process.

## Important Dates

- Submission Due Date: July 31, 2026
- Notice Date: July 8, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has no direct impact on listed securities, trading, or market operations. It carries compliance risk for Investment Advisers who fail to meet the July 31, 2026 deadline, potentially exposing them to regulatory action from BSE/SEBI for non-compliance with accessibility norms under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016.
