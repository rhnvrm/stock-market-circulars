## Summary

BSE has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework as of June 4, 2026. Securities listed in Annexure I will attract a minimum 100% margin requirement effective June 8, 2026, and will be shifted from the Rolling Settlement segment to the Trade-for-Trade segment. Certain securities are also being moved to lower stages within the ESM framework, while others are exiting the framework entirely.

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I will attract minimum 100% margin w.e.f. June 8, 2026 on all open positions as on June 5, 2026 and new positions from June 8, 2026
- Some securities are being moved to Stage I (lower stage) under the ESM Framework w.e.f. June 5, 2026
- All ESM-qualifying securities will shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment w.e.f. June 8, 2026
- Securities shifting to Stage II will be subject to Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction w.e.f. June 5, 2026
- Securities exiting the ESM Framework are listed in Annexure II, effective June 5, 2026
- A consolidated list of all securities under the ESM framework is provided in Annexure III
- ESM shortlisting is purely for market surveillance and should not be construed as adverse action against the company

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the ESM framework previously established under notices dated June 2, 2023; July 18, 2023; August 9, 2024; September 20, 2024; October 4, 2024; and July 25, 2025. Securities under Stage II now face Periodic Call Auction in addition to the Trade-for-Trade restriction and 2% price band.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure minimum 100% margin is collected on all positions in ESM-listed securities from June 8, 2026
- Members must apply the Trade-for-Trade settlement mechanism for ESM-listed securities as of the effective dates
- Securities in Stage II require adherence to the 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction rules
- For clarifications, members may write to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **June 5, 2026**: Securities moved to Stage I take effect; Stage II securities begin Trade-for-Trade with 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction; securities exiting ESM are removed from the framework
- **June 8, 2026**: Minimum 100% margin effective on open positions (as on June 5, 2026) and new positions; ESM securities shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment

## Impact Assessment

The 100% margin requirement significantly increases the capital burden on traders holding or initiating positions in ESM-listed securities, potentially reducing liquidity. The shift to Trade-for-Trade eliminates netting benefits and requires full delivery/payment, further constraining speculative activity. Securities exiting the ESM framework will have their price bands reinstated to pre-ESM levels, unless subject to another surveillance measure. Overall, this update is likely to suppress trading volumes in the affected securities while reinforcing investor protection objectives.
