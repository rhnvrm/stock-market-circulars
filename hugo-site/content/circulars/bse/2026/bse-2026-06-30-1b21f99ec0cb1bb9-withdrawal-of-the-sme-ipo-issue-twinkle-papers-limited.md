## Summary

BSE has notified the withdrawal of the SME IPO of Twinkle Papers Limited, which had opened for subscription on June 29, 2026 and was scheduled to close on July 1, 2026. The withdrawal follows communications from the merchant banker and the company dated June 30, 2026, citing inadvertent non-disclosure of proceedings before the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) in both the Draft Red Herring Prospectus (DRHP) and the Red Herring Prospectus (RHP).

## Key Points

- Twinkle Papers Limited's SME IPO opened on June 29, 2026 (Monday) and was scheduled to close on July 1, 2026 (Wednesday).
- The company and its merchant banker informed BSE on June 30, 2026 of the decision to withdraw the issue.
- Reason for withdrawal: inadvertent omission of disclosure relating to proceedings before the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) from both the DRHP and RHP.
- A Company Withdrawal Letter has been attached to the notice for reference.
- The notice was issued by Mangesh Tayde, Vice President, Listing Business Relationship.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. The withdrawal highlights mandatory disclosure requirements under SEBI's ICDR Regulations, specifically the obligation to disclose material legal proceedings (including NCLT proceedings) in offer documents.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants and investors who have submitted bids for the Twinkle Papers Limited SME IPO should note the withdrawal and expect refund/unblocking of application funds.
- No further action is required by investors; the issue stands cancelled as of June 30, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **June 29, 2026**: IPO issue opening date
- **June 30, 2026**: Withdrawal notification issued by BSE; merchant banker and company communicated withdrawal decision
- **July 1, 2026**: Original scheduled closing date (now cancelled)

## Impact Assessment

This withdrawal directly impacts retail and institutional investors who subscribed to the Twinkle Papers Limited SME IPO between June 29 and June 30, 2026. Subscription funds (ASBA/UPI blocked amounts) will need to be unblocked/refunded. The non-disclosure of NCLT proceedings is a material compliance lapse under SEBI's Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements (ICDR) Regulations, and may attract further regulatory scrutiny for the company and its merchant banker. The incident underscores the importance of comprehensive due diligence and disclosure in offer documents before SEBI filing.
