## Summary

BSE has notified the movement of two securities into GSM Stage III under the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective March 6, 2026. The affected companies are Binny Mills Ltd and ECS Biztech Ltd. GSM Stage III entails heightened surveillance and trading restrictions.

## Key Points

- Binny Mills Ltd (Scrip Code: 535620, ISIN: INE160L01011) moved to GSM Stage III
- ECS Biztech Ltd (Scrip Code: 540063, ISIN: INE925Q01024) moved to GSM Stage III
- Securities marked (#) are moved lower in the GSM Framework due to inclusion in the ESM (Enhanced Surveillance Measure) Framework
- Securities marked ($) are moved lower in the GSM Framework due to inclusion in the IBC (Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code) Framework
- NSE data is referenced where applicable (*)

## Regulatory Changes

The GSM framework is a surveillance mechanism implemented by BSE to protect investor interest and market integrity. Securities placed under GSM Stage III face more stringent trading conditions compared to lower stages, including periodic call auction mechanisms, additional margins, and trade-to-trade settlement requirements.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must ensure clients are aware of the GSM Stage III classification of these securities before executing trades
- Investors holding or planning to trade in Binny Mills Ltd or ECS Biztech Ltd must comply with the applicable GSM Stage III trading conditions
- Additional surveillance margins as prescribed for GSM Stage III are applicable immediately

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: March 6, 2026 (date of circular)

## Impact Assessment

Movement to GSM Stage III significantly restricts trading activity in the affected securities. Investors in Binny Mills Ltd and ECS Biztech Ltd will face reduced liquidity, mandatory trade-to-trade settlement, and potentially higher margin requirements. This classification signals regulatory concern over the price or volume movements in these securities and may dampen investor sentiment. Existing shareholders may find it more difficult to exit positions due to the restricted trading environment imposed under Stage III of the GSM framework.
