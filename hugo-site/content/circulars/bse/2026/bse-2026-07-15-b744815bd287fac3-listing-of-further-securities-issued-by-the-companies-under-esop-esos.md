## Summary

BSE has informed trading members that further new securities issued by eight companies under their Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP) / Employee Stock Option Schemes (ESOS) have been listed and admitted for trading with effect from Thursday, July 16, 2026.

## Key Points

- Dr. Agarwal's Health Care Limited (ISIN INE943P01029, Scrip Code 544350): 43,155 shares of face value ₹1 listed, distinctive numbers 316983029 to 317026183.
- Entero Healthcare Solutions Limited (ISIN INE010601016, Scrip Code 544122): 1,475 shares of face value ₹10 listed, distinctive numbers 43521598 to 43523072.
- Federal Bank Limited (ISIN INE171A01029, Scrip Code 500469): 4,11,242 shares of face value ₹2 listed, distinctive numbers 2468514006 to 2468925247.
- ICICI Bank Limited (ISIN INE090A01021, Scrip Code 532174): 1,51,181 shares of face value ₹2 listed, distinctive numbers 7174006355 to 7174157535.
- Insolation Energy Limited (ISIN INE0LGX01024, Scrip Code 543620): 54,750 shares of face value ₹1 listed, distinctive numbers 220394626 to 220449375.
- Karnataka Bank Limited (ISIN INE614B01018, Scrip Code 532652): 10,000 shares of face value ₹10 listed, distinctive numbers 378219886 to 378229885.
- NIIT Learning Systems Limited (ISIN INE342G01023, Scrip Code 543952): 25,000 shares of face value ₹2 listed, distinctive numbers 137814444 to 137839443.
- Polycab India Limited (ISIN INE455K01017, Scrip Code 542652): 12,175 shares of face value ₹10 listed, distinctive numbers 150635054 to 150647228.
- None of the listed shares carry a lock-in restriction.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes; this is a routine listing approval under existing ESOP/ESOS listing procedures at BSE.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific action required from trading members other than updating trading systems to reflect the increased listed share count for the respective scrips from the effective date.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 15, 2026
- Effective listing and trading date: Thursday, July 16, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The impact is minimal, as these represent routine incremental share listings arising from employee stock option exercises rather than fresh capital-raising events. The additional shares are small in proportion to each company's existing outstanding equity and are unlikely to have a material effect on stock price or trading liquidity.
