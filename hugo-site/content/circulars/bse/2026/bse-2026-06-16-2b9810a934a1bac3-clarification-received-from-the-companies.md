## Summary

BSE's Surveillance department (DOSS) has observed significant price/volume movement in 14 listed companies and has written to them seeking clarifications. As of 16 June 2026, eight companies have submitted their replies (attached), while six companies' responses are still awaited.

## Key Points

- BSE wrote to 14 companies to ensure trading members and investors have the latest relevant information regarding unusual price/volume activity.
- Eight companies have replied; their responses are attached to the notice.
- Six companies have not yet responded and their replies are pending.
- Notice issued by the Surveillance department under Notice No. 20260616-19.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard surveillance inquiry under BSE's ongoing market monitoring framework.

## Compliance Requirements

The six companies listed below are expected to submit clarifications to BSE at the earliest:
1. Amrapali Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 526241)
2. DLF Ltd (Scrip Code: 532868)
3. Elango Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 513452)
4. Fine Line Circuits Ltd (Scrip Code: 517264)
5. Grand Oak Canyons Distillery Ltd (Scrip Code: 523862)
6. Kanungo Financiers Ltd (Scrip Code: 540515)

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** 16 June 2026
- Reply deadline for pending companies: Not specified; compliance expected promptly.

## Impact Assessment

The eight companies that have already responded — Aarti Industries Ltd, All Time Plastics Ltd, Den Networks Ltd, Quadrant Future Tek Ltd, Raghuvansh Agrofarms Ltd, SRU Steels Ltd, Toss The Coin Ltd, and Tribhovandas Bhimji Zaveri Ltd — have fulfilled their immediate compliance obligation. The six non-responding companies, notably DLF Ltd, remain under surveillance scrutiny. No trading restrictions have been imposed at this stage; this is an information-gathering step. Investors in the listed scrips should monitor for any follow-up actions if clarifications are deemed unsatisfactory.
