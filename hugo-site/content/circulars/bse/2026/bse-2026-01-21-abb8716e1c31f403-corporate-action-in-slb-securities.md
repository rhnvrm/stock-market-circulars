## Summary

BSE has notified corporate actions for seven securities available under the Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) scheme. All listed companies are declaring dividends with their respective record dates, ex-dates, and shut periods between January 22-27, 2026. SLB participants must note the foreclosure dates and shut periods during which SLB transactions in these securities will be restricted.

## Key Points

- Seven companies have announced dividend corporate actions affecting SLB securities
- Shut periods range from 1 to 5 days depending on the security
- Ex-dates fall on either January 23 or January 27, 2026
- All corporate actions are dividend-related with specified record dates
- Foreclosure dates apply for existing SLB positions in these securities

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine notification of corporate actions affecting SLB securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- SLB participants must close or settle positions before the shut period begins for each security
- No new SLB transactions permitted during the shut period for affected securities
- Participants must adhere to foreclosure requirements where applicable
- Market participants should plan their SLB strategies considering the dividend ex-dates and shut periods

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - Affects liquidity and availability of seven securities in the SLB segment during the shut periods. Participants with existing positions must plan exits or settlements accordingly.

**Operational Impact:** SLB participants must monitor their positions and ensure compliance with shut period restrictions. Those holding borrowed securities or having lent securities in these names need to manage their positions before the respective shut periods commence.

**Trading Impact:** SLB transactions in these securities will not be permitted during the specified shut periods, potentially affecting hedging and arbitrage strategies that rely on securities lending.
