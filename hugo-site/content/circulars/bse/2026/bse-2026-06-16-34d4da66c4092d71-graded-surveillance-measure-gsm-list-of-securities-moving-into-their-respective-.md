## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260616-27 directing trading members that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure) will be moved to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 17, 2026. These securities will attract progressively stringent surveillance actions depending on their assigned GSM stage (I through IV).

## Key Points

- Securities in the attached Annexure will be shifted to higher GSM stages effective **June 17, 2026**
- GSM stages range from I to IV, with each stage imposing increasingly restrictive trading conditions
- No netting off is allowed; settlement is strictly on a trade-to-trade basis
- Trading members are advised to take adequate precautions while trading in these securities
- Refer to the GSM FAQ at the BSE website or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications

## Regulatory Changes

The GSM framework, originally published on February 23, 2017 and subsequently updated through notices dated March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023, prescribes the following surveillance actions by stage:

| Stage | Surveillance Actions |
|-------|---------------------|
| I | Applicable margin rate of 100% and price band of 5% or lower |
| II | Trade-for-trade with price band of 5% or lower; Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers |
| III | Trade-for-trade with price band of 5% or lower; trading permitted once a week (every Monday/1st trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers |
| IV | Trade-for-trade with price band of 5% or lower; trading permitted once a week (every Monday/1st trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers; no upward movement permitted |

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure compliance with the applicable GSM stage restrictions for each affected security
- No netting off of trades is permitted; all settlements must be on a trade-to-trade basis
- Buyers in Stage II, III, and IV securities must arrange for the required Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) at the prescribed rates
- Members should refer to the Annexure (Annexure_GSM) to identify the specific securities and their assigned GSM stages

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 16, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 17, 2026 — GSM stage movements take effect
- Trading restrictions (weekly trading, ASD requirements) apply from the effective date onward

## Impact Assessment

Movement of securities into higher GSM stages significantly impacts market liquidity and trading activity. Stage III and IV securities are restricted to once-a-week trading, severely limiting exit opportunities for investors. The mandatory ASD of up to 100% of trade value substantially increases the cost of buying these securities, discouraging speculative activity. The trade-for-trade settlement removes netting benefits and increases settlement risk for members. Investors holding these securities should monitor developments closely, as GSM placement signals elevated regulatory concern about price integrity and corporate fundamentals.
