## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that seven companies have fixed record dates for part redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs). Trading in these debentures will be conducted with a reduced face value effective from the respective ex-dates listed below.

## Key Points

- Seven companies have fixed record dates for part redemption of NCDs under the debt segment.
- Trading in the debentures of each company will occur at a reduced face value from the specified ex-date onwards.
- All debentures are privately placed (PVT series).
- Settlement periods span from 640/2026-2027 to 646/2026-2027.
- The notice is issued by Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President - Listing Compliance and Operations.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice under existing listing compliance frameworks for debt securities on BSE.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are required to note the reduced face values effective from the respective ex-dates and update their systems accordingly to reflect the revised face values for each debenture scrip.

## Impact Assessment

Impact is limited to holders of these seven specific privately placed NCD series. Post ex-date, the face value of each debenture will be reduced to the stated amounts, reflecting partial principal repayment. This is a standard corporate action for amortizing debt instruments and does not affect equity markets or broader trading operations.
