## Summary

BSE has notified members that Aditya Birla Sun Life Mutual Fund has imposed subscription limits on the ADITYA BIRLA SUN LIFE GOLD FUND, effective June 8, 2026. Fresh/additional lump sum purchases and switch-ins are capped at ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month. These restrictions have been implemented on the BSE StAR MF Platform.

## Key Points

- Fresh and additional purchases via lump sum mode and switch-ins are restricted to ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month
- The restriction applies to transactions received after the 3:00 PM cut-off on June 8, 2026
- Redemptions, switch-outs, and STP-out transactions remain unrestricted
- Subscription orders exceeding ₹10,00,000 triggered to the RTA on or after 3:00 PM on June 8, 2026 will be rejected and refunded to the investor
- Refunds will be processed after funds are received from the AMC/RTA

## Regulatory Changes

The AMC (Aditya Birla Sun Life Mutual Fund) has issued a Notice-cum-Addendum imposing a monthly per-PAN subscription cap of ₹10,00,000 on ADITYA BIRLA SUN LIFE GOLD FUND. BSE has correspondingly implemented this restriction on the BSE StAR MF Platform as required by the AMC communication.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and distributors must ensure subscription orders for ADITYA BIRLA SUN LIFE GOLD FUND do not exceed ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month
- Orders above the limit submitted after 3:00 PM on June 8, 2026 will be rejected at the RTA level; members should communicate this to investors proactively
- No restrictions apply to redemption, switch-out, or STP-out transactions

## Important Dates

- **June 8, 2026, 3:00 PM**: Effective cut-off time from which the ₹10,00,000 per PAN per month subscription limit applies to ADITYA BIRLA SUN LIFE GOLD FUND

## Impact Assessment

This restriction moderately impacts high-net-worth investors and institutional participants who transact in lump sum amounts exceeding ₹10 lakh per month in the Aditya Birla Sun Life Gold Fund via the BSE StAR MF Platform. Regular SIP investors and those with smaller transaction sizes are unaffected. Exit liquidity is fully preserved as redemptions and switch-outs face no restrictions. The measure is typical for gold funds experiencing large inflows and is intended to manage fund size and liquidity in line with the underlying gold ETF/commodity exposure.
