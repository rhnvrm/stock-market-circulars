## Summary

BSE has issued an update regarding debt securities of Nexus Select Trust, continuing from Exchange Notice No. 20251217-18 dated December 17, 2025. Two scrip codes under Nexus Select Trust will be available for trading with revised terms effective Wednesday, April 22, 2026.

## Key Points

- This notice is a continuation of Exchange Notice No. 20251217-18 dated December 17, 2025
- Two debt scrip codes of Nexus Select Trust are impacted with revised trading terms
- Scrip Code 977372 (ISIN: INE0NDH07068) is affected
- Scrip Code 977376 (ISIN: INE0NDH07076) is affected
- Revised terms are detailed in Annexure I of the circular
- Trading members are advised to take note and act accordingly

## Regulatory Changes

The revised terms for the two debt securities (Scrip Codes 977372 and 977376) are outlined in Annexure I attached to the notice. The specific nature of the revision (e.g., coupon rate, maturity, or other terms) is contained in that annexure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised terms for Scrip Codes 977372 and 977376 before executing trades from April 22, 2026
- Members should review Annexure I for the full details of the revised terms
- For clarifications, members may contact Ms. Pooja Trivedi at +91 22 2272 8844 or debt.listing@bseindia.com, or reach out to Hardik Bhuta, Assistant Vice President

## Important Dates

- **April 21, 2026**: Notice issued by BSE
- **April 22, 2026 (Wednesday)**: Effective date for revised trading terms on Scrip Codes 977372 and 977376
- **December 17, 2025**: Original Exchange Notice No. 20251217-18 issued

## Impact Assessment

This update impacts debt segment traders and investors holding or transacting in Nexus Select Trust's debt securities with the specified ISINs. The revised terms may affect pricing, yield calculations, or other trading parameters. Market participants dealing in these scrips should review Annexure I carefully and ensure their trading systems reflect the updated terms before the effective date of April 22, 2026. The impact is limited to the debt segment and specifically to these two scrip codes.
