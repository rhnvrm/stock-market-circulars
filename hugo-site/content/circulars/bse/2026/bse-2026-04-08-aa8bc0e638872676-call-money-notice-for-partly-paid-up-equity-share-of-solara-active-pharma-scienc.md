## Summary

BSE has activated the Call Money Module on its IBBS (Internet Based Book Building Software) platform for Solara Active Pharma Sciences Limited. This notice pertains to the second and final call on partly paid-up equity shares of the company. Trading Members, Self-Certified Syndicate Banks (SCSBs), and intermediaries are directed to facilitate the collection and submission of call money during the specified issue period.

## Key Points

- Security symbol: **SOLARAFCM** (Partly Paid Equity Shares)
- Call type: Second and Final Call
- Call money amount: **Rs. 112.50 per partly paid-up equity share**
- Total partly paid-up equity shares: **1,19,98,755**
- Market lot: 1
- Issue opens: Thursday, April 23, 2026
- Issue closes: Thursday, May 07, 2026
- Bidding hours: 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.
- Registrar: CAMEO CORPORATE SERVICES LIMITED, Chennai

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. The activation is in accordance with:
- Exchange Notice No. 20210104-37 dated January 4, 2021
- SEBI circular dated December 8, 2020 governing the Call Money Module for SCSBs and Trading Members with 3-in-1 type accounts

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members** with 3-in-1 accounts and **SCSBs** must submit call money notice details collected during the issue period via the IBBS platform.
- Intermediaries are requested to note the activation and act accordingly within the specified window.
- For regulatory/business queries: listing.sales@bseindia.com | 022-2272 8846/5063/8179

## Impact Assessment

This circular directly impacts holders of Solara Active Pharma Sciences Limited's partly paid-up equity shares (symbol: SOLARAFCM). Shareholders will need to pay Rs. 112.50 per share as the second and final call money to convert their partly paid shares into fully paid shares. The total call money collectible across all 1,19,98,755 shares amounts to approximately Rs. 134.99 crore. Trading Members and SCSBs facilitating this process must ensure their IBBS access is active and submissions are completed within the 15-day window. No broader market-wide regulatory impact is anticipated.
