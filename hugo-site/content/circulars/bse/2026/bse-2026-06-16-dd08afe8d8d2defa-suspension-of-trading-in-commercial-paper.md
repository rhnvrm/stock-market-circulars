## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260616-8 informs trading members that multiple companies have fixed record dates for the redemption of their commercial paper instruments. Trading members are advised not to deal in these commercial papers effective from the respective ex-dates.

## Key Points

- Trading in commercial paper of 18+ listed companies is suspended ahead of their redemption record dates
- Two batches of suspensions: ex-date June 19, 2026 (Settlement DR 655/2026-2027) and ex-date June 22, 2026 (Settlement DR 656/2026-2027)
- Suspension applies to both buy and sell transactions by trading members
- All instruments are in the Debt segment under the Listing Operations department

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice under existing exchange rules requiring suspension of trading in debt securities ahead of record dates for corporate actions such as redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must not deal in the commercial paper of the listed companies from their respective ex-dates onwards. Members should update their systems to block trades in the affected ISINs.

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects trading members operating in BSE's debt segment. The suspension is limited to the specific commercial paper ISINs listed and does not impact equity or other securities of the same issuers. The issuers span diverse sectors including NBFCs (Bajaj Finance, Bajaj Housing Finance, Godrej Finance, DSP Finance, Infina Finance), large corporates (Reliance Jio, Titan, Indian Oil, Adani Enterprises, Redington, KEC International, L&T Metro Rail), and financial services (Julius Baer Capital, Birla Group Holdings, 360 ONE, HT Media). Liquidity in these CPs will be extinguished upon redemption, which is a routine end-of-life event for short-term debt instruments.
