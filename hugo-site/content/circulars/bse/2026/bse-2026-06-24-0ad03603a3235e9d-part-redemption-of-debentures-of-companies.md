## Summary

BSE has informed trading members that 12 companies have fixed record dates for part redemption and/or interest payment on their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs). From the respective ex-dates, trading in these debentures will take place at reduced face values as specified below.

## Key Points

- 12 debenture series across multiple companies are undergoing part redemption with effect from record dates spanning 30 June 2026 to 6 July 2026.
- Trading in each debenture will be conducted at the reduced face value from the corresponding ex-date onwards.
- Settlement references fall under DR series 661/2026-2027 through 665/2026-2027.
- Akara Capital Advisors Private Limited appears multiple times with four separate ISIN series being partially redeemed.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This notice implements standard BSE procedures for reflecting part-redemption adjustments in debenture face values during secondary market trading.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the reduced face values and apply them from the respective ex-dates.
- Settlement of transactions must align with the DR settlement references cited for each scrip.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt-segment operational notice. Holders of these NCD series will receive partial principal repayment on the respective record dates, and subsequent secondary market trades will reflect the reduced face values. The impact is confined to NCD holders and debt-market traders dealing in these specific scrips; equity markets are unaffected. The largest single-debenture redemption amount is Rs. 4,90,000 for Nilkamal Limited's NL-7.40% series.
