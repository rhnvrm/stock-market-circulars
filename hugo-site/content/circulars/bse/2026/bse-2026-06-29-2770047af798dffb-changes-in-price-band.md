## Summary

BSE has revised price bands for 21 equity scrips effective June 30, 2026, as a surveillance measure. The revised bands range from 2% to 20%, restricting daily price movement to reduce volatility in these securities.

## Key Points

- 21 scrips across various sectors have had their price bands revised
- Effective date: June 30, 2026
- Bands revised to 2%, 5%, 10%, or 20% depending on the scrip
- Three Vedanta group demerged entities (Aluminium Metal, Oil and Gas, Power) assigned the widest 20% band
- Two scrips (Dhanlaxmi Cotex and Omnitex Industries) assigned the narrowest 2% band, indicating heightened surveillance concern
- Issued by BSE Surveillance Department (DOSS)

## Regulatory Changes

Price bands are revised by BSE's Surveillance Department as part of ongoing market surveillance. Tighter bands (2%) indicate elevated concern over price manipulation or abnormal volatility, while wider bands (20%) may reflect newly listed or recently restructured entities needing broader price discovery room.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised price bands for all 21 listed scrips from June 30, 2026
- Orders placed outside the revised price band limits will be rejected by the exchange trading system
- Members may seek clarification by writing to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 29, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 30, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding positions in these 21 scrips will face restricted daily price movement starting June 30, 2026. The 2% bands on Dhanlaxmi Cotex and Omnitex Industries significantly limit intraday movement. The 20% bands on the three Vedanta demerged entities (Aluminium Metal, Oil and Gas, Power) suggest these are newly listed post-restructuring securities still in price discovery. Ramco Systems at 10% is notable given its larger market profile. Overall market impact is contained to holders of these specific scrips.
