## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260717-26 dated July 17, 2026, informing Trading Members that the price bands applicable to a large number of scrips have been revised from their existing levels. The revised price bands take effect from July 20, 2026. The notice falls under the Trading category, Equity segment, and is issued by the Department of Surveillance and Supervision (DOSS).

## Key Points

- Price bands (daily circuit limits) have been revised for numerous scrips listed on BSE.
- Revised bands range across various percentage slabs, including 1%, 2%, 5%, 10%, and 20%.
- The revision applies to a mix of small-cap and mid-cap companies across sectors, including textiles, finance, technology, pharma, media, and infrastructure.
- Examples of revised price bands include: Aastha Spintex Ltd (20%), ADC India Communications Ltd (5%), Akme Fintrade (India) Ltd (10%), Dhampur Bio Organics Ltd (20%), GSS Infotech Ltd (10%), Krishna Ventures Ltd (2%), Photon Capital Advisors Ltd (1%), and Saptak Chem And Business Ltd (20%), among others.
- The full list contains more than 37 scrips, as indicated by the notice; only a partial list was captured in the source content.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine surveillance-driven adjustment of price bands (circuit filters) applied by BSE as part of its ongoing risk management and market surveillance framework. Price bands are periodically reviewed and revised based on factors such as volatility, liquidity, and trading patterns of individual scrips.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading Members of the Exchange must update their trading systems to reflect the revised price bands for the affected scrips effective July 20, 2026, and ensure orders are placed within the new circuit limits to avoid rejection.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 17, 2026
- Effective Date of Revised Price Bands: July 20, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to the specific scrips listed, most of which are small-cap or illiquid securities. A tightening of price bands (e.g., reduced to 1% or 2%) may constrain intraday price movement and trading activity for affected stocks, while widened bands (e.g., 20%) could allow for greater volatility. Investors and traders holding or tracking these specific scrips should review the revised limits before placing orders on or after July 20, 2026. There is no broader market-wide impact expected from this routine surveillance action.
