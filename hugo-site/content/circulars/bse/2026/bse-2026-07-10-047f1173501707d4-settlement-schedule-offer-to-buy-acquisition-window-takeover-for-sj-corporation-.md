## Summary

BSE, through Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd (ICCL), has released the settlement calendar for the Offer to Buy – Acquisition Window (Takeover) pertaining to SJ Corporation Limited. The bidding window runs from 13 July 2026 to 24 July 2026, with obligation download on 31 July 2026 and final settlement on 3 August 2026.

## Key Points

- Settlement Type: Takeover; Settlement No. 2026088
- Bid Start Date: 13/07/2026; Bid End Date: 24/07/2026
- Obligation Download: 31/07/2026
- Settlement Date: 03/08/2026
- Demat shares must be delivered only via the Early Pay-in of Shares mechanism, available through both CDSL and NSDL
- Members/Custodians must use Market Type "(Takeover)" and Settlement No. 2026088 in the depository system
- No EPN file upload is required by Members/Custodians
- Reference is made to earlier Notice No. 20200528-32 dated 28 May 2020 on the Revised Guidelines for Mechanism for Acquisition of Shares through Stock Exchange

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an operational notice implementing existing takeover settlement mechanisms and prior guidelines (Notice No. 20200528-32).

## Compliance Requirements

- Participants must ensure early pay-in of securities is reflected in BSE/ICCL systems before the prescribed bidding and confirmation window timelines
- Early pay-in must be completed sufficiently in advance of the OTB bidding window closure and custodian confirmation window closure
- CDSL and NSDL early pay-in securities will be earmarked/blocked in the client's account under the specified market type and settlement number
- Members should refer to the MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo file for client-wise stamp duty charges

## Important Dates

- Bid Start Date: 13 July 2026
- Bid End Date: 24 July 2026
- Obligation Download: 31 July 2026
- Settlement Date: 3 August 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, procedural settlement notice relevant primarily to trading members, custodians, and shareholders participating in the SJ Corporation Limited takeover offer. It has minimal broader market impact but is operationally important for parties involved in tendering shares to ensure timely early pay-in and settlement compliance.
