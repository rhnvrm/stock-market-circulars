## Summary

BSE received clarifications from three listed companies in response to surveillance queries regarding unusual volume movements in their securities on January 07, 2026. Emmvee Photovoltaic Power Limited (Scrip: 544608), Indian Metals & Ferro Alloys Limited (Scrip: 533047), and Manaksia Aluminium Company Limited (Scrip: 539045) each confirmed compliance with SEBI Listing Regulations and stated they have not withheld any material information that could affect price/volume behavior.

## Key Points

- BSE Surveillance Department issued volume movement letters to multiple companies on January 7, 2026
- All three companies confirmed compliance with Regulation 30 of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015
- Companies stated no material information or impending announcements were withheld that could impact share price/volume
- All companies attributed the volume/price movements to normal market conditions and market-driven behavior
- Companies confirmed they are not aware of any specific reason for the volume increase

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced in this circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Companies must respond to BSE surveillance queries regarding unusual volume movements
- Continued adherence to Regulation 30 of SEBI LODR for timely disclosure of material events
- Companies must confirm whether any undisclosed material information exists that could affect securities pricing

## Important Dates

- **January 7, 2026**: BSE issued volume movement queries via email
- **January 7, 2026**: Companies submitted clarification responses

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. These are routine surveillance clarifications with no new material information disclosed. The responses indicate normal market activity rather than company-specific developments.

**Investor Impact**: Low. Investors can be assured that companies have confirmed compliance with disclosure norms and no material undisclosed information exists.

**Operational Impact**: None. This is a standard surveillance procedure with companies confirming existing compliance protocols are being followed.
