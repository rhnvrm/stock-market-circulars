## Summary

BSE has notified trading members to stop dealing in commercial papers (CPs) of 15 companies that have set record dates for CP redemption. The affected CPs span settlement DRs 642/2026-2027, 644/2026-2027, and 645/2026-2027, with record dates falling between June 2–7, 2026.

## Key Points

- Trading in the listed commercial paper scrips must cease as these instruments approach their redemption record dates.
- 15 CP instruments across 13 issuers are covered in this notice.
- Record dates range from June 2, 2026 to June 7, 2026.
- Ex-dates coincide with record dates for most scrips, except for Mahindra Rural Housing Finance and InCred Financial Services (ex-date June 5 vs. record date June 7).
- Issued by Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President – Listing Compliance and Operations.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice under existing BSE listing and debt market regulations requiring suspension of trading in CPs prior to their redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must not deal in the commercial papers listed below on or after their respective ex-dates.
- Members should update their systems to block trading in these ISIN-identified instruments.

## Impact Assessment

Impact is limited to participants holding or trading these specific CP instruments in the BSE debt segment. This is a standard pre-redemption suspension with no systemic or equity market implications. Affected issuers include prominent names (Bajaj Finance, Tata group entities, Reliance Retail Ventures, Aditya Birla group), but the notice covers only short-term CP instruments nearing maturity, not their equity or other listed securities.
