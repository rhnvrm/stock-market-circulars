## Summary

BSE has published the Demat Auction notice for Settlement No. 830, dated 02/03/2026. This auction covers securities for which delivery obligations were not met in the normal settlement cycle. The exchange will conduct an auction to source the shortfall quantities on behalf of the buyers whose deliveries are pending.

## Key Points

- Settlement No. 830 auction notice issued on 02/03/2026
- A total of approximately 90 scrips are listed across two columns
- Quantities range from 1 share (multiple scrips) to 8,425 shares (Vodafone Idea - 532822)
- Large quantity lots include: Vodafone Idea (8,425), Mahindra & Mahindra (4,496), Fusion Finance (2,500), Aritas Vinyl (3,000), TGIF Agribusiness (1,200), Chennai Petroleum (1,450), Steel Authority of India (1,050), Pratik Panel (1,000), Insolation Energy (500), IRCON International (500)
- Auction covers shares from sectors including banking, pharma, energy, FMCG, IT, and infrastructure

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational auction notice under existing BSE settlement and auction rules for handling delivery defaults.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members who have short-delivered the listed securities must be aware their obligations will be settled through the auction mechanism
- Auction participants may submit bids to provide the shortfall securities
- Prices for auctioned securities will be determined as per BSE auction price discovery rules
- Buyers of the auctioned securities will receive shares or cash compensation (close-out) as applicable

## Important Dates

- Auction Notice Date: 02/03/2026
- Settlement No.: 830
- Auction session and pay-in/pay-out dates to follow BSE's standard T+1/T+2 auction settlement schedule

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with low market impact. It affects only the specific counterparties involved in the delivery shortfalls for the listed scrips. Notable large-quantity entries for widely-held stocks such as Vodafone Idea (8,425 shares), Mahindra & Mahindra (4,496 shares), and State Bank of India (100 shares) are operationally significant for the relevant clearing members but do not signal broader market or regulatory concerns. No systemic risk is implied.
