## Summary

BSE has issued a notice informing trading members and direct investors about the non-competitive bidding facility available for the auction of State Government Securities (SDL) scheduled by RBI on July 21, 2026. Bidding will be conducted through the NCB-GSec module of the Exchange's iBBS web-based system, in line with RBI's press release dated July 17, 2026 and BSE's earlier circular no. 20191122-28 dated November 22, 2019.

## Key Points

- Non-competitive bidding facility is available via the NCB-GSec module on the iBBS platform (https://ibbs.bseindia.com).
- The auction covers new issues and re-issues of SDLs from multiple states including Assam, Bihar, Delhi, Jammu & Kashmir, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, Tamil Nadu, Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand, and West Bengal.
- Securities include a mix of new SDL issuances (e.g., AS SDL 2036, BR SDL 2041, BR SDL 2044) and re-issues of existing coupon securities (e.g., 7.83% CG 2040, 7.82% CG 2048, 7.86% KL 2042).
- Minimum subscription size is Rs. 100, in multiples of Rs. 10,000, with maximum bid amounts ranging from Rs. 2 crore to Rs. 20 crore depending on the security.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; this notice operationalizes the existing non-competitive bidding facility (introduced via BSE circular 20191122-28) for the specific RBI-announced SDL auction on July 21, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members and direct investors intending to participate must place bids through the NCB-GSec module within the specified bidding windows. Members should ensure client bids are collated and submitted before the members' cut-off deadline.

## Important Dates

- Bid Collection Start: July 20, 2026, 10:00 am onwards (24-hour availability)
- Bid Collection End (Direct Investors): July 20, 2026, till 5:00 pm
- Bid Collection End (Members): July 21, 2026, till 8:00 am
- Auction Date: July 21, 2026
- Settlement Date: July 22, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice affecting only debt market participants (trading members and direct investors) engaging in non-competitive bidding for SDL auctions. It has no direct impact on listed equity securities or broader equity market operations.
