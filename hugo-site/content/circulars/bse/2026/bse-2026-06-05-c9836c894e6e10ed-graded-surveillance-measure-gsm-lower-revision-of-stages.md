## Summary

BSE has announced the lower revision of stages under the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework. Following a coordinated review with other exchanges, certain securities have been deemed eligible to move to lower GSM stages effective June 8, 2026. The list of eligible securities is provided in the attached Annexure.

## Key Points

- Securities eligible for lower GSM stage revision will be moved effective June 8, 2026.
- The review was conducted in coordination with other exchanges as per the GSM framework provisions.
- The GSM framework was originally introduced via Exchange Notice No. 20170223-44 dated February 23, 2017, and updated via Notice No. 20191129-23 dated November 29, 2019.
- Affected securities are listed in the attached Annexure (Annexure_GSM_LOWER).
- Members with clarifications are directed to the BSE GSM FAQ page or may contact bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular implements a downward stage revision within the existing Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework. No new regulatory rules are introduced; this is a periodic review outcome under the established GSM provisions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members should review the Annexure to identify securities moving to lower GSM stages.
- Members should update their systems and client communications accordingly for affected securities.
- For clarifications, members should refer to the FAQ at the BSE GSM page or write to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 5, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 8, 2026 (lower GSM stage revision applies)

## Impact Assessment

The lower revision of GSM stages is a positive development for the affected securities, as it reduces trading restrictions previously imposed under higher GSM stages. Securities at lower GSM stages face fewer constraints on trading frequency, price bands, and settlement, improving liquidity for those counters. The impact is security-specific; the full list of affected stocks is available in the Annexure. Broader market impact is limited as this is a routine periodic review.
