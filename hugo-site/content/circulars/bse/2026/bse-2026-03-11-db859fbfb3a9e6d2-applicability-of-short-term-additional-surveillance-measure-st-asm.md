## Summary

BSE has issued a circular detailing changes to the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective March 12, 2026. Eight securities are newly shortlisted under the ST-ASM 5/15/30 Days framework, one security is moved to a higher ASM stage, no securities are moved to a lower stage, and nine securities are exiting the ST-ASM framework (some transitioning to LT-ASM).

## Key Points

- 8 new securities added to ST-ASM framework (Stage I) effective March 12, 2026
- 1 security (Logiciel Solutions Ltd) moved to a higher ST-ASM stage effective March 12, 2026
- 0 securities moved to a lower ST-ASM stage
- 9 securities removed from ST-ASM framework effective March 12, 2026
- 2 of the exiting securities (Lead Financial Services Ltd, Veerkrupa Jewellers Ltd) are moving to LT-ASM framework
- SME scrips are included among newly shortlisted securities (Identixweb Ltd, Logiciel Solutions Ltd)
- NSE data used for Modern Thread India Ltd classification

## Regulatory Changes

The ST-ASM framework applies enhanced surveillance to securities exhibiting abnormal price/volume movements. Securities under ST-ASM face restrictions including reduced price bands and/or periodic call auction mechanisms over 5, 15, or 30 trading day windows. Stage escalation (as applied to Logiciel Solutions Ltd) means tighter surveillance conditions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must update client margins and trading parameters for all affected securities effective March 12, 2026
- Securities newly added to ST-ASM will be subject to applicable price band and trading restrictions per their assigned stage
- Investors holding or trading in affected securities should review the specific ST-ASM stage conditions applicable to each scrip

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** March 12, 2026 — all additions, stage changes, and removals take effect
- **Circular Date:** March 11, 2026
