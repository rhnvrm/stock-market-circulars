## Summary

BSE has announced the non-competitive bidding (NCB) facility for the Government of India's auction of three Treasury Bills on June 24, 2026. Trading members and direct investors can participate through the NCB-GSec module on BSE's iBBS web-based platform. This notice follows an RBI press release dated June 19, 2026, and is in continuation of Exchange circular no. 20180423-42 (April 26, 2018) which launched the NCB facility for government securities auctions.

## Key Points

- Three T-Bills are being auctioned: 91-day (symbol: 091T230926), 182-day (symbol: 182T231226), and 364-day (symbol: 364T230627)
- All three T-Bills are new issuances (not reissues)
- Bidding is conducted through the NCB-GSec module at https://ibbs.bseindia.com
- Minimum lot size is Rs. 100 for all three securities; minimum bid amount is Rs. 10,000
- Total issue size across all three T-Bills is Rs. 24,000 crore (12,000 + 6,000 + 6,000)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular operationalises the existing NCB framework (per circular 20180423-42) for the upcoming T-Bill auction announced by RBI on June 19, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use the NCB-GSec module on iBBS (https://ibbs.bseindia.com) to place bids
- Members must submit bids by June 24, 2026, 8:00 am; direct investors must bid by June 23, 2026, 5:00 pm
- For new user IDs or password resets, contact Trading Operations at user.trdops@bseindia.com or 022-2272-8005/8215
- Bidding queries: trading.ops@bseindia.com or 022-2272-8376/5626/8226
- Obligation and pay-in queries: ICCL Operations at Collaterals@icclindia.com / settlements@icclindia.com or 022-2272-8468/8704/8264

## Important Dates

| Event | Date & Time |
|---|---|

This is a routine operational notice with low market impact. It enables trading members and retail/direct investors to participate in the government's weekly T-Bill auction through the exchange platform without needing to place competitive bids directly with RBI. No equities or listed corporate securities are affected.
