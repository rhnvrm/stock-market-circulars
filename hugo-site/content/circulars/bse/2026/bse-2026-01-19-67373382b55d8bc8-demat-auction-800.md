## Summary

BSE has published the list of securities for demat auction under settlement number 800 dated January 19, 2026. The auction covers 81 scrips where sellers have failed to deliver securities, requiring the exchange to conduct an auction to fulfill delivery obligations to buyers.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 800
- Auction Date: January 19, 2026
- Total Scrips: 81 companies
- Quantities range from 1 share to 26,000 shares per scrip
- Highest quantity: SWOJAS FOODS (26,000 shares)
- Includes major stocks like Reliance Industries (1,568 shares), ITC (2,535 shares), Infosys (29 shares)

## Regulatory Context

Demat auctions are conducted by the exchange when selling members fail to deliver securities within the settlement cycle. The auction mechanism ensures that buyers receive their entitled securities while creating consequences for settlement failures.

## Compliance Requirements

- Selling members who failed delivery must participate in auction or face penalties
- Buyers will receive securities through auction mechanism
- Auction participants must meet margin and settlement requirements

## Important Dates

- Settlement Number: 800
- Auction Date: January 19, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This auction represents routine settlement enforcement affecting 81 scrips. While it includes some large-cap stocks like Reliance, Infosys, and ITC, the quantities involved are relatively small. The auction ensures market integrity by completing failed deliveries and maintaining investor confidence in settlement mechanisms. Market participants holding these securities should be aware of potential price volatility during the auction process.
