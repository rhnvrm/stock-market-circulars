## Summary

BSE has issued an update to its Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, referencing multiple prior notices dating back to June 2023. Securities listed in Annexure I have met the criteria for inclusion under ESM and will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open and new positions effective July 14, 2026. Some securities already under ESM will move to a lower stage (Stage I), while others will exit the framework entirely (Annexure II) effective July 13, 2026. A consolidated list of all securities currently under the ESM framework is provided in Annexure III.

## Key Points

- Securities meeting ESM criteria (per Annexure I) will require minimum 100% margin on all open positions as of July 13, 2026, and new positions created from July 14, 2026
- Securities continuing under ESM but moving to a lower stage (Stage I) will do so effective July 13, 2026
- Securities qualifying under ESM will shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment effective July 14, 2026
- Securities moving to Stage II will be placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction effective July 13, 2026
- Securities exiting the ESM framework (per Annexure II) will do so effective July 13, 2026
- Price bands for securities exiting ESM will revert to pre-ESM levels, unless another surveillance measure applies
- ESM operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures
- Inclusion under ESM is a market surveillance action and not indicative of adverse findings against the company

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the ongoing ESM framework established via prior circulars (notice nos. 20230602-44, 20230718-46, 20240809-42, 20240920-63, 20241004-65, and 20250725-61). No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a periodic review and reclassification of securities under the existing ESM structure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply minimum 100% margin on affected securities' open positions as of July 13, 2026, and new positions from July 14, 2026
- Members must ensure affected securities are processed under Trade-for-Trade settlement rather than Rolling Settlement where applicable
- Members must apply the 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism for Stage II securities
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- July 10, 2026: Notice issued
- July 13, 2026: Stage migration (to Stage I), Stage II price band/auction changes, and exits from ESM framework take effect; open positions as of this date subject to margin requirement
- July 14, 2026: 100% margin requirement and Trade-for-Trade shift take effect for newly included/qualifying securities

## Impact Assessment

This notice has high impact on the specific securities named in the annexures (not detailed in the provided text), as inclusion in ESM significantly restricts trading through 100% margin requirements and Trade-for-Trade settlement, reducing liquidity and increasing transaction costs for affected stocks. Conversely, securities exiting the framework will see restored trading flexibility and reinstated price bands. Market-wide impact is limited to specific securities under review rather than systemic effects.
