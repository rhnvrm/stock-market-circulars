## Summary

BSE has announced the suspension of trading in commercial papers issued by 8 companies effective from their respective record dates. The suspension is due to redemption of commercial papers scheduled between February 4-6, 2026. Trading members are advised not to deal in these securities from the specified dates.

## Key Points

- Trading suspension applies to 8 commercial paper issuances
- Record dates range from February 4-6, 2026
- All suspensions are for redemption purposes
- Trading members must cease dealing from respective no-dealings dates
- Notice issued under corporate actions category for debt segment

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes. This is a routine suspension notice for commercial paper redemptions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute any trades in the listed commercial papers from their respective no-dealings dates
- Ensure no deals are entered for the following securities:
  - Standard Chartered Securities (India) Limited (ISIN: INE472H14680) - from Feb 4, 2026
  - Mirae Asset Sharekhan Financial Services Limited (ISIN: INE550X14AL8) - from Feb 4, 2026
  - Infina Finance Private Limited (ISIN: INE879F14LG5) - from Feb 5, 2026
  - Bajaj Housing Finance Limited (ISIN: INE377Y14BP3) - from Feb 6, 2026
  - Time Technoplast Limited (ISIN: INE508G14IB5) - from Feb 6, 2026
  - Tata Power Co. Ltd (ISIN: INE245A14KM0) - from Feb 6, 2026
  - National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (ISIN: INE261F14OO4) - from Feb 6, 2026
  - 360 ONE WAM Limited (ISIN: INE466L14FC0) - from Feb 6, 2026

## Important Dates

- **February 4, 2026**: Record date and trading suspension for Standard Chartered Securities and Mirae Asset Sharekhan Financial Services
- **February 5, 2026**: Record date and trading suspension for Infina Finance Private Limited
- **February 6, 2026**: Record date and trading suspension for Bajaj Housing Finance, Time Technoplast, Tata Power, NABARD, and 360 ONE WAM Limited

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to the debt market segment, specifically affecting holders and traders of the listed commercial papers. The suspensions are temporary and procedural, relating to normal redemption processes. Investors holding these securities should be aware that trading will cease from the specified dates. The notice affects both institutional and retail participants in the commercial paper market, though the impact is routine and expected for maturing debt instruments.
