## Summary

Bombay Stock Exchange has published the demat auction list for settlement number 815 dated February 9, 2026. The circular lists 223 scrips across various sectors with their respective scrip codes and quantities subject to auction. This is a routine market operation to settle delivery obligations where securities were not delivered on time.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 815
- Date: February 9, 2026
- Total scrips listed: 223
- Covers diverse sectors including banking, pharmaceuticals, steel, power, technology, FMCG, and infrastructure
- Highest quantity: WELCURE DRUGS (80,004 shares), TOURISM FINANCE (306,529 shares)
- Major stocks included: HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, State Bank of India, Infosys, TCS, Reliance Power, Tata Motors, ITC, among others

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced in this circular. This is a standard operational notice regarding auction proceedings.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and clearing members must note the scrips and quantities listed for auction
- Settlement obligations must be fulfilled as per auction procedures
- Participants should ensure timely delivery of securities to avoid auction situations
- Auction settlement procedures as per BSE regulations apply

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: February 9, 2026
- Settlement Number: 815

## Impact Assessment

This auction list affects 223 securities across multiple sectors. The impact is primarily operational, affecting brokers and clearing members who failed to deliver securities on time. Market participants holding short positions in these securities may face auction obligations. The presence of high-value stocks like HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Infosys, and others indicates potential liquidity requirements for settlement. The highest quantities are in Tourism Finance (306,529 shares) and Welcure Drugs (80,004 shares), which may see price volatility during auction proceedings.
