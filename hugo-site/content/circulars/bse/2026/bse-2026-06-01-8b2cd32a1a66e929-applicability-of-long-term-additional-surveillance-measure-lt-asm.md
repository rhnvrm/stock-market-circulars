## Summary

BSE has issued a notice applying Long Term Additional Surveillance Measures (LT-ASM) on a set of securities pursuant to the revised LT-ASM framework established through various Exchange notices since March 2018. The measures include 100% margin requirements, lower price bands, movement of securities to higher LT-ASM stages, and transfer of certain securities to restricted trading groups (T/XT/MT/TS), effective June 2–4, 2026.

## Key Points

- 100% margins will be applicable from **June 04, 2026** on all open positions as of June 03, 2026, and all new positions created from June 04, 2026 onwards (Annexure I, Part A).
- Securities are being moved to their respective **higher stages** of the LT-ASM Framework effective **June 02, 2026** (Annexure I, Part B).
- **Lower Price Band** shall be applicable effective **June 02, 2026** for securities elevated to higher LT-ASM stages.
- Certain securities will continue in the LT-ASM framework but will be **transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group** effective **June 04, 2026** (Annexure I, Part D).
- Securities **moving out** of the LT-ASM Framework effective June 02, 2026 are listed in Annexure II.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the LT-ASM Framework is provided in Annexure III.
- Shortlisting is based on XBRL submissions by respective listed companies and is purely a market surveillance action, not an adverse action against the company.
- The price band of scrips exiting the framework will revert to the band applicable before shortlisting, unless the scrip is under another surveillance measure.

## Regulatory Changes

The notice references the revised LT-ASM framework as updated through:
- Exchange notice no. 20180321-46 (March 21, 2018) – original framework.
- Exchange notice no. 20181027-1 (October 27, 2018).
- Exchange notice no. 20201204-56 (December 04, 2020).
- Exchange notice no. 20240809-46 (August 09, 2024).
- Exchange notice no. 20240920-63 (September 20, 2024) – most recent revision.
- Exchange notice no. 20210604-41 (June 04, 2021) – governing group transfer provisions.

No new framework changes are introduced; this notice applies the existing revised LT-ASM framework to updated lists of securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Brokers/Members**: Must ensure 100% margins are collected on affected open positions as of June 03, 2026 and on all new positions from June 04, 2026.
- **Market Participants**: Must be aware of lower price bands effective June 02, 2026 for securities moved to higher LT-ASM stages.
- **All Participants**: LT-ASM operates alongside all other prevailing surveillance measures; both sets of restrictions apply concurrently.
- For clarifications, members may contact BSE Surveillance at **bse.surv@bseindia.com**.

## Important Dates

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| June 02, 2026 | Securities moved to higher LT-ASM stages take effect; Lower Price Band applicable; Securities exit LT-ASM Framework (Annexure II) |
| June 03, 2026 | Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin |
| June 04, 2026 | 100% margins effective on open positions (as of June 03) and new positions; Securities transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group |

## Impact Assessment

**High impact** for traders and investors holding positions in shortlisted securities:
- The 100% margin requirement significantly increases the capital cost of holding or taking new positions in affected scrips, likely reducing liquidity and increasing volatility.
- Transfer to T/XT/MT/TS groups restricts normal rolling settlement, requiring trade-for-trade basis settlement and limiting speculative activity.
- Lower price bands cap downside moves per session, which can trap sellers if selling pressure builds.
- The measures are periodic and reviewed regularly, so affected entities should monitor subsequent BSE notices for exits or escalations.
- Shortlisting is surveillance-driven (based on XBRL data) and does not imply fundamental wrongdoing, but the practical trading impact is immediate and material.
