## Summary

BSE has listed new privately placed commercial papers (CPs) from Fedbank Financial Services Limited, Reliance Retail Ventures Limited, 360 ONE Prime Limited, and Birla Group Holdings Private Limited on its Debt Market Segment, effective March 13, 2026. All securities are allotted on March 12, 2026 and are available for trading in dematerialised form with a standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof.

## Key Points

- All securities are traded exclusively in dematerialised form under their respective ISINs.
- Standard trading denomination is Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof; tick size is 1 paise.
- Five commercial paper instruments across four issuers have been freshly listed.
- Credit ratings for all listed CPs are top-tier (A1+ from CRISIL, ICRA, and/or CARE).
- Face value for all instruments is Rs. 5,00,000 per unit.
- Issued on a private placement basis; allotment date is March 12, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine listing notification under BSE's debt segment listing framework for privately placed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form.
- Trading must occur in the standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof.
- For clarifications, trading members may contact BSE's Debt Department at: 22728352 / 8597 / 8995 / 5753 / 8915.

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Moderate. The listing adds liquidity options in the short-to-medium-term debt space for institutional investors. The aggregate issuance is significant — notably Reliance Retail Ventures with 30,000 units (Rs. 1,470 crore face value) and Birla Group Holdings with 8,500 units.

**Investor Relevance:** Relevant to institutional and HNI investors in the debt segment seeking high-rated (A1+) short-duration instruments.

**Interest Rates:** Coupon rates range from 7.23% p.a. (Reliance Retail Ventures, 91-day tenor) to 8.10% p.a. (360 ONE Prime, ~1-year tenor), reflecting prevailing short-term market rates.

**Issuing/Paying Agents:** Federal Bank, ICICI Bank, HDFC Bank, and Axis Bank act as issuing and paying agents for the respective instruments.
