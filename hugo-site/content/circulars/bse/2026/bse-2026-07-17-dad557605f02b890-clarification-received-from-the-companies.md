## Summary

BSE has observed significant price/volume movement in a set of listed scrips and, as part of its surveillance function, wrote to the respective companies seeking clarification. Replies have been received from 9 companies and are attached to the notice, while clarifications remain awaited from 7 other companies.

## Key Points

- Notice issued by BSE's Department of Surveillance (DOSS) under the Company-related category, Equity segment.
- Companies that have replied: Archean Chemical Industries Ltd (543657), Ceenik Exports India Ltd (531119) - flagged for price variation, Fermenta Biotech Ltd (506414), Hardwyn India Ltd (541276), Jindal Worldwide Ltd (531543) - flagged for volume variation, Jivial Industries Ltd (544805), MKP Mobility Ltd (521244), Shree Hari Chemicals Export Ltd (524336), Wanbury Ltd (524212).
- Companies whose replies are still awaited: CMX Holdings Ltd (532217), Darjeeling Industries Ltd (539770), Gconnect Logitech and Supply Chain Ltd (544156), Kinetic Trust Ltd (531274), Mapro Industries Ltd (509762), Sujala Trading & Holdings Ltd (539117), VJTF Eduservices Ltd (509026).
- Actual reply letters from companies are provided as attachments to the notice.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is a routine disclosure/surveillance notice, not a regulatory or policy change.

## Compliance Requirements

Companies listed as "reply awaited" are expected to submit their clarification to BSE regarding the observed price/volume movement. Investors and trading members should refer to the attached replies for company-specific context before trading in these scrips.

## Important Dates

Notice dated 17 July 2026. No specific compliance deadline mentioned for the companies with pending replies.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has limited direct market impact but provides transparency for investors and trading members regarding unusual price or volume activity in the named scrips. The pending replies from 7 companies may warrant continued monitoring, as unresolved clarifications can sometimes precede further surveillance action (e.g., ASM/GSM placement) if unusual activity persists.
