## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260521-30 directing trading members that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure) will be moved to their respective higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective **May 22, 2026**. The move is in accordance with the GSM Framework published on February 23, 2017 and subsequent exchange notices. Affected securities will be subject to progressively stringent surveillance actions depending on the GSM stage assigned.

## Key Points

- Securities in the attached Annexure will be shifted to higher GSM stages from May 22, 2026
- GSM framework operates across four stages (I–IV), each with escalating restrictions
- Settlement for all affected securities will be on a **trade-to-trade basis** (Stages II–IV); no netting off is permitted
- Stage I: 100% applicable margin rate with a price band of 5% or lower
- Stage II: Trade-to-trade, 5% or lower price band, and Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of **50% of trade value** to be deposited by buyers
- Stage III: Trade-to-trade, 5% or lower price band, trading permitted **once a week (every Monday/1st trading day)**, and ASD of **100% of trade value** by buyers
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III with an additional restriction of **no upward price movement**
- Trading members are urged to take adequate precautions while trading in these securities
- For clarifications, members may refer to the BSE GSM FAQ page or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Regulatory Changes

This notice is issued under the GSM Framework originally published via Exchange Notice No. 20170223-44 (February 23, 2017) and updated through subsequent notices including:
- Notice No. 20170303-29 (March 3, 2017)
- Notice No. 20180720-55 (July 20, 2018)
- Notice No. 20191129-23 (November 29, 2019)
- Notice No. 20231117-63 (November 17, 2023)

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is an application of the existing GSM framework to a new set of securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading members** must note the securities listed in the Annexure and apply the corresponding GSM stage restrictions from May 22, 2026
- **Buyers** in Stage II securities must deposit an ASD of 50% of trade value
- **Buyers** in Stage III and IV securities must deposit an ASD of 100% of trade value
- No netting off is allowed; all trades must be settled on a gross/trade-to-trade basis
- Members must ensure clients are aware of the once-a-week trading restriction (Stages III & IV) applicable only on Mondays or the first trading day of the week
- Members should refer to the GSM FAQ at: http://www.bseindia.com/markets/equity/EQReports/graded_surveil_measure.aspx?expandable=6

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** May 21, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 22, 2026 — GSM stage changes apply from this date

## Impact Assessment

Securities moved into higher GSM stages face significantly reduced liquidity and increased trading costs. The mandatory trade-to-trade settlement eliminates intraday netting, increasing capital requirements for traders. The ASD requirement (up to 100% of trade value for Stage III/IV) substantially raises the cost of acquiring these securities, likely suppressing demand. Stage IV securities face the most severe restriction — no upward price movement — effectively capping any potential gains. Retail and institutional investors holding or considering positions in these securities should reassess risk exposure, as the restrictions can lead to sharp price declines and difficulty in exiting positions. The circular does not name specific tickers in the notice body; the full list of affected scrips is available only in the attached Annexure.
