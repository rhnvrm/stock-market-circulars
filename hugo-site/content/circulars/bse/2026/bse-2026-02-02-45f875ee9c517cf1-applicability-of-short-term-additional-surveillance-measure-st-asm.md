## Summary

BSE has issued updates to the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective February 03, 2026. Four securities are being newly shortlisted under the ST-ASM 5/15/30 Days framework: Ceeta Industries Ltd, MTAR Technologies Ltd, Standard Batteries Ltd, and Sterlite Technologies Ltd. Six securities are moving out of the framework: Baazar Style Retail Ltd, Sudarshan Colorants India Ltd, Riddhi Display Equipments Limited, RMC Switchgears Ltd, Shekhawati Industries Ltd, and Western Overseas Study Abroad Limited.

## Key Points

- 4 securities newly shortlisted in Short Term 5/15/30 Days ASM Framework
- 6 securities moving out of ST-ASM Framework
- No securities moving to higher or lower ASM stages within the framework
- Consolidated list includes 16+ securities currently under ST-ASM surveillance
- Framework includes different stages (Stage I, Stage II) based on surveillance intensity
- SME scrips and T+0 scrips are specifically marked in the framework

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must adhere to enhanced surveillance conditions for securities under ST-ASM framework
- Securities in ST-ASM are subject to special trading restrictions including potential margin requirements
- Market participants must monitor staged progression (Stage I, Stage II) for affected securities
- Securities may move between frameworks (LT-ASM, Trade for Trade, GSM, Pledge, ESM) based on surveillance criteria

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** February 03, 2026 - ST-ASM changes become applicable
- **Circular Date:** February 02, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:**
- Enhanced surveillance measures may result in reduced liquidity and higher trading costs for the 4 securities entering ST-ASM
- The 6 securities exiting the framework may experience improved liquidity conditions
- ST-ASM framework applies staged restrictions (5/15/30 days) based on volatility and price movement patterns

**Operational Impact:**
- Brokers and trading members need to update risk management systems for affected securities
- Investors holding these securities should be aware of potential margin requirement changes
- The consolidated list shows 16+ securities remain under various stages of ST-ASM surveillance, indicating continued regulatory focus on market stability

**Trading Implications:**
- Securities under ST-ASM face additional margin requirements and trading restrictions
- Movement between surveillance stages depends on price behavior and trading patterns
- Exit from ST-ASM indicates compliance with stability criteria or migration to other surveillance frameworks
