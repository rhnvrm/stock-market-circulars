## Summary

BSE has notified that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure_GSM) will be moved to their respective higher Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) stages with effect from May 26, 2026. The action is taken under the GSM Framework established via Exchange Notice No. 20170223-44 dated February 23, 2017, and subsequent amendments. Affected securities will face escalating surveillance restrictions depending on the GSM stage assigned.

## Key Points

- Securities in the Annexure will be elevated to higher GSM stages from May 26, 2026.
- Settlement for all GSM-stage securities will be on a trade-to-trade basis; no netting off is permitted.
- Price bands of 5% or lower will apply across all GSM stages.
- Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) requirements apply from Stage II onwards, payable by buyers.
- Trading frequency is restricted to once a week (every Monday or first trading day of the week) for Stage III and Stage IV securities.
- Stage IV securities additionally carry a restriction of no upward price movement.
- Members are advised to take adequate precautions when trading in listed securities.

## Regulatory Changes

The GSM framework defines four progressive surveillance stages:

| Stage | Key Surveillance Actions |
|-------|-------------------------|
| I | 100% applicable margin rate; price band of 5% or lower |
| II | Trade-for-trade; price band 5% or lower; ASD of 50% of trade value (buyers) |
| III | Trade-for-trade; price band 5% or lower; trading once a week (Monday/first trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value (buyers) |
| IV | Trade-for-trade; price band 5% or lower; trading once a week (Monday/first trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value (buyers); no upward price movement |

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure ASD collection from buyers at the prescribed rates for Stage II, III, and IV securities before executing trades.
- No netting off is permitted; all settlements must be done on a trade-to-trade basis.
- For Stage III and IV securities, trades may only be executed on Monday or the first trading day of the week.
- Members should review the GSM FAQ available at the BSE website and direct clarification queries to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** May 26, 2026 — securities in the Annexure move to their respective higher GSM stages.
- **Notice Date:** May 25, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

Affected securities will face significant liquidity constraints and higher trading costs. The mandatory trade-to-trade settlement eliminates intraday netting, increasing capital requirements for traders. ASD obligations of 50%–100% of trade value for Stage II–IV securities substantially raise the cost of buying these stocks. Stage III and IV restrictions limiting trading to once a week severely reduce liquidity. Investors already holding these securities may find exits difficult, particularly at Stage IV where upward price movement is disallowed. The specific list of impacted securities is available in the Annexure_GSM attachment to the original notice.
