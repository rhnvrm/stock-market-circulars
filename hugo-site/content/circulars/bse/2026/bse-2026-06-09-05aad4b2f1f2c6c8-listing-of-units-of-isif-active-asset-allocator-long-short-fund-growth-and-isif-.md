## Summary

BSE has admitted units of iSIF Active Asset Allocator Long-Short Fund – Growth and iSIF Active Asset Allocator Long-Short Fund - Direct Plan – Growth under ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund to dealings on the Exchange in the 'B' Group, effective Wednesday, June 10, 2026. The fund is an interval investment strategy that dynamically invests across equity, debt, equity and debt derivatives, InVITs, and commodity derivatives, including limited short exposure via derivatives.

## Key Points

- Two fund units listed: Growth plan (Scrip Code 544775, ISIN INF109K30059) and Direct Plan – Growth (Scrip Code 544776, ISIN INF109K30067)
- Both units assigned to Group B on BSE
- Market lot is 1 unit; trades must be in dematerialized form per SEBI guidelines dated February 16, 2000
- Total units listed: 348,651,891 (Growth) and 129,510,473 (Direct Plan – Growth)
- Face value and issue price: Rs. 10.00 per unit for both schemes
- BOLT System Scrip IDs: ISIFAAFG and ISIFAAFDG respectively
- Fund manager: ICICI Prudential Asset Management Company Limited
- Registrar and Transfer Agent: Computer Age Management Services Limited (CAMS), Chennai

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. Listing is in accordance with existing SEBI guidelines dated February 16, 2000, requiring mutual fund units to be traded only in dematerialized form.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure all trades in these scrips are settled in dematerialized form only
- Trades must be in unit market lot (one unit per lot)
- Members requiring clarification may contact Mr. Parag Jain (Manager, Listing Operations IPO) at BSE via direct.listing@bseindia.com or Tel: 022-2272-8685

## Important Dates

- Date of Allotment: June 5, 2026
- Effective date of listing and trading: June 10, 2026 (Wednesday)
- Circular date: June 9, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing circular with limited market impact. It enables trading of two new ICICI Prudential interval fund units on BSE, providing liquidity to existing unit holders. The fund's strategy (dynamic allocation including short exposure via derivatives) is relatively niche. Impact is confined to investors in these specific schemes and does not affect broader market operations or other securities.
