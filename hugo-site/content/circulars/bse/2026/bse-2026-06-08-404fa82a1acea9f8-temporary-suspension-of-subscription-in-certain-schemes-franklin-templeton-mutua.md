## Summary

BSE has issued a notice informing members that Franklin Templeton Mutual Fund has requested temporary suspension of lumpsum (fresh and additional) purchases and switch-in transactions in two specific schemes on the BSE StAR MF Platform effective June 08, 2026. This follows an earlier restriction notice (20260518-13) on Franklin India Mutual Fund subscriptions.

## Key Points

- Lumpsum investments (fresh and additional purchases) and switch-in transactions are disabled for the two affected schemes.
- Fresh SIP and STP registrations into the designated schemes are capped at ₹50,000 per PAN per month (first holder/guardian).
- Existing systematic registrations (SIPs, XSIPs, and AMC STPs) already on BSE StAR MF will continue to be processed at the RTA/AMC end.
- Redemption, switch-out, and STP-out transactions remain permitted without any amount restriction.
- This notice is an addendum to Notice No. 20260518-13.

## Regulatory Changes

The following transaction types have been disabled on BSE StAR MF Platform for the affected schemes:
- Fresh lumpsum purchases
- Additional lumpsum purchases
- Switch-in transactions

Fresh SIP and STP registrations are permitted but subject to a cap of ₹50,000 per PAN per month.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members (brokers/distributors) must ensure no lumpsum or switch-in orders are submitted for the two affected schemes.
- Members should inform investors about the ₹50,000 per PAN per month cap for new SIP/STP registrations.
- Members must note that redemptions and switch-outs can proceed normally without restrictions.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** June 08, 2026 — Suspension of lumpsum investments and switch-ins takes effect.

## Impact Assessment

Investors in or seeking to invest in **Franklin U.S. Opportunities Equity Active Fund of Funds** and **Franklin Asian Equity Fund** via BSE StAR MF Platform are directly affected. New lumpsum investments are blocked, limiting access to these international equity schemes. However, existing SIP/STP investors are unaffected as their registrations continue. Redemption liquidity is fully maintained, which mitigates investor risk. The cap on new SIP/STP registrations at ₹50,000/PAN/month may restrict high-value systematic investors. The restriction appears to be linked to regulatory or foreign investment limit concerns typical for overseas fund-of-funds structures.
