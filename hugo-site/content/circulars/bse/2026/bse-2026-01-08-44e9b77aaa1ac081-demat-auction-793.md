## Summary

BSE has announced Demat Auction for Settlement No. 793 dated January 8, 2026. The auction covers 100 different scrips with specified quantities ranging from 1 share to 173,209 shares across various listed companies. This is a routine settlement auction to cover shortages in delivery obligations.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 793
- Date: January 8, 2026
- Total number of scrips: 100 (50 pairs listed in two columns)
- Quantities range from 1 share to 173,209 shares
- Includes major stocks like TCS, Bajaj Auto, HCL Tech, Coal India, Bharti Airtel, and Mahindra & Mahindra
- Highest quantity: Standard Capital (173,209 shares)
- Several mid-cap and small-cap stocks included

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a standard operational auction notice.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing members with shortages in the specified scrips must participate in the auction
- Members must ensure availability of funds for auction participation
- Settlement obligations must be met as per exchange rules
- Successful bidders must complete payment and delivery as per settlement schedule

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: January 8, 2026
- Settlement Number: 793
- Auction execution date would follow standard BSE settlement calendar

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects multiple scrips across market capitalization segments. The auction is necessary to complete settlement obligations and maintain market integrity.

**Operational Impact:** Clearing members with delivery shortages in these scrips must arrange funds and participate in the auction. Failure to deliver can result in penalties and auction consequences.

**Liquidity Impact:** Temporary price impact possible on scrips with larger auction quantities, particularly Standard Capital (173,209 shares), CWD Limited (22,500 shares), Tahmar Enterprises (10,543 shares), and other high-quantity scrips.

**Risk Assessment:** Standard operational risk associated with settlement auctions. Members should ensure adequate risk management and settlement discipline to avoid auction situations.
