## Summary

BSE has issued price band rules for the Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) segment in reference to SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-1/P/CIR/2024/87 dated June 24, 2024. The framework introduces a pre-open session price range, an initial dynamic price band, and a step-up relaxation mechanism during trending markets, aimed at ensuring orderly trading and protecting market integrity.

## Key Points

- Pre-open session: Price range of ±5% of the previous day's close price applies to EGR.
- Initial dynamic price band is set at 10% of the previous close price at the start of the trading day.
- Dynamic price band is relaxed in increments of 5% after each 15-minute cooling-off period if trades occur near the band limit.
- Trigger threshold: if the last trade occurs at 9.90% or more of base price, the band relaxes to 15%; at 14.90% or more, it relaxes to 20%, and so on.
- During the cooling-off period, trading continues within the previous (unrelaxed) price band.
- In exceptional circumstances (extreme international price movements), the price band may be relaxed directly to the required level with appropriate market notice.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular operationalises the price band framework for the EGR segment as envisaged under SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-1/P/CIR/2024/87 (June 24, 2024). It codifies specific numeric thresholds (5% pre-open, 10% initial dynamic band, 5% incremental flex, 15-minute cooling-off) that were not previously specified in BSE notices.

## Compliance Requirements

- EGR segment participants must ensure their order management systems respect the published price band limits.
- Members must not place orders beyond the applicable price band at any point during the trading session.
- Members should monitor for band-relaxation announcements during intra-day trading and adjust order parameters accordingly.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: June 12, 2026 (effective immediately upon issuance).
- Reference SEBI Circular: June 24, 2024.

## Impact Assessment

This circular directly affects members and participants trading in the EGR segment on BSE. The dynamic price band mechanism with incremental 5% relaxations and mandatory 15-minute cooling-off periods is designed to prevent runaway price movements while still allowing price discovery during strong trending markets. The framework is broadly similar to equity-segment circuit-breaker logic, so members familiar with equity trading should find compliance straightforward. No specific stocks are impacted; this applies to all EGR contracts traded on the BSE EGR segment.
