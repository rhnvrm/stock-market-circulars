## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that 15 companies have fixed record dates for the redemption of their Commercial Papers. Trading members are advised not to deal in the commercial papers of these companies effective from their respective ex-dates.

## Key Points

- Trading in the listed commercial papers is suspended effective from their respective ex-dates
- All suspensions are related to scheduled redemption of commercial papers
- Affected companies span NBFC, housing finance, and financial services sectors
- Record dates range from June 25, 2026 to July 9, 2026
- Settlement references are under DR series 659/2026-2027 through 668/2026-2027

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice under BSE's Listing Compliance and Operations department directing trading members to suspend trading in the specified commercial paper instruments ahead of their redemption record dates.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the commercial papers listed in this notice on or after the respective ex-dates
- Members should update their systems to reflect the trading suspension for each affected scrip

## Impact Assessment

This notice has minimal equity market impact as it pertains exclusively to debt instruments (commercial papers). Holders of these commercial papers should note that trading will be suspended from the respective ex-dates and the instruments will be redeemed on the record dates. Bajaj group entities (Bajaj Finance, Bajaj Financial Securities, Bajaj Housing Finance) have the highest concentration with five separate commercial paper instruments maturing across different dates.
