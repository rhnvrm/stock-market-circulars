## Summary

BSE's Department of Surveillance & Supervision (DOSS) has revised the price bands for 17 equity scrips effective June 2, 2026. The majority of affected stocks are moved to a 5% price band, three stocks to a 2% band (Chambal Breweries, Mercury Ev-Tech, Modern Malleables, Picturehouse Media), and one stock (Trio Mercantile & Trading Ltd) to a 10% band.

## Key Points

- 17 scrips have price bands revised effective June 2, 2026
- 13 scrips revised to 5% price band
- 3 scrips revised to 2% price band: Chambal Breweries & Distilleries Ltd (512301), Mercury Ev-Tech Ltd (531357), Modern Malleables Ltd (517336), Picturehouse Media Ltd (532355)
- 1 scrip revised to 10% price band: Trio Mercantile & Trading Ltd (534755)
- Issued under Notice No. 20260601-30 by the Surveillance department

## Regulatory Changes

Price bands restrict the maximum intraday price movement (up or down) for a security. Tighter bands (2%) indicate heightened surveillance concern, while a wider band (10%) for Trio Mercantile & Trading may indicate a relaxation or reclassification. These revisions are made at the discretion of the BSE Surveillance department.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure orders in the listed scrips comply with the new price band limits from June 2, 2026 onwards
- No specific action required from listed companies; this is an exchange-level trading control measure
- Members with queries may contact bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 1, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 2, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding positions in the 17 affected scrips will face revised intraday price movement limits starting June 2, 2026. The tightening to 2% for four stocks (Chambal Breweries, Mercury Ev-Tech, Modern Malleables, Picturehouse Media) signals increased surveillance scrutiny and may reduce liquidity and volatility in these counters. The widening to 10% for Trio Mercantile & Trading Ltd may reflect an easing of prior restrictions. Overall market impact is limited given the small-cap/micro-cap nature of the affected companies.
