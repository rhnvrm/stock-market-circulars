## Summary

BSE has announced its periodic surveillance review revising the list of securities placed on Trade-to-Trade (T/XT/MT/TS) settlement basis, effective July 23, 2026. This action is part of the Exchange's ongoing preventive surveillance measures conducted in consultation with SEBI to safeguard market safety and investor interests.

## Key Points

- Securities satisfying all criteria I, II, and III (per Annexure I) will be transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group effective July 23, 2026
- Securities listed in Annexure II will continue to remain in T/XT/MT/TS/P/Z/ZP Group and will attract a 5% price band
- Settlement for these scrips will be done on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off of positions permitted
- A VAR Margin of 100% will be levied on these scrips as per Exchange Notice No. 20050805-12 dated August 5, 2005
- Shortlisting of securities is based on XBRL submissions by the respective listed companies
- This action is a temporary, periodically reviewed surveillance measure and should not be construed as an adverse action against any company

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes; this is an application of the existing SEBI-approved criteria for shifting securities to/from the Trade-to-Trade segment, applied uniformly across stock exchanges and reviewed periodically. Detailed criteria are available on the BSE surveillance webpage.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading Members are required to:
- Exercise adequate precaution while trading in the affected securities
- Recognize that settlement will occur on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting of positions
- Account for the 100% VAR Margin applicable to these scrips
- Direct any clarification requests to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 20, 2026
- Effective Date of Transfer to T/XT/MT/TS Group: July 23, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This surveillance action affects trading members and investors dealing in the specific scrips listed in the annexures, imposing higher margin requirements (100% VAR) and eliminating position netting, which increases capital requirements and reduces trading flexibility for these securities. The measure aims to curb speculative trading and protect investors but does not indicate any fundamental issue with the listed companies. Overall market impact is limited given the surveillance-specific and temporary nature of the action.
