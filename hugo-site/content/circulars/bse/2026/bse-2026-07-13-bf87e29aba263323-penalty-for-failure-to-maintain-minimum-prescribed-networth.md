## Summary

BSE, in continuation of its Notice No. 20260417-45 dated April 17, 2026 on uniform disciplinary actions across exchanges, has introduced a monetary penalty structure for trading members who report a shortfall in the minimum prescribed networth under the SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 2026. The penalty applies both to shortfalls reported during periodic (half-yearly/annual) submissions and to shortfalls identified during Exchange member inspections.

## Key Points

- Regulation 10(g) of Chapter II of the SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 2026 requires stock brokers to maintain minimum networth as specified under Chapter X at all times, as a condition of their certificate of registration.
- Trading Members must submit a Networth Certificate on a half-yearly basis (as on March 31 and September 30) and an audited Networth Certificate annually (as on March 31).
- Exchanges observed that, in cases of networth shortfall as of these reporting dates, some members subsequently submit a revised certificate showing compliance as of a later date, while still reporting the original shortfall.
- To curb this practice and enforce continuous compliance, Exchanges have jointly introduced penalties for networth shortfalls reported during periodic submissions (Table 1) as well as for shortfalls identified during member inspections (Table 2, per Annexure 1.1 of Notice No. 20260417-45).

## Regulatory Changes

**Table 1 — Penalty for shortfall reported during periodic submission:**
- First instance: Monetary penalty of 2% of the shortfall amount from the required networth (subject to a minimum of Rs. 10,000), or Rs. 2,00,000, whichever is lower.
- Repeat instance (2nd time onward, for consecutive periodic reporting): 50% escalation of the penalty.

**Table 2 — Penalty for shortfall identified during Exchange member inspection (financial/incorrect reporting violations):**
- First instance: Monetary penalty of 5% of the shortfall amount from the required networth, plus administrative action initiated for recoupment of the networth shortfall.
- Repeat instance (2nd time onward): 50% escalation plus administrative action for recoupment of the shortfall.

## Compliance Requirements

- All Trading Members must maintain the minimum prescribed networth at all times as per SB Regulations and Exchange requirements.
- Members must ensure accurate and timely submission of half-yearly Networth Certificates (March 31, September 30) and the annual audited Networth Certificate (March 31).
- Members should not treat a later revised certificate showing compliance as a substitute for addressing an originally reported shortfall, since the shortfall itself now attracts a penalty.

## Important Dates

- Notice date: July 13, 2026.
- Penalty provisions apply with immediate effect to all cases under process and all non-compliances identified or determined after the date of this notice.
- Related prior notice: No. 20260417-45 dated April 17, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

The new penalty framework increases the financial cost of non-compliance with minimum networth requirements for all BSE trading members, reinforcing the regulatory push for continuous (not just point-in-time) networth adequacy. While this does not directly affect listed securities or trading in the market, it raises compliance costs and operational risk for brokers with weak capital buffers, and may prompt affected members to strengthen networth monitoring and reporting processes.
