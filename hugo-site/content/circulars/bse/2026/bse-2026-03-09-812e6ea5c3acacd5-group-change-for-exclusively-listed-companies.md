## Summary

BSE has announced group changes for 18 companies as part of its periodic X/XT segment review. Effective March 17, 2025, seven companies are moving out of the X/XT sub-segment (to B or T groups) and seven companies are moving into the X/XT sub-segment (from B or T groups), with one company moving from B to XB. Trading members are requested to take note of these reclassifications.

## Key Points

- 18 companies are undergoing group changes under the X/XT segment review
- Effective date: March 17, 2025
- Companies moving OUT of X group to B group: Amal Ltd (506597), Fedders Holding Ltd (511628), Leading Leasing Finance and Investment Company Ltd (540360), LKP Finance Limited (507912), Mafatlal Industries Ltd. (500264), Shanti Educational Initiatives Ltd (539921), Spice Lounge Food Works Ltd (539895)
- Companies moving OUT of XT group to T group: Indokem Ltd (504092), Shukra Pharmaceuticals Ltd (524632), String Metaverse Ltd (534535)
- Companies moving INTO X group from B group: Citurgia Biochemicals Ltd. (506373), Gautam Gems Limited (540936), PRAVEG LIMITED (531637), Shubham Polyspin Limited (542019), Z.F. Steering Gear (India) Lim (505163)
- Companies moving INTO XT group from T group: ERAAYA LIFESPACES LIMITED (531035), SIMANDHAR IMPEX LIMITED (544662)
- Mercury EV-Tech Limited (531357) moves from B to XB group

## Regulatory Changes

The X/XT sub-segment classification criteria (as per notice no 20171129-17 dated 29/Nov/2017) include exclusion from X group if:
- Six-month average market capitalization > Rs. 1000 Cr AND number of shareholders > 5000
- Securities are constituents of S&P BSE Sensex, S&P BSE 100, S&P BSE 200, or S&P BSE 500
- Six-month average market capitalization > Rs. 1000 Cr AND Net worth > Rs. 150 Cr
- Net worth > Rs. 150 Cr AND Foreign Institutional Investor holding > 20%
- Securities listed and traded on SME/SME-ITP segment
- Securities in P and Z group (remain in their respective groups)

Securities not meeting any of the above exclusion criteria are eligible for classification under X/XT sub-segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must update their systems to reflect the new group classifications effective March 17, 2025
- No direct compliance action required from listed companies; this is an administrative reclassification by BSE

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: March 09, 2026
- Effective Date of Group Changes: March 17, 2025

## Impact Assessment

The reclassification affects trading conditions for the 18 companies involved. Companies moving into the X/XT sub-segment will be subject to additional surveillance measures applicable to exclusively listed companies with lower liquidity or market capitalization. Companies moving out of the X/XT sub-segment will benefit from reduced surveillance restrictions and broader market access. Trading members need to account for these changes in their trading systems and client advisories.
