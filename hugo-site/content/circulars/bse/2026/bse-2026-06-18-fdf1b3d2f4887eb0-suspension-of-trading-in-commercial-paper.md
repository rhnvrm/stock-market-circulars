## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260618-43) instructing trading members to suspend trading in the commercial paper (CP) of multiple companies that have set record dates for CP redemption between June 22–24, 2026. Members are advised not to deal in these instruments from their respective ex-dates.

## Key Points

- Trading members must cease dealing in commercial paper of listed companies once the ex-date is reached
- Record dates span June 22, 23, and 24, 2026
- Affected instruments cover Debt segment scrips under the Listing Operations department
- Settlement references fall under DR 656/2026-2027 and 657/2026-2027 and 658/2026-2027 series
- At least 20+ companies are covered across three redemption date clusters

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This is a standard operational advisory under BSE's existing commercial paper listing and trading rules, triggered by companies fixing record dates for CP redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute buy or sell transactions in the listed commercial paper instruments of the named companies on or after their respective ex-dates
- Members should update their trading systems to reflect the suspension of these specific ISIN-linked scrips
- The suspension applies specifically to the CP scrips listed; equity or other instruments of the same companies are unaffected

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a direct and immediate impact on debt market participants trading commercial paper on BSE. Trading members must halt secondary market transactions in the specified CP instruments ahead of their respective record dates to ensure orderly redemption settlement. The affected companies span diverse sectors including infrastructure (KEC International), energy (Indian Oil), financial services (Bajaj Finance, SBICAP Securities, ECL Finance, Muthoot group entities), and housing finance (Godrej Housing Finance, Tata Housing). Equity investors in these companies are unaffected. The operational risk for non-compliance includes settlement failures and potential regulatory action.
