## Summary

BSE has listed commercial papers issued on private placement basis by six companies on its debt market segment, effective February 6, 2026. The securities are admitted for trading in dematerialized form with standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs. All listed commercial papers carry A1+ credit ratings from CRISIL, ICRA, or CARE.

## Key Points

- Six companies have listed commercial papers totaling 19,200 units worth Rs. 960 crores
- All securities will trade only in dematerialized form under respective ISIN numbers
- Standard trading denomination is Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size for securities is 1 paise
- Interest rates range from 7.25% to 8.90% per annum
- Maturity periods range from 3 months to 12 months
- All instruments are cumulative interest commercial papers

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative circular with minimal market impact. The listing provides additional short-term debt instruments for institutional investors and adds liquidity to the BSE debt segment. The high credit ratings (A1+) indicate low credit risk. Impact is limited to debt market participants, including banks, mutual funds, and institutional investors seeking short-term fixed-income instruments with yields ranging from 7.25% to 8.90%.
