## Summary

BSE has conducted a periodic review under its Additional Measure framework for securities exhibiting unusual price movement, further to Exchange circular no. 20251107-56 dated November 07, 2025. Securities meeting the inclusion criteria will be placed under this measure effective June 22, 2026, while certain securities will simultaneously be removed.

## Key Points

- This notice follows the framework established in BSE circular 20251107-56 (November 07, 2025).
- A review was carried out to identify securities for placement under the Additional Measure for unusual price movement.
- Annexure I lists securities being added to the framework effective June 22, 2026.
- Annexure II lists securities being removed from the framework effective June 22, 2026.
- Annexure III provides a consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This circular represents a periodic review and update of the existing Additional Measure framework for securities with unusual price movement, maintaining the list of covered securities in line with established criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must take note of securities being added to or removed from the Additional Measure framework.
- Members should refer to the attached annexures for the specific securities affected.
- For clarifications, members may write to BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **June 19, 2026**: Notice date and publication of updated lists.
- **June 22, 2026**: Effective date for inclusion and exclusion of securities under the Additional Measure framework.

## Impact Assessment

Securities placed under this additional measure may face enhanced scrutiny and potentially restrictive trading conditions such as trade-for-trade settlement or price band modifications. Market participants holding or trading the affected securities (listed in Annexure I) should review their positions ahead of the June 22, 2026 effective date. Securities exiting the framework (Annexure II) return to normal trading conditions. The overall market impact is limited to the specific securities identified in the annexures.
