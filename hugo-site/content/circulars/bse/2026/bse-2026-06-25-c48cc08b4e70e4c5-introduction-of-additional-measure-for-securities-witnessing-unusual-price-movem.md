## Summary

BSE has conducted a periodic review under its Additional Measure for Securities Witnessing Unusual Price Movement framework, following up on Exchange circular no. 20251107-56 dated November 07, 2025. Securities satisfying the criteria will be moved into the framework effective June 29, 2026, while others meeting exit criteria will be removed on the same date.

## Key Points

- Review carried out for identification of securities under the Additional Measure (AMS) for unusual price movement.
- Securities in Annexure I are newly included in the framework w.e.f. June 29, 2026.
- Securities in Annexure II are exiting the framework w.e.f. June 29, 2026.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework is provided in Annexure III.
- This action is pursuant to circular no. 20251107-56 dated November 07, 2025.

## Regulatory Changes

Periodic re-assessment of securities under the Additional Measure for Unusual Price Movement framework has resulted in changes to the list of covered securities. The framework is administered by BSE's Department of Surveillance (DOSS).

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must take note of the updated lists (Annexures I, II, and III) and apply applicable trading restrictions to securities newly added to the framework.
- Securities exiting the framework (Annexure II) revert to standard trading conditions from June 29, 2026.
- For clarifications, members may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **June 25, 2026**: Circular issued.
- **June 29, 2026**: Effective date for inclusion of new securities (Annexure I) and exclusion of exiting securities (Annexure II) under the AMS framework.

## Impact Assessment

Securities placed under the Additional Measure for Unusual Price Movement typically face enhanced surveillance conditions such as trade-for-trade settlement or price band restrictions, which can reduce liquidity and increase transaction costs for affected scrips. Market participants holding or trading in newly included securities should review the annexures and adjust their trading strategies accordingly. Securities exiting the framework may see improved liquidity and normalized trading conditions from June 29, 2026.
