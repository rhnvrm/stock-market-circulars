## Summary

BSE has announced the transfer of INDO SMC LIMITED equity shares (Scrip Code: 544681) from the Trade for Trade segment (MT Group) to the Rolling settlement segment (M Group), effective February 04, 2026. This change follows the completion of the SME IPO listing period and represents a transition to more liquid trading conditions.

## Key Points

- Company: INDO SMC LIMITED (SME IPO)
- Scrip Code: 544681
- Current Segment: Trade for Trade (MT Group)
- New Segment: Rolling Settlement (M Group)
- Effective Date: Wednesday, February 04, 2026
- Notice Reference: Continuation of Notice No. 20260120-49 dated January 20, 2026
- Contact: Mr. Nitinkumar Pujari, Tel: 022-2272 8398

## Regulatory Changes

The equity shares will be shifted from the Trade for Trade segment to the Rolling settlement segment, allowing for normal intraday trading and settlement cycles instead of compulsory delivery-based trading.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must update their systems to reflect the new group classification (M Group)
- Traders should adjust their trading strategies as the stock will no longer be subject to Trade for Trade restrictions
- Members requiring clarification should contact the designated BSE official before the effective date

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: January 21, 2026
- Previous Notice Date: January 20, 2026 (Notice No. 20260120-49)
- Effective Date: February 04, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - The move from Trade for Trade to Rolling settlement will increase liquidity and trading flexibility for INDO SMC LIMITED shares. This is a positive development for investors as it removes compulsory delivery requirements and enables intraday trading.

**Operational Impact**: Medium - Trading Members need to update their systems and inform clients about the change in trading conditions. The transition period provides adequate time for necessary adjustments.

**Investor Impact**: Positive - Investors will benefit from improved liquidity, reduced settlement restrictions, and the ability to engage in normal market trading activities including intraday positions.
