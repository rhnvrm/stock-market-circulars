## Summary

BSE, through ICCL (Indian Clearing Corporation Limited), has published the trading and settlement programme for the Mutual Fund segment for July 2026 (01.07.2026 to 31.07.2026). The notice covers settlement schedules for MF subscriptions (non-liquid and liquid) and redemptions, specifying settlement numbers (2627061–2627083), settlement dates, funds pay-in dates, and payout dates.

## Key Points

- Settlement numbers run from **2627061** (01-Jul-2026) to **2627083** (31-Jul-2026), covering all 23 trading days in July 2026.
- For **non-liquid MF subscriptions**, payout to AMCs occurs on T+1 (next business day); funds pay-out happens before 3:00 PM on T day.
- For **liquid MF subscriptions**, payout to AMCs occurs on the same day (T+0) — settlement date equals payout date.
- For **NFO (New Fund Offer)** schemes, funds pay-out to AMCs occurs on T day based on funds confirmation received by ICCL by 6:00 PM.
- The **MF Redemption** schedule follows settlement numbers aligned to transaction dates with securities pay-in on the same day.
- No market holidays appear to interrupt the July 2026 calendar; all weekdays in the month have assigned settlement numbers.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced. This is a routine periodic calendar notice issued by ICCL/BSE under the Mutual Fund segment operational framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- AMCs, RTAs, distributors, and clearing members must align their fund transfer and unit allotment processes to the published settlement dates.
- Funds pay-in must be completed on the designated FUNDSPAYIN date for each settlement number.
- For liquid fund transactions, participants must ensure same-day fund and unit settlement.
- NFO fund confirmation must be received by ICCL by **6:00 PM on T day** to qualify for same-day payout.

- Liquid subscriptions: payout on same day as settlement throughout July 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice with **medium operational impact** for mutual fund market participants. AMCs, clearing members, and RTAs need to update their internal settlement calendars for July 2026. There is no change to existing settlement norms or rules. The T+1 payout cycle for non-liquid funds and T+0 for liquid funds continues as per the established framework. No market-wide or investor-level disruption is expected.
