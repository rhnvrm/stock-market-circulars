## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260305-15 directing the suspension of trading in debt securities of six companies effective March 6, 2025 (reference DR-834/2025-2026). The suspension is due to either redemption of the debt securities or an application for delisting filed by the respective companies. Trading members are advised not to deal in these securities from the effective date.

## Key Points

- Trading in debt securities of six companies is suspended effective March 6, 2025
- Reason for suspension: Redemption or Application for Delisting by the companies
- Reference notice: DR-834/2025-2026
- All trading members must cease dealing in these securities immediately from the effective date
- Issued by Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President – Listing Compliance and Operations

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This is an operational action under existing BSE rules governing debt securities suspension upon redemption or delisting applications. The exchange exercises its authority to halt trading to protect market integrity during corporate lifecycle events.

## Compliance Requirements

- All trading members of BSE must immediately stop dealing in the debt securities of the six listed companies
- Members must note the scrip codes and ISINs to ensure compliance in order management systems
- Any pending orders or positions in these securities should be managed in accordance with exchange guidelines
- Members are required to take note of this circular and ensure their trading systems are updated accordingly

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** March 5, 2026
- **Effective Date of Suspension:** March 6, 2025 (as stated in the circular; note this date appears to reference the settlement year DR-834/2025-2026)

## Impact Assessment

The suspension affects six debt securities across the BSE Debt segment, preventing any new trades in these instruments. Investors holding these securities will be unable to sell them on the exchange post-suspension. The suspensions are triggered by corporate actions (redemption or delisting), indicating these securities are approaching the end of their listed life. Holders should monitor communications from the respective issuers regarding redemption proceeds or delisting procedures. The impact is concentrated on existing holders of these specific instruments and trading members active in the debt segment.
