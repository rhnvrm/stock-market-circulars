## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Equity segment on Saturday, January 03, 2026. Members can participate in either the mock trading session or UAT environment to fulfill regulatory requirements under SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 dated November 24, 2020. This session allows trading members and third-party platform users to test various trading functionalities including exceptional market conditions, call auction sessions, risk-reduction mode, trading halts, and block deals. There will be no new BOLT TWS release for this session.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Saturday, January 03, 2026 for Equity segment
- Members can choose between mock trading session or UAT environment for regulatory compliance
- Third-party trading platform users can test their applications during the session
- Various functionalities available for testing: call auctions, risk-reduction mode, trading halts, block deals
- No new BOLT TWS release planned
- Feedback solicited from all members by 4:30 pm on session day
- Members should refer to Exchange Notice No 20201201-22 dated December 1, 2020 regarding testing requirements

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This mock session enables compliance with existing SEBI circular no. SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 dated November 24, 2020 regarding testing of software used in or related to Trading and Risk Management.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must conduct periodic testing of their trading and risk management software
- Members can fulfill regulatory requirements by participating in either mock trading sessions or UAT environment
- Third-party trading platform vendors should utilize this opportunity to test exceptional market conditions and various functionalities
- Feedback must be submitted by 4:30 pm on the day of mock trading

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: None - This is a mock trading session with no actual market transactions or impact on live trading.

**Operational Impact**: Low - Voluntary participation for members to test and validate their trading systems, risk management software, and third-party platforms. Benefits include:
- Opportunity to test exceptional market conditions in a controlled environment
- Validation of trading platform functionalities including call auctions, block deals, and risk controls
- Compliance with SEBI testing requirements
- System readiness assessment without market risk

**Member Impact**: Positive - Provides testing opportunity for trading members using in-house or third-party systems to ensure operational readiness and regulatory compliance.
