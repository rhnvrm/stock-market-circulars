## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260602-16 informs trading members that five new commercial papers (CPs), issued on a private placement basis at a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, have been listed and admitted to trading on the BSE Debt Segment effective June 2, 2026. The issuing companies are GIC Housing Finance Ltd., Bajaj Housing Finance Limited, Manappuram Finance Limited, Tata Realty and Infrastructure Limited, and Axis Finance Limited.

## Key Points

- All five CPs are traded exclusively in dematerialised form.
- Standard denomination for trading is Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof.
- Tick size is 1 paise.
- All CPs were allotted on June 1, 2026, with redemption dates ranging from August 28 to September 18, 2026.
- Credit ratings range from CRISIL A1+/ICRA A1+/CARE A1+/IND A1+ — all highest short-term ratings.
- Interest rates range from 7.60% to 8.65% p.a. (cumulative).

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational listing notice under BSE's debt market admission process for privately placed debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade the listed CPs only in dematerialised form under the specified ISIN numbers.
- All trades must conform to the standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof.
- Tick size of 1 paise must be observed.
- For clarifications, trading members may contact BSE's Debt Department at 22728352 / 8597 / 8995 / 5753 / 8915.

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date:** June 1, 2026 (for all five CPs)
- **Listing/Trading Effective Date:** June 2, 2026
- **Redemption Dates:**
  - Bajaj Housing Finance CP: August 28, 2026
  - GIC Housing Finance CP: August 31, 2026
  - Manappuram Finance CP: August 31, 2026
  - Tata Realty and Infrastructure CP: August 31, 2026
  - Axis Finance CP: September 18, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing notice with minimal market impact. The CPs are short-term instruments (3-month tenor) with top-tier credit ratings (A1+), indicating high creditworthiness of all issuers. The largest issuance by volume is Bajaj Housing Finance at 15,000 units (Rs. 750 crore notional), followed by Manappuram Finance at 7,000 units (Rs. 350 crore). Overall, this notice is operationally significant only for debt segment traders dealing in these specific instruments.
