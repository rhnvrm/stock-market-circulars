## Summary

BSE has notified trading members of a SEBI final order dated July 24, 2026 (Order No. QJA/BS/ISD/ISD-SEC-7/32504/2026-27) in the matter of front running of trades of Axis Mutual Fund. This follows the earlier Exchange Notice No. 20230228-54 dated February 28, 2023. SEBI has directed that 21 noticees be restrained from accessing the securities market and prohibited from buying, selling, or otherwise dealing in securities, directly or indirectly, for periods ranging from 3 to 7 years.

## Key Points

- SEBI issued a final order in the front-running case involving trades of Axis Mutual Fund.
- 21 noticees have been debarred from the securities market for periods of 3, 5, or 7 years, depending on individual involvement.
- Noticees include individuals (e.g., Viresh Joshi, Sumit Desai, Pranav Vora, Prijesh Kurani, Dharini Kurani, Rekha Kurani, Bindesh Kurani) and entities (M K B Bespoke Audio General, Olga Trading Pvt. Ltd., Visa Capital Partners).
- The period already undergone under the earlier Interim Order cum SCN will be set off against the final debarment period.
- Noticees with open derivative positions as of the order date may close out/square off such positions within 3 months or at contract expiry, whichever is earlier.
- Noticees are permitted to settle pay-in and pay-out obligations for transactions that occurred before close of trading on the order date.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes; this is an enforcement action under existing SEBI regulations against front running of mutual fund trades.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must take note of the restrictions on the 21 noticees and ensure no securities market dealings are facilitated for them during the debarment period.
- Noticees must close out any open derivative positions within 3 months from the order date or at contract expiry, whichever is earlier.
- Pay-in and pay-out settlement for pre-order transactions must be honored.

## Important Dates

- Order Date: July 24, 2026
- Debarment effective from: July 24, 2026 (date of the order)
- Open derivative position close-out deadline: within 3 months from July 24, 2026, or contract expiry, whichever is earlier
- Related earlier notice: February 28, 2023 (Exchange Notice No. 20230228-54)

## Impact Assessment

The order has limited direct impact on the broader securities market or listed stocks since it targets specific individuals and entities rather than any listed company or trading mechanism. However, it reinforces regulatory scrutiny on front-running practices tied to mutual fund trades, which is relevant to compliance and surveillance teams at trading members and asset management companies. Trading members must ensure the debarred entities are not facilitated in market access during their respective restraint periods.
