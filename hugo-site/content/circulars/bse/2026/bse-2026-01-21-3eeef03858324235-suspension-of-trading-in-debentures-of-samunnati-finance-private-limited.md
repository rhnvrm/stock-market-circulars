## Summary

BSE has announced suspension of trading in Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) of Samunnati Finance Private Limited (ISIN: INE551U07209, Code: 973790) effective from January 30, 2026. The suspension is due to the company fixing January 31, 2026 as the record date for redemption of debentures and payment of interest. Trading members are advised not to deal in these debentures from the specified date.

## Key Points

- Company: Samunnati Finance Private Limited
- Debenture Code: SFPL-12.16%-15-2-26-PVT
- ISIN: INE551U07209
- BSE Code: 973790
- Interest Rate: 12.16%
- Record Date: January 31, 2026
- No Dealings From: January 30, 2026
- Purpose: Redemption of Debentures and Payment of Interest
- Notice Number: DR-809/2025-2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory framework changes. This is a standard procedural suspension for corporate action processing.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute any trades in the specified debentures from January 30, 2026
- Trading members must inform their clients holding these debentures about the trading suspension
- Members should ensure compliance with the no-dealing restriction to avoid regulatory issues

## Important Dates

- **January 21, 2026**: Notice date
- **January 30, 2026**: Trading suspension begins (no dealings from this date)
- **January 31, 2026**: Record date for interest payment and debenture redemption

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Limited to holders of specific NCDs of Samunnati Finance Private Limited. The suspension is temporary and procedural in nature, affecting only the debt segment.

**Investor Impact**: Debenture holders whose names appear in records as of January 31, 2026 will be eligible for interest payment and redemption proceeds. Trading is restricted to ensure proper settlement and determination of eligible investors.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members must update their systems to block trading in these debentures and communicate the suspension to affected clients. The suspension prevents any transfers that could affect the beneficiary determination for the corporate action.
