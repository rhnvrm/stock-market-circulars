## Summary

BSE has issued its periodic update on the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework, referencing earlier notices dated March 21, 2018, October 27, 2018, December 04, 2020, August 09, 2024, and September 20, 2024. Under the revised framework, securities listed in Annexure I (Part A) will face 100% margin requirements, additional securities move to higher LT-ASM stages (Annexure I Part B), some securities continue in LT-ASM but get transferred to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups (Annexure I Part D), and other securities exit the LT-ASM framework altogether (Annexure II). A consolidated list of securities currently under the framework is provided in Annexure III.

## Key Points

- 100% margin applies on all open positions as of July 24, 2026 and on new positions created from July 27, 2026 onwards, for securities in Annexure I (Part A).
- Securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages take effect July 23, 2026 (Annexure I Part B).
- A lower price band applies from July 23, 2026 for the relevant securities.
- Securities per Annexure I (Part D) remain in the LT-ASM framework but are transferred to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups effective July 27, 2026.
- Securities exiting the LT-ASM framework effective July 23, 2026 are listed in Annexure II; their price bands revert to pre-shortlisting levels unless subject to another surveillance measure.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the LT-ASM framework is provided in Annexure III.
- Shortlisting is based on XBRL submissions by listed companies and is purely a market surveillance measure, not an adverse action against any company.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice operationalizes the existing LT-ASM framework (originally introduced via notice 20180321-46 and subsequently revised) by publishing the latest periodic review of securities added to, upgraded within, transferred out of, or removed from the framework.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members and market participants must apply 100% margin requirements on the specified securities' open and new positions per the effective dates, observe the revised price bands, and account for the trading group transfers (to T/XT/MT/TS) for affected securities. Participants should also apply this framework in conjunction with all other surveillance measures currently in effect.

## Important Dates

- July 23, 2026: Securities move to higher LT-ASM stages; lower price band becomes applicable; securities exit LT-ASM framework (per Annexure II).
- July 24, 2026: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin.
- July 27, 2026: 100% margin applicable on new positions; securities under Annexure I (Part D) transferred to T/XT/MT/TS groups.

## Impact Assessment

The measure increases trading costs and reduces flexibility for investors holding or trading the affected securities due to 100% margin requirements and tighter price bands, which may reduce short-term liquidity and volatility in these names. Securities exiting the framework benefit from reinstated price bands, potentially easing trading conditions. Overall market impact is limited to the specific securities named in the annexures rather than the broader market.
