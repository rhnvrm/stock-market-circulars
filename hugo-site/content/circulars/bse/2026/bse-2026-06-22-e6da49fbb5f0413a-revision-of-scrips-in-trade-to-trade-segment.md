## Summary

BSE has announced the revision of scrips in the Trade-to-Trade (T2T) segment as part of its periodic surveillance measures aligned with the SEBI framework. Securities satisfying all prescribed criteria will be transferred to the T/XT/MT/TS group effective June 25, 2026, while another set will continue in their current groups with a 5% price band.

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I (meeting criteria I, II, and III) will be transferred to T/XT/MT/TS group from June 25, 2026
- Securities in Annexure II will remain in T/XT/MT/TS/P/Z/ZP group with a 5% price band
- A VAR (Value at Risk) margin of 100% will be levied on affected scrips per Exchange Notice No. 20050805-12 dated August 5, 2005
- No netting off of positions will be permitted; settlement is strictly trade-to-trade
- Shortlisting of securities is based on XBRL submissions by listed companies
- Criteria for shifting securities to/from T2T are decided in consultation with SEBI and applied uniformly across exchanges

## Regulatory Changes

The transfer is executed under the extant SEBI surveillance framework. The criteria for inclusion/exclusion are publicly available on the BSE website. This action is coordinated with other exchanges and applied uniformly. Reference is made to Exchange Notice No. 20050805-12 (dated August 5, 2005) for the 100% VAR margin provision.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must take adequate precaution while trading in the listed securities
- No netting off of positions is allowed for trade-to-trade scrips
- Members must ensure 100% VAR margin is maintained for affected scrips
- Members requiring clarification may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 22, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 25, 2026 (transfer to T/XT/MT/TS group)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine but impactful surveillance action affecting trading liquidity for the listed securities. Moving scrips to the trade-to-trade segment eliminates intraday netting, increasing settlement obligations and operational burden for trading members. The 100% VAR margin requirement raises the cost of trading in these securities. The measure is explicitly temporary and subject to periodic review based on market conditions. BSE has clarified this should not be construed as adverse action against the affected companies.
