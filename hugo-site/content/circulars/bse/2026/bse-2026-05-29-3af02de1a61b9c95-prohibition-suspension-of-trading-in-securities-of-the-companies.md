## Summary

BSE has issued a prohibition/suspension of trading in the securities of two companies as an interim measure, effective Monday, June 01, 2026. During site visits conducted by the Exchange, both companies were found to be non-existent at their respective registered office addresses.

## Key Points

- Trading in securities of two companies is prohibited/suspended from June 01, 2026
- The action follows BSE site visits where companies were not found at their registered office addresses
- This is classified as an interim measure under Listing Compliance-Monitoring
- Both trading members and investors are required to take note of the suspension

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. The suspension is an interim enforcement action under existing listing compliance powers of BSE, triggered by non-existence of companies at their registered addresses.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must immediately cease accepting orders in the affected securities from June 01, 2026
- Investors holding these securities should note that trading will not be permitted until further notice
- No action is required from investors beyond awareness of the trading restriction

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** 29 May 2026
- **Effective Date of Suspension:** Monday, 01 June 2026

## Impact Assessment

| Scrip Code | Company Name |
|---|---|
| 539584 | Sharanam Infraproject and Trading Ltd. |
| 500389 | Silverline Technologies Ltd. |

Existing investors in these two securities will be unable to exit their positions on BSE once the suspension takes effect on June 01, 2026. The inability to locate companies at their registered offices is a significant governance red flag, suggesting potential regulatory or legal issues. Investors should treat holdings in suspended securities as illiquid until the Exchange lifts the suspension or provides further clarification.
