## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260421-26 dated April 21, 2026, informing trading members that 44 companies will undergo a group change from Group X to Group B, effective April 22, 2026. This reclassification impacts the trading mechanism and settlement cycle for these securities.

## Key Points

- 44 companies are being moved from Group X (trade-to-trade settlement / enhanced surveillance) to Group B (normal rolling settlement)
- Change is effective from April 22, 2026 (next trading day)
- All companies are equity segment securities
- The move from X to B generally indicates relaxation of surveillance restrictions and transition to normal trading conditions
- No attachments were included with this notice

## Regulatory Changes

Group X on BSE typically operates under trade-to-trade settlement, where each trade must result in delivery and no netting is permitted. Group B follows normal rolling settlement (T+1). The reclassification from X to B means these securities will transition from mandatory delivery-based settlement to standard rolling settlement, enabling normal buy-sell netting and broader participation.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must update their systems and client-facing platforms to reflect the new group classification for all 44 scrips effective April 22, 2026
- Risk management parameters should be updated accordingly as margin and settlement requirements differ between Group X and Group B
- Members should inform clients holding positions in these securities of the group change

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** April 21, 2026
- **Effective Date:** April 22, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The reclassification from Group X to Group B is generally favorable for the affected companies and their investors. Group B securities attract a broader investor base, including those who avoid trade-to-trade segment due to mandatory delivery requirements. This change may improve liquidity and reduce impact cost for these 44 scrips. The affected companies span diverse sectors including precision castings, textiles, steel, finance, hospitality, agro industries, chemicals, and real estate.
