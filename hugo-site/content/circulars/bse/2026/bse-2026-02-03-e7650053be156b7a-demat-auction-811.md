## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction list for Settlement No. 811 dated February 3, 2026. This auction covers securities shortages across 141 scrips where delivery obligations were not met during the regular settlement cycle. Trading members with short positions must participate in this auction to fulfill their delivery obligations.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 811
- Auction Date: February 3, 2026
- Total Scrips: 141 securities across various market segments
- Quantity Range: From 1 share to 408,383 shares (SPRIGHT AGRO)
- Includes major stocks like Reliance Industries (465 shares), BHEL (100 shares), Tata Motors (311 shares combined)
- Covers diverse sectors including metals, financial services, pharmaceuticals, technology, and manufacturing

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational auction process as per existing BSE settlement procedures.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members with short positions in the listed securities must participate in the demat auction
- Members must ensure adequate funds/securities are available for auction settlement
- Settlement must be completed as per BSE auction settlement timeline
- Non-participation or failed settlement may attract penalties and regulatory action

## Important Dates

- Auction Date: February 3, 2026
- Settlement completion: As per BSE auction settlement calendar (typically T+1 day from auction)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects 141 scrips with trading members having delivery obligations. Notable quantities include SPRIGHT AGRO (408,383 shares), Rajnish Wellness (50,001 shares), and GROWINGTON V (24,000 shares).

**Operational Impact:** Trading members with short positions face potential mark-to-market losses if auction prices are significantly higher than settlement prices. Large quantity auctions may experience higher price volatility.

**Member Impact:** Affected trading members must arrange for securities procurement through auction mechanism, potentially at premium prices, impacting their settlement costs and operational efficiency.
