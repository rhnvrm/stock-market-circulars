## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure) will be moved to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 03, 2026. Affected securities will face escalating surveillance actions based on their assigned GSM stage, ranging from higher margin requirements to severely restricted weekly trading with mandatory additional surveillance deposits.

## Key Points

- Securities as per the Annexure will be migrated to higher GSM stages from June 03, 2026
- Four GSM stages apply, each with progressively stricter trading conditions
- Settlement for all GSM-listed securities is on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed
- Trading members are urged to take adequate precautions while trading in these securities
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Regulatory Changes

The GSM framework, originally established via Exchange notice no. 20170223-44 dated February 23, 2017, and subsequently amended, classifies securities into four surveillance stages:

| Stage | Surveillance Action |
|-------|--------------------|
| I | 100% applicable margin rate; price band of 5% or lower |
| II | Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band; Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers |
| III | Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band; trading permitted once a week (every Monday/1st trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers |
| IV | Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band; trading permitted once a week (every Monday/1st trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers; no upward price movement permitted |

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure buyers in Stage II, III, and IV securities deposit the applicable ASD (50% or 100% of trade value) before or at the time of trade
- No netting off is permitted; all trades must be settled on a gross (trade-to-trade) basis
- For Stage III and IV securities, trading is restricted to once a week (Monday or the first trading day of the week)
- Members must refer to the GSM FAQ at http://www.bseindia.com/markets/equity/EQReports/graded_surveil_measure.aspx?expandable=6 for further guidance

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 02, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 03, 2026 (securities moved to higher GSM stages)

## Impact Assessment

Securities migrated to higher GSM stages face materially reduced liquidity due to trade-for-trade settlement, price band caps of 5% or lower, and mandatory additional surveillance deposits. Stage III and IV securities are particularly affected, with trading restricted to once per week and buyers required to deposit 100% of trade value upfront. These measures significantly increase the cost and complexity of trading in affected securities, which are typically stocks exhibiting unusual price or volume activity. Investors holding such securities should be aware of the enhanced restrictions on exit and entry. The list of specific affected securities is available in the Annexure attached to the original notice.
