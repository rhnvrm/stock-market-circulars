## Summary

BSE has issued this circular detailing changes to the Short Term (ST) Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework effective March 13, 2026. Eight securities are newly shortlisted into the ST 5/15/30 Days ASM framework, nil securities are moving to higher or lower ASM stages, and eight securities are exiting the ST-ASM framework (most due to inclusion in the LT-ASM framework).

## Key Points

- 8 securities newly added to ST-ASM framework (Annexure I, Part A) effective March 13, 2026
- No securities moving to higher or lower ASM stages within the framework
- 8 securities moving out of the ST-ASM framework (Annexure II), primarily due to migration to LT-ASM
- Several securities are flagged per NSE (marked with *); two are SME scrips (marked with @)
- The consolidated list (Annexure III) reflects all securities currently in ST-ASM with their assigned stage (I or II)

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants trading in newly added ST-ASM securities must comply with applicable margin and trading restrictions under the ST-ASM framework from March 13, 2026
- Brokers and investors must note stage assignments (Stage I or II) in the consolidated list, as different stages carry different surveillance conditions
- Securities exiting ST-ASM may be subject to LT-ASM or other surveillance frameworks; participants should verify applicable framework for each security

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** March 13, 2026 — all additions, exits, and stage changes take effect
- **Circular Date:** March 12, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The inclusion of 8 securities in the ST-ASM framework will subject them to enhanced surveillance, including potential restrictions on intraday trading, increased margin requirements, and trade-to-trade settlement depending on the assigned stage. This directly impacts retail and institutional investors holding or trading these securities. The 8 securities exiting ST-ASM (mostly migrating to LT-ASM) will continue to face restrictions under the new applicable framework, meaning no immediate relief for those investors. SME scrips and NSE-flagged securities in the list indicate cross-exchange coordination in surveillance actions.
