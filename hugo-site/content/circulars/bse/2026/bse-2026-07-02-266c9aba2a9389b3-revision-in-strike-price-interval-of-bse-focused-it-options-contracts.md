## Summary

BSE has revised the strike price interval and strike scheme for BSE FOCUSED IT Index options contracts, in partial modification of its earlier Notice No. 20260415-34 dated April 15, 2026 (which introduced Futures and Options contracts on the BSE FOCUSED IT Index). The strike price interval will be reduced from 250 index points to 100 index points, and the minimum number of available strikes will increase from 29 to 71, effective from the August 2026 monthly expiry onwards.

## Key Points

- Strike price interval reduced from 250 index points to 100 index points.
- Minimum number of strikes increased from 29 (14 ITM, 1 ATM, 14 OTM) to 71 (35 ITM, 1 ATM, 35 OTM).
- Options contracts based on the revised strike interval will be added to the equity derivatives contract master file at end of day on July 30, 2026.
- The revised structure becomes applicable for trading with effect from July 31, 2026.
- All new options contracts will be generated per the revised strike price interval framework.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a partial modification of Exchange Notice No. 20260415-34 dated April 15, 2026, which had introduced Futures and Options contracts on the BSE FOCUSED IT Index. The change specifically revises the strike price interval and strike scheme parameters for FOCUSED IT options contracts.

## Compliance Requirements

- For existing August and September 2026 contracts, strikes ending in 250 and 750 with NIL open interest will be discontinued, and new strikes in multiples of 100 will be added to the contract master.
- Contracts ending in 250 and 750 that have existing open interest will remain available for trading until their respective expiries.
- Trading Members are advised to download the updated contract master file from the Member Extranet portal before trading on the effective date.

## Important Dates

- April 15, 2026: Original Notice No. 20260415-34 introducing BSE FOCUSED IT Index F&O contracts.
- July 30, 2026: Revised strike interval contracts made available in the contract master file (end of day).
- July 31, 2026: Revised strike price framework becomes effective for trading.
- August 2026 monthly expiry onwards: Revision applies to new contract generation.

## Impact Assessment

The narrower strike interval (100 points vs. 250 points) will provide traders with significantly more granular strike price choices, more than doubling the minimum number of strikes available (from 29 to 71). This should improve trading flexibility and pricing precision for BSE FOCUSED IT options, particularly benefiting market makers and active options traders. Trading Members must update systems and download the revised contract master ahead of the July 31, 2026 effective date to avoid operational disruptions.
