## Summary

BSE's Surveillance department (DOSS) has observed significant price and volume movements in four scrips and had written to the respective companies seeking clarifications. As of Notice No. 20260525-34 dated 25 May 2026, two companies have submitted their replies, while two others are yet to respond.

## Key Points

- BSE Surveillance detected unusual price/volume activity in four listed scrips
- BSE wrote to all four companies to ensure trading members and investors have access to latest relevant information
- Replies received from: Candour Techtex Ltd (Scrip Code: 522292) and Accord Transformer & Switchgear Ltd (Scrip Code: 544710)
- Replies still awaited from: ZR2 Bioenergy Ltd (Scrip Code: 506640) and Elango Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 513452)
- Notice issued by the Surveillance department under Deputy Vice President Sanjay Jain and Manager Shreya Ghose

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a disclosure notice under BSE's ongoing surveillance framework to maintain market transparency and investor awareness.

## Compliance Requirements

- ZR2 Bioenergy Ltd (Scrip Code: 506640) is required to submit a clarification to BSE Surveillance
- Elango Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 513452) is required to submit a clarification to BSE Surveillance
- Investors and trading members in the mentioned scrips should review the attached company replies for material disclosures

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 25 May 2026
- Clarifications from Candour Techtex Ltd and Accord Transformer & Switchgear Ltd already received as of this notice
- No specific deadline mentioned for pending replies from ZR2 Bioenergy Ltd and Elango Industries Ltd

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a targeted impact on investors holding or trading in the four mentioned scrips. The submission of clarifications by Candour Techtex Ltd and Accord Transformer & Switchgear Ltd may provide material information that could influence trading decisions. The pending responses from ZR2 Bioenergy Ltd and Elango Industries Ltd represent an information gap that investors should be aware of. The notice is part of BSE's standard surveillance mechanism and does not impose trading restrictions; however, continued non-response by the pending companies could escalate to further surveillance actions.
