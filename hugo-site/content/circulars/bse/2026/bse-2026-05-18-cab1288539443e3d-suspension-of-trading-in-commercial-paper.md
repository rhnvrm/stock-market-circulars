## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260518-9 directing trading members not to deal in the commercial papers (CPs) of multiple companies that have fixed record dates for redemption purposes. The suspensions are effective from 19 May 2026 onwards, covering settlements under DR series 633/2026-2027, 639/2026-2027, and 640/2026-2027.

## Key Points

- Trading in commercial papers of 18+ companies is suspended effective on their respective ex-dates
- Suspension applies to the Debt segment on BSE
- Companies have fixed record dates for the purpose of redemption of commercial paper
- Trading members are advised not to deal in the listed CPs from the specified ex-dates
- Most suspensions are clustered around 27 May 2026 (ex-date), under Settlement DR 639/2026-2027

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework introduced. This is a standard operational notice under BSE's Listing Operations / Debt segment procedures for commercial paper redemptions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must cease dealing in the commercial papers of the listed companies from their respective ex-dates
- Members should update their systems to reflect suspension of the specified scrip codes
- No further action required for investors already holding these CPs; redemption will proceed as scheduled

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects debt market participants trading in commercial papers of the listed companies. Impact is localized to CP investors and trading desks in the debt segment. The earliest suspension kicks in on 19 May 2026 for Nuvama Clearing Services Limited (scrip code 731602), with a large cluster of suspensions effective 27 May 2026. All CPs are approaching maturity and will be redeemed per schedule. No equity market impact is expected. Routine in nature and does not signal any credit event or default.
