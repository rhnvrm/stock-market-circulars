## Summary

BSE Limited has declared Trading Member SIC Stocks & Services Pvt. Ltd. (Clearing No. 922) as a defaulter with effect from July 8, 2026, across all segments of the Exchange. The action follows violations of several Rules, Bye-laws, and Regulations of the Exchange. As a consequence, all Authorized Persons affiliated with the firm are no longer permitted to deal in that capacity, and their registrations have been withdrawn.

## Key Points

- Trading Member SIC Stocks & Services Pvt. Ltd. (Clg. No. 922) declared defaulter effective July 8, 2026, in all segments.
- Violations cited include Rule 1, 3(b), 3(c), 3(e), 3(f), 3(m) and 7 of Chapter IV of the Exchange Rules.
- Violations of Bye-law 1(a), 1(b), 1(f) and 2(b) of Chapter XIV and Bye-law 16 of Chapter XIII of the Exchange Bye-laws.
- Violations of Regulation 4.2.1(e), 4.6.2(b) and 4.6.3(f) of Chapter 4 of the Capital Market Segment Regulations.
- Under sub-rule 4(A) & 5 of Rule 8 of SCRR 1957, individuals who served as directors/partners of a defaulter/expelled member may be disqualified from holding director/partner roles in other Exchange member companies/firms.
- A list of individuals who served as directors of the trading member during the violation period is provided in Annexure 1.
- Authorized Persons affiliated with SIC Stocks & Services Pvt Ltd (listed in Annexure 2) are no longer authorized to deal, and their registrations stand withdrawn.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an enforcement action applying existing Exchange Rules, Bye-laws, and Capital Market Segment Regulations against a specific trading member for violations committed.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and market participants must cease all dealings with SIC Stocks & Services Pvt. Ltd. in its capacity as a trading member.
- Authorized Persons previously affiliated with the firm must stop dealing in that capacity immediately, as their registrations have been withdrawn.
- Individuals who served as directors of the firm during the violation period may face disqualification from holding director/partner positions at other Exchange member firms under SCRR Rule 8.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 8, 2026 — defaulter declaration and withdrawal of Authorized Person registrations take immediate effect.

## Impact Assessment

The action directly affects the defaulting trading member, its directors, and its network of Authorized Persons, who lose the ability to operate under the firm. Clients and investors dealing through this member or its Authorized Persons should note the immediate cessation of services and may need to transfer their accounts/holdings elsewhere. The broader market impact is limited, as this pertains to a single trading member rather than listed securities, but it reinforces BSE's investor protection and regulatory enforcement framework.
