## Summary

BSE has announced a migration from the Price Reasonability Check (PRC) mechanism to the Limit Price Protection (LPP) mechanism for Single Stock Derivatives. PRC will cease to operate from July 6, 2026, and LPP will become applicable. A mock trading session is scheduled for July 4, 2026 ahead of the live implementation.

## Key Points

- PRC for Single Stock Derivatives will cease on July 6, 2026
- LPP mechanism will replace PRC for Single Stock Derivatives from trade date July 6, 2026
- Stock Options: LPP% = 40.00%, subject to a minimum absolute value of ₹20
- Stock Futures: LPP% = 3.00%, subject to a minimum absolute value of ₹1.50
- This is a partial modification to Exchange Notice no. 20240628-46 dated June 28, 2024
- Mock trading session scheduled for July 4, 2026 prior to live implementation

## Regulatory Changes

The exchange is replacing the Price Reasonability Check (PRC) with the Limit Price Protection (LPP) mechanism for Single Stock Derivatives. The LPP range is defined as a percentage on the reference price, with a minimum absolute floor value:

| Instrument Type | LPP % on Ref. Price | Minimum Absolute Value |
|---|---|---|
| Stock Options | 40.00% | ₹20 |
| Stock Futures | 3.00% | ₹1.50 |

## Compliance Requirements

- All trading members must take note of the transition from PRC to LPP for Single Stock Derivatives
- Members must participate in or account for the mock trading session on July 4, 2026
- Participants must exercise due care and diligence while placing orders, in accordance with existing circulars and advisories on pre-trade risk controls
- Full compliance with the new LPP mechanism is required from July 6, 2026

## Important Dates

- **July 4, 2026**: Mock trading session for LPP mechanism
- **July 6, 2026**: LPP mechanism goes live for Single Stock Derivatives; PRC ceases to operate

## Impact Assessment

This change affects all trading members active in the equity derivatives segment, specifically those trading Single Stock Options and Single Stock Futures. The shift from PRC to LPP introduces defined percentage-based price bands (40% for options, 3% for futures) with minimum absolute value floors, which may affect order placement strategies and risk controls at the member level. Members with automated trading systems will need to update their pre-trade risk control configurations before the July 6, 2026 effective date.
