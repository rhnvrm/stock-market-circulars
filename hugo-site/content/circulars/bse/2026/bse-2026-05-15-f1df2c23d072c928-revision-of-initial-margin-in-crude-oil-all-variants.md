## Summary

ICCL (Indian Clearing Corporation Limited) has issued a notice revising the initial margin requirements for Crude Oil and all its variants traded on the Commodity Derivatives segment of BSE. This revision falls under the Settlement/RMS (Risk Management System) category and is applicable to all participants trading crude oil contracts.

## Key Points

- Initial margin has been revised for Crude Oil across all variants in the Commodity Derivatives segment
- The revision is issued by ICCL under the Settlement/RMS framework
- All variants of Crude Oil contracts are covered under this revision
- No attachments are referenced; revised margin rates are likely communicated directly through the exchange systems

## Regulatory Changes

ICCL has modified the initial margin percentages/values applicable to Crude Oil (all variants) on the BSE Commodity Derivatives platform. Initial margin revisions are typically triggered by changes in volatility, price movements, or risk parameters assessed by the clearing corporation.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and clearing members must update their margin collection systems to reflect the revised initial margin requirements
- Members must ensure adequate margins are collected from clients before allowing positions in Crude Oil contracts
- Risk and back-office teams should update margin calculators and exposure monitoring tools accordingly

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 15 May 2026
- Effective Date: As specified in the revised margin schedule communicated via exchange systems (not detailed in this notice)

## Impact Assessment

This revision directly impacts all market participants with open or planned positions in Crude Oil contracts on BSE's Commodity Derivatives segment. Higher initial margins increase capital requirements and may reduce leveraged positions, while lower margins could enhance liquidity. Brokers and clearing members must promptly communicate revised margin requirements to clients to prevent margin calls or forced liquidations.
