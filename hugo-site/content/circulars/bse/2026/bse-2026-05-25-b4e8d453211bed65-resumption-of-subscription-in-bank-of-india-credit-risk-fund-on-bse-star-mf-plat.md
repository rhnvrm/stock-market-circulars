## Summary

BSE has notified members that Bank of India Mutual Fund has resumed subscriptions in the **Bank of India Credit Risk Fund** on the BSE StAR MF platform effective **May 25, 2026**. This reverses the temporary suspension of subscriptions that was originally announced via BSE Notice No. 20190701-19 (July 1, 2019), when the scheme was operated under the name BOI AXA Credit Risk Fund by BOI AXA Mutual Fund.

## Key Points

- Subscriptions are now open for **Fresh Purchases**, **Additional Purchases**, and **Systematic Investment Plans (SIP)** under Bank of India Credit Risk Fund.
- The resumption is effective from **May 25, 2026** on the BSE StAR MF platform.
- The scheme was previously suspended under the name BOI AXA Credit Risk Fund (BOI AXA Mutual Fund); it now operates under Bank of India Mutual Fund following the fund house's rebranding/takeover.
- A notice cum addendum issued by Bank of India Mutual Fund is attached to the circular for reference.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This notice reverses an earlier operational suspension (Notice No. 20190701-19) and restores normal subscription functionality for the scheme on the BSE StAR MF platform.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and distributors on the BSE StAR MF platform may now accept and process subscription orders (fresh, additional, and SIP) for Bank of India Credit Risk Fund.
- Members should refer to the attached notice cum addendum from Bank of India Mutual Fund for scheme-specific details and terms.

## Important Dates

- **May 25, 2026**: Effective date for resumption of subscriptions in Bank of India Credit Risk Fund on BSE StAR MF platform.
- **July 1, 2019**: Original suspension notice (Notice No. 20190701-19) for BOI AXA Credit Risk Fund — now reversed.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited and operationally narrow. Investors and distributors who were previously unable to subscribe to the Bank of India Credit Risk Fund through the BSE StAR MF platform can now do so. The scheme is a debt-oriented credit risk fund, and the resumption may attract inflows from investors seeking higher-yield debt instruments. No broader market or systemic impact is anticipated.
