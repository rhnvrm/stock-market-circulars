## Summary

BSE has prohibited trading in securities of Polo Hotels Limited (Scrip Code: 526687) effective Tuesday, January 27, 2026, as an interim measure. This action follows the company's failure to respond to show cause notices and its non-existence at the registered office address during Exchange site visits.

## Key Points

- Trading in Polo Hotels Limited (526687) is prohibited/suspended from January 27, 2026
- Company has not responded to BSE's show cause notices
- Company found non-existent at registered office address during Exchange site visits
- Action taken as interim measure pending further investigation
- Trading members and investors advised to take note

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes. This is an enforcement action under existing compliance requirements for listed companies to maintain proper registered office and respond to Exchange communications.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure no trades are executed in Polo Hotels Limited securities from January 27, 2026
- Investors holding positions should be aware that trading will not be possible until suspension is lifted
- Company required to respond to show cause notices and establish existence at registered office to potentially lift suspension

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: January 23, 2026
- **Trading Suspension Effective Date**: January 27, 2026 (Tuesday)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: High - Complete trading prohibition prevents all buy/sell transactions, affecting liquidity and investor ability to exit positions. Current shareholders cannot liquidate holdings during suspension period.

**Investor Impact**: High - Investors holding Polo Hotels Limited securities face immediate illiquidity and potential capital lock-in until compliance issues are resolved and trading resumes.

**Company Impact**: Critical - Indicates serious operational and compliance concerns, including potential abandonment of registered office and failure to engage with regulatory communications, which may lead to delisting proceedings if unresolved.
