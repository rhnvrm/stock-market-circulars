## Summary

BSE has announced mandatory adjustments to all Futures & Options contracts on Life Insurance Corporation of India (Derivatives Asset Code: LICL, Scrip Code: 543526) on account of a 1:1 bonus issue. The adjustments will be applied at end of day on May 27, 2026, with the ex-date being May 29, 2026. The adjustment factor is 2, meaning strike prices and futures prices will be halved, while market lots and positions will be doubled.

## Key Points

- LIC has fixed **May 29, 2026** as the record date for a 1:1 bonus issue (1 bonus equity share of Rs. 10/- for every 1 existing equity share of Rs. 10/-).
- The adjustment factor is **2**, calculated as (A+B)/B = (1+1)/1 = 2.
- Adjustments will be applied at **end of day on May 27, 2026** (ex-date: May 29, 2026).
- Market lot changes: **700 → 1400** (existing lot multiplied by adjustment factor of 2).
- Strike prices and futures prices will be **divided by 2**.
- All available F&O contracts on LICL are affected.

## Regulatory Changes

In pursuance of SEBI guidelines for adjustment of Futures & Options contracts on announcement of corporate actions, BSE is mandating the following contract-level adjustments across all available LICL derivatives:

1. **Strike Price**: Old strike price ÷ 2 (adjustment factor)
2. **Market Lot**: Old market lot × 2 → revised lot = 1400 (from existing 700)
3. **Position**: Old position × 2
4. **Futures Price**: Old futures price ÷ 2, rounded to nearest tick size

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members in the Equity Derivatives Segment must note the revised contract specifications effective from end of day May 27, 2026.
- Members should update their systems to reflect the new strike prices, lot sizes, and position sizes for all open LICL F&O contracts.
- For clarifications, trading members are requested to contact their designated Relationship Managers at BSE.

## Impact Assessment

This adjustment has a **high operational impact** on all market participants holding open positions in LIC Futures & Options contracts. The 1:1 bonus effectively halves the price of the underlying, requiring proportional adjustments across all contract parameters to maintain economic equivalence for existing positions. Traders and investors must:

- Recalibrate margin requirements based on revised lot sizes (doubled to 1400).
- Update option pricing models to reflect new strike prices (halved).
- Verify that position limits are recalculated with the new adjusted positions.
- Be aware that the economic value of open positions remains unchanged post-adjustment, but contract specifications will differ significantly.
