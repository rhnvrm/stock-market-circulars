## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260710-30) informing trading members that certain securities listed in the accompanying Annexure will move into their respective higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective July 13, 2026. This action is taken under the GSM Framework published on February 23, 2017, along with subsequent related exchange notices, and results in enhanced trading restrictions and surveillance deposit requirements on the affected securities.

## Key Points

- Securities identified in the Annexure_GSM attachment will be moved to higher GSM stages effective July 13, 2026.
- The move is made under the GSM Framework referenced in Exchange notices dated February 23, 2017, March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023.
- Surveillance actions vary by stage (I through IV), ranging from 100% margin with tighter price bands to trade-for-trade settlement with weekly trading windows and up to 100% Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD).
- Stage IV securities face the most severe restrictions: trade-for-trade settlement, trading permitted only once a week, 100% ASD from buyers, and no upward price movement allowed.
- All settlements for GSM securities are done on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off permitted.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing GSM Framework (established February 23, 2017 and amended via subsequent circulars) to a fresh list of securities moving into higher surveillance stages.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the specified margin rates, price bands, ASD requirements, and trading frequency restrictions to the identified securities from the effective date.
- Settlement of trades in these securities must be handled strictly on a trade-to-trade basis, with no netting off allowed.
- Members should consult the Annexure_GSM attachment for the specific list of affected securities and their designated stages.
- Queries can be directed to BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com or via the published GSM FAQ page.

## Important Dates

- **July 13, 2026**: Effective date for the affected securities moving into their respective higher GSM stages.
- **July 10, 2026**: Date of notice issuance.

## Impact Assessment

Securities moved to higher GSM stages will experience significantly reduced liquidity due to trade-for-trade settlement, restricted price bands, and high additional surveillance deposits (up to 100% of trade value for higher stages). Stage III and IV securities face weekly trading windows, severely limiting exit opportunities for existing holders. Investors and traders in the affected securities (specific tickers listed only in the Annexure, which is not included in this notice text) should exercise caution and factor in reduced liquidity and higher capital requirements when trading these names.
