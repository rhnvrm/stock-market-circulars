## Summary

BSE has announced the introduction of futures and options contracts on four individual securities in the Equity Derivatives segment, effective January 30, 2026. The newly added securities are Bajaj Holdings and Investment Limited, Premier Energies Limited, Swiggy Limited, and Waaree Energies Limited. Contract details will be available in the derivatives contract master file at end of day on January 29, 2026.

## Key Points

- Four new securities will have F&O contracts available for trading
- Contracts become tradeable from January 30, 2026
- Securities include Bajaj Holdings (scrip code 500490), Premier Energies (544238), Swiggy (544285), and Waaree Energies (544277)
- Contract master file will be updated on January 29, 2026
- Contract details accessible via BSE extranet and website

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory framework changes. This is an expansion of existing derivative trading facilities to cover additional underlying securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the availability of new contracts
- Members should download updated derivatives contract master file from BSE extranet or website after January 29, 2026
- Standard derivative trading rules and margin requirements apply

## Important Dates

- **January 14, 2026**: Notice date
- **January 29, 2026**: Contract master file updated (end of day)
- **January 30, 2026**: F&O contracts become available for trading

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Expansion of derivative market provides additional hedging and trading opportunities for market participants. These stocks represent diverse sectors (financial services, renewable energy, food delivery, solar equipment), enhancing sectoral coverage in derivatives.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members need to update systems and risk management frameworks to accommodate new contracts. Increased liquidity expected in underlying securities due to derivative trading activity.

**Trader Impact**: Enables leveraged positions, hedging strategies, and arbitrage opportunities for these four securities. Particularly significant for Swiggy and Waaree Energies as relatively recent listings gaining derivative market access.
