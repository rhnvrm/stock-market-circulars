## Summary

BSE has suspended trading in 21 commercial papers issued by various financial institutions and companies due to scheduled redemptions. The suspension affects instruments from major entities including ICICI Securities, Bajaj Finance, Poonawalla Fincorp, and others. Trading members are advised not to deal in these commercial papers from the specified dates.

## Key Points

- 21 commercial paper instruments suspended from trading
- Suspensions effective from January 22-23, 2026
- Record dates set between January 22-26, 2026
- Affects debt segment of BSE
- Companies include major financial services firms and NBFCs
- Trading members prohibited from dealing in listed securities from no dealings date

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice for commercial paper redemptions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the specified commercial papers from the "No Dealings From" dates
- Trading members must observe the record dates for redemption purposes
- Compliance with notices DR-804/2025-2026 and DR-805/2025-2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - Affects debt market participants dealing with commercial papers. The suspension is temporary and limited to specific instruments approaching maturity.

**Operational Impact:** Trading members must update their systems to prevent trades in suspended commercial papers. Investors holding these instruments will receive redemption proceeds as per the record dates.

**Sectoral Impact:** Primarily affects financial services sector as most issuers are NBFCs, financial institutions, and lending companies. This is routine market activity reflecting normal commercial paper lifecycle management.
