## Summary

BSE has released an updated list of 37 securities placed under the Add-on Price Band Framework as of March 2, 2026. This framework applies tighter intraday price movement limits to securities identified through surveillance criteria, typically to curb excessive volatility or irregular trading activity in smaller-cap stocks.

## Key Points

- 37 securities are currently listed under the Add-on Price Band Framework.
- All listed securities are predominantly small-cap and micro-cap companies across sectors including finance, manufacturing, chemicals, textiles, and technology.
- The framework imposes an additional price band (typically reducing the permitted daily price movement) on top of existing circuit filter limits.
- Securities remain on the list until BSE determines they no longer meet the inclusion criteria.
- The list includes companies such as Taparia Tools Ltd (505685), Omansh Enterprises Ltd (538537), Narmada Macplast Drip Irrigation Systems Ltd (517431), and Stellant Securities (India) Ltd (526071), among others.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework has been introduced. This circular represents a periodic update to the existing Add-on Price Band (AOPB) list, reflecting additions or reconfirmations of securities subject to the measure. The framework operates under BSE's broader surveillance and risk management guidelines.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members / Brokers**: Must note the restricted price bands applicable to listed securities and ensure order placement complies with the tighter limits.
- **Investors**: Orders in these securities will be subject to reduced price movement limits; buy/sell orders outside the applicable band will be rejected by the exchange system.
- No specific compliance filing is required from listed companies; this is an exchange-level surveillance action.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: March 2, 2026 (date of circular issuance).
- No expiry date specified; the list remains in effect until the next update is issued by BSE.

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Moderate for holders and traders of the 37 affected securities. The add-on price band restricts daily price movement, reducing liquidity and making it harder to exit positions quickly in volatile market conditions.

**Sectoral Spread**: Companies span diverse sectors — leasing & finance, granites, chemicals, plastics, steel, IT, textiles, and trading — suggesting surveillance is based on individual security behavior rather than sector-wide concerns.

**Investor Caution**: Retail investors should be aware that these securities have been flagged for surveillance due to unusual price or volume activity. This may indicate elevated risk, and due diligence is advised before initiating fresh positions.

**Liquidity Risk**: The tighter price bands may reduce market depth, making large trades difficult to execute at desired prices.
