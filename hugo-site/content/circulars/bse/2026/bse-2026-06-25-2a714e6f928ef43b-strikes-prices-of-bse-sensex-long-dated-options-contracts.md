## Summary

BSE has revised the strike price interval for BSE SENSEX Long Dated Options contracts. Effective June 29, 2026, the strike price interval will be 1000 with a 5-1-5 configuration (5 In-The-Money, 1 At-The-Money, 5 Out-The-Money strikes). This is a periodic review continuation in a series dating back to December 2020.

## Key Points

- Strike price interval set at **1000** for BSE SENSEX Long Dated Options contracts
- Strike structure: **5-1-5 (ITM-ATM-OTM)**
- Effective date: **June 29, 2026**
- This notice is part of a recurring semi-annual review series
- No attachments; trading members to contact their Relationship Managers for clarifications

## Regulatory Changes

The strike price parameters for BSE SENSEX Long Dated Options contracts are updated in a periodic review cycle. Previous reviews were issued on December 1, 2020; December 31, 2020; June 24, 2021; December 30, 2021; June 30, 2022; December 29, 2022; July 3, 2023; December 31, 2023; June 28, 2024; December 27, 2024; June 24, 2025; and December 24, 2025.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the updated strike price interval of 1000 effective June 29, 2026
- Members should update their systems and inform clients trading in BSE SENSEX Long Dated Options accordingly
- For clarifications, trading members must contact their respective BSE Relationship Managers

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 25, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 29, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This change affects trading members and participants active in BSE SENSEX Long Dated Options. The 1000-point strike interval with a 5-1-5 structure determines the range of available strike prices around the current index level, directly influencing hedging strategies and option availability for long-dated positions. The impact is moderate and operational in nature, limited to the derivatives segment.
