## Summary

BSE has issued a notice revising the price band percentages applicable to 21 scrips, effective from July 15, 2026. This is a routine surveillance measure under which the Exchange periodically adjusts the permissible daily price movement limits (price bands) for select securities based on trading and volatility parameters.

## Key Points

- 21 scrips have had their price bands revised from existing levels.
- Revised price bands range from as tight as 2% to as wide as 20%, depending on the scrip.
- Scrips affected include Advit Jewels Ltd, Jivial Industries Ltd, and Riyaasat Lifestyle Ltd (revised to 20%); Just Dial Ltd, Anjani Foods Ltd, Country Condos Ltd, and Polo Queen Industrial and Fintech Ltd (revised to 10%); several others revised to 5%; and Asia Capital Ltd, Chambal Breweries & Distilleries Ltd, Mukat Pipes Ltd, and Setco Automotive Ltd revised to 2%.
- Members with queries may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com.
- Notice signed by Sanjay Jain (Deputy Vice President, Surveillance) and Arpita Joshi (Manager, Surveillance).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulation is introduced; this is an operational application of BSE's existing surveillance framework for periodic price band revision based on scrip-level risk assessment.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must ensure their trading systems reflect the revised price bands for the listed scrips from the effective date to avoid order rejections or breaches of exchange-mandated price limits.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 15, 2026 (all revised price bands take effect from this date)
- Notice Date: July 14, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The revision affects intraday and daily trading flexibility for the 21 named scrips. Tighter price bands (2%) limit volatility and may reduce speculative trading in scrips like Asia Capital, Chambal Breweries, Mukat Pipes, and Setco Automotive, while wider bands (20%) for scrips like Advit Jewels, Jivial Industries, and Riyaasat Lifestyle allow greater price movement. Traders and investors holding or tracking these scrips should adjust order strategies to reflect the new limits. The overall market impact is limited in scope, confined to the specific scrips named.
