## Summary

BSE, in consultation with SEBI and in line with the extant surveillance framework, has announced a revision of scrips placed under the Trade-to-Trade (T/XT/MT/TS) settlement segment. Securities satisfying all three specified criteria (per Annexure I) will be moved into the Trade-to-Trade group effective July 9, 2026, while securities listed in Annexure II will continue to remain in the T/XT/MT/TS/P/Z/ZP groups and will be subject to a 5% price band.

## Key Points

- Action taken under the joint SEBI-Exchange surveillance framework for preventive market safety measures.
- Securities meeting Criteria I, II, and III (per Annexure I) will be transferred to the Trade-to-Trade (T/XT/MT/TS) group.
- Securities in Annexure II will remain in T/XT/MT/TS/P/Z/ZP groups with a 5% price band.
- No netting off of positions is allowed for securities settled on a trade-to-trade basis.
- A VAR Margin of 100% will be levied on these scrips as per Exchange Notice No. 20050805-12 dated August 5, 2005.
- Shortlisting of securities is based on XBRL submissions filed by respective listed companies.
- The transfer is a temporary, periodic surveillance measure and is not to be construed as an adverse action against any company.
- Detailed shifting criteria are published on the BSE surveillance webpage.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a routine, periodic application of the existing SEBI-approved criteria for shifting securities to/from the trade-to-trade segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must exercise adequate precaution while trading in the listed securities given the trade-to-trade settlement basis.
- Trading Members must account for the 100% VAR margin applicable on these scrips.
- No netting off of positions is permitted for affected securities.
- Queries or clarifications should be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 6, 2026
- Effective Date of Transfer to Trade-to-Trade Group: July 9, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to trading members and investors dealing in the specific securities named in the (unattached) Annexure I and II lists. Trade-to-trade classification increases transaction costs and margin requirements (100% VAR) while restricting intraday netting, reducing liquidity and speculative trading in affected scrips. The 5% price band on Annexure II securities further limits price volatility. This is a standard, temporary surveillance measure reviewed periodically and does not signal any company-specific adverse development.
