## Summary

BSE has announced the non-competitive bidding facility for the auction of State Government Securities (SDL) scheduled on June 02, 2026. This follows an RBI press release dated May 29, 2026, and is in continuation of the Exchange circular no. 20191122-28 (November 22, 2019) which launched the non-competitive bidding facility for SDL auctions. Bidding is available through the NCB-GSec module on BSE's iBBS platform.

## Key Points

- Bidding platform: https://ibbs.bseindia.com >> NCB-GSec module
- Bid collection opens: June 01, 2026 from 10:00 AM (24-hour availability)
- Bid collection ends for Direct Investors: June 01, 2026 by 5:00 PM
- Bid collection ends for Members: June 02, 2026 by 8:00 AM
- Auction Date: June 02, 2026
- Settlement Date: June 03, 2026
- Minimum subscription size: Rs. 100; minimum units: 2,200 (varies by security)
- Maximum amount per bidder varies by security (Rs. 5 Cr to Rs. 22 Cr)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine notification under the existing non-competitive bidding framework established via BSE circular no. 20191122-28 dated November 22, 2019.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members wishing to participate must submit bids through the NCB-GSec module on the iBBS platform within the specified timelines. Direct investors must submit by 5:00 PM on June 01, 2026; members have until 8:00 AM on June 02, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **June 01, 2026, 10:00 AM**: Bid collection opens
- **June 01, 2026, 5:00 PM**: Deadline for Direct Investors
- **June 02, 2026, 8:00 AM**: Deadline for Members
- **June 02, 2026**: Auction Date
- **June 03, 2026**: Settlement Date

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine SDL auction notice applicable to trading members and direct investors participating in the debt segment. The securities span multiple state governments including Andhra Pradesh, Gujarat, Jammu & Kashmir, Maharashtra, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, and Telangana, with a mix of new and re-issued SDLs. Total notified amounts range from Rs. 5 Cr to Rs. 22 Cr per security. Impact is limited to debt segment participants using the NCB-GSec platform.
