## Summary

BSE has forwarded SEBI circular no. HO/38/12/11(5)2026-MIRSD-POD/I/14660/2026 dated June 24, 2026, which provides a relaxation in the certification requirement for Persons Associated with Investment Advice (PAIA) specifically involved in sales and other non-core services. All registered Investment Advisers are directed to take note and ensure compliance.

## Key Points

- SEBI has granted a relaxation in the certification requirement applicable to PAIAs engaged in sales and other non-core service functions.
- The relaxation is part of SEBI's Ease of Doing Business initiative aimed at reducing compliance burden for the investment advisory ecosystem.
- BSE Notice No. 20260630-9 disseminates the SEBI circular to all Investment Advisers registered on BSE.
- The original SEBI circular is attached to this BSE notice for reference.

## Regulatory Changes

SEBI circular HO/38/12/11(5)2026-MIRSD-POD/I/14660/2026 (dated June 24, 2026) modifies existing certification requirements under the PAIA framework. Persons Associated with Investment Advice who perform sales and other non-core service roles may now be exempt from or subject to relaxed certification norms, as detailed in the attached SEBI circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- All Investment Advisers must read and review the attached SEBI circular in full.
- Investment Advisers should assess their PAIA workforce—particularly those in sales and non-core service roles—against the revised requirements.
- Ensure internal compliance processes and training requirements are updated to reflect the relaxed certification norms.
- Membership Compliance teams should document adherence to the updated SEBI framework.

## Important Dates

- **SEBI Circular Date:** June 24, 2026
- **BSE Notice Date:** June 30, 2026
- Effective date and any transition period: refer to the attached SEBI circular (HO/38/12/11(5)2026-MIRSD-POD/I/14660/2026) for specific timelines.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a positive operational impact on Investment Advisers by reducing the certification burden on personnel in sales and non-core service functions. It lowers compliance costs and administrative overhead associated with maintaining certifications for PAIA staff who do not directly provide investment advice. The change is part of a broader Ease of Doing Business drive by SEBI and is unlikely to have direct market or trading impact, but Investment Advisers should update their HR and compliance frameworks accordingly.
