## Summary

BSE has listed multiple commercial papers issued on a private placement basis on its Debt Segment, effective February 25, 2026. The securities cover six issuers — Sharekhan Limited, Infina Finance Private Limited, Axis Finance Limited, Godrej Housing Finance Limited, Julius Baer Capital (India) Private Limited, and Nuvama Wealth Finance Limited. All securities are available for trading exclusively in dematerialised form in standard denominations of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof, with a tick size of 1 paise.

## Key Points

- All listed securities are Commercial Papers issued on a private placement basis with face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each
- Trading is permitted only in demat form in standard denominations of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples
- Tick size: 1 paise
- All issuances carry top-tier short-term credit ratings (CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+ or IND A1+)
- Contact: BSE Debt Department — 22728352 / 8597 / 8995 / 5753 / 8915

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notification under BSE's debt market admission process for privately placed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form
- Transactions must be in standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Members requiring clarification should contact the BSE Debt Department

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational listing with low market impact. The commercial papers are short- to medium-term instruments (3 months to 1 year) issued by well-rated non-banking and financial services companies. All carry the highest short-term credit ratings (A1+), indicating minimal credit risk. The listing increases liquidity options in the debt segment for institutional and qualified investors but has no direct impact on equity markets or broader trading operations.
