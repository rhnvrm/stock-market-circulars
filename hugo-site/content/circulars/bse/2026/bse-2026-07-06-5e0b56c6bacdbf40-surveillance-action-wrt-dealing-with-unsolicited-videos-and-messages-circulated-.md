## Summary

BSE has issued an update to its surveillance framework addressing dealing with unsolicited videos and messages circulated on social media platforms, referencing the original Exchange Notice No. 20230224-38 dated February 24, 2023. Based on circulation of unsolicited videos, unusual price/volume movement, and other factors, certain securities (listed in Annexure I) have been shortlisted for GSM Stage IV-like restrictive actions, while other securities (listed in Annexure II) are being removed from this framework.

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I have been shortlisted due to circulation of unsolicited videos and unusual price/volume movement
- Actions similar to GSM (Graded Surveillance Measure) Stage IV are being imposed on these shortlisted scrips
- Securities in Annexure II will move out of this restrictive framework
- This surveillance action operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures
- The shortlisting is purely for market surveillance purposes and should not be construed as an adverse action against the concerned company/entity

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is an update/application of the existing surveillance mechanism first notified via Exchange Notice No. 20230224-38 dated February 24, 2023, targeting securities associated with unsolicited video/message circulation on social media.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply trade-for-trade settlement with a price band of 5% or lower for Annexure I securities from July 7, 2026
- Members must restrict trading in these securities to once a week (every Monday/first trading day) from July 13, 2026
- Members must collect Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) at 100% of trade value from buyers, with no upward price movement permitted, effective July 8, 2026
- Members should update systems to remove Annexure II securities from the restricted framework effective July 7, 2026
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- July 7, 2026: Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band becomes effective for Annexure I securities; Annexure II securities exit the framework
- July 8, 2026: 100% ASD requirement with no upward movement becomes effective
- July 13, 2026: Weekly trading restriction (Monday/first trading day only) becomes effective

## Impact Assessment

This circular has high impact for holders and traders of the specifically shortlisted securities, as it severely restricts liquidity through weekly trading windows, imposes tight price bands, and requires full upfront deposit from buyers, effectively curbing speculative trading activity linked to social media-driven price manipulation. For the broader market, this reinforces BSE's ongoing vigilance against pump-and-dump schemes propagated via social media, supporting market integrity, though its direct impact is confined to a limited set of scrips named in the annexures rather than the market as a whole.
