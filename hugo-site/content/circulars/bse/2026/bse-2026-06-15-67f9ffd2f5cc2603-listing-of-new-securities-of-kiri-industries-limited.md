## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that 51,45,446 new equity shares of Kiri Industries Limited (Scrip Code: 532967, ISIN: INE415I01015) are listed and permitted to trade on the Exchange with effect from Tuesday, June 16, 2026. These shares were issued to Promoters on a preferential basis pursuant to the conversion of warrants, at an issue price of Rs. 369/- per share (face value Rs. 10/- at a premium of Rs. 359/-).

## Key Points

- **New shares listed:** 51,45,446 equity shares of Rs. 10/- each
- **Issue price:** Rs. 369/- per share (face value Rs. 10/- + premium Rs. 359/-)
- **Issued to:** Promoters on a preferential basis via conversion of warrants
- **Effective trading date:** Tuesday, June 16, 2026
- **Distribution numbers:** 60022555 to 65168000
- **Date of allotment:** April 11, 2026
- **ISIN:** INE415I01015
- New shares rank pari-passu with existing equity shares of the company

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification under BSE's Listing Operations department following completion of a preferential allotment.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members of BSE are informed that these new securities are permitted for trading from June 16, 2026. No specific compliance action is required from trading members beyond awareness of the new share count and distribution numbers.

## Important Dates

- **Date of allotment:** April 11, 2026
- **Effective listing/trading date:** June 16, 2026 (Tuesday)
- **Lock-in expiry:** December 19, 2027

## Impact Assessment

The listing of 51,45,446 new equity shares results in an increase in total paid-up equity capital of Kiri Industries Limited. Since these shares are allotted to Promoters via warrant conversion, promoter shareholding will increase, potentially diluting the public float proportionally. All newly listed shares are subject to a lock-in period until December 19, 2027, meaning they cannot be sold in the market until that date. This limits any immediate selling pressure from the new issuance. Existing shareholders should note the dilution effect on EPS and shareholding percentage.
