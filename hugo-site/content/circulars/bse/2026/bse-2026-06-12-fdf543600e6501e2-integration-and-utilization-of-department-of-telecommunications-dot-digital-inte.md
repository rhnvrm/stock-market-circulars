## Summary

BSE has forwarded a SEBI communication advising all Investment Advisers to onboard and actively utilize the Department of Telecommunications' (DoT) Digital Intelligence Platform (DIP). The platform is intended to strengthen cyber fraud prevention through intelligence sharing among financial market participants.

## Key Points

- SEBI has communicated to BSE regarding integration and utilization of DoT's Digital Intelligence Platform (DIP)
- Investment Advisers are specifically directed to onboard the DIP and actively use it for intelligence sharing
- The platform focuses on Financial Fraud Risk Indicator (FRI) integration
- Contact support channels have been provided for both technical and administrative queries

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes; this notice operationalizes an existing SEBI directive requiring Investment Advisers to leverage DoT's DIP infrastructure for cyber fraud prevention.

## Compliance Requirements

- Investment Advisers must onboard the DoT Digital Intelligence Platform (DIP)
- Entities must actively utilize DIP for cyber fraud intelligence sharing
- For technical or API-related issues: diu@cdot.in and diu.ne1@cdot.in
- For administrative or onboarding queries: dirdiu-dot@gov.in and adg1.diu-dot@gov.in

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 12 June 2026
- No specific deadline stated; Investment Advisers are advised to act promptly

## Impact Assessment

This directive applies to all Investment Advisers registered with BSE. While there is no direct market or trading impact, non-compliance with SEBI's cybersecurity directives could expose firms to regulatory scrutiny. The integration with DoT's DIP aims to reduce financial fraud risks by enabling real-time intelligence sharing between telecom and financial regulators.
