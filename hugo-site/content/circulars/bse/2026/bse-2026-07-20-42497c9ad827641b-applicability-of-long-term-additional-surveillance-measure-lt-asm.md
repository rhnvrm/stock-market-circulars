## Summary

BSE has issued an update to the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework, referencing prior notices dated March 21, 2018, October 27, 2018, December 04, 2020, August 09, 2024, September 20, 2024, and June 04, 2021. Securities listed in the accompanying annexures will have 100% margin requirements and lower price bands applied, be moved to higher LT-ASM stages, be transferred to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups, or be removed from the LT-ASM framework entirely, depending on their classification in Annexures I, II, and III.

## Key Points

- 100% margin shall apply from July 23, 2026 on all open positions as of July 22, 2026 and on new positions created from July 23, 2026 onward, for securities in Annexure I (Part A).
- Securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages are listed in Annexure I (Part B), effective July 21, 2026.
- Lower price bands apply from July 21, 2026 for the above securities.
- Securities per Annexure I (Part D) will remain in the LT-ASM framework but be transferred to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups effective July 23, 2026.
- Securities exiting the LT-ASM framework, effective July 21, 2026, are listed in Annexure II.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the LT-ASM framework is provided in Annexure III.
- The framework operates alongside other existing surveillance measures.
- Inclusion under this framework is a surveillance-related action only and is not to be construed as adverse action against the company.
- Price bands for securities exiting the framework revert to pre-shortlisting levels, unless the security remains subject to another surveillance framework.
- Shortlisting is based on companies' XBRL submissions.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing (revised) LT-ASM framework per earlier BSE notices to an updated set of securities, per the periodic review process.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and market participants must apply 100% margin on affected securities' open and new positions from July 23, 2026.
- Ensure correct price band application (lower bands) for affected securities from July 21, 2026.
- Update systems to reflect trading group transfers (to T/XT/MT/TS) for relevant securities from July 23, 2026.
- Reinstate standard price bands for securities exiting the framework, unless another surveillance measure applies.
- Direct queries to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 21, 2026: Lower price bands effective; securities moving out of LT-ASM framework effective; securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages effective.
- July 22, 2026: Reference date for existing open positions subject to 100% margin.
- July 23, 2026: 100% margin effective on open/new positions; trading group transfers effective for Annexure I (Part D) securities.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has high trading impact for the specific securities named in the annexures (not disclosed in the notice text), as it materially raises margin requirements to 100%, restricts price movement via lower price bands, and can shift stocks into more restrictive trading groups (T/XT/MT/TS), reducing liquidity and increasing the cost of holding or trading these positions. Securities exiting the framework see reduced restrictions and eased trading conditions. Overall market-wide impact is limited to the affected securities rather than the broader market.
