## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that three new Treasury Bills (T-Bills) will be listed and admitted to dealings on the Exchange under the G Group – Debt Instruments segment with effect from June 29, 2026. The issuances cover the standard 91-day, 182-day, and 364-day tenors.

## Key Points

- Three T-Bills across three tenors (91-day, 182-day, 364-day) are being listed effective June 29, 2026.
- All securities are classified under G GROUP – DEBT INSTRUMENTS with a market lot of 1.
- Trading in each T-Bill will be suspended two working days before its respective maturity/redemption date (excluding bank holidays).
- Contact for clarifications: 2272 8352 / 5753 / 8597 (DCS-Listing department).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard periodic listing of government short-term debt instruments.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must observe the restriction on trading in these securities during the two working days immediately preceding each T-Bill's maturity date.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing of government Treasury Bills and has minimal market impact. It expands the available short-term sovereign debt instruments on the BSE debt segment, providing trading members with three new risk-free instruments across different tenors. No unusual trading conditions or structural changes are involved.
