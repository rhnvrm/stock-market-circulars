## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260420-40 dated April 20, 2026 informs trading members that 15 companies have fixed record dates for payment of interest and/or redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) and Bonds. Trading members are advised not to deal in these debt instruments around their respective record dates in May 2026.

## Key Points

- 15 companies' NCD/Bond instruments are subject to trading suspension around record dates
- Record dates fall between May 7–11, 2026
- Suspension applies to both interest payment and redemption events
- Settlement DR references are in the 625/2026-2027 to 627/2026-2027 range
- Notice issued by Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President – Listing Compliance and Operations

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice under existing BSE debt market trading suspension rules applicable when companies fix record dates for corporate actions on debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must refrain from dealing in the listed NCD/Bond scrips of the affected companies on and around their respective ex-dates and record dates
- Members should update their systems to block or flag trades in the specified ISIN codes during the restricted period

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine procedural notice with moderate impact on debt market participants. Trading members holding or transacting in these 15 NCD/Bond instruments must observe the trading suspension windows. The affected companies span major NBFCs (Bajaj Finance, Shriram Finance, Kotak Mahindra entities, Piramal Finance) and a PSU (NTPC), making this notice relevant to a broad segment of debt market traders. No impact on equity markets or broader regulatory framework.
