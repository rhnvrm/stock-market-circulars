## Summary

BSE has revised the price bands for 14 scrips in the equity segment effective March 06, 2026. The revisions set price bands at 5%, 10%, or 20% depending on the scrip, replacing their previously existing levels. The circular was issued by the Surveillance department under Notice No. 20260305-46.

## Key Points

- Price bands revised for 14 scrips effective March 06, 2026
- Revised bands range from 5% to 20% depending on the security
- 10 scrips assigned a 5% price band (tighter control)
- 3 scrips assigned a 10% price band
- 1 scrip (Noble Polymers Ltd) assigned a 20% price band
- Circular issued by BSE Surveillance department

## Regulatory Changes

Price bands for the listed scrips have been revised from their existing levels to the new percentages shown above. Price bands are circuit filters that limit the maximum intraday price movement (up or down) for a security. Tighter bands (5%) indicate heightened surveillance scrutiny, while a 20% band (Noble Polymers) represents a relaxation or standard band assignment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised price bands and ensure orders for the affected scrips comply with the new limits effective March 06, 2026
- Members requiring clarification should contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **March 05, 2026**: Circular issued
- **March 06, 2026**: Revised price bands come into effect

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors in the 14 affected scrips will face new daily price movement limits starting March 06, 2026. The majority of scrips (10 out of 14) are being placed under a restrictive 5% band, suggesting increased surveillance attention possibly due to abnormal price movements or volatility concerns. Jindal Poly Films (500227) is the most widely tracked scrip among those listed and its 10% band assignment moderately restricts intraday movement. Noble Polymers receiving a 20% band may indicate a relaxation from a previously tighter band.
