## Summary

BSE has published the list of securities subject to demat auction under Settlement No. 812 dated February 5, 2026. The auction covers 138 different securities from various sectors with quantities ranging from 1 share to 60,000 shares. This is a routine settlement auction process to address delivery shortfalls in the demat system.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 812
- Settlement Date: February 5, 2026
- Total Securities Listed: 138 scrips
- Quantity Range: 1 to 60,000 shares per scrip
- Highest Quantity: STEEL AUTHORITY (500113) - 60,000 shares
- Includes major companies like Reliance Industries, Adani Group companies, Tata Motors, JSW Steel, and others
- Covers diverse sectors including power, metals, banking, pharma, textiles, and technology

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational circular announcing scheduled auction settlement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members with shortages in the listed securities must participate in the demat auction
- Settlement participants must fulfill their delivery obligations as per BSE auction mechanism
- Brokers must ensure availability of securities or arrange through auction process
- Members should check their obligation positions against the published list

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: February 5, 2026
- Settlement Number: 812
- Auction timing and closure: As per standard BSE auction schedule

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects 138 securities across multiple sectors

**Operational Impact:** 
- Direct impact on brokers and clearing members with pending delivery obligations in these scrips
- May cause temporary price volatility in affected securities during auction
- Participants with short positions must arrange securities or participate in auction

**Sector Concentration:**
- Significant representation from metals and mining sector (Adani, JSW Steel, Tata Steel, SAIL, Vedanta, NMDC)
- Multiple banking and financial services stocks (Bajaj Finance, Canara Bank, DCB Bank, IDBI, Motilal Oswal)
- Power sector well-represented (Adani Power, NTPC, Adani Green Energy)
- Real estate and infrastructure companies included (DLF, Lodha, Oberoi Realty)

**Risk Assessment:** Standard auction settlement risk for participants with delivery shortfalls; potential for higher acquisition costs through auction mechanism
