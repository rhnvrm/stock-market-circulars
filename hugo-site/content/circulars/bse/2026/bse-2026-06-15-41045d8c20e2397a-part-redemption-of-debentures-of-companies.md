## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260615-49 dated June 15, 2026 informs trading members that six companies have fixed record dates for part redemption of their privately placed non-convertible debentures (NCDs). Trading in these debentures will continue with a reduced face value effective from the respective ex-dates mentioned below.

## Key Points

- Six NCD issuers have fixed record dates for part redemption during June 17–20, 2026
- Trading members must note that debentures will trade at reduced face values from the respective ex-dates
- All debentures are privately placed instruments listed on the BSE debt segment
- Settlement references are under DR series 653, 654, and 655 of 2026-2027

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice under BSE's Listing Compliance and Operations department informing members of corporate action record dates for debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must update their systems to reflect the reduced face values for the listed debentures from the respective ex-dates
- Trades executed on or after the ex-date must be priced with the reduced face value

## Impact Assessment

Impact is limited to holders of these specific privately placed NCDs and the trading members dealing in BSE's debt segment. The part redemptions reduce the outstanding face value of the debentures, lowering future coupon payments and principal at maturity. No broader equity market or retail investor impact is expected, as these are privately placed debt instruments with a narrow investor base.
