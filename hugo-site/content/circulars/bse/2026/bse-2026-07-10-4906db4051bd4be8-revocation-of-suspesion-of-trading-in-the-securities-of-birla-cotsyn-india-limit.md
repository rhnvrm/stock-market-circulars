## Summary

BSE has announced the revocation of the suspension of trading in the securities of Birla Cotsyn (India) Limited (Scrip Code: 533006), effective from Wednesday, July 15, 2026. This action follows the Exchange's earlier notice dated December 14, 2020 (Notice No. 20201214-11) and is pursuant to an order of the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) dated January 9, 2025.

## Key Points

- Trading in Birla Cotsyn (India) Limited (Scrip Code: 533006) will resume effective July 15, 2026.
- The revocation follows an NCLT order dated January 9, 2025, and references the original suspension notice (No. 20201214-11) dated December 14, 2020.
- The security will be included in the Special Pre-Open Session (SPOS) applicable to IPOs and relisted scrips.
- Trading members should refer to Exchange notice no. 20120216-29 for details on the SPOS mechanism.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is an operational action implementing a prior NCLT order to lift a long-standing trading suspension for a specific company.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure systems recognize the scrip as active from the effective date.
- Trading members should familiarize themselves with the SPOS process for relisted scrips per notice no. 20120216-29.
- Queries can be directed to BSE Listing Compliance and Operations at 022-2272 8706.

## Important Dates

- December 14, 2020: Original suspension notice (No. 20201214-11).
- January 9, 2025: NCLT order enabling revocation.
- July 10, 2026: Notice date.
- July 15, 2026 (Wednesday): Effective date of trading revocation.

## Impact Assessment

The revocation allows shareholders of Birla Cotsyn (India) Limited to trade the company's shares again after a multi-year suspension, restoring liquidity and price discovery for the stock. Trading will initially occur through the Special Pre-Open Session mechanism used for relisted scrips, which may result in wider price discovery volatility on the first day of resumed trading. The impact is largely confined to existing shareholders and prospective investors in this specific security rather than the broader market.
