## Summary

BSE has informed trading members that new securities of IDream Film Infrastructure Company Limited (Scrip Code - 504375) are listed and permitted to trade with effect from Monday, July 20, 2026. The new securities comprise 40,00,000 equity shares issued to a Promoter on a preferential basis pursuant to conversion of warrants, and 26,64,03,280 equity shares issued to Promoters and Non-Promoters on a preferential basis pursuant to a share swap. These new shares rank pari-passu with the company's existing equity shares.

## Key Points

- Scrip Code: 504375; ISIN: INE459E01012
- 40,00,000 equity shares of Rs. 10/- each issued at par to Promoter pursuant to conversion of warrants
- 26,64,03,280 equity shares of Rs. 10/- each issued at par to Promoters and Non-Promoters pursuant to share swap
- Total shares under this listing: 27,04,03,280
- Issue Price: Rs. 10/- per share (at par)
- Distinctive numbers: 150001 to 270553280
- New shares rank pari-passu with existing equity shares
- Lock-in details for the shares are specified in Annexure_I

## Regulatory Changes

None; this is a routine BSE listing notification under Listing Operations for securities issued via preferential allotment.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must recognize the new securities as listed and available for trading from the effective date. Lock-in restrictions on the allotted shares, as detailed in Annexure_I, must be observed by relevant parties.

## Important Dates

- Date of Allotment: May 21, 2026
- Effective date of listing and trading: Monday, July 20, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The listing adds over 27 crore new equity shares to IDream Film Infrastructure Company Limited's tradable base, arising from warrant conversion and a share swap arrangement, both benefiting promoters and non-promoters on a preferential basis. This significantly expands the company's paid-up equity capital and free float dynamics, which shareholders and analysts should factor into valuation and ownership pattern assessments. The presence of lock-in restrictions on a portion of these shares may limit immediate selling pressure post-listing.
