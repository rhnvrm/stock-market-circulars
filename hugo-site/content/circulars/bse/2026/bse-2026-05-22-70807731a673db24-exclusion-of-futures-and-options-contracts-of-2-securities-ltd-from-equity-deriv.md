## Summary

BSE has notified the exclusion of Futures and Options (F&O) contracts for two securities — Nuvama Wealth Management Ltd (NWML, Scrip Code: 543988) and Exide Industries Ltd (EXID, Scrip Code: 500086) — from the Equity Derivatives Segment. Trading in these contracts will be discontinued effective July 31, 2026, pursuant to SEBI's eligibility criteria circular.

## Key Points

- Contracts for NWML and EXID will be discontinued from the Equity Derivatives Segment from **July 31, 2026**.
- No new expiry month contracts will be generated for these securities.
- The exclusion is based on SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-2/P/CIR/2024/116 (August 30, 2024) and BSE Notice No. 20240902-3 (September 02, 2024) on eligibility criteria for entry/exit of stocks in the derivatives segment.

## Regulatory Changes

The exclusion is mandated under SEBI's revised eligibility criteria for stocks in the derivatives segment, as outlined in SEBI Circular dated August 30, 2024, and the corresponding BSE implementation notice dated September 02, 2024.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the discontinuation of new contract generation for NWML and EXID.
- Members should inform clients holding or intending to hold positions in the affected securities' F&O contracts about the wind-down timeline.
- No fresh positions in new expiry months beyond July 2026 may be taken for these securities.

## Important Dates

- **May 27, 2026** — May 2026 contracts expire (existing)
- **June 25, 2026** — June 2026 contracts expire (existing)
- **July 30, 2026** — July 2026 contracts expire (existing; final expiry)
- **July 31, 2026** — Effective date of exclusion; no contracts available for trading

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors with open positions in NWML and EXID futures or options have until July 30, 2026 to manage or close their positions. After July 31, 2026, no F&O contracts for these two securities will be available on BSE. This impacts hedging strategies and leveraged positions tied to these stocks. Market participants should reassess portfolios and risk exposures accordingly.
