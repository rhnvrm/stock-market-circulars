## Summary

BSE has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, introducing new securities subject to minimum 100% margin requirements effective June 12, 2026. Securities already in ESM but qualifying for a stage downgrade will move to Stage I effective June 11, 2026. Securities exiting the ESM framework are also listed. This notice references and builds upon a series of prior ESM notices dating back to June 2023.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open positions as on June 11, 2026 and on new positions created from June 12, 2026.
- Certain securities currently under ESM will be moved to the lower Stage I effective June 11, 2026 (listed in Annexure I).
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under ESM is provided in Annexure III.
- Securities qualifying under ESM will be shifted from Rolling Settlement segment to Trade-for-Trade (TFT) segment effective June 12, 2026.
- Securities moving to Stage II will be subject to Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction effective June 11, 2026.
- Securities exiting the ESM framework effective June 11, 2026 are listed in Annexure II.
- ESM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the exchanges.
- Shortlisting under ESM is purely for market surveillance purposes and should not be construed as adverse action against the concerned company or entity.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice continues the ESM framework established and progressively updated through notices:
- 20230602-44 (June 02, 2023)
- 20230718-46 (July 18, 2023)
- 20240809-42 (August 09, 2024)
- 20240920-63 (September 20, 2024)
- 20241004-65 (October 04, 2024)
- 20250725-61 (July 25, 2025)

The current update introduces a new batch of securities into ESM, adjusts stages for qualifying securities, and removes securities that no longer meet the ESM criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure minimum 100% margin is collected on all open and new positions in newly included ESM securities from June 12, 2026.
- Members must comply with Trade-for-Trade settlement obligations for all securities shifted under ESM.
- For Stage II securities, members must adhere to the 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism.
- Price bands for securities exiting ESM will revert to the price band applicable before ESM inclusion, unless the scrip remains under another surveillance measure.
- Members with clarifications may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **June 11, 2026**: Stage I downgrade effective for qualifying securities; Stage II Trade-for-Trade with 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction effective; securities exiting ESM framework effective.
- **June 12, 2026**: Minimum 100% margin effective on open positions (as on June 11, 2026) and new positions; shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment effective for newly included ESM securities.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has high market impact for the specific securities affected. Inclusion in ESM results in:
- Significantly increased margin requirements (minimum 100%), raising the cost of trading and reducing leverage.
- Migration to Trade-for-Trade settlement, which eliminates intraday netting and requires full delivery/payment, reducing speculative trading.
- Stage II classification further restricts trading with a narrow 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction, severely limiting liquidity.
- Conversely, securities exiting ESM will benefit from restored price bands and return to normal rolling settlement, improving liquidity.

Brokers and traders holding or planning positions in the affected securities must review the annexures and adjust their margin and settlement planning accordingly.
