## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260520-4) directing trading members not to deal in the Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) and Bonds of multiple companies that have fixed record dates for interest payment and/or redemption. The suspension covers 13 debt instruments across 7 companies, with record dates falling between 21 May 2026 and 25 May 2026.

## Key Points

- Trading members are advised not to deal in the listed debentures/bonds of the specified companies around their respective record/ex-dates
- 13 NCD/Bond instruments across 7 companies are affected
- Record dates range from 21 May 2026 to 25 May 2026
- Settlements are under DR series 635/2026-2027 and 636/2026-2027 and 637/2026-2027
- Notice issued by Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President – Listing Compliance and Operations

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice under BSE's standard procedure for suspending trading in debt instruments around corporate action record dates.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must refrain from dealing in the specified debentures/bonds on and around the listed ex-dates
- Members should note that ex-dates and record dates may differ by one day for certain instruments (e.g., CreditAccess Grameen and Samunnati Finance)

## Impact Assessment

Impact is limited to holders and traders of these specific debt instruments in the BSE debt segment. The suspension is temporary and routine, tied to corporate action record dates for interest payment or redemption. Investors holding these NCDs/Bonds should be aware they cannot trade around the ex-dates. The bulk of affected instruments belong to Mahindra & Mahindra Financial Services Limited (6 instruments), suggesting significant maturity/coupon activity for that issuer on 22 May 2026.
