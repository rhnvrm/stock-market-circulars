## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure) will be moved to their respective higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework with effect from June 25, 2026. These securities will attract progressively stringent surveillance actions depending on the GSM stage assigned, ranging from enhanced margins to highly restricted weekly trading sessions.

## Key Points

- Securities per the attached Annexure will move to higher GSM stages effective **June 25, 2026**
- No netting off shall be allowed; settlement will be on a trade-to-trade basis
- Four GSM stages apply, each with escalating restrictions
- Trading members are advised to take adequate precautions when trading in these securities
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Regulatory Changes

This notice operates under the GSM Framework published on February 23, 2017, and subsequent exchange notices (20170223-44, 20170303-29, 20180720-55, 20191129-23, 20231117-63). Securities are being escalated to higher stages based on surveillance criteria defined in those circulars.

## Compliance Requirements

| Stage | Surveillance Actions |
|-------|----------------------|
| I | 100% applicable margin rate; price band of 5% or lower |
| II | Trade-for-trade; 5% or lower price band; Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value by buyers |
| III | Trade-for-trade; 5% or lower price band; trading permitted once a week (every Monday/1st trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value by buyers |
| IV | Trade-for-trade; 5% or lower price band; trading permitted once a week (every Monday/1st trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value by buyers; **no upward movement** |

- No netting off is permitted; all settlement is trade-to-trade
- Buyers must deposit the specified ASD at the applicable stage

## Important Dates

- **June 24, 2026**: Notice issued
- **June 25, 2026**: New GSM stage classifications take effect for listed securities

## Impact Assessment

Securities elevated to higher GSM stages face substantially reduced liquidity and increased cost of trading. Stage III and IV securities are particularly affected, being restricted to once-a-week trading with full trade value ASD requirements. Stage IV additionally prohibits any upward price movement, effectively capping upside for buyers. Trading members and investors holding or intending to trade these securities should reassess positions in light of these restrictions. The full list of affected securities is available in the Annexure attached to the original notice.
