## Summary

BSE has announced updates to the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) Framework effective March 10, 2026. Five new securities have been shortlisted under the framework, one security has been moved to a higher ASM stage, and two securities have been removed from the framework effective March 9, 2026.

## Key Points

- **Part A**: 5 new securities added to Long Term ASM Framework w.e.f. March 10, 2026
- **Part B**: 1 security (Sirohia & Sons Ltd) moved to higher Stage 2 ASM w.e.f. March 10, 2026
- **Part C**: No securities moved to lower ASM stage (Nil)
- **Part D**: No securities directly shortlisted to Stage IV (Nil)
- **Annexure II**: 2 securities removed from LT-ASM Framework w.e.f. March 9, 2026
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under LT-ASM is provided in Annexure III

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply enhanced surveillance measures and applicable margin requirements for securities newly added to the LT-ASM framework
- Securities moved to Stage 2 ASM (Sirohia & Sons Ltd) are subject to stricter trading conditions applicable to that stage
- Securities removed from LT-ASM (Noble Polymers Ltd and OTCO International Ltd) are now subject to ESM and GSM frameworks respectively
- Market participants should update their systems to reflect the revised consolidated list in Annexure III

## Important Dates

- **March 9, 2026**: Noble Polymers Ltd and OTCO International Ltd removed from LT-ASM Framework
- **March 10, 2026**: 5 new securities added to LT-ASM Framework (effective date)
- **March 10, 2026**: Sirohia & Sons Ltd moved to Stage 2 ASM (effective date)

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding positions in the 5 newly added securities (Alfavision Overseas India Ltd, Bhagawati Gas Ltd, Decorous Investment & Trading Co Ltd, Garware Marine Industries Ltd, and Magenta Lifecare Ltd) will face enhanced surveillance measures including higher margin requirements and trade-to-trade settlement restrictions. Sirohia & Sons Ltd faces tighter restrictions following its move to the higher Stage 2 ASM. The removal of Noble Polymers Ltd and OTCO International Ltd from LT-ASM does not represent a relaxation of restrictions, as these securities have been transferred to the ESM and GSM frameworks respectively, which carry their own trading restrictions.
