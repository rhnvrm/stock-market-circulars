## Summary

BSE has notified members that SBI Mutual Fund will launch the SBI Nifty Midcap 150 Momentum 50 ETF FOF as a New Fund Offer (NFO) with SIP facility on the BSE StAR MF Platform. The NFO will be open from July 27, 2026, to August 5, 2026, with a tentative allotment date of August 7, 2026, across six plan/option variants (Regular and Direct plans with Growth, IDCW Payout, and IDCW Reinvestment options).

## Key Points

- Scheme: SBI Nifty Midcap 150 Momentum 50 ETF FOF (classified as DEBT scheme type in the notice)
- Six ISINs covering Regular Plan (Growth, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment) and Direct Plan (Growth, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment)
- NFO with SIP facility available on BSE StAR MF Platform
- Tentative allotment date: August 7, 2026
- Transactions without client fund initiation by 9:00 AM on T+1 day will be cancelled and reported to RTA/AMC with 'N' flag
- Pending transactions with initiated payment remain valid until 3:00 PM on last fund pay-out day or T+5, whichever is earlier
- Cheque and OTM payments accepted subject to clear funds by 6:00 PM on August 7, 2026
- Switch transactions accepted during NFO period, but switch-out funds received on/after allotment date won't be considered for allotment

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an operational notice implementing standard BSE StAR MF Platform procedures for a new fund launch.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure clear funds are available in the ICCL account by 6:00 PM on August 7, 2026, for allotment eligibility
- Members must monitor client fund initiation timelines (9:00 AM T+1) to avoid transaction cancellation
- Members handling cheque/OTM and switch transactions must adhere to the specified cut-off and funds-clearance conditions

## Important Dates

- NFO Period: July 27, 2026 to August 5, 2026
- Fund clearance deadline: 6:00 PM, August 7, 2026
- Tentative Allotment Date: August 7, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, low-impact operational circular relevant primarily to BSE StAR MF Platform members and distributors handling SBI Mutual Fund transactions. It does not affect listed equities, trading in exchange-traded securities, or broader market operations beyond the mutual fund distribution ecosystem.
