## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260623-39 (dated June 23, 2026) informs trading members that 12 companies have fixed record dates for payment of interest or redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) and bonds. Trading members are advised not to deal in these debentures on and around their respective ex-dates, spanning June 30 to July 6, 2026.

## Key Points

- 18 NCD/bond scrips across 12 companies are subject to trading suspension around their record dates
- Record dates range from June 30, 2026 to July 6, 2026
- Suspensions apply in the debt segment under Listing Operations (Corporate Actions category)
- Settlement series referenced is DR 661/2026-2027 through 665/2026-2027
- Both privately placed (PVT) and publicly issued (NCD) debentures are covered
- One zero-coupon (ZC) instrument is included among the listed debentures

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice under existing BSE framework for debt instrument corporate actions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must refrain from dealing in the listed debentures on their respective ex-dates
- Members should note each scrip's specific ex-date and record date to ensure compliance
- Standard settlement and record-date procedures under BSE debt segment rules apply

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market operational notice. Trading in the listed NCDs and bonds will be suspended around their ex-dates to facilitate accurate determination of eligible holders for interest payments or principal redemption. Impact is limited to secondary market participants holding or intending to trade these specific debt instruments. Notably, large-cap issuers such as Bajaj Finance, IIFL Finance, and L&T Finance are included, which may affect liquidity in those specific NCD series during the suspension window.
