## Summary

BSE has issued a notice under its Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, informing trading members that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure_GSM) will move into their respective higher GSM stages with effect from July 21, 2026. This action references prior exchange notices dated February 23, 2017, March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023, and is intended to alert investors and members to increased surveillance actions on the affected securities.

## Key Points

- Securities identified in the Annexure_GSM attachment will be moved to higher GSM stages effective July 21, 2026.
- GSM stages range from Stage I to Stage IV, each carrying progressively stricter surveillance actions.
- Stage I: 100% margin requirement and price band capped at 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, plus 50% Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) on trade value from buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, trading permitted only once a week (Monday/first trading day), and 100% ASD from buyers.
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III but with no upward price movement permitted.
- All settlements for these securities will be done strictly on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing GSM Framework (published February 23, 2017) by moving specific securities to higher surveillance stages based on ongoing monitoring criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin rates, price bands, Additional Surveillance Deposits, and trading frequency restrictions for securities named in the Annexure effective from the specified date.
- Members must ensure trade-to-trade settlement is applied with no netting for these securities.
- Members are advised to exercise adequate precaution while trading in the affected securities.
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com or the published GSM FAQ page.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 21, 2026 — securities move to their respective higher GSM stages.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is concentrated on the specific securities named in the Annexure (not disclosed in the notice body), which will face reduced liquidity due to trade-for-trade settlement, higher margin/deposit requirements, tighter price bands, and in higher stages, restricted weekly trading windows. This is likely to significantly affect trading volumes and price discovery for the impacted stocks, while having limited effect on the broader market.
