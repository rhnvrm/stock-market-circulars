## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, July 04, 2026. The session allows trading members using third-party platforms or in-house systems via IML/ETI APIs to test their trading applications, including exceptional market conditions such as risk-reduction mode and trading halts. BOLT Pro TWS version 13.11 will also be released, with details to be published separately.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, July 04, 2026
- Log-in window: 10:15 AM to 11:00 AM
- Continuous Trading: 11:00 AM to 03:30 PM
- New BOLT Pro TWS version 13.11 to be released, with details via separate circular
- Members may participate in Mock Trading Session or UAT environment to fulfill regulatory testing requirements per SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 dated November 24, 2020
- Trades from mock trading do not attract margin, pay-in/pay-out obligations, or create rights/liabilities
- Members requested to submit feedback on the mock trading session by 4:30 PM

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an operational notice referencing existing SEBI circular requirements on software testing (SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 dated November 24, 2020) and BSE Notice No. 20201201-22 dated December 1, 2020.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members using third-party empaneled vendor platforms or in-house developed systems (via IML/ETI APIs) are encouraged to participate in the mock trading session or the UAT environment to fulfill regulatory testing obligations. Members must provide feedback on the session by 4:30 PM via the contact details provided.

## Important Dates

- Mock Trading Session: Saturday, July 04, 2026
- Log-in: 10:15 AM – 11:00 AM
- Continuous Trading: 11:00 AM – 03:30 PM
- Feedback deadline: 4:30 PM on the same day

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact as this is a routine testing exercise conducted outside normal trading days. It does not affect any listed securities, create financial obligations, or alter regular trading operations. Relevant primarily to trading members and technology vendors for system testing and readiness verification.
