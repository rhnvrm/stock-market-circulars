## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Equity segment on Saturday, May 30, 2026. The session will run from both the Primary Site (PR) and Disaster Recovery Site (DR), enabling trading members and third-party platform users to test trading applications and familiarize themselves with various functionalities including call auction sessions, risk-reduction mode, trading halts, and block deals.

## Key Points

- Mock trading is scheduled for Saturday, May 30, 2026, for the Equity segment
- Both Primary Site (PR) and Disaster Recovery Site (DR) will be active during the session
- Third-party trading platform users can use this session to test their applications
- Testable functionalities include call auction sessions, risk-reduction mode, trading halt, and block deals
- All trades from mock trading carry no margin, pay-in, or pay-out obligations
- Mock trades create no rights or liabilities for participants
- Members are requested to submit feedback by 4:00 pm on the day

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an operational mock trading exercise.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are encouraged to participate actively in the mock trading session
- Members using third-party platforms should use this opportunity to test their applications
- Feedback must be submitted by 4:00 pm on May 30, 2026, via contact numbers or email provided by BSE
- For queries, members may contact their Relationship Manager, BSE Helpdesk, or BSE Tech Support

## Important Dates

- **May 30, 2026 (Saturday):** Mock trading session for Equity segment
- **4:00 pm, May 30, 2026:** Deadline for submitting feedback on the mock trading session

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational exercise with no market or financial impact. All trades executed during the mock session are for testing purposes only and will not result in any margin obligations, pay-in or pay-out obligations, or any legal rights and liabilities. The session primarily benefits trading members by providing an opportunity to validate system readiness and test functionalities under simulated market conditions, including a switchover from Primary to Disaster Recovery site.
