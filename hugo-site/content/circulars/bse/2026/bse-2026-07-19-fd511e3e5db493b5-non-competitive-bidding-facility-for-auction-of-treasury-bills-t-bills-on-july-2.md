## Summary

BSE has issued a notice informing trading members about the non-competitive bidding (NCB) facility available for the auction of three Treasury Bills (T-Bills) to be conducted by RBI on July 22, 2026. Bidding will be facilitated through the NCB-GSec module of BSE's iBBS web-based system, in line with the exchange's earlier circular (20180423-42, dated April 26, 2018) that launched this facility.

## Key Points

- Reference to RBI press release dated July 17, 2026 announcing auction of three Treasury Bills on July 22, 2026.
- Non-competitive bidding facility available via NCB-GSec module on BSE's iBBS platform (https://ibbs.bseindia.com).
- Securities on offer: 91 Day T-Bill (091T211026), 182 Day T-Bill (182T200127), and 364 Day T-Bill (364T220727).
- Issue sizes: Rs. 9,000 Cr (91-day), Rs. 8,000 Cr (182-day), Rs. 7,000 Cr (364-day).
- Minimum lot and multiples: Rs. 10,000; Minimum bid amount: Rs. 100; Maximum bid amount per non-competitive bidder: Rs. 450 Cr (91-day), Rs. 400 Cr (182-day), Rs. 350 Cr (364-day).
- Contact details provided for user ID/password issues, bidding queries, and obligation/pay-in queries with ICCL Operations.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; this is an operational notice implementing the existing NCB-GSec facility (established via BSE circular 20180423-42) for a specific upcoming RBI T-Bill auction.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and direct investors must submit bids within the specified windows via the NCB-GSec module.
- Direct investors must complete bidding by July 21, 2026, 5:00 pm.
- Members must complete bidding by July 22, 2026, 8:00 am.
- Settlement obligations to be coordinated with ICCL Operations.

## Important Dates

- Bid Collection Start Date: July 20, 2026, 10:00 am (24-hour availability)
- Bid Collection End Date (Direct Investors): July 21, 2026, till 5:00 pm
- Bid Collection End Date (Members): July 22, 2026, till 8:00 am
- Auction Date: July 22, 2026
- Settlement Date: July 23, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, procedural notice for the debt/G-Sec trading segment enabling retail and non-competitive participation in the RBI T-Bill auction. It has no direct impact on listed equities or specific stocks, and primarily concerns members and investors participating in government securities auctions.
