## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction list for Settlement No. 802 dated January 21, 2026. The circular lists securities available for auction across various scrips with their respective scrip codes and quantities. This is a standard operational circular for demat auction settlements.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 802
- Date: January 21, 2026
- Total scrips listed: Approximately 140 securities
- Quantities range from 1 unit to 20,000 units depending on the security
- Covers diverse sectors including banking, steel, infrastructure, mutual funds, hospitality, and manufacturing

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced in this circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants involved in the listed securities must participate in the demat auction as per BSE auction guidelines
- Settlement obligations must be fulfilled for the securities listed in Settlement No. 802

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: January 21, 2026 (Settlement No. 802)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with minimal market impact. The auction affects specific scrips where delivery obligations have not been met. Impact is limited to:

- Brokers and trading members with outstanding delivery obligations in the listed securities
- Investors holding positions in the affected scrips may see temporary price movements during auction settlement
- Quantities involved are relatively small for most securities, suggesting limited systemic impact

## Detailed Securities List

The auction includes major companies such as:
- **Financial Services**: ICICI Bank (1 unit), IDBI Bank (1 unit), SBI Life Insurance (1 unit), Tata Capital (336 units)
- **Steel & Metals**: Tata Steel (5 units), Jindal Steel (5 units), Jindal Saw (1,304 units)
- **Consumer Goods**: ITC (11 units), Reliance Industries (3 units), Havells (597 units)
- **Infrastructure**: Man Infracon (1,052 units), Rail Vikas Nigam (4,575 units)
- **Mutual Funds**: Multiple 360 ONE Mutual Fund units, Bandhan MF, Nippon India MF, Zerodha MF

And numerous mid-cap and small-cap securities across various sectors.
