## Summary

BSE has updated the list of securities subject to Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) for companies undergoing the Insolvency Resolution Process (IRP) under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). The changes are effective June 24, 2026, covering both securities newly added to and removed from the IBC surveillance framework.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I have satisfied the criteria for ASM under IBC and will be placed under the framework effective June 24, 2026.
- Securities listed in Annexure II are moving out of the IBC framework effective June 24, 2026.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework is provided in Annexure III.
- The framework will continue to be reviewed periodically.
- Shortlisting under this framework is for market surveillance purposes only and should not be construed as adverse action against the concerned company.
- For securities exiting the framework, price bands will be reinstated to those applicable before the scrip was placed under IBC surveillance, unless the scrip is subject to another surveillance measure.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice references and operates under the following prior BSE circulars establishing the IBC surveillance framework:
- Exchange notice no. 20180608-34 dated June 08, 2018
- Exchange notice no. 20220520-52 dated May 20, 2022
- Exchange notice no. 20230210-55 dated February 10, 2023
- Exchange notice no. 20250905-47 dated September 05, 2025

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the updated list of securities under IBC surveillance and comply with applicable trading restrictions.
- The IBC framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the exchanges.
- For scrips exiting the framework, the price band reverts to the pre-surveillance level unless another surveillance measure applies.
- Members with clarifications may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 23, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 24, 2026 (for both additions to and removals from the IBC framework)

## Impact Assessment

The circular has a targeted impact on the specific securities listed in the annexures. Securities newly placed under IBC ASM will face tightened trading restrictions and revised price bands, reducing liquidity and limiting price movement. Securities exiting the framework will see price band restoration, potentially increasing trading activity. Market participants holding or trading the affected scrips should review the annexures for the specific securities impacted. The framework is a standard regulatory tool and its periodic update signals normal surveillance activity rather than any systemic market concern.
