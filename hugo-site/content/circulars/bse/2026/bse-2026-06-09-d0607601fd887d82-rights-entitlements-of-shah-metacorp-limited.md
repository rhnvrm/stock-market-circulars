## Summary

BSE has informed trading members that Rights Entitlements (REs) for renunciation of Shah Metacorp Limited will commence trading on the Exchange from Thursday, June 11, 2026. The REs will trade under scrip code 751121 (Scrip ID: SHAH-RE1) and will be settled on a T+1 rolling settlement basis on a trade-for-trade basis.

## Key Points

- Rights Entitlements (REs) for Shah Metacorp Limited will be available for trading from June 11, 2026 to June 19, 2026
- Scrip Code: 751121 | Scrip ID: SHAH-RE1 | ISIN: INE482J20021
- Market Lot: 1 | Group: R
- Settlement on T+1 Rolling Settlement basis, trade-for-trade
- REs are tradable in dematerialized form only
- Rights Issue open period: June 11, 2026 to June 24, 2026
- Trading members must inform clients that REs are not ordinary shares
- REs that are neither subscribed nor renounced by the Issue Closing Date will lapse and be extinguished

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This notice follows the framework established in notice no. 20200305-21 (March 05, 2020) and notice no. 20200518-34 (May 18, 2020) governing Rights Entitlements trading.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must inform all clients about the commencement of trading in REs, specifying the ISIN (INE482J20021)
- Clients must be clearly informed that REs are not ordinary shares of Shah Metacorp Limited
- Contract notes/bills for RE purchases must prominently include a disclaimer stating that purchasing REs only grants the right to participate in the Rights Issue or renounce the REs before the issue closes
- Members must ensure settlement on T+1 trade-for-trade basis
- REs must be dealt in dematerialized form only

## Important Dates

- **June 11, 2026**: Commencement of RE trading; Rights Issue opens
- **June 19, 2026**: Last day of RE trading for renunciation
- **June 24, 2026**: Rights Issue closes; REs not subscribed or renounced by this date will lapse

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate and focused impact, primarily affecting existing shareholders of Shah Metacorp Limited who received Rights Entitlements, as well as trading members facilitating transactions in these REs. The trade-for-trade settlement and T+1 rolling settlement basis reduces counterparty risk. Shareholders must act within the trading window (June 11–19) to either subscribe to the rights issue or renounce their entitlements; failure to do so will result in the REs lapsing after June 24, 2026.
