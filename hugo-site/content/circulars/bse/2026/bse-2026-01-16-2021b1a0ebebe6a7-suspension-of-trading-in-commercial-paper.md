## Summary

BSE has announced the suspension of trading in commercial papers of 10 companies effective from their respective record dates (January 20-21, 2026) due to scheduled redemption. Trading members are advised not to deal in these securities from the specified no-dealings dates.

## Key Points

- 10 commercial papers suspended for redemption purpose
- 4 securities have record date of January 20, 2026
- 6 securities have record date of January 21, 2026
- Trading suspension effective from respective record dates
- Applies only to debt market segment
- Notice references: DR-802/2025-2026 and DR-803/2025-2026

## Affected Securities

**Record Date: January 20, 2026**
- Pilani Investment And Industries Corporation Limited (INE417C14850)
- Standard Chartered Capital Limited (INE403G14TL5)
- ONGC Petro additions Limited (INE163N14550)
- Bajaj Financial Securities Limited (INE01C314EB5)

**Record Date: January 21, 2026**
- Julius Baer Capital (India) Private Limited (INE824H14RH2)
- Vivriti Capital Limited (INE01HV14979)
- Godrej Housing Finance Limited (INE02JD14641)
- Nuvama Wealth and Investment Limited (INE523L14AN6)
- Navi Finserv Limited (INE342T14ES5)
- Bajaj Finance Limited (INE296A14E04)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational notice for scheduled commercial paper redemptions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the specified commercial papers from the no-dealings dates
- Members must ensure clients are informed about the trading suspension
- All positions must be settled before the respective record dates

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: January 16, 2026
- **Record Date & No Dealings From**: January 20, 2026 (4 securities)
- **Record Date & No Dealings From**: January 21, 2026 (6 securities)
- **Purpose**: Redemption of Commercial Paper

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to debt market participants holding or trading these specific commercial papers. The suspension is procedural for redemption settlement and represents normal market operations. Investors holding these securities will receive redemption proceeds as per the terms of the commercial paper. No broader market impact expected as these are routine maturity-driven suspensions.
