## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that further securities issued by 18 companies under Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP) and Employee Stock Option Schemes (ESOS) are listed and admitted for trading effective Friday, June 05, 2026. A total of approximately 4.38 million shares across these companies are now tradeable with no lock-in restrictions.

## Key Points

- 18 companies have had new ESOP/ESOS-issued shares listed on BSE effective June 05, 2026
- None of the newly listed shares carry any lock-in period
- Largest allotments: WeWork India Management Limited (1,387,228 shares), Angel One Limited (1,350,620 shares), and Infosys Limited (895,814 shares)
- Smallest allotment: Quick Heal Technologies Limited (500 shares)
- Total new shares admitted for trading: approximately 4,379,340 shares

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice under existing ESOP/ESOS listing framework.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are requested to take note of the new ISIN ranges and scrip codes for the newly listed shares. No additional compliance action is required from trading members beyond updating their systems with the new distribution numbers.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 04, 2026
- **Effective Date for Trading:** June 05, 2026 (Friday)

## Impact Assessment

Routine dilutive impact across 18 listed companies due to ESOP/ESOS share issuances. The incremental share counts are small relative to existing outstanding shares for large-cap names such as Infosys, AXIS Bank, ICICI Lombard, and ICICI Prudential Life Insurance. Mid- and small-cap companies such as Bondada Engineering, Yash Highvoltage, and Odyssey Technologies see proportionally larger additions. No lock-in on any of these shares means they are immediately available for sale by employee-holders, though the quantities are unlikely to have material market impact.
