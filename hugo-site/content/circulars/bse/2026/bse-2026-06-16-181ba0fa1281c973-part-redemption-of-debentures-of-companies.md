## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260616-13 informs trading members that 11 Non-Convertible Debenture (NCD) series issued by various companies have fixed record dates for part redemption and/or interest payment. Post the respective ex-dates, trading in these debentures will occur at reduced face values as specified.

## Key Points

- 11 NCD series across 8 issuers have fixed record dates falling between 22 June and 30 June 2026
- Trading in these debentures will be conducted at reduced face values effective from the respective ex-dates
- All debentures are privately placed (PVT series)
- Settlement references are under series 656/2026-2027, 659/2026-2027, and 661/2026-2027
- Notice issued by Listing Compliance and Operations department

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is an operational notice implementing existing corporate action procedures for part redemption of privately placed NCDs under BSE's debt segment listing framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised reduced face values for each debenture series from the respective ex-dates
- Members should update their systems to reflect the reduced face values for trading purposes
- Trades executed on or after ex-date must reflect the post-redemption reduced face value

## Impact Assessment

This notice primarily affects institutional investors and trading members holding these privately placed NCD series in the BSE debt segment. The part redemptions reduce outstanding principal (face value), which directly affects mark-to-market valuations and any collateral calculations tied to these instruments. The face value reductions range from Rs. 42,857.16 (Muthoot Capital, Keertana Finserv) to Rs. 93,000 (VR Dakshin series), indicating varying redemption tranches. Issuers span NBFC, infrastructure, and airport sectors. No impact on equity markets is anticipated.
