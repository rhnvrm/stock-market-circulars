## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that Jaiprakash Associates Ltd (Scrip Code 532532) will be delisted from BSE effective Thursday, June 18, 2026. The delisting is pursuant to a Resolution Plan approved by the Hon'ble National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT), Allahabad Bench, vide its order dated March 17, 2026. The company has confirmed compliance with all requirements of the NCLT order.

## Key Points

- Jaiprakash Associates Ltd (BSE Scrip Code: 532532) equity shares will be delisted from BSE.
- Delisting is mandated under an NCLT-approved Resolution Plan (Allahabad Bench, order dated March 17, 2026).
- The company has confirmed compliance with all NCLT delisting requirements.
- Effective date of delisting from exchange records: **Thursday, June 18, 2026**.
- Trading members are advised to take note and inform clients accordingly.

## Regulatory Changes

The delisting is not a voluntary or market-driven action but is ordered under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC) framework via an NCLT resolution plan. Once delisted, the equity shares of Jaiprakash Associates Ltd will no longer be tradeable on BSE.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the delisting effective date and ensure no trades are accepted in this scrip on or after June 18, 2026.
- Members should communicate this development to their clients holding positions or shares of Jaiprakash Associates Ltd (Scrip Code 532532).
- For clarifications, trading members may contact Ms. Vaidehi Phatak at Tel. No. 022–2272 8646.

## Important Dates

- **March 17, 2026**: NCLT Allahabad Bench order approving the Resolution Plan.
- **June 11, 2026**: BSE notice date.
- **June 18, 2026**: Effective date of delisting from BSE exchange records.

## Impact Assessment

This delisting has a high impact on retail and institutional investors holding shares of Jaiprakash Associates Ltd. As the delisting is NCLT-mandated under an insolvency resolution process, shareholders will lose the ability to exit via exchange trading from June 18, 2026. Investors should assess their holdings and explore any exit options available under the Resolution Plan before the effective delisting date. The scrip's removal from BSE records is permanent and not subject to reinstatement under normal relisting procedures.
