## Summary

BSE has issued a circular notifying the allocation of equity shares to anchor investors in the public issue of Accord Transformer and Switchgear Limited. Anchor investor allocations are made one day prior to the IPO opening date and are subject to a mandatory lock-in period as prescribed by SEBI regulations.

## Key Points

- Accord Transformer and Switchgear Limited is conducting a public issue (IPO) of equity shares.
- BSE has published the details of allocation made to anchor investors ahead of the public issue opening.
- Anchor investor allocation is a standard pre-IPO process allowing qualified institutional buyers to subscribe at the IPO price before the issue opens to the public.
- The allocated shares are subject to a 30-day lock-in period post allotment as per SEBI (ICDR) Regulations.
- This circular serves as an official record of anchor book building and allocation for the company's IPO.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is a standard procedural notification under SEBI (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2018, governing anchor investor allocations in public issues.

## Compliance Requirements

- Anchor investors are required to adhere to the 30-day lock-in period on allocated shares.
- The company and its lead managers must ensure disclosure of anchor investor details as mandated by SEBI ICDR Regulations.
- BSE members and market participants should note the anchor allocation details for reference during the IPO subscription period.

## Important Dates

- Anchor Investor Allocation Date: February 20, 2026 (one trading day prior to IPO opening).
- Lock-in expiry for anchor investors: 30 days from the date of allotment in the public issue.
- IPO Opening Date: Expected February 21, 2026 (subject to confirmation in the prospectus).

## Impact Assessment

The anchor investor allocation for Accord Transformer and Switchgear Limited signals institutional interest in the IPO. Successful anchor book building typically indicates confidence among qualified institutional buyers and can positively influence retail and non-institutional investor sentiment during the subscription period. The circular has limited direct impact on existing listed securities but is relevant to investors tracking the new listing pipeline on BSE.
