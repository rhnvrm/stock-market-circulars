## Summary

BSE has implemented restrictions on the BSE StAR MF Platform for three PGIM INDIA Mutual Fund overseas fund-of-fund schemes, effective June 5, 2026. Fresh SIP/XSIP registrations are capped at ₹50,000 per day per investor per scheme, and fresh STP-In registrations have been suspended. This notice references the earlier resumption circular (Notice No. 20260518-8 dated May 18, 2026).

## Key Points

- Fresh SIP/XSIP registrations are accepted up to ₹50,000 per day, per investor, per scheme (at Primary holder PAN level), before cut-off time
- Fresh STP-In registrations are not accepted after the cut-off time on June 4, 2026
- All existing SIP/STP registrations prior to the effective date continue to be processed without change
- Redemption, Switch-out, and STP-out transactions are permitted without any amount restriction
- Restrictions are implemented on the BSE StAR MF Platform as per PGIM AMC's Notice-cum-Addendum

## Regulatory Changes

PGIM INDIA AMC has issued a Notice-cum-Addendum restricting subscription flows into three overseas fund-of-fund schemes, likely due to SEBI-mandated overseas investment limits. The changes restrict new systematic inflows while preserving existing investor positions and exit options.

## Compliance Requirements

- Exchange members must enforce the ₹50,000 per day per investor per scheme cap for fresh SIP/XSIP registrations in the affected schemes
- Members must not accept fresh STP-In registrations for these schemes after June 4, 2026 cut-off
- Members should refer to the attached Notice-cum-Addendum from PGIM INDIA Mutual Fund for full details

## Important Dates

- **June 4, 2026**: Last date for fresh STP-In registrations (after cut-off time, no new STP-In accepted)
- **June 5, 2026**: Effective date for ₹50,000 daily cap on fresh SIP/XSIP registrations

## Impact Assessment

The restrictions apply to three specific PGIM INDIA overseas fund-of-fund schemes:
1. PGIM INDIA Global Equity Opportunities Fund of Fund
2. PGIM INDIA Emerging Markets Equity Fund of Fund
3. PGIM INDIA Global Select Real Estate Securities Fund of Fund

The impact is moderate and contained — only new systematic investment registrations are restricted, while existing investors face no disruption to their ongoing SIP/STP plans. Exit liquidity is fully preserved with unrestricted redemptions and switch-outs. The ₹50,000/day cap aligns with similar restrictions applied by other AMCs to overseas fund-of-funds when SEBI's overseas investment limit is approached.
