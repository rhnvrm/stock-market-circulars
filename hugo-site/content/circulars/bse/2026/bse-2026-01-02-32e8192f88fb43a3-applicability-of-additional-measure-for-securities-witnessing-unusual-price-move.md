## Summary

BSE has placed 12 securities under Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) effective January 5, 2026, due to unusual price movements. One new security, Nutricircle Ltd (INE536C01029), has been added to the surveillance framework. The consolidated list includes securities from various sectors that have exhibited abnormal trading patterns requiring enhanced monitoring and control measures.

## Key Points

- 1 security newly shortlisted: Nutricircle Ltd (Scrip Code: 530219)
- Total 12 securities now under Additional Surveillance Measure
- Measures effective from January 5, 2026
- Enhanced surveillance applied to securities showing unusual price movements
- Consolidated list includes companies across infrastructure, technology, manufacturing, and defense sectors

## Regulatory Changes

BSE has invoked additional surveillance framework under its regulatory powers to monitor and control unusual price movements. Securities placed under ASM typically face:
- Enhanced reporting requirements
- Possible price bands or trading restrictions
- Increased disclosure obligations
- Higher margin requirements for trading
- Additional scrutiny of trading patterns

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must be aware of securities under surveillance
- Enhanced due diligence required for transactions in listed securities
- Compliance with any additional margin or trading limits imposed
- Monitoring of client trading activities in affected securities
- Adherence to disclosure norms for unusual price movements

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: January 5, 2026 - Additional surveillance measures become applicable
- **Circular Date**: January 2, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: High - The placement of 12 securities under additional surveillance indicates significant concern about price manipulation or excessive speculation. This directly affects liquidity and trading volumes in these stocks.

**Investor Impact**: Investors holding or trading these securities will face stricter trading conditions, potentially higher margins, and reduced liquidity. The surveillance tag often leads to negative sentiment and price corrections.

**Operational Impact**: Brokers and trading members need to update their systems, inform clients, and ensure compliance with enhanced surveillance requirements. Additional monitoring and reporting burden on market participants.

**Sectoral Spread**: The affected companies span multiple sectors including real estate (Avishkar, Dugar), technology (Citizen Infoline, Colab Platforms), manufacturing (Mardia, SER Industries), defense (RRP Defense), semiconductors (RRP Semiconductor), and FMCG (Nutricircle), indicating broad-based concerns about unusual trading activity across market segments.
