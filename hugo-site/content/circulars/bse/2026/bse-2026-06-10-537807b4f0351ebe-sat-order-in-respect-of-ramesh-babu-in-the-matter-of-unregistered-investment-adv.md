## Summary

The Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT), vide its order dated May 05, 2026, has set aside the SEBI order dated May 25, 2023 against Ramesh Babu in the matter of unregistered investment advisory activities. The matter has been remitted back to SEBI for fresh disposal in accordance with law. SEBI confirmed this development via email on June 10, 2026.

## Key Points

- SAT order dated May 05, 2026 sets aside the earlier SEBI order (dated May 25, 2023) against Ramesh Babu
- The original action concerned unregistered investment advisory activities
- The matter has been remitted to SEBI for fresh disposal in accordance with law
- This notice continues from Exchange Notice No. 20230526-56 dated May 26, 2023
- SEBI confirmed via email dated June 10, 2026 that the 11B order is set aside by SAT

## Regulatory Changes

The SEBI Section 11B order issued against Ramesh Babu in May 2023 has been set aside by SAT. SEBI must now conduct a fresh disposal of the matter in accordance with applicable law.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are advised to take note of the SAT order and ensure compliance with applicable regulations regarding investment advisory activities.

## Important Dates

- May 26, 2023: Original Exchange Notice No. 20230526-56 issued
- May 25, 2023: Original SEBI order against Ramesh Babu
- May 05, 2026: SAT order setting aside the SEBI order
- June 10, 2026: SEBI email confirmation; BSE notice issued

## Impact Assessment

This circular has limited direct market impact. It is a procedural update informing trading members of a change in the regulatory status of an enforcement action against an individual. The matter remains open as it has been remitted to SEBI for fresh consideration, so the broader enforcement action regarding unregistered investment advisory activities continues.
