## Summary

BSE Limited has issued a notice reminding all registered Investment Advisers (IAs) of the mandatory five-year renewal process for their certificate of registration, as required under Regulation 11 read with Schedule II of the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013. Since BSE is recognized by SEBI under Regulation 14 as the Investment Advisers Administration and Supervisory Body (IAASB), IAs must pay renewal fees to both BSE and SEBI, following a specific sequential process.

## Key Points

- IAs must pay the prescribed renewal fee to SEBI every five years to keep their registration valid.
- Renewal fee payment must be made within the period commencing three months prior to expiry of registration validity.
- BSE, as the recognized IAASB, also requires a separate renewal fee every five years for continuation of registration.
- The SEBI renewal fee challan is generated only after the BSE renewal fee payment is completed and confirmed.
- Renewal requests made after expiry of the registration validity period will not be considered, regardless of reason.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; this is a reiteration of the existing renewal process under Regulation 11 and Schedule II of the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013, and BSE's role under Regulation 14 as IAASB.

## Compliance Requirements

Renewal Fee Payable to SEBI:
- Individuals and Partnership Firms: ₹1,000
- Body Corporates, including LLPs: ₹5,000
(Taxes as applicable)

Renewal Fee Payable to BSE Limited:
- Individuals and Partnership Firms: ₹9,000
- Body Corporates, including LLPs: ₹4,95,000
(Taxes as applicable)

Process:
1. IA remits applicable renewal fee to BSE Limited within the prescribed timeline.
2. Upon confirmation of BSE fee payment, SEBI renewal fee challan is generated and shared with the IA.
3. IA remits the SEBI renewal fee within SEBI's specified timeline and submits proof of payment.

Failure to remit the SEBI renewal fee within the prescribed timeline may lead to non-renewal or cancellation of registration by SEBI.

## Important Dates

Renewal fee payment window: commences three months prior to expiry of registration validity. IAs are advised to initiate the process well in advance of their specific expiry dates; no specific calendar deadline is stated in this notice as it applies to all IAs on a rolling five-year cycle.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has no direct market or listed-security impact but is operationally significant for registered Investment Advisers, who must proactively manage a two-stage fee payment process to avoid lapses or cancellation of their SEBI registration. Delays in BSE fee payment can cascade into delayed SEBI challan generation, risking non-renewal despite the IA's intent to comply.
