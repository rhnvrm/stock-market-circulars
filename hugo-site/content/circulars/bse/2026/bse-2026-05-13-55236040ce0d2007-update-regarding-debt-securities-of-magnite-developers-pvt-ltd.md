## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260513-2 dated May 13, 2026, informing trading members of revised terms for the debt securities of Magnite Developers Pvt Ltd. This notice is a continuation of Exchange Notice 20250220-28 dated February 20, 2025. The revised terms take effect from Wednesday, May 14, 2026.

## Key Points

- Scrip Code **974290** with ISIN **INE0M1Q07015** will be available for trading with revised terms effective May 14, 2026.
- This update is in continuation of BSE Exchange Notice 20250220-28 dated February 20, 2025.
- Revised terms are detailed in the Annexure attached to the notice.
- Trading members are advised to take note of the changes before trading in this security.

## Regulatory Changes

The terms applicable to the debt securities of Magnite Developers Pvt Ltd (Scrip Code: 974290, ISIN: INE0M1Q07015) have been revised. The specific revised terms are contained in the Annexure to the notice. This represents an amendment to previously notified conditions for this debt instrument.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note and comply with the revised terms for Scrip Code 974290 (ISIN: INE0M1Q07015) when executing trades from May 14, 2026 onwards.
- Members should review the Annexure for the complete details of the revised terms.
- For clarifications, members may contact the designated BSE officials from the DCS-Listing department.

## Important Dates

- **May 13, 2026** — Notice issued by BSE.
- **May 14, 2026 (Wednesday)** — Effective date for revised terms on Scrip Code 974290.
- **February 20, 2025** — Date of original Exchange Notice 20250220-28 that this update continues from.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a targeted impact on trading members who deal in the debt securities of Magnite Developers Pvt Ltd. The revision of terms for a single scrip (974290) means affected parties — primarily institutional investors or members holding or trading this specific bond — must update their records and trading parameters accordingly. The change is operational in nature and does not signal a broad market-wide regulatory shift. Members should consult the Annexure for precise financial or structural changes to the instrument.
