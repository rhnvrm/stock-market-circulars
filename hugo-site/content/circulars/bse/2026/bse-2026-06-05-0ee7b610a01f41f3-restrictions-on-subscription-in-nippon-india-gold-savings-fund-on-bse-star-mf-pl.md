## Summary

BSE has informed members of subscription restrictions on Nippon India Gold Savings Fund implemented on the BSE StAR MF Platform, effective June 8, 2026, as per an addendum issued by Nippon India Mutual Fund. Lump sum and switch-in purchases are capped at ₹10 lakh per PAN per month, while fresh SIP/XSIP and AMC STP-In registrations are limited to ₹50,000 per PAN per day.

## Key Points

- Fresh/additional lump sum purchases and switch-in transactions are restricted to ₹10,00,000 per PAN per month
- Fresh systematic registrations (SIP/XSIP and AMC STP-In) are permitted up to ₹50,000 per PAN per day across all frequencies
- Existing SIPs, XSIPs, and AMC STPs already registered on BSE StAR MF Platform will continue to be processed at the RTA/AMC end
- Redemption, switch-out, and STP-out transactions remain unrestricted
- Subscription orders above ₹10,00,000 triggered to RTA on or after 3:00 PM on June 5, 2026 will be rejected and refunded once funds are received from AMC/RTA

## Regulatory Changes

Nippon India Mutual Fund has issued an addendum introducing investment caps on the Nippon India Gold Savings Fund. BSE has implemented these changes on the BSE StAR MF Platform in accordance with the AMC's directive. The restrictions apply to both lump sum purchases and new systematic investment registrations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and distributors must ensure that lump sum/switch-in orders for Nippon India Gold Savings Fund do not exceed ₹10,00,000 per PAN per month
- New SIP/XSIP/AMC STP-In registrations must not exceed ₹50,000 per PAN per day
- Subscription orders exceeding the limit submitted after 3:00 PM on June 5, 2026 will be auto-rejected by the system
- Members should communicate the restrictions to investors placing orders in this scheme

## Important Dates

- **June 5, 2026**: Circular notice date; orders above ₹10,00,000 triggered to RTA at or after 3:00 PM will be rejected
- **June 8, 2026**: Effective date for all subscription restrictions on Nippon India Gold Savings Fund

## Impact Assessment

This restriction primarily affects investors seeking large lump sum exposure to gold through the Nippon India Gold Savings Fund via the BSE StAR MF Platform. Retail investors with SIP amounts within ₹50,000 per day and lump sum investments within ₹10 lakh per month are unaffected. The move is consistent with AMC-level portfolio management to manage inflows into gold-linked funds. Existing systematic investors are fully protected as their registrations will continue without interruption. Redemption and switch-out transactions are unrestricted, ensuring liquidity for existing investors.
