## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Currency Derivatives segment on Saturday, July 04, 2026, enabling trading members to test their trading applications, including a new BOLT Pro TWS version 13.11, ahead of its release. Members using third-party or in-house trading platforms via IML/ETI APIs are encouraged to use this session to validate system functionality under various market conditions.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Saturday, July 04, 2026, for Currency Derivatives and Cross Currency Derivatives segments
- Members may participate in the mock session or use the UAT environment to fulfil regulatory testing requirements per SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 dated November 24, 2020
- New BOLT Pro TWS version 13.11 will be released; details to follow via a separate circular
- Trades from mock sessions carry no margin, pay-in/pay-out obligations, and create no rights or liabilities
- Members are requested to submit feedback on the mock session by 4:30 PM on the session day

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an operational/testing notice referencing existing SEBI guidance on software testing (SEBI circular dated November 24, 2020) and BSE's prior notice (20201201-22 dated December 1, 2020).

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members using third-party empaneled vendor platforms or in-house systems via IML/ETI APIs should test their applications during the mock session. Members are requested to provide feedback via the contact details provided (BSE Tech Support and Help Desk) by 4:30 PM on the mock trading day.

## Important Dates

- Mock Trading Date: Saturday, July 04, 2026
- Log-in: 10:15 AM – 11:00 AM
- Continuous Trading (Currency Derivatives): 11:00 AM – 03:30 PM
- Continuous Trading (Cross Currency Derivatives): 11:00 AM – 03:30 PM
- Trade Modification (Currency): 03:40 PM
- Trade Modification (Cross Currency): 03:40 PM
- Feedback submission deadline: 4:30 PM on session day

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, low-impact operational notice affecting only trading members' system testing activities. It has no direct effect on listed securities, stock prices, or investor obligations, as mock session trades do not create margin, settlement, or legal obligations.
