## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Currency Derivatives segment on Saturday, June 06, 2026. The session is intended for members to test their trading applications, including third-party platforms and in-house systems using IML/ETI APIs, and to fulfil regulatory testing requirements per SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 dated November 24, 2020.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session covers both Currency Derivatives and Cross Currency Derivatives.
- Members may participate in the mock session or use the UAT environment to satisfy regulatory testing obligations.
- No new BOLT TWS release will accompany this session.
- Trades from mock trading carry no margin obligations, pay-in/pay-out obligations, or any rights and liabilities.
- Members are encouraged to submit feedback by 4:30 PM on the day of the session.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This notice references the existing requirement under SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 (November 24, 2020) and Exchange Notice No. 20201201-22 (December 1, 2020) regarding testing of software used in or related to Trading and Risk Management.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must fulfil their regulatory testing requirements either by participating in this mock trading session or by testing in the UAT environment.
- Members using third-party empanelled vendor platforms or in-house systems via IML/ETI APIs should use this session to test various functionalities, including exceptional market conditions.

## Important Dates

- **Mock Trading Date:** Saturday, June 06, 2026
- **Log-in Window:** 10:15 AM – 11:00 AM
- **Continuous Trading – Currency Derivatives:** 11:00 AM – 03:30 PM
- **Continuous Trading – Cross Currency Derivatives:** 11:00 AM – 03:30 PM
- **Trade Modification (Currency):** 03:40 PM
- **Trade Modification (Cross Currency):** 03:40 PM
- **Feedback Submission Deadline:** 4:30 PM on June 06, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice with minimal market impact. Participation is voluntary for testing purposes and results in no real financial obligations. Trading members using third-party or in-house systems should plan participation to meet their SEBI-mandated system testing obligations.
