## Summary

BSE has issued a notice under its Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework informing trading members that certain securities, listed in the attached Annexure, will move into their respective higher GSM stages effective July 23, 2026. Securities in these stages will be subject to escalating surveillance actions including higher margins, additional surveillance deposits (ASD), trade-for-trade settlement, and restricted trading frequency.

## Key Points

- Securities specified in Annexure_GSM will move to their respective higher GSM stages effective July 23, 2026.
- The GSM framework has four stages (I-IV), each imposing progressively stricter surveillance actions.
- Stage I: 100% margin and price band capped at 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, and 50% ASD on trade value payable by buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, trading permitted only once a week (Monday or first trading day of the week), and 100% ASD payable by buyers.
- Stage IV: Same restrictions as Stage III, but with no upward price movement permitted.
- All settlements for GSM securities are done on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.
- This action references prior BSE notices dated February 23, 2017, March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing GSM Framework (published February 23, 2017) to move specific securities into higher surveillance stages based on ongoing monitoring criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin rates, price bands, ASD requirements, and trading frequency restrictions to the listed securities from the effective date.
- Members must ensure trade-to-trade settlement is followed for securities in Stage II, III, and IV with no netting permitted.
- Members should refer to the GSM FAQ or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 22, 2026
- Effective Date: Securities move to higher GSM stages from July 23, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects only the specific securities listed in the Annexure (not detailed in the extracted content), which will face reduced liquidity, higher capital requirements (via ASD and margins), and restricted trading windows once moved to higher GSM stages. This is a routine, recurring regulatory surveillance action rather than a market-wide event, but it can materially affect trading and liquidity in the specific stocks involved.
