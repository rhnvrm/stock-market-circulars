## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260622-30 announcing that securities listed in the attached Annexure will be moved to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 23, 2026. The move is pursuant to the GSM Framework published on February 23, 2017 and subsequent exchange notices. Trading members are urged to exercise adequate caution when dealing in these securities.

## Key Points

- Securities in the Annexure will be elevated to higher GSM stages from June 23, 2026.
- Settlement for affected securities will be done on a **trade-to-trade basis** with no netting off allowed.
- Stage-specific surveillance restrictions apply, ranging from enhanced margin requirements to weekly trading restrictions.
- Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50%–100% of trade value is required from buyers depending on the GSM stage.
- Members may refer to the GSM FAQ or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications.

## Regulatory Changes

The GSM framework defines four surveillance stages with progressively stringent restrictions:

| Stage | Surveillance Actions |
|-------|----------------------|
| I | Applicable margin rate shall be 100%; price band of 5% or lower as applicable |
| II | Trade for trade; price band of 5% or lower; Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value to be deposited by buyers |
| III | Trade for trade; price band of 5% or lower; trading permitted once a week (every Monday / 1st trading day of the week); ASD of 100% of trade value to be deposited by buyers |
| IV | Trade for trade; price band of 5% or lower; trading permitted once a week (every Monday / 1st trading day of the week); ASD of 100% of trade value to be deposited by buyers; **no upward movement** |

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised GSM stage designations for securities listed in the Annexure.
- Buyers in Stage II–IV securities must deposit the applicable ASD (50% or 100% of trade value) prior to or at the time of trading.
- No netting off is permitted; all settlements must be conducted on a trade-to-trade basis.
- Members dealing in Stage III or IV securities must restrict client trades to once per week (Monday or first trading day of the week).
- Members are advised to take adequate precautions and communicate the restrictions to their clients.

## Important Dates

- **June 22, 2026**: Notice issued.
- **June 23, 2026**: Effective date — securities moved to their respective higher GSM stages.

## Impact Assessment

The elevation of securities to higher GSM stages materially reduces their liquidity and tradability. Stage I imposes heavy margin requirements (100%), while Stages II–IV further restrict trading through mandatory trade-for-trade settlement, significant ASD requirements (50%–100%), and in the most severe cases (Stages III–IV) limit trading to once per week with no upward price movement. Investors holding these securities may face difficulty exiting positions, and the mandatory ASD increases the capital cost of acquiring them. Trading members should proactively inform clients of these constraints to avoid settlement defaults or margin shortfalls.
