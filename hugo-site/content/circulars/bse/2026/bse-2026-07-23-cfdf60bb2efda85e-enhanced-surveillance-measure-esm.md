## Summary

BSE has issued an update to its Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, referencing a series of prior notices dating back to June 2023. Securities newly qualifying under ESM criteria (Annexure I) will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open and new positions effective July 27, 2026, and will be shifted from the Rolling Settlement segment to the Trade-for-Trade segment. Some existing ESM securities will move to a lower stage (Stage I), while others will move to Stage II, which carries a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction, effective July 24, 2026. A separate list of securities exiting the ESM framework entirely is provided in Annexure II, with a consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework in Annexure III.

## Key Points

- Securities newly added to ESM (per Annexure I) will face minimum 100% margin on all open positions as of July 24, 2026, and new positions from July 27, 2026.
- Some securities remaining in the ESM framework will move down to Stage I effective July 24, 2026.
- Securities shifting to Stage II will be placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction, effective July 24, 2026.
- Securities qualifying under ESM will be moved from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment effective July 27, 2026.
- Securities exiting the ESM framework (Annexure II) will have their price bands reinstated to pre-ESM levels, unless subject to another surveillance measure.
- The ESM framework operates alongside other prevailing surveillance measures and is not to be viewed as an adverse judgment on the company.
- Securities under the framework are reviewed periodically.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice continues the ESM framework established via prior BSE notices (June 2023 through July 2025), updating the classification of securities across ESM stages based on periodic review criteria. No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a routine update to the existing surveillance mechanism.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply minimum 100% margin on ESM-qualifying securities as per Annexure I from the specified dates.
- Members must ensure affected securities are traded only in the Trade-for-Trade segment, not Rolling Settlement, once shifted.
- Members should monitor price band and Periodic Call Auction requirements for securities moving to Stage II.
- Queries should be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 24, 2026: Stage movements (to Stage I and Stage II) take effect; 100% margin applies to open positions as of this date for newly qualifying securities; securities exiting ESM per Annexure II take effect.
- July 27, 2026: New positions in newly qualifying ESM securities attract 100% margin; affected securities shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment.

## Impact Assessment

The 100% margin requirement and shift to Trade-for-Trade segment significantly reduce trading flexibility and liquidity for the affected securities, likely dampening speculative trading activity. Securities moving to Stage II face additional constraints via a tighter 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism, further limiting price discovery and intraday volatility. Conversely, securities exiting the framework regain normal price band treatment, potentially restoring trading activity. Since the specific security names are contained in the referenced annexures (not included in the notice body), market participants should consult the full attachment for the precise list of impacted stocks.
