## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure) will be moved to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 1, 2026. The move imposes progressively stricter trading restrictions depending on the GSM stage assigned to each security.

## Key Points

- Securities will be shifted to higher GSM stages (I through IV) effective June 1, 2026
- All affected securities will be settled on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed
- Applicable surveillance actions escalate with each stage, ranging from higher margin requirements at Stage I to weekly trading restrictions with 100% ASD at Stage IV
- Trading members are advised to exercise adequate precautions when dealing in these securities
- Specific list of affected securities is provided in the attached Annexure (not reproduced in notice body)

## Regulatory Changes

Securities moved into GSM stages will be subject to the following surveillance actions per the GSM Framework published February 23, 2017:

| Stage | Surveillance Actions |
|-------|----------------------|
| I | 100% applicable margin rate; price band of 5% or lower |
| II | Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band; Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers |
| III | Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band; trading permitted once a week (every Monday/first trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers |
| IV | Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band; trading permitted once a week (every Monday/first trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers; no upward price movement permitted |

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must comply with the applicable margin rates and ASD requirements for each GSM stage
- Settlement for all affected securities must be done on a trade-to-trade basis; netting off is not permitted
- Members should refer to the GSM FAQ at http://www.bseindia.com/markets/equity/EQReports/graded_surveil_measure.aspx?expandable=6 for clarifications
- Queries may be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** May 29, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 1, 2026 — securities moved to their respective higher GSM stages

## Impact Assessment

Affected securities will face significantly reduced liquidity and tighter trading conditions. Buyers of Stage II–IV securities must arrange additional funds for surveillance deposits (50%–100% of trade value), raising the cost of participation. Stage III and IV securities are further constrained to once-a-week trading, severely limiting price discovery and exit opportunities. Stage IV securities additionally cannot move upward in price, further restricting investor activity. Market participants holding or considering positions in GSM-listed securities should assess exposure carefully before the June 1, 2026 effective date.
