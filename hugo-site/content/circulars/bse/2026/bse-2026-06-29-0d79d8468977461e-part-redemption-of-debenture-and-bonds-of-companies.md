## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260629-5 informs trading members that several companies have fixed record dates for part redemption (and/or interest payment) of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) and Bonds. Trading in these debentures and bonds will take place at reduced face values effective from the respective ex-dates.

## Key Points

- Multiple companies across Debt segment have fixed record dates for part redemption of NCDs/Bonds.
- Trading in the listed debentures/bonds will occur at reduced face values from their respective ex-dates.
- Record dates fall across three clusters: 02 July 2026, 07 July 2026, and 08 July 2026.
- All ex-dates are the same as record dates (T+0 ex-date structure).
- Settlements are under DR 663/2026-2027, 666/2026-2027, and 667/2026-2027.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is a standard corporate action notification under BSE Listing Operations for the Debt segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note and update systems to reflect reduced face values for the listed scrips from their respective ex-dates.
- Trades executed on or after the ex-date must use the reduced face value as specified.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt-segment corporate action. NCD/bond holders of the listed scrips will receive part redemption proceeds on or around the record dates. The reduced face values reflect partial principal repayment; market pricing of these instruments will adjust accordingly from the ex-date. Trading members operating in the debt segment must update their systems to reflect the new face values to avoid pricing errors. Impact is limited to holders of the specific private-placement NCD series listed.
