## Summary

BSE has issued a notice referencing its earlier GSM framework circulars (dated February 23, 2017, March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023), informing trading members that securities listed in the attached Annexure will move to their respective higher GSM (Graded Surveillance Measure) stages effective July 27, 2026. These securities will be subject to stage-specific surveillance actions as per the GSM Framework.

## Key Points

- Securities specified in the Annexure_GSM attachment will move to higher GSM stages effective July 27, 2026.
- The GSM framework has four stages (I to IV) with progressively stricter surveillance actions.
- Stage I: 100% margin applicable and price band of 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement, price band of 5% or lower, and Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade settlement, price band of 5% or lower, trading permitted only once a week (every Monday or the first trading day of the week), and ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers.
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III but with no upward price movement permitted.
- Settlement for all affected securities will be done strictly on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.
- Members are directed to refer to the GSM FAQ page or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice implements the existing GSM Framework (published February 23, 2017) by moving specific securities identified in the Annexure to higher surveillance stages based on ongoing monitoring criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin rates, price bands, trade-for-trade settlement, and ASD requirements corresponding to each security's new GSM stage.
- Members must ensure no netting off is applied to settlements of affected securities.
- Members should verify the specific stage assignment for each security via the Annexure before executing trades.

## Important Dates

- Effective date of GSM stage movement: July 27, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

Securities moving to higher GSM stages will face reduced liquidity due to trade-for-trade settlement, restricted price bands, and higher margin/ASD requirements. Stage III and IV securities face severe trading restrictions, including weekly-only trading windows, which significantly limits investor exit options and price discovery. Market participants holding or trading in the listed securities should reassess risk exposure and factor in reduced liquidity and higher capital requirements for continued trading.
