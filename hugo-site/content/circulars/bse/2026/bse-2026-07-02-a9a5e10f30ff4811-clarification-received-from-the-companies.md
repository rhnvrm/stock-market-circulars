## Summary

BSE has observed significant price/volume movement in 18 listed scrips in recent trading sessions. As part of its surveillance process, the Exchange wrote to these companies seeking clarification on the reasons behind the movement. This notice communicates that 10 companies have submitted replies (attached to the notice), while 8 companies have yet to respond.

## Key Points

- Companies that have submitted clarification replies:
  1. Centrum Capital Ltd (Scrip Code: 501150)
  2. Grand Oak Canyons Distillery Ltd (Scrip Code: 523862)
  3. IITL Projects Ltd (Scrip Code: 531968)
  4. Jtekt India Ltd (Scrip Code: 520057)
  5. Munjal Auto Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 520059)
  6. Schneider Electric President Systems Ltd (Scrip Code: 544786)
  7. Shah Foods Ltd (Scrip Code: 519031)
  8. Shri Niwas Leasing And Finance Ltd (Scrip Code: 538897)
  9. Sonal Adhesives Ltd (Scrip Code: 526901)
  10. Tasty Bite Eatables Ltd (Scrip Code: 519091)
- Companies whose replies are still awaited:
  1. CMX Holdings Ltd (Scrip Code: 532217)
  2. Dhanvantri Jeevan Rekha Ltd (Scrip Code: 531043)
  3. Elango Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 513452)
  4. Esha Media Research Ltd (Scrip Code: 531259)
  5. Marble City India Ltd (Scrip Code: 531281)
  6. Mayur Leather Products Ltd (Scrip Code: 531680)
  7. SC Agrotech Ltd (Scrip Code: 526081)
  8. Sujala Trading & Holdings Ltd (Scrip Code: 539117)
- Actual reply content is contained in attachments (Replies1, Replies2), not reproduced in the notice text.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is a routine surveillance communication under BSE's ongoing monitoring of unusual price/volume activity, not a new regulatory change.

## Compliance Requirements

- The 8 companies with pending replies are expected to respond to the Exchange's clarification request.
- Trading members and investors should review the attached replies for material information relevant to trading decisions in these scrips.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 02 Jul 2026
- No specific deadline stated for pending replies.

## Impact Assessment

Low direct market impact at the aggregate level, though individual scrip-level impact could be moderate if replies reveal material undisclosed information. This is a standard surveillance disclosure mechanism intended to keep the market informed; it does not itself impose trading restrictions, though continued unusual movement without clarification could lead to further surveillance action (e.g., ASM/GSM placement) for the companies yet to reply.
