## Summary

BSE has announced the compulsory delisting of Stefanos Finance Ltd (Scrip Code: 512215) effective June 01, 2026. The company has remained suspended for more than 6 months, triggering compulsory delisting under the SEBI (Delisting of Equity Shares) Regulations, 2021, pursuant to an order of the Delisting Committee of the Exchange.

## Key Points

- Stefanos Finance Ltd (Scrip Code: 512215) will be compulsorily delisted from BSE effective June 01, 2026.
- The delisting is due to the company remaining suspended for more than 6 months.
- Action is taken pursuant to the Delisting Committee of the Exchange under SEBI (Delisting of Equity Shares) Regulations, 2021.
- Promoters must purchase shares from public shareholders at fair value determined by an independent valuer appointed by the Exchange.
- A Public Notice with the fair value and exit process will be issued shortly.
- The company will be moved to the Dissemination Board of the Exchange post-delisting.

## Regulatory Changes

The delisting is governed by SEBI (Delisting of Equity Shares) Regulations, 2021. Key regulatory provisions applied:

- **Regulation 34(1):** The delisted company, its whole-time directors, persons responsible for compliance, promoters, and companies promoted by any of them are barred from accessing the securities market, seeking listing of equity shares, or acting as an intermediary for **10 years** from the date of delisting.
- **Regulation 34(2):** Where the fair value of the delisted company is positive, transfer (sale, pledge, etc.) of promoter/promoter group equity shares is frozen, and corporate benefits (dividend, rights, bonus, split) are frozen until promoters provide a compliant exit option to public shareholders.
- **Regulation 33(4):** Promoters must provide a public exit option to shareholders in compliance with this sub-regulation.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Promoters:** Must purchase shares from public shareholders at the fair value determined by the Exchange-appointed independent valuer. Transfer and pledge of promoter holdings are frozen until the exit option is provided.
- **Promoters, whole-time directors, and compliance officers:** Ineligible to become directors of any listed company until the exit option is provided to public shareholders.
- **Company and depositories:** Must not effect any transfer (sale, pledge, etc.) of promoter/promoter group equity shares, and must freeze corporate benefits for those shares until the exit condition is met.
- **Queries:** Direct any questions to bse.delistscn@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **May 27, 2026:** Circular/notice issued.
- **June 01, 2026:** Effective date of compulsory delisting from BSE.
- **10 years from June 01, 2026:** End of regulatory bar for promoters/directors from accessing the securities market.

## Impact Assessment

**For Public Shareholders:** Shares of Stefanos Finance Ltd will no longer be tradeable on BSE from June 01, 2026. However, public shareholders retain the right to sell their shares to promoters at the fair value determined by an independent valuer. The Public Notice with specific details of the exit process will be issued shortly.

**For Promoters/Directors:** Subject to a 10-year ban from the securities market and from serving as directors of listed companies until public shareholders are provided a compliant exit. Promoter shareholdings are frozen (no transfers, pledges, or corporate benefits) until exit obligations are met.

**Market Impact:** This is a single small-cap company (Stefanos Finance Ltd) with limited broader market impact. The delisting reflects ongoing enforcement of SEBI's framework for companies that remain suspended without remediation.
