## Summary

BSE has flagged significant price and/or volume movement in 12 listed scrips in recent trading sessions. As part of its surveillance process, the Exchange wrote to these companies seeking clarification and has published the replies received. Six companies have responded, while replies from the remaining six are still awaited.

## Key Points

- Companies that have replied to BSE's clarification request: Aegis Vopak Terminals Ltd (544407), Mangalam Cement Ltd (502157), Rupa & Company Ltd (533552), Ambar Protein Industries Ltd (519471), Marble City India Ltd (531281), Fine Line Circuits Ltd (517264).
- Companies whose replies are still awaited: CMX Holdings Ltd (532217), Elango Industries Ltd (513452), Mayur Leather Products Ltd (531680), SC Agrotech Ltd (526081), Sujala Trading & Holdings Ltd (539117), Tuticorin Alkali Chemicals and Fertilizers Ltd (506808).
- The clarifications relate to unusual price/volume activity observed by the Exchange's surveillance department, intended to keep trading members and investors informed with latest relevant information.
- Replies received from the companies are attached to the notice as annexures.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is a routine surveillance disclosure notice, not a regulatory rule change.

## Compliance Requirements

The six companies with pending replies are expected to respond to BSE's query regarding the price/volume movement in their scrips. Investors and trading members should refer to the attached replies for company-specific clarifications.

## Important Dates

Notice issued and dated 06 July 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This notice serves an informational/transparency purpose for the market, alerting investors to recent unusual price/volume activity in the named scrips and disclosing company responses (or lack thereof). It may influence trading sentiment in these specific stocks, particularly those yet to respond, but has no broader market-wide impact.
