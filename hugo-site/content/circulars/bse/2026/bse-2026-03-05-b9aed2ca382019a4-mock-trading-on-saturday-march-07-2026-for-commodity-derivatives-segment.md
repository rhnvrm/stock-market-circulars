## Summary

BSE has announced a mock trading session for the Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, March 07, 2026. The session is intended for members to test their trading applications, third-party platforms, and in-house systems via IML/ETI APIs. Participation also satisfies regulatory testing requirements under SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 dated November 24, 2020.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Saturday, March 07, 2026 for the Commodity Derivatives segment
- Members may participate in the Mock Trading Session or the UAT environment to fulfill SEBI regulatory testing requirements
- Trading members using third-party empaneled vendor platforms or in-house systems via IML/ETI APIs can test functionalities including risk-reduction mode and trading halt
- No new BOLT TWS release will be issued for this session
- Trades from mock trading will not create margin obligations, pay-in/pay-out obligations, or any rights and liabilities
- Members must submit feedback by 4:30 PM on the day of the session

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This notice references existing obligations under SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 (November 24, 2020) and Exchange Notice No. 20201201-22 (December 1, 2020) regarding testing of software used in or related to Trading and Risk Management.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are encouraged to actively participate in the mock trading session
- Members using third-party or in-house trading platforms should use the session to test their systems for various functionalities including exceptional market conditions
- Feedback must be submitted to BSE by 4:30 PM on March 07, 2026 via the contact details provided

## Important Dates

- **Mock Trading Date:** Saturday, March 07, 2026
- **Log-in Session:** 10:15 AM – 11:00 AM
- **Continuous Trading Session:** 11:00 AM – 03:30 PM
- **Feedback Deadline:** 4:30 PM on March 07, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact. This is a routine operational exercise with no financial obligations arising from executed trades. It primarily affects trading members in the Commodity Derivatives segment who need to validate their systems.
