## Summary

BSE has issued a notice requiring trading members to note that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure) will be moved to their respective higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 04, 2026. The move follows the GSM Framework established via Exchange notice no. 20170223-44 dated February 23, 2017 and subsequent amendments. Affected securities will attract enhanced surveillance actions depending on their assigned GSM stage.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in the attached Annexure will be elevated to higher GSM stages from June 04, 2026
- Four GSM stages exist, each with progressively stricter surveillance actions
- Stage I imposes 100% margin rate and a price band of 5% or lower
- Stage II adds trade-for-trade settlement and an Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers
- Stages III and IV require trade-for-trade settlement, trading permitted only once a week (every Monday or the first trading day of the week), and ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers
- Stage IV additionally prohibits any upward price movement
- No netting off is permitted; all settlements are on a trade-to-trade basis
- Trading members are urged to exercise adequate precautions when trading in these securities

## Regulatory Changes

The circular is issued under the GSM Framework originally published on February 23, 2017 (Exchange notice no. 20170223-44) and subsequently updated via notices dated March 03, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023. The current notice operationalises a periodic review resulting in stage escalation for a subset of monitored securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure compliance with the applicable margin rates (100% for Stage I and above) for the affected securities
- Buyers in Stage II, III, and IV securities must deposit the required Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) — 50% for Stage II and 100% for Stages III and IV — at the time of trade
- Members must ensure no netting off is allowed and that all settlements are conducted on a strict trade-to-trade basis
- Members are advised to refer to the GSM FAQ at http://www.bseindia.com/markets/equity/EQReports/graded_surveil_measure.aspx?expandable=6 or write to bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 03, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 04, 2026 (securities moved to higher GSM stages)
- **Weekly trading window (Stages III & IV):** Every Monday or the first trading day of the week

## Impact Assessment

The escalation of securities to higher GSM stages significantly curtails liquidity and raises the cost of trading for the affected scrips. The imposition of 100% margins at Stage I and trade-for-trade settlement from Stage II onward effectively deters speculative activity. The requirement for buyers to deposit ASD of up to 100% of trade value at Stages III and IV, combined with weekly trading restrictions and the prohibition on upward price movement at Stage IV, can substantially reduce market participation. Investors and traders holding or intending to trade in the listed securities should review the Annexure carefully and assess exposure accordingly before June 04, 2026.
