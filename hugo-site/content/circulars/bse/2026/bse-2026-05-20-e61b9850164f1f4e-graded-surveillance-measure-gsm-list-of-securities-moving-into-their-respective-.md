## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260520-25) informing trading members that certain securities listed in the attached Annexure will be moved to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective May 21, 2026. The GSM framework imposes progressively stringent surveillance actions on securities at each stage to protect investors from abnormal price movements.

## Key Points

- Securities per the attached Annexure (GSM Annexure) will be moved to their respective higher GSM stages effective **May 21, 2026**
- GSM has four stages (I through IV), each with progressively stricter surveillance actions
- Stage I: 100% margin rate with price band of 5% or lower
- Stage II: Trade-to-trade settlement, 5% price band, and 50% ASD on trade value by buyers
- Stage III: Trade-to-trade, 5% price band, weekly trading (every Monday/1st trading day), and 100% ASD by buyers
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III with no upward price movement permitted
- No netting off is allowed; settlement will be on trade-to-trade basis
- Trading members are advised to take adequate precautions while trading in affected securities

## Regulatory Changes

The GSM framework was originally published on February 23, 2017, and has been updated through subsequent notices:
- Exchange notice no. 20170223-44 (February 23, 2017) — original GSM framework
- Exchange notice no. 20170303-29 (March 03, 2017)
- Exchange notice no. 20180720-55 (July 20, 2018)
- Exchange notice no. 20191129-23 (November 29, 2019)
- Exchange notice no. 20231117-63 (November 17, 2023)

This notice implements stage upgrades for a new set of securities under the existing GSM framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members**: Must note and comply with the GSM stage restrictions for all securities listed in the GSM Annexure
- **Buyers**: Must deposit Additional Surveillance Deposits (ASD) as applicable — 50% of trade value at Stage II, 100% of trade value at Stages III and IV
- **Settlement**: All affected securities must be settled on a trade-to-trade basis; no netting off permitted
- **Trading Restrictions**: At Stages III and IV, trading in affected securities is permitted only once a week (every Monday or the first trading day of the week)
- **Price Band**: A 5% or lower price band applies across all GSM stages
- Members may refer to the GSM FAQ at http://www.bseindia.com/markets/equity/EQReports/graded_surveil_measure.aspx?expandable=6 or write to bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: May 20, 2026
- **Effective Date**: May 21, 2026 — securities moved to higher GSM stages

## Impact Assessment

Securities moved to higher GSM stages face severe trading restrictions that significantly reduce their liquidity and attractiveness to investors. The mandatory trade-to-trade settlement eliminates intraday trading benefits, while the 100% margin requirement at Stage I and ASD requirements at Stages II–IV substantially increase the cost of trading. At Stages III and IV, weekly trading restrictions further limit price discovery and market participation. Investors holding positions in GSM-listed securities should be aware that exits may be difficult and costly. The no-upward-movement restriction at Stage IV is particularly severe, potentially trapping investors in declining positions.
