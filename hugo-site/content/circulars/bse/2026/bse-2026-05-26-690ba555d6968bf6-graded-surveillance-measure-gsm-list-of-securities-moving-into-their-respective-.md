## Summary

BSE has issued a notice moving certain securities (listed in Annexure_GSM) into their respective higher Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) stages with effect from May 27, 2026. The move is in continuation of the GSM Framework first published on February 23, 2017 and subsequent amendments. Affected securities will be subject to enhanced surveillance actions depending on the stage they are placed in.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in the attached annexure will be migrated to higher GSM stages effective May 27, 2026.
- GSM stages range from I to IV, with progressively stricter trading restrictions at each stage.
- Stage I imposes 100% margin and a price band of 5% or lower.
- Stage II adds trade-for-trade settlement and a 50% Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) by buyers.
- Stage III further restricts trading to once a week (every Monday or first trading day) with 100% ASD by buyers.
- Stage IV is the same as Stage III but with no upward price movement permitted.
- No netting off is allowed; all settlements will be done on a trade-to-trade basis.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This notice is an operational application of the existing GSM Framework (Exchange notice no. 20170223-44 dated February 23, 2017) and subsequent amendments dated March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must take note of the securities listed in Annexure_GSM and apply the corresponding surveillance actions from May 27, 2026.
- Buyers of Stage II, III, and IV securities must deposit the prescribed Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) at the time of trade.
- Members are advised to take adequate precautions while trading in these securities.
- For clarifications, members may refer to the BSE GSM FAQ page or write to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** May 26, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 27, 2026 — securities migrate to higher GSM stages from this date.

## Impact Assessment

The migration to higher GSM stages imposes meaningful trading restrictions on affected securities, limiting liquidity and increasing costs for buyers through mandatory ASD requirements. Stage III and IV securities are particularly impacted as trading is restricted to once a week with no netting, significantly reducing market participation. Retail and institutional investors holding or trading these securities should reassess their positions given reduced exit liquidity. The circular is a standard periodic surveillance action and does not signal a systemic market concern.
