## Summary

BSE has notified Trading Members that the daily price bands applicable to 30 specified scrips have been revised from their existing levels, effective July 06, 2026. The revised bands range from as tight as 2% to as wide as 20%, reflecting BSE's periodic recalibration of price limits, often linked to surveillance considerations for volatile or thinly traded securities.

## Key Points

- 30 scrips have had their price band percentage revised.
- Revised bands include: 2% (4 scrips), 5% (19 scrips), 10% (4 scrips), and 20% (3 scrips).
- Notable names include OnMobile Global Ltd (10%), Fairchem Organics Ltd (5%), Schneider Electric President Systems Ltd (5%), and Kirloskar Electric Company Ltd.
- Many of the affected scrips are small-cap or surveillance-flagged companies where tighter/wider bands are used to manage volatility.

## Regulatory Changes

BSE has exercised its authority under exchange trading rules to reset the applicable price band (circuit filter) for the listed scrips. This is a routine surveillance-linked adjustment and not a change in overarching regulation.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading Members must update their trading systems/order management to reflect the new price bands for the listed scrips before the effective date. No filings or disclosures are required from the listed companies themselves.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 03, 2026
- Effective Date: July 06, 2026 (new price bands apply from this date)

## Impact Assessment

The revision affects intraday and daily price movement limits for the 30 named scrips, which can influence trading strategies, order placement, and liquidity for these stocks, particularly for those moving to tighter bands (2%) which restrict volatility, or wider bands (20%) which allow greater price swings. Impact is contained to the specific scrips listed and does not affect the broader market.
