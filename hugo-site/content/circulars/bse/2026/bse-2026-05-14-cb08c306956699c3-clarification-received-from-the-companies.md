## Summary

BSE's Surveillance department (DOSS) issued this notice following observation of significant price and/or volume movement in 10 listed companies. To ensure trading members and investors have the latest relevant information, BSE wrote to these companies seeking clarifications. Replies have been received from 4 companies and are attached to this circular, while 6 companies have yet to respond.

## Key Points

- BSE Surveillance identified unusual price/volume activity across 10 scrips
- Companies were contacted to provide relevant disclosures to the market
- 4 companies have submitted their clarifications (attached as replies)
- 6 companies' replies are still awaited as of 14 May 2026
- Notice issued under BSE Notice No. 20260514-23

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is an enforcement/surveillance action under existing BSE monitoring frameworks for unusual market activity.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Companies that have replied:** Adhata Global Ltd (531286), Milgrey Finance & Investments Ltd (511018), Naksh Precious Metals Ltd (539402), Nimbus Projects Ltd (511714) — clarifications attached to circular.
- **Companies with reply awaited:** Anuroop Packaging Ltd (542865), GK Consultants Ltd (531758), Ken Financial Services Ltd (530547), Ritco Logistics Ltd (542383), Semac Construction Ltd (505368), ZR2 Bioenergy Ltd (506640) — these companies are expected to submit their responses to BSE Surveillance promptly.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** 14 May 2026
- No specific deadline mentioned for pending replies; follow-up action by BSE Surveillance is implied if responses are not received.

## Impact Assessment

Investors and trading members holding or tracking the 10 listed scrips should note the unusual price/volume activity flagged by BSE. For the 4 companies that have responded, the attached clarifications provide relevant disclosures. For the remaining 6 companies with pending replies, continued price/volume volatility may persist until clarifications are made public. This is a standard surveillance measure and does not by itself impose trading restrictions, but may precede further action such as ASM/GSM applicability if irregularities are confirmed.
