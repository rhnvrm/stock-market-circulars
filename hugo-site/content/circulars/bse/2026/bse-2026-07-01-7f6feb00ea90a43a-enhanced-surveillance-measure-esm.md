## Summary

BSE has issued an update to its Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, referencing a series of prior notices dating back to June 2023. New securities meeting ESM criteria will be subject to a minimum 100% margin requirement, some existing ESM securities will be downgraded to a lower stage, others will be escalated to Stage II with additional trading restrictions, and certain securities will exit the framework entirely. Full lists of affected securities are provided in Annexures I, II, and III attached to the notice.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I have met the criteria for inclusion under ESM and will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open positions as of July 02, 2026 and new positions created from July 03, 2026.
- Securities continuing under the ESM framework but moving to a lower stage (Stage I) are also listed in Annexure I, effective July 02, 2026.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the ESM framework is provided in Annexure III.
- Securities qualifying under ESM will shift from the Rolling Settlement segment to the Trade-for-Trade segment effective July 03, 2026.
- Securities moving to Stage II will trade under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction, effective July 02, 2026.
- Securities exiting the ESM framework effective July 02, 2026 are listed in Annexure II; their price bands will revert to pre-ESM levels unless subject to another surveillance measure.
- ESM operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchange.
- Inclusion under ESM is characterized as a market surveillance action and not an adverse judgment on the company or entity.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies periodic revisions to the existing ESM framework established via prior BSE notices (June 2023 through July 2025), updating stage classifications and the list of covered securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply 100% margin on newly included ESM securities for open positions as of July 02, 2026 and new positions from July 03, 2026.
- Members must ensure affected securities are processed under Trade-for-Trade settlement rather than Rolling Settlement from July 03, 2026.
- Members must apply the 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism for securities moved to Stage II from July 02, 2026.
- Queries regarding the framework may be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 02, 2026: Stage reclassifications take effect (Stage I downgrades, Stage II escalations with 2% price band/Periodic Call Auction); securities exiting ESM take effect; open positions as of this date are subject to new margin rules.
- July 03, 2026: 100% margin applies to new positions in newly included ESM securities; affected securities shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment.

## Impact Assessment

This notice materially affects liquidity and trading conditions for the specific securities named in the annexures. Stocks newly added to ESM face a 100% margin requirement and a shift to Trade-for-Trade settlement, significantly curbing speculative trading and intraday activity. Securities escalating to Stage II face additional constraints via a tighter 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism, further reducing trading flexibility. Conversely, securities exiting the framework should see restored trading normalcy and reinstated price bands, potentially improving liquidity. Since the specific tickers are contained only in the PDF annexures and not in the extracted text, investors and members should consult the source document directly to identify affected stocks.
