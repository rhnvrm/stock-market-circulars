## Summary

BSE has announced the non-competitive bidding facility for RBI's State Government Securities (SDL) auction scheduled on June 23, 2026. Trading members can participate through the NCB-GSec module on BSE's iBBS web-based platform. A total of 19 SDL securities from 8 states are available, comprising both new issues and re-issues.

## Key Points

- Bidding platform: https://ibbs.bseindia.com >> NCB-GSec module
- 19 SDL securities available across Bihar (BR), Chhattisgarh (CG), Delhi (DL), Gujarat (GJ), Himachal Pradesh (HP), Kerala (KL), Madhya Pradesh (MP), Tamil Nadu (TN), and Uttar Pradesh (UP)
- Mix of new issues (8 securities) and re-issues (11 securities)
- Minimum subscription size: Rs. 1,200 units (Rs. 100 face value each) for most securities
- Minimum amount per bid: Rs. 100; minimum bid amount varies by security
- Maximum amount ranges from Rs. 3 Cr to Rs. 18 Cr depending on the security
- Refers to Exchange circular no. 20191122-28 dated November 22, 2019 for the original facility launch

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This notice operates under the existing non-competitive bidding framework established by Exchange circular no. 20191122-28 (November 22, 2019), in response to RBI's press release dated June 19, 2026 announcing the SDL auction.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use the NCB-GSec module on BSE's iBBS platform (https://ibbs.bseindia.com) to place bids
- Direct investors must submit bids by June 22, 2026 at 5:00 PM
- Members must submit bids by June 23, 2026 at 8:00 AM
- For new user ID creation or password reset: contact user.trdops@bseindia.com or call 022-2272 8005/8215
- For bidding queries: contact trading.ops@bseindia.com or call 022-2272 8376/5626/8226

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice for debt market participants. The auction covers SDL securities from 9 states with a combined notional issuance across new and re-issue tranches. Impact is limited to debt segment trading members and direct investors wishing to participate in the SDL non-competitive bidding window. No equity markets or listed company stocks are affected.
