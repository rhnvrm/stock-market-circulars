## Summary

BSE has informed its members that ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund has revised the minimum application amount for 10 of its schemes. Additionally, the minimum SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) amount for Daily and Monthly frequency has been revised for 3 of those schemes. All changes are effective June 15, 2026.

## Key Points

- Minimum and Additional Application Amount revised for 10 ICICI Prudential schemes
- Minimum SIP amount (Daily & Monthly frequency) revised for 3 schemes
- Effective date: June 15, 2026
- Communication received by BSE from ICICI Prudential AMC; Annexure and Notice-cum-Addendum attached to the circular

## Regulatory Changes

The following schemes have revised Minimum & Additional Application Amounts:

1. ICICI Prudential Short Term Fund
2. ICICI Prudential Arbitrage Fund
3. ICICI Prudential Business Cycle Fund
4. ICICI Prudential FlexiCap Fund
5. ICICI Prudential Large & MidCap Fund
6. ICICI Prudential Income Plus Arbitrage Omni FoF
7. ICICI Prudential Value Fund
8. ICICI Prudential Focused Equity Fund
9. ICICI Prudential Active Momentum Fund
10. ICICI Prudential Multi-Asset Fund

The following schemes have revised Minimum SIP Application Amounts (Daily & Monthly frequency):

1. ICICI Prudential Short Term Fund
2. ICICI Prudential Arbitrage Fund
3. ICICI Prudential Income Plus Arbitrage Omni FoF

## Compliance Requirements

Members and distributors operating on the BSE STAR MF platform should note the revised minimum application and SIP amounts for the affected ICICI Prudential schemes. New subscriptions and SIP registrations from June 15, 2026 must comply with the updated minimums. Refer to the attached Annexure and Notice-cum-Addendum from ICICI AMC for specific revised amounts.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** June 15, 2026 — revised minimum application amounts and SIP amounts apply to all new transactions from this date.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative update by ICICI Prudential AMC. The impact is limited to investors making new lump-sum subscriptions or registering new SIPs in the listed schemes on or after June 15, 2026. Existing SIPs and investments are not affected. Distributors and platform operators on BSE STAR MF should update their systems to reflect the new minimums to avoid transaction rejections.
