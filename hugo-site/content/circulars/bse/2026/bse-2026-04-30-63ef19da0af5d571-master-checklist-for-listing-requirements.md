## Summary

BSE's DCS-Listing department has issued a Master Checklist for Listing Requirements (Notice No. 20260430-51, dated 30 April 2026). This circular provides a consolidated checklist of all listing-related compliance obligations applicable to companies listed or seeking listing on BSE. An annexure containing the detailed checklist has been attached to the notice.

## Key Points

- Issued by BSE's DCS-Listing department under the General segment for company-related matters.
- Consolidates listing requirements into a single master checklist for ease of reference.
- Accompanied by an annexure (AnnexuretoMasterChecklistCircular) containing the detailed compliance checklist.
- Applicable to all companies with listing obligations on BSE.
- Serves as a reference document to ensure adherence to all listing-related regulatory requirements.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular consolidates existing listing requirements into a master checklist format. It does not appear to introduce new regulatory changes but rather provides a comprehensive, updated reference document for companies to comply with BSE's listing norms.

## Compliance Requirements

- Listed companies and companies seeking listing must review and adhere to the requirements outlined in the Master Checklist.
- Companies should use the attached annexure as a compliance reference guide for fulfilling listing obligations.
- Applicable entities under the General segment must ensure all checklist items are satisfied in accordance with BSE's DCS-Listing department guidelines.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** 30 April 2026
- Effective immediately upon issuance unless otherwise specified in the annexure.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on listed companies and companies pursuing listing on BSE. It serves as a comprehensive reference tool that simplifies compliance tracking by consolidating listing requirements. Companies should review the annexure carefully to ensure no obligations are overlooked. There is no immediate market-moving impact, but non-compliance with checklist items could result in regulatory action from the exchange.
