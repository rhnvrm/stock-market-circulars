## Summary

BSE's Surveillance department (DOSS) has observed significant price and volume movements in 23 listed companies. The Exchange wrote to these companies seeking clarification to ensure trading members and investors have access to the latest relevant information. As of April 22, 2026, replies have been received from 10 companies, while 13 companies are yet to respond.

## Key Points

- BSE Surveillance identified abnormal price/volume activity in 23 scrips and sought clarifications from the respective companies.
- 10 companies have submitted their replies, which are attached to this notice.
- 13 companies have not yet responded; replies are still awaited.
- This is a standard surveillance mechanism to protect investor interests by ensuring timely disclosure.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This notice is part of BSE's ongoing surveillance framework under which listed companies are required to provide timely clarifications on unusual market activity in their securities.

## Impact Assessment

Investors and trading members holding or trading in these 23 scrips should review the attached clarifications from the 10 responding companies before making investment decisions. The 13 non-responding companies remain under surveillance scrutiny, and continued non-response may lead to further regulatory action such as enhanced surveillance measures (ASM/GSM) or trading restrictions. The circular underscores BSE's proactive role in maintaining market integrity by ensuring price-sensitive information is disclosed in a timely manner.
