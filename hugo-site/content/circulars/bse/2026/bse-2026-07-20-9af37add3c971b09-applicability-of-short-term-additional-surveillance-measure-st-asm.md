## Summary

BSE has issued a notice identifying securities that meet the criteria for inclusion under the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM), Stage I and Stage II, as per the framework established under earlier exchange notices (dated September 20, 2024, October 27, 2018, July 19, 2019, December 4, 2020, September 25, 2023, and September 20, 2024). The specific list of securities is provided in the attached Annexure I.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I have been placed under ST-ASM Stage I or Stage II based on surveillance criteria.
- ST-ASM Stage I securities: applicable margin shall be 50% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- ST-ASM Stage II securities: applicable margin shall be 100% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- Margins apply to all open positions as on July 21, 2026, and new positions created from July 22, 2026 onward.
- The ASM framework operates in conjunction with other surveillance measures already in effect.
- Inclusion under ASM is purely a surveillance action and should not be construed as an adverse action against the concerned company or entity.
- Price bands of scrips moving out of the ASM framework will revert to the previously applicable price band, unless the scrip remains under another surveillance framework.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework has been introduced; this notice applies the existing ST-ASM framework (per prior circulars) to newly identified securities.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must apply the specified higher margin rates (50% for Stage I, 100% for Stage II) on all open and new positions in the identified securities from the effective date. Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- Margin requirements effective: July 22, 2026
- Applicable to open positions as on: July 21, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The increased margin requirements will raise the cost of trading and may reduce speculative activity and liquidity in the affected securities. Investors and traders holding or trading these stocks should account for higher capital requirements. The measure is a routine surveillance action rather than an indicator of fundamental issues with the companies involved.
