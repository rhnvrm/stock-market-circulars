## Summary

BSE has issued its periodic update on the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework, referencing prior notices dating back to March 2018. The notice details securities being newly added to LT-ASM, securities moving to higher surveillance stages, securities being transferred to the LT-ASM framework under a separate 2021 provision (with a shift to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups), and securities exiting the framework entirely. A consolidated list of all securities currently under LT-ASM is provided as Annexure III.

## Key Points

- Securities newly placed under LT-ASM (Annexure I, Part A) will face 100% margin requirements on all open positions as of July 17, 2026, and on new positions created from July 20, 2026 onwards.
- Securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages (Annexure I, Part B) take effect July 16, 2026, along with a lower price band effective the same date.
- Securities covered under the June 2021 LT-ASM provision (Annexure I, Part D) will continue in the LT-ASM framework but be transferred to the T/XT/MT/TS trading group effective July 20, 2026.
- Securities exiting the LT-ASM framework (Annexure II) are effective July 16, 2026, with their price bands reinstated to pre-shortlisting levels, unless subject to another surveillance measure.
- Shortlisting under this framework is based on XBRL submissions by listed companies and is purely a surveillance measure, not an adverse action against any company.
- The framework operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchange.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a periodic application/review of the existing LT-ASM framework established via earlier notices (20180321-46, 20181027-1, 20201204-56, 20240809-46, 20240920-63, and 20210604-41).

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply 100% margin on relevant open and new positions per the effective dates.
- Members and market participants should refer to the specific annexures (I Part A, B, D; II; III) for the exact list of affected securities.
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 16, 2026: Lower price band applicable; securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages take effect; securities exiting LT-ASM framework take effect.
- July 17, 2026: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin.
- July 20, 2026: 100% margin applicable on open and new positions; securities transferred to T/XT/MT/TS trading group.

## Impact Assessment

The measures increase trading costs and reduce liquidity flexibility for securities newly added to or escalated within the LT-ASM framework, primarily through 100% margin requirements and tighter price bands. Securities exiting the framework see reduced restrictions, with price bands reverting to normal levels. Since the affected securities are identified only in the annexures (not included in this notice text), the specific stock-level impact cannot be determined from the notice alone.
