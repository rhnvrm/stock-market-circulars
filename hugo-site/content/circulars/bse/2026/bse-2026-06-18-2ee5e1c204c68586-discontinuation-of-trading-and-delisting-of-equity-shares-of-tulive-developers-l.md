## Summary

BSE has issued a notice informing trading members that Tulive Developers Ltd (Scrip Code: 505285) will undergo voluntary delisting pursuant to SEBI (Delisting of Equity Shares) Regulations, 2021. Trading in the equity shares will be discontinued effective June 25, 2026, and the scrip will be formally delisted from BSE records effective July 3, 2026.

## Key Points

- Voluntary delisting of Tulive Developers Ltd (Scrip Code: 505285) approved under SEBI (Delisting of Equity Shares) Regulations, 2021
- Trading discontinuation effective Thursday, June 25, 2026
- Formal delisting from BSE exchange records effective Friday, July 3, 2026
- Exit option available to remaining public shareholders for one year from the date of delisting
- Exit price fixed at Rs. 750/- (Rupees Seven Hundred and Fifty only) per equity share
- Acquirers/promoters providing exit option: Altis Properties Private Limited and GKS Technology Park Private Limited

## Regulatory Changes

The delisting is carried out in compliance with SEBI (Delisting of Equity Shares) Regulations, 2021 as amended. The company has completed all required formalities for voluntary delisting.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must cease trading in Tulive Developers Ltd (Scrip Code: 505285) from June 25, 2026 onwards
- Remaining public shareholders wishing to exercise the exit option must submit option forms to the company at the address below within one year from the date of delisting
- **Shareholder contact address:** Tulive Developers Ltd, 21/22, Loha Bhavan, P D Mello Road, Mumbai, Maharashtra - 400009

## Impact Assessment

Public shareholders of Tulive Developers Ltd have a limited window before trading is suspended on June 25, 2026. After delisting on July 3, 2026, shares will no longer be tradeable on BSE. However, remaining public shareholders retain the right to tender their shares to the acquirers — Altis Properties Private Limited and GKS Technology Park Private Limited — at the exit price of Rs. 750/- per share for up to one year post-delisting. Shareholders should act promptly and contact the company or their broker for guidance on the exit option process.
