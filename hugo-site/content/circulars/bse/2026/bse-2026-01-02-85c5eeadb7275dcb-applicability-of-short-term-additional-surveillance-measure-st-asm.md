## Summary

BSE has updated the list of securities under the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective January 5, 2026. A total of 10 securities are being newly included in the ST-ASM framework, while 7 securities are moving out. No securities are being moved to higher or lower stage ASM within the short-term framework. The ST-ASM framework is designed to monitor securities witnessing unusual price movements or volatility.

## Key Points

- 10 securities newly shortlisted in Short Term 5/15/30 Days ASM Framework
- 7 securities moving out of ST-ASM Framework
- Changes effective from January 5, 2026
- Includes both main board and SME scrips
- No securities moving to higher or lower stages within ST-ASM
- Consolidated list provided showing all securities currently under ST-ASM with their respective stages

## Regulatory Framework

The Short Term ASM framework operates on a 5/15/30 days basis to identify and monitor securities with:
- Unusual price movements
- High volatility
- Abnormal trading patterns

Securities under ST-ASM are subject to:
- Enhanced surveillance
- Additional margin requirements
- Potential trading restrictions based on stage

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** January 2, 2026
- **Effective Date:** January 5, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**For Investors and Traders:**
- Securities in ST-ASM framework face higher margin requirements
- May experience reduced liquidity due to trading restrictions
- Investors should exercise caution when trading these securities
- Position limits may apply depending on ASM stage

**Market Impact:**
- Medium impact - affects specific securities rather than broad market
- Regular surveillance update as part of ongoing risk management
- Protects market integrity by monitoring volatile securities

**Compliance Impact:**
- Trading members must ensure compliance with ASM framework requirements
- Enhanced due diligence needed for client trades in these securities
- Risk management systems should be updated to reflect new ASM list
