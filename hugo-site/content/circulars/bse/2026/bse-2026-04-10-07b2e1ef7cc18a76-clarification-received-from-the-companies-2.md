## Summary

BSE's Department of Surveillance (DOSS) issued Notice No. 20260410-41 on April 10, 2026, informing trading members and investors that clarifications have been received from 48 listed companies. These companies were contacted by BSE after significant price and/or volume movements were observed in their respective scrips. The replies (Replies11 through Replies20) are attached to the notice.

## Key Points

- BSE observed significant price/volume movements in 48 scrips and sought clarifications from the respective companies.
- All 48 companies have responded; their replies (Replies11–Replies20) are attached to the original notice.
- The notice covers companies across diverse sectors including cement, agro, logistics, healthcare, fintech, aerospace, energy, and more.
- This is the second batch of clarifications (Batch 2) issued on April 10, 2026, as part of ongoing surveillance activity.
- Notice issued jointly by Sanjay Jain (Deputy Vice President, Surveillance) and Shreya Ghose (Manager, Surveillance).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this notice. It is a disclosure/surveillance communication under BSE's existing framework for monitoring unusual price and volume movements in listed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- No new compliance obligations are imposed on companies or investors by this notice.
- The listed companies have already fulfilled their immediate obligation by submitting clarifications to BSE.
- Trading members and investors are advised to review the attached company replies before making investment decisions in the affected scrips.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** April 10, 2026
- **Notice Number:** 20260410-41

## Impact Assessment

This notice is informational and reflects BSE's active surveillance of unusual market activity. The impact is moderate — investors and trading members in any of the 48 listed scrips should review the attached company clarifications to understand the reasons behind the observed price/volume movements. No trading restrictions or penalties are announced. The breadth of 48 companies signals heightened surveillance activity across the equity segment on this date.
