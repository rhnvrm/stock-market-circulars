## Summary

BSE has issued a notice referencing its earlier GSM framework circulars (dated February 23, 2017, March 03, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023) informing trading members that securities listed in the attached Annexure will move to their respective higher Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) stages with effect from July 06, 2026. These securities will be subject to the surveillance actions applicable to their new GSM stage.

## Key Points

- Securities specified in the Annexure_GSM attachment will move into higher GSM stages effective July 06, 2026.
- GSM Stage I: 100% margin applicable and price band capped at 5% or lower.
- GSM Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement with 5% or lower price band, plus Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers.
- GSM Stage III: Trade-for-trade settlement with 5% or lower price band, trading permitted only once a week (Monday or first trading day of the week), and ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers.
- GSM Stage IV: Same restrictions as Stage III, with the additional condition of no upward price movement permitted.
- All settlements for these securities will be done on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.
- Trading members are advised to exercise adequate precaution while trading in the affected securities.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing GSM Framework (published February 23, 2017) by moving specific securities to higher surveillance stages as per periodic review.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin, price band, trade-for-trade settlement, and ASD requirements corresponding to each security's new GSM stage.
- Members must ensure additional surveillance deposits are collected from buyers as specified before executing trades in the affected securities.
- Queries regarding GSM classification should be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com or the published FAQ.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 06, 2026 — securities move to their respective higher GSM stages.

## Impact Assessment

The move to higher GSM stages significantly restricts trading liquidity for the affected securities through tighter price bands, mandatory trade-for-trade settlement, higher margin/ASD requirements, and in some cases weekly trading windows. This is intended to curb speculative trading and protect investors from potential price manipulation, but it will materially reduce trading volumes and increase transaction costs for market participants holding or trading these securities.
