## Summary

BSE, in coordination with other exchanges, has reviewed securities placed under the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) Framework and identified certain securities eligible to move to lower GSM stages effective July 13, 2026. The list of eligible securities is provided in an annexure to the notice.

## Key Points

- This action follows the GSM Framework introduced via notice no. 20170223-44 dated February 23, 2017, and updated via notice no. 20191129-23 dated November 29, 2019.
- Periodic review of GSM stages is conducted jointly across exchanges.
- Eligible securities will move to lower (less restrictive) GSM stages.
- The specific list of securities is contained in the referenced Annexure (Annexure_GSM_Lower), not reproduced in the notice text.
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com or via the GSM FAQ link provided.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a periodic stage revision under the existing GSM Framework, reducing surveillance stringency for specific eligible securities.

## Compliance Requirements

Members and market participants should refer to the Annexure to identify affected securities and adjust trading/compliance processes according to the revised (lower) GSM stage requirements applicable to those securities.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 10, 2026
- Effective Date of Lower GSM Stage Revision: July 13, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Securities moving to lower GSM stages typically see reduced trading restrictions (e.g., relaxed price bands, reduced margin requirements, or removal from trade-for-trade settlement), which can improve liquidity and investor sentiment for the specific stocks involved. The overall market impact is limited to the affected securities listed in the annexure rather than the broader market.
