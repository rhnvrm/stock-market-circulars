## Summary

BSE has admitted five new Commercial Papers (CPs) to trading on its Debt Market Segment effective June 23, 2026. These securities were issued on a private placement basis by KEC International Ltd., DSP Finance Private Limited, UGRO Capital Limited, HT Media Ltd., and REC Limited. Trading is permitted only in dematerialised form in standard denominations of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof, with a tick size of 1 paise.

## Key Points

- Five commercial papers listed across five issuers effective June 23, 2026
- All securities trade in demat form only with standard lot size of Rs. 5 Lakhs
- Tick size: 1 paise
- Redemption dates range from September 18, 2026 to September 21, 2026
- Interest rates range from 6.58% p.a. (REC Limited) to 9.00% p.a. (UGRO Capital)
- All issuances carry top-tier short-term credit ratings (A1+ from CRISIL, ICRA, CARE, or IND)
- Contact: BSE Debt Department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine admission notice under existing BSE Debt Segment listing framework for privately placed commercial papers.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form
- Standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof must be observed
- Trading members requiring clarification should contact the BSE Debt Department

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice of limited market impact. The listed commercial papers are short-term debt instruments maturing within approximately 90 days and are targeted at institutional investors. REC Limited's issuance is the largest at 49,000 units (face value Rs. 2,450 Cr), while UGRO Capital has the smallest issuance at 500 units. The top-tier A1+ ratings across all issuers indicate low credit risk. Retail investors and equity market participants are not directly affected.
