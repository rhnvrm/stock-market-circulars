## Summary

BSE has announced group reclassification for 17 exclusively listed companies as part of the X/XT segment review, effective June 15, 2026. The changes include companies moving from X to B group (upgrade), B to X group (downgrade), and T to XT group.

## Key Points

- 17 companies are undergoing group changes effective June 15, 2026
- 3 companies are upgraded from X group to B group (less restrictive)
- 13 companies are downgraded from B group to X group (more restrictive)
- 1 company moves from T group to XT group
- Changes are part of the periodic X/XT sub-segment review under Notice No. 20171129-17

## Regulatory Changes

Classifications are governed by Notice No. 20171129-17 dated November 29, 2017. Criteria for exclusion from the X group include:

- Six-month average market capitalisation exceeding Rs. 1,000 Cr and shareholders exceeding 5,000
- Constituents of S&P BSE Sensex, S&P BSE 100, S&P BSE 200, or S&P BSE 500 indices
- Six-month average market capitalisation exceeding Rs. 1,000 Cr and net worth exceeding Rs. 150 Cr
- Net worth exceeding Rs. 150 Cr and FII holding exceeding 20%
- Securities listed on SME/SME-ITP segment
- Securities in P and Z group (remain in respective groups)

Securities not meeting any exclusion criterion are classified under X/XT sub-segment.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must update their systems and communicate the group reclassifications to clients holding positions in the affected scrips. No additional filing is required from the listed companies themselves.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 9, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 15, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Companies moved to the X group face more restrictive trading conditions typically associated with lower liquidity and higher surveillance. The 13 companies downgraded from B to X may see reduced investor interest and trading volumes. The 3 companies upgraded from X to B gain improved market access and liquidity. Eco Recycling Limited's move from T to XT keeps it in the trade-for-trade framework but under the X sub-segment's stricter oversight.
