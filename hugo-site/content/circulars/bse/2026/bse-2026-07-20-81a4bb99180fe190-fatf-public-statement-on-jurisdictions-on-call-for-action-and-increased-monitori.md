## Summary

BSE has informed members of a communication received from SEBI regarding the Financial Action Task Force's (FATF) public statements issued after its June 2026 Plenary. These statements identify jurisdictions with strategic Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Combating the Financing of Terrorism (CFT) deficiencies, categorized into 'High Risk Jurisdictions Subject to a Call for Action' and 'Jurisdictions Under Increased Monitoring'.

## Key Points

- SEBI forwarded FATF's post-June 2026 Plenary public statements to BSE for dissemination to members.
- FATF maintains two categories of jurisdictions: those subject to a call for action (high-risk) and those under increased monitoring.
- Links to both FATF public statements are provided on the FATF website (fatf-gafi.org).
- Following this review, FATF has newly identified Bosnia and Herzegovina, Iraq, and Virgin Islands (UK) as jurisdictions now under increased monitoring.
- Members are advised to take note of these updates for necessary action and compliance.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is an update to FATF's existing jurisdiction monitoring lists, which member entities are expected to reference in their AML/CFT compliance and client due diligence processes.

## Compliance Requirements

Members are required to:

- Note the updated FATF lists of high-risk and increased-monitoring jurisdictions.
- Incorporate awareness of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Iraq, and Virgin Islands (UK) as newly added jurisdictions under increased monitoring into their AML/CFT risk assessments and client onboarding/due diligence procedures.
- Refer to the official FATF website links for the complete and authoritative list of jurisdictions.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 20 July 2026
- Applicable Plenary: June 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal direct market or trading impact and does not pertain to any specific listed security. It is primarily a compliance and risk-management advisory requiring member firms to update their AML/CFT due diligence frameworks in line with FATF's latest jurisdictional risk classifications. No immediate action is required beyond internal compliance updates.
