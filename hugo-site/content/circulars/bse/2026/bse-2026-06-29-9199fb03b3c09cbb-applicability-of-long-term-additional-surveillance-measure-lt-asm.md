## Summary

BSE has issued a notice regarding the applicability of the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework, referencing the original framework notice from March 2018 and subsequent revisions. The circular mandates 100% margins on all open and new positions in LT-ASM-listed securities starting July 2, 2026, and introduces lower price bands and group transfers effective June 30, 2026.

## Key Points

- 100% margins shall be applicable from July 2, 2026 on all open positions as on July 1, 2026 and new positions created thereafter (Annexure I, Part A)
- Securities moved to higher stages within the LT-ASM framework effective June 30, 2026 are listed in Annexure I (Part B)
- Lower Price Band is applicable with effect from June 30, 2026
- Certain securities will continue under LT-ASM but be transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group with effect from July 2, 2026 (Annexure I, Part D)
- Securities exiting the LT-ASM framework effective June 30, 2026 are listed in Annexure II
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the LT-ASM framework is provided in Annexure III
- Shortlisting under this framework is purely for market surveillance purposes and should not be construed as adverse action against the company
- Shortlisting is based on XBRL submissions by the respective listed companies

## Regulatory Changes

The LT-ASM framework has been revised per earlier notices including:
- Exchange notice no. 20181027-1 dated October 27, 2018
- Exchange notice no. 20201204-56 dated December 4, 2020
- Exchange notice no. 20240809-46 dated August 9, 2024
- Exchange notice no. 20240920-63 dated September 20, 2024

Securities exiting the LT-ASM framework will have their price band reinstated to the level applicable before their inclusion, subject to no other surveillance measure placing a different restriction.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must ensure 100% margins are collected on open positions in LT-ASM securities as of July 1, 2026 and on all new positions from July 2, 2026 onwards
- Brokers and trading members must note group changes (to T/XT/MT/TS) for affected securities effective July 2, 2026
- Price band changes effective June 30, 2026 must be adhered to for securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages
- The LT-ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures
- For clarifications, members may contact bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **June 30, 2026**: Lower price band applicable; securities moved to higher LT-ASM stages; securities exiting LT-ASM framework (Annexure II)
- **July 1, 2026**: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin requirement
- **July 2, 2026**: 100% margins effective on open positions and all new positions; securities transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a high market impact as it imposes 100% margin requirements on affected securities, significantly increasing the capital requirement for market participants holding or trading these scrips. The price band restrictions further limit price discovery. Securities being moved to the T/XT/MT/TS group face trade-to-trade settlement obligations, reducing liquidity. Conversely, securities exiting the framework will see margin relaxation and price band restoration, which may improve trading activity in those scrips. The framework applies broadly across equity segment securities identified through XBRL data analysis.
