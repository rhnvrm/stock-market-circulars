## Summary

BSE has announced shut periods for Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) mechanism due to dividend corporate actions in 14 securities. The shut periods range from February 4-5, 2026 to February 12-13, 2026, coinciding with respective ex-dates and book closure/record dates. During these periods, SLB activities will be restricted for the affected securities.

## Key Points

- 14 securities affected by SLB shut periods due to dividend declarations
- Shut periods range from 1-3 days depending on book closure dates
- All corporate actions are dividend payments
- Ex-dates match the book closure/record dates for all securities
- No foreclosure dates specified for any of the securities
- Major PSU and private sector companies included in the list

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational announcement for SLB market participants.

## Compliance Requirements

- SLB market participants must not initiate new lending or borrowing transactions in the affected securities during shut periods
- Existing SLB positions should be monitored for expiry dates falling within shut periods
- Market participants should adjust their SLB strategies accounting for these temporary restrictions

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - The shut periods temporarily restrict SLB activities across 14 securities, which may affect liquidity and hedging strategies for institutional investors and traders who rely on securities lending/borrowing mechanisms.

**Operational Impact:** Market participants engaged in SLB activities need to plan their transactions around these shut periods. The concentration of multiple shut periods in early February (particularly Feb 4-6) may create temporary liquidity constraints in the SLB market.

**Sectoral Coverage:** Diverse sectors affected including power (NTPC, Power Grid), metals (National Aluminium, MOIL), FMCG (Nestle, P&G), finance (Cholamandalam), oil & gas (GAIL), defense (Bharat Dynamics), and healthcare (Dr. Lal Pathlabs), indicating broad market impact.
