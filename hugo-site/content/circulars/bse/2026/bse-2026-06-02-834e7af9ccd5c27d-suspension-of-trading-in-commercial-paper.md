## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260602-22 instructs trading members to cease dealing in the commercial papers of 15 companies that have fixed record dates for the purpose of commercial paper redemption. The suspensions are effective from the respective ex-dates — 04 June, 05 June, and 08 June 2026.

## Key Points

- Trading members must not deal in the listed commercial papers from their respective ex-dates onward.
- 15 commercial paper instruments across 14 companies are covered under this notice.
- All instruments are in the Debt segment under the Listing Operations department.
- Settlement references are DR 644/2026-2027, DR 645/2026-2027, and DR 646/2026-2027.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This is a standard operational notice under BSE's Listing Operations department enforcing trading suspensions ahead of commercial paper redemptions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members of BSE must immediately stop dealing in the commercial papers listed below from their respective ex-dates.
- Members should update their systems to reflect the suspension of these instruments.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a moderate operational impact on debt segment trading members. The suspension of 15 commercial paper instruments ahead of their redemption is routine practice, ensuring orderly wind-down of these short-term debt instruments. Companies affected include a mix of NBFCs, financial services firms, and large corporates such as Titan Company and Balrampur Chini Mills. No equity trading is affected.
