## Summary

BSE has released its periodic update to the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework for companies undergoing Insolvency Resolution Process (IRP) under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). Effective March 10, 2026, no new securities are being added to any IBC surveillance stage, while 7 securities are being excluded from ASM entirely.

## Key Points

- No securities are being newly added to Stage 0 (corporate announcement/disclosure stage) w.e.f. March 10, 2026
- No securities are being moved into Stage 1 of IBC-related ASM w.e.f. March 10, 2026
- No securities are moving from Stage I to Stage II or from Stage II to Stage I
- 7 securities are being fully excluded from IBC-related ASM w.e.f. March 10, 2026
- The consolidated list of securities currently under IBC surveillance remains active with companies at various stages

## Regulatory Changes

The IBC ASM framework categorizes securities into stages based on their insolvency resolution process status:
- **Stage 0**: Receipt of corporate announcement/disclosure from the company
- **Stage 1**: Active Additional Surveillance Measure under IBC
- **Stage 2**: Elevated surveillance under IBC

This update reflects a net reduction in IBC-monitored securities with 7 exclusions and zero new additions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members should note the exclusion of 7 securities from IBC ASM restrictions effective March 10, 2026
- Securities remaining on the consolidated list (Annexure III) continue to be subject to their respective IBC surveillance stage requirements
- Annotations apply: (*) denotes NSE-sourced data; (^) denotes Recommencement Scrips

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: March 10, 2026 — All changes (exclusions and stage movements) take effect
