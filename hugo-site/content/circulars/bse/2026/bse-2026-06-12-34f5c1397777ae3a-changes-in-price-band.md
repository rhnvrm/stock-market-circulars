## Summary

BSE has revised price bands for 27 scrips effective June 15, 2026. The revised bands range from 2% to 20%, indicating tightened surveillance for several securities. Trading members are advised to note the new limits before trading in the affected scrips.

## Key Points

- Price bands revised for 27 scrips across equity segment
- Effective date: June 15, 2026
- Band levels revised to 2%, 5%, 10%, or 20% depending on the scrip
- Notice issued by DOSS (Department of Surveillance & Supervision)
- No attachments; all details contained within the notice

## Regulatory Changes

BSE has administratively revised the price bands (circuit limits) for the listed scrips. Price bands restrict the maximum intraday price movement (up or down) for a security. Tightened bands (e.g., 2% or 5%) typically reflect heightened surveillance concerns or placement under ASM/GSM frameworks.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must update their trading systems and risk management controls to reflect the new price band limits for the 27 affected scrips before the market opens on June 15, 2026. Members with queries may write to BSE for clarification.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 12, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 15, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The revision of price bands to tighter levels (2% or 5%) for the majority of listed scrips signals increased surveillance activity by BSE. Securities with a 2% band (Kairosoft AI Solutions, Kobo Biotech, Pervasive Commodities) face the most restrictive trading limits, potentially reducing liquidity and increasing volatility risk for existing holders. Scrips with a 20% band revision (Amwill Health Care, Gem Aromatics, M.R.Maniveni Foods, Mukta Arts, Sheetal Cool Products) are moving to a more permissive band, which may indicate easing of prior restrictions. Overall impact is limited to the specific securities and their traders; no systemic market impact is expected.
