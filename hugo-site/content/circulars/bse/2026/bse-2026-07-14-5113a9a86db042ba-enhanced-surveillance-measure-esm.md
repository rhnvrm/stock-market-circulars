## Summary

BSE has issued an update to its Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, referencing a series of prior notices dating back to June 2023. Securities listed in Annexure I have newly qualified for ESM and will face a minimum 100% margin requirement effective July 16, 2026. Some securities already under ESM will be moved to a lower stage (Stage I), while others already in the framework will continue under existing terms. A consolidated list of all securities currently under ESM is provided in Annexure III, and securities exiting the framework are listed in Annexure II.

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I satisfy ESM inclusion criteria and will attract minimum 100% margin on all open positions as of July 15, 2026, and on new positions created from July 16, 2026.
- Securities continuing in the ESM framework will be moved to the lower Stage I effective July 15, 2026.
- Qualifying securities will shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade (T2T) segment effective July 16, 2026.
- Securities shifting to Stage II will trade under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction, effective July 15, 2026.
- Securities exiting the ESM framework (Annexure II) will have their price bands reinstated to pre-ESM levels, unless subject to another surveillance measure.
- Annexure III provides the consolidated list of all securities currently under the ESM framework.
- ESM operates alongside all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchange.
- Inclusion under ESM is stated to be purely a surveillance action and not an adverse judgment on the company/entity.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice is a periodic update under the existing ESM framework established via prior BSE notices (June 2023 through July 2025), applying the framework's stage-transition and margin rules to a new set of securities rather than introducing new regulatory provisions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the minimum 100% margin requirement to all open and new positions in Annexure I securities from the specified dates.
- Members must recognize the shift of qualifying securities from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment.
- Members must apply the 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism to securities moving into Stage II.
- Queries should be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 15, 2026: Stage I transition for continuing ESM securities; Stage II securities begin trading under Trade-for-Trade with 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction; exit securities revert to normal price bands.
- July 16, 2026: Minimum 100% margin applies to all open positions (as of July 15) and new positions in Annexure I securities; qualifying securities shift to Trade-for-Trade segment.

## Impact Assessment

Securities entering or remaining in the ESM framework face significantly reduced trading liquidity due to 100% margin requirements and Trade-for-Trade settlement, which eliminates intraday trading and requires actual delivery. The tighter 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction for Stage II securities further restrict price discovery and trading flexibility. Investors and traders holding positions in affected securities should expect higher capital requirements and reduced ability to trade actively. Since the actual security names are contained in annexures not included in the source text, the specific market impact cannot be mapped to individual stocks without the annexure data.
