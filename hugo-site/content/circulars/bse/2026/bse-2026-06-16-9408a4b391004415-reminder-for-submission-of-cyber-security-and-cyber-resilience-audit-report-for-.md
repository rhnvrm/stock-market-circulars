## Summary

BSE has issued a reminder to all trading members to submit the Cyber Security and Cyber Resilience Audit Report for the period ended March 31, 2026. The deadline for submission is June 30, 2026. Members dual-registered with NSE must submit through NSE only, while BSE-only members must use the BEFS portal.

## Key Points

- All trading members must submit the Cyber Security and Cyber Resilience Audit Report for the period ended March 31, 2026.
- Submission deadline is June 30, 2026.
- Trading members registered with both BSE and NSE must submit their audit report to NSE only, as per the technology-based sharing mechanism introduced via Notice No. 20250929-81 dated September 29, 2025.
- Members not registered with NSE must submit through BSE's BEFS portal following the existing process.
- User manuals for both Members (Annexure A) and Auditors (Annexure B) are enclosed to assist with the cyber audit module.
- Non-compliance may result in penal or disciplinary action.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This notice reiterates existing requirements from:
- Exchange Notice No. 20260422-33 dated April 22, 2026
- Exchange Notice No. 20260511-7 dated May 11, 2026
- Exchange Notice No. 20250929-81 dated September 29, 2025 (technology-based sharing mechanism for common submissions across exchanges)

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members registered with NSE: Submit Cyber Security and Cyber Resilience Audit Report to NSE only via NSE's portal.
- Trading members not registered with NSE: Submit the audit report electronically through the BSE BEFS portal on or before June 30, 2026.
- Members should refer to the enclosed user manuals (Annexure A for Members, Annexure B for Auditors) for guidance on using the cyber audit module.

## Important Dates

- **Audit period:** April 1, 2025 – March 31, 2026
- **Submission deadline:** June 30, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is an operational compliance requirement affecting all trading members of BSE. The consolidated submission approach (dual-registered members submitting to NSE only) reduces administrative burden for members registered on both exchanges. Failure to submit by the deadline may attract penal or disciplinary action from the Exchange. No direct impact on market trading, pricing, or listed securities.
