## Summary

BSE has notified that certain securities have met the criteria for inclusion under the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework, under both Stage I and Stage II, as detailed in the attached Annexure. This action is part of the Exchange's ongoing market surveillance framework, building on earlier notices dated September 20, 2024, October 27, 2018, July 19, 2019, December 4, 2020, and September 25, 2023.

## Key Points

- Securities meeting ST-ASM Stage I criteria will be subject to a margin rate of 50% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- Securities meeting ST-ASM Stage II criteria will be subject to a margin rate of 100% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- These margin requirements apply to all open positions as of July 17, 2026, and to new positions created from July 20, 2026 onward.
- The ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchanges.
- Inclusion under ASM is a market surveillance measure and should not be interpreted as an adverse action against the concerned company or entity.
- When a security exits the ASM framework, its price band will revert to the level applicable before inclusion, unless it remains subject to another surveillance framework.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing ST-ASM framework (as revised via prior circulars) to a fresh set of securities identified as meeting the surveillance criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must apply the specified higher margin rates (50% for Stage I, 100% for Stage II securities) on all open and new positions in the identified securities from the effective date. Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 16, 2026: Notice issued
- July 17, 2026: Reference date for open positions
- July 20, 2026: Effective date for revised margin requirements on open and new positions

## Impact Assessment

The increased margin requirements will raise the cost of trading and holding positions in the affected securities, likely reducing speculative activity and leverage in these names. While the measure targets specific securities (listed in the Annexure, not detailed in the notice body), it does not represent a broad market risk but does materially affect liquidity and trading behavior for the affected stocks.
