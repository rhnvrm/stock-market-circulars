## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that further new securities issued by 13 companies under Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP) and Employee Stock Option Schemes (ESOS) are listed and admitted for trading on the Exchange with effect from **Thursday, June 4, 2026**. None of the newly listed shares carry any lock-in restrictions.

## Key Points

- 13 companies have new ESOP/ESOS shares listed effective June 4, 2026
- All securities are admitted without any lock-in period (NA)
- Largest allotment: Lenskart Solutions with 23,04,600 shares (Face Value ₹2)
- ICICI Bank allotted 3,11,170 shares (Face Value ₹2); cumulative shares now exceed 7.17 billion
- Vishal Mega Mart allotted 4,75,500 shares (Face Value ₹10); cumulative shares now exceed 4.67 billion
- Syngene International allotted 7,29,727 shares (Face Value ₹10)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice for admission of ESOP/ESOS-issued shares to trading.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are requested to take note of the newly listed securities and update their systems accordingly. No additional compliance action is required from market participants beyond awareness of the new share counts and ISIN ranges.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 3, 2026
- **Effective Date for Trading:** June 4, 2026 (Thursday)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing notice with low market impact. The allotments represent employee compensation through stock options and do not signal any major corporate event. Share counts are modest relative to existing paid-up capital for most companies. No lock-in applies, so shares are immediately tradable, but volumes are unlikely to be material enough to affect price discovery significantly. Investors and traders should update their records for revised total share counts for the 13 affected companies.
