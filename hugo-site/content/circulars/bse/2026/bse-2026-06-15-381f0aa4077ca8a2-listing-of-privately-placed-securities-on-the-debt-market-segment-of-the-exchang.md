## Summary

BSE has listed five new Commercial Papers (CPs) on the Debt Market Segment effective June 15, 2026. These securities, issued on a private placement basis by Tata Realty and Infrastructure Limited, SBICAP Securities Limited, Bajaj Finance Limited, Aditya Birla Housing Finance Limited, and 360 ONE Prime Limited, are now admitted to trading. All CPs have a face value of Rs. 5 lakh each, were allotted on June 12, 2026, and mature on September 11, 2026 (except 360 ONE Prime, which matures on September 10, 2026).

## Key Points

- All securities trade exclusively in dematerialised form
- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 Lakhs (Rs. 500,000) and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- All five CPs carry top-tier short-term credit ratings (CRISIL A1+ and/or ICRA A1+ and/or CARE A1+)
- Allotment date for all issuers: June 12, 2026
- Redemption date for all (except 360 ONE Prime): September 11, 2026
- Contact for clarifications: BSE Debt Department – 22728352 / 8597 / 8995 / 5753 / 8915

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notice informing trading members of new debt securities admitted to trading.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure all transactions in these CPs are executed only in dematerialised form
- Trades must be in standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size of 1 paise must be observed

## Important Dates

- **Listing / Trading effective date:** June 15, 2026
- **Allotment date (all issuers):** June 12, 2026
- **Redemption date (Tata Realty, SBICAP, Bajaj Finance, Aditya Birla Housing Finance):** September 11, 2026
- **Redemption date (360 ONE Prime):** September 10, 2026
- **Interest payment:** Cumulative, paid at redemption for all issuers

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt segment listing notice with low market-wide impact. The five CPs span well-known issuers in real estate, financial services, and NBFCs, all carrying the highest short-term credit ratings (A1+). The securities are short-term instruments (approximately 91 days), and their admission to BSE's Debt Segment improves secondary market liquidity for institutional and wholesale investors holding these CPs. No equity markets or retail investor segment is affected.
