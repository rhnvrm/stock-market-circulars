## Summary

Edelweiss Mutual Fund is launching the ALTIVA Equity Ex-Top 100 Long-Short Fund (SIF) as a New Fund Offer (NFO) with SIP facility on the BSE StAR MF Platform. The NFO subscription window runs from May 18, 2026, to June 1, 2026, with a tentative allotment date of June 8, 2026.

## Key Points

- NFO period: May 18, 2026 to June 1, 2026 (extended till midnight for lumpsum purchases)
- Six scheme variants offered: Regular and Direct plans, each with Growth, IDCW Payout, and IDCW Reinvestment options
- SIP/XSIP registrations must be authenticated by clients on or before 3:00 PM on June 1, 2026
- Transactions where clients do not initiate funds by 9:00 AM on T+1 day will be cancelled
- Pending transactions remain in system until 3:00 PM on the last day of funds pay-out to AMC or T+5, whichever is earlier
- Cheque and OTM payments accepted during NFO period subject to clear fund receipt by deadline

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is an operational notice for members facilitating transactions under the new SIF NFO on BSE StAR MF Platform.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure clients initiate funds by 9:00 AM on T+1 day to avoid transaction cancellation
- Clear funds must be available in the ICCL account by 6:00 PM on June 4, 2026, for allotment eligibility
- Switch-out funds received on or after the allotment date (June 8, 2026) will not be considered for allotment
- SIP/XSIP registrations must be client-authenticated by 3:00 PM on June 1, 2026

## Important Dates

- **May 18, 2026**: NFO subscription opens
- **June 1, 2026**: NFO closure date; SIP/XSIP authentication deadline at 3:00 PM; lumpsum purchases accepted till midnight
- **June 4, 2026**: Clear funds must be in ICCL account by 6:00 PM
- **June 8, 2026**: Tentative allotment date

## Impact Assessment

This notice primarily affects BSE StAR MF platform members (mutual fund distributors and brokers) facilitating transactions for this NFO. Investors interested in equity long-short strategies via the SIF structure have a new product available. The operational deadlines for fund settlement and cancellation policies are standard NFO procedures and do not introduce significant market-wide impact.
