## Summary

BSE has published the Demat Auction settlement list for Settlement No. 790 dated January 5, 2026. The auction includes securities from 119 companies with varying quantities available for auction. This is a routine settlement process for securities that have failed to deliver in the normal settlement cycle.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 790
- Settlement Date: January 5, 2026
- Total scrips listed: 119 securities across various companies
- Quantities range from 1 share to 613,972 shares
- Largest quantity: WELCURE DRUG (524661) with 613,972 shares
- Other significant quantities include: EVEXIA LIFEC (34,729 shares), LASA Superge (5,000 shares), Rajnish Well (5,000 shares), Luxury Time (3,200 shares)
- Includes securities from major companies like RELIANCE IND, ITC LTD, BAJAJ AUTO, GAIL, COAL INDIA, HINDALCO, STATE BANK O, VEDANTA, SUZLON ENERG, and others

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a standard operational circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and clearing members must participate in the demat auction process for Settlement No. 790
- Securities listed must be settled through the auction mechanism as per BSE's standard settlement procedures
- Participants should note the scrip codes and quantities for auction settlement

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: January 5, 2026
- Auction settlement must be completed as per BSE's standard timeline for Settlement No. 790

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Low - This is a routine demat auction settlement process that occurs regularly to resolve delivery failures. The auction mechanism ensures orderly settlement of outstanding securities.

**Operational Impact:** Low to Medium for affected brokers/clearing members - Those with positions in the listed securities must participate in the auction settlement process. The quantities involved are relatively small for most scrips, though some securities like WELCURE DRUG have significant quantities.

**Investor Impact:** Low - Individual investors are generally not directly affected by demat auction settlements, which are handled at the broker/clearing member level.
