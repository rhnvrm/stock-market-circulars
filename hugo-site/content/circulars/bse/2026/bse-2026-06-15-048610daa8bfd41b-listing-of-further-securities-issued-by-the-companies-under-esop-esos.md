## Summary

BSE has admitted further new equity shares issued under Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP/ESOS) by 16 companies for trading with effect from Tuesday, June 16, 2026. None of the newly listed shares carry any lock-in restrictions.

## Key Points

- 16 companies have had additional ESOP/ESOS shares listed on BSE effective June 16, 2026.
- All newly issued shares are free from lock-in restrictions (NA).
- The largest allotments by share count are: Equitas Small Finance Bank (2,148,829 shares), Wipro Limited (741,918 shares), Federal Bank Limited (738,389 shares), and ZEE Learn Limited (669,286 shares).
- The smallest allotment is Astec Lifesciences Limited with 582 shares.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced by this notice. It is a standard operational listing notice under existing ESOP/ESOS framework rules.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members of BSE are informed to take note of the newly listed securities and update their systems accordingly. No additional compliance action is required from market participants beyond standard trading practices.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 15, 2026
- **Effective Date for Trading:** Tuesday, June 16, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine ESOP/ESOS listing notice with minimal market impact. The share quantities are small relative to existing paid-up capital for most companies. The listing of ESOP shares represents employee compensation being converted to tradable equity, with no lock-in constraints, which could result in minor incremental selling pressure across the 16 companies. The most notable volumes are in Equitas Small Finance Bank (2.1M shares) and Wipro (741K shares), though these remain small fractions of their respective market capitalizations.
