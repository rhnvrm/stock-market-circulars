## Summary

BSE has announced changes to the Short Term (5/15/30 Days) Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective March 16, 2026. Nine securities are being newly shortlisted into the ST-ASM framework, one security is being moved to a higher ASM stage, and nine securities are exiting the ST-ASM framework (some due to inclusion in other surveillance frameworks such as LT-ASM and ESM).

## Key Points

- **9 new securities** added to ST-ASM framework w.e.f. March 16, 2026 (Annexure I, Part A)
- **1 security** (Corporate Merchant Bankers Ltd) moved to a higher ST-ASM stage w.e.f. March 16, 2026
- **NIL securities** moved to a lower ST-ASM stage
- **9 securities** exiting the ST-ASM framework w.e.f. March 16, 2026 (Annexure II)
- Securities marked with `*` are shortlisted/moving as per NSE; securities marked `@` are SME scrips
- Securities marked `*` in exit list are moving out due to inclusion in LT-ASM Framework
- Security marked `$` (Sylph Industries Ltd) is moving out due to inclusion in ESM Framework

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and market participants must note the revised ST-ASM applicability for the listed securities from March 16, 2026
- Securities under ST-ASM are subject to additional surveillance conditions including applicable price bands and periodic review of trading patterns
- Participants dealing in SME scrips marked `@` should take note of their inclusion under the ST-ASM framework
- Brokers must ensure clients are informed of changed surveillance status for affected securities

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** March 13, 2026
- **Effective Date:** March 16, 2026 (all additions, stage changes, and exits)

## Impact Assessment

The inclusion of Adani Total Gas Ltd (a large-cap, widely traded security) in ST-ASM is notable and will attract significant market attention, potentially causing heightened volatility and reduced liquidity for the stock. For the 8 smaller-cap and SME securities newly added, ST-ASM placement typically results in trade-for-trade settlement and tighter surveillance, reducing speculative activity. The exit of Usha Martin Education & Solutions Ltd and Venus Remedies Ltd from ST-ASM into LT-ASM indicates persistent surveillance concerns, while Sylph Industries Ltd's exit into ESM signals escalation of oversight. Overall, the circular moderately tightens market surveillance across a broad set of securities spanning large-cap, small-cap, and SME segments.
