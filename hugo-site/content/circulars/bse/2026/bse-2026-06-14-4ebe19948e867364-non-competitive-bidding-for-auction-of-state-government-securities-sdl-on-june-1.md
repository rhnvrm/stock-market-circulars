## Summary

BSE has announced the non-competitive bidding facility for the auction of State Government Securities (SDL) scheduled on June 16, 2026. This follows an RBI press release dated June 12, 2026, and is in continuation of Exchange circular no. 20191122-28 (November 22, 2019). Trading members can participate via the NCB-GSec module of BSE's iBBS web-based platform.

## Key Points

- Bidding platform: https://ibbs.bseindia.com >> NCB-GSec module
- Covers new and re-issue SDLs from nine states: Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Gujarat, Jammu & Kashmir, Maharashtra, Punjab, Rajasthan, Telangana, and Uttarakhand
- Minimum subscription size: Rs. 100 per unit; minimum amount Rs. 10,000 per bid
- Both new issuances and re-issues are included across various maturities (2032–2056)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is issued in continuation of the existing non-competitive bidding framework established under Exchange circular no. 20191122-28 dated November 22, 2019.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use the NCB-GSec module on the iBBS system to place bids
- Members must submit bids by June 16, 2026, 8:00 AM
- Direct investors must submit bids by June 15, 2026, 5:00 PM
- Minimum bid amount is Rs. 10,000; bids in multiples of Rs. 100

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice for debt market participants. Trading members and direct investors who wish to participate in state government securities auctions via the non-competitive route must ensure timely bid submission through BSE's iBBS platform. The auction covers a broad set of SDL securities across nine states with maturities ranging from 2032 to 2056, offering diversified investment options in the government debt segment. No systemic market impact is expected.
