## Summary

SEBI has issued an enforcement order (Ref: QJA/MN/CFID/CFID-SEC6/32159/2025-26) under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1) and 11B(2) of the SEBI Act, 1992 read with Rule 5 of the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995, against Mediaone Global Entertainment Ltd (MGEL) and six associated individuals. The order stems from an investigation into Eros International Media Limited (EIML), where EIML wrote off ₹63.61 crore during FY 2019-20 as advances to MGEL purportedly for film co-production arrangements.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated Eros International Media Limited (EIML) and detected serious irregularities: diversion of funds, manipulation of books of accounts, and falsification of financial statements
- EIML wrote off ₹63.61 crore in FY 2019-20 as advances to MGEL for alleged film co-production arrangements
- The write-off amount exceeded MGEL's total asset base for the relevant period
- MGEL had reported negligible revenues for several preceding financial years prior to the write-off
- 7 noticees named: MGEL (PAN: AAACR0405M), Suryaraj Kumar (Managing Director), J Murali Manohar, K Sai Prasad (Whole Time Director), M Srinivas Kumar, Timothy Alfred Joseph Moses (Independent Director), and Saraswathy Gopalan (Independent Director)
- BSE suspended trading in MGEL shares from June 27, 2016 to May 03, 2022 for non-compliance of listing regulations; trading resumed May 04, 2022
- MGEL was under liquidation per Madras High Court orders (Sept 9, 2015 and Dec 22, 2016); liquidation lifted per HC order dated January 25, 2023
- MGEL is currently "Active" status, incorporated October 29, 2002, based in Chennai

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is an enforcement/adjudication order applying existing provisions of the SEBI Act, 1992 and the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995.

## Compliance Requirements

- The named noticees are subject to directions under SEBI Act Sections 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1), and 11B(2), which may include restraint from accessing securities markets, disgorgement of unlawful gains, and monetary penalties
- MGEL and its directors/officers named must respond to and comply with the directions issued in this order
- The full 131-page order contains detailed findings and specific compliance directives for each noticee

## Important Dates

- FY 2019-20: EIML wrote off ₹63.61 crore as advances to MGEL
- October 29, 2002: MGEL incorporation date
- June 27, 2016 – May 03, 2022: BSE trading suspension period for MGEL
- September 9, 2015 and December 22, 2016: Madras HC orders for liquidation
- January 25, 2023: Madras HC order lifting liquidation proceedings
- March 2, 2026: Date of this SEBI enforcement order

## Impact Assessment

This is a high-impact enforcement action. The order involves serious allegations of financial fraud — including fund diversion and falsification of financial statements — connected to two listed entities (MGEL and Eros International). The write-off of ₹63.61 crore by EIML to MGEL, an amount exceeding MGEL's entire asset base, indicates potential sham transactions that may have misled investors. The order covers 7 noticees and spans 131 pages, signalling a comprehensive regulatory action. Investors in both MGEL and Eros International Media Limited should note potential market and valuation implications arising from the findings.
