## Summary

BSE has announced the non-competitive bidding facility for three Treasury Bills being auctioned by the Government of India (GoI) through RBI on June 10, 2026. Trading members can participate via the NCB-GSec module on BSE's iBBS web-based platform. The announcement references RBI's press release of June 05, 2026, and the earlier Exchange circular 20180423-42 (April 26, 2018) that launched this facility.

## Key Points

- Three T-Bills are available for non-competitive bidding: 91-day, 182-day, and 364-day maturities
- Bidding platform: https://ibbs.bseindia.com >> NCB-GSec module
- Minimum lot size is Rs. 100 for all three securities
- Minimum bid amount is Rs. 10,000 for all three securities
- Settlement will occur on June 11, 2026

## Securities Details

| Security | Symbol | Type | Issue Size (Cr) | Max Amt (Cr) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 91 Day T-Bill | 091T090926 | New | 12,000 | 600 |
| 182 Day T-Bill | 182T091226 | New | 6,000 | 300 |
| 364 Day T-Bill | 364T100627 | New | 6,000 | 300 |

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This notice operates under the existing non-competitive bidding framework established by Exchange circular 20180423-42 dated April 26, 2018.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use the NCB-GSec module on the iBBS platform to submit bids
- Members must adhere to bid collection deadlines (June 10, 2026 till 8:00 am for members; June 09, 2026 till 5:00 pm for direct investors)
- Bids must comply with minimum lot (Rs. 100) and minimum amount (Rs. 10,000) requirements
- Maximum bid amounts apply per security as listed above

## Important Dates

- **Bid Collection Start:** June 08, 2026, from 10:00 am onwards (24-hour availability)
- **Bid Collection End (Direct Investors):** June 09, 2026, till 5:00 pm
- **Bid Collection End (Members):** June 10, 2026, till 8:00 am
- **Auction Date:** June 10, 2026
- **Settlement Date:** June 11, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with low market impact. It provides standard access to non-competitive bidding in the primary government securities market for BSE trading members and direct investors. The total issue size across all three T-Bills is Rs. 24,000 crore, consistent with regular GoI short-term borrowing activities. No changes to existing procedures or rules are introduced.
