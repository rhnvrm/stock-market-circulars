## Summary

BSE has updated the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework list pursuant to a joint review with SEBI and Stock Exchanges. The notice identifies new securities being placed under GSM, those continuing under existing stages, and those exiting the framework effective June 23, 2026. The GSM framework applies to securities whose prices are not commensurate with their financial health and fundamentals.

## Key Points

- New securities identified for GSM placement are listed in Annexure I along with their monitoring dates.
- Securities in Annexure II will continue under GSM with their applicable stages unchanged.
- Securities in Annexure III will exit the GSM framework effective June 23, 2026.
- ASD (Additional Surveillance Deposit) collected for Annexure III securities will be released effective June 29, 2026.
- Securities placed under GSM Stage VI per SEBI direction (Exchange notice 20170807-31 dated August 07, 2017) will remain in Stage VI until further directions.
- Shortlisting is based on XBRL submissions by the respective listed companies.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice continues the GSM framework originally introduced via Exchange notice no. 20170223-44 (February 23, 2017) and subsequently updated through multiple notices including 20231117-63 (November 17, 2023). The framework targets securities with prices not commensurate with financial fundamentals such as Earnings, Book Value, Fixed Assets, Net Worth, Market Capitalization, and P/E multiples.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must exercise additional due diligence when trading in GSM-listed securities, both on proprietary account and on behalf of clients.
- GSM framework applies in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchanges.
- For clarifications, members may contact bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **June 23, 2026**: Securities in Annexure III move out of the GSM framework.
- **June 29, 2026**: ASD collected on Annexure III securities will be released.

## Impact Assessment

Affected securities face enhanced trading restrictions and additional scrutiny. Trading members dealing in these securities bear heightened due diligence obligations. The exit of Annexure III securities from GSM and the release of ASD provides relief to those companies and their investors. Companies remaining in or newly entering GSM may face reduced liquidity and investor confidence impacts due to the surveillance designation.
