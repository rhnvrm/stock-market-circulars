## Summary

BSE has informed trading members that 20,09,70,560 new equity shares of PC Jeweller Limited (Scrip Code: 534809), of face value Re. 1/- each issued at a premium of Rs. 4.62/- per share, will be listed and permitted to trade on the Exchange with effect from Tuesday, July 7, 2026. These shares were allotted to promoters and non-promoters on a preferential basis pursuant to conversion of warrants.

## Key Points

- New securities: 20,09,70,560 equity shares of Re. 1/- each
- Issue price: Rs. 5.62/- per share (face value Re. 1/- + premium Rs. 4.62/-)
- Allotment basis: Preferential allotment to Promoter and Non-Promoter entities pursuant to conversion of warrants
- Distinctive numbers: 8368587326 to 8569557885
- ISIN: INE785M01021
- New shares rank pari-passu with existing equity shares of the company
- Date of allotment: 28/03/2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes; this is a listing approval notice under BSE's Listing Operations department for shares arising from warrant conversion on a preferential basis.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific action required from trading members or investors beyond updating records to reflect the additional listed equity shares and applicable lock-in restrictions on the newly allotted shares.

## Important Dates

- Date of Allotment: March 28, 2026
- Listing and trading effective from: Tuesday, July 7, 2026
- Lock-in expiry for 18,89,66,880 shares (Dist. Nos. 8368587326-8557554205): January 8, 2028
- Lock-in expiry for 1,20,03,680 shares (Dist. Nos. 8557554206-8569557885): January 7, 2027

## Impact Assessment

This listing increases PC Jeweller's total outstanding and tradable equity share capital, which could have a dilutive effect on existing shareholders' proportional ownership. However, since a significant portion of the newly listed shares (18,89,66,880) remain under lock-in until January 2028 and a smaller tranche (1,20,03,680) until January 2027, the immediate free-float impact and near-term trading supply from this allotment is limited. The listing reflects strengthening of promoter/non-promoter holdings via warrant conversion, which may be viewed as a signal of continued capital infusion into the company.
