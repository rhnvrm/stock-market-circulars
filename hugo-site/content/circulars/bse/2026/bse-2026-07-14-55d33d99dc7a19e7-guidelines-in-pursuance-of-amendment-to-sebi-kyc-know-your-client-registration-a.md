## Summary

BSE has issued its monthly circular reminding trading members of the guidelines under the amended SEBI KYC (Know Your Client) Registration Agency (KRA) Regulations, 2011. The notice references SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/MIRSD/FATF/P/CIR/2023/0144 dated August 11, 2023 and the Exchange's recurring monthly circular series, with the latest prior notice being No. 20260612-26 dated June 12, 2026. It also reiterates the requirement under SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/OIAE/OIAE_IAD-1/P/CIR/2023/0000000163 dated October 3, 2023 for a centralized mechanism to report investor demise through KRAs. Clients whose KYC records uploaded to KRAs between June 1, 2026 and June 30, 2026 remain "On Hold" (for AADHAAR or Non-AADHAAR based OVD) will be barred from trading and from squaring off existing open positions effective July 25, 2026, until they complete KYC validation.

## Key Points

- The notice covers clients whose KYC status is "On Hold" with KRAs, based on uploads made between June 1-30, 2026.
- Affected clients will neither be permitted to trade nor allowed to square off open positions from July 25, 2026 onward.
- Any open positions of non-compliant clients will be allowed to naturally expire on the contract's expiry date rather than being forcibly closed.
- The Exchange will flag non-compliant PANs (as identified by KRAs) as "Not Permitted to Trade" starting July 25, 2026.
- PANs that subsequently become KRA-compliant will be permitted to trade on a T+1 basis, based on data received from KRAs on T day.
- Trading members are reminded that demise data is shared by KRAs on a daily basis, requiring blocking of debit transactions and suspension of trading/UCC for deceased investors per the October 2023 SEBI circular.
- The list of non-compliant clients is available to members at path EQ\TRANSACTION\JUL-2026\14-07-2026, file Non_Validated_Clients_by_KRAs_Clgno_xxxx.TXT.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change is introduced; this is a recurring monthly compliance notice implementing existing SEBI KRA KYC regulations and the October 2023 SEBI circular on centralized investor-demise reporting.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must review the list of non-compliant clients provided via the specified file path and ensure affected clients complete KYC validation before July 25, 2026.
- Members must block debit transactions and suspend trading/UCC activity for accounts of deceased investors as identified through KRA demise data feeds.
- Members should monitor daily KRA compliance updates to reinstate trading permissions for PANs that become compliant (effective T+1).

## Important Dates

- June 1-30, 2026: Period of KYC uploads to KRAs that are being reviewed for "On Hold" status.
- July 14, 2026: Notice issue date; non-compliant client list made available at specified path.
- July 25, 2026: Effective date from which non-compliant PANs will be blocked from trading and from squaring off open positions.

## Impact Assessment

The circular has limited direct market impact but is operationally significant for trading members, who must identify and manage clients with incomplete KYC validation to avoid business disruption. Affected retail/individual clients with unresolved KYC status face trading restrictions starting July 25, 2026, though the natural expiry provision for open positions mitigates forced liquidation risk. No listed company or stock price is directly impacted by this notice.
