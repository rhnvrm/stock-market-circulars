## Summary

BSE has listed and admitted for trading the further new securities issued by 16 companies under their Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP/ESOS). These securities are effective for trading from Tuesday, June 23, 2026. None of the newly issued shares are subject to any lock-in period.

## Key Points

- 16 companies have had new ESOP/ESOS-issued securities listed on BSE
- Trading in all newly listed shares commences June 23, 2026
- No lock-in period applies to any of the listed shares
- Shares span face values of ₹1, ₹2, ₹5, and ₹10
- Physicswallah Limited has the largest allotment with 7,791,768 new shares (₹1 face value)
- IDFC First Bank has the second largest allotment with 2,316,341 new shares

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice informing trading members of the listing of new shares issued under existing employee stock option/ownership schemes.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are required to take note of the newly listed securities and their respective ISIN codes and scrip codes to facilitate trading from June 23, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 22, 2026
- **Trading Effective Date:** June 23, 2026 (Tuesday)
- **Lock-in Expiry:** Not applicable (NA for all 16 companies)

## Impact Assessment

The impact on the market is minimal. These are routine ESOP/ESOS-related listings resulting from employee stock option exercises. The number of shares being added is small relative to the existing equity base of each company. No lock-in restrictions apply, meaning shares are freely tradeable from day one. Market participants may observe marginal dilution in earnings per share for these companies, but the volumes are not significant enough to cause price disruptions.
