## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260702-31 dated July 2, 2026) informing trading members that securities listed in the accompanying annexure will move to their respective higher stages under the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, effective July 3, 2026. This action is taken under the GSM Framework originally published on February 23, 2017, and refers to a series of prior exchange notices dated February 23, 2017, March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023.

## Key Points

- Securities specified in Annexure_GSM will move into higher GSM stages effective July 3, 2026.
- The GSM framework has four stages (I to IV), each with progressively stricter surveillance actions.
- Stage I: 100% margin and price band of 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement with 5% or lower price band, plus 50% Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) from buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade settlement, weekly trading (Monday/first trading day only), and 100% ASD from buyers.
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III but with no upward price movement permitted.
- All settlements for GSM securities are done on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing GSM Framework (established via notices dated February 23, 2017 and subsequently amended) to a fresh list of securities identified in the annexure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure compliance with applicable margin requirements, price bands, and Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) obligations for the affected securities based on their new GSM stage.
- Members must observe trade-for-trade settlement rules with no netting for securities in Stages II, III, and IV.
- Members should refer to the published FAQ on GSM or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 3, 2026 (securities move to new GSM stages)
- Notice Date: July 2, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular imposes tighter trading restrictions—including higher margins, trade-for-trade settlement, additional surveillance deposits, and in some cases weekly trading windows with no upward price movement—on the securities specified in the annexure. These measures are designed to curb speculative trading and protect investors from potential price manipulation, and will directly affect liquidity and trading behavior for the impacted stocks. The broader market impact is limited since it targets a specific list of securities rather than the market as a whole.
