## Summary

BSE has issued a notice referencing its earlier GSM framework circulars (dated February 23, 2017, March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023), informing trading members that securities listed in the attached Annexure will move into their respective higher Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) stages with effect from July 17, 2026. Each GSM stage carries progressively stricter surveillance actions affecting margin requirements, price bands, settlement mode, and trading frequency.

## Key Points

- Securities specified in the Annexure to this notice will be moved to their respective higher GSM stages effective July 17, 2026.
- The GSM framework has four surveillance stages (I through IV), each with escalating restrictions.
- Stage I: 100% applicable margin rate and price band of 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, and Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, trading permitted only once a week (every Monday or the first trading day of the week), and 100% ASD payable by buyers.
- Stage IV: Same restrictions as Stage III, plus no upward price movement permitted.
- All trades in GSM securities are settled on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice operationalizes the existing GSM Framework published on February 23, 2017, by moving specific securities (identified in the Annexure) into higher surveillance stages based on ongoing monitoring.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must identify the securities listed in the Annexure and apply the corresponding stage-wise surveillance actions (margins, price bands, ASD, trading frequency) from the effective date.
- Members must exercise adequate precaution while trading in these securities given the trade-for-trade settlement and no-netting requirement.
- Queries or clarifications should be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com or the published GSM FAQ page.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 17, 2026 — securities move to their respective higher GSM stages.
- Notice Date: July 16, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

Securities moved into higher GSM stages face significantly reduced liquidity due to trade-for-trade settlement, higher margin/ASD requirements, tighter price bands, and (for Stages III and IV) restriction to weekly trading windows. This can materially affect price discovery and increase the cost of holding or trading these securities. Investors and traders holding or considering positions in the affected securities (per the Annexure) should reassess exposure and liquidity risk. The broader market impact is limited to the specific securities named in the annexure rather than systemic.
