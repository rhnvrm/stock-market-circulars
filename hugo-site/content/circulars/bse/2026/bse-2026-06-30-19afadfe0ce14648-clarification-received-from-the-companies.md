## Summary

BSE's Surveillance department (DOSS) has observed significant price/volume movement in 17 scrips. The Exchange had written to these companies seeking clarification. As of June 30, 2026, seven companies have submitted their replies (attached to the notice), while ten companies have yet to respond.

## Key Points

- BSE noticed unusual price/volume activity in 17 listed scrips and sought clarifications from the respective companies.
- Replies have been received from 7 companies: Borosil Scientific Ltd (544184), Indus Towers Ltd (534816), Investment & Precision Castings Ltd (504786), Keystone Realtors Ltd (543669), Linde India Ltd (523457), Shree Ganesh Remedies Ltd (540737), and Skipper Ltd (53856).
- Replies are still awaited from 10 companies: CMX Holdings Ltd (532217), Elango Industries Ltd (513452), Grand Oak Canyons Distillery Ltd (523862), LIC Housing Finance Ltd (500253), Marble City India Ltd (531281), Mayur Leather Products Ltd (531680), Mohite Industries Ltd (532140), SC Agrotech Ltd (526081), Sujala Trading & Holdings Ltd (539117), and UHM Vacation Ltd (544779).
- The notice is issued by the Surveillance department, signed by Deputy Vice President Sanjay Jain and Manager Shreya Ghose.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard surveillance disclosure under BSE's market monitoring framework to ensure trading members and investors have access to the latest relevant company information.

## Compliance Requirements

- The 10 companies with pending replies are expected to submit their clarifications to BSE at the earliest.
- Trading members and investors are advised to refer to the attached replies from the 7 responding companies before making trading decisions in these scrips.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: June 30, 2026
- No specific deadline mentioned for pending replies; companies are expected to respond promptly.

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects investors and trading members in 17 scrips across various sectors. The pending replies from 10 companies, including a large-cap like LIC Housing Finance Ltd, may sustain uncertainty around those stocks. The 7 companies that have already responded provide some transparency to the market. Continued non-response may attract further regulatory action from BSE's surveillance division.
