## Summary

BSE has announced corporate actions (dividends) for six securities in the Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) segment. The circular specifies ex-dates, record dates, and shut periods during which SLB transactions will be restricted for the affected securities.

## Key Points

- Six companies have declared dividends with implications for SLB segment
- Shut periods have been specified during which SLB lending/borrowing will be suspended
- Ex-dates and record dates coincide for all listed securities
- Shut periods range from 1-5 days depending on the security

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine notification of corporate actions affecting SLB securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- SLB participants must note the shut periods and plan their lending/borrowing activities accordingly
- No new lending or borrowing transactions can be initiated during the shut periods for these securities
- Existing positions should be managed considering the upcoming shut periods

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects liquidity in the SLB segment for these specific securities during the shut periods.

**Operational Impact:** SLB participants need to adjust their lending and borrowing strategies around these dates. Securities cannot be lent or borrowed during shut periods, which may temporarily reduce available inventory for short selling or hedging activities.

**Investor Impact:** Investors with SLB positions in these securities must be aware that they cannot enter new transactions during shut periods and should plan accordingly.
