## Summary

BSE has listed the equity shares of Shankara Buildpro Limited following a scheme of arrangement. The circular provides the post-scheme shareholding pattern of the resulting company as on December 23, 2025, filed under Regulation 31 of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015. The company has 24,249,326 total equity shares with promoter holding at 40.18% and public holding at 59.82%.

## Key Points

- Total issued equity shares: 24,249,326 (all fully paid-up)
- Promoter & Promoter Group shareholding: 9,742,537 shares (40.18%)
- Public shareholding: 14,506,789 shares (59.82%)
- Total number of shareholders: 28,666 (4 promoters, 28,662 public)
- All shares are held in dematerialized form
- No partly paid-up shares, convertible securities, warrants, depository receipts, locked-in shares, or pledged shares
- The equity shares were previously unlisted

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is a listing event following a scheme of arrangement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Shareholding pattern filed pursuant to Regulation 31 of SEBI LODR Regulations, 2015
- Post-scheme shareholding pattern submitted as per stock exchange requirements
- Declaration confirms no issuance of partly paid-up shares, convertible securities, warrants, depository receipts, locked-in shares, or pledged/encumbered shares
- Number of shares to be issued matches the shareholding pattern submitted at the time of seeking trading approval

## Important Dates

- Shareholding pattern date: December 23, 2025
- Circular publication date: January 7, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This listing adds Shankara Buildpro Limited to BSE's listed universe following a corporate restructuring. The company demonstrates compliance with minimum public shareholding requirements (59.82% public holding exceeds the 25% regulatory minimum). With nearly 28,666 shareholders and all shares in demat form, the company shows good retail participation. The absence of pledged shares and locked-in shares indicates no immediate liquidity concerns or promoter financing issues. Investors can now trade these equity shares on BSE's platform.
