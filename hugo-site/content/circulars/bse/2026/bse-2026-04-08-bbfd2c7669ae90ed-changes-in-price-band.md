## Summary

BSE (Notice No. 20260408-35) has revised the price bands for over 54 equity scrips to 10%, effective April 9, 2026. This notice from the Department of Surveillance and Supervision (DOSS) applies to trading members and impacts investors holding or trading in the listed scrips.

## Key Points

- Price band revised to **10%** for all listed scrips in this notice
- Effective date: **April 09, 2026**
- Covers 54+ scrips spanning diverse sectors including manufacturing, finance, real estate, agri, technology, and logistics
- Issued by BSE Department of Surveillance and Supervision (DOSS)
- No attachments; notice itself contains the full list

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the revised 10% price band for all listed scrips from the open of trading on April 9, 2026
- Order management systems and risk frameworks must be updated to reflect the new band limits before market open
- No action required from individual investors directly; brokers and trading members are responsible for compliance

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** April 08, 2026
- **Effective Date:** April 09, 2026 (from market open)

## Impact Assessment

The revision of price bands to 10% for these scrips limits intraday price movement, potentially reducing volatility and speculative trading. This is a standard surveillance measure typically applied to scrips exhibiting abnormal price movements or as part of periodic band reviews. Investors in affected scrips should note that their ability to exit positions at wide intraday swings will be constrained. The broad scope (54+ scrips) suggests a systematic review rather than individual stock-specific action. Day traders and short-term investors are most affected; long-term holders face minimal impact.
