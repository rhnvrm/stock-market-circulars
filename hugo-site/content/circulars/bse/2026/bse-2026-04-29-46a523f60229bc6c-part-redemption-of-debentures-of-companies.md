## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260429-6 informs trading members that 10 companies have fixed record dates for payment of interest and/or part redemption on their non-convertible debentures (NCDs) and bonds. Trading in these debentures/bonds will continue with reduced face values effective from the respective ex-dates.

## Key Points

- Ten companies across the debt segment have scheduled part redemptions of NCDs/bonds between May 4 and May 29, 2026
- Trading in affected debentures will be conducted at reduced face values from the respective ex-dates
- All ex-dates coincide with record dates, indicating same-day settlement basis
- Settlement DRs span series 622/2026-2027 through 640/2026-2027
- Issued by Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President – Listing Compliance and Operations

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational notice under existing BSE listing and debt segment framework for corporate actions on privately placed NCDs.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note updated (reduced) face values for all listed debentures from their respective ex-dates
- Members should ensure trades in these instruments reflect the reduced face values post ex-date
- Members are advised to update their systems accordingly for each scrip code listed

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a moderate operational impact on debt market participants. NCD holders of the 10 listed companies will see a reduction in the face value of their holdings, reflecting the partial repayment of principal. IOT Utkal Energy Services Limited is notable for having three separate debenture series undergoing part redemption on the same date (13/05/2026). Trading members operating in the debt segment must update their systems to reflect reduced face values from the respective ex-dates to avoid pricing discrepancies. The notice does not impact equity markets or equity trading members.
