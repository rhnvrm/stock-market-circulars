## Summary

BSE has forwarded a SEBI circular (No. SEBI/HO/DDHS/DDHS-PoD-2/I/11700/2026 dated May 15, 2026) governing the permitted use of fresh borrowings for Infrastructure Investment Trusts (InvITs) whose net borrowings exceed forty-nine percent (49%) of the value of InvIT assets. The circular is effective immediately.

## Key Points

- SEBI issued Circular No. SEBI/HO/DDHS/DDHS-PoD-2/I/11700/2026 on May 15, 2026, addressing fresh borrowing restrictions for over-leveraged InvITs.
- The circular defines permitted uses of fresh borrowings specifically for InvITs where net borrowings breach the 49% threshold of total InvIT asset value.
- BSE Notice No. 20260520-8 disseminates this circular to listed entities under the Debt segment.
- The full attachment (PermittedfreshborrowingsInvITscircular) contains the detailed provisions.

## Regulatory Changes

SEBI has issued new guidance on what constitutes a permitted use of fresh borrowings for InvITs that have exceeded the standard 49% net borrowing limit relative to their asset value. This provides a framework for InvITs operating above the leverage threshold to undertake new debt under defined conditions.

## Compliance Requirements

- All listed InvITs must review their current net borrowing levels relative to total asset value.
- InvITs with net borrowings exceeding 49% of InvIT asset value must restrict fresh borrowings to only the permitted uses outlined in the SEBI circular.
- Listed entities are required to take note of and comply with the SEBI circular immediately.

## Important Dates

- **May 15, 2026**: SEBI circular issued.
- **May 20, 2026**: BSE notice disseminated; circular in force with immediate effect.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a targeted impact on InvITs that carry net borrowings above the 49% threshold. It introduces regulatory guardrails on how such InvITs may deploy fresh debt, reducing systemic risk in the infrastructure debt segment. InvITs operating below the 49% threshold are unaffected. The immediate enforceability means non-compliant InvITs must review and potentially restructure planned borrowing activities without delay.
