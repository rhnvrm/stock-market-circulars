## Summary

BSE has conducted a review under its Additional Measure (AMS) framework for securities exhibiting unusual price movement. Pursuant to Exchange circular no. 20251107-56 dated November 07, 2025, securities satisfying inclusion criteria are being added and others are being removed from the framework, both effective June 01, 2026.

## Key Points

- Review carried out for identification of securities under the Additional Measure for unusual price movement framework.
- Securities meeting inclusion criteria are listed in Annexure I and will be placed under the measure effective June 01, 2026.
- Securities moving out of the framework are listed in Annexure II, also effective June 01, 2026.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework is provided in Annexure III.
- Members may seek clarifications by writing to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular updates the list of securities subject to the Additional Measure framework introduced under BSE circular 20251107-56 (November 07, 2025). The framework targets securities with unusual price movements and imposes additional trading-related conditions on such scrips.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must take note of the revised list of securities under the Additional Measure framework.
- Members must ensure compliance with any enhanced trading conditions applicable to newly included securities from June 01, 2026.
- Members dealing in securities being removed from the framework should note the relaxation of those measures from the same date.

## Important Dates

- **May 29, 2026**: Circular issued.
- **June 01, 2026**: Effective date for both inclusion of new securities (Annexure I) and exclusion of securities moving out (Annexure II).

## Impact Assessment

The circular has a targeted impact on the specific securities listed in the attached Annexures. Securities added to the Additional Measure framework may face tighter trading conditions, potentially reducing liquidity for those scrips. Securities removed from the framework will revert to standard trading conditions, which may improve their liquidity. Market participants holding or trading the affected securities should review the Annexures to assess their exposure and adjust trading strategies accordingly.
