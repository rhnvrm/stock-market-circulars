## Summary

The Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd. (ICCL) has issued a reminder regarding the transition to accessing the RTRMS (Real-Time Risk Management System) application through Single Sign-On (SSO). Originally announced via ICCL Circular no. 20260212-15 dated February 12, 2026, the implementation date has been postponed to **March 14, 2026** to ensure a smooth transition for all members and participants.

## Key Points

- The RTRMS application will be accessed exclusively through Single Sign-On (SSO) effective March 14, 2026.
- This is a postponement from the original implementation date set in the February 12, 2026 circular.
- All BSE members and participants are required to transition to the SSO-based login.
- Separate support contacts are provided for SSO login queries and user mapping queries.

## Regulatory Changes

Access to the RTRMS application will be migrated from direct login to a centralized Single Sign-On (SSO) system. This is an operational/technical change mandated by ICCL for all clearing members and participants.

## Compliance Requirements

- All members/participants must ensure they are set up to access RTRMS via SSO before March 14, 2026.
- Members needing assistance with SSO login should contact: **BSE.SSOsupport@bseindia.com** or call **022-2272 5033**.
- Members needing assistance with mapping of users in the RTRMS application should contact: **bsehelp@bseindia.com** or call **022-6915 8500 / 022-4572 0400 / 022-4572 0600**.

## Important Dates

- **February 12, 2026**: Original ICCL Circular no. 20260212-15 issued mandating SSO transition.
- **March 6, 2026**: Reminder circular issued with postponement notice.
- **March 14, 2026**: Revised implementation deadline for mandatory SSO-based access to RTRMS.

## Impact Assessment

This change affects all BSE members and participants who use the RTRMS application for risk management and settlement operations. The postponement provides additional preparation time, but full compliance is mandatory by March 14, 2026. Members who have not yet completed user mapping or SSO configuration should do so immediately to avoid disruption to their RTRMS access.
