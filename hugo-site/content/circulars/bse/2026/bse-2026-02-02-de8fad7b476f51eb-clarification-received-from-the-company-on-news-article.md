## Summary

BSE has issued a surveillance notice seeking clarification from CreditAccess Grameen Ltd (Scrip Code: 541770) regarding news articles appearing in various newspapers, websites, and television channels. The exchange is verifying the accuracy of these news reports to inform members and investors. The company's response is currently awaited. The news article reference is from Moneycontrol.

## Key Points

- BSE Surveillance Department has sought clarification from CreditAccess Grameen Ltd
- Action taken to verify news articles appearing across multiple media platforms
- Purpose is to inform members and investors about the accuracy of news reports
- Company response is currently pending
- Notice issued by Sanjay Jain (Deputy Vice President, Surveillance) and Soumil Shah (Deputy Vice President, Surveillance)
- Scrip Code: 541770
- News source referenced: Moneycontrol

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced in this circular. This is a routine surveillance action by the exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- CreditAccess Grameen Ltd is required to provide clarification to BSE regarding the news articles in question
- The company must respond to the exchange's query to inform stakeholders about the accuracy of media reports

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: February 2, 2026
- **Notice Number**: 20260202-29
- Response deadline not specified in the circular

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - The surveillance action indicates BSE is monitoring news flow that may be affecting the stock. Investors should await the company's clarification before making trading decisions based on the news articles in question.

**Operational Impact**: Low to Medium - The company needs to allocate resources to review the news articles and provide accurate information to the exchange.

**Investor Impact**: Pending clarification from the company will help investors assess the veracity of news reports and make informed decisions. Until clarification is received, uncertainty may affect trading activity in the stock.
