## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that several companies have fixed record dates for part redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs). Trading in the debentures of these companies will be conducted at reduced face values effective from the respective ex-dates.

## Key Points

- Eight companies (across ten scrip codes) are undertaking part redemption of NCDs with record dates between 12–14 June 2026
- Ex-dates for most scrips are 12 June 2026; Satin Creditcare Network Limited and Laxmi India Finance Limited have ex-dates of 12 June 2026 with record dates of 14 June 2026 and 13 June 2026 respectively
- All notices are under Settlement DR 650/2026-2027
- Reduced face values vary significantly by scrip, ranging from Rs. 2,500/- to Rs. 616,700/- per debenture
- Trading members must ensure transactions reflect the reduced face values from the applicable ex-dates

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard corporate action notification under existing BSE listing and debt segment regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must update their systems to reflect the reduced face values for the affected debentures from the respective ex-dates
- Trades in these debentures must be executed at the reduced face values on and after the ex-date

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects holders and traders of the listed NCDs. Investors holding these debentures will receive part redemption payments reducing the outstanding face value of their holdings. Trading members must reflect the updated face values in their systems to ensure correct pricing. The impact is limited to debenture market participants; there is no effect on equity markets or broader trading operations.
