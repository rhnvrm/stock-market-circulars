## Summary

BSE has notified the movement of certain securities to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 22, 2026. The affected securities, listed in the attached Annexure, will be subject to enhanced surveillance actions corresponding to their respective GSM stage, ranging from increased margin requirements at Stage I to highly restricted weekly trading with full Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) at Stage IV.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in the Annexure will move to higher GSM stages from June 22, 2026
- Four GSM stages apply, each with progressively stricter trading restrictions
- Stage I: 100% applicable margin rate with price band of 5% or lower
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% price band, ASD of 50% of trade value by buyers
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade, 5% price band, weekly trading (Mondays only), ASD of 100% of trade value by buyers
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III with an additional restriction of no upward price movement
- No netting off is permitted; all settlement is on trade-to-trade basis

## Regulatory Changes

This notice is issued under the GSM Framework originally published on February 23, 2017 (Exchange notice no. 20170223-44), with subsequent amendments on March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023. Securities are being elevated to higher GSM stages as part of ongoing surveillance oversight of equity market participants.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must take adequate precautions while trading in GSM-listed securities
- Buyers in Stage II must deposit ASD of 50% of trade value
- Buyers in Stages III and IV must deposit ASD of 100% of trade value
- No netting off is permitted in any GSM stage; all trades settle on a trade-to-trade basis
- For Stage III and IV securities, trading is restricted to once a week (every Monday or first trading day of the week)
- Members may refer to the GSM FAQ or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 19, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 22, 2026 (Monday — GSM stage movements take effect)

## Impact Assessment

The movement of securities to higher GSM stages significantly restricts their tradability and liquidity. Stage III and IV securities face the most severe constraints — weekly trading windows and full trade-value ASD requirements will deter participation and compress volumes. Investors holding these securities may face difficulty exiting positions. The trade-for-trade settlement across all stages eliminates intraday netting, increasing capital requirements for traders. Members should review the Annexure to identify affected securities and notify clients accordingly.
