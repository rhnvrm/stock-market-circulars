## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Equity segment on Saturday, February 07, 2026. This session allows trading members to test their systems and fulfill regulatory requirements as per SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 dated November 24, 2020. There will be no new BOLT TWS release for this session. Members can participate either in the mock trading session or UAT environment to meet compliance obligations.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Saturday, February 07, 2026 for Equity segment
- No new BOLT TWS release planned
- Members can choose between mock trading session or UAT environment for regulatory compliance
- Third-party trading platform users can test various functionalities including exceptional market conditions
- Testing opportunities include: call auction sessions, risk-reduction mode, trading halt, block deals, and other features
- Feedback required from all members by 4:30 pm on the mock trading day
- Members should refer to Exchange Notice No 20201201-22 dated December 1, 2020 regarding testing requirements

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This circular facilitates compliance with existing SEBI requirements under circular no. SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 dated November 24, 2020 regarding testing of software used in trading and risk management.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must participate in either mock trading session or UAT environment to fulfill regulatory requirements
- Members using third-party trading platforms should utilize this opportunity to test their trading applications
- All members are requested to provide feedback by 4:30 pm on the mock trading day
- Members should test various functionalities including exceptional market conditions, call auctions, risk-reduction mode, trading halts, and block deals

- Log-in: 10:15 am - 10:45 am

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** None - This is a mock trading session with no real market transactions.

**Operational Impact:** Low - Routine testing session that allows members to validate their trading systems and ensure compliance with regulatory requirements. Members can test various trading functionalities in a controlled environment without affecting live markets.

**Member Impact:** Participation is voluntary but recommended for maintaining system readiness and regulatory compliance. Third-party platform users have an opportunity to test their applications comprehensively.
