## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction list for Settlement No. 810 dated February 2, 2026. The auction includes 96 securities across various sectors where delivery shortages have occurred. The list specifies scrip codes, names, and quantities available for auction to facilitate settlement of outstanding delivery obligations.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 810
- Date: February 2, 2026
- Total securities affected: 96 different scrips
- Covers major stocks including GAIL, ICICI Bank, TCS, Tata Motors, Siemens, Vedanta, and others
- Quantities range from as low as 1 share to as high as 500,000 shares (Rajnish Wellness - 500,000 shares, South Indian Bank - 253,000 shares)
- Includes securities from various sectors: banking, infrastructure, energy, metals, technology, and consumer goods

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational circular for demat auction settlement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members with delivery shortages must participate in the auction or procure securities to fulfill obligations
- Members must ensure settlement of their outstanding delivery positions through the auction mechanism
- Members should refer to BSE auction rules and procedures for participation requirements

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: February 2, 2026 (Settlement No. 810)
- Auction participants must comply with BSE's prescribed timelines for auction settlement

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - Affects 96 securities with varying quantities. Large auction quantities in some scrips (e.g., Rajnish Wellness - 500,000 shares, South Indian Bank - 253,000 shares) could impact price discovery during auction.

**Operational Impact:** Directly impacts trading members with delivery shortages in these securities. Members must arrange funds and participate in auction to fulfill settlement obligations.

**Investor Impact:** Limited direct impact on regular investors. Primarily operational matter for trading members and brokers to settle delivery obligations.
