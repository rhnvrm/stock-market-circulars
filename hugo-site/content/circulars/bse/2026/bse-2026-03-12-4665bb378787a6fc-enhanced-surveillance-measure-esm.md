## Summary

BSE has issued an update to the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework effective March 13, 2026. No new securities are being added to ESM. Shukra Bullions Ltd (Scrip Code: 531506, ISIN: INE561E01015) is being moved to a higher ESM stage. No securities are being moved to a lower stage or removed from the ESM framework. The consolidated list includes a large number of securities across ESM Stage I and Stage II.

## Key Points

- No new securities shortlisted into ESM framework w.e.f. March 13, 2026 (Nil)
- Shukra Bullions Ltd (531506 / INE561E01015) moved to a higher ESM stage w.e.f. March 13, 2026
- No securities moved to a lower ESM stage (Nil)
- No securities moving out of ESM framework (Nil)
- Consolidated list contains securities across ESM Stage I and Stage II
- Annotations: \* denotes securities as per NSE; # denotes SME scrips

## Regulatory Changes

The ESM framework stage assignment for Shukra Bullions Ltd has been elevated to a higher stage, subjecting it to stricter surveillance and trading conditions. No securities have been delisted from ESM or added fresh to the framework in this update.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and investors must note the revised ESM stage for Shukra Bullions Ltd (531506)
- All securities in the consolidated ESM list remain subject to their respective ESM stage restrictions (trade-to-trade settlement, price bands, margin requirements, etc.)
- Members should ensure compliance with applicable ESM stage conditions for all listed securities in Annexure III

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** March 13, 2026 — all changes to ESM stage assignments take effect

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited in scope, affecting primarily Shukra Bullions Ltd which faces tighter trading restrictions due to its elevation to a higher ESM stage. The broader market impact is low since no new securities are entering ESM and none are exiting. The consolidated list (Annexure III) confirms that a significant number of securities remain under ESM surveillance across Stage I and Stage II, indicating ongoing regulatory oversight of lower-liquidity and higher-risk scrips. Investors holding positions in any ESM-listed security should be aware of the associated settlement and margin conditions.
