## Summary

BSE Surveillance (Department of Surveillance & Supervision - DOSS) has issued Notice No. 20260424-17 dated 24 April 2026, identifying 30 companies that exhibited significant price and/or volume movement in recent trading sessions. The Exchange wrote to all listed companies seeking clarifications to ensure trading members and investors have access to the latest relevant information. Replies have been received from 11 companies, while 19 companies are yet to respond.

## Key Points

- BSE observed significant price/volume movement in 30 equity-segment scrips and initiated clarification requests under its surveillance mandate.
- 11 companies have submitted their replies (attached as Replies1 and Replies2 to the notice).
- 19 companies have not yet responded and remain under surveillance scrutiny.
- The notice is issued by the Surveillance department (DOSS) and signed by Deputy Vice President Sanjay Jain and Manager Shreya Ghose.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This notice is part of BSE's ongoing surveillance framework to protect investors by ensuring timely disclosure of material information when abnormal trading patterns are detected.

## Compliance Requirements

- The 19 companies that have not yet replied are expected to submit their clarifications to BSE Surveillance promptly.
- All listed companies are obligated to respond to BSE queries regarding unusual price/volume activity under SEBI's listing obligations and disclosure requirements.
- Continued non-response may result in additional surveillance actions, including trading restrictions.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** 24 April 2026
- **Reply Deadline:** Not explicitly stated; companies are expected to respond without delay given active surveillance status.

## Impact Assessment

This is a standard surveillance disclosure. For the 11 companies that have replied, investor concerns about undisclosed information may be partially addressed upon review of the attached clarifications. For the 19 non-responding companies, continued surveillance scrutiny could lead to escalated measures such as trading halts or placement under the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) or Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework if material information remains undisclosed. Retail investors should review the attached company replies before trading in these securities.
