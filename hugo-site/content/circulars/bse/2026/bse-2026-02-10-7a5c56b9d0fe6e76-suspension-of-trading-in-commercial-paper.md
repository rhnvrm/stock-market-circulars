## Summary

BSE has suspended trading in commercial papers of 13 companies effective from their respective record dates due to redemption. Five companies have a record date of February 13, 2026, and eight companies have a record date of February 16, 2026. Trading members are advised not to deal in these commercial papers from the specified dates.

## Key Points

- 13 companies have fixed record dates for commercial paper redemption
- 5 commercial papers suspended from February 13, 2026
- 8 commercial papers suspended from February 16, 2026
- Trading members instructed not to deal in listed commercial papers
- All suspensions fall under DR-820/2025-2026 or DR-821/2025-2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard corporate action notification for commercial paper redemptions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the specified commercial papers from their respective no-dealings dates
- Members must ensure systems are updated to prevent trading in suspended instruments
- Compliance with record date requirements for redemption processing

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - affects debt market participants holding or trading these specific commercial papers. No impact on equity markets.

**Operational Impact**: Trading platforms must update suspension lists to prevent inadvertent trading in these instruments during redemption period.

**Investor Impact**: Holders of these commercial papers will receive redemption proceeds as per the terms. No new positions can be established from the suspension dates.
