## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that dealings in Rights Entitlements (REs) for renunciation of MARG TECHNO-PROJECTS LIMITED will be permitted on the Exchange from Monday, June 29, 2026 to Monday, July 1, 2026. The REs are tradable in dematerialized form only and will be settled on a T+1 rolling settlement basis on a trade-for-trade basis.

## Key Points

- Rights Entitlements (REs) for MARG TECHNO-PROJECTS LIMITED will be available for trading/renunciation from June 29 to July 1, 2026.
- Scrip Code: 751124, Scrip ID: MTPL-RE, ISIN: INE245H20018, Group: R, Market Lot: 1.
- Settlement is on T+1 rolling basis; trades settled on trade-for-trade basis.
- REs are tradable in dematerialized form only.
- The underlying Rights Issue is open from June 29, 2026 to July 6, 2026.
- REs not subscribed or renounced before the Issue Closing Date will lapse and be extinguished.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This notice is issued under the framework established by BSE notices 20200305-21 (March 5, 2020) and 20200518-34 (May 18, 2020) governing rights entitlement trading.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must inform all clients about the commencement of trading in REs, specifying the ISIN (INE245H20018), and clarify that REs are not ordinary shares.
- Trading members must include a prominent disclaimer in contract notes/bills for RE purchases, stating that purchase only confers the right to participate in the rights issue or renounce the REs before issue close.
- Members must ensure clients are aware that unsubscribed and unrenounced REs will lapse after the Issue Closing Date.

## Important Dates

- **June 29, 2026**: Trading in REs commences; Rights Issue opens.
- **July 1, 2026**: Last day for trading/renunciation of REs.
- **July 6, 2026**: Rights Issue closes.
- After July 6, 2026: Unrenounced/unsubscribed REs lapse and are extinguished.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a moderate and targeted impact, primarily affecting existing shareholders of MARG TECHNO-PROJECTS LIMITED who hold rights entitlements and trading members facilitating renunciation transactions. The short three-day trading window (June 29–July 1) and trade-for-trade settlement basis limit speculative activity. Shareholders must act within the window to either subscribe or renounce their entitlements to avoid lapse.
