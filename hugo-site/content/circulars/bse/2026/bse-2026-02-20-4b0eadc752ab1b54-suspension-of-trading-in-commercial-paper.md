## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260220-54 (DR-829/2025-2026 and DR-830/2025-2026) instructs all trading members to immediately cease dealing in the commercial papers of 25 companies that have fixed record dates of 26 February 2026 or 27 February 2026 for redemption. The suspension takes effect from the respective record date for each instrument.

## Key Points

- Trading members must not deal in the listed commercial papers from their respective "No Dealings From" dates.
- 22 commercial papers have a record date of **26 February 2026** (no dealings from 26 Feb 2026).
- At least 3 commercial papers have a record date of **27 February 2026** (no dealings from 27 Feb 2026).
- All suspensions are for the purpose of **Redemption of Commercial Paper**.
- Covered under circular references DR-829/2025-2026 and DR-830/2025-2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No permanent regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notification under BSE's debt market procedures requiring cessation of trading ahead of commercial paper redemption record dates.

## Compliance Requirements

- **All trading members** on BSE must immediately halt buying or selling of the listed commercial paper instruments from their respective "No Dealings From" dates.
- Members should update their systems and desk controls to block trades in these ISINs from the effective dates.
- Reference circulars DR-829/2025-2026 and DR-830/2025-2026 should be noted for record-keeping.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt-segment administrative action with **medium impact** on debt market participants. Trading members active in the commercial paper segment must comply by ceasing to deal in the specified instruments from the stated dates. There is no direct impact on equity markets. Investors holding these commercial papers should expect full redemption on the respective record dates. The circular affects a broad set of issuers spanning housing finance, financial services, manufacturing (Tata Steel, KEC International), and NBFC sectors, indicating normal end-of-tenor redemptions for short-term debt instruments maturing around 26–27 February 2026.
