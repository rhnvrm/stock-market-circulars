## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that additional equity shares issued by 14 companies under Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP/ESOS) are listed and admitted for trading on the Exchange effective **Tuesday, June 2, 2026**. None of the newly listed securities are subject to any lock-in period.

## Key Points

- 14 companies have issued fresh shares under ESOP/ESOS schemes that are now listed on BSE.
- Effective date for trading: **June 2, 2026**.
- No lock-in period applies to any of the listed shares.
- HDFC Bank has the largest allotment in absolute terms: **1,316,960 shares** (Face Value ₹1).
- IDFC First Bank has the second largest allotment: **2,594,143 shares** (Face Value ₹10).
- All shares have been assigned distribution number ranges confirming exact ISIN-level traceability.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice under the existing ESOP/ESOS listing framework.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are required to take note of the newly listed securities and their respective ISIN codes and distribution number ranges. No additional compliance action is required from market participants.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 1, 2026
- **Effective Trading Date:** June 2, 2026 (Tuesday)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, low-impact notice. The newly issued shares represent standard ESOP/ESOS allotments and will have negligible dilution effect on the overall float of the large-cap companies involved (HDFC Bank, Axis Bank, HDFC Life, IDFC First Bank, Aditya Birla Capital). For smaller companies such as Globus Spirits (12,373 shares) and Gopal Snacks (18,400 shares), the allotments are minimal. No market disruption is anticipated.
