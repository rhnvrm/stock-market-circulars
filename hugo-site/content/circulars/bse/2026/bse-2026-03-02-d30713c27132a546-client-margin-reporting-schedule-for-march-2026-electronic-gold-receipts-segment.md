## Summary

Indian Clearing Corporation Limited (ICCL) has published the client margin reporting schedule for the Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) segment for March 2026. The schedule specifies, for each trade date, the last date by which members must report client margins and the date from which penalties will apply for non-compliance.

## Key Points

- The schedule covers trade dates from 02-03-2026 through 31-03-2026.
- For each trade date, a "Last Date of Margin Reporting" and a "Penalty Date" are specified.
- Penalty dates fall one business day after the last reporting date in most cases.
- The schedule accounts for market holidays (e.g., no trading on certain dates, with gaps visible in the schedule).
- Issued by Manoj Panchal and Omkar Hatkar, both Deputy Managers at ICCL.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is a routine periodic schedule issued under existing margin reporting obligations for the Electronic Gold Receipts segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members trading in the Electronic Gold Receipts segment must report client margins by the "Last Date of Margin Reporting" for each respective trade date.
- Failure to report by the stipulated deadline will attract penalties effective from the "Penalty Date".
- Members should update their internal compliance calendars with the dates provided in this circular.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a routine operational impact on members participating in the Electronic Gold Receipts segment. Members must ensure timely client margin reporting to avoid penalties. The reporting window extends into early April 2026 for trades executed at end of March. No broader market or systemic impact is expected from this circular.
