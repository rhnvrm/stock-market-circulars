## Summary

The Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd. (ICCL) has issued a revised settlement schedule for the Offer to Buy – Acquisition Window (Takeover) pertaining to Kwality Wall's (India) Limited. This revision references the original Notice No. 20260417-6 and updates the obligation download and settlement dates for Settlement No. 2026038.

## Key Points

- Settlement Type: Takeover; Settlement No. 2026038
- Bid period ran from 23/04/2026 to 07/05/2026
- Revised Obligation Download Date: 13/05/2026
- Revised Settlement Date: 14/05/2026
- Demat shares must be delivered exclusively via the Early Pay-in of Shares mechanism available through both depositories
- Members/Custodians must use Market Type "(Takeover)" and Settlement No. 2026038 in the depository system
- Stamp Duty charges are available per client in the MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo file

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This notice revises previously communicated settlement calendar dates under Notice No. 20260417-6. Members are also directed to refer to Notice No. 20200528-32 (dated 28 May 2020) regarding the Revised Guidelines of Mechanism for acquisition of shares through Stock Exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and Custodians must deliver demat shares only through the Early Pay-in of Shares mechanism
- Correct Market Type "(Takeover)" and Settlement No. 2026038 must be entered in the depository system
- Members should refer to MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo file for client-wise Stamp Duty charges
- Any clarifications should be directed to ICCL Help Desk at 022-69158590 / 45720490 / 45720690 or iccl.helpdesk@icclindia.com

## Important Dates

- Bid Start Date: 23/04/2026
- Bid End Date: 07/05/2026
- Revised Obligation Download Date: 13/05/2026
- Revised Settlement Date: 14/05/2026

## Impact Assessment

This revision affects members and custodians participating in the Kwality Wall's (India) Limited takeover acquisition window. The updated settlement timeline provides a short operational window (13–14 May 2026) for obligation download and final settlement. Participants must ensure timely Early Pay-in of shares to avoid settlement failures. The impact is limited to entities directly involved in this specific takeover transaction.
