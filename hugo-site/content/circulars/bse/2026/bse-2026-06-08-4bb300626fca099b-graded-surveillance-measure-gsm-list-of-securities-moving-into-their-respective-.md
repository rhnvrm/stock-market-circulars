## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that securities listed in the attached Annexure will be moved to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 09, 2026. The move is pursuant to the GSM Framework published on February 23, 2017 and subsequent exchange notices. Affected securities will attract escalating surveillance actions depending on their assigned GSM stage.

## Key Points

- Securities in the Annexure will shift to higher GSM stages from June 09, 2026
- Four GSM stages with progressively stricter trading restrictions apply
- Stage I: 100% applicable margin rate and price band of 5% or lower
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% price band, and Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value to be deposited by buyers
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% price band, trading permitted once a week (every Monday/1st trading day), ASD of 100% of trade value by buyers
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III with an additional restriction of no upward price movement
- No netting off is permitted; all settlement on trade-to-trade basis

## Regulatory Changes

Securities are being escalated within the existing GSM framework originally established via Exchange Notice No. 20170223-44 (February 23, 2017), with subsequent amendments via notices dated March 03, 2017; July 20, 2018; November 29, 2019; and November 17, 2023. This notice implements a periodic review outcome moving securities to higher GSM stages.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must take adequate precautions while trading in GSM-listed securities
- Buyers in Stage II must deposit ASD of 50% of trade value
- Buyers in Stages III and IV must deposit ASD of 100% of trade value
- No netting off of positions is permitted in any GSM stage; all trades settled on trade-to-trade basis
- Members may refer to the BSE FAQ on GSM at http://www.bseindia.com/markets/equity/EQReports/graded_surveil_measure.aspx?expandable=6 or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications

## Important Dates

- **June 08, 2026**: Notice issued
- **June 09, 2026**: GSM stage movements take effect for securities listed in the Annexure

## Impact Assessment

Securities moved to higher GSM stages face significantly reduced liquidity and tradability. Stage III and IV securities are tradable only once a week, with mandatory 100% ASD deposits by buyers, effectively creating high barriers to entry for retail and institutional participants. The 5% price band further limits daily price discovery. Investors holding these securities may find exit opportunities constrained, and the restrictions signal heightened regulatory concern about trading patterns or fundamentals of the affected companies. Market participants should review the Annexure carefully to identify specific securities impacted.
