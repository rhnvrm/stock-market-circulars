## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, July 25, 2026, to be conducted from both the disaster recovery (DR) site and the primary (PR) site. The session allows trading members, including those using third-party trading platforms of empaneled vendors or in-house systems via ETI APIs, to test their trading applications under various functionalities, including exceptional market conditions such as risk-reduction mode and trading halts.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, July 25, 2026.
- Session will run from both DR (disaster recovery) and PR (primary) sites.
- No new BOLT TWS Release will be issued for this session.
- Members can test functionalities like risk-reduction mode and trading halt.
- Applicable to trading members using third-party empaneled vendor platforms or in-house systems through ETI APIs.
- Members are requested to submit feedback on the mock trading session by 3:00 pm on the day of the session.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an operational/testing notice, not a regulatory change.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are encouraged to actively participate in the mock trading session and submit feedback via the contact numbers/email provided (BSE Tech Support: 022-22728053, bse.tech@bseindia.com; Help Desk: 022-45720400/600, 022-69158500, bsehelp@bseindia.com) by 3:00 pm on the session day.

## Important Dates

- **Mock Trading Date:** Saturday, July 25, 2026
- **Log-in:** 07:30 am – 08:00 am
- **Continuous Trading (DR):** 08:00 am – 10:45 am
- **Switchover to PR:** 12:30 pm – 01:15 pm
- **Continuous Trading (PR):** 01:15 pm – 02:00 pm
- **Feedback Deadline:** 3:00 pm on session day

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market impact as it pertains solely to a testing exercise. Trades executed during the mock session will not attract margin obligations, pay-in/pay-out obligations, or create any rights and liabilities. The notice is primarily relevant to trading members and technology vendors to ensure system readiness and business continuity preparedness for the Commodity Derivatives segment.
