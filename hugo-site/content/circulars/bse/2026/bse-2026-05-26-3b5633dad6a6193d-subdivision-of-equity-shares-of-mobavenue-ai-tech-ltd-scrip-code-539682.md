## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that Mobavenue AI Tech Ltd (Scrip Code: 539682) has fixed **June 12, 2026** as the record date for the subdivision of its equity shares. Each existing equity share of Rs.10/- face value will be split into five equity shares of Rs.2/- face value each.

## Key Points

- Stock split ratio: 1 share of Rs.10/- face value → 5 shares of Rs.2/- face value
- Record date: June 12, 2026
- New sub-divided paid-up value effective from: June 12, 2026
- Existing ISIN INE622Q01019 (Rs.10/- paid up) will be invalid for exchange transactions on or after June 12, 2026
- New ISIN for Rs.2/- paid-up shares will be communicated via a separate notice
- Segment: Dematerialised Securities – Rolling Settlement

## Regulatory Changes

The subdivision is being executed under BSE Notice DR-650/2026-2027. The existing ISIN INE622Q01019 will cease to be valid for exchange transactions from June 12, 2026 onwards. A new ISIN will be assigned to the sub-divided shares.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must cease using ISIN INE622Q01019 for transactions on or after June 12, 2026
- Members should await the separate BSE notice announcing the new ISIN for the Rs.2/- paid-up shares before executing post-record-date trades
- Back-office systems should be updated to reflect the new face value and ISIN upon issuance of the new ISIN notice

## Important Dates

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Circular Date | May 26, 2026 |
| Record Date | June 12, 2026 |

| New ISIN effective | June 12, 2026 (details via separate notice) |

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine corporate action (stock split) for Mobavenue AI Tech Ltd, a smaller-cap listed company. The 5-for-1 split will reduce the face value from Rs.10/- to Rs.2/-, increasing the number of shares in circulation fivefold while proportionally reducing the per-share price. This is typically undertaken to improve liquidity and affordability of the stock. Trading members and depository participants must ensure timely ISIN updates; failure to do so could result in settlement issues post-record date. Shareholders holding the stock in demat form will have their holdings automatically updated by the depository on the record date.
