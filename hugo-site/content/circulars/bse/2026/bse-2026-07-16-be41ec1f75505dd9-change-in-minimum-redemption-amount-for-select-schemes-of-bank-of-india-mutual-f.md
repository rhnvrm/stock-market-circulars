## Summary

BSE has informed members of a communication received from Bank of India Asset Management Company (AMC) regarding a revision in the minimum redemption amount applicable to select schemes of Bank of India Mutual Fund. The revised minimum redemption amount takes effect from July 17, 2026.

## Key Points

- BSE received communication from BANK OF INDIA AMC regarding a change in minimum redemption amount for select schemes
- Existing minimum redemption amount: Rs. 1000/- and in multiples of Re. 1/- thereafter
- Revised minimum redemption amount: Rs. 1/- and in multiples of Re. 1/- thereafter
- The change significantly lowers the entry threshold for investors seeking partial redemptions
- An Annexure and Notice-cum-Addendum issued by Bank of India AMC is attached to the notice for detailed reference
- Notice signed by Arunkumar Ganesan (VP - Mutual Fund) and Mahesh Tare (AVP - Mutual Fund)

## Regulatory Changes

The minimum redemption amount for select schemes of Bank of India Mutual Fund has been revised from a minimum of Rs. 1000/- (in multiples of Re. 1/- thereafter) to a minimum of Rs. 1/- (in multiples of Re. 1/- thereafter). This is an operational change communicated by the AMC and disseminated by BSE for the benefit of trading members handling mutual fund transactions.

## Compliance Requirements

Members dealing in mutual fund units on the BSE platform (BSE StAR MF) should update their systems and processes to reflect the revised minimum redemption amount for the affected schemes. Members should refer to the Annexure and Notice-cum-Addendum for the specific list of schemes covered under this revision.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 17, 2026
- Notice Date: July 16, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact operational notice affecting only mutual fund investors and distributors dealing with select Bank of India Mutual Fund schemes. The reduction in minimum redemption amount from Rs. 1000 to Re. 1 improves flexibility for investors wishing to make smaller partial redemptions, but has no bearing on equity markets, listed securities, or broader trading operations.
