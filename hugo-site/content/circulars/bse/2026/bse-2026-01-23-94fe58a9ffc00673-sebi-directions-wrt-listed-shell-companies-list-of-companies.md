## Summary

BSE has announced that Alka India Ltd. (Scrip Code: 530889) will be removed from Stage 3 of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective January 29, 2026. This follows an NCLT-approved resolution plan dated February 07, 2025. Simultaneously, the trading suspension on the company's securities will be revoked, and the stock will be part of the Special Pre-open Session (SPOS) for IPOs and relisted scrips.

## Key Points

- Alka India Ltd. (Scrip Code: 530889) to exit Stage 3 of GSM framework
- Action taken pursuant to NCLT order dated February 07, 2025 approving resolution plan
- Trading suspension on the securities will be revoked
- Stock will participate in Special Pre-open Session (SPOS) for IPOs and relisted scrips
- Reference to previous notices: 20220506-12 (May 06, 2022) and 20240214-11 (February 14, 2024)
- Trading members should refer to notice 20120216-29 for SPOS details

## Regulatory Changes

The company is being moved out of the restrictive Stage 3 surveillance category under SEBI's shell company framework. This change reflects successful completion of a resolution plan approved by the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT), indicating the company has addressed the concerns that led to its classification as a shell company.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the exit from Stage 3 GSM framework
- Trading members should familiarize themselves with SPOS procedures by referring to notice 20120216-29
- Market participants should take note of the special pre-open session applicability
- For clarifications, contact BSE at 022-2272 8083

## Important Dates

- **January 23, 2026**: Circular issue date
- **January 29, 2026 (Thursday)**: Effective date for exit from Stage 3 GSM framework
- **January 29, 2026 (Thursday)**: Trading suspension revocation date
- **February 07, 2025**: NCLT order date approving resolution plan

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: The removal from Stage 3 GSM and lifting of trading suspension represents a positive development for Alka India Ltd. shareholders, as it restores normal trading conditions. The stock had been subject to enhanced surveillance and trading restrictions due to shell company classification.

**Operational Impact**: Trading will resume under SPOS mechanism, which provides a controlled re-entry into regular trading. This ensures price discovery in a fair and transparent manner on the first day of resumed trading.

**Investor Impact**: Shareholders can now trade the securities freely, though they should be aware of the SPOS process. The successful resolution plan approval indicates improved corporate governance and operational status of the company.
