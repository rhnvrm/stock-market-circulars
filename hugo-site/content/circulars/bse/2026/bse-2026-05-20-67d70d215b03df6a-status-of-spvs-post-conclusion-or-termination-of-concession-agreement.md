## Summary

BSE has forwarded a SEBI circular (No. SEBI/HO/DDHS/DDHS-PoD-2/I/11698/2026 dated May 15, 2026) concerning the regulatory status of Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) following the conclusion or termination of their Concession Agreements. The circular is effective immediately and applies to listed entities in the Debt segment.

## Key Points

- SEBI issued Circular No. SEBI/HO/DDHS/DDHS-PoD-2/I/11698/2026 on May 15, 2026
- The circular addresses the status of SPVs once their Concession Agreement concludes or is terminated
- Effective immediately upon issuance
- Relevant to the Debt segment and monitored by BSE's Listing Compliance and Monitoring department
- The full SEBI circular (attachment: StatusofSPVCircular) is referenced for detailed provisions

## Regulatory Changes

SEBI has introduced guidance clarifying the regulatory and listing status of SPVs post-conclusion or termination of Concession Agreements. Listed SPV entities must review the attached SEBI circular for specific provisions governing their obligations and status under these circumstances.

## Compliance Requirements

- Listed entities that are SPVs under concession agreements must take note of the SEBI circular
- Entities must comply with the provisions outlined in the SEBI circular with immediate effect
- BSE's Listing Compliance and Monitoring department oversees adherence

## Important Dates

- **SEBI Circular Date:** May 15, 2026
- **BSE Notice Date:** May 20, 2026
- **Effective Date:** Immediate effect from date of SEBI circular (May 15, 2026)

## Impact Assessment

This circular primarily affects infrastructure-sector SPVs listed on BSE's Debt segment whose concession agreements are concluding or have been terminated. It provides regulatory clarity on post-concession obligations, which is important for debt investors and lenders exposed to such entities. The impact is targeted and medium in scope, as it applies to a specific category of listed entities rather than the broader market.
