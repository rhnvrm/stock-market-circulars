## Summary

BSE has informed members that, pursuant to earlier notice no. 20260619-33 dated June 19, 2026, the TATA MULTI SECTOR PASSIVE FOF will reopen for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions, including SIP, STP and SWP facilities, on the BSE StAR MF Platform with effect from July 14, 2026.

## Key Points

- TATA MULTI SECTOR PASSIVE FOF reopens for ongoing transactions on BSE StAR MF Platform from July 14, 2026.
- Six scheme/plan variants are covered: Regular Plan (Growth, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment) and Direct Plan (Growth, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment), all under the Equity category.
- ISINs specified for each variant: INF277KA1ES2, INF277KA1EU8, INF277KA1ET0, INF277KA1EV6, INF277KA1EX2, INF277KA1EW4.
- STP (Systematic Transfer Plan) minimum installment requirements are specified based on investment amount ranges to avoid rejection by the AMC.
- TATA AMC has confirmed that members must select the minimum number of installments as per the prescribed table when registering STPs across all frequencies (Daily/Weekly/Monthly/Quarterly).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; this is an operational update implementing a previously announced scheme reopening (referenced notice no. 20260619-33 dated June 19, 2026).

## Compliance Requirements

- Members/distributors processing STP registrations for this scheme must ensure the minimum installment count matches the investment amount range:
  - Rs. 500 to Rs. 999: minimum 12 installments
  - Rs. 1000 to Rs. 1999: minimum 6 installments
  - Rs. 2000 and above: minimum 3 installments
- Non-compliance with these installment requirements may result in rejection of STP registration by the AMC.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 14, 2026 — scheme reopens for ongoing subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, STP and SWP transactions.
- Reference Notice Date: June 19, 2026 (notice no. 20260619-33).
- Notice Issue Date: July 13, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine mutual fund platform operations notice affecting BSE StAR MF Platform members and distributors handling TATA MULTI SECTOR PASSIVE FOF transactions. It has no direct impact on listed equity securities or broader market trading activity, but is relevant for mutual fund distributors and investors seeking to resume systematic transactions in this specific fund scheme.
