## Summary

BSE has informed trading members that two companies have fixed record dates for part redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs). Following the redemption, trading in the respective debentures will continue at reduced face values from the specified ex-dates.

## Key Points

- ReNew Solar Energy (Jharkhand Five) Private Limited (Scrip Code: 976003, ISIN: INE154Z07011) has fixed 18 June 2026 as both the record date and ex-date, with the debenture face value reducing to Rs. 91,170 per debenture under Settlement DR 654/2026-2027.
- UGRO Capital Limited (Scrip Code: 975822, ISIN: INE583D07497) has fixed 26 June 2026 as the record date and 25 June 2026 as the ex-date, with the debenture face value reducing to Rs. 20,000 per debenture under Settlement DR 659/2026-2027.
- Trading members are requested to note that trading in the debentures will be conducted with the reduced face value from the respective ex-dates onward.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice for part redemption of NCDs as per existing debt segment listing compliance procedures.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must update their systems to reflect the reduced face values for these debentures from the applicable ex-dates.
- Settlements for trades done on or after the ex-date must be carried out at the reduced face values.

## Important Dates

| Company | Record Date | Ex Date | Settlement DR | Reduced Face Value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ReNew Solar Energy (Jharkhand Five) Pvt Ltd | 18 Jun 2026 | 18 Jun 2026 | 654/2026-2027 | Rs. 91,170/- per debenture |
| UGRO Capital Limited | 26 Jun 2026 | 25 Jun 2026 | 659/2026-2027 | Rs. 20,000/- per debenture |

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects holders of the specific NCD scrips of ReNew Solar Energy (Jharkhand Five) and UGRO Capital. Post part-redemption, the face value per debenture will be reduced, and secondary market trading will reflect the new reduced face values. Debenture holders as of the respective record dates will receive the part-redemption payment. The impact is limited to existing NCD holders of these two issuers and does not affect equity markets.
