## Summary

BSE has issued a clarificatory circular on the mandatory Digital Accessibility Audit requirements for Trading Members (TMs) under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, referencing SEBI Circular No. SEBI/HO/ITD-1/ITD_VIAP/P/CIR/2025/131 dated September 25, 2025, and Exchange notice no. 20250927-1 dated September 27, 2025. The clarification follows representations from Trading Members and aims to standardize implementation of accessibility audits across the industry in consultation with SEBI.

## Key Points

- All digital platforms of Trading Members must undergo an Accessibility Audit conducted by an IAAP (International Association of Accessibility Professionals)-certified accessibility professional.
- For standard applications/platforms used across multiple Trading Members without customisation (e.g., front-office CTCL solutions, back-office client access, e-KYC products, mobile/internet trading apps), TMs may coordinate with platform vendors to obtain a single accessibility audit report/certificate applicable to all users of that standard platform.
- Vendors providing such reports must issue a covering letter to each Trading Member specifying the platform details (including version number) and the credentials of the IAAP-certified auditor, along with a copy of the Audit Report.
- A checkpoint will be added to the Standardised Terms of Reference (TOR) for System Audits requiring System Auditors to validate that the vendor-provided IAAP certificate pertains to the standard, non-customised version of the platform.
- For TMs not required to submit a System Audit report, an alternative validation mechanism will be prescribed later.
- For platforms/applications that are unique or customised to a specific Trading Member (including websites, mobile/web apps, back-office/KYC systems), the TM must independently commission its own accessibility audit via an IAAP-certified professional.
- The circular clarifies that vendors are under no regulatory obligation to obtain or provide an Accessibility Audit Report; any such arrangement is purely contractual between the vendor and the Trading Member.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular does not introduce new regulatory obligations but clarifies and operationalizes existing SEBI requirements (SEBI Circular dated September 25, 2025) regarding accessibility compliance under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016. It introduces a pragmatic vendor-report-sharing mechanism for standard, non-customised platforms to reduce duplicative audit costs across Trading Members.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must ensure all digital platforms undergo IAAP-certified accessibility audits.
- For standard/vendor-supplied platforms, TMs may rely on a vendor-obtained audit report accompanied by a covering letter and closure status of audit findings.
- For customised/in-house platforms, TMs must commission independent accessibility audits.
- System Auditors (where applicable) must validate vendor-certificate applicability as part of the Standardised TOR.

## Important Dates

No new deadlines are specified in this circular; it references the original SEBI circular dated September 25, 2025, and BSE notice dated September 27, 2025. An alternative mechanism for TMs not subject to System Audit will be prescribed subsequently (date not specified).

## Impact Assessment

The impact is primarily operational and compliance-related for Trading Members, with low direct market impact. The clarification is expected to ease compliance burden by allowing shared vendor audit reports for standardised platforms, reducing costs and duplication for TMs using common software solutions, while still mandating independent audits for customised systems. No listed companies or securities are directly affected.
