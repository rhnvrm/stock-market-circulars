## Summary

BSE's Surveillance department (DOSS) issued Notice No. 20260416-29 dated April 16, 2026, regarding significant price and/or volume movement observed in 34 listed companies. The Exchange had written to these companies seeking clarifications to ensure trading members and investors have access to the latest relevant information. This notice discloses which companies have responded and which have not yet replied.

## Key Points

- BSE observed significant price/volume movement in 34 companies and sought clarifications from each
- 16 companies have submitted replies (attached as Replies1 and Replies2 to the notice)
- 18 companies have not yet submitted replies as of the notice date
- Action is part of BSE's routine market surveillance to protect investor interests
- Notice signed by Sanjay Jain (Deputy Vice President, Surveillance) and Shreya Ghose (Manager, Surveillance)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard surveillance disclosure under BSE's market monitoring framework requiring listed companies to explain unusual price or volume activity in their scrips.

## Impact Assessment

This notice is primarily informational for trading members and investors. The 16 companies that have already replied have fulfilled their disclosure obligation; investors should review the attached clarifications (Replies1 and Replies2) to assess any price-sensitive information. The 18 companies that have not yet replied remain under regulatory scrutiny, and continued non-response may attract further surveillance action from BSE. No trading halts or circuit modifications have been announced in this notice. Investors in scrips of the non-responding companies should exercise caution until clarifications are received.
