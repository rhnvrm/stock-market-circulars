## Summary

BSE has announced Demat Auction for Settlement No. 833 dated March 6, 2026. This auction covers securities that could not be delivered by sellers during normal settlement, requiring BSE to conduct a compulsory auction to fulfil the obligations of buyers. Approximately 90 scrips across various sectors are listed for auction.

## Key Points

- Settlement No. 833 demat auction conducted by Bombay Stock Exchange Ltd., Mumbai
- Date of auction: March 6, 2026
- Approximately 90 securities listed across diverse sectors including IT, pharma, energy, metals, and financial services
- Quantities range from 1 share (multiple scrips) to 27,542 shares (Route Mobile)
- Large quantity lots include: Route Mobile (27,542), Sofcom Systems (17,625), Vikran Engineering (5,607), Chandrima Me (9,762), Anupam Finse (13,909), Transgene Bio (12,213)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine operational circular under existing BSE auction settlement procedures for handling short deliveries.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants with short delivery positions in the listed scrips must participate in the auction process
- Buyers of auctioned securities will receive their shares through this settlement mechanism
- Sellers failing delivery are subject to auction prices plus applicable penalties as per BSE regulations

## Important Dates

- Auction Date: March 6, 2026
- Settlement No.: 833

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate, targeted impact. It directly affects buyers who did not receive shares in the normal settlement cycle for the listed scrips and sellers whose deliveries were short. The auction mechanism ensures buyers receive their securities. Notably large auction quantities for Route Mobile (27,542 shares), Sofcom Systems (17,625), Anupam Finserv (13,909), and Transgene Biomedicals (12,213) may indicate liquidity or operational issues in those specific counters. Most other scrips have very small quantities (1–200 shares), suggesting isolated short deliveries.
