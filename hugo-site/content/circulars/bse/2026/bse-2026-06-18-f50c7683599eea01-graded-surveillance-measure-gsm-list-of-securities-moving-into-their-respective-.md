## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure) will be moved to their respective higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework with effect from June 19, 2026. The move triggers progressively stringent surveillance actions depending on the assigned GSM stage.

## Key Points

- Securities in the Annexure will be elevated to higher GSM stages from June 19, 2026.
- Four GSM stages apply, each with escalating surveillance restrictions.
- Settlement for all affected securities will be on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting-off permitted.
- Trading members are advised to exercise adequate precautions while dealing in these securities.
- Clarifications can be sought via the BSE FAQ link or by writing to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Regulatory Changes

The GSM framework, originally published on February 23, 2017 (Notice No. 20170223-44) and subsequently amended, prescribes the following surveillance actions by stage:

| Stage | Surveillance Actions |
|-------|----------------------|
| I | 100% applicable margin rate AND price band of 5% or lower |
| II | Trade-for-trade with 5% price band; Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value deposited by buyers |
| III | Trade-for-trade with 5% price band; trading permitted once a week (every Monday / 1st trading day of the week); ASD of 100% of trade value deposited by buyers |
| IV | Same as Stage III with the additional restriction of no upward price movement |

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the GSM stage assignments for all securities listed in the Annexure.
- No netting-off is permitted; all trades must settle on a gross (trade-to-trade) basis.
- Buyers in Stage II securities must deposit ASD equal to 50% of trade value; buyers in Stage III and IV securities must deposit ASD equal to 100% of trade value.
- Members must apply the applicable margin rates and price bands as specified for each stage.
- Members are encouraged to review the GSM FAQ and advise their clients accordingly.

## Important Dates

- **June 18, 2026** – Notice date; GSM stage migration announced.
- **June 19, 2026** – Effective date; securities move to their respective higher GSM stages.

## Impact Assessment

The migration of securities to higher GSM stages significantly restricts trading activity and increases costs for market participants in those scrips. Trade-to-trade settlement eliminates intraday netting, reducing speculative activity. ASD requirements (50%–100% of trade value) substantially increase the capital burden on buyers, dampening demand. Stage III and IV securities are further limited to once-a-week trading, drastically reducing liquidity. Stage IV additionally caps any upward price movement. Collectively, these measures aim to deter manipulation and protect investors in illiquid or suspicious securities, but also render affected stocks difficult to trade for legitimate market participants until they are moved out of GSM stages.
