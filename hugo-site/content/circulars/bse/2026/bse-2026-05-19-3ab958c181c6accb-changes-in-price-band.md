## Summary

BSE has revised the price bands for six equity scrips to 5%, effective May 20, 2026. This tightening of price bands is a surveillance measure that limits the maximum permissible daily price movement for these securities, typically applied to scrips exhibiting unusual price or volume activity.

## Key Points

- Six scrips have had their price bands revised downward to 5%
- The revision is effective from May 20, 2026
- All affected scrips are in the Equity segment
- Action is taken by the DOSS (Department of Surveillance) department
- Trading members are directed to contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications

## Regulatory Changes

The price band for the following scrips has been revised to 5%:

| Scrip Code | Scrip Name | Revised Price Band |
|------------|------------|--------------------|
| 544628 | Astron Multigrain Ltd | 5% |
| 540597 | Containerway International Ltd | 5% |
| 533296 | Future Market Networks Ltd | 5% |
| 514322 | Kamadgiri Fashion Ltd | 5% |
| 539814 | LE Lavoir Ltd | 5% |
| 539835 | Superior Finlease Ltd | 5% |

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised price bands and ensure orders placed in these scrips comply with the new 5% circuit limits effective May 20, 2026
- Any pending orders exceeding the revised price band limits may need to be cancelled and re-entered within permissible ranges
- Members requiring clarification should write to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** May 19, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 20, 2026

## Impact Assessment

A 5% price band is among the most restrictive circuit limits applied to equity scrips on BSE, significantly curtailing daily price movement. This action by the Surveillance department typically signals concerns about price manipulation, unusual trading patterns, or speculative activity in these scrips. Investors and traders holding positions in these six stocks will face limited exit or entry options within a single trading session. The restriction reduces volatility risk but also constrains liquidity, potentially making it harder to execute large orders at desired prices.
