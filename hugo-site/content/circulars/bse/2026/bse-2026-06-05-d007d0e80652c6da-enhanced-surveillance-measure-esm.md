## Summary

BSE has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework effective June 8–9, 2026. Newly added securities will attract a minimum 100% margin and be shifted from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment. Certain securities will be downgraded to Stage I, while others will exit the ESM framework entirely.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I satisfy ESM inclusion criteria and will attract minimum 100% margin on all open positions as on June 8, 2026, and new positions from June 9, 2026
- Some securities in Annexure I will be moved to a lower stage (Stage I) under the ESM Framework w.e.f. June 8, 2026
- All ESM-qualifying securities will be shifted from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment w.e.f. June 9, 2026
- Securities moving to Stage II will trade under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction w.e.f. June 8, 2026
- Securities exiting the ESM Framework are listed in Annexure II, effective June 8, 2026
- A consolidated list of all securities under ESM is provided in Annexure III
- ESM shortlisting is a market surveillance action and should not be construed as adverse action against the concerned company

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the ESM framework originally established via notice no. 20230602-44 (June 2, 2023) and subsequently revised by notices in July 2023, August 2024, September 2024, October 2024, and July 2025. The current update revises the list of securities under surveillance with inclusions, stage downgrades, and exclusions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure minimum 100% margin collection on all open positions in newly included ESM securities as on June 8, 2026, and all new positions from June 9, 2026
- Members must adhere to Trade-for-Trade settlement rules for all ESM-listed securities from June 9, 2026
- Stage II securities must comply with the 2% price band restriction and Periodic Call Auction mechanism from June 8, 2026
- For clarifications, members may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **June 8, 2026**: Stage I downgrades take effect; Stage II securities begin Trade-for-Trade with 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction; ESM framework exits become effective
- **June 9, 2026**: 100% margin requirement applies to open positions held as of June 8, 2026, and all new positions; newly included securities shift to Trade-for-Trade segment

## Impact Assessment

The ESM framework update has significant market impact on affected securities. The mandatory 100% margin requirement substantially increases the cost of holding positions, potentially reducing participation and liquidity. The shift to Trade-for-Trade settlement eliminates netting benefits and increases settlement risk for market participants. Stage II securities face further restrictions with a tight 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction, severely limiting intraday trading. Securities exiting the framework will have their price bands reinstated to pre-ESM levels, unless they remain under another surveillance measure.
