## Summary

BSE has updated the list of securities subject to the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) for companies undergoing the Insolvency Resolution Process (IRP) under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). New securities satisfying the criteria have been added, and certain securities are being removed from the framework, both effective June 18, 2026.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I now qualify under the IBC-linked Additional Surveillance Measure, effective June 18, 2026.
- Securities listed in Annexure II are being removed from the IBC Framework effective June 18, 2026.
- Annexure III provides the full consolidated list of all securities currently under the IBC Framework.
- The framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by exchanges.
- Placement under the framework is purely a market surveillance action and should not be construed as adverse action against the company.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the IBC surveillance framework in continuation of earlier notices: 20180608-34 (June 8, 2018), 20220520-52 (May 20, 2022), 20230210-55 (February 10, 2023), and 20250905-47 (September 5, 2025). The periodic review results in additions and removals from the monitored securities list.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants and members must note the updated lists of securities entering and exiting the IBC Framework.
- Trading members should apply the applicable price bands for affected scrips accordingly.
- For securities moving out of the framework, the price band will revert to the band applicable before shortlisting, unless the scrip is subject to another surveillance measure (in which case that measure's price band prevails).
- Queries may be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **June 17, 2026**: Notice date.
- **June 18, 2026**: Effective date for securities entering and exiting the IBC Framework.

## Impact Assessment

Securities placed under this IBC surveillance framework are subject to additional trading restrictions, including modified price bands, which can limit intraday price movements and affect liquidity. Investors holding or trading these scrips should review the updated Annexures I, II, and III attached to the circular. Securities exiting the framework may see their prior price bands restored, potentially increasing trading flexibility. The measure is market-surveillance-driven and carries no direct regulatory or legal implications for the underlying insolvency proceedings.
