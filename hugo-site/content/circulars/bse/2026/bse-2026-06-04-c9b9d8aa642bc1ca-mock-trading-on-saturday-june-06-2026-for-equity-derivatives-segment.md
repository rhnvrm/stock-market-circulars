## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Equity Derivatives segment on Saturday, June 06, 2026. The session is intended for trading members to test their trading applications and fulfill regulatory requirements per SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 dated November 24, 2020.

## Key Points

- Mock trading is scheduled for Saturday, June 06, 2026, for the Equity Derivatives segment.
- Members may participate in the Mock Trading Session or UAT environment to meet regulatory testing requirements.
- Trading members using third-party platforms (IML/ETI APIs) can test functionalities including risk-reduction mode and trading halt scenarios.
- There will be no new BOLT TWS Release for this session.
- Feedback is requested from all members by 4:30 PM on the day of the session.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This session supports compliance with SEBI circular no. SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 (November 24, 2020) and Exchange Notice No. 20201201-22 (December 1, 2020) regarding testing of software used in or related to Trading and Risk Management.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must participate in the Mock Trading Session or UAT environment to fulfill SEBI regulatory requirements for system testing.
- Members are encouraged to test all relevant functionalities including exceptional market conditions (risk-reduction mode, trading halt, etc.).
- Feedback must be submitted by 4:30 PM on June 06, 2026, via the provided contact numbers or email addresses.

## Important Dates

- **Mock Trading Date:** Saturday, June 06, 2026
- **Log-in window:** 10:15 AM – 11:15 AM
- **Pre-Open Order Entry:** 11:00 AM – 11:07/08 AM
- **Pre-Open Order Matching:** 11:08 AM – 11:12 AM
- **Buffer Period:** 11:12 AM – 11:15 AM
- **Continuous Trading:** 11:15 AM – 03:30 PM
- **Feedback Deadline:** 4:30 PM on June 06, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice with no impact on live markets or listed securities. It affects trading members of the Equity Derivatives segment who are required to test their systems. Participation is encouraged to ensure system readiness and regulatory compliance.
