## Summary

BSE Index Services Private Limited announced changes to the BSE LARGEMID (60:40) STABLE DIVIDEND 50 Index. Four securities are being added and four are being dropped, effective at the open of Monday, June 22, 2026. The changes will be reflected in the end-of-day corporate action file (*.SDE) on June 19, 2026.

## Key Points

- Four stocks added and four stocks removed from the BSE LARGEMID (60:40) STABLE DIVIDEND 50 Index
- Changes effective at market open on June 22, 2026 (Monday)
- End-of-day corporate action file (*.SDE) updated on June 19, 2026
- Queries to be directed to bseindex@bseindia.com

## Index Changes

### Additions

| BSE Ticker | Company |
|---|---|
| 532555 | NTPC Ltd. |
| 511218 | Shriram Finance Limited |
| 532814 | Indian Bank |
| 532296 | Glenmark Pharmaceuticals Ltd. |

### Removals

| BSE Ticker | Company |
|---|---|
| 500480 | Cummins India Ltd. |
| 500180 | HDFC Bank Ltd. |
| 532174 | ICICI Bank Ltd. |
| 532321 | Zydus Lifesciences Limited |

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a periodic index rebalancing by BSE Index Services Private Limited (formerly Asia Index Pvt. Ltd.), a wholly owned subsidiary of BSE Ltd.

## Compliance Requirements

No direct compliance requirements for market participants. Funds, ETFs, and portfolios benchmarked against or tracking the BSE LARGEMID (60:40) STABLE DIVIDEND 50 Index will need to rebalance their holdings accordingly before or at the open on June 22, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **June 19, 2026**: Circular issued; end-of-day corporate action file (*.SDE) updated
- **June 22, 2026 (Monday)**: Changes effective at market open

## Impact Assessment

This rebalancing affects passive investors and fund managers tracking the BSE LARGEMID (60:40) STABLE DIVIDEND 50 Index. Notably, two major private-sector banks — HDFC Bank and ICICI Bank — are being dropped, while public-sector names NTPC and Indian Bank are added alongside Shriram Finance and Glenmark Pharmaceuticals. This shift may reflect a change in dividend yield or stability criteria within the index methodology. Tracking funds will face rebalancing activity in the affected securities around the June 22 effective date.
