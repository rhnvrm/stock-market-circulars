## Summary

BSE has notified that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure) will be moved to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 15, 2026. The move applies escalating surveillance actions depending on the stage assigned, ranging from increased margin requirements at Stage I to severely restricted weekly trading with no upward price movement at Stage IV.

## Key Points

- Securities in the Annexure will be placed in higher GSM stages from June 15, 2026
- GSM framework was originally published via Exchange notice no. 20170223-44 dated February 23, 2017, with subsequent amendments
- Stage I: 100% applicable margin rate and price band of 5% or lower
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade with 5% price band and Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value by buyers
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade with 5% price band, trading permitted once a week (every Monday/1st trading day), and ASD of 100% of trade value by buyers
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III with an additional restriction of no upward price movement
- No netting off is permitted; all settlements are done on a trade-to-trade basis

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes. This notice operates under the existing GSM framework established via notices dated February 23, 2017; March 03, 2017; July 20, 2018; November 29, 2019; and November 17, 2023.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the securities in the Annexure and apply the relevant surveillance actions per the GSM stage assigned
- Buyers in Stage II securities must deposit ASD of 50% of trade value
- Buyers in Stage III and IV securities must deposit ASD of 100% of trade value
- No netting off is permitted for any GSM securities; all trades are on a trade-to-trade basis
- Members are advised to take adequate precautions while trading in these securities

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 12, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 15, 2026 (securities move to their respective higher GSM stages)

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding securities moved to higher GSM stages will face significantly restricted trading conditions. Stage III and IV securities allow trading only once per week (Monday or first trading day), making exit or entry positions difficult. The ASD requirement of up to 100% of trade value substantially increases the capital burden for buyers. Stage IV's prohibition on upward price movement further limits trading activity. Members should review the Annexure carefully and update their risk management systems accordingly before the June 15, 2026 effective date.
