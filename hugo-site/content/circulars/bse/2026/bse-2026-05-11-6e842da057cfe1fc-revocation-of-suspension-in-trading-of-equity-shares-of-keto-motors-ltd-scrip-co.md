## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260511-28 informing trading members that the suspension in trading of equity shares of Keto Motors Ltd. (Scrip Code: 537392) will be revoked with effect from May 19, 2026. Trading will resume in the 'XT' group as mandated under SEBI's Master Circular on the subject.

## Key Points

- Trading suspension for Keto Motors Ltd. (Scrip Code: 537392) is being revoked effective May 19, 2026
- Trading will resume under the 'XT' group classification
- Revocation is pursuant to SEBI Master Circular No. HO/49/14/14(7)2025-CFD-POD2/I/3762/2026 dated January 30, 2026
- The Master Circular supersedes erstwhile SEBI Circular No. SEBI/HO/CFD/CMD/CIR/P/2020/12 dated January 22, 2020
- A company profile (Annexure I) is attached to the notice
- The Information Memorandum will be available on BSE's website under Corporates → Corporate Filings → Information Memorandum → Revocation

## Regulatory Changes

The revocation is governed by SEBI Master Circular No. HO/49/14/14(7)2025-CFD-POD2/I/3762/2026 dated January 30, 2026, which replaced the earlier SEBI Circular No. SEBI/HO/CFD/CMD/CIR/P/2020/12 dated January 22, 2020. Trading in revoked-suspension securities is classified under the 'XT' group.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are required to take note of the revocation and update their systems accordingly
- Members should refer to the Information Memorandum available on the BSE website before executing trades in the scrip
- Trading must be conducted under the 'XT' group designation as specified

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** May 11, 2026
- **Revocation Effective Date:** May 19, 2026 (trading resumes from this date)

## Impact Assessment

The revocation of suspension allows investors and trading members to resume buying and selling equity shares of Keto Motors Ltd. (Scrip Code: 537392) from May 19, 2026. The 'XT' group classification typically implies enhanced surveillance and specific trading conditions, which may affect liquidity and price discovery. Existing shareholders who were unable to exit positions during the suspension period will regain the ability to trade. The impact is confined to this single scrip and its stakeholders.
