## Summary

BSE has issued a notice informing trading members that certain securities (listed in the accompanying Annexure) will be moved to their respective higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework with effect from July 10, 2026. This action is taken under the GSM Framework published on February 23, 2017, and subsequent related notices, and is intended to enhance market surveillance and safeguard investor interests.

## Key Points

- Securities specified in the Annexure will move to higher GSM stages effective July 10, 2026.
- The GSM framework has four stages (I to IV), each with progressively stricter surveillance actions.
- All affected securities will trade on a trade-for-trade basis, with no netting off of trades permitted.
- Trading members are advised to exercise adequate precautions while dealing in these securities.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing GSM Framework (established via notices dated February 23, 2017, March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023) to a fresh set of securities. Surveillance actions by stage are as follows:

- **Stage I**: Applicable margin rate of 100% and price band of 5% or lower, as applicable.
- **Stage II**: Trade-for-trade with price band of 5% or lower, plus Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers.
- **Stage III**: Trade-for-trade with price band of 5% or lower, trading permitted only once a week (every Monday or the first trading day of the week), and ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers.
- **Stage IV**: Same as Stage III, but with no upward price movement permitted.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must identify securities listed in the Annexure and apply the corresponding GSM stage restrictions from the effective date.
- All trades in the affected securities must be settled on a trade-to-trade basis, with no netting off allowed.
- Members must collect the applicable margins and Additional Surveillance Deposits from clients as per the security's GSM stage.
- Queries should be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com or referenced against the published GSM FAQ.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: July 10, 2026 — securities move to their respective higher GSM stages.
- **Notice Date**: July 9, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, recurring surveillance action rather than a market-wide structural change. For the specific securities named in the Annexure, the impact is significant: reduced liquidity due to weekly trading restrictions (Stage III/IV), higher capital requirements from increased margins and ASD, and constrained price movement. Investors and traders holding or considering these securities should review the Annexure to confirm which stocks are affected and adjust trading strategies accordingly.
