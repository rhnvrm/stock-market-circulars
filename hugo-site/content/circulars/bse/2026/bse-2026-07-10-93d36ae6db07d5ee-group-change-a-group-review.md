## Summary

BSE has notified Trading Members of a periodic review revision to its Group 'A' companies list, effective from 15th July 2026. As part of this review, 49 companies will move out of Group 'A' (Annexure I), while 40 companies currently in Group 'B' will move into Group 'A' (Annexure II). The complete revised list of Group 'A' scrips is provided as Annexure III.

## Key Points

- The revision is part of BSE's periodic (typically semi-annual) review of Group 'A' classification.
- 49 companies move out of Group 'A', including Bombay Dyeing & Mfg.Co.Ltd, Delta Corp Ltd, SpiceJet Ltd, TTK Prestige Ltd, G R Infraprojects Ltd, Gujarat State Petronet Ltd, ITI Ltd, and Sunteck Realty Ltd.
- 40 companies move from Group 'B' to Group 'A', including HDB Financial Services Ltd, National Securities Depository Ltd, Indigo Paints Ltd, Siemens Energy India Ltd, Anthem Biosciences Ltd, Ather Energy Ltd, and Thyrocare Technologies Ltd.
- The change takes effect from 15th July 2026.
- The full revised Group 'A' list is attached as Annexure III to the notice.

## Regulatory Changes

BSE periodically reviews and revises the composition of its Group 'A' category of listed securities based on defined eligibility criteria (such as trading activity, market capitalisation, and compliance record). This notice implements the outcome of the latest review cycle, reclassifying 89 companies in total (49 moving out, 40 moving in).

## Compliance Requirements

Trading Members must update their systems and internal records to reflect the revised Group 'A' classification for the affected scrips effective 15th July 2026. Margin, surveillance, and trading rules applicable specifically to Group 'A' securities will apply to newly added companies and cease to apply to companies moving out, from the effective date.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: 15th July 2026
- Notice Date: 10th July 2026

## Impact Assessment

This reclassification affects trading norms, margin requirements, and derivative eligibility considerations tied to Group 'A' status for the 89 companies involved. Companies moving into Group 'A' (such as HDB Financial Services, NSDL, Indigo Paints, and Siemens Energy India) may see increased visibility and potentially altered margin/trading treatment, while companies moving out (such as SpiceJet, Delta Corp, and TTK Prestige) will transition to Group 'B' norms. The impact is primarily operational/procedural for trading members and does not reflect a fundamental change in the affected companies' business fundamentals.
