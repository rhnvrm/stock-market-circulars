## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that effective Monday, July 27, 2026, the equity shares of Gulf Lloyds (India) Limited will be listed and admitted to dealings on the Exchange under the 'MT' Group of Securities (SME segment). Per SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012, the scrip will trade in the Trade-for-Trade segment for the first 10 trading days.

## Key Points

- Company: Gulf Lloyds (India) Limited, registered office in Ahmedabad, Gujarat
- Number of securities: 67,29,200 equity shares of Rs. 10/- each, fully paid up
- Distinctive number range: 1 to 6729200
- Scrip ID/Abbreviated Name on BOLT: GULFLLOYDS
- Scrip Code: 544834
- ISIN: INE1WDC01012
- Market Lot: 1200 shares
- Issue Price: Rs. 100/- per share (Face value Rs. 10/-, premium Rs. 90/-)
- Date of allotment in public issue: July 23, 2026
- Trading permitted only in dematerialised form as per SEBI guidelines dated February 16, 2000
- Market Maker: Prabhat Financial Services Limited (SEBI Reg. No. INZ000169433)
- Registrar to the issue: KFin Technologies Limited (SEBI Reg. No. INR000000221)

## Regulatory Changes

None; this is an operational listing notice implementing existing SEBI framework for SME IPO listings and trade-for-trade requirements under CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure trades in this scrip are executed only in dematerialised form and in minimum market lot of 1200 equity shares.
- The scrip will be part of the Special Pre-open Session (SPOS) on the listing day, July 27, 2026, per Exchange notice no. 20120216-29.
- The company has executed corporate action to mark the lock-in period in depository records as per Annexure I.

## Important Dates

- Date of Allotment: July 23, 2026
- Listing and trading commencement: Monday, July 27, 2026
- Trade-for-Trade segment applicable for 10 trading days from listing

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to the newly listed scrip GULFLLOYDS and its market participants, including the market maker, registrar, and shareholders subject to lock-in. Trade-for-trade status and minimum lot size of 1200 shares will reduce speculative trading and intraday volatility in the initial trading period, which is standard practice for new SME listings.
