## Summary

BSE's Department of Surveillance (DOSS) has revised the price bands for 23 equity scrips, effective June 18, 2026. The new price bands range from 2% to 10%, replacing their existing levels. This action is part of BSE's ongoing market surveillance to manage volatility and protect investor interest.

## Key Points

- 23 scrips have their price bands revised effective June 18, 2026.
- Revised price bands are set at 2% (4 scrips), 5% (14 scrips), or 10% (5 scrips).
- The revision is issued by BSE's Surveillance department under Notice No. 20260617-32.
- Scrips with a 2% band face the tightest daily movement restrictions: Jolly Plastic Industries Ltd (507968), Meera Industries Ltd (540519), Sunita Tools Ltd (544001), Tirupati Innovar Ltd (539040), and Virgo Global Ltd (532354).
- Scrips revised to 10% band: Bluspring Enterprises Ltd (544414), Gowra Leasing & Finance Ltd (530709), Naapbooks Ltd (543351), Nurture Well Industries Ltd (531889).
- All remaining 14 scrips are revised to a 5% price band.

## Regulatory Changes

The price bands for the listed scrips are being reduced from their existing levels to tighter bands (2%, 5%, or 10%). Price band tightening is a standard surveillance tool used by BSE to curb excessive speculation or volatility in individual securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised price bands for all 23 listed scrips and ensure orders placed on or after June 18, 2026, comply with the new limits.
- Any clarifications may be directed to BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 17, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 18, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding or intending to trade in any of the 23 affected scrips will face tighter daily price movement limits starting June 18, 2026. Scrips moved to a 2% band will see the most restricted intraday and day-on-day price range, potentially reducing liquidity and increasing impact cost for those securities. The 10% band scrips represent a moderate restriction. Overall market impact is limited, as these are primarily small- and mid-cap or SME-listed companies. The action signals heightened surveillance attention on these securities.
