## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that three new Treasury Bills (T-Bills) will be listed and admitted to dealings on the Exchange in the G Group — Debt Instruments segment with effect from June 5, 2026. The T-Bills cover 91-day, 182-day, and 364-day tenors.

## Key Points

- Three new T-Bills listed effective June 5, 2026 under G Group — Debt Instruments
- Tenors covered: 91-day (maturing Sep 3, 2026), 182-day (maturing Dec 3, 2026), and 364-day (maturing Jun 3, 2027)
- Market lot for all three securities is 1
- Trading will be suspended 2 working days prior to each security's Maturity/Redemption Date (T minus 2 trading days, excluding bank holidays)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification under DCS-Listing for new government debt instruments.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must note that the listed T-Bills will not be available for trading two (2) working days prior to the Maturity/Redemption Date (i.e., T minus 2 trading days, excluding bank holidays). Members should update their systems accordingly.

## Impact Assessment

Routine listing of government Treasury Bills with low market impact. These are risk-free sovereign instruments that provide liquidity options for institutional participants in the debt segment. No significant operational changes are required beyond standard trade cut-off procedures near maturity dates.
