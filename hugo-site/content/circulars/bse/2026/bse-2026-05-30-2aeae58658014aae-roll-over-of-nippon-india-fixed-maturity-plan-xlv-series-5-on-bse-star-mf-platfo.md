## Summary

BSE has notified members that Nippon India Mutual Fund has revised the maturity date of **Nippon India Fixed Maturity Plan – XLV – Series 5** from **June 01, 2026** to **July 31, 2029**. This rollover extends the fund's tenure by approximately three years and two months. The notice references prior BSE notices (No. 20230329-2 and 20250701-64) related to the original NFO launch of this scheme on the BSE StAR MF Platform.

## Key Points

- Nippon India Fixed Maturity Plan – XLV – Series 5 will **not mature** on its originally scheduled date of June 01, 2026.
- The revised maturity date is **July 31, 2029**, extending the lock-in by approximately 38 months.
- BSE received formal communication from Nippon India Mutual Fund regarding the revision.
- Members are directed to the attached notice cum addendum issued by Nippon India AMC for full details.
- The scheme continues to be available on the BSE StAR MF Platform under its existing structure.

## Regulatory Changes

The maturity date of a closed-ended Fixed Maturity Plan has been revised through an addendum to the scheme documents, as permitted under SEBI mutual fund regulations governing FMP rollovers. The change is effected via a notice cum addendum issued by the AMC (Nippon India AMC).

## Compliance Requirements

- BSE members and distributors operating on the BSE StAR MF Platform should update their records to reflect the new maturity date of July 31, 2029.
- Members should communicate the revised maturity date to affected investors holding units of Nippon India Fixed Maturity Plan – XLV – Series 5.
- Members should refer to the attached Nippon India AMC addendum for any additional investor-facing disclosures or action required.

## Important Dates

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Notice Date | May 30, 2026 |
| Original Maturity Date | June 01, 2026 |
| **Revised Maturity Date** | **July 31, 2029** |

## Impact Assessment

This rollover has a **high impact** on existing unitholders of the scheme who were expecting redemption proceeds on June 01, 2026. Their investment will remain locked in for an additional ~38 months. Investors who had planned liquidity around the original maturity date will need to reassess their financial plans. From a platform operations perspective, BSE StAR MF members will need to update system records and notify affected investors. The rollover is a standard mechanism available under SEBI regulations, but the extended duration represents a significant change to the investment terms.
