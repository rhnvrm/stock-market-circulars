## Summary

BSE has issued an update to its Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework applicable to companies undergoing Insolvency Resolution Process (IRP) under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). Referencing earlier notices dated June 8, 2018, May 20, 2022, February 10, 2023, and September 5, 2025, the Exchange has identified securities that now satisfy the criteria for inclusion under this framework, as well as securities being removed from it, both effective July 16, 2026.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I have met the criteria for the IBC-related Additional Surveillance Measure and will be placed under the framework effective July 16, 2026.
- Securities listed in Annexure II are moving out of the IBC Framework effective the same date.
- Annexure III provides a consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework.
- Securities under this framework are reviewed periodically.
- This surveillance framework operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchange.
- Inclusion under the framework is purely a market surveillance measure and should not be interpreted as an adverse action against the company or entity.
- For securities exiting the framework, the price band reverts to the level applicable prior to shortlisting, unless the security remains subject to another surveillance framework, in which case that framework's price band applies.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing IBC-related Additional Surveillance Measure framework (established via prior notices in 2018, 2022, 2023, and 2025) to an updated list of securities.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must note the updated inclusion and exclusion lists (Annexures I, II, and III) and apply the relevant surveillance and price band conditions to affected securities from July 16, 2026. Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **July 16, 2026**: Effective date for both new inclusions (Annexure I) and exclusions (Annexure II) under the IBC surveillance framework.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to the specific securities named in the annexures (not detailed in the available text), which will face additional trading surveillance and price band restrictions upon inclusion, or relief from such restrictions upon exclusion. Market-wide impact is low, but affected companies' shareholders and traders should review the annexures for specific stock-level implications.
