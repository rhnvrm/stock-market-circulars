## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that 23,06,372 new equity shares of Eris Lifesciences Limited (Scrip Code: 540596, ISIN: INE406M01024) have been listed and are permitted to trade on the Exchange with effect from Tuesday, March 10, 2026. These shares were issued on a preferential basis to non-promoters at an issue price of Rs. 1835.35 per share (face value Rs. 1/- each, premium of Rs. 1834.35/-).

## Key Points

- **Company:** Eris Lifesciences Limited (Scrip Code: 540596)
- **Number of Shares:** 23,06,372 equity shares of Rs. 1/- each
- **Issue Price:** Rs. 1835.35/- per share (premium of Rs. 1834.35/-)
- **Allottees:** Non-Promoters (preferential basis)
- **Pari-passu:** New shares rank pari-passu with existing equity shares
- **Distribution Numbers:** 137956022 to 140262393
- **ISIN:** INE406M01024
- **Trading effective from:** March 10, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notification pursuant to a preferential allotment already completed.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are informed to update their systems to reflect the new securities available for trading from March 10, 2026.
- The newly listed shares are subject to a lock-in restriction and cannot be traded until the lock-in period expires.

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment:** January 16, 2026
- **Trading Commencement:** March 10, 2026
- **Lock-in Period Expires:** February 14, 2029 (all 23,06,372 shares under lock-in until this date)

## Impact Assessment

The listing adds approximately 23.06 lakh equity shares to Eris Lifesciences' tradeable float, representing a moderate equity dilution for existing shareholders. However, since all newly issued shares are under lock-in until February 14, 2029, there is no immediate increase in free-float or selling pressure. The preferential allotment at Rs. 1835.35/- per share indicates a capital raise at a significant premium, suggesting positive market sentiment or strategic investor participation. Market impact is expected to be minimal in the short term due to the lock-in constraint.
