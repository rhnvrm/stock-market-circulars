## Summary

BSE has issued a notice regarding the applicability of the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework, referencing prior notices dating back to March 2018. The notice outlines revisions including 100% margin requirements, movement of securities to higher LT-ASM stages, transfer to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups, and securities exiting the framework, all as per attached annexures (I, II, and III) listing the specific securities affected.

## Key Points

- 100% margins shall apply on all open positions as on July 14, 2026, and on new positions created from July 15, 2026 onwards, for securities listed in Annexure I (Part A).
- Securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages effective July 13, 2026 are listed in Annexure I (Part B).
- Lower Price Band shall be applicable w.e.f. July 13, 2026 for the relevant securities.
- Securities under Annexure I (Part D) shall continue in the LT-ASM framework but be transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group w.e.f. July 15, 2026.
- Securities moving out of the LT-ASM framework w.e.f. July 13, 2026 are listed in Annexure II.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework is provided in Annexure III.
- Shortlisting is based on XBRL submissions by listed companies and is purely a surveillance measure, not an adverse action against any company.
- This LT-ASM framework operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice implements the revised LT-ASM framework referenced in earlier BSE circulars (dated March 21, 2018; October 27, 2018; December 4, 2020; August 9, 2024; September 20, 2024; and June 4, 2021), applying updated margin requirements, price bands, and group classifications to a periodically reviewed list of securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and market participants must apply 100% margins on affected securities from the specified dates.
- Participants should note the transfer of certain securities to T/XT/MT/TS trading groups.
- Members should monitor the applicable price bands, which will be reinstated to pre-shortlisting levels once a security exits the framework, unless it remains subject to another surveillance measure.
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 13, 2026: Lower price band applicability; securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages; securities moving out of LT-ASM framework.
- July 14, 2026: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin.
- July 15, 2026: 100% margin applicable on new positions; securities transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group.

## Impact Assessment

The measure increases trading costs and restrictions (via 100% margins and tighter price bands) for securities newly placed or escalated within the LT-ASM framework, potentially reducing liquidity and trading activity in those specific stocks. Securities exiting the framework will see reduced margin burden and reinstated price bands, which may improve trading conditions. Overall market impact is contained to the specific securities named in the annexures rather than the broader market.
