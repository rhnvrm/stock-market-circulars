## Summary

BSE has notified trading members of an ex parte interim order issued by SEBI (Order No. WTM/AS/WRO/WRO-DIV-3/32505/2026-27 dated July 24, 2026) against Stark Investments and other associated entities for allegedly conducting unregistered Investment Advisory and unregistered Portfolio Management Services activities.

## Key Points

- SEBI has restrained the named entities (Stark Investments and Ors.) from buying, selling, or dealing in securities, or associating with the securities market directly or indirectly, until further orders.
- Entities with open positions in exchange-traded derivative contracts as of the order date may close out or square off such positions within 3 months from the order date, or at contract expiry, whichever is earlier.
- The entities are permitted to settle pay-in and pay-out obligations for transactions that took place before close of trading on the date of the order.
- The order takes immediate effect and remains in force until further orders from SEBI.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory framework changes; this is an enforcement action under existing SEBI regulations governing investment advisers and portfolio managers.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are advised to take note of the order and ensure compliance by not facilitating any securities market dealings for the restrained entities, in line with the restraint directions issued by SEBI.

## Important Dates

- Order Date: July 24, 2026
- Effective Date: July 24, 2026 (immediate effect)
- Derivative position close-out window: within 3 months from the order date or contract expiry, whichever is earlier

## Impact Assessment

The order directly restrains the named unregistered entities from market participation and does not affect listed securities, trading systems, or the broader market. Impact is limited to the restrained entities and any counterparties who may have dealt with them; trading members must ensure no further facilitation of transactions for these entities.
