## Summary

BSE has revised the Additional Delivery Centres parameter for Gold Futures contracts. Effective April 27, 2026, all existing additional delivery centres (New Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Bangalore, and Cochin) will be removed (set to NIL) for Gold June 2026 Futures and all subsequent contracts.

## Key Points

- BSE is removing all Additional Delivery Centres for Gold Futures contracts
- The change applies to Gold June 2026 Futures contracts and all contracts onwards
- Existing additional delivery centres include: New Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Bangalore, and Cochin — all being removed
- Revised specification is effective from April 27, 2026
- Members are required to inform their clients about this change
- Detailed revised specifications are provided in the Annexure to the notice

## Regulatory Changes

The Additional Delivery Centres specification for Gold Futures contracts is being revised as follows:

| Contract | Existing Additional Delivery Centres | Revised Additional Delivery Centres |
|---|---|---|
| Gold June 2026 Futures and onwards | New Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Bangalore, Cochin | NIL |

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must take note of the revised delivery centre specifications
- Members are obligated to inform their clients about the removal of additional delivery centres
- Participants with open positions or planned physical delivery in Gold Futures should review and update their delivery arrangements accordingly

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** April 16, 2026
- **Effective Date:** April 27, 2026 (applicable from Gold June 2026 Futures and onwards)

## Impact Assessment

This change has a moderate operational impact on commodity derivatives participants trading Gold Futures. By removing all additional delivery centres, BSE is consolidating delivery logistics, which may affect participants who relied on regional centres for physical settlement. Traders and members involved in physical delivery of gold under futures contracts will need to revise their delivery planning from the June 2026 contract cycle. The change does not affect financial settlement or trading activity directly, but impacts physical delivery mechanisms for commodity participants.
