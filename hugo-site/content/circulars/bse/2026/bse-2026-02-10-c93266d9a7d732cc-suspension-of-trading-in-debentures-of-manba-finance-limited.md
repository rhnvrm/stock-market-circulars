## Summary

BSE has suspended trading in Non-Convertible Debentures of MANBA FINANCE LIMITED (ISIN: INE939X07119, Scrip Code: 975425) effective February 13, 2026. The suspension is due to the company fixing a record date for redemption of debentures and payment of interest. Trading members are advised not to deal in these debentures from the suspension date.

## Key Points

- Debenture: MFL-13.25%-28-2-26-PVT (13.25% coupon rate)
- ISIN: INE939X07119
- Scrip Code: 975425
- Record Date: February 13, 2026
- No Dealings From: February 13, 2026
- Purpose: Redemption of Debentures and Payment of Interest
- Circular Reference: DR-820/2025-2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard corporate action notification for debenture redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute any trades in the specified debentures of MANBA FINANCE LIMITED from February 13, 2026
- Trading members are required to take note of the suspension and communicate to clients holding these securities
- Debenture holders on record date (February 13, 2026) will be eligible for interest payment and redemption proceeds

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: February 10, 2026
- **Record Date**: February 13, 2026
- **No Dealings From**: February 13, 2026
- **Debenture Maturity**: February 28, 2026 (implied from debenture nomenclature)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: High - Complete trading suspension affects liquidity for existing holders of these debentures. Investors cannot exit positions after February 12, 2026.

**Investor Impact**: High - Debenture holders must be aware that trading will cease, and only those holding on record date will receive redemption proceeds and final interest payment.

**Operational Impact**: Medium - Trading members need to update their systems to block trading in this specific ISIN and communicate suspension to affected clients.

**Duration**: Short-term - Suspension is linked to upcoming redemption on February 28, 2026, after which the debentures will cease to exist.
