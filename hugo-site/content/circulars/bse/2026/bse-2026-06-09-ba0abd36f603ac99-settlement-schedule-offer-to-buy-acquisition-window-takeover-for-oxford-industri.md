## Summary

The Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd. (ICCL) has published the settlement calendar for the Offer to Buy – Acquisition Window (Takeover) for Oxford Industries Limited. Bidding will be open from June 12, 2026, to June 25, 2026, under Settlement No. 2026069, with the final settlement date of July 2, 2026.

## Key Points

- Settlement Type: Takeover (Settlement No. 2026069)
- Bid Start Date: June 12, 2026
- Bid End Date: June 25, 2026
- Obligation Download Date: July 1, 2026
- Settlement Date: July 2, 2026
- Demat shares must be delivered exclusively via the Early Pay-in of Shares mechanism available through both depositories (CDSL and NSDL)
- Members/Custodians must use Market Type "(Takeover)" and Settlement No. 2026069 in the depository system
- No EPN file upload is required for early pay-in
- Members should refer to file `MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo` for client-wise Stamp Duty charges

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. Members are directed to refer to Notice No. 20200528-32 (dated May 28, 2020) regarding settlement as per Revised Guidelines of the Mechanism for Acquisition of Shares through Stock Exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and Custodians must ensure early pay-in of securities is reflected in BSE/ICCL systems before prescribed bid acceptance/confirmation timelines on the BSE OTB platform.
- Early pay-in must be completed sufficiently in advance of the closure of the BSE OTB Bidding window and the Custodian confirmation window.
- CDSL and NSDL securities will be earmarked/blocked in the client's account under the requisite market type and settlement number.

## Impact Assessment

This notice is operationally relevant to stock exchange members and custodians participating in the Oxford Industries Limited takeover offer. The early pay-in requirement via depository systems (CDSL/NSDL) imposes a timing discipline on participants to ensure bids are supported by earmarked securities before the OTB window closes. Failure to comply with early pay-in timelines could result in bid rejection. Broader market impact is limited as this pertains to a specific corporate acquisition event.
