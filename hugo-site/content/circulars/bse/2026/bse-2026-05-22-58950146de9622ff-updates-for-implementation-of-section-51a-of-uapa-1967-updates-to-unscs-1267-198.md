## Summary

BSE has forwarded a SEBI communication regarding updates to the UNSC's 1267/1989 ISIL (Da'esh) and Al-Qaida Sanctions List. Per UNSC Note SC/16365 dated May 21, 2026, 7 entries have been removed from the list. Members are required to scan existing and future accounts against the updated list and comply with procedures under Section 51A of the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967.

## Key Points

- UNSC Note SC/16365 (May 21, 2026) removes 7 entries from the ISIL (Da'esh) and Al-Qaida Sanctions List.
- The latest sanctions list is accessible on the UN Security Council website for both the 1267/1989 (ISIL/Al-Qaida) and 1988 (Taliban) committees.
- SEBI updates on UNSC sanctions amendments are also published on the SEBI website under "Media and Notifications" → "UNSC Sanctions Committee List".
- Delisting requests may be forwarded to the UN-appointed independent Ombudsperson.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework introduced. This notice reflects an amendment (removal of 7 entries) to the existing UNSC sanctions list maintained under resolution 1267/1989 and implemented in India via Section 51A of UAPA, 1967.

## Compliance Requirements

- Scan all existing accounts to ensure no account is held by or linked to any sanctioned individual or entity.
- Apply the same screening to all future accounts.
- Comply with SEBI's AML/CFT Guidelines (clause 54, dated June 06, 2024) and the Central Government Order dated February 02, 2021.
- Forward any delisting requests received to:
  - Joint Secretary (CTCR), MHA: jsctcr-mha@gov.in
  - MEA, Joint Secretary (UNP): jsunp@mea.gov.in
- Inform affected individuals/entities that they may apply to the UN Ombudsperson for delisting.
- Visit the SEBI website regularly for further updates to the sanctions list.

## Important Dates

- **May 21, 2026**: UNSC Note SC/16365 issued, removing 7 entries.
- **May 22, 2026**: BSE notice issued; compliance effective immediately.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine compliance notice with no direct market or trading impact. All SEBI-registered intermediaries (brokers, depositories, etc.) must update their account screening processes to reflect the 7 removed entries. The operational burden is minimal and limited to account compliance checks. No stocks or instruments are directly affected.
