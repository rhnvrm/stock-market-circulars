## Summary

BSE has revised price bands for 29+ equity scrips effective April 20, 2026, via Notice No. 20260417-46 dated April 17, 2026. The revised bands range from 2% to 20%, replacing prior levels. Trading members must account for these revised limits when executing orders in the affected securities.

## Key Points

- Price bands revised for 29+ scrips across diverse sectors including chemicals, media, electronics, logistics, and financial services
- Effective date: April 20, 2026
- Revised price band categories: 2% (2 scrips), 5% (13 scrips), 10% (11 scrips), 20% (2 scrips)
- Issued by Department of Stock Surveillance (DOSS)
- No attachments; full details contained within the notice

## Regulatory Changes

Price bands have been revised from their existing levels for the listed scrips. The new bands restrict daily price movement to the specified percentage above or below the previous close.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the new price band limits for all affected scrips starting April 20, 2026
- Orders exceeding the revised price band limits will be rejected by the exchange system
- No action required from listed companies; this is a market-level trading restriction

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** April 17, 2026
- **Effective Date:** April 20, 2026 (Monday)

## Impact Assessment

The revision of price bands primarily affects retail and institutional traders in these scrips. Tighter bands (2%–5%) restrict volatility for potentially illiquid or surveillance-flagged securities, reducing risk of manipulation. The two scrips with 20% bands (HCKK Ventures, Highness Microelectronics) are given wider latitude, suggesting relaxed surveillance or reclassification. Dish TV and Kirloskar Electric, being larger-cap names, face tighter constraints consistent with their liquidity profiles.
