## Summary

BSE has notified members of temporary restrictions on lumpsum subscriptions in **HDFC Gold ETF Fund of Fund** on the BSE StAR MF Platform, effective June 5, 2026. Fresh and additional purchases via lumpsum mode and switch-in transactions are capped at ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month (based on the first holder). The restriction applies to transactions received after the 3:00 PM cut-off time on June 5, 2026.

## Key Points

- Lumpsum and switch-in subscriptions in HDFC Gold ETF Fund of Fund are capped at ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month (first holder)
- Restriction applies to transactions received after 3:00 PM on June 5, 2026
- SIP inflows, ASTP-In, and all outflows (redemption, switch-out, STP-out) are permitted without any amount restriction
- Subscription orders above ₹10,00,000 triggered to RTA on or after the close of business hours on June 5, 2026 will be rejected and refunded once funds are received from AMC/RTA

## Regulatory Changes

HDFC Mutual Fund has issued a Notice-cum-Addendum imposing temporary subscription limits, which BSE has duly implemented on the BSE StAR MF Platform. This measure restricts large lumpsum inflows into the gold ETF fund of fund, likely to manage liquidity or underlying asset capacity constraints.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and distributors must ensure lumpsum and switch-in orders for HDFC Gold ETF Fund of Fund do not exceed ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month after the cut-off time
- Orders exceeding the limit triggered after business hours on June 5, 2026 will be rejected; members should inform investors accordingly
- Refer to the attached Notice-cum-Addendum from HDFC Mutual Fund for full details

## Important Dates

- **June 5, 2026, 3:00 PM**: Cut-off time after which the ₹10,00,000 per PAN per month cap on lumpsum and switch-in subscriptions becomes effective
- **June 5, 2026, close of business**: Orders above ₹10,00,000 triggered to RTA on or after this time will be rejected and refunded

## Impact Assessment

This restriction has a moderate impact limited to investors seeking to make large lumpsum investments or switch-in transactions into HDFC Gold ETF Fund of Fund. Regular SIP investors and existing investors redeeming or switching out are unaffected. The per-PAN monthly cap of ₹10,00,000 is likely aimed at managing inflows into the underlying gold ETF due to capacity or liquidity constraints. Investors with large planned lumpsum investments in this scheme will need to stagger purchases across calendar months or consider alternative gold fund options.
