## Summary

BSE has issued a notice informing market participants about the opening of an open offer to buy shares of TRIO MERCANTILE & TRADING LIMITED through the Acquisition Window mechanism for takeovers. The offer is made by Acquirer Kaushik Jagannath Joshi along with Persons Acting in Concert (PACs) Amita Kaushik Joshi, Krishna Kaushik Joshi, Radhika Kaushik Joshi, and M/s. Joshi Kaushikkumar Jagannath HUF.

## Key Points

- Open offer made under SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011
- Offer price: Rs. 1.25 per equity share, payable in cash
- Up to 3,39,68,300 equity shares to be acquired, representing 50.00% of the total issued, fully paid-up equity and voting capital
- Equity shares have a face value of Rs. 2.00 each
- Acquirer: Kaushik Jagannath Joshi; PACs: Amita Kaushik Joshi, Krishna Kaushik Joshi, Radhika Kaushik Joshi, and M/s. Joshi Kaushikkumar Jagannath HUF
- Letter of Offer attached to the notice for market participant reference

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; the offer follows the existing mechanism for acquisition of shares through the Stock Exchange pursuant to Tender-Offers under Takeovers, Buy Back and Delisting, as per SEBI circulars dated April 13, 2015 and Dec 09, 2016, and subsequent BSE guideline revisions (Feb 10, 2017; April 24, 2019; May 28, 2020; Nov 2, 2020; Aug 25, 2021).

## Compliance Requirements

Trading Members and Custodians must process the tender offer in accordance with the Revised Guidelines of Mechanism for acquisition of shares through Stock Exchange, and refer to the Letter of Offer and BSE website for full offer details and procedures.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 24, 2026
- Offer Opening Date: July 29, 2026 (Wednesday)
- Offer Closing Date: August 11, 2026 (Tuesday)

## Impact Assessment

The open offer affects shareholders of Trio Mercantile & Trading Limited who may tender shares for cash at Rs. 1.25 per share. Trading members and custodians need to be aware of the offer window to facilitate client participation. Impact is limited to the target company's stock and its shareholders rather than the broader market.
