## Summary

BSE has amended the Designated Market Maker (DMM) framework for Index Derivatives contracts in the Equity Derivatives Segment. Specifically, the maximum permissible bid-offer spread for SENSEX, BANKEX, and Focused IT Index Futures contracts has been widened from 0.10% to 0.25% of the bid price. This change takes effect on July 15, 2026. All other provisions of the existing DMM framework remain unchanged.

## Key Points

- The revision applies exclusively to Index Futures contracts for SENSEX, BANKEX, and Focused IT indices.
- The maximum permissible bid-offer spread is widened from 0.10% to 0.25% of the bid price.
- All other DMM framework provisions remain in effect without modification.
- This notice is a continuation of Exchange Notice No. 20260422-2 dated April 22, 2026.
- Detailed revised guidelines are available in Annexure 1 attached to the notice.

## Regulatory Changes

| Index | Instrument | Existing Spread | Revised Spread |
|---|---|---|---|
| SENSEX, BANKEX & Focused IT | Futures | Max 0.10% of bid price | Max 0.25% of bid price |

All other aspects of the DMM framework for Index Derivatives contracts remain unchanged.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members participating as Designated Market Makers in SENSEX, BANKEX, and Focused IT Index Futures must update their quoting systems to align with the revised spread requirement of up to 0.25% of bid price.
- Members should refer to Annexure 1 of the notice for the complete and revised DMM framework guidelines.
- No action is required for DMM obligations in other index derivatives not covered by this amendment.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 29, 2026
- **Effective Date:** July 15, 2026
- **Reference Notice:** Exchange Notice No. 20260422-2 dated April 22, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This amendment relaxes the bid-offer spread obligation for DMMs in SENSEX, BANKEX, and Focused IT Index Futures, giving market makers more room to quote wider spreads. While this eases the operational burden on DMMs — particularly during volatile market conditions — it may modestly reduce liquidity quality (tighter spreads) for end participants in these contracts. The impact is moderate and primarily affects trading members registered as DMMs in these specific index futures products. Market participants trading these futures should be aware that quoted spreads may widen up to 0.25% of the bid price post July 15, 2026.
