## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260224-6 informs trading members that new commercial papers (CPs) issued on a private placement basis by five issuers have been listed and admitted to trading on the BSE Debt Segment effective February 24, 2026. Trading is permitted only in dematerialised form with a standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof, and a tick size of 1 paise.

## Key Points

- Five commercial paper instruments across four issuers have been freshly listed on the BSE Debt Segment.
- All securities are tradeable only in demat form in standard denominations of Rs. 5 lakhs.
- Tick size for all listed securities is 1 paise.
- All CPs carry top-tier short-term credit ratings (CRISIL A1+ and/or IND A1+ / ICRA A1+).
- Trading members requiring clarification may contact the BSE Debt Department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing admission notice under existing BSE Debt Segment guidelines for privately placed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure all trades in the listed CPs are executed in demat form only.
- Trades must be in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof.
- Members must use the assigned ISIN numbers and scrip codes for each instrument.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing notice with minimal market impact. The listed instruments are short-term commercial papers with tenors ranging from approximately 3 months (SBICAP, KEC) to 12 months (Bajaj Housing Finance, Birla Group Holdings). All issuers carry the highest short-term credit ratings (A1+), indicating low credit risk. The listing provides institutional investors and debt segment participants with additional high-quality short-term instruments for trading. No impact on equity markets or broader market operations is anticipated.
