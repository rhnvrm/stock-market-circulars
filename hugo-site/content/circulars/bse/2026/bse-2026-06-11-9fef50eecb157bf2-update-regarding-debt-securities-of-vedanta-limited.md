## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that debt securities of Vedanta Limited (VEDL) listed as Annexure I will be transferred to Vedanta Aluminium Metal Limited (VAML) with effect from Monday, June 15, 2026. This transfer is pursuant to a Composite Scheme of Arrangement approved under Sections 230 to 232 of the Companies Act, 2013.

## Key Points

- Debt securities of Vedanta Limited (Demerged Company) specified in Annexure I will stand transferred to Vedanta Aluminium Metal Limited (Resulting Company 1 / VAML) from June 15, 2026.
- The scheme is a Composite Scheme of Arrangement involving Vedanta Limited and four resulting companies.
- Trading members are advised to take note of the scrip transfers and update their records accordingly.
- Contact point for queries: Nilima Burghate, +91 22 2272 5272, bse.schemes@bseindia.com.

## Regulatory Changes

The transfer of debt securities is effected under a court-approved Composite Scheme of Arrangement under Sections 230 to 232 and other applicable provisions of the Companies Act, 2013. Post-transfer, the debt scrips will be associated with Vedanta Aluminium Metal Limited rather than Vedanta Limited.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the transfer of the listed debt securities from Vedanta Limited to Vedanta Aluminium Metal Limited as per the Annexure I details.
- Members holding or dealing in these securities should update their systems and records to reflect the new issuer entity (VAML) effective June 15, 2026.
- Members with queries should contact the designated BSE officer before the effective date.

## Important Dates

- **June 11, 2026**: Notice date; trading members informed of upcoming transfer.
- **June 15, 2026 (Monday)**: Effective date of transfer of debt securities from Vedanta Limited to Vedanta Aluminium Metal Limited.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has high impact on holders of Vedanta Limited debt securities listed in Annexure I. The demerger restructuring replaces the issuer entity for these instruments, which may affect credit assessments, counterparty risk profiles, and collateral valuations. Trading members and institutional holders should review their exposure to the affected scrips and ensure compliance with any internal policies triggered by a change in issuer entity. The broader Composite Scheme also involves Vedanta Power Limited, Vedanta Oil and Gas Limited, and Vedanta Iron and Steel Limited as resulting companies, indicating a wide-ranging corporate restructuring of the Vedanta group.
