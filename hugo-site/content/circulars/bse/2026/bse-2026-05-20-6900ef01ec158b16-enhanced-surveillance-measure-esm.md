## Summary

BSE has issued a notice under its Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework announcing new securities to be included under ESM, stage movements for existing ESM securities, and securities exiting the framework. Newly included securities will attract a minimum 100% margin requirement effective May 22, 2026, and will be moved from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade settlement.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I are newly included under ESM and will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open positions as on May 21, 2026 and new positions from May 22, 2026
- Certain securities will be moved to a lower Stage (Stage I) under the ESM Framework effective May 21, 2026
- Securities qualifying under ESM will be shifted from Rolling Settlement segment to Trade-for-Trade (T2T) segment effective May 22, 2026
- Securities shifting to Stage II will be placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction effective May 21, 2026
- Securities exiting the ESM Framework effective May 21, 2026 are listed in Annexure II
- A consolidated list of all securities under the ESM framework is provided in Annexure III
- ESM operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the exchanges

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the ESM framework in line with prior notices: 20230602-44 (June 2, 2023), 20230718-46 (July 18, 2023), 20240809-42 (August 9, 2024), 20240920-63 (September 20, 2024), 20241004-65 (October 4, 2024), and 20250725-61 (July 25, 2025). Securities under ESM are subject to periodic review and may be moved between stages or removed from the framework based on surveillance criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure minimum 100% margin is collected on all open positions in newly included ESM securities as on May 21, 2026
- 100% margin must be collected on all new positions in ESM securities created from May 22, 2026
- Members must comply with Trade-for-Trade settlement rules for all ESM-listed securities
- Stage II securities must adhere to the 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism
- For clarifications, members may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **May 21, 2026**: Stage I downgrade effective; Stage II Trade-for-Trade with 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction begins; securities exit ESM framework
- **May 22, 2026**: Minimum 100% margin requirement effective on all open positions (as of May 21) and new positions; shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment effective

## Impact Assessment

The inclusion of securities under ESM imposes significant trading restrictions that reduce liquidity and increase cost of trading for affected scrips. The mandatory 100% margin requirement substantially raises capital requirements for traders and investors holding or taking positions in these securities. The shift to Trade-for-Trade settlement eliminates netting benefits and intraday trading flexibility. Stage II securities face the additional constraint of a 2% daily price band and Periodic Call Auction, further limiting price discovery and liquidity. Securities exiting the ESM framework will have their price bands reinstated to pre-ESM levels, unless subject to another surveillance framework. BSE emphasizes that ESM inclusion is a market surveillance measure and should not be construed as adverse action against the concerned company.
