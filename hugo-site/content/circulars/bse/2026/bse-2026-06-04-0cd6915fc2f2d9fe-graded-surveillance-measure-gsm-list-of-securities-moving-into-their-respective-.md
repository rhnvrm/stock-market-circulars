## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260604-28) informing trading members that certain equity securities will be moved to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 05, 2026. The securities and their respective target GSM stages are listed in the attached Annexure. Enhanced surveillance actions specific to each stage will apply from that date.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in the attached Annexure will move to higher GSM stages effective **June 05, 2026**.
- Settlement for all affected securities will shift to **trade-for-trade basis** (Stages II–IV); no netting off is permitted.
- Stage I imposes a 100% margin rate and a price band of 5% or lower.
- Stages II–IV impose progressively stricter restrictions including Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) requirements of 50%–100% of trade value to be deposited by buyers.
- Stages III and IV further restrict trading to **once a week (every Monday or the first trading day of the week)**.
- Stage IV additionally disallows any upward price movement.

## Regulatory Changes

The GSM framework, originally published on February 23, 2017 (Exchange Notice 20170223-44) and subsequently updated via notices in 2017, 2018, 2019, and 2023, governs the escalation of surveillance measures on securities exhibiting abnormal price or volume behaviour. This circular enforces the stage-wise movement of specified securities under that framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading members** must note the updated GSM stage for each affected security and apply the corresponding surveillance actions from June 05, 2026.
- **Buyers** of Stage II securities must deposit ASD of 50% of trade value.
- **Buyers** of Stage III and IV securities must deposit ASD of 100% of trade value.
- No netting off is allowed; all trades in affected securities settle on a trade-for-trade basis.
- Members should exercise adequate precautions while trading in these securities.
- For clarifications, members may refer to the GSM FAQ or write to **bse.surv@bseindia.com**.

## Important Dates

- **June 04, 2026**: Notice issued.
- **June 05, 2026**: Effective date — securities move to their respective higher GSM stages.

## Impact Assessment

Movement to higher GSM stages significantly impairs the liquidity and tradability of affected securities. The mandatory trade-for-trade settlement eliminates netting benefits, increasing settlement risk and cost for members. The ASD requirements (50%–100% of trade value) create substantial capital barriers for buyers, likely suppressing demand and trading volumes. Stages III and IV — permitting trading only once per week — effectively constrain price discovery and may trigger further selling pressure. Investors holding these securities face reduced exit liquidity, and the prohibition on upward movement in Stage IV effectively caps any recovery. Members are advised to communicate these restrictions clearly to clients holding or transacting in the affected securities.
