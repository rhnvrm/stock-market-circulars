## Summary

BSE SME Exchange has published a notice (No. 20260302-20) informing that the Draft Offer Document of Quanto Agroworld Limited has been filed with the SME Exchange. The document is hosted on the BSE SME website and is open for public comments for a minimum period of 21 days from the date of this notice, in accordance with Schedule VI of the SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018.

## Key Points

- Quanto Agroworld Limited filed its Draft Offer Document with BSE SME Exchange on 28 February 2026
- The document is available on the BSE SME website at www.bsesme.com/PublicIssues/SMEIPODRHP.aspx
- Public comments are invited for a minimum period of 21 days from 02 March 2026
- The Draft Offer Document contains all disclosures as specified under Schedule VI of SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018
- Comments must be submitted via email to equity.ipo@bseindia.com

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This notice is issued in compliance with existing SEBI (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2018, specifically Schedule VI, which mandates public availability and comment period for SME IPO draft offer documents.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members of the public, investors, and market participants wishing to provide feedback on the Draft Offer Document of Quanto Agroworld Limited must submit comments within 21 days from 02 March 2026
- Comments should be sent to: equity.ipo@bseindia.com
- No specific compliance obligations are imposed on brokers or trading members by this notice

## Important Dates

- **Date of Filing by Company:** 28 February 2026
- **Notice Date:** 02 March 2026
- **Public Comment Deadline:** On or before 23 March 2026 (21 days from notice date)

## Impact Assessment

This notice has minimal immediate market impact as it pertains to a pre-IPO disclosure requirement for a single SME company (Quanto Agroworld Limited). It is a routine procedural step in the SME IPO process. Existing market participants, brokers, and listed companies face no direct obligations. Potential impact is limited to prospective investors or analysts who may wish to review and comment on the draft offer document before the IPO proceeds to the next stage.
