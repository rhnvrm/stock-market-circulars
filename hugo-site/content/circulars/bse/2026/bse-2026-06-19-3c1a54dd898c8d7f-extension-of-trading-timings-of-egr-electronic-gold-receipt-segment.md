## Summary

BSE is extending trading hours for the Electronic Gold Receipt (EGR) segment and discontinuing the post-closing session effective Monday, June 29, 2026. This notice updates the trading schedule in continuation of earlier circulars (20221021-42, 20221124-21, and 20260430-23). Continuous trading in the EGR segment will now run until 11:30 pm (or 11:55 pm during US daylight saving periods).

## Key Points

- Extended continuous trading hours: 09:00 am to 11:30 pm / 11:55 pm (US DST) on normal trading days
- Post-closing session is discontinued from June 29, 2026
- Pre-open order entry: 08:45 am to 08:53 am (with random stoppage in last 1 minute)
- Afternoon Block Deal Window: 03:05 pm to 03:20 pm
- Auction market time: 05:00 pm to 05:30 pm
- Trade modification deadline: 11:45 pm / 11:59 pm
- Evening session on trading holidays also extended: continuous trading from 05:15 pm to 11:30 pm / 11:55 pm
- Log-in for holiday evening session starts at 02:45 pm

## Regulatory Changes

The post-closing session in the EGR segment is discontinued. Revised trading hours are effective June 29, 2026, superseding prior schedules under circulars 20221021-42, 20221124-21, and master circular 20260430-23.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised trading hours schedule and update their systems accordingly
- All trade modifications must be completed before the prescribed cut-off time (11:45 pm / 11:59 pm)
- Bhav copy will be generated after closure of the trading session

## Important Dates

- **June 29, 2026**: Revised trading hours effective; post-closing session discontinued

## Impact Assessment

This change affects trading members active in the EGR segment. The extension of continuous trading hours to 11:30 pm / 11:55 pm aligns EGR timings more closely with international gold market hours, potentially improving price discovery and liquidity. Six EGR securities are currently available for trading across three gold denominations (100 mg, 1 gram, 10 grams) in two purity grades (995 and 999). Members must update their systems to reflect the removal of the post-closing session and the extended trade modification window.
