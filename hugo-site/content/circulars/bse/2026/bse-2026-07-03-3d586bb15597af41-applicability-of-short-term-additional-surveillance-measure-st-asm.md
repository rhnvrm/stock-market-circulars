## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260703-27 dated July 3, 2026 identifies securities that have met the criteria for inclusion under the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework, specifically under Stage I and Stage II. The notice references prior circulars dated September 20, 2024, October 27, 2018, July 19, 2019, December 4, 2020, September 25, 2023, and September 20, 2024 that established and revised the ASM framework.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I have satisfied criteria for ST-ASM Stage I inclusion
- Additional securities listed in Annexure I have satisfied criteria for ST-ASM Stage II inclusion
- ASM framework operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures
- Inclusion under ASM is purely for market surveillance purposes and is not an adverse reflection on the company/entity
- Price bands for scrips exiting the framework will revert to pre-shortlisting levels, unless subject to another surveillance framework

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework introduced; this notice applies the existing ST-ASM framework (established via prior circulars) to a fresh set of securities identified as meeting Stage I and Stage II criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the specified higher margin rates on all open and new positions in the identified securities
- Stage I securities: margin of 50% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at 100%
- Stage II securities: margin of 100% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at 100%
- Members with queries may contact bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 3, 2026
- Effective Date: July 7, 2026 (applicable on all open positions as of July 6, 2026, and new positions created from July 7, 2026)

## Impact Assessment

The increased margin requirements will raise the cost of trading and holding positions in the affected securities, likely reducing speculative activity and improving liquidity discipline. Market participants holding or trading these specific securities face higher capital requirements, but the broader market impact is limited since this targets a specific list of securities under routine surveillance monitoring rather than systemic market conditions.
