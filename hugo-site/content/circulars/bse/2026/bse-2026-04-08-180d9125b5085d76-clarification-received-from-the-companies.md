## Summary

BSE's Surveillance department (DOSS) issued Notice No. 20260408-38 dated 08 April 2026, noting significant price and/or volume movement in 29 scrips. The Exchange had written to these companies to ensure trading members and investors have access to the latest relevant information. Clarifications have been received from 16 companies, while replies remain awaited from the remaining 13.

## Key Points

- BSE observed unusual price/volume movement in 29 listed companies and sought clarifications.
- 16 companies have submitted replies, which are attached to the notice.
- 13 companies have not yet responded; their replies are still awaited.
- The notice is issued by the Surveillance department under the Equity segment.
- Signed by Sanjay Jain (Deputy Vice President, Surveillance) and Shreya Ghose (Manager, Surveillance).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This notice is part of BSE's ongoing market surveillance process to maintain transparency and informed trading.

## Compliance Requirements

- The 13 companies for which replies are awaited are expected to submit their clarifications to BSE promptly.
- Trading members and investors are advised to review the attached replies from the 16 responding companies before trading in these scrips.

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects 29 stocks across various sectors. The receipt of clarifications from 16 companies may help reduce information asymmetry and speculative activity in those scrips. For the 13 companies yet to respond, continued uncertainty may sustain elevated volatility. Investors and traders should review company-specific replies before taking positions in any of the listed securities. No immediate trading restrictions have been imposed as part of this notice.
