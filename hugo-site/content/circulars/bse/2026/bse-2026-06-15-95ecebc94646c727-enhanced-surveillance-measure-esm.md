## Summary

BSE has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework effective June 16-17, 2026. New securities meeting the ESM criteria will attract a minimum 100% margin requirement and be shifted from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade settlement. Some previously listed ESM securities are being moved down to Stage I, while others are being removed from the framework entirely.

## Key Points

- Securities included in ESM (Annexure I) will attract a minimum **100% margin** on all open positions as on June 16, 2026 and new positions from June 17, 2026
- Newly included ESM securities will shift from Rolling Settlement to **Trade-for-Trade segment** w.e.f. June 17, 2026
- Securities moving to **Stage II** will additionally be subject to a **2% price band** and **Periodic Call Auction** w.e.f. June 16, 2026
- Securities meeting criteria for **Stage I downgrade** (from higher stage) are listed in Annexure I
- Securities **exiting the ESM framework** w.e.f. June 16, 2026 are listed in Annexure II
- A consolidated list of all securities under ESM is provided in Annexure III
- The ESM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the ESM framework originally established under notice no. 20230602-44 (June 2, 2023) and subsequently amended via notices dated July 18, 2023, August 9, 2024, September 20, 2024, October 04, 2024, and July 25, 2025. The current update reflects the periodic review cycle of the ESM framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading members** must ensure minimum 100% margin is collected on ESM-listed securities for all open positions as on June 16, 2026 and new positions created from June 17, 2026
- Members must note that Stage II securities are subject to Trade-for-Trade settlement with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism
- The shortlisting of securities under ESM is purely for market surveillance and should not be construed as adverse action against the company
- For clarifications, members may contact: **bse.surv@bseindia.com**

## Important Dates

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| June 15, 2026 | Notice issued |
| June 16, 2026 | Stage I downgrade effective; Stage II price band (2%) and Periodic Call Auction effective; securities exit from ESM framework |
| June 17, 2026 | 100% margin requirement effective on open positions; shift to Trade-for-Trade segment effective for new inclusions |

## Impact Assessment

The ESM framework imposes significant trading restrictions on affected securities:

- **Liquidity impact**: Shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade eliminates netting benefits and requires full upfront delivery, severely restricting liquidity in affected stocks
- **Cost impact**: The 100% margin requirement substantially increases the capital required to hold positions, discouraging speculative activity
- **Stage II securities** face the most severe restrictions with an additional 2% price band limiting daily price movement and Periodic Call Auction replacing continuous trading
- **Positive signal for exiting securities**: Stocks removed from the ESM framework will have their price bands reinstated to pre-ESM levels, provided they are not subject to any other surveillance measure
- The periodic review mechanism ensures securities are assessed on an ongoing basis for potential reclassification or removal
