## Summary

BSE has admitted to trading five new commercial paper (CP) issuances on its Debt Market Segment effective June 19, 2026. The securities were issued on a private placement basis by Dolat Algotech Limited (two CPs), Birla Group Holdings Private Limited, Can Fin Homes Ltd., and Grasim Industries Ltd. All CPs are to be traded exclusively in dematerialised form in standard denominations of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof.

## Key Points

- All securities trade in dematerialised form only with a tick size of 1 paise and standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs.
- Five commercial papers across four issuers are newly listed under this notice.
- Credit ratings are strong: CRISIL A1+ and/or ICRA A1+ across all issuers.
- Interest is cumulative and paid at redemption for all instruments.
- Issuing and paying agents are Axis Bank Limited (for Dolat Algotech and Birla Group Holdings) and HDFC Bank Ltd (for Can Fin Homes); Grasim's agent is not specified in available content.
- Trading members may contact the debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915 for clarifications.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing admission notice under BSE's debt market segment framework for privately placed commercial papers.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form.
- Trades must be in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof.
- Applicable tick size of 1 paise must be observed.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice with limited market-wide impact. The listings provide institutional investors and trading members access to short-term debt instruments from highly rated issuers. The commercial papers carry top-tier short-term credit ratings (CRISIL A1+ / ICRA A1+), indicating negligible credit risk. Issue prices range from Rs. 470,443.50 to Rs. 491,809.50 against a face value of Rs. 5,00,000, reflecting market-determined yields. Total notional value across all five CPs is substantial (Grasim alone at 16,000 units × Rs. 5 lakhs = Rs. 800 crore), making this notable for debt market liquidity but with no direct equity market implications.
