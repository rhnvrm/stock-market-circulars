## Summary

BSE has informed Trading Members that several companies have fixed record dates for payment of interest and/or part redemption of their outstanding Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs). Trading in these debentures will occur at a reduced face value from the specified ex-date/record date onward, reflecting the part redemption amount.

## Key Points

- Nine NCD series across eight companies are affected by part redemption/interest payment record dates.
- Reduced face values range from Rs. 2,500 to Rs. 91,667 per debenture depending on the series.
- All instruments are privately placed NCDs traded in the Debt segment.
- DR (Distinctive Record) year for all listed instruments is 2027.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes; this is a routine corporate action notice for debt securities under Listing Operations.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading Members must ensure trades in the listed debentures reflect the reduced face value from the applicable ex-date. No action required from equity investors.

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects only holders of the specific NCD series listed and has no bearing on equity markets. Debenture holders will receive interest/part redemption payments as per schedule, and post-ex-date trading will reflect the reduced face values. Impact is limited to the debt segment participants of the named issuers.
