## Summary

BSE has revised price bands for 14 equity scrips effective March 06, 2026, under its surveillance framework. The majority of affected securities have been moved to a 5% price band, two scrips to a 10% band, and one scrip (Noble Polymers Ltd) to a 20% band. These revisions are intended to regulate price volatility in the identified securities.

## Key Points

- Price bands revised for 14 scrips effective March 06, 2026
- 10 scrips revised to 5% price band: ACI Infocom, Akme Fintrade, AVI Polymers, Omnitex Industries, Sawaca Enterprises, Tarmat, Tree House Education & Accessories, True Green Bio Energy, Udayshivakumar Infra, V B Industries
- 2 scrips revised to 10% price band: F Mec International Financial Services, Jindal Poly Films, Landmark Global Learning
- 1 scrip revised to 20% price band: Noble Polymers Ltd
- Circular issued by BSE Surveillance department (Sanjay Jain, Deputy Vice President & Arpita Joshi, Manager)
- Queries to be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised price bands and ensure orders are placed within the new limits for the affected scrips from March 06, 2026 onwards
- No specific action required from listed companies; this is an exchange-level surveillance measure
- Members with queries may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** March 05, 2026
- **Effective Date:** March 06, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors in the 14 affected scrips will face tighter or adjusted daily price movement limits starting March 06, 2026. Securities moved to a 5% band will have the most restrictive intraday price range, limiting both upside and downside movement to 5% from the previous close. This may reduce liquidity and increase impact cost for positions in these scrips. Jindal Poly Films (500227), a relatively larger and more liquid security, being moved to a 10% band may attract wider attention. The revision signals heightened surveillance focus on these securities, potentially due to unusual price movements or volume patterns.
