## Summary

BSE has issued a notice moving certain securities to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 08, 2026. The affected securities are listed in the attached Annexure. Depending on the assigned GSM stage (I through IV), these securities will face progressively stricter trading restrictions, margin requirements, and settlement conditions.

## Key Points

- Securities in the Annexure will be elevated to higher GSM stages from June 08, 2026
- GSM stages range from Stage I (enhanced margins) to Stage IV (maximum restrictions with no upward price movement)
- All stages at III and IV restrict trading to once per week (every Monday or first trading day of the week)
- Settlement shall be on trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed
- Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) required from buyers at Stages II, III, and IV
- Reference framework originally published on February 23, 2017 and subsequently updated

## Regulatory Changes

The following GSM stage-wise surveillance actions apply:

| Stage | Surveillance Actions |
|-------|---------------------|
| I | 100% applicable margin rate; price band of 5% or lower as applicable |
| II | Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band; ASD of 50% of trade value deposited by buyers |
| III | Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band; trading once a week (Monday/first trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value deposited by buyers |
| IV | Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band; trading once a week (Monday/first trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value deposited by buyers; no upward price movement |

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must take adequate precautions while trading in affected GSM securities
- Settlement must be done on trade-to-trade basis; no netting off is permitted
- Buyers of securities at Stage II, III, and IV must deposit the applicable ASD percentage of trade value
- Members may refer to the GSM FAQ at http://www.bseindia.com/markets/equity/EQReports/graded_surveil_measure.aspx?expandable=6 for clarifications
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 05, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 08, 2026 — securities begin operating under new GSM stages

## Impact Assessment

Securities elevated to higher GSM stages face significantly reduced liquidity and increased trading costs. Stage III and IV securities are restricted to weekly trading only, making it extremely difficult for investors to enter or exit positions. The mandatory ASD requirement of up to 100% of trade value substantially increases the capital burden on buyers. Stage IV additionally prohibits any upward price movement, severely constraining price discovery. Investors holding affected securities should note the trade-to-trade settlement requirement, which eliminates the ability to net off intraday positions. The specific securities affected are listed in the Annexure attached to the original notice.
