## Summary

BSE/ICCL has published the settlement calendar for the Offer to Buy – Acquisition Window (Takeover) for Senthil Infotek Limited (Settlement No. 2026074). The bidding window runs from June 18 to July 2, 2026, with obligations downloadable on July 13 and final settlement on July 14, 2026. Members and custodians must use the Early Pay-in of Shares mechanism via CDSL or NSDL.

## Key Points

- Settlement Type: Takeover | Settlement No.: 2026074
- Bid Start Date: June 18, 2026; Bid End Date: July 2, 2026
- Obligation Download Date: July 13, 2026; Settlement Date: July 14, 2026
- Early pay-in of demat shares is mandatory through CDSL/NSDL depository systems using Market Type "(Takeover)" and Settlement No. 2026074
- CDSL & NSDL early pay-in securities will be earmarked/blocked in the client's account under the requisite market type and settlement number
- No EPN file upload is required from Members or Custodians
- Members should refer to the MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo file for client-wise Stamp Duty charges
- Reference Notice No. 20200528-32 (May 28, 2020) for revised guidelines on share acquisition through Stock Exchange

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This notice follows existing SEBI/BSE guidelines for the Offer to Buy – Acquisition Window mechanism under the Takeover Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members/Custodians must complete early pay-in of securities in the depository system sufficiently in advance of the BSE OTB Bidding window closure and Custodian confirmation window
- Early pay-in must be reflected in BSE/ICCL systems before prescribed timelines for bid acceptance/confirmation
- Use Market Type "(Takeover)" and Settlement No. 2026074 in depository instructions
- No EPN file upload required
- For stamp duty details, refer to the MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo file

## Impact Assessment

This circular is operationally relevant to brokers (Members) and Custodians participating in the Senthil Infotek takeover offer. The impact is limited to those entities involved in this specific acquisition window. Investors tendering shares must ensure their brokers/custodians complete early pay-in on time to avoid bid rejection. The broader market impact is low, as this pertains to a single company's takeover process.
