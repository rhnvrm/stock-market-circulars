## Summary

BSE has listed privately placed commercial paper securities from multiple companies on its debt market segment effective January 16, 2026. The circular provides details of fresh listings from Navi Finserv Limited, Kotak Mahindra Prime Limited, and Tata Steel Limited, as well as further listings from Bajaj Financial Securities Limited, Kotak Mahindra Prime Limited, and Aditya Birla Housing Finance Limited.

## Key Points

- All securities will be traded only in dematerialized form under specified ISIN numbers
- Trading denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs standard and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- Market lot size: 1 unit
- Securities include commercial paper with tenures ranging from approximately 1.5 months to 1 year
- Interest rates range from 6.15% to 10.30% per annum
- All instruments carry cumulative interest payment structure

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for privately placed debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Trading members should use the specified ISIN numbers and scrip codes for trading
- For clarifications, trading members may contact BSE debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: January 16, 2026 (trading admission date)
- **Allotment Dates**: January 14, 2026 and January 16, 2026 (varies by issuer)
- **Redemption Dates**: Range from February 3, 2026 to January 15, 2027

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Low - This is a routine listing notification for debt market participants. The listing of commercial paper provides short-term funding options for the issuing companies and investment opportunities for debt market investors.

**Operational Impact**: Low - Standard operational procedures apply for trading these instruments on the BSE debt segment.

**Investor Impact**: Provides debt investment opportunities with varying risk-return profiles based on credit ratings (A1 to A1+) and interest rates (6.15% to 10.30% p.a.). The instruments offer relatively short-term investment horizons suitable for money market participants.
