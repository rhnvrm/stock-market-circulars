## Summary

BSE's Surveillance Department sought clarification from IIFL Finance Limited (Scrip Code: 532636) and Piramal Finance Limited (Scrip Code: 544597) regarding a news article published on Moneycontrol.com on March 4, 2026, titled "Piramal Finance and IIFL in exploratory talks for an MFI deal: Sources." Both companies responded on the same date with their respective clarifications.

## Key Points

- IIFL Finance stated there is no undisclosed information that could account for recent share price movement and attributed it to market sentiment.
- IIFL Finance acknowledged evaluating various strategic opportunities but confirmed no information requiring disclosure under Regulation 30 of SEBI LODR exists as of March 4, 2026.
- IIFL Finance confirmed no new regulatory or legal proceedings have been initiated against the company.
- Piramal Finance categorically stated the news article is **factually incorrect** and denied the reported exploratory talks.
- Piramal Finance reiterated its commitment to comply with Regulation 30 of SEBI Listing Regulations and to disclose any material developments promptly.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. The clarifications are made in compliance with existing Regulation 30 of the SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015.

## Compliance Requirements

- Both companies are required to promptly notify stock exchanges of any information mandating disclosure under Regulation 30 of SEBI LODR.
- Companies must respond to surveillance queries from BSE/NSE regarding unusual price/volume movements linked to news reports.

## Important Dates

- **March 4, 2026**: News article published on Moneycontrol.com.
- **March 4, 2026**: BSE surveillance query issued to both companies.
- **March 4, 2026**: Both IIFL Finance and Piramal Finance submitted their respective clarifications.

## Impact Assessment

The clarifications are likely to have a moderating effect on speculative trading in both IIFL Finance (532636) and Piramal Finance (544597). Piramal Finance's outright denial of the reported talks removes near-term deal speculation premium from the stock. IIFL Finance's response is more nuanced — it does not deny evaluating strategic opportunities in general but confirms no disclosable event exists currently, which may leave some residual market uncertainty. Investors should monitor both counters for any subsequent Regulation 30 disclosures.
