## Summary

BSE has released an updated list of securities shortlisted under the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, effective March 16, 2026. A total of 53+ companies have been added to the GSM list based on Criteria I and/or Criteria II. These securities will be subject to enhanced surveillance measures including trade-to-trade settlement and potential price band restrictions.

## Key Points

- 53+ securities have been shortlisted under the GSM framework effective March 16, 2026
- Securities are classified under Criteria I, Criteria II, or both Criteria I & II
- Companies span multiple sectors including finance, pharma, agriculture, chemicals, and manufacturing
- GSM is a surveillance measure applied to securities exhibiting unusual price movements without corresponding fundamental changes
- Investors holding these securities should be aware of enhanced trading restrictions

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the updated GSM list and apply appropriate trading restrictions for these securities from March 16, 2026
- Securities under GSM Criteria I are typically subject to trade-to-trade settlement with applicable price bands
- Securities under GSM Criteria II face additional restrictions based on the graded level assigned
- Investors in these securities should consult their brokers regarding applicable trading conditions
- Companies listed under GSM should engage with BSE to understand requirements for removal from the GSM list

## Important Dates

- **March 6, 2026**: Circular issued by BSE
- **March 16, 2026**: Effective date for inclusion of listed securities in the GSM framework

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a high impact on investors and traders holding positions in the 53+ securities listed. GSM placement typically results in:

- **Reduced liquidity**: Trade-to-trade settlement eliminates intraday trading, significantly reducing trading volumes
- **Price discovery impact**: Price bands imposed under GSM restrict price movement, potentially trapping investors
- **Margin requirements**: Enhanced margin requirements may apply to GSM securities
- **Investor sentiment**: GSM listing signals regulatory concern about the fundamental-price disconnect, often causing further price decline
- **Sector concentration**: The list spans finance, pharma, chemicals, agriculture, and manufacturing sectors, suggesting broad surveillance sweep rather than sector-specific concerns

Investors holding any of the 53+ listed securities should review their positions before March 16, 2026 and consult their brokers regarding the specific GSM stage and associated trading restrictions applicable to each security.
