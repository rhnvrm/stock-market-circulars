## Summary

BSE has listed commercial papers issued on private placement basis by six companies on the Debt Market Segment effective January 20, 2026. The securities are admitted to trading in dematerialized form only, with standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof.

## Key Points

- Six companies have listed commercial papers: 360 ONE Prime Limited (8,500 units), Bajaj Finance Limited (1,000 units), Julius Baer Capital (India) Private Limited (7,000 units), Muthoot Fincorp Limited (11,500 units), Infina Finance Private Limited (6,000 units), and Adani Enterprises Ltd (further listing)
- All securities trade only in dematerialized form under respective ISIN numbers
- Standard trading denomination is Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size for all securities is 1 paise
- Face value for all commercial papers is Rs. 500,000 per unit
- Interest rates range from 6.95% p.a. (Bajaj Finance) to 8.83% p.a. (Muthoot Fincorp)
- All listed commercial papers carry CRISIL A1+ and/or ICRA A1+ credit ratings
- Maturity periods range from 3 months (Bajaj Finance - April 2026) to 1 year (others - January 2027)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced in this circular.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance requirements for trading members beyond standard debt market trading procedures.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: January 20, 2026 (listing and trading commencement)
- **Allotment Dates**: January 19-20, 2026
- **Redemption Dates**:
  - Bajaj Finance: April 20, 2026
  - 360 ONE Prime: January 18, 2027
  - Julius Baer Capital: January 19, 2027
  - Muthoot Fincorp: January 18, 2027
  - Infina Finance: January 18, 2027

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact. This is a routine administrative notification informing trading members about new debt instruments available for trading on the debt segment. The listing provides additional investment options for debt market participants but does not affect equity markets or broader trading operations. For queries, trading members may contact the debt department at specified phone numbers (22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915).
