## Summary

BSE has issued a notice under its Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, informing trading members that certain securities (listed in an attached Annexure) will move into their respective higher GSM stages with effect from July 16, 2026. This action is taken pursuant to the GSM Framework published on February 23, 2017, and subsequent related notices. Securities placed in higher GSM stages face progressively stricter trading restrictions, including reduced price bands, trade-for-trade settlement, additional surveillance deposits, and limited trading frequency.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in the Annexure will move to higher GSM stages effective July 16, 2026.
- The GSM framework has four stages (I to IV), each with escalating surveillance actions.
- Stage I: 100% margin and price band of 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band and 50% Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) from buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band, trading permitted only once a week (Monday/first trading day), and 100% ASD from buyers.
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III but with no upward price movement permitted.
- All settlements for these securities will be done on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.
- Reference is made to prior Exchange notices dated February 23, 2017, March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing GSM Framework (established February 23, 2017 and amended via subsequent notices) to a new set of securities identified in the Annexure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin rates, price bands, ASD requirements, and trading frequency restrictions to the affected securities from the effective date.
- Members must ensure trade-for-trade settlement (no netting off) is applied where required.
- Members should refer to the GSM FAQ on the BSE website or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 16, 2026 — securities move to their respective higher GSM stages.
- Notice Date: July 15, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a high trading impact on the affected securities, as movement to a higher GSM stage significantly restricts liquidity through tighter price bands, mandatory trade-for-trade settlement, high additional surveillance deposits, and in higher stages, weekly-only trading windows with no upward price movement. Investors and traders holding or considering these securities should expect reduced liquidity and increased capital requirements for trading. The overall market impact is limited to the specific securities named in the Annexure, which was not included in the extracted text.
