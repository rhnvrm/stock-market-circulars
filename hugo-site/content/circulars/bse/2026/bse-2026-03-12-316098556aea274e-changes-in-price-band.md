## Summary

BSE has revised price bands for 11 equity scrips effective March 13, 2026, under Notice No. 20260312-24. The revisions are implemented by the Surveillance department and affect securities across multiple sectors including healthcare, energy, steel, media, and finance.

## Key Points

- Price bands revised for 11 scrips effective March 13, 2026
- Band limits range from 2% (most restrictive) to 20% (least restrictive)
- Changes are initiated by BSE Surveillance department
- Two scrips assigned the most restrictive 2% band: Shukra Bullions Ltd
- Two scrips assigned the least restrictive 20% band: Bhagawati Gas Ltd and Bhandari Hosiery Exports Ltd
- Members may seek clarifications at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised price bands for the 11 listed scrips
- Orders placed outside the revised price band limits will be rejected by the exchange system
- Members should update internal systems and risk management parameters accordingly
- For clarifications, contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** March 12, 2026
- **Effective Date:** March 13, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The price band revisions affect intraday trading limits for 11 scrips. Scrips assigned tighter bands (2%–5%) such as Shukra Bullions, Amwill Health Care, Jindal Poly Films, and Venus Remedies face more restrictive daily price movement, typically indicating heightened surveillance concern. Scrips assigned 20% bands (Bhagawati Gas, Bhandari Hosiery Exports) may be experiencing relaxation of prior restrictions or volatility normalization. The 10% band applied to DCM Financial Services, Gita Renewable Energy, Mahamaya Steel Industries, Picturehouse Media, and Usha Martin Education represents a standard surveillance-level adjustment. Overall market impact is moderate and localized to traders and investors holding positions in these specific scrips.
