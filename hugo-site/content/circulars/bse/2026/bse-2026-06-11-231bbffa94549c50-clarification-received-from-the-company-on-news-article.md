## Summary

BSE Surveillance (Notice No. 20260611-31) has observed significant price and volume movement in 11 listed equity scrips. The Exchange wrote to these companies seeking relevant information to ensure trading members and investors are adequately informed. Replies have been received from 5 companies, while 6 others are yet to respond.

## Key Points

- BSE Surveillance flagged 11 companies for unusual price/volume activity
- Clarifications (replies) have been received from 5 companies and are attached to this notice
- 6 companies have not yet responded; replies remain awaited
- Notice issued jointly by Deputy Vice President and Manager, Surveillance, BSE Ltd

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced by this notice. It is a surveillance communication requiring listed companies to disclose material information in response to observed unusual market activity.

## Compliance Requirements

- Companies that have not yet responded must submit clarifications to BSE Surveillance at the earliest
- Trading members and investors are advised to review the attached replies from responding companies before making trading decisions in these scrips
- Companies are obligated under SEBI LODR Regulations to promptly disclose information that may explain unusual price/volume movements

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** 11 June 2026
- Replies from the 6 pending companies are awaited; no explicit deadline stated in the notice

## Impact Assessment

Investors and trading members holding or transacting in the 11 listed scrips should exercise caution. The Exchange's surveillance action signals elevated scrutiny of these securities. For the 5 companies that have responded, the attached clarifications should be reviewed to assess whether the price/volume movement was based on material developments. The 6 companies yet to respond remain under active surveillance, and further action (such as trading restrictions) is possible if responses are not forthcoming or reveal undisclosed material information.
