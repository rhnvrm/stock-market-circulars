## Summary

BSE has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework effective early June 2026. Securities listed in Annexure I will attract a minimum 100% margin from June 4, 2026. Certain securities are being downgraded to Stage I, while others are exiting the framework entirely. All newly included securities will move from Rolling Settlement to the Trade-for-Trade segment.

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I will attract minimum 100% margin on all open positions as on June 3, 2026, and new positions from June 4, 2026
- Some securities are being moved to a lower stage (Stage I) under ESM Framework w.e.f. June 3, 2026
- Securities qualifying under ESM will shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment w.e.f. June 4, 2026
- Securities moving to Stage II will be subject to Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction w.e.f. June 3, 2026
- Securities exiting the ESM Framework w.e.f. June 3, 2026 are listed in Annexure II
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under ESM is provided in Annexure III
- ESM placement is purely a market surveillance action and should not be construed as adverse action against the company

## Regulatory Changes

This notice references and builds upon prior ESM circulars: 20230602-44, 20230718-46, 20240809-42, 20240920-63, 20241004-65, and 20250725-61. The current update introduces new inclusions, stage downgrades, and exits under the existing ESM framework. Price bands for exiting securities will revert to pre-ESM levels unless the scrip remains under another surveillance measure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure minimum 100% margin is collected on all open and new positions in newly included ESM securities from the effective dates
- Trading in Stage II securities must comply with the 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism
- Members should treat affected securities as Trade-for-Trade from the applicable effective dates
- For clarifications, members may contact BSE at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **June 3, 2026**: Stage I downgrade effective; Stage II Trade-for-Trade with 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction begins; securities exit ESM framework
- **June 4, 2026**: Minimum 100% margin applies on new positions and existing open positions; shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment effective for newly included securities

## Impact Assessment

The ESM update has a high impact on the affected securities. Securities moved into or remaining in ESM face elevated margin requirements (minimum 100%), restricted price movement (2% band for Stage II), and reduced liquidity due to Trade-for-Trade settlement. These restrictions can significantly affect retail and institutional participation. Securities exiting the framework will see restored price bands and normal settlement, which may improve liquidity. Market participants holding or considering positions in ESM-listed securities should review Annexures I, II, and III for the complete and current list.
