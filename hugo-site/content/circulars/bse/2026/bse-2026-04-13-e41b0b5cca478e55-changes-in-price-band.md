## Summary

BSE's Department of Surveillance (DOSS) has revised the price bands for 19 equity scrips effective April 15, 2026. The revised bands range from 2% to 10%, replacing their previously applicable levels. This is a standard surveillance action to manage volatility and protect investors in select securities.

## Key Points

- Price band revisions apply to 19 scrips across various sectors effective April 15, 2026.
- Bands revised to 2% for 5 scrips: DCM Financial Services, Dhruva Capital Services, Hi-Klass Trading and Investment, Pro Clb Global, Ramgopal Polytex.
- Bands revised to 5% for 9 scrips: Chandra Prabhu International, Containe Technologies, Hemang Resources, Lords Ishwar Hotels, Prudential Sugar Corporation, RDB Real Estate Constructions, Welcure Drugs & Pharmaceuticals, Zenith Steel Pipes & Industries, Zodiac Ventures.
- Bands revised to 10% for 5 scrips: Atlas Cycles (Haryana), Nanta Tech, PAN HR Solution, Panafic Industrials, Parsvnath Developers.
- Action taken by BSE Surveillance department (DOSS); contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications.

## Regulatory Changes

Price bands for the listed scrips have been revised from their existing levels to tighter limits (2%, 5%, or 10%). Price bands restrict the maximum intraday price movement permitted for a security on the exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised price bands and ensure trading systems reflect the new limits from April 15, 2026.
- No action required from listed companies; this is an exchange-level surveillance measure.
- Members seeking clarification should contact bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **April 13, 2026**: Notice issued.
- **April 15, 2026**: Revised price bands come into effect for all 19 scrips.

## Impact Assessment

The tightening of price bands is a surveillance tool used by BSE to curb excessive speculation or abnormal price movements in select securities. The 2% band is the most restrictive and applies to scrips under heightened watch. Traders and investors in the affected scrips will face limited intraday price movement, reducing both risk and liquidity. Scrips moved to tighter bands may experience reduced trading volumes. This action signals that BSE's surveillance team has identified unusual activity or elevated risk in these 19 securities.
