## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, July 18, 2026. The session allows trading members using third-party trading platforms of empaneled vendors or in-house developed systems through ETI APIs to test their trading applications, including functionality for exceptional market conditions such as risk-reduction mode and trading halts. Additionally, the new BOLT Pro TWS version 13.25 will be released, with details to be published via a separate circular.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Commodity Derivatives segment on July 18, 2026
- Two mock trading sessions will be conducted during the day
- Trading members can test third-party or in-house ETI API-based trading applications
- Testing covers exceptional market conditions including risk-reduction mode and trading halt
- New BOLT Pro TWS version 13.25 to be released, with details in a separate circular
- Trades from mock sessions carry no margin obligation, pay-in/pay-out obligation, or legal rights/liabilities
- Members requested to provide feedback on the mock trading session by 6:30 PM

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an operational/testing notice, not a regulatory change.

## Compliance Requirements

No mandatory compliance action required. Trading members using third-party or in-house ETI API-based systems are encouraged to participate for testing purposes. Members are requested to submit feedback on the mock trading session by 6:30 PM on the day of the session.

## Important Dates

- **July 18, 2026 (Saturday)**: Mock trading session
  - Session 1: Log-in 08:30 AM–09:00 AM; Continuous Trading 09:00 AM–12:25 PM; Trade Modification 12:25 PM–12:35 PM
  - Session 2: Log-in 02:45 PM–03:00 PM; Continuous Trading 03:00 PM–05:30 PM; Trade Modification 05:30 PM–05:40 PM
- Feedback deadline: 6:30 PM on July 18, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact as this is a technical testing exercise conducted outside normal trading days with no real financial consequences. The session helps ensure system readiness and stability for trading members using third-party or in-house developed trading applications, reducing operational risk for the Commodity Derivatives segment ahead of live trading.
