## Summary

The Indian Clearing Corporation Limited (ICCL) has issued this notice to all BSE members forwarding a SEBI circular (HO/47/11/11(1)2026-MRD-POD3/I/13804/2026) dated June 15, 2026. The SEBI circular prescribes norms for base price determination, price bands, call auction mechanisms in the pre-open session, and close-out procedures specifically applicable to Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs).

## Key Points

- ICCL is forwarding SEBI Circular No. HO/47/11/11(1)2026-MRD-POD3/I/13804/2026 dated June 15, 2026 for member awareness
- The circular covers four key areas: base price norms, price band norms, call auction in pre-open session, and close-out procedure for ETFs
- The full SEBI circular is enclosed as Annexure-1 for reference
- All BSE members are advised to take note of the norms prescribed

## Regulatory Changes

SEBI has issued a comprehensive circular on June 15, 2026 establishing or revising norms governing ETF trading mechanics. The changes cover:
- **Base Price**: Norms for determining the base price of ETFs
- **Price Bands**: Framework for applicable price band limits on ETF securities
- **Call Auction (Pre-Open Session)**: Rules governing ETF participation in or conduct during the pre-open call auction session
- **Close-out Procedure**: Prescribed procedures for closing out ETF positions

## Compliance Requirements

- All BSE members must take note of the SEBI circular and the norms prescribed therein
- Members should refer to Annexure-1 (the full SEBI circular) for detailed procedural requirements
- Compliance with the updated base price, price band, and call auction norms is mandatory for ETF trading activity

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: June 15, 2026
- **SEBI Circular Date**: June 15, 2026 (HO/47/11/11(1)2026-MRD-POD3/I/13804/2026)
- Effective date of the norms: as prescribed in the enclosed SEBI circular (Annexure-1)

## Impact Assessment

This circular has high operational impact on all market participants trading in ETFs on BSE. Changes to base price methodology, price bands, and pre-open call auction rules can directly affect ETF price discovery, liquidity, and risk management. Members involved in ETF market-making, arbitrage, or institutional ETF trading should review Annexure-1 carefully to ensure their systems and procedures are aligned with the updated SEBI norms. The close-out procedure changes may also affect settlement and risk desk operations at member firms.
