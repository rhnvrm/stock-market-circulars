## Summary

BSE has issued a Master Circular consolidating all previous circulars issued by the Surveillance department for the Equity Derivatives Segment. This circular serves as a single reference document for all policy and operational matters related to surveillance in the equity derivatives segment. No new regulations are introduced; this is a consolidation exercise.

## Key Points

- BSE periodically consolidates departmental circulars into a single master circular for ease of reference
- This circular consolidates all Surveillance department circulars pertaining to the Equity Derivatives Segment
- All actions taken under previously rescinded guidelines remain valid and unaffected
- Rights, privileges, obligations, liabilities, and penalties accrued under rescinded guidelines continue to remain in effect
- The master circular is available on the BSE website under Members -> Information for Members

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This circular is a consolidation of existing circulars issued by the BSE Surveillance department for the Equity Derivatives Segment. Previously rescinded guidelines are superseded by this master circular, but their historical applicability remains intact.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members are required to refer to this Master Circular as the single authoritative document for surveillance-related policies and operational matters in the Equity Derivatives Segment
- Members should ensure compliance with all provisions outlined in the consolidated Master Circular (attachment: Master_Circular_EDS_2026)
- For clarifications, members should contact BSE at 022-2272 3030 or email bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** 30 April 2026
- **Notice No.:** 20260430-28
- Effective immediately upon issuance; supersedes all previously issued individual circulars covered under this consolidation

## Impact Assessment

This master circular impacts all exchange members participating in the Equity Derivatives Segment. As a consolidation document, it simplifies compliance by providing a single reference point for surveillance policies. There is no immediate operational impact beyond serving as an updated reference document. Members should review the attached Master_Circular_EDS_2026 to ensure ongoing compliance with all surveillance requirements. The continuity clause ensures no disruption to ongoing proceedings, penalties, or obligations established under previously rescinded guidelines.
