## Summary

BSE has issued an update to its Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, referencing a series of prior notices dating back to June 2023. Securities listed in Annexure I have met the criteria for ESM inclusion and will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open and new positions effective July 07, 2026. Certain securities already under ESM will move down to a lower Stage I, some will exit the framework entirely (Annexure II), and others will move to Stage II with additional trading restrictions. A consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework is provided in Annexure III.

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I now qualify for ESM and require 100% margin on all open positions (as of July 06, 2026) and new positions from July 07, 2026.
- Securities continuing in ESM but moving to a lower Stage I are also listed in Annexure I, effective July 06, 2026.
- Securities exiting ESM entirely are listed in Annexure II, effective July 06, 2026.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the ESM framework is provided in Annexure III.
- Securities newly qualifying under ESM shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment effective July 07, 2026.
- Securities shifting to Stage II are placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism effective July 06, 2026.
- ESM inclusion is purely a market surveillance measure and should not be viewed as an adverse action against the company.
- Once a security exits ESM, its price band reverts to the level applicable before ESM inclusion, unless it remains under another surveillance framework.
- ESM operates in conjunction with other surveillance measures already in force.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice continues the ESM framework established under earlier BSE notices (June 02, 2023; July 18, 2023; August 09, 2024; September 20, 2024; October 04, 2024; and July 25, 2025). No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a periodic review and update applying existing ESM criteria to a new set of securities, with movements between stages and exits based on ongoing surveillance review.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply minimum 100% margin on all open and new positions for securities newly included in ESM, effective July 07, 2026.
- Members must ensure ESM-qualifying securities are traded only in the Trade-for-Trade segment, not Rolling Settlement, from July 07, 2026.
- For securities moved to Stage II, members must observe the 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction trading mechanism from July 06, 2026.
- Queries or clarifications should be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 06, 2026: Stage movements (to Stage I or Stage II), ESM exits, and open position margin basis take effect.
- July 07, 2026: 100% margin applies to new positions; newly qualifying securities shift to Trade-for-Trade segment.

## Impact Assessment

The ESM framework significantly restricts trading in affected securities by imposing high margin requirements and moving them to the Trade-for-Trade segment, which curbs speculative trading and reduces liquidity. Investors and traders holding or considering positions in securities named in the annexures face higher capital requirements and settlement restrictions. Securities exiting the framework regain more normal trading conditions, including restored price bands, improving their liquidity profile. Since exact security names are contained only in the annexures (not reproduced in the notice text), affected market participants should consult the annexures directly to determine specific impact on their holdings.
