## Summary

BSE has disseminated Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) notifications pertaining to Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) and the Social Stock Exchange (SSE). The notice references two gazette notifications — GSR415 and GSR416 — along with a Press Information Bureau press release, signalling regulatory updates to the CSR framework and/or SSE-related rules applicable to listed companies.

## Key Points

- BSE Notice No. 20260601-37 dated 01 June 2026 forwards MCA gazette notifications to market participants.
- Two gazette notifications are referenced: GSR415 and GSR416, both likely amending the Companies (CSR Policy) Rules or related SSE provisions.
- A Press Information Bureau press release accompanies the notifications, providing official government communication on the changes.
- The notice is addressed to Listed Companies under the SSE segment, managed by the DCS-Listing department.

## Regulatory Changes

The MCA has issued gazette notifications GSR415 and GSR416, which are expected to amend provisions under the Companies Act 2013 related to Corporate Social Responsibility and/or the Social Stock Exchange framework. The exact nature of the amendments would be detailed in the attached gazette documents. These may include changes to eligibility criteria, reporting obligations, or operational rules for entities registered on the Social Stock Exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Listed companies with CSR obligations should review GSR415 and GSR416 for any changes to CSR policy rules, eligible activities, or spending thresholds.
- Entities registered or intending to register on the Social Stock Exchange should assess the impact of the gazette amendments on their listing and disclosure obligations.
- Companies should ensure internal policies and board CSR committee mandates are updated in line with the revised MCA rules.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 01 June 2026
- Effective dates of the MCA gazette notifications (GSR415, GSR416) would be as specified within those gazette documents.

## Impact Assessment

The dissemination of MCA notifications via BSE affects all listed companies that have CSR obligations under the Companies Act 2013 and any non-profit organisations or social enterprises participating in or planning to participate in the Social Stock Exchange. The medium-term impact depends on the substance of GSR415 and GSR416; if they introduce new compliance mandates or relax existing ones, companies will need to update their CSR reporting and governance structures accordingly. No immediate trading impact is anticipated.
