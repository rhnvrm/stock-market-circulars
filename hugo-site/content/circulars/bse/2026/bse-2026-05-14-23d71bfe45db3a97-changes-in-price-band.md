## Summary

BSE's Department of Surveillance (DOSS) has revised the price bands for 15 equity scrips effective May 15, 2026. The revised bands range from 2% to 10%, with several scrips assigned the most restrictive 2% band, indicating heightened surveillance concern for those securities.

## Key Points

- Price bands revised for 15 scrips across BSE equity segment effective May 15, 2026
- Bands revised to three levels: 2% (most restrictive), 5%, and 10%
- Five scrips assigned the tightest 2% band: Atvo Enterprises Ltd (532090), Chiraharit Ltd (544561), Kuber Udyog Ltd (539408), Munoth Financial Services Ltd (531821), Vashu Bhagnani Industries Ltd (532011)
- Four scrips revised to 10% band: Black Box Ltd (500463), Camex Ltd (524440), DMR Engineering Ltd (543410), Naksh Precious Metals Ltd (539402)
- Six scrips revised to 5% band: Aten Papers & Foam Ltd (544417), Bombay Super Hybrid Seeds Ltd (544757), Chemfab Alkalis Ltd (541269), FGP Ltd (500142), Niraj Cement Structurals Ltd (532986), TeleCanor Global Ltd (530595)
- Action initiated by DOSS (Department of Surveillance and Supervision)

## Regulatory Changes

Price bands restrict the maximum permissible intraday price movement for a security. The revised bands replace the previously applicable limits for these 15 scrips. Tighter bands (2%) are typically applied to securities under enhanced surveillance due to price volatility, low liquidity, or other risk factors identified by the exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised price bands and ensure order placement complies with new limits from May 15, 2026
- Orders placed outside the revised price band will be rejected by the exchange trading system
- Members may seek clarification by writing to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** May 14, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 15, 2026 (next trading day)

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding positions in the 15 affected scrips will face restricted intraday price movement from May 15, 2026. The 2% band stocks face the greatest constraint, potentially reducing liquidity and widening bid-ask spreads. Stocks moved to lower bands may signal ongoing surveillance scrutiny. The 10% band assignments represent a more permissive level and may reflect normalization for those scrips. Overall market impact is limited given the small number and relatively obscure nature of the affected companies.
