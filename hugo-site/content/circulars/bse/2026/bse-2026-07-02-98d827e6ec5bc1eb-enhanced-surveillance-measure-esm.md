## Summary

BSE has issued its periodic update on the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, referencing a series of prior notices dating back to June 2023. The notice identifies securities newly qualifying for ESM inclusion (subject to 100% margin), securities moving to a lower stage (Stage I), securities shifting up to Stage II (with tighter trading restrictions), and securities exiting the framework entirely. Specific security names are listed in the attached Annexures I, II, and III, which are not included in the notice text.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I have met the criteria for ESM inclusion and will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open positions as of July 3, 2026 and new positions created from July 6, 2026.
- Some securities continuing under ESM will be moved to a lower Stage I effective July 3, 2026 (also listed in Annexure I).
- Securities newly qualifying under ESM will be shifted from the Rolling Settlement segment to the Trade-for-Trade segment effective July 6, 2026.
- Securities shifting to Stage II will be placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction, effective July 3, 2026.
- Securities exiting the ESM framework effective July 3, 2026 are listed in Annexure II; a consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework is in Annexure III.
- ESM inclusion is purely a market surveillance measure and should not be construed as an adverse action against the concerned company.
- Price bands for securities exiting ESM will revert to their pre-ESM applicable band, unless the scrip remains subject to another surveillance framework.
- ESM operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchanges.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice is a periodic implementation update under the existing ESM framework (originally established via notices dated June 2, 2023, July 18, 2023, August 9, 2024, September 20, 2024, October 4, 2024, and July 25, 2025). No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a routine application of existing criteria to reclassify specific securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply minimum 100% margin on open and new positions in newly added ESM securities from the effective dates.
- Members must ensure newly qualifying securities are traded only in the Trade-for-Trade segment from July 6, 2026.
- Members should observe the revised price band (2%) and Periodic Call Auction mechanism for securities moving to Stage II from July 3, 2026.
- Queries should be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 3, 2026: Stage I movement, Stage II shift (2% price band, Periodic Call Auction), and ESM exits take effect.
- July 6, 2026: 100% margin requirement and Trade-for-Trade segment shift take effect for newly qualifying securities.

## Impact Assessment

Securities newly placed under ESM or moved to Stage II face materially reduced liquidity due to 100% margin requirements, Trade-for-Trade settlement, and tighter price bands, which can significantly affect trading volumes and price discovery for the specific stocks involved. Securities exiting the framework should see restored trading flexibility and reinstated price bands. Since the actual list of affected securities is contained in the unattached annexures, the broader market impact cannot be assessed from this notice text alone, but the framework itself is a standard, recurring surveillance mechanism with no systemic market-wide implications.
