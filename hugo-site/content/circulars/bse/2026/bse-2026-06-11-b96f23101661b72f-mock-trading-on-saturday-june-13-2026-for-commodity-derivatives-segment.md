## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, June 13, 2026. The session will run from both the primary site (PR) and disaster recovery site (DR), giving trading members an opportunity to test their platforms, ETI API-based systems, and third-party or in-house trading applications under various market conditions.

## Key Points

- Mock trading is scheduled on Saturday, June 13, 2026 for the Commodity Derivatives segment.
- Both primary (PR) and disaster recovery (DR) sites will be used during the session.
- Members using third-party empaneled vendor platforms or in-house ETI API-based systems can participate.
- There will be no new BOLT TWS release.
- Trades from mock sessions carry no margin, pay-in, pay-out obligations, or legal liabilities.
- Members are encouraged to submit feedback by 5:00 pm on the day of the session.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an operational testing notice.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are requested to actively participate in the mock trading session.
- Feedback must be submitted to BSE by 5:00 pm on June 13, 2026 via the contact details provided.
- Members with queries may contact their Relationship Manager, BSE Helpdesk, or BSE Tech Support.

## Important Dates

- **Mock Trading Date:** Saturday, June 13, 2026
- **Log-in Window:** 09:15 am – 10:00 am
- **Continuous Trading (Primary Site):** 10:00 am – 01:00 pm
- **Switchover to Disaster Recovery Site:** 01:00 pm – 01:45 pm
- **Continuous Trading (DR Site):** 01:45 pm – 03:45 pm
- **Feedback Deadline:** 5:00 pm on June 13, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Impact is minimal. This is a routine infrastructure and system readiness test with no financial obligations or market consequences. It provides trading members the opportunity to validate their trading systems, including testing exceptional market conditions such as risk-reduction mode and trading halts. Participation is voluntary but strongly encouraged by BSE to ensure system reliability.
