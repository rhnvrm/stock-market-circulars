## Summary

ICCL (Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd.) has published the settlement calendar for the Offer to Buy – Acquisition Window (Takeover) of BABA ARTS LIMITED. The bidding window is open from June 9, 2026 to June 22, 2026, with settlement scheduled for July 7, 2026 under Settlement No. 2026067.

## Key Points

- Settlement Type: Takeover; Settlement No. 2026067
- Bid Start Date: June 9, 2026; Bid End Date: June 22, 2026
- Obligation Download Date: July 6, 2026
- Settlement Date: July 7, 2026
- Demat shares must be delivered exclusively via the Early Pay-in of Shares mechanism available on both CDSL and NSDL depositories
- Market Type to use in depository system: "(Takeover)" with Settlement No. 2026067
- CDSL & NSDL early pay-in securities will be earmarked/blocked in client accounts under the requisite market type and settlement number
- Members and Custodians are NOT required to upload any EPN file
- Members should refer to MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo file for client-wise Stamp Duty charges

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. Members are directed to refer to Notice No. 20200528-32 dated May 28, 2020, regarding settlement as per Revised Guidelines of Mechanism for acquisition of shares through Stock Exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members/Custodians must ensure early pay-in of securities is reflected in BSE/ICCL systems before prescribed timelines for accepting/confirming bids on the BSE OTB platform
- Early pay-in must be completed sufficiently in advance of the closure of the BSE OTB Bidding window (for member bids) and the Confirmation window (for Custodian confirmations)
- Use Market Type "(Takeover)" and Settlement No. 2026067 when submitting in depository systems
- No EPN file upload required

## Important Dates

## Impact Assessment

This notice is operational in nature and directly affects Members and Custodians participating in the BABA ARTS LIMITED takeover offer via BSE's OTB (Offer to Buy) platform. Participants must coordinate their depository early pay-in processes with the BSE OTB bidding/confirmation windows to avoid rejection of bids. The impact is limited to parties involved in this specific acquisition window; no broader market-wide impact is expected.
