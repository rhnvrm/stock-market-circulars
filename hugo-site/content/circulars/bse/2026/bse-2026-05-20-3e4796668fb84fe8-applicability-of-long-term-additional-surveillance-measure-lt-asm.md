## Summary

BSE has issued a notice under the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework imposing 100% margins on select securities effective May 25, 2026, applicable to all open positions as of May 22, 2026 and new positions created thereafter. Additionally, securities are being moved to higher stages of the LT-ASM framework and others are being removed from it, both effective May 21, 2026.

## Key Points

- 100% margins will apply from May 25, 2026 on all open positions as on May 22, 2026 and new positions created from May 25, 2026 onwards (Annexure I, Part A)
- Securities moved to higher stages of LT-ASM framework effective May 21, 2026 (Annexure I, Part B)
- Lower Price Band applicable from May 21, 2026 for securities in elevated LT-ASM stages
- Certain securities to continue in LT-ASM but transferred to T / XT / MT / TS Group effective May 25, 2026 (Annexure I, Part D)
- Securities moving out of the LT-ASM framework effective May 21, 2026 listed in Annexure II
- Consolidated list of all securities under the framework provided in Annexure III
- Shortlisting is based on XBRL submissions by listed companies and is purely for market surveillance purposes

## Regulatory Changes

This notice is issued under the revised LT-ASM framework, with reference to the following prior notices:
- Exchange notice no. 20180321-46 dated March 21, 2018 (original LT-ASM framework)
- Exchange notice no. 20181027-1 dated October 27, 2018
- Exchange notice no. 20201204-56 dated December 04, 2020
- Exchange notice no. 20210604-41 dated June 04, 2021
- Exchange notice no. 20240809-46 dated August 09, 2024
- Exchange notice no. 20240920-63 dated September 20, 2024 (latest revision)

The LT-ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the exchanges.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure 100% margins are collected on all open positions as of May 22, 2026 and new positions from May 25, 2026 for securities listed in Annexure I (Part A)
- Members must apply the Lower Price Band for securities moved to higher LT-ASM stages from May 21, 2026
- Securities in Annexure I (Part D) must be transferred to the T / XT / MT / TS Group effective May 25, 2026
- For clarifications, members may contact bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **May 21, 2026**: Stage upgrades within LT-ASM framework take effect; Lower Price Band applicable; securities exit LT-ASM framework (Annexure II)
- **May 22, 2026**: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margins
- **May 25, 2026**: 100% margin requirement effective for open positions (as of May 22) and all new positions; transfer to T / XT / MT / TS Group effective

## Impact Assessment

The imposition of 100% margins significantly increases the cost of holding or creating new positions in affected securities, reducing leverage and liquidity for traders and investors. This measure is a strong surveillance action intended to curb speculative activity in securities flagged through XBRL data analysis. Securities moved to higher LT-ASM stages face increasingly restrictive trading conditions including tighter price bands. The exit of certain securities from the framework (Annexure II) provides relief to those stocks, with price bands reinstated to pre-LT-ASM levels unless another surveillance measure applies. Market participants should review the attached annexures carefully to identify affected securities and adjust their risk and margin management accordingly.
