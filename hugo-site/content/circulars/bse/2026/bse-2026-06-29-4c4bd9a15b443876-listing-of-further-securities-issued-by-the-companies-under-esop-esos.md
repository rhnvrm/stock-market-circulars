## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that further new securities issued by 14 companies under Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP/ESOS) are listed and admitted for trading on the Exchange with effect from Tuesday, June 30, 2026. None of the newly listed shares are subject to any lock-in period.

## Key Points

- 14 companies have issued new shares under ESOP/ESOS schemes, all admitted for trading from June 30, 2026.
- No lock-in applies to any of the newly listed securities.
- Total new shares span a wide range — from 1,250 shares (UTI AMC) to over 1.03 crore shares (Meesho Limited).
- Face values vary across companies: ₹1, ₹2, ₹5, and ₹10.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice informing trading members of newly admitted securities under existing ESOP/ESOS frameworks.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are requested to take note of the newly listed securities and update their systems accordingly for trading from June 30, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **Effective date for trading:** Tuesday, June 30, 2026
- **Notice date:** June 29, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact. These are routine ESOP/ESOS allotments representing incremental share capital additions. The largest addition is Meesho Limited with 1,03,59,200 new shares (ISIN: INE0VDM01015), followed by HDFC Bank Limited with 5,18,036 shares. All allotments are standard employee compensation-related issuances with no lock-in, meaning they are immediately tradeable.
