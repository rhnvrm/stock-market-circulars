## Summary

ICCL (Indian Clearing Corporation Limited) has announced market wide position limits (MWPL) for two scrips newly introduced in the Equity Derivatives Segment: GE Vernova T&D India Ltd (BSE: 522275) and Radico Khaitan Ltd (BSE: 532497). These limits are effective from May 27, 2026, and are issued in pursuance of Notice No. 20260321-1 dated March 21, 2026, and in partial modification of circular no. 20231229-46 dated December 29, 2023.

## Key Points

- Two scrips newly introduced in Equity Derivatives: GE Vernova T&D India Ltd and Radico Khaitan Ltd
- Limits apply to Stock Brokers, FPIs (Category I & II), and Mutual Funds
- Position limits are defined across four categories: Market Wide, Client/NRI/MF Scheme, TM Proprietary, and TM (Proprietary + Client) / FPI Cat I / MF
- All limits are effective from May 27, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

In partial modification of circular no. 20231229-46 dated December 29, 2023, and in pursuance of Notice No. 20260321-1 dated March 21, 2026, the following market wide position limits are now applicable for the two newly introduced scrips in the Equity Derivatives Segment.

## Compliance Requirements

All Members and Participants must ensure that their positions (including client and proprietary positions) in the stock derivatives contracts of the below scrips do not exceed the prescribed limits:

| Scrip Code | Name | Market Wide Limit (Shares) | Client/NRI/MF Scheme | TM Proprietary | TM (Prop+Client)/FPI Cat I/MF |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 522275 | GE Vernova T&D India Ltd | 1,88,19,420 | 18,81,875 | 37,63,875 | 56,45,750 |
| 532497 | Radico Khaitan Ltd | 1,20,10,217 | 12,00,900 | 24,01,950 | 36,03,000 |

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** May 27, 2026 — Position limits applicable from this date

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational update that enables derivatives trading in two newly listed scrips. Stock brokers, FPIs (Category I & II), and Mutual Funds trading in GE Vernova T&D India Ltd or Radico Khaitan Ltd derivatives must monitor their open positions against these prescribed limits to remain compliant. Breaching these limits may attract penalty or forced position closure by ICCL. The limits are moderate in size, suggesting standard treatment consistent with mid-cap scrips entering the derivatives segment.
