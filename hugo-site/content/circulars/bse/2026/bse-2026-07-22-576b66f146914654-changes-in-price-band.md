## Summary

BSE has notified Trading Members that the price bands applicable to 16 specified scrips have been revised from their existing levels, with effect from July 23, 2026. The revised bands range from 2% to 10% depending on the scrip.

## Key Points

- 16 scrips have had their price bands revised.
- Revised price bands range from 2% (e.g., DCM Shriram Fine Chemicals, Gaudium IVF and Women Health, Hi-Klass Trading and Investment, India Cements Capital) to 10% (e.g., Nanavati Ventures, Newtrac Foods & Beverages, Polymac Thermoformers, Shalimar Paints, Valencia Nutrition).
- Scrips with 5% revised bands include Indian Link Chain Manufacturers, Indobell Insulations, NCL Research & Financial Services, Olatech Solutions, Rishabh Digha Steel & Allied Products, SC Agrotech, and Trishakti Industries.
- Members with queries can contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine surveillance-driven price band revision under BSE's Department of Surveillance (DOSS) rather than a new regulatory framework. Price bands act as circuit filters restricting the maximum permissible price movement for a scrip in a trading session.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading Members must update their trading systems to reflect the revised price bands for the listed scrips before the effective date and ensure orders comply with the new circuit limits.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 22, 2026
- Effective Date: July 23, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The revision affects intraday and session-level price volatility limits for the 16 named scrips, most of which appear to be small-cap or thinly traded securities. Tighter bands (2-5%) will constrain price discovery and reduce volatility for affected stocks, while wider bands (10%) allow greater movement. Market-wide impact is limited given the narrow scope of scrips affected, but investors and traders holding these specific securities should adjust order placement strategies accordingly.
