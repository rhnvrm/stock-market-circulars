## Summary

BSE Clearing Limited (BSECL) has issued a notice informing market participants that the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) has released additional guidelines, vide Circular No. NPCI/UPI/OC/215A/2025-26, governing the usage of UPI APIs. The guidelines mandate that UPI AutoPay mandate execution requests be initiated only during NPCI-defined non-peak hours.

## Key Points

- NPCI has issued additional guidelines on UPI API usage via Circular No. NPCI/UPI/OC/215A/2025-26.
- UPI AutoPay mandate execution requests must be initiated only during non-peak hours as defined by NPCI.
- Peak hours are specified as 10:00 AM to 1:00 PM and 5:00 PM to 9:30 PM.
- Market participants are required to review existing UPI AutoPay mandate execution processes.
- Mandate execution requests must be scheduled only during the prescribed non-peak hours.
- Stakeholders must take necessary actions to comply with directions from NPCI and communications from respective Payment Aggregators.

## Regulatory Changes

NPCI has introduced a restriction on the timing of UPI AutoPay mandate execution requests, confining them to non-peak windows to reduce load on UPI infrastructure during high-traffic periods. This is communicated to BSE market participants via BSECL's Mutual Fund department.

## Compliance Requirements

- All concerned stakeholders (including intermediaries facilitating UPI AutoPay-based mutual fund transactions) must review their current mandate execution scheduling processes.
- Execution requests should be reprogrammed to run only outside the peak windows of 10:00 AM–1:00 PM and 5:00 PM–9:30 PM.
- Entities must coordinate with respective Payment Aggregators to ensure alignment with NPCI directions.
- The notice and NPCI Circular No. NPCI/UPI/OC/215A/2025-26 (annexed) should be treated as important communications requiring compliance action.

## Important Dates

- Notice issued: 24 Jul 2026 (Notice No. 20260724-1).
- No specific future compliance deadline stated; action is expected to be taken promptly to align mandate execution scheduling with the non-peak hour requirement.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is primarily operational for mutual fund RTAs, payment aggregators, and platforms processing UPI AutoPay-based SIP/mandate transactions, who will need to adjust execution scheduling systems. There is no direct impact on listed securities or trading of specific stocks. Investors using UPI AutoPay for SIP mandates may experience shifts in the exact timing of mandate debits, though execution should still occur within the same day outside peak windows.
