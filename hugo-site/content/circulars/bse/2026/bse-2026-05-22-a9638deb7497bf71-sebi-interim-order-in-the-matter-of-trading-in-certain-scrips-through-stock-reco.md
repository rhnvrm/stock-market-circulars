## Summary

SEBI, vide order no. WTM/KV/ISD/ISD-SEC-7/32413/2026-27 dated May 22, 2026, has issued an interim order restraining 7 individuals from buying, selling, or dealing in securities either directly or indirectly with immediate effect. The order pertains to alleged trading in certain scrips through stock recommendations made on social media platforms.

## Key Points

- 7 individuals — all sharing the surname Gupta — are restrained from trading in securities with immediate effect
- The order is in connection with stock recommendations given on social media platforms
- Noticees with open positions in exchange-traded derivative contracts may close out or square off within 3 months from the order date, or at contract expiry, whichever is earlier
- Existing pay-in and pay-out obligations for transactions executed before close of trading on May 22, 2026 may be settled
- The full order is available on the SEBI website at www.sebi.gov.in

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes. This is an enforcement action under existing SEBI powers issuing an ex-parte interim order under securities law provisions.

## Compliance Requirements

- All trading members must take note of this order and ensure compliance immediately
- Members must prevent the 7 named individuals from executing any buy, sell, or dealing transactions in securities
- The restriction applies directly and indirectly in any manner

## Important Dates

- **Order Date:** May 22, 2026 (effective immediately)
- **Derivative Position Close-out Deadline:** Within 3 months from May 22, 2026 (i.e., by August 22, 2026) or at contract expiry, whichever is earlier

## Impact Assessment

This interim order has immediate market impact for the named individuals, who are completely barred from securities transactions. Trading members across all exchanges must block these PANs from executing trades. The case highlights ongoing SEBI vigilance around social media-based stock tipping and potential market manipulation, signaling continued enforcement action in this space. No broad market disruption is expected, but brokers must act promptly to ensure compliance.
