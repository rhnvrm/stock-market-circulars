## Summary

BSE has issued a clarification, as advised by SEBI, regarding the applicability of SEBI regulatory exemptions to listed entities undergoing the Pre-Packaged Insolvency Resolution Process (PPIRP) under Chapter III-A of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016 (IBC). The clarification establishes that exemptions available under various SEBI Regulations for Resolution Plans approved under Section 31 of the IBC are **not** available for Resolution Plans approved under Section 54L of the IBC (the PPIRP framework).

## Key Points

- SEBI regulatory exemptions applicable to Resolution Plans under Section 31 of the IBC do not extend to Section 54L (PPIRP) resolution plans.
- Listed entities undergoing PPIRP must comply with **all** applicable procedural and regulatory requirements under relevant SEBI Regulations.
- The clarification covers all SEBI Regulations, not limited to any specific regulation.
- This advisory was issued at SEBI's direction and is binding on listed companies.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular does not introduce new regulations but clarifies the scope of existing exemptions. The key distinction is:

- **Section 31 IBC (CIRP):** SEBI exemptions for Resolution Plans are available.
- **Section 54L IBC (PPIRP):** SEBI exemptions for Resolution Plans are **not** available.

Listed entities undergoing PPIRP cannot rely on IBC-related SEBI exemptions and must navigate the full regulatory framework under SEBI.

## Compliance Requirements

- Listed entities currently undergoing or planning to undergo PPIRP must ensure full compliance with all applicable SEBI Regulations.
- Companies must not assume that exemptions granted under the CIRP framework (Section 31) are transferable to the PPIRP framework (Section 54L).
- Legal and compliance teams of listed companies involved in PPIRP proceedings should review their obligations under relevant SEBI Regulations, including but not limited to LODR, SAST, and ICDR regulations.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** 01 June 2026
- **Effective:** Immediately upon issuance; no separate effective date specified.

## Impact Assessment

This clarification has a targeted but significant impact on listed entities undergoing the PPIRP framework. Companies that may have assumed a regulatory exemption pathway similar to standard CIRP proceedings will need to reassess their compliance obligations. The impact on the broader market is limited, as PPIRP applies only to MSMEs and specific eligible entities, but for affected companies, the compliance burden is materially higher. Legal counsel and compliance officers of PPIRP-eligible listed entities should treat this as a high-priority advisory.
