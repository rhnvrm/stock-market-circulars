## Summary

Indian Clearing Corporation Limited (ICCL) has issued the client margin reporting schedule for March 2026 for the Commodity Derivatives Segment. The notice (No. 20260302-11) details the last permissible date for margin reporting and the corresponding penalty date for each trade date during the month.

## Key Points

- Covers all trading days in the Commodity Derivatives Segment from 02-Mar-2026 through 31-Mar-2026.
- Each trade date has a designated Last Date of Margin Reporting and a Penalty Date.
- Margin reporting deadlines extend into early April 2026 for trade dates at month-end.
- The final trade date covered (31-Mar-2026) has a penalty date of 09-Apr-2026.
- Issued jointly by Manoj Panchal and Omkar Hatkar, Deputy Managers at ICCL.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is a routine monthly schedule circular issued in accordance with existing SEBI/ICCL margin reporting norms for the Commodity Derivatives Segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members of the Commodity Derivatives Segment must ensure client margin reports are submitted by the Last Date of Margin Reporting for each respective trade date.
- Failure to report by the stipulated deadline will attract penalties effective from the Penalty Date.
- Members should calendar all reporting deadlines to avoid non-compliance charges.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on members participating in the Commodity Derivatives Segment. Brokers and clearing members must align their back-office processes with the published schedule to avoid penalties. The reporting window is typically around 5-7 trading days post trade date. End-of-month trade dates (26-Mar to 31-Mar) carry extended reporting deadlines into early April due to public holidays (Good Friday on 03-Apr-2026 implied by the gap to 06-Apr). No market-wide or investor-facing impact is expected.
