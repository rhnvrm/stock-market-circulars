## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that 18 companies have fixed record dates for redemption of their commercial papers. Trading members are advised not to deal in the commercial papers of these companies effective from their respective ex-dates (which are the same as the record dates).

## Key Points

- 18 commercial paper issuers across multiple settlement cycles (DR 640 to DR 643 of 2026-2027) are affected
- Record dates and ex-dates are identical for all issuers, ranging from 29 May 2026 to 3 June 2026
- Trading is suspended to facilitate orderly redemption of these debt instruments
- Scrip codes range from 729667 to 731501 on the BSE Debt segment
- Issuers include prominent NBFCs such as Bajaj Finance, Manappuram Finance, AAVAS Financiers, Capri Global Capital, UGRO Capital, and Nuvama Wealth Finance, as well as corporates like KEC International, Network18, Reliance Jio Infocomm, and 360 ONE group entities

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice under existing BSE listing and trading regulations for debt instruments.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute any buy or sell transactions in the listed commercial papers of the specified companies on or after their respective ex-dates
- Members should update their systems to block or flag these scrip codes ahead of the ex-dates

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a contained, routine impact on the BSE Debt segment. The trading suspension is a standard pre-redemption measure ensuring no secondary market transactions occur in instruments that are about to be redeemed. Holders of these commercial papers will receive principal repayment on the respective record dates. There is no equity market impact. The issuers span NBFCs, infrastructure, media, and financial services sectors, reflecting normal end-of-tenor commercial paper redemptions across the June 2026 settlement cycle.
