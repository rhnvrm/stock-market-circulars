## Summary

BSE has issued its periodic update to the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, referencing a series of earlier notices dating back to June 2023. Securities listed in Annexure I have met the criteria for ESM inclusion and will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open and new positions effective July 17, 2026. Certain securities already under ESM will move down to Stage I, while others will move up to Stage II with tighter trading restrictions. A separate list of securities will exit the ESM framework effective July 16, 2026, with their price bands reinstated to pre-ESM levels (subject to no other surveillance measure applying).

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I newly qualify for ESM and will require minimum 100% margin on all open positions (as of July 16, 2026) and new positions from July 17, 2026.
- Securities continuing in ESM but moving to the lower Stage I are also included in Annexure I.
- Securities shifting to Stage II will be placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction, effective July 16, 2026.
- All ESM-qualifying securities move from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment effective July 17, 2026.
- Securities exiting the framework (Annexure II) effective July 16, 2026 will have their price bands reinstated to pre-ESM levels, unless subject to another surveillance framework.
- Annexure III provides a consolidated list of all securities currently under the ESM framework.
- ESM operates alongside other prevailing surveillance measures and inclusion does not imply adverse findings against the company.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing ESM framework (established via BSE notices dated June 2, 2023, July 18, 2023, August 9, 2024, September 20, 2024, October 4, 2024, and July 25, 2025) to a fresh set of securities based on periodic review.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply minimum 100% margin on open positions (as of July 16, 2026) and new positions (from July 17, 2026) in newly qualifying ESM securities.
- Members must ensure Stage II securities trade under Trade-for-Trade with 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction from July 16, 2026.
- Members must monitor the transition of securities exiting ESM and the reinstatement of applicable price bands.
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 16, 2026: Stage movements (to Stage I/Stage II) and ESM exits take effect; open positions as of this date subject to new margin requirement.
- July 17, 2026: New ESM inclusions attract minimum 100% margin on open and new positions; affected securities shift to Trade-for-Trade segment.

## Impact Assessment

Securities newly placed under ESM or moved to Stage II face significantly reduced liquidity due to 100% margin requirements, Trade-for-Trade settlement, and tight price bands, likely depressing trading volumes and increasing price volatility risk for holders. Conversely, securities exiting ESM or moving to Stage I should see improved trading flexibility and normalized price bands, which may support liquidity recovery. Specific stock tickers were not enumerated in the notice text (contained in the referenced Annexures), so investors must consult the attached annexure for the exact list of affected securities.
