## Summary

BSE has issued updated currency conversion factors to be used for monitoring the aggregate position limit of USD 100 million equivalent across currency derivative pairs — GBP-INR, EUR-INR, and JPY-INR. The new factors become effective from July 30, 2026, superseding those issued under notice no. 20260325-50, which remain applicable until July 29, 2026.

## Key Points

- New conversion factors effective July 30, 2026: USD to EURO = 0.86, USD to GBP = 0.75, USD to JPY = 159.28
- Applies to monitoring aggregate position limits of USD 100 million equivalent across GBP-INR, EUR-INR, and JPY-INR contracts
- Existing conversion factors from notice no. 20260325-50 remain valid through July 29, 2026
- Positions in excess of USD 100 million equivalent require clients to disclose underlying exposure to their Trading Member
- Trading members must also maintain positions within limits prescribed in para 4 of notice no. 20150430-20 dated April 30, 2015

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the conversion factors previously set under Exchange notice no. 20260325-50, continuing a practice established since notices 20150430-20 (April 30, 2015) and 20180403-39 (April 3, 2018), which introduced limits on underlying exposure requirements for currency derivatives.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use the updated conversion factors from July 30, 2026 for aggregate position monitoring
- Clients whose cross-currency positions exceed USD 100 million equivalent must provide disclosure of underlying exposure to their Trading Member
- Trading members must ensure their own positions remain within the limits specified in notice no. 20150430-20
- For clarifications, trading members may contact BSE at 022-2272 3030

## Important Dates

- **July 29, 2026**: Last day conversion factors from notice no. 20260325-50 are applicable
- **July 30, 2026**: New conversion factors (USD/EURO: 0.86, USD/GBP: 0.75, USD/JPY: 159.28) come into effect

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine periodic revision of conversion factors with moderate operational impact on trading members participating in currency derivative segments. The change affects how aggregate cross-currency positions are calculated against the USD 100 million limit. Trading members will need to update their internal risk and compliance systems to apply the revised rates from July 30, 2026. The JPY factor (159.28) reflects continued USD strength relative to the yen, while EURO and GBP factors indicate modest USD strength against those currencies.
