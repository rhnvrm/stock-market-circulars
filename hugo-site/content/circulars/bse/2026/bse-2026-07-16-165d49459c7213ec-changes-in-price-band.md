## Summary

BSE has notified Trading Members that the price bands applicable to 20 specified scrips have been revised from their existing levels, effective July 17, 2026. The revised price bands range from 2% to 20% depending on the individual scrip, as part of the Exchange's ongoing surveillance and risk-monitoring framework.

## Key Points

- The revision affects 20 scrips across various price band categories (2%, 5%, 10%, and 20%).
- Notable scrips with revised bands include Allcargo Global Ltd (10%), B & A Ltd (10%), Crazy Snacks Ltd (20%), Gold Coin Health Foods Ltd (20%), Swastika Investmart Ltd (10%), and Trishakti Industries Ltd (10%).
- Several scrips have been assigned tighter 2% price bands, including Gyan Developers & Builders Ltd, Marg Techno Projects Ltd, Munoth Financial Services Ltd, Photon Capital Advisors Ltd, Sarthak Global Ltd, Shah Foods Ltd, SJ Corporation Ltd, and SW Investments Ltd.
- Scrips with 5% price bands include Ceenik Exports India Ltd, Chandni Machines Ltd, Global Surfaces Ltd, Jai Mata Glass Ltd, and Kahan Packaging Ltd.
- Kizi Apparels Ltd has been assigned a 10% price band.
- No attachment was provided with this notice; the full list is contained within the circular text.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine surveillance-driven adjustment to daily price bands (circuit filters) applied by BSE under its market surveillance framework. Such revisions are typically made based on volatility, trading patterns, or other risk parameters identified by the Exchange's Surveillance Department (DOSS).

## Compliance Requirements

Trading Members are required to update their trading systems to reflect the revised price bands for the listed scrips from the effective date. No specific action is required from investors beyond awareness of the new trading limits for these securities.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 16, 2026
- Effective Date: July 17, 2026 (revised price bands come into effect)

## Impact Assessment

The impact is scrip-specific and moderate in scope, affecting the daily price movement limits for 20 listed companies. Investors and traders holding or trading these scrips should note the revised bands, as tighter bands (e.g., 2%) may limit intraday price volatility while wider bands (e.g., 20%) allow for greater price swings. This is a routine surveillance measure rather than an indicator of company-specific fundamental developments.
