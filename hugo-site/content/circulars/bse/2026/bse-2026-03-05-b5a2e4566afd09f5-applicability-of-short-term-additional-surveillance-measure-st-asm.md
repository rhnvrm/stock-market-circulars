## Summary

BSE has announced changes to the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective March 06, 2026. Eight securities are newly shortlisted under the Short Term 5/15/30 Days ASM Framework, no securities are being moved to higher or lower stages, and nine securities are being removed from the ST-ASM framework (some due to migration to other surveillance frameworks such as LT-ASM and ESM).

## Key Points

- 8 securities newly added to ST-ASM Framework (Stage I) effective March 06, 2026
- No securities moved to higher or lower ASM stages in this update
- 9 securities removed from ST-ASM Framework effective March 06, 2026
- Some securities marked with `*` are listed on NSE as well
- Securities marked `$` are moving out due to inclusion in ESM Framework
- Securities marked `*` in the exit list are moving out due to inclusion in LT-ASM Framework

## Regulatory Changes

The ST-ASM framework applies additional surveillance conditions, including higher margin requirements and trade-for-trade settlement obligations, to securities identified as potentially exhibiting abnormal price or volume behavior. This circular updates the list in accordance with BSE's ongoing market surveillance obligations under SEBI guidelines.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Brokers and members** must apply the applicable ST-ASM margins and trading restrictions for newly added securities from March 06, 2026
- **Investors** holding or trading in affected securities must be aware of enhanced margin requirements under the ST-ASM framework
- Securities exiting ST-ASM but entering LT-ASM (Golechha Global Finance, Vanta Bioscience) or ESM (Laddu Gopal Online Services) remain under surveillance — members must apply the relevant framework rules accordingly

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** March 06, 2026 — all additions, removals, and stage changes take effect
- **Circular Date:** March 05, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors in the 8 newly added securities will face increased margin requirements and potential trading restrictions under the ST-ASM framework starting March 06, 2026. Notably, Tejas Networks Ltd (a relatively large-cap technology company) being added is significant and may draw wider market attention. The 9 securities exiting ST-ASM provide some relief to their holders, though 3 of them are transitioning to stricter surveillance frameworks (LT-ASM or ESM), meaning trading restrictions may persist or intensify for those scrips.
