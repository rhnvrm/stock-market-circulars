## Summary

BSE issued Notice No. 20260625-16 dated June 25, 2026, informing trading members and investors that the Exchange had observed significant price/volume movements in 20 listed scrips. BSE had written to all companies seeking explanations; clarifications have been received from 9 companies, while replies are still awaited from the remaining 11.

## Key Points

- BSE observed abnormal price/volume activity in 20 listed equity scrips and sought clarifications from respective companies.
- Replies have been received and attached from 9 companies: Brainbees Solutions Ltd (544226), Coral Laboratories Ltd (524506), Genus Power Infrastructures Ltd (530343), Igarashi Motors India Ltd (517380), Menon Pistons Ltd (531727), Oriental Hotels Ltd (500314), Pearl Global Industries Ltd (532808), Shoppers Stop Ltd (532638), and Steelcast Ltd (513517).
- Replies are still awaited from 11 companies: Andrew CMX Holdings Ltd (532217), Sujala Trading & Holdings Ltd (539117), Andrew Yule & Company Ltd (526173), Elango Industries Ltd (513452), Frontier Capital Ltd (508980), Marble City India Ltd (531281), Mayur Leather Products Ltd (531680), Mohite Industries Ltd (532140), Prabhhans Industries Ltd (530361), Sahara Maritime Ltd (544056), and Grand Oak Canyons Distillery Ltd (523862).
- The notice is issued by the Surveillance Department (DOSS) to ensure trading members and investors have access to the latest relevant information.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard surveillance action under BSE's existing framework to ensure market transparency and investor protection.

## Compliance Requirements

- The 11 companies that have not yet responded are required to submit their clarifications to BSE at the earliest.
- Trading members and investors are advised to review the attached replies from the 9 responding companies before making investment decisions in these scrips.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: June 25, 2026
- No specific deadline mentioned for pending replies from the remaining 11 companies.

## Impact Assessment

Investors and trading members in the 20 affected scrips should exercise caution. The receipt of clarifications from 9 companies provides some transparency, but the 11 companies with pending replies remain under active surveillance scrutiny. Continued non-response from these companies may lead to further regulatory action including trading restrictions or enhanced surveillance measures. The circular affects a diverse set of companies across sectors, suggesting broad market surveillance activity rather than a sector-specific concern.
