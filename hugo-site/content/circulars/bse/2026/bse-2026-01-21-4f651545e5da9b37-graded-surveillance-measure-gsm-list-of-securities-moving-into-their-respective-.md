## Summary

BSE has announced the movement of 10 securities into various stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework. Five securities are moving to GSM Stage I, three to Stage II, one to Stage III, and one to Stage IV. The GSM framework applies enhanced surveillance and trading restrictions to securities exhibiting unusual price movements or volatility.

## Key Points

- 10 securities are being moved into different GSM stages effective from the specified date
- GSM Stage I: 5 securities (Softbpo Global Services, Ganon Products, Auto Pins India, Geetanjali Credit, Sailani Tours)
- GSM Stage II: 3 securities (Aviva Industries, Nikki Global Finance, Jindal Leasefin)
- GSM Stage III: 1 security (Futuristic Securities)
- GSM Stage IV: 1 security (Jpt Securities)
- Securities may move to lower GSM stages if included in ESM or IBC frameworks

## Regulatory Framework

- Securities marked (#) may move to lower GSM stages due to inclusion in Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) Framework
- Securities marked ($) may move to lower GSM stages due to inclusion in Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC) Framework
- GSM framework is aligned with NSE guidelines

## Compliance Requirements

- Investors should be aware of enhanced surveillance measures applicable to these securities
- Higher GSM stages typically involve additional trading restrictions such as trade-for-trade settlement, price bands, and margin requirements
- Market participants should review GSM-specific trading conditions before executing transactions

## Impact Assessment

- Trading in these securities will be subject to enhanced surveillance and potential restrictions
- Higher GSM stages indicate greater regulatory concern about price volatility or unusual trading patterns
- Investors may face reduced liquidity and higher margin requirements
- Movement to higher GSM stages serves as a cautionary signal for market participants
