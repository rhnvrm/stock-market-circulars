## Summary

BSE Clearing Corporation (ICCL) has released the settlement calendar for the Offer to Buy – Acquisition Window (Buyback) for TeamLease Services Limited. The buyback bidding window runs from July 9, 2026 to July 15, 2026, with obligation download on July 20, 2026 and final settlement on July 21, 2026.

## Key Points

- Settlement Type: Buyback
- Settlement Number: 2026086
- Bid Start Date: 09/07/2026
- Bid End Date: 15/07/2026
- Obligation Download: 20/07/2026
- Settlement Date: 21/07/2026
- Demat shares must be delivered only through the Early Pay-in of Shares mechanism, available in both CDSL and NSDL depositories.
- Members/Custodians must use Market Type "(Buyback)" and Settlement No. 2026086 in the depository system.
- Reference to prior Notice No. 20200528-32 dated May 28, 2020 regarding Revised Guidelines for Mechanism for Acquisition of Shares through Stock Exchange.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is a routine operational settlement notice implementing existing buyback/OTB acquisition window guidelines.

## Compliance Requirements

- Participants must ensure early pay-in of securities is reflected in BSE/ICCL systems before prescribed bid confirmation timelines on the OTB platform.
- Early pay-in must be completed in the depository system well in advance of the bidding window closure and custodian confirmation deadlines.
- CDSL and NSDL will earmark/block early pay-in securities in the client's account under the specified market type and settlement number.
- Members and Custodians are NOT required to upload any EPN file.
- Members should refer to the MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo file for client-wise stamp duty charges.

## Important Dates

- Bid Start Date: July 9, 2026
- Bid End Date: July 15, 2026
- Obligation Download: July 20, 2026
- Settlement Date: July 21, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This notice is procedural and primarily impacts trading members and custodians participating in the TeamLease Services buyback acquisition window, ensuring orderly settlement of shares tendered. It does not directly affect general market trading but is important for buyback participants to meet operational deadlines and avoid settlement failures.
