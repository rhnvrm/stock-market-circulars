## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure) will be moved to their respective higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 29, 2026. These securities will attract enhanced surveillance actions based on their assigned GSM stage, ranging from increased margin requirements to severely restricted weekly trading with Additional Surveillance Deposits.

## Key Points

- Securities in the attached Annexure will be moved to higher GSM stages from June 29, 2026
- Four GSM stages exist with progressively stricter surveillance actions
- Stage I: 100% applicable margin rate with price band of 5% or lower
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade with 5% price band and Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value by buyers
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade with 5% price band, trading permitted once a week (every Monday/1st trading day), ASD of 100% of trade value by buyers
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III with an additional restriction of no upward price movement
- No netting off allowed; settlement on trade-to-trade basis
- Trading members are advised to take adequate precautions while trading in affected securities

## Regulatory Changes

The notice references the GSM framework originally published on February 23, 2017 (Exchange notice no. 20170223-44) and subsequently updated through notices dated March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023. The current notice represents an application of the existing GSM framework — moving specific securities to higher stages — rather than a change to the framework itself.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note that affected securities will be on trade-to-trade basis with no netting off permitted
- Buyers in Stage II securities must deposit ASD of 50% of trade value
- Buyers in Stage III and Stage IV securities must deposit ASD of 100% of trade value
- Members should review the full list of affected securities in the attached Annexure
- For clarifications, members may refer to the GSM FAQ at http://www.bseindia.com/markets/equity/EQReports/graded_surveil_measure.aspx?expandable=6 or write to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **June 25, 2026**: Notice issued
- **June 29, 2026**: Effective date for securities moving to higher GSM stages

## Impact Assessment

Securities moved to higher GSM stages face significantly reduced liquidity and increased trading costs. Stage III and IV securities are particularly impacted, as they can only be traded once per week (Mondays or first trading day of the week), buyers must deposit 100% ASD, and all settlements are on a trade-to-trade basis. Stage IV securities face an additional constraint of no upward price movement, effectively preventing price appreciation. Investors holding these securities may face difficulty exiting positions, and the enhanced margin and deposit requirements increase the capital burden for buyers. Trading members should proactively communicate these restrictions to their clients.
