## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that 11 companies have fixed record dates for payment of interest and/or redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) and Bonds. Trading members are advised not to deal in these debentures/bonds from their respective ex-dates.

## Key Points

- Trading suspension applies to debentures/bonds of 11 companies in the debt segment
- Suspensions are triggered by record dates set for interest payment or redemption
- Ex-dates and record dates coincide for all listed instruments
- All settlement references are under the 2026-2027 DR series
- Issued by Marian D'Souza, AVP – Listing Compliance and Operations

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice under existing BSE listing and trading regulations governing debt instruments.

- **21 April 2026**: Record/Ex date for IndiGrid Infrastructure Trust NCD
- **30 April 2026**: Record/Ex date for Nuvama Wealth Finance NCD

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects only the debt segment instruments of the listed companies. Trading members must cease transactions in the specified NCDs/Bonds from the respective ex-dates. The suspensions are temporary and routine, coinciding with interest payment or redemption events. Holders of these instruments will receive their due interest or redemption proceeds as per the terms. No equity trading is affected. The instruments span NBFC, infrastructure, housing finance, and public sector undertaking sectors.
