## Summary

BSE has issued updates to the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective January 28, 2026. The circular identifies securities being newly added to the 5/15/30 Days ASM framework, those moving between ASM stages, and those exiting the framework. Four securities are being newly shortlisted, one security is moving to a higher stage, and six securities are moving out of the ST-ASM framework.

## Key Points

- 4 securities newly shortlisted in Short Term 5/15/30 Days ASM Framework (Stage I)
- 1 security (Amit Securities Ltd) moving to higher Stage II ASM
- 6 securities moving out of ST-ASM Framework
- Changes effective from January 28, 2026
- Framework includes both regular and SME scrips
- Consolidated list shows 14+ securities currently under ST-ASM surveillance

## Regulatory Framework

Securities may exit ST-ASM due to inclusion in other frameworks:
- (*) Long Term ASM (LT-ASM) Framework
- (**) Trade for Trade Framework
- (#) Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) Framework
- (^) Pledge Framework
- ($) Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) Framework

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised ST-ASM applicable securities
- Enhanced surveillance measures apply to all listed securities in the framework
- Different stages (I, II) indicate escalating surveillance intensity
- Special marking applies for SME scrips (@) and T+0 scrips (~)

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** January 28, 2026
- **Circular Date:** January 27, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:**
- Trading restrictions and enhanced surveillance on newly added securities may reduce liquidity
- Securities moving to higher stages face stricter monitoring
- Exit from ST-ASM framework provides relief to 6 securities, potentially improving trading conditions

**Investor Impact:**
- Investors should exercise caution when trading ST-ASM securities
- Enhanced surveillance indicates potential price volatility or unusual trading patterns
- Stage II securities face more stringent monitoring than Stage I

**Operational Impact:**
- Brokers and trading members must update systems to reflect new ST-ASM classifications
- Risk management systems should incorporate enhanced margin and position limits for affected securities
