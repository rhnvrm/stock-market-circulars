## Summary

BSE has announced shut periods for Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) operations for nine securities undergoing corporate actions. The circular covers dividend announcements for eight companies and one mutual fund unit split, with shut periods ranging from February 4-5, 2026 to February 19-20, 2026. During these shut periods, the affected securities will not be available for lending or borrowing transactions.

## Key Points

- Nine securities affected by corporate actions in SLB segment
- Eight dividend announcements and one mutual fund unit split
- Shut periods implemented to facilitate corporate action processing
- Ex-dates and record dates align with book closure dates
- SLB participants must note trading restrictions during shut periods

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational circular for corporate action management in the SLB segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- SLB participants must not initiate new lending or borrowing transactions for affected securities during shut periods
- Existing positions may need to be managed considering the shut period restrictions
- Market participants should track ex-dates and record dates for dividend entitlement purposes

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects liquidity in the SLB segment for nine securities across different time periods in February 2026. Participants need to plan their lending and borrowing strategies around these shut periods.

**Operational Impact:** SLB market participants must adjust their trading and position management strategies to account for the unavailability of these securities during specified shut periods. The staggered shut periods minimize overall market disruption.
