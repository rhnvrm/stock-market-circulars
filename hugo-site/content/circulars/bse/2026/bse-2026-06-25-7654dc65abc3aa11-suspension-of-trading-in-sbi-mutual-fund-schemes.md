## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that 8 mutual fund schemes belonging to SBI Mutual Fund — covering SBI Fixed Maturity Plan (FMP) Series 44 (1855 Days) and Series 45 (1840 Days) — will cease trading effective June 29, 2026, as the schemes mature on their scheduled redemption dates.

## Key Points

- Trading in all 8 SBI FMP schemes (Series 44 and Series 45) is suspended from June 29, 2026
- The suspension is triggered by the maturity/redemption of these closed-ended fixed maturity plans
- Both regular and direct plan variants, including growth and dividend/IDCW payout options, are affected
- The circular is issued under reference DR-660/2026-2027

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This is a standard operational notice under existing mutual fund listing and trading regulations, triggered by the maturity of closed-ended FMP schemes.

Trading members are advised not to deal in any of the following SBI Mutual Fund schemes on or after June 29, 2026:

## Important Dates

- **June 25, 2026**: Notice issued by BSE
- **June 29, 2026**: Effective date of trading suspension for all 8 SBI FMP schemes

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to trading members and investors who hold or transact in these 8 SBI Fixed Maturity Plan schemes on the BSE Mutual Fund segment. As these are closed-ended debt funds approaching their scheduled maturity, the trading suspension is expected and investors will receive redemption proceeds directly from the fund house as per the FMP terms. No broader market disruption is anticipated.
