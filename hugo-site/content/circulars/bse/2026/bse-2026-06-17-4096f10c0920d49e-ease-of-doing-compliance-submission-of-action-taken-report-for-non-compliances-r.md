## Summary

ICCL (Indian Clearing Corporation Limited) has prescribed a new framework to ease compliance and ensure timely closure of non-compliances reported in half-yearly Internal Audit Reports submitted by clearing members. Clearing members must now submit certified Action Taken Reports (ATRs) within prescribed deadlines, failing which existing enforcement actions under ICCL circular No. 20231229-11 will apply. This framework is effective for all internal audit reports from the half-year ended March 31, 2026 onwards.

## Key Points

- Clearing members must submit an Action Taken Report (ATR) for every area where non-compliance is reported in the internal audit report.
- All non-compliances must be closed within 2 months from the due date of Internal Audit Report submission.
- ATR due dates: **July 31** for audit period ending March 31; **January 31** for audit period ending September 30.
- ATRs must be certified by the clearing member's empanelled Internal Auditor, covering at least one month of sample verification.
- Detailed submission procedures for ATRs will be communicated via a subsequent circular.
- Non-submission or failure to close observations by the due date will trigger enforcement actions per ICCL circular No. 20231229-11 (December 29, 2023).
- Critical non-compliances (e.g., misutilisation of client collaterals, net worth deficiencies) will face immediate enforcement without waiting for ATR submission.
- The circular also reiterates SEBI's norms on Appointment and Rotation of Internal Auditors as per SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MIRSD/MIRSD2/CIR/P/2016/95 dated September 26, 2016.

## Regulatory Changes

A structured ATR-based closure mechanism is introduced for internal audit non-compliances, replacing the earlier direct enforcement-only approach. The framework aligns ICCL's practices with circulars already issued by stock exchanges, creating a two-step process: remediation via ATR first, enforcement only upon failure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing members must review all internal audit non-compliances and prepare ATRs for each reported area.
- ATRs must be certified by the empanelled Internal Auditor confirming compliance status with at least one month of sample verification.
- Members must bring the circular provisions to the attention of their respective internal auditors.
- Members must adhere to SEBI norms on appointment and rotation of internal auditors, including obtaining declarations of independence and sharing auditor details with the exchange.

## Important Dates

- **Effective from**: Half-year ended March 31, 2026 and onwards.
- **ATR due date for period ending March 31**: July 31 of the same year.
- **ATR due date for period ending September 30**: January 31 of the following year.
- Submission procedure details to be communicated via a subsequent ICCL circular.

## Impact Assessment

This circular directly impacts clearing members who are subject to ICCL's oversight. The new framework provides a structured remediation window (2 months post internal audit due date) before enforcement is triggered, reducing the risk of penalties for members who proactively address audit findings. However, critical violations such as client collateral misutilisation or net worth breaches remain subject to immediate enforcement, maintaining investor protection safeguards. No direct impact on listed securities or broader market operations.
