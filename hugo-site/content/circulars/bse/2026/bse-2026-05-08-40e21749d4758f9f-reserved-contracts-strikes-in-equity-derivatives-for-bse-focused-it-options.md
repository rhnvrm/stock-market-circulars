## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260508-29 announcing the introduction of reserved options contracts with additional strike prices for BSE FOCUSED IT options. This is a continuation of the earlier notice no. 20251028-51 dated October 28, 2025, which established the reserved contracts (strikes) framework in Equity Derivatives.

## Key Points

- Reserved options contracts with additional strike prices will be introduced for BSE FOCUSED IT options.
- This notice extends the existing reserved contracts framework previously established under notice no. 20251028-51 (October 28, 2025).
- Trading members are required to take note and make necessary changes in their trading applications.
- Front-end trading application vendors are also required to update their systems as needed.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This notice extends the existing reserved contracts (strikes) mechanism in Equity Derivatives to cover BSE FOCUSED IT options, adding additional strike prices under the already-established framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must review their trading applications and make necessary updates to accommodate reserved contracts for BSE FOCUSED IT options.
- Front-end trading application vendors must ensure their platforms support the additional strike prices for BSE FOCUSED IT options.
- For queries, members may contact BSE via:
  - **General:** bsehelp@bseindia.com | 022-45720400/600 & 022-69158500
  - **IML:** iml.info@bseindia.com | 022-2272 8705/8885
  - **Technical:** bse.tech@bseindia.com | 022-2272 8053

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** May 8, 2026
- No specific effective date or deadline is mentioned in the circular; members should implement changes promptly.

## Impact Assessment

This update has a moderate operational impact on trading members and front-end application vendors who deal in BSE FOCUSED IT options. They are required to update their trading systems to support the new reserved strike prices. The broader market impact is limited as this is an extension of an existing mechanism rather than a new policy change. No direct impact on listed stocks or indices is indicated.
