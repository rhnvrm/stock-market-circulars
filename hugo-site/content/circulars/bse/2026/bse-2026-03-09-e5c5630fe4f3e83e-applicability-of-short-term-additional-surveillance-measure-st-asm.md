## Summary

BSE has announced changes to the Short Term (ST) Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework effective March 10, 2026. Five securities are being newly added to the ST-ASM framework, one security is being moved to a higher ASM stage, and four securities are being removed from the ST-ASM framework. The circular also provides a consolidated list of all securities currently under the ST 5/15/30 Days ASM framework.

## Key Points

- 5 securities newly shortlisted in ST-ASM Framework (Part A, Annexure I) effective March 10, 2026
- 1 security (Jindal Poly Films Ltd) moving to a higher ASM stage within ST-ASM framework
- NIL securities moving to lower ASM stage
- 4 securities exiting the ST-ASM framework (Annexure II)
- 3 of the 5 newly added securities are SME scrips (marked @)
- Consolidated list (Annexure III) includes securities at Stage I and Stage II of ST-ASM

## Regulatory Changes

The ST-ASM framework applies additional surveillance measures with trade-to-trade settlement or price band restrictions at 5, 15, or 30-day intervals. Securities are classified into stages (I, II, etc.) with higher stages implying more restrictive trading conditions. Movement between stages is based on continued surveillance triggers.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note revised ASM applicability for the listed securities effective March 10, 2026
- Securities newly added to ST-ASM will be subject to applicable price bands and/or trade-for-trade settlement
- Members should update client communications regarding affected securities
- SME scrips marked @ are subject to ST-ASM rules applicable to SME segment

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** March 10, 2026 — all additions, stage movements, and removals come into effect
- **Circular Date:** March 9, 2026
