## Summary

BSE Index Services Private Ltd announced the reconstitution results for over 50 BSE indices. The changes will take effect at the open of trading on Monday, June 22, 2026. The reconstitution covers sectoral indices, factor-based indices, and multi-cap indices, with additions and deletions across all affected benchmarks.

## Key Points

- Reconstitution is effective at market open on **June 22, 2026**
- Over 50 indices are being reconstituted across sectoral, thematic, and factor categories
- The BSE 1000 MULTICAP EQUAL SIZE WEIGHTED (25%) sees the largest change: 40 additions and 40 deletions
- BSE 500 MOMENTUM 50 sees 22 additions and 21 deletions
- BSE 500 SHARIAH sees 7 additions and 18 deletions (net reduction)
- Several indices see only additions with no drops: BSE FAST MOVING CONSUMER GOODS (+3), BSE HOUSING INR (+1), BSE INSURANCE (+1), BSE CAPITAL MARKETS (+4), BSE HOSPITALS (+1)
- BSE IPO sees 5 deletions and no additions
- Some indices (BSE HEALTHCARE, BSE OIL & GAS, BSE POWER AND ENERGY, BSE SENSEX EQUAL WEIGHT, BSE PSU BANK, BSE MIDSMALL PRIVATE BANKS QUALITY TILT, BSE TOP 10 BANKS) were mentioned but content was truncated

## Regulatory Changes

This is a scheduled periodic reconstitution by BSE Index Services Private Ltd and does not represent a regulatory rule change. Index compositions are updated to reflect changes in market capitalisation, liquidity, and factor eligibility.

## Compliance Requirements

- Fund managers and ETF operators tracking any of the affected indices must rebalance their portfolios to reflect the new compositions effective June 22, 2026
- Index derivatives and structured products referencing these indices should be reviewed for any adjustments required

## Important Dates

- **Announcement Date:** June 12, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 22, 2026 (at market open)

## Impact Assessment

This reconstitution affects a wide range of passive investment vehicles including ETFs and index funds benchmarked to BSE sectoral, thematic, and factor indices. The largest turnovers are in the BSE 1000 MULTICAP EQUAL SIZE WEIGHTED (25%), BSE 500 MOMENTUM 50, and BSE SMALLCAP 500 series, which may generate noticeable rebalancing activity in mid- and small-cap segments around the effective date. The net reduction in BSE 500 SHARIAH (18 drops vs 7 adds) and BSE CONSUMER DISCRETIONARY (7 drops vs 3 adds) reflects tightening eligibility in those segments.
