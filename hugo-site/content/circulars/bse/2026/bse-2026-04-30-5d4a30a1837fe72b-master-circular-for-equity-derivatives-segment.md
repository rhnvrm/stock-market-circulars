## Summary

BSE has issued a Master Circular for the Equity Derivatives Segment (Notice No. 20260430-19), consolidating all previously issued notices and circulars from the Trading Operations Department into a single reference document. This serves as the definitive compliance and operational guide for members participating in the Equity Derivatives (F&O) Segment.

## Key Points

- BSE has compiled all applicable notices and circulars for the Equity Derivatives Segment into one Master Circular
- The document is a consolidation of circulars issued by the Trading Operations Department
- Two attachments are provided: the main EQD Master Circular 2026 and Part III covering EOD File Formats for the F&O Segment
- Issued under Notice No. 20260430-19 dated 30 April 2026
- Covers the Derivatives segment under the Trading category

## Regulatory Changes

This Master Circular does not introduce new regulations but consolidates existing notices and circulars for the Equity Derivatives Segment. It supersedes individual circulars as the primary reference for all trading members operating in the F&O segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- All members trading in the Equity Derivatives Segment should refer to this Master Circular as the authoritative source for applicable rules and operational procedures
- Members should review the EOD File Formats document (Part III) for updated end-of-day file format specifications for the F&O segment
- Prior individual circulars referenced within the Master Circular remain applicable as consolidated herein

## Important Dates

- **Issue Date:** 30 April 2026
- Effective immediately upon issuance

## Impact Assessment

This Master Circular has high operational significance for all BSE members and participants in the Equity Derivatives (F&O) Segment. By consolidating all relevant notices and circulars into a single document, it simplifies compliance and reference for trading members. The inclusion of EOD file format specifications is particularly relevant for back-office and technology teams. No immediate trading disruptions are expected, but members are advised to review the full document to ensure alignment with all consolidated requirements.
