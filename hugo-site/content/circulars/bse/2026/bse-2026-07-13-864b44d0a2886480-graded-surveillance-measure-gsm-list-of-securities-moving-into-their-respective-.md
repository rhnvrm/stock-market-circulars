## Summary

BSE has issued a notice referencing its GSM Framework circulars dated February 23, 2017, March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023. Trading members are informed that securities listed in the attached Annexure_GSM will move to their respective higher Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) stages effective July 14, 2026, subjecting them to enhanced surveillance actions based on their assigned stage.

## Key Points

- Securities identified in the Annexure_GSM attachment will move to higher GSM stages effective July 14, 2026.
- GSM has four stages (I to IV), each carrying progressively stricter trading restrictions.
- Stage I: 100% applicable margin and price band capped at 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, plus Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, trading permitted only once a week (Monday/first trading day), and 100% ASD payable by buyers.
- Stage IV: Same restrictions as Stage III, with the additional condition of no upward price movement permitted.
- All affected securities settle on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.
- Members can refer to the GSM FAQ page or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice implements existing GSM Framework provisions (originally published February 23, 2017) by moving specific securities to higher surveillance stages.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin rates, price bands, ASD requirements, and trading frequency restrictions corresponding to each security's new GSM stage.
- Members must ensure trade-for-trade settlement with no netting for the affected securities.
- Members should exercise adequate precaution while trading in the identified securities.

## Important Dates

- Effective date of stage movement: July 14, 2026.
- Notice date: July 13, 2026 (Notice No. 20260713-34).

## Impact Assessment

The move to higher GSM stages significantly restricts liquidity and trading flexibility for the affected securities through higher margins, mandatory trade-for-trade settlement, ASD deposits, and reduced trading frequency (weekly trading at Stage III/IV). This is a standard, recurring surveillance action rather than a market-wide event, but it materially impacts trading conditions and investor exit options for the specific securities named in the (unlisted here) annexure.
