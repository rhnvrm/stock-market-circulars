## Summary

BSE has issued a notice informing trading members of the suspension of trading in Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) of multiple companies that have fixed record dates for the purpose of payment of interest or redemption. Members are advised not to deal in these debentures around their respective record and ex-dates falling between 30 June and 10 July 2026.

## Key Points

- 13 NCD instruments across 11 companies are subject to trading suspension around their record dates
- Record dates and ex-dates are the same for all listed instruments
- Suspensions cover settlement series 661/2026-2027 through 669/2026-2027
- Trading members must refrain from dealing in the listed debentures on or around the specified dates
- Companies span finance, housing finance, infrastructure, and fintech sectors

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational notice under existing BSE listing and compliance procedures requiring suspension of trading in debt instruments around their record dates for corporate actions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the specified NCD scrips on or before their respective ex-dates
- Members should note the ISIN numbers and scrip codes listed to identify restricted instruments
- Compliance is mandatory for all trading members of the Exchange in the Debt segment

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects debt segment trading members dealing in private placement NCDs across 11 issuers. The suspension windows are short-term and date-specific, with no broader equity or derivatives market impact. Investors and members holding these debentures should plan trades ahead of the respective ex-dates to avoid non-compliance. The circular is routine in nature and does not indicate any credit or default risk associated with the listed instruments.
