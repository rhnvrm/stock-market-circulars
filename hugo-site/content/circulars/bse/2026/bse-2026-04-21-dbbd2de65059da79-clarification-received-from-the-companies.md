## Summary

BSE's Department of Surveillance (DOSS) issued Notice No. 20260421-21 on 21 April 2026 regarding significant price and volume movement observed in 36 listed equity scrips. To protect investor interests and ensure timely disclosure, BSE wrote to all 36 companies seeking clarifications. Replies have been received from 21 companies and are attached to the notice; 15 companies are still awaited.

## Key Points

- BSE observed unusual price/volume movement in 36 equity scrips and initiated clarification requests under its surveillance mandate.
- 21 companies have responded; their replies (Replies1, Replies2, Replies3) are attached to the notice.
- 15 companies have not yet responded and their replies are pending.
- The notice is issued under the equity segment by the DOSS department.
- Signed by Sanjay Jain (Deputy Vice President, Surveillance) and Shreya Ghose (Manager, Surveillance).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This notice is a standard surveillance action under existing BSE framework for monitoring abnormal price/volume activity in listed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- The 15 companies listed under "Reply is waited" must submit their clarifications to BSE at the earliest.
- Trading members and investors are advised to review the attached replies from the 21 responding companies before making investment decisions in these scrips.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine surveillance disclosure. The 21 companies that have already replied have fulfilled their immediate compliance obligation; investors should review those responses for any material disclosures explaining the unusual price/volume activity. The 15 non-responding companies remain under scrutiny and may face further regulatory action if replies are not provided. No direct trading restrictions are imposed by this notice, but the underlying abnormal price/volume movements in these scrips warrant caution for retail investors.
