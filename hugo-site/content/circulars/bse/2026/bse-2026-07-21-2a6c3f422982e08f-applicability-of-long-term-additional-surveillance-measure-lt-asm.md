## Summary

BSE has issued an update regarding the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework, referencing multiple prior notices dating back to 2018. The notice outlines securities being newly placed under LT-ASM, securities moving to higher stages within the framework, securities exiting the framework, and securities transferring to different trading groups (T/XT/MT/TS), along with associated margin and price band changes, all detailed in attached annexures.

## Key Points

- 100% margin requirement applicable from July 24, 2026 on all open positions as of July 23, 2026, and on new positions created from July 24, 2026 onwards, for securities listed in Annexure I (Part A)
- Securities moving into higher stages of the LT-ASM framework effective July 22, 2026 are listed in Annexure I (Part B)
- Lower Price Band applicable effective July 22, 2026
- Securities continuing in LT-ASM framework but transferring to T/XT/MT/TS Group effective July 24, 2026 are listed in Annexure I (Part D), per the June 4, 2021 notice framework
- Securities moving out of the LT-ASM framework effective July 22, 2026 are listed in Annexure II
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the LT-ASM framework is provided in Annexure III
- Shortlisting under this framework is based on market surveillance criteria and XBRL submissions by listed companies, and is not an adverse action against any company
- Price bands for securities exiting the framework will revert to pre-shortlisting levels, unless the security remains subject to another surveillance framework

## Regulatory Changes

This notice applies the revised LT-ASM framework as established through a series of BSE notices (20180321-46, 20181027-1, 20201204-56, 20240809-46, 20240920-63) and the June 4, 2021 notice (20210604-41) governing group transfers for LT-ASM securities.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members and market participants dealing in the affected securities must apply 100% margins on open and new positions per the effective dates, observe the revised lower price bands, and account for group reclassification (T/XT/MT/TS) where applicable. Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 22, 2026: Lower price band effective; securities moving into higher LT-ASM stages and securities exiting the framework take effect
- July 23, 2026: Reference date for existing open positions subject to 100% margin
- July 24, 2026: 100% margin applicable on open and new positions; securities transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group

## Impact Assessment

Affected securities will face significantly higher trading costs due to 100% margin requirements and tighter price bands, likely reducing liquidity and intraday volatility for those stocks. Group transfers to T/XT/MT/TS segments may further restrict trading mechanisms (e.g., trade-for-trade settlement). Since specific stock names are contained only in the referenced annexures (not in the extracted text), affected investors and traders should consult the full annexures for exact security-level impact.
