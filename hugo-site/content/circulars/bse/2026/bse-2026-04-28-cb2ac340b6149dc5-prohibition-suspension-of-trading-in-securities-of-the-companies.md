## Summary

BSE has prohibited/suspended trading in the equity securities of two companies — Sunshine Capital Ltd (Scrip: 539574) and Golkonda Aluminium Extrusions Ltd (Scrip: 513309) — effective Wednesday, April 29, 2026. The action follows site visits by the Exchange during which both companies were found to be non-existent at their respective registered office addresses. The suspension is imposed as an interim measure under BSE's listing compliance framework.

## Key Points

- BSE conducted physical site visits to the registered office addresses of these companies.
- Neither company was found to be present/operational at their registered office.
- Trading in both securities is prohibited/suspended with immediate effect from April 29, 2026.
- The measure is classified as interim, indicating further regulatory action may follow.
- Trading members and investors are explicitly requested to take note of the suspension.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulations introduced. The suspension is enforced under existing BSE listing compliance and monitoring powers, which allow the Exchange to take interim action against companies that fail to maintain a verifiable registered office presence.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members**: Must not execute any buy or sell orders in the securities of Sunshine Capital Ltd (539574) and Golkonda Aluminium Extrusions Ltd (513309) from April 29, 2026 onwards.
- **Investors**: Should take note that these securities are suspended from trading; existing holdings cannot be transacted on the Exchange until the suspension is lifted.
- The companies themselves are implicitly required to resolve the registered office issue to seek revocation of the suspension.

## Important Dates

- **April 28, 2026**: Notice issued by BSE.
- **April 29, 2026 (Wednesday)**: Effective date of trading prohibition/suspension for both securities.

## Impact Assessment

Investors currently holding shares of Sunshine Capital Ltd or Golkonda Aluminium Extrusions Ltd will be unable to trade these securities on BSE from April 29, 2026. The suspension is an interim measure, meaning liquidity is fully halted until the Exchange resolves or lifts the action. The inability to locate companies at registered offices suggests potential corporate governance failures or defunct operations, posing significant risk to existing shareholders. The interim nature of the order leaves open the possibility of permanent suspension or delisting if the companies do not demonstrate compliance.
