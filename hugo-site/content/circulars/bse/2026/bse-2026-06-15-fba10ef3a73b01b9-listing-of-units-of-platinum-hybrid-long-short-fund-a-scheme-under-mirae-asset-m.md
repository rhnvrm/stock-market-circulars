## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that units of six plans under the Platinum Hybrid Long-Short Fund (an interval investment strategy by Mirae Asset Mutual Fund, investing in equity and debt securities with limited short exposure via derivatives) are listed and admitted to dealings on the Exchange in the 'B' Group, effective Tuesday, June 16, 2026. All units were allotted on June 10, 2026 at a face value and issue price of Rs. 10.00 per unit.

## Key Points

- Fund name: Platinum Hybrid Long-Short Fund under Mirae Asset Mutual Fund
- Investment Manager: Mirae Asset Investment Managers (India) Private Limited
- Listed on BSE in Group 'B' with market lot of 1 unit
- Face value and issue price: Rs. 10.00 per unit for all plans
- Date of allotment: June 10, 2026
- Trading effective date: June 16, 2026
- Units will only be traded in dematerialised form as per SEBI guidelines dated February 16, 2000
- Registrar and Transfer Agent: KFin Technologies Limited, Hyderabad

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. The listing is in compliance with existing SEBI guidelines dated February 16, 2000 requiring mutual fund units to be traded exclusively in dematerialised form.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure all trades in these scrips are conducted in dematerialised form only
- Trades must be in unit market lot (minimum 1 unit)
- Members should update their systems with the new scrip codes and BOLT IDs for all six plans

## Important Dates

- Date of Allotment: June 10, 2026
- Listing and trading effective date: June 16, 2026 (Tuesday)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine mutual fund listing notice with low market impact. It provides liquidity on BSE for investors holding units of Platinum Hybrid Long-Short Fund across its six plan variants. The fund is an interval strategy with equity-debt exposure and limited short positions via derivatives. Existing unitholders and prospective buyers can now transact on the exchange platform. No impact on broader equity or debt markets is anticipated.
