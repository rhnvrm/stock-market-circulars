## Summary

BSE has informed trading members that effective Tuesday, July 7, 2026, the equity shares of Kratikal Tech Limited will be listed and admitted to dealings on the Exchange under the 'MT' Group of Securities in the SME segment. Per SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012, the scrip will trade in the Trade-for-Trade segment for the first 10 trading days.

## Key Points

- Company: Kratikal Tech Limited, registered office in Noida, Uttar Pradesh
- 1,11,00,545 equity shares of Rs. 10/- each fully paid up (distinctive numbers 1 to 11,100,545)
- Scrip ID/Abbreviated Name on BOLT: KRATIKAL; Scrip Code: 544811; ISIN: INE1L0M01019
- Market Lot: 1,000 shares; trading permitted only in dematerialised form
- IPO issue price: Rs. 135 per share (face value Rs. 10 + premium Rs. 125)
- Date of allotment in the public issue: July 3, 2026
- Scrip will be part of the Special Pre-open Session (SPOS) on July 7, 2026
- Market Maker: Spread X Securities Private Limited
- Registrar to the issue: KFin Technologies Limited
- Lock-in details as per Annexure I; shareholding pattern as per Annexure II

## Regulatory Changes

None; this is an operational listing notice implementing existing SEBI/BSE listing and Trade-for-Trade framework requirements for newly listed SME issues.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure trades in KRATIKAL are executed only in dematerialised form and in the minimum market lot of 1,000 shares
- The company has completed the necessary corporate action to mark the lock-in period in depository records
- Members must comply with Trade-for-Trade settlement rules for this scrip for the first 10 trading days

## Important Dates

- July 3, 2026: Date of allotment in the public issue
- July 6, 2026: Notice date
- July 7, 2026: Listing date; scrip included in Special Pre-open Session (SPOS); Trade-for-Trade period begins

## Impact Assessment

The listing enables secondary market trading in Kratikal Tech Limited shares, providing liquidity for IPO allottees and new investors. The Trade-for-Trade restriction for 10 trading days limits intraday speculation and requires actual delivery, which is standard for new SME listings and reduces near-term volatility risk. Impact is limited to stakeholders in this specific scrip and the SME segment, with no broader market-wide effect.
