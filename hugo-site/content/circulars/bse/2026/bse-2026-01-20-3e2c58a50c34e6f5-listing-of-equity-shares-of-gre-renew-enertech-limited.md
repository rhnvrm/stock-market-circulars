## Summary

GRE RENEW ENERTECH LIMITED has filed its shareholding pattern under Regulation 31(1)(a) of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015, following the listing of its equity shares post-IPO on January 20, 2026. The filing shows the company has 9 promoter/promoter group shareholders holding 10,000,000 shares and 722 public shareholders holding 4,288,000 shares.

## Key Points

- Total shareholding: 14,288,000 equity shares
- Promoter & Promoter Group: 9 shareholders holding 10,000,000 shares (69.99%)
- Public Shareholders: 722 shareholders holding 4,288,000 shares (30.01%)
- Filing date: January 20, 2026 (Post-IPO)
- Locked-in shares: Yes (for both promoter and public categories)
- No partly paid-up shares, convertible securities, warrants, or ESOPs outstanding
- No shares pledged or under non-disposal undertaking
- No differential voting rights or significant beneficial owners

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is a standard shareholding pattern disclosure required under existing regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Companies must file shareholding pattern under Regulation 31 of SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015
- Post-IPO filing is mandatory to establish baseline shareholding structure
- Listed entities must declare status of partly paid shares, convertible securities, warrants, ESOPs, depository receipts, locked-in shares, pledged shares, and encumbrances

## Important Dates

- **January 20, 2026**: Date of shareholding pattern filing (Post-IPO)
- Report filed under Regulation 31(1)(a)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - This is the initial shareholding disclosure for a newly listed company, providing transparency to investors about ownership structure. The 70-30 promoter-public split indicates reasonable public float as per regulatory requirements.

**Operational Impact**: Low - Standard regulatory disclosure with no operational changes required.

**Investor Impact**: Medium - Important information for investors to understand the ownership structure, particularly the presence of locked-in shares which restricts immediate selling by promoters and some public shareholders, providing stability in early trading days.
