## Summary

BSE has issued an updated notice on the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, referencing a series of prior notices dating back to June 2023. Securities listed in Annexure I have met the criteria for inclusion under ESM and will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open and new positions effective July 21, 2026. Some securities already under ESM will move to a lower stage (Stage I), while others will exit the framework entirely (Annexure II). A consolidated list of all securities currently under ESM is provided in Annexure III.

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I qualify for ESM inclusion and will attract minimum 100% margin from July 21, 2026 on open positions (as of July 20, 2026) and new positions.
- Securities continuing under ESM will be moved to a lower stage (Stage I) effective July 20, 2026.
- Securities qualifying under ESM will shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment w.e.f. July 21, 2026.
- Securities shifting to Stage II will be placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction w.e.f. July 20, 2026.
- Securities exiting the ESM framework (Annexure II) will do so effective July 20, 2026, with price bands reinstated to pre-ESM levels (unless subject to another surveillance measure).
- ESM operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures.
- Inclusion under ESM is a surveillance action only and should not be construed as adverse to the company/entity.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice is a periodic update applying the existing ESM framework (established via earlier notices from June 2023 through July 2025) to a revised list of securities, with stage transitions and exits.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply minimum 100% margin on securities newly added to ESM from July 21, 2026.
- Members must ensure affected securities are traded under Trade-for-Trade settlement rather than Rolling Settlement.
- Members must observe the revised price band (2%) and Periodic Call Auction mechanism for securities moved to Stage II.
- Queries should be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 20, 2026: Stage transitions (to Stage I), exits from ESM (Annexure II), and Stage II Trade-for-Trade/price band/Periodic Call Auction changes take effect.
- July 21, 2026: 100% margin requirement and Trade-for-Trade shift for newly qualifying securities (Annexure I) take effect.

## Impact Assessment

Securities newly placed under ESM face significantly reduced trading flexibility due to 100% margin requirements and Trade-for-Trade settlement, which curbs speculative trading and liquidity. Securities moving to Stage II face additional constraints via tighter price bands and periodic call auctions, further limiting price discovery and intraday trading. Conversely, securities exiting ESM regain normal settlement and price band treatment, improving tradability. Overall market impact is high for the specific scrips involved, though the measure itself is a routine surveillance mechanism rather than a company-specific adverse action.
