## Summary

BSE has informed trading members that KALIND LTD (Scrip Code: 526935) has fixed 24 July 2026 as the record date for a sub-division (stock split) and bonus issue of its equity shares. Existing shares of Rs.10 face value will be sub-divided into five shares of Rs.2 each, followed by a bonus issue of one additional Rs.2 share for every two shares held (post-split).

## Key Points

- Company: KALIND LTD, Scrip Code 526935, ISIN INE377D01018 (existing, Rs.10 paid-up)
- Corporate action: Sub-division of one existing equity share of Rs.10 each into five equity shares of Rs.2 each
- Additional action: Bonus issue of 1 equity share of Rs.2 each for every 2 equity shares of Rs.2 each held
- Record date: 24 July 2026
- The existing ISIN (Rs.10 paid-up) will not be valid for exchange transactions on or after 24 July 2026
- A new ISIN for the Rs.2 paid-up shares will be announced separately by BSE
- Approximately 30,47,25,000 equity shares are to be allotted on 27 July 2026 per the company's letter dated 8 July 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory policy change; this is a routine corporate action notice implementing a company-approved stock split combined with a bonus issue, requiring an ISIN change for the sub-divided shares.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must update systems to reflect the sub-division and bonus adjustments effective from the record date
- Existing ISIN INE377D01018 (Rs.10 paid-up) becomes invalid for trading from 24 July 2026
- Members should track the new ISIN notification for the Rs.2 paid-up shares once issued by BSE

## Important Dates

- 09 July 2026: Notice issued by BSE
- 24 July 2026: Record date for sub-division and bonus issue; effective date for new paid-up value (Rs.2) and old ISIN invalidation
- 27 July 2026: Expected allotment date for approximately 30,47,25,000 equity shares

## Impact Assessment

The combined 1:5 split and 1:2 bonus will significantly increase KALIND LTD's outstanding share count and proportionally reduce its per-share price, improving retail affordability and liquidity. Shareholders as of the record date will receive both the sub-divided and bonus shares. The ISIN change requires operational adjustments by depositories, brokers, and trading systems, but has no broader market-wide impact beyond this single stock.
