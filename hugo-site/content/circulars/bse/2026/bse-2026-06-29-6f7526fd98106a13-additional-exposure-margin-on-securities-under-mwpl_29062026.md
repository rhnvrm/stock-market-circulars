## Summary

BSE Clearing Limited (ICCL) has announced the levy of additional exposure margin at 15% in the equity derivatives segment on select securities under the Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL) framework. This is effective from July 1, 2026, immediately following the expiry of June 2026 contracts, and remains applicable until the July 2026 expiry.

## Key Points

- ICCL will levy additional exposure margin at **15%** in the equity derivatives segment.
- The margin applies to securities where the **top 10 clients account for more than 20% of MWPL**.
- Where additional surveillance margin is also applicable, the **higher of the two margins** (additional exposure margin or additional surveillance margin) will be levied.
- Securities are identified based on **3-month rolling data** and reviewed on a **monthly basis**.
- The specific securities subject to this margin are listed in the attached Annexure.
- This continues the framework established under BSE Notice dated March 18, 2019.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This is a monthly review and update under the existing Additional Exposure Margin on Securities under MWPL circular originally issued on March 18, 2019. The list of affected scrips in the Annexure is updated based on the latest 3-month rolling data.

## Compliance Requirements

- All members in the **equity derivatives segment** must ensure adequate margin collection from clients holding positions in the scrips listed in the Annexure.
- Where a security is subject to both additional exposure margin and additional surveillance margin, the **higher margin rate must be applied**.
- Members should review the Annexure to identify affected securities and communicate margin requirements to relevant clients.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** July 1, 2026 (immediately following expiry of June 2026 contracts)
- **Expiry of Applicability:** July 2026 expiry
- **Notice Date:** June 29, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on derivatives market participants. Traders and brokers with client positions in the Annexure-listed securities will need to collect or maintain additional margin buffers. The 15% additional exposure margin increases the cost of holding positions in concentrated securities, which may reduce speculative activity in those scrips. The monthly review mechanism ensures the list remains current with concentration risk data, limiting prolonged undue burden on less-concentrated securities.
