## Summary

BSE's Department of Surveillance (DOSS) has revised the price bands for 19 equity scrips, effective April 22, 2026. The revised bands range from 2% to 10%, restricting daily price movement for these securities as a surveillance measure.

## Key Points

- 19 scrips have been assigned revised price bands effective April 22, 2026
- Price bands revised to 2%: GDL Leasing & Finance Ltd (530855), Hypersoft Technologies Ltd (539724), Source Industries (India) Ltd (521036), Sparc Electrex Ltd (531370)
- Price bands revised to 5%: Adhbhut Infrastructure Ltd (539189), Choksi Asia Ltd (530427), Envair Electrodyne Ltd (500246), Highway Infrastructure Ltd (544477), Kirloskar Electric Company Ltd (533193), Panth Infinity Ltd (539143), Patron Exim Ltd (543798), Tokyo Plast International Ltd (500418), Waaree Technologies Ltd (539337), Yogi Sungwon (India) Ltd (522209)
- Price bands revised to 10%: Davin Sons Retail Ltd (544331), Kakatiya Cement Sugar & Industries Ltd (500234), Manaksia Steels Ltd (539044), S & T Corporation Ltd (514197), Triumph International Finance India Ltd (532131)
- Issued by Surveillance department under Deputy Vice President Sanjay Jain and Manager Arpita Joshi

## Regulatory Changes

Price bands for the listed scrips have been changed from their existing levels to the revised percentages specified. This limits the maximum permissible intraday price movement for each scrip to the stated band percentage above or below the previous day's closing price.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure orders placed in these scrips comply with the revised price band limits from April 22, 2026
- Members may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** April 21, 2026
- **Effective Date:** April 22, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding positions in these 19 scrips will face restricted daily price movement limits starting April 22, 2026. The tighter bands (2%) on four scrips indicate heightened surveillance concern, potentially limiting liquidity and exit opportunities for holders. The 10% band scrips retain more flexibility. This is a routine surveillance action and does not indicate any fundamental issue with the companies but signals that BSE is monitoring these securities for unusual trading activity.
