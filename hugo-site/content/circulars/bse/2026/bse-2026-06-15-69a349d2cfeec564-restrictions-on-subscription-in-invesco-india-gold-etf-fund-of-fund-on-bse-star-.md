## Summary

BSE has notified its members that Invesco Mutual Fund has imposed a subscription limit on the Invesco India Gold ETF Fund of Fund scheme. Effective June 15, 2026 (after the 3:00 PM cut-off time), fresh and additional purchases via lump sum and switch-in transactions are capped at ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month at the first holder level. This restriction has been duly implemented on the BSE StAR MF Platform.

## Key Points

- Subscription cap of ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month applies to Fresh/Additional Purchases via lump sum mode and Switch-in transactions in Invesco India Gold ETF Fund of Fund.
- The restriction is effective for transactions received after the 3:00 PM cut-off on June 15, 2026.
- Redemption, Switch-out, and STP-out transactions remain unrestricted with no amount cap.
- Subscription orders above ₹10,00,000 triggered to the RTA on or after 3:00 PM on June 15, 2026 will be rejected and refunded to investors once funds are received from AMC/RTA.
- The change originates from a Notice-cum-Addendum issued by Invesco Mutual Fund, a copy of which is attached to the BSE notice.

## Regulatory Changes

Invesco Mutual Fund has amended the terms of subscription for the Invesco India Gold ETF Fund of Fund scheme by introducing a monthly subscription ceiling of ₹10,00,000 per PAN (at the first holder level). This impacts lump sum purchases and switch-in transactions but does not alter the rules for outflows such as redemptions, switch-outs, or STPs.

## Compliance Requirements

- BSE StAR MF Platform members must ensure that subscription orders for Invesco India Gold ETF Fund of Fund do not exceed ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month for lump sum and switch-in modes.
- Orders breaching this limit that are triggered to the RTA on or after 3:00 PM on June 15, 2026 will be rejected; members should inform investors accordingly.
- Members should refer to the attached Notice-cum-Addendum from Invesco Mutual Fund for complete details.

## Important Dates

- **June 15, 2026**: Effective date of the subscription restriction.
- **3:00 PM on June 15, 2026**: Cut-off time after which the limit is enforced; transactions above the cap received after this time will be rejected.

## Impact Assessment

This restriction primarily impacts high-value investors seeking to deploy more than ₹10,00,000 per month into the Invesco India Gold ETF Fund of Fund via lump sum or switch-in routes on the BSE StAR MF Platform. Smaller retail investors are unlikely to be affected. Exit routes (redemption, switch-out, STP-out) remain fully open, limiting systemic impact. The restriction likely reflects AMC-level portfolio management considerations, potentially related to managing inflows into the gold ETF underlying the fund of fund.
