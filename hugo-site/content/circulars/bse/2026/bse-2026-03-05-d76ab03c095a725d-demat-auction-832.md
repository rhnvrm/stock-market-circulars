## Summary

BSE has published the Demat Auction No. 832 for settlement date March 5, 2026. The auction lists 91 securities across a wide range of sectors that are subject to auction due to delivery shortfalls in the settlement process.

## Key Points

- Settlement No. 832, dated 05/03/2026
- Total of 91 scrips listed for demat auction
- Quantities range from 1 unit (e.g., JTL Industries, Kotak Mahindra Bank) to 51,001 units (Leading Leasing)
- Large quantity auctions include: Leading Leasing (51,001), Vinny Overseas (10,100), APM Industries (2,710), Bharat Coking Coal (4,189), Meta Infotech (1,600)
- Notable large-cap stocks included: Reliance Industries (3,437 units), HDFC Bank (5 units), Infosys (15 units), Ashok Leyland (500 units)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine auction notice under existing BSE settlement and auction framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Sellers with delivery shortfalls for the listed securities are subject to auction settlement
- Buyers with undelivered securities will receive auction proceeds or close-out prices as per BSE rules
- Participants must comply with auction settlement obligations by the specified settlement date

## Important Dates

- Auction Date / Settlement Date: March 5, 2026
- Settlement No.: 832

## Impact Assessment

This is a standard demat auction notice with medium market impact. The auction affects 91 securities across sectors including banking, infrastructure, technology, pharmaceuticals, and commodities. The largest single auction is for Leading Leasing (51,001 units), which may have localized price impact. Blue-chip stocks such as Reliance Industries (3,437 units) and HDFC Bank appear in small quantities and are unlikely to see significant price disruption. Market participants holding short delivery positions in the listed scrips will face auction-based settlement at potentially higher prices.
