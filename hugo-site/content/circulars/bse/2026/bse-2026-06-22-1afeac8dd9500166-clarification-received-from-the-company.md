## Summary

BSE's Surveillance Department (DOSS) has observed significant price and/or volume movement in the scrips of six listed companies. As part of its investor protection mandate, the Exchange wrote to these companies seeking clarifications. A reply has been received from Gowra Leasing & Finance Ltd (Scrip Code: 530709), and the same has been attached to this notice. Replies from the remaining five companies are still awaited.

## Key Points

- BSE observed unusual price/volume movement in six equity scrips.
- The Exchange issued query letters to all six companies to ensure trading members and investors have access to the latest relevant information.
- Reply received from: **Gowra Leasing & Finance Ltd** (Scrip Code: 530709) — reply attached.
- Replies still awaited from five companies (see list below).
- Notice issued by Sanjay Jain (Deputy Vice President, Surveillance) and Shreya Ghose (Manager, Surveillance).

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced by this circular. It is a disclosure/surveillance notice under BSE's ongoing market oversight function.

## Compliance Requirements

- The five companies for which replies are awaited must respond to BSE's query promptly:
  1. Harikanta Overseas Ltd (Scrip Code: 544769)
  2. Amrapali Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 526241)
  3. Elango Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 513452)
  4. Grand Oak Canyons Distillery Ltd (Scrip Code: 523862)
  5. Balaji Telefilms Ltd (Scrip Code: 532382)
- Trading members and investors should review the attached reply from Gowra Leasing & Finance Ltd before trading in its scrip.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** 22 June 2026
- No specific deadline mentioned for pending replies; companies are expected to respond without undue delay.

## Impact Assessment

This notice signals heightened surveillance activity around these six scrips. Investors and traders in these counters should exercise caution. The attachment of Gowra Leasing & Finance Ltd's clarification may help explain recent price/volume movement in that scrip. The absence of replies from the remaining five companies indicates ongoing scrutiny; further action (such as trading restrictions or ASM/ESM placement) could follow if satisfactory explanations are not provided.
