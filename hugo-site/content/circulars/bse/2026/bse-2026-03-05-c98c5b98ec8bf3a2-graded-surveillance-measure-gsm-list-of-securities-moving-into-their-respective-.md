## Summary

BSE has announced the movement of two securities into GSM Stage I under the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework. The affected companies are Virya Resources Ltd (Security Code: 512479, ISIN: INE661K01010) and Sikozy Realtors Ltd (Security Code: 524642, ISIN: INE528E01022).

## Key Points

- Two securities have been moved into GSM Stage I effective from the date of this circular.
- Virya Resources Ltd (512479 | INE661K01010) is placed under GSM Stage I.
- Sikozy Realtors Ltd (524642 | INE528E01022) is placed under GSM Stage I.
- Securities marked with (#) may be moved to a lower GSM stage due to inclusion in the ESM (Enhanced Surveillance Measure) framework.
- Securities marked with ($) may be moved to a lower GSM stage due to inclusion in the IBC (Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code) framework.

## Regulatory Changes

The GSM framework is a surveillance mechanism implemented by SEBI and stock exchanges to protect investor interests by identifying and monitoring securities that exhibit abnormal price movements or other indicators of potential manipulation. Placement under GSM Stage I subjects the security to additional scrutiny and trading restrictions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must ensure clients are informed of the GSM status of these securities before executing trades.
- Investors holding or intending to trade in Virya Resources Ltd or Sikozy Realtors Ltd must be aware of the applicable trading restrictions under GSM Stage I.
- Market participants should refer to the detailed GSM framework guidelines for stage-specific restrictions including trade-to-trade settlement and applicable price bands.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: 5 March 2026 (date of circular issuance).

## Impact Assessment

The placement of Virya Resources Ltd and Sikozy Realtors Ltd under GSM Stage I will result in increased trading restrictions, including possible trade-to-trade settlement requirements and reduced price bands. Existing investors may face reduced liquidity and limited ability to exit positions easily. This action signals regulatory concern over the trading patterns or fundamentals of these companies and warrants heightened caution from retail and institutional investors alike.
