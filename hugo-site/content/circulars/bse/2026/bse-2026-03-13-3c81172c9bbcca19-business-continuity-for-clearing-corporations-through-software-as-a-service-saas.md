## Summary

ICCL will conduct a mock session on March 14, 2026 to test Two-way SaaS portability using the ICCL@NCL instance. When the BSE trading system switches to its Disaster Recovery (DR) site (scheduled around 1:45 PM), all ICCL clearing members will be required to login exclusively through the ICCL@NCL SaaS instance instead of the regular RTRMS system.

## Key Points

- Mock session scheduled for **March 14, 2026** to test Two-way SaaS portability on ICCL@NCL instance
- BSE trading system will switch to DR at approximately **1:45 PM**; simultaneously, ICCL@NCL SaaS instance will be invoked
- During the mock, ICCL clearing members will **not** be able to login to the regular RTRMS system
- Members must login through ICCL@NCL using existing credentials already provided
- Applicable systems: N-MASS, CIM, and NCMS.exe
- Cash deposit (CIM) SaaS link: https://rtrmssaas.icclindia.com/CIM/
- Position and collateral allocation (NMASS) SaaS link: https://rtrmssaas.icclindia.com/

## Regulatory Changes

No permanent regulatory changes. This circular is an operational notice for a scheduled mock/drill under the business continuity framework established by ICCL circulars dating back to March 2023 (No. 20230329-18, 20230413-49, 20230421-41) and December 2023 (No. 20231229-17).

## Compliance Requirements

- All ICCL clearing members must participate in the mock session on March 14, 2026
- Members must be prepared to switch login to the ICCL@NCL SaaS instance (N-MASS, CIM, NCMS.exe) when BSE DR is invoked around 1:45 PM
- Members should use their pre-issued credentials for the SaaS instance
- For detailed mock timings, members should refer to BSE circulars No. 20260313-01, 20260313-02, and 20260313-04 dated March 13, 2026
- Queries may be directed to: SaaS.Ops@icclindia.com or risk.monitoring@icclindia.com

## Important Dates

- **March 14, 2026**: Mock session for Two-way SaaS portability testing
- **~1:45 PM on March 14, 2026**: BSE trading system switches to DR; ICCL@NCL SaaS instance invoked

## Impact Assessment

This mock session affects all ICCL clearing members who must temporarily redirect their system access to the SaaS-based ICCL@NCL instance during the drill window. There is no market-wide trading impact beyond the scheduled mock period. Members unprepared for the switchover may face temporary operational disruption during the test. The exercise is part of ongoing business continuity preparedness and does not affect listed securities or trading rules.
