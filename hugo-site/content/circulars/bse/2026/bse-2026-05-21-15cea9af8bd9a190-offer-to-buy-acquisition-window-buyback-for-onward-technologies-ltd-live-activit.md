## Summary

BSE has announced the Live Activities Schedule for the Offer to Buy (OTB) – Acquisition Window (Buyback) for Onward Technologies Ltd (Scrip Code: 517536, Symbol: ONWARDTEC, ISIN: INE229A01017). The buyback window is open from Friday, 22nd May 2026 to Friday, 29th May 2026, with an offer price of Rs. 328.00 per share for up to 5,48,780 equity shares.

## Key Points

- **Security:** Onward Technologies Ltd (ONWARDTEC), Scrip Code 517536, ISIN INE229A01017
- **Offer Size:** Up to 5,48,780 equity shares
- **Offer Price:** Rs. 328.00 per share
- **Face Value:** Rs. 10.00
- **Market Lot:** 1
- **Opening Date:** Friday, 22nd May 2026
- **Closing Date:** Friday, 29th May 2026
- **Session Timings:** 9:15 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. (Bid Offering, Modifications/Cancellation, Give-up/Take-up)
- **Take-up on last day (29th May 2026):** Extended until 4:00 p.m.
- **Eligible Bidding Categories:** FII, FI, MF, IC, Other QIBs, NIB, Company, Individual

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This notice operationalizes the OTB mechanism per existing Exchange Notices 20150930-1 (dated 30 September 2015) and 20170203-29 (dated 3 February 2017).

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must ensure the Unique Client Code (UCC) of investors is registered and updated with the correct PAN Number and Category in the Exchange UCC database.
- Trading Members may bid only for investors holding shares in demat form.
- Members and Custodians should note that no End of Issue files will be generated in Extranet if no securities are accepted.
- For Leased Line URL access configuration, refer to Exchange Circular No. 20170503-12.

## Impact Assessment

This circular is directly relevant to shareholders of Onward Technologies Ltd and trading/custodian members facilitating the buyback. The offer price of Rs. 328.00 per share provides an exit opportunity for existing shareholders. The buyback size of up to 5,48,780 shares is modest in scope. Trading members must ensure compliance with UCC and demat requirements before placing bids on behalf of clients. No broader market-wide impact is anticipated.
