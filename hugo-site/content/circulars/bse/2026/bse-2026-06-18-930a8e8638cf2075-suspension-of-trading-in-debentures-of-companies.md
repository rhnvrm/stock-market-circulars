## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260618-42) directing trading members not to deal in Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) and Bonds of multiple companies that have fixed record dates for interest payment and/or redemption. The suspensions apply across settlement cycles 657/2026-2027, 658/2026-2027, and 659/2026-2027.

## Key Points

- Trading members are advised to refrain from dealing in the listed NCD/Bond instruments on or around their respective ex-dates
- Record dates fall between June 23–26, 2026, with ex-dates one business day prior (or same day)
- Instruments span private placement (PVT) NCDs and pass-through certificates (PTC) across the debt segment
- Three settlement cycles (657, 658, 659 of FY 2026-27) are involved

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is a standard procedural notice under existing BSE listing operation guidelines for debt instruments approaching redemption or interest payment dates.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the debentures/bonds of the listed companies around their respective ex-dates
- Members should update their systems to flag these instruments as restricted for trading during the suspension period

## Important Dates

- **June 23, 2026**: Record date for Avanse Financial Services (INE087P07345) and Indel Money (INE0BUS07BL0)
- **June 24, 2026**: Record date for Sakthi Finance (×2), Sammaan Capital (×2), Kerala Financial Corporation, EAAA India Alternatives, Tulip Growth 12 2023
- **June 25, 2026**: Record/ex-date for Aditya Birla Housing Finance, Satya Microcapital (×2), Kotak Mahindra Prime, Spandana Sphoorty Financial; ex-date for L&T Finance and Midland Microfin
- **June 26, 2026**: Record date for L&T Finance (INE235P07464) and Midland Microfin (INE884Q07715)

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects debt market participants trading in the listed NCD/Bond instruments. The suspension is temporary and routine, triggered by upcoming interest payment or redemption events. Sixteen or more instruments from issuers across NBFC, housing finance, microfinance, and financial services sectors are impacted. Investors holding these instruments should be aware of ex-dates to ensure eligibility for interest/redemption proceeds.
