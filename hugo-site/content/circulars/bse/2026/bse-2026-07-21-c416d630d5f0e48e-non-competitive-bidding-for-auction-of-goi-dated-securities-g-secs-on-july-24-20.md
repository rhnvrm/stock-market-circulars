## Summary

BSE has issued a notice informing trading members about the non-competitive bidding facility available for the RBI auction of four Government of India (GoI) dated securities scheduled for July 24, 2026. Bidding will be conducted through the NCB-GSec module of BSE's iBBS web-based system, referencing RBI's press release dated July 20, 2026, and BSE's earlier circular (20180423-42) on the non-competitive bidding facility.

## Key Points

- RBI announced the sale of four dated securities via auction on July 24, 2026.
- Bidding is facilitated through BSE's iBBS platform under the NCB-GSec module (https://ibbs.bseindia.com).
- New GS 2041 (NewGS41J26): issue size Rs. 17,000 crore, minimum subscription 100 units, minimum amount Rs. 10,000, maximum amount Rs. 2 crore.
- 7.43% GS 2076 (743GS76J26): issue size Rs. 11,000 crore, minimum subscription 100 units, minimum amount Rs. 10,000, maximum amount Rs. 2 crore.
- Both securities are re-issues.
- Queries on user ID/password reset or bidding can be directed to BSE's Trading Operations team.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this notice operationalizes the existing non-competitive bidding facility under BSE circular no. 20180423-42 dated April 23, 2018, in line with RBI's auction announcement.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members and direct investors participating in non-competitive bidding must submit bids within the specified windows via the NCB-GSec module, adhering to minimum subscription units and maximum bid amount caps per security.

## Important Dates

- Bid Collection Start Date: July 21, 2026, 10:00 am onwards (24-hour availability)
- Bid Collection End Date (Members): July 24, 2026, till 8:00 am
- Bid Collection End Date (Direct Investor): July 23, 2026, till 5:00 pm
- Auction Date: July 24, 2026
- Settlement Date: July 27, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt-market operational notice affecting trading members and direct investors participating in the RBI's G-Sec auction via BSE's non-competitive bidding platform. It has no direct impact on listed equities or broader market operations.
