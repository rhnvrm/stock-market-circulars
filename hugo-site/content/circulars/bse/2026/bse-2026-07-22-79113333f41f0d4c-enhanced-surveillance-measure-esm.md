## Summary

BSE has issued an updated list of securities under the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, referencing prior notices dating back to June 2023. Securities meeting the ESM criteria (per Annexure I) will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open and new positions effective July 24, 2026, and will move from the Rolling Settlement segment to Trade-for-Trade. Some securities already under ESM will be downgraded to Stage I (lower stage), while others will move to Stage II with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction. Securities exiting the framework are listed in Annexure II, and a consolidated list is in Annexure III.

## Key Points

- Securities meeting ESM criteria (Annexure I) will attract minimum 100% margin on all open positions as of July 23, 2026 and new positions from July 24, 2026
- Securities continuing in ESM will move to a lower Stage I under the framework effective July 23, 2026
- Securities shifting to Stage II will be under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction effective July 23, 2026
- Securities qualifying under ESM will shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment effective July 24, 2026
- Securities exiting ESM (Annexure II) effective July 23, 2026 will have price bands reinstated to pre-ESM levels, unless subject to another surveillance framework
- ESM operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures
- Inclusion under ESM is a surveillance action, not an adverse comment on the company
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the ongoing ESM framework established via prior BSE notices (20230602-44, 20230718-46, 20240809-42, 20240920-63, 20241004-65, and 20250725-61). It reclassifies securities across ESM stages based on periodic review criteria, without altering the framework's structural rules.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must apply minimum 100% margin on ESM-qualifying securities from the specified dates, ensure affected securities are traded strictly on a Trade-for-Trade basis, and observe the applicable price bands and Periodic Call Auction mechanism for Stage II securities.

## Important Dates

- July 23, 2026: Stage revisions take effect; securities exit ESM framework; open positions as of this date subject to margin; Stage II price band and Periodic Call Auction begin
- July 24, 2026: 100% margin applies to new positions; shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment takes effect

## Impact Assessment

The 100% margin requirement and shift to Trade-for-Trade settlement significantly reduce trading liquidity and leverage availability for affected securities, likely curbing speculative activity and price volatility. Investors holding open positions in newly included securities face immediate margin funding requirements, while those in securities exiting ESM benefit from restored price bands and normal settlement, improving tradability.
