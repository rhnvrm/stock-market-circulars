## Summary

Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) has announced a Demat Auction for **Settlement No. 825** dated February 23, 2026. The auction covers 103 scrips in which sellers failed to deliver shares in dematerialised form, requiring the exchange to procure these shares through an auction process to honour buyer obligations.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: **825**
- Auction Date: **February 23, 2026**
- Total scrips under auction: **103**
- Largest quantity lots: NOVARTIS INDIA (4031 shares), Vistar Amar (4002), BGIL FILMS & TV (3602), MRC AGROTECH (3000), SUNDARAM MULTI PAP (1943)
- Covers a broad cross-section of BSE-listed equities ranging from large-caps (INFOSYS, HDFC BANK, TATA STEEL, L&T, SBI) to small/micro-caps
- Quantities per scrip range from 1 share to 4031 shares

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice under BSE's settlement and auction framework for handling demat delivery shortfalls.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Brokers/Members with short positions** in the listed scrips for Settlement No. 825 must participate in or comply with the auction process
- **Buying brokers** should expect fulfillment of pending deliveries through the auction mechanism
- Members should review their settlement obligations for the listed scrip codes and quantities

## Important Dates

- **Auction Date:** February 23, 2026 (Settlement No. 825)
- Delivery and settlement timelines follow standard BSE auction settlement procedures post-auction date

- **Small/micro-cap scrips** with large quantities relative to liquidity (e.g., BGIL FILMS, SSPDL, SUNDARAM MULTI PAP) may experience more pronounced short-term impact
- The auction is a routine mechanism ensuring settlement integrity; no systemic risk is indicated
- Brokers with short delivery positions in these scrips will bear close-out charges as per BSE norms
