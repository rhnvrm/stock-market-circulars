## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that 14 IT sector scrips will not be available for trading under the T+0 rolling settlement cycle on Monday, May 11, 2026. These securities will still be tradeable under the regular T+1 settlement cycle. The restriction applies to prominent technology companies listed on BSE.

## Key Points

- 14 IT sector scrips are excluded from T+0 rolling settlement on May 11, 2026
- Normal T+1 settlement trading remains unaffected for these scrips
- All affected scrips are identified with a '#' suffix in their symbol
- The notice is issued by BSE's Trading Operations department
- Issued under Notice No. 20260507-15 dated May 7, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No permanent regulatory changes. This is a temporary, date-specific operational restriction suspending T+0 settlement eligibility for the listed scrips on May 11, 2026 only.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note that T+0 settlement orders cannot be placed for the listed 14 scrips on May 11, 2026
- Members should inform their clients accordingly to avoid order rejection
- Normal T+1 settlement trades may proceed as usual for these scrips

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** May 7, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 11, 2026 (Monday) — T+0 settlement unavailable for listed scrips

## Impact Assessment

The restriction affects traders and investors who rely on same-day (T+0) settlement for the 14 listed IT sector stocks. Day traders and arbitrageurs seeking intraday fund settlement will need to use the standard T+1 settlement cycle instead. The impact is limited to a single trading session and does not affect the broader market or long-term investors. All affected companies are large-cap IT sector constituents, so trading volumes under T+0 could be significant for affected participants.
