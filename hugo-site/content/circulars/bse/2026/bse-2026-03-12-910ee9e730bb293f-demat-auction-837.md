## Summary

BSE has published the Demat Auction notice for Settlement No. 837, dated 12 March 2026. The auction lists securities across approximately 80 companies where shares are to be auctioned, typically arising from short delivery or non-delivery obligations in normal market settlement.

## Key Points

- Settlement No. 837 dated 12/03/2026 issued by Bombay Stock Exchange Ltd., Mumbai
- Lists ~80 securities across diverse sectors subject to demat auction
- Quantities range from 1 share (e.g., ARCHIDPLY IN, RISHABH DIGH) to 883,228 shares (SPICEJET LTD)
- Notable large-quantity auction entries include SPICEJET LTD (883,228), ROYAL INDIA (122,593), EVEXIA LIFEC (47,197), and FERTILIZERS (24,008)
- Covers equity shares across large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap companies

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice under existing BSE settlement and auction framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants with outstanding short delivery obligations for the listed securities must participate in or be aware of the auction process
- Clearing members should ensure settlement of auction obligations as per BSE norms
- Buyers in the auction must fulfill payment obligations within specified timelines

## Important Dates

- Auction Date: 12 March 2026 (Settlement No. 837)

## Impact Assessment

Low market impact overall. The auction affects individual securities in specific quantities and is a routine settlement mechanism. The highest-volume entry, SPICEJET LTD at 883,228 shares, may have marginal price impact given its liquidity profile. Most other entries involve small quantities (single digits to a few thousand shares) and are unlikely to cause notable price movement. Participants holding short positions in the listed scrips face auction settlement at potentially higher prices if the auction rate exceeds the original trade price.
