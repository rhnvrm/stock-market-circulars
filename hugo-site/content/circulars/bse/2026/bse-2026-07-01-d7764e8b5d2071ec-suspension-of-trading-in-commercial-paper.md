## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that several companies have fixed record dates for the redemption of their Commercial Paper (CP) instruments. Trading members are advised not to deal in the Commercial Paper of these companies from the specified dates, as the instruments are approaching maturity/redemption.

## Key Points

- The notice lists CP instruments from multiple issuers including Julius Baer Capital (India), Navi Finserv Limited, Tata Steel Limited, Krazybee Services Ltd, Capri Global Capital Limited, Bajaj Financial Securities Limited, Bajaj Finance Limited, Kotak Mahindra Prime Limited, ICICI Home Finance Company Limited, Tata Realty and Infrastructure Limited, Sikka Ports & Terminals Ltd, and 360 ONE Alternates Asset Management.
- Each entry specifies the Scrip Code, Scrip ID, ISIN Number, Record Date, Ex Date, and Settlement DR (deliverable) reference for the respective CP.
- Record dates range from 08 July 2026 through 21 July 2026, with corresponding ex-dates on the same days.
- No attachments were included with this notice.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine operational notice under BSE's Debt segment / Listing Operations department; it does not introduce new regulations but implements standard pre-redemption trading suspension procedures for Commercial Paper.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must refrain from dealing in the listed Commercial Paper ISINs on or after the respective record/ex-dates mentioned.
- Settlement obligations tied to these CPs should reference the corresponding settlement number (e.g., 667/2026-2027, 668/2026-2027, etc.) as indicated for each instrument.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has low market impact as it pertains solely to routine pre-maturity trading suspension for specific Commercial Paper instruments in the debt segment. It affects only trading members dealing in these particular short-term debt instruments and does not impact equity markets, broader debt indices, or the general operations of the listed companies involved.
