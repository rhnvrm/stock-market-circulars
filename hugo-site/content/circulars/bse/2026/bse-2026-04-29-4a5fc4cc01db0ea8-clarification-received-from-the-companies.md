## Summary

BSE's Surveillance Department (DOSS) issued Notice No. 20260429-14 on 29 April 2026, addressing significant price and volume movements observed in 28 listed scrips. The Exchange had written to all these companies to ensure trading members and investors have access to the latest relevant information. Clarifications have been received from 13 companies, while replies from the remaining 15 companies are still awaited.

## Key Points

- BSE observed significant price/volume movement in 28 scrips and initiated clarification requests.
- 13 companies have submitted replies, which are attached to the notice (Replies1 and Replies2).
- 15 companies have not yet responded; their replies are awaited.
- The notice is issued by the Surveillance Department (Deputy Vice President Sanjay Jain and Manager Shreya Ghose).
- Segment covered: Equity.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is a standard surveillance-driven clarification notice under existing BSE monitoring frameworks for unusual price and volume activity in listed equities.

## Compliance Requirements

- The 15 companies that have not yet responded must submit clarifications to BSE's Surveillance Department regarding the observed price/volume movements in their scrips.
- Investors and trading members are advised to review the attached replies from the 13 responding companies before making trading decisions in these scrips.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 29 April 2026
- No specific deadline mentioned for pending replies; companies under surveillance are expected to respond promptly.

## Impact Assessment

This notice signals heightened regulatory scrutiny on 28 mid-to-small cap equities. Investors holding or tracking these scrips should review the submitted clarifications for any material disclosures. The 15 non-responding companies remain under active surveillance, which may result in further regulatory action or trading restrictions if replies are not forthcoming. The notice has a moderate impact on market participants invested in these specific securities but no broader systemic implications.
