## Summary

HSBC Mutual Fund is launching HSBC GOLD ETF as a New Fund Offer (NFO) on the BSE StAR MF Platform. The NFO window runs from March 16, 2026 to March 18, 2026 (extended till midnight for lumpsum purchases). Both the regular plan and direct plan are offered under the Equity category, with a tentative allotment date of March 23, 2026.

## Key Points

- HSBC GOLD ETF and HSBC GOLD ETF - Direct Plan will be available on BSE StAR MF Platform from March 16–18, 2026
- Both schemes are categorised under Equity with a tentative allotment date of March 23, 2026
- NFO is extended till midnight on March 18, 2026 for lumpsum purchases on BSE StAR MF Platform
- Transactions where clients have not initiated funds by 9:00 AM on T+1 will be cancelled; a CC with N flag will be reported to RTA/AMC
- Transactions with initiated payments remain pending until 3:00 PM on the last day of funds payout to AMC, or T+5, whichever is earlier
- Cheques and OTM payments are acceptable during the NFO period, subject to clear funds in ICCL account

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an operational notice for the launch of a new ETF NFO on BSE StAR MF Platform.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure clients initiate funds by 9:00 AM on T+1 to avoid transaction cancellation
- Clear funds must be available in the ICCL Account by 6:00 PM on March 20, 2026 for unit allotment under HSBC GOLD ETF NFO
- Cheque and OTM payments must result in clear funds in ICCL account by 6:00 PM on March 20, 2026

## Important Dates

- **March 16, 2026**: NFO opens
- **March 18, 2026**: NFO closes (extended till midnight for lumpsum purchases)
- **March 20, 2026 by 6:00 PM**: Clear funds must be available in ICCL Account
- **March 23, 2026**: Tentative allotment date for both HSBC GOLD ETF and HSBC GOLD ETF - Direct Plan

## Impact Assessment

This circular is relevant to BSE members and investors participating in mutual fund transactions via the BSE StAR MF Platform. The NFO provides an opportunity to invest in a gold-backed ETF. Operationally, members need to communicate fund initiation deadlines to clients to avoid transaction cancellations. The payment and clearing timelines (T+1 fund initiation, T+5 pending period, ICCL deadline of March 20) require careful coordination with clients, particularly for cheque and OTM payment modes.
