## Summary

BSE has listed and admitted for trading further new securities issued by 12 companies under their ESOP (Employee Stock Option Plan) / ESOS (Employee Stock Option Scheme) schemes. The new securities are effective for trading from Tuesday, June 9, 2026. None of the listed shares are subject to any lock-in period.

## Key Points

- 12 companies have had additional shares listed pursuant to ESOP/ESOS exercises
- All new securities are effective for trading from June 9, 2026
- No lock-in restrictions apply to any of the newly listed shares
- Largest listing by volume: Meesho Limited with 5,604,880 shares (face value ₹1)
- Second largest: ICICI Bank Limited with 1,210,306 shares (face value ₹2)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice informing trading members of newly listed securities arising from employee stock option exercises.

## Compliance Requirements

All market participants and trading members are requested to take note of the newly listed securities and their respective ISIN codes, scrip codes, and share ranges for trading purposes.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 8, 2026
- **Effective Trading Date:** June 9, 2026 (Tuesday)

## Impact Assessment

Routine ESOP/ESOS listing with minimal market impact. The newly listed shares represent employee stock option exercises across 12 companies spanning banking, financial services, consumer goods, technology, and education sectors. The largest issuances — Meesho (5,604,880 shares) and ICICI Bank (1,210,306 shares) — are small relative to their total outstanding share counts and are unlikely to cause significant price movements.
