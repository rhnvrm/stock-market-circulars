## Summary

BSE has updated the list of securities subject to Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) for companies undergoing the Insolvency Resolution Process (IRP) under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). New securities satisfying the IBC criteria are being added (Annexure I), certain securities are moving out of the framework (Annexure II), and a consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework is provided (Annexure III). All changes are effective June 19, 2026.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I have satisfied the IBC-ASM criteria and will be placed under the framework from June 19, 2026.
- Securities in Annexure II are being removed from the IBC framework effective June 19, 2026.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the IBC framework is provided in Annexure III.
- The IBC surveillance framework operates alongside all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the exchanges.
- Placement under this framework is purely a market surveillance action and should not be construed as an adverse action against the company.
- For securities exiting the framework, the price band will be reinstated to the band applicable before shortlisting, unless the scrip remains under another surveillance measure.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the IBC-ASM framework first established under Exchange Notice No. 20180608-34 (June 8, 2018) and subsequently amended via notices dated May 20, 2022, February 10, 2023, and September 5, 2025. No changes to the framework rules are introduced; this is a periodic list revision.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised list of securities under the IBC framework and apply applicable trading restrictions accordingly.
- For securities exiting the framework, members should update their systems to reflect the reinstated price bands effective June 19, 2026.
- Any clarifications may be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **June 19, 2026**: Effective date for addition of new securities (Annexure I) and removal of securities (Annexure II) from the IBC surveillance framework.

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding securities placed under or removed from the IBC-ASM framework will see changes in applicable price bands and trading conditions from June 19, 2026. Securities added to the framework typically face tighter price bands and may be moved to trade-for-trade settlement, increasing trading friction and margin requirements. Securities exiting the framework will revert to standard price bands (subject to no other active surveillance measure), potentially improving liquidity. The specific scrips affected are detailed in the attached annexures.
