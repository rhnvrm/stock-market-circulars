## Summary

ICCL (Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd.) has issued the settlement calendar for the Offer to Buy – Acquisition Window (Buyback) for Sarla Performance Fibers Limited. The buyback bidding window runs from May 21 to May 27, 2026, with obligations downloadable on June 3 and final settlement on June 4, 2026. Members and custodians must use early pay-in of shares via CDSL/NSDL depositories.

## Key Points

- Settlement Type: Buyback under Offer to Buy – Acquisition Window mechanism
- Settlement Number: 2026054
- Bidding window: May 21, 2026 to May 27, 2026
- Demat shares must be delivered exclusively through the Early Pay-in of Shares mechanism (available on both CDSL and NSDL)
- Members/Custodians must use Market Type "(Buyback)" and Settlement No. 2026054 in the depository system
- No EPN file upload is required; CDSL & NSDL will earmark/block securities in clients' accounts automatically
- Members should refer to file MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo for client-wise Stamp Duty charges

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. Members are directed to refer to existing Notice No. 20200528-32 dated May 28, 2020, regarding settlement as per the Revised Guidelines of Mechanism for Acquisition of Shares through Stock Exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and Custodians must ensure early pay-in of securities is reflected in BSE/ICCL systems **before** the prescribed timelines for accepting/confirming bids on the BSE OTB platform
- Early pay-in must be completed sufficiently in advance of the closure of the BSE OTB Bidding window for members and the Confirmation window for Custodians
- Use Market Type "(Buyback)" and Settlement No. 2026054 in all depository system entries
- No EPN file upload needed; securities will be earmarked/blocked automatically by depositories

## Important Dates

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Bid Start Date | May 21, 2026 |
| Bid End Date | May 27, 2026 |
| Obligation Download | June 3, 2026 |
| Settlement Date | June 4, 2026 |

## Impact Assessment

This notice is operationally relevant to stock exchange members and custodians participating in the Sarla Performance Fibers Limited buyback. The early pay-in requirement means participants must plan depository operations in advance of each bid window closure to avoid rejection of bids. The impact is limited to participants in this specific buyback and does not affect the broader market.
