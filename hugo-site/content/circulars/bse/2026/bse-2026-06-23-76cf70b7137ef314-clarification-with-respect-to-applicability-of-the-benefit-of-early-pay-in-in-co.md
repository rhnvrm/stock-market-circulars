## Summary

BSE Clearing Limited (BSECL) has issued Notice No. 20260623-1 dated 23 June 2026 providing clarification regarding the applicability of the early pay-in benefit in the Commodity Derivatives Segment. The notice is addressed to all market participants operating in this segment and is accompanied by Annexure-1 outlining specific details.

## Key Points

- BSE Clearing Limited (BSECL) is the issuing department under the Settlement/RMS category.
- The notice clarifies how the early pay-in benefit applies within the Commodity Derivatives Segment.
- Annexure-1 is attached and contains supporting details or conditions related to the clarification.
- The notice falls under the General segment and is applicable to all relevant participants.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice serves as a clarification rather than a new regulatory mandate. It provides additional guidance on the existing framework governing early pay-in of securities in the Commodity Derivatives Segment, ensuring uniform interpretation and application across market participants.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants in the Commodity Derivatives Segment should review this notice and Annexure-1 to understand the precise conditions under which the early pay-in benefit is applicable.
- Clearing members and trading members should update their internal settlement procedures to reflect this clarification.
- Any queries regarding applicability should be directed to BSECL's Settlement/RMS department.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** 23 June 2026
- **Notice Number:** 20260623-1
- Effective date of clarification is not separately specified; the clarification is deemed to apply from the date of the notice.

## Impact Assessment

This clarification impacts clearing members and trading members active in BSE's Commodity Derivatives Segment. Early pay-in benefits typically allow participants to reduce their margin obligations by delivering securities ahead of settlement deadlines. Clarity on applicability helps participants optimise capital usage and reduces ambiguity in margin calculations. The operational impact is moderate, as it refines existing practices without introducing entirely new requirements.
