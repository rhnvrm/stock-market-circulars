## Summary

BSE has issued a notice moving select securities to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 2, 2026. The affected securities, listed in the attached Annexure, will be subject to progressively stricter surveillance actions depending on the GSM stage they are assigned to.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in the Annexure will be moved to higher GSM stages from June 2, 2026.
- Four GSM stages apply, each with increasing levels of trading restrictions.
- Stage I: 100% applicable margin rate with a price band of 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, and Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade settlement, 5% or lower price band, trading permitted once a week (every Monday/first trading day), and ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers.
- Stage IV: Same as Stage III with an additional restriction of no upward price movement.
- No netting off is allowed; settlement will be done strictly on a trade-to-trade basis.
- Trading members are advised to take adequate precautions while dealing in these securities.

## Regulatory Changes

The GSM framework was originally established via Exchange notice no. 20170223-44 (February 23, 2017) and subsequently updated through notices dated March 3, 2017; July 20, 2018; November 29, 2019; and November 17, 2023. This circular implements stage escalations under the existing framework without introducing new regulatory provisions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure buyers of Stage II, III, and IV securities deposit the required ASD (50% or 100% of trade value as applicable) before execution.
- All trades in the affected securities must be settled on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting.
- For Stage III and IV securities, trades may only be executed on Mondays (or the first trading day of the week).
- Members should refer to the GSM FAQ available at http://www.bseindia.com/markets/equity/EQReports/graded_surveil_measure.aspx?expandable=6 for detailed guidance.
- Queries may be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **June 2, 2026**: Effective date for GSM stage escalation of the securities listed in the Annexure.

## Impact Assessment

The escalation to higher GSM stages significantly restricts liquidity and increases the cost of trading for the affected securities. Buyers face mandatory ASD deposits of 50%–100% of trade value, and Stage III/IV securities are further limited to once-a-week trading with no upward price movement allowed in Stage IV. These measures are designed to protect investors from speculative or potentially manipulated securities. Market participants holding or tracking these stocks should review the Annexure to identify affected scrips and plan their trading strategies accordingly.
