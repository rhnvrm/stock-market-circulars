## Summary

BSE has announced corporate actions for seven securities in the Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) segment. All listed corporate actions are dividend-related with corresponding shut periods during which SLB activities will be restricted.

## Key Points

- Seven companies have declared dividends with upcoming ex-dates and record dates
- SLB shut periods will be enforced for all securities during their respective corporate action periods
- All corporate actions are dividend distributions
- Ex-dates range from January 23-27, 2026
- Record dates range from January 23-27, 2026

## Important Dates

**SRF Ltd.**
- Ex Date: January 27, 2026
- Record Date: January 27, 2026
- Shut Period: January 23-27, 2026

**Havells India Limited**
- Ex Date: January 23, 2026
- Record Date: January 23, 2026
- Shut Period: January 22-23, 2026

**DCM Shriram Limited**
- Ex Date: January 23, 2026
- Record Date: January 24, 2026
- Shut Period: January 22-24, 2026

**TIPS Music Limited**
- Ex Date: January 23, 2026
- Record Date: January 23, 2026
- Shut Period: January 22-23, 2026

**United Spirits Limited**
- Ex Date: January 27, 2026
- Record Date: January 27, 2026
- Shut Period: January 23-27, 2026

**Persistent Systems Limited**
- Ex Date: January 27, 2026
- Record Date: January 27, 2026
- Shut Period: January 23-27, 2026

**Oberoi Realty Limited**
- Ex Date: January 23, 2026
- Record Date: January 23, 2026
- Shut Period: January 22-23, 2026

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants engaged in SLB transactions must observe the shut periods for affected securities
- No new SLB positions should be initiated during the shut periods
- Existing SLB positions may be subject to restrictions during corporate action periods

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects liquidity in the SLB segment for seven actively traded securities during the specified shut periods.

**Operational Impact:** SLB participants need to adjust their lending and borrowing strategies around the dividend record dates and shut periods. The restrictions are temporary and limited to specific date ranges for each security.
