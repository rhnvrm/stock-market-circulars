## Summary

BSE has suspended trading in Commercial Paper (CP) instruments of 22 companies effective from their respective record dates (February 10-11, 2026) due to scheduled redemptions. Trading members are advised not to deal in these CPs from the specified dates.

## Key Points

- 22 Commercial Paper instruments suspended from trading
- 17 CPs have record date of February 10, 2026 with trading suspension from same date
- 5 CPs have record date of February 11, 2026 with trading suspension from same date
- Suspension purpose: Redemption of Commercial Paper
- All notices filed under reference DR-817/2025-2026 (Feb 10) and DR-818/2025-2026 (Feb 11)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice for CP redemptions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the listed Commercial Papers from their respective suspension dates
- All affected CPs are suspended from trading effective from their record dates

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects debt market participants holding or trading these specific Commercial Papers. Temporary suspension for redemption purposes is standard practice.

**Operational Impact:** Trading members must ensure compliance and avoid dealing in suspended CPs. Companies include major conglomerates (Adani, Reliance, Bajaj) and financial services firms.

**Investor Impact:** CP holders will receive redemption proceeds as per schedule. No trading opportunity during suspension period.
