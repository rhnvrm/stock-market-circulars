## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260615-48 directing trading members to suspend dealing in commercial papers of multiple companies that have fixed record dates for redemption. Two batches of suspensions are effective June 17 and June 18, 2026, under Settlement Nos. 653/2026-2027 and 654/2026-2027 respectively.

## Key Points

- Trading members are advised not to deal in the listed commercial papers from their respective ex-dates onward
- Eight companies have record date of **June 17, 2026** (Settlement 653/2026-2027)
- At least twelve companies have record date of **June 18, 2026** (Settlement 654/2026-2027)
- Both the record date and ex-date are the same for all listed securities
- This is a routine debt market operation triggered by scheduled commercial paper maturities

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice under existing BSE listing and debt segment regulations requiring suspension of trading in commercial paper ahead of redemption record dates.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members** must refrain from executing any trades in the listed commercial papers on or after their respective ex-dates
- Members dealing in debt instruments must update their systems to reflect these suspension notices promptly

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a **low** market impact as it pertains exclusively to the debt segment and covers routine commercial paper redemptions. Holders of these CPs should note that trading will be suspended from the ex-date and redemption proceeds will be settled under the respective settlement numbers. Equity markets and broader trading operations are unaffected. The suspension is temporary and procedurally standard, ensuring orderly redemption of maturing commercial paper instruments.
