## Summary

The Government of Maharashtra has declared January 15, 2026 as a public holiday under Section 25 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881. This will result in a settlement holiday for BSE clearing and settlement operations. The Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd. will communicate revised settlement schedules subsequently.

## Key Points

- January 15, 2026 declared as public holiday by Government of Maharashtra
- Declaration made under Section 25 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881
- Settlement operations will be affected on this date
- Revised settlement schedules will be communicated separately
- Notice issued by Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd.

## Regulatory Changes

No permanent regulatory changes. This is a one-time public holiday declaration affecting settlement operations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants should note the settlement holiday on January 15, 2026
- Participants should await subsequent communication regarding revised settlement schedules
- Payment and settlement obligations should be adjusted according to the revised schedule once communicated

## Important Dates

- **January 15, 2026**: Settlement holiday (public holiday)
- **Notice Date**: January 09, 2026
- Revised settlement schedules to be communicated subsequently

## Impact Assessment

**Operational Impact**: All settlement operations scheduled for January 15, 2026 will be affected. This includes payment obligations, securities delivery, and other clearing activities.

**Market Participants**: Trading members, clearing members, and custodians need to adjust their settlement expectations and cash flow planning for transactions that would have settled on this date.

**Timeline**: The settlement holiday will cause a one-day shift in settlement cycles for affected transactions. Detailed revised schedules will be provided in a subsequent circular.

**Scope**: Applies to all segments where January 15, 2026 was scheduled as a settlement date under normal settlement cycles.
