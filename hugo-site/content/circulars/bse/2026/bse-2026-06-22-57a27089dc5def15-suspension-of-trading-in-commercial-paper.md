## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260622-33 informs trading members that 16 companies have fixed record dates for the redemption of their Commercial Paper (CP) instruments. Members are directed not to deal in the commercial paper of these companies effective from their respective ex-dates.

## Key Points

- 16 companies across two settlement batches (659/2026-2027 and 660/2026-2027) have fixed record dates for CP redemption
- Trading members must not deal in the listed commercial paper scrips from the specified ex-dates
- Two groups of ex-dates apply: June 25, 2026 (majority of CPs) and June 29, 2026 (four CPs)
- Issued by the Listing Compliance and Operations department, BSE

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice under existing BSE framework for commercial paper redemption suspensions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must cease all transactions in the listed commercial paper instruments from the specified ex-dates
- Members should ensure their systems reflect the trading suspension for each listed scrip code

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects debt segment participants dealing in short-term commercial paper instruments. The suspension is routine and time-bound, applying only until the respective redemption record dates. No equity trading is impacted. Companies span diverse sectors including finance, infrastructure, FMCG, and engineering, reflecting typical CP issuance activity. Trading members with open positions in these instruments should ensure orderly wind-down before the effective ex-dates.
