## Summary

BSE has conducted its periodic review for the "Additional Measure for securities witnessing unusual price movement" (AMS) framework, as per the earlier circular no. 20251107-56 dated November 07, 2025. Based on this review, certain securities have been identified for inclusion into the AMS framework, while others have been identified for exclusion, both effective July 20, 2026. A consolidated list of securities currently under the framework has also been published.

## Key Points

- Securities satisfying AMS criteria are listed in Annexure I and will be placed under the measure effective July 20, 2026.
- Securities no longer meeting AMS criteria are listed in Annexure II and will move out of the measure effective July 20, 2026.
- Annexure III provides the consolidated list of all securities currently under the AMS framework.
- This action follows the framework established under BSE circular no. 20251107-56 dated November 07, 2025.
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this circular applies the existing AMS framework (established via circular 20251107-56) through a routine periodic review, updating the list of securities subject to additional surveillance measures.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the additional surveillance measures to securities newly included in Annexure I from the effective date.
- Trading members should note the securities exiting the measure per Annexure II and adjust monitoring/compliance processes accordingly.
- Members should refer to the attached Annexure for the specific list of ISINs/scrip codes affected.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 17, 2026
- Effective Date for inclusions and exclusions: July 20, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Securities newly placed under AMS will likely face additional trading restrictions such as higher margins, price band constraints, or other risk-containment measures, which may reduce liquidity and increase trading costs for these stocks. Conversely, securities moving out of AMS should see normalized trading conditions. The overall market impact is limited to the specific securities named in the annexures rather than the broader market.
