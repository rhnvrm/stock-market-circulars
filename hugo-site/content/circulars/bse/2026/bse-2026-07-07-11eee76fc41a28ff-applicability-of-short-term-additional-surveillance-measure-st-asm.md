## Summary

BSE has notified that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure I) have met the criteria for inclusion under the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) Stage I and Stage II. These measures impose additional margin requirements on trading in the identified securities, effective July 09, 2026.

## Key Points

- Securities satisfying ST-ASM Stage I criteria will attract a margin of 50% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- Securities satisfying ST-ASM Stage II criteria will attract a margin of 100% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at a maximum of 100%.
- These margin requirements apply to all open positions as on July 08, 2026, and to new positions created from July 09, 2026.
- The ASM framework operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchange.
- Inclusion under ASM is purely a market surveillance measure and should not be viewed as an adverse action against the concerned company or entity.
- When a scrip moves out of the ASM framework, its price band will be reinstated to the level applicable before shortlisting, unless it remains under another surveillance framework.
- This notice references prior circulars dated September 20, 2024, October 27, 2018, July 19, 2019, December 04, 2020, and September 25, 2023 regarding the ASM framework revisions.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice applies the existing ST-ASM framework (as revised via prior circulars) to a fresh set of securities identified per the applicable criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must apply the specified higher margin rates (50% for Stage I, 100% for Stage II securities) on all open and new positions in the identified securities starting July 09, 2026. Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 07, 2026
- Effective Date for margin measures: July 09, 2026 (applicable to open positions as on July 08, 2026, and new positions from July 09, 2026)

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors in the affected securities will face significantly higher margin requirements, which may reduce trading volumes and liquidity in these scrips. The measure is intended to curb speculative trading and excessive volatility rather than reflect any adverse fundamental development in the concerned companies. Market participants should identify the specific securities from the referenced Annexure I to assess direct impact.
