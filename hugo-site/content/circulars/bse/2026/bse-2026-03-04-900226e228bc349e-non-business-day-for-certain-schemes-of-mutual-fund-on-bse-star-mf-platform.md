## Summary

BSE has notified non-business days in March 2026 for select mutual fund schemes across four AMCs — Invesco, DSP, Kotak, and Nippon India — on the BSE StAR MF Platform. On these dates, the affected schemes will not process transactions. Investors and distributors must plan purchases, redemptions, and switches accordingly.

## Key Points

- Invesco India schemes (Pan European Equity FoF, Global Equity Income FoF, Global Consumer Trends FoF, EQQQ Nasdaq-100 ETF FoF) will observe a non-business day on **March 19, 2026**.
- DSP Value Fund will observe a non-business day on **March 17, 2026**.
- All DSP Debt Schemes (including DSP Liquidity Fund, DSP Overnight Fund) and several DSP overseas/FOF schemes will observe a non-business day on **March 19, 2026**; however, DSP Gold ETF FOF, DSP Multi Asset Allocation Fund, DSP Silver ETF FOF, and DSP Equity Savings Fund will remain open for business.
- Kotak overseas/FOF schemes (Global Emerging Market, US Specific Equity Passive, Global Innovation, International REIT) will observe non-business days on **March 3, March 6, March 26, and March 31, 2026** (specific schemes vary by date).
- Nippon India Japan Equity Fund will observe a non-business day on **March 20, 2026**.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice issued periodically by BSE to inform market participants of AMC-declared non-business days.

## Compliance Requirements

- Distributors and investors transacting through BSE StAR MF Platform must avoid submitting transactions for the affected schemes on the listed non-business dates.
- Transactions submitted on non-business days will not be processed; investors should plan ahead to avoid missed NAV dates or settlement delays.

## Impact Assessment

Impact is limited and operational in nature. The affected schemes are primarily international fund-of-funds and debt schemes that track overseas markets or are linked to foreign market holidays. Retail and institutional investors using BSE StAR MF for these schemes should be aware of the blackout dates to avoid transaction failures. No broad market impact is expected.
