## Summary

BSE's Department of Surveillance and Supervision (DOSS) issued Notice No. 20260413-32 on 13 April 2026, reporting that it had observed significant price and/or volume movements in 55 listed equity scrips. The Exchange wrote to all these companies seeking clarifications to ensure trading members and investors have access to the latest relevant information. Replies have been received from 36 companies; 19 companies are yet to respond.

## Key Points

- BSE identified 55 equity scrips with unusual price/volume activity warranting clarification.
- 36 companies have submitted replies, which are attached to the notice (Replies1 through Replies5).
- 19 companies have not yet responded and are under pending status.
- The notice is issued under the Equity segment by the DOSS department.
- Signed by Sanjay Jain (Deputy Vice President, Surveillance) and Shreya Ghose (Manager, Surveillance).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this notice. It is an informational disclosure under BSE's ongoing market surveillance framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- The 19 companies listed under "Reply is waited" are expected to submit their clarifications to BSE promptly.
- Trading members and investors should review the attached replies (Replies1–Replies5) for the 36 responding companies before making trading decisions in those scrips.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** 13 April 2026
- No explicit deadline is stated for pending replies, but regulatory norms typically require prompt response.

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects 55 stocks primarily in the SME and small-cap segments. The disclosure of company replies is intended to curb information asymmetry amid unusual trading activity. Investors holding or trading in these scrips should review the attached replies for material disclosures. The 19 companies still awaiting reply may face additional surveillance measures or trading restrictions if they do not respond in a timely manner.
