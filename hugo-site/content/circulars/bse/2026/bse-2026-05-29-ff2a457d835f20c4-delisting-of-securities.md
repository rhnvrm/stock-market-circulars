## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that the debt scrip codes of several companies, which have been under suspension, will be delisted with effect from 1st June 2026. The notice covers non-convertible debentures (NCDs) and other debt instruments across at least 10 companies.

## Key Points

- Delisting is effective from **1st June 2026**
- All scrips listed were previously suspended before this delisting action
- Covers debt segment securities (NCDs and bonds) only
- At least 17 scrip codes are confirmed delisted across multiple issuers (notice is partially truncated)
- Segment: Debt | Category: Corporate Actions | Department: Listing Operations

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework introduced. This is an operational action under existing BSE listing and delisting norms for debt securities that have been suspended.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members should update their systems to remove these scrip codes from the active debt segment with effect from 1st June 2026
- No further trading will be permitted in these scrips post-delisting
- Members holding positions or client holdings in these scrips should take appropriate action before the effective date

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** 29 May 2026
- **Delisting Effective Date:** 1 June 2026
