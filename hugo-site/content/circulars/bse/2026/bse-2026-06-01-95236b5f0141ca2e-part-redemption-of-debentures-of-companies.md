## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260601-13 informs trading members that multiple companies have fixed record dates for payment of interest and/or part redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) and bonds. Post the respective ex-dates, trading in these debentures will be conducted at reduced face values.

## Key Points

- Ten or more NCD series across multiple issuers are undergoing part redemption
- Kolte-Patil Developers Ltd has five NCD series with record/ex-date of 02 June 2026
- Grihum Housing Finance Limited has record/ex-date of 05 June 2026
- Inbrew Beverages Private Limited has record/ex-date of 08 June 2026
- Hari and Company Investments Madras Private Limited has record/ex-date of 09 June 2026
- AAVAS Financiers Limited, UGRO Capital Limited, and MONEYBOXX Finance Limited all have record/ex-date of 10 June 2026
- All settlements fall under the 2027 DR settlement cycle

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is a standard operational notice under existing BSE listing and trading rules governing debt securities with part redemption events.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the reduced face values effective from the respective ex-dates
- Trades in the listed debentures from ex-date onwards must reflect the new reduced face value
- Members should update their systems to reflect the revised face values for each affected scrip code

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a moderate operational impact on trading members and institutional investors holding these NCD series in the debt segment. Debenture holders will receive partial principal repayment on the respective record dates, and the face value of the instruments will be reduced accordingly for all subsequent trades. The multiple Kolte-Patil Developers NCD series (five tranches) is notable, indicating a significant scheduled amortization for that issuer. Inbrew Beverages carries the highest per-unit face value reduction (Rs. 7.26 lakh per debenture), suggesting a smaller-lot private placement instrument. Overall market impact is limited to participants in the debt segment.
