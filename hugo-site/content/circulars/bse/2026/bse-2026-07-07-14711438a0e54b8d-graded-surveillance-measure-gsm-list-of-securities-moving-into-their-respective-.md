## Summary

BSE has issued a notice under its Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework informing trading members that certain securities, listed in the attached annexure, will be moved to their respective higher GSM stages effective July 8, 2026. The GSM framework, first introduced via notice dated February 23, 2017 and subsequently amended, is designed to alert and protect investors from trading in securities exhibiting abnormal price/volume behavior not commensurate with fundamentals, by progressively restricting trading conditions as a security moves through GSM Stages I to IV.

## Key Points

- Securities specified in the Annexure_GSM attachment will move to higher GSM stages effective July 8, 2026.
- The action is taken under the GSM Framework originally published February 23, 2017, with subsequent amendments via notices dated March 3, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023.
- Surveillance actions escalate by stage:
  - Stage I: 100% margin and price band of 5% or lower.
  - Stage II: Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band and 50% Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) on trade value from buyers.
  - Stage III: Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band, trading permitted only once a week (Monday/first trading day), and 100% ASD from buyers.
  - Stage IV: Same as Stage III but with no upward price movement permitted.
- All affected securities will settle on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.
- Trading members are advised to take adequate precautions while dealing in the listed securities.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice is an operational update applying the existing GSM Framework (established 2017, amended through 2023) to a fresh list of securities identified in the annexure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin rates, price bands, ASD requirements, and trade-for-trade settlement conditions corresponding to each security's new GSM stage.
- Members must ensure no netting off occurs in settlement of these securities.
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com or referenced against the published GSM FAQ.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 8, 2026 — securities move into their respective higher GSM stages.
- Notice Date: July 7, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

Securities moving into higher GSM stages face substantially reduced liquidity due to trade-for-trade settlement, tighter price bands, higher margin/ASD requirements, and in later stages, restricted trading frequency (weekly) and no upward price movement. This directly affects shareholders and traders in the specific securities named in the annexure (not disclosed in the notice body), while having negligible impact on the broader market. Trading members must update their systems and risk controls to reflect the revised surveillance parameters before the effective date.
