## Summary

BSE has issued a circular detailing changes to the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework effective February 23, 2026. Nine new securities are being added to the framework, six securities are being moved to lower ASM stages, none are being elevated to higher stages, and nine securities are exiting the LT-ASM framework entirely.

## Key Points

- **9 securities** newly shortlisted under LT-ASM framework (Part A) effective February 23, 2026
- **Nil** securities moving to higher ASM stages (Part B)
- **6 securities** moving to lower ASM stages (Part C) effective February 23, 2026
- **Nil** securities shortlisted for Direct Stage IV LT-ASM (Part D)
- **9 securities** exiting the LT-ASM framework (Annexure II) effective February 23, 2026
- Vardhman Polytex Ltd (514175) inclusion is as per NSE data

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must apply the applicable LT-ASM trading restrictions (e.g., enhanced margins, trade-for-trade settlement) for newly added securities from February 23, 2026
- Members must update their systems to reflect stage changes for securities in Part C
- Trading restrictions applicable to the 9 exiting securities under LT-ASM must be lifted from February 23, 2026, subject to any other applicable surveillance framework
- Securities exiting LT-ASM may still be under other frameworks (Trade for Trade, GSM, ESM, IBC) where marked accordingly

## Impact Assessment

The newly added securities (Part A) will face increased surveillance with restrictions such as higher margins and potentially trade-for-trade settlement, which may reduce liquidity and increase trading costs for investors in these scrips. Securities moving to lower stages (Part C) will see a relaxation of surveillance intensity, potentially improving liquidity. The nine securities exiting the LT-ASM framework will return to normal trading conditions (unless under another surveillance framework), which could improve investor confidence and liquidity in those stocks. Rajratan Global Wire and Chennai Petroleum Corporation, being relatively well-known companies, may see notable market reactions upon exit from the framework.
