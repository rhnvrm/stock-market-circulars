## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that eight companies have fixed record dates for part redemption of their non-convertible debentures/bonds. Trading in these debentures will continue with a reduced face value effective from the respective ex-dates.

## Key Points

- Eight NCDs across seven companies are undergoing part redemption with ex-dates of 9 June and 11 June 2026.
- Trading in each debenture will be conducted at the reduced face value from the ex-date onwards.
- Settlements are under cycles 647/2026-2027 and 649/2026-2027.
- Companies span housing finance, NBFC, and green finance sectors.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine corporate action notice under BSE's listing compliance framework for debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the reduced face values and apply them from the respective ex-dates.
- All transactions in the listed debentures on and after the ex-date must reflect the revised face values as stated.

## Impact Assessment

This notice is of medium impact, affecting only holders and traders of the specific NCD scrips listed. Debt segment participants must update their systems with the revised face values effective from the ex-dates to ensure correct settlement. No equity market impact is expected. Debenture holders will receive partial principal repayment, reducing their outstanding exposure to each issuer accordingly.
