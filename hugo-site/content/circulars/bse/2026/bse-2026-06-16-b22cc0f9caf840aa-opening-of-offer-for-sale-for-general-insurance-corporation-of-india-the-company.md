## Summary

The President of India, acting through the Ministry of Finance, has exercised the Oversubscription Option in the Offer for Sale (OFS) of General Insurance Corporation of India (GIC Re). This notice (BSE Notice No. 20260616-16) is a continuation of Exchange Notice No. 20260615-40 dated June 15, 2026. The total offer size now stands at 87,720,000 equity shares, representing 5.00% of GIC Re's total paid-up equity share capital, offered through the designated OFS windows of BSE and NSE.

## Key Points

- The Seller (President of India / Ministry of Finance) has formally exercised the Oversubscription Option.
- Base Offer Size: 35,088,000 equity shares (2.00% of paid-up equity) – offered on T Day (June 16, 2026) for Non-Retail Investors only.
- Oversubscription Option exercised: additional 52,632,000 equity shares (3.00% of paid-up equity).
- Total Offer Size: 87,720,000 equity shares (5.00% of total paid-up equity share capital).
- Face value of each equity share: Rs. 5/-.
- 8,772,000 equity shares (10% of total offer) are reserved for Retail Investors on T+1 Day (June 17, 2026), subject to receipt of valid bids.
- Employee Offer: up to 20,000 equity shares available for eligible employees of GIC Re under the OFS guidelines.
- Eligible employees may bid up to Rs. 5,00,000; however, initial allocation will be considered only up to Rs. 2,00,000.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework introduced. This notice operates under the existing SEBI OFS guidelines and is an update to the previously announced OFS (Exchange Notice No. 20260615-40 dated June 15, 2026), confirming the exercise of the Oversubscription Option.

## Compliance Requirements

- Non-Retail Investors who placed bids on T Day (June 16, 2026) and have un-allotted bids may choose to carry forward to T+1 Day (June 17, 2026).
- Retail Investors must place bids on T+1 Day (June 17, 2026) through the designated OFS window on BSE/NSE.
- Eligible employees wishing to participate must bid within the employee reservation of up to 20,000 shares; bids are subject to the Rs. 2,00,000 first-instance allocation cap (up to Rs. 5,00,000 overall).
- Brokers and trading members must route OFS orders through the designated separate window on BSE and NSE.

## Important Dates

- **June 15, 2026**: Original OFS announcement (Exchange Notice No. 20260615-40).
- **June 16, 2026 (T Day)**: OFS window open for Non-Retail Investors only.
- **June 17, 2026 (T+1 Day)**: OFS window open for Retail Investors, eligible employees, and Non-Retail Investors carrying forward un-allotted bids.

## Impact Assessment

The exercise of the full Oversubscription Option is a significant government disinvestment event. The expanded sale of 5% of GIC Re's equity (87.72 million shares) increases supply in the market substantially compared to the base offer. Short-term selling pressure on the GIC Re stock (GICRE) is likely during and immediately after the OFS window. Retail investors get a dedicated window on June 17 with a 10% reservation, providing meaningful retail participation. The transaction is a direct reduction in government stake in GIC Re, which is in line with the broader disinvestment programme of the Ministry of Finance.
