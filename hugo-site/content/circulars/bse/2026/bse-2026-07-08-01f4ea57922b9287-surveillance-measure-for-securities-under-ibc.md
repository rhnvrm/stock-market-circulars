## Summary

BSE has issued an update to its Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework applicable to companies undergoing Insolvency Resolution Process (IRP) under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). Certain securities have newly met the criteria for inclusion under this framework effective July 09, 2026, while other securities are being removed from the framework on the same date. A consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework has also been provided for reference.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in Annexure I now satisfy the criteria for the IBC-related Additional Surveillance Measure, effective July 09, 2026.
- Securities listed in Annexure II are moving out of the IBC Framework, effective July 09, 2026.
- Annexure III provides the consolidated list of all securities currently under the Framework.
- Securities under this framework are reviewed periodically.
- This surveillance framework operates in conjunction with other surveillance measures already in force.
- Inclusion under the framework is purely a market surveillance measure and does not represent an adverse view of the concerned company/entity.
- Price bands for securities exiting the framework will revert to their pre-shortlisting levels, unless the scrip remains subject to another surveillance framework, in which case that framework's price band will apply.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice is a periodic update applying the existing Additional Surveillance Measure for IBC-related companies, as originally established via notices dated June 08, 2018, May 20, 2022, February 10, 2023, and September 05, 2025.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must note the updated list of securities under the IBC surveillance framework and apply relevant trading restrictions/price bands accordingly. Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **July 09, 2026** — Effective date for securities entering (Annexure I) and exiting (Annexure II) the IBC Surveillance Framework.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to the specific securities named in the annexures (not detailed in the notice body), which will face additional surveillance restrictions and price band adjustments. Securities exiting the framework will see their price bands reinstated to pre-shortlisting levels, potentially easing trading constraints. Overall market impact is low, as this is a routine, periodic compliance action rather than a broad policy change.
