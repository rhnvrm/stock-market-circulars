## Summary

BSE has announced the listing of 1,35,75,360 equity shares of GABION TECHNOLOGIES INDIA LIMITED. The shares are categorized into pre-lock-in securities (99,75,360 shares with varying lock-in periods from 1-3 years), anchor investor shares (10,22,400 shares with 1-3 month lock-in), and IPO shares (25,77,600 shares with no lock-in). All securities are in demat form.

## Key Points

- Total equity shares listed: 1,35,75,360
- Pre-lock-in shares: 99,75,360 with three different lock-in periods
- Anchor investor shares: 10,22,400 with short-term lock-in
- IPO shares: 25,77,600 with no lock-in restrictions
- All shares in demat form only
- Distinctive numbers range: 1 to 13,575,360

## Lock-in Details

### Pre-Lock-in Securities (99,75,360 shares)
- 27,15,072 shares: Lock-in from 02-01-2026 to 10-02-2029 (3 years)
- 36,29,394 shares: Lock-in from 02-01-2026 to 10-02-2028 (2 years)
- 36,30,894 shares: Lock-in from 02-01-2026 to 10-02-2027 (1 year)

### Anchor Investor Securities (10,22,400 shares)
- 5,10,400 shares: Lock-in from 10-01-2026 to 07-02-2026 (28 days)
- 5,12,000 shares: Lock-in from 10-01-2026 to 08-04-2026 (88 days)

### IPO Securities (25,77,600 shares)
- No lock-in restrictions apply

## Compliance Requirements

- Shares must remain locked-in as per specified periods
- Pre-lock-in shareholders cannot transfer shares until respective lock-in expiry dates
- Anchor investors must maintain holdings until their lock-in periods end
- All securities maintained in demat form only

## Important Dates

- Pre-lock-in start date: 02-01-2026
- Anchor lock-in start date: 10-01-2026
- First anchor unlock date: 07-02-2026
- Second anchor unlock date: 08-04-2026
- Pre-lock-in expiry dates: 10-02-2027, 10-02-2028, and 10-02-2029

## Impact Assessment

This listing enables GABION TECHNOLOGIES INDIA LIMITED shares to trade on BSE. The staggered lock-in structure prevents immediate selling pressure, with only 25,77,600 IPO shares (19% of total) freely tradable. The three-tier pre-lock-in system (1-3 years) and anchor investor restrictions (1-3 months) provide stability during initial trading. Investors should note the significant portion of shares under lock-in when assessing liquidity and trading volumes.
