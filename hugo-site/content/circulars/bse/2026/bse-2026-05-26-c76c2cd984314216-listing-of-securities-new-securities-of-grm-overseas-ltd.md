## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that new equity shares of GRM Overseas Ltd (Scrip Code: 531449, ISIN: INE192H01020) are listed and permitted to trade on the Exchange effective Wednesday, May 27, 2026. The new securities comprise 77,18,000 shares issued on a preferential basis via warrant conversion and 1,54,36,000 bonus shares allotted in a 2:1 ratio on those shares.

## Key Points

- **Preferential Allotment (Warrant Conversion):** 77,18,000 equity shares of Rs. 2/- each issued at a premium of Rs. 148/- (issue price Rs. 150/-) to Promoters and Non-Promoters; distinctive numbers 184056001–191774000.
- **Bonus Shares:** 1,54,36,000 equity shares allotted as bonus in ratio 2:1 on the above preferential shares; distinctive numbers 191774001–207210000.
- **Total New Shares:** 2,31,54,000 equity shares (distinctive numbers 184056001–207210000).
- All new shares rank pari-passu with existing equity shares.
- Date of Allotment: 06 February 2026.
- Trading permitted from: 27 May 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notification under BSE's Listing Operations framework following completion of a preferential allotment and bonus issue.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are informed to update their systems to reflect the new securities available for trading from May 27, 2026. No specific compliance action is required from trading members beyond standard trade processing.

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment:** 06 February 2026
- **Trading Commencement:** 27 May 2026
- **Lock-in Expiry (36,24,000 shares – Dist. Nos. 184056001–187680000):** 30 November 2027
- **Lock-in Expiry (1,95,30,000 shares – Dist. Nos. 187680001–207210000):** 30 November 2026

## Impact Assessment

The listing adds 2,31,54,000 new equity shares to GRM Overseas Ltd's tradeable float. Of these, 1,95,30,000 shares remain under lock-in until November 2026, and 36,24,000 shares until November 2027, limiting immediate dilution impact on the open market. The preferential issue at Rs. 150/- per share (Rs. 2 face value + Rs. 148 premium) via warrant conversion reflects a pre-agreed pricing event. The 2:1 bonus allotment on those shares effectively lowers the average cost for allottees. Existing shareholders should note the increase in total share capital.
