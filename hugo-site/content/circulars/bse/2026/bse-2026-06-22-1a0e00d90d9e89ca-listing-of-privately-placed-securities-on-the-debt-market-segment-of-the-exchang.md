## Summary

BSE Notice 20260622-21 informs trading members that six new privately placed debt securities have been listed and admitted to trading on the BSE Debt Segment effective June 22, 2026. The securities span credit ratings from BBB- to AAA and maturities ranging from June 2027 to June 2031.

## Key Points

- Six fresh listings admitted to BSE Debt Segment on private placement basis effective June 22, 2026
- All securities trade exclusively in dematerialised form; tick size is 1 paise for all
- Face value and paid-up value of Rs. 1,00,000 per unit for all six instruments
- Credit ratings range from IVR/IND BBB- to CARE/CRISIL/ICRA AAA
- Further details available via Placement Memoranda at https://www.bseindia.com/markets/debt/memorandum_data.aspx
- Contact: BSE Debt Department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915 for clarifications

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notice under existing BSE Debt Segment admission framework for privately placed instruments.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form using the respective ISIN numbers
- All transactions subject to standard BSE Debt Segment tick size of 1 paise
- Put/Call options, if any, are governed by the respective Disclosure Documents

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice with minimal market-wide impact. The six instruments collectively represent a diverse set of issuers across NBFCs, housing finance companies, and a REIT (Knowledge Realty Trust). High-rated instruments from Mahindra Rural Housing Finance (AAA) and Knowledge Realty Trust (AAA) may attract institutional interest. Lower-rated instruments from Achiievers Finance (BBB-) and Monedo Financial Services (BBB-) carry higher credit risk. Aadhar Housing Finance's floating-rate instrument (3-month T-Bill + spread) offers interest rate risk mitigation. All instruments are privately placed and available only to eligible investors in demat form on the BSE Debt Segment.
