## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260605-48 (dated June 5, 2026) advises all trading members that multiple companies have fixed record dates for interest payment or redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) and Bonds. Trading members are instructed not to deal in these debentures/bonds around the respective ex-dates and record dates.

## Key Points

- Trading in the listed NCDs/Bonds of the specified companies is suspended around their respective record dates.
- Record dates fall between June 10–14, 2026, with corresponding ex-dates.
- The suspension applies to instruments in the Debt segment under Listing Operations.
- Settlement is under DR 648/2026-2027, 649/2026-2027, and 650/2026-2027 depending on the record date.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard periodic notice issued by BSE under its Listing Operations framework for the Debt segment to facilitate smooth interest payment and redemption processing.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must refrain from dealing in the debentures/bonds of the listed companies from their respective ex-dates.
- Members should update their systems to block trading in the specified ISIN codes around the notified ex-dates.

## Impact Assessment

This is a standard operational notice with medium impact confined to trading members active in the BSE Debt segment. Holders of the listed NCD/Bond instruments will have their interest or redemption proceeds processed on the respective record dates. Secondary market trading in these instruments will be temporarily suspended, which is routine and expected by market participants. No broader market disruption is anticipated.
