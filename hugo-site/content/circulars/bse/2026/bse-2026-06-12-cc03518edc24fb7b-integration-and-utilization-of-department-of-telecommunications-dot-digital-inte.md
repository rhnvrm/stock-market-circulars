## Summary

BSE has issued a notice forwarding a SEBI communication regarding the integration and utilization of the Department of Telecommunications' (DoT) Digital Intelligence Platform (DIP). Trading members are advised to onboard and actively use DIP for intelligence sharing to strengthen cyber fraud prevention.

## Key Points

- BSE is passing on SEBI's directive to trading members regarding DoT's Digital Intelligence Platform (DIP).
- Trading members must onboard and actively utilize DIP for intelligence sharing related to cyber fraud.
- The platform is intended to share Financial Fraud Risk Indicator (FRI) intelligence.
- Contact details for support are provided for both technical/API issues and administrative/onboarding queries.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular does not introduce new regulations but implements an inter-ministerial directive from SEBI requiring trading members to integrate with the DoT's DIP infrastructure as part of broader cyber fraud prevention efforts.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are required to onboard onto the DoT Digital Intelligence Platform (DIP).
- Members must actively utilize DIP for intelligence sharing on cyber fraud.
- For technical or API-related integration issues, members should contact: diu@cdot.in and diu.ne1@cdot.in.
- For administrative or onboarding queries, members should contact: dirdiu-dot@gov.in and adg1.diu-dot@gov.in.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 12 June 2026
- No specific deadline for onboarding is mentioned; members are advised to act promptly.

## Impact Assessment

This notice primarily impacts trading members (brokers) who are required to integrate with the government's DIP system. The operational burden involves API integration and onboarding to the new platform. There is no direct market or trading impact, but non-compliance could attract regulatory scrutiny. The initiative is part of a broader government effort to curb cyber and financial fraud in the securities market.
