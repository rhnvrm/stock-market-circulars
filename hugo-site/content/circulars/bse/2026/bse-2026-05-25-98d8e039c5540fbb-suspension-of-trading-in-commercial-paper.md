## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (20260525-57) informing trading members that 15 companies have fixed record dates for redemption of their Commercial Paper instruments. Trading members are advised not to deal in these commercial papers effective their respective ex-dates, ranging from 29 May 2026 to 4 June 2026.

## Key Points

- 15 commercial paper instruments across 14 companies are subject to trading suspension ahead of redemption
- Record dates and ex-dates are the same for all listed instruments
- Instruments span redemption dates of 29 May, 2 June, 3 June, and 4 June 2026
- Settlement references are in the DR 640–644/2026-2027 series
- Notice issued by Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President – Listing Compliance and Operations

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine operational notice under existing BSE debt segment trading rules requiring suspension of commercial paper trading prior to redemption record dates.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must cease all dealings in the listed commercial paper instruments effective their respective ex-dates
- Members should update their systems to block trades in the affected ISINs from the specified ex-dates

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market operational notice with limited broader market impact. Trading members active in BSE's debt segment must ensure they do not execute trades in these 15 commercial paper instruments from their respective ex-dates. The majority of redemptions (11 instruments) fall on 4 June 2026. Notable issuers include Tata Steel Limited, KEC International, Bajaj Housing Finance, IndoStar Capital Finance, Kotak Mahindra Prime, and Navi Finserv. No equity trading is affected.
