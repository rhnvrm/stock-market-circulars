## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Equity Derivatives segment on Saturday, June 13, 2026. The session will run from both the Primary Site (PR) and Disaster Recovery Site (DR), giving trading members an opportunity to test their trading applications and ETI API integrations under various market conditions.

## Key Points

- Mock trading is scheduled for Saturday, June 13, 2026, for the Equity Derivatives segment.
- Sessions will be conducted from both Primary Site (PR) and Disaster Recovery Site (DR).
- Trading members using third-party empaneled vendor platforms or in-house systems via ETI APIs are eligible to participate.
- No new BOLT TWS Release will accompany this mock session.
- Trades from mock trading carry no margin obligation, pay-in, or pay-out obligations, and create no rights or liabilities.
- Members are requested to submit feedback by 5:00 pm on the day of the session.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an operational notice for a scheduled mock trading event.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are encouraged (not mandated) to participate actively in the mock trading sessions.
- Feedback should be submitted to BSE Tech Support (022-22728053 / bse.tech@bseindia.com) or BSE Help Desk (022-45720400/600 & 022-69158500 / bsehelp@bseindia.com) by 5:00 pm on June 13, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **June 13, 2026 (Saturday):** Mock trading session for Equity Derivatives segment.
  - Log-in: 09:15 am – 10:00 am
  - Pre-Open Order Entry (PR): 10:00 am – 10:07/08 am
  - Pre-Open Order Matching (PR): 10:08 am – 10:12 am
  - Buffer Period (PR): 10:12 am – 10:15 am
  - Continuous Trading (PR): 10:15 am – 01:00 pm
  - Switchover to DR: 01:00 pm – 01:45 pm
  - Pre-Open Order Entry (DR): 01:45 pm – 01:52/53 pm
  - Pre-Open Order Matching (DR): 01:53 pm – 01:57 pm
  - Buffer Period (DR): 01:57 pm – 02:00 pm
  - Continuous Trading (DR): 02:00 pm – 03:45 pm
  - Trade Modification: until 04:00 pm
- **Feedback deadline:** 5:00 pm on June 13, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market impact as mock trading sessions are purely for testing and familiarization. No actual trades, obligations, or liabilities are created. The session is primarily relevant to trading members who wish to validate their ETI API integrations, third-party platforms, or in-house systems against scenarios such as risk-reduction mode and trading halts. Participation is voluntary.
