## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260527-14) informing trading members that five companies have fixed record dates for the redemption of their Commercial Paper instruments. Trading members are advised not to deal in these commercial paper instruments effective from their respective ex-dates.

## Key Points

- Five commercial paper instruments across five issuers are suspended from trading ahead of their redemption record dates
- Suspension applies to debt segment commercial paper instruments only, not equity shares
- Record dates fall between 29 May 2026 and 4 June 2026
- DSP Finance Private Limited has two separate commercial paper instruments being suspended
- Issued by the Listing Operations department under Corporate Actions category

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational notice under existing BSE procedures for commercial paper redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must cease dealing in the listed commercial paper instruments on and after their respective ex-dates
- Members should update their systems to reflect the trading suspension for the specified scrip codes and ISINs

## Impact Assessment

This notice has minimal market impact as it pertains only to short-term commercial paper instruments nearing maturity redemption. The suspension is a routine procedural step to prevent trading in instruments that are about to be redeemed. Investors holding these instruments will receive redemption proceeds on the respective settlement dates (Settlement DR series 640/2026-2027 and 643-644/2026-2027). No impact on equity trading in the underlying companies.
