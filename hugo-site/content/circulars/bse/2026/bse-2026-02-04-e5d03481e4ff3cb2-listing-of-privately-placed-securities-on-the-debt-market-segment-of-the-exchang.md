## Summary

BSE has listed commercial papers issued on a private placement basis by five companies on its debt market segment, effective February 4, 2026. The securities include CPs from Bajaj Finance Limited (14,000 units), Sharekhan Limited (10,000 units), Nuvama Wealth Finance Limited (4,000 units across two ISINs), Aditya Birla Housing Finance Limited (8,000 units), and Mahindra Lifespace Developers Limited. All securities will trade in dematerialized form with a standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof.

## Key Points

- Five companies have listed commercial papers totaling multiple tranches on BSE Debt Segment
- All securities trade only in dematerialized form under specified ISIN numbers
- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- All listed CPs carry CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+ credit ratings
- Interest rates range from 7.36% to 8.85% per annum
- Redemption periods vary from 1 month to 1 year
- Allotment date: February 3, 2026 for all listed securities

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market-wide impact as it pertains to routine debt segment listings. Impact is limited to:

- Debt market participants and institutional investors in commercial papers
- Trading members dealing in debt securities
- Investors seeking short-term debt instruments with high credit ratings
- No impact on equity markets or broader trading operations

The listed commercial papers provide additional short-term investment options for debt investors with maturity periods ranging from 1 month to 1 year, all carrying top-tier credit ratings (A1+).
