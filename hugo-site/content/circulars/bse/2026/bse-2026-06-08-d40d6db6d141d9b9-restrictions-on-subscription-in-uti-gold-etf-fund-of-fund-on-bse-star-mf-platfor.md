## Summary

BSE has notified members of subscription restrictions on UTI Gold ETF Fund of Fund on the BSE StAR MF Platform, effective June 8, 2026. Fresh and additional purchases via lump sum and switch-in transactions are capped at ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month at the first holder level, as directed by UTI Mutual Fund.

## Key Points

- Subscription cap of ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month applies to fresh/additional purchases via lump sum mode and switch-in transactions
- Restriction applies to transactions received after the cut-off time of 3:00 PM on June 8, 2026
- Redemption, switch-out, and STP-out transactions are permitted without any amount restriction
- Subscription orders above ₹10,00,000 triggered to RTA on or after 3:00 PM on June 8, 2026 will be rejected and refunded to the investor after funds are received from AMC/RTA
- The Notice-cum-Addendum issued by UTI Mutual Fund is attached for member reference

## Regulatory Changes

UTI Mutual Fund has issued a Notice-cum-Addendum introducing a monthly subscription limit of ₹10,00,000 per PAN (at first holder level) for UTI Gold ETF Fund of Fund. BSE has implemented these changes on the StAR MF Platform accordingly.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure that fresh/additional purchase orders and switch-in orders for UTI Gold ETF Fund of Fund do not exceed ₹10,00,000 per PAN per calendar month
- Orders breaching this limit submitted after 3:00 PM on June 8, 2026 will be rejected at the RTA level and refunded
- No restrictions apply to redemption, switch-out, or STP-out transactions

## Important Dates

- **June 8, 2026**: Effective date of the subscription restriction
- **3:00 PM, June 8, 2026**: Cut-off time after which the ₹10,00,000 limit applies to incoming transactions

## Impact Assessment

This restriction primarily affects investors attempting large lump sum investments or switch-ins into UTI Gold ETF Fund of Fund exceeding ₹10 lakh per month. The cap is relatively high, so retail investors making modest periodic investments are unlikely to be impacted. High-net-worth individuals and institutional investors using the BSE StAR MF platform for significant gold fund allocations will need to plan transactions accordingly. Redemption and exit transactions remain unrestricted, limiting any liquidity concern for existing investors.
