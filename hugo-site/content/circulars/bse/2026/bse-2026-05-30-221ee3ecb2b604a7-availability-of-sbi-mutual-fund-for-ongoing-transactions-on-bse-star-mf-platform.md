## Summary

BSE has notified members that SBI Mutual Fund will be reopened for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions, including SIP, STP, and SWP facilities, on the BSE StAR MF Platform with effect from June 1, 2026. This follows Notice No. 20260513-30 dated May 13, 2026.

## Key Points

- SBI Mutual Fund reopens for ongoing transactions on BSE StAR MF from June 1, 2026
- Both subscription and redemption facilities restored, along with SIP, STP, and SWP
- Two debt index fund schemes are covered under this notice
- SIP investment amount range and minimum instalment requirements specified by SBI AMC
- Monthly SIP of Rs. 500–999 requires a minimum of 12 instalments; Rs. 1,000 and above requires a minimum of 6 instalments

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This notice reinstates transaction facilities for SBI Mutual Fund schemes on the BSE StAR MF Platform following a prior suspension referenced in Notice No. 20260513-30.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure SIP/XSIP investment amounts and minimum instalment requirements as communicated by SBI AMC are applied for the two schemes
- Monthly SIP of Rs. 500 to Rs. 999: minimum 12 instalments
- Monthly SIP of Rs. 1,000 and above: minimum 6 instalments

## Important Dates

- **May 13, 2026**: Original notice (20260513-30) regarding SBI Mutual Fund suspension issued
- **May 30, 2026**: This notice issued
- **June 1, 2026**: SBI Mutual Fund reopened for ongoing transactions on BSE StAR MF Platform

## Impact Assessment

Limited operational impact, confined to distributors and investors transacting in two SBI debt index fund schemes via the BSE StAR MF Platform. The reinstatement of SIP, STP, SWP, subscription, and redemption facilities restores normal operations. No broader market or regulatory implications expected. The two schemes—SBI CRISIL-IBX 10:90 GILT+SDL INDEX - DEC 2029 INDEX FUND and SBI NIFTY G-SEC JUL 2031 INDEX FUND—are niche debt index products targeting specific maturity buckets in government securities.
