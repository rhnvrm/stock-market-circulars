## Summary

BSE has announced Demat Auction Settlement No. 832 dated March 5, 2026. This circular lists approximately 80 securities across various companies that are subject to a demat auction. These auctions are conducted by BSE to resolve instances of short delivery or non-delivery of shares in the cash segment, ensuring settlement obligations are met.

## Key Points

- Settlement No. 832 was announced on March 5, 2026
- Approximately 80 scrips are listed across a wide range of sectors and market capitalizations
- Quantities per scrip range from as low as 1 share (e.g., JTL Industries, Kotak Mahindra Bank) to as high as 51,001 shares (Leading Leases)
- Notable high-quantity scrips include: Leading Leases (51,001), Vinny Overseas (10,100), Bharat Coking Coal (4,189), Reliance Industries (3,437), APM Industries (2,710)
- Covers large-cap and small-cap stocks including Reliance Industries, Infosys, HDFC Bank, Kotak Mahindra Bank, Nestle India, and Bajaj Hindustan

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is a standard demat auction settlement circular published in accordance with existing BSE settlement procedures for handling short deliveries in the cash market segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants who have short delivered the listed securities are subject to auction settlement
- Buyers affected by non-delivery will receive settlement through the auction process
- Members must comply with BSE's standard demat auction procedures for Settlement No. 832

## Important Dates

- **Auction Date:** March 5, 2026 (Settlement No. 832)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with moderate market impact. The auction covers a broad cross-section of equities, including blue-chip stocks such as Reliance Industries (3,437 shares), HDFC Bank, Infosys, and Kotak Mahindra Bank. Large-quantity auctions in stocks like Leading Leases (51,001 shares) and Vinny Overseas (10,100 shares) may cause temporary price pressure in those scrips. Overall, this is standard settlement infrastructure activity with localized impact on affected scrips.
