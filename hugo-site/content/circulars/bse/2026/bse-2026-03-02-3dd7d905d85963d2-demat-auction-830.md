## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction list for Settlement No. 830 dated 02/03/2026. A total of 90 scrips are listed with their respective quantities to be auctioned due to failure of delivery by sellers in the normal market segment. Buyers whose shares remain undelivered will receive shares through this auction process or be closed out at prevailing rates.

## Key Points

- Settlement No. 830 demat auction conducted on 02/03/2026
- 90 scrips listed across diverse sectors including banking, pharma, energy, IT, and FMCG
- Quantities range from 1 share (multiple scrips) to 8,425 shares (VODAFONE IDEA)
- Large-volume auction lots: VODAFONE IDEA (8,425), MAHINDRA & MAHINDRA (4,496), ARITAS VINYL (3,000), FUSION FINANCE (2,500), PARLE INDUSTRIES (1,771), CHENNAI PETROLEUM (1,450), TGIF AGRIBUSINESS (1,200)
- Covers both large-cap stocks (SBI, HCL Technologies, DRREDDY, Federal Bank) and SME/small-cap scrips

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine settlement auction notice under existing BSE settlement and delivery default procedures.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members with delivery shortfalls must participate in or be subject to the demat auction process
- Buying brokers whose clients have not received shares will receive delivery through auction or monetary close-out compensation
- Members must ensure timely settlement to avoid auction-related penalties and close-out charges

## Important Dates

- Auction Date: 02/03/2026
- Settlement No.: 830

## Impact Assessment

This is a standard settlement-cycle auction notice with no systemic risk implications. The presence of well-known large-caps such as MAHINDRA & MAHINDRA, STATE BANK OF INDIA, HCL TECHNOLOGIES, FEDERAL BANK, and DR. REDDY'S LABORATORIES with relatively small quantities suggests isolated delivery failures rather than broad market stress. The larger quantities in VODAFONE IDEA (8,425) and FUSION FINANCE (2,500) may reflect continued liquidity or settlement pressures in those counters. Affected buyers will either receive shares via auction or cash close-out at the highest price recorded between the trading day and auction day.
