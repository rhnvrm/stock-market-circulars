## Summary

BSE has issued an update to its Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, referencing a series of earlier notices dating back to June 2023. Securities listed in Annexure I have met the criteria for inclusion under ESM and will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open and new positions starting July 10, 2026. Certain securities already under ESM will move to a lower stage (Stage I), while others will move to the higher Stage II with tighter trading restrictions. A separate list of securities exiting the ESM framework is also provided, along with a consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework.

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I have satisfied ESM inclusion criteria and will attract minimum 100% margin from July 10, 2026.
- Some existing ESM securities will be moved down to Stage I of the framework effective July 9, 2026.
- Securities newly qualifying under ESM will shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment effective July 10, 2026.
- Securities moving to Stage II will be placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction, effective July 9, 2026.
- Securities exiting the ESM framework (Annexure II) will have their price bands reinstated to pre-ESM levels, unless subject to another surveillance measure.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under ESM is provided in Annexure III.
- Placement under ESM is purely a market surveillance measure and should not be viewed as an adverse action against the company.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice continues the ESM framework established via prior BSE notices (June 2023 through July 2025), applying periodic review-based reclassification of securities across ESM stages based on updated surveillance criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must ensure minimum 100% margin collection on open and new positions in the newly added ESM securities from July 10, 2026, and must recognize the shift of qualifying securities from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade segment. Members should refer to Annexures I, II, and III for the specific list of affected securities and direct clarifications to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- July 9, 2026: Securities move to lower Stage I; Stage II securities become subject to 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction; securities exit ESM per Annexure II.
- July 10, 2026: 100% margin applies on all open positions (as of July 9, 2026) and new positions in newly qualifying ESM securities; affected securities shift to Trade-for-Trade segment.

## Impact Assessment

The measure significantly increases trading costs and liquidity constraints for affected securities due to the 100% margin requirement and shift to Trade-for-Trade settlement, likely reducing speculative trading activity. Securities moving to Stage II face additional restrictions via tighter price bands and periodic call auctions, further limiting price discovery and liquidity. Conversely, securities exiting ESM or moving to a lower stage will see eased trading conditions and reinstated price bands, which may improve liquidity for those names.
