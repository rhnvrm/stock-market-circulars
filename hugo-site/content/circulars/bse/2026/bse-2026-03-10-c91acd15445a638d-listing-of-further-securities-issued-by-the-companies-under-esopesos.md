## Summary

BSE has listed and admitted for trading the further new securities issued by 9 companies under Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP/ESOS). These securities are effective for trading on the Exchange from **Wednesday, March 11, 2026**. None of the newly listed shares are subject to any lock-in period.

## Key Points

- 9 companies have listed new ESOP/ESOS shares effective March 11, 2026
- Total allotments range from 9,560,386 shares (SBFC Finance) to 9,560 shares (SBFC Finance ESOP) down to 9,850 shares (CreditAccess Grameen)
- No lock-in period applies to any of the listed securities
- Notice issued by Marian Dsouza (AVP) and Bhakti Wankhede (Deputy Manager) on behalf of BSE Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice confirming the listing of new securities issued pursuant to existing ESOP/ESOS schemes of the respective companies.

## Compliance Requirements

- All market participants are required to take note of the newly listed securities and their distribution numbers
- Trading members should update their systems to reflect the availability of these new shares for trading from March 11, 2026
- No lock-in restrictions apply; shares are freely tradable from the effective date

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** March 10, 2026
- **Effective Trading Date:** Wednesday, March 11, 2026
- **Lock-in Expiry:** Not applicable (NA for all securities)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing notice with moderate market impact. The most significant allotment is SBFC Finance Limited with 9,560,386 shares, which represents a notable addition to its floating stock and could have a modest dilutive effect. Medi Assist (151,685 shares), Sai Life Sciences (139,007 shares), and Swiss Military Consumer Goods (225,500 shares) also see meaningful additions. The remaining companies — Amber Enterprises, Ather Energy, CreditAccess Grameen, Gopal Snacks, and IndusInd Bank — have smaller allotments relative to their existing share capital, making the dilution impact minimal. Since no shares are under lock-in, all newly listed securities are immediately available for trading.
