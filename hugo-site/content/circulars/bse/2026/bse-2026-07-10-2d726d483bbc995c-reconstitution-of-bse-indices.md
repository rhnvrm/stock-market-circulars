## Summary

BSE Index Services Private Limited has announced the results of its monthly reconstitution of BSE Indices. The changes involve stocks being dropped from the BSE IPO and BSE SME IPO indices. These reconstitution changes will take effect at the market open on Monday, July 20, 2026.

## Key Points

- The reconstitution pertains to the BSE IPO index and the BSE SME IPO index.
- Ten stocks are being dropped from the BSE IPO index: Oswal Pumps Limited, Arisinfra Solutions Limited, Ellenbarrie Industrial Gases Limited, Kalpataru Limited, HDB Financial Services Limited, Sambhv Steel Tubes Limited, Indogulf Cropsciences Limited, Crizac Limited, Travel Food Services Limited, and Smartworks Coworking Spaces Limited.
- Sixteen stocks are being dropped from the BSE SME IPO index: Aten Papers & Foam Limited, Abram Food Limited, AJC Jewel Manufacturers Limited, Icon Facilitators Limited, Supertech Ev Limited, Ace Alpha Tech Limited, Valencia India Limited, Neetu Yoshi Limited, Adcounty Media India Limited, Vandan Foods Limited, Marc Loire Fashions Limited, Cryogenic Ogs Limited, Meta Infotech Limited, Chemkart India Limited, Glen Industries Limited, and Asston Pharmaceuticals Limited.
- These actions will be reflected in that night's end-of-day corporate action file (*.SDE).
- Queries can be directed to bseindex@bseindia.com.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine index maintenance action rather than a regulatory change. It reflects the standard methodology under which newly listed IPO stocks are removed from the BSE IPO and BSE SME IPO indices after completing their eligibility period within these tracking indices.

## Compliance Requirements

No direct compliance action is required from listed companies. Index fund managers, ETF providers, and other market participants who track or replicate the BSE IPO or BSE SME IPO indices will need to rebalance their portfolios to reflect the index changes effective July 20, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **July 10, 2026**: Reconstitution announcement date.
- **July 20, 2026**: Effective date of the reconstitution at market open.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited primarily to passive funds and index-tracking products benchmarked to the BSE IPO and BSE SME IPO indices, which will need to rebalance holdings around the July 20, 2026 effective date. There is no direct impact on trading, listing status, or compliance obligations for the affected companies, and the change does not affect BSE's flagship indices such as the Sensex.
