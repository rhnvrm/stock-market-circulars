## Summary

Amagi Media Labs Limited announced the allocation of 2,22,95,799 equity shares to anchor investors at ₹361 per share on January 12, 2026. The allocation was finalized by the Board of Directors in consultation with Book Running Lead Managers including Kotak Mahindra Capital, Citigroup Global Markets India, Goldman Sachs (India) Securities, IIFL Capital Services, and Avendus Capital. The IPO comprises a fresh issue of up to ₹8,160 million and an offer for sale of 26,942,343 equity shares by selling shareholders.

## Key Points

- Total anchor allocation: 2,22,95,799 equity shares at ₹361 per share
- Face value: ₹5 per equity share
- Share premium: ₹356 per equity share
- Total anchor book size: ₹804.67 crores approximately
- Fresh issue component: ₹8,160 million
- Offer for sale: 26,942,343 equity shares
- 22 anchor investors allocated shares including major mutual funds and insurance companies
- Largest allocation: ICICI Prudential Technology Fund (9.91% of anchor portion)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a standard anchor investor allocation disclosure as per SEBI IPO guidelines.

## Compliance Requirements

- Disclosure of anchor investor allocation details to stock exchanges (BSE and NSE)
- Compliance with SEBI ICDR Regulations for IPO anchor investor process
- Mandatory reporting of allocation details including investor names, shares allocated, and allocation price

## Important Dates

- January 12, 2026: Board meeting held and anchor allocation finalized
- January 12, 2026: Disclosure made to BSE and NSE

## Impact Assessment

The anchor investor allocation represents strong institutional interest in Amagi Media Labs Limited's IPO. The participation of prominent mutual funds including SBI, ICICI Prudential, Aditya Birla Sun Life, HDFC, Fidelity, and Motilal Oswal indicates confidence in the company's prospects. The anchor book comprises quality institutional investors across 22 entities, which typically signals positive market reception for the upcoming public issue. The allocation at ₹361 per share will serve as the price reference for the retail and institutional portions of the IPO.
