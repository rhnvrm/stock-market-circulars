## Summary

BSE has announced a mock trading session for the Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, May 30, 2026, operating from both the primary site (PR) and disaster recovery site (DR). The session is intended for trading members to test their trading applications and familiarize themselves with various functionalities under simulated market conditions.

## Key Points

- Mock trading covers the Commodity Derivatives segment only
- Sessions run from both primary site (PR) and disaster recovery site (DR)
- Third-party platform users and in-house ETI API developers are encouraged to participate
- Testable scenarios include risk-reduction mode, trading halt, and other exceptional market conditions
- Trades from mock sessions carry no margin obligations, pay-in, or pay-out obligations
- No legal rights or liabilities are created from mock trades
- Members must submit feedback by 4:00 pm on the same day

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an operational notice for a one-time mock trading session.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members using third-party or in-house trading systems via ETI APIs are requested to actively participate
- Members are requested to provide feedback on the mock trading session by 4:00 pm on May 30, 2026
- Contact BSE Tech Support (022-22728053 / bse.tech@bseindia.com) for technical queries
- Contact BSE Help Desk (022-45720400/600 & 022-69158500 / bsehelp@bseindia.com) for general queries

## Important Dates

- **Mock Trading Date:** Saturday, May 30, 2026
- **Log-in Window:** 09:15 am – 10:00 am
- **Continuous Trading (Primary Site):** 10:00 am – 12:00 pm
- **Switchover to Disaster Recovery Site:** 12:00 pm – 12:45 pm
- **Continuous Trading (DR Site):** 12:45 pm – 02:45 pm
- **Feedback Deadline:** 4:00 pm on May 30, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact. This session is purely for testing and system familiarization. Commodity derivatives trading members using third-party vendor platforms or in-house ETI API systems should treat this as an opportunity to validate system readiness, test failover to the disaster recovery site, and simulate exceptional conditions such as risk-reduction mode and trading halts. No actual financial obligations arise from participation.
