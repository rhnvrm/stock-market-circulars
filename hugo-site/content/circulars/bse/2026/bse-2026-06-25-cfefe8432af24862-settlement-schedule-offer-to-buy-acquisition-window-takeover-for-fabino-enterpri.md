## Summary

BSE (via Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd.) has published the settlement calendar for the Offer to Buy – Acquisition Window (Takeover) pertaining to Fabino Enterprises Limited. The bidding window opens on June 29, 2026 and closes on July 10, 2026, with obligations downloadable on July 16 and final settlement on July 17, 2026 (Settlement No. 2026077).

## Key Points

- Settlement Type: Takeover; Settlement No. 2026077
- Bid window: June 29, 2026 to July 10, 2026
- Obligation download date: July 16, 2026
- Settlement date: July 17, 2026
- Demat shares must be delivered exclusively via the Early Pay-in of Shares mechanism available on both CDSL and NSDL
- Market Type "(Takeover)" and Settlement No. 2026077 must be used in depository systems
- Members and Custodians are NOT required to upload any EPN file
- Members should refer to MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo file for client-wise Stamp Duty charges

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. Members and Custodians are directed to refer to earlier Notice No. 20200528-32 (dated May 28, 2020) regarding settlement as per Revised Guidelines of Mechanism for Acquisition of Shares through Stock Exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and Custodians must ensure early pay-in of securities is reflected in BSE/ICCL systems before prescribed timelines for bid acceptance/confirmation on the BSE OTB platform
- Early pay-in must be completed sufficiently in advance of the closure of the BSE OTB Bidding window (for member bids) and the Confirmation window (for Custodian confirmations)
- CDSL and NSDL early pay-in securities will be earmarked/blocked in client accounts under the requisite market type and settlement number

## Impact Assessment

This circular is operationally significant for members and custodians participating in the Fabino Enterprises Limited takeover acquisition window. It defines mandatory timelines and procedural requirements for early pay-in of demat shares. Failure to comply with prescribed early pay-in deadlines may result in bids not being accepted. The broader market impact is limited to participants directly involved in this specific acquisition offer.
