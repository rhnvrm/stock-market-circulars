## Summary

BSE has notified trading members of the non-competitive bidding facility for the auction of four GoI dated securities on June 12, 2026, as announced by RBI on June 8, 2026. Members may submit bids via the NCB-GSec module on the iBBS platform. Two securities are available for bidding: 6.36% GS 2031 and 7.71% GS 2066.

## Key Points

- Bidding is through the NCB-GSec module at https://ibbs.bseindia.com
- Bid collection opens June 9, 2026 from 10:00 am (24-hour availability)
- Member bid submission deadline: June 12, 2026 till 8:00 am
- Direct investor bid submission deadline: June 11, 2026 till 5:00 pm
- Auction date: June 12, 2026; Settlement date: June 15, 2026
- 6.36% GS 2031 (symbol: 636GS31J26) — Re-issue, issue size Rs. 21,000 Cr, min 100 units, min Rs. 10,000
- 7.71% GS 2066 (symbol: 771GS66J26) — Re-issue, issue size Rs. 11,000 Cr, min 100 units, min Rs. 10,000
- References Exchange circular 20180423-42 dated April 23, 2018 for the original facility launch

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine auction notification under the existing non-competitive bidding framework established by Exchange circular 20180423-42.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members wishing to participate must access the NCB-GSec module on iBBS
- Bids must be submitted within the stated collection windows
- For new user ID creation or password reset, contact Trading Operations at user.trdops@bseindia.com or 022-2272-8005/8215
- For bidding queries, contact trading.ops@bseindia.com or 022-2272-8376/5626/8226
- For obligation, pay-in, and collateral queries, contact ICCL Operations at Collaterals@icclindia.com / settlements@icclindia.com or 022-2272-8468/8704/8264

## Important Dates

| Event | Date/Time |

| Settlement Date | June 15, 2026 |

## Impact Assessment

Limited to debt segment trading members participating in GoI securities auctions. This is a standard periodic notification with no market-wide impact. Members active in the NCB-GSec space should note the bid submission deadlines to avoid missing participation windows. The re-issuance of both securities (6.36% GS 2031 and 7.71% GS 2066) is routine and does not signal any policy change.
