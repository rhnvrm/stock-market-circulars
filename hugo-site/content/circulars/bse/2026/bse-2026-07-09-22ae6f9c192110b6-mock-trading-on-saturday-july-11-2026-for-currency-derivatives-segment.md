## Summary

BSE has announced a mock trading session scheduled for Saturday, July 11, 2026, covering the Currency Derivatives and Cross Currency Derivatives segments. The session allows trading members using third-party trading platforms of empaneled vendors or in-house developed systems through ETI APIs to test their trading applications, including under exceptional market conditions. The exercise coincides with the upcoming release of BOLT Pro TWS version 13.20, details of which will be published via a separate circular.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Currency Derivatives and Cross Currency Derivatives segments on July 11, 2026.
- Open to trading members using third-party empaneled vendor platforms or in-house systems via ETI APIs.
- New BOLT Pro TWS version 13.20 will be released; details to follow in a separate circular.
- Trades from the mock session carry no margin, pay-in, or pay-out obligations and create no rights or liabilities.
- Members are requested to actively participate and submit feedback by 3:00 PM on the session day.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes; this is an operational notice for a scheduled testing exercise.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members using third-party or in-house trading systems are encouraged to participate in the mock trading session and submit feedback to BSE by 3:00 PM via the provided contact numbers or email. No mandatory compliance action is required.

## Important Dates

- **Log-in:** 09:15 AM to 10:00 AM, July 11, 2026
- **Continuous Trading (Currency Derivatives):** 10:00 AM to 02:00 PM
- **Continuous Trading (Cross Currency Derivatives):** 10:00 AM to 02:00 PM
- **Trade Modification (Currency):** Until 02:10 PM
- **Trade Modification (Cross Currency):** Until 02:10 PM
- **Feedback deadline:** 3:00 PM, July 11, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, low-impact operational notice aimed at helping trading members test system readiness ahead of the BOLT Pro TWS version 13.20 rollout. It does not affect listed securities, market pricing, or investor obligations, and has no financial or trading impact beyond the testing scope for participating members.
