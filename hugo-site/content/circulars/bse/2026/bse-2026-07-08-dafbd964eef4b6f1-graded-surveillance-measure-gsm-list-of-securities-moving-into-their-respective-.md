## Summary

BSE has issued a notice under its Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, informing trading members that certain securities (listed in the attached Annexure) will move into their respective higher GSM stages with effect from July 09, 2026. Each stage carries progressively stricter surveillance actions, including reduced price bands, higher margin requirements, trade-for-trade settlement, and restricted trading frequency.

## Key Points

- Securities specified in the Annexure_GSM attachment will be moved to higher GSM stages effective July 09, 2026.
- The action is taken under the GSM Framework published February 23, 2017, and referenced across multiple subsequent exchange notices (2017–2023).
- Surveillance actions vary by stage:
  - Stage I: 100% margin and price band of 5% or lower.
  - Stage II: Trade-for-trade with 5% or lower price band, plus 50% Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) from buyers.
  - Stage III: Trade-for-trade, weekly trading (only Monday/first trading day of the week), plus 100% ASD from buyers.
  - Stage IV: Same as Stage III, with no upward price movement permitted.
- All affected securities will settle on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.

## Regulatory Changes

No change to the GSM framework itself; this notice applies the existing framework's stage-migration mechanism to a new set of securities identified in the Annexure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin rates, price bands, ASD requirements, and trading frequency restrictions for each security based on its new GSM stage.
- Members must ensure trade-to-trade settlement is followed for the listed securities with no netting off.
- Queries can be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com or the published GSM FAQ page.

## Important Dates

- Effective date of stage migration: July 09, 2026.
- Notice date: July 08, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

Investors and trading members dealing in the affected securities (per the Annexure) face reduced liquidity, higher capital requirements (ASD deposits), and constrained trading windows in the higher GSM stages. This is a routine, recurring surveillance action rather than a market-wide event, but it has high impact for holders/traders of the specific securities affected.
