## Summary

The Indian Clearing Corporation Limited (ICCL) has published the client margin reporting schedule for the Currency Derivatives Segment for March 2026. The circular specifies trade dates, the last date by which margin reporting must be completed, and the corresponding penalty dates for non-compliance.

## Key Points

- Covers trade dates from 02-Mar-2026 to 30-Mar-2026
- Each trade date has a designated last date for margin reporting (typically 5–7 trading days after the trade date)
- Penalty dates follow one trading day after the last reporting date
- The schedule extends into early April 2026 for late-March trade dates
- Issued jointly by Manoj Panchal and Omkar Hatkar, Deputy Managers at ICCL

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is a routine monthly schedule circular consistent with existing margin reporting obligations under the Settlement/RMS framework for the Currency Derivatives Segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must complete client margin reporting for each trade date by the specified last date of margin reporting
- Failure to report by the deadline will attract penalties as per the penalty date listed
- Members should ensure their back-office and reporting systems are aligned with the published schedule

## Impact Assessment

This circular has moderate operational impact for members active in the Currency Derivatives Segment. Timely compliance is required to avoid penalties. The schedule accounts for trading holidays, with penalty dates pushed forward accordingly. Members should note that several end-of-March trade dates have reporting and penalty deadlines extending into April 2026, requiring advance planning.
