## Summary

BSE has issued a notice imposing Long Term Additional Surveillance Measures (LT-ASM) on select securities effective June 2026. The measures include 100% margins on open and new positions, lower price bands, movement of securities to higher LT-ASM stages, and transfer of certain securities to T/XT/MT/TS groups.

## Key Points

- 100% margins applicable from June 05, 2026 on all open positions as of June 04, 2026 and all new positions thereafter
- Securities moved to higher LT-ASM stages effective June 03, 2026 (Annexure I, Part B)
- Lower Price Band applicable for affected securities effective June 03, 2026
- Certain securities to continue under LT-ASM but transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group effective June 05, 2026
- Securities moving out of LT-ASM Framework effective June 03, 2026 listed in Annexure II
- Consolidated list of all securities under the LT-ASM Framework provided in Annexure III
- Shortlisting is based on XBRL submissions by listed companies and is purely for market surveillance purposes

## Regulatory Changes

This notice references the revised LT-ASM framework introduced via Exchange notice no. 20180321-46 (March 21, 2018) and subsequent revisions including notices dated October 27, 2018, December 04, 2020, August 09, 2024, and September 20, 2024. The current notice applies the framework to a new set of securities and updates the stage classifications for existing ones.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must maintain 100% margins on all open positions in affected securities as of June 04, 2026
- Members must comply with the lower price band restrictions effective June 03, 2026
- Trading in securities moved to T/XT/MT/TS groups must follow the applicable group trading rules from June 05, 2026
- Members may contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications

## Important Dates

- **June 03, 2026**: Lower Price Band applicable; securities moved to higher LT-ASM stages; securities moving out of LT-ASM Framework
- **June 04, 2026**: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margins
- **June 05, 2026**: 100% margins effective on all open and new positions; securities transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group

## Impact Assessment

The imposition of 100% margins significantly increases the cost of holding or creating positions in affected securities, effectively restricting speculative activity and reducing liquidity. The lower price band further limits downside trading. Securities transferred to trade-for-trade groups (T/XT/MT/TS) face settlement on a gross basis, eliminating netting benefits. These measures together can materially reduce trading volumes in affected stocks. The framework is periodically reviewed and does not constitute an adverse regulatory action against the companies themselves.
