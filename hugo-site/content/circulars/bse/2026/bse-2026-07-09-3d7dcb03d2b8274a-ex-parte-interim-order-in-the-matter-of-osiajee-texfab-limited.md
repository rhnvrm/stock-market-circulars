## Summary

SEBI, vide order no. WTM/KM/IVD-1/ID17/32480/2026-27 dated July 09, 2026, has issued an ex-parte interim order restraining 19 noticees from buying, selling or dealing in securities of Osiajee Texfab Limited, either directly or indirectly, until further orders. BSE has notified all trading members of this order and directed them to ensure compliance.

## Key Points

- Noticees No. 1-19 are restrained from buying, selling or dealing in securities of Osiajee Texfab Limited, in any manner, directly or indirectly, until further orders.
- Noticees are permitted to settle pay-in and pay-out obligations for transactions that took place before close of trading on the date of the order.
- Depositories are allowed to debit/credit accounts solely for the purpose of settling such pre-existing transactions.
- Directions against Noticee No. 13 apply only in its proprietary capacity.
- The order is effective from July 09, 2026, the date of issuance.
- A copy of the SEBI order is available on the SEBI website (www.sebi.gov.in) and attached to the BSE notice.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes; this is an enforcement action under SEBI's existing investigative powers against specific noticees connected to Osiajee Texfab Limited.

## Compliance Requirements

All trading members of BSE must take note of the restraining order and ensure that none of the 19 restrained noticees are permitted to buy, sell, or otherwise deal in securities of Osiajee Texfab Limited, either directly or indirectly, until SEBI issues further orders. Members should facilitate only the settlement of pay-in/pay-out obligations for trades executed prior to the order date.

## Important Dates

- Order Date: July 09, 2026
- Effective Date: July 09, 2026 (immediate)
- Notice No.: 20260709-34, dated July 09, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The order directly restricts trading activity for the 19 named noticees in Osiajee Texfab Limited shares, which may reduce liquidity and trading volume in the scrip. Market-wide impact is limited given the order pertains to a specific, likely small-cap/illiquid security, but it underscores continued SEBI vigilance against potential market manipulation or unauthorized trading practices. Trading members must update compliance systems to flag and block orders from the restrained entities in this scrip.
