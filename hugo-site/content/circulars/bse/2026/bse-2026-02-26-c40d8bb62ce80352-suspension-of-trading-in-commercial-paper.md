## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260226-3 dated 26 February 2026 informs trading members that 18 companies have fixed record dates for redemption of their Commercial Papers. Trading members are advised not to deal in the commercial papers of these companies from their respective record dates onwards. The suspensions fall under two circulars: DR-833/2025-2026 (record date 05 March 2026) and DR-834/2025-2026 (record date 06 March 2026).

## Key Points

- 18 companies' commercial papers are subject to trading suspension ahead of redemption
- 10 companies have a record date of **05 March 2026** (no dealings from 05/03/2026)
- 8 companies have a record date of **06 March 2026** (no dealings from 06/03/2026)
- All suspensions are for the purpose of **Redemption of Commercial Paper**
- Governed under Debt segment circular references DR-833/2025-2026 and DR-834/2025-2026
- Issued by Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President – Listing Compliance And Operations

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice under existing BSE debt market regulations requiring suspension of trading in commercial paper instruments prior to their redemption record dates.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must **cease all dealings** in the listed commercial papers from their respective record dates
- No trades should be executed in these CP instruments on or after the specified 'No Dealings From' date
- Members must update their systems to block trading in the affected ISINs from the applicable dates

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a **medium impact** on debt market participants. CP holders and debt traders must ensure no transactions are executed in these instruments from the respective suspension dates. The affected companies span diversified sectors including financial services (Axis Finance, Kotak Mahindra Prime, ICICI Securities, Motilal Oswal, A.K. Capital, Capri Global, Fedbank, Nuvama, Mirae Asset), infrastructure (KEC International, L&T Metro Rail), manufacturing (Tata Steel, Dalmia Cement), real estate (Mahindra Lifespace), FMCG/retail (Titan, Reliance Retail), and conglomerates (Birla Group, 360 ONE). Equity trading in these companies remains unaffected; only the specific CP instruments listed are subject to suspension.
