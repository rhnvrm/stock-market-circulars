## Summary

BSE has issued a notice referencing RBI's July 13, 2026 press release announcing the sale of four Government of India (GoI) dated securities through auction on July 17, 2026. In continuation of Exchange circular no. 20180423-42 dated April 23, 2018, on the non-competitive bidding (NCB) facility, trading members are informed of the bidding parameters and schedule for participating via the NCB-GSec module of BSE's iBBS web-based system.

## Key Points

- Reference: RBI press release dated July 13, 2026 announcing auction of four dated securities on July 17, 2026.
- Bidding facility: Non-competitive bidding via NCB-GSec module on https://ibbs.bseindia.com.
- Three securities detailed with issue sizes: 6.03% GS 2029 (603GS29J26) – Rs. 11,000 Cr re-issue; 6.68% GS 2033 (668GS33J26) – Rs. 11,000 Cr re-issue; 7.24% GS 2055 (724GS55J26) – Rs. 10,000 Cr re-issue.
- Minimum subscription: 100 units and multiples of 2 units; Min/Max amount Rs. 10,000.
- Queries can be directed to BSE Trading Operations or ICCL for obligation and pay-in related matters.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; this is an operational notice implementing the existing NCB facility framework established under BSE circular 20180423-42 (April 23, 2018), in line with RBI's periodic G-Sec auction announcements.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must submit non-competitive bids through the NCB-GSec module within the specified bid collection windows.
- Direct investors must ensure bids are placed with members before the earlier cut-off (July 16, 2026, 5:00 pm).
- Members should ensure new user IDs or password resets, if needed, are coordinated with BSE Trading Operations in advance of the bidding window.

## Important Dates

- Bid Collection Start: July 14, 2026, 10:00 am onwards (24-hour availability)
- Bid Collection End (Direct Investor): July 16, 2026, till 5:00 pm
- Bid Collection End (Members): July 17, 2026, till 8:00 am
- Auction Date: July 17, 2026
- Settlement Date: July 20, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, procedural notice affecting only debt segment trading members and direct investors participating in the G-Sec non-competitive bidding process. It has no direct bearing on listed equities or broader market sentiment, and primarily serves to ensure operational readiness for the scheduled RBI auction.
