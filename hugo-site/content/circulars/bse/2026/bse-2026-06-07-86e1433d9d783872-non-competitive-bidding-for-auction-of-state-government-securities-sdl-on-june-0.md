## Summary

BSE has notified trading members of the non-competitive bidding (NCB) facility for the State Government Securities (SDL) auction scheduled on June 09, 2026. The auction is in response to a June 05, 2026 RBI press release announcing the Government of India's SDL securities auction. Members can participate through the NCB-GSec module of BSE's iBBS web-based system.

## Key Points

- Bidding is available via the NCB-GSec module at https://ibbs.bseindia.com
- 18 SDL securities across 10 states are available for non-competitive bidding
- Both new issues and reissuances are included in this auction
- Minimum subscription size is Rs. 100 per security with multiples of Rs. 100
- Minimum bid amount is Rs. 10,000 per security
- This continues the facility launched under Exchange circular no. 20191122-28 dated November 22, 2019

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This notice operates under the existing non-competitive bidding framework established by BSE circular 20191122-28 and the RBI press release dated June 05, 2026 announcing the SDL auction.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members wishing to participate must submit bids through the NCB-GSec module on BSE's iBBS platform
- Direct investors must submit bids by June 08, 2026 at 5:00 PM
- Members must submit bids by June 09, 2026 at 8:00 AM
- Members should adhere to the minimum and maximum bid amount limits specified per security

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice for debt segment participants. Trading members and direct investors interested in state government securities have an opportunity to participate in the primary auction for 18 SDL securities from Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Jammu & Kashmir, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Manipur, Mizoram, Tamil Nadu, and Uttar Pradesh. The aggregate maximum issuance across all securities is approximately Rs. 152 crore. The short bidding window (opening June 08 morning, closing June 09 at 8 AM for members) requires members to act promptly. Settlement will occur on T+1 basis on June 10, 2026.
