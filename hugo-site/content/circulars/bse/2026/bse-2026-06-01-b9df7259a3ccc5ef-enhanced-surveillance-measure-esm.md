## Summary

BSE has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework effective June 2026. Securities listed in Annexure I will attract a minimum 100% margin from June 3, 2026 on all open and new positions, and will be shifted from the Rolling Settlement segment to the Trade-for-Trade (TfT) segment. Additionally, certain securities are being moved to lower ESM stages or removed from the framework entirely.

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I will attract minimum **100% margin** w.e.f. June 3, 2026 on all open positions as on June 2, 2026 and new positions from June 3, 2026
- Affected securities will be shifted from Rolling Settlement to **Trade-for-Trade segment** w.e.f. June 3, 2026
- Securities moving to **Stage II** will be subject to Trade-for-Trade with a **2% price band** and **Periodic Call Auction** w.e.f. June 2, 2026
- Some securities are being moved to a **lower stage (Stage I)** under ESM framework w.e.f. June 2, 2026
- Securities exiting the ESM framework are listed in **Annexure II**, effective June 2, 2026
- A consolidated list of all securities under ESM is provided in **Annexure III**
- ESM shortlisting is purely for market surveillance purposes and should not be construed as adverse action against the company
- Price band for securities exiting ESM will revert to the pre-ESM applicable band, unless subject to another surveillance measure

## Regulatory Changes

This notice references and continues the ESM framework established under prior BSE notices:
- Notice 20230602-44 (June 2, 2023)
- Notice 20230718-46 (July 18, 2023)
- Notice 20240809-42 (August 9, 2024)
- Notice 20240920-63 (September 20, 2024)
- Notice 20241004-65 (October 4, 2024)
- Notice 20250725-61 (July 25, 2025)

Securities under ESM operate in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure **100% margin collection** on all open and new positions in securities listed in Annexure I from June 3, 2026
- Members must adhere to Trade-for-Trade settlement obligations for affected securities
- For Stage II securities, members must comply with the **2% price band** and participate through the **Periodic Call Auction** mechanism
- For clarifications, members may contact: **bse.surv@bseindia.com**

## Important Dates

| Event | Effective Date |
|---|---|
| Securities moving to lower Stage (Stage I) | June 2, 2026 |
| Stage II securities: Trade-for-Trade + 2% price band + Periodic Call Auction | June 2, 2026 |
| Securities exiting ESM framework | June 2, 2026 |
| 100% margin on open positions (as on June 2, 2026) | June 3, 2026 |
| Shift to Trade-for-Trade segment for newly included securities | June 3, 2026 |

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a **high market impact** for affected securities:

- **Liquidity reduction**: The shift from Rolling Settlement to Trade-for-Trade eliminates netting benefits and increases settlement risk, significantly reducing trading activity in affected stocks
- **Capital constraints**: 100% margin requirement means traders cannot use leverage, greatly restricting participation and liquidity
- **Price discovery limitations**: Stage II securities are further constrained by a 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism, reducing intraday price discovery
- **Positive for exits**: Securities moving out of ESM will see restrictions lifted and price bands restored, potentially improving liquidity
- **Investor caution**: Inclusion under ESM signals heightened surveillance due to abnormal price/volume patterns, warranting increased investor scrutiny
- The periodic review mechanism ensures that securities can exit the framework once they no longer meet the criteria for heightened surveillance
