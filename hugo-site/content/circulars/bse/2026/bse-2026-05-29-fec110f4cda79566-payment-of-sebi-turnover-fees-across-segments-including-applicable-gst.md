## Summary

BSE has notified trading members about the upcoming debit of SEBI turnover fees for May 2026 across all segments — Cash, Derivatives, Currency Derivatives, Commodity Derivatives, Interest Rate Derivatives, New Debt, Repo, and EGR. GST at 18% applies on turnover fees effective July 18, 2022, and the combined debit will occur on June 03, 2026.

## Key Points

- SEBI turnover fees plus 18% GST will be debited from trading members' settlement (valan) accounts or Exchange dues accounts on June 03, 2026 (3rd working day of June).
- Turnover data and debit amounts will be available in EXTRANET (current date > EQ > Transaction) on or before June 02, 2026.
- GST at 18% is applicable on the amount in column 26 of the TF2A file.
- Invoices for May 2026 will be available on EXTRANET on or before June 02, 2026, under the path: Periodic files > 2026 > ZIP file named `SEBIMAY2026.Member_Code`.
- SEBI turnover fee debits will continue to be made separately with appropriate narration.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This notice reaffirms the existing GST applicability of 18% on SEBI turnover fees as effective from July 18, 2022, per Exchange notice no. 20260430-2.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure sufficient funds are available in their settlement (valan) account or Exchange dues account before June 03, 2026.
- Members should download and review turnover fee files (TF2A through TF2J series) from EXTRANET by June 02, 2026.
- GST liability should be calculated based on column 26 of the TF2A file.

## Important Dates

- **June 02, 2026**: Turnover data, debit amounts, and invoices available on EXTRANET.
- **June 03, 2026**: SEBI turnover fees (including 18% GST) debited from settlement/Exchange dues accounts.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice affecting all BSE trading members across Cash, Derivatives, Currency Derivatives, Commodity Derivatives, Interest Rate Derivatives, New Debt, Repo, and EGR segments. Members must ensure adequate liquidity in their settlement accounts ahead of the June 03 debit to avoid shortfalls. The 18% GST component adds to the effective cost of turnover fees. No market-wide structural or pricing impact is anticipated.
