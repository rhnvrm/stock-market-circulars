## Summary

BSE has listed new commercial papers issued on a private placement basis by five companies on its debt market segment effective January 8, 2026. The listed securities include commercial papers from Tata Housing Development Company Limited, ICICI Securities Primary Dealership Limited, Tata Steel Limited (two issuances), Muthoottu Mini Financiers Limited, and Nuvama Wealth and Investment Limited. All securities will trade only in dematerialized form with a standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof.

## Key Points

- Five companies have listed commercial papers totaling 38,400 units (Rs. 1,920 crores aggregate)
- All securities trade only in demat form under respective ISIN numbers
- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples
- Tick size: 1 paise
- Market lot: 1 unit
- Interest rates range from 6% to 9.65% per annum
- Maturity periods range from 41 to 90 days
- All issuances carry A1+ credit ratings from various agencies

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form
- Trading must be in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Trading members requiring clarification may contact BSE debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date of Listing**: January 8, 2026
- **Tata Housing Development CP**: Allotment January 7, 2026; Redemption February 25, 2026
- **ICICI Securities Primary Dealership CP**: Allotment January 8, 2026; Redemption March 16, 2026
- **Tata Steel CP (First Issue)**: Allotment January 7, 2026; Redemption February 17, 2026
- **Tata Steel CP (Second Issue)**: Allotment January 7, 2026; Redemption March 25, 2026
- **Muthoottu Mini Financiers CP**: Allotment January 7, 2026; Redemption April 7, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact. This is a routine operational circular for debt market participants. The listing provides institutional investors with additional short-term debt investment options from investment-grade issuers. The commercial papers carry strong credit ratings (A1+ from CARE, CRISIL, ICRA, and IND) and offer competitive interest rates. Impact is limited to debt market segment participants and does not affect equity markets or retail investors.
