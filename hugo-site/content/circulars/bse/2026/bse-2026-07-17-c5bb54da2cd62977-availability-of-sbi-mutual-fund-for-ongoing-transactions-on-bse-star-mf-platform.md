## Summary

BSE has notified members that, pursuant to its earlier Notice No. 20260706-41 dated July 06, 2026, the SBI MUTUAL FUND shall be reopened for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions, along with SIP, STP and SWP facilities, on the BSE StAR MF Platform with effect from July 20, 2026. The notice lists six scheme plans under the SBI CRISIL-IBX SDL Index - June 2034 Index Fund and specifies SIP investment amount ranges and minimum installment requirements as communicated by SBI AMC.

## Key Points

- Six SBI CRISIL-IBX SDL Index - June 2034 Index Fund scheme plans reopen for ongoing transactions on BSE StAR MF Platform from July 20, 2026
- Plans covered: Regular Plan Growth, Regular Plan IDCW Payout, Regular Plan IDCW Reinvestment, Direct Plan Growth, Direct Plan IDCW Payout, Direct Plan IDCW Reinvestment (all Debt category)
- ISINs: INF200KB1BL4, INF200KB1BM2, INF200KB1BN0, INF200KB1BO8, INF200KB1BP5, INF200KB1BQ3
- SIP, STP and SWP facilities are enabled alongside regular subscription and redemption
- SIP investment amount ranges: Rs. 500 to Rs. 999 requires minimum 12 monthly installments; Rs. 1,000 and above requires minimum 6 monthly installments

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an operational follow-up to BSE Notice No. 20260706-41 dated July 06, 2026, reopening previously restricted transactions for the named schemes.

## Compliance Requirements

BSE StAR MF Platform members handling SBI Mutual Fund transactions must ensure their systems recognize the reopening of ongoing subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, STP and SWP for the listed scheme ISINs effective July 20, 2026, and apply the specified SIP investment amount and installment rules.

## Important Dates

- July 06, 2026: Original notice (20260706-41) referenced
- July 17, 2026: Current notice issued
- July 20, 2026: Effective date for reopening of ongoing transactions

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice limited to mutual fund distributors and investors using the BSE StAR MF Platform for the specified SBI debt index fund schemes. It has no direct bearing on listed equity securities or broader market operations.
