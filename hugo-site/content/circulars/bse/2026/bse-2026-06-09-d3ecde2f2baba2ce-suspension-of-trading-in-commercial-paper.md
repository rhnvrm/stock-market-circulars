## Summary

BSE has notified trading members to suspend dealing in commercial papers of 17 companies that have fixed record dates for redemption of their commercial paper instruments. The record dates fall on 12 June 2026 for most companies and 14 June 2026 for Navi Finserv Limited and Avanse Financial Services Limited. All settlements are under DR 650/2026-2027.

## Key Points

- Trading members must not deal in the commercial papers of the 17 listed companies effective from their respective ex-dates (12 June 2026)
- All 17 instruments have the same ex-date of 12/06/2026 regardless of differing record dates
- Settlement reference for all instruments: DR 650/2026-2027
- Two companies (Navi Finserv Limited and Avanse Financial Services Limited) have a record date of 14/06/2026, while the rest are 12/06/2026
- Notice covers both financial services companies and industrial corporates

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational notice under BSE's listing operations framework requiring trading suspension ahead of commercial paper redemption record dates.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members of BSE must immediately cease dealing in the commercial paper instruments listed above
- Compliance is mandatory from the respective ex-dates (12 June 2026 for all instruments)
- Applies to all trading members active in the Debt segment

## Important Dates

- **Ex-Date for all instruments:** 12 June 2026
- **Record Date (most companies):** 12 June 2026
- **Record Date (Navi Finserv Limited, Avanse Financial Services Limited):** 14 June 2026
- **Settlement Reference:** DR 650/2026-2027

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice with medium market impact, primarily affecting debt segment participants and institutional investors holding these commercial paper instruments. The suspension affects 17 instruments maturing around 12-14 June 2026. Retail equity market participants are largely unaffected; the notice is directed at trading members active in the BSE Debt segment. Notable issuers include large-cap companies such as Bajaj Finance, Tata Steel, JSW Energy, and APL Apollo Tubes, whose commercial paper positions will be redeemed at face value on the respective record dates.
