## Summary

BSE has activated the Call Money Module of the IBBS (Internet Based Book Building Software) platform for PVV Infra Limited's First and Final Call on its partly paid-up equity shares (symbol: PVVCM). Trading Members, SCSBs, and intermediaries are notified to participate in collecting and submitting call money details during the issue period.

## Key Points

- PVV Infra Limited has requested activation of BSE's Call Money Module on the IBBS platform
- This is a First and Final Call on partly paid-up equity shares
- Security symbol for the call money notice is **PVVCM**
- Total partly paid-up equity shares subject to call: **9,86,64,284**
- Call money amount payable per share: **Rs. 3.75**
- Registrar: Skyline Financial Services Private Limited (SEBI Reg. No. INR000003241)
- Module is available to SCSBs and Trading Members with 3-in-1 type accounts

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. The notice is issued in accordance with:
- BSE Exchange Notice No. 20210104-37 dated January 4, 2021
- SEBI circular dated December 8, 2020 governing the Call Money Module for SCSBs and Trading Members

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members** and **SCSBs** must use the IBBS Call Money Module to submit call money notice details collected during the issue period
- Bidding window is strictly **10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.** on each day of the issue period
- For regulatory/business rule queries: contact Listing Sales team at listing.sales@bseindia.com or 022-2272 8846/5063/8179
- For bidding queries: contact IBBS Operations at ibbs.ops@bseindia.com or 022-2272 8376/8436/8226
- For user ID creation or password reset: contact IBBS Operations at user.trdops@bseindia.com or 022-2272 8005/8215

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a focused impact on holders of PVV Infra Limited's partly paid-up equity shares who must pay Rs. 3.75 per share as the First and Final Call. Intermediaries — particularly SCSBs and Trading Members with 3-in-1 accounts — must ensure operational readiness on the IBBS platform before May 15, 2026. The total call money to be collected amounts to approximately Rs. 36.99 crore (9,86,64,284 shares × Rs. 3.75). Broader market impact is limited to participants dealing in PVVCM.
