## Summary

BSE has notified all market participants that The President of India, acting through the Ministry of Railways, Government of India, as the promoter of Indian Railway Finance Corporation Limited (IRFC), proposes to sell equity shares via the Offer for Sale (OFS) mechanism on BSE and NSE. The base offer size is 13,06,85,060 equity shares (1% of total paid-up equity share capital, face value Rs. 10 each), with an option to additionally sell an equal number of shares under the oversubscription option, bringing the total potential offer to 2% of the paid-up equity share capital.

## Key Points

- **Seller:** The President of India, represented by the Ministry of Railways, Government of India (Promoter of IRFC)
- **Base Offer Size:** Up to 13,06,85,060 equity shares representing 1% of total paid-up equity share capital
- **Oversubscription Option:** Additional 13,06,85,060 equity shares (1%), making total up to 2% if exercised
- **Face Value:** Rs. 10 per share
- **T Day (June 24, 2026):** Open for Non-Retail Investors only
- **T+1 Day (June 25, 2026):** Open for Retail Investors, Employees, and Non-Retail Investors carrying forward un-allotted bids
- **Exchanges:** BSE Limited and National Stock Exchange of India Limited (NSE)
- Trading members and custodians are directed to refer to prior OFS bidding guideline notices

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This notice operates under the existing OFS framework established by multiple SEBI circulars, including:
- SEBI/HO/MRD/DOPI/CIR/P/2018/159 (December 28, 2018) — Review of OFS through Stock Exchange Mechanism
- SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-3/P/CIR/2023/10 (January 10, 2023) — Comprehensive Framework on OFS
- SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-3/P/CIR/2024/6 (January 23, 2024) — OFS Framework for Employees
- SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-3/P/CIR/2024/82 (June 14, 2024)

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members and Custodians must refer to BSE notice nos. 20190118-43, 20200701-27, 20230127-14, 20240221-47, and 20240701-19 for revised OFS bidding guidelines
- Participants must ensure bids comply with applicable SEBI OFS circulars and BSE guidelines
- Shares offered are held in dematerialized form in demat accounts with the relevant depository participant

## Impact Assessment

This OFS is significant given IRFC's status as a government-backed NBFC and the scale of the offering (up to 2% of total equity share capital). The two-day window structure allows phased participation — non-retail investors on Day 1 and retail investors, employees, and carry-forward non-retail bids on Day 2. Market participants should expect increased trading volumes in IRFC shares on June 24-25, 2026. The government's decision to divest via OFS signals a potential monetization or dilution of its stake, which could affect IRFC's share price depending on the floor price set and demand from institutional and retail investors.
