## Summary

BSE has published the Demat Auction list for Settlement No. 796 dated January 13, 2026. The auction involves 108 securities (54 unique scrips listed in the document) with varying quantities subject to auction due to delivery shortages. This is a routine market mechanism to close out failed delivery obligations.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 796
- Auction Date: January 13, 2026
- Total scrips affected: 108 entries across multiple securities
- Quantities range from 1 share to over 1 million shares (Sunshine Capital: 1,086,379 shares)
- Highest quantity auctions: Sunshine Capital (1,086,379), India Homes (29,640), Subex Ltd (25,000), PRO CLB Global (22,500)
- Includes major stocks like Reliance Industries (31 shares), HCL Tech (585 shares), Tata Motors (250+67 shares)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard demat auction notice pursuant to existing settlement and auction procedures.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers with delivery shortages must participate in the auction
- Settlement obligations must be fulfilled through the auction mechanism
- Affected parties must arrange for securities or settle at auction prices

## Important Dates

- Settlement No.: 796
- Auction Notice Date: January 13, 2026
- Auction execution will follow BSE's standard timeline for settlement 796

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects 108 securities with varying quantities. While the list is extensive, most quantities are relatively small except for a few outliers.

**Operational Impact:** Affects brokers and clients who failed to deliver securities in the specified settlement. They will need to buy securities through auction or pay price differences.

**Investor Impact:** Limited direct impact on general investors. Primary impact on specific brokers/clients with delivery failures who must fulfill obligations through auction mechanism at potentially higher prices.
