## Summary

BSE has updated the list of securities subject to Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) for companies undergoing Insolvency Resolution Process (IRP) under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). New securities have been added (Annexure I), securities have been removed (Annexure II), and a consolidated list is provided (Annexure III), all effective May 29, 2026.

## Key Points

- Securities satisfying IBC/IRP criteria have been added to the ASM framework with effect from May 29, 2026 (Annexure I).
- Securities moving out of the IBC Framework are listed in Annexure II, also effective May 29, 2026.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the IBC Framework is provided in Annexure III.
- The IBC surveillance framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by exchanges.
- Shortlisting under this framework is purely for market surveillance purposes and does not constitute an adverse action against the company.
- Price bands for scrips exiting the framework will be reinstated to the pre-shortlisting price band, unless the scrip remains under another surveillance measure.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice updates the IBC surveillance framework list first established under Exchange Notice No. 20180608-34 (June 8, 2018), with subsequent updates via notices dated May 20, 2022, February 10, 2023, and September 5, 2025. The periodic review mechanism remains unchanged.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members/market participants must note the revised list of securities under the IBC Framework and adjust trading activity accordingly.
- For scrips exiting the framework, the applicable price band reverts to the pre-shortlisting band (subject to no other surveillance measure being in force).
- Any clarifications may be directed to bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **May 27, 2026**: Notice date.
- **May 29, 2026**: Effective date for additions to and removals from the IBC surveillance framework.

## Impact Assessment

Securities added to the IBC ASM framework will face stricter trading controls (e.g., trade-for-trade settlement, reduced price bands), limiting liquidity and potentially increasing volatility. Securities exiting the framework will see relaxed trading restrictions and restored price bands, which may improve liquidity. Market participants holding positions in affected scrips should review exposure ahead of May 29, 2026. The consolidated Annexure III provides a reference for all currently restricted securities.
