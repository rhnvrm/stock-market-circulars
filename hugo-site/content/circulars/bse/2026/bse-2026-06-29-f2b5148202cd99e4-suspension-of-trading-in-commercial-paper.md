## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20260629-6 directing trading members to suspend dealing in commercial papers of multiple companies that have fixed record dates for redemption. A total of 16 commercial paper instruments across 9 companies are covered, with record/ex-dates falling between 1 July 2026 and 16 July 2026.

## Key Points

- Trading members are advised not to deal in the listed commercial papers effective from their respective ex-dates
- 16 commercial paper instruments across 9 issuers are affected
- All redemptions fall within the 2026-2027 settlement series (DR numbers 662 through 673)
- Issuers include prominent NBFCs and financial services companies
- Record dates and ex-dates are the same for each instrument

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational notice under existing BSE debt segment rules requiring suspension of trading in commercial paper instruments ahead of their redemption record dates.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must cease dealing in the specified commercial paper scrips from their respective ex-dates
- Members should update their systems to reflect the trading suspension for each affected ISIN on the applicable dates

## Impact Assessment

This notice affects debt segment participants trading in commercial papers of the listed companies. The suspension is a routine pre-redemption measure and does not indicate financial stress. Bajaj Finance, Bajaj Financial Securities, and Muthoot Mcred each have multiple instruments affected. Trading members with positions in these CPs must ensure compliance with the suspension directives from the respective ex-dates to avoid regulatory violations.
