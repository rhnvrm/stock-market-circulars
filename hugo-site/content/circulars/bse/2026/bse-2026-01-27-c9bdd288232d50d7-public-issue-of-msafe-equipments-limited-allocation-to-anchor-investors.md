## Summary

BSE announces the allocation of shares to anchor investors in the Initial Public Offer (IPO) of MSAFE EQUIPMENTS LIMITED. The anchor investor allocation is completed one day prior to the issue opening for public subscription, as per SEBI regulations.

## Key Points

- Anchor investor allocation completed for MSAFE EQUIPMENTS LIMITED IPO
- Allocation details published as per SEBI (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2018
- Anchor investors are typically qualified institutional buyers (QIBs) who bid before the issue opens to retail investors
- The allocation price and number of shares allocated to anchor investors has been finalized
- Anchor portion lock-in: 30 days from date of allotment for 50% of allocated shares, 90 days for remaining 50%

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard disclosure as per existing SEBI ICDR Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- The issuer must disclose anchor investor allocation details to stock exchanges
- Lead managers are required to upload allocation details on exchange platforms
- Anchor investors must comply with lock-in requirements as per SEBI regulations
- Investors can review anchor allocation before deciding on public issue subscription

## Important Dates

- Anchor Investor Allocation Date: January 27, 2026
- Public Issue Opening: Expected January 28, 2026 (day after anchor allocation)
- Lock-in Period: 30 days for 50% allocation, 90 days for remaining 50%

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - Anchor investor allocation provides price discovery and indicates institutional appetite for the IPO. Strong anchor participation often signals positive sentiment and can influence retail investor participation.

**Investor Impact**: Medium - Retail and non-institutional investors can evaluate anchor allocation details, including the number of anchor investors, allocation amount, and price, to make informed decisions about participating in the public issue.

**Operational Impact**: Low - Standard IPO process step with no operational changes for market participants.
