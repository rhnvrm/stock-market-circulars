## Summary

BSE has issued its periodic update to the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) list for companies undergoing Insolvency Resolution Process (IRP) under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). Effective March 10, 2026, no new securities are being added to any stage of the IBC surveillance framework, while seven securities are being excluded from ASM. A consolidated list of securities currently under IBC surveillance across various stages is also provided.

## Key Points

- No new securities added to Stage 0 (Receipt of corporate announcement/disclosure) w.e.f. March 10, 2026
- No new securities added to Stage 1 (IRP under IBC) w.e.f. March 10, 2026
- No securities moving from Stage I to Stage II or Stage II to Stage I
- Seven securities excluded from ASM for IBC companies w.e.f. March 10, 2026
- Consolidated list of remaining IBC-monitored securities includes companies at Stage 0 and Stage 1
- Some securities marked with (*) as per NSE data; some marked (^) as Recommencement Scrips

## Regulatory Changes

The IBC surveillance framework operates across multiple stages:
- **Stage 0**: Receipt of corporate announcement/disclosure from the company
- **Stage 1**: Securities under Insolvency Resolution Process (IRP)
- **Stage 2**: Higher surveillance stage for advanced IBC cases

This update reflects no escalations or de-escalations between stages, but removes seven companies that have completed or exited the insolvency process.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and market participants must note the exclusion of the seven securities from ASM-IBC framework effective March 10, 2026
- Normal trading rules will apply to excluded securities post removal from ASM
- Securities remaining on the consolidated list continue to be subject to applicable ASM restrictions per their respective stages

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: March 10, 2026 — all changes (exclusions and consolidated list) take effect
- **Circular Date**: March 9, 2026
