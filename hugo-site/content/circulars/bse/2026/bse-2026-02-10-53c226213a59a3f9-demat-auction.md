## Summary

BSE has announced a demat auction for Settlement No. 816 dated February 10, 2026. The auction covers 156 scrips across various sectors, with quantities ranging from 1 to 80,730 shares. This auction is conducted to settle obligations where delivery of securities has failed, requiring the exchange to purchase securities through auction to complete settlement.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 816
- Date: February 10, 2026
- Total scrips involved: 156 securities
- Largest quantity: Blue Cloud Software (80,730 shares)
- Other significant quantities: HIT KIT Global (78,626), Royal India (8,131), Somi Conveyor (9,969), Unison Metal (9,999)
- Mix of large-cap and small-cap securities included
- Sectors covered include banking, power, engineering, textiles, finance, and technology

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard market operations circular for conducting demat auction as per existing SEBI and BSE auction framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members who have failed to deliver securities in Settlement No. 816 must participate in the auction
- Successful bidders must deliver the auctioned securities as per BSE auction settlement guidelines
- Defaulting members will be liable for auction price differences and penalties as per BSE rules
- All auction participants must comply with margin and fund requirements specified by BSE

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: February 10, 2026
- Auction Date: To be conducted as per BSE auction schedule (typically within 2-3 trading days of settlement)
- Pay-in/Pay-out: As per standard auction settlement cycle

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - While the auction involves 156 scrips, most quantities are relatively small except for a few securities. The auction mechanism ensures orderly settlement completion.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - Affects members who failed to deliver securities and must now participate in auction or face penalties. Market makers and liquidity providers may see temporary trading opportunities.

**Investor Impact:** Low to Medium - Shareholders of auctioned securities may see temporary price volatility during auction period. Defaulting clients may face higher settlement costs due to auction premiums.
