## Summary

BSE has notified trading members of partial redemption of Pass-Through Certificates (PTCs) issued under India Universal Trust AL1 across three series (A1, A2, and A3). The partial redemption results in reduced face values per PTC effective May 22, 2026, and trading members are required to trade these instruments at the revised face values from that date.

## Key Points

- Three PTC series under India Universal Trust AL1 have undergone partial redemption
- Series A1 (ISIN: INE16J715019, Scrip Code: 976050): Reduced face value of Rs. 43,388.64 per PTC
- Series A2 (ISIN: INE16J715027, Scrip Code: 976051): Reduced face value of Rs. 75,79,990.30 per PTC
- Series A3 (ISIN: INE16J715035, Scrip Code: 976052): Reduced face value of Rs. 88,43,104.49 per PTC
- Reference: DR-636/2026-2027

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework introduced. This is an operational update reflecting reduced face values of existing PTC instruments following partial principal repayment to certificate holders.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade all three PTC series at the revised reduced face values effective May 22, 2026
- Members are required to take note of the updated face values and ensure correct pricing on the debt market segment

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** May 21, 2026
- **Effective Date for Revised Face Value Trading:** May 22, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The partial redemption reduces outstanding principal on these PTCs, lowering the face value at which they trade on the BSE Debt Market Segment. Series A2 and A3 carry substantially higher per-unit face values (Rs. 75+ lakh and Rs. 88+ lakh respectively), indicating these are institutional-grade instruments with limited retail participation. The change is operationally significant for trading members holding or dealing in these specific ISINs, but has no broader systemic market impact.
