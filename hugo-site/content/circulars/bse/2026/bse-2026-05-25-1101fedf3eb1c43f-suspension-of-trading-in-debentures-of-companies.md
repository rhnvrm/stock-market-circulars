## Summary

BSE has issued a notice (No. 20260525-8) informing trading members that 10 companies have fixed record dates for payment of interest or redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs). Trading members are advised not to deal in the debentures of these companies around their respective record/ex-dates.

## Key Points

- Trading suspension applies to NCDs of 10 companies spanning record dates from May 26 to May 31, 2026
- Suspension is triggered by record dates set for interest payment or redemption of debentures
- Settlement DR references range from 638/2026-2027 to 640/2026-2027
- Two NCDs each affected for 360 ONE Prime Limited and Navi Finserv Limited
- Notice issued by Marian D'Souza, Assistant Vice President – Listing Compliance and Operations, dated May 18, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice under BSE's listing compliance framework requiring trading suspension around NCD record dates for interest/redemption events.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the listed debentures on and around the specified ex-dates
- Members should refer to the scrip codes and ISINs listed below to identify affected instruments
- Compliance is mandatory for all trading members of the exchange in the Debt segment

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a medium operational impact on debt segment trading members. The suspension affects 11 NCD instruments across 10 companies for short windows around their respective record dates. The affected companies include major financial institutions (HDFC Bank, L&T Finance, Shriram Finance, Poonawalla Fincorp) and NBFCs, with coupon rates ranging from 7% to 12.10%. Trading members must update their systems to block transactions in the specified ISINs around the ex-dates to remain compliant. No broader market impact is expected as this is a routine procedure.
