## Summary

BSE observed significant price/volume movement in a set of listed scrips and, as part of its surveillance process, wrote to the respective companies seeking clarification. Replies have been received and published for four companies, while nine other companies have yet to respond.

## Key Points

- BSE issued clarification requests to 13 companies following unusual price/volume activity in their scrips.
- Replies have been received from and attached for:
  - B. L. Kashyap and Sons Ltd (Scrip Code: 532719)
  - BF Investment Ltd (Scrip Code: 533303)
  - Exato Technologies Ltd (Scrip Code: 544626)
  - Sangam India Ltd (Scrip Code: 514234)
- Replies are still awaited from:
  - BF Utilities Ltd (Scrip Code: 532430)
  - CMX Holdings Ltd (Scrip Code: 532217)
  - La Tim Metal & Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 505693)
  - Mapro Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 509762)
  - Shalimar Wires Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 532455)
  - Shankara Buildpro Ltd (Scrip Code: 544517)
  - Sujala Trading & Holdings Ltd (Scrip Code: 539117)
  - Unjha Formulations Ltd (Scrip Code: 531762)
  - VJTF Eduservices Ltd (Scrip Code: 509026)
- Notice issued by BSE Surveillance Department (DOSS), signed by Sanjay Jain (Deputy Vice President) and Shreya Ghose (Manager), Surveillance.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is a routine surveillance disclosure notice under BSE's ongoing monitoring of price and volume movements, not a new regulatory change.

## Compliance Requirements

- The nine companies with replies still awaited are expected to respond to BSE's clarification request in due course.
- Investors and trading members should refer to the attached replies for details provided by the four responding companies.

## Important Dates

- Notice date: 14 July 2026
- No specific deadline mentioned for pending replies.

## Impact Assessment

This notice is primarily informational, providing market participants with the latest available information on scrips that have shown unusual price/volume activity. It does not impose trading restrictions but signals heightened surveillance attention on the listed scrips, particularly the nine companies yet to respond, which investors may wish to monitor for potential follow-up action from BSE.
