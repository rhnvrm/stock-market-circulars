## Summary

BSE has listed four new commercial paper (CP) instruments issued on a private placement basis on its Debt Market Segment, effective May 7, 2026. The issuers are Infina Finance Private Limited, Nuvama Wealth Finance Limited, 360 ONE Alternates Asset Management Limited, and Tata Realty and Infrastructure Limited. All securities are admitted to trading in dematerialised form with a standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof, and a tick size of 1 paise.

## Key Points

- Four commercial paper instruments freshly listed on the BSE Debt Segment effective May 7, 2026
- All securities tradeable in demat form only, in denominations of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples
- Tick size: 1 paise
- All instruments were allotted on May 6, 2026
- Interest rates range from 7.00% to 8.20% p.a. (cumulative)
- All instruments carry top-tier short-term credit ratings (A1+)
- Queries to be directed to BSE Debt Department: 22728352 / 8597 / 8995 / 5753 / 8915

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine listing notification under existing BSE debt segment listing framework for privately placed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form
- Trades must be executed in standard denominations of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Members should reference the respective ISIN numbers for each instrument when placing orders

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing notice with limited broader market impact. The four commercial paper instruments are short-term instruments (tenors ranging roughly 3–6 months) issued by well-rated entities, all carrying A1+ ratings from at least one major agency (CRISIL, ICRA, or CARE). The listing provides liquidity options to holders of these instruments on the BSE Debt Segment. Impact is confined to institutional participants and trading members active in the debt segment.
