## Summary

The President of India, acting through the Department of Financial Services, Ministry of Finance, has exercised the oversubscription option in the Offer for Sale (OFS) of Central Bank of India. The total offer size has been doubled to 72,41,12,102 equity shares, representing 8% of the Bank's total issued and paid-up equity share capital. Non-retail investors were eligible to bid on May 22, 2026 (T Day), while retail investors and non-retail investors carrying forward unallotted bids may participate on May 25, 2026 (T+1 Day).

## Key Points

- The oversubscription option of 36,20,56,051 equity shares (4%) has been fully exercised in addition to the base offer size of 36,20,56,051 equity shares (4%)
- Total offer size: 72,41,12,102 equity shares (8% of total issued and paid-up equity share capital)
- 72,411,212 equity shares (10% of the total offer) are reserved for Retail Investors on T+1 day (May 25, 2026)
- An additional 75,00,000 shares (~0.1% of total paid-up capital) will be offered to employees
- The OFS is conducted through a designated window on BSE and NSE
- Original OFS was announced via Exchange Notice No. 20251201-40 dated December 01, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This notice is an operational update exercising the oversubscription option as permitted under the original OFS framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Non-retail investors must have submitted bids on T Day (May 22, 2026) through the designated BSE/NSE OFS window
- Non-retail investors with unallotted bids from T Day may carry forward to T+1 Day
- Retail investors must submit valid bids on T+1 Day (May 25, 2026) to be eligible for allotment
- Allotment to retail investors is subject to receipt of valid bids

## Important Dates

- **May 22, 2026 (T Day):** OFS open for Non-Retail Investors only
- **May 25, 2026 (T+1 Day):** OFS open for Retail Investors and Non-Retail Investors carrying forward unallotted bids
- **Reference Notice Date:** December 01, 2025 (original OFS announcement, Notice No. 20251201-40)

## Impact Assessment

This is a significant government disinvestment event. The exercise of the full oversubscription option (doubling the offer from 4% to 8% of the Bank's capital) signals strong non-retail demand on T Day. The large additional supply of approximately 72 crore shares entering the market may exert short-term downward pressure on Central Bank of India's share price. Retail investors should note the T+1 Day window and the 10% retail reservation quota. The employee allocation of 75 lakh shares provides a participatory element for Bank staff.
