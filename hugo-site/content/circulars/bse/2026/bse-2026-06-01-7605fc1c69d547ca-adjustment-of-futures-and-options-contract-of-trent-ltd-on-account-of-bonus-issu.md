## Summary

BSE has announced the adjustment of all Futures & Options contracts on Trent Ltd (Scrip Code: 500251, Derivatives Asset Code: TRNT) in accordance with SEBI guidelines, following the company's announcement of a 1:2 bonus issue. Adjustments will be applied at end of day on June 3, 2026, with June 4, 2026 as the ex-date.

## Key Points

- Bonus issue ratio: 1 bonus equity share (Rs. 1 face value) for every 2 existing equity shares (Rs. 1 face value)
- Record date: June 4, 2026; ex-date: June 4, 2026
- Adjustment factor: (1+2)/2 = **1.5**
- All available F&O contracts on TRNT will be adjusted at end of day June 3, 2026
- Adjustments cover strike price, market lot, open positions, and futures price

## Regulatory Changes

In compliance with SEBI guidelines for corporate action adjustments to F&O contracts, the following changes will be applied:

1. **Strike Price**: Old strike price ÷ 1.5
2. **Market Lot**: Old market lot × 1.5
   - June expiry: 100 → **150**
   - July expiry onwards: 150 → **225**
3. **Open Position**: Old position × 1.5
4. **Futures Price**: Old futures price ÷ 1.5 (rounded to nearest tick size)

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members in the Equity Derivatives Segment must note the revised contract specifications effective from June 4, 2026
- Members should contact their designated Relationship Managers for further clarifications
- No action is required by members; BSE will apply adjustments automatically at end of day on June 3, 2026

## Important Dates

- **June 3, 2026**: Adjustment applied at end of day
- **June 4, 2026**: Ex-date and record date for Trent Ltd bonus issue

## Impact Assessment

This is a standard corporate action adjustment impacting all open F&O positions on Trent Ltd. Derivatives traders holding June and post-June expiry contracts will see their strike prices reduced and lot sizes increased by a factor of 1.5, keeping the economic value of positions neutral. The revised market lots (150 for June expiry, 225 for July onwards) will increase the notional contract size per lot. Traders should review margin requirements post-adjustment, as increased lot sizes may affect margin obligations.
