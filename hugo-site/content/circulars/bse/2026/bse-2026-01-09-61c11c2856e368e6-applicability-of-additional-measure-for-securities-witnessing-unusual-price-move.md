## Summary

BSE has placed 3 securities under Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) effective January 12, 2025, due to unusual price movements. The circular provides both the newly added securities and a consolidated list of all 15 securities currently under this enhanced monitoring framework.

## Key Points

- 3 securities newly added to Additional Surveillance Measure effective January 12, 2025
- Consolidated list contains 15 securities under ASM framework
- Measure applies to securities witnessing unusual price movement
- Enhanced monitoring and surveillance applies to listed securities

## Regulatory Changes

BSE continues to implement Additional Surveillance Measures as part of its market integrity framework to monitor securities experiencing unusual price movements. This measure ensures enhanced oversight and investor protection.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the securities under Additional Surveillance Measure
- Enhanced monitoring and surveillance norms apply to these securities
- Investors should exercise caution while trading in securities under ASM
- Market participants should comply with any additional requirements specific to ASM securities

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date for New Additions:** January 12, 2025
- **Circular Date:** January 9, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The addition of 3 more securities to the ASM framework indicates BSE's proactive approach to market surveillance. Trading in these 15 securities may be subject to additional scrutiny, price limits, or other regulatory measures. Investors and traders should be aware that securities under ASM typically face enhanced monitoring due to volatility or unusual trading patterns, which may affect liquidity and trading dynamics.
