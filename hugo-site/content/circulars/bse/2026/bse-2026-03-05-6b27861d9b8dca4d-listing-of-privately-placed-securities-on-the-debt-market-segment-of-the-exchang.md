## Summary

BSE Notice No. 20260305-34 informs trading members that six new commercial papers issued on a private placement basis have been listed and admitted to trading on the BSE Debt Segment effective March 5, 2026. The issuers are KEC International Ltd., Birla Group Holdings Private Limited, 360 ONE WAM Limited, Can Fin Homes Ltd., Network 18 Media & Investments Limited, and IGH Holdings Private Limited.

## Key Points

- All securities are traded exclusively in dematerialised form under their respective ISINs
- Standard denomination is Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size for all securities is 1 paise
- All commercial papers have a face value and paid-up value of Rs. 5,00,000 each
- Redemption dates are in June 2026 (short-term instruments, ~3 months tenure)
- Interest is cumulative for all issuances

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notification under existing BSE debt market listing framework for privately placed commercial papers.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form
- Trading must occur in standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Members requiring clarification may contact BSE Debt Department at: 22728352 / 8597 / 8995 / 5753 / 8915

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational listing notification with low market-wide impact. The commercial papers are short-term debt instruments (approximately 90-day tenure) maturing in early June 2026. The issuers include well-known entities such as KEC International, 360 ONE WAM, Can Fin Homes, and Network 18, along with private entities Birla Group Holdings and IGH Holdings. Interest rates range from 6.97% to 7.99% p.a., reflecting prevailing short-term money market rates. The total notional value across disclosed issuances is significant (e.g., Network 18 alone: 6,000 units × Rs. 5 lakh = Rs. 300 crore). Institutional and debt segment traders should update their systems with the new scrip codes and ISINs. No equity market impact is expected.
