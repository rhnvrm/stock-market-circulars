## Summary

BSE has issued a notice activating the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework on select securities. Effective May 26, 2026, 100% margins will apply on all open and new positions for securities listed in Annexure I (Part A). Securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages and securities exiting the framework are also detailed, with a lower price band applicable from May 22, 2026.

## Key Points

- 100% margin requirement applicable from May 26, 2026 on all open positions as on May 25, 2026 and new positions thereafter (Annexure I, Part A)
- Securities moved to higher LT-ASM stages effective May 22, 2026 are listed in Annexure I (Part B)
- Lower Price Band applicable from May 22, 2026 for securities moved to higher stages
- Securities in Annexure I (Part D) remain in LT-ASM but will be transferred to T / XT / MT / TS Group effective May 26, 2026
- Securities exiting the LT-ASM framework effective May 22, 2026 are listed in Annexure II
- Consolidated list of all securities under the framework is provided in Annexure III
- Shortlisting is based on XBRL submissions by listed companies and is a surveillance action, not an adverse finding against the company

## Regulatory Changes

This notice is issued under the revised LT-ASM framework, with reference to the following prior notices:
- Exchange notice no. 20180321-46 dated March 21, 2018 (original LT-ASM framework)
- Exchange notice no. 20181027-1 dated October 27, 2018
- Exchange notice no. 20201204-56 dated December 04, 2020
- Exchange notice no. 20210604-41 dated June 04, 2021
- Exchange notice no. 20240809-46 dated August 09, 2024
- Exchange notice no. 20240920-63 dated September 20, 2024 (latest revision)

The LT-ASM framework applies in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must maintain 100% margins on open positions in LT-ASM securities as of May 25, 2026 and on new positions from May 26, 2026
- Brokers and traders must comply with lower price band restrictions effective May 22, 2026 for securities moving to higher LT-ASM stages
- Securities exiting the framework will have their prior price band reinstated, subject to no other surveillance measure applying
- Members with queries may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **May 22, 2026**: Lower price band applicable for securities in higher LT-ASM stages (Annexure I, Part B); securities exit from LT-ASM framework (Annexure II)
- **May 25, 2026**: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin
- **May 26, 2026**: 100% margin requirement effective for Annexure I (Part A) securities; transfer to T/XT/MT/TS Group for Annexure I (Part D) securities

## Impact Assessment

The imposition of 100% margins significantly increases the cost of holding or creating positions in affected securities, likely reducing liquidity and trading volumes. Securities moved to higher LT-ASM stages face both margin and price band restrictions, further constraining market activity. The framework is purely surveillance-driven and BSE has clarified it should not be interpreted as an adverse action against the listed company. Securities exiting the framework will see price band normalization, potentially improving their trading conditions. Market participants should review the attached annexures to identify affected securities and adjust their positions or risk management accordingly.
