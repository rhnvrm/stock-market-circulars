## Summary

BSE has issued a notice under its Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) Framework informing trading members that securities listed in the accompanying Annexure will move to their respective higher GSM stages with effect from July 22, 2026. This action follows the GSM Framework established via a series of exchange notices dated February 23, 2017, March 03, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, and November 17, 2023. Securities placed in higher GSM stages face progressively stricter trading restrictions designed to enhance market integrity and protect investors from abnormal price movements not commensurate with financial health or fundamentals.

## Key Points

- Securities specified in the Annexure_GSM attachment will move to their respective higher GSM stages effective July 22, 2026.
- The GSM framework has four stages (I through IV), each carrying escalating surveillance actions.
- Stage I: 100% margin applicable and price band capped at 5% or lower.
- Stage II: Trade-for-trade basis with 5% or lower price band, plus Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers.
- Stage III: Trade-for-trade basis with 5% or lower price band, trading permitted only once a week (Monday or first trading day of the week), and ASD of 100% of trade value payable by buyers.
- Stage IV: Same restrictions as Stage III, with the additional condition of no upward price movement permitted.
- All settlements for GSM-flagged securities are done on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this notice implements the existing GSM Framework (originally published February 23, 2017, and subsequently updated) by moving specific securities into higher surveillance stages based on ongoing monitoring.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must identify securities listed in the Annexure_GSM and apply the corresponding stage-wise surveillance actions (margin requirements, price bands, trading frequency restrictions, and ASD collection) from the effective date.
- Members must ensure trade-for-trade settlement is applied with no netting off for affected securities.
- Members should refer to the published FAQ on GSM or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: July 21, 2026
- Effective Date of Stage Movement: July 22, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a high impact on trading activity for the specific securities named in the Annexure, as they will face materially reduced liquidity, higher capital requirements (via ASD and margin), and constrained trading frequency (in higher stages, only weekly trading is permitted). Investors and traders holding or intending to trade these securities should expect wider bid-ask friction and reduced ability to exit positions quickly. Since the exact list of affected securities is contained only in the annexure attachment and not reproduced in the notice text, market participants must consult the Annexure_GSM document directly to determine specific impact on individual holdings.
