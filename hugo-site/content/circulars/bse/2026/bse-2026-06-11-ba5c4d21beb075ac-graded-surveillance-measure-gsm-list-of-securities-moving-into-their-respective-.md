## Summary

BSE has issued a notice moving select securities to higher stages of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 12, 2026. The affected securities are listed in an attached Annexure and will be subject to enhanced surveillance actions depending on their assigned GSM stage (I through IV). Trading members are advised to take adequate precautions while trading in these securities.

## Key Points

- Securities listed in the Annexure will move to higher GSM stages from June 12, 2026.
- Four GSM stages are defined, each with progressively stricter trading restrictions.
- Settlement for all GSM-stage securities will be on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed.
- Buyers may be required to deposit Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% or 100% of trade value depending on the stage.
- Stage III and IV securities are restricted to once-a-week trading (every Monday or first trading day of the week).
- Members are directed to refer to the GSM FAQ or contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications.

## Regulatory Changes

The notice references the GSM framework originally published on February 23, 2017 (Notice No. 20170223-44) and subsequently amended via notices dated March 3, 2017; July 20, 2018; November 29, 2019; and November 17, 2023. Securities in the Annexure are being escalated to higher GSM stages under this existing framework.

## Compliance Requirements

| GSM Stage | Surveillance Actions |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Stage I | 100% applicable margin rate; price band of 5% or lower |
| Stage II | Trade-for-trade; price band of 5% or lower; ASD of 50% of trade value deposited by buyers |
| Stage III | Trade-for-trade; price band of 5% or lower; trading once a week (Monday/1st trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value by buyers |
| Stage IV | Trade-for-trade; price band of 5% or lower; trading once a week (Monday/1st trading day); ASD of 100% of trade value by buyers; no upward price movement allowed |

- Trading members must ensure trade-to-trade settlement with no netting off for all affected securities.
- Buyers must arrange for ASD deposits as applicable to the relevant GSM stage.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 11, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 12, 2026 (GSM stage movements take effect)

## Impact Assessment

Securities moved to higher GSM stages face materially reduced liquidity and increased trading costs. Stage III and IV restrictions (once-a-week trading and full ASD requirements) effectively deter speculative activity and can lead to sharp price corrections. Investors and traders holding these securities should review their positions ahead of the effective date. The trade-for-trade settlement mechanism eliminates intraday netting, increasing capital requirements for all participants. The list of specific affected securities is available in the attached Annexure to the original notice.
