## Summary

BSE has listed and admitted for trading new securities issued by 13 companies under their Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP/ESOS), effective Thursday, June 18, 2026. The companies span banking, insurance, pharma, and other sectors, with share counts ranging from 4,390 (Sheela Foam) to 31,60,300 (HDFC Bank). None of the newly listed shares carry any lock-in restrictions.

## Key Points

- 13 companies have new ESOP/ESOS shares listed for trading from June 18, 2026
- No lock-in period applies to any of the listed securities
- Largest issuance by volume: HDFC Bank (31,60,300 shares, face value ₹1)
- Largest issuance by share count in higher face value: Piramal Pharma (21,00,000 shares, face value ₹10)
- Smallest issuance: Sheela Foam (4,390 shares, face value ₹5)
- All securities are equity shares admitted to the Exchange

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notification under existing ESOP/ESOS listing procedures.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members of BSE are required to take note of the new ISINs and scrip codes admitted for trading. No additional compliance action is required from market participants beyond awareness of the new tradeable share quantities.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 17, 2026
- **Effective Trading Date:** June 18, 2026 (Thursday)
