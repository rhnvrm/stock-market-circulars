## Summary

BSE, as part of its surveillance framework aligned with SEBI guidelines, has decided to revise the list of scrips under the Trade-to-Trade (T/XT/MT/TS) settlement segment. Securities listed in Annexure I meeting all three prescribed criteria will be moved to the Trade-to-Trade group effective May 29, 2026, while those in Annexure II will continue in their current restricted groups with a 5% price band.

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I satisfying criteria I, II, and III will be transferred to T/XT/MT/TS Group effective May 29, 2026.
- Securities in Annexure II will remain in T/XT/MT/TS/P/Z/ZP Group with a 5% price band.
- No netting off of positions will be permitted for securities under Trade-to-Trade settlement.
- A VAR (Value at Risk) Margin of 100% will be levied on all affected scrips per Exchange Notice No. 20050805-12 dated August 5, 2005.
- Shortlisting of securities is based on XBRL submissions by the respective listed companies.
- The criteria for shifting scrips to/from Trade-to-Trade are determined in consultation with SEBI and applied uniformly across exchanges.

## Regulatory Changes

Selected securities will move to compulsory Trade-to-Trade settlement, meaning each buy and sell transaction must result in delivery — no intraday netting is permitted. The applicable groups are T, XT, MT, and TS. This action is consistent with SEBI's extant surveillance framework and is applied uniformly across stock exchanges.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must take adequate precaution while trading in the affected securities.
- Settlement must be done strictly on a trade-to-trade basis; no netting of positions is allowed.
- Trading Members must ensure collection of 100% VAR margin on these scrips.
- Members requiring clarification may write to BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **May 25, 2026** — Notice date; circular issued.
- **May 29, 2026** — Effective date for transfer of Annexure I securities to Trade-to-Trade segment.

## Impact Assessment

This is a temporary and periodic surveillance measure intended to protect market integrity and investor interests. Traders holding or planning to trade in the affected securities will face stricter settlement obligations (mandatory delivery) and higher margin requirements (100% VAR), increasing the cost and reducing the flexibility of trading. The measure does not constitute an adverse finding against the underlying companies. The restriction will be reviewed periodically based on market conditions.
