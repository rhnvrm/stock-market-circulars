## Summary

BSE has updated the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework for companies undergoing Insolvency Resolution Process (IRP) under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). Securities listed in Annexure I have newly qualified under the framework, while those in Annexure II are moving out — both changes are effective June 23, 2026. A consolidated list of all securities currently under the framework is provided in Annexure III.

## Key Points

- Securities in Annexure I have satisfied the criteria for ASM under IBC and will be placed under the framework effective June 23, 2026.
- Securities in Annexure II are moving out of the IBC Framework effective June 23, 2026.
- The consolidated list of all securities currently under the IBC Surveillance Framework is available in Annexure III.
- The framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by exchanges.
- Shortlisting under this framework is purely for market surveillance purposes and should not be construed as adverse action against the company.

## Regulatory Changes

This notice references and builds upon prior BSE notices:
- Notice No. 20180608-34 dated June 08, 2018
- Notice No. 20220520-52 dated May 20, 2022
- Notice No. 20230210-55 dated February 10, 2023
- Notice No. 20250905-47 dated September 05, 2025

The IBC-linked ASM framework is periodically reviewed; this update reflects the latest review cycle.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants and members must note the updated lists of securities entering and exiting the IBC surveillance framework.
- Trading in newly added securities will be subject to the conditions of the ASM framework from June 23, 2026.
- For clarifications, members may contact BSE Surveillance at bse.surv@bseindia.com.

## Important Dates

- **June 22, 2026**: Notice date.
- **June 23, 2026**: Effective date for securities moving into and out of the IBC ASM framework.

## Impact Assessment

Securities placed under the IBC ASM framework are subject to enhanced surveillance conditions, which typically include restricted price bands and trade-for-trade settlement, directly affecting liquidity and trading activity. Securities exiting the framework will have their price bands reinstated to pre-framework levels, provided they are not subject to any other surveillance measure. Investors and traders holding or monitoring these securities should review the Annexure lists in the attached PDF to identify affected scrips and adjust their trading strategies accordingly.
