## Summary

BSE has listed five new Commercial Papers (CPs) issued on a private placement basis by four companies — Tata Steel Limited (two issuances), Aarti Industries Ltd, Nuvama Wealth and Investment Limited, and DSP Finance Private Limited — on the BSE Debt Segment. All securities are admitted to trading effective June 8, 2026, and will be traded exclusively in dematerialised form in standard denominations of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof, with a tick size of 1 paise.

## Key Points

- Five Commercial Papers across four issuers are freshly listed on the BSE Debt Segment effective June 8, 2026.
- All CPs trade only in demat form in standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples; tick size is 1 paise.
- Tata Steel Limited has two CP issuances: one maturing July 23, 2026 (Scrip Code 731830, ISIN INE081A14HB8, 2,000 units at 6.85% p.a.) and one maturing September 3, 2026 (Scrip Code 731831, ISIN INE081A14HA0, 38,000 units at 7.42% p.a.).
- Aarti Industries Ltd CP matures September 7, 2026 (Scrip Code 731832, ISIN INE769A14AI8, 3,000 units at 8.40% p.a.).
- Nuvama Wealth and Investment Limited CP matures September 4, 2026 (Scrip Code 731833, ISIN INE523L14BM6, 5,000 units at 8.87% p.a.).
- DSP Finance Private Limited CP matures September 4, 2026 (Scrip Code 731834, ISIN INE422H14479, 4,000 units at 8.25% p.a.).
- All issuances carry top-tier short-term credit ratings (ICRA A1+, IND A1+, CRISIL A1+, CARE A1+).

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational listing notice under BSE's Debt Capital Market segment procedures.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members may trade these securities only in dematerialised form.
- Standard denomination is Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof.
- Trading members requiring clarifications should contact the BSE Debt Department at 22728352 / 8597 / 8995 / 5753 / 8915.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing notice with limited broader market impact. The five Commercial Papers expand the tradeable universe on the BSE Debt Segment for institutional and eligible debt market participants. The issuers all carry A1+ ratings from multiple agencies (ICRA, IND, CRISIL, CARE), indicating very low credit risk. Tata Steel's larger issuance (38,000 units at Rs. 5 lakhs each = Rs. 1,900 crore notional) is the most significant by volume. Interest rates range from 6.85% to 8.87% p.a., reflecting varying credit spreads and maturity profiles among the issuers. No impact on equity markets or broader regulatory framework.
