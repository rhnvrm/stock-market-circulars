## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that further new securities issued by 13 companies under their Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP/ESOS) have been listed and admitted for trading on the exchange effective Friday, May 8, 2026. None of the newly listed shares are subject to any lock-in period.

## Key Points

- 13 companies have listed additional shares arising from ESOP/ESOS allotments
- All new securities are effective for trading from May 8, 2026
- No lock-in restrictions apply to any of the listed securities
- Total shares listed span a wide range, from 4,250 (L&T Technology Services) to 396,119 (Federal Bank)
- Notice issued under BSE Notice No. 20260507-12, Category: Company Related, Segment: Equity

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice informing trading members of new securities admitted to trading following employee stock option exercises.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members are informed and may trade these securities from May 8, 2026. No additional compliance action is required from members beyond awareness of the new ISIN ranges.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** May 7, 2026
- **Effective Trading Date:** May 8, 2026 (Friday)

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact expected. The newly listed shares represent incremental additions to existing share counts for well-known, liquid large-cap and mid-cap stocks. The largest additions by share count are Federal Bank (396,119 shares), Axis Bank (355,074 shares), and Bluspring Enterprises (279,703 shares). Yes Bank adds 223,153 shares to an already very high share count. All allotments are free of lock-in, so shares are immediately tradeable, but the volumes are small relative to existing floats and are unlikely to cause price disruption.
