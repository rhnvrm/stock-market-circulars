## Summary

BSE Clearing Ltd. (formerly Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd.) has informed all members and custodians that the international holiday previously declared for Friday, July 03, 2026 (as per Exchange Circular No. 20251230-29 dated December 30, 2025) has been cancelled. As a result, July 03, 2026 will now be treated as a normal working/settlement day, and custodial rejections on this date shall attract applicable penalties.

## Key Points

- The international holiday scheduled for July 03, 2026 stands cancelled.
- This reverses the holiday designation earlier communicated via Circular No. 20251230-29 dated December 30, 2025.
- Custodial rejections occurring on July 03, 2026 will attract applicable penalties, as the day is no longer treated as a holiday.
- The notice applies to all members and custodians operating under BSE Clearing Ltd.

## Regulatory Changes

The prior holiday classification for July 03, 2026, communicated under Notice No. 20251230-29, has been withdrawn/cancelled. No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a calendar/operational correction.

## Compliance Requirements

Members and custodians must treat July 03, 2026 as a regular settlement/working day for custodial confirmation and rejection processes. Any custodial rejections processed on this date will be subject to applicable penalties as per standard settlement rules, since the holiday exemption no longer applies.

## Important Dates

- December 30, 2025: Original circular (20251230-29) declaring July 03, 2026 as an international holiday.
- July 02, 2026: Notice date of cancellation (Notice No. 20260702-45).
- July 03, 2026: Date on which the previously declared holiday is cancelled and normal settlement/custodial activity resumes.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is primarily operational, affecting members' and custodians' settlement scheduling and custodial confirmation workflows for July 03, 2026. Entities that had planned around the holiday must adjust operations to treat it as a working day, and failure to process custodial confirmations on time will result in penalties. No direct impact on listed securities, trading, or stock prices is expected.
