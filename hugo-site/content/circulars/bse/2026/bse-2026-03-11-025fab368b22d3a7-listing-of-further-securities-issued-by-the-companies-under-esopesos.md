## Summary

BSE has notified market participants that further new securities issued by 19 companies under Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP) and Employee Stock Option Schemes (ESOS) are listed and admitted for trading on the exchange with effect from **Thursday, March 12, 2026**. None of the newly listed shares are subject to any lock-in period.

## Key Points

- 19 companies have listed new shares arising from ESOP/ESOS exercises
- Effective trading date: March 12, 2026
- No lock-in period applies to any of the listed securities
- Total issuances range from 3,623 shares (Cyient Ltd) to 1,818,422 shares (Ujjivan Small Finance Bank)
- Companies span multiple sectors including banking, pharma, IT, and infrastructure

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational notice under BSE listing regulations for the admission of new shares arising from employee stock option exercises.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must update their records to reflect the new shares and updated distribution numbers for each company
- No action required from investors; shares are directly admitted to trading
- Companies have fulfilled their listing obligations by notifying BSE of ESOP/ESOS exercises

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** March 11, 2026
- **Effective Trading Date:** March 12, 2026 (Thursday)
- **Lock-in Period:** Not applicable for any of the 19 companies

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing circular with low immediate market impact. The newly listed ESOP/ESOS shares represent a marginal increase in the free float of each company. The largest issuance — Ujjivan Small Finance Bank (1,818,422 shares) — may have a slightly higher dilution effect. ICICI Bank's addition of 485,368 shares and One 97 Communications' (Paytm) 217,265 shares are also notable. Since no lock-in period applies, all shares are immediately tradeable, though the volumes are small relative to existing outstanding shares for most companies.
