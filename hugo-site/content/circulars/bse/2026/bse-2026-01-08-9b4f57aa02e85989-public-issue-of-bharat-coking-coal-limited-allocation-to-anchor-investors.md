## Summary

BSE has issued a circular regarding the allocation of shares to anchor investors in the public issue of Bharat Coking Coal Limited. This allocation represents the anchor investor portion of the IPO, which typically occurs one day before the issue opens to the general public. Anchor investors are qualified institutional buyers who provide price stability and demonstrate institutional confidence in the offering.

## Key Points

- Company: Bharat Coking Coal Limited
- Transaction Type: Allocation to Anchor Investors in Public Issue
- Circular Date: January 8, 2026
- Exchange: Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE)
- The anchor investor allocation is part of the company's initial public offering process
- Anchor investors are typically institutional investors who bid one day before the IPO opens

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular follows existing SEBI guidelines for anchor investor participation in public issues under the SEBI (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2018.

## Compliance Requirements

- Anchor investors must comply with lock-in requirements as per SEBI regulations (typically 30 days from date of allotment for 50% of shares, and 90 days for remaining shares)
- The company and lead managers must disclose anchor investor details including names, allocation amounts, and prices
- Allocation details must be made public before the issue opens to retail and other investors
- The anchor investor price serves as the floor price for the public issue

## Important Dates

- Circular Issue Date: January 8, 2026
- Anchor Investor Allocation Date: January 8, 2026
- Public issue is expected to open shortly after anchor allocation (specific dates to be confirmed in the prospectus)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - Anchor investor allocation is a standard pre-IPO step that demonstrates institutional interest in Bharat Coking Coal Limited. The success and pricing of anchor allocation often influences retail investor sentiment when the issue opens.

**Sector Impact:** The listing of Bharat Coking Coal Limited adds to the coal and mining sector representation on Indian stock exchanges, providing investors with another option in the energy and commodities space.

**Investor Impact:** The anchor allocation provides price discovery and indicates institutional confidence levels. Investors can review anchor investor details and allocation patterns to gauge demand for the IPO before making investment decisions.
