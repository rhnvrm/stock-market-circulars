## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that eight companies have fixed record dates for part redemption of their Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs). Trading in the debentures of these companies will continue with reduced face values effective from the respective ex-dates listed below.

## Key Points

- Eight companies have fixed record dates for payment of interest and/or part redemption of NCDs under the Debt segment.
- All debentures involved are privately placed (PVT) instruments.
- Trading members must note that debenture trading will occur at reduced face values from the ex-date onwards.
- Settlement references are under the 624/2026-2027 and 628–631/2026-2027 series.
- Signed by Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President – Listing Compliance and Operations.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This notice is a standard operational communication under BSE's listing compliance framework for debt instruments undergoing part redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must update their systems to reflect the reduced face values for the affected debenture scrips from the respective ex-dates.
- Members must ensure debenture trading for the listed ISINs transitions to the reduced face values as specified.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a moderate operational impact on trading members active in BSE's debt segment. Holders of these privately placed NCDs will see the face value of their instruments reduced as part of the scheduled part redemption, returning a portion of principal. Trading members must ensure their systems reflect the updated face values from the respective ex-dates to avoid pricing or settlement errors. No equity market impact is expected.
