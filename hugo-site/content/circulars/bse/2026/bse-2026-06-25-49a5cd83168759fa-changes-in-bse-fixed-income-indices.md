## Summary

BSE Index Services Private Ltd. (formerly Asia Index Pvt. Ltd.) has announced a change in the constituents of the BSE 8–13-year G-Sec Index, effective Tuesday, June 30, 2026. One government security is being added and one is being removed. The BSE 4–8-year G-Sec Index remains unchanged.

## Key Points

- The BSE 8–13-year G-Sec Index will undergo a constituent change effective June 30, 2026
- **Added**: 6.94% GS 2036 (ISIN: IN0020260025), maturing May 11, 2036
- **Dropped**: 6.79% GS 2034 (ISIN: IN002024126), maturing October 7, 2034
- BSE 4–8-year G-Sec Index remains unchanged
- Queries can be directed to bseindex@bseindia.com

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine periodic rebalancing of the BSE 8–13-year G-Sec Index by BSE Index Services Private Ltd., a wholly owned subsidiary of BSE Ltd. The change updates eligible constituents to reflect the current universe of government securities falling within the 8–13-year maturity band.

## Compliance Requirements

Fund managers, ETFs, and index-tracking products benchmarked to the BSE 8–13-year G-Sec Index should adjust portfolio holdings to reflect the updated index composition by the effective date. No regulatory filings are mandated by this circular.

## Important Dates

- **June 25, 2026**: Circular notice date
- **June 30, 2026**: Effective date for BSE 8–13-year G-Sec Index constituent change

## Impact Assessment

The substitution of 6.79% GS 2034 (maturing October 2034) with 6.94% GS 2036 (maturing May 2036) extends the average maturity and slightly increases the coupon yield profile of the BSE 8–13-year G-Sec Index. Index-tracking funds and ETFs will need to rebalance accordingly, potentially incurring minor transaction costs. Retail investors are not directly affected; the impact is confined to institutional participants and fund managers with mandates tied to this index.
