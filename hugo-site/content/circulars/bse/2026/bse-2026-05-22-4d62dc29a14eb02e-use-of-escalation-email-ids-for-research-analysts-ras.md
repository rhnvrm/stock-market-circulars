## Summary

BSE has designated two dedicated escalation email IDs exclusively for Research Analysts (RAs) to handle matters that remain unresolved through regular operational channels. Members are advised to use these IDs only for genuine escalations and not for routine correspondence.

## Key Points

- Two-tier escalation framework established for Research Analyst-related matters
- Escalation 1 email (`iara.escalation1@bseindia.com`) to be used when the primary email is not replied to or resolved within 7 days
- Escalation 2 email (`iara.escalation2@bseindia.com`) to be used when Escalation 1 email is not replied to or resolved within 7 days
- Escalation email IDs must NOT be marked in routine correspondence
- Regular communications should continue through designated operational email channels

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory rule changes. This notice establishes an internal escalation process for Research Analyst-related communications with BSE's Membership Compliance department.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members dealing with Research Analyst matters must follow the two-tier escalation process before using the escalation IDs
- Escalation IDs must be reserved strictly for unresolved matters after the 7-day response window lapses at each tier
- Routine correspondence must continue through standard operational email channels; escalation IDs must not be copied on routine emails

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 22 May 2026
- Effective: Immediately upon issuance
- Response window at each escalation tier: 7 days

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market or financial impact. This is a purely administrative notice affecting BSE members who engage with Research Analyst-related processes. It improves the grievance redressal mechanism for RAs by formalising a structured escalation path, which may result in faster resolution of compliance and membership queries.
