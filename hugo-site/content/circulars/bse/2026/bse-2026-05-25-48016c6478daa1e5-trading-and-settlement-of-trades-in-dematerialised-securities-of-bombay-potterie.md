## Summary

BSE has notified trading members that Bombay Potteries & Tiles Ltd (scrip code: 502216, ISIN: INE06AE01018) has signed agreements with both depositories. The scrip's group classification is being revised from ZP to Z, and the market lot is being reduced from 5 to 1 equity share, effective 26 May 2026. Trading in the scrip remains under suspension and the revised parameters will apply upon revocation of the suspension.

## Key Points

- Bombay Potteries & Tiles Ltd has executed depository agreements with both NSDL and CDSL
- Scrip will continue in the Trade to Trade (T2T) segment for reasons other than non-signing of depository agreements
- All trades must be settled in compulsory demat form
- Market lot revised from 5 to 1 equity share
- Group reclassified from ZP to Z effective 26 May 2026
- Netting of trades is not permitted
- Shortages will be directly closed-out
- Trading remains suspended; revised parameters apply on revocation

## Regulatory Changes

| Parameter | Current | Revised | Effective Date |
|-----------|---------|---------|----------------|
| Group | ZP | Z | 26 May 2026 |
| Market Lot | 5 | 1 | 26 May 2026 |

ISIN: INE06AE01018

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised group and market lot for scrip 502216
- All trades must be settled in compulsory demat form only
- Netting is not permitted; each trade must be settled gross
- Shortages will be handled via direct close-out mechanism

## Important Dates

- **25 May 2026**: Circular issuance date
- **26 May 2026**: Revised group (Z) and market lot (1) become effective
- Revocation of suspension: Date not specified; revised parameters apply upon revocation

## Impact Assessment

The immediate market impact is limited as trading in the scrip remains suspended. Once the suspension is revoked, trading members will need to transact in a minimum lot of 1 share (reduced from 5) under the Z group, with mandatory demat settlement and no netting. The group change from ZP (Trade to Trade, non-depository) to Z reflects the company's compliance with depository agreement requirements, signalling a potential path toward normalised trading upon suspension revocation.
