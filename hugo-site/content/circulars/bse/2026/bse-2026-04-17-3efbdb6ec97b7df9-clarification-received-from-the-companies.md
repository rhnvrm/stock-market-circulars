## Summary

BSE's Surveillance Department (DOSS) issued Notice No. 20260417-26 on April 17, 2026, informing trading members and investors that the Exchange had observed significant price/volume movements in 43 listed scrips. BSE had written to these companies seeking clarifications, and replies have been received from 23 companies while 20 companies have not yet responded.

## Key Points

- BSE observed unusual price/volume movements in 43 scrips and sought clarifications from respective companies
- Replies received from 23 companies including Ahasolar Technologies, Ashapura Minechem, Bonlon Industries, DMCC Speciality Chemicals, Eraaya Lifespaces, Gaudium IVF, Hindustan Composites, Jinkushal Industries, Khyati Global Ventures, Kirloskar Electric, Kisaan Parivar Industries, Narmada Macplast Drip Irrigation, Nidhi Granites, NIS Management, NLC India, Panth Infinity, S.A.L. Steel, Saffron Industries, Simplex Infrastructures, Sonata Software, Swan Corp, Yug Decor, and Zenith Exports
- Replies still awaited from 20 companies including Asia Pack, Cargosol Logistics, Checkpoint Trends, Fortune International, Gajanan Securities, Galada Finance, Goel Food Products, Indus Finance, K K Silk Mills, K&R Rail Engineering, Milgrey Finance, Narmadesh Brass Industries, Naturite Agro Products, Rexnord Electronics, Sampat Aluminium, Supertech EV, Tamilnadu Telecommunications, Triumph International Finance India, Vishal Bearings, and ZR2 Bioenergy
- Four attachment documents (Replies1, Replies2, Replies3, Replies4) contain the actual company responses
- Notice issued by Deputy Vice President Sanjay Jain and Manager Shreya Ghose of Surveillance department

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard surveillance action under BSE's existing market monitoring framework to ensure price discovery integrity and investor awareness.

## Compliance Requirements

- Companies that have not yet responded (20 listed) are expected to submit clarifications to BSE promptly
- Trading members and investors should review the attached company replies (Replies1 through Replies4) for relevant information before making trading decisions in these scrips
- Companies must provide timely responses to BSE surveillance queries when price/volume anomalies are detected

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: April 17, 2026
- No specific deadline mentioned for pending replies; companies with outstanding clarifications should respond at the earliest

## Impact Assessment

This circular has moderate market impact. Investors holding or considering positions in the 43 named scrips should review the attached clarifications to understand the reasons behind the observed price/volume movements. The 20 companies with pending replies may face continued scrutiny. The circular is part of BSE's routine surveillance mechanism and does not impose trading restrictions, but signals heightened regulatory attention on these stocks. Investors in mid-cap and small-cap scrips (the majority of named companies) should exercise caution until full clarifications are available.
