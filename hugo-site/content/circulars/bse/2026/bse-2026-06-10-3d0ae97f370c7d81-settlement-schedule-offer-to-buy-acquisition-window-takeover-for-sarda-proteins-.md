## Summary

ICCL (Indian Clearing Corporation Ltd.) has issued the settlement calendar for the Offer to Buy – Acquisition Window (Takeover) for Sarda Proteins Limited. The bidding window runs from June 11, 2026 to June 24, 2026 under Settlement No. 2026072, with final settlement on June 30, 2026.

## Key Points

- Settlement Type: Takeover (Settlement No. 2026072)
- Bid window open: June 11, 2026 to June 24, 2026
- Obligation download date: June 29, 2026
- Settlement date: June 30, 2026
- Demat shares must be delivered exclusively via the Early Pay-in of Shares mechanism available on both CDSL and NSDL
- Market Type "(Takeover)" and Settlement No. 2026072 must be used in the depository system
- Members and Custodians are NOT required to upload any EPN file
- Members should refer to the MBRSTD_OTB_CM_mmm_SettNo file for client-wise Stamp Duty charges

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. Members are directed to refer to Notice No. 20200528-32 dated May 28, 2020, regarding settlement as per the Revised Guidelines of Mechanism for Acquisition of Shares through Stock Exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and Custodians must complete the early pay-in of securities in the Depository system well in advance of the BSE OTB Bidding window closure for bid placement and Custodian confirmation window
- Early pay-in effect must be reflected in BSE/ICCL systems before prescribed timelines for bid acceptance/confirmation
- CDSL and NSDL early pay-in securities will be earmarked/blocked in client accounts under the requisite market type and settlement number
- No EPN file upload is required from Members or Custodians

## Important Dates

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Bid Start Date | June 11, 2026 |
| Bid End Date | June 24, 2026 |
| Obligation Download | June 29, 2026 |
| Settlement Date | June 30, 2026 |

## Impact Assessment

This notice directly affects stockbrokers (Members) and Custodians participating in the Sarda Proteins Limited takeover offer. The early pay-in mechanism is mandatory — participants must ensure timely delivery of demat shares through CDSL or NSDL to avoid rejection of bids. No broader market impact is expected; the circular is procedural and specific to this takeover transaction.
