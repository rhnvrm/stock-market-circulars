## Summary

BSE has informed its members that the temporary suspension on subscriptions into ICICI Prudential Midcap Fund on the BSE StAR MF platform, originally imposed via Notice No. 20240313-2, is being partially lifted. Specifically, the restriction on Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) and Systematic Transfer Plan (STP-In) subscriptions into the scheme will be removed effective July 16, 2026, based on communication received from ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund.

## Key Points

- Reference to earlier Notice No. 20240313-2 regarding temporary suspension of subscriptions in certain ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund schemes.
- Restriction on SIP and STP-In into ICICI Prudential Midcap Fund is being removed on the BSE StAR MF platform.
- The removal takes effect from July 16, 2026.
- Restriction on fresh/additional Lumpsum purchases and Switch-IN from other ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund schemes into this scheme will continue until further notice.
- An addendum/notice issued by ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund is attached to the circular.
- Circular signed by Arunkumar Ganesan (VP - Mutual Fund) and Mahesh Tare (AVP - Mutual Fund).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; this is an update/reversal of a prior operational restriction imposed on mutual fund subscriptions via the BSE StAR MF platform, based on instructions received directly from the fund house (ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund).

## Compliance Requirements

BSE members and StAR MF platform participants should update their systems/processes to permit SIP and STP-In transactions into ICICI Prudential Midcap Fund from July 16, 2026, while continuing to block Lumpsum purchases and Switch-IN transactions into the scheme until further notice.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date for removal of SIP/STP-In restriction: July 16, 2026
- Notice Date: July 15, 2026
- Original suspension reference: Notice No. 20240313-2

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to investors and distributors using the BSE StAR MF platform for ICICI Prudential Midcap Fund transactions. Investors will regain the ability to invest via SIP and STP-In modes, offering more flexibility for systematic/periodic investment, though lumpsum and switch-in investments remain restricted. There is no direct impact on listed equity securities or broader market trading.
