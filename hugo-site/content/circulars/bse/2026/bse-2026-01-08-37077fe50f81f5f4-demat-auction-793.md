## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction list for Settlement No. 793 dated January 8, 2026. The auction covers 101 scrips from various companies with quantities ranging from 1 share to 173,209 shares. This is a standard market operation for handling delivery obligations where shares need to be auctioned in dematerialized form.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 793
- Date: January 8, 2026
- Total scrips listed: 101 (from 91 distinct companies)
- Quantity range: 1 share (multiple scrips) to 173,209 shares (STANDARD CAP)
- Highest quantity auctions: STANDARD CAP (173,209 shares), CWD Limited (22,500 shares), TAHMAR ENTER (10,543 shares), BALU FORGE I (4,902 shares), SHINE FASHIO (4,000 shares)
- Covers diverse sectors including technology, manufacturing, financial services, automotive, and infrastructure

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a routine auction notice.

## Compliance Requirements

- Affected brokers and clearing members must participate in the demat auction process for the listed scrips
- Settlement obligations must be fulfilled as per BSE auction procedures
- All transactions to be completed in dematerialized form

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: January 8, 2026 (Settlement No. 793)
- Auction schedule to follow standard BSE demat auction timelines

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - affects 101 scrips across various sectors. The auction process may create temporary price volatility in affected securities, particularly those with larger quantities.

**Operational Impact**: Standard demat auction process for handling delivery failures. Brokers with outstanding obligations in listed scrips must participate.

**Investor Impact**: Shareholders of affected companies may see temporary price movements during auction. Largest impact expected on scrips with significant quantities like STANDARD CAP, CWD Limited, and TAHMAR ENTER.

**Sector Coverage**: Broad market impact across technology (HCL Tech, TCS, Infronics), banking/finance (YES Bank, ICICI Prudential, Jio Financial), automotive (Bajaj Auto, Mahindra & M, Rico Auto), infrastructure (Rail Vikas, BHEL), and manufacturing sectors.
