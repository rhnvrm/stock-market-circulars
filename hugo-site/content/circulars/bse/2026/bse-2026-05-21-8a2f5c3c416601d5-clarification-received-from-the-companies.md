## Summary

BSE's Surveillance department (DOSS) observed significant price and/or volume movement in seven listed equity scrips. The Exchange wrote to the concerned companies seeking clarifications in the interest of informed trading. Replies have been received from four companies and are attached to the notice, while three companies are still awaited.

## Key Points

- BSE detected unusual price/volume movement in seven equity scrips and initiated clarification requests under its surveillance mandate.
- Replies received from: Avalon Technologies Ltd (543896), Siemens Energy India Ltd (544390), Optimus Finance Ltd (531254), and IIRM Holdings India Ltd (526530).
- Replies still awaited from: ZR2 Bioenergy Ltd (506640), Accord Transformer & Switchgear Ltd (544710), and Containerway International Ltd (540597).
- The clarifications are made available to trading members and investors to ensure access to latest relevant information.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This notice is issued under BSE's existing surveillance framework to ensure market transparency and investor protection.

## Compliance Requirements

- Companies under surveillance are required to respond promptly to BSE's clarification requests.
- ZR2 Bioenergy Ltd, Accord Transformer & Switchgear Ltd, and Containerway International Ltd must submit their responses at the earliest.
- Trading members and investors are advised to review the attached replies from the four responding companies before taking trading positions.

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: 21 May 2026
- Replies from four companies: Attached as of 21 May 2026
- Replies pending from three companies: No deadline specified; awaited as of notice date

## Impact Assessment

Investors holding or considering positions in any of the seven scrips should review the attached clarifications (where available) before transacting. The three companies that have not yet responded remain under active surveillance, and their scrips may be subject to continued monitoring or further regulatory action if replies are delayed. The notice serves as a transparency measure and does not itself impose trading restrictions, but continued surveillance attention signals elevated regulatory focus on these counters.
