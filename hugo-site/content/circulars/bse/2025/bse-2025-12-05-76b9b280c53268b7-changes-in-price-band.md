## Summary

BSE has announced revisions to price bands for 30 securities, effective from December 08, 2025. The revised price bands range from 5% to 20%, replacing their existing levels. This surveillance measure affects companies across various sectors and is aimed at managing volatility and trading activity in these scrips.

## Key Points

- 30 securities will have revised price bands effective December 08, 2025
- Price bands have been set at three levels: 5%, 10%, and 20%
- Most restrictive band (5%) applies to 15 securities
- Mid-level band (10%) applies to 11 securities
- Least restrictive band (20%) applies to 4 securities
- Notice issued by BSE Surveillance department
- Trading members can contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications

## Regulatory Changes

The exchange has implemented revised price band restrictions as a surveillance measure. Price bands limit the maximum price movement (both upward and downward) allowed for a security during a trading session. The tighter the band, the more restricted the price movement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure compliance with revised price bands from December 08, 2025
- All trades in affected securities must be executed within the specified price band limits
- Members should update their trading systems to reflect new price band restrictions
- Risk management systems should be adjusted accordingly

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** December 05, 2025
- **Effective Date:** December 08, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:**
- Reduced intraday volatility in affected securities
- Potential impact on liquidity as tighter bands may limit trading activity
- Circuit filters will trigger more frequently for securities with tighter bands (5%)

**Trading Impact:**
- Traders and investors will have limited price movement range during trading sessions
- Day trading strategies may need adjustment for affected securities
- Securities with 5% bands face most restrictive trading conditions

**Investor Impact:**
- Protection against excessive price volatility
- May face difficulty exiting positions quickly during high volatility
- Long-term investors less affected than short-term traders

This measure is part of BSE's surveillance mechanism to maintain orderly trading and protect investor interests in securities that may exhibit unusual price movements or volatility patterns.
