## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper issued by Balkrishna Industries Limited on private placement basis with effect from December 04, 2025. The instrument comprises 2000 units with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, carrying a CRISIL A1+ rating. The paper has been issued at a discount with an issue price of Rs. 4,92,811.50 and will mature on March 02, 2026. ICICI Bank Limited acts as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- 2000 units of Commercial Paper listed on BSE Debt segment
- Face Value: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- Issue Price: Rs. 4,92,811.50 (issued at discount)
- Credit Rating: CRISIL A1+
- Scrip Code: 730703, Scrip ID: BIL041225
- ISIN: INE787D14219
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Tick Size: 1 paise
- Issuing and Paying Agent: ICICI Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard debt instrument listing notification.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE787D14219
- Trading shall take place in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Securities will be traded with a tick size of 1 paise
- Trading members requiring clarification may contact BSE debt department on 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: December 04, 2025
- **Listing Date**: December 04, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: March 02, 2026
- **Tenure**: Approximately 88 days

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine commercial paper listing that affects only participants in the debt segment who deal with short-term debt instruments of Balkrishna Industries Limited.

**Operational Impact**: Standard operational procedures apply for debt segment trading members. The instrument adds to the available short-term debt investment options in the BSE Debt segment.

**Investor Impact**: Provides institutional investors and high net-worth individuals with a highly-rated (CRISIL A1+) short-term investment option with approximately 3-month maturity. The discount-based issuance offers implicit yield for the 88-day period.
