## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper issued by Birla Group Holdings Private Limited on private placement basis. The listing comprises 3000 units with face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, totaling Rs. 150 crore. The securities are listed on the BSE Debt segment effective December 19, 2025, with Axis Bank Limited acting as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- **Issuer**: Birla Group Holdings Private Limited
- **Instrument**: Commercial Paper issued on private placement basis
- **Quantity**: 3000 units
- **Face Value**: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- **Issue Price**: Rs. 4,91,604.50 per unit
- **Total Issue Size**: Rs. 150 crore (approximately)
- **Market Lot**: 1 unit
- **Scrip Code**: 729052
- **Scrip ID**: BGHP210325
- **Detail Name**: BGHPL-20-03-26-CP
- **ISIN**: INE09OL14HB4 (Further listing under same ISIN)
- **Credit Rating**: CRISIL A1+, ICRA A1+
- **Issuing and Paying Agent**: Axis Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note that these securities will be traded only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE09OL14HB4
- Trading shall take place in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size for the securities is 1 paise
- Trading members requiring clarification may contact the debt department on 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: December 19, 2025
- **Listing Date**: December 19, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: March 20, 2026
- **Tenor**: Approximately 91 days

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine commercial paper listing with minimal market impact. The high credit ratings (CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+) indicate strong creditworthiness of the issuer. The listing provides additional short-term debt instruments for institutional investors and expands the debt market. No impact on equity markets or broader trading operations. Trading is restricted to dematerialized form with minimum trading lot of Rs. 5 lakhs, targeting institutional participants.
