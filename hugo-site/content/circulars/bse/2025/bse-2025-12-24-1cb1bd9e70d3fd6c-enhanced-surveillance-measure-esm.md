## Summary

BSE has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework effective December 26, 2025. Six securities are being newly shortlisted under ESM, one security will move to a higher ESM stage, and no securities are exiting the framework. The circular provides a consolidated list of securities currently under various ESM stages.

## Key Points

- 6 securities newly added to Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework
- 1 security (RDB Real Estate Constructions Ltd) moving to higher ESM stage
- No securities moving to lower ESM stages
- No securities exiting ESM framework
- Changes effective from December 26, 2025
- Consolidated list includes 26+ securities across ESM Stage I and Stage II

## Securities Added to ESM Framework

**New Entries (6 securities):**

1. CDG Petchem Ltd (Scrip Code: 534796, ISIN: INE198N01017)
2. Galada Power & Telecommunication Ltd (Scrip Code: 504697, ISIN: INE255C01026)
3. Lyons Corporate Market Ltd (Scrip Code: 531441, ISIN: INE100F01019)
4. Mega Nirman and Industries Ltd (Scrip Code: 539767, ISIN: INE216Q01010)
5. SC Agrotech Ltd (Scrip Code: 526081, ISIN: INE895E01017)
6. Silver Oak India Ltd (Scrip Code: 531635, ISIN: INE870J01019)

**Moving to Higher ESM Stage:**

- RDB Real Estate Constructions Ltd (Scrip Code: 544346, ISIN: INE0AK901016)

## Regulatory Changes

The Enhanced Surveillance Measure framework applies additional monitoring and trading restrictions to securities based on risk parameters. Securities in ESM framework typically face:

- Additional surveillance deposits
- Trade-for-trade settlement (no intraday trading)
- Price bands and other trading restrictions
- Progressive stages (Stage I, II, III, etc.) with increasing restrictions

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure awareness of ESM status before executing trades
- Additional margin/deposit requirements apply for ESM securities
- Price discovery sessions and special trading conditions as per ESM stage
- Investors should exercise heightened caution when trading ESM securities

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** December 26, 2025
- All changes to ESM framework take effect from market opening on this date

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:**

- Limited impact on broader market as affected securities are small-cap/mid-cap stocks
- Liquidity constraints expected for the 6 newly added securities
- Trading volumes likely to reduce due to trade-for-trade settlement requirements

**Investor Impact:**

- Existing holders of affected securities may face reduced liquidity
- Higher transaction costs due to additional surveillance deposits
- Increased risk awareness required for these counters

**Operational Impact:**

- Brokers need to update risk management systems
- Additional margin collection required from clients
- Enhanced due diligence for orders in ESM securities
