## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction schedule for Settlement No. 787 dated December 31, 2025. The auction covers 154 scrips with varying quantities to be auctioned due to short delivery or other settlement obligations. The list includes major stocks across sectors including banking, automobile, pharmaceutical, power, technology, and industrial companies.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 787
- Auction Date: December 31, 2025
- Total Scrips: 154 stocks listed for auction
- Major stocks included: Reliance Industries (2,150 shares), Suzlon Energy (72,000 shares), Rama Steel Tubes (268,000 shares), NMDC (12,000 shares), Siemens (2,250 shares), JSW Energy (13,794 shares)
- Sectors covered: Banking, Automotive, Pharmaceuticals, Power & Energy, Technology, Industrial, Real Estate, Telecommunications
- Auction mechanism triggered due to short delivery in settlement process

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a routine operational circular for settlement auction.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members with short delivery obligations must participate in the demat auction
- Members must ensure adequate funds for auction purchases
- Settlement obligations must be fulfilled as per auction results
- Members must comply with BSE auction settlement timelines and procedures

## Important Dates

- **Auction Date**: December 31, 2025
- **Settlement No.**: 787

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - The auction covers 154 scrips with significant quantities in some stocks like Suzlon Energy (72,000 shares) and Rama Steel Tubes (268,000 shares). While this represents routine settlement operations, the quantities involved could create temporary price movements in affected stocks during auction execution.

**Operational Impact**: Affects trading members with short delivery positions who must participate in the auction. The wide range of stocks from large-caps (Reliance, TCS, HDFC Bank equivalents) to mid and small-caps indicates broad market participation in this settlement cycle.

**Investor Impact**: Limited direct impact on regular investors. The auction primarily affects institutional participants and brokers managing settlement obligations.
