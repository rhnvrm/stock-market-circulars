## Summary

BSE announces that DIVINITI EQUITY LONG SHORT FUND scheme will reopen for ongoing subscription and redemption transactions along with SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) and SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) facilities on the BSE StAR MF Platform effective December 03, 2025. This follows previous Notice No. 20251107-65. Six plan variants are available covering Regular and Direct plans with Growth, IDCW Payout, and IDCW Reinvestment options.

## Key Points

- DIVINITI EQUITY LONG SHORT FUND reopening for ongoing transactions on BSE StAR MF Platform
- Effective date: December 03, 2025
- Six scheme variants available: Regular Plan (Growth, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment) and Direct Plan (Growth, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment)
- SIP and SWP facilities will be available
- Scheme category: Equity
- All ISINs range from INF00XX30019 to INF00XX30068
- Reference to previous Notice No. 20251107-65

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an operational notice regarding the availability of a mutual fund scheme for ongoing transactions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and investors should note the reopening date of December 03, 2025
- Transactions can be processed through the BSE StAR MF Platform from the effective date
- Both subscription and redemption requests will be accepted along with SIP and SWP enrollment

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** December 02, 2025
- **Effective Date:** December 03, 2025 - Scheme reopens for ongoing subscription, redemption, SIP, and SWP transactions

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Minimal. This is a fund-specific operational notice with no broader market implications.

**Investor Impact:** Low to moderate for investors specifically interested in DIVINITI EQUITY LONG SHORT FUND. The reopening provides access to an equity long-short strategy with multiple plan options (Regular/Direct) and investment modes (lump sum/SIP).

**Operational Impact:** Standard operational update for BSE StAR MF Platform members who can now process transactions for this fund scheme across all six variants.
