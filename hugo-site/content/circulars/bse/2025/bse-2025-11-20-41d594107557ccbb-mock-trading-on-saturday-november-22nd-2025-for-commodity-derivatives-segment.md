## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, November 22, 2025. The session is designed for trading members using third-party platforms or in-house developed systems through ETI APIs to test their trading applications. The mock session will simulate various market conditions including risk-reduction mode and trading halts. No new BOLT TWS release will be implemented. All trades executed during this session will not create any margin, pay-in/pay-out obligations, or legal rights and liabilities.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Commodity Derivatives segment on November 22, 2025
- Available for members using third-party empaneled vendor platforms or in-house systems via ETI APIs
- Testing will cover various functionalities including exceptional market conditions, risk-reduction mode, and trading halts
- No new BOLT TWS release will be implemented
- Mock trades will not attract any margin obligations or pay-in/pay-out requirements
- Mock trades will not create any rights or liabilities
- Members are requested to provide feedback by 5:00 PM on the same day

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine mock trading session for testing purposes.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are encouraged to participate actively in the mock trading session
- Members must submit feedback for the mock trading session by 5:00 PM on November 22, 2025
- Contact BSE Tech Support (022-22728053; bse.tech@bseindia.com) or Help Desk (022-45720400/600 & 022-69158500; bsehelp@bseindia.com) for queries or clarifications

## Important Dates

**November 22, 2025 (Saturday):**
- Log-in: 11:15 AM to 12:00 PM
- Continuous Trading: 12:00 PM to 4:00 PM
- Trade Modification: 4:00 PM to 4:10 PM
- Feedback submission deadline: 5:00 PM

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** None. This is a mock trading session with no real trading obligations.

**Operational Impact:** Low. Provides an opportunity for trading members to test their systems and familiarize themselves with platform functionalities under simulated market conditions including exceptional scenarios. Members using third-party platforms or in-house developed systems can validate their trading applications' performance and readiness.
