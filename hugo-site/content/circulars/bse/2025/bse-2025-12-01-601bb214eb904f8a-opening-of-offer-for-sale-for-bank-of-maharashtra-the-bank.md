## Summary

BSE has announced the opening of Offer for Sale (OFS) for Bank of Maharashtra. This circular notifies market participants about the OFS mechanism through which the Government of India or other selling shareholders will divest their stake in the bank through the stock exchange platform.

## Key Points

- Offer for Sale (OFS) for Bank of Maharashtra shares is being opened
- The sale will be conducted through the BSE stock exchange mechanism
- This represents a disinvestment opportunity in a public sector bank
- Trading members and investors can participate in the OFS process
- The OFS follows SEBI guidelines for share sales through stock exchange platforms

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. The circular follows existing SEBI OFS framework and guidelines.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure proper bidding procedures are followed during the OFS
- All participants must comply with SEBI OFS regulations
- Proper settlement and allocation processes must be adhered to
- Trading members should inform their clients about the OFS opportunity

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: December 1, 2025
- OFS opening date and specific timelines would be mentioned in the detailed schedule (refer to the PDF for exact dates and times)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** High - Offer for Sale of a public sector bank can result in significant trading volumes and potential price movement in Bank of Maharashtra shares.

**Investor Impact:** High - Provides opportunity for institutional and retail investors to acquire shares at potentially competitive prices through the OFS mechanism.

**Liquidity Impact:** The OFS will increase trading activity and liquidity in Bank of Maharashtra shares during the offer period.

**Government Disinvestment:** This appears to be part of the government's disinvestment program for public sector banks, reducing government stake in Bank of Maharashtra.
