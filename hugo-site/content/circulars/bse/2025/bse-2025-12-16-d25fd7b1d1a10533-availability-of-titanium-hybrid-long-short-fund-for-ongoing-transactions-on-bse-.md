## Summary

BSE has notified members that the TITANIUM HYBRID LONG-SHORT FUND scheme has been reopened for ongoing subscription and redemption transactions, along with SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) and SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) facilities on the BSE StAR MF Platform. This reopening is effective from December 17, 2025, following an earlier suspension referenced in Notice No. 20251121-68.

## Key Points

- TITANIUM HYBRID LONG-SHORT FUND reopened for ongoing transactions on BSE StAR MF Platform
- Available facilities include subscription, redemption, SIP, and SWP
- Six scheme variants available: Regular Plan (Growth, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment) and Direct Plan (Growth, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment)
- All variants are classified as Hybrid (NC) schemes
- Six ISINs listed: INF277K30013, INF277K30039, INF277K30021, INF277K30047, INF277K30062, INF277K30054
- References prior suspension notice 20251121-68

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an operational notification regarding the resumption of trading activities for a previously suspended mutual fund scheme.

## Compliance Requirements

BSE members should:
- Note the availability of the fund for transactions effective December 17, 2025
- Enable subscription, redemption, SIP, and SWP facilities for all six scheme variants
- Ensure systems are updated to process transactions in all listed ISINs

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: December 16, 2025
- **Effective Date**: December 17, 2025 - Fund reopens for ongoing transactions

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine operational notification affecting only investors and distributors of the TITANIUM HYBRID LONG-SHORT FUND scheme.

**Operational Impact**: Low. BSE members need to ensure their platforms are configured to accept transactions for this fund starting December 17, 2025.

**Investor Impact**: Positive for existing and potential investors in this fund, as it restores access to subscription, redemption, and systematic transaction facilities that were previously unavailable.
