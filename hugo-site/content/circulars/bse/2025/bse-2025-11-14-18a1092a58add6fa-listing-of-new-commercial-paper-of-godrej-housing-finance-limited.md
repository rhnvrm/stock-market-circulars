## Summary

BSE has listed two new series of Commercial Paper issued by Godrej Housing Finance Limited on private placement basis, effective November 14, 2025. The first series (ISIN: INE02JD14591) comprises 4,000 units of Rs. 5 lakh each totaling Rs. 200 crores, while the second series (ISIN: INE02JD14682) comprises 2,000 units totaling Rs. 100 crores. Both instruments are rated CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+, with ICICI Bank Limited acting as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- Two Commercial Paper series listed on BSE Debt segment
- Series 1: 4,000 units (Scrip Code: 728766, ISIN: INE02JD14591) allotted on November 14, 2025
- Series 2: 2,000 units (Scrip Code: 730561, ISIN: INE02JD14682) allotted on November 13, 2025
- Face value: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit for both series
- Credit Rating: CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+ for both instruments
- Trading only in dematerialized form
- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- ICICI Bank Limited is the Issuing and Paying Agent

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine listing notification.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under specified ISIN numbers
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size of 1 paise must be adhered to

## Important Dates

- **Series 1 (GHFL-13-2-26-CP)**:
  - Allotment Date: November 14, 2025
  - Redemption Date: February 13, 2026
  - Issue Price: Rs. 492,437.25

- **Series 2 (GHFL-12-2-26-CP)**:
  - Allotment Date: November 13, 2025
  - Redemption Date: February 12, 2026
  - Issue Price: Rs. 492,425.00

## Impact Assessment

This listing has minimal market impact as it pertains to short-term debt instruments in the debt segment. The Commercial Papers have 3-month tenures and are targeted at institutional investors. The high credit ratings (A1+) indicate strong creditworthiness of Godrej Housing Finance Limited. This is a routine corporate treasury operation with no implications for equity markets or broader trading operations.
