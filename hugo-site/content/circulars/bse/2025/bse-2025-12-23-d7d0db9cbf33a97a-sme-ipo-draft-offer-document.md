## Summary

BSE has made available the Draft Offer Document of Aureate Tradde Limited for public review and comments. The document was filed on December 23, 2025, and contains all disclosures required under Schedule VI of the ICDR Regulations. The draft will remain available on the exchange website for at least 21 days for stakeholders to submit their comments.

## Key Points

- Draft Offer Document filed by Aureate Tradde Limited on December 23, 2025
- Document contains all disclosures as specified under Schedule VI of ICDR Regulations
- Available on BSE SME website at www.bsesme.com/PublicIssues/SMEIPODRHP.aspx
- Public comments invited within 21 days from notice date
- Comments to be submitted to equity.ipo@bseindia.com
- Notice issued by Hardik Bhuta, Assistant Vice President

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard procedural filing as per existing ICDR Regulations requiring public disclosure and comment period for SME IPO draft offer documents.

## Compliance Requirements

- The draft offer document will be available for public review for a minimum of 21 days
- Stakeholders can submit comments on the draft offer document to equity.ipo@bseindia.com
- Comment period ends 21 days from December 23, 2025 (January 13, 2026)
- Document accessible through BSE SME website for transparency

## Important Dates

- **Filing Date**: December 23, 2025
- **Comment Period**: 21 days from December 23, 2025
- **Comment Deadline**: January 13, 2026
- **Notice Date**: December 23, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market impact as it pertains to a single SME company's IPO process. The draft offer document is a standard regulatory requirement for companies planning to list on the SME platform. Impact is limited to:

- Potential investors interested in Aureate Tradde Limited's IPO
- Market participants who wish to provide feedback on the draft document
- The company's ability to proceed with its public offering after incorporating feedback

No immediate trading or operational impact on existing market participants or listed securities.
