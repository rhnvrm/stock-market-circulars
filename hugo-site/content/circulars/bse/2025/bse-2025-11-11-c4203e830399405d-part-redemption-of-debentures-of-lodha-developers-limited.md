## Summary

LODHA DEVELOPERS LIMITED has announced part redemption of its Non-Convertible Debentures (ISIN: INE670K07307, Code: 976923) with a record date of November 14, 2025. Following the redemption, trading in these debentures will commence with a reduced face value of Rs. 92,966 per debenture effective from November 14, 2025 (Settlement No. DR-756/2025-2026).

## Key Points

- Company: LODHA DEVELOPERS LIMITED
- Debenture Series: LDL-Reset Rate-21-01-28-PVT
- ISIN: INE670K07307
- Scrip Code: 976923
- Record Date: November 14, 2025
- New Reduced Face Value: Rs. 92,966 per debenture
- Trading with reduced face value effective from: November 14, 2025
- Settlement Number: DR-756/2025-2026
- Segment: IRD (Indian Rupee Debt)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory framework changes. This is a standard corporate action for part redemption of debentures as per existing regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must note that trading in the debentures will be conducted with reduced face value effective November 14, 2025
- Trading Members should update their systems to reflect the new face value of Rs. 92,966 per debenture
- Settlement systems must be adjusted for Settlement No. DR-756/2025-2026

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: November 11, 2025
- **Record Date**: November 14, 2025
- **Effective Date for Reduced Face Value Trading**: November 14, 2025
- **Settlement Number**: DR-756/2025-2026

## Impact Assessment

**Debenture Holders**: Holders of these debentures on the record date will receive partial redemption proceeds. The remaining debentures will continue trading at the reduced face value.

**Trading Impact**: From November 14, 2025, all trades in this debenture series will be executed at the reduced face value of Rs. 92,966 per debenture instead of the original face value.

**Market Segment**: This affects only the IRD (Indian Rupee Debt) segment and is specific to this particular debenture series of LODHA DEVELOPERS LIMITED.

**Liquidity**: Part redemption may affect liquidity in these debentures as the outstanding amount is reduced.
