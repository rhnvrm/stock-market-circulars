## Summary

BSE has revised the price bands for 11 securities effective December 3, 2025. The price bands range from 2% to 20%, representing modifications from their existing levels. These changes are implemented as part of surveillance measures to control volatility and trading activity in these scrips.

## Key Points

- 11 securities will have revised price bands effective December 3, 2025
- Price bands range from 2% (most restrictive) to 20% (least restrictive)
- Three scrips have the tightest 2% band: Milestone Furniture, Permanent Magnets, and Shikhar Consultants
- Four scrips have 5% bands: Gilada Finance, Infinity Infoway, Shining Tools, and South West Pinnacle Exploration
- Two scrips have 10% bands: Droneacharya Aerial Innovations and Parmeshwar Metal
- Two scrips have 10% bands: Shreeshay Engineers
- One scrip (Haryana Capfin) has the widest 20% band
- Trading members can contact bse.surv@bseindia.com for clarifications

## Regulatory Changes

Price band revisions are regulatory tools used by the exchange to:
- Control excessive volatility in securities
- Prevent unusual price movements
- Protect investor interests through enhanced surveillance
- Manage market manipulation risks

The tighter bands indicate these securities are under enhanced surveillance measures.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must update their systems to reflect the new price bands
- All trades in these scrips must comply with the revised price limits from December 3, 2025
- Trading terminals and risk management systems should be configured accordingly
- Members should ensure order placement adheres to new band restrictions

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** December 2, 2025
- **Effective Date:** December 3, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Trading Impact:**
- Reduced intraday volatility for affected securities
- Limited price discovery during high volatility periods
- Potential for circuit filter hits during significant news or events
- Reduced speculative trading activity

**Liquidity Impact:**
- Tighter bands (especially 2% and 5%) may reduce trading volumes
- Market makers and arbitrageurs face increased operational constraints
- Investors may experience difficulty in executing large orders at desired prices

**Investor Impact:**
- Enhanced price protection for retail investors
- Reduced risk of manipulation and artificial price movements
- May limit exit opportunities during adverse conditions

**Market Sentiment:**
Price band tightening typically signals exchange concerns about specific securities and may indicate underlying issues with trading patterns, corporate developments, or surveillance findings.
