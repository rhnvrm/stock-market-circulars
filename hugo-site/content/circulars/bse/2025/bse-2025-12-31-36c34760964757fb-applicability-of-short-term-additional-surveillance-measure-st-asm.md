## Summary

BSE has released the updated list of securities subject to Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective January 01, 2026. The circular identifies 9 securities newly entering the ST-ASM framework, 1 security moving to higher stage ASM, and 4 securities exiting the framework. The ST-ASM framework applies 5/15/30 days staged surveillance measures to securities exhibiting unusual price movements or volatility.

## Key Points

- 9 securities shortlisted for ST-ASM framework from January 01, 2026
- Includes 2 SME scrips (Riddhi Display Equipments Ltd, Waa Solar Ltd)
- 4 securities aligned with NSE surveillance measures
- Pulsar International Ltd continues in ST-ASM but moves to higher stage
- 4 securities moving out of ST-ASM framework
- Shree Metalloys Ltd exits ST-ASM due to inclusion in ESM Framework
- Consolidated list includes 14+ securities across various ST-ASM stages

## Regulatory Framework

The Short Term ASM framework operates on a 5/15/30 days surveillance mechanism with multiple stages (I, II, III, etc.) based on severity of price movements or volatility patterns. Securities in ST-ASM may face additional trading restrictions including price bands, additional margin requirements, or reduced trading frequency.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** January 01, 2026
- All changes to ST-ASM classification become applicable from market opening on this date
