## Summary

BSE has listed privately placed debt securities of Brookfield India Real Estate Trust on its Debt Market Segment effective December 23, 2025. The securities carry a 7.06% annual interest rate with quarterly payments, rated CRISIL AAA/Stable and ICRA AAA/Stable, and mature on December 20, 2030.

## Key Points

- Issuer: Brookfield India Real Estate Trust (BIRET)
- Scrip Code: 977393, Scrip ID: 706BIREI30
- ISIN: INE0FDU07018
- Quantity: 200,000 units with market lot of 1
- Face Value: Rs. 100,000 per unit
- Issue Price: Rs. 100,000 per unit
- Date of Allotment: December 22, 2025
- Interest Rate: 7.06% per annum (paid quarterly)
- Credit Rating: CRISIL AAA/Stable, ICRA AAA/Stable
- Trading in dematerialized form only
- Tick size: 1 paise

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for privately placed debt instruments.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE0FDU07018
- Members should refer to the Placement Memorandum available at https://www.bseindia.com/markets/debt/memorandum_data.aspx for complete details
- For clarifications, trading members may contact BSE debt department on 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: December 22, 2025
- **Listing/Trading Start**: December 23, 2025
- **First Interest Payment**: December 31, 2025
- **Interest Payment Frequency**: Quarterly from December 31, 2025 to December 20, 2030
- **Redemption Date**: December 20, 2030

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine listing of privately placed debt securities with no impact on equity markets or broader trading activities.

**Investor Impact**: Low to medium for debt market participants. The AAA credit rating indicates high creditworthiness, and the 7.06% coupon rate provides clarity for fixed income investors interested in REIT debt instruments.

**Operational Impact**: Limited to debt market trading members who need to be aware of the new scrip code, ISIN, and trading parameters. The dematerialized-only trading requirement is standard for debt securities.
