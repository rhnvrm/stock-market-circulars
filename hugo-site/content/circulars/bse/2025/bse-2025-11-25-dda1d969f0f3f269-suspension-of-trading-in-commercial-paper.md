## Summary

BSE has announced the suspension of trading in commercial paper of 13 companies effective from November 27 to December 4, 2025. The suspensions are due to companies fixing record dates for redemption of their commercial paper instruments. Trading members are advised not to deal in these securities from their respective no-dealings dates.

## Key Points

- 13 commercial paper instruments will face trading suspension
- Suspensions are for redemption purposes
- No dealings period ranges from November 27 to December 4, 2025
- Multiple instruments from Julius Baer Capital (India) Private Limited included
- Major companies affected include Bajaj Finance, HDFC Securities, Reliance Retail Ventures, and Grasim Industries
- Each security has specific ISIN codes and BSE codes assigned

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the specified commercial paper from the respective no-dealings dates
- All transactions must cease before the record dates fixed by the companies
- Trading members should update their systems to reflect the trading suspension for these securities

## Important Dates

- **November 27, 2025**: Record date and no dealings start for 2 securities
- **December 1, 2025**: Record date and no dealings start for 1 security
- **December 2, 2025**: Record date and no dealings start for 2 securities
- **December 3, 2025**: Record date and no dealings start for 4 securities
- **December 4, 2025**: Record date and no dealings start for 4 securities

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine corporate action affecting the debt market segment. The impact is limited to holders and traders of these specific commercial paper instruments. The staggered redemption dates over an 8-day period indicate normal business operations. Market participants holding these securities will receive redemption proceeds on their respective record dates. No broader market disruption is expected as this is standard practice for maturing commercial paper instruments.
