## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper securities issued by Reliance Jio Infocomm Limited on private placement basis, effective December 19, 2025. The listing comprises 23,000 Commercial Papers with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, issued at Rs. 4,92,603 per unit. These debt instruments carry CARE A1+ and CRISIL A1+ credit ratings and mature on March 18, 2026.

## Key Points

- Issuer: Reliance Jio Infocomm Limited
- Security Type: Commercial Paper (Private Placement)
- Quantity Listed: 23,000 units
- Face Value: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- Issue Price: Rs. 4,92,603 per unit
- Total Issue Size: Rs. 11,500 crore
- Scrip Code: 730820
- Scrip ID: RJIL181225
- ISIN: INE110L14UK6
- Credit Rating: CARE A1+, CRISIL A1+
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Tick Size: 1 paise
- Issuing and Paying Agent: Axis Bank Ltd

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for new debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE110L14UK6
- Trading shall take place in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Securities will be traded on BSE Debt segment with tick size of 1 paise
- Trading members requiring clarification may contact BSE Debt Department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- Allotment Date: December 18, 2025
- Listing Date: December 19, 2025
- Redemption Date: March 18, 2026
- Tenure: 90 days

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine commercial paper listing with minimal market impact. The securities are issued by Reliance Jio Infocomm Limited, a well-established subsidiary of Reliance Industries, and carry top-tier credit ratings (A1+). The listing provides institutional investors with short-term debt investment options backed by high credit quality. Trading is restricted to dematerialized form with minimum lot size of Rs. 5 lakhs, making it accessible primarily to institutional and high-net-worth investors. No impact on equity markets or retail investors.
