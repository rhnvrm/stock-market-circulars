## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) segment on Saturday, November 08th, 2025. This session is designed for trading members to test their trading applications, including third-party platforms and in-house systems using ETI APIs. The mock trading will cover various functionalities including exceptional market conditions, risk-reduction mode, and trading halts. No actual trading obligations will arise from this session.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Saturday, November 08th, 2025 for EGR segment
- No new BOLT TWS Release will be implemented
- Trading members can test third-party trading platforms or in-house developed systems through ETI APIs
- Mock session will include testing of exceptional market conditions, risk-reduction mode, and trading halt scenarios
- No margin obligations, pay-in/pay-out obligations, or legal rights/liabilities will result from mock trades
- Feedback requested from all participants by 5:00 pm on the same day

## Trading Session Schedule

**November 08th, 2025 Timings:**

- **Log-in:** 11:15 am to 12:00 pm
- **Pre-Open Order Entry Period:** 12:00 pm to 12:08 pm
- **Matching Period:** 12:08 pm to 12:15 pm
- **Continuous Trading:** 12:15 pm to 04:00 pm
- **Block Deal Window:** 01:00 pm to 01:15 pm
- **Closing:** 04:00 pm to 04:10 pm
- **Post-Closing:** 04:10 pm to 04:20 pm

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are encouraged to participate actively in the mock trading session
- All members should provide feedback by 5:00 pm on November 08th, 2025
- Members should test their respective trading applications for various functionalities during the session

## Important Dates

- **Mock Trading Date:** Saturday, November 08th, 2025
- **Feedback Deadline:** 5:00 pm on November 08th, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine testing session with no actual market impact. The mock trading session provides an opportunity for market participants to familiarize themselves with the EGR segment trading platform and test their systems under simulated market conditions. No real trades, settlements, or obligations will result from this session. The impact is limited to operational testing and system readiness verification.
