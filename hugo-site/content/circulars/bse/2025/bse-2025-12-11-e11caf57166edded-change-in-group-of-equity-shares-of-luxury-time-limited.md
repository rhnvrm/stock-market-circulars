## Summary

BSE has announced that the equity shares of LUXURY TIME LIMITED (Scrip Code: 544635) will be transferred from the Trade for Trade segment (MT Group) to the Rolling segment (M Group) effective Friday, December 26, 2025. This change follows the company's SME IPO listing and represents a standard transition to normal trading operations.

## Key Points

- Stock: LUXURY TIME LIMITED (Scrip Code: 544635)
- Current Classification: MT Group (Trade for Trade segment)
- New Classification: M Group (Rolling segment)
- Effective Date: Friday, December 26, 2025
- Segment: SME
- Notice follows previous Exchange Notice No. 20251210-77 dated December 10, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

The trading mechanism for LUXURY TIME LIMITED will change from Trade for Trade (T2T) settlement to Rolling settlement. This means:
- Shares will move from MT Group to M Group
- Normal rolling settlement cycle will apply instead of compulsory delivery-based trading
- Enhanced liquidity and flexibility for traders

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members should note the group change effective December 26, 2025
- Ensure systems and processes are updated to reflect the new group classification
- For queries, Trading Members may contact Mr. Anurag Jain on Tel. No. 022-2272 8822

## Important Dates

- **December 10, 2025**: Previous notice issued (Notice No. 20251210-77)
- **December 11, 2025**: Current notice issued
- **December 26, 2025**: Effective date for group change from MT to M Group

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - The transition from T2T to rolling settlement will increase trading flexibility and potentially improve liquidity for LUXURY TIME LIMITED shares. This is a positive development for the stock as it indicates successful completion of the initial post-IPO surveillance period.

**Operational Impact**: Low - Standard procedural change that Trading Members routinely handle. No significant operational disruption expected.

**Investor Impact**: Medium - Existing and potential investors will benefit from improved liquidity and the ability to conduct intraday trading, as the stock will no longer be restricted to compulsory delivery-based trading.
