## Summary

BSE has suspended trading in commercial paper of 16 companies effective from their respective record dates set for redemption purposes. The suspension dates range from December 5 to December 10, 2025. Trading members are explicitly advised not to deal in these commercial papers from the specified dates.

## Key Points

- 16 companies have fixed record dates for commercial paper redemption
- Trading suspension effective from respective record dates
- Earliest suspension: December 5, 2025 (2 issuers)
- Latest suspension: December 10, 2025 (14 issuers)
- Trading members prohibited from dealing in listed commercial papers
- Notice issued under Corporate Actions category for Debt segment

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational circular enforcing existing trading suspension protocols for securities approaching redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members**: Must not execute trades in the specified commercial papers from their respective "No Dealings From" dates
- **Market Participants**: Should note the ISIN codes and scrip codes of affected commercial papers to avoid transaction attempts
- **Brokers**: Must update their trading systems to block orders for these securities

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Limited to debt market participants holding or trading these specific commercial papers. No equity market impact.

**Operational Impact**: Trading platforms and broker systems must implement blocks on these 16 ISINs to prevent inadvertent trading during the suspension period.

**Investor Impact**: Holders of these commercial papers will receive redemption proceeds as per the terms. No secondary market liquidity available from the suspension dates until redemption completion.

**Sector Coverage**: Diverse sectors affected including financial services, real estate, media, cement, steel, retail, and manufacturing, indicating broad commercial paper issuance activity across industries.
