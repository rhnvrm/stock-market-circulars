## Summary

BSE has published the Demat Auction list for Settlement No. 759 dated November 20, 2025. This circular contains a comprehensive list of securities and their respective quantities that are part of this auction settlement. The list includes 102 different securities across various sectors including banking, power, pharmaceuticals, technology, and manufacturing.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 759
- Settlement Date: November 20, 2025
- Total securities listed: 102 different scrips
- Quantities range from 1 share to 54,435 shares depending on the security
- Covers diverse sectors including banking, pharmaceuticals, technology, real estate, and manufacturing

## Securities Involved

The auction includes securities from major companies such as:
- Banking: Canara Bank (100 shares), Punjab National Bank (1 share), State Bank of India (50 shares), Union Bank (12 shares)
- Power & Energy: Tata Power (270 shares), Oil and Natural Gas (1 share)
- Technology: Intellect Design (450 shares), Infronics Systems (55 shares)
- Pharmaceuticals: Emcure Pharma (15,000 shares), Orient Cerafin (5 shares)
- Manufacturing: Bharti Airtel (2 shares), Siemens (2 shares), Havells India (227 shares)

Notably high quantities include:
- Shanti Education: 54,435 shares
- Spright Agro: 10,002 shares
- Emcure Pharma: 15,000 shares
- SECUREKLOUD: 6,643 shares

## Compliance Requirements

Participants in the demat auction settlement must ensure proper settlement of the listed securities according to BSE's auction settlement procedures and timelines.

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: November 20, 2025
- Settlement Number: 759

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with limited market-wide impact. The auction settlement affects specific quantities of shares across multiple companies, primarily impacting participants who have failed to deliver securities in regular settlements. The quantities involved are relatively small for most securities, suggesting minimal market disruption. The auction mechanism ensures proper settlement and maintains market integrity.
