## Summary

MANBA FINANCE LIMITED has announced part redemption and interest payment for two series of non-convertible debentures. The company has fixed record dates for this corporate action, and trading in these debentures will commence with reduced face values from October 10, 2025.

## Key Points

- Two series of debentures are subject to part redemption and interest payment
- MFL-12.6%-27-12-25-PVT (ISIN: INE939X07101) with record date October 12, 2025
- MFL-11.75%-26-6-26-PVT (ISIN: INE939X07135) with record date October 10, 2025
- Both debentures will trade with reduced face values effective October 10, 2025
- Settlement number: DR-733/2025-2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced in this circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the reduced face values for both debenture series
- Trading with reduced face values begins October 10, 2025
- Record dates must be observed for determining eligible debenture holders

## Important Dates

- **October 10, 2025**: Record date for MFL-11.75%-26-6-26-PVT debentures
- **October 10, 2025**: Effective date for trading with reduced face value for both series (Settlement DR-733/2025-2026)
- **October 12, 2025**: Record date for MFL-12.6%-27-12-25-PVT debentures

## Debenture Details

### Series 1: MFL-12.6%-27-12-25-PVT
- **ISIN**: INE939X07101
- **Code**: 975290
- **Record Date**: October 12, 2025
- **Reduced Face Value**: Rs. 8,333 per debenture
- **Purpose**: Part redemption and interest payment

### Series 2: MFL-11.75%-26-6-26-PVT
- **ISIN**: INE939X07135
- **Code**: 975775
- **Record Date**: October 10, 2025
- **Reduced Face Value**: Rs. 44,440 per debenture
- **Purpose**: Part redemption and interest payment

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine corporate action affecting only holders of specific MANBA FINANCE LIMITED debentures. The part redemption will reduce the outstanding face value of these debt instruments. Trading members dealing in these securities should update their systems to reflect the new face values from the effective date. Market-wide impact is minimal as this affects a limited set of debt securities.
