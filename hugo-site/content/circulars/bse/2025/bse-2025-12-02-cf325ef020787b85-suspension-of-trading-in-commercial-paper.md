## Summary

BSE has suspended trading in commercial paper of 16 companies effective December 5-10, 2025. The suspension is due to record dates fixed for redemption of commercial papers. Trading members are advised not to deal in the specified commercial papers from the respective no-dealing dates.

## Key Points

- 16 companies have fixed record dates for commercial paper redemption
- Trading suspension ranges from December 5 to December 10, 2025
- Notice issued under Corporate Actions category for Debt segment
- Trading members must not deal in these commercial papers from specified dates
- Companies span multiple sectors including financial services, real estate, cement, steel, and retail

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine corporate action notification for commercial paper redemptions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute trades in the listed commercial papers from the specified no-dealing dates
- Members must ensure their systems and operations reflect the trading suspension for affected securities
- Compliance with record date requirements for redemption purposes

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects liquidity in commercial paper market for select issuers during redemption period. Short-term impact only.

**Operational Impact:** Trading desks dealing in debt instruments must update their systems to prevent trades in suspended commercial papers. Investors holding these instruments should note the redemption schedule.

**Sectoral Spread:** Diverse impact across financial services (NBFC, securities firms), manufacturing (cement, steel), consumer goods (retail, jewelry), real estate, and media sectors.

**Duration:** Limited 5-6 day suspension period per security, indicating routine maturity-based redemptions rather than distress situations.
