## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction list for Settlement No. 751 dated November 10, 2025. The auction includes securities from various companies where delivery obligations were not met, requiring auction of shares to complete settlement. The list contains approximately 120+ scrip codes with varying quantities ranging from 1 share to 33,500 shares.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 751
- Settlement Date: November 10, 2025
- Total number of securities involved: 120+ scrips
- Quantities range from single shares to thousands of shares
- Largest auction quantities: WARDWIZARD I (33,500 shares), STANDARD CAP (25,000 shares), VODAFONE IDE (22,000 shares)
- Auction conducted to fulfill delivery obligations for failed settlements

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational circular for routine demat auction settlements.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members with failed delivery obligations must participate in the auction process
- Settlement of auctioned securities must be completed as per BSE settlement procedures
- Members responsible for short deliveries will bear auction costs and penalties as applicable

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: November 10, 2025
- Auction Settlement Number: 751

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Low - This is a routine settlement mechanism affecting only specific trading members who failed to deliver securities.

**Operational Impact**: Low to Medium - Affects brokers and trading members with delivery failures who must participate in the auction to close their positions.

**Investor Impact**: Low - Individual investors are generally not directly impacted unless their broker had delivery failures. The auction mechanism ensures settlement completion and maintains market integrity.

**Notable High-Volume Auctions**:
- WARDWIZARD I: 33,500 shares
- STANDARD CAP: 25,000 shares
- VODAFONE IDE: 22,000 shares
- SAGILITY: 15,433 shares
- AERPACE: 6,462 shares
- HB STOCKHOLD: 5,770 shares

The auction mechanism is a critical market operation tool that ensures settlement discipline and protects investors from delivery failures.
