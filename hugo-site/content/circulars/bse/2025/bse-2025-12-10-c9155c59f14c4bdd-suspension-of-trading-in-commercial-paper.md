## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20251210-7 informing trading members about the suspension of trading in commercial paper of 10 companies due to upcoming redemption. The suspension is effective from the respective record dates set between December 12-15, 2025. Trading members are advised not to deal in these commercial papers from the specified no-dealings dates.

## Key Points

- 10 companies have fixed record dates for commercial paper redemption
- Trading suspension applies to debt segment
- Notice issued by Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President â€“ Listing Compliance and Operations
- Notice references: DR-776/2025-2026 and DR-777/2025-2026
- All affected instruments are commercial papers with specific ISIN codes

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine corporate action notification for commercial paper redemptions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the listed commercial papers from their respective no-dealings dates
- Trading members must observe the record dates for redemption purposes
- Members should update their systems to prevent transactions in these instruments from the suspension dates

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects debt market liquidity for specific commercial paper instruments

**Operational Impact:** Medium - trading members must ensure compliance with no-dealings restriction across 10 different commercial paper issues

**Investor Impact:** Low to Medium - investors holding these commercial papers will receive redemption proceeds as per scheduled maturity, but cannot trade these instruments from suspension dates

**Affected Entities:** Debt market participants, institutional investors, treasury desks, and trading members dealing in commercial paper of the listed issuers including financial services companies (SBICAP, Godrej Finance, Julius Baer, HDFC Securities, Bajaj Financial Securities, DSP Finance), infrastructure trust (Vertis), and corporates (Tata Steel, Redington)
