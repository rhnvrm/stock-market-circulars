## Summary

BSE has issued an oversubscription notice for the Offer for Sale (OFS) of Indian Overseas Bank (IOB) shares. The OFS represents a government disinvestment initiative where shares of the public sector bank are being offered to institutional and retail investors through the stock exchange platform.

## Key Points

- Offer for Sale announced for Indian Overseas Bank shares
- Oversubscription noticed, indicating strong investor demand
- OFS conducted through BSE electronic platform
- Part of government's disinvestment program for public sector banks
- Separate categories for retail and institutional investors

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a market operation notice regarding OFS activity.

## Compliance Requirements

- Participants must follow OFS bidding guidelines as per SEBI regulations
- Institutional and retail investors must bid through their respective brokers
- Settlement and allocation to be done as per OFS framework
- Successful bidders to receive allotment confirmation and shares credited to demat accounts

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: December 17, 2025
- OFS Opening Date: As specified in the offer document
- OFS Closing Date: As specified in the offer document
- Settlement and Allotment: As per standard T+2 settlement cycle or as specified

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** High - The oversubscription of Indian Overseas Bank's OFS indicates robust investor appetite for public sector banking stocks. Strong subscription levels typically support price stability and liquidity.

**Investor Impact:** High - Retail and institutional investors participating in the OFS have shown significant interest, which may influence secondary market trading in IOB shares.

**Sector Impact:** Medium to High - Successful OFS of a PSU bank demonstrates investor confidence in the banking sector and government disinvestment program, potentially encouraging similar initiatives.
