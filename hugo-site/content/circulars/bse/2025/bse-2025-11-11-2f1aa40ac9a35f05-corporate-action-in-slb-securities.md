## Summary

BSE has issued a circular detailing corporate actions for 23 securities in the Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) segment. All listed companies are declaring dividends with corresponding record dates, ex-dates, and shut periods between November 11-26, 2025. The shut periods prevent new SLB transactions from being initiated during the record date period to ensure proper dividend entitlement.

## Key Points

- 23 companies announcing dividend payments with SLB shut periods
- Record dates range from November 11, 2025 to November 26, 2025
- Ex-dates align with record dates for all securities
- Shut periods are one day before the record date through the record date itself
- One exception: MOIL Limited has ex-date on November 14 with record date on November 15
- All corporate actions are dividend-related with no stock splits, bonuses, or rights issues

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine operational circular informing market participants of upcoming corporate actions in SLB securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must not initiate new SLB transactions during the specified shut periods for each security
- Existing SLB positions must be managed considering the dividend record dates
- Brokers and clearing members should inform clients about SLB restrictions during shut periods
- Settlement obligations must be completed before shut period commencement

## Impact Assessment

**Market Operations Impact:** Medium - affects liquidity in SLB segment for 23 securities during shut periods, but durations are short (1-3 days per security).

**Investor Impact:** Medium - investors holding these securities will receive dividends if held through record date. SLB participants must plan positions accordingly to avoid settlement failures.

**Trading Impact:** Low to Medium - no trading halts, but SLB functionality temporarily restricted for each security during its shut period.

**Sectoral Coverage:** Broad impact across multiple sectors including shipping, energy, FMCG, auto, media, metals, financial services, and infrastructure, indicating healthy dividend distribution across the market.
