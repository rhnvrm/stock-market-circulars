## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper issued by Bajaj Financial Securities Limited on private placement basis effective November 4, 2025. The instrument comprises 2500 units of Rs. 5 lakh face value each, totaling Rs. 125 crore, with a 92-day tenor maturing on February 3, 2026. The securities are rated CRISIL A1+ and IND A1+, with ICICI Bank Limited serving as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- Security Type: Commercial Paper issued on private placement basis
- Face Value: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- Total Quantity: 2500 units (Rs. 125 crore aggregate)
- Issue Price: Rs. 4,91,757.50 per unit (1.65% discount)
- Allotment Date: November 3, 2025
- Maturity Date: February 3, 2026
- Tenor: 92 days
- Scrip Code: 730487
- Scrip ID: BFSL31125
- ISIN: INE01C314DD3
- Credit Ratings: CRISIL A1+, IND A1+
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Tick Size: 1 paise
- Issuing and Paying Agent: ICICI Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notice for privately placed debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE01C314DD3
- Trading shall be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Securities will be traded on the BSE Debt segment
- All trades must comply with standard debt market trading protocols

## Important Dates

- **November 3, 2025**: Allotment date
- **November 4, 2025**: Listing and trading commencement date
- **February 3, 2026**: Redemption date

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine short-term debt instrument listing with no impact on equity markets or broader market operations.

**Operational Impact**: Standard listing procedure for debt segment participants. Trading members can access the securities through normal debt trading channels.

**Investor Impact**: Limited to institutional investors participating in the commercial paper market. The high credit ratings (A1+) indicate strong creditworthiness and low credit risk for the 92-day tenor.
