## Summary

BSE has announced that equity shares of GLOBAL OCEAN LOGISTICS INDIA LIMITED (Scrip Code: 544665), an SME IPO listing, will be transferred from the Trade for Trade segment (MT Group) to the Rolling settlement segment (M Group) effective January 08, 2026. This change follows the Exchange's previous notice dated December 23, 2025.

## Key Points

- Company: GLOBAL OCEAN LOGISTICS INDIA LIMITED (SME IPO)
- Scrip Code: 544665
- Current Segment: Trade for Trade segment (MT Group)
- New Segment: Rolling segment (M Group)
- Effective Date: Thursday, January 08, 2026
- Notice Reference: Continuation of Notice No. 20251223-35 dated December 23, 2025
- Contact: Mr. Anurag Jain, Tel: 022-2272 8822

## Regulatory Changes

The trading mechanism for GLOBAL OCEAN LOGISTICS INDIA LIMITED shares will change from Trade for Trade settlement to Rolling settlement. This represents a relaxation of trading restrictions, as Trade for Trade segments typically have stricter settlement requirements compared to Rolling settlement segments.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading Members are required to:
- Update their systems to reflect the group change from MT Group to M Group
- Ensure compliance with Rolling segment trading norms effective January 08, 2026
- Contact Mr. Anurag Jain for clarifications or further details

## Important Dates

- **December 23, 2025**: Initial notice (No. 20251223-35) issued
- **December 24, 2025**: Current notice issued
- **January 08, 2026**: Effective date for segment transfer from MT Group to M Group

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: The transfer from Trade for Trade to Rolling settlement is generally positive for the stock, as it indicates improved market confidence and compliance. Rolling settlement typically offers better liquidity and trading flexibility compared to the more restrictive Trade for Trade segment.

**Trading Impact**: Investors and trading members will benefit from enhanced liquidity and the ability to engage in intraday trading and carry forward positions, which are not available in the Trade for Trade segment.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members must update their internal systems and processes to accommodate the group change and ensure seamless transition to Rolling settlement norms.
