## Summary

BSE has published the trading and settlement programme for the Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) segment for January 2026. The circular provides detailed settlement numbers, trading dates, and pay-in/pay-out dates for 21 settlements scheduled throughout the month.

## Key Points

- Settlement schedule covers period from January 1, 2026 to January 31, 2026
- Total of 21 settlements scheduled (SL-189/25-26 through SL-209/25-26)
- Depository settlement numbers range from 2526189 to 2526209
- Trading date, 6A7A entry, and confirmation occur on the same day for each settlement
- Pay-in/pay-out occurs on T+1 basis (next business day)
- Pay-in time is 08:00 a.m.
- Members must submit pay-in instructions to Depositories/Banks by 07:50 a.m.
- No trading on weekends (Saturdays and Sundays)
- Settlement breaks on January 26, 2026 (Republic Day holiday)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine operational circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must submit pay-in instructions to Depositories/Banks latest by 07:50 a.m. on pay-in day
- Pay-in must be completed by 08:00 a.m. on the designated pay-in/pay-out date
- Members need to adhere to the published settlement schedule for SLB transactions

## Important Dates

- **Settlement Period**: January 1, 2026 to January 31, 2026
- **First Settlement**: SL-189/25-26 - Trading on January 1, 2026, Pay-in/Pay-out on January 2, 2026
- **Last Settlement**: SL-209/25-26 - Trading on January 30, 2026, Pay-in/Pay-out on February 2, 2026
- **No Trading Days**: January 4-5, 11-12, 18-19, 24-26, 31, 2026
- **Pay-in Time**: Daily at 08:00 a.m. (instructions by 07:50 a.m.)

## Impact Assessment

**Operational Impact**: Low - This is a standard monthly settlement calendar that enables market participants to plan their SLB segment activities for January 2026.

**Market Impact**: Low - No impact on trading rules or market structure. Provides operational clarity for securities lending and borrowing transactions.

**Member Impact**: Low - Routine information that members use for operational planning. No changes to existing procedures or timelines.
