## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, November 08, 2025. This session is designed for trading members using third-party platforms, empaneled vendor systems, or in-house developed systems through ETI APIs to test their trading applications and familiarize themselves with various functionalities, including exceptional market conditions like risk-reduction mode and trading halts. There will be no new BOLT TWS Release for this session.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Commodity Derivatives segment on November 08, 2025
- Available for members using third-party trading platforms, empaneled vendors, or in-house systems via ETI APIs
- Testing includes various functionalities: risk-reduction mode, trading halt, and exceptional market conditions
- No new BOLT TWS Release
- Mock trades will not create any margin obligations, pay-in/pay-out obligations, rights, or liabilities
- Members are encouraged to provide feedback by 5:00 PM on the session day

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine testing and familiarization session.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are requested to participate actively in the mock trading session
- Members should provide feedback by 5:00 PM on the day of the mock session
- Contact BSE Tech Support (022-22728053, bse.tech@bseindia.com) or Help Desk (022-45720400/600, 022-69158500, bsehelp@bseindia.com) for queries or clarifications

## Important Dates

- **November 08, 2025**: Mock Trading Session
  - Log-in: 11:15 AM to 12:00 PM
  - Continuous Trading: 12:00 PM to 04:00 PM
  - Trade Modification: 04:00 PM to 04:10 PM
- **Feedback Deadline**: 5:00 PM on November 08, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This mock trading session has minimal market impact as it is purely for testing and familiarization purposes. No actual trading obligations, margin requirements, or settlement processes will be triggered. The session provides an opportunity for trading members to ensure their systems are functioning properly and to test various market scenarios in a controlled environment. Active participation is encouraged to help BSE improve system robustness and efficiency.
