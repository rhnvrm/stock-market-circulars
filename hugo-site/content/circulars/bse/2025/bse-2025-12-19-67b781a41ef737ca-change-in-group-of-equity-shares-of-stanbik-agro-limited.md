## Summary

BSE has announced that equity shares of STANBIK AGRO LIMITED (Scrip Code: 544659), currently trading in the Trade for Trade segment (MT Group), will be transferred to the Rolling segment under M Group effective January 05, 2026. This change follows the company's SME IPO listing and represents a transition to normal trading operations.

## Key Points

- STANBIK AGRO LIMITED shares (Scrip Code: 544659) currently trade in Trade for Trade segment (MT Group)
- Shares will be transferred to Rolling segment (M Group) from January 05, 2026
- This follows Exchange Notice No. 20251218-50 dated December 18, 2025
- The company is an SME IPO listing on BSE
- Trading Members can contact Mr. Anurag Jain (022-2272 8822) for further details

## Regulatory Changes

The trading segment classification for STANBIK AGRO LIMITED is being upgraded from Trade for Trade (MT Group) to Rolling segment (M Group). This change indicates the stock has met the criteria for normal rolling settlement trading, moving away from the restricted Trade for Trade framework typically applied to newly listed or surveillance stocks.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must update their systems to reflect the new group classification (M Group) for Scrip Code 544659
- Members should prepare for rolling settlement procedures applicable to M Group stocks
- All trading activities must comply with Rolling segment rules effective from the implementation date

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: December 19, 2025
- **Effective Date**: January 05, 2026 (Monday)
- **Reference Notice**: No. 20251218-50 dated December 18, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This segment transfer is positive for STANBIK AGRO LIMITED as it indicates improved trading conditions and liquidity. The move from Trade for Trade (which requires delivery-based trading) to Rolling segment allows for more flexible trading including intraday trading. This typically leads to increased trading volumes and liquidity. For investors, this provides better entry and exit opportunities. Trading Members will need to adjust their risk management and position tracking systems to accommodate the new segment classification.
