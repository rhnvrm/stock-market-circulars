## Summary

BSE has announced the transfer of equity shares of DACHEPALLI PUBLISHERS LIMITED (Scrip Code: 544667) from the Trade for Trade segment (MT Group) to the Rolling segment (M Group), effective January 13, 2026. This change follows the company's SME IPO listing and represents a transition to more regular trading operations.

## Key Points

- Company: DACHEPALLI PUBLISHERS LIMITED - SME IPO
- Scrip Code: 544667
- Current Segment: Trade for Trade segment (MT Group)
- New Segment: Rolling segment (M Group)
- Effective Date: Tuesday, January 13, 2026
- Notice Reference: Continuation of Exchange Notice No. 20251229-31 dated December 29, 2025
- Contact Person: Mr. Anurag Jain, Tel: 022-2272 8822
- Issued By: Hardik Bhuta, Assistant Vice President

## Regulatory Changes

The equity shares will be shifted from MT Group (Trade for Trade) to M Group (Rolling segment). This migration allows for:
- Regular rolling settlement mechanism instead of compulsory delivery-based trading
- Enhanced liquidity potential for the stock
- Standard market operations typical of established listings

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must update their systems to reflect the group change
- Members should contact Mr. Anurag Jain for clarifications or operational details
- Trading parameters and risk management systems need adjustment for rolling segment operations

## Important Dates

- **December 29, 2025**: Initial notice (No. 20251229-31) issued
- **December 30, 2025**: Current circular issued
- **January 13, 2026**: Effective date for transfer to Rolling segment (M Group)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - The transition from Trade for Trade to Rolling segment typically increases liquidity and trading flexibility, potentially attracting more market participants.

**Operational Impact:** Trading members must update their systems and processes to accommodate the group change. The shift enables intraday trading and standard settlement cycles, which may increase trading volumes.

**Investor Impact:** Investors will benefit from improved liquidity and the ability to engage in regular market transactions rather than being restricted to compulsory delivery-based trading.
