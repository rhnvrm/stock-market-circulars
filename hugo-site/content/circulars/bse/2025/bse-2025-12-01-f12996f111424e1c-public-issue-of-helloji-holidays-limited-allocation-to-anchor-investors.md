## Summary

Helloji Holidays Limited announced the allocation of 2,64,000 equity shares to anchor investors as part of its SME IPO. The Board of Directors, in consultation with the Book Running Lead Manager, finalized the allocation on December 01, 2025. The anchor investor allocation price was set at Rs. 118 per equity share, including a share premium of Rs. 108 per share, resulting in total allocation amount of Rs. 3,11,52,000.

## Key Points

- Total equity shares allocated to anchor investors: 2,64,000 shares
- Allocation price: Rs. 118 per equity share (including share premium of Rs. 108)
- Total amount allocated: Rs. 3,11,52,000
- Two anchor investors participated with equal allocation of 50% each
- Rajasthan Global Securities Private Limited allocated 1,32,000 shares for Rs. 1,55,76,000
- Longthrive Capital VCC-Trendview Capital Fund allocated 1,32,000 shares for Rs. 1,55,76,000
- Company CIN: U63040DL2012PLC452865
- Company registered office: WA89, 3rd Floor, Shakarpur, Delhi-110092

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a standard disclosure related to anchor investor allocation in an SME IPO process.

## Compliance Requirements

- Disclosure of anchor investor allocation details to BSE as per SEBI IPO guidelines
- Publication of allocation information on exchange website for public transparency
- Terms used must align with definitions in the Red Herring Prospectus

## Important Dates

- **December 01, 2025**: Board meeting held to finalize anchor investor allocation
- **December 01, 2025**: Circular issued to BSE

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine disclosure for an SME IPO with minimal market-wide impact. The anchor investor allocation represents the pre-IPO placement portion reserved for qualified institutional buyers. The equal split between two anchor investors shows balanced participation. The allocation is relevant primarily to IPO participants and prospective investors in Helloji Holidays Limited. The company operates in the travel and tourism sector (Flights, Hotels, Holidays, Cruises, MICE, Cabs). No broader market or regulatory implications beyond standard IPO procedures.
