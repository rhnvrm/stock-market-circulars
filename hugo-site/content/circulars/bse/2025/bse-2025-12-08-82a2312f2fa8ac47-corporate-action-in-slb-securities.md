## Summary

BSE has announced corporate actions for securities under Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) mechanism. Two companies are affected: Deccan Gold Mines Ltd. is undertaking a Right Issue of Equity Shares, and eClerx Services Limited is conducting a Buyback of Shares. Both corporate actions have specific record dates, ex-dates, and shut periods during which normal trading activities will be restricted.

## Key Points

- Deccan Gold Mines Ltd. - Right Issue of Equity Shares scheduled with record date on December 9, 2025
- eClerx Services Limited - Buyback of Shares scheduled with record date on December 17, 2025
- Both securities will have shut periods affecting SLB transactions
- Ex-dates coincide with record dates for both corporate actions
- Foreclosure dates align with respective record dates

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a notification of scheduled corporate actions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must note the shut periods for SLB transactions
- Trading members and clearing members should update their systems with ex-dates and record dates
- Investors holding positions in these securities should be aware of the corporate action schedules
- SLB transactions for affected securities must be closed before the respective shut periods

## Important Dates

### Deccan Gold Mines Ltd.
- Ex Date: December 9, 2025
- Record Date: December 9, 2025
- Foreclosure Date: December 9, 2025
- Shut Period: December 8, 2025 to December 9, 2025

### eClerx Services Limited
- Ex Date: December 17, 2025
- Record Date: December 17, 2025
- Foreclosure Date: December 17, 2025
- Shut Period: December 16, 2025 to December 17, 2025

## Impact Assessment

The impact is moderate and specific to shareholders and SLB participants of the two affected companies. For Deccan Gold Mines shareholders, the right issue provides an opportunity to subscribe to additional shares. For eClerx Services shareholders, the buyback may result in value realization opportunities. During the shut periods, SLB transactions for these securities will be restricted, which may affect short-term liquidity for borrowing and lending activities. Market participants should plan their positions accordingly to avoid disruption during the shut periods.
