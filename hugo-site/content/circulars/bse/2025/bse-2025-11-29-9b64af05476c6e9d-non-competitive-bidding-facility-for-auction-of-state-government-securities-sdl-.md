## Summary

BSE has announced the availability of non-competitive bidding facility for the auction of 27 State Government Securities (SDL) scheduled for December 02, 2025. The auction covers securities from 16 states with a total issue size of Rs. 33,350 crores. Trading members can participate through the NCB-GSec module of BSE's iBBS web-based system with bid collection starting December 01, 2025.

## Key Points

- 27 SDL securities available for auction across 16 Indian states
- Total issue size: Rs. 33,350 crores
- 26 new issues and 1 re-issue (6.74% MH 2029)
- Minimum subscription units: 100 (Rs. 10,000)
- Maximum bid amount ranges from Rs. 3.5 crores to Rs. 20 crores per security
- Bidding through NCB-GSec module on iBBS platform (https://ibbs.bseindia.com)
- Reference to RBI press release dated November 28, 2025
- Continuation of facility launched via circular 20191122-28 dated November 22, 2019

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular operationalizes the existing non-competitive bidding facility framework for the upcoming SDL auction.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must submit bids through the NCB-GSec module of BSE's iBBS system
- Direct investors: Submit bids by December 01, 2025
- Trading members: Submit bids by December 02, 2025 till 8:00 AM
- Adhere to minimum subscription units of 100 (Rs. 10,000)
- Respect maximum bidding limits specific to each security
- Contact designated BSE/ICCL teams for user ID creation, password reset, bidding queries, and settlement-related issues

## Important Dates

- **November 28, 2025**: RBI press release announcing SDL auction
- **December 01, 2025, 10:00 AM**: Bid collection starts (24-hour availability)
- **December 01, 2025**: Bid collection end for direct investors
- **December 02, 2025, 8:00 AM**: Bid collection end for trading members
- **December 02, 2025**: Auction date
- **December 03, 2025**: Settlement date

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - provides liquidity and investment opportunities in state government debt instruments across 16 states. The total auction size of Rs. 33,350 crores represents significant state borrowing activity.

**Participant Impact**: Trading members and direct investors in debt segment can participate in SDL primary market through simplified non-competitive bidding mechanism without needing to quote yields.

**Operational Impact**: Requires trading members to coordinate bid submissions within tight timelines, particularly the 8:00 AM deadline on auction day for member bids.

**Geographic Spread**: Securities cover major states including Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Gujarat, Himachal Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Meghalaya, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh, and West Bengal, providing diversified investment options across state credits.
