## Summary

BSE has listed commercial papers issued by multiple companies on a private placement basis on the BSE Debt Segment effective December 22, 2025. The listed securities include commercial papers from Infina Finance Private Limited, Birla Group Holdings Private Limited, Bajaj Finance Limited (two tranches), Aditya Birla Lifestyle Brands Limited, and Navi Finserv Limited. All securities will trade in dematerialized form only.

## Key Points

- Commercial papers from 6 issuances by 5 different companies have been listed
- Total quantity listed: 38,500 units across all issuances
- All securities trade in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Trading permitted only in dematerialized form under specific ISIN numbers
- Tick size for all securities is 1 paise
- Interest rates range from 6.15% to 7.45% per annum
- All commercial papers have cumulative interest payment structure

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for privately placed debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size of 1 paise must be maintained for all listed commercial papers
- Market lot size is 1 for all securities

## Important Dates

- **Listing Effective Date:** December 22, 2025
- **Allotment Dates:** December 19, 2025 (4 issuances) and December 22, 2025 (1 issuance)
- **Redemption Dates:** Range from January 22, 2026 to November 12, 2026
- **Interest Payment Dates:** Cumulative payment on respective redemption dates

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative listing of debt securities with minimal market impact. The commercial papers provide short-term funding options for the issuing companies and investment opportunities for debt market participants. No changes to trading systems, processes, or compliance frameworks are required. The listing expands the universe of debt instruments available on BSE's debt segment.
