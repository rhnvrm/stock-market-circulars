## Summary

Gawar Waranga Highways Private Limited has announced part redemption of its non-convertible debentures along with interest payment. The record date is set for December 16, 2025. Two debenture series are affected: GWHPL-7.53%-30-6-39-PVT (INE0Q3B07019) and GWHPL-7.7%-30-6-39-PVT (INE0Q3B07027). Trading in these debentures will commence with reduced face value effective from December 16, 2025.

## Key Points

- Part redemption of two series of non-convertible debentures
- Record date fixed as December 16, 2025
- Reduced face value will be Rs. 98,000 per debenture (down from Rs. 100,000)
- Both debenture series affected: 7.53% and 7.7% coupon rates
- Interest payment will be made along with part redemption
- Trading with reduced face value effective from December 16, 2025 under settlement DR-778/2025-2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard corporate action notice.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the reduced face value effective date
- Debentures will trade at Rs. 98,000 per debenture from December 16, 2025
- Applicable to settlement number DR-778/2025-2026

## Important Dates

- **Record Date**: December 16, 2025
- **Effective Date for Reduced Face Value**: December 16, 2025
- **Settlement Number**: DR-778/2025-2026

## Impact Assessment

**Affected Securities:**

1. **GWHPL-7.53%-30-6-39-PVT** (ISIN: INE0Q3B07019, Code: 977257)
   - Original Face Value: Rs. 100,000 per debenture
   - Reduced Face Value: Rs. 98,000 per debenture
   - Maturity: June 30, 2039

2. **GWHPL-7.7%-30-6-39-PVT** (ISIN: INE0Q3B07027, Code: 977258)
   - Original Face Value: Rs. 100,000 per debenture
   - Reduced Face Value: Rs. 98,000 per debenture
   - Maturity: June 30, 2039

**Market Impact**: Limited impact as this affects private placement debentures. Debenture holders will receive partial redemption payment of Rs. 2,000 per debenture (2% of face value) along with interest. The remaining investment continues with reduced principal amount until maturity in 2039.
