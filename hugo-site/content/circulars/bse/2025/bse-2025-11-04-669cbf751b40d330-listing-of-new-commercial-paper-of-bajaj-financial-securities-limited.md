## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper issued by Bajaj Financial Securities Limited on a private placement basis. The instrument comprises 2,500 securities with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, totaling Rs. 125 crore. The Commercial Paper carries CRISIL A1+ and IND A1+ ratings, was allotted on November 3, 2025, and will mature on February 3, 2026. Trading commenced on November 4, 2025, on the BSE Debt segment.

## Key Points

- Scrip Code: 730487, Scrip ID: BFSL31125, ISIN: INE01C314DD3
- Total issue size: 2,500 securities Ã— Rs. 5,00,000 = Rs. 125 crore
- Issue price: Rs. 491,757.50 per security (discount to face value)
- Credit rating: CRISIL A1+ and IND A1+ (highest short-term ratings)
- Tenure: 92 days (November 3, 2025 to February 3, 2026)
- Trading denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Market lot: 1 security
- Tick size: 1 paise
- Issuing and Paying Agent: ICICI Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE01C314DD3
- Trading must occur in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples
- Members requiring clarification should contact BSE debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: November 3, 2025
- **Listing Date**: November 4, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: February 3, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine short-term debt instrument listing with a 3-month tenure. The Commercial Paper provides Bajaj Financial Securities Limited with short-term funding at competitive rates given the top-tier credit ratings.

**Investor Impact**: Limited to institutional and high-net-worth investors in the debt market seeking short-term, highly-rated instruments. The A1+ ratings from both CRISIL and India Ratings indicate lowest credit risk in the short-term category.

**Operational Impact**: None for equity market participants. Debt market trading members can now trade this instrument on BSE's debt platform.
