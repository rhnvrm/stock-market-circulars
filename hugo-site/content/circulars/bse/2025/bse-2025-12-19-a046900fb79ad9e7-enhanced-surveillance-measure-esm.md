## Summary

BSE has announced comprehensive updates to its Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) Framework effective December 22, 2025. The circular details three categories of changes: 7 securities entering ESM for the first time, 19 securities exiting ESM framework, 1 security moving to higher ESM stage, and 16 securities moving to lower ESM stages. The ESM framework is designed to alert investors about securities with abnormal price movements or other concerns requiring enhanced surveillance.

## Key Points

- 7 new securities shortlisted for ESM framework effective December 22, 2025
- 19 securities being removed from ESM framework
- 1 security (Chambal Breweries & Distilleries Ltd) moving to higher ESM stage
- 16 securities moving to lower ESM stages, indicating improved compliance
- Changes apply to both mainboard and SME segment securities
- Framework aims to protect investor interests through enhanced monitoring

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:**
- Securities entering ESM will face additional surveillance measures including price bands, trade-for-trade settlement, or other restrictions
- Enhanced disclosure requirements may apply to securities under ESM
- Increased investor awareness about potential risks in affected securities
- Exit from ESM indicates improved market behavior and compliance for 19 securities
- Movement to lower ESM stages suggests improving conditions for 16 securities

**Investor Impact:**
- Investors in newly included ESM securities should exercise increased caution
- Trading restrictions may reduce liquidity in ESM securities
- Enhanced surveillance aims to protect retail investors from excessive volatility
- Securities exiting ESM may see improved trading conditions and investor confidence

**Compliance Impact:**
- Listed companies must comply with enhanced reporting requirements if under ESM
- Periodic reviews determine stage progression or exit from ESM framework
- Framework encourages better corporate governance and market discipline
