## Summary

BSE has listed equity shares of Tata Motors Limited, formerly known as TML Commercial Vehicles Limited. The company has filed its shareholding pattern under Regulation 31 of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015. The shareholding structure shows Promoter & Promoter Group holding 42.56% and Public shareholders holding 57.44% of total equity.

## Key Points

- Company name changed from TML Commercial Vehicles Limited to Tata Motors Limited
- Total shares held: 3,682,331,373 equity shares
- Promoter & Promoter Group: 1,567,369,812 shares (42.56%)
- Public shareholders: 2,114,961,561 shares (57.44%)
- Total shareholders: 6,647,799 (10 promoter group + 6,647,789 public)
- Outstanding ESOPs exist for public shareholders (2,666,563 shares)
- Fully diluted share capital: 3,684,997,936 shares
- No partly paid shares, convertible securities, warrants, depository receipts, locked-in shares, or pledged shares
- Tata Sons Private Limited (TSPL) is exempted under SBO Rules as a core investment company registered with RBI

## Regulatory Changes

This filing represents a corporate restructuring where TML Commercial Vehicles Limited has been renamed to Tata Motors Limited. The shareholding pattern filing complies with Regulation 31 of SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015.

## Compliance Requirements

- Shareholding pattern filed by Company Secretary Sudipto Kumar Das
- Declaration submitted confirming no partly paid shares, convertible securities, warrants, locked-in shares, pledged shares, or NDUs
- Confirmation that no significant beneficial owners exist apart from TSPL (which is exempt)
- Voting rights align with shareholding percentages

## Important Dates

- Filing Date: November 10, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This is a significant corporate action involving the restructuring and renaming of a major Tata Group entity. The shareholding pattern reveals a balanced ownership structure with promoters holding substantial minority stake (42.56%) while maintaining significant public float (57.44%). The absence of pledged shares, locked-in securities, or complex capital structures indicates clean shareholding. Investors and market participants should update their records with the new company name. The exemption of TSPL under SBO Rules is standard for core investment companies registered with RBI.
