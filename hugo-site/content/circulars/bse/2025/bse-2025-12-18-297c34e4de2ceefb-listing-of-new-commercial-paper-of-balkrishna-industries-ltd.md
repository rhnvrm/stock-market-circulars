## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper issued by Balkrishna Industries Ltd. (BIL) on private placement basis effective December 18, 2025. The instrument consists of 2,000 units with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each (total Rs. 100 crore), issued at a discount of Rs. 492,940 per unit. The Commercial Paper carries a CRISIL A1+ rating and will mature on March 13, 2026 (85-day tenure). ICICI Bank Limited acts as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- Securities: Commercial Paper issued on private placement basis
- Quantity: 2,000 units of Rs. 5,00,000 each (Total: Rs. 100 crore)
- Scrip Code: 730801
- Scrip ID: BIL181225
- ISIN: INE787D14227
- Credit Rating: CRISIL A1+
- Issue Price: Rs. 492,940 per unit (discount to face value)
- Allotment Date: December 18, 2025
- Redemption Date: March 13, 2026
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Tick Size: 1 paise
- Issuing and Paying Agent: ICICI Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced by this circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE787D14227
- Trading shall take place in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Securities will be traded on BSE Debt segment only
- Trading Members requiring clarifications may contact the debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Listing Date**: December 18, 2025
- **Allotment Date**: December 18, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: March 13, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine debt instrument listing on the BSE Debt segment with limited market-wide implications.

**Operational Impact**: Low. Only affects trading members dealing in debt securities and specifically those interested in short-term commercial paper of Balkrishna Industries Ltd. The instrument provides a short-term (85-day) investment opportunity with high credit quality (A1+ rating).

**Investor Impact**: Relevant only for institutional investors and high-net-worth individuals participating in the debt market, given the minimum trading denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs.
