## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction results for Settlement No. 742 dated October 27, 2025. The auction includes 98 securities from various companies with quantities ranging from 1 share to 283,046 shares. This is a routine settlement process where securities are auctioned to fulfill delivery obligations that could not be met through normal market mechanisms.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 742
- Settlement Date: October 27, 2025
- Total Securities Listed: 98 different scrips
- Quantity Range: 1 share (minimum) to 283,046 shares (maximum for Enbee Trade)
- Largest Auction Quantities: Enbee Trade (283,046 shares), Remedium Life (20,999 shares), SpiceJet (5,514 shares)
- Includes blue-chip stocks like HDFC Bank, Tata Motors, Nestle India, and HCL Technologies
- Covers diverse sectors including banking, manufacturing, infrastructure, pharmaceuticals, and technology

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a routine operational circular for demat auction settlement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members involved in the auction must settle their obligations as per BSE settlement guidelines
- Participants must ensure timely payment and delivery of auctioned securities
- Brokers with shortages in the relevant settlement must participate in the auction process
- Settlement must be completed according to BSE's prescribed timeline for Settlement No. 742

## Important Dates

- **Settlement Date**: October 27, 2025
- **Settlement Number**: 742
- Specific auction timings and payment deadlines would be communicated separately to participating members

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - The auction affects 98 securities with varying liquidity profiles. While most quantities are small, some significant volumes (like 283,046 shares of Enbee Trade) could impact specific scrips.

**Operational Impact**: The auction indicates delivery failures in the normal settlement cycle, requiring intervention through the auction mechanism. This is a standard clearing and settlement process.

**Investor Impact**: Shareholders of affected companies may see temporary price volatility during auction execution. Members with short positions will face auction prices which typically include penalty charges.

**Notable Securities**: The inclusion of major stocks like HDFC Bank (50 shares), Bharat Heavy Electricals (152 shares), and Federal Bank (150 shares) suggests delivery issues across market segments, though quantities are relatively small for these large-cap stocks.
