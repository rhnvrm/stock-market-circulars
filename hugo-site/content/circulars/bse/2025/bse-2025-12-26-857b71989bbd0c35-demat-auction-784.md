## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction list for Settlement No. 784 dated December 26, 2025. The auction covers 103 scrips where delivery obligations have failed, requiring settlement through auction mechanism. Quantities range from 1 share to 19,824 shares across various companies including large-cap stocks like Bajaj Auto, Castrol India, Mahindra & Mahindra, and Vedanta.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 784
- Auction Date: December 26, 2025
- Total Scrips: 103 companies
- Largest quantity: Excel Realty (19,824 shares)
- Includes blue-chip stocks: Bajaj Auto (1 share), Castrol India (1,004 shares), M&M (5 shares), Vedanta (169 shares)
- Notable quantities: Nyssa Corporation (2,672), ITC Hotels (5,760), Modi Rubber (6,505), Shyamkamal (10,022)
- Mixed-cap coverage: Large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap stocks

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational circular for demat auction settlement process.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members with outstanding delivery obligations must participate in the auction settlement
- Members must arrange for shares or accept auction settlement prices
- Auction participants must comply with BSE auction settlement procedures
- Payment and delivery obligations as per auction settlement mechanism

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: December 26, 2025
- Settlement Number: 784

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - Affects 103 scrips with varying liquidity levels. Large quantities in some illiquid stocks may cause price volatility during auction.

**Operational Impact**: Moderate - Trading members with failed deliveries must arrange shares or participate in auction, potentially at unfavorable prices.

**Member Impact**: Significant for members with large exposures, particularly Excel Realty (19,824 shares), Shyamkamal (10,022 shares), and Modi Rubber (6,505 shares). Blue-chip stocks have minimal quantities suggesting isolated incidents.

**Price Impact**: Likely elevated for illiquid small-cap and mid-cap stocks; minimal impact expected on large-cap stocks with small auction quantities.
