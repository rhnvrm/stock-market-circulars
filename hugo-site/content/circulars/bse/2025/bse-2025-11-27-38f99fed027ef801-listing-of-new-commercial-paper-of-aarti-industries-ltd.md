## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper issued by Aarti Industries Ltd on private placement basis effective November 27, 2025. The issuance consists of 3000 units with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, maturing on February 26, 2026. The securities carry CRISIL A1+ and IND A1+ ratings and will trade exclusively in dematerialized form on the BSE Debt segment.

## Key Points

- 3000 units of Commercial Paper listed with face value of Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- Total issue size: Rs. 150 crores (3000 x Rs. 5,00,000)
- Issue price: Rs. 4,91,906 per unit (discount of Rs. 8,094)
- Scrip Code: 730664, Scrip ID: AIL271125
- ISIN: INE769A14AB3
- Credit Rating: CRISIL A1+, IND A1+
- Market lot: 1 unit
- Trading tick size: 1 paise
- IndusInd Bank appointed as Issuing and Paying Agent
- Trading only in dematerialized form with standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for privately placed debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure trading occurs only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE769A14AB3
- Trades must be executed in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Minimum tick size of 1 paise must be maintained
- Trading members requiring clarification should contact BSE debt department at specified contact numbers (22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915)

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date:** November 27, 2025
- **Listing Date:** November 27, 2025
- **Redemption Date:** February 26, 2026
- **Tenor:** 91 days (approximately 3 months)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Low - This is a routine short-term debt instrument listing with a 3-month maturity period. Commercial paper issuances are standard treasury operations for corporate funding needs.

**Operational Impact:** Minimal - Trading members can now trade these securities on the BSE Debt segment following standard procedures for debt instruments.

**Investor Impact:** Limited to institutional investors and high net-worth individuals given the Rs. 5 lakh minimum denomination requirement. The high credit ratings (A1+) indicate strong creditworthiness and low default risk for the short tenure.
