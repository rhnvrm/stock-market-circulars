## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction list for Settlement No. 763 dated November 26, 2025. The circular lists securities from various companies that are subject to demat auction settlement, including quantities to be auctioned. This is a routine market operation to settle outstanding delivery obligations through the auction mechanism.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 763
- Settlement Date: November 26, 2025
- Total securities listed: Over 120 scrips
- Quantities range from 1 share to 81,866 shares depending on the security
- Major quantities include: Welcure Drugs (81,866 shares), Vodafone Idea (51,475 shares), Tata Power (30,450 shares), Lesha Industries (23,804 shares), Laddu Gopal (25,000 shares)
- Covers diverse sectors including banking, pharma, technology, manufacturing, and infrastructure

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a routine demat auction settlement notice.

## Compliance Requirements

- Concerned brokers and clearing members must participate in the demat auction process for Settlement No. 763
- Settlement obligations must be fulfilled as per BSE's auction settlement mechanism
- Participants must ensure availability of funds and securities for auction settlement

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: November 26, 2025
- Settlement Number: 763

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects multiple securities across various sectors, but quantities for most scrips are relatively small.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - brokers and clearing members with outstanding delivery obligations in these securities must participate in the auction settlement.

**Investor Impact:** Medium - investors with pending delivery positions in the listed securities will have their obligations settled through the auction mechanism, which may involve price variations from normal market prices.

**Sector Coverage:** Broad impact across banking (Bank of Baroda, Bank of India, IDBI, Karnataka Bank, Yes Bank), pharmaceuticals (Glenmark, Shilpa Medicare, Torrent Pharma), technology (Infosys, Tata Technologies), infrastructure (GMR Airports, RVNL, Tata Power), and other sectors.
