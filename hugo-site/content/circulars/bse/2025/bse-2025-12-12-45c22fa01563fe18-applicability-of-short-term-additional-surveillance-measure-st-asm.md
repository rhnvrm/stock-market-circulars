## Summary

BSE has updated the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective December 15, 2025. Nine securities will be newly shortlisted in the ST-ASM framework, five securities will exit the framework (moving to other surveillance mechanisms), and one security will move to a lower ASM stage while continuing in the framework.

## Key Points

- 9 securities newly shortlisted in Short Term 5/15/30 Days ASM Framework (including 2 SME scrips)
- 5 securities moving out of ST-ASM Framework due to inclusion in other surveillance frameworks
- 1 security (Eraaya Lifespaces Ltd) moving to lower Stage ASM while continuing in framework
- No securities moving to higher Stage ASM
- Consolidated list includes 11+ securities currently under ST-ASM Stage I surveillance
- Changes effective from December 15, 2025

## Regulatory Framework

The ST-ASM framework is part of BSE's surveillance mechanism to monitor securities showing abnormal price movements or volatility. Securities under this framework face enhanced monitoring and may have trading restrictions including:
- Periodic call auctions
- Additional margin requirements
- Trade-to-trade settlement
- Price bands and quantity limits

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** December 15, 2025
- All changes to ST-ASM framework applicability take effect from this date

## Impact Assessment

**Trading Impact:**
- Securities entering ST-ASM will face enhanced surveillance measures affecting liquidity
- Investors in affected securities should expect potential restrictions on intraday trading
- Higher margin requirements may apply
- Securities exiting ST-ASM move to other frameworks (LT-ASM, ESM, T2T) with different restrictions

**Market Participants:**
- Traders and investors holding positions in affected securities should review their strategies
- SME segment investors should note that 2 SME scrips are newly entering surveillance
- Brokers need to update systems to reflect new margin and trading requirements

**Consolidated List:**
The circular provides a consolidated list showing Stage I ASM securities including AK Capital Services, Amkay Products, Brijlaxmi Leasing, Capital Trust, Dalmia Industrial Development, Eldeco Housing, Epsom Properties, and others, indicating ongoing surveillance across multiple securities.
