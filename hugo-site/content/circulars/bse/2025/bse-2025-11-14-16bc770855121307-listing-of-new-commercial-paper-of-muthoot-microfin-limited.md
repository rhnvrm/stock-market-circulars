## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper issued by Muthoot Microfin Limited on private placement basis. The securities are admitted to dealings on the BSE Debt segment effective November 14, 2025. The issue comprises 500 units with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, rated CRISIL A1+, and carries a maturity period of 6 months.

## Key Points

- 500 units of Commercial Paper listed with face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each
- Issue price: Rs. 4,79,317 per unit
- Total issue size: Rs. 25 crores (500 units Ã— Rs. 5 lakhs)
- Credit rating: CRISIL A1+ (highest short-term rating)
- Market lot: 1 unit
- Trading only in dematerialized form
- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- Scrip Code: 730570
- Scrip ID: MML141125
- ISIN: INE046W14129
- Issuing and Paying Agent: Federal Bank Ltd

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for newly issued debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE046W14129
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Trading members requiring clarification should contact BSE debt department on 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: November 14, 2025
- **Date of Allotment**: November 14, 2025
- **Listing Date**: November 14, 2025
- **Date of Redemption**: May 13, 2026
- **Tenure**: 180 days (6 months)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine commercial paper listing that expands the debt securities available on BSE's debt segment. The issue provides short-term funding to Muthoot Microfin Limited.

**Investor Impact**: Low to moderate for debt investors. The CRISIL A1+ rating indicates highest credit quality for short-term instruments, making it suitable for investors seeking short-term deployment with strong credit profile.

**Operational Impact**: No operational changes for trading members beyond standard debt trading procedures. The securities follow standard BSE debt segment trading norms with dematerialized trading requirement.
