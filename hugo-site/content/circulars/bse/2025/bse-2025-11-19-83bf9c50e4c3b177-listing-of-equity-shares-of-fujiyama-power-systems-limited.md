## Summary

Fujiyama Power Systems Limited has filed its shareholding pattern under Regulation 31(1)(a) of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015. This is a post-issue filing dated November 18, 2025, following the company's equity share listing. The filing includes declarations regarding various types of securities and encumbrances.

## Key Points

- Listed entity: Fujiyama Power Systems Limited
- Security type: Equity Shares
- Filing under: Reg. 31(1)(a) - Post Issue
- Reference date: November 18, 2025
- Company has issued partly paid-up shares (YES)
- Company has issued convertible securities (YES)
- Company has shares in locked-in status (YES)
- No warrants, ESOPs, depository receipts, or significant beneficial owners
- No promoter share encumbrances (pledges, NDUs, or other)
- No equity shares with differential voting rights

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a standard compliance filing following IPO listing requirements.

## Compliance Requirements

- Listed entities must file shareholding patterns under Regulation 31 of SEBI LODR Regulations 2015
- Declaration of various security types and encumbrances is mandatory
- Post-issue shareholding pattern must be filed promptly after listing
- The filing covers promoter & promoter group, public, and non-promoter non-public shareholding categories

## Important Dates

- **November 18, 2025**: Reference date for shareholding pattern (Post-Issue)
- **November 19, 2025**: Circular publication date

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - This is the first post-IPO shareholding pattern for Fujiyama Power Systems Limited, providing transparency on ownership structure to investors. The presence of locked-in shares, partly paid-up shares, and convertible securities indicates typical post-IPO capital structure arrangements.

**Investor Impact**: Investors can now access detailed shareholding information including promoter holdings, public shareholding, voting rights distribution, and lock-in details. The presence of convertible securities suggests potential dilution upon conversion.

**Operational Impact**: Standard compliance requirement with no operational changes for the company or market participants.
