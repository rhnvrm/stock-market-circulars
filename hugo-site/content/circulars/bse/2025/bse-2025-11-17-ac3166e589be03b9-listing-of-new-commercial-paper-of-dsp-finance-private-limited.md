## Summary

DSP Finance Private Limited has listed two series of Commercial Papers (CPs) on the BSE Debt segment effective November 17, 2025. The first series (DFPL-13-2-26-CP) consists of 2,000 units worth Rs. 100 crores with maturity on February 13, 2026. The second series (DFPL-14-5-26-CP) also consists of 2,000 units worth Rs. 100 crores with maturity on May 14, 2026. Both instruments are rated ICRA A1+ and were allotted on November 14, 2025.

## Key Points

- Two series of Commercial Papers listed on BSE Debt segment
- Series 1: 2,000 units (Scrip Code: 730574, ISIN: INE422H14099) maturing February 13, 2026
- Series 2: 2,000 units (Scrip Code: 730575, ISIN: INE422H14081) maturing May 14, 2026
- Face value and paid-up value: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit for both series
- Issue price: Rs. 4,91,183 (Series 1) and Rs. 4,82,186 (Series 2)
- Credit rating: ICRA A1+ for both instruments
- ICICI Bank Limited acts as Issuing and Paying Agent
- Trading only in dematerialized form with standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs
- Tick size: 1 paise

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under the specified ISIN numbers
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Market lot size is 1 unit

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment**: November 14, 2025
- **Listing Date**: November 17, 2025
- **Redemption Date (Series 1)**: February 13, 2026
- **Redemption Date (Series 2)**: May 14, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine commercial paper listing with minimal market impact. The instruments provide short-term funding to DSP Finance Private Limited through the debt market. The ICRA A1+ rating indicates high credit quality. Trading members can now trade these instruments on the BSE Debt segment in standard lots of Rs. 5 lakhs. The listing expands the available short-term debt instruments for institutional investors and enhances liquidity options in the debt market.
