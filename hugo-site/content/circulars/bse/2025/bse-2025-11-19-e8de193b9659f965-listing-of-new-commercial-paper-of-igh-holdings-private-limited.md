## Summary

BSE has listed 1000 units of Commercial Paper issued by IGH Holdings Private Limited on private placement basis with effect from November 19, 2025. The instruments carry ratings of CARE A1+ and CRISIL A1+, with a face value of Rs.5,00,000 each and redemption date of February 18, 2026. Axis Bank Limited acts as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- Quantity: 1000 Commercial Papers of Rs.5,00,000 each
- Total Issue Size: Rs.50 crores (1000 x Rs.5,00,000)
- Scrip Code: 730599
- Scrip ID: IGHH191125
- ISIN: INE02FN14671
- Credit Ratings: CARE A1+ and CRISIL A1+
- Issue Price: Rs.4,91,604.50 per unit
- Face/Paid-up Value: Rs.5,00,000 per unit
- Trading denomination: Rs.5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Tick Size: 1 paise
- Trading Mode: Dematerialized form only
- Issuing and Paying Agent: Axis Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a standard listing notification for debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE02FN14671
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs.5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size of 1 paise must be followed for price movements
- Securities will be traded on BSE Debt segment

## Important Dates

- Date of Allotment: November 19, 2025
- Listing Date: November 19, 2025
- Redemption Date: February 18, 2026
- Tenor: 91 days (approximately 3 months)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine commercial paper listing with minimal market impact. The instruments are short-term debt securities (91-day tenor) issued by IGH Holdings Private Limited, a private company. The high credit ratings (A1+ from both CARE and CRISIL) indicate strong creditworthiness. The listing provides institutional investors and trading members access to highly-rated short-term debt instruments. No impact on equity markets or retail investors. Trading will be limited to the debt segment with standard denomination of Rs.5 lakhs, making it accessible primarily to institutional participants.
