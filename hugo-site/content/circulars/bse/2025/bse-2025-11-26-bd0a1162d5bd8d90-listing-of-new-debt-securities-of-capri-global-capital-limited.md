## Summary

BSE has listed new debt securities issued by Capri Global Capital Limited on a private placement basis, effective November 26, 2025. The listing comprises 5,000 non-convertible debentures with a face value of Rs. 1,00,000 each, carrying an interest rate of 9.25% per annum with monthly interest payments. The securities are rated ACUITE AA/Stable and IVR AA/Positive, and will mature on May 24, 2028.

## Key Points

- 5,000 debt securities listed on BSE Debt segment
- Scrip Code: 977314, Scrip ID: 925CGCL28B
- ISIN: INE180C07239
- Face Value and Issue Price: Rs. 1,00,000 per security
- Interest Rate: 9.25% per annum (paid monthly)
- Allotment Date: November 24, 2025
- Credit Ratings: ACUITE AA/Stable, IVR AA/Positive
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Tick Size: 1 paise
- Trading: Demat form only
- No Put/Call options

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for new debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE180C07239
- Members should refer to the Placement Memorandum available at https://www.bseindia.com/markets/debt/memorandum_data.aspx for detailed information
- For clarifications, members may contact BSE debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: November 24, 2025
- **Listing Date**: November 26, 2025
- **First Interest Payment**: December 24, 2025
- **Interest Payment Schedule**: Monthly from December 24, 2025 to May 24, 2028
- **Redemption Date**: May 24, 2028

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Low - This is a routine debt security listing for private placement NCDs with limited public market impact.

**Investor Impact**: Low to Medium - Relevant primarily to institutional investors and debt market participants interested in AA-rated corporate debt securities. The monthly interest payment structure provides regular income.

**Operational Impact**: Low - Standard listing process with no special trading conditions beyond mandatory demat trading requirement.
