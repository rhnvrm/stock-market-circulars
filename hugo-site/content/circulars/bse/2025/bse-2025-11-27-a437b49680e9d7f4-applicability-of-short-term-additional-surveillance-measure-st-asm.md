## Summary

BSE has issued updates to the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective November 28, 2025. Four securities are being newly shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage I, six securities are moving out of the framework, and a consolidated list of all securities currently under ST-ASM has been provided. The framework uses 5/15/30 days monitoring periods to identify securities with unusual price movements or trading patterns.

## Key Points

- 4 securities newly shortlisted in ST-ASM Stage I framework (3 SME scrips, 1 regular)
- 6 securities moving out of ST-ASM framework (2 SME scrips, 4 regular)
- No securities moving to higher or lower ASM stages within ST-ASM framework
- Consolidated list shows at least 16 securities under ST-ASM Stage I
- Changes effective from November 28, 2025
- Framework includes special markers for NSE alignment (*), SME scrips (@), and T+0 scrips (~)

## Regulatory Framework

**ST-ASM Purpose:** Identifies securities with abnormal price movements or trading patterns over short periods (5/15/30 days)

**Stage Progression:** Securities can move between stages based on continued surveillance triggers or move out entirely if concerns are addressed or other frameworks apply

**Exit Reasons:** Securities may exit ST-ASM due to inclusion in:
- LT-ASM Framework (*)
- Trade for Trade Framework (**)
- GSM Framework (#)
- Pledge Framework (^)
- ESM Framework ($)

## Compliance Requirements

- Securities under ST-ASM Stage I face enhanced surveillance and potential trading restrictions
- Investors should exercise caution when trading these securities
- Trading members must comply with ASM framework requirements for order placement and execution
- Enhanced margin requirements may apply

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** November 28, 2025
- All changes to ST-ASM classifications take effect from market opening on this date

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects 10 securities with changes and at least 16 total securities under surveillance

**Trading Impact:** Securities newly added to ST-ASM may face reduced liquidity and increased margin requirements, potentially leading to price volatility

**Investor Impact:** Investors holding or trading affected securities should be aware of enhanced surveillance status and potential trading restrictions

**SME Segment:** Significant portion of changes affect SME scrips, indicating continued focus on smaller company surveillance
