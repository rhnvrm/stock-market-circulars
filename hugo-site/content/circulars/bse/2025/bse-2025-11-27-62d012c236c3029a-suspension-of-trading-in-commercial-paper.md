## Summary

BSE has announced the suspension of trading in commercial papers issued by 13 companies effective from their respective record dates between December 2-8, 2025. Trading members are advised not to deal in these commercial papers from the specified dates as the companies have fixed record dates for redemption purposes.

## Key Points

- 13 commercial papers suspended for trading due to redemption
- Record dates range from December 2, 2025 to December 8, 2025
- Trading members instructed to cease dealings from respective record dates
- Includes commercial papers from major financial institutions and holding companies
- Notice issued under Corporate Actions category for Debt segment

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute any trades in the specified commercial papers from their respective "No Dealings From" dates
- Trading members should update their systems to prevent inadvertent trading in suspended securities
- Ensure clients are informed about the trading suspension and redemption schedule

## Important Dates

- **December 2, 2025**: Trading suspension and record date for 2 commercial papers
- **December 3, 2025**: Trading suspension and record date for 2 commercial papers
- **December 5, 2025**: Trading suspension and record date for 1 commercial paper
- **December 8, 2025**: Trading suspension and record date for 8 commercial papers
- **Notice Date**: November 27, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This circular has limited market-wide impact as it pertains to routine redemption of commercial papers, a standard corporate action in the debt market. The suspension affects short-term debt instruments issued by financial institutions and holding companies. Trading members dealing in debt securities need to ensure compliance with the no-dealing restrictions. The commercial papers involved are reaching maturity and will be redeemed as per their terms, representing normal money market operations rather than any adverse development.
