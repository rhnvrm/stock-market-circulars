## Summary

BSE has announced that equity shares of LUXURY TIME LIMITED (Scrip Code: 544635), an SME IPO listing, will be transferred from the Trade for Trade segment (MT Group) to the Rolling segment (M Group) effective Friday, December 26, 2025. This change indicates the company's transition from restricted trading to normal rolling settlement.

## Key Points

- LUXURY TIME LIMITED shares currently trade in Trade for Trade segment (MT Group)
- Transfer to Rolling segment (M Group) effective December 26, 2025
- Scrip Code: 544635
- Company listed on BSE SME platform
- Notice follows Exchange Notice No. 20251210-77 dated December 10, 2025
- Trading Members can contact Mr. Anurag Jain (Tel: 022-2272 8822) for details

## Regulatory Changes

The trading mechanism for LUXURY TIME LIMITED shares will change from:
- **From**: Trade for Trade segment (MT Group) - requires full delivery and payment
- **To**: Rolling segment (M Group) - allows normal T+2 settlement with intraday trading

This is a standard progression for SME IPOs after initial listing period, moving from restricted to normal trading.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must update their systems to reflect the group change
- Existing positions in MT Group should be squared off or carried forward as per exchange rules
- Trading Members should inform clients about the change in trading mechanism
- Post-migration, normal margin and settlement rules of M Group will apply

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: December 11, 2025
- **Effective Date**: December 26, 2025 (Friday)
- **Previous Notice Reference**: December 10, 2025 (Notice No. 20251210-77)

## Impact Assessment

**Positive Impact**: 
- Improved liquidity as shares move to rolling settlement
- Intraday trading becomes possible
- Reduced transaction costs for investors
- Lower margin requirements compared to Trade for Trade

**Operational Impact**:
- Trading Members must update internal systems
- Risk management parameters need adjustment
- Clients may see increased trading flexibility

**Market Significance**: This transition typically indicates successful completion of post-IPO stabilization period and validates the company's trading credentials for normal market operations.
