## Summary

BSE has issued a Demat Auction notice for Settlement No. 768 dated December 3, 2025. The auction lists 86 securities across various scrips with specific quantities to be auctioned. This is a standard settlement mechanism to address delivery shortfalls in the clearing and settlement process.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 768
- Auction Date: December 3, 2025
- Total number of scrips involved: 86 different securities
- Quantities range from 1 share to 89,925 shares depending on the scrip
- Notable high-quantity auctions include AVANCE TECH (89,925 shares), TRESCON LIMITED (46,993 shares), MANGALAM GLOBAL (19,622 shares), HINDUSTAN CONSTRUCTION (16,000 shares)
- Covers diverse sectors including banking, pharmaceuticals, automobiles, technology, and heavy industries

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced in this circular. This is a routine operational notice.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members who have failed to deliver securities must participate in the demat auction or arrange for delivery
- Members with obligations must settle their positions as per BSE clearing and settlement procedures
- Auction participants must have sufficient funds/securities to fulfill auction obligations
- Settlement must be completed as per the specified settlement cycle

## Important Dates

- **Auction Date**: December 3, 2025
- **Settlement No.**: 768

## Impact Assessment

**Operational Impact**: Medium - Affects 86 different securities with delivery obligations that need to be settled through auction mechanism.

**Market Impact**: Medium - While this is a routine settlement process, the auction involves significant quantities in some scrips (e.g., AVANCE TECH with 89,925 shares, TRESCON with 46,993 shares) which could have price implications.

**Member Impact**: Direct impact on trading members with pending delivery obligations who must either arrange delivery or participate in the auction at potentially unfavorable prices.

**Investor Impact**: Low to Medium - Retail investors are generally not directly affected unless they are counterparties to the failed deliveries. The auction mechanism ensures settlement completion.
