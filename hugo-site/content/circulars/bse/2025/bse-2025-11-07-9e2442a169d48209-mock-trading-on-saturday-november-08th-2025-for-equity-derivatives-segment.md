## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Equity Derivatives segment on Saturday, November 8th, 2025. This session is designed to allow trading members to test their trading platforms, including third-party systems and in-house developed applications using ETI APIs. The mock session will enable testing of various functionalities such as risk-reduction mode, trading halt, and exceptional market conditions. There will be no new BOLT TWS release associated with this mock session.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Equity Derivatives segment on Saturday, November 8th, 2025
- No new BOLT TWS release will be implemented
- Trading members can test third-party platforms, empaneled vendor systems, and in-house ETI API-based applications
- Testing includes risk-reduction mode, trading halt, and exceptional market conditions
- Mock trades will not create any margin obligations, pay-in/pay-out obligations, rights, or liabilities
- Feedback from all members is requested by 5:00 PM on the mock trading day
- Contact support available: BSE Tech Support (022-22728053, bse.tech@bseindia.com) and Help Desk (022-45720400/600, 022-69158500, bsehelp@bseindia.com)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced in this circular. This is a routine mock trading session announcement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are encouraged to participate actively in the mock trading session
- Members should provide feedback on the mock trading session by 5:00 PM on November 8th, 2025
- Members can contact their respective Relationship Manager or BSE support teams for queries or clarifications

## Important Dates

- **November 8th, 2025 (Saturday)**
  - Log-in: 11:15 AM to 12:15 PM
  - Continuous Trading: 12:15 PM to 04:00 PM
  - Trade Modification: 04:00 PM to 04:15 PM
  - Feedback deadline: 5:00 PM

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** None. This is a mock trading session with no impact on actual market operations.

**Operational Impact:** Low. The session provides an opportunity for trading members to test and familiarize themselves with their trading systems in a simulated environment. No new software release is involved, minimizing technical risk.

**Member Impact:** Voluntary participation for testing purposes. Members using third-party platforms or in-house systems can validate their applications and prepare for exceptional market scenarios. No financial obligations or liabilities will arise from mock trades.
