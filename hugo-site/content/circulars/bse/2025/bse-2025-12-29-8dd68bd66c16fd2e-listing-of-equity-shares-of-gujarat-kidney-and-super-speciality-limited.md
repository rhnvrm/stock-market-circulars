## Summary

Gujarat Kidney and Super Speciality Limited has filed its post-issue shareholding pattern under Regulation 31(1)(a) of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015. The filing pertains to equity shares and is dated December 26, 2025.

## Key Points

- Company has issued partly paid-up shares (YES)
- Company has locked-in shares (YES)
- No convertible securities issued
- No warrants issued
- No outstanding ESOPs granted
- No depository receipts issued against shares
- No promoter shares are pledged or encumbered
- No non-disposal undertaking on promoter shares
- No equity shares with differential voting rights
- No significant beneficial owners

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard compliance filing under existing SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015.

## Compliance Requirements

- Gujarat Kidney and Super Speciality Limited is required to file shareholding pattern under Regulation 31(1)(a) of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015
- The filing includes declaration regarding partly paid-up shares, convertible securities, warrants, ESOPs, depository receipts, locked-in shares, pledged shares, and encumbered shares
- The tabular format for disclosure must follow prescribed SEBI format showing summary statement of holding of specified securities

## Important Dates

- **December 26, 2025**: Reference date for shareholding pattern (post-issue)
- **December 29, 2025**: Circular publication date

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine disclosure required for newly listed companies.

**Operational Impact**: Standard compliance requirement. The presence of locked-in shares and partly paid-up shares indicates this is likely a recent IPO listing with standard lock-in provisions for promoters/pre-IPO shareholders.

**Investor Impact**: Informational filing that provides transparency on shareholding structure. Investors can review the detailed shareholding pattern to understand promoter holding, public holding, and any restrictions on share transferability.
