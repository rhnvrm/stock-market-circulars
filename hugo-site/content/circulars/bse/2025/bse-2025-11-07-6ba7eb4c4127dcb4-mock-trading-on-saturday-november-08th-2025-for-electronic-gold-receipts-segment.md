## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) segment on Saturday, November 08, 2025. This session is designed for trading members using third-party platforms or in-house developed systems to test their trading applications under various market conditions including risk-reduction mode and trading halts. No new BOLT TWS release will be deployed. All trades executed during this mock session will not create any margin, pay-in/pay-out obligations, rights, or liabilities.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for EGR segment on November 08, 2025
- No new BOLT TWS release
- Trading session timings: Login at 11:15 AM, continuous trading from 12:15 PM to 4:00 PM
- Mock trades will not attract any obligations or create rights/liabilities
- Members requested to provide feedback by 5:00 PM on session day
- Opportunity to test exceptional market conditions like risk-reduction mode and trading halts

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced in this circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are encouraged to actively participate in the mock trading session
- Members must provide feedback on the mock trading session by 5:00 PM on November 08, 2025
- Contact BSE Helpdesk (022-45720400/600 & 022-69158500; bsehelp@bseindia.com) or BSE Tech Support (022-22728053; bse.tech@bseindia.com) for queries

## Important Dates

- **November 08, 2025**: Mock trading session for EGR segment
  - 11:15 AM - 12:00 PM: Log-in
  - 12:00 PM - 12:08 PM: Pre-Open Order Entry period
  - 12:08 PM - 12:15 PM: Matching period
  - 12:15 PM - 4:00 PM: Continuous Trading
  - 1:00 PM - 1:15 PM: Block Deal Window
  - 4:00 PM - 4:10 PM: Closing
  - 4:10 PM - 4:20 PM: Post-closing
- **By 5:00 PM on November 08, 2025**: Feedback submission deadline

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: None. This is a mock trading session with no real trading obligations.

**Operational Impact**: Low. Trading members using third-party platforms or in-house systems can utilize this opportunity to test their applications and familiarize themselves with various market conditions. The session provides a risk-free environment for testing trading functionalities without creating any actual obligations or liabilities.
