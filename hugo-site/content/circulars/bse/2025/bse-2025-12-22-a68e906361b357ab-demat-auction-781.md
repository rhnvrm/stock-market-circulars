## Summary

BSE has released the demat auction settlement schedule for Settlement No. 781 dated December 22, 2025. The auction covers 107 different scrips with varying quantities ranging from 1 share to 8,756 shares across diverse sectors including automotive, banking, energy, pharmaceuticals, technology, and consumer goods.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 781
- Settlement Date: December 22, 2025
- Total scrips involved: 107 securities
- Largest quantity: HDB Financial (8,756 shares)
- Second largest: Healthy Life (8,000 shares)
- Notable companies include major stocks like Adani Enterprises, Reliance Power, Tata Motors, ICICI Bank, and others
- Quantities range from single shares to several thousand shares per scrip

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a standard operational settlement notice.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must settle the auctioned securities as per BSE auction settlement procedures
- Members with shortages in the listed scrips must participate in the auction or face consequences as per exchange rules
- Settlement obligations must be fulfilled within the prescribed timeline

## Important Dates

- **Settlement Date**: December 22, 2025
- **Settlement Number**: 781

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - The auction involves 107 scrips across various sectors. While individual quantities are relatively small for most securities, the breadth of securities affected is significant.

**Trading Member Impact**: Medium to High - Members with shortages in these securities will need to participate in the auction, potentially at unfavorable prices.

**Investor Impact**: Low to Medium - Specific to investors/brokers who failed to deliver shares in previous settlements. The auction mechanism ensures settlement completion but may result in financial losses for defaulting members.

**Notable High-Volume Auctions**:
- HDB Financial: 8,756 shares
- Healthy Life: 8,000 shares  
- Ola Electric: 5,760 shares
- Vikran Engineering: 2,921 shares
- DCX Systems: 1,516 shares

The presence of prominent large-cap stocks like Adani group companies, Tata Motors, ICICI Bank, and Reliance Power indicates settlement failures across market segments.
