## Summary

Axis Finance Limited has listed new Commercial Paper on BSE's Debt segment on December 15, 2025. The securities were issued on private placement basis with a total value of Rs. 200 crores (4000 units of Rs. 5,00,000 each). The commercial paper carries CRISIL A1+ and IND A1+ credit ratings and has a maturity period of less than one month, with redemption scheduled for January 8, 2026. Axis Bank Limited serves as the Issuing and Paying agent.

## Key Points

- 4000 units of Commercial Paper issued, each with face value of Rs. 5,00,000
- Issue price: Rs. 4,97,699 per unit (discount of Rs. 2,301)
- Allotment date: December 12, 2025
- Redemption date: January 8, 2026 (27-day tenure)
- Credit Rating: CRISIL A1+ and IND A1+
- ISIN: INE891K14PP2
- Scrip Code: 730764, Scrip ID: AFL121225
- Market lot: 1 unit
- Trading only in dematerialized form
- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE891K14PP2
- Trading restricted to standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples
- Securities available for trading on BSE Debt segment
- Queries to be directed to BSE debt department at phone numbers: 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** December 15, 2025
- **Allotment Date:** December 12, 2025
- **Listing Date:** December 15, 2025
- **Redemption Date:** January 8, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine commercial paper listing with minimal market impact. The listing provides short-term financing to Axis Finance Limited and offers debt market participants an A1+ rated investment option with approximately 27-day maturity. The high credit rating indicates strong credit quality. Impact is limited to institutional investors and debt market participants, as the minimum investment requirement of Rs. 5 lakhs excludes retail participation. No effect on equity markets or broader investor sentiment.
