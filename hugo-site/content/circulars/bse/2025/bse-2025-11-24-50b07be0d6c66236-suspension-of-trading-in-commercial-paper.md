## Summary

BSE has announced the suspension of trading in 15 commercial papers issued by various companies due to scheduled redemptions. Trading members are advised not to deal in these securities from the specified no-dealings dates. The redemptions are scheduled for December 1-2, 2025, with record dates set accordingly.

## Key Points

- 15 commercial papers from 13 different issuers affected
- 4 securities with December 1, 2025 redemption date
- 11 securities with December 2, 2025 redemption date
- Trading suspension begins on respective record dates
- Notice issued under DR-767/2025-2026 and DR-768/2025-2026
- Affects debt segment corporate actions

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice for commercial paper redemptions following established procedures.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members**: Must not deal in the listed commercial papers from the specified "No Dealings From" dates
- **Market Participants**: Should note the record dates and avoid transactions in affected securities
- **Compliance Period**: December 1-2, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Limited to debt segment commercial paper holders. This is a routine redemption process with no broader market implications.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members must update their systems to prevent trades in these 15 securities from the specified dates. Investors holding these commercial papers will receive redemption proceeds as per the terms.

**Issuer Diversity**: Affects multiple sectors including housing finance, securities, media, retail, power, manufacturing, and real estate, indicating normal commercial paper market activity.

**Liquidity**: Temporary trading suspension is standard procedure for securities approaching redemption, ensuring orderly settlement of maturing instruments.
