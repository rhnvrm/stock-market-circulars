## Summary

BSE has announced suspension of trading in partly paid equity shares of Adani Enterprises Ltd (Scrip Code 890227) effective December 23, 2025. The suspension is due to the company fixing a record date to determine eligible shareholders for receiving the first call notice. Shareholders of partly paid shares must pay Rs.450 per share (Re.0.25 face value + Rs.449.75 premium), representing 25% of the total issue price of Rs.1,800.

## Key Points

- Trading in partly paid equity shares of Adani Enterprises Ltd (Scrip Code 890227) will be suspended
- Record date fixed as December 23, 2025
- First call payment amount: Rs.450 per partly paid equity share
- Payment breakdown: Re.0.25 towards face value and Rs.449.75 towards premium
- First call represents 25% of total issue price of Rs.1,800 per share
- Settlement number: DR-783/2025-2026
- Trading members advised not to deal in these shares from the specified date

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard corporate action procedure for partly paid shares where the company is collecting subsequent call payments from shareholders.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute any deals in partly paid equity shares (Scrip Code 890227) of Adani Enterprises Ltd from December 23, 2025
- Trading members are required to take note of this suspension and inform their clients accordingly
- Eligible shareholders of partly paid shares will receive first call notice for payment of Rs.450 per share

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: December 19, 2025
- **Record Date**: December 23, 2025
- **No Dealings From Date**: December 23, 2025
- **Settlement Number**: DR-783/2025-2026

## Impact Assessment

**Trading Impact**: High - Complete suspension of trading in partly paid equity shares prevents any transactions in these securities, affecting liquidity for existing holders.

**Shareholder Impact**: High - Shareholders holding partly paid shares must prepare to pay the first call amount of Rs.450 per share. Failure to pay when called may result in forfeiture of shares as per company rules.

**Market Operations**: The suspension ensures clean record date determination and prevents confusion regarding eligibility for the first call payment. This protects both the company and shareholders from settlement issues.

**Financial Commitment**: Shareholders need to arrange funds for the call payment, which represents 25% of the total commitment of Rs.1,800 per share (total commitment Rs.1,800 per partly paid share).
