## Summary

Bombay Stock Exchange has published the list of securities under demat auction for settlement number 764 dated November 27, 2025. The auction includes 108 scrips across various sectors with quantities ranging from 1 to 43,800 shares. This auction is conducted to fulfill settlement obligations where sellers failed to deliver securities.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 764
- Date: November 27, 2025
- Total number of scrips: 108 (54 listed in each column)
- Highest quantity: Darshan Orna (43,800 shares)
- Includes major blue-chip stocks like Infosys, Tata Steel, BHEL, Bajaj Auto, and Larsen & Toubro
- Notable stocks with smaller quantities include Reliance Power, GAIL India, Hero Motocorp, and Tata Elxsi

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced in this circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must participate in the demat auction to fulfill their settlement obligations
- Buyers whose securities were not delivered must be compensated through the auction mechanism
- Trading members with outstanding delivery obligations must ensure availability of funds for auction purchases
- Settlement must be completed as per BSE settlement calendar

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: November 27, 2025
- Settlement Number: 764
- Auction date: To be conducted as per BSE settlement schedule for settlement 764

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - The auction includes 108 securities spanning multiple sectors, indicating delivery failures across the market. The presence of high-value blue-chip stocks suggests significant settlement obligations.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - Brokers with failed deliveries face additional costs through auction purchases at potentially higher prices. Clients expecting delivery may face delays until auction completion.

**Financial Impact:** The auction involves notable quantities in some scrips (Darshan Orna: 43,800 shares, SVP Global: 13,215 shares, Setco Automotive: 1,344 shares), which could result in significant financial implications for defaulting members due to price differences between original transaction and auction prices.

**Sectoral Impact:** Diversified impact across sectors including energy (Adani Energy, Asian Energy, Tata Power), automotive (Bajaj Auto, Hero Motocorp, Escorts), pharmaceuticals (Aurobindo Pharma, Lyka Labs, Marksans Pharma), IT (Infosys, Hexaware), and infrastructure (L&T, BHEL, NBCC).
