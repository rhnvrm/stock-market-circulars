## Summary

BSE has announced the availability of non-competitive bidding facility for the RBI auction of Government of India dated securities scheduled for November 14, 2025. Trading members and direct investors can participate in the auction of two G-secs (6.68% GS 2040 and 6.90% GS 2065) through BSE's iBBS web-based NCB-GSec module. The total issue size is Rs. 28,000 crores with settlement scheduled for November 17, 2025.

## Key Points

- Two G-secs available for non-competitive bidding: 6.68% GS 2040 (re-issue of Rs. 16,000 crores) and 6.90% GS 2065 (re-issue of Rs. 12,000 crores)
- Bidding through BSE's iBBS web-based system NCB-GSec module at https://ibbs.bseindia.com
- Minimum subscription units: 100 units (Rs. 10,000)
- Maximum bidding amount: Rs. 2 crores per security
- Bid collection starts November 11, 2025 from 10:00 am (24-hour availability)
- Different deadlines for trading members (November 14, 8:00 am) and direct investors (November 13, 5:00 pm)
- Reference to earlier circular no. 20180423-42 dated April 23, 2018 regarding NCB facility launch

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This circular provides operational details for an existing non-competitive bidding facility established in 2018.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must submit bids through the NCB-GSec module of BSE's iBBS system
- Trading members must complete bid submission by November 14, 2025 at 8:00 am
- Direct investors must complete bid submission by November 13, 2025 at 5:00 pm
- Minimum bidding amount of Rs. 10,000 (100 units) and maximum of Rs. 2 crores per security must be adhered to
- Contact details provided for user ID creation, password reset, bidding queries, and obligation/pay-in queries

## Important Dates

- **November 10, 2025**: RBI press release announcing auction
- **November 11, 2025**: Bid collection opens at 10:00 am
- **November 13, 2025**: Bid collection ends for direct investors at 5:00 pm
- **November 14, 2025**: Bid collection ends for trading members at 8:00 am; Auction date
- **November 17, 2025**: Settlement date

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with minimal market impact. It facilitates participation of retail investors and trading members in the government securities auction through the non-competitive bidding route. The circular applies only to debt segment participants interested in G-sec investments. No changes to existing procedures or new compliance obligations. The two securities being auctioned are re-issues of existing G-secs with long tenure (2040 and 2065 maturity).
