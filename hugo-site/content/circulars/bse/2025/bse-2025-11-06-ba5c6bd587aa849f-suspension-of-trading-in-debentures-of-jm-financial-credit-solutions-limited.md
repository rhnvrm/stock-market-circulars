## Summary

BSE has suspended trading in specific debentures of JM Financial Credit Solutions Limited effective November 7, 2025. The suspension is due to the company setting a record date of November 8, 2025 for partial redemption (buyback at investor request) and interest payment on its 8.80% Non-Convertible Debentures maturing December 19, 2025.

## Key Points

- Trading suspended for debentures with ISIN INE651J07952 (Code: 974918)
- Suspension effective from November 7, 2025
- Record date set for November 8, 2025
- Debenture series: JMFCSL 8.80% maturing 19-12-2025 (Private)
- Purpose: Partial redemption due to buyback at investor request and interest payment
- Trading members advised not to deal in these debentures

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a standard corporate action procedure requiring trading suspension to establish record date for payment processing.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute any deals in the specified debentures from November 7, 2025
- Trading members must inform their clients holding these debentures about the suspension
- Ensure proper settlement of existing trades before suspension date

## Important Dates

- **November 6, 2025**: Notice issued (Notice No. 20251106-7, Ref: DR-751/2025-2026)
- **November 7, 2025**: Trading suspension begins (no dealings from this date)
- **November 8, 2025**: Record date for partial redemption and interest payment
- **December 19, 2025**: Original maturity date of debentures

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Limited to holders of this specific debenture series. The suspension prevents secondary market trading temporarily but ensures orderly processing of the corporate action.

**Investor Impact**: Debenture holders whose names appear on the record date will receive partial redemption proceeds and interest payment. Those seeking liquidity cannot trade during suspension period.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members need to update their systems to block trading in this security and communicate with affected clients. Standard corporate action processing will apply for eligible debenture holders.
