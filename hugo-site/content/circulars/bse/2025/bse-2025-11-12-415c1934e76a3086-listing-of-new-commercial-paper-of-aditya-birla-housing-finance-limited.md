## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Papers issued by Aditya Birla Housing Finance Limited on private placement basis. The listing consists of 8500 securities of face value Rs. 5,00,000 each, totaling Rs. 425 crore, admitted to dealings on the BSE Debt segment effective November 12, 2025. The instruments carry CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+ credit ratings and will mature on February 10, 2026.

## Key Points

- **Quantity**: 8500 Commercial Papers
- **Face Value**: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- **Issue Price**: Rs. 4,92,546 per unit
- **Scrip Code**: 730545
- **Scrip ID**: ABHF111125
- **ISIN Number**: INE831R14FK5
- **Credit Rating**: CRISIL A1+, ICRA A1+
- **Market Lot**: 1 unit
- **Trading**: Dematerialized form only
- **Standard Denomination**: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- **Tick Size**: 1 paise
- **Issuing and Paying Agent**: HDFC Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for privately placed debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE831R14FK5
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size compliance of 1 paise must be maintained
- For clarifications, trading members may contact BSE debt department on 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: November 11, 2025
- **Listing Date**: November 12, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: February 10, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine commercial paper listing for short-term debt financing by Aditya Birla Housing Finance Limited. The 91-day tenor instruments are targeted at institutional investors for liquidity management.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members gain access to a new debt instrument with strong credit ratings (A1+ from both CRISIL and ICRA), providing investment options for clients seeking short-term debt exposure to the housing finance sector. The high denomination (Rs. 5 lakh minimum) limits participation to institutional and high net-worth investors.
