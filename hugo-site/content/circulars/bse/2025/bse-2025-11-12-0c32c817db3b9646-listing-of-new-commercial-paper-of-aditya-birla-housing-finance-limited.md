## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper securities issued by Aditya Birla Housing Finance Limited on private placement basis. The listing comprises 8,500 units with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, issued at a discount price of Rs. 4,92,546. The securities are available for trading on the BSE Debt segment effective 12th November 2025, with maturity on 10th February 2026.

## Key Points

- **Issuer:** Aditya Birla Housing Finance Limited
- **Security Type:** Commercial Paper (Private Placement)
- **Quantity:** 8,500 units
- **Face Value:** Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- **Issue Price:** Rs. 4,92,546 per unit
- **Scrip Code:** 730545
- **Scrip ID:** ABHF111125
- **ISIN:** INE831R14FK5
- **Credit Rating:** CRISIL A1+, ICRA A1+
- **Market Lot:** 1 unit
- **Trading Mode:** Dematerialised form only
- **Standard Denomination:** Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- **Tick Size:** 1 paise
- **Issuing and Paying Agent:** HDFC Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notification for debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form under ISIN INE831R14FK5
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size of 1 paise must be adhered to for all transactions
- Trading members requiring clarifications should contact BSE debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date:** 11th November 2025
- **Listing Date:** 12th November 2025
- **Redemption Date:** 10th February 2026
- **Tenure:** Approximately 91 days

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Minimal. This is a routine commercial paper listing that provides short-term debt instruments for institutional investors and debt market participants.

**Operational Impact:** None for equity market participants. Relevant only to debt segment traders dealing with commercial papers and short-term debt instruments.

**Investor Impact:** The high credit ratings (CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+) indicate strong creditworthiness, making these instruments suitable for conservative debt investors seeking short-term investment opportunities with minimal credit risk.
