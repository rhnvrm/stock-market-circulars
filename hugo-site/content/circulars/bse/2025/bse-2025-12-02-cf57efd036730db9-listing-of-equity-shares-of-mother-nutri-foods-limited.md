## Summary

BSE has listed 12,535,020 equity shares of Mother Nutri Foods Limited following its Initial Public Offering (IPO). The listing includes various categories of shares with different lock-in periods: promoter shares locked until December 2026-2028, anchor investor shares with 30-90 day lock-in periods, and IPO-free shares available for immediate trading. All shares are fully paid and held in demat form.

## Key Points

- Total shares listed: 12,535,020 equity shares
- All shares are fully paid and held in demat form
- Multiple lock-in categories with varying restrictions
- Anchor investor allocation: 2,239,200 shares (distinctive numbers 9,827,821 to 12,535,020)
- OFS-free shares: Part of the allocation with shorter lock-in periods
- IPO-free shares: Available for immediate trading post-listing

## Lock-in Details

### Promoter and Strategic Holdings
- Various tranches locked from November 25-27, 2025 until December 13, 2026/2027/2028
- Long-term lock-in periods ranging from 1 to 3 years
- Distinctive number ranges: 1 to 10,767,420

### Anchor Investor Lock-in
- Lock-in period: December 1, 2025 to January 31, 2026 (30 days)
- Lock-in period: December 1, 2025 to March 1, 2026 (90 days)
- Total anchor shares: 2,239,200

### IPO-Free Shares
- No lock-in restrictions
- Available for immediate trading

## Important Dates

- Listing date: December 2, 2025
- Anchor lock-in start: December 1, 2025
- Anchor 30-day lock-in expiry: December 31, 2025
- Anchor 90-day lock-in expiry: March 1, 2026
- Promoter lock-in start: November 25-27, 2025
- Promoter lock-in expiry: December 13, 2026/2027/2028 (varying by tranche)

## Impact Assessment

The listing of Mother Nutri Foods Limited introduces a new security to the BSE platform with approximately 17.86% of total shares available for near-term trading (anchor investors post lock-in and IPO-free shares). The staggered lock-in periods will gradually increase the tradeable float over the next 1-3 years, potentially impacting liquidity and price discovery. Investors should note the significant promoter lock-in indicating long-term commitment, while the anchor allocation suggests institutional interest in the IPO.
