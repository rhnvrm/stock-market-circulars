## Summary

BSE announces the listing of Mirae Asset BSE 500 Dividend Leaders 50 ETF, an open-ended scheme tracking the BSE 500 Dividend Leaders 50 Total Return Index. The ETF will commence trading on December 19, 2025, in the 'B' Group with scrip code 544661. A total of 1,884,753 units were allotted at an issue price of Rs. 35.32 per unit.

## Key Points

- Scrip Code: 544661
- Scrip ID: DIVIDEND
- ISIN: INF769K01PY8
- Number of Units Listed: 1,884,753 units
- Face Value: Rs. 10.00 per unit
- Issue Price: Rs. 35.32 per unit
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Group: B
- Date of Allotment: December 15, 2025
- Trading Mode: Dematerialized form only
- Investment Manager: Mirae Asset Investment Managers (India) Private Limited
- Registrar: KFIN Technologies Limited

## Market Makers

- Mirae Asset Capital Markets (India) Private Limited
- Kanjalochana Finserve Private Limited
- East India Securities Limited
- Parwati Capital Markets Privates Limited
- Vaibhav Stock & Derivatives Broking Private Limited
- IRage Broking Services LLP

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard ETF listing following SEBI guidelines dated February 16, 2000, requiring mutual fund units to be traded in dematerialized form only.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note that units will only be traded in dematerialized form as per SEBI guidelines
- Trades will be executed in unit market lot (i.e., one unit)
- No specific compliance actions required from market participants

## Important Dates

- **December 15, 2025**: Date of allotment
- **December 18, 2025**: Notice date
- **December 19, 2025**: Listing effective date and commencement of trading

## Impact Assessment

Low market impact. This is a routine ETF listing that adds a new dividend-focused investment product to the exchange. The ETF provides investors with exposure to high dividend-yielding stocks from the BSE 500 index. Trading members gain access to a new tradeable security with designated market makers ensuring liquidity. No operational changes or compliance burden for existing market participants.
