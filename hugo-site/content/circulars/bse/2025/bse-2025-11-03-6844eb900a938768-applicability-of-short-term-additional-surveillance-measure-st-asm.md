## Summary

BSE has updated the list of securities under the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective November 04, 2025. The circular identifies 19 securities being newly shortlisted for ST-ASM, 1 security moving to a higher stage within the framework, and 11 securities moving out of the ST-ASM framework.

## Key Points

- 19 securities newly shortlisted in Short Term 5/15/30 Days ASM Framework
- Includes both mainboard and SME scrips (SME scrips marked with @)
- 1 security (Pace E-Commerce Ventures Ltd) moving to higher stage ASM
- 11 securities exiting ST-ASM framework
- Several securities exiting due to inclusion in other surveillance frameworks (LT-ASM, GSM, ESM, etc.)
- Changes effective from November 04, 2025

## Regulatory Framework

The Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure is applied to securities that exhibit:
- Unusual price movements
- Abnormal trading volumes
- Potential market manipulation concerns

Securities under ST-ASM are subject to:
- Higher margin requirements
- Trade-to-trade settlement
- Price bands
- Additional disclosure requirements

## Exit Reasons

Securities may exit ST-ASM due to inclusion in other frameworks:
- (*) LT-ASM Framework (Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure)
- (**) Trade for Trade Framework
- (#) GSM Framework (Graded Surveillance Measure)
- (^) Pledge Framework
- ($) ESM Framework (Enhanced Surveillance Measure)

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** November 04, 2025
- All changes to ST-ASM framework applicability take effect from market opening on this date

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure compliance with ST-ASM framework requirements for affected securities
- Enhanced margin requirements apply to all positions in ST-ASM securities
- Trade-to-trade settlement mandatory for these securities
- Price bands applicable as per ST-ASM stage (5/15/30 days)

## Impact Assessment

**Trading Impact:**
- Reduced liquidity for securities entering ST-ASM due to higher margins and trade-to-trade settlement
- Investors holding positions in these securities will face higher capital requirements
- Improved liquidity expected for 11 securities exiting the framework

**Market Participants Affected:**
- Retail and institutional investors with positions in affected securities
- Brokers managing margin requirements for clients
- Market makers and liquidity providers

**Risk Mitigation:**
- ST-ASM framework aims to protect investors from excessive volatility
- Enhanced surveillance helps detect and prevent potential market manipulation
- Staged approach (5/15/30 days) allows graduated response to market conditions
