## Summary

BSE announces the availability of non-competitive bidding facility for Government of India dated securities auction scheduled for November 21, 2025. Two securities will be auctioned: 6.01% GS 2030 (re-issue of Rs. 18,000 crores) and 7.09% GS 2074 (re-issue of Rs. 12,000 crores). Trading members and direct investors can submit bids through BSE's iBBS web-based NCB-GSec module.

## Key Points

- Two G-secs offered for auction: 6.01% GS 2030 (Symbol: 601GS30N25) and 7.09% GS 2074 (Symbol: 709GS74N25)
- Total issue size: Rs. 18,000 crores for 6.01% GS 2030 and Rs. 12,000 crores for 7.09% GS 2074
- Both are re-issue securities
- Minimum subscription units: 100 with multiples of 100
- Minimum bid amount: Rs. 10,000
- Maximum bid amount: Rs. 2 crores per security
- Bidding platform: BSE iBBS web-based system (https://ibbs.bseindia.com) NCB-GSec module
- Reference to Exchange circular no. 20180423-42 dated April 23, 2018

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is a routine implementation of the existing non-competitive bidding facility for G-sec auctions established under Exchange circular no. 20180423-42 dated April 23, 2018.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use BSE's iBBS web-based NCB-GSec module for bid submission
- Members must adhere to specified bid collection timelines
- Direct investors have different bid collection end date compared to members
- Participants must comply with minimum and maximum bidding limits
- Members must contact specified teams for user ID creation, password reset, bidding queries, and settlement-related issues

## Important Dates

- **November 17, 2025**: RBI press release announcing the auction
- **November 18, 2025, 10:00 AM**: Bid collection starts (24-hour availability)
- **November 20, 2025, 5:00 PM**: Bid collection end for Direct Investors
- **November 21, 2025, 8:00 AM**: Bid collection end for Trading Members
- **November 21, 2025**: Auction date
- **November 24, 2025**: Settlement date

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Low. This is a routine G-sec auction announcement affecting only debt market participants interested in government securities. The non-competitive bidding facility provides retail investors and trading members access to government securities auctions.

**Operational Impact**: Low to Medium for trading members and direct investors participating in G-sec auctions. They need to ensure timely bid submission through the NCB-GSec module within specified deadlines.

**Liquidity Impact**: The combined issue size of Rs. 30,000 crores (Rs. 18,000 crores + Rs. 12,000 crores) will add to government securities market liquidity.
