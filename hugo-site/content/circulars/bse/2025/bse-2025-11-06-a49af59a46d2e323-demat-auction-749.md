## Summary

BSE has issued the demat auction list for Settlement No. 749 dated November 6, 2025. The circular lists 196 securities with their respective scrip codes and quantities to be auctioned due to failed delivery obligations. This is a routine settlement process for securities where delivery obligations were not met in normal settlement.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 749
- Settlement Date: November 6, 2025
- Total Securities Listed: 196 scrips across various sectors
- Highest quantity for auction: YES BANK LTD (18,992 shares)
- Major blue-chip stocks included: BAJAJ FINANCE (2,800), INFOSYS (5,335), STATE BANK OF INDIA, RELIANCE group companies
- Covers equities from large-cap to small-cap segments

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational circular for demat auction settlement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members with shortages in the listed securities must participate in the auction
- Members must fulfill their delivery obligations through the auction mechanism
- Payment and settlement to be completed as per BSE auction settlement guidelines
- Non-fulfillment may result in penalties and further action as per exchange regulations

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: November 6, 2025
- Auction Date: As per standard T+2 auction timeline (typically 2 days after settlement date)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Low - This is a routine operational process affecting only specific trading members with failed delivery obligations.

**Trading Impact:** Limited to auction participants who failed to deliver securities in the normal settlement cycle.

**Investor Impact:** Minimal - Does not affect regular investors or market trading. Only impacts members with short positions in demat settlement.

**Operational Scope:** The auction covers a diverse range of securities from large-cap stocks like INFOSYS, BAJAJ FINANCE, and NTPC to smaller companies, indicating delivery failures across market segments. The largest position is YES BANK with 18,992 shares, followed by POWER GRID (19,774 shares) and CANARA BANK (25,787 shares).
