## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, November 29, 2025. This session is designed for trading members to test their trading applications, including third-party platforms and in-house systems using ETI APIs. The mock trading will simulate various market scenarios including exceptional conditions like risk-reduction mode and trading halts. No real obligations will arise from this session.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for November 29, 2025
- No new BOLT TWS Release planned
- Trading members can test third-party or in-house trading systems via ETI APIs
- Various market conditions will be simulated including risk-reduction mode and trading halts
- Mock trades will not create any margin, pay-in, pay-out obligations or legal rights/liabilities
- Feedback requested from all participants by 3:00 PM on session day
- Active participation encouraged for system testing and familiarization

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine mock trading session.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are requested to participate actively in the mock trading session
- Members must provide feedback by 3:00 PM on November 29, 2025
- Contact BSE Tech Support or Help Desk for queries or clarifications
- TWS users and system developers should coordinate with their Relationship Manager

## Important Dates

- **November 29, 2025**: Mock trading session
  - Log-in: 09:15 AM to 10:00 AM
  - Continuous Trading: 10:00 AM to 01:00 PM
  - Trade Modification: 01:00 PM to 01:10 PM
- **Feedback Deadline**: 3:00 PM on November 29, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: None. This is a mock trading session with no real market transactions.

**Operational Impact**: Low. Provides trading members an opportunity to test and familiarize themselves with their trading systems and various market condition scenarios. This is a routine testing exercise to ensure system readiness and does not affect live trading operations or create any financial obligations.
