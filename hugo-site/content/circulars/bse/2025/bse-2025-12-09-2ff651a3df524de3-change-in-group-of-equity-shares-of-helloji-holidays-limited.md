## Summary

BSE has announced that the equity shares of HELLOJI HOLIDAYS LIMITED (Scrip Code: 544630), currently listed on the SME platform, will be transferred from the Trade for Trade segment (MT Group) to the Rolling segment (M Group) effective December 23, 2025. This change follows a previous notice dated December 8, 2025, and represents a shift to more flexible trading arrangements for the stock.

## Key Points

- Company: HELLOJI HOLIDAYS LIMITED (SME IPO)
- Scrip Code: 544630
- Current Segment: Trade for Trade segment (MT Group)
- New Segment: Rolling segment (M Group)
- Effective Date: Tuesday, December 23, 2025
- Contact Person: Mr. Anurag Jain, Tel: 022-2272 8822
- Circular Issued By: Hardik Bhuta, Assistant Vice President

## Regulatory Changes

The stock will transition from a more restrictive trading environment to a standard rolling settlement mechanism:

- **From MT Group (Trade for Trade)**: Restrictive segment requiring compulsory delivery with limited speculation
- **To M Group (Rolling Settlement)**: Standard rolling settlement allowing T+2 settlement cycle with greater liquidity

This change indicates the stock has met BSE's criteria for graduation from the stricter Trade for Trade segment to normal rolling settlement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must update their systems to reflect the group change from MT to M Group
- Trading Members should contact Mr. Anurag Jain for operational details and clarifications
- Members need to prepare for the transition by December 23, 2025
- All pending trades in MT Group should be settled before the migration date

## Important Dates

- **December 8, 2025**: Previous related notice issued (Notice No. 20251208-28)
- **December 9, 2025**: Current notice issued (Notice No. 20251209-3)
- **December 23, 2025**: Effective date for transfer to Rolling segment (M Group)

## Impact Assessment

**Positive Impacts:**
- Enhanced liquidity for HELLOJI HOLIDAYS LIMITED shares
- Greater trading flexibility for market participants
- Reduced settlement restrictions allowing for more active trading
- Improved price discovery mechanism through rolling settlement
- Better access for retail and institutional investors

**Operational Impact:**
- Trading Members need to update internal systems and risk management parameters
- Margin requirements may change with the shift to rolling settlement
- Brokers should inform clients holding or interested in trading this scrip
- The stock becomes eligible for intraday trading and other rolling segment benefits

**Market Significance:**
This transition typically occurs when a newly listed SME stock demonstrates adequate stability and trading activity, signaling BSE's confidence in the stock's market readiness for standard trading mechanisms.
