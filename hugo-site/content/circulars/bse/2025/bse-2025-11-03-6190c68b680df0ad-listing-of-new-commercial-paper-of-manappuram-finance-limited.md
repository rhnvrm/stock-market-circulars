## Summary

BSE has listed new commercial papers issued by Manappuram Finance Limited on private placement basis effective November 3, 2025. The listing comprises 1000 commercial papers with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each (total Rs. 50 crore), issued at a discount price of Rs. 4,68,959 per unit. The instruments carry CARE A1+ and CRISIL A1+ credit ratings and will mature on September 16, 2026.

## Key Points

- **Instrument**: Commercial Paper on private placement basis
- **Quantity**: 1000 units
- **Face Value**: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- **Issue Price**: Rs. 4,68,959 per unit (discount pricing)
- **Total Issue Size**: Rs. 50 crore
- **Scrip Code**: 730478
- **Scrip ID**: MFL311025
- **ISIN**: INE522D14OT3
- **Credit Rating**: CARE A1+, CRISIL A1+
- **Market Lot**: 1 unit
- **Tick Size**: 1 paise
- **Issuing and Paying Agent**: AXIS BANK

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for debt instruments.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE522D14OT3
- Trading shall take place in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Securities must be traded on BSE Debt segment only
- All trading to be conducted through demat accounts

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: October 31, 2025
- **Listing Date**: November 3, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: September 16, 2026
- **Tenor**: Approximately 10.5 months

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine commercial paper listing on the debt segment with no impact on equity markets.

**Operational Impact**: No operational changes for trading members. Standard debt trading procedures apply.

**Investor Impact**: The commercial paper provides short-term investment opportunity with high credit ratings (A1+ from both CARE and CRISIL), indicating strong repayment capacity. The discount pricing (issue price Rs. 4,68,959 vs face value Rs. 5,00,000) implies an effective yield for investors.

**Liquidity**: Trading in Rs. 5 lakh denominations limits participation to institutional and high-net-worth investors.
