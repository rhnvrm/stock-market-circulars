## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction list for Settlement No. 757 dated November 18, 2025. The auction includes 109 securities across various companies with quantities ranging from 1 share to 116,725 shares. This is a standard settlement mechanism for resolving delivery shortfalls in dematerialized securities.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 757
- Auction Date: November 18, 2025
- Total Securities Listed: 109 scrips
- Scrip codes range from 500087 (CIPLA) to 590108 (NIPPON INDIA)
- Largest quantity: 116,725 shares of Billionbrain (scrip code 544603)
- Multiple blue-chip stocks included: ICICI Bank (8 shares), State Bank of India (100 shares), CIPLA (15 shares), HCL Tech (10 shares)
- Notable quantities: RICO AUTO (10,000 shares), B.C. POWER C (6,085 shares), CATVISION (26,171 shares), SUYOG TELEMA (3,179 shares), Easy Trip (3,000 shares)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced in this circular. This is a standard operational notice for demat auction settlements.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and clearing members must note the securities listed for auction
- Participants with short delivery positions in these scrips must participate in or settle through the auction process
- Settlement obligations must be fulfilled as per BSE settlement calendar and procedures
- Members should ensure adequate funds/securities are available for auction settlement

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: November 18, 2025 (Settlement No. 757)
- Auction execution and settlement to follow standard BSE auction timeline

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects 109 securities with varying liquidity levels. Large-cap stocks have minimal quantities, limiting broader market impact.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - trading and clearing members with positions in listed securities must ensure compliance with auction settlement procedures.

**Investor Impact:** Low to Medium - primarily affects institutional investors and brokers with failed delivery obligations. Retail investors holding these securities are not directly impacted unless they have pending delivery obligations.

**Sectoral Distribution:** Diverse - includes banking (ICICI Bank, Canara Bank, Central Bank, State Bank), technology (HCL Tech, Saksoft), aviation (SpiceJet), consumer (Swiggy), power (Tata Power, GMR Power), and various other sectors, indicating no concentrated sectoral stress.
