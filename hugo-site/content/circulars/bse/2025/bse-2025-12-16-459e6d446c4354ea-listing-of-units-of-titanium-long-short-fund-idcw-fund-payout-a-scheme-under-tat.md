## Summary

BSE has announced the listing of units of Titanium Long-Short Fund IDCW Fund payout, a close-ended equity scheme under Tata Mutual Fund, effective December 17, 2025. The scheme invests in equity and equity-related instruments as well as debt and money market instruments, including limited short exposure through derivatives. A total of 124,994 units have been allotted at Rs. 10.01 per unit.

## Key Points

- **Fund Name**: Titanium Hybrid Long-Short Fund Regular Plan IDCW Payout
- **Investment Manager**: Tata Asset Management Pvt Ltd
- **Number of Units**: 124,994 units
- **Scrip Code**: 544652
- **ISIN**: INF277K30039
- **Face Value**: Rs. 10.00 per unit
- **Issue Price**: Rs. 10.01 per unit
- **Market Lot**: 1 unit
- **Group**: B
- **Trading Mode**: Dematerialized form only
- **Allotment Date**: December 11, 2025
- **Listing Date**: December 17, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notification following SEBI guidelines dated February 16, 2000, which mandate that mutual fund units be traded only in dematerialized form.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure that all trades in these units are executed in dematerialized form only
- Trades will be effected in unit market lot (i.e., one unit)
- Trading members should note the scrip details for proper identification on the BOLT system

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: December 11, 2025
- **Listing and Trading Commencement Date**: December 17, 2025 (Wednesday)
- **Circular Issue Date**: December 16, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This listing has minimal market-wide impact as it involves a standard mutual fund unit listing with a relatively small number of units (124,994). The impact is limited to:

- **Investors**: Provides liquidity option for unitholders of this close-ended scheme
- **Trading Members**: Need to update their systems with the new scrip code and ISIN for trading
- **Market Operations**: Routine addition to B Group securities list

The fund's strategy involves equity and debt investments with limited short exposure through derivatives, making it a hybrid long-short scheme suitable for investors seeking tactical allocation strategies.
