## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper issued by Birla Group Holdings Private Limited on private placement basis effective December 8, 2025. The listing comprises 4000 Commercial Papers with a face value of Rs.5,00,000/- each, carrying CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+ credit ratings. The instruments will be traded exclusively in dematerialized form on the BSE Debt segment with a maturity period of 3 months.

## Key Points

- **Issuer**: Birla Group Holdings Private Limited
- **Security Type**: Commercial Paper (Private Placement)
- **Quantity**: 4000 units
- **Face Value**: Rs.5,00,000/- per unit
- **Issue Price**: Rs.4,91,845.50/- per unit
- **Market Lot**: 1 unit
- **Scrip Code**: 729327
- **Scrip ID**: BGHP70525
- **Detail Name**: BGHPL-9-3-26-CP
- **ISIN**: INE09OL14HE8 (Further listing under same ISIN)
- **Credit Rating**: CRISIL A1+, ICRA A1+
- **Issuing and Paying Agent**: Axis Bank Limited
- **Trading Denomination**: Rs.5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- **Tick Size**: 1 paise

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a routine listing notification.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE09OL14HE8
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs.5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- All transactions must adhere to the tick size of 1 paise
- Trading members requiring clarification may contact BSE debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Listing Date**: December 8, 2025
- **Allotment Date**: December 8, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: March 9, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine commercial paper listing with minimal market impact. The issuance adds short-term debt instruments to the BSE Debt segment, providing institutional investors with additional investment options in highly-rated commercial paper (A1+ rating from both CRISIL and ICRA). The 3-month maturity period and private placement nature indicate this is standard corporate treasury management activity with no broader implications for equity markets or retail investors.
