## Summary

BSE has announced a demat auction for Settlement No. 757 dated November 18, 2025. The auction involves delivery shortages across 109 scrips from various companies. Quantities range from single shares to tens of thousands of shares across different sectors including banking, power, pharmaceuticals, technology, and consumer goods.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 757
- Date: November 18, 2025
- Total scrips involved: 109 different securities
- Sectors affected: Banking, Technology, Power, Pharmaceuticals, Consumer Goods, Real Estate, Manufacturing
- Quantity range: From 1 share to 116,725 shares depending on the scrip
- Major quantities include: Billionbrain (116,725 shares), CATVISION L (26,171 shares), RICO AUTO IN (10,000 shares), B.C. POWER C (6,085 shares)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a standard operational circular for demat auction procedures.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants with delivery shortages must participate in the demat auction
- Settlement obligations must be fulfilled as per BSE auction guidelines
- Members must ensure delivery of securities as per the auction results

## Important Dates

- **Settlement Date:** November 18, 2025
- **Auction Date:** To be conducted as per standard BSE auction timeline for Settlement No. 757

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - The auction affects over 100 securities across multiple sectors, indicating widespread delivery shortages in this settlement cycle.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - Brokers and trading members with open positions in these securities will need to participate in the auction or face potential penalties.

**Investor Impact:** Low to Medium - Individual investors with pending deliveries in these scrips may experience delays or price variations due to auction mechanisms.

**Notable Securities:** Large quantity shortages in smaller/mid-cap stocks like Billionbrain (116,725 shares), CATVISION L (26,171 shares), and RICO AUTO IN (10,000 shares) suggest potential liquidity issues or settlement failures in these counters.
