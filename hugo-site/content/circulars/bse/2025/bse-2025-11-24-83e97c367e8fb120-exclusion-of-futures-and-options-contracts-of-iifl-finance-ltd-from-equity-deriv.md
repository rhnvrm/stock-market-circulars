## Summary

BSE has announced the exclusion of IIFL Finance Ltd from the Equity Derivatives segment effective January 30, 2026. This action follows SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-2/P/CIR/2024/116 dated August 30, 2024 regarding eligibility criteria of stocks in derivatives. No new expiry month contracts will be generated for IIFL Finance Ltd from the discontinuation date.

## Key Points

- IIFL Finance Ltd (Scrip Code: 532636, Derivatives Asset Code: IIFL) will be removed from equity derivatives trading
- Discontinuation effective from January 30, 2026
- No new expiry month contracts will be created after announcement
- Existing unexpired contracts will continue until their natural expiry
- Decision based on review of eligibility criteria for stocks in derivatives segment

## Regulatory Changes

This exclusion is implemented pursuant to:
- SEBI Circular Ref. No: SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-2/P/CIR/2024/116 dated August 30, 2024 regarding eligibility criteria of stocks
- BSE Notice no 20240902-3 dated September 02, 2024 regarding review of eligibility criteria for entry/exit of stocks in derivatives segment

The stock no longer meets the eligibility criteria required for continuation in the equity derivatives segment.

## Compliance Requirements

**For Trading Members:**
- Note the discontinuation timeline and plan for position management
- Inform clients holding positions in IIFL Finance derivatives
- Ensure proper risk management for existing open positions
- No new positions should be initiated beyond existing contract months
- Monitor existing contracts through their expiry dates

## Important Dates

- **November 24, 2025**: Notice issued
- **November 27, 2025**: November 2025 contracts expiry
- **December 24, 2025**: December 2025 contracts expiry
- **January 29, 2026**: January 2026 contracts expiry (final expiry)
- **January 30, 2026**: Complete discontinuation - no contracts available for trading

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:**
- All existing F&O positions in IIFL Finance Ltd must be closed or rolled before final expiry
- Traders and hedgers using IIFL derivatives need to find alternative hedging instruments
- Potential increase in volatility in existing contracts as expiry approaches
- Liquidity may decrease in later expiry months as discontinuation approaches

**Operational Impact:**
- Trading members must update systems to prevent new contract generation
- Risk management systems need adjustment for position limits
- Client communication required for position holders
- Market makers and arbitrageurs will exit the security

**Investor Impact:**
- High impact for traders with open positions in IIFL Finance derivatives
- Hedging strategies using IIFL F&O will need restructuring
- Speculators must close positions within the specified timeline
- No future trading opportunities in IIFL derivatives from January 30, 2026
