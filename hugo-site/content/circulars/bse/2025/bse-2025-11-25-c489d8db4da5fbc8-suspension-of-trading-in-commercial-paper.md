## Summary

BSE has announced the suspension of trading in commercial paper (CP) issued by 13 companies effective from November 27 to December 5, 2025. The suspension is due to record dates being fixed for redemption of these commercial papers. Trading members are advised not to deal in these securities from the specified dates.

## Key Points

- 13 commercial paper securities affected across multiple issuers
- Trading suspension period: November 27, 2025 to December 5, 2025
- Purpose: Redemption of commercial paper at maturity
- Record dates have been fixed by respective companies
- Trading members must not deal in these securities from the no-dealings date

**November 27, 2025 (Record & No Dealings Date):**
- Mindspace Business Parks REIT (MBPR-28-11-25-CP, ISIN: INE0CCU14088)
- Kirloskar Ferrous Industries Ltd. (KFIL-28-11-25-CP, ISIN: INE884B14671)

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute any trades in the listed commercial paper from the respective no-dealings dates
- Members must ensure compliance with the trading suspension for redemption processing
- All dealings must cease one business day before or on the record date as specified

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** November 25, 2025
- **First Suspension Date:** November 27, 2025 (2 securities)
- **Last Suspension Date:** December 4, 2025 (4 securities)
- **Record Dates:** Range from November 27 to December 4, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This circular has limited market impact as it pertains to standard operational procedures for commercial paper redemption. Debt market participants holding these specific commercial papers should note:

- Trading liquidity in these 13 CPs will be unavailable from specified dates
- Redemption will proceed as scheduled on respective maturity dates
- No impact on other debt securities or equity markets
- Routine corporate action with no broader market implications
- Investors holding these securities to maturity are unaffected

The suspension is temporary and procedural, allowing for orderly redemption of maturing commercial paper instruments.
