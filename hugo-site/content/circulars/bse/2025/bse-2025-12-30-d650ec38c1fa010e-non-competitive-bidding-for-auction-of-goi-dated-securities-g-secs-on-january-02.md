## Summary

BSE has announced the availability of non-competitive bidding facility for the RBI auction of Government of India dated securities scheduled for January 02, 2026. Trading members and direct investors can participate in the auction for 6.48% GS 2035 (re-issue) worth Rs. 32,000 crores through BSE's iBBS NCB-GSec module.

## Key Points

- Security offered: 6.48% GS 2035 (Re-issue)
- Issue size: Rs. 32,000 crores
- Minimum subscription units: Rs. 10,000 (in multiples of Rs. 100)
- Maximum amount per bid: Rs. 2 crores
- Bidding platform: iBBS web-based system (https://ibbs.bseindia.com)
- Non-competitive bidding facility available through BSE's NCB-GSec module
- Reference circular: BSE circular no. 20180423-42 dated April 23, 2018

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular continues the existing non-competitive bidding framework established in April 2018 for G-sec auctions conducted by RBI.

## Compliance Requirements

**For Trading Members:**
- Submit bids through NCB-GSec module on iBBS platform
- Ensure bids comply with minimum (Rs. 10,000) and maximum (Rs. 2 crores) limits
- Use multiples of Rs. 100 for bid amounts
- Contact relevant teams for user ID creation, password reset, or bidding queries

**For Direct Investors:**
- Must submit bids by earlier deadline (January 01, 2026, 5:00 PM)

## Important Dates

- **December 30, 2025, 10:00 AM**: Bid collection starts (24-hour availability)
- **January 01, 2026, 5:00 PM**: Bid collection ends for Direct Investors
- **January 02, 2026, 8:00 AM**: Bid collection ends for Trading Members
- **January 02, 2026**: Auction date
- **January 05, 2026**: Settlement date

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Low - This is a routine government securities auction notification affecting only debt market participants. No impact on equity trading.

**Operational Impact:** Low - Standard procedure for trading members already familiar with the NCB-GSec module. Support teams available for technical assistance.

**Investor Impact:** Minimal - Provides investment opportunity in government securities for institutional and retail participants through non-competitive route, ensuring allocation at weighted average price of competitive auction.
