## Summary

Manappuram Finance Limited has listed two series of Commercial Paper on BSE Debt segment effective December 4, 2025. Series 1 comprises 5,500 units worth Rs. 275 crores with 12-month tenure, and Series 2 comprises 7,500 units worth Rs. 375 crores with 3-month tenure. Both instruments are rated CARE A1+ and CRISIL A1+ with Axis Bank as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- Two Commercial Paper series listed with face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each
- Series 1 (MFL031225): 5,500 units, issue price Rs. 4,64,425, redemption on December 3, 2026
- Series 2 (MFL031225A): 7,500 units, issue price Rs. 4,91,727, redemption on March 2, 2026
- Credit Rating: CARE A1+ and CRISIL A1+ for both series
- Allotment date: December 3, 2025
- Trading only in dematerialized form
- Axis Bank appointed as Issuing and Paying Agent

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under specified ISIN numbers (INE522D14OZ0 and INE522D14PB8)
- Trading must occur in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size for the securities is 1 paise
- Trading members can contact debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915 for clarifications

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: December 4, 2025
- **Allotment Date**: December 3, 2025
- **Listing Effective Date**: December 4, 2025
- **Redemption Date (Series 1 - MFL031225)**: December 3, 2026
- **Redemption Date (Series 2 - MFL031225A)**: March 2, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine private placement listing of short-term debt instruments.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members can now trade these Commercial Paper instruments in the BSE Debt segment. The instruments provide short-term investment options with high credit ratings (A1+) for investors seeking exposure to Manappuram Finance Limited's debt.

**Investor Impact**: Provides institutional and qualified investors with liquid, highly-rated short-term debt instruments from a financial services company. The differential pricing reflects the tenure difference - longer tenure (12 months) commands a deeper discount compared to shorter tenure (3 months).
