## Summary

BSE has listed new secured non-convertible debentures issued by Avigna Housing Private Limited on private placement basis, effective November 11, 2025. The issue comprises 32,500 debentures with a face value of Rs. 1,00,000 each, totaling Rs. 325 crore. The securities will trade exclusively in dematerialized form on the BSE Debt segment.

## Key Points

- **Issuer**: Avigna Housing Private Limited
- **Quantity**: 32,500 debentures
- **Face Value**: Rs. 1,00,000 per debenture
- **Total Issue Size**: Rs. 325 crore
- **Scrip Code**: 977283
- **Scrip ID**: 4AHPL29
- **ISIN**: INE0QJD07018
- **Credit Rating**: ACUITE BB/Stable
- **Interest Rate**: 4% per annum
- **Interest Payment**: Monthly from November 30, 2025 to May 6, 2029
- **Market Lot**: 1 debenture
- **Tick Size**: 1 paise
- **Trading Mode**: Dematerialized form only
- **Put/Call Option**: Not Applicable

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for privately placed debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE0QJD07018
- Members should refer to the Placement Memorandum available at https://www.bseindia.com/markets/debt/memorandum_data.aspx for detailed terms
- For clarifications, trading members may contact the BSE debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment**: November 7, 2025
- **Listing Date**: November 11, 2025
- **First Interest Payment**: November 30, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: May 6, 2029
- **Interest Payment Frequency**: Monthly (from November 30, 2025 to May 6, 2029)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt securities listing with minimal market impact. The securities are privately placed NCDs of Avigna Housing Private Limited rated ACUITE BB/Stable, indicating below investment grade credit quality. The 4% coupon rate with monthly interest payments and approximately 3.5-year tenure provides investors with a regular income option. Trading members dealing in debt securities can now facilitate transactions in these instruments on the BSE Debt segment.
