## Summary

BSE has revised the circuit filter percentage to 2% for 71 securities across various sectors. This revision implements stricter price band limits, restricting the maximum price movement of these securities to 2% (both upper and lower circuits) during trading sessions. The circular lists all affected securities with their scrip codes, ISIN numbers, and company names.

## Key Points

- Circuit filter adjusted to 2% for 71 securities
- Includes companies across diverse sectors: real estate, manufacturing, textiles, finance, steel, packaging, and trading
- Notable companies include Ansal Properties & Infrastructure Ltd (500013), Future Enterprises Ltd (523574), Winsome Yarns Ltd (514348), and SPEL Semiconductor Ltd (517166)
- All affected securities listed with complete identification details (Scrip Code, ISIN, Company Name)
- Tighter circuit filters typically indicate higher volatility concerns or surveillance measures

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** The 2% circuit filter significantly restricts intraday price volatility for these 71 securities, potentially reducing liquidity and making it harder for large orders to be executed. This can lead to multiple circuit hits during volatile sessions.

**Trading Impact:** Traders and algorithmic systems must adjust their strategies to account for the narrower price bands. Day trading and momentum strategies may be particularly affected due to the limited price movement allowed.

**Investor Impact:** Long-term investors may face challenges in entering or exiting positions quickly, especially during periods of significant news or market movements. The measure provides downside protection but also limits upside potential within a single trading session.

**Liquidity Impact:** Tighter circuits typically reduce market liquidity as willing buyers and sellers cannot transact beyond the 2% limits, potentially leading to order accumulation at circuit levels.
