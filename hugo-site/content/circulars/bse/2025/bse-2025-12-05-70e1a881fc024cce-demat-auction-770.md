## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction list for Settlement No. 770 dated December 5, 2025. The auction includes 74 scrips across various companies where securities will be auctioned due to non-delivery by sellers. The quantities range from 1 share to over 119,000 shares depending on the scrip.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 770
- Auction Date: December 5, 2025
- Total scrips involved: 74 securities
- Largest quantity: 119,138 shares of Avance Technologies (Scrip Code: 512149)
- Notable companies include Infosys, Biocon, Tech Mahindra, Bank of Maharashtra, India Cements, Sun Pharma, Zydus Lifesciences, and Ola Electric
- Quantities range from as low as 1 share to over 100,000 shares

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced in this circular. This is a standard operational notice for demat auction proceedings.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members/brokers who have failed to deliver securities must participate in the auction or arrange delivery
- Buyers affected by non-delivery will receive securities through the auction mechanism
- Settlement obligations must be fulfilled as per BSE auction procedures

## Important Dates

- Settlement Number: 770
- Circular Date: December 5, 2025
- Auction proceedings to be conducted as per standard BSE settlement cycle

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects 74 different securities across various sectors

**Operational Impact:** The auction will ensure settlement completion for transactions where sellers failed to deliver securities. This is a routine market mechanism to protect buyers and ensure settlement integrity.

**Affected Parties:**
- Selling brokers who failed to deliver securities will need to purchase from auction
- Buying brokers will receive their securities through auction mechanism
- Shareholders of the listed companies may see temporary price movements during auction

**Key Statistics:**
- Highest quantity: Avance Technologies (119,138 shares)
- Other significant quantities: Mega Corporation (10,000 shares), Diligent Industries (2,961 shares), India Cement (6,930 shares), Parmeshwar M (6,000 shares)
- Several blue-chip stocks included with smaller quantities (Infosys: 9 shares, Tech Mahindra: 1 share, Biocon: 405 shares)
