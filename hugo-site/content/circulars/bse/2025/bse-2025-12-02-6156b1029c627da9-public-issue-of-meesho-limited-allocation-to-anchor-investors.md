## Summary

BSE has issued a circular regarding the allocation of shares to anchor investors for the public issue of Meesho Limited. This represents the pre-IPO allocation phase where qualified institutional buyers (QIBs) bid for shares at a discovered price before the issue opens to the general public.

## Key Points

- Anchor investor allocation completed for Meesho Limited IPO
- Process conducted in accordance with SEBI (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2018
- Anchor investors are typically large institutional investors who provide stability and price discovery
- Allocation details disclosed as per regulatory requirements
- Standard procedure before main IPO bidding period begins

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard disclosure as per existing SEBI regulations for public issues.

## Compliance Requirements

- Issuer and merchant bankers must disclose anchor investor allocation details
- Information to be made available to public before IPO opens
- Transparency requirements under SEBI ICDR Regulations must be followed
- Lock-in period applicable for anchor investor shares as per regulations

## Important Dates

- Anchor investor allocation date: December 2, 2025
- Main IPO bidding dates to follow anchor allocation
- Specific dates for public bidding period to be announced separately

## Impact Assessment

The anchor investor allocation for Meesho Limited IPO indicates institutional interest in the company. The quality and quantity of anchor investors can influence retail investor sentiment and overall subscription levels. This is a standard pre-IPO process that provides price discovery and helps establish investor confidence. The impact is assessed as medium as it represents a significant milestone in the IPO process but does not directly affect existing market operations or listed securities.
