## Summary

BSE announces the allocation of equity shares to anchor investors in the public issue of Nanta Tech Limited. The anchor investor allocation is conducted one day prior to the issue opening date as per SEBI regulations, providing early indication of institutional interest in the offering.

## Key Points

- Anchor investor allocation completed for Nanta Tech Limited's public issue
- Allocation conducted as per SEBI (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations
- Anchor investors are qualified institutional buyers (QIBs) who bid for shares before the issue opens to public
- Anchor allocation helps in price discovery and signals institutional confidence
- Details of allocation including number of shares, price, and investor names disclosed as per regulatory requirements

## Regulatory Framework

The anchor investor allocation follows SEBI guidelines which mandate:
- Allocation to be completed one day before issue opening
- Lock-in period of 90 days for anchor investors (30 days for up to 50% of allocation if issue price equals or exceeds anchor price)
- Disclosure of allocation details to ensure transparency
- Anchor portion limited to specific percentage of QIB allocation

## Compliance Requirements

The issuer company (Nanta Tech Limited) and lead managers must:
- Disclose complete details of anchor allocation
- Maintain allocation records as per SEBI requirements
- Ensure lock-in compliance for allocated shares
- Update issue prospectus with anchor allocation details

## Important Dates

- Anchor Investor Allocation Date: December 21, 2025 (typically T-1 day)
- Circular Publication Date: December 22, 2025
- Issue Opening Date: December 22, 2025 (expected)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - Anchor allocation provides insights into institutional demand and helps establish price discovery for the IPO. Strong anchor allocation typically indicates positive market sentiment.

**Investor Impact:** Medium - Retail and other investors can assess institutional interest before bidding. The allocation details and pricing serve as important reference points.

**Operational Impact:** The company proceeds with public issue opening following successful anchor allocation, moving forward in the capital raising process.
