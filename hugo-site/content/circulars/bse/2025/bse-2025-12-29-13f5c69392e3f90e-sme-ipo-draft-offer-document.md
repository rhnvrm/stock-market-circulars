## Summary

BSE has announced the availability of the Draft Offer Document for SILVERSTORM PARKS AND RESORTS LIMITED's SME IPO, filed on December 28, 2025. The document contains all disclosures as specified under Schedule VI of the ICDR Regulations and is available on the stock exchange website for public comments for a minimum period of 21 days.

## Key Points

- Draft Offer Document filed by SILVERSTORM PARKS AND RESORTS LIMITED on December 28, 2025
- Document complies with Schedule VI of ICDR Regulations
- Available on BSE SME website: www.bsesme.com/PublicIssues/SMEIPODRHP.aspx
- Public comments invited for 21 days from notice date
- Comments to be submitted to equity.ipo@bseindia.com
- Notice issued by Hardik Bhuta, Assistant Vice President, BSE

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard procedural notice for SME IPO draft offer document filing as per existing ICDR Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Interested parties and public can review the Draft Offer Document on BSE SME website
- Comments, if any, must be submitted within 21 days from December 29, 2025 (by January 19, 2026)
- Comments to be sent to equity.ipo@bseindia.com
- Company must address public comments as per ICDR Regulations before final filing

## Important Dates

- **December 28, 2025**: Draft Offer Document filed by SILVERSTORM PARKS AND RESORTS LIMITED
- **December 29, 2025**: Notice date and start of 21-day public comment period
- **January 19, 2026**: Last date for submitting public comments (21 days from notice)

## Impact Assessment

This notice impacts potential investors, market participants, and stakeholders interested in the SME segment. The 21-day public comment period is a mandatory regulatory requirement allowing transparency and public scrutiny before the IPO proceeds. The Draft Offer Document provides comprehensive information about SILVERSTORM PARKS AND RESORTS LIMITED's business, financials, risk factors, and offer details. Stakeholders should review the document and submit feedback if they have concerns or require clarifications before the company proceeds with the final offer document and IPO launch.
