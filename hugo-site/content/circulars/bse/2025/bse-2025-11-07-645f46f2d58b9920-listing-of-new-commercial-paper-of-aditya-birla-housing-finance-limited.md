## Summary

BSE has listed 5500 new Commercial Papers issued by Aditya Birla Housing Finance Limited on private placement basis with effect from November 7, 2025. The instruments are valued at Rs. 5,00,000/- each with a total issue size of Rs. 275 crores, carrying credit ratings of CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+. The securities are listed on the BSE Debt segment under scrip code 730508.

## Key Points

- Securities: Commercial Paper issued on private placement basis
- Face Value: Rs. 5,00,000/- per unit
- Issue Price: Rs. 4,92,546/- per unit
- Quantity: 5500 units (total value: Rs. 275 crores)
- Scrip Code: 730508
- Scrip ID: ABHF061125
- Detail Name: ABHFL-5-2-26-CP
- ISIN Number: INE831R14FJ7
- Credit Rating: CRISIL A1+, ICRA A1+
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Trading Denomination: Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick Size: 1 paise
- Trading Mode: Dematerialised form only
- Issuing and Paying Agent: HDFC Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for new debt instruments.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form under ISIN INE831R14FJ7
- Trading shall be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Members requiring clarification may contact the debt department at BSE on 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: November 7, 2025
- Listing Effective Date: November 7, 2025
- Actual/Deemed Date of Allotment: November 6, 2025
- Date of Redemption: February 5, 2026 (91-day tenor)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Low - This is a routine commercial paper listing that adds short-term debt instruments to the BSE Debt segment. The impact is limited to debt market participants and institutional investors.

**Investor Impact:** The listing provides liquidity to holders of these commercial papers and offers investment opportunities in highly-rated (A1+) short-term debt instruments of Aditya Birla Housing Finance Limited.

**Operational Impact:** Minimal - Standard trading procedures apply with securities traded in dematerialised form only, maintaining consistency with existing debt market operations.
