## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Papers issued by Northern Arc Capital Ltd on private placement basis effective December 19, 2025. A total of 400 Commercial Papers with face value of Rs. 5,00,000/- each have been admitted to trading on the BSE Debt segment. The instruments carry a CARE A1+ credit rating and will be redeemed on March 19, 2026. HDFC Bank Limited serves as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- 400 Commercial Papers of Rs. 5 lakhs each listed on BSE Debt segment
- Scrip Code: 730816, Scrip ID: NACL191225
- ISIN Number: INE850M14CK3
- Credit Rating: CARE A1+
- Face Value and Paid-up Value: Rs. 5,00,000/- each
- Issue Price: Rs. 4,90,328/-
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Date of Allotment: December 19, 2025
- Date of Redemption: March 19, 2026
- Trading in dematerialised form only
- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- Issuing and Paying Agent: HDFC Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard new listing notification for debt instruments.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form under ISIN INE850M14CK3
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Members should contact BSE debt department (22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915) for any clarifications

## Important Dates

- **Listing Date**: December 19, 2025
- **Allotment Date**: December 19, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: March 19, 2026
- **Tenor**: 90 days (approximately 3 months)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine listing of short-term debt instruments on private placement basis.

**Investor Impact**: Low. Relevant primarily to institutional investors and debt market participants who invest in highly-rated commercial papers. The CARE A1+ rating indicates high credit quality.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members dealing in debt securities need to update their systems with the new scrip details for trading purposes. The securities will trade in standard lots of Rs. 5 lakhs, making them accessible primarily to institutional and high-net-worth investors.
