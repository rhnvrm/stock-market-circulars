## Summary

BSE has suspended trading in commercial paper of 20 companies effective from their respective record dates set for redemption purposes. The suspensions span from December 23, 2025 to December 30, 2025, covering major corporates and financial institutions. Trading members are advised not to deal in these commercial papers from the specified no-dealings dates.

## Key Points

- 20 commercial paper issues suspended for trading due to redemption
- Companies include major names: Adani Enterprises, Bajaj Finance, L&T, ICICI Home Finance, Avenue Supermarts (DMart)
- Suspensions effective between December 23-30, 2025
- Record dates fixed between December 23-30, 2025
- DR (Daily Report) numbers assigned: DR-783 through DR-787 for FY 2025-2026
- Trading members instructed not to deal in these securities from no-dealings dates

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notice for commercial paper redemptions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute deals in the listed commercial papers from their respective no-dealings dates
- All market participants must note the record dates for redemption purposes
- Compliance with suspension period mandatory for all exchange members

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects liquidity in commercial paper market for 20 issues over 8-day period. Major blue-chip companies and NBFCs included.

**Investor Impact:** Investors holding these commercial papers should note redemption schedules. No trading allowed during suspension period.

**Operational Impact:** Trading members must update systems to block trades in these securities. Standard redemption process with predetermined timelines.

**Sectoral Coverage:** Diverse - includes NBFCs (Bajaj Finance, ICICI Home Finance, Muthoottu), retail (Avenue Supermarts), infrastructure (L&T), cement (JK Cement), and financial services companies.
