## Summary

BSE has announced group changes for 20 exclusively listed companies as part of the X/XT segment review, effective December 15, 2025. The changes involve companies moving between X, XT, B, and T groups based on market capitalization, net worth, shareholder base, and FII holdings criteria established in notice 20171129-17 dated November 29, 2017.

## Key Points

- 20 companies undergoing group changes effective December 15, 2025
- 10 companies moving from X group to B group (improved classification)
- 3 companies moving from XT group to T group (improved classification)
- 7 companies moving to X/XT groups (more restrictive classification)
- Group changes based on periodic X/XT segment review
- Classification criteria include market cap, net worth, shareholder count, and FII holdings

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. The circular implements existing X/XT segment classification criteria established in notice 20171129-17 dated November 29, 2017.

**Exclusion Criteria from X Group:**
- Six-month average market cap > Rs. 1000 Cr AND shareholders > 5000
- Constituent of S&P BSE Sensex, BSE 100, BSE 200, or BSE 500
- Six-month average market cap > Rs. 1000 Cr AND net worth > Rs. 150 Cr
- Net worth > Rs. 150 Cr AND FII holding > 20%
- Listed and traded on SME/SME-ITP segment
- Securities in P and Z groups remain in respective groups

## Compliance Requirements

**For Trading Members:**
- Note the group changes for affected companies
- Update trading systems and risk management parameters for reclassified securities
- Adjust trading strategies based on new group classifications
- Ensure compliance with group-specific trading rules effective December 15, 2025

## Important Dates

- **December 9, 2025**: Circular issued
- **December 15, 2025**: Effective date for group changes

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:**
- Companies moving to X/XT groups may experience reduced liquidity and higher price volatility due to more restrictive trading conditions
- Companies moving out of X/XT groups benefit from improved trading flexibility and potentially better liquidity
- Trading members need to adjust risk management and position limits based on new group classifications

**Operational Impact:**
- Trading systems require updates to reflect new group classifications
- Investors and traders need to be aware of different trading rules applicable to each group
- Price discovery mechanisms may be affected during the transition period

**Investor Impact:**
- Securities in X/XT groups typically face additional surveillance measures
- Group changes reflect changes in company fundamentals (market cap, net worth, shareholder base)
- Investors should review holdings in affected companies and understand implications of group classifications
