## Summary

BSE has issued a circular detailing corporate actions (dividends) for 23 companies that will impact Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) transactions. The circular specifies ex-dates, record dates, and shut periods during which SLB transactions will be restricted for these securities. All actions relate to dividend announcements scheduled between November 11, 2025 and November 26, 2025.

## Key Points

- 23 companies have declared dividends with upcoming ex-dates and record dates
- SLB shut periods range from 1-3 days for each security
- Earliest ex-date is November 11, 2025 (Indian Metals & Ferro Alloys Limited)
- Latest ex-date is November 26, 2025 (Power Finance Corporation Ltd)
- All corporate actions are dividend-related
- Shut periods begin one day before the ex-date and end on the record date

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard notification of corporate actions affecting SLB securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must not enter new SLB transactions for the specified securities during their respective shut periods
- Existing SLB positions must be managed considering the dividend ex-dates and record dates
- Traders and clearing members must ensure compliance with the specified shut periods for each security

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects 23 securities across various sectors including shipping, energy, FMCG, automotive, and metals. SLB market participants need to adjust their lending and borrowing strategies around the specified shut periods.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - clearing members and trading members must ensure their systems and processes account for the SLB restrictions during shut periods. Failure to comply could result in rejected transactions.

**Liquidity Impact:** Low to Medium - temporary restrictions on SLB transactions may reduce liquidity in the securities lending market for these specific stocks during their shut periods, though the impact is limited to 1-3 days per security.
