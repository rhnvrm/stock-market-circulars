## Summary

BSE has issued a demat auction notice for Settlement No. 757 dated November 18, 2025. The notice lists 109 scrips across various sectors that will be auctioned due to delivery failures. The quantities range from 1 share to 116,725 shares depending on the security.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 757
- Auction Date: November 18, 2025
- Total scrips listed: 109 securities
- Includes large-cap stocks like State Bank of India (100 shares), ICICI Bank (8 shares), HCL Tech (10 shares)
- Also includes mid and small-cap securities with varying quantities
- Largest quantity: Billionbrain (116,725 shares)
- Some scrips have minimal quantities (1-5 shares)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a routine auction notice for settlement obligations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and clearing members must note the securities listed for auction
- Participants failing to deliver securities must participate in the auction process
- Settlement obligations must be fulfilled through the auction mechanism
- Parties must comply with BSE auction procedures and timelines

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: November 18, 2025 (Settlement No. 757)
- Auction execution date to be determined per BSE auction schedule

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - The auction affects 109 securities including some liquid large-cap stocks, though quantities are generally small. State Bank of India, ICICI Bank, Canara Bank, and other banking stocks are included.

**Operational Impact**: Medium - Brokers with outstanding delivery obligations in these securities must arrange for auction participation. The varying quantities suggest scattered delivery failures across multiple participants.

**Investor Impact**: Low to Medium - Investors whose shares are subject to auction may face price volatility. However, most quantities are relatively small except for a few small-cap stocks like Billionbrain (116,725 shares) and B.C. Power (6,085 shares).

**Liquidity Impact**: The auction will provide a mechanism to close out failed settlements and restore normal trading in affected securities.
