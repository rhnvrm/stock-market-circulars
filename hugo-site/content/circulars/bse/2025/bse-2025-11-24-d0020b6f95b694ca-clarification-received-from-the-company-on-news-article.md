## Summary

Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL) has submitted a clarification to BSE and NSE in response to a news article published on Moneycontrol dated November 24, 2025, titled "HAL shares drop up to 9% after Tejas fighter jet crashes in Dubai: Here's what analysts say". The company clarified that since the aircraft was in service of the customer, there was no undisclosed information requiring announcement. HAL had already provided clarification about the Dubai Air Show incident through a separate filing on the same date.

## Key Points

- HAL shares dropped up to 9% following news of Tejas fighter jet incident at Dubai Air Show
- Company responded to stock exchange queries regarding the news article
- HAL confirmed no negotiations were taking place (query response: Not Applicable)
- Company stated the aircraft was in service of customer at the time of incident
- No undisclosed material information that could explain the share price movement
- HAL had already issued separate clarification about the Dubai Air Show incident (Letter No. CO/SEC/4(7)/2025-26/ BSE & NSE Filing/56)
- No regulatory or legal proceedings initiated
- Filing submitted by Company Secretary Shailesh Bansal

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced in this circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Company fulfilled disclosure obligations by responding to stock exchange queries about news article and price movement
- Confirmed compliance with disclosure norms regarding material information

## Important Dates

- **November 24, 2025**: Date of news article publication on Moneycontrol
- **November 24, 2025**: Date of HAL's clarification filing to BSE and NSE
- **November 24, 2025**: Date of previous clarification letter about Dubai Air Show incident (Filing/56)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: HAL shares experienced significant downward movement of up to 9% following media reports about the Tejas fighter jet incident at Dubai Air Show. The company's clarification aims to provide transparency and address market concerns.

**Operational Impact**: Since the aircraft was in customer service, HAL indicated the incident falls under customer operations rather than company testing or demonstration activities. This distinction is important for understanding liability and operational responsibility.

**Disclosure Impact**: The company maintains that all material information has been appropriately disclosed and there is no undisclosed information that would explain the price movement beyond the publicly reported incident.

**Investor Impact**: The clarification provides investors with official company response to media reports and confirms no hidden material developments or ongoing negotiations that were previously undisclosed.
