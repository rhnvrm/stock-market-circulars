## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper issued by Manappuram Finance Limited on private placement basis. The instrument comprises 2,000 units of Rs. 5,00,000/- each (total issue size Rs. 100 crore), listed on the BSE Debt segment with effect from December 5, 2025. The Commercial Paper carries ratings of CARE A1+ and CRISIL A1+, with Axis Bank Limited acting as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- **Issuer**: Manappuram Finance Limited
- **Security Type**: Commercial Paper issued on private placement basis
- **Face Value**: Rs. 5,00,000/- per unit
- **Issue Price**: Rs. 4,64,515.50 per unit
- **Total Quantity**: 2,000 units (Total issue size: Rs. 100 crore)
- **Market Lot**: 1 unit
- **Scrip Code**: 730695
- **Scrip ID**: MFL031225
- **Detail Name**: MFL-3-12-26-CP
- **ISIN Number**: INE522D14OZ0 (Further listing under same ISIN)
- **Credit Rating**: CARE A1+, CRISIL A1+
- **Allotment Date**: December 4, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: December 3, 2026
- **Tenor**: 364 days
- **Issuing and Paying Agent**: Axis Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced in this circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade the securities only in dematerialized form under the specified ISIN Number INE522D14OZ0
- Trading shall take place in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- The tick size for the securities is 1 paise
- All trading must be conducted through the BSE Debt segment

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: December 5, 2025
- **Listing Date**: December 5, 2025
- **Deemed Date of Allotment**: December 4, 2025
- **Date of Redemption**: December 3, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt instrument listing with minimal market-wide impact. The listing provides Manappuram Finance Limited with short-term funding at competitive rates, as evidenced by the high credit ratings (A1+ from both CARE and CRISIL). The Commercial Paper is relevant primarily to institutional investors in the debt market seeking highly-rated short-term instruments. The standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs ensures that only institutional and high-net-worth participants can trade these securities. No impact on equity markets or retail investors is anticipated.
