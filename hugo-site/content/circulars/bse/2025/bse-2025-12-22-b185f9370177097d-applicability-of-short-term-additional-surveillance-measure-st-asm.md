## Summary

BSE has issued updates to the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective December 23, 2025. The circular identifies 5 securities newly shortlisted for ST-ASM, 1 security moving to higher stage ASM, and 9 securities moving out of the framework. The ST-ASM framework applies enhanced surveillance on securities exhibiting abnormal price movements over short timeframes (5/15/30 days).

## Key Points

- 5 securities newly shortlisted for Short Term 5/15/30 Days ASM Framework
- 1 security (SC Agrotech Ltd) continuing in ST-ASM but moving to higher Stage ASM
- 9 securities moving out of ST-ASM Framework
- Several securities marked as SME scrips (@) and T+0 scrips (~)
- Some exclusions are due to inclusion in other surveillance frameworks (LT-ASM, ESM)
- Effective date: December 23, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

The ST-ASM framework continues BSE's surveillance mechanism for securities showing unusual price volatility. Securities are placed in stages (I, II, III) based on severity, with progressive restrictions on trading.

**New Inclusions in ST-ASM:**
1. Dynavision Ltd (517238)
2. Logiciel Solutions Ltd@ (544625)
3. NDA Securities Ltd (511535)
4. Pulsar International Ltd (512591)
5. Takyon Networks Ltd@ (544471)

**Moving to Higher Stage:**
- SC Agrotech Ltd (526081)

**Moving Out of ST-ASM:**
Securities moving out include those transferred to other frameworks:
- Onix Solar Energy Ltd* - moved to LT-ASM Framework
- TCI Finance Ltd$ - moved to ESM Framework
- 7 other securities meeting exit criteria

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised ST-ASM applicability for affected securities
- Enhanced due diligence required for securities under ST-ASM framework
- Members must inform clients about applicable surveillance measures
- Price discovery mechanisms and position limits as per ST-ASM stage apply

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: December 23, 2025
- **Circular Date**: December 22, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - The changes affect 14 securities across various categories. Investors in these stocks will face enhanced surveillance measures including potential price bands, trading restrictions, or additional margin requirements.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members must update systems and client communications for affected securities. Securities moving to higher stages face stricter surveillance, while those exiting ST-ASM may see relaxed restrictions unless moved to other frameworks.

**Investor Impact**: Shareholders of newly included securities should expect increased scrutiny and potential trading restrictions. The consolidated list shows multiple securities remaining under various ST-ASM stages, indicating ongoing monitoring of volatility and trading patterns.
