## Summary

BSE has announced the suspension of trading in Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) of JM Financial Products Limited (ISIN: INE523H07BX5, Code: 974922) effective November 7, 2025. The suspension is implemented due to the company's scheduled partial redemption through buyback at investor request and interest payment, with a record date set for November 8, 2025. Trading members are advised not to deal in these debentures during the suspension period.

## Key Points

- Trading suspended for JM Financial Products Limited NCDs (8.8% coupon, maturity 23-12-25)
- ISIN: INE523H07BX5, BSE Code: 974922
- No dealings permitted from November 7, 2025
- Record date fixed for November 8, 2025
- Purpose: Partial redemption due to buyback at investor request and interest payment
- Circular reference: DR-751/2025-2026
- Issued by: Marian Dsouza, Assistant Vice President â€“ Listing Operations (CRD)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard corporate action notification for debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute any trades in the specified debentures from November 7, 2025
- All trading members are required to take note of the suspension and update their systems accordingly
- Members must ensure compliance with the no-dealings restriction for debenture ISIN INE523H07BX5

## Important Dates

- **November 6, 2025**: Circular issuance date
- **November 7, 2025**: Trading suspension effective date (no dealings from this date)
- **November 8, 2025**: Record date for partial redemption and interest payment

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Limited to holders of specific JM Financial Products Limited debentures. The suspension is temporary and related to corporate action processing rather than financial distress.

**Investor Impact**: Debenture holders eligible as per the record date will receive partial redemption proceeds and interest payments. Trading liquidity is suspended during the corporate action period.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members must update their systems to block trades in the specified debenture. Back-office operations need to process the partial redemption and interest payment for eligible holders.
