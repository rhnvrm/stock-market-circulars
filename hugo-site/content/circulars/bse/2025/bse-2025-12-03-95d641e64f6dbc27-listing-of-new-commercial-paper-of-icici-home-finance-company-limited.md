## Summary

ICICI HOME FINANCE COMPANY LIMITED has listed new Commercial Paper instruments on the BSE Debt segment on December 3, 2025. The issuance comprises 2,500 units of Rs. 5 lakh each, totaling Rs. 125 crores, issued on a private placement basis. The instruments carry dual credit ratings of CARE A1+ and ICRA A1+, with ICICI Bank Limited serving as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- Total quantity: 2,500 Commercial Paper units
- Face value: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- Issue price: Rs. 4,97,854.50 per unit (discount to face value)
- Scrip Code: 730693
- Scrip ID: IHF031225
- ISIN: INE071G14HH3
- Credit ratings: CARE A1+ and ICRA A1+
- Market lot: 1 unit
- Trading denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- Trading only in dematerialized form
- Issuing and Paying Agent: ICICI Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for debt securities issued on private placement basis.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE071G14HH3
- Trading must occur in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Trading members requiring clarification should contact BSE debt department at specified phone numbers (22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915)

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: December 3, 2025
- **Date of Allotment**: December 3, 2025
- **Listing Date**: December 3, 2025 (effective from today)
- **Redemption Date**: December 29, 2025
- **Maturity Period**: 26 days

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine short-term debt instrument listing with minimal market impact. The 26-day maturity Commercial Paper provides short-term financing for ICICI HOME FINANCE COMPANY LIMITED. The high credit ratings (A1+ from both CARE and ICRA) indicate strong creditworthiness. The discount issuance (Rs. 4,97,854.50 vs Rs. 5,00,000 face value) represents the implicit interest cost. This listing does not affect equity markets or broader trading operations and is relevant primarily for debt market participants and institutional investors dealing in money market instruments.
