## Summary

BSE issued surveillance queries to three companies regarding unusual price or volume movements. All three companies—Hindware Home Innovation Limited (Scrip: 542905), Jay Ushin Ltd (Scrip: 513252), and Kati Patang Lifestyle Limited (Scrip: 531126)—responded confirming compliance with disclosure requirements and stating no undisclosed material events affecting their operations.

## Key Points

- **Hindware Home Innovation Limited**: Responded to volume increase query on November 4, 2025, confirming no material events and adherence to timely disclosure requirements
- **Jay Ushin Ltd**: Clarified media reports about rare earth magnet imports from China, stating the company has been importing these materials for automotive products for several years through authorized Indian traders
- **Kati Patang Lifestyle Limited**: Confirmed all material information has been disclosed in accordance with Regulation 30 of SEBI LODR 2015
- All companies attributed price/volume movements to market forces beyond their control

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced.

## Compliance Requirements

- Companies confirmed compliance with Regulation 30 of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015
- All material price-sensitive information disclosed in timely and transparent manner
- Companies maintaining adherence to corporate governance disclosure principles outlined in Regulation 4

## Important Dates

- **November 3, 2025**: BSE query sent to Kati Patang Lifestyle Limited (Ref: L/SURV/ONL/PV/AJ/2025-2026/3169)
- **November 4, 2025**: BSE query sent to Hindware Home Innovation (Ref: L/SURV/ONL/PV/AA/2025-2026/654) and Jay Ushin Ltd
- **November 4, 2025**: All three companies submitted clarifications

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. These are routine surveillance responses confirming no undisclosed material information. The clarifications provide transparency but do not reveal any new fundamental developments.

**Operational Impact**: None. Companies confirmed normal operations with no material changes.

**Investor Impact**: Low. Jay Ushin's clarification about rare earth magnet imports may address investor concerns arising from media reports, confirming this is an existing business practice rather than a new development. Price and volume movements attributed to normal market forces.
