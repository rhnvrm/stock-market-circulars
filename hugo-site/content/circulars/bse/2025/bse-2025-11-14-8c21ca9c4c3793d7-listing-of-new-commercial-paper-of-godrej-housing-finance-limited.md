## Summary

BSE has listed two new series of Commercial Papers issued by Godrej Housing Finance Limited on private placement basis, effective November 14, 2025. The first series (ISIN: INE02JD14591) consists of 4,000 units of Rs. 5 lakhs each totaling Rs. 200 crores, while the second series (ISIN: INE02JD14682) comprises 2,000 units totaling Rs. 100 crores. Both instruments carry CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+ ratings with ICICI Bank Limited as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- Total of 6,000 Commercial Paper units listed aggregating Rs. 300 crores
- Face value and paid-up value: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- First series (GHFL-13-2-26-CP): 4,000 units, issue price Rs. 492,437.25, allotted on November 14, 2025
- Second series (GHFL-12-2-26-CP): 2,000 units, issue price Rs. 492,425.00, allotted on November 13, 2025
- Credit rating: CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+ for both series
- Trading in dematerialized form only
- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- ICICI Bank Limited appointed as Issuing and Paying Agent

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for new debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure transactions are conducted only in dematerialized form under the specified ISIN numbers
- Trading must be in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- All trades subject to tick size of 1 paise
- Instruments to be traded on BSE Debt segment only

## Important Dates

- **First Series (GHFL-13-2-26-CP)**:
  - Allotment Date: November 14, 2025
  - Listing Date: November 14, 2025
  - Redemption Date: February 13, 2026 (91 days)

- **Second Series (GHFL-12-2-26-CP)**:
  - Allotment Date: November 13, 2025
  - Listing Date: November 14, 2025
  - Redemption Date: February 12, 2026 (91 days)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt listing with minimal market impact. The listing of commercial paper provides short-term funding to Godrej Housing Finance Limited and offers institutional investors additional short-term debt investment options with high credit ratings. The instruments have a tenure of approximately 91 days and are primarily relevant for institutional investors and debt market participants. No impact on equity markets or retail investors.
