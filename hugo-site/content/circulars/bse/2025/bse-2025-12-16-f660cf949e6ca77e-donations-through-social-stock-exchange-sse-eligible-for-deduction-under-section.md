## Summary

BSE has issued a clarification regarding the eligibility of donations made through the Social Stock Exchange (SSE) for tax deduction under Section 80G of the Income-tax Act, 1961. This circular provides guidance to donors and Not-for-Profit Enterprises (NPEs) registered on the SSE platform regarding the tax treatment of donations.

## Key Points

- Donations made through the Social Stock Exchange (SSE) platform are eligible for tax deduction under Section 80G of the Income-tax Act, 1961
- NPEs registered on SSE can receive donations that qualify for 80G tax benefits
- Clarification issued to address queries from donors and NPEs regarding tax treatment
- Applies to all eligible NPEs listed on the SSE platform
- Donors can claim tax deductions as per the provisions of Section 80G

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a clarification of existing tax provisions applicable to SSE donations.

## Compliance Requirements

- NPEs must ensure they meet Section 80G registration requirements to enable donors to claim deductions
- Donors should verify the 80G eligibility status of NPEs before claiming deductions
- NPEs should provide necessary documentation to donors for tax filing purposes
- All donations through SSE must comply with Income-tax Act provisions

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: December 16, 2025
- Effective: Immediate

## Impact Assessment

**Positive Impact:**
- Encourages donations through the SSE platform by confirming tax benefits
- Provides clarity to donors and NPEs on tax treatment
- Supports growth of the Social Stock Exchange ecosystem
- Benefits NPEs by making donations more attractive to donors

**Market Impact:** Low - primarily affects NPE segment and individual/corporate donors, does not impact equity or debt trading segments

**Stakeholders Affected:** Not-for-Profit Enterprises (NPEs) registered on SSE, individual and corporate donors, tax consultants
