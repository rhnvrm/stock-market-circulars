## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction list for Settlement No. 763 dated November 26, 2025. The auction covers 122 securities across various sectors including banking, pharmaceuticals, technology, manufacturing, and financial services. Quantities range from single shares to over 81,000 shares depending on the security.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 763
- Auction Date: November 26, 2025
- Total Securities Listed: 122 scrips
- Highest Quantity: WELCURE DRUGS (81,866 shares)
- Other High-Volume Securities: VODAFONE IDEA (51,475 shares), TATA POWER (30,450 shares), LADDU GOPAL (25,000 shares)
- Covers diverse sectors: Banking, Pharmaceuticals, Technology, Manufacturing, Financial Services
- Includes both large-cap and small-cap securities

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational circular for demat auction settlement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and market participants with outstanding delivery obligations for the listed securities must participate in the auction
- Settlement must be completed as per BSE auction settlement procedures
- Participants must ensure adequate funds/securities are available for auction settlement
- Non-compliance may result in penalties as per BSE regulations

## Important Dates

- **Auction Date**: November 26, 2025
- **Settlement Number**: 763

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - Affects 122 securities across multiple sectors. While this is a routine auction, the quantities involved (particularly for WELCURE DRUGS, VODAFONE IDEA, and TATA POWER) may create short-term price pressure.

**Operational Impact**: Medium - Brokers with delivery shortfalls in these securities must participate in the auction, which may require arranging additional funds or securities. The wide range of securities (from 1 share to 81,866 shares) indicates varied exposure across market participants.

**Investor Impact**: Low to Medium - Retail investors generally not directly affected unless they have pending settlements in these scrips. Institutional participants with large positions may experience settlement-related impacts.
