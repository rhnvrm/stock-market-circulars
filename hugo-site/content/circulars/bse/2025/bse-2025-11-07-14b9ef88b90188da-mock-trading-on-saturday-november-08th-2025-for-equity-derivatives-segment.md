## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Equity Derivatives segment on Saturday, November 08, 2025. This session is designed for trading members using third-party trading platforms of empaneled vendors or in-house developed systems through ETI APIs to test their trading applications. The mock session will simulate various functionalities including exceptional market conditions such as risk-reduction mode and trading halt. No new BOLT TWS Release will be implemented. All trades executed during this mock session will not create any rights, liabilities, or obligations including margins, pay-in, or pay-out.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Saturday, November 08, 2025
- Available for trading members using third-party platforms or in-house systems through ETI APIs
- Testing opportunity for various functionalities including exceptional market conditions
- No new BOLT TWS Release will be implemented
- Mock trades will not attract any margin obligations or settlement responsibilities
- Feedback requested from all members by 5:00 PM on session day
- Contact details provided for technical support and helpdesk assistance

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine mock trading session for testing and familiarization purposes.

## Compliance Requirements

No compliance requirements. Participation is voluntary but encouraged. Members are requested to:
- Actively participate in the mock trading session
- Provide feedback by 5:00 PM on the session day
- Contact BSE Tech Support or Helpdesk for any queries or clarifications

## Important Dates

**November 08, 2025 - Mock Trading Session Schedule:**
- **11:15 AM - 12:15 PM:** Log-in window
- **12:15 PM - 04:00 PM:** Continuous Trading session
- **04:00 PM - 04:15 PM:** Trade Modification window
- **By 5:00 PM:** Deadline for feedback submission

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** None. This is a mock trading session with no impact on live markets.

**Operational Impact:** Low. Members have the opportunity to test their trading systems and familiarize themselves with platform functionalities in a simulated environment. No actual settlement obligations or margin requirements will be created. The session provides a risk-free environment for testing exceptional market conditions such as risk-reduction mode and trading halts.
