## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) segment on Saturday, October 11, 2025. This session is designed for trading members using third-party platforms or in-house developed systems to test their trading applications and various functionalities including exceptional market conditions, risk-reduction mode, and trading halts. No new BOLT TWS Release will be implemented. All trades executed during this mock session will not create any margin, pay-in/pay-out obligations, rights, or liabilities.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Electronic Gold Receipts segment on October 11, 2025
- Available for members using third-party trading platforms or in-house systems through ETI APIs
- Testing opportunity for various functionalities including risk-reduction mode and trading halt
- No new BOLT TWS Release will be implemented
- Mock trades will not create any actual obligations or liabilities
- Feedback must be submitted by 2:00 pm on the same day

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a testing session announcement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are requested to participate actively in the mock trading session
- Members must provide feedback for the mock trading session by 2:00 pm on October 11, 2025
- Contact BSE Helpdesk or BSE Tech Support team for queries or clarifications

## Important Dates

- **October 11, 2025**: Mock Trading Session
  - Log-in: 08:15 am - 09:00 am
  - Pre-Open Order Entry: 09:00 am - 09:08 am
  - Matching period: 09:08 am - 09:15 am
  - Continuous Trading: 09:15 am - 12:00 pm
  - Block Deal Window: 11:00 am - 11:15 am
  - Closing: 12:00 pm - 12:10 pm
  - Post-closing: 12:10 pm - 12:20 pm
- **Feedback Deadline**: 2:00 pm on October 11, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: None. This is a mock trading session with no real market impact.

**Operational Impact**: Low. This is an optional testing opportunity for trading members to familiarize themselves with the Electronic Gold Receipts segment and test their trading systems. Members using third-party platforms or in-house developed systems can use this session to validate their applications under various market conditions. No actual trades, obligations, or liabilities will result from this session.
