## Summary

BSE has published the demat auction settlement schedule for Settlement No. 747 dated November 3, 2025. The auction covers 118 scrips from various sectors including banking, technology, energy, pharmaceuticals, and manufacturing. Quantities range from 1 share to 8,159 shares depending on the scrip. This auction is conducted to settle delivery obligations where sellers have failed to deliver securities.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 747
- Settlement Date: November 3, 2025
- Total Scrips: 118 companies
- Highest quantity: FINEOTEX CHEMICALS (8,159 shares)
- Notable large-cap stocks included: RELIANCE INDUSTRIES (100 shares), AXIS BANK (379 shares), HCL TECH (5 shares), JSW STEEL (18 shares)
- Multiple banking stocks: CANARA BANK, IDBI BANK, UNION BANK, CSB BANK, RBL BANK, YES BANK
- PSU stocks: BHARAT ELECTRONICS, BHARAT PETROLEUM, CHENNAI PETROLEUM, HINDUSTAN ORGANIC, TAMILNADU PETROPRODUCTS
- Technology stocks: HCL TECH, COFORGE, INTELLECT DESIGN, NEWGEN SOFTWARE, TATA TECH

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational circular for demat auction settlement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers with outstanding delivery obligations must participate in the demat auction
- Settlement participants must arrange for delivery of securities as per auction results
- Payment and delivery obligations must be met as per BSE auction settlement procedures
- Defaulting members will be subject to penalties as per BSE regulations

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: November 3, 2025
- Settlement Number: 747

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - affects 118 scrips but with relatively small quantities for most stocks. The largest quantity is 8,159 shares of FINEOTEX CHEMICALS.

**Operational Impact**: Medium - brokers with delivery defaults must participate in auction to close out positions. Failed delivery may result in auction purchases at prevailing market rates plus applicable charges.

**Investor Impact**: Low to Medium - primarily affects specific brokers and their clients who had delivery obligations. May cause temporary price volatility in affected scrips during auction execution.

**Sector Distribution**: Broad-based across banking, technology, energy, pharmaceuticals, manufacturing, and other sectors indicating widespread but limited delivery defaults across market segments.
