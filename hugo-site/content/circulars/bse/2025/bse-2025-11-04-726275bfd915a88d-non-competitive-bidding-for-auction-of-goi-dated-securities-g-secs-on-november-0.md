## Summary

BSE has announced the availability of non-competitive bidding facility for the auction of Government of India dated securities scheduled for November 07, 2025. Following RBI's press release dated November 03, 2025, trading members can participate in the auction of 6.48% GS 2035 (re-issue) worth Rs. 32,000 crores through BSE's NCB-GSec module on the iBBS web-based platform.

## Key Points

- Security being auctioned: 6.48% GS 2035 (Symbol: 648GS35N25)
- Type: Government Security (Re-issue)
- Total issue size: Rs. 32,000 crores
- Minimum subscription units: 100 units (multiples thereof)
- Minimum bidding amount: Rs. 10,000
- Maximum bidding amount: Rs. 2 crores
- Bidding platform: iBBS web-based system NCB-GSec module (https://ibbs.bseindia.com)
- Settlement date: November 10, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is in continuation of Exchange circular no. 20180423-42 dated April 23, 2018, regarding the launch of non-competitive bidding facility for Government Securities auctions conducted by RBI.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members intending to participate must:

- Submit bids through the NCB-GSec module of BSE's iBBS platform
- Ensure bids are placed within the specified timelines
- Adhere to minimum and maximum bidding amounts (Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 2 crores)
- Subscribe in units of 100 and multiples thereof
- Contact specified BSE/ICCL teams for:
  - New user ID creation or password reset: user.trdops@bseindia.com (022-2272 8005/8215)
  - Bidding queries: trading.ops@bseindia.com (022-2272 8376/5626/8226)
  - Obligation & pay-in queries: Collaterals@icclindia.com, settlements@icclindia.com (022-2272 8468/8704/8264)

## Important Dates

- **November 04, 2025, 10:00 AM**: Bid collection starts (24-hour availability)
- **November 06, 2025, 5:00 PM**: Bid collection end date for direct investors
- **November 07, 2025, 8:00 AM**: Bid collection end date for trading members
- **November 07, 2025**: Auction date
- **November 10, 2025**: Settlement date

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with minimal market impact. It provides trading members and direct investors access to participate in the G-sec auction through the non-competitive bidding route. The circular primarily affects debt market participants interested in government securities investment. No impact on equity markets or individual stock prices. The re-issue of 6.48% GS 2035 provides investment opportunity in government debt instruments with settlement in T+3 days.
