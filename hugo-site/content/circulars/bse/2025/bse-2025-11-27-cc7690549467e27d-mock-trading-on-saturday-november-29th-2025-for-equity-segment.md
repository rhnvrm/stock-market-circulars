## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Equity segment on Saturday, November 29th, 2025. This session is designed to allow trading members, including those using third-party trading platforms, to test their trading applications and various market functionalities. The mock session will simulate exceptional market conditions including call auction sessions, risk-reduction mode, trading halts, and block deals. There will be no new BOLT TWS release associated with this mock session.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for November 29th, 2025 (Saturday)
- Available for Equity segment only
- Third-party trading platform users can participate
- Opportunity to test various functionalities including exceptional market conditions
- No new BOLT TWS release
- Feedback requested from all members by 3:00 PM on the same day
- Multiple trading sessions including T+1, T+0, block deals, and call auctions will be simulated

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine mock trading session for testing purposes.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are encouraged to participate and test their systems
- Members must provide feedback by 3:00 PM on November 29th, 2025
- Contact BSE Helpdesk (022-45720400/600 & 022-69158500; bsehelp@bseindia.com) or BSE Tech Support (022-22728053; bse.tech@bseindia.com) for queries

## Important Dates

- **November 29th, 2025**: Mock trading session for Equity segment
- **3:00 PM, November 29th, 2025**: Deadline for feedback submission

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** None - This is a mock trading session with no impact on live markets or actual trading.

**Operational Impact:** Low - Voluntary participation for trading members to test and validate their trading systems. Benefits include the ability to test various exceptional market scenarios in a controlled environment before they occur in live trading.

**Member Impact:** Positive - Provides trading members and third-party platform vendors an opportunity to ensure their systems function correctly under various market conditions, improving system reliability and preparedness for exceptional scenarios.
