## Summary

BSE has announced the movement of Vintage Securities Ltd. (Security Code: 531051, ISIN: INE153C01015) into GSM Stage I under the Graded Surveillance Measure framework. This action is part of BSE's ongoing market surveillance and regulatory oversight to monitor securities exhibiting unusual price movements or trading patterns.

## Key Points

- Vintage Securities Ltd. (Code: 531051) moved to GSM Stage I
- ISIN: INE153C01015
- Securities in lower GSM stages may be moved down if included in ESM (Enhanced Surveillance Measure) Framework
- Securities in lower GSM stages may be moved down if included in IBC (Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code) Framework
- GSM framework alignment referenced with NSE

## Regulatory Changes

The security has been placed under enhanced surveillance through the GSM Stage I framework. This typically involves:
- Additional disclosure requirements
- Potential trading restrictions or price bands
- Increased monitoring by the exchange
- Possible impact on margin requirements

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants trading in Vintage Securities Ltd. should be aware of GSM Stage I conditions
- Brokers must ensure compliance with applicable surveillance measures
- Investors should review additional risk disclosures associated with GSM securities
- The company may be required to provide additional disclosures or clarifications

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: December 26, 2025
- Circular Date: December 26, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - The inclusion in GSM Stage I may result in reduced liquidity and increased volatility for Vintage Securities Ltd. Trading volumes may decline as institutional investors often avoid securities under surveillance measures.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - Brokers and market participants will need to apply GSM-specific procedures for this security, including enhanced risk disclosures to clients and potential additional margin requirements.

**Investor Impact:** Existing and potential investors should exercise increased caution when trading this security. The GSM classification indicates the exchange has identified concerns warranting enhanced surveillance, which may include unusual price movements, low liquidity, or governance issues.
