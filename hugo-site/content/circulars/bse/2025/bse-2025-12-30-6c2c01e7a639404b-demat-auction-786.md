## Summary

BSE has issued a demat auction notice for Settlement No. 786 dated December 30, 2025. The circular lists 100 scrips across various sectors that are being put up for auction to fulfill delivery obligations. The quantities range from 1 share to over 263,000 shares depending on the security.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 786
- Auction Date: December 30, 2025
- Total number of scrips: 100 securities
- Largest quantity: TIMEX GROUP (263,431 shares)
- Other significant quantities include: PRAKASH STEEL (73,002 shares), PATEL CHEM S (20,800 shares), PULSAR INTER (14,740 shares), PMC FINCORP (2,000 shares)
- Covers diverse sectors including banking, mutual funds, infrastructure, metals, textiles, and manufacturing
- Includes major stocks like Adani Enterprises, Infosys, Titan Company, Vedanta, NMDC, and several public sector banks

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational circular for demat auction proceedings.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and clearing members must note the securities listed for auction
- Participants with outstanding delivery obligations must participate in the auction process
- Members must ensure adequate funds/securities for auction settlement
- All auction procedures as per BSE rules and SEBI regulations must be followed

## Important Dates

- Auction Date: December 30, 2025
- Settlement No.: 786

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - The auction affects 100 securities with varying quantities. While routine in nature, large quantities in certain scrips (like TIMEX GROUP with 263,431 shares) could impact short-term price discovery and liquidity.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - Trading and clearing members with short positions in these securities will need to participate in the auction, potentially at unfavorable prices, affecting their settlement costs.

**Investor Impact:** Low to Medium - Investors in affected securities may observe temporary price volatility during auction proceedings. The auction mechanism ensures delivery obligations are met, maintaining market integrity.
