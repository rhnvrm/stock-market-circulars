## Summary

BSE has issued a clarification confirming that donations made through the Social Stock Exchange (SSE) platform are eligible for tax deductions under Section 80G of the Income-tax Act, 1961. This clarification is aimed at enhancing transparency and encouraging participation in the SSE framework by providing tax benefits to donors.

## Key Points

- Donations made through the Social Stock Exchange (SSE) qualify for tax deductions under Section 80G
- This applies to donations made to registered Not-for-Profit Organizations (NPOs) and Social Enterprises on the SSE platform
- The clarification aims to promote social impact investing and philanthropy through the SSE framework
- Donors can claim tax benefits as per the provisions of Section 80G of the Income-tax Act, 1961
- This measure is expected to boost donor participation and funding for social causes listed on SSE

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a clarification regarding the applicability of existing Income Tax Act provisions to SSE donations.

## Compliance Requirements

- NPOs and Social Enterprises on SSE should ensure they maintain proper Section 80G registration and certification
- Donors should obtain proper donation receipts for claiming tax deductions
- Organizations should comply with reporting requirements under the Income-tax Act for Section 80G donations

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: December 16, 2025
- Effective immediately for all eligible donations through SSE

## Impact Assessment

**Positive Impact:**
- Enhances attractiveness of SSE platform for donors by providing tax benefits
- Expected to increase fundraising capabilities for NPOs and Social Enterprises listed on SSE
- Promotes social impact investing and philanthropic activities
- Provides clarity on tax treatment of SSE donations, reducing uncertainty

**Market Impact:**
- May lead to increased participation in SSE by both donors and social organizations
- Strengthens the SSE ecosystem by offering tax incentives comparable to traditional donation channels
- No direct impact on equity or derivative trading segments
