## Summary

BSE has issued an update to the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) Framework effective December 11, 2025. Five securities have been newly shortlisted for inclusion in ESM, including B.C. Power Controls Ltd, Frontier Capital Ltd, Kesoram Industries Ltd, Maruti Interior Products Ltd (SME), and Transglobe Foods Ltd. Additionally, two securities - Continental Controls Ltd and Rotographics (India) Ltd - will be moved to higher ESM stages. No securities are moving out of the ESM framework or being moved to lower stages.

## Key Points

- 5 securities newly added to Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework
- 2 securities moved to higher ESM stages (Continental Controls Ltd, Rotographics India Ltd)
- No securities moving out of ESM framework
- No securities being moved to lower ESM stages
- Changes effective from December 11, 2025
- Consolidated list includes 26+ securities under various ESM stages
- Framework includes both main board and SME scrips

## Regulatory Framework

The Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) is a market surveillance mechanism implemented by BSE to enhance market integrity and safeguard investor interest. Securities are placed under ESM based on various criteria including price volatility, market capitalization, trading patterns, and corporate governance concerns.

**ESM Framework Stages:**
- Securities can be placed in different stages (I, II, etc.) based on surveillance parameters
- Higher stages typically involve stricter surveillance and trading restrictions
- Movement between stages depends on ongoing assessment of risk parameters

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members**: Must ensure compliance with ESM trading restrictions for affected securities
- **Investors**: Should be aware that securities under ESM may have additional trading conditions including:
  - Trade-for-trade settlement (no intraday squaring off)
  - 100% upfront margin requirements
  - Price bands and other restrictions
- **Listed Companies**: Should maintain compliance with listing requirements and disclosure norms to avoid ESM classification
