## Summary

Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) has announced a demat auction for Settlement No. 754 dated November 13, 2025. The auction involves 103 securities from various companies with specific quantities ranging from 1 share to 39,798 shares depending on the scrip. This is a routine settlement mechanism to address short delivery situations in the trading system.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 754
- Auction Date: November 13, 2025
- Total Securities Involved: 103 scrips from various companies
- Quantity Range: From 1 share (multiple scrips) to 39,798 shares (Money Masters Leasing & Finance Ltd)
- Major quantity auctions include: Money Masters Leasing & Finance (39,798 shares), Murae Organics (16,258 shares), Union Bank of India (4,200 shares), Cupid Ltd (4,023 shares)
- Covers diverse sectors including banking, automobiles, pharmaceuticals, technology, infrastructure, and manufacturing

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational auction process as per existing BSE settlement regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members with short delivery positions must participate in the auction or arrange delivery of securities
- Clearing members must ensure settlement of their obligations as per BSE clearing and settlement norms
- Members must have sufficient funds/securities to meet auction obligations
- Auction prices will be determined as per BSE auction mechanism rules

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: November 13, 2025
- Auction Date: November 13, 2025 (Settlement No. 754)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - Affects 103 securities but with relatively small quantities for most scrips, limiting overall market disruption.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - Trading and clearing members with short positions in these securities must participate in the auction, which may result in additional costs due to auction premiums.

**Investor Impact:** Low to Medium - Primarily affects institutional traders and broker-dealers rather than retail investors. However, auction purchases at premium prices may temporarily impact market prices of affected securities.

**Systemic Impact:** Low - Routine settlement mechanism that ensures smooth functioning of the clearing and settlement system by addressing delivery failures.
