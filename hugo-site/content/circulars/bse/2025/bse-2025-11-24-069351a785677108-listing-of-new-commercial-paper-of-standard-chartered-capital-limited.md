## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper issued by Standard Chartered Capital Limited on a private placement basis. The instrument consists of 3,000 units with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, totaling Rs. 150 crores. The commercial paper carries credit ratings of CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+, with an issue price of Rs. 4,85,324.50 per unit and a redemption date of April 30, 2026. HDFC Bank Limited serves as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- Total quantity: 3,000 units of Rs. 5 lakh face value each
- Scrip Code: 730636
- Scrip ID: SCCL241125
- ISIN Number: INE403G14TW2
- Issue Price: Rs. 4,85,324.50 per unit (discount to face value)
- Credit Rating: CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Trading denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- Issuing and Paying Agent: HDFC Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for a new debt instrument.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE403G14TW2
- Trading shall be conducted in standard denominations of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- The tick size of 1 paise must be adhered to for all transactions
- Trading members requiring clarifications should contact the BSE debt department

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: November 24, 2025
- **Allotment Date**: November 24, 2025
- **Listing Date**: November 24, 2025 (effective from today)
- **Redemption Date**: April 30, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This listing has minimal market impact as it is a routine commercial paper listing on the debt segment. The instrument is rated A1+ by both CRISIL and ICRA, indicating high credit quality and low credit risk. The listing provides an additional short-term debt investment option for institutional investors and debt market participants. Trading is restricted to dematerialized form with specific denomination requirements, limiting participation to institutional and high-net-worth investors. The 5-month tenure (November 2025 to April 2026) makes it a short-term money market instrument suitable for treasury management and liquidity deployment.
