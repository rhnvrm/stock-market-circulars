## Summary

PGIM India Multi Asset Allocation Fund will reopen for ongoing subscription and redemption transactions on BSE StAR MF Platform effective December 3, 2025. The fund will also support systematic investment plans (SIP), systematic transfer plans (STP), and systematic withdrawal plans (SWP). Six plan variants are available covering Regular and Direct plans with IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment, and Growth options.

## Key Points

- PGIM India Multi Asset Allocation Fund reopens for transactions from December 3, 2025
- Available on BSE StAR MF Platform for subscription and redemption
- SIP, STP, and SWP facilities enabled
- Six scheme variants available: Regular and Direct plans with three options each
- All schemes categorized as Hybrid (NC) funds
- Notice references previous communication 20251110-11 regarding the fund

## Scheme Details

### Available Plans

1. **Regular Plan - IDCW Payout** (ISIN: INF663L014A5)
2. **Regular Plan - IDCW Reinvestment** (ISIN: INF663L015A2)
3. **Regular Plan - Growth** (ISIN: INF663L013A7)
4. **Direct Plan - IDCW Payout** (ISIN: INF663L011A1)
5. **Direct Plan - IDCW Reinvestment** (ISIN: INF663L012A9)
6. **Direct Plan - Growth** (ISIN: INF663L010A3)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an operational notification about fund availability.

## Compliance Requirements

No compliance requirements. This is an informational notice for market participants and investors.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: December 3, 2025 - Fund reopens for ongoing transactions
- **Notice Date**: December 2, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This notice affects only investors interested in PGIM India Multi Asset Allocation Fund.

**Operational Impact**: Low. The reopening provides investment and redemption access to investors who wish to transact in this hybrid fund through the BSE StAR MF Platform.

**Investor Impact**: Positive for investors seeking multi-asset allocation strategies, as the fund becomes available for fresh investments and systematic transaction facilities after a temporary closure.
