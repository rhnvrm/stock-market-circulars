## Summary

BSE has announced that the equity shares of Game Changers Texfab Limited (Scrip Code: 544594) will be transferred from the Trade for Trade segment (MT Group) to the Rolling segment (M Group) effective November 19, 2025. This change follows the company's SME IPO listing and represents a transition to more flexible trading arrangements.

## Key Points

- Company: Game Changers Texfab Limited (SME IPO)
- Scrip Code: 544594
- Current Segment: Trade for Trade (MT Group)
- New Segment: Rolling Segment (M Group)
- Effective Date: Friday, November 19, 2025
- Notice issued in continuation to Exchange Notice No. 20251103-44 dated November 03, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

The transfer from Trade for Trade segment to Rolling segment represents a change in trading mechanism:
- Trade for Trade (T2T) requires compulsory delivery with no intraday trading
- Rolling segment allows normal trading with T+1 settlement cycle and intraday positions
- This change typically indicates improved market confidence in the security

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must update their systems to reflect the new group classification
- All dealings in the equity shares will shift to M Group from MT Group
- Trading Members can contact Mr. Anurag Jain on Tel. No. 022-2272 8822 for further details
- Members should prepare for the transition before the effective date

## Important Dates

- Notice Date: Tuesday, November 04, 2025
- Previous Notice: Monday, November 03, 2025 (Notice No. 20251103-44)
- Effective Date of Transfer: Friday, November 19, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - The shift from T2T to Rolling segment is positive for liquidity as it removes delivery restrictions and allows intraday trading. This typically results in increased trading volumes and improved price discovery.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - Trading Members need to update systems and inform clients about the change in trading mechanism. Investors will benefit from increased flexibility in trading the stock.

**Investor Impact:** Positive - Investors will have more trading flexibility with the ability to take intraday positions and not face compulsory delivery requirements, potentially improving market participation.
