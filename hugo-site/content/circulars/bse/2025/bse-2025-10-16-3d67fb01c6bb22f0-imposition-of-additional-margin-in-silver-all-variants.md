## Summary

Indian Clearing Corporation Limited (ICCL) has announced the imposition of a 2% additional margin on Silver near month futures contracts across all variants (SILVER, SILVERM, SILVERKG). This measure follows a periodic review of risk management practices and aims to mitigate systemic risk in the commodity derivatives segment. The change takes effect from October 17, 2025 (Begin of Day).

## Key Points

- Additional margin of 2% levied on all Silver near month futures contracts
- Applies to three variants: SILVER (expiry Nov 5, 2025), SILVERM (expiry Oct 31, 2025), and SILVERKG (expiry Oct 31, 2025)
- Measure implemented following periodic risk management review
- Objective is to mitigate systemic risk in commodity derivatives
- Notice issued by Chief Risk Officer - Risk Management, ICCL

## Regulatory Changes

The margin requirements for Silver futures contracts have been increased by 2% across all near month contract variants. This represents an enhancement to existing margin framework under ICCL's risk management protocols.

## Compliance Requirements

- All members and participants must ensure adequate margin coverage for Silver positions
- Additional 2% margin must be maintained for affected Silver futures contracts
- Members should adjust their collateral and margin calculations accordingly
- Risk monitoring and position management systems should be updated to reflect new margin levels

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** October 16, 2025
- **Effective Date:** October 17, 2025 (Begin of Day)
- **Contract Expiries Affected:**
  - SILVER: November 5, 2025
  - SILVERM: October 31, 2025
  - SILVERKG: October 31, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Moderate impact on silver futures traders who will need to allocate additional capital for margin requirements. The 2% increase may reduce leverage and potentially impact trading volumes in the near term.

**Operational Impact:** Members and participants need to ensure sufficient margin funding and may need to adjust position sizes or add collateral. Risk management systems require updates to incorporate the new margin parameters.

**Risk Mitigation:** The measure strengthens the risk framework for silver derivatives trading and enhances market stability by reducing potential systemic risks associated with silver price volatility.
