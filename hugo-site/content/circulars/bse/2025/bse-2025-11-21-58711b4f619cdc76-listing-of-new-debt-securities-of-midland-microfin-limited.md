## Summary

BSE has listed new debt securities issued by Midland Microfin Limited on private placement basis effective November 21, 2025. The securities comprise 50,000 debentures with a face value of Rs. 10,000 each, totaling Rs. 50 crore. The securities carry a 12% per annum interest rate with quarterly payments and will mature on April 4, 2027. Trading will be conducted exclusively in dematerialized form on the BSE Debt segment.

## Key Points

- 50,000 debentures listed with face value of Rs. 10,000 each (total Rs. 50 crore)
- Scrip Code: 977306, Scrip ID: 12MML27
- ISIN: INE884Q07806
- Credit Rating: ACUITE A-
- Interest Rate: 12% per annum, paid quarterly
- Allotment Date: November 20, 2025
- Maturity Date: April 4, 2027
- Market Lot: 1 debenture
- Tick Size: 1 paise
- Trading only in dematerialized form
- No put/call options available

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities exclusively in dematerialized form under ISIN INE884Q07806
- Members must use the specified tick size of 1 paise for trading
- Trading members should refer to the Placement Memorandum available at https://www.bseindia.com/markets/debt/memorandum_data.aspx for detailed information
- For clarifications, members may contact BSE debt department on 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: November 20, 2025
- **Listing Date**: November 21, 2025
- **First Interest Payment**: February 20, 2026 (quarterly thereafter)
- **Maturity Date**: April 4, 2027
- **Interest Payment Frequency**: Quarterly from February 20, 2026 to April 4, 2027

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing notification with minimal market impact. The private placement debt securities of Midland Microfin Limited provide institutional investors with an investment avenue in the microfinance sector at a 12% return. The ACUITE A- credit rating indicates adequate credit quality. The listing expands BSE's debt market offerings but is not expected to significantly affect overall market dynamics or trading volumes.
