## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of 10 government securities on the capital market segment effective November 10, 2025. This includes 7 State Development Loans (SDLs) from Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Goa, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, and Rajasthan, along with 3 Treasury Bills with varying maturity periods. All securities will trade in a standard lot size of 100 units.

## Key Points

- 7 State Development Loans (SDLs) from various states being listed
- 3 Treasury Bills (T-bills) with maturity periods of 91 days, 182 days, and 364 days
- All securities will have a uniform lot size of 100 units
- Coupon rates for SDLs range from 6.66% to 7.48%
- Securities will be identified by designated codes for trading purposes
- Trading regulations per NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A apply

## Listed Securities Details

**State Development Loans:**
1. SDL TN 7.14% 2035 (Tamil Nadu) - Maturity: Nov 06, 2035
2. SDL TN 7.46% 2055 (Tamil Nadu) - Maturity: Nov 06, 2055
3. SDL TS 7.45% 2057 (Telangana) - Maturity: Nov 06, 2057
4. SDL GA 7.25% 2036 (Goa) - Maturity: Nov 06, 2036
5. SDL KL 7.48% 2045 (Kerala) - Maturity: Nov 06, 2045
6. SDL AP 7.3% 2037 (Andhra Pradesh) - Maturity: Nov 06, 2037
7. SDL RJ 6.66% 2029 (Rajasthan) - Maturity: Nov 06, 2029

**Treasury Bills:**
1. GOI TBILL 364D - Maturity: Nov 06, 2026
2. GOI TBILL 182D - Maturity: May 08, 2026
3. GOI TBILL 91D - Maturity: Feb 06, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This circular implements existing regulations under NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A, specifically Regulation 3.1.1 (admission to dealings) and Regulation 2.5.5 (lot sizes).

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use designated security codes for these securities
- All trades must be executed in the specified lot size of 100 units
- Members should update their systems to accommodate the new securities before the effective date

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** November 06, 2025
- **Effective Date:** November 10, 2025
- **Trading Commencement:** November 10, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with minimal market impact. The listing of government securities expands the debt market offerings on NSE's capital market segment. These securities provide fixed-income investment options for institutional and retail investors seeking government-backed instruments. The uniform lot size of 100 units maintains consistency with existing debt market practices. No operational changes are required for equity market participants.
