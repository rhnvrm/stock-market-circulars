## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed commercial papers from 7 companies to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective August 7, 2025. The circular lists 10 commercial paper securities with face values of ₹5 lakh each, issued by companies including Godrej Consumer Products, Angel One, Blue Star, Tata Steel, Welspun Living, Godrej Industries, and IIFL Finance.

## Key Points

- 10 commercial paper securities listed across 7 companies
- All securities have face value of ₹5,00,000 each
- Securities allotted on August 6, 2025
- Maturity periods range from September 2025 to February 2026
- Total quantity varies from 500 to 12,000 securities per issue

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a routine listing notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of NSE Debt Market Trading Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Securities admitted for trading on debt market segment
- Designated security codes assigned as per annexure
- Companies must comply with ongoing debt market regulations

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: August 7, 2025
- **Allotment Date**: August 6, 2025
- **Redemption Dates**: Range from September 19, 2025 to February 26, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Positive impact on debt market liquidity with addition of new commercial paper instruments. Provides institutional and retail investors with short to medium-term investment options across diverse sectors including consumer goods, financial services, steel, and textiles.
