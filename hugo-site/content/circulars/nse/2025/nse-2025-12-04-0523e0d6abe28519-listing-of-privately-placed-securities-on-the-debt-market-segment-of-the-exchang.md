## Summary

NSE has admitted 9 privately placed debt securities to the Debt Market Segment effective December 04, 2025. The listing includes 7 commercial papers from EPL Limited, HSBC Investdirect Financial Services, Godrej Industries, SIDBI, Muthoot Finance, Kotak Securities, and Deutsche Investments India, along with 2 non-convertible debentures from TATA Projects Limited and Cholamandalam Investment and Finance Company Limited.

## Key Points

- **Total Securities Listed**: 9 debt instruments (7 commercial papers + 2 debentures)
- **Effective Date**: December 04, 2025
- **Regulatory Basis**: Regulation 3.1.1 of NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations
- **Largest Issue**: SIDBI commercial paper worth Rs 255 crore (51,000 securities Ã— Rs 5 lakh face value)
- **Longest Tenure**: Muthoot Finance CP maturing December 03, 2026 (365 days)
- **Debenture Tenure**: Both TATA Projects and Cholamandalam debentures have 3-year tenure (2028 maturity)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine listing notification under existing NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations 3.1.1.

## Compliance Requirements

- Exchange members are informed about the admission of these securities to dealings on the Debt Market Segment
- Designated security codes as specified in the annexure must be used for trading these instruments
- Standard debt market trading and settlement procedures apply

## Important Dates

- **Circular Issue Date**: December 04, 2025
- **Effective Listing Date**: December 04, 2025
- **Earliest Redemption**: March 02, 2026 (EPL CP)
- **Latest Redemption**: December 03, 2026 (Muthoot Finance CP) for commercial papers; December 02, 2028 (Cholamandalam) for debentures

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Low - This is a routine operational circular informing members about new debt instruments available for trading. The listing provides additional debt investment opportunities but does not affect equity markets or impose new regulatory requirements.

**Operational Impact**: Minimal - Members can now trade these securities on the debt market segment using the designated security codes. No changes to existing trading procedures or systems required.

**Investor Impact**: Low - Provides institutional investors with additional short-term (3-12 months) and medium-term (3 years) debt investment options from established issuers across financial services, infrastructure, and industrial sectors.
