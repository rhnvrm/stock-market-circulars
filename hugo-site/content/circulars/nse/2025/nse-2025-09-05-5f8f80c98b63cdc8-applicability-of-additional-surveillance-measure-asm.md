## Summary

NSE has issued updates to the Long-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework effective September 8, 2025. Four securities are being added to Long-Term ASM Framework Stage-I, while nine securities are being excluded from the ASM framework entirely.

## Key Points

- 4 securities shortlisted for Long-Term ASM Framework Stage-I
- 2 securities moved from Short-Term ASM (STASM) to Long-Term ASM (LTASM)
- 9 securities to be excluded from ASM framework
- No securities moving between different ASM stages
- Changes effective from September 8, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

**Securities Added to Long-Term ASM Stage-I:**
- Astron Paper & Board Mill Limited (ASTRON) - moved from STASM, also satisfies BSE criteria
- Cartrade Tech Limited (CARTRADE)
- Newjaisa Technologies Limited (NEWJAISA) - moved from STASM
- Sahasra Electronic Solutions Limited (SAHASRA)

**Securities Excluded from ASM Framework:**
- Alkali Metals Limited (ALKALI)
- Arvee Laboratories (India) Limited (ARVEE)
- Bang Overseas Limited (BANG)
- Centum Electronics Limited (CENTUM)
- IFCI Limited (IFCI)
- Mangalore Chemicals & Fertilizers Limited (MANGCHEFER)
- Megastar Foods Limited (MEGASTAR)
- MMTC Limited (MMTC)
- SRM Contractors Limited (SRM)

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must adhere to applicable surveillance measures for securities in Long-Term ASM Stage-I
- Enhanced monitoring and reporting requirements for ASM securities
- Securities moving to Stage-IV would shift from Rolling Settlement (EQ) to Trade for Trade (BE) on T+3 basis

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** September 8, 2025
- All ASM framework changes become applicable from this date

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:**
- Enhanced surveillance on 4 securities may reduce speculative trading
- 9 securities being excluded from ASM framework will see relaxed surveillance measures
- Trading volumes and liquidity patterns may be affected for impacted securities
- Investors should expect increased monitoring and potential trading restrictions for ASM Stage-I securities
