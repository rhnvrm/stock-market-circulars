## Summary

NSE has admitted multiple privately placed debt securities to dealings on its Debt Market Segment effective November 07, 2025. This includes 8 commercial papers (CPs) from various companies totaling Rs 27,500 units and 2 other debt securities (bonds/debentures). The circular provides detailed specifications including ISINs, issue prices, face values, allotment dates, and redemption dates for each security.

## Key Points

- Total of 8 commercial papers listed from 6 different issuers
- Tata Steel Limited issued CP worth 15,000 units (Rs 7,500 crore) maturing on February 10, 2026
- Godrej group companies (Godrej Industries, Godrej Properties, Godrej Agrovet) issued 5 CPs totaling 7,500 units
- All CPs have face value of Rs 5,00,000 per unit
- Two bond issues listed: Aseem Infrastructure Finance Limited (8.05% 2032) and Summit Digitel Infrastructure Limited (7.11% 2030)
- All securities allotted on November 06, 2025
- CP redemption periods range from 24 days to 91 days

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine listing notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members should note the designated security codes for these instruments
- Trading members can facilitate transactions in these securities on the debt market segment from November 07, 2025
- Standard debt market trading regulations apply to these listed securities

## Important Dates

- **Listing Date**: November 07, 2025
- **Allotment Date**: November 06, 2025 (for all securities)
- **Earliest CP Redemption**: December 01, 2025 (Godrej Agrovet Sr 45)
- **Latest CP Redemption**: February 10, 2026 (Tata Steel)
- **Bond Redemption Dates**: October 29, 2032 (AIFL) and 2030 (SDIL)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with minimal market impact. The listing provides institutional investors and debt market participants access to privately placed securities of established corporate issuers. The commercial papers offer short-term investment opportunities (24-91 days tenure) while the bonds provide longer-term fixed income options. No impact on equity markets or trading operations for cash/derivatives segments.
