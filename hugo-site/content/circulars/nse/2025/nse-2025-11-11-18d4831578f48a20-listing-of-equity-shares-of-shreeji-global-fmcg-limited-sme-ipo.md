## Summary

The National Stock Exchange has notified the listing of equity shares of Shreeji Global FMCG Limited (Symbol: SHETHJI) on the EMERGE SME Platform effective November 12, 2025. The company issued 22,760,000 equity shares of Rs. 10/- face value at Rs. 125/- per share. The security will initially trade in Series 'ST' (Trade for Trade Surveillance Segment with Settlement Type 'W') and will subsequently be shifted to Series 'SM' (Normal Rolling Segment) as per SEBI regulations.

## Key Points

- Company Symbol: SHETHJI
- ISIN: INE1FL101016
- Face Value: Rs. 10/-
- Issue Price: Rs. 125/- per share
- Total Securities: 22,760,000 equity shares
- Market Lot Size: 1,000 equity shares
- Market Maker Maximum Permissible Spread: Within 10%
- Trading Segment: Initially Series 'ST' (TFTS), later to be shifted to Series 'SM'
- Pre-open session applicable for IPO category
- Available for continuous market trading

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. The circular follows existing SEBI regulations:
- SEBI circular no. CIR/MRD/DP/01/2012 & CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012 regarding pre-open session and surveillance measures
- Exchange Circular No. NSE/CMTR/37202 dated March 14, 2018 regarding continuous market trading

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must identify the security by its designated code (SHETHJI) for trading purposes
- Trading must be conducted in specified lot sizes of 1,000 equity shares
- Market makers must maintain spread within 10%
- Lock-in details to be followed as per Annexure I
- Company must maintain registered office at The Spire, Office No. 1205, Rajkot, Gujarat

## Impact Assessment

This listing has limited market impact as it pertains to a single SME company on the EMERGE platform. The Trade for Trade Surveillance Segment classification indicates enhanced monitoring for this security. The market lot size of 1,000 shares may limit retail participation initially. Market makers B.N. Rathi Securities Limited and SVCM Securities Private Limited will provide liquidity. The subsequent shift to normal rolling segment will depend on compliance with SEBI surveillance requirements.
