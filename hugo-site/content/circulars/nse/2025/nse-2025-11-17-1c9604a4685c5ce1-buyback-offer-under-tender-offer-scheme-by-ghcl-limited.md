## Summary

GHCL Limited will conduct a buyback of equity shares under the Tender Offer scheme using NSE's web-based bidding platform. The company will buyback up to 41,37,931 equity shares at a fixed price of Rs. 725 per share. The bidding period runs from November 20-26, 2025, and is open to all eligible investor categories through NSE's trading members.

## Key Points

- Buyback offer for up to 41,37,931 equity shares of GHCL Limited
- Fixed offer price: Rs. 725 per share
- Face value: Rs. 10 per share
- Lot size: 1 equity share
- ISIN: INE539A01019
- Merchant Banker: Systematix Corporate Services Limited
- Registrar: MUFG Intime India Private Limited
- All trading members of Capital Market segment eligible to participate
- Existing IPO bidding platform users can use same credentials
- New users can access through admin terminal (Adminxxxxx format)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This buyback follows the existing Tender Offer Scheme as per NSE circular NSE/CMTR/52776 dated June 29, 2022.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must facilitate client participation through NSE's web-based bidding platform
- Access portal at: https://eipo.nseindia.com
- Members without existing access must create branches and users via admin terminal
- Custodial participants must provide confirmations during bidding hours and extended window on closure day

## Important Dates

- **Issue Period**: November 20-26, 2025
- **Bidding Hours**: 9:15 AM to 3:30 PM (all days)
- **Custodial Confirmation Hours**: 9:15 AM to 4:00 PM (including issue closure day)
- **Circular Date**: November 17, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Shareholder Impact**: GHCL shareholders have opportunity to tender shares at Rs. 725, representing a premium/discount that shareholders should evaluate against current market price.

**Market Impact**: Limited to GHCL stock with approximately 41.38 lakh shares (buyback size). Trading activity may increase during the tender offer period as shareholders decide on participation.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members must ensure systems and user access are configured for the bidding period. The web-based platform provides standardized process for all participant categories including FIIs, mutual funds, insurance companies, banks, corporate bodies, and individual investors.
