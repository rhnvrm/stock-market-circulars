## Summary

National Stock Exchange has admitted Fabtech Technologies Limited equity shares for trading on the Capital Market segment effective October 07, 2025. The company has issued 44,450,794 equity shares of Rs. 10/- face value at an issue price of Rs. 191/- per share. The security will trade under symbol 'FABTECH' with ISIN INE0HF201011, initially in Series 'BE' and subsequently shifted to Series 'EQ' as per SEBI regulations.

## Key Points

- **Company**: Fabtech Technologies Limited
- **Symbol**: FABTECH
- **ISIN**: INE0HF201011
- **Series**: BE* (initially), to be shifted to EQ
- **Face Value**: Rs. 10/- per share
- **Issue Price**: Rs. 191/- per share
- **Total Securities**: 44,450,794 equity shares
- **Market Lot**: 1 share
- **Trading Commences**: October 07, 2025
- **Pre-open Session**: Applicable for IPO category as per SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/01/2012 & CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This circular implements existing regulations for IPO listings per SEBI circular no. CIR/MRD/DP/01/2012 & CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use designated security codes for identification on the trading system
- Securities to be traded in specified lot sizes (market lot of 1)
- Compliance with pre-open session requirements for IPO category scrips

## Important Dates

- **Listing Date**: October 07, 2025
- **Circular Effective Date**: October 07, 2025
- **Lock-in Expiry Dates**: Various tranches locked until April 05, 2026 and April 05, 2027 (detailed in Annexure I)
- **Financial Year**: April 2025 - March 2026

## Impact Assessment

This listing adds a new trading instrument to the NSE Capital Market segment. The IPO pricing at Rs. 191 per share represents a significant premium to face value (19.1x). With approximately 27% of shares (12,058,555) available for immediate trading and the remainder under lock-in, initial liquidity may be moderate. Impact is primarily relevant to IPO investors and those interested in the company's sector. The shift from BE to EQ series will follow standard SEBI protocols for new listings.
