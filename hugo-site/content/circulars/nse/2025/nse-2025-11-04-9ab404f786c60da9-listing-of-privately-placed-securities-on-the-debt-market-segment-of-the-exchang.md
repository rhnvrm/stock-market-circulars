## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed securities to dealings on the Debt Market Segment with effect from November 04, 2025. The circular lists six issuances: five commercial papers (CP) and one other debt security (bond). Total value of commercial papers is Rs. 87.5 crores (17,500 securities Ã— Rs. 5 lakhs face value) and the bond issuance is Rs. 2,486 crores.

## Key Points

- Five commercial papers issued by Aditya Birla Money Limited, Godrej Properties Limited, IIFL Finance Limited, Godrej Agrovet Limited, and Motilal Oswal Financial Services Limited
- One bond issued by Power Finance Corporation Limited with 7.10% coupon rate maturing in 2040
- All commercial papers have face value of Rs. 5,00,000 per security
- Commercial paper maturities range from 88 to 91 days (January-February 2026)
- All securities allotted on November 03, 2025 and listed on November 04, 2025
- Unique ISIN codes assigned to each security for trading identification

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard notification issued in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must note the designated security codes for trading purposes
- Securities admitted to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective November 04, 2025
- Trading participants should update their systems with the ISIN codes and security details provided

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date:** November 03, 2025 (all securities)
- **Listing Date:** November 04, 2025 (effective date for trading)
- **CP Redemption Dates:** January 30, 2026 to February 02, 2026
- **Bond Redemption Date:** November 12, 2040 (PFC bond)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Minimal. This is a routine listing notification for privately placed debt securities. These securities are typically traded among institutional investors and do not affect retail markets.

**Operational Impact:** None for general market participants. Only relevant to debt market segment participants who may trade these specific securities.

**Liquidity:** Commercial papers provide short-term financing options for the issuing companies. The PFC bond adds a long-tenure debt instrument to the market with a 15-year maturity period.

**Investor Base:** Primarily institutional investors including mutual funds, insurance companies, and banks that participate in private placement markets.
