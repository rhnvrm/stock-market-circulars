## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of privately placed debt securities from two companies on the capital market segment effective December 03, 2025. The securities include zero-coupon bonds from Indian Railway Finance Corporation Limited maturing in 2035 and 7.28% bonds from Toyota Financial Services India Limited maturing in 2028.

## Key Points

- Two debt securities admitted to NSE capital market segment
- Effective date: December 03, 2025
- Market lot size: 1 unit for both securities
- Series designation: N0 for both issues
- Compliance with NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A (Regulation 3.1.1 and 2.5.5)

## Listed Securities Details

### Indian Railway Finance Corporation Limited (IRFC)
- **Symbol**: 0IRFC35
- **Issue**: IRFC 0% 2035 Sr 192
- **Coupon Rate**: 0% (zero-coupon bond)
- **Maturity Date**: December 01, 2035
- **ISIN**: INE053F08536
- **Market Lot**: 1

### Toyota Financial Services India Limited (TFSIL)
- **Symbol**: 728TFSIL28
- **Issue**: TFSIL 7.28% 2028 Sr S56
- **Coupon Rate**: 7.28%
- **Maturity Date**: December 28, 2028
- **ISIN**: INE692Q07605
- **Market Lot**: 1

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notification pursuant to existing NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use designated security codes for identification on the trading system
- Trading must be conducted in specified lot sizes (1 unit for both securities)
- Securities to be traded in N0 series on capital market segment

## Important Dates

- **Circular Issue Date**: December 02, 2025
- **Effective Date**: December 03, 2025
- **IRFC Bond Maturity**: December 01, 2035
- **TFSIL Bond Maturity**: December 28, 2028

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact. This is a routine administrative notification for private placement debt securities. The listing provides additional fixed-income investment options for institutional investors but does not affect broad market operations or equity trading. The zero-coupon IRFC bond and fixed-coupon TFSIL bond add to the debt market depth but represent company-specific rather than market-wide developments.
