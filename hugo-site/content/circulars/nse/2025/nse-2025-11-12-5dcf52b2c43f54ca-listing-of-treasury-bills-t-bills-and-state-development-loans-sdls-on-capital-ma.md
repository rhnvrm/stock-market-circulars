## Summary

NSE has admitted 12 State Development Loans (SDLs) and 3 Treasury Bills (T-bills) for trading on the capital market segment effective November 14, 2025. The circular specifies the security codes, ISIN codes, coupon rates, maturity dates, and lot sizes for each instrument.

## Key Points

- 12 SDLs from various states (Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Jammu & Kashmir, Maharashtra, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, Mizoram, Uttar Pradesh) admitted for trading
- 3 Treasury Bills with tenors of 91 days, 182 days, and 364 days listed
- All securities will have a uniform lot size of 100 units
- Trading will commence on November 14, 2025
- Securities will be identified by their designated codes on the trading system
- SDLs have coupon rates ranging from 7.01% to 7.46%
- SDL maturity dates span from 2032 to 2056

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine notification under Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use the designated security codes for trading these instruments
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot sizes of 100 units
- All securities will trade in the SG (Government Securities) and TB (Treasury Bills) market segments

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: November 12, 2025
- **Effective Date**: November 14, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative circular with minimal market impact. The listing of government securities provides additional investment options for institutional investors and debt market participants. The uniform lot size of 100 units ensures standardized trading. No impact on equity markets or specific companies.
