## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of three privately placed debt securities on the capital market segment effective November 04, 2025. The securities include bonds from Toyota Financial Services India Limited (7.3% coupon, maturing January 2029), Motilal Oswal Finvest (8.75% coupon, maturing October 2027), and Profectus Capital Private Limited (9.75% coupon, maturing April 2028). All securities will trade with a market lot of 1 unit in Series N0.

## Key Points

- Three new debt securities admitted to dealings on NSE capital market segment
- Effective date: November 04, 2025
- All securities have a market lot size of 1 unit
- Securities will trade in Series N0
- Toyota Financial Services India Limited: Symbol 73TFSIL29, ISIN INE692Q07597, 7.3% coupon rate, maturity January 15, 2029
- Motilal Oswal Finvest: Symbol 875MOF27, ISIN INE01WN07136, 8.75% coupon rate, maturity October 29, 2027
- Profectus Capital Private Limited: Symbol 975PCPL28, ISIN INE389Z07088, 9.75% coupon rate, maturity April 30, 2028

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a notification pursuant to existing Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

Market participants should note the designated security codes and lot sizes for trading purposes. The securities will be identified only by their designated codes on the trading system.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: November 03, 2025
- **Effective Date**: November 04, 2025
- **Maturity Dates**:
  - Toyota Financial Services: January 15, 2029
  - Motilal Oswal Finvest: October 29, 2027
  - Profectus Capital Private Limited: April 30, 2028

## Impact Assessment

Limited market impact as this is a routine listing notification for privately placed debt securities. The listing provides additional investment options in the debt market with varying coupon rates (7.3% to 9.75%) and maturity profiles. The securities are likely restricted to institutional and qualified investors given their private placement nature. No impact on equity markets or broader market operations.
