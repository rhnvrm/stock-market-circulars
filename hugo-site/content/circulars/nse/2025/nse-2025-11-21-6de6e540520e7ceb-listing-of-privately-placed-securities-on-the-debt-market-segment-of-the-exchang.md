## Summary

The National Stock Exchange has admitted privately placed Commercial Paper (CP) securities from ten issuers to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective November 21, 2025. The circular lists securities from HDFC Securities Limited, ICICI Securities Limited, Aditya Birla Money Limited, Godrej Industries Limited, Aditya Birla Capital Limited, Nuvoco Vistas Corporation Limited, Godrej Agrovet Limited, Jio Credit Limited, and HSBC Investdirect Financial Services (I) Limited, with redemption dates ranging from December 2025 to May 2026.

## Key Points

- Ten issuers have listed Commercial Paper securities on NSE's debt market segment
- Total of 10 distinct CP issuances with varying quantities and redemption dates
- All securities have a face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Allotment dates are primarily November 20-21, 2025
- Issue prices range from ₹4,83,226 to ₹4,97,538 per unit
- Total quantity ranges from 1,000 to 11,000 securities per issuance

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification issued in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance actions required from market participants. This is an informational circular notifying members of new debt securities available for trading.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date of Listing**: November 21, 2025
- **Allotment Dates**: November 20-21, 2025
- **Redemption Dates**: 
  - December 18, 2025 (Nuvoco Vistas - earliest)
  - February 18-20, 2026 (Multiple issuers)
  - March 24, 2026 (ICICI Securities)
  - May 21, 2026 (HSBC Investdirect - latest)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with minimal market impact. The listing of these Commercial Paper securities expands the available debt instruments on NSE's debt market segment, providing investors with additional short-term debt investment options from established corporate issuers. The securities have tenures ranging from approximately 28 days to 182 days, typical for commercial paper instruments. No impact on equity markets or broader regulatory framework.
