## Summary

National Stock Exchange has announced the launch of Axis Gold and Silver Passive Fund of Funds (FOF) New Fund Offer (NFO) on the NSE MF Invest Platform. The NFO will be available for transactions from December 10, 2025 to December 22, 2025 until 11:59 PM, with allotment scheduled for December 29, 2025.

## Key Points

- Two scheme variants available: Direct Growth (ISIN: INF846KA1408) and Regular Growth (ISIN: INF846KA1390)
- Both schemes categorized as Debt schemes
- NFO transaction period: December 10-22, 2025 (till 11:59 PM)
- Allotment date: December 29, 2025
- SIP registration not allowed during NFO period
- Clear funds must be received in designated Clearing Corporation account by December 29, 2025 at 12:30 PM

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine NFO launch notification.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must ensure clear funds through any payment mode are received in the designated Clearing Corporation account on or before December 29, 2025 at 12:30 PM
- Members must facilitate transactions for the NFO schemes within the specified timeframe
- SIP registrations cannot be processed during the NFO period

## Important Dates

- **December 10, 2025**: NFO opening date (transactions begin)
- **December 22, 2025, 11:59 PM**: NFO closing date (last date for transactions)
- **December 29, 2025, 12:30 PM**: Deadline for clear funds receipt in Clearing Corporation account
- **December 29, 2025**: Allotment date for both schemes

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market-wide impact as it pertains to a specific mutual fund NFO launch. The impact is limited to:

- NSE members who facilitate mutual fund transactions on the NSE MF Invest Platform
- Investors interested in gold and silver passive fund of funds exposure
- Axis Mutual Fund distribution operations

The notification is primarily operational and informational, requiring members to prepare their systems and processes to handle NFO transactions for these specific schemes during the designated period. No broader market or regulatory implications exist beyond routine fund launch procedures.
