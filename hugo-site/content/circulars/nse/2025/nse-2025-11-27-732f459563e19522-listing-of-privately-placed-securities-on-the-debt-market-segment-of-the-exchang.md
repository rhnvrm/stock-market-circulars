## Summary

NSE has admitted multiple privately placed securities to dealings on its Debt Market Segment effective November 27, 2025. The circular lists commercial papers (CPs) issued by 8 companies and debt securities (debentures) from 2 companies, with ISINs, face values, issue prices, allotment dates, and redemption dates specified.

## Key Points

- 8 Commercial Paper issues listed from companies including IIFL Finance (2 series), Cholamandalam Investment, Kotak Securities, L&T Finance, HSBC Investdirect, Ramco Cements, and Aditya Birla Money
- 2 Debt Security issues listed from Cholamandalam Investment (8.40% 2032 Sr SD 78) and Sundaram Finance (7.05% series - details truncated)
- All securities admitted under Regulation 3.1.1 of NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations
- Total CP quantity ranges from 108 units (IIFL CP Sr 2559) to 10,000 units (CIAFCL and LTFL)
- Face value for all CPs is ₹5,00,000 per unit
- CP tenures range from 30 days (Ramco Cements) to 365 days (IIFL Finance Sr 2559)
- Debt securities include 7-year tenure debenture from Cholamandalam with 8.40% coupon rate

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine notification under existing NSE Debt Market Trading Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. Standard listing admission process for privately placed debt securities.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** November 27, 2025
- **Allotment Dates:** November 26-27, 2025
- **CP Redemption Dates:** Range from December 26, 2025 (Ramco Cements) to November 25, 2026 (IIFL Finance Sr 2559)
- **Debenture Redemption Date:** November 25, 2032 (Cholamandalam 8.40% Sr SD 78)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Minimal. Routine listing of debt instruments providing additional investment options in the debt market segment.

**Operational Impact:** No impact on trading members or market participants beyond standard debt market trading operations.

**Issuer Details:**
- IIFL Finance: 2 CP series totaling 608 units worth ₹304 crores
- Cholamandalam Investment: 10,000 CP units (₹5,000 crores) plus 50,000 debenture units (₹5,000 crores)
- L&T Finance: 10,000 CP units (₹5,000 crores)
- Kotak Securities: 5,000 CP units (₹2,500 crores)
- Other issuers: Smaller CP issues ranging from 1,000 to 2,000 units
