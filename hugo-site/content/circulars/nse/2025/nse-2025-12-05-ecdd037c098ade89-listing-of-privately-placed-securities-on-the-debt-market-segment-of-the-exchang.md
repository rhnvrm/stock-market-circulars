## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed commercial paper securities from multiple financial and corporate entities to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective December 05, 2025. The circular lists 11 commercial paper issuances from companies including Tata Capital Housing Finance Limited, HSBC Investdirect Financial Services, Godrej Industries Limited, ICICI Securities Limited, IIFL Finance Limited, Aseem Infrastructure Finance Limited, and L&T Finance Limited.

## Key Points

- Total of 11 commercial paper securities listed on NSE debt market segment
- All securities allotted on December 04, 2025
- Redemption dates range from December 30, 2025 to December 04, 2026
- Face value for all securities: ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Total quantity across all issuances: 49,600 securities
- All issuances are commercial paper (CP) instruments

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members are notified of the designated security codes for these commercial paper instruments
- Securities are available for trading on the NSE debt market segment from December 05, 2025
- All standard debt market trading regulations apply

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: December 04, 2025 (all securities)
- **Listing Effective Date**: December 05, 2025
- **Earliest Redemption**: December 30, 2025 (LTFL CP 30/12/25)
- **Latest Redemption**: December 04, 2026 (AIFL CP 04/12/26 Sr 27)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Low - This is a routine listing of privately placed debt securities with no impact on equity markets or broader market operations.

**Operational Impact**: Minimal - Standard debt market listing process. These commercial paper instruments are now available for secondary market trading on NSE's debt platform.

**Investor Impact**: Relevant only to debt market participants and institutional investors dealing in commercial paper. These are typically high-value instruments (₹5 lakh face value) meant for institutional participation.

**Liquidity**: Adds to the depth of corporate debt instruments available for trading in the secondary market.
