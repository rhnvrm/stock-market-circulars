## Summary

NSE has notified the trading holidays for the Capital Market Segment for calendar year 2026. A total of 15 trading holidays are scheduled on weekdays, along with 4 holidays falling on weekends. The circular also confirms that Muhurat Trading will be conducted on Sunday, November 8, 2026, with timings to be notified later.

## Key Points

- 15 trading holidays scheduled for 2026 on weekdays
- Muhurat Trading to be conducted on Sunday, November 8, 2026 (Diwali Laxmi Pujan)
- Timings for Muhurat Trading will be notified separately
- 4 additional holidays fall on Saturday/Sunday: Mahashivratri (Feb 15), Id-Ul-Fitr (Mar 21), Independence Day (Aug 15), and Diwali Laxmi Pujan (Nov 8)
- Circular issued under Clause 2 of Chapter IX of Bye-Laws and Regulation 2.3.1 of Part A Regulations

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard annual notification of trading holidays.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance requirements. All members should plan their trading activities and operations around the announced holiday schedule.

## Important Dates

- **All dates listed above**: Trading will remain closed
- **November 8, 2026 (Sunday)**: Muhurat Trading session (timings to be announced)
- Market participants should note these dates for planning purposes

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - All market participants need to plan their trading and settlement schedules around these holidays. The 15 weekday holidays are spread across the year covering major national and religious festivals.

**Operational Impact**: Medium - Members need to adjust their operational calendars, staffing, and client communication accordingly. The Muhurat Trading session on a Sunday requires special operational planning.

**Planning Considerations**: Market participants should note that several holidays fall in Q1 2026 (7 holidays between January-April) and Q4 2026 (4 holidays between October-December), which may affect trading volumes and settlement cycles during these periods.
