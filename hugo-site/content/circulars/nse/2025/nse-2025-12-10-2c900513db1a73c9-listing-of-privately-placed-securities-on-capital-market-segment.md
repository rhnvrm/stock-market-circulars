## Summary

NSE has admitted three privately placed debt securities to trading on the Capital Market segment effective December 11, 2025. The securities include one market-linked debenture from Alpha Alternatives Financial Services Private Limited maturing in 2030, and two fixed-coupon debentures from Sammaan Capital Limited with coupon rates of 8.86% and 8.79% respectively, both maturing in December 2030. All securities will trade with a market lot size of 1.

## Key Points

- Three debt securities listed on NSE Capital Market segment
- Alpha Alternatives Financial Services: AAFS Market Linked 2030 (Symbol: AAFS30B, ISIN: INE0L6807260)
- Sammaan Capital Limited: 8.8570% 2030 Series II (Symbol: 886SCL30, ISIN: INE148I07YH4)
- Sammaan Capital Limited: 8.7923% 2030 Series I (Symbol: 879SCL30, ISIN: INE148I07YI2)
- All securities have market lot size of 1
- Trading designation follows NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This circular implements existing regulations under Regulation 3.1.1 and 2.5.5 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A for admission of securities to trading.

## Compliance Requirements

- Securities must be identified by designated codes for trading purposes
- Trading must be conducted in specified lot sizes (1 unit for all three securities)
- Members must update their systems to recognize new security codes and symbols
- Trading parameters effective from December 11, 2025

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: December 10, 2025
- **Effective Date**: December 11, 2025 (trading commencement)
- **Maturity Dates**:
  - AAFS30B: July 23, 2030
  - 886SCL30: December 9, 2030
  - 879SCL30: December 9, 2030

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market-wide impact. This is a routine administrative circular for listing privately placed debt securities. The impact is limited to:

- **Issuers**: Provides liquidity platform for Alpha Alternatives and Sammaan Capital debt instruments
- **Debt Market Participants**: Additional trading opportunities in corporate debt segment
- **Market Infrastructure**: Systems update required to accommodate new security codes
- **Investors**: Enhanced secondary market access for holders of these privately placed securities

The fixed coupon rates (8.86% and 8.79%) reflect prevailing market conditions for corporate debt with 5-year maturity profiles.
