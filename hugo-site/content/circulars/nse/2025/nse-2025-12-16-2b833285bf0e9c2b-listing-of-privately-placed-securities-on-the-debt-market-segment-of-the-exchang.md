## Summary

NSE has admitted 10 commercial papers (CPs) from 10 different companies to trading on its Debt Market Segment effective December 16, 2025. These are privately placed debt securities with maturity periods ranging from 3 months to 7 months, with face values of Rs. 5 lakh each. The total value of securities listed is approximately Rs. 1,390 crore.

## Key Points

- 10 commercial papers from various companies admitted to NSE Debt Market Segment
- All securities allotted on December 15, 2025 (except ICICI Securities which was allotted on March 17, 2025)
- Face value of Rs. 5,00,000 per security across all CPs
- Maturity periods range from 88 days to 218 days
- Total quantity of 28,300 securities listed
- All are fresh issues of commercial papers
- Trading effective from December 16, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. Members are notified of the admission of these securities to trading on the Debt Market Segment.

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: December 15, 2025 (for 9 securities); March 17, 2025 (for ICICI Securities)
- **Listing Date**: December 16, 2025 (effective date for trading)
- **Redemption Dates**: Range from March 13, 2026 to July 21, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine listing of institutional debt instruments on the debt market segment. These commercial papers are traded among institutional investors and do not affect equity markets or retail investors.

**Operational Impact**: Standard listing procedures. The designated security codes and ISINs enable trading and settlement of these debt securities on NSE's debt market platform.

**Investor Impact**: Relevant only to institutional investors and wholesale debt market participants who trade in commercial papers. No impact on retail equity investors.
