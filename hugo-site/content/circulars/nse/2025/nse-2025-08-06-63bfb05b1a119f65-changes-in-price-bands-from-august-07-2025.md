## Summary

NSE has revised price bands for 2,923 securities effective August 07, 2025, under Capital Market Segment Regulation Part-A, 2.5. The changes include securities categorized into different price band percentages ranging from 2% to 40%, with some securities having no band restrictions.

## Key Points

- Total 2,923 securities affected across 6 different price band categories
- 106 securities assigned 2% price band (highest restriction)
- 453 securities with 5% price band
- 170 securities with 10% price band
- 217 securities with no price band restrictions
- 1,972 securities remain in standard categories
- 5 securities assigned 40% price band (lowest restriction)
- Securities with derivative products maintain 10% daily operating range
- Close-ended Mutual Funds subject to 10% price bands

## Regulatory Changes

- Price band revisions implemented under existing Capital Market Segment Regulation Part-A, 2.5
- Limited Physical Market will have same price bands as normal market
- Daily price band reviews to be updated on NSE website
- Securities with derivatives maintain existing 10% operating range despite other band assignments

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must adhere to revised price bands from August 07, 2025
- Members should monitor daily price band updates on NSE website
- Queries to be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in
- Same price bands apply to both normal and Limited Physical Market segments

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: August 07, 2025
- **Circular Date**: August 06, 2025
- **Reference**: NSE/SURV/69527, Circular 604/2025

## Impact Assessment

- **Trading Impact**: Moderate - affects daily price movement limits for nearly 3,000 securities
- **Market Liquidity**: Potential reduction in volatility for securities with tighter bands (2%, 5%)
- **Risk Management**: Enhanced surveillance for securities in restrictive categories
- **Operational Impact**: Members need to update trading systems and risk controls
- **Investor Impact**: Limited daily price movements for securities under restrictive bands may affect short-term trading strategies
