## Summary

NSE has notified the suspension of trading in 24 debt securities effective from various dates between December 30, 2025 and January 27, 2026. The suspensions are due to maturity and redemption of these instruments. The affected securities include Treasury Bills (T-Bills), Sovereign Gold Bonds, Government Securities, and State Government Bonds.

## Key Points

- 24 debt securities will be suspended from trading on NSE Capital Market segment
- Suspensions are in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.2 of NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A
- All suspensions are due to maturity/redemption of the securities
- Suspension dates range from December 30, 2025 to January 27, 2026
- Affected instruments include 13 T-Bills, 4 Sovereign Gold Bonds, 2 Government Securities, and 5 State Government Bonds

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine notification under existing Regulation 3.1.2 of NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A regarding suspension of securities upon maturity.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance actions required from members. This is an informational circular notifying members about scheduled suspensions of debt securities reaching maturity.

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Minimal. This is a routine operational matter affecting the debt securities segment only. The suspensions are expected and scheduled well in advance based on the maturity dates of these instruments.

**Operational Impact:** No impact on equity trading or other market segments. Investors holding these securities will receive redemption proceeds as per the terms of the instruments.

**Investor Impact:** Investors holding these securities should note the suspension dates and plan for redemption proceeds. No trading will be possible in these securities from their respective suspension dates.
