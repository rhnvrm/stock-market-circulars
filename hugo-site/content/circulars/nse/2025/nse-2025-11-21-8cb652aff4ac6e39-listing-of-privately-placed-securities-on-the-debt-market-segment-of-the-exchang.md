## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed commercial papers from 10 issuers to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective November 21, 2025. The circular lists commercial papers totaling 49,500 securities with face value of ₹5 lakh each, issued by financial services companies and corporates with redemption dates ranging from December 2025 to May 2026.

## Key Points

- Total of 10 commercial paper issuances listed on NSE debt market segment
- Effective date of listing: November 21, 2025
- All securities are commercial papers with face value of ₹5,00,000 each
- Total quantity: 49,500 securities aggregating ₹2,475 crore face value
- Allotment dates: November 20-21, 2025
- Redemption periods: 27 days to 182 days
- Issuers include HDFC Securities, ICICI Securities, Aditya Birla group entities, Godrej group companies, Nuvoco Vistas, Jio Credit, and HSBC Investdirect

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with minimal market impact. The listing of these commercial papers provides additional short-term debt instruments for institutional investors in the debt market. These privately placed securities are typically traded among institutional participants and do not affect retail investors or equity markets. The circular demonstrates continued activity in the corporate debt market with major financial services and corporate entities raising short-term funds through commercial paper issuances.
