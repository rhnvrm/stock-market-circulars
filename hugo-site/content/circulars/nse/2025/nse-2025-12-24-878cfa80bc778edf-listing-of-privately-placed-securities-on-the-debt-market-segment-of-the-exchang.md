## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed debt securities to trading on its Debt Market Segment effective December 24, 2025. The listing includes 6 commercial papers from major corporates (Tata Capital Limited, Sundaram Home Finance, Godrej Industries, EPL Limited, Jio Credit Limited, and Nirma Limited) and 3 debenture issues from Akme Fintrade and Phoenix Arc Private Limited.

## Key Points

- Total of 6 commercial paper issues listed with tenures ranging from 3 to 12 months
- Total of 3 debenture issues listed with tenures of 2-3 years
- All securities admitted to dealings with effect from December 24, 2025
- Issued under Regulation 3.1.1 of NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations
- Securities allotted on December 22-23, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine notification under existing NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants can trade these securities using the designated security codes provided in the circular
- All trading must comply with Regulation 3.1.1 of NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations
- Securities are available for trading exclusively on the debt market segment

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: December 24, 2025 (listing and commencement of trading)
- **Allotment Dates**: December 22-23, 2025
- **Redemption Dates**: Range from March 13, 2026 to December 22, 2028

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market impact as it pertains to routine debt market listings. The securities are privately placed and will only be traded by institutional participants in the debt segment. No impact on equity markets. The listing provides additional investment opportunities for debt market participants and enhances liquidity in corporate debt instruments.
