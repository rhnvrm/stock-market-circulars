## Summary

The National Stock Exchange of India has announced that PGIM India Multi Asset Allocation Fund will be reopened for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions with effect from December 03, 2025. The fund will be available on the NSE MF Invest Platform with SIP (Systematic Investment Plan), STP (Systematic Transfer Plan), and SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) facilities.

## Key Points

- PGIM India Multi Asset Allocation Fund reopening for ongoing transactions
- Available on NSE MF Invest Platform
- Facilities include subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, STP, and SWP
- Six scheme options available: Direct and Regular plans with Growth and IDCW (Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal) variants
- All schemes categorized under Equity category
- Circular issued to all NSE members

## Scheme Details

The following six schemes will be available:

1. **PGIM India Multi Asset Allocation Fund Direct Plan Growth** (ISIN: INF663L010A3)
2. **PGIM India Multi Asset Allocation Fund Regular Plan Growth** (ISIN: INF663L013A7)
3. **PGIM India Multi Asset Allocation Fund Direct Plan IDCW Payout** (ISIN: INF663L011A1)
4. **PGIM India Multi Asset Allocation Fund Regular Plan IDCW Payout** (ISIN: INF663L014A5)
5. **PGIM India Multi Asset Allocation Fund Direct Plan IDCW Reinvestment** (ISIN: INF663L012A9)
6. **PGIM India Multi Asset Allocation Fund Regular Plan IDCW Reinvestment** (ISIN: INF663L015A2)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an operational announcement regarding the availability of a mutual fund scheme.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance requirements for members. This is an informational circular notifying members of the fund's availability for transactions.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: December 02, 2025
- **Effective Date**: December 03, 2025 (fund reopens for ongoing transactions)

This circular has minimal market impact as it pertains to a specific mutual fund scheme's operational availability. The reopening provides additional investment options for market participants seeking multi-asset allocation strategies in the equity category.
