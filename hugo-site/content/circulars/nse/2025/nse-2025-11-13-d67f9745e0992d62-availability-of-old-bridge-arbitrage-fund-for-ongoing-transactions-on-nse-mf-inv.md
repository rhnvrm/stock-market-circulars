## Summary

The National Stock Exchange of India has announced that the Old Bridge Arbitrage Fund of Old Bridge Mutual Fund will be reopened for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions along with SIP (Systematic Investment Plan), STP (Systematic Transfer Plan), and SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) facilities on the NSE MF Invest Platform effective November 14, 2025. The fund offers six plan options across Regular and Direct plans with Growth and IDCW (Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal) variants.

## Key Points

- Old Bridge Arbitrage Fund reopening for ongoing transactions on NSE MF Invest Platform
- Effective date: November 14, 2025
- Six scheme options available: Regular Plan (Growth, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment) and Direct Plan (Growth, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment)
- All schemes categorized under Equity category
- Facilities available: Subscriptions, Redemptions, SIP, STP, and SWP
- ISINs: INF0S5R01091, INF0S5R01109, INF0S5R01117, INF0S5R01125, INF0S5R01075, INF0S5R01083

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is an operational notification about fund availability.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance requirements. Members are informed of the scheme availability for transactional purposes.

## Important Dates

- **November 13, 2025**: Circular issued
- **November 14, 2025**: Old Bridge Arbitrage Fund reopens for ongoing transactions

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market impact as it pertains to routine mutual fund platform operations. Investors interested in arbitrage strategies will have access to Old Bridge Arbitrage Fund schemes through the NSE MF Invest Platform. The reopening provides additional investment options for market participants but does not affect trading operations, compliance frameworks, or broader market structure. Impact is limited to investors and distributors utilizing the NSE MF Invest Platform for mutual fund transactions.
