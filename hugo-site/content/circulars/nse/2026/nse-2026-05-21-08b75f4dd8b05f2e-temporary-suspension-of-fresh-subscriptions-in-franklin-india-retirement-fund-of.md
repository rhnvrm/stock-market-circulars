## Summary

NSE has suspended fresh subscriptions in Franklin India Retirement Fund on the NSE MF Invest Platform effective May 20, 2026, as directed by Franklin Templeton Mutual Fund. This action is taken to comply with SEBI's Categorization and Rationalization of Mutual Fund Schemes under Chapter 3 of the SEBI Master Circular for Mutual Fund dated March 20, 2026. The suspension is temporary and applicable until further notice.

## Key Points

- Fresh/additional purchases via lump sum mode are suspended with effect from May 20, 2026
- Fresh SIP and STP registrations into Franklin India Retirement Fund are discontinued
- Switch-in transactions from other Franklin Templeton schemes are not permitted
- Existing SIP and STP mandates will continue to remain operational
- Redemptions, Systematic Withdrawal Plans (SWP), Switch-out, and STP-out remain unrestricted
- The suspension is driven by SEBI's Mutual Fund Categorization and Rationalization requirements
- NSE circular reference: NSE/NMFTM/74296 | Circular Ref. No: 1385/2026

## Regulatory Changes

The suspension is mandated under Chapter 3 of the SEBI Master Circular for Mutual Fund dated March 20, 2026, which governs Categorization and Rationalization of Mutual Fund Schemes. Franklin Templeton's AMC and Trustee Board have acted to align the scheme with these regulatory requirements.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members on the MF Invest Platform must ensure no fresh subscription orders are accepted for Franklin India Retirement Fund
- No fresh SIP/STP registrations or Switch-in orders should be processed
- Members should inform their clients about the restriction and direct them to SWP, Switch-out, or Redemption options if they wish to exit

## Important Dates

- **May 19, 2026**: Date of Franklin Templeton addendum
- **May 20, 2026**: Effective date of suspension
- **May 21, 2026**: NSE circular issued to all members
- **Until further notice**: Duration of the suspension

## Impact Assessment

The suspension limits new investment flows into Franklin India Retirement Fund via NSE MF Invest Platform. Existing investors are not impacted in terms of their current holdings, ongoing SIPs/STPs, or ability to redeem. New investors or those planning to increase exposure cannot do so until the suspension is lifted. The restriction is a regulatory compliance measure and not indicative of any financial stress in the scheme. Investors seeking retirement-oriented mutual fund options will need to consider alternative schemes during this period.
