## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a revised list of approved securities and banks applicable for the month of August 2026, updating its previous circular no. 076/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/74877) dated June 25, 2026. The circular provides updated haircut rates, market-wide limits, and member-specific limits for various categories of collateral accepted from clearing members and custodians.

## Key Points

- The revised list covers seven annexures: (1) equity shares, (2) ETFs (non-cash component), (3) ETFs (cash component), (4) Sovereign Gold Bonds and G-Sec/T-Bills, (5) open-ended mutual funds, (6) corporate bonds, and (7) empaneled banks for BGs and FDRs.
- Applicable haircut rates for equity securities are based on VAR (Value at Risk) margin methodology.
- A value-based prudential norm applies: exclusion of a pledged security should not result in a margin shortfall of more than 25% at a Clearing Member level.
- G-Sec/T-Bills carry a shut period of two days; members must release G-Sec/T-Bills at least two days before maturity.
- Members who are also banks must note that G-Sec provided as collateral should not be reckoned for SLR purposes and should not be used for trading.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory policy changes; this is a periodic revision of the approved securities/banks list and applicable haircuts/limits under NCL's existing collateral risk management framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- All Members, Custodians, and PCMs must apply the updated market-wide and member-specific limits and haircut rates specified in the annexures when providing collateral.
- Members must ensure G-Sec/T-Bills are released at least two days prior to maturity due to the two-day shut period.
- Bank members must exclude G-Sec collateral from SLR computation and refrain from using it for trading.

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: July 22, 2026
- Effective Period: For the month of August 2026
- Supersedes: Circular no. 076/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/74877) dated June 25, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, recurring monthly update affecting collateral valuation and risk management for NSE Clearing members, custodians, and PCMs. It has operational relevance for margin and collateral management but does not directly move stock prices or affect retail investors. Members must update their systems and risk processes to reflect the revised limits and haircuts to remain compliant and avoid margin shortfalls.
