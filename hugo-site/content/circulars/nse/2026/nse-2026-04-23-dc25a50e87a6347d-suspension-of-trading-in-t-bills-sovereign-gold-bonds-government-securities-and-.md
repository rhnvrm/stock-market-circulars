## Summary

NSE has notified the suspension of trading in 20 debt market securities — comprising Treasury Bills (T-Bills), a Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB), a Government Security (GS), and State Government Bonds (SG) — effective from their respective suspension dates between April 28, 2026 and May 25, 2026. The suspensions are routine and occur on account of scheduled maturity/redemption under Regulation 3.1.2 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.

## Key Points

- 13 Treasury Bills (TB series) suspended between April 28 and May 25, 2026
- 1 Sovereign Gold Bond (SGBMAY26, ISIN: IN0020180033) suspended from April 30, 2026
- 1 Government Security (GS100526C, ISIN: IN000526C011) suspended from May 6, 2026
- 5 State Government Bonds (SG series) suspended between May 4 and May 25, 2026
- All suspensions are due to maturity; no discretionary or punitive action involved
- Issued under Regulation 3.1.2 of NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notification under existing NSE regulations requiring suspension of securities upon their scheduled redemption date.

## Compliance Requirements

No action required by members beyond ceasing to trade in the listed securities from their respective suspension dates. Members should update their systems to reflect the suspension of the 20 instruments as per the schedule below.

## Impact Assessment

Impact is minimal and routine. These are scheduled maturities of short-to-medium term debt instruments. Holders of these securities will receive redemption proceeds as per the terms of each instrument. There is no impact on equity markets. The Sovereign Gold Bond SGBMAY26 maturing on April 30, 2026 is the earliest suspension and may be of note to SGB investors. Overall, this circular is a standard housekeeping notice with no broader market implications.
