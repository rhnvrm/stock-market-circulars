## Summary

NSE has issued a circular identifying securities that meet the criteria for inclusion under the Long-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework. Three securities - ARENTERP (Rajdarshan Industries Limited), CELLPOINT (Cell Point (India) Limited), and SPECTRUM (Spectrum Electrical Industries Limited) - have been shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Stage-I effective July 27, 2026. No securities were shortlisted under Stage-IV, and no securities moved between stages (Stage-I to II, II to III, or I to IV) in this cycle.

## Key Points

- Long-Term ASM Stage-I applicable to: ARENTERP (INE610C01014), CELLPOINT (INE0O0001013), SPECTRUM (INE01EO01010)
- No securities shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Stage-IV this cycle
- No securities moved from Stage-I to Stage-II, Stage-II to Stage-III, or Stage-I to Stage-IV
- Applicable margin rate of 100% on all open and new positions in the listed securities
- ASM framework operates alongside other prevailing surveillance measures
- Shortlisting is based purely on market surveillance criteria and is not an adverse action against the company

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework introduced; this circular applies the existing Long-Term ASM framework (established via prior circulars dated October 27, 2018 through September 20, 2024) to newly identified securities meeting the surveillance criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

Members must apply 100% margin on all open positions in ARENTERP, CELLPOINT, and SPECTRUM as of July 28, 2026, and on all new positions created from July 29, 2026 onwards. No securities in this cycle require shifting from Rolling Settlement (EQ series) to Trade-for-Trade (BE series), as no securities qualified under Stage-IV criteria.

## Important Dates

- July 27, 2026: Effective date for shortlisting of securities under Long-Term ASM Stage-I
- July 28, 2026: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin
- July 29, 2026: 100% margin becomes applicable on open and new positions

## Impact Assessment

Trading in ARENTERP, CELLPOINT, and SPECTRUM will face significantly higher margin requirements (100%), which typically reduces speculative trading, increases the cost of taking positions, and can dampen liquidity and price volatility in these stocks. The absence of any Stage-IV additions or stage escalations this cycle indicates no securities faced trade-for-trade restrictions or further surveillance intensification. Impact is limited to the three named securities and their traders/investors, with no broader market-wide implications.
