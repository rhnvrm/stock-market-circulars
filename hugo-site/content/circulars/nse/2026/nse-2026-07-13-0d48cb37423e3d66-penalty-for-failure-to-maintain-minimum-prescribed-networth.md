## Summary

NSE, in continuation of its earlier circular NSE/INSP/73792 dated April 17, 2026 on uniform disciplinary actions across exchanges, has introduced a monetary penalty framework for trading members who report a shortfall in the minimum prescribed networth during periodic submissions. Regulation 10(g) of the SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 2026 requires stock brokers to maintain minimum networth as specified under Chapter X of the regulations at all times. NSE noted that some trading members were reporting networth shortfalls at the half-yearly/annual reporting dates (March 31 and September 30) while submitting revised networth certificates for a later date to show compliance. To address this, exchanges have jointly introduced penalties for such shortfalls.

## Key Points

- Trading members must submit networth to the Exchange on a half-yearly basis (as on March 31 and September 30) and an audited networth annually as on March 31.
- A monetary penalty now applies for any shortfall in networth reported during periodic submissions.
- First instance penalty: 2% of the shortfall amount from the required minimum networth (subject to a minimum of Rs. 10,000) or Rs. 2,00,000, whichever is lower.
- Repeat instance (2nd time onwards for consecutive periodic reporting): 50% escalation on the penalty.
- Separately, for networth shortfalls identified during Exchange member inspections (as per Annexure 1.1 of NSE/INSP/73792), a different, higher penalty applies: 5% of the shortfall amount on first instance, plus administrative action for recoupment of the shortfall; 50% escalation plus administrative action for repeat instances.

## Regulatory Changes

Introduction of a two-tier penalty structure for networth shortfalls:
1. Shortfalls self-reported during periodic submissions - lower penalty (2% escalating 50% per repeat instance, capped at Rs. 2,00,000 or subject to Rs. 10,000 minimum for first instance).
2. Shortfalls identified during Exchange inspection - higher penalty (5% escalating 50% per repeat instance) plus mandatory administrative action for recoupment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must maintain minimum prescribed networth at all times per SB Regulations, 2026.
- Continue submitting half-yearly networth (March 31, September 30) and audited annual networth (March 31).
- Any shortfall reported, whether self-reported or inspection-identified, will now attract monetary penalties and, in some cases, administrative action for recoupment.

## Important Dates

- Circular issued: July 13, 2026 (Ref. No. 66/2026, Download Ref No. NSE/COMP/75164).
- Penalty provisions effective immediately, applicable to all cases under process and all non-compliances identified or determined after the date of this circular.
- Related prior circular: NSE/INSP/73792 dated April 17, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

The circular increases the financial and compliance burden on trading members who fail to maintain the SEBI-prescribed minimum networth, closing a previous loophole where members could report a shortfall and later submit a revised certificate to appear compliant. This does not directly affect listed securities or trading activity but strengthens broker-level financial discipline and market integrity, indirectly benefiting investor protection.
