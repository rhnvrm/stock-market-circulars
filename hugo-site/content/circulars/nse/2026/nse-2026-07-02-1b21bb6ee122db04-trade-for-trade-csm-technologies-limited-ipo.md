## Summary

NSE has announced that CSM Technologies Limited, whose shares were placed under the trade-for-trade segment (BE series) following its IPO, will transition to the rolling settlement segment (EQ series) with effect from July 16, 2026. This move follows the earlier circular (NSE/CML/74968 dated July 01, 2026) and is in line with SEBI guidelines under circular reference CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012.

## Key Points

- CSM Technologies Limited (Symbol: CSM) will move from trade-for-trade (BE series) to rolling settlement (EQ series).
- The transition follows standard SEBI-mandated monitoring period applicable to newly listed IPO securities.
- Reference to prior Exchange circular NSE/CML/74968 dated July 01, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; this is an implementation of existing SEBI guidelines (CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012) governing the transition of newly listed securities from trade-for-trade to normal trading.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must update their systems to reflect the change in trading series for CSM from BE to EQ effective the stated date. No other special compliance action is required from members.

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: July 02, 2026
- Effective Date of Series Change: July 16, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The shift to EQ series enables normal rolling settlement (T+1) for CSM Technologies shares, replacing the trade-for-trade mechanism that required compulsory delivery-based settlement without netting. This typically increases trading flexibility and liquidity for the stock, benefiting active traders, though it also removes the additional settlement safeguard that trade-for-trade status provided during the stock's initial listing period.
