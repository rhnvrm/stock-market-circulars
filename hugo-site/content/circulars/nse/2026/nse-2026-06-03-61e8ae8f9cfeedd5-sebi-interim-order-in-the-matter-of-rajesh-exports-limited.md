## Summary

SEBI has issued Interim Order No. WTM/KV/CFID/CFID-SEC6/32431/2026-27 dated June 03, 2026, restraining Rajesh Mehta (PAN: ADKPM0172C) from buying, selling, or dealing in securities of Rajesh Exports Limited (REL), either directly or indirectly, until further orders. NSE has notified all its members to take note and ensure compliance. The order follows a SEBI investigation covering the period April 01, 2020 to March 31, 2024.

## Key Points

- Rajesh Mehta (PAN: ADKPM0172C) is restrained from trading in Rajesh Exports Limited securities until further orders
- SEBI Order reference: WTM/KV/CFID/CFID-SEC6/32431/2026-27 dated June 03, 2026
- NSE Circular Ref: NSE/INVG/74537 (Circular No. 041/2026)
- Investigation period covered: April 01, 2020 to March 31, 2024
- Rajesh Exports Limited (PAN: AAACR8642N) is also named as a noticee in the SEBI order
- The consolidated list of SEBI-debarred entities is maintained on the NSE website

## Regulatory Changes

SEBI has invoked powers under Sections 11(1), 11(4), and 11B of the SEBI Act, 1992 to pass an interim restraint order. Prima facie findings include:
- Non-cooperation by the noticees during investigation
- Non-disclosure of material consolidated financial information
- Misrepresentation of financial statements on both consolidated and standalone basis
- Incorrect consolidation of financial statements
- False claim of "Investment in Gold Mines in Africa"
- Incorrect adjustment of trade receivables against trade payables
- Misutilization of funds by REL

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must take note of the restraint order against Rajesh Mehta and ensure he is not permitted to buy, sell, or deal in securities of Rajesh Exports Limited
- Members must refer to the consolidated list of SEBI-debarred entities available at: https://www.nseindia.com/regulations/member-sebi-debarred-entities
- Queries may be directed to dl-invsg-all@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- Investigation Period: April 01, 2020 to March 31, 2024
- SEBI Order Date: June 03, 2026
- NSE Circular Date: June 03, 2026
- Restraint effective: Immediately, until further orders from SEBI

## Impact Assessment

This interim order carries high market and regulatory significance. The prima facie findings of financial misrepresentation across multiple dimensions — including misrepresentation of consolidated and standalone financials, alleged fictitious gold mine investments in Africa, and fund misutilization — indicate potential large-scale fraud at Rajesh Exports Limited. The restraint on Rajesh Mehta, a key associated entity, signals the likelihood of further enforcement action. Investors in RAJESHEXPO should be aware of heightened regulatory scrutiny. The full SEBI order spanning 109 pages is available on the SEBI website (http://www.sebi.gov.in).
