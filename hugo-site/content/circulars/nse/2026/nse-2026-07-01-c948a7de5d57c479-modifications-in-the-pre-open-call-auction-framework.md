## Summary

NSE has issued an FAQ document clarifying revisions to the Pre-open Session call auction framework applicable to all equity market securities, including SME securities, partly paid-up securities, and InvITs/REITs. The revised framework restructures the 15-minute pre-open window (9:00 am to 9:15 am) by splitting the order entry period into two distinct sub-periods with different order modification rules, effective from September 07, 2026.

## Key Points

- The pre-open call auction mechanism continues to apply to all equity market securities, including SME securities, partly paid-up securities, and InvITs/REITs.
- The overall pre-open session duration remains 15 minutes (9:00 am to 9:15 am).
- Order Entry Period is now split into two sub-periods:
  - 9:00 am - 9:05 am: Order entry, modification, and cancellation permitted for both limit and market orders.
  - 9:05 am - 9:10 am: Order entry, modification, and cancellation permitted only for limit orders; no modification/cancellation allowed for market orders. A system-driven random closure applies in the last 2 minutes of this sub-period.
- Order Matching & Trade Confirmation Period is revised to 9:10 am - 9:12 am (previously 9:08 am - 9:12 am), during which opening price determination, order matching, and trade confirmation occur immediately after order entry closes.
- Buffer Period remains unchanged at 9:12 am - 9:15 am, covering the transition from pre-open to the Continuous Trading Session (CTS).
- In the event of an index-based market-wide circuit filter breach or a trading outage, the market will reopen with a pre-open session, with timings communicated separately on that day.

## Regulatory Changes

The order entry window for market orders has been shortened (now closing at 9:05 am instead of 9:08 am), while limit orders retain an extended entry/modification/cancellation window until 9:10 am. The order matching and trade confirmation period has been correspondingly shifted to begin at 9:10 am instead of 9:08 am. This is a refinement of the call auction microstructure intended to change how and when market vs. limit orders can be modified or cancelled during price discovery.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and market participants must update their order entry systems and internal trading workflows to reflect the revised timing windows and the differentiated treatment of market orders versus limit orders during the 9:05 am - 9:10 am window.
- Members should ensure their systems account for the system-driven random closure in the last two minutes of the limit-order-only sub-period.
- No action is required for security eligibility, as the scope of applicable securities (all equity, SME, partly paid-up, InvITs/REITs) remains unchanged.

## Important Dates

- FAQ document dated/updated: July 01, 2026
- Effective date of revised pre-open framework: September 07, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The change primarily affects the mechanics of price discovery during the pre-open session rather than any specific listed company or stock. Trading members and algorithmic/order management systems will need to adjust for the revised timing structure, particularly the restriction on market order modification/cancellation after 9:05 am. The overall session length and transition to continuous trading remain unchanged, limiting broader market impact, though systems and operational readiness updates will be needed well ahead of the September 07, 2026 effective date.
