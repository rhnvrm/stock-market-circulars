## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has published the settlement calendar for the Electronic Gold Receipt (EGR) segment for June 2026. The calendar covers two settlement types: Normal T+1 settlements (Type 6) and Auction settlements (Type 7), running from June 1 to June 30, 2026.

## Key Points

- Settlement Type 6 (Normal T+1) covers settlement numbers 2026011 through 2026032, from June 1 to June 30, 2026
- Settlement Type 7 (Auction) covers settlement numbers 2026011 through 2026032, from June 2 to July 1, 2026
- Multiple settlements are scheduled on June 29, 2026; as a result, auction 2026030 will not be conducted — shortages will be covered in auction 2026031 instead
- The last Normal settlement (2026032) for trade date June 30 settles on July 1, 2026
- Circular issued under Regulation 6.2 of the National Securities Clearing Corporation (Capital Market Regulations, 1997)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine periodic calendar notification issued under existing Regulation 6.2 of the NSCCL Capital Market Regulations, 1997.

## Compliance Requirements

EGR segment members should note the settlement schedules and ensure timely fulfilment of obligations per the published dates. Members with positions on June 29, 2026 should note that auction 2026030 is cancelled; shortages will roll into settlement 2026031.

## Important Dates

- **June 1, 2026**: First Normal (T+1) settlement of the month (2026011)
- **June 2, 2026**: First Auction settlement of the month (2026011)
- **June 29, 2026**: Multiple settlements scheduled; auction 2026030 cancelled, shortages moved to 2026031
- **June 30, 2026**: Last trade date for Normal settlement; settles July 1, 2026
- **July 1, 2026**: Last settlement date in the calendar (Normal 2026032 and Auction 2026032)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with low market impact. It provides schedule clarity for EGR segment participants for the upcoming month. The only notable operational point is the cancellation of auction settlement 2026030 on June 29 due to multiple settlements being scheduled on that date, with shortages redirected to 2026031.
