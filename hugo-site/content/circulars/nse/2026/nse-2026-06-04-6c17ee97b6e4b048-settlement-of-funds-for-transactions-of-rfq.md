## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has issued a clarification regarding the settlement of funds for transactions executed on the Request for Quote (RFQ) platform. The circular specifies that when a participant transfers single funds for multiple orders or multiple funds for multiple orders, the allocation will be done on a First-In-First-Out (FIFO) basis based on the receipt of funds and orders. It also partially modifies an earlier circular to explicitly permit funds pay-in for Government Securities, State Development Loans (SDLs), and Treasury Bills to the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) account held by NSE Clearing Limited.

## Key Points

- Fund settlements for RFQ trades can be made via Net Banking, UPI, or NEFT/RTGS to a virtual account.
- When single funds are transferred for multiple orders, or multiple funds for multiple orders, allocation follows FIFO order of receipt.
- No change in the existing process for funds transferred to the RBI account of NSE Clearing Ltd.
- Partial modification of circular NCL/DS/43380 dated January 30, 2020: funds pay-in for G-Secs, SDLs, and T-Bills on RFQ can now be explicitly transferred to the RBI account of NSE Clearing Ltd.
- Excess and unutilized funds will be returned to participants by T+4 day.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular partially modifies **NCL/DS/43380 dated January 30, 2020**, extending the permitted payment destination (RBI account of NSE Clearing Ltd) explicitly to cover funds pay-in for Government Securities, State Development Loans, and Treasury Bills traded on the RFQ platform. It also formally codifies the FIFO allocation rule for multi-order/multi-fund scenarios.

## Compliance Requirements

- All Participants, Custodians, and Members dealing in RFQ trades must note the FIFO allocation rule when transferring single or multiple fund amounts for multiple orders.
- No additional action is required for existing participants using the RBI account payment channel.
- Participants should account for the T+4 timeline for return of excess or unutilized funds.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 4, 2026
- **Reference Circular:** NCL/DS/74002 dated April 30, 2026 (consolidated circular)
- **Modified Circular:** NCL/DS/43380 dated January 30, 2020
- **Excess Fund Return:** T+4 day from transaction date

## Impact Assessment

The operational impact is low and confined to debt market participants (participants, custodians, and members) active on the RFQ platform. The FIFO clarification helps avoid ambiguity in multi-order fund transfers and reduces settlement disputes. The explicit inclusion of G-Secs, SDLs, and T-Bills for RBI account pay-in formalizes an existing practice, causing no disruption. The T+4 return timeline for excess funds is a standard operational parameter participants should factor into their liquidity planning.
