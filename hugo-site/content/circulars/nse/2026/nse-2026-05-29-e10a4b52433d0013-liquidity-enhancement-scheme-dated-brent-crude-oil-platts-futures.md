## Summary

NSE is introducing a Liquidity Enhancement Scheme (LES) for Dated Brent Crude oil (Platts) Futures effective June 15, 2026, in accordance with SEBI circular SEBI/HO/CDMRD/DMP/CIR/P/2018/55 dated March 26, 2018. The scheme aims to encourage active participation and market development by appointing a designated Market Maker (MM) through a competitive bidding process.

## Key Points

- LES in Dated Brent Crude oil (Platts) Futures contract launches on June 15, 2026.
- One Market Maker will be appointed via competitive bidding for an initial period of 3 months.
- Market Makers must provide 2-way continuous quotes on all business days for near-month futures contracts (Line 1 & Line 2).
- Minimum monthly presence requirement is 85% of trading hours.
- Minimum lot obligations: 4 lots (Line 1) and 3 lots (Line 2) for both bid and ask sides.
- Maximum bid-ask spread: INR 12 for Line 1; Line 2 within ±3 from Line 1.
- Trading hours for presence computation: 9:10 AM to 11:20 PM / 11:45 PM (daylight saving dependent).
- Penalty of INR 5 lakhs for Market Makers who discontinue within 2 weeks of empanelment.
- A 15-day notice is mandatory to exit after the initial 2-week period.

## Regulatory Changes

NSE is implementing LES under the framework permitted by SEBI vide circular SEBI/HO/CDMRD/DMP/CIR/P/2018/55 (March 26, 2018), which allows exchanges to introduce liquidity enhancement schemes in commodity derivative contracts. This marks the extension of LES to Dated Brent Crude oil (Platts) Futures on NSE's Commodity Derivatives segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Interested trading members must register with the Exchange to participate as a Market Maker.
- Eligibility criteria: minimum net worth of ₹5 Crores, no serious disciplinary action in the last one year, and must be a registered algo member in the NSE Commodity segment.
- Applicants must submit bids in the format specified in Annexure I on or before June 3, 2026 (by 17:00 hrs).
- Designated Market Makers must fulfil continuous 2-way quote obligations as per their submitted bids; no changes will be entertained post-appointment.
- Performance will be measured on a monthly average basis; non-fulfilment of obligations may lead to termination.

## Important Dates

- **June 3, 2026 (by 17:00 hrs):** Deadline for submission of bids by interested Market Maker applicants.
- **June 15, 2026:** Effective date for launch of LES in Dated Brent Crude oil (Platts) Futures.
- **3 months from June 15, 2026:** Initial tenure of the appointed Market Maker.

## Impact Assessment

This scheme is targeted at trading members active in NSE's Commodity Derivatives segment, particularly those with algo trading capabilities and sufficient net worth. The introduction of LES is expected to improve liquidity and tighten spreads in the Dated Brent Crude oil (Platts) Futures contract, benefiting all participants in this market. The impact on broader equity or index markets is minimal. Members considering market-making should carefully evaluate the quoting obligations, penalty provisions, and exit notice requirements before applying.
