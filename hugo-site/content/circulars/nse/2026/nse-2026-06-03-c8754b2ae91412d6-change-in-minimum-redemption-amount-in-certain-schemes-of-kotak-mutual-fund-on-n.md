## Summary

NSE has communicated to its members that Kotak Mahindra Mutual Fund has revised the minimum redemption amount (including switch-out) across six of its schemes. The revision takes effect on June 03, 2026, reducing the minimum redemption threshold significantly to make partial redemptions more accessible for investors on the NSE MF Invest platform.

## Key Points

- Minimum redemption amount revised from ₹1,000 or 100 units to ₹100 or 10 units (or account balance, whichever is lower)
- Change applies to all plans under each of the six affected schemes
- Switch-out transactions are also subject to the revised minimum
- The amendment forms an integral part of the updated SID and KIM for the affected schemes
- All other terms and conditions of the schemes remain unchanged

## Regulatory Changes

Kotak Mahindra Asset Management Company Limited has issued a Notice-cum-Addendum to the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM) for the following six schemes:

| Scheme Name | Existing Minimum | Revised Minimum |
|---|---|---|
| Kotak Income Plus Arbitrage Omni FOF | ₹1,000 or 100 units | ₹100 or 10 units |
| Kotak Flexicap Fund | ₹1,000 or 100 units | ₹100 or 10 units |
| Kotak Large & Midcap Fund | ₹1,000 or 100 units | ₹100 or 10 units |
| Kotak Nifty Next 50 Index Fund | ₹1,000 or 100 units | ₹100 or 10 units |
| Kotak Business Cycle Fund | ₹1,000 or 100 units | ₹100 or 10 units |
| Kotak Contra Fund | ₹1,000 or 100 units | ₹100 or 10 units |

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members and distributors operating on the NSE MF Invest platform must update their systems to reflect the revised minimum redemption thresholds for these six schemes.
- The revised limits apply to both redemption and switch-out transactions across all plan variants of each scheme.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: June 03, 2026
- **Notice Date**: June 02, 2026 (Kotak AMC addendum); June 03, 2026 (NSE circular)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational update with low market impact. The reduction in minimum redemption amount from ₹1,000/100 units to ₹100/10 units benefits retail investors by allowing smaller partial redemptions and switch-out transactions without needing to liquidate larger amounts. There is no impact on scheme performance, NAV, or broader market operations. Distributors and platforms will need to update their order validation parameters accordingly.
