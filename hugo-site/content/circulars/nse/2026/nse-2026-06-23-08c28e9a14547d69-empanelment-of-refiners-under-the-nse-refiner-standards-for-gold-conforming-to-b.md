## Summary

NSE has empaneled two new refiners — AUGMONT ENTERPRISES LIMITED and GGC Gujrat Gold Centre Private Limited — under its NSE Refiner Standards (NRS) framework for gold conforming to BIS standards. Their refined gold coins and bars are now eligible for settlement against delivery obligations in all NSE gold futures contracts and for the creation of Electronic Gold Receipts (EGRs) traded on the NSE platform. The circular is effective immediately.

## Key Points

- AUGMONT ENTERPRISES LIMITED and GGC Gujrat Gold Centre Private Limited have been empaneled under NSE Refiner Standards (NRS).
- Gold bars and coins from these refiners can be used for physical delivery settlement in all NSE gold futures contracts.
- These refiners are also eligible to facilitate creation of NSE Electronic Gold Receipts (EGRs), as per NSE/EGR/74224 dated May 14, 2026.
- Making charges are Nil for Gold Futures and GoldM Futures; Rs. 300 per coin for Gold 10G Futures, Gold Guinea Futures, and Gold 1 Gram Futures.
- Gold delivered must strictly adhere to contract specifications and quality parameters prescribed by NSE.
- Circular is effective immediately upon issuance.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular expands the list of approved refiners under the NSE Refiner Standards framework, continuing the process initiated by NSE/COM/73215 dated March 10, 2026. No changes to the NRS framework itself are introduced — only new entities have been qualified and added.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members facilitating physical delivery in NSE gold futures must ensure gold sourced from these refiners meets contract specifications and quality parameters.
- Making charges must be applied as specified, exclusive of applicable taxes, levies, and other charges.
- Deliveries must conform to BIS standards as mandated under the NRS framework.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** June 23, 2026 (immediate effect)
- **Predecessor Circular:** NSE/COM/73215 dated March 10, 2026 (NRS framework establishment)
- **EGR Eligibility Circular:** NSE/EGR/74224 dated May 14, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The empanelment of AUGMONT ENTERPRISES LIMITED and GGC Gujrat Gold Centre Private Limited broadens the supply base of NRS-approved refiners, potentially improving availability of eligible gold for physical delivery and EGR creation on NSE. This is beneficial for commodity derivatives market participants seeking physical settlement in gold futures, as it reduces concentration risk among approved refiners. The nil making charges for standard Gold Futures and GoldM Futures maintain cost efficiency for institutional delivery, while the Rs. 300 per coin charge for smaller denomination contracts (Gold 10G, Gold Guinea, Gold 1 Gram) is consistent with the additional handling involved.
