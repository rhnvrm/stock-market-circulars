## Summary

NSE has issued a revised list of equity shares that are eligible to form part of the non-cash component of liquid assets for the Electronic Gold Receipt (EGR) segment. The circular specifies overall permitted quantities across all segments and memberwise permitted quantities for the EGR segment for each approved security.

## Key Points

- The applicable haircut for listed securities is Value at Risk (VAR)-based.
- A value-based prudential norm applies: exclusion of a pledged security must not result in a margin shortfall of more than 25% at a Clearing Member level.
- The list (Annexures 1–7) covers equity shares eligible as non-cash collateral, along with approved banks.
- Each security is identified by symbol, ISIN, and full security name, with an overall permitted quantity across all segments and a memberwise permitted quantity specific to the EGR segment.
- For most listed securities, the memberwise permitted quantity for the EGR segment is marked as "N.A".

## Regulatory Changes

This is a periodic revision to the approved securities list. No new regulatory framework is introduced; the update reflects changes in eligible scrips and permitted quantities effective from the circular date.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing Members must ensure that collateral pledged as non-cash liquid assets comprises only securities from this revised approved list.
- Clearing Members must ensure that the removal of any single pledged security does not cause a margin shortfall exceeding 25% at the Clearing Member level.
- Members should update their collateral management systems to reflect the revised permitted quantities.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: 2026-05-22 (date of circular issuance).

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on Clearing Members participating in the EGR segment, as they must reconcile their current collateral holdings against the revised approved list and permitted quantities. Securities removed from the list or with reduced permitted quantities may require substitution. The VAR-based haircut methodology and the 25% prudential norm cap on margin shortfall remain unchanged, limiting broader market disruption.
