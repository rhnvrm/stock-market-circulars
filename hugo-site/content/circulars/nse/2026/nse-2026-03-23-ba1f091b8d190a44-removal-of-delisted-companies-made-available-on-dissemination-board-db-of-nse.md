## Summary

NSE has announced the removal of four compulsory delisted companies from its Dissemination Board (DB) effective March 24, 2026. The Exchange had previously withdrawn the delisting of these companies, necessitating their removal from the DB. The affected companies are Gammon India Limited, Era Infra Engineering Limited, Indus Fila Limited, and K.S. Oils Limited.

## Key Points

- Four companies will be removed from NSE's Dissemination Board effective March 24, 2026
- The removal follows the Exchange's withdrawal of compulsory delisting for each company
- Companies affected: GAMMONIND (Gammon India Limited), ERAINFRA (Era Infra Engineering Limited), INDUSFILA (Indus Fila Limited), KSOILS (K.S. Oils Limited)
- Circular issued by Listing Department under Ref. No. NSE/LIST/C/2026/0489

## Regulatory Changes

The Exchange is reversing the availability of these securities on the Dissemination Board. The original circulars that placed these companies on the DB were:
- GAMMONIND: NSE/CML/72956
- ERAINFRA: NSE/CML/71602
- INDUSFILA: NSE/CML/60759
- KSOILS: NSE/CML/67778

## Compliance Requirements

All NSE members are requested to note that the four listed companies shall no longer be available on the Dissemination Board from March 24, 2026. Members should update their systems and inform relevant clients accordingly.

## Important Dates

- **March 23, 2026**: Circular issued
- **March 24, 2026**: Effective date — companies removed from NSE Dissemination Board

## Impact Assessment

This change affects investors and members who may have been trading or holding positions in these four securities on the Dissemination Board. The withdrawal of compulsory delisting status is a positive development for the companies concerned, as it signals a regulatory reversal. However, the removal from the DB means these securities will no longer be accessible through that platform. Members should communicate this change to clients holding or monitoring these securities.
