## Summary

NSE has informed members that ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund is revising the minimum application amounts for purchase, additional purchase, and Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) under Daily, Weekly, Fortnightly, and Monthly frequencies for certain schemes, effective June 15, 2026, on the NSE MF Invest platform.

## Key Points

- Minimum fresh purchase / switch-in amount reduced from Rs. 5,000/- to Rs. 500/- (plus multiples of Re. 1/-)
- Minimum additional purchase / switch-in amount reduced from Rs. 1,000/- to Rs. 500/- (plus multiples of Re. 1/-)
- Changes apply to SIP under Daily, Weekly, Fortnightly, and Monthly frequencies
- Approved by ICICI Prudential Trust Limited (the Trustees)
- NSE Download Ref No: NSE/NMFTM/74669 | Circular Ref. No: 1430/2026

## Regulatory Changes

The Notice-cum-Addendum to the Scheme Information Documents (SIDs) and Key Information Memorandums (KIMs) of select schemes has been approved by ICICI Prudential Trust Limited. The affected schemes are:

- ICICI Prudential Short Term Fund
- ICICI Prudential Arbitrage Fund
- ICICI Prudential Business Cycle Fund
- ICICI Prudential Flexicap Fund
- ICICI Prudential Large & Midcap Fund
- ICICI Prudential Focused Equity Fund
- ICICI Prudential Active Momentum Fund

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members operating on the NSE MF Invest platform must update their systems to reflect the revised minimum application amounts for the above schemes
- All fresh purchase, additional purchase, and SIP transactions must comply with the new minimums of Rs. 500/- effective June 15, 2026

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 12, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 15, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This change lowers the entry barrier for retail investors across several ICICI Prudential equity and debt schemes on the NSE MF Invest platform. By reducing the minimum investment from Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 500 for fresh purchases and from Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 500 for additional purchases, ICICI Prudential is broadening accessibility to these funds. The impact on overall market operations is minimal; however, the revision may increase transaction volumes from smaller ticket investors. No action is required from members beyond system or workflow updates to accept the new lower minimums.
