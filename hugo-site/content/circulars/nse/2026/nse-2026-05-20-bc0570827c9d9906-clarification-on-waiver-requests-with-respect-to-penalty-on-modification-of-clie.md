## Summary

NSE's Inspection Department (Circular Ref. No: 25/2026) has issued clarifications to Trading Members on the eligibility criteria for seeking penalty waivers related to client code modifications. The circular defines what constitutes a 'genuine error' — specifically regarding similarity in client codes or names — and sets limits on the frequency of such waivers per quarter.

## Key Points

- Trading Members have been submitting waiver requests without properly ascertaining the genuineness of errors as per SEBI guidelines
- A client code modification is considered a 'genuine error' only if the original and modified codes are 'similar' by defined criteria
- Similarity Criterion 1: Error in a single character of the client code (e.g., 248 → 249 is allowed; 248 → 259 is not)
- Similarity Criterion 2: Single instance of interchange between two adjacent characters (e.g., 248 → 284 is allowed; 248 → 842 is not)
- No more than one waiver per quarter may be granted to a Trading Member for client code modification errors
- If a waiver is granted for a modification from code AB to BA, no further waivers are allowed to that member for the remainder of the quarter

## Regulatory Changes

This circular does not introduce new regulations but clarifies the application of existing rules under:
- SEBI Circular CIR/DNPD/6/2011 dated July 5, 2011
- SEBI Circular CIR/MRD/DP/29/2014 dated October 21, 2014
- SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/MRD2/PoD-2/CIR/P/2023/171 dated October 16, 2023
- Exchange Circular NSE/INVG/56395 dated April 17, 2023 (Point No. 7 governs the waiver procedure)

The clarification explicitly defines allowable and non-allowable code modifications with sample illustrations to remove ambiguity.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must verify that client code modifications meet the similarity criteria before submitting waiver requests
- Members must ensure the error is due to communication, punching, or typing mistakes where original and modified codes are similar
- Modifications within relatives (as defined under the Companies Act, 2013) may also qualify as genuine errors
- Members are capped at one waiver per quarter for client code modification errors; exceeding this is not permitted regardless of the nature of the error
- Members must not submit waiver claims for modifications that change more than one character or involve non-adjacent character interchanges

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: May 20, 2026
- Effective: Immediately upon issuance
- Quarterly waiver limit applies on a rolling quarter basis

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on Trading Members across all segments. Members who previously submitted broad or loosely justified waiver requests will face stricter scrutiny and potential rejection of claims. The one-waiver-per-quarter cap introduces a hard limit that could result in financial penalties for members with multiple genuine errors in a quarter. Back-office and compliance teams at brokerages will need to update internal checklists to pre-screen waiver requests against the defined similarity criteria before submission to NSE.
