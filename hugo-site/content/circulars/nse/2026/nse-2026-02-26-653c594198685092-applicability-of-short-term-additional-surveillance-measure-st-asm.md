## Summary

NSE has issued a circular announcing the applicability of the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective February 27, 2026. Five securities have been newly shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage-I, one security (EASEMYTRIP) has been excluded from the ASM framework, and no securities are moving between Stage-I and Stage-II in either direction.

## Key Points

- 5 securities newly added to ST-ASM Framework Stage-I effective February 27, 2026
- No securities shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage-II
- No securities moving from Stage-I to Stage-II or Stage-II to Stage-I
- 1 security (EASEMYTRIP - Easy Trip Planners Limited) excluded from ASM Framework effective February 27, 2026
- Consolidated ASM list contains at least 34 securities under Stage-I surveillance

## Regulatory Changes

The ST-ASM framework is a surveillance mechanism used by NSE to monitor securities exhibiting abnormal price/volume movements. Securities placed under ST-ASM are subject to enhanced margin requirements and other trading restrictions to curb excessive speculation and protect market integrity.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must apply applicable ST-ASM margin requirements for newly added securities from February 27, 2026
- Clients holding or trading in ASM-listed securities must maintain higher margins as prescribed
- EASEMYTRIP margins and restrictions revert to normal from February 27, 2026 upon exclusion
