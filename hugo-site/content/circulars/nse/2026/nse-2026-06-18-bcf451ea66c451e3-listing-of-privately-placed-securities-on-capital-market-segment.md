## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of three privately placed debt securities to dealings on the Capital Market segment effective June 19, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A. Lot sizes for each security are also specified per Regulation 2.5.5.

## Key Points

- Three new debt securities admitted to NSE Capital Market segment effective June 19, 2026
- All securities are in Series N0 with a market lot of 1
- Securities are identified solely by their designated codes for trading purposes
- Lot sizes as specified in the Annexure govern all trading in these instruments

## Securities Listed

| Company | Symbol | Issue Description | Coupon Rate | Maturity Date | ISIN |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Magnum Ventures Limited | 18MVL31 | MVL 18% 2031 | 18.00% | 31-Mar-2031 | INE387I07021 |
| NIIF Infrastructure Finance Limited | 788NIIF29 | NIIF 7.88% 2029 Sr PP2 | 7.88% | 21-Aug-2029 | INE246R07905 |
| Jio Credit Limited | 815JCL31 | JCL 8.15% 2031 Sr VI | 8.15% | 16-Jun-2031 | INE282H07067 |

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular implements existing provisions under:
- Regulation 3.1.1 – Admission of securities to dealings on NSE Capital Market segment
- Regulation 2.5.5 – Lot size specifications for admitted securities

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use designated security codes (18MVL31, 788NIIF29, 815JCL31) exclusively when trading these instruments
- All trades must adhere to the market lot size of 1 unit per security
- No further action required from members beyond awareness of new listings

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 18, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 19, 2026 (commencement of trading)

## Impact Assessment

Low market impact. This is a routine notification for the admission of three privately placed non-convertible debentures (NCDs) to NSE's capital market segment. The listing provides a secondary market trading venue for these instruments. The Magnum Ventures bond carries a notably high coupon of 18%, while NIIF Infrastructure Finance and Jio Credit bonds carry more typical rates of 7.88% and 8.15% respectively. Impact is limited to institutional and retail participants specifically interested in these debt instruments.
