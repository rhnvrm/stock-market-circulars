## Summary

NSE's Listing Department has issued circular 0765/2026 (Ref: NSE/CML/74172) notifying the suspension of trading in four privately placed Non-Convertible Securities (NCS) on the NSE Debt Market segment. All four instruments will be suspended with effect from May 13, 2026, on account of redemption, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A.

## Key Points

- Four privately placed non-convertible securities are being suspended from trading effective May 13, 2026
- All suspensions are due to redemption of the respective instruments
- The action is taken under Regulation 3.1.2 of NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A
- Issuers include a major scheduled commercial bank (HDFC Bank) and three non-banking financial entities
- Circular issued by Vishakha Kine, Manager, NSE Listing Department

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. The suspensions are a standard procedural action under the existing Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A, which governs the suspension of debt securities upon redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members of NSE are notified to cease trading in the four listed ISINs from May 13, 2026
- No further trading activity should be undertaken in these instruments post the suspension date
- Existing holders should coordinate with their respective depositories and issuers for redemption proceeds

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 12, 2026
- **Suspension Effective Date:** May 13, 2026 (for all four securities)

## Impact Assessment

The impact is confined to investors and traders holding these four specific ISIN-level instruments in the NSE Debt Market. All four securities are being redeemed, meaning the issuers will return principal to holders as per the terms of the original issuance. There is no adverse credit event; this is an orderly maturity-driven suspension. HDFC Bank (INE040A08708) is the most prominent issuer among the four, but as a routine NCS redemption, broader market impact is negligible. Holders of these instruments should expect redemption proceeds to be credited per the respective debenture trust deed timelines.
