## Summary

NSE has issued a circular stating that the equity shares of IC Electricals Company Limited (Symbol: ICELCO), which is an SME IPO listed security, will be transferred from the trade for trade segment (Series: ST) to the rolling settlement segment (Series: SM) with effect from July 24, 2026. This follows an earlier NSE circular (Ref No. NSE/CML/75132 dated July 09, 2026) and is issued pursuant to SEBI guidelines under circular reference no. CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012.

## Key Points

- Security: IC Electricals Company Limited (Symbol: ICELCO)
- Current segment: Trade for Trade (Series: ST)
- New segment: Rolling settlement (Series: SM)
- Effective date of transition: July 24, 2026
- Issued under SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012
- Related prior circular: NSE/CML/75132 dated July 09, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this circular applies existing SEBI guidelines on trade for trade segment classification to reclassify ICELCO from the ST series to the SM (rolling settlement) series.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must update their systems to reflect the change in series for ICELCO from ST to SM effective July 24, 2026, and ensure trades in this security are settled under normal rolling settlement rules from that date onward.

## Important Dates

- July 09, 2026: Prior NSE circular (Ref No. NSE/CML/75132) referenced
- July 10, 2026: Date of this circular
- July 24, 2026: Effective date for transfer of ICELCO from ST to SM series

## Impact Assessment

The move from trade for trade to rolling settlement typically eases trading conditions for a security by removing the mandatory delivery-based settlement restriction, potentially improving liquidity and enabling intraday trading. This is a routine post-IPO market operations update relevant primarily to traders and holders of ICELCO shares, with limited impact beyond this specific stock.
