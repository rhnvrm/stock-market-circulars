## Summary

NSE has issued a revised list of equity shares approved to form part of the non-cash component of liquid assets. The circular covers Annexures 1 through 6 and specifies the overall permitted quantity across all segments and the memberwise permitted quantity for the Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) segment for each eligible security.

## Key Points

- The applicable haircut for all listed securities is VAR (Value at Risk)-based.
- A value-based prudential norm applies: exclusion of a pledged security must not result in a margin shortfall of more than 25% at a Clearing Member level.
- Each security is listed with its Symbol, ISIN, Security Name, Overall Permitted Quantity across all segments, and Memberwise Permitted Quantity for the SLB segment.
- Memberwise permitted quantity for the SLB segment is marked as "N.A" for all listed securities in Annexure 1.
- The list covers a broad universe of large- and mid-cap equities across sectors.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a periodic revision to the approved securities list for non-cash liquid assets. The VAR-based haircut methodology and the 25% prudential norm for pledged security exclusion remain in effect as standing policy.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing Members must ensure their non-cash collateral portfolios include only securities from the revised approved list.
- Members must not allow exclusion of any pledged security if it results in a margin shortfall exceeding 25% at the Clearing Member level.
- Members operating in the SLB segment must adhere to memberwise permitted quantity limits as specified in the annexures.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: 2026-05-22 (date of circular issuance); members should update their collateral eligibility lists accordingly.

## Impact Assessment

This update affects Clearing Members and Trading Members who use equity shares as non-cash components of liquid assets for margin purposes. The revised list determines which securities can be pledged as collateral and in what quantities, directly impacting margin management and funding strategies. The 25% prudential cap on margin shortfall from pledged security exclusions limits concentration risk at the clearing member level. Members holding securities that may have been removed from or added to the approved list must rebalance their collateral portfolios promptly.
