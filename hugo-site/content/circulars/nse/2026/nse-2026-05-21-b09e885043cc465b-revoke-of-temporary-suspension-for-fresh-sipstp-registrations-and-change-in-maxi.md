## Summary

NSE has issued Circular No. 1386/2026 (Download Ref No: NSE/NMFTM/74301) dated May 21, 2026, communicating the revocation of the temporary suspension on fresh SIP/STP registrations and the removal of the lumpsum investment limit in Baroda BNP Paribas Aqua Fund of Fund. These restrictions, originally imposed via Notice-cum-Addendum No. 48/2022 dated June 27, 2022, are being lifted effective May 20, 2026. The changes are made available to NSE MF Invest Platform members.

## Key Points

- Fresh SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) and STP (Systematic Transfer Plan) registrations in Baroda BNP Paribas Aqua Fund of Fund are now permitted.
- The earlier lumpsum investment cap of ₹5,00,000 per day per investor (at Primary Holder PAN level) has been removed.
- All subscriptions remain subject to overall overseas investment limits available at the AMC level.
- The AMC reserves the right to re-suspend or restrict subscriptions if overseas headroom is fully utilised.
- Changes are effective from May 20, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

The changes are issued pursuant to SEBI letter no. SEBI/HO/OW/IMD-II/DOF3/P/25095/2022 dated June 17, 2022, which permitted mutual funds to resume overseas securities investments within available headroom without breaching limits as of February 1, 2022 end of day.

- **Lumpsum limit removed:** Daily per-investor cap of ₹5,00,000 on lumpsum investments (including switch-ins) is withdrawn.
- **Fresh SIP/STP allowed:** Restrictions on fresh SIP and STP registrations are lifted.
- **Overseas investment cap retained:** Investments must not breach overseas investment limits as on February 1, 2022. Actual capacity is restricted to the difference between February 1, 2022 levels and current reduced exposure.
- **AUM-linked moderation:** AMC may moderate fresh subscriptions based on a cap linked to a percentage of average AUM invested in overseas securities/ETFs over the preceding three calendar months.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE MF Invest Platform members should update their systems to allow fresh SIP/STP registrations and remove the lumpsum subscription cap for this scheme.
- Members must ensure that investments are processed within the overseas investment headroom available at the AMC level.
- Members should be prepared for possible future suspension notices from the AMC if overseas limits are exhausted.

## Important Dates

- **May 18, 2026:** Date of Notice-cum-Addendum No. 40/2026 issued by Baroda BNP Paribas Asset Management India Pvt. Ltd.
- **May 20, 2026:** Effective date for removal of lumpsum limit and resumption of fresh SIP/STP registrations.
- **May 21, 2026:** Date of NSE circular communicating these changes to members.
- **February 1, 2022 (reference date):** Baseline date for overseas investment limits that continue to govern permissible investment headroom.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate positive impact for retail and institutional investors seeking exposure to the Baroda BNP Paribas Aqua Fund of Fund, which invests in overseas water-themed securities/ETFs. The restoration of SIP/STP access enables systematic investing, which was suspended since mid-2022 due to SEBI-imposed overseas investment limits. However, the practical capacity for new investments remains constrained by available overseas headroom at the AMC level, meaning the actual incremental investment capacity may be limited. The AMC retains the right to re-impose restrictions at any time, introducing some uncertainty for long-term SIP commitments.
