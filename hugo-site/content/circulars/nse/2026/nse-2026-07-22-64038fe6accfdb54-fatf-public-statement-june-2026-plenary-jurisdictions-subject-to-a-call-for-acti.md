## Summary

NSE has forwarded a communication from SEBI regarding the Financial Action Task Force's (FATF) Public Statements issued after its June 2026 Plenary session. These statements identify jurisdictions with strategic anti-money laundering and combating the financing of terrorism (AML/CFT) deficiencies, categorized into two lists: jurisdictions subject to a call for action (high-risk) and jurisdictions under increased monitoring.

## Key Points

- The FATF released updated Public Statements following its June 2026 Plenary meeting.
- Two categories of jurisdictions have been identified: those subject to a call for action (high-risk jurisdictions) and those under increased monitoring.
- Links to both FATF public statements have been provided on the FATF website.
- Members are advised to regularly monitor the SEBI website for updates on this matter.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this circular; it is a periodic informational update reflecting FATF's ongoing assessment of jurisdictions with AML/CFT deficiencies, as communicated by SEBI.

## Compliance Requirements

Members are required to take note of the FATF's updated lists of high-risk and monitored jurisdictions for necessary due diligence and compliance actions, particularly relating to AML/CFT obligations for client accounts and transactions linked to these jurisdictions. Members should regularly check the SEBI website for further updates.

## Important Dates

Circular issued and effective July 22, 2026 (Circular Ref. No. 35/2026).

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal direct market impact as it does not pertain to any specific listed security or trading activity. It primarily serves a compliance and due-diligence function for member brokers regarding AML/CFT risk jurisdictions, requiring no immediate operational changes beyond ongoing monitoring.
