## Summary

NSE has issued its periodic update of the list of approved securities and banks that can be used as non-cash components of liquid assets/collateral by clearing members in the Capital Market (CM) segment. The circular includes multiple annexures covering equity shares eligible as collateral, applicable haircuts (VAR-based), overall permitted quantity limits across segments, and memberwise permitted quantities. This is a routine, recurring update published to keep the approved list current with market conditions and risk parameters.

## Key Points

- The list specifies equity shares that qualify as the non-cash component of liquid assets for clearing members.
- Each security is subject to a VAR-based (Value at Risk) haircut for margin computation purposes.
- A value-based prudential norm applies: exclusion of a pledged security should not result in a margin shortfall of more than 25% at a Clearing Member level.
- The annexures list the Symbol, ISIN, Security Name, Overall Permitted Quantity across all segments, and Memberwise Permitted Quantity for the CM segment for hundreds of securities (e.g., 360ONE, 3M India, Aadhar Housing Finance, Aarti Industries, ABB India, Adani group companies, Ajanta Pharma, Alkem Laboratories, and many others).
- The circular contains seven annexures (Annexure 1 through 7) covering different aspects of approved securities and banks.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory policy changes are introduced; this circular is an operational update refreshing the approved securities list and associated permitted quantities in line with existing NSE risk management norms.

## Compliance Requirements

Clearing members must ensure that collateral submitted as non-cash liquid assets is limited to securities on this approved list, within the specified overall and memberwise permitted quantities, and must monitor haircuts applied to pledged securities to avoid breaching the 25% margin shortfall threshold.

## Important Dates

No specific compliance deadline is mentioned; the revised list takes effect upon publication and supersedes the previous version until the next periodic update.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has low direct market impact as it is an administrative/operational update affecting collateral management practices of clearing members rather than trading activity or corporate actions for specific listed companies. It is relevant primarily to brokers, clearing members, and risk management teams who need to ensure compliant collateral usage.
