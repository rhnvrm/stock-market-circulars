## Summary

NSE notified the listing of privately placed securities on the Debt Market Segment effective June 16, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The circular covers fresh issuances of Commercial Papers (CPs) by 11 companies, with face values of ₹5,00,000 each, allotted on June 15–16, 2026, and maturing between August and September 2026.

## Key Points

- 11 commercial paper issuances admitted to the NSE Debt Market Segment with effect from June 16, 2026
- All securities are Fresh Issues under the Private Placement route
- Face value of each CP: ₹5,00,000; issue prices range from ~₹4,90,821 to ₹4,92,866 (issued at discount)
- Allotment dates: June 15, 2026 (majority) and June 16, 2026 (Kotak Securities, Godrej Agrovet Sr 2)
- Redemption dates range from August 28, 2026 to September 15, 2026 (tenors of approximately 74–92 days)
- Circular also references Other Debt Securities (content truncated in source)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notification under existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance obligations for members or investors. Members trading in the Debt Market Segment should note the designated security codes (ISINs) assigned to each newly listed CP as specified in the Annexure.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing notification with no material impact on equity markets or broader market structure. The listed CPs represent short-term borrowing instruments (74–92 day tenors) by well-established corporates. The largest issuance by quantity is Reliance Retail Ventures Ltd (67,000 units at ₹5 lakh face value = ₹3,350 crore notional), followed by ICICI Securities (19,000 units = ₹950 crore). Tata Steel and Kotak Securities also have sizable issuances. These are standard money-market instruments and their listing on the NSE Debt Market Segment provides secondary market liquidity. No action required by general investors or exchange members beyond noting the new ISINs for trading purposes.
