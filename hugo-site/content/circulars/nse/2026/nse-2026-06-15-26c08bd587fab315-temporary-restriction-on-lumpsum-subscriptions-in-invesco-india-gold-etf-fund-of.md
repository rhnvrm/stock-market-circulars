## Summary

NSE has implemented a temporary restriction on lumpsum subscriptions in Invesco India Gold ETF Fund of Fund (Gold FoF) on the NSE MF Invest Platform, as directed by Invesco Mutual Fund. Lumpsum purchases and switch-ins into the Gold FoF are now capped at INR 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month at the first holder level. Separately, Invesco India Gold Exchange Traded Fund has also restricted direct subscriptions by large investors (investing more than Rs. 25 crore) with the Fund.

## Key Points

- Lumpsum purchase and switch-in transactions into Invesco India Gold ETF Fund of Fund are capped at INR 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month (first holder level)
- The restriction on the Gold FoF applies to transactions received before the cut-off time of 3:00 PM on June 15, 2026
- For Invesco India Gold Exchange Traded Fund, large investor subscriptions (above Rs. 25 crore) directly with the Fund are not accepted from June 15, 2026
- Subscriptions in creation unit size directly with the Fund by Market Makers are exempt from the Gold ETF restriction
- The restriction is temporary and will remain in effect until further notice
- All other terms and conditions of the Scheme Information Documents (SIDs) and Key Information Memorandums (KIMs) remain unchanged

## Regulatory Changes

Invesco Asset Management (India) Pvt. Ltd. issued a Notice-cum-Addendum dated June 12, 2026, introducing temporary subscription restrictions on two schemes:
1. **Invesco India Gold Exchange Traded Fund**: Large investor direct subscriptions (>Rs. 25 crore) suspended from June 15, 2026.
2. **Invesco India Gold ETF Fund of Fund**: Lumpsum purchases and switch-ins restricted to INR 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month. NSE has implemented corresponding changes on the NSE MF Invest Platform.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must note the revised maximum lumpsum subscription limit of INR 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month for Invesco India Gold ETF Fund of Fund on the NSE MF Invest Platform
- Members should communicate this restriction to their clients investing or planning to invest via lumpsum or switch-in in this scheme
- Transactions exceeding the INR 10 lakh monthly cap will not be processed
- Systematic investment plans (SIPs) are not mentioned and remain unaffected unless separately communicated

## Important Dates

- **June 12, 2026**: Invesco Asset Management issues Notice-cum-Addendum
- **June 15, 2026 (3:00 PM cut-off)**: Restriction on Invesco India Gold ETF Fund of Fund lumpsum purchases/switch-ins takes effect on NSE MF Invest Platform
- **June 15, 2026**: Large investor (>Rs. 25 crore) direct subscriptions in Invesco India Gold Exchange Traded Fund cease to be accepted
- **Until further notice**: Temporary restriction remains in force

## Impact Assessment

This restriction primarily affects retail and HNI investors who make large lumpsum investments in Invesco India Gold ETF Fund of Fund via the NSE MF Invest Platform. Investors seeking to invest more than INR 10 lakh per month via lumpsum routes will need to explore alternative gold investment options. The restriction does not affect SIP or other systematic investment modes (unless separately notified). The broader gold ETF market impact is limited, as the restriction is scheme-specific and temporary. Distributors and advisors on the NSE MF Invest Platform should update their order management processes to flag transactions exceeding the monthly cap.
