## Summary

NSE has issued guidelines (Circular Ref. No. 30/2026) informing trading members that clients whose KYC status is 'On Hold' at KYC Registration Agencies (KRAs) — for uploads made between May 1, 2026 and May 31, 2026 — will not be permitted to trade on the Exchange or square off open positions from June 26, 2026. This applies to both AADHAAR and Non-AADHAAR based Officially Valid Documents (OVDs). The Exchange will flag non-compliant PANs provided by KRAs as 'Not Permitted to Trade'.

## Key Points

- Clients with KYC 'On Hold' status (AADHAAR and Non-AADHAAR OVD) uploaded to KRA during May 1–31, 2026 will be barred from trading and square-off of open positions from June 26, 2026.
- Open positions of such non-compliant clients will naturally expire on the respective contract expiry dates.
- Non-compliant PANs, as provided by KRAs, will be flagged by NSE as 'Not Permitted to Trade' effective June 26, 2026.
- Once a client achieves KRA compliance, they will be permitted to trade on T+1 basis, based on information received from KRA by the Exchange on T Day.
- Trading members must also block debit transactions and suspend/inactivate UCC of deceased investors as per SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/OIAE/OIAE_IAD-1/P/CIR/2023/0000000163 (October 3, 2023); KRAs share demise data daily.
- The list of non-compliant clients is available on ENIT Member Portal under: ENIT > Log > Downloads > `<TM Code>_Non-Validated_Clients_by_KRA`.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued pursuant to:
- SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/MIRSD/FATF/P/CIR/2023/0144 dated August 11, 2023, on KYC KRA Regulations.
- SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/OIAE/OIAE_IAD-1/P/CIR/2023/0000000163 dated October 3, 2023, on centralized mechanism for reporting investor demise through KRAs.
- Previous NSE Circular NSE/ISC/74160 dated May 12, 2026, on simplification of KYC process and rationalisation of Risk Management Framework at KRAs.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must identify clients on the 'Non-Validated Clients by KRA' list from ENIT portal and ensure they are not permitted to place new trades or square off open positions from June 26, 2026.
- Members must ensure that debit transactions are blocked and UCCs are inactivated/closed for deceased investors, using the daily demise data shared by KRAs.
- Members should advise affected clients to comply with KRA validation requirements to restore trading access.
- For queries, members should contact uci@nse.co.in or call Toll Free No. 1800 266 0050 (option 5).

## Important Dates

- **May 1–31, 2026**: Period for KYC uploads to KRA that are subject to this circular if found 'On Hold'.
- **June 26, 2026**: Effective date — non-compliant clients (KYC 'On Hold') will not be permitted to trade or square off open positions.
- **T+1**: Date from which a client who subsequently achieves KRA compliance will be allowed to resume trading.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a high operational impact on trading members who have clients with unvalidated or 'On Hold' KYC status. Members must proactively download and review the non-compliant client list from ENIT before June 26, 2026, and notify affected clients. Failure to act could result in trading disruptions, client complaints, and regulatory scrutiny. The restriction applies broadly across all security segments and includes prevention of square-off of existing open positions, which may expose clients to unhedged risk if their KYC is not resolved before contract expiry.
