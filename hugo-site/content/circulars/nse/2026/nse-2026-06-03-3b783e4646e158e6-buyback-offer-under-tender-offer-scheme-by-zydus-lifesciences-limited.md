## Summary

NSE has activated the web-based bidding platform for Zydus Lifesciences Limited's buyback offer under the Tender Offer scheme. The offer runs from June 4 to June 10, 2026, with an offer price of Rs. 1260 per share for up to 87,30,158 equity shares. All Capital Market segment trading members are eligible to participate using existing IPO bidding platform credentials.

## Key Points

- Zydus Lifesciences Limited (NSE symbol: ZYDUSLIFE, Series: BB) is conducting a fixed-price buyback at Rs. 1260 per share
- Issue size: up to 87,30,158 equity shares (ISIN: INE010B01027)
- Bidding platform: https://eipo.nseindia.com — existing IPO platform user IDs are valid
- Members without access can use the admin terminal (login: Adminxxxxx where xxxxx is Member Code)
- Merchant Banker: ICICI Securities Limited; Registrar: MUFG Intime India Private Limited
- Eight investor categories are eligible: FII, MF, IC, FI, Other QIBs, Bodies Corporate, Individuals, Other Non-Institutional Investors

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. The offer follows the existing Tender Offer Scheme framework per NSE circular NSE/CMTR/52776 dated June 29, 2022.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use existing IPO bidding platform credentials or set up access via the admin terminal before the offer opens
- Members without system access must log in to the admin terminal, create branches and users prior to participation
- Custodial confirmations must be submitted within prescribed timings each day during the issue period

## Important Dates

- **Issue Period:** June 4, 2026 to June 10, 2026
- **Daily Bidding Time:** 9:15 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.
- **Custodial Confirmation (intra-day):** 9:15 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.
- **Custodial Confirmation (issue closure day):** 9:15 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.

## Impact Assessment

This circular is operationally relevant to NSE Capital Market segment trading members whose clients hold ZYDUSLIFE shares and wish to participate in the buyback. The fixed price of Rs. 1260 provides a defined exit opportunity for shareholders. The use of the existing NSE IPO bidding infrastructure minimises onboarding friction. Impact is moderate and limited to the duration of the offer window.
