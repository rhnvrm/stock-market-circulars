## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed debt securities to dealings on its Debt Market Segment effective May 21, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The listing covers fresh issues of commercial papers from five issuers, one fresh debt security from Toyota Financial Services India, and re-issues of commercial papers from ICICI Securities and NABARD.

## Key Points

- Eight issuers in total: five fresh CP issuances, one fresh NCD, and two re-issued CP series
- All commercial papers have a face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit
- All fresh CPs were allotted on May 20, 2026 with redemption on August 19, 2026 (approx. 91 days)
- Toyota Financial Services India issued a 7.61% coupon NCD maturing May 19, 2028 (2-year tenor)
- Re-issued ICICI Securities CPs carry varying redemption dates: June 22, July 10, and August 18, 2026
- NABARD CP series also included in re-issues (details partially available in circular)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine notification under existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations, confirming admission of pre-approved securities to dealings.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements imposed on members. Members may trade these securities under their respective designated security codes and ISINs as listed in the Annexure.

## Impact Assessment

Minimal broader market impact. This is a routine debt market listing circular affecting institutional participants who trade in short-term money market instruments and corporate debt. The commercial papers represent short-term borrowings by well-known financial institutions (Tata Capital Housing Finance, HDFC Securities, Kotak Securities, Axis Securities, Godrej Industries, ICICI Securities) and a development finance institution (NABARD). The Toyota Financial Services NCD provides a 2-year fixed-rate instrument at 7.61%. No impact on equity markets or retail investors.
