## Summary

NSE has notified a revised list of privately issued Corporate Bonds that are eligible for DVP-3 (Delivery versus Payment - 3) settlement on the Institutional Platform of the Debt Segment. The annexure lists the updated set of eligible ISINs across various issuers including REC Limited, National Bank for Financing Infrastructure and Development (NaBFID), LIC Housing Finance, Aditya Birla Capital, Tata Capital Housing Finance, NHPC, Power Finance Corporation (PFC), Indian Railway Finance Corporation (IRFC), Export-Import Bank of India, Power Grid Corporation, ICICI Bank, HDFC Bank, Axis Bank, State Bank of India, NABARD, Nuclear Power Corporation, and Hyderabad Metropolitan Development Authority, among others.

## Key Points

- The circular provides an updated Annexure-I containing instrument names, symbols, ISIN codes, issue dates, maturity dates, coupon details, credit ratings, and issued capital for eligible corporate bonds.
- Instruments span a wide range of maturities, from bonds maturing in 2027 through to 2042 (e.g., Hyderabad Metropolitan Development Authority bond maturing 14-Mar-2042).
- Issuers include public sector financial institutions (REC, PFC, IRFC, NABARD, NaBFID, EXIM Bank), housing finance companies (LIC Housing Finance), banks (ICICI, HDFC, Axis, SBI), infrastructure/power entities (NHPC, Power Grid, Nuclear Power Corporation), and municipal/development authorities (HMDA).
- Credit ratings and coupon types (fixed/floating) are specified per instrument to help participants assess eligibility for DVP-3 settlement.

## Regulatory Changes

This is an operational update revising the existing list of eligible securities rather than introducing a new regulatory framework. It reflects periodic additions/removals of ISINs eligible for DVP-3 settlement on NSE's Institutional Platform of the Debt Segment.

## Compliance Requirements

Market participants (institutional investors, clearing members, custodians) trading or settling privately placed corporate bonds on the Institutional Platform must refer to the updated Annexure-I to confirm which ISINs qualify for DVP-3 settlement before executing trades.

## Important Dates

Specific instrument maturity dates are listed in Annexure-I, ranging from 2027 to 2042. The circular itself takes effect from the date of issue (2026-07-14 as per circular listing).

## Impact Assessment

The update is primarily operational, ensuring that institutional participants have an accurate, current reference for which privately placed corporate bonds are eligible for DVP-3 settlement. It does not directly affect equity markets or listed stock prices but is important for debt market settlement infrastructure, custodians, and institutional investors managing fixed-income portfolios.
