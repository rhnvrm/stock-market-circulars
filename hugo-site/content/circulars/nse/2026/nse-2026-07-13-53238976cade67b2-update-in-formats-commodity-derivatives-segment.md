## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued updated formats for documentation used by Clearing Members in the Commodity Derivatives Segment, specifically the format of Bank Guarantee for Margin Deposit (Fungible) as detailed in Annexure 1(A) and 1(B). The circular provides the revised template that banks approved by NCL must use when issuing bank guarantees on behalf of Clearing Members to meet margin deposit requirements.

## Key Points

- The revised Annexure 1(A) specifies the standard format for a Fungible Bank Guarantee for Margin Deposit to be furnished by an approved bank in favour of NSE Clearing Limited (NCL).
- The guarantee format applies to Clearing Members structured as individual proprietors, partnership firms/LLPs, or companies incorporated under applicable Companies Acts.
- The Bank Guarantee is governed by the terms of a Master Agreement executed between the issuing bank and NCL.
- The liability of the issuing bank under the guarantee is capped at the specified guarantee amount.
- The format requires details such as BG number, date, clearing member identity, registered office address, and the bank guarantee value (in figures and words).

## Regulatory Changes

The circular updates the standardized formats/annexures (1(A) and 1(B)) governing Bank Guarantee documentation for margin deposits in the Commodity Derivatives Segment, ensuring uniformity in the paperwork submitted by approved banks on behalf of Clearing Members.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing Members maintaining margin deposits via bank guarantees must ensure their bank uses the updated Annexure 1(A)/1(B) format going forward.
- Approved banks issuing guarantees to NCL must align documentation with the revised format, including references to the Master Agreement and accurate guarantee value details.
- Existing bank guarantees may need to be reissued or updated to conform to the new format as required by NCL.

## Important Dates

No specific effective date, transition period, or deadline is mentioned in the extracted content; the format appears effective upon issuance of the circular.

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact, procedural/administrative circular primarily affecting the operational and compliance workflows of Clearing Members and their banks in the Commodity Derivatives Segment. It does not affect trading, listing, or market pricing, and has no direct implication for listed stocks or investors.
