## Summary

NSE's Surveillance department has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework effective June 12–15, 2026. Two securities — DCM Limited and Eurotex Industries and Exports Limited — are newly included in ESM Stage I, while Supreme Engineering Limited is escalated from Stage I to Stage II. No securities are being removed from the framework in this update. All affected Stage I securities will be moved from the Rolling Settlement segment (EQ/SM series) to the Trade-for-Trade segment (BE/ST series) effective June 15, 2026.

## Key Points

- DCM Limited (NSE: DCM, ISIN: INE498A01018) added to ESM Stage I w.e.f. June 12, 2026
- Eurotex Industries and Exports Limited (NSE: EUROTEXIND, ISIN: INE022C01012) added to ESM Stage I w.e.f. June 12, 2026
- Supreme Engineering Limited (NSE: SUPREMEENG, ISIN: INE319Z01021) moved from ESM Stage I to Stage II w.e.f. June 12, 2026
- No securities are being excluded from the ESM framework in this circular
- No securities are moving from Stage II back to Stage I
- ESM operates alongside all other prevailing surveillance measures on the exchanges

## Regulatory Changes

Securities newly placed or already under ESM Stage I will be shifted from the Rolling Settlement segment (Series: EQ/SM) to the Trade-for-Trade segment (Series: BE/ST) effective June 15, 2026. Securities in ESM Stage II will be subjected to Trade-for-Trade settlement with a restrictive 2% price band under the Periodic Call Auction mechanism effective June 12, 2026. This circular references and builds upon prior NSE circulars: NSE/SURV/56948, NSE/SURV/57609, NSE/SURV/63361, NSE/SURV/64066, NSE/SURV/64400, and NSE/SURV/69315.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must apply a minimum 100% margin on open positions in ESM-listed securities as on June 12, 2026, effective from June 15, 2026
- Minimum 100% margin must also be collected on all new positions in these securities created from June 15, 2026 onwards
- Members must ensure trading in ESM Stage I securities occurs only in the BE/ST (Trade-for-Trade) series from June 15, 2026
- Trading in ESM Stage II securities (e.g., SUPREMEENG) must comply with the 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction from June 12, 2026
- Members are advised that ESM classification is a surveillance action and should not be represented as adverse action against the company

## Important Dates

- **June 12, 2026**: ESM Stage I inclusions and stage changes take effect; Stage II price band restrictions under Periodic Call Auction begin
- **June 12, 2026**: 100% margin applicable on all open positions as of this date
- **June 15, 2026**: Segment shift from EQ/SM (Rolling Settlement) to BE/ST (Trade-for-Trade) takes effect for newly added Stage I securities (DCM, EUROTEXIND)
- **June 15, 2026**: 100% margin requirement effective for new positions created from this date

## Impact Assessment

The inclusion of DCM Limited and Eurotex Industries in ESM Stage I, combined with the escalation of Supreme Engineering Limited to Stage II, will have a significant liquidity impact on these stocks. The mandatory shift to Trade-for-Trade settlement eliminates netting benefits for traders, increases capital requirements, and typically results in reduced trading volumes and wider bid-ask spreads. The 100% margin requirement substantially increases the cost of holding or creating positions. For SUPREMEENG under Stage II, the 2% price band with Periodic Call Auction further curtails price movement and intraday trading, making the stock effectively very illiquid. Investors holding these securities should be aware of the heightened risk and restricted exit options. The ESM designation is based purely on market surveillance criteria (price/volume anomalies, financials) and does not constitute a regulatory action against the company itself.
