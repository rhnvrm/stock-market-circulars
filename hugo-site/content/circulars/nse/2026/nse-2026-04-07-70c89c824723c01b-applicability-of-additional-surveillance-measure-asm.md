## Summary

NSE has announced the inclusion of three securities under the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LTASM) Stage I framework effective April 08, 2026. A 100% margin requirement will apply on all open positions as of April 09, 2026 and all new positions from April 10, 2026 onwards. No securities were shortlisted for Stage IV, Stage I to Stage II, Stage II to Stage III, or Stage I to Stage IV transitions.

## Key Points

- Three securities added to Long Term ASM Stage I effective April 08, 2026
- 100% margin applicable from April 10, 2026 on all open and new positions
- OMNI (Omnitech Engineering Limited) moved from Short Term ASM (STASM) to Long Term ASM (LTASM) framework
- No securities shortlisted for Stage IV or any inter-stage movements
- ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures
- Shortlisting is purely for market surveillance purposes and not an adverse action against the company

## Regulatory Changes

Securities included under Long Term ASM Stage I are subject to enhanced margin requirements. Securities qualifying under Criteria VII (Stage IV) would be shifted from Rolling Settlement (Series: EQ) to Trade-for-Trade segment (Series: BE) on a T+3 basis, though no securities were shortlisted under this criterion in this circular.

Reference circulars: NSE/SURV/39265, NSE/SURV/45111, NSE/SURV/46557, NSE/SURV/48506, NSE/SURV/52090, NSE/SURV/63362, NSE/SURV/64066.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure 100% margin is collected on all open positions in the listed securities as on April 09, 2026
- 100% margin must be collected on all new positions in these securities from April 10, 2026 onwards
- Members should note the ASM framework applies alongside all other existing surveillance measures

## Important Dates

- **April 07, 2026**: Circular issuance date
- **April 08, 2026**: Effective date for LTASM Stage I inclusion of securities
- **April 09, 2026**: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin
- **April 10, 2026**: 100% margin requirement becomes effective for open and new positions
