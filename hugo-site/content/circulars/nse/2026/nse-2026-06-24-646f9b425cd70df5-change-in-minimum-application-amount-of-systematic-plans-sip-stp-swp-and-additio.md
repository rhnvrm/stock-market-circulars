## Summary

QSIF (offered by Quant Mutual Fund) has issued Notice cum Addendum No. 10/2026, modifying provisions of the Investment Strategy Information Document (ISID), Key Information Memorandum (KIM), and Statement of Additional Information (SAI). The key change is a significant reduction in the minimum Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) amount — from Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 1,000 (with multiples of Re. 1/- thereafter, minimum 6 installments) — along with revisions to STP, SWP, and additional purchase amounts. These changes are effective Thursday, June 25, 2026.

## Key Points

- Minimum SIP amount reduced from **Rs. 10,000/-** to **Rs. 1,000/-** per installment (multiples of Re. 1/-), with a minimum of 6 installments
- Weekly SIP minimum revised from Rs. 10,000/- to Rs. 1,000/- (multiples of Re. 1/-)
- Fortnightly SIP minimum also revised (consistent with the overall reduction)
- Changes apply to SIP, STP (Systematic Transfer Plan), SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan), and additional purchase amounts
- Applies across five QSIF investment strategies listed on the NSE MF Invest Platform

## Regulatory Changes

The following sections of the ISID/KIM/SAI are modified:
- Section 1, Part I — Highlights/Summary of Investment Strategy: Clause XIV (Minimum Application Amount/Switch in)
- Section 1, Part I — Highlights/Summary of Investment Strategy: Clause XXVI (Special Product/Facility available during NFO and on ongoing basis)

The minimum SIP amount is revised as follows:

| Parameter | Existing | Revised |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum SIP Amount | Rs. 10,000/- and multiples of Re. 1/- | Rs. 1,000/- and multiples of Re. 1/- |
| Minimum Installments | 6 | 6 |
| Weekly SIP Minimum | Rs. 10,000/- | Rs. 1,000/- |

## Compliance Requirements

- Distributors, RTAs, and the NSE MF Invest Platform must update their systems to reflect the revised minimum application amounts effective June 25, 2026
- Existing SIP/STP/SWP mandates below the old minimum but at or above the new minimum should be accepted
- Fund documentation (ISID, KIM, SAI) stands updated per this addendum

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** Thursday, June 25, 2026
- **Circular/Addendum Date:** June 24, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The reduction in minimum SIP amount from Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 1,000 lowers the entry barrier for retail investors into QSIF's long-short strategies by 90%, potentially broadening the investor base. The affected strategies are:

1. QSIF Equity Long-Short Fund
2. QSIF Hybrid Long-Short Fund
3. QSIF Equity Ex-Top 100 Long-Short Fund
4. QSIF Active Asset Allocator Long-Short Fund
5. QSIF Sector Rotation Long-Short Fund

This is a positive change for retail investor accessibility and may lead to increased systematic inflows into these strategies. Operational impact is limited to system updates on the NSE MF Invest Platform and intermediary platforms.
