## Summary

NSE has admitted multiple privately placed Commercial Papers (CPs) to dealings on its Debt Market Segment with effect from June 22, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Key Points

- 11+ Commercial Papers from major corporates admitted to NSE Debt Market Segment effective June 22, 2026
- All securities are short-term Commercial Papers with face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Issue prices range from approximately ₹4,90,140 to ₹4,91,933.50 (at a discount to face value)
- Allotment dates: June 18–19, 2026; Redemption dates: September 16–18, 2026 (approximately 90-day tenor)
- Circular Ref. No: 0994/2026, Download Ref No: NSE/CML/74809

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance requirements for market participants beyond standard debt market trading norms. Members are notified of the designated security codes assigned to each instrument as specified in the Annexure.

## Impact Assessment

Low market impact. These are routine short-term debt instrument listings for well-known corporate issuers seeking to raise working capital via the commercial paper route. The largest issuance is Reliance Retail Ventures Limited at 62,000 units (face value ~₹3,100 crore). These listings have no direct bearing on the equity markets or broader regulatory framework. Relevant only to debt market participants and institutional investors tracking CP issuances from these corporates.
