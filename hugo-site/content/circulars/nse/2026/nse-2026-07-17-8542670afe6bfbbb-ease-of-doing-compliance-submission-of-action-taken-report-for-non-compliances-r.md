## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a reminder to all Clearing Members regarding the framework for submission of Action Taken Reports (ATR) for non-compliances reported in their Internal Audit Reports, as originally prescribed via NCL Circular No. NCL/CMPL/74723 dated June 15, 2026. Clearing Members must close all non-compliances identified in the internal audit report within 2 months from the due date of submission of the Internal Audit Report, and for the half year ended March 31, 2026, the ATR submission deadline is July 31, 2026.

## Key Points

- This circular reiterates the framework established under NCL/CMPL/74723 dated June 15, 2026.
- Clearing Members must close all non-compliances reported in the Internal Audit Report within 2 months from the due date of Internal Audit Report submission.
- The Action Taken Report must be certified by the Clearing Member's empaneled Internal Auditor.
- The ATR certification must confirm the status of compliance and cover at least a one-month sample verification period.
- The framework applies to all internal audit reports from the half year ended March 31, 2026 onwards.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change is introduced; this circular reaffirms and reminds members of the existing ATR submission framework introduced under NCL/CMPL/74723.

## Compliance Requirements

All Clearing Members (including those clearing for other Stock-Brokers and/or Custodial Participants) must:
- Submit the Action Taken Report to internalaudit@nsccl.co.in.
- Use the subject line format: "ATR for Half year ended March 31, 2026 <Member Code_Member Name>".
- Ensure the ATR is certified by their empaneled Internal Auditor, confirming compliance status and covering at least one month of sample verification.

## Important Dates

- June 15, 2026: Original circular (NCL/CMPL/74723) prescribing the ATR framework.
- Half year ended March 31, 2026: Applicable audit period.
- July 31, 2026: Due date for submission of Action Taken Report for the half year ended March 31, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine compliance and administrative reminder affecting only NCL Clearing Members. It carries no direct implication for listed securities, trading activity, or broader market participants, but non-compliance could expose affected Clearing Members to regulatory scrutiny or penalties.
