## Summary

NSE has admitted 10 privately placed Commercial Paper (CP) securities to the Debt Market Segment effective May 25, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All securities were allotted on May 22, 2026, with redemption dates ranging from June 2026 to February 2027.

## Key Points

- 10 Commercial Paper (CP) instruments listed on NSE Debt Market Segment effective May 25, 2026
- All CPs allotted on May 22, 2026 via private placement
- Issuers include major corporates: HPCL, ICICI Securities, HDFC Securities, Axis Securities, Tata Communications, Godrej Industries, CEAT, Tata Projects, Skoda Auto Volkswagen India, and HSBC Investdirect Financial Services
- Face value is ₹5,00,000 per unit for all except Tata Communications (₹50,000)
- Largest issuance: HPCL with 90,000 units (short-term CP maturing June 19, 2026)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. Listing is in pursuance of existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No additional compliance requirements for members. Designated security codes are assigned per the Annexure. Members may trade these CPs in the Debt Market Segment using the allotted ISINs from the effective date.

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date (all issuers):** May 22, 2026
- **Effective Listing Date:** May 25, 2026
- **HPCL CP Redemption:** June 19, 2026
- **CEAT CP Redemption:** June 17, 2026
- **ICICI Securities / HDFC Securities / Axis Securities / HSBC Investdirect / Tata Communications / Godrej Industries CP Redemption:** August 21, 2026
- **Tata Projects CP Redemption:** August 20, 2026
- **Skoda Auto Volkswagen India CP Redemption:** February 16, 2027

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing notification with no impact on equity markets. The admission of these CPs provides liquidity and price discovery for institutional investors in the debt segment. The largest issuance by HPCL (90,000 units at ₹5,00,000 face value = ₹450 crore notional) is a short-term instrument maturing in under a month. Overall market impact is minimal and confined to debt market participants.
