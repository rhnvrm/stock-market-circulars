## Summary

NSE has announced the applicability of the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework effective June 23-24, 2026. Eleven securities have been newly shortlisted for ESM Stage I, while two securities are being elevated from Stage I to Stage II. All newly included Stage I securities will be moved from the Rolling Settlement segment (Series: EQ/SM) to the Trade-for-Trade segment (Series: BE/ST) effective June 24, 2026.

## Key Points

- 11 securities newly included in ESM Stage I effective June 24, 2026
- 2 securities (GLFL, RSSOFTWARE) moving from ESM Stage I to Stage II effective June 23, 2026
- Minimum 100% margin required on all open positions as on June 23, 2026 and new positions from June 24, 2026
- All Stage I additions shifted from Rolling Settlement (EQ/SM) to Trade-for-Trade (BE/ST) segment
- Stage II securities placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction effective June 23, 2026
- ESM operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must apply a minimum 100% margin on all open positions in the listed securities as on June 23, 2026
- Minimum 100% margin must be collected on all new positions in these securities from June 24, 2026
- Securities in ESM Stage I (EQ/SM series) must be moved to the Trade-for-Trade segment (BE/ST series)
- Stage II securities must be traded under Periodic Call Auction with a 2% price band
- The ESM framework operates alongside all other surveillance measures imposed by exchanges

## Important Dates

- **June 23, 2026**: ESM Stage I to Stage II migration effective; Stage II Periodic Call Auction with 2% price band effective; 100% margin applicable on open positions
- **June 24, 2026**: New ESM Stage I inclusions effective; shift from EQ/SM to BE/ST segment effective; 100% margin on new positions

## Impact Assessment

The inclusion of 13 securities under ESM (11 new Stage I, 2 escalated to Stage II) imposes significant trading restrictions that will substantially reduce liquidity for these stocks. The mandatory shift to Trade-for-Trade settlement eliminates netting benefits and increases settlement risk for traders. The 100% margin requirement effectively doubles the capital required to hold positions, likely leading to forced unwinding by leveraged participants. Stage II securities face even tighter restrictions with a 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction, severely limiting intraday price discovery. NSE has clarified that ESM classification is purely a surveillance action and should not be construed as adverse action against the concerned companies.
