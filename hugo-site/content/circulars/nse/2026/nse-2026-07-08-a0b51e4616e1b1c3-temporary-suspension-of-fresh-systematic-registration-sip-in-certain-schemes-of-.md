## Summary

NSE has informed members that, based on communication received from PGIM India Mutual Fund, certain designated schemes have been disabled for fresh Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) registrations on the NSE MF Invest Platform with effect from July 9, 2026. This follows PGIM India Asset Management's Notice Cum Addendum dated July 7, 2026, which temporarily suspends fresh subscriptions (including fresh SIP registrations) in select overseas fund-of-fund schemes due to SEBI-mandated overseas investment limits at the mutual fund level.

## Key Points

- The designated schemes affected are PGIM India Global Equity Opportunities Fund of Fund, PGIM India Emerging Markets Equity Fund of Fund, and PGIM India Global Select Real Estate Securities Fund of Fund.
- Fresh SIP registrations in these schemes will not be accepted after the cut-off timing of July 8, 2026.
- Redemptions, Systematic Withdrawal Plans (SWP), Switch-out, and STP-out transactions remain unrestricted.
- Existing SIPs and STPs already registered will continue to remain operational.
- This suspension follows earlier notices dated March 9, 2026, May 15, 2026, June 4, 2026, and June 10, 2026 regarding reopening and revision of subscription limits in these schemes.

## Regulatory Changes

The suspension stems from SEBI's letter (Ref No. SEBI/HO/OW/IMD-II/DoF3/P/25095/2022 dated June 17, 2022), which permits mutual funds to accept subscriptions and invest in overseas funds/securities only up to the headroom available without breaching overseas investment limits set at the mutual fund level as of EOD February 1, 2022. This Notice Cum Addendum forms an integral part of the Scheme Information Documents (SIDs) and Key Information Memorandums (KIMs) of the designated schemes.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must stop accepting fresh SIP registration requests for the designated PGIM India Mutual Fund schemes after the cut-off on July 8, 2026.
- Members should continue processing redemptions, SWPs, switch-outs, and STP-outs as normal.
- Existing systematic transactions (SIP/STP) already registered should not be disrupted.

## Important Dates

- July 7, 2026: PGIM India AMC issues Notice Cum Addendum.
- July 8, 2026: Cut-off timing after which fresh SIP registrations will not be accepted.
- July 9, 2026: Effective date of temporary suspension of fresh subscriptions/SIP registrations in designated schemes.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited and operational, affecting only fresh SIP registrations in three specific overseas fund-of-fund schemes of PGIM India Mutual Fund on the NSE MF Invest Platform. Investors with existing SIPs, and those seeking redemptions or switches, are unaffected. The restriction arises from regulatory headroom constraints on overseas investment limits rather than any scheme performance or governance issue, and is consistent with a series of similar suspensions and reopenings since March 2026.
