## Summary

NSE has notified non-business days for certain DSP Mutual Fund schemes on the NSE MF Invest Platform for June 2026. Subscriptions, redemptions, and switches in the listed schemes will not be processed on June 1, 2026 and June 19, 2026.

## Key Points

- Two non-business days are designated: **June 1, 2026** and **June 19, 2026**
- The affected AMC is **DSP Mutual Fund**
- A wide range of DSP schemes across equity, overseas, and multi-asset categories are impacted
- Transactions on the NSE MF Invest Platform for these schemes will not be accepted on the specified dates
- The circular includes scheme-level ISIN details for each affected scheme

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine operational notification designating non-business days as permitted under NSE MF Invest Platform operating guidelines.

## Compliance Requirements

- Distributors, investment advisers, and investors using the NSE MF Invest Platform should note that transactions in the listed DSP schemes cannot be placed on June 1 and June 19, 2026
- Members and participants of the NSE MF Invest Platform must ensure no orders are submitted for affected schemes on those dates

## Important Dates

- **June 1, 2026**: Non-business day for DSP Value Fund (all variants), DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni Fund of Fund (all variants), DSP Healthcare Fund (all variants), DSP Multi Asset Allocation Fund (all variants), and DSP US Specific Equity Omni Fund of Fund (all variants)
- **June 19, 2026**: Non-business day for DSP Value Fund, DSP World Gold Mining Overseas Equity Omni Fund of Fund, DSP Global Clean Energy Overseas Equity Omni Fund of Fund, DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni Fund of Fund, and DSP Natural Resources and New Energy Fund (all variants)

## Impact Assessment

Impact is limited and operational in nature. Investors and distributors transacting in the listed DSP schemes via the NSE MF Invest Platform should plan transactions around the two non-business days. No impact on trading of other securities or other fund houses. The affected schemes span overseas equity funds of funds, healthcare, multi-asset, and value fund categories under DSP AMC.
