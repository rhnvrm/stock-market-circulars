## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed commercial papers (CPs) from 10 corporate issuers to dealings on its Debt Market Segment with effect from June 19, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All securities were allotted on June 18, 2026.

## Key Points

- 11 commercial paper issuances from 8 distinct companies admitted to NSE Debt Market Segment
- All CPs allotted on June 18, 2026; effective for trading from June 19, 2026
- Face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit across all issuances
- Redemption tenors range from ~3 months (September 2026) to ~12 months (June 2027)
- Total securities across all issuances: approximately 85,200 units

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. Listing is in pursuance of existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance action required. Members dealing in debt market instruments should note the designated security codes (ISINs) assigned to each newly listed CP for trading purposes.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing notification with minimal market impact. The commercial papers span short to medium tenors (3–12 months) and are issued by investment-grade corporates and financial institutions including a government-owned entity (NABARD). Equity market participants are not directly affected. Debt market members should update their systems with the new ISINs and security codes to facilitate secondary market trading in these instruments.
