## Summary

NSE's Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) segment is launching two new gold products — **GOLD10MG99** (999 purity) and **GOLD10MG95** (995 purity) — each representing 10 milligrams of gold. Both products trade Monday through Friday, settle on a T+1 basis, and require compulsory delivery in demat form.

## Key Points

- Two new EGR products introduced: GOLD10MG99 (999 purity) and GOLD10MG95 (995 purity)
- Trading unit is 1 EGR unit = 10 milligrams of gold
- Deposit/withdrawal unit is 100 milligrams
- Maximum order size is 1 kg
- Tick size (minimum price movement) is ₹0.01
- Trading hours: Monday–Friday, 9:00 am to 11:30 pm / 11:55 pm (adjusted for US daylight saving time)
- Settlement is T+1 (one working day from trade date)
- Compulsory delivery in demat form for all open buy and sell positions
- Price band starts at 10% of the previous closing price, with dynamic relaxation in 5% increments
- Margin requirements include VaR Margin, Extreme Loss Margin, Intraday Crystallised Mark-to-Market Margin, and Mark-to-Market Margin

## Regulatory Changes

NSE is expanding its EGR segment by adding 10-milligram gold receipt products in two purity grades. Quality specifications require LBMA Good Delivery Standard approval or other NSE-approved suppliers, with a supplier's quality certificate or any other standard specified by SEBI.

## Compliance Requirements

- All participants trading GOLD10MG99 or GOLD10MG95 must ensure compulsory delivery of all open positions (buy and sell) in demat form on the designated delivery day
- Delivery unit is 1 unit and multiples thereof
- Gold must conform to 999 or 995 purity as applicable, with LBMA Good Delivery Standard or NSE-approved supplier certification

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: June 11, 2026
- Launch/effective date not explicitly stated in the circular; participants should monitor NSE announcements for the trading commencement date

## Impact Assessment

The launch of 10-milligram EGR units lowers the entry barrier for retail and small investors looking to hold gold in demat form, compared to larger-unit gold instruments. The availability of both 999 and 995 purity grades gives participants flexibility aligned with international gold quality standards. The T+1 compulsory delivery model ensures settlement discipline. Market participants in the EGR and commodity segments should update their systems for the new product symbols GOLD10MG99 and GOLD10MG95.
