## Summary

NSE's Listing Department (Circular Ref. No. 0813/2026, Download Ref No: NSE/CML/74315) notifies all members that ten privately placed commercial paper (CP) issues have been admitted to dealings on the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange with effect from May 20, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Key Points

- Ten fresh Commercial Paper issues from nine issuers are admitted to trading on the NSE Debt Market Segment effective May 20, 2026.
- All CPs have a face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit and were allotted on May 19, 2026.
- Maturities range from approximately 90 days (August 2026) to approximately 180 days (November 2026).
- Total securities across all issues: 66,200 units with an aggregate face value of approximately ₹3,310 crore.
- Issue prices reflect market-determined discount rates ranging from approximately 6.7% to 9.1% annualised yield.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. The admissions are made in pursuance of the existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- No new compliance requirements for exchange members beyond normal debt market trading procedures.
- Members may trade the listed CPs using the designated security codes specified in the annexure.
- Standard KYC and debt market segment trading rules apply.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing circular with low market impact. The admission of these commercial papers provides short-term liquidity instruments from well-known financial institutions and corporates (IIFL, ICICI Securities, NABARD, Godrej Industries, Cholamandalam, Aditya Birla Capital, and Motilal Oswal) to debt market participants. ICICI Securities is the largest issuer by quantity (17,000 units across three series), while Aditya Birla Capital has the largest single issue (15,000 units). IIFL Samasta Finance's longer-dated CP (November 2026) carries a noticeably higher discount, reflecting tenor and credit spread. No impact on equity markets or broader compliance obligations is anticipated.
