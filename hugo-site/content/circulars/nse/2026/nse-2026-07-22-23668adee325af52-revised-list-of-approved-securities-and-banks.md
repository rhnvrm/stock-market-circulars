## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has issued a revised list of approved securities and banks applicable to the Currency Derivatives (CD) segment, updating the previous circular (Download Ref No: NCL/CD/74873, dated Jun 25, 2026). The circular provides updated annexures covering equity shares, ETFs, government securities/T-Bills, mutual funds, and empaneled banks that qualify as collateral, along with their respective market-wide limits, member-specific limits, and haircut rates. This circular is effective for the month of August 2026.

## Key Points

- Annexure 1: List of equity shares (non-cash component of liquid assets) with market-wide limits, member-specific limits, and VAR-based haircut rates.
- Annexure 2: List of Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs) (cash component of liquid assets) with applicable limits and haircut rates.
- Annexure 3: List of Sovereign Gold Bonds and GOI Securities (G-Sec)/T-Bills (cash component of liquid assets) with applicable haircut rates.
- Annexure 4: List of open-ended mutual funds acceptable as collateral, with market-wide limits and haircut rates.
- Annexure 5: List of banks empaneled for issuance of Bank Guarantees (BGs) and Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDRs).
- A value-based prudential norm applies: exclusion of a pledged security should not result in a margin shortfall of more than 25% at a Clearing Member level.
- G-Sec/T-Bills carry a shut period of two days; members must release G-Sec/T-Bills at least two days before maturity.
- Members who are also banks must note that G-Sec provided as collateral should not be reckoned for SLR purposes and should not be used for trading.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a periodic (monthly) revision of the approved collateral securities/banks list and associated haircut and limit parameters under NSE Clearing's existing risk management framework for the CD segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing members, custodians, and professional clearing members (PCMs) must update their collateral valuation and margin systems in line with the revised annexures.
- Ensure pledged/collateral security exclusions do not cause margin shortfalls exceeding 25% at the Clearing Member level.
- Release G-Sec/T-Bills at least two days prior to maturity due to the two-day shut period.
- Bank members must ensure G-Sec collateral is not counted toward SLR requirements and is not used for trading purposes.

## Important Dates

- Circular date: Jul 22, 2026
- Effective period: Month of August 2026
- Supersedes/updates circular no. 026/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CD/74873) dated Jun 25, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, recurring operational circular affecting collateral management practices of clearing members, custodians, and PCMs in the Currency Derivatives segment. It does not directly impact listed company stock prices or trading activity but is relevant for risk and collateral management teams to ensure continued compliance with margin and haircut requirements for August 2026.
