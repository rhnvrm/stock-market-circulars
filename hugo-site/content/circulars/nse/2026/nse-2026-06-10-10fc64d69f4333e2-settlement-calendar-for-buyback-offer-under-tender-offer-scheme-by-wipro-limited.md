## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has published the settlement calendar for the Wipro Limited buyback offer under the Tender Offer scheme (Settlement No. 2026071). The trading window runs from June 11 to June 17, 2026, with obligations downloadable on June 23 and final settlement on June 24, 2026.

## Key Points

- Settlement Type X, Settlement No. 2026071 has been assigned for this buyback offer.
- Trade start date: June 11, 2026; Trade end date: June 17, 2026.
- Obligation download date: June 23, 2026; Settlement date: June 24, 2026.
- Demat shares must be tendered exclusively via Early Pay-in of Shares through the depository mechanism under Market Type Buyback.
- Early pay-in of shares on the trade end date must be completed well in advance of 03:30 PM for order acceptance.
- Securities and funds pay-out will occur post receipt of funds pay-in on the Settlement Day.
- A Tender Offer Acceptance Report will be provided to members detailing accepted, partially accepted, and rejected orders with reason codes.
- Applicable Securities Transaction Tax (STT) will be collected on the respective settlement day.
- NCL may notify a revised settlement schedule in case of delays from the RTA or delays in funds pay-in by the buying trading member.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular operationalises the previously announced buyback offer referenced in NCL/CMPT/73996 dated April 30, 2026 and NSE/CMTR/74619 dated June 09, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must tender demat shares only through the Early Pay-in mechanism of the depositories.
- Early pay-in on the trade end date (June 17, 2026) must be submitted before 03:30 PM.
- Members should refer to the scheme document for all other details not covered in this circular.
- Members should review Annexure I (rejection codes 01–20) to understand reasons for partial or non-acceptance of orders, including issues such as signature mismatches, invalid transfer deeds, ineligible shares, demat account name mismatches, and excess shares tendered beyond eligibility.

## Impact Assessment

This circular is operationally significant for members, custodians, and PCMs participating in the Wipro buyback tender offer. It defines the precise settlement timeline and procedural requirements for share tendering. The early pay-in mechanism and STT collection obligations require members to plan their operations accordingly. The provided rejection code list (Annexure I) helps members pre-validate submissions to minimise rejection risk. Overall market impact is limited as this pertains to a specific corporate action for Wipro Limited.
