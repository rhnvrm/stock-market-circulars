## Summary

NSE has notified members that Groww Mutual Fund has changed the names of two of its schemes on the NSE MF Invest platform with immediate effect. The renaming is part of broader amendments made pursuant to SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026 and the SEBI Master Circular for Mutual Funds dated March 20, 2026.

## Key Points

- Groww Dynamic Bond Fund has been renamed to **Groww Dynamic Term Fund**
- Groww Short Duration Fund has been renamed to **Groww Short Term Fund**
- Changes are effective immediately on the NSE MF Invest platform
- The renaming is in line with updated SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026
- A Notice cum Addendum (No. 62/2025-2026) has been issued by Groww Asset Management Ltd. covering additional regulatory amendments across all schemes

## Regulatory Changes

- All references to SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 1996 in SID/KIM/SAI documents now read as SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026
- All references to the SEBI Master Circular dated June 27, 2024 now read as the SEBI Master Circular dated March 20, 2026
- Regulation numbers, clauses, and paragraph references have been updated accordingly
- Scheme type descriptions across multiple Groww schemes updated per Clause 3.8 of the SEBI Master Circular

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members dealing in Groww Mutual Fund schemes on the MF Invest platform should note the updated scheme names
- Scheme Information Documents (SID), Key Information Memorandums (KIM), and Statement of Additional Information (SAI) stand amended with effect from April 01, 2026
- All consequential changes are deemed effective from the Effective Date (April 01, 2026) for applicable schemes

## Important Dates

- **April 01, 2026**: Effective date for SID/KIM/SAI amendments pursuant to SEBI Regulations 2026
- **April 06, 2026**: NSE circular issued; scheme name changes effective immediately on NSE MF Invest platform

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact administrative change. The renaming of the two schemes reflects regulatory terminology alignment under the new SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026 and does not affect the investment mandate, fund management, or investor holdings. Members and investors should update their records to reflect the new scheme names but no action is required with respect to existing investments.
