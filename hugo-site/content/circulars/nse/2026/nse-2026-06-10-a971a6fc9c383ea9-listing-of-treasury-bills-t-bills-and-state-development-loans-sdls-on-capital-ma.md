## Summary

NSE has admitted 10 new government securities — 7 State Development Loans (SDLs) and 3 Government of India Treasury Bills — for trading on the Capital Market segment effective June 12, 2026. All securities carry a uniform lot size of 100 units and are identified by their designated security codes as specified in the Annexure to Circular Ref. No. 0932/2026 (Download Ref: NSE/CML/74644).

## Key Points

- 7 SDLs issued by Jammu & Kashmir, Gujarat (2), Uttar Pradesh (2), Mizoram, and Manipur with coupon rates ranging from 7.63% to 7.82% and maturities between 2037 and 2051
- 3 GOI Treasury Bills introduced: 91-day (matures 10-Sep-2026), 182-day (matures 10-Dec-2026), and 364-day (matures 10-Jun-2027)
- All securities trade in the SG (SDL) or TB (T-Bill) series on the capital market segment
- Uniform lot size of 100 for all 10 securities
- Securities must be identified solely by their designated codes for trading purposes

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. Admission is pursuant to existing Regulation 3.1.1 (admission of securities to dealings) and Regulation 2.5.5 (lot sizes) of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use the designated security codes (symbols) for all trading in these securities
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot size of 100 units per security
- No additional compliance obligations imposed on members beyond standard trading norms

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 10, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 12, 2026 (all 10 securities admitted for trading)
- **SDL Maturity Dates:** June 10, 2037 (GJ) through June 10, 2051 (JK)
- **T-Bill Maturity Dates:** September 10, 2026 (91D), December 10, 2026 (182D), June 10, 2027 (364D)
