## Summary

NSE has updated the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective May 22, 2026. Ten securities are newly added to Stage I, ten are excluded from the framework, and no securities move between Stage I and Stage II in this cycle.

## Key Points

- 10 securities added to ST-ASM Stage I effective May 22, 2026
- 0 securities added to ST-ASM Stage II (Nil)
- 0 securities moved from Stage I to Stage II (Nil)
- 0 securities moved from Stage II to Stage I (Nil)
- 10 securities excluded from the ASM framework effective May 22, 2026
- NOIDATOLL (Noida Toll Bridge Company Limited) excluded from ST-ASM and moved to ESM framework

## Regulatory Changes

The ST-ASM framework is a surveillance mechanism applied to securities exhibiting abnormal price or volume movements. Securities placed under ST-ASM are subject to additional margin requirements and trade-for-trade settlement to curb speculative activity.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must apply revised margin and settlement obligations for newly added securities from May 22, 2026
- Trading in ST-ASM Stage I securities is subject to enhanced surveillance margins
- Members should update their systems to reflect the revised consolidated ASM list

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** May 22, 2026 — all additions and exclusions take effect
