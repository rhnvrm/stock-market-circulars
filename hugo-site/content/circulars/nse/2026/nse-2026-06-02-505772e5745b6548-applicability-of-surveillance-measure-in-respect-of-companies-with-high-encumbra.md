## Summary

NSE has updated its surveillance framework for companies with high promoter share encumbrance under Reg. 28(3) of SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations 2011. Seven securities are being added to the measure with a 35% minimum margin requirement effective June 5, 2026, while five securities are being removed effective June 3, 2026.

## Key Points

- 7 securities added to the high encumbrance surveillance measure, attracting a minimum 35% margin in both Equity and Equity Derivatives segments
- 35% margin applies to all open positions as on June 04, 2026 and all new positions created from June 05, 2026
- 5 securities are eligible for removal from the framework effective June 03, 2026
- This measure operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the exchanges
- Shortlisting is for market surveillance purposes only and should not be construed as adverse action against the company
- Company representations against inclusion must be received by NSE by 5:00 PM on June 03, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued in continuation of NSE/SURV/42507 dated October 24, 2019 and NSE/SURV/44169 dated April 17, 2020. The securities list under the high encumbrance surveillance framework is being updated based on periodic review criteria tied to Reg. 28(3) of SEBI (SAST) Regulations 2011, which governs disclosure of encumbrance of shares by promoters.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members**: Must ensure a minimum 35% margin is collected on equity and equity derivatives positions in the newly added securities from June 05, 2026
- **Margin application**: Applies to all open positions as of end-of-day June 04, 2026 and to all new positions from June 05, 2026 onwards
- **Affected companies**: May submit representations to NSE at surveillance@nse.co.in by 5:00 PM on June 03, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The addition of high-profile names such as **Vedanta Limited (VEDL)** and **Hindustan Zinc Limited (HINDZINC)** — both large-cap companies with significant F&O liquidity — means this circular has substantial market impact. Traders holding long or short positions in these securities across cash and derivatives segments will face elevated margin requirements from June 5, 2026, potentially triggering margin calls and forced position reductions. **Nazara Technologies (NAZARA)** and **Nuvama Wealth Management (NUVAMA)** are actively traded mid-cap names that will similarly see higher margin obligations. The removal of five securities (VENTIVE, HEXT, PATELENG, SAGILITY, BLKASHYAP) partially offsets this by reducing margin burden for holders of those stocks.
