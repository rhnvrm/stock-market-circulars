## Summary

NSE has shortlisted two securities under the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) Stage I effective June 15, 2026. Enhanced margin requirements apply from June 16, 2026. No securities were added to Stage II, and no stage transitions (Stage I to II or II to I) were made in this circular.

## Key Points

- KIRANVYPAR (Kiran Vyapar Limited, ISIN: INE555P01013) added to ST-ASM Stage I
- VADILALIND (Vadilal Industries Limited, ISIN: INE694D01016) added to ST-ASM Stage I
- ST-ASM Stage II list remains nil; no stage transitions this cycle
- Shortlisting is purely a market surveillance action and does not constitute an adverse finding against the company
- ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued under the ST-ASM framework established via prior circulars NSE/SURV/39265 (Oct 27, 2018), NSE/SURV/46557 (Dec 04, 2020), NSE/SURV/52144 (Apr 28, 2022), NSE/SURV/58558 (Sep 25, 2023), and NSE/SURV/64066 (Sep 20, 2024). No new framework changes are introduced; this is a periodic shortlisting update.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Stage I securities (KIRANVYPAR, VADILALIND):** Applicable margin = 50% or existing margin, whichever is higher, subject to a maximum cap of 100%
- Margin applies to all open positions as on June 15, 2026 and new positions created from June 16, 2026
- Members must ensure adequate margin collection from clients holding or creating positions in shortlisted securities
- On exit from the framework, price band reverts to the pre-ASM applicable band unless the scrip is under another surveillance measure

## Important Dates

- **June 12, 2026:** Circular issued
- **June 15, 2026:** Shortlisting effective date for ST-ASM Stage I inclusion
- **June 16, 2026:** Enhanced margin requirements become applicable (50% min for Stage I, 100% min for Stage II)

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding positions in KIRANVYPAR or VADILALIND face significantly higher margin requirements starting June 16, 2026. The minimum 50% margin under Stage I may force position reductions for under-margined accounts. Stage II (100% margin) has no new entrants this cycle, limiting broader market disruption. The measure is surveillance-driven and does not signal any regulatory or legal action against the companies. Market participants should monitor subsequent circulars for potential stage escalations or exits.
