## Summary

Bank of India Investment Managers Private Limited (AMC) and Bank of India Trustee Services Private Limited (Trustees) have decided to resume subscriptions, switches, and pre-registered investments in Bank of India Credit Risk Fund effective May 25, 2026. This lifts the restriction on new subscriptions that has been in place since June 28, 2019.

## Key Points

- Subscriptions in Bank of India Credit Risk Fund are being resumed after nearly seven years of restriction.
- All modes of investment — including switches and pre-registered investments (e.g., SIPs) — will be accepted from May 25, 2026.
- The scheme is an open-ended debt fund predominantly investing in AA and below-rated corporate bonds (excluding AA+ rated bonds), carrying moderate interest rate risk and relatively high credit risk.
- The original restriction was imposed via Notice-cum-Addendum dated June 28, 2019.
- This is Notice-cum-Addendum No. 09/2026-27 to the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM).
- All other terms and conditions of the SID and KIM remain unchanged.

## Regulatory Changes

The June 28, 2019 restriction on all subscriptions, switch-in applications, and pre-registered investments in Bank of India Credit Risk Fund (formerly BOI AXA Credit Risk Fund) is being lifted with effect from May 25, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- Distributors and investment advisers operating on the NSE MF Invest Platform should update their systems to accept subscription requests in this scheme from the effective date.
- RTAs and platform operators must enable subscription, switch-in, and SIP processing for Bank of India Credit Risk Fund from May 25, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **May 15, 2026**: Date of Notice-cum-Addendum issued by AMC.
- **May 25, 2026**: Effective date — subscriptions, switches, and pre-registered investments resume.
- **June 28, 2019**: Original restriction date (reference).

## Impact Assessment

This resumption reopens investment access to Bank of India Credit Risk Fund for retail and institutional investors on the NSE MF Invest Platform. Given the scheme's high credit risk profile (AA and below rated corporate bonds), investors should carefully review the SID and KIM before investing. The reopening may attract inflows but the credit risk nature of the fund warrants caution. Impact is limited to participants in this specific scheme and does not affect broader market operations.
