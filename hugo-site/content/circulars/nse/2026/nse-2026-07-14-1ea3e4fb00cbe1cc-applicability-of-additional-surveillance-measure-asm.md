## Summary

NSE has notified changes to the Long-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) Framework, effective from July 15, 2026. New securities have been shortlisted into Stage I, two securities move from Stage I to Stage II, and one security moves from Stage III to Stage IV. No securities have been excluded from the ASM framework in this cycle.

## Key Points

- New entrants to Long-Term ASM Stage I: SAB Events & Governance Now Media Limited (SABEVENTS) and SKM Egg Products Export (India) Limited (SKMEGGPROD).
- No securities shortlisted into Stage IV in this cycle.
- Securities moving from Stage I to Stage II: Country Condo's Limited (COUNCODOS) and Shardul Securities Limited (SHARDUL).
- No movements from Stage II to Stage III, Stage I to Stage IV, or Stage II to Stage IV.
- Sigma Advanced Systems Limited (SIGMAADV) moves from Stage III to Stage IV; under Criteria VII, Stage IV scrips shift from Rolling Settlement (EQ series) to Trade for Trade (BE series) on a T+3 basis.
- No movements from Stage IV to Stage III, Stage III to Stage II, or Stage II to Stage I.
- No securities excluded from the ASM Framework (Annexure II) in this cycle.
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the ASM Framework, with their respective stage, is provided as an annexure.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular applies routine periodic revisions under NSE's existing Long-Term ASM Framework criteria; no new regulatory criteria are introduced.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members and brokers must apply the applicable ASM stage-wise trading restrictions (e.g., higher margins, price band monitoring) to the newly added and reclassified securities from the effective date. For SIGMAADV, members must ensure the transition from Rolling Settlement (EQ) to Trade for Trade (BE) settlement is reflected in trading systems within the T+3 window.

## Important Dates

- Effective date for all stage changes and shortlisting: July 15, 2026.
- SIGMAADV settlement type change to Trade for Trade (BE series): T+3 basis from date of circular issuance.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to the specific securities named (SABEVENTS, SKMEGGPROD, COUNCODOS, SHARDUL, SIGMAADV), which will face increased surveillance, higher margin requirements, and in SIGMAADV's case, a shift to trade-for-trade settlement, likely reducing liquidity and increasing volatility for that stock. No broader market impact is expected.
