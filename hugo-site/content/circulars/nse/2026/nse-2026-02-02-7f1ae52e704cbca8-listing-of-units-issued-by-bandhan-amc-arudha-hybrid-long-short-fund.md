## Summary

NSE has admitted units of Bandhan AMC Limited's Arudha Hybrid Long-Short Fund for trading on the Capital Market segment effective February 03, 2026. Four plan options are listed with designated symbols and ISINs: Regular Plan (Growth and Monthly IDCW) and Direct Plan (Growth and Half-yearly IDCW). Total units allotted across all plans exceed 38 million units with face value and issue price of Rs. 10/- per unit.

## Key Points

- Four plan variants listed: ARSIFHRG (Regular-Growth), ARSIFHRMD (Regular-Monthly IDCW), ARSIFHDG (Direct-Growth), ARSIFHRHD (Direct-Half yearly IDCW)
- Total units: 4,299,785 (Regular Growth), 99,995 (Regular Monthly IDCW), 33,868,656 (Direct Growth), 99,995 (Direct Half-yearly IDCW)
- Face value and paid-up value: Rs. 10/- per unit
- Issue price: Rs. 10/- per unit
- Market lot: 1 unit
- Allotment date: January 28, 2026
- Series: SF (Scheme Fund)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This circular implements existing regulations under NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A (Regulation 3.1.1 for admission and Regulation 2.5.5 for lot sizes).

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must note the new security codes for trading purposes
- Securities shall be identified only by designated codes (ARSIFHRG, ARSIFHRMD, ARSIFHDG, ARSIFHRHD)
- Trading must be conducted in specified lot sizes (Market lot: 1)
- Trustee: Bandhan Mutual Fund Trustee Limited, Mumbai
- AMC: Bandhan AMC Limited, Mumbai
- Registrar: Computer Age Management Services Limited, Chennai

## Important Dates

- Circular date: February 02, 2026
- Allotment date: January 28, 2026
- Listing effective date: February 03, 2026
- Circular effective date: February 03, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Low market impact. This is a standard mutual fund unit listing on NSE's Capital Market segment. The listing provides investors access to Bandhan's hybrid long-short fund strategy through exchange trading. No direct impact on equity securities or existing member operations. The fund offers multiple plan options (Regular/Direct) with different IDCW (Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal) frequencies to suit varying investor preferences.
