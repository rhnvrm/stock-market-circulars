## Summary

NSE has notified that five securities have met the criteria for inclusion under Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) Stage I, effective July 10, 2026. No securities were shortlisted for ST-ASM Stage II or for movement between stages in this cycle. This circular is issued in continuation of earlier ASM framework circulars (NSE/SURV/39265, NSE/SURV/46557, NSE/SURV/52144, NSE/SURV/58558 and NSE/SURV/64066).

## Key Points

- Five securities placed under ST-ASM Stage I: FORCAS (Forcas Studio Limited), FRESHARA (Freshara Agro Exports Limited), TARSONS (Tarsons Products Limited), TEAMTECH (Teamtech Formwork Solutions Limited), and ZSARACOM (Saraswati Commercial India Limited)
- No securities added to ST-ASM Stage II this cycle
- No securities shortlisted to move from Stage I to Stage II or from Stage II to Stage I
- ASM shortlisting is based purely on market surveillance parameters and is not to be construed as an adverse action against the company
- ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures

## Regulatory Changes

No change to the ASM framework itself; this circular applies the existing Short-Term ASM Stage I criteria to newly identified securities under NSE's ongoing surveillance process.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable margin rate of 50% or the existing margin (whichever is higher, capped at 100%) on all open and new positions in the identified securities from July 10, 2026
- Members should account for margin impact on existing client positions in the affected securities as on July 9, 2026
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- July 9, 2026: Reference date for open positions subject to the revised margin
- July 10, 2026: Effective date for the 50% (or higher) margin requirement on FORCAS, FRESHARA, TARSONS, TEAMTECH and ZSARACOM

## Impact Assessment

The increased margin requirement (50% or existing margin, whichever is higher) will raise the cost of trading and holding positions in the five affected securities, likely dampening speculative activity and volume in these specific stocks. The impact is contained to these securities and does not represent a systemic market change; however, investors and traders holding positions in FORCAS, FRESHARA, TARSONS, TEAMTECH, or ZSARACOM should account for higher margin outlays from July 10, 2026.
