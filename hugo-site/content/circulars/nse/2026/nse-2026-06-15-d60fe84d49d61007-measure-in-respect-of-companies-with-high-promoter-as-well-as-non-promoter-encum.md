## Summary

NSE has issued a surveillance circular under its framework for companies with high promoter and non-promoter encumbrance as per Regulation 28(3) of the SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011. Aqylon Nexus Limited (AQYLON) has been added to the framework effective June 16, 2026, while no securities are being excluded. Seven securities in total are now under the consolidated framework, all of which will attract a minimum 75% margin in the equity and equity derivatives segments from June 18, 2026.

## Key Points

- Aqylon Nexus Limited (AQYLON, INE416A01051) is newly added to the surveillance measure effective June 16, 2026.
- No securities are being removed from the framework in this review.
- All securities under this framework will attract a **minimum 75% margin** in both the Equity and Equity Derivatives segments.
- The 75% margin applies to all open positions as of June 17, 2026, and all new positions created from June 18, 2026 onwards.
- This measure is in continuation of Exchange circular NSE/SURV/51189 dated January 31, 2022.
- Shortlisting under this measure is not to be construed as an adverse action against any company.

## Regulatory Changes

The framework is governed by Regulation 28(3) of the SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011, which mandates disclosure of encumbrance on promoter shareholding. NSE activates enhanced surveillance (75% margin) on securities where both promoter and non-promoter encumbrance levels are high, as determined through periodic review.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members** must ensure that clients holding or taking positions in the listed securities maintain a minimum 75% margin in the equity and equity derivatives segments.
- Members should apply the enhanced margin to all open positions as of June 17, 2026 (end of day), and all new positions from June 18, 2026.
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in.

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding positions in any of the seven affected securities — AQYLON, BEDMUTHA, BLKASHYAP, JAYNECOIND, RKEC, STEELXIND, and THYROCARE — will face substantially higher margin requirements (75%) in both cash equity and equity derivatives segments. This significantly increases the cost of carrying or building positions and may lead to forced position squaring for undercapitalised accounts. The addition of AQYLON is the only change in this cycle; the other six securities remain under ongoing surveillance. This measure operates alongside all other prevailing exchange-level surveillance mechanisms and is subject to periodic review.
