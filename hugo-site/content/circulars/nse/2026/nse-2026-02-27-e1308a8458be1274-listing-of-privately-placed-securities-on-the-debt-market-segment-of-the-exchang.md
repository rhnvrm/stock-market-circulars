## Summary

NSE has admitted multiple privately placed Commercial Papers (CPs) to dealings on its Debt Market Segment effective February 27, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The issuances span short-to-medium tenors (approximately 1 to 12 months) from a mix of large corporates and financial institutions.

## Key Points

- Circular Ref: NSE/CML/73052 | Circular No: 0346/2026 | Department: Listing
- All securities are Commercial Papers (CPs) admitted under private placement
- Effective date of dealings: February 27, 2026
- Face value for all CPs: ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Tenors range from ~1 month (Nuvoco Vistas, redemption 30-Mar-2026) to ~12 months (Aditya Birla Capital, redemption 26-Feb-2027)
- Total units across disclosed issuances: 44,000+ units (content truncated in source)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard operational listing notification issued under Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- No new compliance obligations for members
- Members dealing in these CPs must use the designated security codes as specified in the Annexure
- Standard debt market trading rules and settlement procedures apply

## Important Dates

- **Effective date of dealings:** February 27, 2026
- **Earliest redemption:** March 30, 2026 (Nuvoco Vistas CP)
- **Latest redemption (disclosed):** February 26, 2027 (Aditya Birla Capital CP Sr 175)
- **Allotment dates:** February 26–27, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing circular with no impact on equity markets. The listings represent short-to-medium term money market instruments from established corporates and financial institutions. Debt market participants and institutional investors in CPs should note the new ISINs and security codes for trading purposes. The aggregate issuance size is substantial (estimated ₹180+ crore across disclosed securities at issue price), reflecting active CP market activity. No systemic or regulatory impact on the broader market.
