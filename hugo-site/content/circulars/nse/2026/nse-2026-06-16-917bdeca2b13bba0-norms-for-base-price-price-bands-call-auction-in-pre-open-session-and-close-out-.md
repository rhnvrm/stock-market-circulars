## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (formerly NSCCL) issued circular NCL/CMPT/74712 on June 16, 2026, forwarding SEBI circular HO/47/11/11(1)2026-MRD-POD3/I/13804/2026 dated June 15, 2026. The circular revises the framework governing base price determination, price bands, call auction in pre-open session, and close-out procedures for Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs) across Equity, Debt, and Commodity segments.

## Key Points

- Base price for ETFs changes from T-2 day NAV to T-1 day Closing Price (last 30 minutes VWAP)
- If no trading in last 30 minutes of T-1 day, the Last Traded Price (LTP) of the day is used as base price
- If no trade at all on T-1 day, the latest available closing NAV is used as base price
- Base price to be adjusted for corporate actions
- Fixed ±20% price band for Equity and Debt ETFs is being replaced with dynamic price bands aligned to underlying asset price range
- Overnight ETFs currently carry a +5% price band (existing regime)
- Stock Exchanges and AMCs must jointly implement T-1 day closing NAV as base price by April 1, 2027
- Changes arise from SMAC recommendations, public consultation, and stakeholder discussions

## Regulatory Changes

The existing fixed price band of +20% (based on T-2 NAV) for most ETFs is being replaced. The new framework:

1. **Base Price**: Shifts to T-1 day Closing Price (last 30-minute VWAP), with fallbacks to LTP and then latest available NAV.
2. **Price Bands**: Dynamic price bands to be introduced for Equity ETFs and Debt ETFs (excluding Overnight and Liquid ETFs), replacing the fixed 20% band, to better align with the price range of the underlying assets.
3. **Call Auction (Pre-Open Session)**: Updated norms for call auction mechanism in the pre-open session for ETFs.
4. **Close-Out Procedure**: Revised close-out mark-up procedures for ETFs.
5. **Future Migration**: Full migration to T-1 closing NAV as base price mandated by April 1, 2027.

This circular is issued pursuant to SEBI Master Circular for Stock Exchanges and Clearing Corporations dated December 30, 2024 (Paragraphs 2.3.1 and 2.5 of Chapter 1).

## Compliance Requirements

- **All Members of NSE Clearing Limited**: Take note of revised ETF trading norms and update internal systems accordingly.
- **Stock Exchanges**: Implement new base price and price band computation methodology for ETFs.
- **Asset Management Companies (AMCs)**: Collaborate with Stock Exchanges to address operational challenges for implementing T-1 closing NAV as base price by April 1, 2027.
- **All Recognized Clearing Corporations**: Adhere to revised close-out procedures and call auction norms for ETFs.

## Important Dates

- **June 15, 2026**: SEBI circular HO/47/11/11(1)2026-MRD-POD3/I/13804/2026 issued.
- **June 16, 2026**: NSE Clearing circular NCL/CMPT/74712 issued forwarding SEBI directive to all members.
- **April 1, 2027**: Deadline for Stock Exchanges and AMCs to implement T-1 day closing NAV as the base price for ETFs.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has **high** market and operational impact:

- **Price Discovery**: Shifting from T-2 NAV to T-1 VWAP reduces the lag in base price by one trading day, enabling more accurate reflection of current market conditions.
- **Price Band Accuracy**: Dynamic price bands better track the volatility of underlying assets, reducing instances of artificial limit-hits that can disrupt ETF liquidity.
- **Operational Burden**: AMCs and exchanges must invest in system changes to support T-1 NAV-based base price by April 2027, posing a moderate operational challenge.
- **Market Participants**: Arbitrageurs, market makers, and institutional ETF traders will need to recalibrate hedging and pricing models.
- **Retail Investors**: Improved price band alignment with underlying NAV reduces risk of ETFs trading at anomalous premiums/discounts during volatile sessions.
- **Segments Affected**: Equity ETFs, Debt ETFs (excluding Overnight and Liquid ETFs) — with Overnight ETFs retaining their existing 5% band under the interim regime.
