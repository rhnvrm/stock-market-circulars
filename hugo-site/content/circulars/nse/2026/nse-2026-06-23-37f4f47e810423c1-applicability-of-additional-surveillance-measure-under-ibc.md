## Summary

NSE has issued Circular No. 468/2026 (Download Ref No: NSE/SURV/74831) dated June 23, 2026, updating the list of securities under the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) for companies as per the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). The changes take effect from June 24, 2026.

## Key Points

- Aksh Optifibre Limited (AKSHOPTFBR, ISIN: INE523B01011) has been newly shortlisted under ASM IBC for "Receipt of corporate announcement / disclosure from the company" w.e.f. June 24, 2026.
- No securities have been added to ASM IBC Stage I, or moved from Stage I to Stage II, or from Stage II to Stage I in this update.
- No securities have been excluded from ASM IBC in Annexure II.
- The ASM IBC framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the exchanges.
- NSE clarifies that shortlisting under ASM IBC is purely for market surveillance purposes and should not be construed as an adverse action against the concerned company.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued with reference to prior circulars:
- NSE/SURV/37979 dated June 08, 2018
- NSE/SURV/52368 dated May 20, 2022
- NSE/SURV/55582 dated February 10, 2023
- NSE/SURV/70074 dated September 05, 2025

No structural changes to the ASM IBC framework are introduced; this is a periodic update to the list of shortlisted securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must take note of the updated ASM IBC lists provided in Annexure I and Annexure II.
- Members must apply applicable surveillance conditions to securities shortlisted under ASM IBC as per the existing framework.
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 23, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 24, 2026 (for all additions and exclusions listed in Annexure I and Annexure II)

## Impact Assessment

The immediate impact is confined to Aksh Optifibre Limited (AKSHOPTFBR), which faces heightened surveillance following a corporate announcement or disclosure related to IBC proceedings. The consolidated ASM IBC list continues to carry Aban Offshore Limited (ABAN), AGS Transact Technologies Limited (AGSTRA), CLC Industries Limited (CLCIND), Gensol Engineering Limited (GENSOL), and at least one additional security (IMPEXFERRO, data truncated) at Stage I. No securities are being escalated to Stage II or removed from the framework this cycle, indicating a relatively stable surveillance posture. Market participants trading in these securities should be aware of the additional surveillance conditions applicable under the ASM IBC framework.
