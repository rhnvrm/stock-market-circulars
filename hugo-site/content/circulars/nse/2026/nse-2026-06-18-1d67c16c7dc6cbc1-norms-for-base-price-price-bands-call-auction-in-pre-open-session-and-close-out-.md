## Summary

SEBI has issued a circular (Ref. No: HO/47/11/11(1)2026-MRD-POD3/I/13804/2026, dated June 15, 2026) revising the norms for base price determination, price bands, call auction in pre-open sessions, and close-out procedures for Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs). NSE has forwarded this circular to all its members via Circular Ref. No. 86/2026 (Download Ref No: NSE/CMTR/74767). The changes address the lag issue in the current T-2 NAV-based base price and the mismatch between ETF price bands and the price range of underlying assets.

## Key Points

- The base price for ETFs will change from T-2 day NAV to T-1 day Closing Price (last 30 minutes VWAP) immediately.
- If no trading occurred in the last 30 minutes of T-1, the Last Traded Price (LTP) is used; if no trade on T-1 at all, the latest available closing NAV is used.
- Base price will be adjusted for corporate actions.
- Stock Exchanges and AMCs must jointly implement T-1 closing NAV as base price effective April 1, 2027.
- Revised price bands are introduced for Equity ETFs, Debt ETFs (excluding Overnight and Liquid ETFs), Overnight ETFs, Liquid ETFs, and Commodity ETFs.
- New call auction norms for pre-open sessions apply to ETFs.
- Close-out mark-up procedures for ETFs have been revised.
- The circular follows recommendations from the Working Group of Stock Exchanges, Secondary Market Advisory Committee (SMAC), public consultation, and stakeholder discussions.

## Regulatory Changes

- **Base Price**: Shifts from T-2 day NAV to T-1 day closing price (last 30 minutes VWAP). AMCs and exchanges must migrate to T-1 closing NAV by April 1, 2027.
- **Price Bands**: Fixed +20% price band (and +5% for Overnight ETFs) replaced with a revised framework differentiated by ETF type (Equity, Debt, Overnight, Liquid, Commodity ETFs), designed to be more commensurate with the price range of underlying assets.
- **Call Auction**: New norms prescribed for call auction in pre-open sessions for ETFs.
- **Close-out Procedure**: Revised close-out mark-up norms for ETFs.
- References SEBI Master Circular for Stock Exchanges and Clearing Corporations dated December 30, 2024 (Paragraphs 2.3.1 and 2.5 of Chapter 1).

## Compliance Requirements

- **All Recognized Stock Exchanges**: Must update ETF base price calculation and price band systems to reflect T-1 VWAP; work jointly with AMCs to implement T-1 closing NAV base price by April 1, 2027.
- **All Recognized Clearing Corporations**: Must adopt revised close-out mark-up procedures.
- **All Asset Management Companies (AMCs) of Mutual Funds**: Must collaborate with stock exchanges to address operational challenges for T-1 NAV base price implementation by April 1, 2027.
- **Association of Mutual Funds in India (AMFI)**: To note and disseminate.
- **NSE Members**: To note the revised norms and comply accordingly.

## Important Dates

- **June 15, 2026**: SEBI circular issued (HO/47/11/11(1)2026-MRD-POD3/I/13804/2026).
- **June 18, 2026**: NSE circular forwarded to members (Circular Ref. No. 86/2026).
- **April 1, 2027**: Deadline for Stock Exchanges and AMCs to implement T-1 closing NAV as base price for ETF price band determination.

## Impact Assessment

This is a high-impact regulatory change affecting all ETFs traded on Indian stock exchanges (equity, debt, and commodity categories). The shift from T-2 NAV to T-1 VWAP reduces the base price lag by one day, making ETF price bands more responsive to actual market movements and better aligned with underlying asset price ranges. The revised price band framework is expected to reduce excessive intraday price deviations while still accommodating legitimate price discovery. AMCs and exchanges face operational work to implement the T-1 closing NAV system by April 2027. ETF investors and arbitrageurs benefit from tighter, more accurate price bands that reflect real-time NAV more closely. The change may also affect ETF liquidity dynamics during market stress events where the old fixed ±20% band provided a wider buffer.
