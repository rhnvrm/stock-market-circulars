## Summary

NSE's Commodity Derivatives department has announced modifications to the Silver Micro Futures contract parameters via Circular Ref. No. 31/2026. The contract will be renamed from 'Silver Micro Futures' to 'Silver 100 grams Futures' with corresponding changes to the trading unit, symbol, and quotation base value, effective from June 1, 2026 for contracts expiring in June 2026 and onwards.

## Key Points

- Contract renamed from 'Silver Micro Futures' to 'Silver 100 grams Futures'
- Trading and delivery unit reduced from 1 Kg to 100 grams
- Symbol changed from SILVERMIC to SILVER100
- Quotation/base value changed from Rs/1Kg to Rs/100 grams
- Changes apply to June 2026 expiry contracts and all subsequent contracts
- Trading members must upload updated co_contract.gz file before trading on June 1, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

The following contract parameters for Silver Micro Futures are being revised:

| Parameter | Existing | Modified |
|---|---|---|
| Trading & Delivery Unit | 1 Kg | 100 Grams |
| Symbol | SILVERMIC | SILVER100 |
| Quotation/Base Value | Rs/1Kg | Rs/100 grams |

These changes are issued under Download Ref No: NSE/COM/74277 by the Commodity Derivatives department.

## Compliance Requirements

- All trading members must note the revised contract parameters for Silver futures
- Members are required to download and upload the latest co_contract.gz file from NSE Extranet (Path: /comtftp/comtcommon) before commencing trading on June 1, 2026
- Members should update their trading systems to reflect the new symbol (SILVER100) and revised lot size (100 grams)
- Contact NSE for queries: Toll Free No. 1800-266-0050 (Option 1) or email msm@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- **May 18, 2026**: Circular issued by NSE
- **June 1, 2026**: Effective date for modified contract parameters; co_contract.gz file must be uploaded before trading begins
- **June 2026 expiry onwards**: All Silver futures contracts will trade under the new parameters (SILVER100, 100 grams unit)

## Impact Assessment

This modification reduces the contract size significantly from 1 Kg to 100 grams, making Silver futures more accessible to a broader range of participants with lower capital requirements per lot. Trading members need to update their back-office and trading systems to accommodate the symbol change from SILVERMIC to SILVER100 and the new pricing basis. Failure to upload the updated contract file before June 1, 2026 may disrupt trading operations. Members with open positions in SILVERMIC contracts approaching the June 2026 expiry should assess the transition impact on their portfolio valuation and margin requirements.
