## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of further issues of equity shares for 13 companies on the Capital Market segment with effect from June 24, 2026. The issuances arise from preferential allotments, ESOP allotments, and conversion of warrants. Each security is assigned a designated code and market lot size as per the annexure.

## Key Points

- 13 companies listed for further issue of equity shares effective June 24, 2026
- Issues include preferential allotments, ESOP grants, and warrant conversions
- All securities carry a market lot size of 1
- Pari passu status confirmed for detailed companies
- Certain allotments carry lock-in periods extending to December 2026 or January 2027/2028
- Issued under NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A, Regulations 3.1.1 and 2.5.5

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework introduced. The circular is issued in pursuance of existing NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A (Regulation 3.1.1 for admissions and Regulation 2.5.5 for lot sizes).

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must identify and trade the newly listed securities only by their designated NSE codes
- Lot sizes as specified in the annexure must be adhered to for all trading in these securities
- Lock-in restrictions must be observed for applicable allotments (e.g., Blue Coast Hotels lock-in until December 30, 2026; Ester Industries tranches locked until January 17, 2027 and January 17, 2028)

## Important Dates

- **June 23, 2026** — Circular issued
- **June 24, 2026** — Effective date for trading of all further issues
- **May 13, 2026** — Date of allotment for Blue Coast Hotels (2,487,100 shares, preferential)
- **June 8, 2026** — Date of allotment for CreditAccess Grameen (15,348 shares, ESOP)
- **May 12, 2026** — Date of allotment for Ester Industries (6,708,851 shares, warrant conversion)
- **December 30, 2026** — Lock-in expiry for Blue Coast Hotels shares
- **January 17, 2027** — Lock-in expiry for Ester Industries tranche (2,911,383 shares)
- **January 17, 2028** — Lock-in expiry for Ester Industries tranche (3,797,468 shares)

## Impact Assessment

This circular increases the freely tradeable share supply for each of the 13 companies from June 24, 2026. For companies with large allotments (e.g., Ester Industries: ~6.7 million shares from warrant conversion), there may be minor dilution pressure. Lock-in conditions mitigate near-term selling risk for preferential and warrant-conversion tranches. ESOP allotments (e.g., CreditAccess Grameen: 15,348 shares) are small relative to outstanding shares and carry negligible market impact. The circular is operational in nature and does not signal any policy change.
