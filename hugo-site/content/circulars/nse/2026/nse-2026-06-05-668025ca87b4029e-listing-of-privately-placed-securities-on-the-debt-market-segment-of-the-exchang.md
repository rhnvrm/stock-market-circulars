## Summary

NSE has admitted multiple privately placed Commercial Papers (CPs) to dealings on the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange with effect from June 05, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All securities were allotted on June 04, 2026.

## Key Points

- Nine Commercial Paper issuances from eight corporates admitted to NSE Debt Market Segment effective June 05, 2026.
- All CPs were allotted on June 04, 2026 with redemption dates ranging from July 2026 to December 2026.
- Face value for all CPs is ₹5,00,000 per unit; issue prices reflect prevailing money market discount rates.
- Largest issuance by quantity: Export Import Bank of India with 60,000 units (ISEC CP 03/09/26 - 23,800 units next largest).

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification under existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for members. Members may trade these securities on the Debt Market Segment using the designated security codes listed in the Annexure.

## Impact Assessment

Routine debt market operations circular with minimal market impact. These short-term money market instruments (tenors of 5 weeks to 6 months) provide liquidity management tools for issuers. Relevant primarily to debt market participants and institutional investors tracking CP issuances from these corporates. No impact on equity market trading or surveillance obligations.
