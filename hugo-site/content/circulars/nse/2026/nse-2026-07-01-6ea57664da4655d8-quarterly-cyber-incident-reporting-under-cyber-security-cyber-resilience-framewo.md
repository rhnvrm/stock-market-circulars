## Summary

NSE has reminded all Trading Members to submit their Quarterly Cyber Incident Report for the quarter ending June 30, 2026, under the SEBI Cyber Security & Cyber Resilience Framework (CSCRF) for Regulated Entities. The report must be filed through the Member Portal on or before July 15, 2026, following the process outlined in Annexure 1 of the circular.

## Key Points

- References SEBI circular SEBI/HO/ITD-1/ITD_CSC_EXT/P/CIR/2024/113 dated August 20, 2024, and NSE circular NSE/INSP/63502 dated August 21, 2024, on the Cyber Security & Cyber Resilience Framework for SEBI Regulated Entities.
- Trading members must report Cyber Incident(s) for the quarter ending June 30, 2026, via the Member Portal path: ENIT > ENIT-NEW-TRADE > Trade > Incident Report > Quarterly Report Submission.
- The Admin must first assign the "ENIT New Trade" role to a designated officer before submission can occur.
- If no breach occurred, members select "No" and check the "Nil Submission" box before uploading the report; if a breach occurred, members select "Yes", enter incident details, and can save as draft before final submission.
- After completing the form, members must click "Generate PDF", review the downloaded report, and upload it via the "Choose File" button.
- For immediate (non-quarterly) cyber incident reporting, members should refer to NSE circular NSE/INSP/66040 dated January 8, 2025, on the SOP for handling Cyber Security Incidents.
- Non-compliance in periodic submissions is subject to financial disincentives, penalties, and disciplinary action as detailed in Annexure 2 and per NSE circular NSE/INSP/73792 dated April 17, 2026 (Annexure 1.2).
- Queries or support requests can be directed to DL-SYSCYB@nse.co.in.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this circular reiterates and operationalizes the existing SEBI Cyber Security & Cyber Resilience Framework (CSCRF) reporting obligations for the June 2026 quarter.

## Compliance Requirements

All trading members must file the Quarterly Cyber Incident Report (including Nil submissions where applicable) through the Member Portal by the stated deadline, following the step-by-step submission process in Annexure 1. Non-compliant or late submissions may attract financial disincentives, penalties, or disciplinary action as specified in Annexure 2.

## Important Dates

- Reporting period: Quarter ending June 30, 2026
- Submission deadline: On or before July 15, 2026
- Circular date: July 1, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, recurring compliance obligation applicable to all trading members with no direct impact on listed securities or market trading activity. However, failure to comply by the deadline exposes non-complying trading members to financial disincentives and disciplinary action, making timely internal coordination and portal submission operationally important for compliance teams.
