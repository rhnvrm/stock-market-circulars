## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of four privately placed debt securities to dealings on the National Stock Exchange Capital Market segment, effective April 28, 2026. The securities are issued by IndoStar Capital Finance Limited (three series) and Muthoot Finance Limited (one series). All securities are listed under Series N0 with a market lot size of 1.

## Key Points

- Four debt securities admitted to the NSE Capital Market segment effective April 28, 2026
- Three securities are from IndoStar Capital Finance Limited bearing a 9.25% coupon rate, maturing in 2027 and 2028
- One security is from Muthoot Finance Limited bearing an 8.52% coupon rate, maturing in 2031
- All securities are in Series N0 with a market lot size of 1
- Circular issued under Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A
- Issued by Bansri Gosalia, Senior Manager, NSE

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular is issued under existing Regulation 3.1.1 (admission of securities to dealings) and Regulation 2.5.5 (lot size specifications) of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use the designated security codes for trading these instruments on the NSE system
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot sizes (1 unit per lot for all four securities)
- No additional compliance obligations imposed on members beyond standard trading norms

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** April 27, 2026
- **Effective Date:** April 28, 2026 (securities admitted to dealings)
- **IndoStar Capital Finance (925ICFL27A & 925ICFL27) Maturity:** November 22, 2027
- **IndoStar Capital Finance (925ICFL28) Maturity:** January 24, 2028
- **Muthoot Finance (852MFL31) Maturity:** April 23, 2031

The high coupon rates (8.52%–9.25%) reflect the credit profiles of the issuers. IndoStar Capital Finance's three separate ISINs for similar instruments may indicate multiple tranches of the same issuance. Impact is confined to investors in these specific instruments.
