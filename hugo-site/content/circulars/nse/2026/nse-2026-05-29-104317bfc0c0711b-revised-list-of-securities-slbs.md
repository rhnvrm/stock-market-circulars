## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL), under its Securities Lending & Borrowing Scheme (SLBS) department, has issued the revised list of securities eligible for SLBS transactions for the month of June 2026. This circular (Ref. No. 054/2026, Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/74462) is addressed to all Participants and Custodians and is issued with reference to SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/18/2013 dated May 30, 2013 and NSE Clearing's consolidated circular NCL/CMPT/73999 dated April 30, 2026.

## Key Points

- The revised list (Annexure I) specifies all securities available for lending and borrowing transactions under SLBS for June 2026.
- The list contains 176+ securities spanning equities, ETFs, and other eligible instruments.
- The circular supersedes the prior month's eligible securities list in the context of June 2026 transactions.
- Issued by Anil Suvarna, Associate Vice President, NSE Clearing Limited.
- Contact: 18002660050 (IVR Option 2) | support@nsccl.co.in

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes. The circular is issued in compliance with the existing SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/18/2013 (May 30, 2013) governing the Securities Lending and Borrowing Scheme, and updates the approved securities universe on a monthly basis as required.

## Compliance Requirements

- All Participants and Custodians must refer to Annexure I for the updated list of securities eligible for SLBS transactions effective June 2026.
- Participants should ensure that any lending or borrowing transactions in June 2026 are limited to securities on this revised list.
- Securities not appearing on the Annexure I list are not eligible for SLBS transactions during June 2026.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 29, 2026
- **Effective Period:** June 2026 (for SLBS transactions)
- **Reference Circular:** NCL/CMPT/73999 dated April 30, 2026 (prior consolidated circular)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine monthly operational update with low market impact. The eligible securities universe for SLBS in June 2026 includes over 176 symbols covering large-cap equities, mid-cap stocks, ETFs (including banking, auto, sectoral, and index ETFs), and other approved instruments. Participants and custodians should update their internal systems to reflect the June 2026 approved list. No securities are added or removed in a manner that would constitute a significant market event; the circular is procedural in nature.
