## Summary

NSE's Surveillance department has revised price bands for 3,283 securities effective June 05, 2026, under Circular Ref. No. 414/2026 (NSE/SURV/74555). The revision assigns securities to bands ranging from 2% to no band, based on their surveillance classification. A separate Annexure V lists securities whose price bands have been specifically revised.

## Key Points

- **2% band**: 42 securities (Annexure I) — most restrictive category, includes companies under close surveillance
- **5% band**: 565 securities (Annexure II)
- **10% band**: 177 securities (Annexure III)
- **No band**: 211 securities (Annexure IV) — securities with derivative products available; these retain a 10% daily operating range
- **20% band**: 2,288 securities — default band for securities not in Annexures I–IV
- **40% band**: 0 securities
- **Total securities covered**: 3,283
- Price bands in the Limited Physical Market will mirror those in the normal market
- Daily price band review updates will be published on the NSE website
- All close-ended Mutual Funds will have a 10% price band (either way)

## Regulatory Changes

Price bands are revised in pursuance of **Capital Market Segment Regulation Part-A, 2.5**. Securities placed in the 2% and 5% bands are subject to the most restrictive daily movement limits, typically reflecting elevated surveillance concerns. Securities in the "No Band" category that have derivative products available are subject to a 10% daily operating range.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must adhere to the updated price band limits for all affected securities from June 05, 2026
- Orders placed outside the applicable price band will be rejected by the trading system
- Members should review Annexure V for securities with revised price bands and update their trading systems accordingly
- For queries, members may contact: surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: June 04, 2026
- **Effective Date**: June 05, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine but operationally significant surveillance circular. The 42 securities placed under the 2% band and 565 under the 5% band face the most significant trading restrictions, limiting daily price movement and potentially reducing liquidity. Traders and market makers in these securities need to adjust strategies accordingly. The 2,288 securities under the default 20% band are unaffected relative to standard market conditions. Securities in the "No Band" category (typically large-cap F&O stocks) remain subject to a 10% daily operating range via derivative market rules.
