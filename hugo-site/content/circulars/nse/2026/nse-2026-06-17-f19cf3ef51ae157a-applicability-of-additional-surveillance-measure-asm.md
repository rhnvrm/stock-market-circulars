## Summary

NSE has updated the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework effective June 18, 2026. Three securities have been added to the Long-Term ASM (LTASM) Framework Stage I, and four securities have been excluded from ASM (transferred to the Enhanced Surveillance Measure / ESM framework). No securities moved between existing ASM stages.

## Key Points

- Three securities added to Long-Term ASM Stage I effective June 18, 2026.
- MSTCLTD (Mstc Limited) was moved from the Short-Term ASM (STASM) framework to LTASM Stage I.
- Four securities excluded from ASM framework, all moved to ESM framework.
- No stage-to-stage movements (Stage I→II, II→III, etc.) — all such lists are Nil.
- Stage IV securities (criteria VII) are subject to Trade-for-Trade (Series: BE) settlement on a T+3 basis.

## Regulatory Changes

| 2 | ORCHPHARMA | Orchid Pharma Limited | INE191A01027 |
| 3 | UNIVCABLES | Universal Cables Limited | INE279A01012 |

|---------|--------|---------------|------|

*Moved from LTASM to ESM framework.

**Stage Movement Lists:** All stage-to-stage movements (Stage I↔II, II↔III, III↔IV, etc.) are Nil for this update.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants and brokers must update their systems to reflect the revised ASM list effective June 18, 2026.
- Securities under LTASM Stage IV (criteria VII) must be traded under the Trade-for-Trade (BE series) settlement on a T+3 basis from the date of the circular.
- Investors holding the newly added securities should be aware of the additional margin and trading restrictions applicable under LTASM Stage I.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** June 18, 2026 — All additions, exclusions, and stage changes take effect.

## Impact Assessment

The changes affect seven specific securities. Investors and traders in MSTCLTD, ORCHPHARMA, and UNIVCABLES will face enhanced surveillance conditions under LTASM Stage I, which typically involves additional margin requirements and closer monitoring. The four securities excluded from ASM (DSFCL, NAMOEWASTE, OWAIS, TRANSWORLD) are moving to the stricter ESM framework, indicating continued regulatory concern about their trading patterns. No stage escalations or de-escalations occurred among existing LTASM securities this cycle.
