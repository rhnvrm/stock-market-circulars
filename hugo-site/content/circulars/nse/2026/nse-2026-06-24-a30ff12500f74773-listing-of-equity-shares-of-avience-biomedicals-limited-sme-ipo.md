## Summary

NSE has announced the listing of equity shares of Avience Biomedicals Limited under the SME IPO segment. The circular includes the post-issue shareholding pattern as of 23 June 2026, showing a total of 5,485,056 shares held by 630 shareholders. Promoters hold 64.59% of the company with all promoter shares locked in.

## Key Points

- Total shares outstanding: 5,485,056 fully paid-up equity shares
- Total shareholders: 630 (7 promoter group + 623 public)
- Promoter & Promoter Group holding: 3,542,885 shares (64.59%)
- Public holding: 1,942,171 shares (35.41%)
- All shares are held in dematerialized form
- Promoter shares are 100% locked in (3,542,885 shares)
- 898,171 public shares (46.25% of public holding) are in lock-in
- No partly paid shares, convertible securities, warrants, or ESOPs issued
- No pledging or encumbrances on promoter shares
- No significant beneficial owner disclosed

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard listing disclosure filed under Regulation 31(1)(a) of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations for the post-issue shareholding pattern.

## Compliance Requirements

- Avience Biomedicals Limited has submitted the shareholding pattern in the prescribed format per SCRR, 1957 and SEBI LODR Regulations
- The listed entity has declared compliance with all applicable disclosure requirements
- Lock-in restrictions apply to promoter shares and a portion of public shares per SME IPO regulations

## Important Dates

- Post-issue shareholding pattern quarter ending: 23 June 2026
- Listing date: on or around 24 June 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine SME IPO listing with limited broader market impact. The strong promoter lock-in (100% of promoter shares locked) provides some near-term price stability. Public float is approximately 35.41% of total shares, with a significant portion of public shares also subject to lock-in, which may limit liquidity in the initial trading period. Investors should note the relatively small share count and SME segment classification, which typically implies lower liquidity compared to main board listings.
