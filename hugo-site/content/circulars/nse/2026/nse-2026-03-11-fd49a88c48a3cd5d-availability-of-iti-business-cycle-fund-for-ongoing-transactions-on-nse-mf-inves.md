## Summary

NSE has informed its members that ITI Business Cycle Fund of ITI Mutual Fund will be reopened for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions, along with SIP (Systematic Investment Plan), STP (Systematic Transfer Plan), and SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) facilities on the NSE MF Invest Platform, effective March 12, 2026.

## Key Points

- ITI Business Cycle Fund reopens for ongoing transactions on NSE MF Invest Platform from March 12, 2026
- All six plan variants (Regular and Direct, across Growth, IDCW Payout, and IDCW Reinvestment options) are covered
- SIP, STP, and SWP facilities will be available alongside standard subscriptions and redemptions
- All six scheme variants fall under the Equity category

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice regarding the availability of an existing mutual fund scheme on the NSE MF Invest Platform.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance requirements for exchange members. Members are simply informed of the availability of this fund for transaction facilitation on the platform.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** March 12, 2026 — ITI Business Cycle Fund reopens for ongoing subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, STP, and SWP on NSE MF Invest Platform.

## Impact Assessment

Low market impact. This circular affects mutual fund distributors and investors who use the NSE MF Invest Platform and wish to transact in ITI Business Cycle Fund. The reopening provides liquidity and flexibility for investors in all six plan variants of the fund. No broader market or systemic implications.
