## Summary

NSE has notified that GenXAI Analytics Limited (Symbol: GENXAI), which was listed under the Trade for Trade segment (Series: ST) following its SME IPO, will be moved to the Rolling segment (Series: SM) effective June 29, 2026. This follows the prior Exchange Circular NSE/CML/74665 dated June 11, 2026, and is in compliance with SEBI guidelines under CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012.

## Key Points

- GENXAI will transition from Trade for Trade (Series: ST) to Rolling segment (Series: SM)
- Effective date of transfer: June 29, 2026
- This is a follow-up to Exchange Circular NSE/CML/74665 dated June 11, 2026
- The transfer is in accordance with SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular implements the standard post-IPO transition protocol under SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012, which governs the movement of newly listed SME securities from the Trade for Trade segment to the normal Rolling segment after the mandatory lock-in period.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance actions required from members beyond updating their trading systems to reflect the new series (SM) for GENXAI from the effective date. Members should ensure their systems are updated prior to June 29, 2026 to handle trades in the Rolling segment for this security.

## Important Dates

- **June 11, 2026**: Prior Exchange Circular NSE/CML/74665 issued
- **June 12, 2026**: This circular issued (NSE/CML/74674)
- **June 29, 2026**: Effective date of transfer from Trade for Trade (ST) to Rolling segment (SM)

## Impact Assessment

The transition from Trade for Trade to Rolling segment is a positive development for GENXAI shareholders and traders. In the Trade for Trade segment, each trade must result in delivery — netting of positions is not permitted and intraday trading is not allowed. Moving to the Rolling segment enables intraday trading and netting, potentially increasing liquidity and trading activity in the stock. This is a standard post-SME-IPO milestone and signals the stock has completed its mandatory Trade for Trade phase.
