## Summary

NSE will conduct a mock trading session in the Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) segment on Saturday, July 04, 2026, following up on an earlier circular dated May 13, 2026. This mock session covers both the Primary Site and Disaster Recovery (DR) Site trading sessions. No new NEAT+ version release is associated with this mock session, with the current version 1.0.0 remaining applicable.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Saturday, July 04, 2026 in the EGR segment.
- Trading Session-1 from Primary Site: Pre-open 12:45-12:53, Normal Market open 13:00, Block Deal Window 13:45-14:00, Auction Market 13:30-14:15, Normal Market close 16:00.
- Trading Session-2 from DR Site: Pre-open 16:30-16:38, Normal Market open 16:45, Normal Market close 17:30, Trade modification end 17:40.
- Live Re-login window: 18:30 to 19:00.
- No new NEAT+ version release; existing version 1.0.0 available at /egftp/egcommon/NEATPlus100 on NSE Extranet.
- Default password for mock session: "Neat@EG1".
- Trades executed during mock sessions will not result in any fund pay-in or pay-out.
- Members can visit www.nseindia.com for live updates or call the toll-free number 1800-266-0050 (Option 1) for support.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an operational notice continuing from the circular dated May 13, 2026 (Download No. 74203) regarding scheduled mock trading readiness testing.

## Compliance Requirements

Members trading in the EGR segment should ensure their systems are configured per the interactive login parameters (Gateway Router IP 172.19.14.86, Port 11887) and market data broadcast parameters (Multicast IPs 239.50.50.63/239.50.50.68) provided in the circular annexures, and participate in the mock session to validate readiness for both Primary and DR site operations.

## Important Dates

- Mock trading date: Saturday, July 04, 2026
- Live Re-login window: 18:30 - 19:00 hrs on July 04, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market impact as it pertains to a routine mock trading readiness exercise for the EGR segment with no financial settlement implications. It is primarily an operational/technical notice for NSE members to ensure system readiness, including DR site failover testing, ahead of live trading.
