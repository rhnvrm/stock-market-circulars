## Summary

NSE has informed its members that UTI Mutual Fund is revising the minimum investment, additional purchase, SIP, and redemption amounts for the UTI Gold ETF Fund of Fund (FoF) scheme on the NSE MF Invest platform. The changes take effect from May 29, 2026, and substantially lower entry and exit thresholds, making the scheme accessible to a much wider base of retail investors.

## Key Points

- Minimum initial investment reduced from Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 25 (in multiples of Re. 1)
- Minimum SIP amount (daily, weekly, monthly) reduced from Rs. 500 to Rs. 25 (in multiples of Re. 1)
- Minimum quarterly SIP amount remains unchanged at Rs. 1,500
- Subsequent minimum investment under a folio reduced from Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 25 (in multiples of Re. 1)
- Minimum redemption amount reduced from Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 25 (in multiples of Re. 1)
- All other terms and conditions of the scheme remain unchanged

## Regulatory Changes

This addendum (No. 11/2026-27 issued by UTI AMC on May 25, 2026) forms an integral part of the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM) of the UTI Gold ETF Fund of Fund. The SID/KIM should be read in conjunction with this addendum.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members operating on the NSE MF Invest platform must apply the revised minimum thresholds for purchase, additional purchase, SIP, and redemption transactions in the UTI Gold ETF Fund of Fund from May 29, 2026 onwards.
- Investors and distributors should refer to the updated SID/KIM incorporating this addendum.

## Important Dates

- **May 25, 2026**: Addendum issued by UTI Asset Management Company Limited
- **May 27, 2026**: NSE circular issued to members
- **May 29, 2026**: Revised minimum amounts take effect on NSE MF Invest platform

## Impact Assessment

The significant reduction in minimum investment thresholds (from Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 25 for initial purchase, and from Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 25 for redemption and subsequent investments) substantially lowers the barrier to entry for retail and small investors seeking gold exposure through this fund-of-fund structure. This move aligns with broader industry trends toward micro-investing and democratizing access to gold ETF investments. The change is operationally straightforward for platform participants but may increase transaction volumes and new investor onboarding for this scheme.
