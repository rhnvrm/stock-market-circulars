## Summary

NSE's Surveillance department has issued Circular 462/2026 (Ref: NSE/SURV/74808) directing the transfer of 2 securities from the rolling segment to Trade for Trade (TFT) segment effective June 25, 2026. Additionally, 5 securities will continue in the TFT segment under the current fortnightly surveillance review. No netting off will be allowed; all settlement will be on a trade-to-trade basis.

## Key Points

- 2 securities shifted from Rolling (EQ) to Trade for Trade (BE) series effective June 25, 2026: ODIGMA and DAICHI
- No securities shifted from SM to ST series (Nil)
- 4 securities continue in TFT segment (BE/BZ series): SUPREMEENG, EQUIPPP, MORARJEE, TCIFINANCE
- 1 security continues in TFT segment (ST/SZ series): TRIDHYA
- Price band of 5% or lower applies to all affected securities
- Settlement will be on trade-to-trade basis; no netting off permitted
- Action is purely for market surveillance and should not be construed as adverse action against the companies

## Regulatory Changes

Pursuant to Capital Market Segment Trading Regulations Part-A, 2.6, the following series changes take effect June 25, 2026:

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must note that ODIGMA and DAICHI will no longer be available in the rolling segment (EQ series) from June 25, 2026
- Members must exercise adequate precaution while trading in these securities as settlement is on trade-to-trade basis
- No netting off is permitted for any of the listed securities
- Members may refer to criteria for TFT shifts at: https://www.nseindia.com/regulations/movement-securities-periodic-review
- Queries can be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- **June 22, 2026**: Circular issued
- **June 25, 2026 (Thursday)**: Effective date for ODIGMA and DAICHI shifting to Trade for Trade (BE) segment

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding or trading in ODIGMA and DAICHI will face restricted trading conditions from June 25, 2026, with a 5% price band and mandatory trade-to-trade settlement. This eliminates intraday trading possibilities (no netting off), increasing settlement risk and reducing liquidity for these counters. The criteria cited — Price Earnings Multiple, Price Variation & Market Capitalization — suggest these securities exhibited elevated valuation or volatility metrics. The 5 securities continuing in TFT (SUPREMEENG, EQUIPPP, MORARJEE, TCIFINANCE, TRIDHYA) remain under surveillance with existing restrictions maintained.
