## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed commercial papers (CPs) from 10 companies to dealings on its Debt Market Segment effective March 10, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All instruments were allotted on March 9, 2026.

## Key Points

- 10 fresh commercial paper issuances admitted to NSE Debt Market Segment effective March 10, 2026
- All CPs have a face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Tenors range from approximately 44 days (CEAT) to 12 months (Motilal Oswal, Muthoot Finance)
- Circular Ref. No: 0416/2026, Download Ref No: NSE/CML/73223

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. Admission is made under the existing framework of Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. Members are informed of the new security codes as specified in the Annexure for trading purposes.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing circular with minimal market impact. The admission of these commercial papers provides liquidity in the short-term debt market for institutional investors. The issuers are well-known, established financial and industrial companies. Aggregate issuance across all 10 CPs totals approximately 72,500 units at face value of ₹500,000 each, representing roughly ₹36,250 crore in face value. No action is required from equity market participants.
