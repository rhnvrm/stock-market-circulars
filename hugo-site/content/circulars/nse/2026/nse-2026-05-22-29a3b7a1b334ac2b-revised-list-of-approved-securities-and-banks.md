## Summary

NSE has published a revised list of equity shares and banks approved for use as non-cash components of liquid assets for the COMM (Commodity) segment. The circular provides Annexures 1 through 7 detailing eligible securities, applicable haircuts (VAR-based), overall permitted quantities across all segments, and member-wise permitted quantities specific to the COMM segment.

## Key Points

- The applicable haircut for all listed securities is Value at Risk (VAR)-based.
- A value-based prudential norm applies: exclusion of a pledged security must not result in a margin shortfall of more than 25% at a Clearing Member level.
- Each security is listed with its symbol, ISIN, full security name, overall permitted quantity across all segments, and member-wise permitted quantity for the COMM segment.
- Member-wise permitted quantity for the COMM segment is marked as 'N.A' for the listed equity shares, indicating no separate COMM-specific cap beyond the overall limit.
- The list covers a wide range of large and mid-cap equities across diverse sectors.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a periodic revision to the approved securities list. No new regulatory framework is introduced; the update reflects changes in eligible securities and their permitted collateral quantities as determined by NSE.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing Members must ensure that pledged securities used as non-cash collateral are drawn from the updated approved list.
- Clearing Members must adhere to the prudential norm that removing any single pledged security does not cause a margin shortfall exceeding 25% at the member level.
- Members should update their collateral management systems to reflect the revised permitted quantities.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: 2026-05-22 (date of circular issuance; members should apply the revised list immediately upon receipt).

## Impact Assessment

This update directly impacts Clearing Members in the COMM segment who use equity shares as non-cash collateral. Members holding securities that may have been added, removed, or had their permitted quantities revised will need to rebalance their collateral portfolios. The 25% prudential norm safeguard limits concentration risk. Overall market impact is low to medium as this is a routine operational update, but individual members with concentrated collateral positions in affected securities may need to take corrective action promptly.
