## Summary

NSE has admitted 12 privately placed commercial paper (CP) securities from 11 companies to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective June 12, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Key Points

- 12 fresh commercial paper issuances from 11 companies admitted to NSE Debt Market Segment
- All securities are short-term CPs with face value of ₹5,00,000 each
- Most CPs mature on September 10, 2026; one series matures October 15, 2026; one matures September 9, 2026
- Allotment dates range from June 11–12, 2026
- Axis Securities issued two separate CP series (Sr 21 and Sr 22) with different maturities

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This circular is a routine notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations confirming admission of privately placed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for members. Exchange members dealing in debt instruments should update their systems with the newly assigned security codes and ISINs as specified in the Annexure.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** June 12, 2026 (admission to dealings)
- **Allotment Dates:** June 11–12, 2026 (varies by security)
- **Redemption Dates:**
  - September 9, 2026: CEAT Limited CP
  - September 10, 2026: Godrej & Boyce, ICICI Securities, Godrej Properties, NABARD, HDFC Securities, Axis Securities (Sr 21), Tata Capital Housing Finance, Godrej Industries, Aditya Birla Money
  - October 15, 2026: Axis Securities (Sr 22)
