## Summary

The National Stock Exchange has moved LCC INFOTECH LIMITED (Symbol: LCCINFOTEC) to Stage II of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective January 13, 2026. The security will also undergo a series change from EQ to BE. Trading will be restricted to Trade for Trade basis with a 5% price band and buyers must deposit an Additional Surveillance Deposit of 50% of trade value.

## Key Points

- LCC INFOTECH LIMITED (ISIN: INE938A01021) moves to GSM Stage II
- Series change from EQ to BE implemented
- Trade for Trade settlement with no netting off allowed
- 5% or lower price band applicable
- Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value required from buyers
- Changes effective from January 13, 2026
- Members advised to take adequate precautions while trading

## Regulatory Changes

The circular implements Stage II surveillance measures under the GSM framework, which is more stringent than Stage I. The GSM framework has four progressive stages:

- **Stage I**: 100% margin rate with 5% or lower price band
- **Stage II**: Trade for Trade with 5% or lower price band and 50% ASD
- **Stage III**: Trade for Trade with 5% or lower price band, weekly trading only (Mondays), and 100% ASD
- **Stage IV**: Trade for Trade with 5% or lower price band, weekly trading only (Mondays), 100% ASD, and no upward price movement

The series change from EQ (Equity) to BE (Book Entry) indicates heightened surveillance classification.

## Compliance Requirements

- **NSE Members**: Must ensure Trade for Trade settlement for LCCINFOTEC
- **Buyers**: Must deposit Additional Surveillance Deposit of 50% of trade value
- **Settlement**: No netting off allowed; all trades must be settled on trade-to-trade basis
- **Price Band**: Strict adherence to 5% or lower price band
- **Precautionary Measures**: Members must exercise adequate caution while trading in this security

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: January 12, 2026
- **Effective Date**: January 13, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The movement to GSM Stage II will significantly impact trading in LCC INFOTECH LIMITED:

- **Liquidity Impact**: Trade for Trade settlement with no netting off will severely restrict liquidity
- **Capital Requirements**: 50% ASD increases capital requirements for buyers, likely reducing trading volumes
- **Price Discovery**: 5% price band limits price movement and may affect efficient price discovery
- **Market Perception**: Series change to BE and GSM Stage II classification signals regulatory concerns about the security
- **Investor Access**: Heightened restrictions may deter retail participation
- **Settlement Risk**: Trade-to-trade basis eliminates position squaring flexibility
