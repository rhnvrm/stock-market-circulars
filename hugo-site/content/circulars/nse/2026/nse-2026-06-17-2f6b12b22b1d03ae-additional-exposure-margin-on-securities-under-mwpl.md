## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has mandated an additional exposure margin of 15% on 17 securities in the equity derivatives segment. The margin applies where the top 10 clients collectively hold more than 20% of the Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL) for a given security. The framework is based on a 3-month rolling review and will be effective from July 1, 2026.

## Key Points

- Additional exposure margin of **15%** levied in equity derivatives segment on identified securities
- Triggered when top 10 clients account for more than **20% of MWPL** for a security
- Where additional surveillance margin (ASM) is also applicable, the **higher** of the two margins will be levied
- Securities are identified using **3-month rolling data** and reviewed on a **monthly basis**
- Framework reference: NSE circular NSE/INVG/40472 dated March 18, 2019
- 17 securities shortlisted as of this circular (Annexure 1)

## Regulatory Changes

This circular updates the list of securities subject to additional exposure margin under the existing MWPL concentration framework. The 15% additional exposure margin framework itself was established in March 2019; this circular refreshes the applicable securities list effective July 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members / Clearing Members** must ensure additional 15% exposure margin is collected from clients holding F&O positions in the 17 listed securities
- Where a security is simultaneously under ASM, members must levy the **higher** of the two applicable margins
- Members should update their margin systems ahead of the July 1, 2026 effective date

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 17, 2026
- **Effective Date:** July 1, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The additional 15% exposure margin increases the cost of holding F&O positions in 17 high-concentration securities, including large-cap names such as Adani Enterprises, Ambuja Cements, BHEL, SAIL, and Vodafone Idea. Members and clients with significant open interest in these contracts will face higher margin requirements from July 1, 2026. The monthly review mechanism means the list may shrink or expand in subsequent months depending on concentration patterns. The overlap clause with ASM ensures no double-counting of margin obligations where surveillance margins already apply.
