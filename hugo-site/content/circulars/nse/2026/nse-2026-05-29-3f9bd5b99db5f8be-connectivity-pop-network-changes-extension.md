## Summary

NSE has extended the migration deadline for self-service connectivity links at the existing Sify POP at Kohinoor-Kurla, Mumbai. Members using self-service links must complete migration to the new Sify PARLE POP by end of day June 8, 2026, after which the old POP will be discontinued by the Telecom Network Integrator (TNI).

## Key Points

- Migration deadline for self-service links at Kohinoor-Kurla (Mumbai) Sify POP extended to June 8, 2026.
- Continuation of NSE/MSD/74250 dated May 15, 2026; original discontinuation date was May 29, 2026.
- Only self-service links are covered by this extension; managed links were handled separately.
- TNI (Sify Technologies Limited) has confirmed substantial migration progress for self-service subscribers.
- Members failing to migrate by the revised deadline risk loss of connectivity to the Exchange for trading.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes. This is an operational extension of a previously announced migration timeline for POP infrastructure managed by Sify Technologies Limited as NSE's appointed Telecom Network Integrator.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members subscribed to self-service links at the Kohinoor-Kurla, Mumbai Sify POP must complete migration to the new PARLE POP by EOD June 8, 2026.
- For queries, members must contact the TNI (Sify) using details in Annexure 1 – Sify Handbook.
- Members are solely responsible for their network architecture, assets, and connectivity; NSEIL acts only as a facilitator.

## Important Dates

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Original POP migration announcement | August 5, 2025 |
| Prior migration deadline | May 29, 2026 |
| Revised (final) discontinuation deadline | June 8, 2026 |

## Impact Assessment

This extension provides additional time for trading members who have not yet completed migration of self-service connectivity links at Kohinoor-Kurla, Mumbai. Members who miss the June 8, 2026 deadline will lose connectivity to NSE's trading systems, directly impacting their ability to trade. The impact is confined to members using self-service links at this specific POP location. NSEIL disclaims liability for connectivity failures and places full responsibility on participants to maintain their network connectivity.
