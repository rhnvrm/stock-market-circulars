## Summary

NSE has informed its members that the RedHex Hybrid Long-Short Fund NFO, under the Specialized Investment Fund (SIF) category of HSBC Mutual Fund, will be reopened for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions — along with SIP and SWP facilities — on the NSE MF Invest Platform effective June 25, 2026. The fund aims to generate regular returns and capital appreciation by investing in fixed income securities and equity/equity-related securities, including limited short exposure through derivatives.

## Key Points

- RedHex Hybrid Long-Short Fund (HSBC MF) will be available on NSE MF Invest Platform from June 25, 2026
- 10 scheme variants are available covering Regular and Direct plans with Growth, IDCW Reinvestment, IDCW Payout, Annual IDCW Reinvestment, and Annual IDCW Payout options
- All 10 schemes are categorised as Debt under NSE's classification
- Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) transactions (Monthly/Quarterly) will be processed on the first Monday of every month
- Redemption requests will be processed every Monday, subject to a 10 working days redemption notice period
- Minimum investment for lump sum: Rs. 10,00,000/- and in multiples of Re. 1/- thereafter
- The fund targets a hybrid long-short strategy using both fixed income and equity derivatives

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is an operational notice announcing platform availability of an existing SIF-category fund.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must note the redemption notice period of 10 working days before processing redemption requests
- SWP transactions must be processed only on the first Monday of each month (Monthly/Quarterly)
- Members should refer to Annexure I (AMC process note) for detailed operational procedures on redemption and SWP processing

## Important Dates

- **June 25, 2026**: NSE MF Invest Platform availability begins for subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, and SWP

## Impact Assessment

This circular has low market impact as it is a routine platform onboarding notice for a specialized mutual fund product. The SIF category targets high-net-worth investors given the minimum investment threshold of Rs. 10 lakh. The 10 working-day redemption notice period and Monday-only processing are operational constraints that members and investors must factor into liquidity planning. No broader market or trading operations are affected.
