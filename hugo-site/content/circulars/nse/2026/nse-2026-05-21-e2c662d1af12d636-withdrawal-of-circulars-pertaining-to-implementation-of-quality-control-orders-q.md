## Summary

NSE is withdrawing two earlier circulars — NSE/COM/71308 (November 14, 2025) and NSE/COM/68091 (May 20, 2025) — that had implemented Quality Control Order (QCO) requirements for commodity futures contracts. This follows the Ministry of Mines, Govt. of India's decision to withdraw QCOs for Aluminium, Copper, Primary Lead, Refined Zinc, and Refined Nickel. The original quality specifications and additional deliverable grades in place before the QCO implementation are hereby restored, effective June 1, 2026.

## Key Points

- Ministry of Mines has issued notifications withdrawing QCOs for Aluminium, Copper, Primary Lead, Refined Zinc, and Refined Nickel
- NSE withdraws circulars NSE/COM/71308 (Nov 14, 2025) and NSE/COM/68091 (May 20, 2025) in full
- Pre-QCO quality specifications and additional deliverable grades are restored for all eight affected contracts
- IS 27:2023 / BIS Standard Mark requirement is removed from Lead Futures and Lead Mini Futures specifications
- Quality assessment reverts to Certificate of Analysis (CoA) issued by the producer, with only LME approved brands accepted
- Effective from June 1, 2026 for contracts expiring in June 2026 and onwards
- Affected contracts: Lead Futures, Lead Mini Futures, Nickel Futures, Zinc Futures, Zinc Mini Futures, Aluminium Futures, Alumini Futures, Copper Futures

## Regulatory Changes

The revised (pre-QCO) quality specifications are detailed in the Annexure. Key change for Lead Futures and Lead Mini Futures: the requirement to conform to IS 27:2023 and bear the BIS Standard Mark under a Bureau of Indian Standards license is removed. The restored specification requires Lead Ingots with minimum purity of 99.97%, with only LME approved brands accepted and quality assessed via CoA from the producer. Additional deliverable grade remains LME-approved brands with minimum 99.98% purity, plus any other NSE-approved Lead producer brand verified by producer CoA. Similar reversions apply to Nickel, Zinc, Zinc Mini, Aluminium, Alumini, and Copper contracts per the Annexure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Warehousing Service Providers (WSPs) must update delivery and quality-check procedures to apply pre-QCO specifications from June 1, 2026
- Quality assessment must rely on Certificate of Analysis (CoA) issued by the producer
- Only LME approved brands are accepted for standard deliverable grade across affected contracts
- NSE-approved producer brands remain eligible for additional deliverable grades as per the Annexure
- Members should communicate the revised specifications to clients engaged in physical delivery of these commodity futures

## Important Dates

- **May 21, 2026** — Circular issued (NSE/COM/74331, Ref No: 32/2026)
- **June 1, 2026** — Effective date; revised (pre-QCO) quality specifications apply for contracts expiring June 2026 and onwards
- **November 14, 2025** — Date of withdrawn circular NSE/COM/71308 (Lead, Lead Mini, Nickel, Zinc, Zinc Mini)
- **May 20, 2025** — Date of withdrawn circular NSE/COM/68091 (Aluminium, Alumini, Copper)

## Impact Assessment

Medium market impact. Commodity derivatives participants — particularly those involved in physical delivery of Lead, Nickel, Zinc, Aluminium, and Copper futures — need to update their delivery documentation and quality assessment processes before June 2026 contract expiries. The removal of BIS/IS 27:2023 certification requirements for Lead contracts reduces compliance overhead for producers and WSPs. Since the change restores previously familiar specifications, operational adjustments should be straightforward. No equity stocks are directly affected; impact is confined to commodity derivatives segment participants.
