## Summary

NSE has enabled the web-based bidding platform for the buyback offer by Sarla Performance Fibers Limited under the Tender Offer Scheme (per NSE circular NSE/CMTR/52776 dated June 29, 2022). The buyback is for up to 40,00,000 equity shares at a fixed price of Rs. 110 per share, open from May 21 to May 27, 2026.

## Key Points

- Buyback offer for up to 40,00,000 equity shares of Sarla Performance Fibers Limited (SARLAPOLY, Series: BB)
- Fixed offer price of Rs. 110 per share (face value Rs. 1)
- Bidding platform: https://eipo.nseindia.com
- All Capital Market segment trading members are eligible to participate
- Members already using the IPO bidding platform can use existing credentials
- Members without access can log in via admin terminal using Adminxxxxx (xxxxx = Member Code)
- ISIN: INE453D01025
- Merchant Banker: Monarch Networth Capital Limited
- Registrar: MUFG Intime India Private Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. The offer follows the existing Tender Offer Scheme framework as per NSE circular NSE/CMTR/52776 dated June 29, 2022.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members wishing to participate must log in to the web-based bidding platform at https://eipo.nseindia.com
- Members without existing access must use the admin terminal to create branches and users before participating
- Custodial confirmations must be submitted within the stipulated time windows
- Bidding must be conducted within designated hours (9:15 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. during issue period; 9:15 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. on closure day for custodial confirmation)

## Important Dates

- **Issue Period:** May 21, 2026 to May 27, 2026
- **Bidding Hours (regular):** 9:15 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.
- **Custodial Confirmation on Closure Day:** 9:15 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.

## Impact Assessment

This circular is operationally relevant for trading members of the Capital Market segment and shareholders of Sarla Performance Fibers Limited. The buyback at Rs. 110 per share offers an exit opportunity to existing shareholders across multiple investor categories including FIIs, Mutual Funds, Insurance Companies, Banks, Bodies Corporate, and Individuals. Broader market impact is limited given the company-specific scope, but members must ensure timely platform access setup to avoid missing the bidding window.
