## Summary

NSE's Surveillance department has revised the conversion ratios used to determine USD 1 million equivalents in EUR, GBP, and JPY for position limit compliance in the Currency Derivatives segment. The updated ratios replace those issued on March 25, 2026, and will be applicable from July 30, 2026.

## Key Points

- USD 1 million is now equivalent to EUR 0.86 million
- USD 1 million is now equivalent to GBP 0.75 million
- USD 1 million is now equivalent to JPY 159.28 million
- The aggregate position limit across all exchanges remains USD 100 million equivalent
- Clients exceeding USD 100 million equivalent must disclose underlying exposure to their Trading Member
- Previous ratios from circular NSE/SURV/73478 (March 25, 2026) remain valid until July 29, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

The revised ratios update the cross-currency equivalents of USD 1 million as outlined in earlier circulars NSE/SURV/29575 (April 29, 2015) and NSE/SURV/37410 (April 03, 2018). The underlying position limit framework and disclosure requirements remain unchanged; only the conversion factors are being updated to reflect current exchange rates.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must ensure client positions across all exchanges are within USD 100 million equivalent using the new conversion ratios from July 30, 2026
- Clients with positions exceeding USD 100 million equivalent must provide disclosure of underlying exposure to their Trading Member
- All entities must ensure positions remain within the limits prescribed in para 5 of circular NSE/SURV/29575 dated April 29, 2015
- For queries, members may write to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- **June 25, 2026**: Circular issued
- **July 29, 2026**: Last date for applicability of previous conversion ratios (NSE/SURV/73478, March 25, 2026)
- **July 30, 2026**: New conversion ratios become effective

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine recalibration of currency conversion factors and carries medium operational impact for Trading Members and their clients active in the Currency Derivatives segment. Firms with large cross-currency positions (near the USD 100 million threshold) will need to recalculate their limit utilisation using the updated ratios before July 30, 2026. The change primarily affects risk management systems and compliance monitoring tools that automate position limit checks.
