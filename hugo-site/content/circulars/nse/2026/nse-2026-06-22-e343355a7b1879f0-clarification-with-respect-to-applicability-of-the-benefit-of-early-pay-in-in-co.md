## Summary

SEBI has issued a clarification revising paragraph 11.3.1 of Chapter 11 of the SEBI Master Circular (SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-1/P/CIR/2023/136) dated August 4, 2023, for the Commodity Derivatives Segment. The revision clarifies the scope of margin exemptions available to market participants who deposit certified goods (early pay-in) with Clearing Corporation accredited warehouses. NSE Clearing Limited has forwarded this SEBI circular to all members of the Commodity Derivatives Segment.

## Key Points

- Clearing Corporations must provide early pay-in facility, allowing market participants to deposit certified goods at accredited warehouses against relevant derivatives contracts.
- For positions where early pay-in has been made, Clearing Corporations **may** (based on risk perception) exempt the imposition of **all types of margins**.
- However, **mark-to-market (MTM) margins must continue to be collected** from market participants against such positions — this is the key clarification distinguishing MTM from other margin types.
- The revision was prompted by stakeholder representations and deliberations by the Working Group on review of the regulatory framework for Agricultural Commodity Derivatives and the Commodity Derivatives Advisory Committee (CDAC).

## Regulatory Changes

Paragraph 11.3.1 of the SEBI Master Circular for Commodity Derivatives Segment (August 4, 2023) is revised as follows:

> "Clearing Corporations shall provide early pay-in facility to market participants permitting them to deposit certified goods to the Clearing Corporation accredited warehouse against relevant derivatives contracts. For such positions against which early pay-in has been made, based on risk perception, Clearing Corporations may exempt imposition of all types of margins. However, Clearing Corporations shall continue to collect mark to market margins from such market participants against such positions."

The key change is the explicit carve-out requiring MTM margins to be collected even when all other margins are exempted under early pay-in.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Stock Exchanges and Clearing Corporations** must make necessary system changes to implement the revised paragraph 11.3.1.
- They must disseminate the provisions of this circular to their members and publish the same on their websites.
- Clearing Corporations must ensure MTM margin collection continues for early pay-in positions even where other margins are exempted.

## Important Dates

- **Circular date (SEBI):** June 19, 2026
- **NSE Clearing forwarding date:** June 22, 2026
- **Effective date:** September 21, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on Clearing Corporations and their members in the Commodity Derivatives Segment. By explicitly mandating that MTM margins must still be collected even when other margins are exempted under early pay-in, SEBI ensures baseline risk coverage is maintained. Clearing Corporations retain discretion over exempting other margin types based on risk perception. Members who actively use the early pay-in facility will need to ensure their margin accounting systems correctly distinguish between MTM and non-MTM margin obligations. No specific stocks or tickers are affected; the change is segment-wide for commodity derivatives.
