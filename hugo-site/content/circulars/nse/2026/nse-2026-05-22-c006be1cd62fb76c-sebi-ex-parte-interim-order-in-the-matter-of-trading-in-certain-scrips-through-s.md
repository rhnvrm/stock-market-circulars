## Summary

SEBI's Whole Time Member has issued an ex-parte interim order (WTM/KV/ISD/ISD-SEC-7/32413/2026-27) against seven members of the Gupta family — Hemant Gupta, Rohan Gupta, Aniket Gupta, Sharon Gupta, Leana Gupta, Rajani Gupta, and Purvangi Gupta — for fraudulent trading in certain scrips. The noticees allegedly used accounts on X (formerly Twitter) and other social media platforms to post stock recommendations in scrips primarily listed on the NSE SME Platform, and simultaneously traded in those scrips to generate wrongful gains.

## Key Points

- SEBI's internal market surveillance detected social media accounts on X publishing posts designed to influence retail investors to buy specific SME-listed scrips.
- Seven noticees, all members of the Gupta family, are identified as operators and/or profit-makers/beneficiaries in the alleged fraudulent scheme.
- The modus operandi involved posting stock recommendations ahead of or alongside coordinated trading to profit from price movements induced by those recommendations.
- The 234-page order provides detailed illustrations of stock recommendations posted vis-à-vis trades executed, covering multiple scrips.
- SEBI calculated wrongful gains made by each noticee and has imposed joint and several liability on all parties.
- The order is passed under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11B(1), and 11D of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulations are introduced. The order applies existing provisions of:
- SEBI Act, 1992 (Sections 11, 11B, 11D)
- SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices Relating to Securities Market) Regulations

## Compliance Requirements

- The named noticees are directed to cease and desist from the fraudulent activities described.
- Noticees are subject to restraint/freeze orders as determined appropriate by SEBI pending full investigation.
- The order is interim and ex-parte; noticees will have an opportunity to file replies and seek hearing at a subsequent stage.

## Important Dates

- Order reference: WTM/KV/ISD/ISD-SEC-7/32413/2026-27
- Circular published: 2026-05-22
- Investigation: Ongoing; interim order issued pending conclusion

## Impact Assessment

This order signals continued SEBI enforcement action against 'pump-and-dump' schemes conducted via social media, particularly targeting SME-listed scrips which are more susceptible to price manipulation due to lower liquidity. Investors in SME-platform scrips recommended on social media should exercise heightened caution. Market intermediaries should note SEBI's active surveillance of social media platforms for investment-influencing content, and unregistered investment advisers operating via X or similar platforms face significant regulatory risk.
