## Summary

NSE's Listing Department has notified all exchange members that the ISIN code for the equity shares of Krishana Phoschem Limited (symbol: KRISHANA) will change effective July 3, 2026. The change is necessitated by a sub-division of the face value of equity shares from Rs. 10/- per share to Rs. 2/- per share. The new ISIN INE506W01020 will be applicable for all trades executed on and from the ex-date.

## Key Points

- Krishana Phoschem Limited (NSE symbol: KRISHANA) is undergoing a face-value sub-division from Rs. 10/- to Rs. 2/-.
- A new ISIN — **INE506W01020** — has been assigned to the post-split equity shares.
- The new ISIN is effective for all trades from the **ex-date of July 3, 2026**.
- Members must update their systems to reflect the new ISIN before trading commences on July 3, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

The ISIN change is a direct consequence of a corporate action (stock sub-division / stock split). NSE's Listing Department is formally notifying members of the updated identifier as required under its listing and trading rules.

## Compliance Requirements

- All exchange members and trading participants must update their back-office, settlement, and depository systems to replace the old ISIN with the new ISIN **INE506W01020** for Krishana Phoschem Limited.
- Trades, margins, and positions referencing the old ISIN must be reconciled and migrated prior to the ex-date.
- Members should coordinate with their depository participants (DPs) to ensure client demat accounts reflect the new ISIN post-record date.

## Important Dates

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Circular Date | June 30, 2026 |
| Ex-date / New ISIN Effective Date | July 3, 2026 |

## Impact Assessment

The ISIN change is an operational matter arising from the sub-division of Krishana Phoschem's equity shares. Members and investors holding KRISHANA shares will see their holdings reflected under the new ISIN (INE506W01020) in their demat accounts post the record date. The sub-division increases the number of shares proportionally (5:1 ratio, Rs. 10 to Rs. 2), which typically improves liquidity and affordability of the stock. No change in the company's market capitalisation results from the split itself. Trading systems, order management systems, and risk systems must be updated before market open on July 3, 2026 to avoid settlement failures or position mismatches.
