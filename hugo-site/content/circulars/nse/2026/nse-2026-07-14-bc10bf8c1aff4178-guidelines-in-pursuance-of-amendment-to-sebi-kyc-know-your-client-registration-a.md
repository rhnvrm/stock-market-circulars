## Summary

NSE has issued guidelines pursuant to the SEBI circular on KYC (KRA) Regulations, 2011, referencing SEBI's August 2023 circular and NSE's monthly circular series on the subject, the latest being dated June 12, 2026. The circular reiterates the requirement under SEBI's October 2023 circular on centralized reporting of investor demise, requiring Regulated Entities to block debit transactions, suspend trading accounts, and inactivate/close UCCs for deceased investors, with demise data shared daily by KRAs. Separately, and as the operative action item, clients whose KYC records uploaded to KRAs between June 1 and June 30, 2026 remain in "On Hold" status (for both Aadhaar-based and non-Aadhaar OVD-based KYC) will not be permitted to trade or square off open positions from July 25, 2026, until validation requirements are met. Any resulting open positions will expire naturally at contract expiry.

## Key Points

- Clients with KYC records marked "On Hold" by KRAs (uploaded June 1-30, 2026) will be barred from trading and from squaring off open positions starting July 25, 2026.
- Applies to both Aadhaar-based and non-Aadhaar OVD-based KYC records.
- Open positions for non-compliant clients will not be closable by the client and will expire naturally at contract expiry.
- NSE will flag non-compliant PANs (as provided by KRAs) as "Not Permitted to Trade" effective July 25, 2026.
- PANs that subsequently become KRA-compliant will be permitted to trade on T+1, based on KRA information received by the Exchange on T Day.
- Demise data for investors is shared by KRAs to Trading Members on a daily basis; Regulated Entities must block debits, suspend trading accounts, and inactivate/close UCCs for deceased investors across all Exchanges.
- List of non-compliant clients is available to members via the NSE Member Portal (enit.nseindia.com/MemberPortal) under ENIT > Log > Downloads, in the file named "<TM Code>_Non-Validated_Clients_by_KRA".

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change is introduced; this circular operationalizes existing SEBI requirements (SEBI/HO/MIRSD/FATF/P/CIR/2023/0144 dated August 11, 2023, and SEBI/HO/OIAE/OIAE_IAD-1/P/CIR/2023/0000000163 dated October 3, 2023) and continues NSE's recurring monthly circular series (most recently NSE/ISC/74672 dated June 12, 2026) identifying non-validated KYC clients for the relevant reporting period.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must download and review the list of non-compliant clients from the Member Portal (ENIT > Log > Downloads) and take necessary action, including informing affected clients ahead of the trading restriction date.
- Members must ensure clients with "On Hold" KYC status complete validation requirements with the KRA before July 25, 2026 to retain trading access.
- Regulated Entities must continue to block debit transactions and suspend/inactivate UCCs for investors reported as deceased via KRA data feeds.
- Queries should be directed to uci@nse.co.in or NSE's toll-free number (1800 266 0050, option 5).

## Important Dates

- KYC upload period covered: June 1, 2026 - June 30, 2026
- Effective date of trading restriction for non-compliant PANs: July 25, 2026
- Compliant PANs reinstated for trading on T+1 basis, following KRA data received by Exchange on T Day
- Circular issue date: July 14, 2026 (Circular Ref. No. 31/2026)

## Impact Assessment

This circular has high operational impact for Trading Members and directly affects clients with unresolved KYC validation, who will lose the ability to trade or exit open positions starting July 25, 2026. Members must proactively identify affected clients from the published list and prompt them to complete KYC validation to avoid disruption. Failure to act could result in clients being unable to manage risk on open positions, which will instead expire at contract maturity, potentially causing financial exposure for both clients and members. The measure reinforces SEBI's broader KYC data-quality and investor-protection framework, including demise-reporting obligations.
