## Summary

NSE has notified members of the merger of HDFC FMP 1359D September 2022 - Series 46 and HDFC FMP 1406D August 2022 - Series 46 (merging schemes) into HDFC Banking and PSU Debt Fund (surviving scheme). The merger is effective June 30, 2026. Investors who do not wish to participate in the merger may redeem or switch to other HDFC Mutual Fund schemes without exit load during the exit window.

## Key Points

- Merging schemes: HDFC FMP 1359D September 2022 - Series 46 and HDFC FMP 1406D August 2022 - Series 46
- Surviving scheme: HDFC Banking and PSU Debt Fund
- Merger effective date: June 30, 2026 (or next business day if non-business day)
- Exit load waiver window: May 29, 2026 to June 29, 2026 (upto 3:00 PM on June 29)
- 12 scheme options (plans/options) will be merged across both FMP series covering Direct Growth, Regular Growth, Direct IDCW Payout, Regular IDCW Payout variants
- Redemption or switch facility available through NSE MF Invest platform during the exit window

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework introduced. The merger follows notice cum addendum issued by HDFC Mutual Fund and applicable SEBI regulations for scheme mergers.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE Members are required to take note of the scheme merger and the revised ISINs post-merger
- Surviving scheme ISINs for mapping: INF179KA1IZ7 (HDFC Banking and PSU Debt Fund Direct Plan Growth), INF179KA1JC4 (Regular Growth), INF179KA1JA8 (Direct Plan IDCW Payout), INF179KA1JD2 (Regular IDCW Payout)
- Members should update their systems to reflect merged ISINs effective June 30, 2026
- Investor communication regarding exit option should be facilitated through NSE MF Invest platform

## Important Dates

- **May 29, 2026**: Start of exit window (redemption/switch without exit load)
- **June 29, 2026 (upto 3:00 PM)**: End of exit window for redemption or switch without exit load
- **June 30, 2026**: Merger effective date (or next business day)

## Impact Assessment

Investors in HDFC FMP 1359D September 2022 - Series 46 and HDFC FMP 1406D August 2022 - Series 46 will have their holdings converted into HDFC Banking and PSU Debt Fund units post-merger. The 12 plan/option combinations (covering growth and IDCW payout variants in both direct and regular plans) will map to four surviving scheme options. Investors who prefer not to continue in the surviving open-ended debt fund have a one-month exit window without exit load cost. The merger consolidates two maturing FMPs into an ongoing debt fund, which changes the investment character from fixed-term to open-ended. No impact on broader market operations; effect is limited to existing FMP unit holders.
