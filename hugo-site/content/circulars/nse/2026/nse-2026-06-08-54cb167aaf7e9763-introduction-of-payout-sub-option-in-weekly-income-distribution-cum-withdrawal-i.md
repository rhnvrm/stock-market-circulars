## Summary

NSE has issued a circular (Ref: NSE/NMFTM/74601, Circular Ref. No: 1420/2026) informing members about the introduction of a Payout sub-option under the Weekly Income Distribution cum Withdrawal (IDCW) option of Baroda BNP Paribas Money Market Fund. The change is effective from June 09, 2026, on the NSE MF Invest Platform.

## Key Points

- A new Weekly IDCW Payout sub-option has been introduced for Baroda BNP Paribas Money Market Fund.
- Applicable to both Direct Plan (ISIN: INF251K01VT5) and Regular Plan (ISIN: INF251K01VS7).
- Both schemes fall under the Debt category.
- The addendum is issued by Baroda BNP Paribas Asset Management India Private Limited (AMC), SEBI Registration No. MF/018/94/2, CIN: U65991MH2003PTC142972.
- The scheme is an open-ended debt scheme investing in money market instruments with relatively low interest rate risk and moderate credit risk.

## Regulatory Changes

As per Notice-cum-Addendum No. 45/2026 issued by Baroda BNP Paribas Mutual Fund, the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM) of Baroda BNP Paribas Money Market Fund have been amended to include a Payout sub-option under the Weekly IDCW option. Previously, this payout sub-option was not available; the revised provision adds it alongside the existing reinvestment and other sub-options.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members are requested to take note of the changes on the NSE MF Invest Platform.
- No specific compliance action is required from members beyond awareness; investors wishing to opt for the new Weekly IDCW Payout sub-option may do so from June 09, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 08, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 09, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact operational update. The introduction of the Weekly IDCW Payout sub-option expands the distribution options available to investors in Baroda BNP Paribas Money Market Fund on the NSE MF Invest Platform. It provides greater flexibility for investors seeking regular income payouts on a weekly basis. There is no impact on existing investments, NAV computation, or trading operations.
