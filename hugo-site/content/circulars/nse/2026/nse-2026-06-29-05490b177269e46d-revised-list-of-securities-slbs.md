## Summary

NSE has issued a revised list of securities eligible under the Securities Lending and Borrowing Scheme (SLBS) via Annexure I. The list contains over 200 symbols spanning large-cap equities, mid-cap stocks, ETFs, and sector funds across all major industries.

## Key Points

- The revised Annexure I supersedes any prior published list of SLBS-eligible securities.
- The list includes a broad cross-section of NSE-listed equities and ETFs, covering sectors such as banking, pharma, infrastructure, FMCG, IT, energy, and financial services.
- ETFs and index-tracking instruments (e.g., BANKBEES, ABSLBANETF, AXISNIFTY, AUTOIETF) are included alongside individual equity symbols.
- Participants in the SLBS must reference this updated list to determine which securities can be lent or borrowed on the platform.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes are introduced. This circular reflects a periodic revision to the approved securities list under the existing SLBS framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Stock brokers, custodians, and SLBS participants must update their systems to reflect the revised eligible securities list.
- Lending or borrowing of securities not present in the revised Annexure I is not permitted under the scheme.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: As of the circular date (2026-06-29); participants should treat this list as current immediately upon issuance.

## Impact Assessment

This update has medium operational impact for institutional participants, proprietary desks, and mutual funds engaged in securities lending. Inclusion or exclusion of specific symbols affects hedging and yield-enhancement strategies. The list broadly covers the most liquid NSE-listed securities, so disruption to existing SLBS positions is expected to be limited. ETF inclusion supports lending activity in passive investment vehicles.
