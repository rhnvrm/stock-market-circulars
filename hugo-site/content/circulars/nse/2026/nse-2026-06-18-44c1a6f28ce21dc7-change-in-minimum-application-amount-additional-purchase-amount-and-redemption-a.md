## Summary

NSE has informed members of changes to minimum application amounts, minimum additional purchase amounts, and minimum redemption/switch-out amounts for The Wealth Company Liquid Fund and The Wealth Company Arbitrage Fund on the NSE MF Invest platform. The changes were implemented by Wealth Company Asset Management Holdings Private Limited effective June 16, 2026.

## Key Points

- Minimum application amount reduced from Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 100 (in multiples of Rs. 1 thereafter)
- Minimum additional purchase amount reduced from Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 100 (in multiples of Rs. 1 thereafter)
- Minimum redemption/switch-out amount changed from 'any amount or any number of units' to Rs. 1 (in multiples of Rs. 1 thereafter)
- Applicable to The Wealth Company Liquid Fund and The Wealth Company Arbitrage Fund
- Changes are effective from Tuesday, June 16, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

Wealth Company Asset Management Holdings Private Limited has issued a Notice-cum-Addendum (No. 14, 2026-27) to the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM) of the affected schemes. The addendum forms an integral part of the SID and KIM.

| Particulars | Existing | Revised |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum Application Amount | Rs. 1,000 and in multiples of Rs. 1 | Rs. 100 and in multiples of Rs. 1 |
| Minimum Additional Purchase Amount | Rs. 1,000 and in multiples of Rs. 1 | Rs. 100 and in multiples of Rs. 1 |
| Minimum Redemption / Switch-out Amount | Any amount or any number of units | Rs. 1 and in multiples of Rs. 1 |

## Compliance Requirements

NSE members are informed to take note of these revised thresholds on the NSE MF Invest platform. No specific compliance action is required from members beyond updating their operational procedures to reflect the new minimums.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** Tuesday, June 16, 2026
- **Circular Date:** June 18, 2026
- **Addendum Date:** June 15, 2026 (signed in Mumbai)

## Impact Assessment

The reduction in minimum investment thresholds lowers the barrier to entry for retail investors wishing to invest in The Wealth Company Liquid Fund and Arbitrage Fund. Reducing the minimum from Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 100 for purchases and setting a nominal Rs. 1 minimum for redemptions increases flexibility and accessibility. The operational impact on NSE MF Invest platform members is minimal; this is a routine scheme parameter update with no broader market or systemic implications.
