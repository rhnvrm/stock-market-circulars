## Summary

NSE has issued a revised list of approved securities and banks for the debt segment. The circular specifies Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs), Government Securities (G-Sec), and Treasury Bills (T-Bills) that are accepted as collateral forming part of the cash component of liquid assets. All SGB/G-Sec/T-Bills issued by RBI during the month are accepted from the date of issue. A uniform haircut of 10% applies to all listed SGBs.

## Key Points

- All SGBs, G-Sec, and T-Bills issued by RBI during the month are accepted as collateral from the date of issue
- A uniform applicable haircut of 10% (0.1) applies to all listed Sovereign Gold Bonds
- The list covers 42+ Sovereign Gold Bond series ranging from 2026 to 2032 maturities
- SGBs are listed with ISIN, Security Description, Symbol, and Applicable Haircut
- Circular includes Annexure 1 (SGBs/G-Sec/T-Bills), Annexure 2, and Annexure 3 covering the full approved securities and banks list

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine periodic revision of the approved securities list. No structural regulatory changes are indicated; the haircut of 10% remains consistent across all Sovereign Gold Bond series.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing members and participants must update their collateral management systems to reflect the revised approved securities list
- Only securities listed in the approved annexures may be submitted as collateral for the cash component of liquid assets
- Members should ensure collateral submissions reference correct ISINs and symbols as listed

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: 2026-05-22 (date of circular issuance)
- New RBI-issued SGB/G-Sec/T-Bills accepted from date of issue on an ongoing basis

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on clearing members and trading participants in the debt segment who manage collateral positions. The inclusion of 42+ SGB series with 10% haircuts provides participants with a wide range of eligible collateral instruments. No stocks or equity instruments are directly affected. The routine nature of the revision means minimal market disruption, but members must update their internal systems to reflect the current approved list to avoid collateral shortfalls.
