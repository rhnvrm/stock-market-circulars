## Summary

NSE is removing 16 exclusively listed companies of de-recognized, non-operational, or exited stock exchanges from its Dissemination Board (DB), effective May 22, 2026. The removals are based on SEBI circulars from 2016, 2017, and the 2023 Master Circular (SEBI/HO/MRD2/PoD-2/CIR/P/2023/171).

## Key Points

- 16 companies will be removed from NSE's Dissemination Board with effect from May 22, 2026
- Removals fall into four categories: Strike Off status, Dissolution (Liquidation), Amalgamation, and listing on another exchange with nationwide trading terminals
- Action is pursuant to SEBI circulars dated October 10, 2016; August 01, 2017; and October 16, 2023

## Regulatory Changes

This circular operationalizes the SEBI framework for managing exclusively listed companies of de-recognized/non-operational/exited exchanges. Once removed from the DB, these companies will no longer be available for trading on NSE's Dissemination Board.

## Compliance Requirements

All NSE members are informed that the 16 listed companies will not be available on the NSE Dissemination Board from May 22, 2026. Members should update their systems and notify affected clients accordingly.

## Important Dates

- **May 21, 2026**: Circular issued
- **May 22, 2026**: Effective date of removal from Dissemination Board for all 16 companies

## Impact Assessment

**Companies removed due to Strike Off status (MCA):**
1. Divya Enterprises Limited
2. Fi Sofex Limited

**Companies removed due to Dissolved (Liquidation) status (MCA):**
3. Rishyashringa Jewellers Limited

**Companies removed due to Amalgamated status (MCA):**
4. Kasthuri Mills Limited
5. Royalsoft Services Limited

**Companies removed due to listing on another exchange with nationwide trading terminals:**
6. Verum Properties Limited (formerly Fairdeal Properties Limited)
7. Alba Polymers Limited (formerly Gowri Polymers Limited)
8. Gujarat Winding Systems Limited (formerly Hi-Tech Winding Systems Limited)
9. Nexus Surgical and Medicare Limited (formerly Nexus Commodities and Technologies Limited)
10. Osiajee Texfab Limited (formerly Osiajee Housing Developers Limited)
11. Flora Corporation Limited (formerly Royce Marine Products Limited)
12. Nutricircle Limited (formerly Shreeyash Industries Limited)
13. Amarkantak Traders Limited (formerly Shubh Poultries Limited)
14. Variman Global Enterprises Limited (formerly Springfields Infraventures Limited)
15. Solis Marketing Limited (formerly Surya Marketing Limited)
16. Tarang Projects & Consultant Limited

The impact is limited to shareholders and investors in these 16 small-cap companies, who will lose access to DB trading on NSE from May 22, 2026. Companies re-listed on other nationwide exchanges retain trading access there.
