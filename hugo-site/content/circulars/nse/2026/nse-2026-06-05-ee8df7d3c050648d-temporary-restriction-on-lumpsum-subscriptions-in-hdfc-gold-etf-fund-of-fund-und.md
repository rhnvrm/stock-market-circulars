## Summary

NSE has implemented temporary restrictions on lumpsum subscriptions in HDFC Gold ETF Fund of Fund (FOF) on the NSE MF Invest Platform, as directed by HDFC Mutual Fund. Lumpsum purchases and switch-ins into the FOF are now capped at Rs. 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month (at first holder level). Additionally, large investor direct subscriptions in HDFC Gold ETF (minimum Rs. 25 crore) will be suspended from June 8, 2026.

## Key Points

- Lumpsum purchases and switch-ins into HDFC Gold ETF Fund of Fund are restricted to a maximum of Rs. 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month (at first holder level)
- The Rs. 10 lakh cap applies to transactions received after cut-off time (3:00 PM) on June 5, 2026
- For HDFC Gold ETF (direct), large investor subscriptions of minimum Rs. 25 crore will not be accepted from June 8, 2026
- The restriction is described as temporary, with no end date specified
- All other terms and conditions of the schemes remain unchanged
- SIP and other systematic investment modes are not affected by this restriction

## Regulatory Changes

HDFC Mutual Fund issued an Addendum to the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM) for HDFC Gold ETF and HDFC Gold ETF Fund of Fund, introducing the following changes:

- **HDFC Gold ETF FOF**: Maximum lumpsum subscription/switch-in capped at Rs. 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month at first holder level
- **HDFC Gold ETF**: Subscription transactions by large investors (investing minimum Rs. 25 crore) directly with HDFC Mutual Fund will not be accepted from June 8, 2026

The addendum is dated June 04, 2026 and forms an integral part of the SID/KIM of the schemes.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members and distributors must ensure lumpsum purchase/switch-in transactions for HDFC Gold ETF FOF do not exceed Rs. 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month at first holder level
- Transactions submitted after 3:00 PM on June 5, 2026 are subject to the new limit
- Members should not process large investor direct subscriptions (minimum Rs. 25 crore) in HDFC Gold ETF from June 8, 2026
- Members are advised to communicate this change to their clients

## Important Dates

- **June 5, 2026, 3:00 PM**: Rs. 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month cap on lumpsum subscriptions/switch-ins in HDFC Gold ETF FOF becomes effective
- **June 8, 2026**: Suspension of large investor (minimum Rs. 25 crore) direct subscriptions in HDFC Gold ETF becomes effective
- **June 4, 2026**: Date of HDFC Mutual Fund addendum

## Impact Assessment

This restriction primarily affects high-value investors seeking to make large lumpsum investments in HDFC Gold ETF and its Fund of Fund. The measure is likely driven by broader economic and market conditions, possibly related to elevated gold prices or fund inflow management concerns. Retail investors making investments below Rs. 10 lakh per month are unaffected. Systematic investment plans (SIPs) and existing holdings are not impacted. The restriction on HDFC Gold ETF large investor subscriptions (Rs. 25 crore minimum) targets institutional or ultra-HNI investors dealing directly with the fund house. Members on the NSE MF Invest Platform must update their transaction processing systems to enforce the new limits.
