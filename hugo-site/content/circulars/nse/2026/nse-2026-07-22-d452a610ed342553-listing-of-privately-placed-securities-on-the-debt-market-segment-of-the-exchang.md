## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of several privately placed commercial papers (CPs) to dealings on the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange, effective July 22, 2026, under Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The circular lists both fresh issues and re-issues of CPs from a mix of corporates, NBFCs, and financial institutions, along with their ISINs, face values, issue prices, allotment dates, and redemption dates.

## Key Points

- Fresh CP issues listed: Godrej Industries Limited (GIL CP 19/10/26 Sr 43), NTPC Limited (NTPC CP 19/08/26), Export Import Bank of India (EXIM CP 11/12/26 Sr 9), IIFL Finance Limited (IIFL CP 15/12/26 Sr 2630), Godrej Consumer Products Limited (GCPL CP 19/10/26 Sr 10), Hero FinCorp Limited (HFL CP 19/10/26), DCM Shriram Limited (DSL CP 18/09/26), and Axis Securities Limited (ASL CP 21/10/26 Sr 30).
- Re-issued CPs listed: ICICI Securities Limited (ISEC CP 22/09/26) and HDFC Securities Limited (HSL CP 19/10/2026).
- All securities carry a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 per unit, with issue prices reflecting the applicable discount for each CP's tenor.
- Most securities were allotted on July 21, 2026, with Axis Securities' CP allotted on July 22, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is a routine notification under existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations formalizing the listing of privately placed debt instruments.

## Compliance Requirements

No action required from listed companies or investors beyond standard debt market settlement and trading procedures. Members should note the designated security codes (ISINs) specified in the Annexure for trading purposes.

## Important Dates

- Effective listing date: July 22, 2026
- Allotment dates: July 21, 2026 (majority of issues) and July 22, 2026 (Axis Securities CP)
- Redemption dates range from August 19, 2026 (NTPC CP) to December 15, 2026 (IIFL Finance CP)

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal direct market impact on equity trading or pricing, as it pertains solely to the debt market segment. It is primarily relevant for debt market participants, fixed-income investors, and treasury desks tracking short-term corporate borrowing (commercial paper) activity by these issuers, which can serve as an indicator of their short-term liquidity and funding costs.
