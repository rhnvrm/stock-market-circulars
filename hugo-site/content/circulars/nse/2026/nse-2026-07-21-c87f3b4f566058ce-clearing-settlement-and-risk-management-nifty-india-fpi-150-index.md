## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a circular clarifying that all existing clearing, settlement, and risk management procedures applicable to equity derivatives will extend to the newly introduced futures and options contracts on the Nifty India FPI 150 Index. This follows the earlier circular (103/2026, dated July 16, 2026) that introduced these contracts. The circular ensures members are aware that no separate or new risk framework is being created for these instruments.

## Key Points

- Circular Ref. No. 089/2026 (Download Ref No. NCL/CMPT/75298), dated July 21, 2026, issued by NSE Clearing Limited, Futures & Options Department.
- References earlier circular 050/2026 (NSE/CMPT/73997) dated April 30, 2026, and circular 103/2026 (NSE/FAOP/75239) dated July 16, 2026, which introduced Futures and Options contracts on the Nifty India FPI 150 Index.
- All existing clearing and settlement procedures for equity derivatives will apply mutatis mutandis to Nifty India FPI 150 Index futures and options contracts.
- Existing risk management measures, including initial margins and minimum margins, will apply to these new contracts.
- Clearing Corporation retains its existing right to close out positions in these contracts, consistent with other equity derivatives.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. The circular confirms that the existing Equity Derivatives Segment regulations and risk management framework extend to the newly launched Nifty India FPI 150 Index derivative contracts.

## Compliance Requirements

Members dealing in Nifty India FPI 150 Index futures and options contracts must continue to comply with existing margin requirements (initial and minimum margins) and other risk management obligations applicable to equity derivatives trading.

## Important Dates

- April 30, 2026: Reference circular 050/2026 (NSE/CMPT/73997) issued.
- July 16, 2026: Reference circular 103/2026 (NSE/FAOP/75239) introducing Futures and Options Contracts on Nifty India FPI 150 Index issued.
- July 21, 2026: Current circular issued, effective immediately.

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact procedural circular that provides continuity and clarity for clearing members trading the newly launched Nifty India FPI 150 Index derivatives. It does not introduce new compliance burdens or alter existing risk parameters, but ensures members apply established margining and settlement processes to the new index contracts.
