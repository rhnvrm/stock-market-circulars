## Summary

NSE has issued a revised list of equity shares eligible as the non-cash component of liquid assets for the Capital Market (CM) segment. The circular specifies overall permitted quantities across all segments and memberwise permitted quantities for each approved security, along with applicable haircut methodology.

## Key Points

- Applicable haircut for listed securities is Value at Risk (VAR)-based.
- Value-based prudential norm: exclusion of a pledged security must not result in a margin shortfall exceeding 25% at a Clearing Member level.
- The list covers hundreds of equity shares with their ISIN codes, security names, overall permitted quantities, and CM segment memberwise permitted quantities.
- This is Annexure 1 (equity shares for non-cash liquid assets); the circular also includes Annexures 2–7 covering additional categories and approved banks.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes; this is a periodic revision of the approved securities list under existing margin and liquid asset guidelines for the CM segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing Members must ensure that securities used as non-cash liquid assets are drawn only from this revised approved list.
- Pledged securities must comply with the 25% margin shortfall prudential norm at the Clearing Member level.
- Memberwise and overall permitted quantity caps must be observed when pledging securities.

## Important Dates

- Effective date: 2026-05-22 (date of circular issuance).

## Impact Assessment

This update affects Clearing Members and trading members in the CM segment who use approved equity shares as collateral for liquid asset requirements. Changes to the permitted quantity limits or additions/removals from the approved list may require members to rebalance their collateral portfolios. The VAR-based haircut methodology remains standard. Overall market impact is routine and operational in nature.
