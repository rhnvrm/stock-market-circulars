## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of equity shares of Hexagon Nutrition Limited (symbol: HEXAGON) on the Capital Market segment effective June 12, 2026. The securities will initially trade in Series 'BE' under the pre-open IPO session and subsequently shift to Series 'EQ' as per SEBI guidelines. The issue price is Rs. 45/- per share with a face value of Re. 1/-.

## Key Points

- Symbol: HEXAGON; ISIN: INE0JUI01012
- Issue price: Rs. 45/- per share; Face value: Re. 1/-
- Total shares listed: 122,918,109 (approx. 12.29 crore)
- Market lot: 1 share
- Series: Initially 'BE' (pre-open IPO category), to be shifted to 'EQ' subsequently
- Registrar: KFin Technologies Limited, Mumbai
- Company Secretary & Compliance Officer: Ms. Vedanti Swapnil Vartak

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. Listing is in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A. Pre-open session applicability is governed by SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/01/2012 & CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use the designated security codes (HEXAGON) for all trading purposes.
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot size of 1.
- Members must ensure participation in the pre-open IPO session as applicable.

## Important Dates

- Listing effective date: June 12, 2026
- Lock-in schedules for promoter/pre-IPO shares:
  - 4,726,444 shares locked until December 14, 2026
  - 62,080,661 shares locked until December 14, 2026
  - 24,583,800 shares locked until December 14, 2027
  - 4,628,847 shares locked until July 9, 2026
  - 4,628,849 shares locked until September 7, 2026
  - 667,500 shares and 21,602,008 shares: Free (no lock-in)

## Impact Assessment

The listing introduces Hexagon Nutrition Limited, a Mumbai-based nutrition company, to NSE's capital market segment. With approximately 12.29 crore shares at an issue price of Rs. 45/-, the implied market cap at listing is approximately Rs. 553 crore. A significant portion of shares (~74%) are subject to lock-in, which limits near-term float and may affect price discovery. Traders and IPO allottees should note the initial 'BE' series designation requiring trade-for-trade settlement before migration to the regular 'EQ' series.
