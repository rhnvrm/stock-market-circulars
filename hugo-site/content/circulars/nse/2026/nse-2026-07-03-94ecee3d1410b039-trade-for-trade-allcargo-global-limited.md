## Summary

NSE has issued a circular announcing that Allcargo Global Limited (Symbol: AGL) will be transferred from the Trade for Trade segment (Series: BE) to the Rolling segment (Series: EQ) with effect from July 17, 2026. This follows the Exchange's earlier circular no. NSE/CML/74976 dated July 01, 2026 and is issued pursuant to SEBI guidelines under circular reference no. CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012.

## Key Points

- Allcargo Global Limited (Symbol: AGL) moves out of the Trade for Trade (T2T) segment.
- The security will shift from Series BE to Series EQ, enabling normal rolling settlement.
- The change follows up on a prior NSE circular (NSE/CML/74976 dated July 01, 2026).
- Issued under SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this circular applies existing SEBI T2T surveillance guidelines to reclassify a specific security's trading series.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must update their systems to reflect AGL's series change from BE to EQ effective the stated date and ensure client-facing trading platforms and settlement processes are updated accordingly.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 17, 2026 — AGL moves from Series BE (Trade for Trade) to Series EQ (Rolling segment).
- Circular Date: July 03, 2026.
- Reference: Prior circular NSE/CML/74976 dated July 01, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This transition allows intraday trading and normal rolling settlement in AGL shares, which was previously restricted to trade-for-trade (delivery-based) settlement. This generally improves liquidity and trading flexibility for market participants holding or trading Allcargo Global Limited shares, while removing the additional delivery-based settlement constraint that had been in place.
