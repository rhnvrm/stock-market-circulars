## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has revised the cut-off timings for cash and FDR collateral addition and release, as well as the release timings for pledged securities in the Futures and Options segment. This revision follows feedback received from members on the earlier timings notified via circular ref. no. 050/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/73997 dated April 30, 2026). The revised timings will be effective from July 22, 2026.

## Key Points

- Cash and FDR Addition cut-off time moved from 7:30 PM to 9:00 PM.
- Cash and FDR Release (Intraday) cut-off time moved from 8:00 PM to 9:00 PM.
- Cash and FDR Release (End of Day) cut-off time remains unchanged at 8:00 PM.
- Cash and FDR Release (Allocation) cut-off time moved from 8:00 PM to 11:30 PM.
- Pledge securities release now follow 16 defined batch timings from 8:30 AM through 8:00 PM, plus an End-of-Day (EOD) batch at 9:00 PM.
- Members seeking release of repledged securities for pay-in requirements must submit requests one day prior to the pay-in date, not on the pay-in date itself.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular amends the collateral addition and release timings previously prescribed under circular ref. no. 050/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/73997 dated April 30, 2026). The changes apply to Cash and FDR collateral processing as well as the batch-wise release schedule for pledged securities.

## Compliance Requirements

All Members, Custodians, and Professional Clearing Members (PCMs) must align their internal collateral management and treasury operations with the revised cut-off times. Requests for release of repledged securities needed for pay-in must be submitted one day in advance of the pay-in date.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 22, 2026
- Reference circular: 050/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/73997), dated April 30, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The extended cut-off times, particularly for Cash and FDR Addition (up to 9:00 PM) and Allocation Release (up to 11:30 PM), provide members and custodians additional operational flexibility for end-of-day collateral management. The introduction of granular, staggered batch timings for pledged securities release throughout the trading day should improve liquidity management for clearing members. The impact is operational rather than market-moving, primarily affecting back-office and treasury workflows of NSE Clearing members and custodians rather than trading in specific securities.
