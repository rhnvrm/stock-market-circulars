## Summary

NSE has identified 17 companies as non-compliant with Regulation 31 of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015 — specifically, the requirement to submit Shareholding Pattern — for two consecutive quarters ending December 31, 2025 and March 31, 2026. While these companies would ordinarily be transferred to Z Category (BZ/SZ Series) effective June 29, 2026, they are already trading in Z Category pursuant to earlier Exchange Circulars. Their Z Category status and Trade for Trade settlement therefore continues unchanged.

## Key Points

- 17 companies identified as non-compliant with Regulation 31 (Shareholding Pattern submission) for Q3 FY26 and Q4 FY26
- All 17 were already placed in Z Category under prior NSE circulars; no fresh transfer is needed
- Trades in these securities continue to be settled on a **Trade for Trade (TFT) Basis**
- Effective date for the new Z Category eligibility: **June 29, 2026** (no operational change since they are already in Z)
- Action taken under SEBI Master Circular No. HO/49/14/14(7)2025-CFD-POD2/I/3762/2026 (last updated January 30, 2026)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this circular. It reaffirms the existing Z Category placement of the listed securities under the framework of the SEBI Master Circular on non-compliance with LODR Regulations. The SEBI Master Circular was originally issued on July 11, 2023 and last updated on January 30, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- The 17 listed companies must file their Shareholding Pattern as required under Regulation 31 of SEBI LODR Regulations, 2015 to be considered for removal from Z Category
- Members of the Exchange are advised to note that trades in these securities must be settled on Trade for Trade Basis — no netting or carry-forward is permitted
- Investors and brokers should treat these securities as Z Category (BZ/SZ Series) scrips

- **June 15, 2026**: Circular issued

- **Non-compliance periods**: December 31, 2025 (Q3 FY26) and March 31, 2026 (Q4 FY26)

## Impact Assessment

The practical impact of this circular is limited since all 17 companies are already in Z Category and subject to Trade for Trade settlement. However, it signals continued regulatory scrutiny of these companies for failing to meet basic LODR disclosure requirements over two consecutive quarters. Investors holding these securities should be aware of the associated liquidity constraints under TFT settlement and the distressed compliance status of the issuers. Notable names include Gensol Engineering, Reliance Home Finance, Future Enterprises, and HDIL — all of which have faced significant corporate governance or financial stress in recent years.
