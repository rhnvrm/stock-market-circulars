## Summary

NSE is conducting a mock trading session on Saturday, June 13, 2026 for the Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) segment. The session covers two trading sessions — one from the Primary site and one from the Disaster Recovery (DR) site — followed by a live re-login session from the Primary site to prevent login issues on the following trading day, Monday, June 15, 2026.

## Key Points

- Mock trading is scheduled for June 13, 2026 across Primary and BCP/DR sites.
- Session 1 (Primary site): Pre-open 08:45–08:53, Normal Market 09:00–11:00.
- Session 2 (DR site): Pre-open 11:45–11:53, Normal Market 12:00–17:00, Auction Market 14:30–15:15, Trade modification end 17:15.
- Live re-login from Primary site: 18:30–19:00.
- No changes required to NEAT Adapter settings; live session settings from June 12, 2026 will apply.
- All outstanding orders will be purged before trading begins from the BCP site.
- No financial obligations arise from trades executed during mock sessions.
- Extranet/Connect2NSE will be unavailable 10:00–11:00 and 18:00–21:00.
- Default mock password: `Neat@EG1`.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an operational drill to test BCP/DR readiness.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must retain NEAT Adapter settings from the live session of June 12, 2026 to connect to all mock sessions.
- Members using NNF software must clear outstanding orders from Session 1 before trading from the BCP site.
- In the event of a non-graceful shutdown from the Primary site, members must follow the procedures in circular NSE/MSD/48662 dated June 18, 2021 to restore system consistency.
- Members should review Annexures 1–5 for detailed instructions on mock trading, login parameters, market data broadcast parameters (EGR segment), NEATPlus EG software installation, and DR switchover guidelines.

## Important Dates

- **June 13, 2026**: Mock trading session (Primary and DR sites).
- **June 15, 2026**: Next live trading day; re-login session on June 13 is intended to prevent login issues on this date.

## Impact Assessment

Impact is limited to EGR segment members who are expected to participate in the mock drill. No trades result in financial settlement. The non-graceful Primary site shutdown during the DR switchover requires members to take manual corrective action on their end. Overall market impact is negligible as this is a scheduled preparedness exercise.
