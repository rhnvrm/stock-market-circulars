## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued the revised list of approved securities and empaneled banks for the Debt Segment, effective July 2026. This supersedes the previous circular 009/2026 (NCL/DS/74374) dated May 22, 2026. The circular covers three annexures detailing accepted collateral instruments and their applicable haircut rates.

## Key Points

- Annexure 1 lists GOI Securities (G-Sec), T-Bills, and Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs) accepted as the cash component of liquid assets, with applicable haircut rates (10% for SGBs).
- Annexure 2 lists Corporate Bonds accepted as the non-cash component of liquid assets, along with market-wide applicable limits and haircut rates.
- Annexure 3 lists banks empaneled for issuance of Bank Guarantees (BGs) and Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDRs).
- G-Sec/T-Bills carry a shut period of two days; members must release these securities at least two days before maturity.
- Members who are also banks must not reckon G-Sec provided as collateral for their SLR requirements, and must not use such securities for trading.

## Regulatory Changes

No structural policy changes; this is a periodic refresh of the approved securities and bank lists for July 2026, updating the list from the prior month's circular (009/2026 dated May 22, 2026).

## Compliance Requirements

- Members/Custodians/PCMs must ensure collateral submitted consists only of securities on the revised approved list.
- Members must release G-Sec/T-Bills at least two days before maturity to account for the two-day shut period.
- Member-banks must not use pledged G-Sec collateral for SLR compliance or trading purposes.

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: June 25, 2026
- Effective Period: July 2026
- Previous Circular: 009/2026 dated May 22, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational update affecting Members, Custodians, and Primary Clearing Members (PCMs) in the NSE Debt Segment. The impact is limited to collateral management operations — entities must verify their submitted collateral remains on the updated approved list for July. No market-wide trading disruptions are expected. The SGB haircut rate remains at 10% across all listed series.
