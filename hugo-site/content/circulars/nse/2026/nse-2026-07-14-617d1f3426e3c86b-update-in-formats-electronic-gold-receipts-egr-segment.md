## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has revised the standard annexure formats (Annexure 5(A), 5(B), 5(C), 5(D)) that members and banks use for submitting, confirming, renewing, and releasing Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDRs) placed as margin or security deposits with NCL. The updated formats now explicitly include "Electronic Gold Receipts" as a selectable deposit segment, alongside the existing segments such as Capital Market, Currency Derivatives, Futures & Options, Debt Market, and Commodity Derivatives.

## Key Points

- Annexure 5(A): Format of letter by a member for submission of an FDR to NCL, now including a checkbox for the "Electronic Gold Receipts" segment in addition to existing segments.
- Annexure 5(B): Format of letter to be provided by the issuing bank confirming the FDR is lien-marked in favour of NCL, with no lock-in period and payable on demand.
- Annexure 5(C): Format of letter from the bank for renewal of an FDR where the FDR number changes, capturing old and new FDR details.
- Annexure 5(D) (referenced but not fully captured in extracted content): Likely covers a related format, such as FDR release or renewal without change in FDR number.
- All formats require submission on the member's or bank's official letterhead with authorized signatory seal and signature.

## Regulatory Changes

The core regulatory/procedural change is the addition of "Electronic Gold Receipts" as a recognized deposit segment in the standardized FDR submission letter (Annexure 5(A)). This aligns the collateral/margin deposit documentation framework with the EGR segment now operating on the exchange.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing members submitting FDRs as margin or security deposit for the EGR segment must use the updated Annexure 5(A) format, indicating the EGR segment checkbox.
- Banks issuing or renewing FDRs on behalf of members must use the revised Annexure 5(B) and 5(C) formats and confirm lien marking in favour of NCL, unrestricted encashment rights, and protection of principal.
- Members and banks should ensure use of the current format for all future FDR submissions, renewals, and confirmations to NCL.

This is a low-impact, purely administrative/operational circular affecting documentation formats used by clearing members and banks for FDR-based collateral deposits with NCL. It has no direct effect on trading, listed securities, or investors, but ensures proper documentation compliance for members participating in the EGR segment.
