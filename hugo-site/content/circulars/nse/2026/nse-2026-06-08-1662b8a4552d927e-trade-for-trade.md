## Summary

NSE has issued a surveillance circular (Ref No: NSE/SURV/74599, Circular 423/2026) shifting 7 securities from the Rolling segment to the Trade for Trade (TFT) segment effective June 11, 2026 (Thursday), with a price band of 5% or lower. An additional 4 securities already in TFT will continue under surveillance. The action is taken under Capital Market Segment Trading Regulations Part-A, 2.6, to ensure market safety and safeguard investor interests.

## Key Points

- 5 securities moved from Rolling segment (EQ series) to Trade for Trade segment (BE series) with 5% price band from June 11, 2026
- 2 securities moved from Rolling segment (SM series) to Trade for Trade segment (ST series) with 5% price band from June 11, 2026
- All shifted securities triggered criteria: Price Earnings Multiple, Price Variation & Market Capitalization
- 4 securities continue in TFT segment (BE/BZ series) under ongoing surveillance; Nil in ST/SZ continuation list
- Settlement for all TFT securities will be on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off allowed
- Shifted securities will no longer be available in rolling segment (EQ/SM series)

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must note that shifted securities will no longer be available in the EQ/SM rolling segment from June 11, 2026
- Members must exercise adequate precaution while trading in these securities as no netting off is permitted
- All trades in TFT securities must be settled on a gross, trade-to-trade basis
- Members should inform clients about the change in trading segment and associated settlement obligations
- Criteria for shifting securities to/from TFT segment are available at: https://www.nseindia.com/regulations/movement-securities-periodic-review

## Important Dates

- **June 8, 2026:** Circular issued
- **June 11, 2026 (Thursday):** Effective date for shift of 7 securities to Trade for Trade segment (BE/ST series)

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a high trading impact on the listed securities. Moving securities to Trade for Trade restricts trading flexibility significantly: investors cannot net off buy and sell positions, increasing settlement risk and capital requirements. The 5% daily price band further limits liquidity and price discovery. All 7 newly shifted securities triggered multiple surveillance criteria (Price Earnings Multiple, Price Variation, and Market Capitalization), suggesting elevated risk profiles. The 4 continuing TFT securities (ALKALI, CLCIND, DHARAN, IMPEXFERRO) remain under heightened scrutiny as they qualified for fortnightly review continuation. Traders and investors holding positions in any of these securities should reassess their exposure ahead of the June 11 effective date.
