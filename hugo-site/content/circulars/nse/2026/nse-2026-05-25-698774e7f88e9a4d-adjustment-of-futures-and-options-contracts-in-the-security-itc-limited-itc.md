## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) is adjusting Futures and Options contracts in ITC Limited (symbol: ITC) pursuant to NSE Circular No. 68/2026. The adjustment is triggered by a dividend of Rs. 8/- per share. May 26, 2026 is the last cum-dividend date and May 27, 2026 is the ex-dividend date.

## Key Points

- Adjustment of all ITC futures contracts based on the daily mark-to-market (MTM) settlement price on May 26, 2026 (last cum-dividend date)
- Open futures positions will be carried forward at the daily settlement price **less Rs. 8/-** (dividend amount)
- From May 27, 2026 (ex-dividend date), daily MTM settlement resumes under normal procedures
- Begin-of-day margins on May 27, 2026 will be computed using the adjusted carry-forward value
- Intra-day margins from May 27, 2026 will be computed based on relevant traded prices at the time of intra-day SPAN risk parameter file generation
- This circular is issued by the Futures & Options department of NSE Clearing Limited (Ref: NCL/CMPT/74396, Circular No. 063/2026)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes. This is a standard operational adjustment under existing NSE/NCL procedures for F&O contract adjustment on account of corporate actions (dividend).

## Compliance Requirements

- All members (clearing and trading) holding open ITC futures positions must account for the adjusted carry-forward prices effective May 27, 2026
- Members should ensure their risk and margin systems reflect the adjusted settlement prices from the ex-dividend date
- No separate action required by members; the clearing corporation will carry out the adjustment automatically

## Important Dates

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| May 25, 2026 | Circular issued by NSE Clearing Limited |
| May 26, 2026 | Last cum-dividend date; MTM settlement price reduced by Rs. 8/- for open ITC futures positions |
| May 27, 2026 | Ex-dividend date; normal F&O settlement procedures resume; begin-of-day margins computed on adjusted values |

## Impact Assessment

This adjustment affects all market participants holding open Futures and Options positions in ITC Limited. The futures contract prices will be reduced by Rs. 8/- (the dividend amount) at the end of May 26, 2026, ensuring that holders of futures contracts are not disadvantaged relative to spot holders who receive the dividend. The impact is operationally routine and limited to ITC F&O positions; broader market impact is low. Members should verify that their back-office and risk systems correctly process the adjusted carry-forward values to avoid margin discrepancies on May 27, 2026.
