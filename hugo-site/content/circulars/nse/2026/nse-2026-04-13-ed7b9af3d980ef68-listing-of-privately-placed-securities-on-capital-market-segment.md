## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of privately placed debt securities of SMFG India Credit Company Limited on the capital market segment. The bond series (SICCL 7.65% 2031 Sr 117) will be admitted to dealings effective April 15, 2026, under symbol 765SICCL31 with ISIN INE535H07CT5.

## Key Points

- SMFG India Credit Company Limited's 7.65% bond maturing in 2031 admitted to NSE capital market segment
- Symbol: **765SICCL31**, Series: **N0**, Market Lot: **1**
- Coupon rate: **7.65%**, Maturity date: **April 9, 2031**
- ISIN: **INE535H07CT5**
- Circular reference: NSE/CML/73721, Circular Ref. No: 0614/2026
- Issued pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is issued in routine compliance with Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A, notifying securities admitted to dealings and their designated codes, and Regulation 2.5.5 specifying applicable lot sizes.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members are notified that trading in this security must use the designated symbol code **765SICCL31**
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot size of **1** unit
- Members should update their systems to recognise the new security code effective April 15, 2026

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** April 13, 2026
- **Effective/Trading Commencement Date:** April 15, 2026
- **Bond Maturity Date:** April 9, 2031

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing notification with low broader market impact. The listing enables secondary market trading of SMFG India Credit Company Limited's privately placed bond on NSE's capital market segment, improving liquidity for existing bondholders. Relevant primarily to fixed-income investors and bond market participants. SMFG India Credit Company Limited (formerly Fullerton India Credit Company) is a major NBFC, making this instrument of interest to institutional and retail debt investors seeking exposure to NBFC paper with a 7.65% yield through April 2031.
