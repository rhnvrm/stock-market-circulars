## Summary

NSE has issued Circular No. 361/2026 (Ref: NSE/SURV/74209) dated May 14, 2026, notifying the placement of MORARJEE TEXTILES LIMITED (Symbol: MORARJEE, ISIN: INE161G01027) into Stage I of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, effective May 15, 2026. This action is being taken in alignment with BSE's directive.

## Key Points

- MORARJEE TEXTILES LIMITED (ISIN: INE161G01027) is being moved to Stage I of GSM with effect from May 15, 2026.
- The security is being added as per BSE's directive.
- Stage I imposes a 100% applicable margin rate on trades.
- A price band of 5% or lower will be applicable.
- All NSE members are advised to exercise adequate caution while trading in this security.

## Regulatory Changes

The security is placed under Stage I of the GSM framework as per the guidelines established in earlier circulars: NSE/SURV/34262 (Feb 23, 2017), NSE/SURV/38389 (Jul 20, 2018), NSE/SURV/42790 (Nov 29, 2019), NSE/SURV/59425 (Nov 17, 2023), NSE/SURV/64066 (Sep 20, 2024), and NSE/SURV/73167 (Mar 06, 2026). The GSM framework provides a progressive 4-stage surveillance escalation mechanism.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must apply a 100% margin rate when executing trades in MORARJEE TEXTILES LIMITED.
- Members must enforce the 5% (or lower) price band for this security.
- Members are required to take adequate precaution and inform their clients about the GSM placement.
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 14, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 15, 2026 (GSM Stage I restrictions apply from this date)

## Impact Assessment

The placement of MORARJEE TEXTILES LIMITED in GSM Stage I significantly restricts normal trading activity. The 100% margin requirement means traders must fully fund positions upfront, eliminating leverage and substantially reducing trading volumes. The 5% price band limits daily price movement, curbing volatility but also constraining price discovery. Retail and institutional investors holding or planning to trade this stock will face higher capital requirements. Liquidity is expected to decrease. If the security progresses to higher GSM stages, further restrictions including Trade-for-Trade settlement, weekly-only trading, and Additional Surveillance Deposits would apply.
