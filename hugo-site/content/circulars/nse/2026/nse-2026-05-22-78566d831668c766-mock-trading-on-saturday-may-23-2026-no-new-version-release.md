## Summary

NSE will conduct a mock trading session in the Capital Market segment on Saturday, May 23, 2026. The session covers both Primary Site (Trading Session-1) and Disaster Recovery (DR) Site (Trading Session-2) operations. No new NEAT+ version is being released for this mock; existing versions 7.8.7 and 7.8.8 remain applicable.

## Key Points

- Mock trading scheduled for Saturday, May 23, 2026 in the Capital Market segment
- Two trading sessions: Session-1 from Primary Site and Session-2 from DR Site
- No new NEAT+ version release for this mock session
- NEAT+ versions 7.8.7 and 7.8.8 are applicable; version 7.8.7 discontinuation date is June 06, 2026 (mock)
- New NEAT Adapter application version 1.0.27 (Windows and Linux) should be used with GR PORT 10823
- All outstanding orders will be purged before each session
- Trades during mock sessions carry no financial obligation (no fund pay-in or pay-out)
- Members must ensure only valid and compliant UCC/PAN are used

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine operational mock trading circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use NEAT+ version 7.8.7 or 7.8.8 for the mock session
- Members using NNF software must manually clear orders in their systems before each session
- Members should use NEAT Adapter version 1.0.27 with GR PORT 10823 for Capital Market segment login
- Refer to circular NSE/MSD/73993 dated April 30, 2026 for interactive connectivity details
- Refer to circular NSE/MSD/73674 dated April 09, 2026 for NEAT Adapter Exe application details
- Members may use Mock Trading or Simulated Environment to meet SEBI regulatory requirements (per NSE/MSD/46441 and SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234)

## Important Dates

- **May 23, 2026**: Mock trading session date
- **June 06, 2026**: Discontinuation date for NEAT+ version 7.8.7 (mock)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine mock/drill exercise with no financial impact on markets or participants. Trades executed during the mock session will not result in any fund pay-in or pay-out. The session is intended to help members test their systems, validate connectivity parameters, and ensure readiness for live trading including DR site switchover procedures. Impact is limited to operational preparation for Capital Market segment members.
