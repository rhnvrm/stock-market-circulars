## Summary

NSE's Surveillance department has shortlisted 10 securities under the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) Stage I, effective June 23, 2026. No securities have been added to ST-ASM Stage II (Nil). The action follows prior ASM circulars and mandates enhanced margin requirements on affected scrips.

## Key Points

- 10 securities added to ST-ASM Stage I with a minimum applicable margin of 50% (or existing margin, whichever is higher), capped at 100%.
- ST-ASM Stage II has no new additions (Nil).
- Margin requirements apply to all open positions as on June 22, 2026 and all new positions created from June 23, 2026 onwards.
- Shortlisting is purely for market surveillance purposes and should not be construed as adverse action against the concerned companies.
- Upon exit from the framework, the price band of a scrip reverts to its pre-ASM level, unless covered by another surveillance measure.

## Regulatory Changes

Securities meeting the ST-ASM criteria have been shortlisted under Stage I of the framework in accordance with Exchange Circular nos. NSE/SURV/39265, NSE/SURV/46557, NSE/SURV/52144, NSE/SURV/58558, and NSE/SURV/64066. The ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must collect a minimum margin of 50% (or existing margin, whichever is higher) on ST-ASM Stage I securities, subject to a maximum cap of 100%.
- Members must apply updated margin requirements on all open positions held as on June 22, 2026 and on any new positions created from June 23, 2026.
- Members should refer to NSE FAQs on Additional Surveillance Measure for detailed guidance: https://www.nseindia.com/regulations/additional-surveillance-measure
- Queries can be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in.

## Important Dates

- **June 19, 2026**: Circular issued.
- **June 22, 2026**: Reference date for open positions subject to new margin requirements.
- **June 23, 2026**: ST-ASM Stage I margin requirements become effective for open and new positions.

## Impact Assessment

Trading in the 10 shortlisted Stage I securities will become more capital-intensive from June 23, 2026, as market participants must maintain at least 50% margin (up to 100%). This may reduce speculative activity, compress trading volumes, and increase costs for existing position holders in these scrips. The securities span diverse sectors including environment/water treatment (CEWATER), prefab construction (EPACKPEB), carbon materials (HIGREEN), financial services (HYBRIDFIN, WTICAB), laminates (LAMOSAIC), cloud services (MCLOUD), real estate (MRIL), power (POWERICA), and IT systems (SAHANA). Stage II (100% margin) has no additions this cycle, suggesting the exchange found no securities meeting the more stringent Stage II criteria.
