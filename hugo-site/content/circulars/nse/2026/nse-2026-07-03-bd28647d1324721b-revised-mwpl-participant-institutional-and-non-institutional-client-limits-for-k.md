## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has issued a revision to the Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL) along with Participant, Institutional Client and Non-Institutional Client limits under the Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) Scheme for four symbols: KRISHANA, MBAPL, HEALTHADD and MIDQ50ADD. This revision follows the earlier circular NCL/CMPT/73999 dated April 30, 2026, and a partial modification via circular NCL/CMPT/74959 dated June 30, 2026. The new limits are significantly higher than the old limits and will apply for the month of July 2026, effective July 06, 2026.

## Key Points

- Circular Ref. No: 069/2026, Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/75043, dated July 03, 2026
- Revised limits apply to KRISHANA, MBAPL, HEALTHADD and MIDQ50ADD in the SLB Scheme
- Old MWPL: KRISHANA 1,673,350; MBAPL 2,211,817; HEALTHADD 82,532; MIDQ50ADD 455,101 shares
- New MWPL: KRISHANA 8,366,753; MBAPL 11,059,088; HEALTHADD 825,322; MIDQ50ADD 4,551,019 shares
- Institutional, Participant and Non-Institutional client limits have been correspondingly revised upward for all four symbols
- Issued by Anil Suvarana, Associate Vice President, NSE Clearing Limited

## Regulatory Changes

The Market Wide Position Limit and associated Participant, Institutional Client and Non-Institutional Client limits for the four symbols have been increased substantially (roughly 5x for KRISHANA, MBAPL and MIDQ50ADD, and 10x for HEALTHADD) compared to the previously notified limits.

## Compliance Requirements

All Participants and Custodians dealing in KRISHANA, MBAPL, HEALTHADD and MIDQ50ADD under the SLB Scheme must adhere to the revised MWPL, Participant, Institutional Client and Non-Institutional Client limits effective July 06, 2026.

## Important Dates

- July 03, 2026: Circular issued
- July 06, 2026: Revised limits become effective
- Applicable for the month of July 2026

## Impact Assessment

The upward revision of position limits increases the permissible lending and borrowing activity in these four securities under the SLB Scheme, providing greater flexibility to Participants and clients. The impact is limited to market participants actively trading or lending/borrowing these specific symbols, with no broader market-wide implications.
