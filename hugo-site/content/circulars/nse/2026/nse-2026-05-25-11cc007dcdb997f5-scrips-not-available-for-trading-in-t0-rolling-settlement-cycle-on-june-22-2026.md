## Summary

NSE has notified all members that four securities — Adani Enterprises Limited (ADANIENT), Cyient Limited (CYIENT), L&T Technology Services Limited (LTTS), and TVS Motor Company Limited (TVSMOTOR) — will not be available for trading in the T+0 rolling settlement cycle on June 22, 2026. This is due to these securities moving in or out of an index on which derivatives contracts are available for trading, as a result of index rebalancing.

## Key Points

- Four scrips are excluded from T+0 rolling settlement on June 22, 2026: ADANIENT, CYIENT, LTTS, and TVSMOTOR
- The exclusion is triggered by index rebalancing — securities are moving in or out of an index on which derivatives contracts are listed
- This circular references Chapter 1.6 of Exchange circular NSE/CMTR/73927 dated April 28, 2026, which governs T+0 rolling settlement in the Capital Market segment
- The restriction applies only to T+0 settlement; normal T+1 or other settlement cycles are not mentioned as affected

## Regulatory Changes

No permanent regulatory changes. This is a one-time operational notification under the existing T+0 rolling settlement framework established by NSE/CMTR/73927 dated April 28, 2026. The exclusion is a standard provision under Chapter 1.6 of that circular, which allows NSE to temporarily remove scrips from T+0 eligibility during index rebalancing events.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must note that the four listed securities (ADANIENT, CYIENT, LTTS, TVSMOTOR) cannot be traded in the T+0 rolling settlement cycle on June 22, 2026
- Members should update their trading systems and inform their clients accordingly to avoid settlement failures
- No action is required other than ensuring trades in these scrips on June 22, 2026 are routed through standard settlement cycles, not T+0

## Important Dates

- **May 25, 2026**: Circular issued
- **June 22, 2026**: Date on which ADANIENT, CYIENT, LTTS, and TVSMOTOR are not available for T+0 rolling settlement

## Impact Assessment

The impact is moderate and time-bound. Traders and investors who rely on same-day (T+0) settlement for these four scrips will need to use alternative settlement cycles on June 22, 2026. The restriction is driven by index rebalancing, which may also coincide with elevated trading volumes and volatility in these scrips. Members with automated T+0 trading strategies for these securities must adjust their systems for that date. The affected companies span large-cap and mid-cap segments (Adani Enterprises, TVS Motor, L&T Technology Services, Cyient), so the operational impact could be notable for active traders in these names.
