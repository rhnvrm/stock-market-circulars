## Summary

NSE has issued a circular (Ref No: NSE/SURV/74476, Circular Ref. No: 400/2026) updating the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework for companies under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). No new securities are being added to any ASM IBC stage, while 8 securities have satisfied the exclusion criteria and are moving out of the restrictive framework, effective June 01, 2026.

## Key Points

- No securities are being newly shortlisted under ASM IBC (Annexure I shows 'Nil' for all categories including Stage I, Stage I to II upgrades, and Stage II to I downgrades)
- 8 securities listed in Annexure II satisfy the exclusion criteria and are exiting the ASM IBC framework effective June 01, 2026
- ASM IBC framework continues to operate in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures
- Price bands for securities exiting the framework will revert to the band applicable before ASM IBC shortlisting, unless covered by another surveillance measure
- Shortlisting under ASM IBC is a market surveillance action and should not be construed as an adverse action against the company

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued under the ongoing ASM IBC framework established by earlier circulars (NSE/SURV/37979 dated June 08, 2018; NSE/SURV/52368 dated May 20, 2022; NSE/SURV/55582 dated February 10, 2023; NSE/SURV/70074 dated September 05, 2025). No structural changes to the framework are introduced; this is a periodic update to the list of shortlisted securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE Members must take note of the updated ASM IBC security lists
- Trading in the 8 exiting securities will revert to normal price bands (subject to no other surveillance framework applying)
- Members should communicate updates to their clients regarding applicable price bands for the affected securities
- For queries, members may contact surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: May 29, 2026
- Effective Date: June 01, 2026 (for both Annexure I additions and Annexure II exclusions)

## Impact Assessment

The exit of 8 securities from ASM IBC stages is a positive development for those stocks, as it removes the heightened surveillance-related trading restrictions. Notable exits include GENSOL (Gensol Engineering Limited) and RELINFRA (Reliance Infrastructure Limited), both high-profile companies. The removal of ASM IBC restrictions may lead to increased trading activity and wider price bands for these securities. The fact that Annexure I shows 'Nil' for all new shortlistings indicates no incremental tightening of surveillance in this update cycle. Market participants should verify whether any of the 8 securities remain under other surveillance measures (GSM, ESM, etc.) before assuming full restoration of normal trading conditions.
