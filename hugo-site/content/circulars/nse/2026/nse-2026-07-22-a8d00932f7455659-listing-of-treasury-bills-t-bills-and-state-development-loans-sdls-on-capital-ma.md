## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of three Treasury Bills (T-bills) and ten State Development Loans (SDLs) for dealings on the Capital Market segment, effective July 24, 2026. The circular specifies the designated security codes, series, lot sizes, coupon rates, and maturity dates for each instrument, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.

## Key Points

- 13 new debt securities admitted to trading on NSE Capital Market segment from July 24, 2026
- Instruments include 3 Treasury Bills (91-day, 182-day, and 364-day) and 10 State Development Loans issued by various states
- All securities carry a uniform lot size of 100
- Trading will be identified solely by designated security codes and conducted in the specified lot sizes
- SDLs span states including Odisha, Assam, Delhi, West Bengal, Uttarakhand, Madhya Pradesh, and Bihar

## Regulatory Changes

None; this is a routine notification under existing Regulations 3.1.1 and 2.5.5 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A regarding admission of new securities and their lot sizes.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must ensure trades in these securities are executed using the correct designated security codes and in the specified lot size of 100 units.

## Important Dates

- Effective date: July 24, 2026
- Maturities range from October 22, 2026 (91-day T-bill) to July 22, 2048 (SDL UK 7.7% 2048)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative listing that expands the set of tradable government debt instruments on NSE's Capital Market segment. It has no direct bearing on equity market operations or listed companies, but is relevant to debt market participants, primary dealers, and institutional investors trading in government securities.
