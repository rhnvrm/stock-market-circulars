## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a partial modification to its earlier circular (018/2026, dated April 30, 2026) regarding bank guarantees accepted as collateral in the Currency Derivatives Segment. The update clarifies minimum validity periods and claim period rules for both electronic and physical bank guarantees used for margin deposits.

## Key Points

- Circular reference: NCL/CD/74058 (Circular Ref. No. 019/2026), issued May 05, 2026
- Partially modifies Item 10.3.3.2 (iii) of Part B of circular 018/2026 (NCL/CD/73998, April 30, 2026)
- Two distinct rules established: one for Electronic Bank Guarantees and one for Physical Bank Guarantees
- No changes to existing collateral formats for providing bank guarantees

## Regulatory Changes

**Electronic Bank Guarantee:**
- Minimum validity period: 7 days
- Minimum claim period: 7 days
- If no separate claim period is specified, minimum total period shall be 14 days
- If the issuing bank does not provide a specific claim period beyond the expiry date, the maturity period shall be reduced by 7 days (treated as the claim period)

**Physical Bank Guarantee:**
- Minimum validity period: 3 months
- If the issuing bank does not provide a specific claim period beyond the expiry date, the maturity period shall be reduced by 7 days (treated as the claim period)

## Compliance Requirements

- All members of the Currency Derivatives Segment must ensure bank guarantees submitted as margin collateral comply with the updated validity and claim period requirements
- Members should verify that bank guarantees from issuing banks include an explicit claim period; if absent, the effective maturity is reduced by 7 days accordingly
- No changes required to existing collateral submission formats

## Important Dates

- Circular date: May 05, 2026
- References prior circular NCL/CD/73998 dated April 30, 2026
- No explicit future effective date stated; modification is immediate upon issuance

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on clearing members in the Currency Derivatives Segment. Members must review bank guarantees they currently hold or intend to submit to ensure compliance with updated minimum validity and claim period thresholds. The reduction of effective maturity by 7 days (when no explicit claim period is stated) may affect planning for guarantee renewals. No market-wide or systemic impact is expected; the change is administrative and risk-management oriented, aimed at ensuring adequate claim windows for NSE Clearing to enforce margin deposits.
