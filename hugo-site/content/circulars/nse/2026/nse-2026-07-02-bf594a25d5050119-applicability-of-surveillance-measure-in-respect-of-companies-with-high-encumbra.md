## Summary

NSE has issued a circular updating the list of securities subject to the Surveillance Measure applicable to companies with high 'Encumbrance' as per Regulation 28(3) of SEBI (SAST) Regulations, 2011. This continues the framework introduced via earlier circulars dated October 24, 2019 and April 17, 2020. Four securities (EMBDL, GPTINFRA, PATELENG, THEMISMED) have been newly included and will attract a minimum 35% margin, while six securities (AAVAS, GENUSPOWER, NAZARA, RTNPOWER, TRUALT, HINDZINC) have been excluded from the framework.

## Key Points

- Annexure I lists 4 securities newly added to the high-encumbrance surveillance framework: Embassy Developments Limited (EMBDL), GPT Infraprojects Limited (GPTINFRA), Patel Engineering Limited (PATELENG), and Themis Medicare Limited (THEMISMED).
- Annexure II lists 6 securities excluded from the framework: Aavas Financiers Limited (AAVAS), Genus Power Infrastructures Limited (GENUSPOWER), Nazara Technologies Limited (NAZARA), RattanIndia Power Limited (RTNPOWER), TruAlt Bioenergy Limited (TRUALT), and Hindustan Zinc Limited (HINDZINC).
- Annexure III provides a consolidated list of 10 securities currently under the framework, including MSP Steel & Power Limited (MSPL), Artemis Medicare Services Limited (ARTEMISMED), Indo Borax & Chemicals Limited (INDOBORAX), Nuvama Wealth Management Limited (NUVAMA), Vedanta Limited (VEDL), NRB Bearing Limited (NRBBEARING), and the 4 newly added securities.
- This surveillance measure operates alongside other prevailing surveillance measures and is subject to periodic review.
- Inclusion under this framework is purely a market surveillance action and should not be construed as an adverse regulatory action against the company.

## Regulatory Changes

Securities are shortlisted or excluded based on satisfying/no longer satisfying the criteria for high promoter 'Encumbrance' under Regulation 28(3) of SEBI (SAST) Regulations, 2011. This is a periodic update, not a new regulatory framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must apply a minimum 35% margin in the Equity and Equity Derivatives segments on the newly included securities (Annexure I), applicable to all open positions as of July 06, 2026 and new positions created from July 07, 2026.
- Companies wishing to represent against inclusion in the framework must submit their representation to the Exchange by 5:00 PM on July 03, 2026.
- Members should update risk and margin systems to reflect the revised inclusion/exclusion list.

## Important Dates

- **July 03, 2026**: Exclusion of Annexure II securities from the framework takes effect; deadline (5:00 PM) for company representations against inclusion.
- **July 06, 2026**: Reference date for open positions subject to the new 35% minimum margin.
- **July 07, 2026**: 35% minimum margin becomes effective on Annexure I securities for open and new positions.

## Impact Assessment

The 35% minimum margin requirement on EMBDL, GPTINFRA, PATELENG, and THEMISMED will materially increase the cost of trading and could reduce liquidity and leverage available to traders in these stocks, particularly in the derivatives segment. Conversely, removal of AAVAS, GENUSPOWER, NAZARA, RTNPOWER, TRUALT, and HINDZINC from the framework eases margin requirements and may improve trading activity in these names. Market participants holding positions in the affected securities should reassess margin funding and position sizing ahead of the effective dates.
