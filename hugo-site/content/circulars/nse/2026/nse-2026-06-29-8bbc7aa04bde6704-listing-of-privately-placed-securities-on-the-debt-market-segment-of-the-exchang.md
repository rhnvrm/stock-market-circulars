## Summary

NSE, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations, has admitted privately placed Commercial Papers (CPs) from ten companies to dealings on its Debt Market Segment with effect from June 29, 2026. All securities were allotted on June 25, 2026, with face value of ₹5,00,000 each and varying redemption dates.

## Key Points

- Ten companies have had their Commercial Papers listed on NSE's Debt Market Segment effective June 29, 2026
- All CPs were allotted on June 25, 2026
- Face value for all instruments is ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Redemption periods range from short-term (July 2026) to medium-term (March 2027)
- Issued under NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Regulation 3.1.1
- Circular reference: NSE/CML/74918, Ref No. 1037/2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification under existing NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

All Members of NSE are notified to take note of the newly admitted securities and their designated security codes as specified in the Annexure. No additional compliance action is required beyond standard debt market trading procedures.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with no broad market impact. The listing enables institutional participants to trade these Commercial Papers on NSE's Debt Market Segment, providing liquidity to existing holders. The largest issuance by volume is Sundaram Finance Limited (20,000 units) and Axis Securities Limited (10,000 units). Two CPs have longer durations extending to March 2027 (Toyota Financial Services and ICICI Securities), while the remainder mature by September 2026.
