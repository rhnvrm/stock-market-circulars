## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued revised formats for bank guarantees and related documents used by Clearing Members to maintain margin deposits in the Currency Derivatives segment. The circular provides updated annexures, including the Format of Bank Guarantee for Margin Deposit (Fungible), to be used by Clearing Members and approved banks going forward.

## Key Points

- The circular attaches revised Annexure formats (e.g., Annexure 1(A), 1(B)) applicable to the Currency Derivatives segment.
- Annexure 1(A) provides the standard format for a Bank Guarantee for Margin Deposit (Fungible) to be issued by an NCL-approved bank in favour of NSE Clearing Limited.
- The guarantee format requires details of the Clearing Member (individual proprietor, partnership/LLP, or company), the guarantee amount, and reference to a Master Agreement executed between the issuing bank and NCL.
- The bank's liability under the guarantee is capped at the specified guaranteed amount as stated in the format.

## Regulatory Changes

NCL has updated the standardized formats/templates that Clearing Members and their bankers must use when submitting bank guarantees as margin deposits for the Currency Derivatives segment. This is a documentation/format standardization change rather than a change in margin policy itself.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing Members in the Currency Derivatives segment intending to submit or renew bank guarantees for margin deposits must use the revised formats specified in the annexures.
- Approved banks issuing guarantees on behalf of Clearing Members need to align their guarantee documentation with the updated format, including reference to the Master Agreement with NCL.

## Important Dates

No specific effective date or deadline is detailed in the extracted content; Clearing Members should refer to the full circular for the applicability date of the revised formats.

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact operational/compliance circular affecting documentation procedures for Clearing Members and banks in the Currency Derivatives segment. It does not affect trading, listed securities, or market pricing, but non-compliance with the correct format could cause delays in processing margin deposit guarantees.
