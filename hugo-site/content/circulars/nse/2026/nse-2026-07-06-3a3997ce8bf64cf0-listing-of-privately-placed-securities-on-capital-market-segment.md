## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of privately placed debt securities on the Capital Market segment, effective July 07, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A. The circular specifies the designated security codes and lot sizes for three fresh issues from NIIF Infrastructure Finance Limited and Alpha Alternatives Financial Services Private Limited.

## Key Points

- Three privately placed debt securities are being freshly admitted to dealings on NSE's Capital Market segment.
- NIIF Infrastructure Finance Limited: Symbol 795NIIF36A (Series N0), "NIIF 7.95% 2036 Sr PP3", coupon rate 7.95%, maturity 29-May-2036, ISIN INE246R07921, market lot 1.
- NIIF Infrastructure Finance Limited: Symbol 780NIIF31 (Series N0), "NIIF 7.80% 2031 Sr PP3", coupon rate 7.80%, maturity 27-Nov-2031, ISIN INE246R07913, market lot 1.
- Alpha Alternatives Financial Services Private Limited: Symbol AAFS32A (Series N0), "AAFS Mkt lkd 2032 CAR M", coupon rate reset-based (market-linked), maturity 24-Feb-2032, ISIN INE0L6807351, market lot 1.
- Each security will be identified for trading purposes solely by its designated code, and will trade in the specified lot size.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is a routine notification under existing Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A, governing admission and lot sizes of newly listed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must recognize and trade these securities only by their designated security codes (795NIIF36A, 780NIIF31, AAFS32A) and adhere to the specified market lot of 1 unit each from the effective date.

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: July 06, 2026
- Effective Date of Listing: July 07, 2026
- Maturity Dates: 27-Nov-2031 (780NIIF31), 24-Feb-2032 (AAFS32A), 29-May-2036 (795NIIF36A)

## Impact Assessment

This circular affects only holders and prospective traders of these specific privately placed debt instruments; it has minimal impact on broader equity or debt markets. It formalizes exchange-level tradability for institutional/qualified investors holding these bonds, but does not affect any publicly listed equity stocks.
