## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (formerly NSCCL) has issued the settlement calendar for May 2026 for the Capital Market Segment. The calendar covers five settlement types: Normal Rolling T+1 (Type M), Normal Auction T+1 (Type A), Trade for Trade T+1 (Type Z), ITP for SME T+1 (Type B), and Normal Rolling T+0 (Type 5), pursuant to Regulation 6.2 of the NSCCL Capital Market Regulations, 1997.

## Key Points

- Settlement schedules cover the full month of May 2026 (04-May-26 to 29-May-26)
- Five settlement types are covered: M (Normal Rolling T+1), A (Auction Normal), Z (Trade for Trade T+1), B (ITP for SME T+1), and 5 (Normal Rolling T+0)
- Settlement numbers for Normal Rolling T+1 run from 2026081 to 2026099
- Settlement on 28-May-26 is skipped (likely a holiday/non-business day)
- Last settlement of May (2026099) settles on 01-Jun-26
- Contact for queries: 1800 266 0050 (IVR Option 2) or securities_ops@nsccl.co.in

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notification issued under Regulation 6.2 of the National Securities Clearing Corporation (Capital Market Regulations, 1997).

## Compliance Requirements

- All Members, Custodians, and PCMs must adhere to the prescribed settlement dates for each settlement type
- Custodial confirmation must be completed by the dates specified in the calendar (typically T+1 business day after trade date for T+1 settlements)
- Participants must ensure funds and securities obligations are met by the respective settlement dates

## Important Dates

- Settlement period: 04-May-2026 to 29-May-2026
- Settlement numbers: 2026081 (04-May-26) through 2026099 (29-May-26)
- Notable gap: No settlement on 28-May-26 (trades on 27-May-26 settle on 29-May-26)
- Final May settlement date: 01-Jun-2026 (for trades on 29-May-26)
- Circular date: 06-Apr-2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine monthly circular with medium operational impact. All capital market participants — members, custodians, and PCMs — need to align their back-office and settlement systems with the published calendar. The inclusion of both T+1 and T+0 settlement schedules reflects the dual-track settlement framework currently in operation. No market disruption is expected; participants should note the holiday-adjusted settlement dates (e.g., trades on 08-May-26 and 22-May-26 settle on 11-May-26 and 25-May-26 respectively, indicating weekends/holidays).
