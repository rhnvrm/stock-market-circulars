## Summary

The National Stock Exchange of India has declared Thursday, January 15, 2026 as a trading holiday for the debt segment on account of Municipal Corporation Elections in Maharashtra. This circular partially modifies the earlier exchange circular NSE/DS/71968 dated December 23, 2025.

## Key Points

- Trading holiday declared for debt segment only
- Date: Thursday, January 15, 2026
- Reason: Municipal Corporation Elections in Maharashtra
- Applies to all members and participants of the debt segment
- Modifies previous circular NSE/DS/71968 dated December 23, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

This circular serves as a partial modification to the previously announced holiday calendar for 2026, specifically adding January 15, 2026 as an additional trading holiday for the debt segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and participants of the debt segment must note the trading holiday
- Operational adjustments required for settlement schedules around this date
- No trading activity permitted in debt segment on January 15, 2026

## Important Dates

- **January 15, 2026**: Trading holiday for debt segment
- **Circular Issue Date**: January 09, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This trading holiday will impact all debt segment trading activities on January 15, 2026. Members and participants need to plan their trading and settlement operations accordingly. The holiday is specific to the debt segment and does not affect other market segments unless separately notified. Settlement cycles and obligation calculations will be adjusted to account for this non-trading day.
