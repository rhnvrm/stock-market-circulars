## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued revised formats (Annexure 1(A) and 1(B)) for Bank Guarantees furnished by Clearing Members towards margin deposits in the Capital Market Segment. Annexure 1(A) covers the format for a Fungible Bank Guarantee for Margin Deposit, while Annexure 1(B) covers a related format, both to be used by banks approved by NCL when issuing guarantees on behalf of Clearing Members (individuals, partnership/LLP firms, or companies).

## Key Points

- Revised bank guarantee formats apply to margin deposits maintained by Clearing Members with NCL in the Capital Market Segment.
- The guarantee must be issued by a bank approved by NCL and is governed by the terms of a Master Agreement executed between the issuing Bank and NCL.
- The format accommodates Clearing Members structured as sole proprietorships, partnership/LLP firms, or companies incorporated under the Companies Act.
- The Bank's liability under the guarantee is capped at the specified guarantee amount stated in the instrument.
- The guarantee must specify the BG number, date, and the value of the margin deposit being secured.

## Regulatory Changes

NSE/NCL has updated the standardized templates for Bank Guarantees used for margin deposit purposes, ensuring consistency in the legal language, party descriptions, and liability caps across guarantees submitted by approved banks on behalf of Clearing Members.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing Members relying on bank guarantees for margin deposits must ensure guarantees are issued using the updated Annexure 1(A)/1(B) formats.
- Approved banks issuing such guarantees must align with the revised format and reference the Master Agreement executed with NCL.
- Clearing Members and their banks should update internal documentation templates to reflect the revised format before submission to NCL.

## Important Dates

Effective date: 2025-07-25 (date of circular). No additional transition timeline specified in the extracted content.

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact operational/administrative circular affecting documentation standards for Clearing Members' margin deposit bank guarantees. It does not affect trading, listed securities, or market pricing, but ensures continued regulatory compliance for Clearing Members maintaining bank guarantee-based margin deposits with NCL.
