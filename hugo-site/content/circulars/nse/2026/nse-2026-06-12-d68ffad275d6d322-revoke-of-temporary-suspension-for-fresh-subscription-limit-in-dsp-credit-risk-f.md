## Summary

NSE has notified its members that DSP Mutual Fund is discontinuing the temporary aggregate investment cap of INR 10 crore per PAN in DSP Credit Risk Fund. The restriction, which was in place since January 1, 2026, will be lifted effective June 15, 2026. Post this date, fresh subscriptions through all modes — Purchase, Switch-in, SIP, and STP-in — will no longer be subject to any per-PAN cap on the NSE MF Invest Platform.

## Key Points

- DSP Credit Risk Fund had imposed a per-PAN aggregate investment cap of INR 10 crore since January 1, 2026, covering existing investments and all fresh inflows.
- DSP Mutual Fund has issued a Notice-cum-Addendum to the SID and KIM of the scheme, formally revoking the restriction.
- The cap is being discontinued with effect from **June 15, 2026**.
- Fresh subscriptions via Purchase, Switch-in, SIP, and STP-in will be unrestricted from that date.
- NSE has updated the NSE MF Invest Platform accordingly.

## Regulatory Changes

An addendum has been issued to the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM) of DSP Credit Risk Fund. The addendum overrides any conflicting provisions in the existing SID/KIM regarding the INR 10 crore per-PAN aggregate investment restriction. All other provisions of the SID and KIM remain unchanged.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and distributors on the NSE MF Invest Platform should note the updated subscription parameters for DSP Credit Risk Fund effective June 15, 2026.
- No further action is required from investors beyond being aware that the cap has been lifted.
- DSP Asset Managers Private Limited requests unit holders to keep their PAN, KYC, email, mobile number, and nominee details updated with the AMC, and to link PAN with Aadhaar.

## Important Dates

- **January 1, 2026**: Aggregate investment cap of INR 10 crore per PAN introduced in DSP Credit Risk Fund.
- **June 8, 2026**: Date of the Notice-cum-Addendum issued by DSP Asset Managers Private Limited.
- **June 12, 2026**: NSE circular date notifying members of the change.
- **June 15, 2026**: Effective date for removal of the per-PAN investment cap.

## Impact Assessment

The removal of the investment cap restores full subscription flexibility in DSP Credit Risk Fund for investors and distributors on the NSE MF Invest Platform. High-net-worth investors and institutional participants who may have been constrained by the INR 10 crore PAN-wise limit can now invest without restriction. This change may see increased inflows into the scheme, particularly via bulk purchases, Switch-ins, SIPs, and STPs. The operational impact on the platform is minimal as NSE has already incorporated the change.
