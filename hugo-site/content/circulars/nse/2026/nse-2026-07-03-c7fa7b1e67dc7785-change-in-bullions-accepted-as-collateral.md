## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a partial modification to its earlier circular (Download Ref No: NCL/COM/0156 dated April 30, 2026) concerning bullion accepted as collateral. Clearing members in the Commodity Derivatives Segment can now place Gold and Silver bullion as collateral in multiples of 1 gram, replacing the previous requirement of multiples of 1 kg.

## Key Points

- Bullion (Gold and Silver) accepted as collateral can now be placed in multiples of 1 gm.
- This replaces the earlier requirement of multiples of 1 kg.
- All other provisions related to bullion collateral remain unchanged.
- The change applies to all Members, Custodians, and PCM (Professional Clearing Members) in the Commodity Derivatives Segment.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular partially modifies Item 6 of Part B of circular ref. no. 74020 (Download Ref No: NCL/COM/0156 dated April 30, 2026), specifically reducing the minimum denomination unit for bullion collateral from 1 kg to 1 gm.

## Compliance Requirements

Clearing members, custodians, and PCMs dealing in bullion collateral must adjust their internal systems and processes to accommodate collateral submissions in gram-level multiples starting the effective date.

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: July 03, 2026
- Effective Date: July 06, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The move to gram-level multiples significantly increases flexibility for clearing members in pledging bullion as collateral, allowing for more precise and efficient collateral management, particularly benefiting smaller members or those with limited bullion holdings. This is an operational enhancement with no direct market-wide trading impact but improves capital efficiency within the commodity derivatives clearing ecosystem.
