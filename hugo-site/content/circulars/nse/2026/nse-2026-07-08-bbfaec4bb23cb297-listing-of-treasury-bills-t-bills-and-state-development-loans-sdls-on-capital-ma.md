## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of 16 State Development Loans (SDLs) issued by various state governments and 3 Government of India Treasury Bills (T-bills) on its Capital Market segment. The securities, along with their designated symbols, series, lot sizes, and ISIN codes, are specified in the circular annexure. This listing takes effect from July 10, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Key Points

- 16 SDLs from states including Himachal Pradesh, Kerala, Sikkim, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, West Bengal, Jharkhand, Jammu & Kashmir, Bihar, Manipur, and Chhattisgarh are being listed.
- 3 Treasury Bills are being listed: 91-day T-bill maturing 08-Oct-2026, 182-day T-bill maturing 07-Jan-2027, and 364-day T-bill maturing 08-Jul-2027.
- All securities are listed under the "SG" (state government securities) and "TB" (treasury bill) series with a uniform lot size of 100.
- Coupon rates on the SDLs range from 7.07% to 7.65%, with maturities spanning 2031 to 2052.
- Each security is identified by a unique symbol and ISIN code as specified in the annexure.
- Trading and identification of these securities on the system will be strictly by their designated codes and specified lot sizes.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is a routine notification under existing NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations (Regulation 3.1.1 for admission of securities and Regulation 2.5.5 for lot sizes).

## Compliance Requirements

Members must trade these securities using only their designated security codes and in the specified lot size of 100 units. No additional compliance action is required beyond adherence to standard trading protocols for these newly admitted instruments.

## Important Dates

- **July 8, 2026**: Circular issued.
- **July 10, 2026**: Effective date for listing and trading of the SDLs and T-bills on the Capital Market segment.
- **Maturity dates**: Range from October 8, 2026 (91-day T-bill) to July 8, 2052 (SDL WB 7.65% 2052).

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, low-impact administrative circular for debt market participants and members dealing in government securities. It does not affect equity trading, corporate actions, or listed companies. Relevance is limited to institutional and retail investors trading in state development loans and treasury bills on NSE's capital market segment.
