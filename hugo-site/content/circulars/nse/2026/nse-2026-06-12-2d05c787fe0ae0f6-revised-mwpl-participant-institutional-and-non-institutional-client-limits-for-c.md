## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has revised the Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL) and the position limits for Participants, Institutional Clients, and Non-Institutional Clients for the symbol **CUB** (City Union Bank) in the Securities Lending and Borrowing Scheme (SLBS) for June 2026. The revision is effective from **June 15, 2026**, and is a partial modification of the earlier circular NCL/CMPT/74480 dated May 29, 2026.

## Key Points

- MWPL for CUB increases from 74,304,726 shares to **99,072,943 shares**.
- Institutional Client Limit increases from 7,430,472 shares to **9,907,294 shares**.
- Participant Limit increases from 7,430,472 shares to **9,907,294 shares**.
- Non-Institutional Client Limit increases from 743,047 shares to **990,729 shares**.
- This revision supersedes limits set under NCL/CMPT/74480 (May 29, 2026), which itself partially modified NCL/CMPT/73999 (April 30, 2026).

## Regulatory Changes

Position limits in the SLBS for CUB are being revised upward across all categories, reflecting a change in the underlying free-float or eligible shares for the securities lending segment.

| Category | Old Limit (Shares) | New Limit (Shares) |
|---|---|---|
| Market Wide Position Limit | 74,304,726 | 99,072,943 |
| Institutional Client Limit | 7,430,472 | 9,907,294 |
| Participant Limit | 7,430,472 | 9,907,294 |
| Non-Institutional Client Limit | 743,047 | 990,729 |

## Compliance Requirements

- All Participants and Custodians in the SLBS must take note of the revised limits for CUB.
- Positions held in the SLBS for CUB must comply with the new limits from the effective date onward.
- No specific action is required other than ensuring ongoing compliance with the updated thresholds.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 12, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 15, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative update with limited market impact. The upward revision of MWPL and participant limits for CUB in the SLBS provides marginally greater lending and borrowing capacity for this security. It affects only participants and clients actively engaged in the Securities Lending and Borrowing Scheme for CUB, and has no direct bearing on equity trading or derivatives markets.
