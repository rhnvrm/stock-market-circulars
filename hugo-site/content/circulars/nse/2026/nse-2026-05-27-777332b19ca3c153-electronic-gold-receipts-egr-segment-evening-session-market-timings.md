## Summary

NSE's Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) department (Circular Ref. No: 10/2026, Download Ref: NSE/EGR/74440) clarifies evening session market timings for EGR trading, supplementing the earlier circular NSE/EGR/74203 dated May 13, 2026. On specific national holidays, while morning sessions remain closed, the evening session (17:00–23:30/23:55 hrs) will remain open.

## Key Points

- Evening session for EGR trading runs from 17:00 hrs to 23:30 hrs (during US Daylight Saving Spring season) or 23:55 hrs (after US Daylight Saving Fall season).
- Pre-open session starts at 16:45 hrs; random closure occurs within the last minute, latest by 16:53 hrs.
- Trade modification time ends at 23:45 hrs (Spring DST) or 23:59 hrs (Fall DST).
- On listed holidays, the morning session (09:00–17:00 hrs) is closed but the evening session remains open.
- Exchange will endeavor to provide trading system access by 12:00 hrs on such holidays for login and connectivity checks.
- All other details from circular NSE/EGR/74203 dated May 13, 2026 remain unchanged.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular serves as a clarification to Chapter 2.1 (Trading Holidays) and Chapter 2.2 (Trading Hours) of NSE/EGR/74203 dated May 13, 2026. No new rules are introduced; existing provisions are clarified regarding the evening session specifically.

## Compliance Requirements

- EGR segment members should note revised holiday-specific session schedules and update their systems accordingly.
- Members are advised to log in as early as possible on holiday dates (by 12:00 hrs) to avoid any inconvenience during the evening session.

## Impact Assessment

This circular primarily affects members and participants trading in the EGR segment on NSE. The clarification ensures members are aware that trading activity in gold receipts can continue via the evening session even on designated morning-session holidays. This is operationally significant for EGR traders who need to plan their connectivity and order management systems around these dates. There is no broader equity market or systemic impact.
