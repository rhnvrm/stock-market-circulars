## Summary

NSE has announced the applicability of the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework with updates effective June 17, 2026. Nine securities have been newly added to ST-ASM Stage I, while ten securities have been excluded from the ASM framework (two moved to LTASM, one moved to ESM, and seven fully removed).

## Key Points

- 9 securities added to ST-ASM Stage I effective June 17, 2026
- No securities shortlisted for ST-ASM Stage II
- No movement between Stage I and Stage II
- 10 securities excluded from the ASM framework effective June 17, 2026
- 2 securities (BLUSPRING, NIBE) moved from ST-ASM to LTASM framework
- 1 security (NRL - Nupur Recyclers Limited) moved from ST-ASM to ESM framework

## Regulatory Changes

The ST-ASM framework is updated periodically by NSE based on surveillance criteria. Securities placed under ST-ASM are subject to enhanced margin requirements and trade-for-trade settlement to curb excessive speculation and price volatility.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and traders must apply enhanced margin requirements for securities placed under ST-ASM Stage I and Stage II
- Trading members must ensure clients are informed of ASM status changes for affected securities
- Settlement for ST-ASM securities follows trade-for-trade (T+1) basis

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** June 17, 2026 — All additions, exclusions, and stage movements become applicable
