## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued the settlement calendar for the buyback offer under the Tender Offer scheme by Sarla Performance Fibers Limited. This circular (NCL/CMPT/74304) follows the earlier circular NCL/CMPT/73996 dated April 30, 2026, and NSE/CMTR/74297 dated May 19, 2026. Members, custodians, and PCMs are directed to note the applicable trading and settlement dates and related instructions.

## Key Points

- Settlement Type: X, Settlement No.: 2026054
- Trade window: May 21, 2026 to May 27, 2026
- Obligation download date: June 3, 2026
- Settlement date: June 4, 2026
- Demat shares must be tendered via Early Pay-in through depository mechanism for Market Type Buyback
- Early pay-in of shares on the trade end date must be completed well before 3:30 PM
- Securities and funds payout will occur post receipt of funds pay-in on settlement day
- STT (Securities Transaction Tax) will be collected on the respective settlement day
- A Tender Offer Acceptance Report will be provided detailing accepted, partially accepted, and rejected orders with reason codes

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular operationalizes the previously announced buyback offer by providing the specific settlement schedule under the existing tender offer framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must place Early Pay-in of shares through the depository's early pay-in mechanism (Market Type: Buyback)
- Early pay-in on the trade end date (May 27, 2026) must be submitted well before 3:30 PM
- Members should refer to the scheme document cited in circular NCL/CMPT/73996 for all other details
- For queries, contact the help desk: Toll Free 1800 266 00 50 (Option 2) or email ncl_tenderoffer@nsccl.co.in
- NCL may revise the settlement schedule in case of delay in acceptance details from RTA or delay in funds pay-in by the buying trading member

## Impact Assessment

This circular is relevant to members, custodians, and PCMs participating in the Sarla Performance Fibers Limited buyback tender offer. The settlement window is well-defined and follows standard NCL tender offer procedures. Participants must ensure timely early pay-in of demat shares and monitor the Tender Offer Acceptance Report for order outcomes. The 26-code rejection reason annexure (Annexure I) clarifies grounds for partial or non-acceptance, including issues such as signature mismatches, PAN mismatch, demat account/name mismatch, bidding above exit price, and duplicate bids. Market impact is limited to participants in this specific buyback offer.
