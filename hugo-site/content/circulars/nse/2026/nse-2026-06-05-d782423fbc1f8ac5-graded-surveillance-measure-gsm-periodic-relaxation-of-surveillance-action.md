## Summary

NSE has conducted a periodic review of securities under the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, in coordination with other exchanges. Three securities are eligible to move to lower GSM stages effective June 08, 2026, indicating improvement in their surveillance standing.

## Key Points

- Periodic GSM review conducted in coordination with other exchanges
- Three securities eligible for relaxation of surveillance actions w.e.f. June 08, 2026
- LCCINFOTEC (LCC Infotech Limited) moves from GSM Stage II to Stage I, with a series change from BE to EQ
- GFSTEELS (Grand Foundry Limited) moves from GSM Stage IV to Stage III
- UNIVAFOODS (Univa Foods Limited) moves from GSM Stage IV to Stage III
- Reference circulars: NSE/SURV/34262 (Feb 23, 2017), NSE/SURV/42790 (Nov 29, 2019), NSE/SURV/59425 (Nov 17, 2023), NSE/SURV/64066 (Sep 20, 2024), NSE/SURV/74254 (May 15, 2026)

## Regulatory Changes

The following changes to GSM stage classifications are effective June 08, 2026:

| Symbol | Security Name | ISIN | Series | From Stage | To Stage |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| LCCINFOTEC | LCC Infotech Limited* | INE938A01021 | EQ | II | I |
| GFSTEELS | Grand Foundry Limited | INE534A01028 | BE | IV | III |
| UNIVAFOODS | Univa Foods Limited | INE275F01019 | BE | IV | III |

*Series change from BE to EQ for LCCINFOTEC

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members should note the revised GSM stage classifications for the listed securities
- Trading in these securities must comply with the restrictions applicable to their new, lower GSM stages from June 08, 2026
- Members should refer to the GSM FAQs at https://www.nseindia.com/regulations/graded-surveillance-measure for detailed framework provisions
- For queries, members may contact surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- **June 05, 2026**: Circular issued
- **June 08, 2026**: Effective date for revised GSM stage classifications

## Impact Assessment

The relaxation of GSM stages for these three securities indicates improved compliance or financial parameters as assessed under the periodic review. Moving to lower stages reduces trading restrictions, potentially improving liquidity and market access for these stocks. LCCINFOTEC's simultaneous series change from BE (trade-for-trade) to EQ (rolling settlement) is significant as it restores normal settlement conditions alongside the GSM stage reduction. GFSTEELS and UNIVAFOODS remain under GSM surveillance at Stage III, meaning some restrictions continue to apply. Overall market impact is limited given the small number of securities involved.
