## Summary

NSE has informed its members that the QSIF Active Asset Allocator Long-Short Fund NFO, offered by Quant SIF Mutual Fund under the Specialized Investment Fund (SIF) framework, will be reopened for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions on the NSE MF Invest Platform effective April 24, 2026. The facility includes SIP, STP, and SWP options across six plan variants.

## Key Points

- Six scheme variants are being made available spanning Direct and Regular plans, each with IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment, and Growth options
- All six variants are categorised as Debt under the SIF framework
- Effective date for availability on NSE MF Invest Platform: April 24, 2026
- SIP, STP, and SWP facilities will be available alongside lump-sum subscriptions and redemptions
- Contact for support: support@nseinvest.com | Toll Free: 1800 2100 940

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This circular operationalises the availability of an existing SIF-category NFO on the NSE MF Invest Platform in accordance with the Specialized Investment Fund framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members offering mutual fund investment services should update their platforms to reflect the availability of these six QSIF scheme variants from April 24, 2026
- Members should ensure client eligibility checks are in place as SIF products typically have higher minimum investment thresholds and are intended for sophisticated/institutional investors

## Important Dates

- **April 24, 2026**: Schemes go live on NSE MF Invest Platform for ongoing subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, STP, and SWP

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited and operational in nature. This notice expands the universe of schemes available on NSE MF Invest Platform under the SIF framework. The six scheme variants (ISINs: INF966L30217, INF966L30225, INF966L30233, INF966L30241, INF966L30258, INF966L30266) are all debt-categorised and part of a long-short strategy, which is a relatively niche product. Broader market impact is minimal; the primary relevance is to NSE members distributing Quant SIF Mutual Fund products via the platform.
