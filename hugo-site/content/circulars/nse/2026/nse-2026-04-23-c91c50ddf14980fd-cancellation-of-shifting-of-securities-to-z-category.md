## Summary

NSE has cancelled the previously announced decision to shift Vikas Lifecare Limited (symbol: VIKASLIFE) to the Z Category (BZ Category) of trading. The original shift was announced via Circular Ref. No. 0627/2026 dated April 15, 2026, due to non-compliance with Regulation 33 of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015, specifically for failure to submit financial results for two consecutive quarters ending September 30, 2025 and December 31, 2025. The cancellation is effective April 27, 2026.

## Key Points

- NSE cancels the shifting of VIKASLIFE to Z Category (BZ Category) with effect from April 27, 2026
- Original circular (Ref. No. 0627/2026, dated April 15, 2026) had announced the shift due to non-submission of financial results
- Non-compliance pertained to Regulation 33 of SEBI LODR Regulations, 2015 for Q2 (Sep 30, 2025) and Q3 (Dec 31, 2025)
- Only one company is affected: Vikas Lifecare Limited (VIKASLIFE)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change is introduced. This circular reverses a previously announced enforcement action under Regulation 33 of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015, which mandates timely submission of financial results by listed entities.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance obligations are imposed on members or market participants by this circular. Members are requested to take note of the cancellation and update their records accordingly. Vikas Lifecare Limited remains responsible for meeting its ongoing financial disclosure obligations under Regulation 33.

## Important Dates

- **April 15, 2026**: Original circular (Ref. No. 0627/2026) announced shifting of VIKASLIFE to Z Category
- **April 23, 2026**: Present circular issued cancelling the shift
- **April 27, 2026**: Effective date from which the Z Category shift was to have occurred (now cancelled)

## Impact Assessment

The cancellation is positive for existing holders and traders of VIKASLIFE, as Z Category classification typically subjects securities to trade-to-trade settlement and heightened surveillance, increasing transaction costs and reducing liquidity. By cancelling the shift, normal trading conditions for VIKASLIFE are preserved. The broader market impact is minimal given the action is limited to a single scrip. The reversal may indicate that Vikas Lifecare Limited has taken steps toward compliance or that procedural grounds warranted withdrawal of the earlier action.
