## Summary

NSE has admitted Rights Entitlements (REs) of Shah Metacorp Limited for trading on the Capital Market segment effective June 11, 2026. The REs, identified by symbol SHAH-RE1 and ISIN INE482J20021, will trade in the Trade to Trade (compulsory demat) segment with a market lot of 1.

## Key Points

- Shah Metacorp Limited REs (Symbol: SHAH-RE1) admitted to NSE Capital Market segment from June 11, 2026
- 102,415,595 Rights Entitlements available for trading
- Trading in Trade to Trade segment under compulsory demat for all investors
- Market lot size: 1
- Series: BE
- ISIN: INE482J20021
- Trading members must prominently inform clients that REs are distinct from ordinary shares

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. The circular is issued in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A, governing admission of securities and lot size specifications respectively.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must inform all clients about commencement of trading in REs, specifically mentioning the ISIN (INE482J20021) and clarifying these are not ordinary shares
- Contract notes and bills for RE purchases must prominently include a disclaimer stating that purchase of REs only confers the right to participate in the rights issue or renounce REs before issue closes
- Disclaimer must note that REs neither subscribed nor renounced on or before the Issue Closing Date shall lapse and be extinguished
- All trading in REs must use the designated security code SHAH-RE1 and be conducted in compulsory demat mode

## Important Dates

- **June 11, 2026**: Commencement of trading in Rights Entitlements (effective date of circular)
- **June 11–19, 2026**: Trading period for renunciation of Rights Entitlements
- **June 19, 2026**: Last day to renounce or subscribe REs
- **June 22, 2026**: Date of suspension of trading in REs

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate, targeted impact limited to existing shareholders of Shah Metacorp Limited who are eligible for the rights issue. The 9-day trading window (June 11–19, 2026) allows shareholders to either renounce their entitlements by selling on the exchange or apply for shares in the rights issue. With 102.4 million REs, the issue is sizeable. The Trade to Trade segment designation prevents intraday speculation and requires delivery-based settlement, reducing speculative trading risk. REs that go unsubscribed and unrenounced will lapse after the issue closes on June 19, 2026, with suspension occurring June 22, 2026.
