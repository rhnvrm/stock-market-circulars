## Summary

SEBI has passed a final order (WTM/AS/ISD/ISD-SEC-4/32507/2026-27) under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1) and 11B(2) of the SEBI Act, 1992 against six Noticees, culminating proceedings initiated via an Interim Order cum Show Cause Notice dated April 23, 2025. The order relates to alleged front running of trades of Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC), referred to as the "Big Client," by Madhav Stock Vision Pvt. Ltd. (MSVPL), a SEBI-registered stock broker, and five associated individuals, during the investigation period from April 1, 2020 to December 1, 2023.

According to SEBI's investigation, dealers at brokers empanelled by LIC allegedly leaked non-public information (NPI) about LIC's impending orders to individuals connected to MSVPL, who then placed front-running trades ahead of LIC's orders to generate illicit profits, which were subsequently shared among the Noticees.

## Key Points

- Noticees: (1) Madhav Stock Vision Pvt. Ltd. (MSVPL) — front runner/profit maker; (2) Jyotiswaroop Nandkishore Purohit and (3) Pankit Bhagwati Jhaveri — dealers at Bhagwandas Gordhandas Financial Pvt. Ltd. (BGFPL), an empanelled broker of LIC, acting as information carriers; (4) Rajesh Bhagwati Jhaveri and (5) Ajay Sampatraj Jain — alleged recipients of NPI who directed MSVPL's front-running trades; (6) Rajkumar Prabhu Damani.
- SEBI's internal alert system flagged that trades of LIC placed through its empanelled brokers were being front run by MSVPL.
- Search and seizure operations were conducted at multiple Noticee locations on December 4, 2023.
- Evidence gathered included Call Data Records (CDRs) linking brokers of LIC to dealers/directors of MSVPL, proximity of Noticees 2 and 3 to dealing desks of four stock brokers authorized to handle LIC's orders, trading pattern comparisons between MSVPL and LIC, recorded telephonic conversations, KYC and bank statement analysis, and statements recorded under oath.
- The Noticees were categorized into three groups: front runner/profit maker (MSVPL), information carriers (Noticees 2 and 3 at BGFPL), and alleged beneficiaries/directors who used the NPI to trade (Noticees 4 and 5), with Noticee 6 also implicated.
- Profits generated from front running were allegedly shared among the Noticees, including transfers among their related entities.

## Regulatory Changes

This is an enforcement order and does not introduce new regulatory changes; it applies existing SEBI Act provisions (Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1) and 11B(2)) to penalize front running activity by a registered intermediary and connected individuals.

## Compliance Requirements

- Stock brokers and their dealers must maintain strict confidentiality of client order information, particularly for large institutional clients, and prevent leakage of non-public information.
- Market participants should be aware that SEBI actively monitors trading patterns via internal alert systems to detect front running and conducts search and seizure operations, call data record analysis, and forensic review of trading and bank records as part of enforcement.
- Registered intermediaries found facilitating or engaging in front running face directions under Sections 11, 11B and related SEBI Act provisions, which may include market restraint, disgorgement of profits, and other remedial directions.

## Important Dates

- Investigation Period: April 1, 2020 to December 1, 2023
- Search and seizure operations: December 4, 2023
- Interim Order cum Show Cause Notice issued: April 23, 2025
- Final Order issued: 2026 (Order No. WTM/AS/ISD/ISD-SEC-4/32507/2026-27)

## Impact Assessment

The order directly affects Madhav Stock Vision Pvt. Ltd. and the five individual Noticees named, with implications for their ability to operate as market intermediaries and potential financial penalties or disgorgement of ill-gotten profits. For the broader market, this case reinforces SEBI's continued vigilance against front running of large institutional client orders (such as LIC's), signaling to stock brokers and dealers the risks of misusing non-public order information. While the direct market impact is limited to the parties involved, the order serves as a compliance deterrent for brokers and dealers handling large institutional flows.
