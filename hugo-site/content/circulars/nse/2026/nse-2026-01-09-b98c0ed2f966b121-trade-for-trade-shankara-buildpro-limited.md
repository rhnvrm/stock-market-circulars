## Summary

National Stock Exchange has announced that Shankara Buildpro Limited (Symbol: BUILDPRO) will be transferred from the trade for trade segment (Series: BE) to the rolling segment (Series: EQ) effective January 23, 2026. This follows Exchange Circular no. NSE/CML/72191 dated January 07, 2026 and is in accordance with SEBI guidelines (circular reference no. CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012).

## Key Points

- Shankara Buildpro Limited (BUILDPRO) to move from BE series to EQ series
- Transfer from trade for trade segment to rolling segment
- Change effective from January 23, 2026
- Action taken pursuant to SEBI guidelines from January 2012
- Follows previous circular NSE/CML/72191 dated January 07, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

The security will transition from trade-for-trade settlement (BE series) to normal rolling settlement (EQ series). Trade-for-trade is a restrictive segment where intraday trading is not permitted and delivery is compulsory. Moving to rolling segment allows normal trading with T+1 settlement and intraday trading capabilities.

## Compliance Requirements

- All trading members must note the segment change for BUILDPRO
- Update trading systems to reflect the new series classification (EQ) from January 23, 2026
- Ensure clients are informed of the change in trading segment and its implications

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** January 09, 2026
- **Effective Date:** January 23, 2026 - Shankara Buildpro Limited transfers to rolling segment (EQ series)
- **Reference Circular:** NSE/CML/72191 dated January 07, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Positive Impact:** The move from trade-for-trade to rolling segment is generally favorable for the stock as it:
- Increases liquidity by allowing intraday trading
- Removes compulsory delivery requirement
- Provides more trading flexibility to market participants
- Indicates improved compliance or normalized trading patterns

**Trading Impact:** Traders and investors in BUILDPRO will have greater flexibility in their trading strategies from January 23, 2026 onwards, with the ability to conduct intraday trades and benefit from standard T+1 settlement cycles.
