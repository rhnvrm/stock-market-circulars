## Summary

NSE has issued a clarification following an amendment to the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Rules, 1957 (SCRR) published in the Gazette of India on May 19, 2025. The amendment inserts a new proviso to Rule 8(1)(f) and 8(3)(f) clarifying that investments by a member shall not be construed as 'business' unless they involve client funds, client securities, or arrangements creating a financial liability on the broker. This modifies the illustrative list of non-compliant activities issued via NSE/COMP/50957 dated January 07, 2022.

## Key Points

- A new proviso has been inserted into Rule 8(1)(f) and Rule 8(3)(f) of SCRR, 1957 via Gazette notification dated May 19, 2025.
- Investments by a member do not constitute 'business' unless they involve client funds/securities or create a financial liability on the broker.
- Points 1 and 2 of the 2022 illustrative non-compliance list are retained but modified in scope.
- Points 3 to 12 of the 2022 illustrative non-compliance list are deleted entirely.
- FAQs clarifying permitted and prohibited activities are provided in Annexure-A.

## Regulatory Changes

The Department of Economic Affairs, Ministry of Finance amended SCRR, 1957 with the following proviso added to both Rule 8(1)(f) and Rule 8(3)(f):

> "Provided further that investments made by a member shall not be construed as business except when such investments involve client funds or client securities or relate to arrangements which are in the nature of creating a financial liability on the broker."

Consequently, Exchange Circular NSE/COMP/50957 (January 07, 2022) is partially superseded:
- **Point 1 (modified):** Issuing Corporate Guarantees towards credit facilities availed by any entity, including group companies (subsidiaries & associates) of the Member — remains non-compliant.
- **Point 2 (modified):** Deposit pledged with the bank for overdraft facilities availed by any entity, including group companies of the Member — remains non-compliant.
- **Points 3–12:** Deleted; activities previously listed thereunder are no longer categorised as non-compliance under Rule 8(1)(f) and 8(3)(f).

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must not issue corporate guarantees for credit facilities availed by group entities.
- Members must not pledge deposits for overdraft facilities availed by group entities.
- Members must not engage in unauthorised collective investment/portfolio management schemes promising fixed, guaranteed, or regular returns or capital protection — prohibited under Clause 20 of SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 2026.
- Members must not enter into arrangements with clients to extend loans or finance securities transactions directly or indirectly, except as permitted for Margin Trading purposes.
- Members should review their current activities against the revised illustrative list and update internal compliance frameworks accordingly.

## Important Dates

- **May 19, 2025:** Gazette of India notification amending SCRR, 1957 published.
- **June 23, 2026:** NSE circular issued providing clarification and modified illustrative list.

## Impact Assessment

This circular reduces the compliance burden on trading members by deleting items 3–12 from the earlier prohibitions list, effectively permitting a broader range of investment activities that do not involve client funds/securities or create financial liabilities on the broker. However, the retained prohibitions on corporate guarantees and deposit pledges for group entity credit facilities remain strictly enforced. Members engaged in activities previously covered under the deleted points should confirm their activities now fall within the permitted scope. The FAQ in Annexure-A provides additional guidance on specific scenarios such as unauthorised collective investments and client financing arrangements.
