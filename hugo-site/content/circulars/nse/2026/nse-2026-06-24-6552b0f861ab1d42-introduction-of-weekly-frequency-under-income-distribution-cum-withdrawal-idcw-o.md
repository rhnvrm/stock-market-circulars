## Summary

NSE has notified all members that Nippon India Mutual Fund (NIMF) / Nippon Life India Asset Management Limited (NAM India) has introduced a Weekly Payout of Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal (IDCW) option under two existing debt schemes — Nippon India Ultra Short Duration Fund and Nippon India Money Market Fund — with effect from June 24, 2026. Four new plan/option combinations have been added on the NSE MF Invest Platform.

## Key Points

- Four new scheme options are now live on NSE MF Invest Platform under Weekly IDCW payout frequency.
- The weekly IDCW will be declared every Friday or the next working day if Friday is a holiday.
- The face value for the new weekly IDCW option is Rs. 1,000 per unit.
- Both Regular Plan and Direct Plan variants are introduced for each fund.
- There is no guarantee or assurance on the rate or regularity of IDCW distribution; it is subject to availability of distributable surplus.
- The Trustees reserve the right to declare IDCW based on distributable surplus.
- This change is per Addendum No. 36 issued by Nippon India Mutual Fund, modifying the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM).

## Regulatory Changes

The SID and KIM of Nippon India Money Market Fund and Nippon India Ultra Short Duration Fund have been amended to incorporate the new 'Weekly Payout of Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal option' under both the IDCW Option and the Direct Plan - IDCW Option.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members dealing in mutual funds are required to take note of the new scheme options and ISINs added on the NSE MF Invest Platform.
- No specific action is mandated on members beyond awareness and operational readiness to transact in the new scheme options.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** June 24, 2026 (immediate effect)
- **Weekly IDCW Declaration Day:** Every Friday, or next working day if Friday is a holiday.

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact, routine product expansion by Nippon India Mutual Fund. The addition of weekly IDCW options caters to investors seeking more frequent income distributions from short-duration debt instruments. It broadens investor choice without altering existing scheme structures or imposing new compliance burdens on market participants. The two funds involved — Ultra Short Duration and Money Market — are relatively low-risk debt categories, and the weekly IDCW feature is primarily relevant for income-seeking retail and institutional investors.
