## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed commercial papers (CPs) from seven companies to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective February 02, 2026. The circular provides details of 10 commercial paper issuances with their respective ISINs, face values, issue prices, allotment dates, and redemption dates.

## Key Points

- Total of 10 commercial paper instruments listed from 7 different issuers
- All securities allotted on January 30, 2026
- Face value of ₹5,00,000 per security across all issuances
- Redemption periods range from 13 days to 6 months
- Total quantity: 27,000 securities representing ₹13.5 billion in face value

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is a standard notification pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. This is an informational circular notifying market participants of newly listed debt securities.

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: January 30, 2026 (all securities)
- **Listing Effective Date**: February 02, 2026
- **Redemption Dates**: Range from February 13, 2026 to July 30, 2026 depending on the instrument

## Impact Assessment

Routine administrative action with minimal market impact. The listing of these commercial papers provides additional short-term debt instruments for institutional investors in the debt market segment. The issuances represent standard treasury operations by financial services companies and one manufacturing company (CEAT). No significant impact on equity markets or broader trading operations.
