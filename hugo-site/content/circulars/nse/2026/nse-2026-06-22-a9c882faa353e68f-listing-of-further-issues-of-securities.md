## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of further issues of equity shares for 16 companies on the Capital Market segment, effective June 23, 2026. All allotments relate to Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOPs) and the newly listed shares rank pari passu with existing equity shares with no lock-in applicable.

## Key Points

- Circular Ref. No.: 0996/2026 issued under NSE/CML/74812
- 16 companies are listing further equity shares allotted under ESOP schemes
- All securities admitted to dealings effective June 23, 2026
- All new shares rank pari passu with existing shares; no lock-in applicable
- Market lot size is 1 for all securities
- Securities tradeable via their existing designated codes on the Capital Market (EQ) series

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. Listings are in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members are informed of the new securities admitted to dealings
- Trading must use the designated security codes as specified in the Annexure
- Lot sizes as specified in the Annexure must be adhered to for system trading

## Important Dates

- Date of Circular: June 22, 2026
- Effective Date for Trading: June 23, 2026
- Allotment Dates vary by company:
  - Acme Solar Holdings Limited: June 18, 2026 (723,642 shares; ISIN INE622W01025)
  - Campus Activewear Limited: June 18, 2026 (60,541 shares; ISIN INE278Y01022)
  - D.B.Corp Limited: June 8, 2026 (8,753 shares; ISIN INE950I01011)
  - The Federal Bank Limited: allotment details partially provided (ISIN INE171A01029)
  - Honasa Consumer Limited, IDFC First Bank, Kfin Technologies, Karnataka Bank, Lodha Developers, Motilal Oswal Financial Services, Nuvama Wealth Management, Polycab India, Rategain Travel Technologies, Tech Mahindra, Yes Bank, Physicswallah: details in full Annexure of the circular

## Impact Assessment

Impact is minimal. These are routine ESOP listings representing small incremental increases in the floating share supply for each company. No change in share price or trading mechanism is expected. Existing shareholders face negligible dilution. Members need to ensure their systems recognize the new distinctive number ranges for each security.
