## Summary

NSE's Surveillance department has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework effective June 04-05, 2026. Nine securities are newly included under ESM Stage I, one security (DCMFINSERV) is moved from Stage I to Stage II, and no securities are moved from Stage II back to Stage I. All newly included Stage I securities will be subject to a minimum 100% margin requirement and shifted from Rolling Settlement (EQ/SM series) to Trade-for-Trade settlement (BE/ST series).

## Key Points

- 9 securities added to ESM Stage I effective June 04, 2026, with 100% margin applicable from June 05, 2026
- Securities shifting to ESM Stage I will move from Rolling Settlement (EQ/SM) to Trade-for-Trade (BE/ST) w.e.f. June 05, 2026
- DCMFINSERV (DCM Financial Services Limited) moves from ESM Stage I to Stage II w.e.f. June 04, 2026
- Stage II securities placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction w.e.f. June 04, 2026
- No securities are moving from Stage II back to Stage I
- ESM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures
- Shortlisting under ESM is purely for market surveillance and should not be construed as adverse action against the company

## Regulatory Changes

This circular references and updates the ESM framework originally established through circulars NSE/SURV/56948, NSE/SURV/57609, NSE/SURV/63361, NSE/SURV/64066, NSE/SURV/64400, and NSE/SURV/69315 (dated June 02, 2023 through July 25, 2025). The updated annexures revise the list of securities under the ESM framework accordingly.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Members/Brokers:** Must ensure 100% margin is collected on all open positions in ESM Stage I securities as on June 04, 2026, and for all new positions created from June 05, 2026
- **Trading systems:** Must account for the series change from EQ/SM to BE/ST for Stage I securities and enforce Trade-for-Trade settlement
- **Stage II securities:** Must be traded under Periodic Call Auction with a 2% price band
- Members should update client-facing communications to reflect the changed trading conditions for affected securities

The 100% margin requirement significantly increases the cost of holding or trading these securities, effectively reducing liquidity. The shift to Trade-for-Trade settlement eliminates netting benefits, requiring full delivery on both buy and sell sides.
