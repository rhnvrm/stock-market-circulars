## Summary

NSE has issued a surveillance circular moving LCC Infotech Limited (Symbol: LCCINFOTEC, ISIN: INE938A01021) to Stage II of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) effective May 13, 2026. The security's trading series will also change from EQ to BE. Buyers will be required to deposit an Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value, and all settlement will be on a trade-to-trade basis with no netting off permitted.

## Key Points

- LCCINFOTEC (LCC Infotech Limited, ISIN: INE938A01021) moved to GSM Stage II effective May 13, 2026
- Series changes from EQ to BE
- Trading will be in Trade for Trade (T2T) mode with a price band of 5% or lower
- Buyers must deposit Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of the trade value
- No netting off is allowed; settlement is strictly on a trade-to-trade basis
- Members are advised to take adequate precautions while trading in this security
- Queries can be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Regulatory Changes

The security is escalated from its prior GSM stage to Stage II under the NSE Graded Surveillance Measure framework, as referenced in earlier circulars NSE/SURV/34262, NSE/SURV/38389, NSE/SURV/42790, NSE/SURV/59425, NSE/SURV/64066, and NSE/SURV/73167. The trading series designation changes from EQ (normal equity) to BE (trade-for-trade), which alters settlement mechanics and margin requirements for all market participants.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must note the change in trading conditions for LCCINFOTEC effective May 13, 2026
- Buyers are required to deposit ASD of 50% of trade value before executing trades
- No intraday netting is permitted; each trade must be settled independently on a trade-to-trade basis
- Members must inform clients trading in this security about the revised settlement and deposit requirements

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 12, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 13, 2026 (Stage II GSM applies from this date)

## Impact Assessment

The move to GSM Stage II significantly restricts trading flexibility for LCCINFOTEC. The mandatory 50% ASD requirement increases the capital burden on buyers, likely reducing liquidity and trading volumes. The shift from EQ to BE series and the elimination of intraday netting increases settlement risk and operational complexity for brokers and traders. The 5% price band limits intraday price discovery. Retail investors and traders holding or planning to trade this stock face substantially higher margin requirements and reduced exit flexibility.
