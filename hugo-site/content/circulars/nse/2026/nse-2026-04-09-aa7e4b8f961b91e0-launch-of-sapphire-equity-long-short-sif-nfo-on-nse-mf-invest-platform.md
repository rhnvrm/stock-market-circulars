## Summary

NSE has announced the availability of Sapphire Equity Long-Short SIF NFO of Franklin SIF Mutual Fund on the NSE MF Invest Platform. The NFO subscription window runs from April 10, 2026 to April 24, 2026 (till 11:59 PM), with allotment scheduled for April 29, 2026. Six scheme variants are available across Regular and Direct plans with Growth and IDCW options.

## Key Points

- Franklin SIF Mutual Fund's Sapphire Equity Long-Short SIF NFO opens on NSE MF Invest Platform from April 10, 2026
- Subscription window closes April 24, 2026 at 11:59 PM
- Six scheme variants offered: Regular Growth, Regular IDCW Reinvestment, Regular IDCW Payout, Direct Growth, Direct IDCW Reinvestment, Direct IDCW Payout
- All six schemes fall under the Equity category (SIF - Specialised Investment Fund)
- ISINs range from INF090I30014 to INF090I30063
- Allotment date for all schemes: April 29, 2026
- Clear funds must be received in the designated Clearing Corporation account by April 29, 2026 at 10:00 AM

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is an operational announcement for a new NFO listing on the NSE MF Invest Platform under the Specialised Investment Fund (SIF) framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must ensure clear funds through any payment mode are received in the designated Clearing Corporation account on or before April 29, 2026 at 10:00 AM
- Members should process client subscriptions within the NFO window (April 10–24, 2026)

## Important Dates

- **April 10, 2026**: NFO opens on NSE MF Invest Platform
- **April 24, 2026 (11:59 PM)**: NFO subscription window closes
- **April 29, 2026 (10:00 AM)**: Deadline for clear funds in Clearing Corporation account
- **April 29, 2026**: Allotment date for all six scheme variants

## Impact Assessment

This is a medium-impact operational announcement for NSE members participating in the mutual fund distribution platform. The Sapphire Equity Long-Short SIF is a Specialised Investment Fund — a relatively new category in Indian mutual funds allowing long-short equity strategies, which may attract sophisticated investors. Members need to ensure operational readiness for processing transactions and fund settlement within the specified timelines. No broader market-wide impact is expected.
