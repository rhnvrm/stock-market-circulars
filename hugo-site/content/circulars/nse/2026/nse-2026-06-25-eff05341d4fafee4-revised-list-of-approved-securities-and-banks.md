## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued its revised list of approved securities and empaneled banks applicable for the month of July 2026. This circular supersedes the previous circular no. 053/2026 (NCL/CMPT/74372) dated May 22, 2026. The update covers equity shares, ETFs, Sovereign Gold Bonds, GOI Securities/T-Bills, Open-Ended Mutual Funds, and banks empaneled for Bank Guarantees (BGs) and Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDRs) acceptable as collateral.

## Key Points

- Revised approved securities list is effective for **July 2026**.
- Six annexures are provided covering different asset classes used as liquid assets (cash and non-cash components).
- Annexure 1: Equity shares (non-cash component) with market-wide limits, member-specific limits, and VAR-based haircut rates.
- Annexure 2: Exchange Traded Funds (non-cash component) with applicable limits and haircut rates.
- Annexure 3: Exchange Traded Funds (cash component) with applicable limits and haircut rates.
- Annexure 4: Sovereign Gold Bonds and GOI Securities/T-Bills (cash component) with haircut rates.
- Annexure 5: Open-Ended Mutual Funds acceptable as collateral with market-wide limits and haircut rates.
- Annexure 6: List of banks empaneled for issuance of BGs and FDRs.
- G-Sec/T-Bills carry a **shut period of two days**; members must release these securities at least two days before maturity.
- Members who are also banks must not reckon G-Sec provided as collateral for SLR purposes and must not use them for trading.
- A value-based prudential norm applies: exclusion of a pledged security should not result in a margin shortfall of more than **25% at a Clearing Member level**.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes. This is a periodic revision of the approved securities universe for collateral under the SLBS framework, updating quantities, limits, and eligible instruments effective July 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- **All Participants and Custodians** must update their collateral management systems to reflect the revised lists for July 2026.
- Members holding G-Sec/T-Bills as collateral must ensure release at least **two days before maturity** to account for the shut period.
- Member-banks must ensure G-Sec pledged as collateral are excluded from SLR calculations and not used for trading activity.
- Collateral pledged must comply with the prudential norm: no single security exclusion should cause more than 25% margin shortfall at Clearing Member level.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 25, 2026
- **Effective Period:** July 2026
- **Previous Circular Reference:** 053/2026 dated May 22, 2026 (NCL/CMPT/74372)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine monthly operational update with medium impact on participants and custodians active in the SLBS segment. Entities must review the updated annexures to ensure compliance with revised permissible quantities and haircut rates. Changes to the approved list could affect collateral planning for July, particularly if any securities have been added or removed. Members with G-Sec/T-Bill collateral nearing maturity must act promptly given the two-day shut period requirement. No broader market impact is anticipated.
