## Summary

NSE has invoked the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework for 9 securities, placing them under ST-ASM Stage I effective June 23, 2026. Enhanced margin requirements of 50% (or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at 100%) will apply to both open and new positions from June 24, 2026. No securities have been added to Stage II, and no movements between stages have been announced.

## Key Points

- 9 securities shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage I effective June 23, 2026
- Margin requirement for Stage I: 50% or existing margin, whichever is higher (max 100%)
- Margin applies to all open positions as on June 23, 2026 and new positions from June 24, 2026
- Stage II additions: Nil
- Stage I to Stage II movements: Nil
- Stage II to Stage I movements: Nil
- ASM classification is a surveillance action and does not imply adverse findings against the company

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued under the ST-ASM framework established via prior circulars NSE/SURV/39265 (Oct 27, 2018), NSE/SURV/46557 (Dec 04, 2020), NSE/SURV/52144 (Apr 28, 2022), NSE/SURV/58558 (Sep 25, 2023), and NSE/SURV/64066 (Sep 20, 2024). The ST-ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by exchanges.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must ensure applicable margin rates (50% or higher for Stage I, up to 100%) are collected on all open positions in the listed securities as on June 23, 2026
- Enhanced margins must be applied to all new positions created from June 24, 2026
- Members should refer to FAQs on the ASM framework at https://www.nseindia.com/regulations/additional-surveillance-measure
- Queries can be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- **June 22, 2026**: Circular issued
- **June 23, 2026**: Securities shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage I become effective; enhanced margins applicable on open positions
- **June 24, 2026**: Enhanced margin requirements effective for new positions

## Impact Assessment

The 9 securities placed under ST-ASM Stage I face elevated margin requirements that will increase the cost of holding and trading these securities. Traders and investors with existing positions must arrange for additional margin by June 24, 2026. The affected securities span multiple sectors including finance (ESAFSFB, NIACL), industrials (NELCO, TIL, BHADORA), petroleum (PANAMAPET), apparel (SPAL), jewellery (SHUBHLAXMI), and adhesive tapes (SRIVASAVI). Retail and institutional participants with leveraged positions in these securities may face margin calls or be forced to reduce positions.
