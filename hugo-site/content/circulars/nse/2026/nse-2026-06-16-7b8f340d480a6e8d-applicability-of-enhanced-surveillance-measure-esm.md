## Summary

NSE has announced the applicability of Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) for multiple securities effective June 17, 2026. Eleven securities are newly shortlisted under ESM Stage-I, and one security (ATCENERGY) is being moved from ESM Stage-I to Stage-II. No securities are moving from Stage-II to Stage-I, and no securities are being excluded from the ESM framework in this update.

## Key Points

- 11 securities newly placed under ESM Stage-I effective June 17, 2026
- 1 security (ATCENERGY - ATC Energies System Limited) moves from ESM Stage-I to Stage-II effective June 17, 2026
- No securities moving from Stage-II to Stage-I (Nil)
- No securities excluded from ESM framework (Nil)
- All 11 newly added Stage-I securities will migrate from EQ/SM series to BE/ST series effective June 18, 2026
- The consolidated ESM list contains 23 securities under Stage-II and a large number under Stage-I

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the change in trading series for all 11 newly added ESM Stage-I securities from EQ/SM to BE/ST effective June 18, 2026
- ESM securities are subject to trade-for-trade settlement, meaning no netting of positions is permitted
- Additional surveillance margins apply to all securities under ESM
- Stage-II securities (including ATCENERGY) are subject to more stringent conditions than Stage-I
- Members and clients holding positions in these securities should review their exposure in light of the enhanced surveillance restrictions

## Important Dates

- **June 17, 2026**: ESM Stage-I additions and Stage-I to Stage-II movement become effective
- **June 18, 2026**: Series change from EQ/SM to BE/ST for all 11 newly shortlisted Stage-I securities

## Impact Assessment

The placement of 11 securities under ESM Stage-I and escalation of ATCENERGY to Stage-II will significantly impact trading in these securities. ESM restrictions include mandatory trade-for-trade settlement (T+2 gross settlement), which eliminates intraday netting and increases capital requirements for traders. The series change from EQ/SM to BE/ST further signals heightened regulatory oversight. Investors holding these securities may face reduced liquidity and wider bid-ask spreads. Stage-II placement for ATCENERGY represents an escalation indicating continued surveillance concerns. The consolidated list now includes over 23 Stage-II securities and a substantial number of Stage-I securities, reflecting broad NSE surveillance activity across small and mid-cap segments.
