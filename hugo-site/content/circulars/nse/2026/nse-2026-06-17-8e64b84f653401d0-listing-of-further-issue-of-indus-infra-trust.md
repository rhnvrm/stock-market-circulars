## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of further units of Indus Infra Trust (symbol: INDUSINVIT) on the Capital Market segment effective June 18, 2026. The listing covers two tranches: 142,857,142 units allotted on a QIP basis and 25,210,084 units allotted on a preferential basis, both at an issue price of ₹119 per unit and dated June 16, 2026.

## Key Points

- **Symbol:** INDUSINVIT | **Series:** IV | **ISIN:** INE0NHL23019
- **Issue Price:** ₹119 per unit
- **Date of Allotment:** June 16, 2026
- **QIP Tranche:** 142,857,142 units (Distinctive Numbers: 468148690 to 611005831) — no lock-in
- **Preferential Tranche:** 25,210,084 units (Distinctive Numbers: 442938606 to 468148689) — subject to lock-in per Annexure II
- Both tranches are Pari Passu with existing units
- Trading effective from **June 18, 2026**

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is issued in pursuance of:
- Regulation 3.1.1 of NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A — admission of securities to dealings
- Regulation 2.5.5 of NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A — lot sizes for admitted securities

## Compliance Requirements

- Securities must be identified by their designated codes for trading purposes
- Trading must be conducted in the lot sizes specified in the Annexure
- Units allotted in dematerialized mode have been temporarily credited under a temporary ISIN (IN8*********) per SEBI circulars CIR/MRD/DP/21/2012 (August 2, 2012) and CIR/MRD/DP/24/2012 (September 11, 2012) pending activation of the permanent ISIN

## Important Dates

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Date of Allotment | June 16, 2026 |

## Impact Assessment

The listing adds approximately 168 million new units of Indus Infra Trust to the tradeable float on NSE. The QIP tranche (142.86M units) is immediately freely tradeable, which could exert short-term supply pressure on INDUSINVIT. The preferential tranche (25.21M units) is partially locked in — 3.78M units until June 2029 and 21.43M units until June 2027 — limiting their immediate market impact. Overall, this is a routine corporate action consequent to a fundraise, with no change to the trust's structure or regulatory framework.
