## Summary

NSE has notified all members that the **Arudha Equity Long Short Fund** of **Bandhan Mutual Fund**, operating under the Specialized Investment Fund (SIF) framework, will be reopened for ongoing transactions on the **NSE MF Invest Platform** with effect from **March 30, 2026**. This covers subscriptions, redemptions, and systematic plan facilities (SIP, STP, SWP) across 31 scheme variants.

## Key Points

- Arudha Equity Long Short Fund (Bandhan MF SIF) reopens for ongoing transactions on NSE MF Invest Platform from March 30, 2026.
- Facilities available: subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, STP, and SWP.
- 31 scheme variants are enabled, spanning Regular and Direct plans across Growth, Daily/Weekly/Fortnightly/Monthly/Quarterly/Half-yearly/Annual/Periodic IDCW, and Reinvestment sub-options.
- All schemes fall under the **Equity** category.
- ISINs range from INF194K30358 to INF194K30655 (Bandhan AMC fund codes).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes. This circular operationalizes the availability of an existing SIF-category fund on the NSE MF Invest Platform, consistent with SEBI's Specialized Investment Fund guidelines.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members (distributors/participants on NSE MF Invest Platform) should update their systems to enable transactions in the listed 31 scheme ISINs from March 30, 2026.
- No additional compliance action required beyond platform readiness.

## Important Dates

- **March 27, 2026** — Circular issued (Ref: NSE/NMFTM/73492, Circular Ref. No: 1295/2026).
- **March 30, 2026** — Effective date for ongoing subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, STP, and SWP on NSE MF Invest Platform.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has **medium operational impact** for NSE MF Invest Platform participants. Distributors and investors seeking exposure to Bandhan MF's equity long-short SIF strategy can now transact seamlessly through the platform. The availability of SIP/STP/SWP facilities broadens systematic investment access. Given the SIF classification, this fund targets sophisticated investors, limiting broad retail impact. No market-wide or systemic implications are expected.
