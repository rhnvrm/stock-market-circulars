## Summary

NSE has notified changes to the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective June 18, 2026. Six securities have been newly added to ST-ASM Stage I, three securities have been excluded from the ASM framework (one of which moves to the Long-Term ASM framework), and no securities are being shifted between Stage I and Stage II. An updated consolidated list of all securities currently under ASM is also provided.

## Key Points

- 6 securities added to ST-ASM Stage I effective June 18, 2026
- 0 securities added to ST-ASM Stage II
- 0 securities moved from Stage I to Stage II
- 0 securities moved from Stage II to Stage I
- 3 securities excluded from the ASM framework effective June 18, 2026
- MSTCLTD (Mstc Limited) moves from ST-ASM to the Long-Term ASM (LTASM) framework
- Consolidated ASM list updated to reflect all current inclusions

## Regulatory Changes

The ST-ASM framework is a surveillance mechanism applied by NSE to securities exhibiting abnormal price or volume movements. Placement under ST-ASM results in mandatory additional margin requirements and possible trade-for-trade settlement, restricting speculative activity in the affected scrips.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must apply enhanced margin requirements for securities placed under ST-ASM Stage I and Stage II.
- Clients holding or transacting in these securities must comply with revised margin and settlement norms.
- MSTCLTD transitions to LTASM; brokers should update surveillance classification accordingly.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date for all changes:** June 18, 2026
