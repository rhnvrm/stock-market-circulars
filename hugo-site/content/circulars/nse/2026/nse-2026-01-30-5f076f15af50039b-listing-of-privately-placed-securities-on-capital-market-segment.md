## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of four privately placed debt securities on the Capital Market segment, effective February 01, 2026. The securities include debentures from Bajaj Auto Credit Limited (2 issues), Cholamandalam Investment and Finance Company Limited (1 issue), and IFL Finance Limited (1 issue). All securities will trade with a market lot size of 1.

## Key Points

- Four new debt securities admitted to dealings on NSE Capital Market segment
- Effective date: February 01, 2026
- Market lot size: 1 for all securities
- Securities include corporate bonds with coupon rates ranging from 7.83% to 11.75%
- Maturity dates range from 2028 to 2030
- Trading to be conducted using designated security codes (symbols) specified in the circular

## Securities Listed

### Bajaj Auto Credit Limited
1. **8BACL30** (Series N0)
   - Issue: BACL 8% 2030
   - Coupon Rate: 8%
   - Maturity: September 25, 2030
   - ISIN: INE18UV08010

2. **8BACL30A** (Series N0)
   - Issue: BACL 8% 2030
   - Coupon Rate: 8%
   - Maturity: October 15, 2030
   - ISIN: INE18UV08028

### Cholamandalam Investment and Finance Company Limited
- **783CIFCL28** (Series N0)
   - Issue: CIFCL 7.83% 2028 Sr 659
   - Coupon Rate: 7.83%
   - Maturity: June 28, 2028
   - ISIN: INE121A07SR9

### IFL Finance Limited
- **1175IFL28** (Series N0)
   - Issue: IFL 11.75% 2028
   - Coupon Rate: 11.75%
   - Maturity: January 29, 2028
   - ISIN: INE01XO07033

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must identify securities only by designated codes specified in the annexure
- Trading must be conducted in lot sizes as specified (market lot of 1 for all securities)
- Members must comply with Regulation 2.5.5 regarding lot sizes

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: January 30, 2026
- **Effective Date**: February 01, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative notification with minimal market impact. The listing of privately placed debt securities provides additional investment options for institutional and qualified investors in the debt market segment. No impact on equity markets or existing trading operations. The securities are privately placed corporate bonds and will have limited liquidity compared to publicly issued securities.
