## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has issued the revised Market Wide Position Limits (MWPL) along with Participant, Institutional Client, and Non-Institutional Client limits for securities and ETFs eligible under the Securities Lending and Borrowing Scheme (SLBS) for the month of July 2026. This follows up on the earlier NCL Circular No. NCL/CMPT/73999 dated April 30, 2026, and provides the updated limits as detailed in Annexure I.

## Key Points

- NSE Clearing Limited's Securities Lending & Borrowing Scheme (SLBS) department has published revised position limits effective for July 2026.
- The circular (Ref No. NCL/CMPT/74959, Circular Ref. No. 065/2026) updates limits previously communicated under NCL/CMPT/73999 dated April 30, 2026.
- Annexure I contains the detailed, security-wise breakdown covering Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL), Participant Limit, Institutional Client Limit, and Non-Institutional Client Limit (in number of shares) for several hundred securities and ETFs, including names such as 360ONE, 3MINDIA, 63MOONS, AADHARHFC, ABB, ABBOTINDIA, ABCAPITAL, ACC, ADANIENT, ADANIGREEN, ADANIPORTS, ADANIPOWER, and many others.
- For each security, the Participant Limit is set at 10% of the MWPL, the Institutional Client Limit at 10% of the MWPL, and the Non-Institutional Client Limit at 1% of the MWPL.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a routine periodic (monthly) revision of quantitative position limits under the existing SLBS framework, applicable for the month of July 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

All Participants and Custodians registered under the SLBS are required to ensure that their lending/borrowing positions and client positions remain within the revised MWPL, Participant, Institutional Client, and Non-Institutional Client limits specified in Annexure I for the respective securities and ETFs.

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: June 30, 2026
- Applicable Period: July 2026
- Reference to prior circular: NCL/CMPT/73999 dated April 30, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, recurring monthly update affecting SLBS market participants, custodians, and institutional/non-institutional clients who engage in securities lending and borrowing. It does not represent a corporate action or material event for any individual listed company, but compliance with the revised limits is mandatory for entities active in the SLBS segment to avoid breaching position limits.
