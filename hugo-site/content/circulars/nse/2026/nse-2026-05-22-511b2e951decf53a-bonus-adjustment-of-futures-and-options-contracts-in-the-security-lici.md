## Summary

NSE has announced the adjustment of Futures and Options (F&O) contracts for Life Insurance Corporation of India (Symbol: LICI) in accordance with SEBI guidelines on corporate action adjustments. The adjustment is triggered by a 1:1 bonus issue with an ex-date and effective date of May 29, 2026. The adjustment factor is 2, and the revised market lot will be 1400.

## Key Points

- **Symbol:** LICI (Life Insurance Corporation of India)
- **Corporate Action:** Bonus Issue
- **Bonus Ratio:** 1:1
- **Face Value:** Rs 10/-
- **Ex-date / Effective Date:** May 29, 2026
- **Adjustment Factor:** 2
- **Revised Market Lot:** 1400
- Revised option strike prices will be communicated one day prior to the ex-date/effective date
- Adjusted prices and lot sizes may appear in decimal places; these will be rounded off to the nearest tick size and nearest integer respectively

## Regulatory Changes

Pursuant to SEBI guidelines on corporate action adjustments for equity derivatives, all outstanding LICI futures and options contracts will be adjusted with effect from May 29, 2026. The methodology for position adjustments will be separately communicated by the respective Clearing Corporation.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must load the updated contract file (`NSE_FO_contract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz`) and spread file (`NSE_FO_spdcontract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz`) on their trading applications **before trading on May 29, 2026**.
- These files are available on the Extranet server under directory `faoftp/faocommon`.
- Updated MII contract and spread files are also available on the NSE website at: https://www.nseindia.com/all-reports-derivatives
- Members should refer to the corporate actions adjustments page for full adjustment details: https://www.nseindia.com/products-services/equity-derivatives-corporate-actions-adjustments

## Impact Assessment

All market participants holding or trading LICI F&O contracts are affected. The 1:1 bonus results in an adjustment factor of 2, halving futures and option strike prices and doubling the lot size to 1400. Traders must ensure their trading systems are updated with the new contract files before the market opens on May 29, 2026, to avoid discrepancies in order placement. Position adjustment methodology will be separately notified by the Clearing Corporation.
