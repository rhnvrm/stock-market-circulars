## Summary

NSE has informed members that the Prism Hybrid-Long Short Fund NFO under Specialized Investment Fund (SIF) of Jioblackrock Mutual Fund will be available for transactions on the NSE MF Invest Platform from June 29, 2026 to July 13, 2026 (till 6:00 PM). Allotment is scheduled for July 17, 2026.

## Key Points

- NFO subscription window: June 29, 2026 to July 13, 2026 (till 06:00 PM)
- Fund house: Jioblackrock Mutual Fund
- Two scheme variants available: Direct Growth and Regular Growth
- Both schemes classified under the Equity category
- Allotment date: July 17, 2026
- ISIN for Direct Growth: INF22M030027
- ISIN for Regular Growth: INF22M030019

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This circular is an informational notice regarding the availability of a new NFO on the NSE MF Invest Platform under the Specialized Investment Fund (SIF) framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure that clear funds through any payment mode are received in the designated Clearing Corporation account on or before July 16, 2026 till 11:59 PM.
- Members facilitating transactions should make the schemes available to clients within the specified subscription window.

## Important Dates

- **June 29, 2026**: NFO subscription opens on NSE MF Invest Platform
- **July 13, 2026 (till 6:00 PM)**: NFO subscription closes
- **July 16, 2026 (till 11:59 PM)**: Deadline for clear funds to reach the designated Clearing Corporation account
- **July 17, 2026**: Allotment date for both Direct Growth and Regular Growth schemes

## Impact Assessment

This circular has limited broader market impact as it pertains to the launch of a specific NFO under the SIF category. Members operating on the NSE MF Invest Platform need to ensure operational readiness to process transactions for these two scheme variants within the specified window. Investors interested in hybrid long-short strategies under the SIF framework can subscribe through NSE MF Invest Platform. The payment deadline of July 16 is critical for members to communicate to clients to avoid failed transactions.
