## Summary

NSE has issued a circular (Ref No: NSE/COMP/74753, Circular Ref. No: 56/2026) advising all trading members to integrate with and actively utilize the Department of Telecommunications' (DoT) Digital Intelligence Platform (DIP). This directive follows a communication from SEBI aimed at strengthening cyber fraud prevention through coordinated intelligence sharing across financial institutions, telecom operators, and law enforcement agencies.

## Key Points

- NSE directs all trading members to onboard the DoT Digital Intelligence Platform (DIP) available at https://diu.sancharsaathi.gov.in
- DIP connects over 1,400 organizations including banks, financial institutions, law enforcement, and regulators for bi-directional intelligence sharing
- Members must integrate their systems with the Financial Fraud Risk Indicator (FRI) via DIP and adopt real-time response protocols for flagged mobile numbers
- Members are encouraged to submit Action Taken Reports (ATR) on DIP against FRI alerts to support continuous feedback
- API-based integration with DIP is available for automated onboarding
- Mobile numbers are a key fraud vector — DIP enables risk-based classification and monitoring of numbers used in fraud

## Regulatory Changes

This circular implements SEBI's directive for financial market intermediaries to integrate with DoT's DIP. While not introducing new SEBI regulations, it extends the existing cyber fraud prevention framework by mandating use of a government intelligence-sharing platform. Members are expected to adopt real-time response protocols (alerts, transaction delays, warnings, transaction decline) for mobile numbers flagged by the Financial Fraud Risk Indicator (FRI).

## Compliance Requirements

- **Onboarding**: Trading members must register on DoT DIP; API-based integration is supported
- **FRI Integration**: Integrate member systems with the Financial Fraud Risk Indicator (FRI) via DIP
- **Real-Time Protocols**: Implement response protocols for flagged mobile numbers, including alerts, transaction delays, warnings, or transaction declines
- **Intelligence Sharing**: Share lists of suspected mobile numbers with DoT via DIP for re-verification
- **Customer Care Data**: Upload official customer care numbers to create whitelisted data
- **Action Taken Reports**: Submit ATRs on DIP against FRI alerts
- **Contact for Technical/API issues**: diu@cdot.in and diu.ne1@cdot.in
- **Contact for Administrative/Onboarding queries**: dirdiu-dot@gov.in and adg1.diu-dot@gov.in

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: June 17, 2026
- No specific deadline mentioned; members are advised to onboard and integrate at the earliest

## Impact Assessment

This circular has moderate operational impact on trading members. It requires members to undertake new onboarding and technical integration efforts with DoT DIP, including potential API development or configuration. The measure is primarily protective — aimed at reducing financial fraud committed via misuse of mobile numbers (e.g., OTP interception, account takeover). Members who integrate will gain access to real-time risk signals on mobile numbers, helping prevent unauthorized transactions. Non-compliance could expose members to regulatory risk if fraud incidents occur that DIP integration could have mitigated. No direct market or stock-specific impact is expected.
