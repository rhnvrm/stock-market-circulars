## Summary

SEBI passed a Final Order under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1) and 11B(2) of the SEBI Act, 1992, in the matter of front-running by Ashok Maheshwari and seven other connected noticees, pursuant to directions from the Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT). The case originated from an Interim Order-cum-Show Cause Notice dated April 26, 2024, alleging that Ashok Maheshwari, a dealer at Kotak Securities Limited responsible for executing trades for Portfolio Management Service provider Unifi Capital Pvt. Ltd. (the "Big Client"), leaked non-public order information that was used by six other connected noticees to front-run Unifi's trades between April 1, 2021 and May 31, 2022.

## Key Points

- Noticees include Ashok Maheshwari (Kotak Securities dealer), Darshan Bakul Shah, Khusboo Darshan Shah, Darshan Bakul Shah (HUF), Benzer Department Stores Pvt. Ltd., Mihir Dhirajalal Savla, CHL Stock Concepts Pvt. Ltd., and Chirag Mahendra Shah.
- Unifi Capital executed 47.28% of its gross traded value through Kotak Securities during the investigation period, with Maheshwari responsible for placing its orders.
- Noticees 2, 3, and 4's monthly gross traded value rose to 4.3 times pre-investigation-period levels, with 27.01% of scrip days and 56.31% of intraday scrip days common with the Big Client's trades.
- 80.52% of intraday trading profits of these noticees were made on scrip days common with the Big Client, indicating a pattern of trading ahead of Unifi's orders.
- The matter previously proceeded through an Interim Order/SCN (April 26, 2024) alleging violations of Sections 11C(3) and 12A(a),(b),(c),(e) of the SEBI Act and Regulations 3(a)-(d), 4(1) and 4(2)(q) of the PFUTP Regulations.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an enforcement order applying existing SEBI Act and PFUTP Regulations provisions to the specific noticees following a SAT order remanding or directing further action in the case.

## Compliance Requirements

The named noticees are subject to SEBI's final directions under the cited sections, which typically include disgorgement of unlawful gains, restraint from securities market access, and other remedial directions. Kotak Securities Limited and other stock brokers should ensure dealer-level surveillance and information barriers to prevent similar misuse of client order information.

## Important Dates

- Investigation Period: April 1, 2021 to May 31, 2022
- Interim Order-cum-Show Cause Notice: April 26, 2024
- Final Order: 2026 (pursuant to SAT order; exact final order date within the full document)

## Impact Assessment

The order primarily affects the individual and corporate noticees named, with reputational and potential financial consequences (disgorgement, market bans). Broader market impact is limited but reinforces SEBI's ongoing scrutiny of front-running by broker dealers handling institutional/PMS order flow, which may prompt brokers to strengthen internal controls around order information confidentiality.
