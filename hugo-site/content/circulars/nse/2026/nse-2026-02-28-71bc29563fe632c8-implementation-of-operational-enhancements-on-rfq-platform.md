## Summary

NSE has implemented additional operational enhancements to its Request for Quote (RFQ) platform, in continuation of earlier circular NSE/DS/70879 dated October 16, 2025. These enhancements, effective March 02, 2026, are directed by SEBI and aim to improve platform efficiency, transparency, and user experience for debt segment participants.

## Key Points

- Enhanced bond information now displays shut period, put/call dates, cash flows, rating, and sector data sourced via NSDL
- Market watch screen updated to include the same key bond parameters
- Users can now customize market watch by issuer, sector, rating, instrument type, maturity, and value
- New broad clubbed quoting facility allows participants to place clubbed bids/offers in homogenous assets at issuer, rating, coupon, or maturity bucket level
- All enhancements are effective from March 02, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

Enhancements are implemented per SEBI direction and recommendations as a continuation of the operational improvement roadmap for the RFQ platform. No new regulatory obligations are introduced; these are platform-level feature additions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Participants are requested to note the aforementioned changes effective March 02, 2026
- No specific compliance actions required from participants; changes are platform-side implementations
- For queries, participants may contact the RFQ Platform Helpdesk at 1800 266 0050 (Option 1) or email dl-debt-bd@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- **February 28, 2026**: Circular issued (Download Ref No: NSE/DS/73049, Circular Ref. No: 04/2026)
- **March 02, 2026**: All enhancements become effective

## Impact Assessment

These enhancements are positive for debt segment participants, providing richer bond data (shut period, put/call dates, cash flows, rating, sector) directly on the platform via NSDL integration. The customized market watch and broad clubbed quoting facility improve workflow efficiency for institutional participants dealing in homogenous debt instruments. Overall impact is moderate and operational in nature, with no adverse implications for market participants.
