## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has revised the cut-off timings for cash and FDR collateral addition and release, as well as the release timing batches for pledged securities. This follows feedback received from members on the earlier timings notified vide circular ref. no. 0168/2026 dated May 13, 2026. The revised timings will be effective from July 22, 2026.

## Key Points

- Cash and FDR Addition cut-off time revised from 7:30 PM to 10:00 PM
- Cash and FDR Release (Intraday) cut-off revised from 8:00 PM to 9:00 PM
- Cash and FDR Release (End of Day) remains unchanged at 8:00 PM
- Cash and FDR Release (Allocation) remains unchanged at 11:30 PM
- Pledge securities release timings restructured into 16 batches plus an EOD batch, ranging from 8:30 AM (Batch 1) to 9:00 PM (EOD)
- Members must request release of repledged securities towards pay-in requirements one day prior to the pay-in date, not on the pay-in date itself

## Regulatory Changes

This circular supersedes the collateral timing framework issued earlier under Download Ref No: NCL/EGR/74200 dated May 13, 2026 (Circular Ref. No: 0168/2026). The change extends the cash/FDR addition window by 2.5 hours and the intraday release window by 1 hour, while introducing a more granular multi-batch schedule for pledge securities release throughout the trading day.

## Compliance Requirements

- All Members, Custodians, and PCMs must align their internal collateral processing systems with the new cut-off times
- Requests for release of repledged securities needed for pay-in must be submitted one day prior to the pay-in date
- Members should update internal operational workflows and inform relevant desks (risk, operations, custody) of the revised timings

## Important Dates

- Effective date: July 22, 2026
- Prior circular referenced: May 13, 2026 (Circular Ref. No: 0168/2026, Download Ref No: NCL/EGR/74200)

## Impact Assessment

The revised timings provide members with an extended window for cash and FDR collateral addition (up to 10:00 PM) and intraday release (up to 9:00 PM), improving operational flexibility for managing margin and collateral requirements. The expanded pledge securities release batch schedule (16 batches plus EOD) offers more frequent release windows throughout the day, which should help members manage liquidity and margin obligations more efficiently. This is an operational/process change with no direct impact on listed securities or market pricing, but is relevant for risk and operations teams at member firms, custodians, and PCMs.
