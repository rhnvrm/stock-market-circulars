## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of 10 government securities — 3 Treasury Bills (T-bills) and 7 State Development Loans (SDLs) — for trading on the Capital Market segment effective June 29, 2026. All securities have a lot size of 100 units. Security codes and ISIN details are specified in the Annexure.

## Key Points

- 3 GOI Treasury Bills admitted: 91-day, 182-day, and 364-day maturities
- 7 SDLs admitted covering Gujarat, Himachal Pradesh, Delhi, and Uttar Pradesh
- All securities carry a lot size of 100
- Securities are identified solely by their designated NSE codes for trading purposes
- Effective date: June 29, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. Circular issued pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 (securities admitted to dealings) and Regulation 2.5.5 (lot sizes) of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

Members must use the designated NSE security codes when trading these instruments and adhere to the prescribed lot size of 100 units for all listed securities.

## Impact Assessment

Routine operational circular with no equity market or retail investor impact. The listing provides debt market participants access to freshly auctioned government securities on the NSE capital market segment. SDL coupon rates range from 7.43% to 7.78%, reflecting current state borrowing costs. No action required from equity market members.
