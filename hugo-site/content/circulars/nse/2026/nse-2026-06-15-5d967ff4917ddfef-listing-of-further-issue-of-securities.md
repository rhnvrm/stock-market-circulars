## Summary

NSE's Listing department (Circular Ref: NSE/CML/74720, 0965/2026) notifies the admission of further issued securities for 24 companies to dealings on the NSE Capital Market segment with effect from June 16, 2026. The issues are predominantly equity shares allotted under Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOPs).

## Key Points

- 24 companies have had further issues of equity shares listed on NSE, effective June 16, 2026.
- Most allotments are ESOP shares issued to employees.
- Each security is identified by its designated symbol and code; trading lot size is 1 for all listed securities.
- All newly listed shares rank pari passu with existing shares and have no lock-in.
- Issued pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular operates under existing NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations (Regulation 3.1.1 for admission to dealings; Regulation 2.5.5 for lot sizes).

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use the designated security codes for trading in these securities.
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot sizes (Market Lot: 1 for all securities listed).

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 15, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 16, 2026 (securities admitted to dealings)
- **Allotment Dates (sample):**
  - Aditya Birla Capital Limited: June 10, 2026 (60,177 shares, ISIN INE674K01013)
  - Astec LifeSciences Limited: June 1, 2026 (582 shares, ISIN INE563J01010)
  - Cipla Limited: June 4, 2026 (44,770 shares, ISIN INE059A01026)

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact. These are routine ESOP-related further issues representing small additions to the existing share capital of each company. All shares are pari passu with existing equity and carry no lock-in, making them immediately tradeable from June 16, 2026. No change in trading mechanics or market structure is involved. Companies affected include large-caps (Wipro, ICICI Bank, Cipla, Mankind Pharma) and mid/small-caps across diverse sectors.
