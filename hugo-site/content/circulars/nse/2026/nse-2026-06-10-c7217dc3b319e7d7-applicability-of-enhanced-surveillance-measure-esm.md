## Summary

NSE has issued Circular 432/2026 under the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework, adding six securities to ESM Stage I and upgrading one security from Stage I to Stage II effective June 11–12, 2026. Affected securities will attract a minimum 100% margin and will be moved from the Rolling Settlement segment (EQ/SM) to the Trade-for-Trade segment (BE/ST).

## Key Points

- Six securities added to ESM Stage I effective June 11, 2026, with 100% margin and EQ/SM → BE/ST migration from June 12, 2026
- One security (TANKUP – Tankup Engineers Limited) upgraded from ESM Stage I to Stage II effective June 11, 2026
- Stage II securities placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction
- No securities excluded from the ESM framework in this circular
- ESM classification is a market surveillance action and should not be construed as adverse action against the company

## Regulatory Changes

The ESM framework update is issued under references to prior circulars NSE/SURV/56948, NSE/SURV/57609, NSE/SURV/63361, NSE/SURV/64066, NSE/SURV/64400, and NSE/SURV/69315. Securities satisfying ESM criteria are reclassified to the Trade-for-Trade segment, and Stage II securities are additionally subject to a 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must apply a minimum 100% margin on all open positions in newly included ESM securities as on June 11, 2026, and on new positions from June 12, 2026
- Trading in affected securities must be conducted in the Trade-for-Trade (BE/ST) series from June 12, 2026
- Stage II security (TANKUP) must be traded under Periodic Call Auction with a 2% price band from June 11, 2026
- ESM measures apply in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures

## Important Dates

- **June 10, 2026**: Circular issued
- **June 11, 2026**: ESM Stage I inclusion effective; TANKUP moves to Stage II; Stage II price band restrictions begin
- **June 12, 2026**: 100% margin requirement effective on new positions; EQ/SM to BE/ST migration effective

The mandatory 100% margin and Trade-for-Trade settlement significantly restrict liquidity and leverage for affected securities. Stage II classification further limits price movement to 2% per auction session, making these stocks highly illiquid. Retail and institutional participants holding positions in these securities will face immediate margin calls and constrained exit options.
