## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has issued a circular introducing an additional exposure margin of 15% in the equity derivatives segment on securities where the top 10 clients account for more than 20% of the Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL). This framework builds on the existing exposure margin norms outlined in NSE circular NSE/INVG/40472 dated March 18, 2019. Where additional surveillance margin already applies to a security, the higher of the two additional margins (exposure margin vs. surveillance margin) will be levied. The list of securities is determined using 3-month rolling data and will be reviewed monthly. Eighteen securities have been identified for this framework, effective July 31, 2026.

## Key Points

- Additional exposure margin of 15% applies in the equity derivatives segment to securities where top 10 clients hold more than 20% of MWPL.
- Where additional surveillance margin (ASM-type) is already applicable, the higher of the additional exposure margin or additional surveillance margin will be levied — the two are not cumulative.
- Securities are identified based on 3 months of rolling client concentration data.
- The list will be reviewed and updated on a monthly basis.
- 18 securities have been shortlisted in the initial list (Annexure 1), including ADANIENSOL, ADANIENT, AMBUJACEM, AUROPHARMA, BANDHANBNK, BHEL, CONCOR, GLENMARK, IDEA, IEX, LICHSGFIN, MANAPPURAM, NBCC, NMDC, PATANJALI, RBLBANK, SAIL, and INDUSTOWER.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular operationalizes an additional margin framework referencing the exposure margin norms from NSE circular NSE/INVG/40472 (March 18, 2019). It introduces a client-concentration-based trigger (top 10 clients > 20% of MWPL) for imposing a supplementary 15% exposure margin on identified securities in the equity derivatives segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing members and trading members must factor in the additional 15% exposure margin for the 18 listed securities (and any future additions via monthly review) when computing margin requirements in equity derivatives.
- Where a security already attracts additional surveillance margin, members should apply the higher of the surveillance margin or the new exposure margin, not both.
- Members should monitor monthly updates to the security list as it is reviewed based on rolling 3-month data.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** July 16, 2026
- **Effective Date:** July 31, 2026
- Review Frequency: Monthly, based on 3-month rolling data

## Impact Assessment

The additional exposure margin increases the cost of carrying derivative positions in the 18 identified securities, potentially reducing leverage and open interest in these contracts. Market participants with concentrated positions in these names may face higher margin outflows, which could dampen speculative activity and improve risk containment for securities with high client concentration relative to MWPL. The impact is moderate and largely operational/margin-related rather than affecting the underlying business fundamentals of the listed companies.
