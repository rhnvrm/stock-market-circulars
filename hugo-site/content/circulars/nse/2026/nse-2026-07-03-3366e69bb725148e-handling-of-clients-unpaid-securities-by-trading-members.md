## Summary

NSE has forwarded SEBI Circular No. HO/38/11/(9)2026-MIRSD-POD/I/15382/2026 dated July 03, 2026, regarding the "Handling of Client's Unpaid Securities by Trading Members." The circular amends Paragraph 46 of the Master Circular for Stock Brokers (dated June 17, 2025), revising the provisions originally introduced via SEBI circulars dated June 20, 2019 and November 11, 2022. The amendment follows representations from the Brokers' Industry Standards Forum (ISF) seeking alignment with regulatory developments such as mandatory pay-out of securities directly to clients' demat accounts, and to address operational challenges faced by trading members.

## Key Points

- SEBI has amended Paragraph 46 of the Master Circular for Stock Brokers dealing with handling of client's unpaid securities by Trading Members (TM) and Clearing Members (CM).
- For trades not covered under Margin Trading Facility (MTF), pay-out of unpaid securities must be made directly to the client's demat account, followed by an auto-pledge (without specific client instruction) marked "unpaid," in favor of a new dedicated account called the "Client Unpaid Securities Pledgee Account (CUSPA)" to be opened by the TM.
- After pledge creation, TMs must send a communication (email/SMS) to clients informing them of their funds obligation and the TM's right to sell the securities if the client fails to meet the obligation.
- TMs are required to formulate and maintain a policy (standalone or as part of the Risk Management Policy) for handling unpaid securities, covering invocation processes, reasons, manner, and timing, and communicate this policy to clients prior to implementation.
- The revision responds to industry representations from the Brokers' Industry Standards Forum (ISF) seeking easier compliance amid regulatory and market changes, including mandatory direct pay-out of securities to client demat accounts.

## Regulatory Changes

Paragraph 46 of the SEBI Master Circular for Stock Brokers (dated June 17, 2025) has been amended. Key changes include introduction of the Client Unpaid Securities Pledgee Account (CUSPA) mechanism replacing/updating prior handling provisions, and a mandated auto-pledge process tied directly to pay-out into client demat accounts rather than retention by the TM.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must open a CUSPA account for holding auto-pledged unpaid securities.
- TMs must implement auto-pledge creation (marked "unpaid") upon pay-out of unpaid securities to client demat accounts for non-MTF trades.
- TMs must send timely client communications (email/SMS) regarding funds obligations and the TM's right to sell unpaid securities upon client default.
- TMs must formulate, maintain, and disseminate a documented policy on handling unpaid securities, including invocation triggers, reasons, and timelines, prior to implementation.
- Members must comply with any further operational guidelines issued by exchanges pursuant to this circular.

## Important Dates

- SEBI Circular Ref No. HO/38/11/(9)2026-MIRSD-POD/I/15382/2026: dated July 03, 2026.
- NSE Circular Ref No. 35/2026 (Download Ref No. NSE/INSP/75046): dated July 03, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

The amendment affects operational and compliance processes for all trading members handling unpaid client securities, requiring changes to back-office and risk management systems (setup of CUSPA accounts, auto-pledge workflows, and client communication protocols). While this is a compliance/process-level change rather than a market-moving event, non-compliance could expose trading members to regulatory action. The circular does not directly affect specific listed securities or trading volumes.
