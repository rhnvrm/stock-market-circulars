## Summary

NSE has circulated Notice-cum-Addendum No. SIF/05/2026 issued by Mirae Asset Trustees Private Limited regarding changes to certain features of the Platinum Hybrid Long-Short Fund, a strategy under Platinum SIF (Specialized Investment Fund) brought to you by Mirae Asset Mutual Fund. The changes pertain to the minimum application/switch-in amount for accredited investors and the minimum amount for Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) instalments, effective Wednesday, July 1, 2026.

## Key Points

- The minimum application/switch-in amount for accredited investors is being reduced from Rs. 5,00,000 to Rs. 1,00,000, with both retaining multiples of Re. 1,000/- thereafter.
- The minimum SIP (Monthly and Quarterly Systematic Investment Plan) instalment amount is being reduced from Rs. 50,000/- to Rs. 5,000/-, with the multiple changing from Re. 1,000/- to Re. 1/- thereafter.
- The minimum number of SIP instalments remains at 5.
- The minimum total investment amount by an investor via SIP must remain not less than Rs. 10,00,000/-.
- These changes update Part I (Highlights/Summary of the Investment Strategy) of the Investment Strategy Information Document (ISID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM) of Platinum Hybrid Long-Short Fund.

## Regulatory Changes

The Statement of Additional Information (SAI), ISID, and KIM of Platinum Hybrid Long-Short Fund are amended to reflect lower minimum application and SIP thresholds, as approved by Mirae Asset Trustees Private Limited. All other terms and conditions of the SAI, ISID, and KIM remain unchanged.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE MF Invest Platform members and distributors must update their systems to reflect the revised minimum application and SIP amounts for Platinum Hybrid Long-Short Fund.
- Eligible accredited investors and their distributors should be informed of the revised minimum investment thresholds prior to the effective date.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: Wednesday, July 1, 2026 — revised minimum application and SIP amounts come into force.

## Impact Assessment

The change lowers the entry barrier for accredited investors and SIP participants in the Platinum Hybrid Long-Short Fund by reducing minimum investment and instalment thresholds, potentially broadening accessibility within the eligible accredited investor base. The impact is operational and limited to platform/member-level system updates and investor communication, with no effect on listed securities or broader market trading activity.
