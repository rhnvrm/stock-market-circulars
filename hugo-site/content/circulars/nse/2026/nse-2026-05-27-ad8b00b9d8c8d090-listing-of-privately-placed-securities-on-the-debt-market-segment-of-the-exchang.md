## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed Commercial Papers (CPs) of ten companies to the Debt Market Segment effective May 27, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All securities were allotted on May 26, 2026, with redemption dates ranging from June 2026 to November 2026.

## Key Points

- Ten companies have listed Commercial Papers on NSE's Debt Market Segment effective May 27, 2026
- All CPs have a face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit and were allotted on May 26, 2026
- Issue prices reflect short-term discount yields ranging from approximately 1.6% to 4% below face value
- Tata Steel issued the largest tranche (11,500 units; ₹573 crore notional)
- L&T Finance issued 8,000 units and Motilal Oswal Financial Services issued 7,800 units
- ICICI Securities has the longest tenor among listed CPs (redemption: November 20, 2026)
- The IIFL Finance entry was truncated in the source document

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational circular notifying admission of new debt securities under existing Debt Market Trading Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

Members of NSE are notified to take note of the new security codes assigned per the Annexure for trading purposes. No additional compliance action is required from listed companies or members beyond standard debt market trading procedures.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing circular with low impact on equity markets. The Commercial Papers represent short-term borrowing instruments (3–6 month tenors) used by these companies for working capital and treasury management. The combined issuance spans multiple sectors including steel (Tata Steel), financial services (ICICI Securities, HDFC Securities, L&T Finance, Motilal Oswal, IIFL Finance, Aditya Birla Capital), chemicals (SRF), tyres (CEAT), and diversified conglomerates (Godrej Industries). Institutional debt investors and treasury desks should update their security master with the new ISIN codes and NSE-assigned security codes.
