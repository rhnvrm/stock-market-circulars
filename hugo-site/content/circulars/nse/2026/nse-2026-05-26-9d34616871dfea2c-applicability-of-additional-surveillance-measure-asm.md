## Summary

NSE has issued a circular updating the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework effective May 27, 2026. Two securities have been moved from the Short-Term ASM (STASM) framework into Long-Term ASM (LTASM) Stage I, while one security has been excluded from the ASM framework and shifted to the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework. All other stage transitions within the Long-Term ASM framework show no changes (Nil).

## Key Points

- **DSSL** (Dynacons Systems & Solutions Limited, INE417B01040) moved from Short-Term ASM to Long-Term ASM Stage I w.e.f. May 27, 2026
- **UFBL** (United Foodbrands Limited, INE382M01027) moved from Short-Term ASM to Long-Term ASM Stage I w.e.f. May 27, 2026
- **VERTOZ** (Vertoz Limited, INE188Y01031) excluded from ASM framework w.e.f. May 27, 2026 and moved to ESM framework
- No securities shortlisted for Long-Term ASM Stage IV in this circular
- No inter-stage movements (Stage I↔II, II↔III, III↔IV) within the Long-Term ASM framework this cycle
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the ASM framework is included as an annexure

## Regulatory Changes

The NSE has updated the Long-Term ASM framework under its surveillance mandate. Securities shortlisted under Stage IV criteria (Criteria VII) are shifted from Rolling Settlement (Series: EQ) to Trade for Trade settlement (Series: BE) on a T+3 basis from the date of issuance of the circular. The movement of VERTOZ from LTASM to ESM represents an escalation of surveillance intensity for that security.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants and investors holding or trading DSSL and UFBL should note the enhanced surveillance and any associated trading restrictions applicable under LTASM Stage I
- Trading members must ensure compliance with applicable margin and settlement requirements for securities placed under ASM
- VERTOZ transitions to ESM framework requirements; trading members should review ESM-specific obligations for that security
- Participants should refer to the consolidated ASM list (Annexure - Consolidated ASM sheet) for the full current list of securities under the framework

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** May 27, 2026 — all changes to ASM framework applicability take effect
- **Circular Date:** May 26, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The impact is moderate and confined to the three named securities. DSSL and UFBL face heightened scrutiny and potential trading restrictions under Long-Term ASM Stage I, which may reduce liquidity and increase margin requirements for these counters. VERTOZ, while exiting ASM, now falls under the stricter ESM framework, which typically imposes more stringent trading conditions. Broader market impact is minimal as no mass stage escalations or new Stage IV (Trade-for-Trade) inclusions were announced in this circular.
