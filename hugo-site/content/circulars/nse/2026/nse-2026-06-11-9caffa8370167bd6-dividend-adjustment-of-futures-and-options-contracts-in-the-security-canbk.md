## Summary

NSE has announced the adjustment of Futures and Options contracts for CANBK (Canara Bank) in accordance with SEBI guidelines for corporate action adjustments. The adjustment is triggered by a dividend of Rs 4.20 per share (face value Rs 2) with an ex-date and effective date of June 12, 2026. Revised option strike prices for all CANBK contracts expiring on June 30, 2026 are detailed in Annexure 1.

## Key Points

- **Symbol:** CANBK (Canara Bank)
- **Corporate Action:** Dividend of Rs 4.20/- per share (Face Value Rs 2/-)
- **Ex-date / Effective Date:** June 12, 2026
- Revised strike prices are lower by approximately Rs 4.20 from original strikes (e.g., 104.00 → 99.80, 120.00 → 115.80)
- Adjustments apply to OPTSTK contracts expiring June 30, 2026
- Revised futures base prices and lot sizes may appear in decimal and will be rounded to nearest tick size / integer

## Regulatory Changes

Pursuant to SEBI guidelines on corporate action adjustments for derivatives, NSE is revising option strike prices and futures base prices for CANBK contracts. The adjustment factor reflects the declared dividend amount of Rs 4.20 per share.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must load the updated `contract.gz` / MII contract file (`NSE_FO_contract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz`) and spread file (`NSE_FO_spdcontract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz`) on their trading applications **before trading on the ex-date (June 12, 2026)**.
- Files are available from the `faoftp/faocommon` directory on the Extranet server and on the NSE website under all-reports-derivatives.
- Position adjustment methodology will be separately communicated by the respective Clearing Corporation.

## Important Dates

| Event | Date |
|---|---|

## Impact Assessment

All traders and members holding open positions in CANBK Futures and Options contracts must update their contract files before June 12, 2026 to reflect the adjusted strike prices. Failure to load updated contract files could result in trading on stale strikes. The dividend adjustment reduces strike prices by approximately Rs 4.20 across all listed CANBK strikes for the June 30, 2026 expiry series. This is a standard, non-disruptive adjustment and does not alter the economic value of existing positions.
