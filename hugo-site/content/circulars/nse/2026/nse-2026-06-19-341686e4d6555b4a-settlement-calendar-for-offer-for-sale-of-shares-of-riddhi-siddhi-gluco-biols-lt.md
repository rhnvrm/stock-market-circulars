## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued the settlement calendar for the Offer for Sale (OFS) of shares of Riddhi Siddhi Gluco Biols Ltd. The OFS spans two order dates — June 22 and June 23, 2026 — with settlements scheduled for June 23 and June 24, 2026 respectively. This circular references the earlier circular NCL/CMPT/73996 dated April 30, 2026.

## Key Points

- OFS order date for non-retail (IS) investors: June 22, 2026; settlement date: June 23, 2026
- OFS order date for retail (RS) and non-retail (IS) investors: June 23, 2026; settlement date: June 24, 2026
- Non-retail (IS) category carries 100% margin for H-type settlements and 0% for T-type settlements
- Retail (RS) category carries 100% margin with no custodial confirmation required
- Custodial confirmation deadline for IS category on Day 1: June 22, 2026 by 03:30 PM
- Custodial confirmation deadline for IS category on Day 2: June 23, 2026 by 03:30 PM
- T-type IS confirmation on settlement day by 07:30 AM

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular implements settlement procedures under the existing framework established by circular NCL/CMPT/73996 dated April 30, 2026, which stipulates that OFS settlements follow existing secondary market transaction rules.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and custodians must adhere to the stated settlement calendar for both non-retail and retail categories
- Funds Pay-in must be completed by T+1 day, 11:00 AM
- Securities and Funds Pay-out will be processed by T+1 day, 01:30 PM
- Applicable Securities Transaction Tax (STT) and stamp duty will be collected on the respective settlement day
- For queries, members should contact the help desk at 1800 266 0050 (Option 2) or ofs_ncl@nsccl.co.in

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on members, custodians, and portfolio custodian members (PCMs) participating in the Riddhi Siddhi Gluco Biols OFS. Participants must ensure timely funds pay-in and monitor custodial confirmation windows closely. The two-day OFS window (June 22-23) with T+1 settlement aligns with standard secondary market procedures, minimising systemic risk. STT and stamp duty obligations on respective settlement days add compliance touchpoints for back-office teams.
