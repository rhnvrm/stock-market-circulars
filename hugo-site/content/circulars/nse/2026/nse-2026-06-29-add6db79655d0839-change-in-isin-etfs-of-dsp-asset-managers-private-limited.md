## Summary

NSE has notified members that the ISIN codes for two DSP Mutual Fund ETFs will change effective July 3, 2026. The change is necessitated by a sub-division of the unit face value from Rs. 10/- to Re. 1/- for both ETFs. Members must update their systems to use the new ISINs for all trades on and after the ex-date.

## Key Points

- Two DSP ETFs are undergoing a unit sub-division from Rs. 10/- to Re. 1/- per unit
- New ISINs are assigned to both ETFs effective July 3, 2026
- The new ISINs apply to all trades executed on or after the ex-date of July 3, 2026
- Circular issued by NSE Listing Department under Ref No: NSE/CML/74912

## Regulatory Changes

The ISIN codes for the following ETFs are being changed:

| Symbol | Fund Name | New ISIN | Remarks |
|--------|-----------|----------|---------|
| MIDQ50ADD | DSP Nifty Midcap 150 Quality 50 ETF | INF740KA1ZF3 | Sub-division from Rs. 10/- to Re. 1/- |
| HEALTHADD | DSP Nifty Healthcare ETF | INF740KA1ZG1 | Sub-division from Rs. 10/- to Re. 1/- |

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must update their trading and back-office systems to reflect the new ISINs for both ETFs
- All trades on or after July 3, 2026 must reference the new ISIN codes
- Holdings and position records should be updated accordingly to avoid settlement failures

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 29, 2026
- **Ex-date / Effective Date:** July 3, 2026 — new ISINs become operative for all trades from this date

## Impact Assessment

The ISIN change is an administrative consequence of the unit sub-division and does not alter the underlying investment exposure of the ETFs. However, members and custodians need to ensure timely system updates to prevent trade rejections or settlement mismatches. Investors holding these ETFs will see their unit count increase proportionally (10x) while the per-unit NAV adjusts downward, keeping the total investment value unchanged. The lower face value may improve liquidity by making individual units more accessible to retail investors.
