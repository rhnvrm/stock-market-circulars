## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has revised the Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL) and position limits for Participants, Institutional Clients, and Non-Institutional Clients for the symbol **TRENT** under the Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) Scheme. The revision is effective from **June 05, 2026** and represents an approximately 50% increase across all limit categories for the month of June 2026.

## Key Points

- Circular reference: NCL/CMPT/74544 (Circular Ref. No: 058/2026)
- Partially modifies earlier circular NCL/CMPT/74480 dated May 29, 2026, which itself referenced NCL/CMPT/73999 dated April 30, 2026
- All four limit categories for TRENT are revised upward by approximately 50%
- Applies to the SLB Scheme for June 2026

## Regulatory Changes

### Old Limits

| Category | Limit (No. of Shares) |
|---|---|
| Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL) | 22,393,658 |
| Institutional Client Limit | 2,239,365 |
| Participant Limit | 2,239,365 |
| Non-Institutional Client Limit | 223,936 |

### New Limits

| Category | Limit (No. of Shares) |
|---|---|
| Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL) | 33,590,487 |
| Institutional Client Limit | 3,359,048 |
| Participant Limit | 3,359,048 |
| Non-Institutional Client Limit | 335,904 |

## Compliance Requirements

- All Participants and Custodians are required to take note of the revised limits
- Existing positions exceeding the new limits (not applicable here as limits are being increased) do not require action; however, participants must ensure new positions comply with the revised limits effective June 05, 2026

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 04, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 05, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The ~50% upward revision in MWPL and related limits for TRENT in the SLB segment increases the headroom available for securities lending and borrowing activity. Participants and institutional clients with existing or planned SLB positions in TRENT will benefit from greater flexibility. The revision is consistent with periodic rebalancing of SLB limits and does not impose any new compliance burden — it relaxes the existing position cap.
