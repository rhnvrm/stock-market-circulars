## Summary

NSE has informed trading members that Bajaj Auto Limited will conduct a buyback offer under the Tender Offer scheme, using the Exchange's web-based bidding platform. The offer follows the framework set out in NSE circular NSE/CMTR/52776 dated June 29, 2022.

## Key Points

- Bajaj Auto Limited will buy back up to 46,94,000 equity shares at a fixed price of Rs. 12,000 per equity share.
- Buyback offer type: Fixed price, Tender Offer scheme.
- Security symbol: BAJAJ-AUTO, Series: BB, ISIN: INE917I01010, Face Value: Rs. 10 per equity share, Lot size: 1 equity share.
- Merchant Banker: Kotak Mahindra Capital Company Limited; Registrar: Kfin Technologies Limited.
- All Capital Market segment trading members are eligible to participate via the Tender Offer Facility (https://eipo.nseindia.com).
- Members already using the IPO bidding platform can use existing login credentials; new members can use the admin terminal to create branches/users, with login ID format Adminxxxxx (xxxxx = Member Code).
- Bidding categories include FII, MF, IC, FI, Other QIBs, Bodies Corporate, Individuals, and Other Non-Institutional Investors.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; this circular operationalizes the existing Tender Offer Scheme framework (NSE/CMTR/52776 dated June 29, 2022) for the Bajaj Auto buyback.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use the designated web-based bidding platform (https://eipo.nseindia.com) for bid entry.
- Members without existing access must log into the admin terminal to set up branches and users before bidding.
- Custodial confirmations must be completed within the specified time windows during the issue period.

## Important Dates

- Issue Period: July 1, 2026 to July 7, 2026
- Bidding Time and Custodial Confirmation: 9:15 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. daily
- Custodial Confirmation on Issue Closure (July 7, 2026): 9:15 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.

## Impact Assessment

The circular is operational in nature, enabling trading members to facilitate client participation in the Bajaj Auto buyback. It directly affects Bajaj Auto shareholders considering tendering shares and trading members handling the bidding process, but has limited broader market impact.
