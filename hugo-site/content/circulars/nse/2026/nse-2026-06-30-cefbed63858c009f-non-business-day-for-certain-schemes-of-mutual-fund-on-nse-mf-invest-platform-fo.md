## Summary

NSE has issued a circular informing members that, based on communications received from various Asset Management Companies (AMCs), certain mutual fund schemes will observe non-business days during July 2026 on the NSE MF Invest Platform. On these designated non-business days, Subscriptions, Redemptions, Switches, SIPs, STPs, and SWPs will not be permitted for the affected schemes as listed in the accompanying Annexure I.

## Key Points

- The circular (Download Ref No: NSE/NMFTM/74936, Circular Ref. No: 1459/2026) is issued by the NMF TM Segment of NSE.
- Non-business days apply to specific schemes for which AMCs have intimated disable dates, primarily international/overseas fund-of-funds and a healthcare-focused fund.
- Affected AMCs/schemes include Axis (Greater China Equity FOF, Global Equity Alpha FOF, Global Innovation FOF), Bandhan (US Equity FOF), and DSP (Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FOF, Healthcare Fund).
- All plan/option variants (Direct/Regular, Growth/IDCW Payout/IDCW Reinvestment) of the listed schemes are affected, each identified by distinct ISINs.
- Disable dates noted in Annexure I include 01.07.2026 and 03.07.2026 for different scheme groups.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory or policy change is introduced; this is an operational notification passing through AMC-communicated non-business days to NSE MF Invest Platform members.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members dealing in the listed schemes must ensure no subscription, redemption, switch, SIP, STP, or SWP transactions are placed for the affected schemes on their respective disable dates.
- Members should refer to Annexure I for the complete list of scheme names, ISINs, and applicable disable dates and update their systems/processes accordingly.

## Important Dates

- Disable Date: July 1, 2026 (01.07.2026) — applicable to a subset of listed schemes.
- Disable Date: July 3, 2026 (03.07.2026) — applicable to another subset of listed schemes (including Bandhan US Equity FOF and DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FOF variants).
- Circular issued: June 30, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to investors and distributors transacting in the specific international/overseas fund-of-funds and the DSP Healthcare Fund listed in Annexure I, who will be unable to execute transactions on the designated non-business days. There is no impact on equity markets, listed securities, or trading in other mutual fund schemes not named in the annexure. Operational risk is low, provided members update their transaction processing calendars to reflect the disable dates.
