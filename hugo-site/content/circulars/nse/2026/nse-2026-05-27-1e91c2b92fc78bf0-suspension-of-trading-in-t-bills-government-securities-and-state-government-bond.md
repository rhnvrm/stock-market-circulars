## Summary

NSE has notified the suspension of trading in 16 debt securities — comprising 91-day, 182-day, and 364-day Treasury Bills, Central Government Securities (GS), and State Government Bonds (SG) — effective on their respective suspension dates between June 2 and June 25, 2026, on account of maturity/redemption.

## Key Points

- 16 securities across three categories (T-Bills, G-Secs, State Development Loans) will be suspended from trading on their respective suspension dates.
- Suspension is triggered by scheduled maturity (redemption) as per NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A, Regulation 3.1.2.
- T-Bills account for 10 of the 16 securities; 3 are Central Government Securities; 2 are State Government Bonds (Rajasthan).
- Suspension dates range from June 2, 2026 to June 25, 2026.
- Circular issued by Dhwani Shah, Manager, Listing Department, NSE.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is issued in pursuance of the existing framework under Regulation 3.1.2 of the NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A, which mandates suspension of securities upon maturity.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must ensure no trading occurs in the listed securities from their respective suspension dates.
- Members holding or facilitating client positions in these instruments should ensure orderly exit or settlement prior to the suspension dates.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, low-impact circular. All suspensions are on account of scheduled maturity and are anticipated by market participants. Affected instruments are short-to-medium term government debt instruments with no bearing on equity markets. Investors and members holding these securities will receive redemption proceeds as per RBI/Government of India schedules. No disruption to broader market operations is expected.
