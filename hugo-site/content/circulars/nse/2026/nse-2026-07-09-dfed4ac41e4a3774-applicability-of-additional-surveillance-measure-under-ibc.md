## Summary

NSE issued a circular under its Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework for companies undergoing proceedings under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). Effective July 10, 2026, Future Consumer Limited (FCONSUMER) has been shortlisted under the category "Receipt of corporate announcement / disclosure from the company" based on satisfying the applicable IBC-related ASM criteria. No securities were newly added to or moved between Stage I and Stage II of ASM (IBC) in this cycle, and no securities were excluded. The consolidated ASM (IBC) list continues to include previously flagged securities such as AGSTRA, CLCIND, GENSOL, IMPEXFERRO, and RELINFRA (list truncated in source), all currently at Stage I.

## Key Points

- Future Consumer Limited (FCONSUMER, ISIN INE220J01025) has been shortlisted for ASM (IBC) under the "Receipt of corporate announcement/disclosure" category effective July 10, 2026.
- No new securities were added to Stage I or Stage II of ASM (IBC).
- No securities moved from Stage I to Stage II, or from Stage II to Stage I.
- No securities were excluded from the ASM (IBC) framework in this update.
- The consolidated ASM (IBC) list includes AGSTRA, CLCIND, GENSOL, IMPEXFERRO, RELINFRA, and others (Stage I), reflecting the cumulative set of companies under this surveillance category.
- This measure operates alongside all other prevailing surveillance frameworks (e.g., other ASM stages, GSM) applied by exchanges.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is a periodic update under the existing ASM (IBC) framework established via earlier NSE circulars (NSE/SURV/37979 dated June 08, 2018; NSE/SURV/52368 dated May 20, 2022; NSE/SURV/55582 dated February 10, 2023; and NSE/SURV/70074 dated September 05, 2025). No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a routine shortlisting/exclusion update.

## Compliance Requirements

Members must note the updated ASM (IBC) status of the listed securities and apply the associated trading restrictions and surveillance conditions applicable under the ASM (IBC) framework in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures. Queries can be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 10, 2026 — new ASM (IBC) shortlisting for FCONSUMER takes effect.

## Impact Assessment

The inclusion of FCONSUMER under ASM (IBC) may lead to increased margin requirements, trading restrictions, or heightened scrutiny for trades in this security, potentially affecting liquidity and price volatility for the stock. The impact is company-specific rather than market-wide, and the exchange explicitly notes that shortlisting is a surveillance measure, not an adverse judgment on the company. Other previously listed ASM (IBC) securities remain unchanged in status.
