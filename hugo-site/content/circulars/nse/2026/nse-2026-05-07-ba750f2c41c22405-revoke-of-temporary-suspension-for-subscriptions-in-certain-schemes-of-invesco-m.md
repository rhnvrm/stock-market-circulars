## Summary

NSE has revoked the temporary suspension on subscriptions in three Invesco India Fund of Fund schemes on the NSE MF Invest Platform. Effective May 08, 2026, lumpsum purchases, Switch-ins, and fresh registrations for Systematic Investment Plans (SIP) and Systematic Transfer Plans (STP) are resumed in these schemes as communicated by Invesco Mutual Fund.

## Key Points

- Subscriptions are resumed effective May 08, 2026 on NSE MF Invest Platform
- Three Invesco India Fund of Fund schemes are impacted
- Resumption covers lumpsum purchase, Switch-in, and fresh SIP/STP registrations
- The circular references a notice cum addendum issued by Invesco Mutual Fund as enclosure
- Circular issued by NSE NMF TM Segment; Ref No: NSE/NMFTM/74100, Circular Ref. No: 1359/2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework introduced. This circular revokes a prior temporary suspension, restoring normal subscription operations for the listed schemes on NSE MF Invest Platform.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members are advised to take note of the resumption and update their systems/processes accordingly
- Members should refer to the attached notice cum addendum issued by Invesco Mutual Fund for full details
- Ensure client-facing platforms reflect availability of these schemes for subscription from May 08, 2026

## Important Dates

- **May 07, 2026**: Circular issued by NSE
- **May 08, 2026**: Effective date for resumption of subscriptions in affected Invesco schemes

## Impact Assessment

The impact is moderate for retail investors and distributors who use the NSE MF Invest Platform to invest in international fund-of-fund offerings from Invesco. The three affected schemes — Global Equity Income, Pan European Equity, and Global Consumer Trends — are overseas feeder funds, which may have been suspended due to regulatory or capacity constraints on the underlying foreign funds. Resumption restores investor access to these international diversification products. No broader market or systemic impact is expected.
