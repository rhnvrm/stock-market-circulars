## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a circular (Download Ref No: NCL/COM/75349, Circular Ref. No: 0267/2026) dated July 22, 2026, providing the revised list of approved securities and banks for the Commodity Derivatives Segment. This update supersedes the previous circular no. 0221/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/COM/74874) dated June 25, 2026, and covers equity shares, ETFs, Sovereign Gold Bonds, G-Sec/T-Bills, open-ended mutual funds, corporate bonds, and empaneled banks acceptable as collateral, along with their applicable market-wide limits, member-specific limits, and haircut rates.

## Key Points

- The circular provides seven annexures covering different categories of approved collateral:
  - Annexure 1: Equity shares (non-cash component of liquid assets) with market-wide limits, member-specific limits, and haircut rates
  - Annexure 2: Exchange Traded Funds (non-cash component of liquid assets) with applicable limits and haircuts
  - Annexure 3: Exchange Traded Funds (cash component of liquid assets) with applicable limits and haircuts
  - Annexure 4: Sovereign Gold Bonds and GOI Securities (G-Sec)/T-Bills (cash component of liquid assets) with haircut rates
  - Annexure 5: Open-ended mutual funds acceptable as collateral with market-wide limits and haircut rates
  - Annexure 6: Corporate bonds (non-cash component of liquid assets) with market-wide limits and haircut rates
  - Annexure 7: List of banks empaneled for issuance of Bank Guarantees (BGs) and Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDRs)
- G-Sec/T-Bills carry a shut period of two days; members must release G-Sec/T-Bills at least two days before maturity.
- Members who are also banks must note that G-Sec provided as collateral should not be reckoned for SLR purposes and should not be used for trading.
- The circular is signed by Huzefa Mahuvawala, Chief Risk Officer, NSE Clearing Limited.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine periodic update to the approved collateral list rather than a new regulatory policy. It refreshes eligible securities, their quantity limits, and haircut rates used for collateral valuation in the commodity derivatives segment, replacing the prior list issued in June 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

Members, Custodians, and Professional Clearing Members (PCMs) must align their collateral submissions with the revised list of approved securities, respective haircuts, and permitted quantities specified in Annexures 1-7. Members holding G-Sec/T-Bills as collateral must release them at least two days prior to maturity, and bank-members must ensure G-Sec collateral is excluded from SLR computation and not used for trading purposes.

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: July 22, 2026
- Effective Period: For the month of August 2026
- Supersedes: Circular no. 0221/2026 dated June 25, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a low-to-medium operational impact, primarily affecting clearing members' collateral management and margin computations in the commodity derivatives segment. It does not directly affect trading of specific stocks but ensures continued accuracy in collateral valuation, haircut application, and risk management practices among NSE Clearing members, custodians, and PCMs.
