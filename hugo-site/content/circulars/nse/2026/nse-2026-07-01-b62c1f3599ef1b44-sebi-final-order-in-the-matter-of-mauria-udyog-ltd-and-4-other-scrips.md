## Summary

NSE has issued a circular referencing SEBI's original order (WTM/SM/IVD/ID9/27532/2023-24 dated June 19, 2023) which restrained multiple entities connected to Mauria Udyog Ltd. and 4 other scrips from accessing the securities market, and prohibited them from buying, selling, or otherwise dealing in securities. Subsequently, per SEBI Order No. WTM/AS/IVD-2/ID16/32460/2026-27 dated June 30, 2026, SEBI has disposed of proceedings against two noticees — Mr. Dashrath Yadav (PAN: AEOPY5916K, Noticee 46) and Ms. Saroj Yadav (PAN: AFPPY4880A, Noticee 69) — without any direction or penalty, since their roles in the fraudulent scheme could not be established. SEBI has directed the Exchange to comply with this order, and NSE members have been advised to take note.

## Key Points

- Original SEBI order (June 19, 2023) restrained several entities linked to Mauria Udyog Ltd. and 4 other scrips from the securities market for a specified period.
- Final order dated June 30, 2026 (WTM/AS/IVD-2/ID16/32460/2026-27) disposes of proceedings against Mr. Dashrath Yadav and Ms. Saroj Yadav without penalty or direction.
- The final order lists 23+ entities/individuals originally named in the matter, including Mauria Udyog Ltd., Vee Em Infocenter Ltd., Robert Resources Ltd., Econo Trade India Ltd., and Econo Broking Pvt. Ltd. (erstwhile Bansal Finstock).
- The consolidated list of SEBI-debarred entities remains available on the NSE website for member reference.
- Members must continue ensuring compliance with restrictions applicable to entities still under restraint.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. This is an update/disposal action under an existing SEBI order issued under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1), and 11B(2) of the SEBI Act, 1992, clearing two specific individuals from further restraint due to insufficient evidence of involvement in the fraudulent scheme.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must update their internal debarred-entity records to reflect the disposal of proceedings against Mr. Dashrath Yadav and Ms. Saroj Yadav.
- Members must continue to restrict dealings for the remaining entities still named under the SEBI restraint order.
- Members should refer to the consolidated debarred entities list at nseindia.com/regulations/member-sebi-debarred-entities for the current status.
- Queries can be directed to NSE's Investigation Department at dl-invsg-all@nse.co.in.

## Important Dates

- June 19, 2023: Original SEBI order (WTM/SM/IVD/ID9/27532/2023-24) restraining entities.
- June 30, 2026: SEBI final order (WTM/AS/IVD-2/ID16/32460/2026-27) disposing proceedings against two noticees.
- July 01, 2026: NSE circular (Ref No. 46/2026) communicating the update to members.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited primarily to compliance and surveillance operations at member firms, who must update records regarding the two cleared individuals. There is no direct trading impact on the listed scrips (Mauria Udyog Ltd. and others) beyond the pre-existing restraint status of other named entities, which remains unchanged. This is a routine regulatory follow-up rather than a market-moving event.
