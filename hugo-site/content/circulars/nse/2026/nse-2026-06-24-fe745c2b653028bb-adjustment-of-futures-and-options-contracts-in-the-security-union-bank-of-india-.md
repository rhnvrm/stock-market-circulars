## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has announced the adjustment of Futures and Options (F&O) contracts in UNION BANK OF INDIA (UNIONBANK) following a dividend of Rs. 5/- per share. The adjustment applies to all open positions in UNIONBANK futures and options contracts, with the last cum-dividend date being July 02, 2026, and the ex-dividend date being July 03, 2026.

## Key Points

- Dividend amount: Rs. 5/- per share
- Last cum-dividend date: July 02, 2026
- Ex-dividend date: July 03, 2026
- All open futures positions in UNIONBANK will be marked-to-market on July 02, 2026, and carried forward at the daily settlement price less Rs. 5/-
- For options, Rs. 5/- will be deducted from all cum-dividend strike prices on the ex-dividend date, adjusted to the nearest tick size
- From July 03, 2026, normal daily mark-to-market settlement procedures resume
- Begin-of-day margins on July 03, 2026, will be computed based on adjusted carry forward values

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued pursuant to NSE Circular No. 90/2026 (Download No. NSE/FAOP/74827) dated June 23, 2026. The clearing corporation will carry out the following adjustments:

- **Futures contracts**: Daily mark-to-market settlement price of each relevant futures contract will be reduced by Rs. 5/- (dividend amount) on the last cum-dividend date (July 02, 2026). Open positions are carried forward at the adjusted price.
- **Options contracts**: All cum-dividend strike prices will be reduced by Rs. 5/- on the ex-dividend date (July 03, 2026) and rounded to the nearest tick size to derive new strike prices.

## Compliance Requirements

- All clearing members and trading members with open positions in UNIONBANK futures and options contracts must take note of the adjustment methodology and revised settlement prices.
- Intra-day margins from July 03, 2026, will be computed based on relevant traded prices at the time intra-day SPAN risk parameter files are generated.

## Impact Assessment

All market participants holding open positions in UNIONBANK futures and options contracts will see their positions adjusted around the ex-dividend date. 

**Futures example**: A long/short position of 4,425 units valued at Rs. 175.00 (Rs. 7,74,375) will be adjusted to Rs. 170.00 (Rs. 7,52,250) after the Rs. 5/- dividend deduction — a per-position value reduction of Rs. 22,125.

**Options**: Existing strike prices will be reduced by Rs. 5/- and rounded to the nearest tick size, creating new adjusted strike prices effective from the ex-dividend date. All open positions will be mapped to the new adjusted strikes automatically by the clearing corporation.

The impact is moderate and procedural — this is a standard corporate action adjustment and does not represent a change in the economic value of positions but ensures proper pricing continuity across the dividend event.
