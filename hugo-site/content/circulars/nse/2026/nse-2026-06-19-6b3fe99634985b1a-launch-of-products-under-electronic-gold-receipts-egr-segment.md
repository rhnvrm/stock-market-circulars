## Summary

NSE has announced the launch of two new products under the Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) segment: **GOLD1KG99** (999 purity) and **GOLD1KG95** (995 purity). Both products represent 1 kg of gold per EGR unit and will be available for trading from June 22, 2026. The circular is issued under Ref. No. NSE/EGR/74803 (Circular Ref. No. 17/2026).

## Key Points

- Two new EGR products launched: GOLD1KG99 (999 purity) and GOLD1KG95 (995 purity)
- Trading unit is 1 EGR unit = 1 kg of gold for both products
- Deposit and withdrawal unit is 1 kg
- Quotation/base value is ₹ per 1 kg of gold
- Tick size (minimum price movement) is ₹10 for both products
- Maximum order size is 100 kg
- Trading hours: Monday–Friday, 9:00 am to 11:30 pm / 11:55 pm (subject to US daylight saving time)
- Settlement is T+1 (one working day from the date of transaction)
- Delivery is compulsory — all open buy and sell positions must result in compulsory delivery in demat form

## Regulatory Changes

- New product symbols GOLD1KG99 and GOLD1KG95 introduced in the EGR segment
- Price bands set at 10% of previous closing price initially; dynamic bands relaxed in 5% increments if market trends in either direction
- Margins applicable: VaR Margin, Extreme Loss Margins, Intraday Crystallised Mark to Market Margin, and Mark to Market Margin
- Quality specifications for GOLD1KG99: 999 purity, LBMA Good Delivery Standard approved or NSE-approved suppliers with quality certificate
- Quality specifications for GOLD1KG95: 995 purity (full specs as per Annexure 1)

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure all open positions in GOLD1KG99 and GOLD1KG95 result in compulsory delivery in demat form on the designated delivery day
- Members must comply with margin requirements (VaR, Extreme Loss, MTM) for these new EGR products
- Delivery and withdrawal must be in multiples of 1 kg units

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 19, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 22, 2026 (trading commences)

## Impact Assessment

The launch of GOLD1KG99 and GOLD1KG95 expands NSE's EGR segment, providing an additional avenue for gold trading in demat form. The compulsory delivery mechanism ensures physical gold backing for all trades. The two purity tiers (999 and 995) cater to different market preferences. The impact is moderate — primarily relevant to commodities brokers, gold market participants, and investors seeking exposure to physical gold through the exchange mechanism. The T+1 settlement and compulsory delivery model distinguishes EGR from futures-based gold derivatives.
