## Summary

NSE has issued its Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) update effective June 25, 2026. Six securities are newly added to ST-ASM Stage I, while six securities are excluded from the ASM framework — two of which move to the Long-Term ASM (LTASM) framework and one to the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework.

## Key Points

- 6 securities added to ST-ASM Stage I effective June 25, 2026
- No securities added to ST-ASM Stage II
- No securities moving between Stage I and Stage II
- 6 securities excluded from the ASM framework effective June 25, 2026
- 2 excluded securities (AEROENTER, JAYBARMARU) moved to LTASM framework
- 1 excluded security (DEEM) moved to ESM framework
- 3 excluded securities (AEGISLOG, MOTISONS, SCML) fully removed from ASM

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the revised ST-ASM applicability and apply appropriate margin and trading restrictions for newly added securities from June 25, 2026
- Securities added to ST-ASM Stage I are subject to enhanced surveillance conditions including higher margins and trade-for-trade settlement as applicable under the framework
- Members dealing in excluded securities should update their systems to reflect the removal or reclassification from the ASM framework

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 24, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 25, 2026 — new ST-ASM Stage I additions and exclusions come into force

## Impact Assessment

The six newly added securities (DHARIWAL, IGARASHI, MENNPIS, ORIENTHOT, SLONE, SPCENET) will face enhanced surveillance conditions from June 25, 2026, including higher margin requirements and possible trade-for-trade settlement, which may reduce liquidity and impact short-term price discovery. Investors holding these securities should be aware of the added restrictions. For the six excluded securities, three (AEGISLOG, MOTISONS, SCML) are fully relieved from ASM, potentially improving their liquidity profile, while AEROENTER and JAYBARMARU moving to LTASM and DEEM moving to ESM indicates continued regulatory scrutiny under different frameworks.
