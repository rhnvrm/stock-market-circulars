## Summary

NSE has informed members that, based on communication from Franklin Templeton Mutual Fund, it has disabled fresh registrations under Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) and Systematic Transfer Plan (STP) for the Franklin India Asian Equity Fund and Franklin U.S. Opportunities Equity Active Fund of Funds schemes on the NSE MF Invest Platform, effective immediately. The suspension is aimed at preventing a breach of industry-wide overseas investment limits permitted by SEBI.

## Key Points

- Fresh SIP and STP registrations into Franklin India Asian Equity Fund and Franklin U.S. Opportunities Equity Active Fund of Funds are suspended on the NSE MF Invest Platform.
- Redemptions, Systematic Withdrawal Plans (SWP), Switch-out, and STP-out transactions are unaffected and continue as normal.
- Existing SIP/STP registrations made prior to the effective date will remain operational.
- The restriction does not apply to unitholders under the IDCW Reinvestment Option.
- Total investment in overseas funds/securities across all Franklin Templeton Mutual Fund (FTMF) schemes is capped at the level existing as of February 1, 2022.
- This follows prior addenda dated May 15, 2026 and June 5, 2026 that suspended fresh/additional lump sum purchases into these schemes.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; the suspension implements compliance with Para 13.11 of the SEBI Master Circular for Mutual Funds dated March 20, 2026, regarding overseas investment limits for mutual funds.

## Compliance Requirements

NSE members must stop accepting fresh SIP/STP registrations for the two designated Franklin schemes on the NSE MF Invest Platform. Existing SIPs/STPs, redemptions, SWPs, and switch-outs should continue to be processed normally.

## Important Dates

- Effective date of suspension: July 9, 2026
- Franklin Templeton addendum date: July 8, 2026
- Prior related addenda: May 15, 2026 and June 5, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to investors seeking new SIP/STP registrations in two specific overseas-focused Franklin Templeton schemes. Existing investors and other transaction types (redemptions, SWPs, switches) are unaffected. This is an operational/compliance measure with no direct impact on listed securities, trading, or broader market functioning.
