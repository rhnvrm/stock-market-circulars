## Summary

NSE has completed its quarterly review of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework. New securities are being added to the GSM list effective June 29, 2026 (Annexure I), existing securities continue to remain under GSM (Annexure II), and certain securities will exit the GSM framework effective June 23, 2026 (Annexure III), with their Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) to be released on June 29, 2026.

## Key Points

- 14+ securities shortlisted for inclusion in the GSM framework effective June 29, 2026 (Annexure I)
- Securities in Annexure II continue to remain under GSM framework
- Securities in Annexure III exit the GSM framework effective June 23, 2026
- ASD collected on Annexure III securities will be released on June 29, 2026
- GSM operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures
- Securities placed under GSM Stage VI per SEBI directive remain in Stage VI until further directions
- Trading members must exercise additional due diligence for all GSM-listed securities

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued in continuation of NSE/SURV/34262 dated February 23, 2017 (GSM framework introduction) and subsequent circulars including NSE/SURV/74254 dated May 15, 2026. The GSM framework applies to securities whose price is not commensurate with financial health and fundamentals such as Earnings, Book Value, Fixed Assets, Net Worth, Market Capitalisation, and P/E multiple.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Trading Members**: Must exercise additional due diligence when trading GSM-listed securities on own account or on behalf of clients.
- **Market Participants**: Must note that GSM framework applies alongside all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by Exchanges.
- Securities placed under GSM Stage VI per NSE/SURV/35548 (August 7, 2017) and NSE/SURV/45666 (September 10, 2020) shall continue to remain in GSM Stage VI until further directions.
- For queries, contact: surveillance@nse.co.in

## Impact Assessment

Placement under GSM typically involves significant trading restrictions such as trade-for-trade settlement, additional surveillance deposits, and reduced price bands. This quarterly review adds over 14 securities to the framework, indicating ongoing regulatory concern about price-to-fundamentals mismatches in these counters. Notably, high-profile names such as Gensol Engineering Limited and Reliance Home Finance Limited are among the new additions. Investors holding these securities should expect reduced liquidity and higher trading costs. The exit of Annexure III securities from GSM and the associated ASD release provides some relief for those investors.
