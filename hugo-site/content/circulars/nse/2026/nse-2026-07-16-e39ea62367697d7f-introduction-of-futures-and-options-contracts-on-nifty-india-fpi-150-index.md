## Summary

NSE has announced the introduction of Futures and Options contracts on the Nifty India FPI 150 Index, effective from August 12, 2026, pursuant to approval received from SEBI. The circular sets out the contract specifications, including lot size, strike scheme, settlement type, price bands, and the initial expiry cycle for the new index derivative.

## Key Points

- New F&O contracts on Nifty India FPI 150 Index become available for trading in the Futures & Options segment from August 12, 2026.
- Symbol for both futures and options: NIFTYFPI (Instrument types FUTIDX and OPTIDX).
- Contract/lot size: 1,100 units; tick size: Re 0.05.
- Trading cycle: 3 serial monthly contracts, with expiry on the last Tuesday of the expiry month (or previous trading day if that Tuesday is a holiday).
- Options are European style (Call - CE, Put - PE), with narrow range (step value 5, scheme 20-1-20) and wide range (step value 20, scheme 12-1-12) strike schemes.
- Both futures and options are cash settled; final settlement price is based on the index closing value on the last trading day.
- Quantity freeze limit set at 8,500; spread contracts available for futures (M1-M2, M1-M3, M2-M3).
- No change in the structure of any existing reports.
- Contract files (contract.gz, NSE_FO_contract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz, NSE_FO_spdcontract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz) on the NSE Extranet will reflect the new contracts from end of day August 11, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

This introduction follows SEBI approval for launching Futures and Options contracts on a new underlying index, the Nifty India FPI 150 Index. No amendments to existing bye-laws, rules, or regulations are indicated beyond the addition of this new contract.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must load the updated contract file into their trading applications before the start of trading on August 12, 2026.
- Members should ensure their systems can process the new NIFTYFPI symbol, including the specified tick size, lot size, and strike scheme parameters.
- Settlement procedures and further operational details will be communicated separately by the respective clearing corporations.

## Important Dates

- August 11, 2026 (end of day): Updated contract and MII files made available on NSE Extranet.
- August 12, 2026: Trading in Nifty India FPI 150 Index Futures and Options commences.
- Initial expiry cycle: August 25, 2026; September 29, 2026; October 27, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

The launch expands the derivatives product suite available to trading members, offering a new hedging and trading instrument tied to the Nifty India FPI 150 Index. The operational impact is primarily on trading members, who need to update systems and load new contract files ahead of the launch date. There is no direct impact on existing listed securities, contracts, or individual stock tickers.
