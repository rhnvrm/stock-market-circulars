## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of several privately placed commercial papers to dealings on the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange, effective July 15, 2026, in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The circular lists fresh issues and one re-issue of commercial paper (CP) instruments along with their designated security codes (ISINs).

## Key Points

- Fresh issue commercial papers listed for: IIFL Finance Limited (INE530B14IC8), HDFC Securities Limited (INE700G14UX7), ICICI Securities Limited (INE763G14M02), Tata Steel Limited (INE081A14HF9), Godrej Properties Limited (INE484J14E80), Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited (INE257A14AJ2), and Indian Oil Corporation Limited (INE242A14ZA0).
- Re-issue commercial paper listed for Godrej Properties Limited (INE484J14E72, GPL CP 17/08/26 Sr 55).
- Face value of each CP is Rs. 5,00,000 per unit; issue prices vary based on discount/yield, ranging from approximately Rs. 4,89,972.5 to Rs. 4,97,322.5 per unit.
- Quantities allotted range from 500 to 16,000 securities across the different issuers.
- Dematerialized debentures/bonds are credited under temporary ISINs (IN8*********) per SEBI circular SEBI/HO/DDHS/CIR/P/2020/198 dated October 5, 2022.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is a routine notification under existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations governing admission of privately placed securities to the Debt Market Segment.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance action required from members beyond noting the new security codes for trading purposes on the Debt Market Segment.

## Important Dates

- Effective date of listing: July 15, 2026
- Date of Allotment: July 14-15, 2026 (varies by instrument)
- Redemption dates range from August 13, 2026 to October 13, 2026, depending on the instrument

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market impact as it is a routine administrative listing of privately placed commercial papers on the debt segment. It enables trading members to deal in these newly admitted debt instruments but does not affect equity trading, corporate actions, or broader market operations.
