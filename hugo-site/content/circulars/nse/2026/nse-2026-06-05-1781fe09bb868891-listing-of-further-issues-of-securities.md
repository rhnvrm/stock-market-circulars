## Summary

NSE's Listing department has notified the admission of further equity shares of two SME EMERGE companies — Magson Retail and Distribution Limited (symbol: MAGSON) and Sharp Chucks and Machines Limited (symbol: SCML) — to dealings on the Capital Market segment effective June 08, 2026. Both issuances are preferential allotments with applicable lock-in periods.

## Key Points

- Both securities are listed on the NSE SME EMERGE platform under Series SM.
- Magson Retail (MAGSON): 4,40,000 equity shares of Rs. 10 each issued at Rs. 93.25 per share via preferential allotment; allotted on April 15, 2026.
- Sharp Chucks and Machines (SCML): 5,75,000 equity shares of Rs. 10 each issued at Rs. 76.12 per share via preferential basis; allotted on March 19, 2025.
- Market lot for both securities is 1,000 shares.
- Both securities are pari passu with existing equity shares.
- ISINs are currently activated under temporary ISIN (IN8*) pending activation of the permanent ISIN per SEBI circulars dated August 02, 2012 and September 11, 2012.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. Listing is in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.1 (admission to dealings) and Regulation 2.5.5 (lot size) of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use the designated security codes (MAGSON / SCML) for all transactions.
- All trades must be executed in the prescribed market lot of 1,000 shares.
- Lock-in restrictions apply: MAGSON shares are locked in until December 30, 2027; SCML shares are locked in until January 03, 2028.

## Important Dates

- **Effective date for trading:** June 08, 2026
- **MAGSON allotment date:** April 15, 2026
- **MAGSON lock-in expiry:** December 30, 2027
- **SCML allotment date:** March 19, 2025
- **SCML lock-in expiry:** January 03, 2028

## Impact Assessment

Limited market impact. Both listings are routine further issues for SME EMERGE companies with small float sizes (4,40,000 and 5,75,000 shares respectively). The lock-in periods restrict near-term liquidity for the allottees. Existing shareholders are unaffected beyond the dilution already factored at allotment. Traders should note the 1,000-share minimum lot size applicable to SME EMERGE securities.
