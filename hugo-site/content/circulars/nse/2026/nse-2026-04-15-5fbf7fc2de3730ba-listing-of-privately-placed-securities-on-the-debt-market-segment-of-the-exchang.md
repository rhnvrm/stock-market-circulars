## Summary

NSE has admitted 10 privately placed Commercial Papers (CPs) from 9 companies to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective April 15, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All securities were allotted on April 13, 2026, with redemption dates ranging from May 2026 to February 2027.

## Key Points

- 10 Commercial Paper instruments from 9 issuers listed on NSE Debt Market Segment
- All CPs allotted on April 13, 2026; listed effective April 15, 2026
- Face value uniformly ₹5,00,000 per unit across all instruments
- Issue prices at discount to face value, implying yields between ~4.8% and ~6.5% annualised
- Mahindra & Mahindra Financial Services CP (Sr 4) has the longest tenor — redemption February 26, 2027
- HDB Financial Services CP (Sr 333) has the shortest tenor — redemption May 5, 2026
- Two separate CP tranches listed for Astec LifeSciences Limited (Sr 192 and Sr 193)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members dealing in debt instruments should note the new security codes assigned per the Annexure
- No additional compliance action required for equity market participants

## Impact Assessment

Low market impact. This is a routine debt market listing notification covering short-to-medium term Commercial Papers from established corporates and financial institutions. The issuers include well-known names (Tata group, Mahindra group, ICICI, Kotak, Godrej, HDB Financial), indicating normal treasury/funding activity. No material implications for equity markets or broader market operations. Relevant primarily to institutional investors and debt market participants tracking CP issuances.
