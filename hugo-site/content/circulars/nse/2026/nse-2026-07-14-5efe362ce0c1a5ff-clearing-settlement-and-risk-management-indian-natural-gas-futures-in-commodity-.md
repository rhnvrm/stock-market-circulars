## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a circular detailing the clearing, settlement, and risk management framework applicable to Indian Natural Gas Futures contracts trading in the Commodity Derivatives Segment of NSE. This follows the earlier circular (NSE/COM/75115 dated July 08, 2026) that introduced the product. NCL will provide clearing, settlement, and risk management services for trades executed on these futures contracts, with detailed procedures specified in an annexure covering settlement mechanism, pricing, and margin requirements.

## Key Points

- Contracts will be cash-settled.
- Last day of trading is the last business day of the contract expiry month.
- Daily Settlement Price (DSP) for unexpired contracts is based on the last half-hour weighted average price (minimum 10 trades) or the weighted average of the last 10 trades of the day, or as otherwise decided by the relevant authority.
- Final Settlement Price (Due Date Rate) is the monthly weighted average price of actual deliveries on the Indian Gas Exchange (IGX) for the entire contract month, excluding gas traded at ceiling price, ssLNG trades, and long duration contracts.
- Initial Margin will be computed using SPAN® or an equivalent system, targeting at least 99% VaR coverage subject to SEBI-prescribed minimum floors, varying by volatility category.
- Spread margin benefit: minimum 25% of initial margin levied per leg, with maximum benefit capped at 75%, applicable only when each leg is among the first six expiring contracts.
- Intraday Crystallized Losses (ICMTM) will be calculated and levied for trades resulting in closed-out positions.
- Extreme Loss Margin (ELM) of 1% on gross open positions applies in addition to initial margin, deducted from clearing member liquid assets in real time; no ELM benefit for spread positions.
- Pre-expiry margins apply during the last five trading days before expiry, increasing by 5% each day.
- Additional and/or special margins may be imposed in case of additional volatility (details truncated in source annexure).

## Regulatory Changes

This circular operationalizes the clearing and risk management framework for a new product line (Indian Natural Gas Futures) rather than amending existing regulations. It builds upon the product introduction circular NSE/COM/75115 dated July 08, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

Clearing members trading Indian Natural Gas Futures must adhere to the prescribed margining framework, including initial margin, spread margin conditions, ELM, ICMTM computation, and escalating pre-expiry margins during the last five trading days before contract expiry. Members should direct queries to NSE Clearing Limited.

## Important Dates

- Prior circular reference: July 08, 2026 (NSE/COM/75115) - product introduction.
- Current circular date: July 14, 2026 (Circular Ref. No. 249/2026).
- Pre-expiry margin escalation applies during the last five trading days prior to each contract's expiry date.

## Impact Assessment

The circular is operationally relevant for clearing members and market participants trading Indian Natural Gas Futures, establishing the risk and margining discipline for the new contract. It does not directly affect listed equities or broader market-wide trading rules, limiting its impact to participants in the Commodity Derivatives Segment engaging with this specific product.
