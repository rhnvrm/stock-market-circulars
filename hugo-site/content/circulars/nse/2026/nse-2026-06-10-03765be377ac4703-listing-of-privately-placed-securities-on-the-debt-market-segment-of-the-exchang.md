## Summary

NSE has admitted ten privately placed Commercial Papers (CPs) to its Debt Market Segment effective June 10, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All instruments were allotted on June 9, 2026, with most maturing on September 8, 2026.

## Key Points

- Ten fresh Commercial Paper issues admitted to the NSE Debt Market Segment effective June 10, 2026
- All CPs carry a face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Issuers include major financial institutions (SIDBI, NABARD) and private sector companies
- Allotment date for all listed CPs: June 9, 2026
- Redemption date for most CPs: September 8, 2026 (approximately 91-day tenor)
- 5Paisa Capital CP has a longer tenor maturing October 9, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

All members of the NSE are notified of the new security codes assigned to these instruments as specified in the Annexure. Members dealing in debt market instruments should update their systems with the designated security codes.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** June 10, 2026 (admission to dealings)
- **Allotment Date (all):** June 9, 2026
- **Redemption Date (most):** September 8, 2026
- **Redemption Date (5Paisa Capital):** October 9, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact. This is a standard operational listing notification for short-term debt instruments. The largest issuance is SIDBI's 1,20,000 units (total face value ₹600 crore), followed by ICICI Securities' 31,000 units (₹155 crore). Issue prices reflect prevailing short-term money market rates. No equity market implications.
