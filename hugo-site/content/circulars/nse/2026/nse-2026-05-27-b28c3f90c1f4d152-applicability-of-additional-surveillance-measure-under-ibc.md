## Summary

NSE has issued an update to the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework applicable to companies under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), effective May 29, 2026. No new securities are being added under any ASM IBC stage this cycle, but one security — Shri Ram Switchgears Limited (SRIRAM) — is being excluded from the ASM IBC framework.

## Key Points

- No new securities are being shortlisted under ASM IBC (Annexure I entries are all Nil).
- SRIRAM (Shri Ram Switchgears Limited, ISIN: INE634W01012) is excluded from ASM IBC effective May 29, 2026.
- No stage transitions (Stage I to Stage II or Stage II to Stage I) are occurring this cycle.
- ASM IBC operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures.
- Shortlisting under ASM IBC is purely for market surveillance and should not be construed as adverse action against the company.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular references and continues the ASM IBC framework established under earlier circulars:
- NSE/SURV/37979 dated June 08, 2018
- NSE/SURV/52368 dated May 20, 2022
- NSE/SURV/55582 dated February 10, 2023
- NSE/SURV/70074 dated September 05, 2025

The framework applies Additional Surveillance Measures to companies undergoing insolvency proceedings under IBC.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must note the updated ASM IBC applicability effective May 29, 2026.
- Members should inform clients holding SRIRAM that it is no longer under ASM IBC restrictions from May 29, 2026.
- Trading in securities on the consolidated ASM IBC list remains subject to applicable restrictions.
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 27, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 29, 2026 (for both new inclusions and exclusions)

## Impact Assessment

This update has limited immediate market impact as no new securities are being added to ASM IBC. The exclusion of SRIRAM (Shri Ram Switchgears Limited) is a positive development for that security, as it will no longer be subject to ASM IBC trading restrictions from May 29, 2026. The consolidated ASM IBC list continues to include companies such as Aban Offshore Limited (ABAN), AGS Transact Technologies Limited (AGSTRA), BIL Vyapar Limited (BILVYAPAR), and CLC Industries Limited (CLCIND) at Stage I, among others. Investors and traders in these securities should continue to monitor applicable surveillance restrictions.
