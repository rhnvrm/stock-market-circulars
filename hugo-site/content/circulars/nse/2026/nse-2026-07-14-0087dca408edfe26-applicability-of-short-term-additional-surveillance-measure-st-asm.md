## Summary

NSE has issued a circular under its Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework, identifying securities that meet the criteria for inclusion under ST-ASM Stage I. Kalyan Jewellers India Limited (KALYANKJIL), Karnika Industries Limited (KARNIKA), and C.E. Info Systems Limited (MAPMYINDIA) have been shortlisted under Stage I effective July 15, 2026. No securities were shortlisted under Stage II, and no securities moved between stages in this update. Separately, Amir Chand Jagdish Kumar (Exports) Limited (AMIRCHAND) has been excluded from the ASM framework effective July 15, 2026.

## Key Points

- Three securities added to ST-ASM Stage I: Kalyan Jewellers India Limited (KALYANKJIL), Karnika Industries Limited (KARNIKA), and C.E. Info Systems Limited (MAPMYINDIA).
- No securities were added to ST-ASM Stage II in this circular.
- No securities moved from Stage I to Stage II or vice versa.
- Amir Chand Jagdish Kumar (Exports) Limited (AMIRCHAND) has been excluded from the ASM framework.
- This action is part of NSE's ongoing surveillance framework and is not to be construed as an adverse action against the concerned companies.
- The ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes; this circular applies the existing ST-ASM framework (per prior circulars NSE/SURV/39265, NSE/SURV/46557, NSE/SURV/52144, NSE/SURV/58558, and NSE/SURV/64066) to newly identified securities based on periodic review criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

Members must apply the specified margin rates on all open and new positions in the identified securities from the effective date. No additional filing or disclosure action is required from the listed companies themselves, as the measure is a market surveillance action.

## Important Dates

- July 15, 2026: Effective date for inclusion of KALYANKJIL, KARNIKA, and MAPMYINDIA under ST-ASM Stage I, and exclusion of AMIRCHAND from the ASM framework.
- July 16, 2026: Applicable margin rate of 50% (or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at 100%) applies to all open positions as of July 15, 2026 and new positions created from this date.

## Impact Assessment

Traders and members holding or trading positions in KALYANKJIL, KARNIKA, and MAPMYINDIA will face significantly higher margin requirements (50% or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at 100%), which may reduce leverage, increase trading costs, and dampen speculative activity in these stocks. The measure signals heightened price/volatility surveillance on these securities but is a standard risk-management tool rather than a punitive or fundamental business action against the companies.
