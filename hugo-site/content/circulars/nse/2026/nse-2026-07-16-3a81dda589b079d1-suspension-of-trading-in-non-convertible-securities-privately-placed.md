## Summary

NSE has notified the suspension of trading in three privately placed Non-Convertible Securities (NCDs) with effect from July 17, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A. The suspension is on account of redemption of the respective securities.

## Key Points

- Trading will be suspended in NCDs of three issuers effective 17-July-2026.
- The reason for suspension across all three securities is redemption.
- Affected securities:
  - Sundaram Finance Limited (ISIN: INE660A08BU4)
  - Hero FinCorp Limited (ISIN: INE957N08029)
  - Sammaan Capital Limited (ISIN: INE148I07FJ9)
- Circular issued by NSE's Listing Department under Download Ref No. NSE/CML/75233 and Circular Ref. No. 1160/2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an operational notification under existing Regulation 3.1.2 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A governing suspension of debt securities upon redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

Members dealing in these ISINs must cease trading in the specified securities from the suspension date and update their systems/records accordingly. No further action is prescribed beyond compliance with the suspension.

## Important Dates

- Suspension effective date: 17-July-2026 (for all three securities)
- Circular date: July 16, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to holders and traders of these specific privately placed NCDs, which are being suspended as part of the routine redemption process. There is no broader market impact expected, as this is a standard operational action affecting a small set of debt instruments reaching maturity/redemption.
