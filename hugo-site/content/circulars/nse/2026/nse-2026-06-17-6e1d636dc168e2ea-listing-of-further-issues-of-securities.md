## Summary

NSE's Listing Department (Circular Ref. No. 0980/2026, Download Ref. No. NSE/CML/74748) notifies the admission of further securities to dealings on the NSE Capital Market segment with effect from **June 18, 2026** for 14 companies. The new securities arise primarily from ESOP allotments and, in the case of Gujarat Gas Limited, from a Scheme of Arrangement.

## Key Points

- Effective date for trading of all newly listed securities: **June 18, 2026**
- Issued under Regulation 3.1.1 (admission to dealings) and Regulation 2.5.5 (lot sizes) of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A
- Trading must use the designated security codes; lot size is 1 for all listed securities
- All new shares rank pari passu with existing shares; no lock-in applicable
- 14 companies covered spanning banking, insurance, pharma, finance and consumer sectors

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This circular is a routine notification under existing NSE listing regulations admitting further issued securities to trading.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use the designated security codes when trading the newly admitted securities
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot sizes as per the Annexure
- No additional compliance action required from listed companies beyond the allotment already completed

## Important Dates

| Event | Date |

| Circular issued | June 17, 2026 |

| Allotment date — Cartrade Tech (ESOP) | June 11, 2026 |

## Impact Assessment

**Aadhar Housing Finance Limited (AADHARHFC):** 8,58,855 equity shares of Rs. 10 allotted under ESOP (distinctive numbers 436440045–437298899); minor dilution.

**Cartrade Tech Limited (CARTRADE):** 95,000 equity shares of Rs. 10 allotted under ESOP (distinctive numbers 47928036–48023035); minimal dilution.

**Gujarat Gas Limited (GUJGASLTD):** 62,27,14,719 equity shares of Rs. 2 allotted pursuant to Scheme of Arrangement (distinctive numbers 688390126–1311104844); significant share count increase reflecting corporate restructuring already disclosed.

**HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, ICICI Lombard, ICICI Prudential, Monarch Networth Capital, Piramal Pharma, Prime Securities, Rubicon Research, Sheela Foam, TATA Consumer Products, Ujjivan Small Finance Bank:** Further details in the full Annexure (content truncated in source); likely ESOP or other corporate-action allotments.

Overall market impact is low. These are incremental share additions from pre-approved corporate actions and do not signal new regulatory obligations for market participants.
