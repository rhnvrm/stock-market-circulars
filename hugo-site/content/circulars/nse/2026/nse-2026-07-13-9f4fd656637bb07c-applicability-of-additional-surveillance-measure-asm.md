## Summary

NSE has issued its periodic update on the Long-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) Framework, effective July 14, 2026. No new securities were shortlisted for Stage I, Stage IV, or most stage transitions this cycle. The only movement is Soma Textiles & Industries Limited (SOMATEX, ISIN INE314C01013), which moves from Stage I to Stage II of the Long-Term ASM Framework. A consolidated list of all securities currently under the ASM framework (Stages I-IV) was also published, and no securities were excluded from the framework in this update.

## Key Points

- No new securities shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Stage I or Stage IV (Nil)
- One security, SOMATEX, moves from Stage I to Stage II effective July 14, 2026
- No transitions reported for Stage II→III, I→IV, II→IV, III→IV, IV→III, III→II, or II→I (all Nil)
- No securities excluded from the ASM framework in this cycle (Annexure II: Nil)
- Consolidated ASM list published covering all securities currently in Stages I-IV
- Securities shortlisted under Criteria VII (Stage IV) are shifted from Rolling Settlement (EQ series) to Trade for Trade (BE series) on a T+3 basis

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine periodic (bi-weekly) update under NSE's existing Long-Term ASM Framework, which categorizes securities into Stages I through IV based on surveillance criteria such as price/volume volatility, client concentration, and other market indicators. No new framework rules were introduced; this circular applies existing criteria to update the list of affected securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply the applicable ASM restrictions (e.g., higher margins, price band constraints) to SOMATEX upon its move to Stage II
- Members must ensure compliance with Trade for Trade settlement (BE series) for any Stage IV securities under Criteria VII
- Brokers should update internal risk and surveillance systems to reflect the revised stage classifications

## Important Dates

- Effective date for all changes: July 14, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The direct market impact is limited, as only one security (SOMATEX) changes status in this cycle, moving to a stricter surveillance stage (Stage II), which typically entails increased margin requirements and potential price band restrictions, likely reducing liquidity and increasing trading costs for that stock. The broader consolidated ASM list continues to affect a large number of already-listed securities, maintaining ongoing surveillance-related trading constraints across the market, but represents no incremental change for those names in this specific circular.
