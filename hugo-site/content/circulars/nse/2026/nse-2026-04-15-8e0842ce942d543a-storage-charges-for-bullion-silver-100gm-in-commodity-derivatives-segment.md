## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has issued a circular specifying the storage charges applicable for Silver (100gm) contracts in the Commodity Derivatives Segment. This follows the modification in Silver Mini Futures and Options contract parameters (NSE/COM/73673, April 09, 2026) and the earlier storage charges circular for Base Metal and Bullion (NCL/COM/72862, February 18, 2026).

## Key Points

- Storage charge for Silver (100gm unit): Rs. 0.50 per 100gm/bar per day
- Transaction charges per deposit: Rs. 350 per transaction
- Window delivery charges: Rs. 350 per transaction
- In-city door delivery (within 25 km radius): Rs. 2,500 per delivery up to 800kg; Rs. 5 per additional kg above 800kg
- Loading/unloading charges: Rs. 35 per bar per activity for both window and door delivery
- GST and applicable government levies will be charged additionally

## Regulatory Changes

This circular supplements the contract parameter modification for Silver Mini Futures and Options (circular NSE/COM/73673 dated April 09, 2026) by defining the associated storage and vault charge structure for the newly configured Silver 100gm contract unit.

## Compliance Requirements

- All members trading in Silver 100gm contracts in the Commodity Derivatives Segment must take note of the applicable storage and ancillary charges.
- Charges for vault transactions, delivery, loading/unloading, and applicable taxes are to be paid directly to the vault by members.
- Members should factor these costs into their delivery and settlement planning for Silver contracts.

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: April 15, 2026
- Effective: Immediately, applicable to Silver (100gm) contracts in the Commodity Derivatives Segment

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a limited and operational impact, primarily affecting members who opt for physical delivery of Silver (100gm) contracts in the Commodity Derivatives Segment. The charges are routine and represent standard vault, logistics, and handling costs. The introduction of a per-day storage fee (Rs. 0.50/100gm/bar) and transaction-based ancillary charges provides cost transparency for members managing physical silver positions. No broader market or pricing impact is expected.
