## Summary

NSE has moved BLUE CHIP INDIA LIMITED (symbol: BLUECHIP, ISIN: INE657B01025) to Stage III of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) effective June 9, 2026. Under Stage III, trading is restricted to Trade for Trade settlement with a 5% (or lower) price band, permitted only once a week (every Monday or the first trading day of the week), and buyers must deposit 100% of trade value as Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD).

## Key Points

- BLUECHIP (BLUE CHIP INDIA LIMITED, INE657B01025) escalated to GSM Stage III from June 9, 2026
- Settlement basis: Trade for Trade (T2T) — no netting off allowed
- Price band: 5% or lower as applicable
- Trading frequency: Once per week (every Monday or first trading day of the week)
- ASD requirement: 100% of trade value, to be deposited by buyers
- Circular Ref No: 422/2026 | Download Ref: NSE/SURV/74574
- ISIN noted as per BSE

## Regulatory Changes

This circular continues the GSM framework established under earlier NSE circulars: NSE/SURV/34262, NSE/SURV/38389, NSE/SURV/42790, NSE/SURV/59425, NSE/SURV/64066, NSE/SURV/73167, and NSE/SURV/74254 (dated February 23, 2017 through May 15, 2026). The security is being escalated within the existing GSM tiered framework to Stage III, tightening restrictions beyond Stage II (which required 50% ASD without weekly trading limitations).

## Compliance Requirements

- **Members/Brokers**: Must take adequate precaution while trading in BLUECHIP; settlement is strictly on trade-to-trade basis with no netting permitted
- **Buyers**: Must deposit ASD equivalent to 100% of trade value before transacting
- **Trading Desks**: Must restrict client orders in BLUECHIP to Mondays (or first trading day of the week) only
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: June 8, 2026
- **Effective Date**: June 9, 2026 (Stage III restrictions apply from this date)

## Impact Assessment

Moving to Stage III GSM severely restricts liquidity in BLUECHIP. Existing holders face substantially reduced exit windows (trading allowed only once per week), and new buyers face a 100% upfront ASD requirement, effectively doubling the capital needed per trade. The trade-for-trade settlement eliminates intraday netting, further deterring speculative activity. Investors holding positions in this security should be aware of the limited trading windows and elevated settlement obligations. This escalation signals continued regulatory concern over the security's trading patterns or fundamentals.
