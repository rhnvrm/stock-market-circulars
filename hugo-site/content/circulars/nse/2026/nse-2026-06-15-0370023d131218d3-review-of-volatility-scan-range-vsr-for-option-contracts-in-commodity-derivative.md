## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has announced the applicable Volatility Scan Range (VSR) for option contracts in the Commodity Derivatives Segment for the month of July 2026. This circular is issued in continuation of circular no. 0712/2026 dated May 15, 2026, and in reference to SEBI circular SEBI/HO/CDMRD/DRMP/CIR/P/2021/08 dated January 11, 2021.

## Key Points

- VSR for July 2026 has been reviewed and published for six commodity option contracts and their variants.
- Copper VSR: 5%
- Gold VSR: 5%
- Silver VSR: 6%
- Zinc VSR: 5%
- Crude Oil VSR: 30% (significantly elevated compared to other commodities)
- Natural Gas VSR: 6%
- Issued by Huzefa Mahuvawala, Chief Risk Officer, NSE Clearing Limited.

## Regulatory Changes

The VSR rates are updated on a monthly basis as per the framework established under SEBI circular SEBI/HO/CDMRD/DRMP/CIR/P/2021/08 dated January 11, 2021. The July 2026 VSR supersedes the rates published in circular 0712/2026 dated May 15, 2026 for the month of June 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

All members of NSE Clearing Limited trading in the Commodity Derivatives Segment must take note of the updated VSR values and ensure their margin calculations and risk management systems reflect the revised rates effective for July 2026.

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: June 15, 2026
- Applicable Period: July 2026
- Previous circular reference: NCL/COM/74231 dated May 15, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The updated VSR rates directly affect margin requirements for option contracts in the Commodity Derivatives Segment. Members will need to ensure sufficient collateral is maintained based on these revised rates. The notably high VSR of 30% for Crude Oil reflects heightened volatility expectations and will require significantly higher margin buffers for Crude Oil option positions compared to other commodities. The remaining commodities (Copper, Gold, Silver, Zinc, Natural Gas) have moderate VSR rates of 5–6%.
