## Summary

NSE's Listing Department (Circular Ref. No. 0937/2026, Download Ref No: NSE/CML/74656) notifies that privately placed commercial papers (CPs) from 10 issuers have been admitted to dealings on the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange effective June 11, 2026. All securities are fresh issuances allotted on June 10, 2026.

## Key Points

- 10 commercial paper issuances admitted to NSE Debt Market Segment effective June 11, 2026
- All CPs were allotted on June 10, 2026 with face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Issuers include public sector entities (NABARD, IOCL, EXIM Bank) and private sector corporates
- Majority of CPs mature on September 9, 2026; one matures June 23, 2026 (IOCL) and one December 7, 2026 (MMFSL)
- Issued under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is issued in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations, which mandates notification of newly admitted debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

All members dealing in the Debt Market Segment should note the new security codes assigned to these CPs as specified in the Annexure. No additional compliance action required from equity market members.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative listing notification with no market-moving implications. The largest issuances by quantity are EXIM Bank (80,000 units), NABARD (45,000 units), and LIC Housing Finance (40,000 units), representing significant short-term borrowing by public sector entities. Issue prices reflect prevailing money market rates with IOCL's short-dated CP (13-day tenor) priced closest to face value (₹498,907 vs ₹500,000), while MMFSL's 6-month paper carries the deepest discount (₹481,937.5). Impact is confined to debt market participants and institutional investors in the CP segment.
