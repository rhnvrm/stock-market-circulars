## Summary

NSE has issued a revised list of approved securities and banks eligible as the non-cash component of liquid assets for the F&O segment. The circular specifies overall permitted quantities across all segments and memberwise permitted quantities for the F&O segment, with haircuts applied on a VAR (Value at Risk) basis.

## Key Points

- Applicable haircut for listed securities is VAR-based.
- A value-based prudential norm applies: exclusion of a pledged security must not result in a margin shortfall of more than 25% at a Clearing Member level.
- The list (Annexure 1) covers hundreds of equity shares with their ISIN, overall permitted quantity across all segments, and memberwise permitted quantity for the F&O segment.
- Memberwise permitted quantity for all listed securities in Annexure 1 is marked as N.A. for the F&O segment.
- Additional annexures (2–7) cover further categories of approved securities and banks.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a periodic revision to the approved securities list, updating permitted quantities and eligible scrips for non-cash collateral. No structural change to the haircut framework is indicated; the update reflects current market capitalisation and liquidity assessments.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing Members must ensure pledged securities used as non-cash liquid assets are drawn from this revised approved list.
- Members must ensure that removing any pledged security from collateral does not cause a margin shortfall exceeding 25% at the Clearing Member level.
- Members should update their internal collateral management systems to reflect the revised permitted quantities.

## Important Dates

- Circular date: 2026-05-22. The revised list is effective from the date of issue unless otherwise specified by NSE.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has medium operational impact for Clearing Members and custodians in the F&O segment who rely on equity shares as non-cash collateral. The update changes the universe of eligible securities and their permitted quantities, requiring members to review and potentially rebalance collateral pools. No immediate market-wide disruption is expected, but members holding securities removed from or reduced in the approved list must arrange alternative collateral promptly.
