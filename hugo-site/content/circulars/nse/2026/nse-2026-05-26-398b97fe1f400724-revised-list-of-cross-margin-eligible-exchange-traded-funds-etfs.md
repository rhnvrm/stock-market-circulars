## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a revised list of Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs) eligible for cross margining in the Capital Market Segment. This circular (Ref. No. 0186/2026, Download Ref: NCL/CMPT/74390) supersedes the earlier circular no. 0148/2026 dated April 27, 2026. The revised list is effective from May 27, 2026.

## Key Points

- The revised list of cross margin eligible ETFs is effective from **May 27, 2026**.
- This is an update to circular no. 0148/2026 (NCL/CMPT/73923) dated April 27, 2026.
- Each eligible ETF has a specified minimum quantity required (in multiples thereof) for cross-margin eligibility.
- ETFs span major indices and sectors including Nifty 50, Nifty Bank, Nifty Next 50, Midcap 150, PSU Bank, IT, Pharma, Auto, and CPSE.
- Multiple fund houses are represented: Nippon India, SBI, ICICI Prudential, HDFC, Kotak Mahindra, Mirae Asset.

## Regulatory Changes

The eligible ETF universe for cross margining has been revised. The updated list replaces the previously issued list from April 27, 2026. Minimum quantity thresholds have been defined per ETF symbol and must be held in the specified multiples to qualify for cross-margin benefits.

## Compliance Requirements

- All members of NSE Clearing Limited in the Capital Market Segment must note the revised list and updated minimum quantity requirements.
- Members using ETFs for cross-margin purposes must ensure their ETF holdings meet the minimum quantity thresholds specified for each eligible symbol.
- Positions in ETFs not on the revised list will no longer be eligible for cross-margin benefits from May 27, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 26, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 27, 2026
- **Supersedes:** Circular no. 0148/2026 dated April 27, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular impacts members who utilize ETF positions for cross-margin offset in the Capital Market Segment. Members must review their ETF holdings against the revised eligible list and ensure compliance with the updated minimum quantity requirements by May 27, 2026. The inclusion of 23+ ETF symbols across diverse fund houses and index categories provides broad cross-margin coverage. Changes in minimum quantities (e.g., HDFCNIFBAN at 30,000 units, PHARMABEES at 25,000 units) may require members to adjust position sizes to retain cross-margin benefits. Overall operational impact is moderate and primarily affects risk and margin management workflows.
