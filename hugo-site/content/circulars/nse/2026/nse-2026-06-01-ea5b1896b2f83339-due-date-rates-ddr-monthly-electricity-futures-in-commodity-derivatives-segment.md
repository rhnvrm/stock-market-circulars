## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has fixed the Due Date Rate (DDR) for the Monthly Electricity Futures (ELECMBL) contract that expired on May 29, 2026. The DDR has been set at ₹3804.00 per MWh and will be used for final settlement of the expired contract.

## Key Points

- DDR fixed at ₹3804.00 per MWh for ELECMBL contract expiring May 29, 2026
- Base quantity unit is 1 MWh
- Issued under Circular Ref. No. 191/2026 (Download Ref: NCL/COM/74481)
- References earlier circulars NCL/COM/69054 (July 10, 2025) and NCL/COM/69390 (July 30, 2025) for the DDR procedure

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular applies the existing DDR procedure for Monthly Electricity Futures as established by circulars NCL/COM/69054 and NCL/COM/69390.

## Compliance Requirements

Members holding open positions in the ELECMBL contract that expired on May 29, 2026 must note the DDR of ₹3804.00 per MWh for final settlement computation.

## Important Dates

- **May 29, 2026**: Expiry date of the ELECMBL Monthly Electricity Futures contract
- **June 01, 2026**: Date of DDR circular issuance

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular affecting members with open positions in the ELECMBL Monthly Electricity Futures contract expired on May 29, 2026. The impact is limited to final settlement cash flows for that specific contract. No broader market or regulatory impact is anticipated.
