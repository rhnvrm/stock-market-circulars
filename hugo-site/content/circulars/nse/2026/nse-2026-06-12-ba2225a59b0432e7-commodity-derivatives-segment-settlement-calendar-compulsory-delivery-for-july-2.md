## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has notified the Settlement Calendar for Compulsory Delivery in the Commodity Derivatives Segment for July 2026 (Circular Ref. No: 0207/2026). The calendar covers futures and options contracts across precious metals (Gold, Silver) and base metals (Aluminium, Copper, Lead, Nickel, Zinc and their mini variants).

## Key Points

- Settlement mode is IMS (Integrated Market Settlement) with T+1 settlement type for all listed contracts.
- Contracts covered include: GOLDM, GOLD1G, GOLD, SILVER, SILVERM, SILVER100, GOLDGUINEA, GOLD10G, ALUMINIUM, ALUMINI, COPPER, LEAD, LEADMINI, NICKEL, ZINC, ZINCMINI.
- Delivery intention window is 10:00 AM to 9:00 PM on respective delivery marking dates.
- Option contracts for SILVER follow the compulsory delivery logic with exercise and assignment provisions.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine periodic notification of the settlement calendar for the upcoming month.

## Compliance Requirements

- All members of the Commodity Derivatives Segment must adhere to the specified delivery pay-in and pay-out schedules.
- Members must ensure timely delivery of commodities and funds as per the settlement numbers (260626 through 260703) assigned to each settlement date.
- Charges for weight differential, premium/discount, settlement dues, and making charges apply as per standard norms.
- Penalties for non-delivery (Dr/Cr) will be levied on defaulting members.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational notice affecting commodity derivatives members who deal in physical delivery contracts. Members involved in gold, silver, and base metals futures and options must plan their logistics and fund arrangements in accordance with the published schedule. No market-wide policy impact; compliance is mandatory for participating members to avoid non-delivery penalties.
