## Summary

NSE has published the list of securities identified for the Reversal Trade Cancellation Mechanism (RTCM) in the Capital Market segment, effective for August 2026. RTCM is a surveillance safeguard mechanism designed to identify and cancel potentially erroneous or manipulative reversal trades executed within a short time window in securities that are typically illiquid or subject to abnormal price/volume patterns. The circular lists several hundred securities symbols that fall under this mechanism for the coming month.

## Key Points

- The RTCM applies to a large set of identified securities (several hundred symbols) as listed in the accompanying CSV file `CM_RTCM_aug2026.csv`.
- Securities under RTCM are subject to monitoring where reversal trades (buy followed by sell, or vice versa, in the same security within a defined short interval) executed at prices deviating significantly may be flagged and potentially cancelled.
- The list is updated periodically (monthly) based on trading pattern analysis, liquidity, and volatility criteria.
- Market participants trading in these securities should be aware of the additional scrutiny and potential trade cancellations under this mechanism.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this circular is a periodic (monthly) update to the existing RTCM applicability list maintained by NSE as part of its surveillance measures for the Capital Market segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and brokers must ensure client orders in the listed securities are executed with awareness that reversal trades may be subject to cancellation under RTCM rules.
- Members should update internal risk and surveillance systems to reflect the revised list of RTCM-applicable securities for August 2026.
- No separate filing or compliance submission is required from listed companies; this is an operational/surveillance notice.

## Important Dates

- The RTCM list is applicable for the month of August 2026 (effective circular period: August 2026).

## Impact Assessment

The inclusion of a security under RTCM increases scrutiny on trading activity and may lead to cancellation of certain reversal trades, which can affect short-term trading strategies and liquidity provision in these stocks. While this does not directly impact company fundamentals, traders and market makers active in these securities should factor in the added surveillance risk. The broad list spanning many small and mid-cap names suggests continued regulatory focus on curbing potential price manipulation in less liquid segments of the market.
