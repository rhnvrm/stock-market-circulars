## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of 3 Treasury Bills (T-bills) and 7 State Development Loans (SDLs) on the Capital Market segment, effective July 17, 2026. The circular specifies the designated security codes, lot sizes, coupon rates, and maturity dates for each instrument as per Regulations 3.1.1 and 2.5.5 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.

## Key Points

- 3 new Treasury Bills admitted for trading: 91-day, 182-day, and 364-day T-bills maturing on 15-Oct-2026, 14-Jan-2027, and 15-Jul-2027 respectively.
- 7 new State Development Loans admitted for trading, issued by Gujarat, Mizoram, Tamil Nadu, and Andhra Pradesh, with coupon rates ranging from 7.38% to 7.72%, maturing between 2034 and 2041.
- All instruments have a lot size of 100.
- Securities will be identified on the trading system by their designated codes and traded in the specified lot sizes.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulations introduced; this is a routine notification under existing Regulation 3.1.1 (listing of securities) and Regulation 2.5.5 (lot sizes) of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

Members must update trading systems to recognize the new security codes and adhere to the specified lot size of 100 units when trading these instruments.

## Important Dates

- Effective date: July 17, 2026
- T-bill maturities: October 15, 2026; January 14, 2027; July 15, 2027
- SDL maturities: July 15, 2034 (Tamil Nadu); July 15, 2035 (Gujarat); July 15, 2036 (Andhra Pradesh); July 15, 2038 (Gujarat and Mizoram); July 15, 2041 (Mizoram and Tamil Nadu)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, low-impact administrative listing of government debt securities on the Capital Market segment. It affects debt market participants and members dealing in T-bills and SDLs but has no bearing on equity securities, corporate actions, or broader market operations.
