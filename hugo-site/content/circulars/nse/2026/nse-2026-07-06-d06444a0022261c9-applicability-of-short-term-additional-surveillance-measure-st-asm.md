## Summary

NSE has notified the applicability of the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework for a set of securities effective July 07, 2026. Six securities have been shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage I. No securities were shortlisted under Stage II, and no securities moved between Stage I and Stage II in this cycle.

## Key Points

- Six securities placed under ST-ASM Stage I: Amir Chand Jagdish Kumar (Exports) Limited (AMIRCHAND), Aditya Ultra Steel Limited (AUSL), Eleganz Interiors Limited (ELGNZ), IKIO Technologies Limited (IKIO), Sylvan Plyboard (India) Limited (SYLVANPLY), and Taal Tech Limited (TAALTECH)
- No securities shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage II
- No securities moved from Stage I to Stage II or vice versa
- The ASM framework operates alongside all other prevailing surveillance measures
- Inclusion is purely a market surveillance measure and not an adverse action against the company

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued under the existing ST-ASM framework established via earlier circulars (NSE/SURV/39265, NSE/SURV/46557, NSE/SURV/52144, NSE/SURV/58558, and NSE/SURV/64066). No new regulatory framework changes were introduced; this is a periodic update applying existing criteria to newly qualifying securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must apply a margin rate of 50% of the transaction value, or the existing margin rate, whichever is higher, subject to a maximum cap of 100%, on the Stage I securities
- Margins apply to all open positions as of July 07, 2026, and new positions created from July 08, 2026 onward
- Members should refer to the published FAQs for further clarification on ASM criteria and application

## Important Dates

- July 07, 2026: Effective date for securities being shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage I
- July 08, 2026: Effective date for applicable margin requirements on open and new positions

## Impact Assessment

The increased margin requirement (50% or higher) will raise the cost of trading in the six listed securities, likely reducing trading volumes and liquidity for these stocks. Investors and traders holding or planning positions in AMIRCHAND, AUSL, ELGNZ, IKIO, SYLVANPLY, and TAALTECH should account for higher capital requirements. The impact is company-specific and does not signal broader market risk, though it may affect short-term price volatility in these individual securities.
