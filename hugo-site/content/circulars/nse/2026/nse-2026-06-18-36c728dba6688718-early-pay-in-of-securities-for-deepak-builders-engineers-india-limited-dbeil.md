## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has issued guidance for members, custodians, and PCMs regarding early pay-in (EPI) of securities for Deepak Builders & Engineers India Limited (DBEIL) in connection with a face value split (sub-division) from Rs 10 per share to Re 1 per share. The record date and ex-date for the corporate action is June 19, 2026.

## Key Points

- DBEIL (ISIN: INE0OPA01019) is undergoing a face value split from Rs 10 to Re 1 per share
- Record date and ex-date are both June 19, 2026
- Members must make early pay-in in the **old/existing ISIN** for the ex-date/record date
- For a sale of 10 shares, early pay-in in depository must be in old ISIN for a quantity of **1 share** (adjusted for the 10:1 split ratio)
- After the record date, pay-in/early pay-in instructions must be executed in the **new ISIN**
- Client-wise early pay-in allocation files should **not** be uploaded when using block mechanism for EPI
- Custodians providing EPI through pool accounts must upload client-wise allocation details based on actual traded quantity (e.g., 10 shares)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular references the existing consolidated circular NCL/CMPT/73996 dated April 30, 2026 (Item 10.18) on Early Pay-in of Securities for Margin Exemption, and SEBI circular NCL/CMPT/53386 dated August 22, 2022 on Block Mechanism in demat accounts for sale transactions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must submit early pay-in in old ISIN for settlements on June 19, 2026 (Settlement No. 2026114)
- For June 22, 2026, EPI for Settlement No. 2026114 should use old ISIN; for Settlement No. 2026115, use new ISIN
- Custodians using pool accounts for EPI must upload client-wise allocation files reflecting actual traded quantities
- Block mechanism EPI users must not upload client-wise allocation files
- All participants must adhere to NCL circular NCL/CMPT/53386 on block mechanism

## Important Dates

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a targeted operational impact on market participants trading DBEIL around the corporate action date. Members and custodians must carefully manage ISIN transitions during the settlement window to avoid errors. The face value split (10:1) requires quantity adjustments in EPI instructions — using the old ISIN with pre-split quantities. Failure to comply with the EPI instructions could result in margin exemption issues or settlement failures for affected trades.
