## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of six fresh commercial paper (CP) issues on the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange, effective July 14, 2026, under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The issuers include Godrej Properties Limited, Astec LifeSciences Limited, Godrej Industries Limited, NTPC Limited, IIFL Finance Limited, and IIFL Samasta Finance Limited.

## Key Points

- Godrej Properties Limited: GPL CP 17/08/26 Sr 55, ISIN INE484J14E72, 2,000 securities, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,96,671, allotted 13-Jul-2026, redemption 17-Aug-2026
- Astec LifeSciences Limited: ALSL CP 13/10/26 Sr 198, ISIN INE563J14DQ3, 500 securities, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,90,177.50, allotted 13-Jul-2026, redemption 13-Oct-2026
- Godrej Industries Limited: GIL CP 12/10/26 Sr 40, ISIN INE233A149K5, 1,500 securities, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,92,026.50, allotted 13-Jul-2026, redemption 12-Oct-2026
- NTPC Limited: NTPC CP 09/10/26, ISIN INE733E14CF8, 50,000 securities, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,91,760, allotted 13-Jul-2026, redemption 09-Oct-2026
- IIFL Finance Limited: IIFL CP 09/10/26, ISIN INE530B14IB0, 300 securities, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,90,422.50, allotted 13-Jul-2026, redemption 09-Oct-2026
- IIFL Samasta Finance Limited: ISFL CP 01/01/27, ISIN INE413U14380, 800 securities, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,80,635, allotted 13-Jul-2026, redemption 01-Jan-2027

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is a routine notification under existing NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations for admission of privately placed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

No action required from members beyond recognizing the designated security codes for these newly admitted instruments for trading purposes on the Debt Market Segment.

## Important Dates

- Effective listing date: July 14, 2026
- Date of allotment (all issues): July 13, 2026
- Redemption dates range from August 17, 2026 to January 1, 2027, varying by issue

## Impact Assessment

This circular is procedural and pertains to short-term corporate debt instruments (commercial paper) rather than equity securities. It has no direct bearing on equity share price or trading activity for the listed companies. The listing enables trading of these CP instruments on NSE's Debt Market Segment, relevant primarily to institutional debt market participants and treasury desks tracking short-term corporate borrowing activity.
