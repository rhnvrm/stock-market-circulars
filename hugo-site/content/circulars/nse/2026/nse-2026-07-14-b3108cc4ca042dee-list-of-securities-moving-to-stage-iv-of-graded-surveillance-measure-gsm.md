## Summary

NSE has announced that two securities, GFSTEELS (Grand Foundry Limited) and UNIVAFOODS (Univa Foods Limited), will move to Stage IV of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective July 15, 2026. This follows a series of earlier circulars dating back to February 2017 that established and updated the GSM framework.

## Key Points

- Two securities are moving to GSM Stage IV: GFSTEELS (ISIN: INE534A01028) and UNIVAFOODS (ISIN: INE275F01019)
- Effective date: July 15, 2026
- Stage IV is the most restrictive GSM stage, involving trade-for-trade settlement, weekly trading windows, full additional surveillance deposit, and a ban on upward price movement
- This action continues from a series of prior circulars dated February 23, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, November 17, 2023, September 20, 2024, May 15, 2026, and June 19, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this circular applies the existing GSM Stage IV surveillance actions to the two named securities under the established framework referenced in prior circulars (NSE/SURV/34262, NSE/SURV/38389, NSE/SURV/42790, NSE/SURV/59425, NSE/SURV/64066, NSE/SURV/74254, and NSE/SURV/74795).

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure trading in these securities is conducted on a trade-for-trade basis with a price band of 5% or lower
- Trading is permitted only once a week (every Monday or the first trading day of the week)
- Buyers must deposit an Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) equal to 100% of trade value
- No upward price movement is permitted in these securities
- No netting off is allowed since settlement is trade-to-trade
- Members are advised to exercise adequate precaution while trading in these securities
- Queries can be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- July 14, 2026: Circular issued (Circular Ref. No: 528/2026, Download Ref No: NSE/SURV/75193)
- July 15, 2026: Effective date for GFSTEELS and UNIVAFOODS moving to GSM Stage IV

## Impact Assessment

This action significantly restricts trading liquidity and price discovery for GFSTEELS and UNIVAFOODS. Investors holding these securities will face reduced ability to exit positions, limited to once-weekly trading windows, and a ban on upward price movement will cap potential gains. The 100% ASD requirement for buyers substantially increases the capital cost of acquiring these shares, likely deterring new buying interest and further reducing liquidity. This is a high-impact regulatory action for holders and prospective traders of these two specific securities, though it has minimal broader market impact given the narrow scope.
