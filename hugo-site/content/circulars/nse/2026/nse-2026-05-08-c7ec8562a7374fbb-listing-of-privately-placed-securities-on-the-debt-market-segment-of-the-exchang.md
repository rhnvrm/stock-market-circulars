## Summary

NSE's Listing Department has admitted privately placed securities to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective May 8, 2026, under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. Eight commercial paper (CP) instruments from seven issuers have been listed, comprising six fresh issues and one re-issue.

## Key Points

- Total of 7 fresh-issue commercial papers and 1 re-issue commercial paper admitted to the NSE Debt Market Segment
- All securities are short-term commercial papers with redemption dates ranging from June 30, 2026 to November 3, 2026
- Face value for all CPs is ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Issuers include Tata Capital Housing Finance, ICICI Securities (multiple tranches), Hero FinCorp, IIFL Finance, IIFL Home Finance, and HDFC Securities
- Circular reference: NSE/CML/74131, Ref No. 0751/2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification issued under the existing framework of Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for members. This circular serves as notification of security admission; designated security codes are specified in the annexure for trading reference.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** May 8, 2026 — all listed securities admitted to dealings
- **Allotment Dates:** May 7–8, 2026
- **Redemption Schedule:**
  - ISEC CP 30/06/26 (INE763G14I16): June 30, 2026
  - ISEC CP 03/08/26 — Re-issue (INE763G14H90): August 3, 2026
  - TCHFL CP 06/08/26 (INE033L14OW8): August 6, 2026
  - ISEC CP 06/08/26 (INE763G14I24): August 6, 2026
  - HFL CP 06/08/26 Sr 5 (INE957N14KF7): August 6, 2026
  - HSL CP 07/08/2026 (INE700G14TJ8): August 7, 2026
  - IHFL CP 15/09/26 Sr 1 (INE477L14FV7): September 15, 2026
  - IIFL CP 03/11/26 Sr 2606 (INE530B14HH9): November 3, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing circular with minimal market impact. The commercial papers represent short-term funding instruments for financial sector entities. ICICI Securities (ISEC) is the most active issuer with three tranches totalling ₹7,250 crore face value. HDFC Securities has the largest single tranche at 6,000 units (₹3,000 crore face value). The listings reflect normal short-term borrowing activity by NBFCs and financial intermediaries and have no direct impact on equity markets.
