## Summary

NSE has forwarded a Gazette of India notification (S.O. 3623(E)) dated 4 July 2026, issued by the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India, under Section 35 of the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967 (UAPA). The notification designates 23 individuals as terrorists and adds their names to the Fourth Schedule of the Act. Among those named is Masood Ilyas Kashmiri (alias Mufti Masood Ilyas, alias Masood Ilyas, alias Abu Mohammad, alias M. Masood Ilyas), son of Sardar Mohammad Ilyas Khan, aged approximately 41 years, presently residing in Rawalkot (Pakistan-occupied Jammu and Kashmir), who is associated with the proscribed terrorist organisation Jaish-e-Mohammad. He has been added as serial number 58 in the Fourth Schedule for his alleged involvement in supplying arms and ammunition, recruiting youth via social media and encrypted applications, and participation in terrorist attacks resulting in deaths and injuries.

## Key Points

- Ministry of Home Affairs notification under Section 35(1)(a) and 35(2) of UAPA, 1967, dated 4 July 2026.
- 23 individuals designated as terrorists and added to the Fourth Schedule of the UAPA.
- Masood Ilyas Kashmiri (Jaish-e-Mohammad) added as entry serial number 58, following existing entry 57.
- Designation is based on the Central Government's belief that the individual is involved in terrorism, including arms supply, online recruitment, and involvement in terrorist attacks.
- Circular is issued for information and compliance by market participants as part of standard regulatory/AML-CFT dissemination.

## Regulatory Changes

The Fourth Schedule to the UAPA, 1967 is amended to insert new serial numbers and corresponding entries for the 23 designated individuals, following the existing serial number 57. This is a statutory amendment via Gazette notification, not a market-structure or trading-related regulatory change.

## Compliance Requirements

Market intermediaries (brokers, depository participants, mutual funds, and other regulated entities) are expected to update their sanctions/watchlist screening databases to include the newly designated individuals as part of standard Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering/Combating the Financing of Terrorism (AML/CFT) obligations, in line with UAPA and PMLA requirements.

## Important Dates

- Notification date: 4 July 2026 (Gazette of India, S.O. 3623(E))
- Circular disseminated by NSE: 22 July 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has no direct bearing on listed securities, trading activity, or specific stocks. Its impact is limited to compliance and AML/CFT screening obligations for market intermediaries, who must ensure the newly designated individuals are incorporated into client screening and due diligence processes. No market or price impact is expected.
