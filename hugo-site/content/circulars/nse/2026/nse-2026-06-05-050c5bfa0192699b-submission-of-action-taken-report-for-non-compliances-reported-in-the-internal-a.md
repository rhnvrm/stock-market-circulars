## Summary

NSE has issued a manual (Annexure A) detailing the process for NSE members to submit Action Taken Reports (ATR) in response to non-compliances identified in their Internal Audit Reports (IAR). The submission is performed through the NSE Member Portal and involves auditor login creation, registration, conflict of interest declaration, and final ATR submission with management comments.

## Key Points

- ATR submission is done via the NSE Member Portal at https://inspection.nseindia.com/MemberPortal/
- Members must create or assign the 'Internal Audit-ATR' role to the auditor's user ID before ATR submission
- Auditor registration is required for every ATR cycle, even for previously registered auditors
- The auditor must submit a Conflict of Interest (COI) declaration before the member can proceed
- Members must provide management comments for each non-complied clause and indicate acceptance (Y/N) for each compliance status marked by the auditor
- The final ATR (Annexure 2) must be downloaded and digitally signed by the member

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This circular provides procedural guidance for an existing compliance requirement — submission of Action Taken Reports against Internal Audit Report findings.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must log in to the NSE Member Portal and set up auditor login with the 'Internal Audit-ATR' role
- Members must register their auditor under the 'ATR Auditor Registration' section for the relevant half-year period
- Auditor must submit the Conflict of Interest form; member must then confirm it from their end
- After the auditor submits the ATR, members must review it, provide management comments, and submit their responses
- Members must download, digitally sign, and submit the final Action Taken Report in PDF format

## Important Dates

No specific deadlines are mentioned in the available content. Submission timelines are tied to the relevant half-year Internal Audit Report cycle.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a medium operational impact limited to NSE trading members. It standardises and digitises the ATR submission workflow, reducing manual coordination between members and auditors. No direct market, pricing, or securities-specific impact. Members who fail to complete ATR submission risk inspection-related non-compliance findings.
