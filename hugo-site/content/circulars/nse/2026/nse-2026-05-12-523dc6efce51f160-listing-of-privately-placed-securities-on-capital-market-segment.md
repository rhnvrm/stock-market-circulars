## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of two privately placed debt securities on its Capital Market segment effective May 13, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A. The securities are debentures issued by Ambium Finserve Limited and Lloyds Metals And Energy Limited, each with a market lot size of 1.

## Key Points

- Two privately placed securities admitted to dealings on NSE Capital Market segment from May 13, 2026
- Both securities carry a market lot size of 1
- Securities are identified by designated codes for trading purposes
- Lot sizes specified per Regulation 2.5.5 of NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A
- Circular reference: NSE/CML/74166, Circular Ref. No. 0763/2026

## Securities Listed

**Ambium Finserve Limited**
- Symbol: 1116AFL27
- Series: N0
- Market Lot: 1
- Issue Description: AFL 11.16% 2027
- Coupon Rate: 11.16%
- Date of Maturity: December 5, 2027
- ISIN: INE0RU307254

**Lloyds Metals And Energy Limited**
- Symbol: 8LMAEL32
- Series: N0
- Market Lot: 1
- Issue Description: LMAEL 8% 2032
- Coupon Rate: 8%
- Date of Maturity: May 7, 2032
- ISIN: INE281B08052

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is issued under existing Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members are notified to recognize these securities by their designated codes only for trading purposes
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot sizes as per the Annexure

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: May 12, 2026
- Effective Date for Trading: May 13, 2026
- Maturity of AFL 11.16% 2027 (INE0RU307254): December 5, 2027
- Maturity of LMAEL 8% 2032 (INE281B08052): May 7, 2032

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing notification with limited broad market impact. The two debt securities are privately placed instruments now admitted to the NSE Capital Market segment, providing a secondary market trading avenue for holders. The Ambium Finserve debenture offers a higher coupon of 11.16% maturing in 2027, while the Lloyds Metals and Energy debenture carries an 8% coupon maturing in 2032. Impact is confined to investors holding or trading these specific instruments.
