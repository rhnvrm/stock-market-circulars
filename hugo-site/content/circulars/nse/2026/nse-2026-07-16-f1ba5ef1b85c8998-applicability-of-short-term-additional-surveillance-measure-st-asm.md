## Summary

NSE has issued its periodic update to the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework, effective July 17, 2026. Seven securities have been newly shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage I, no securities were placed under Stage II or moved between stages, and two securities have been excluded from the ASM framework entirely. A consolidated list of all securities currently under the ASM framework was also published.

## Key Points

- 7 securities shortlisted under ST-ASM Framework Stage I w.e.f. July 17, 2026: D. P. Abhushan Limited (DPABHUSHAN), Fermenta Biotech Limited (FERMENTA), Hardwyn India Limited (HARDWYN), Readymix Construction Machinery Limited (READYMIX), Saj Hotels Limited (SAJHOTELS), Vraj Iron and Steel Limited (VRAJ), and Wanbury Limited (WANBURY)
- No securities shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage II
- No securities moved from Stage I to Stage II or from Stage II to Stage I
- 2 securities excluded from the ASM framework: Freshara Agro Exports Limited (FRESHARA) and Saraswati Commercial India Limited (ZSARACOM)
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under the ASM framework (Stage I) was published, covering roughly 30+ symbols including BUILDPRO, EBGNG, FINOPB, KALYANKJIL, MAPMYINDIA, TARSONS, UNICHEMLAB, and others

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine periodic update under NSE's Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure framework, which identifies securities exhibiting high volatility, price movement, or other surveillance-relevant characteristics for additional monitoring and trading restrictions.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members and market participants must apply the applicable ASM Stage I margin and trading restrictions (such as higher margins) to the newly shortlisted securities from the effective date, and may resume normal trading conditions for the excluded securities.

## Important Dates

- Effective date for all inclusions, stage movements, and exclusions: July 17, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Securities newly placed under ST-ASM Stage I (DPABHUSHAN, FERMENTA, HARDWYN, READYMIX, SAJHOTELS, VRAJ, WANBURY) will face increased margin requirements and trading surveillance, which may reduce liquidity and increase transaction costs for these stocks. Conversely, FRESHARA and ZSARACOM being excluded from ASM should see restrictions eased, potentially improving trading conditions. The overall market impact is limited to the specific securities named, as this is a routine, periodically-updated surveillance mechanism rather than a broad market event.
