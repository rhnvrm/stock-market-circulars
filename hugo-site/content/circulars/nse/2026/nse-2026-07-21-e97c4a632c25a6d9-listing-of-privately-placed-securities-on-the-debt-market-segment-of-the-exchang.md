## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of various privately placed securities on the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange, effective July 21, 2026, under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The securities include fresh issue commercial papers from Mahindra & Mahindra Financial Services Limited, HDFC Securities Limited, Tata Steel Limited, ICICI Securities Limited, Godrej Industries Limited, IIFL Finance Limited, Kotak Securities Limited, and Axis Securities Limited, along with a non-convertible debenture from TVS Motor Company Limited, and a re-issue commercial paper from IIFL Finance Limited.

## Key Points

- Fresh issue commercial papers listed for: Mahindra & Mahindra Financial Services (INE774D14TW3), HDFC Securities (INE700G14UY5), Tata Steel (INE081A14HG7), ICICI Securities (INE763G14M10), Godrej Industries (INE233A149M1), IIFL Finance (INE530B14IE4), Kotak Securities (INE028E14XJ1), and Axis Securities (INE110O14IZ3)
- Other debt security: TVS Motor Company Limited 7.30% NCD due 2029 (INE494B08044), face value Rs. 1,00,000, allotted July 17, 2026, redeeming July 17, 2029
- Re-issue commercial paper: IIFL Finance Limited (INE530B14ID6)
- All securities carry face values predominantly of Rs. 500,000 (commercial papers) issued at a discount to face value

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes; this is a routine notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations confirming admission of privately placed securities to dealings on the Debt Market Segment.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance action required from members beyond noting the security codes and ISINs for trading purposes on the Debt Market Segment.

## Important Dates

- Effective date of listing: July 21, 2026
- Date of allotment (most securities): July 20-21, 2026
- TVS Motor NCD allotment: July 17, 2026; redemption: July 17, 2029
- Various commercial paper redemption dates ranging from August 2026 to February 2027

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact, routine administrative circular reflecting standard admission of privately placed debt instruments to the Debt Market Segment. It primarily affects debt market participants and members trading in these specific securities, with no broader market-wide implications.
