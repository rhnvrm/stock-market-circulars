## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of privately placed securities to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective June 25, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The securities include six commercial papers and three debt bonds issued by prominent corporates.

## Key Points

- Six commercial papers (CPs) admitted to trading: Godrej Industries, Godrej Properties, Axis Securities, ICICI Securities, HPCL, and NTPC
- Three debt securities (DB) admitted: Western Capital Advisors Private Limited and Godrej Industries Limited (two series)
- All commercial papers were allotted on June 24, 2026 and are effective from June 25, 2026
- NTPC's CP is the largest issuance with 60,000 units at face value ₹5,00,000 each
- Godrej Industries issued both CP and two series of long-dated bonds maturing in 2031

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

All Members of NSE are notified of the new security codes assigned (as per the Annexure) for trading purposes on the Debt Market Segment.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt market listing circular with no equity market impact. Debt market participants can now trade these instruments under the assigned security codes. Noteworthy issuances include NTPC's large CP (60,000 units × ₹5,00,000 face value = ₹3,000 crore notional) and Godrej Industries' dual long-dated bonds at 8.23% coupon maturing in 2031. ICICI Securities' CP has a longer tenure (maturing March 2027) compared to the other CPs which mature in September 2026.
