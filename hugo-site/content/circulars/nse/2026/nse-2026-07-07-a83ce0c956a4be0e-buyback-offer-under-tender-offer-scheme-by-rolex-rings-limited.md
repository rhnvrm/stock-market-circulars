## Summary

NSE has informed trading members that Rolex Rings Limited will conduct its buyback offer under the Tender Offer Scheme using NSE's web-based bidding platform. The live bidding session will run from July 9, 2026 to July 15, 2026, allowing eligible trading members of the Capital Market segment to participate in the buyback on behalf of shareholders.

## Key Points

- Rolex Rings Limited has approached NSE to use its web-based bidding platform for the buyback offer.
- The offer follows the Tender Offer Scheme as per NSE circular NSE/CMTR/52776 dated June 29, 2022.
- Issue period: July 9, 2026 to July 15, 2026.
- Bidding and custodial confirmation window: 9:15 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.; custodial confirmation on issue closure extends to 4:00 p.m.
- Live bidding URL for members: https://eipo.nseindia.com
- All Capital Market segment trading members are eligible to participate; existing IPO bidding platform users can use existing credentials.
- Members without platform access can use the admin terminal to create branches and users, with login ID format Adminxxxxx (xxxxx = Member Code).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this circular operationalizes the existing Tender Offer Scheme framework (NSE/CMTR/52776 dated June 29, 2022) for a specific buyback offer.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use the designated web-based bidding platform (https://eipo.nseindia.com) to place bids during the specified issue period and timings.
- Members without existing platform access must set up branches/users via the admin terminal using the Adminxxxxx login format.
- Bids must be submitted within the specified investor category (FII, MF, IC, FI, OTH, CO, IND, NOH) per Annexure 1.

## Important Dates

- Issue Period: July 9, 2026 to July 15, 2026
- Daily Bidding and Custodial Confirmation Time: 9:15 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.
- Custodial Confirmation on Issue Closure: 9:15 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.

## Impact Assessment

This circular is operationally significant for trading members and shareholders of Rolex Rings Limited participating in the buyback (up to 1,00,00,000 equity shares at Rs. 180 per share, face value Re. 1). It does not represent a broader market-wide regulatory change but facilitates a company-specific corporate action through NSE's established Tender Offer Scheme infrastructure.
