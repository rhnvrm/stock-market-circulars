## Summary

NSE has informed members that UTI Mutual Fund is changing the face value of UTI Liquid Fund units on the NSE MF Invest platform effective June 19, 2026. The face value will be revised from Rs. 10/- per unit to Rs. 1,000/- per unit. The scheme will be unavailable for transactions during a closure window of June 17–19, 2026, resuming post market hours (3:00 PM) on June 19, 2026.

## Key Points

- Face value of UTI Liquid Fund changes from Rs. 10/- per unit to Rs. 1,000/- per unit on NSE MF Invest platform
- Change is effective June 19, 2026
- Scheme unavailable for transactions from June 17 to June 19, 2026 (two working days closure)
- Transactions resume post market hours (3:00 PM) on June 19, 2026
- NAV will adjust to reflect the face value change without any adverse effect on investors
- The scheme will also be unavailable through NSE's Mutual Fund Service System (MFSS) and BSE-StAR platform during the closure period

## Regulatory Changes

The face value of UTI Liquid Fund is being revised as per Addendum No. 14/2026-27 issued by UTI Asset Management Company Limited. The addendum is an integral part of the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM), and should be read in conjunction with those documents.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must note the revised face value for UTI Liquid Fund effective June 19, 2026
- Members should not execute transactions in UTI Liquid Fund through NSE MF Invest platform between June 17 and June 19, 2026
- Investors are advised to consult their own tax advisors regarding any tax implications arising from the change in face value

## Important Dates

- **June 17, 2026**: Closure period begins; scheme unavailable for transactions
- **June 18, 2026**: Scheme remains unavailable (second working day of closure)
- **June 19, 2026 (effective date)**: Face value change takes effect; transactions resume post 3:00 PM

## Impact Assessment

This is an operational change with moderate impact on investors and members using the NSE MF Invest platform for UTI Liquid Fund transactions. The two-day transaction blackout (June 17–19, 2026) may affect investors who require liquidity during this period, as the scheme will be unavailable across NSE MFSS and BSE-StAR platforms. However, NAV will adjust proportionally to reflect the new face value, ensuring no financial loss to unit holders. All other features of the scheme remain unchanged.
