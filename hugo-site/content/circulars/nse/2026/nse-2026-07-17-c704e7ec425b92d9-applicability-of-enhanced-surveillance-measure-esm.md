## Summary

NSE has updated the list of securities under its Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework. SAJHOTELS (Saj Hotels Limited) has been newly added to ESM Stage I, SWASTIK (Swastik Pipe Limited) has been moved from ESM Stage I to Stage II, and no securities have moved from Stage II back to Stage I. Separately, a set of securities including AFIL (Akme Fintrade (India) Limited), DBOL (Dhampur Bio Organics Limited), ENSER (Enser Communications) and others have been excluded from the ESM framework altogether.

## Key Points

- Securities newly shortlisted under ESM Stage I (Annexure I) will attract a minimum 100% margin on all open positions as on July 20, 2026, and on new positions created from July 21, 2026.
- SAJHOTELS shifts from the Rolling Settlement segment (EQ/SM series) to the Trade-for-Trade segment (BE/ST series) w.e.f. July 21, 2026.
- SWASTIK moves from ESM Stage I to Stage II and will trade under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction w.e.f. July 20, 2026.
- No securities are listed as moving from Stage II back to Stage I in this cycle.
- Securities listed in Annexure II (including AFIL, DBOL, ENSER, and others per the full annexure) are excluded from the ESM framework w.e.f. July 20, 2026.
- The price band of a scrip exiting ESM reverts to its pre-ESM applicable band, unless it remains subject to another surveillance framework, in which case that framework's band prevails.
- The ESM framework operates alongside all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by the Exchange.
- Inclusion under ESM is purely a market surveillance action and should not be interpreted as adverse commentary on the company.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is an operational update under NSE's existing ESM framework (per earlier circulars NSE/SURV/56948, 57609, 63361, 64066, 64400, and 69315), applying the periodic review criteria to reclassify specific securities across ESM stages and out of the framework. No new regulatory framework is introduced.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE trading members must apply a minimum 100% margin on open and new positions in SAJHOTELS from July 21, 2026.
- Members must ensure SAJHOTELS trades are routed under the BE/ST (Trade-for-Trade) series from July 21, 2026.
- Members must apply the 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism to SWASTIK from July 20, 2026, as it moves to Stage II.
- Members should update systems to reflect the removal of Annexure II securities from ESM restrictions and reinstate applicable price bands where no other surveillance measure applies.
- Queries can be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in.

## Important Dates

- July 20, 2026: Stage transitions and price band changes take effect (Stage I to Stage II shift for SWASTIK; ESM exclusions per Annexure II); margin applies to open positions as on this date.
- July 21, 2026: New ESM Stage I inclusion (SAJHOTELS) takes effect, along with segment shift from EQ/SM to BE/ST and 100% margin on new positions.

## Impact Assessment

The inclusion of SAJHOTELS under ESM Stage I with a 100% margin requirement and shift to Trade-for-Trade will significantly reduce trading liquidity and increase the cost of holding/trading the stock. SWASTIK's move to Stage II tightens restrictions further with a 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction, curbing intraday price movement and trading flexibility. Conversely, securities exiting the ESM framework via Annexure II (AFIL, DBOL, ENSER, and others) should see restored normal trading conditions and price bands, improving liquidity for those counters. Overall market impact is stock-specific rather than systemic, but is high-impact for holders and traders of the affected securities.
