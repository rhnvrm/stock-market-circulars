## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a circular to all Mutual Fund Service System (MFSS) participants informing them about the extension of the facility to create standing instructions for Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) and Systematic Transfer Plan (STP) for mutual fund units held in demat form. This follows a SEBI circular (SEBI/HO/47/14/13(2)2026-MRD-POD2/I/16590/2026) dated July 17, 2026, which mandates this facility extension. The SEBI circular is enclosed as an Annexure to this circular.

## Key Points

- NCL has notified all MFSS participants of the extended SWP/STP standing instruction facility for demat-held mutual fund units
- The circular references and encloses the underlying SEBI circular dated July 17, 2026
- This applies specifically to mutual fund units held in demat (dematerialized) form
- The facility covers both Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) and Systematic Transfer Plan (STP) instructions

## Regulatory Changes

SEBI circular SEBI/HO/47/14/13(2)2026-MRD-POD2/I/16590/2026 dated July 17, 2026, extends the facility for creating standing instructions for SWP/STP for mutual fund units held in demat form. NCL is implementing this SEBI directive within the MFSS segment.

## Compliance Requirements

All MFSS participants are required to take note of the SEBI circular and the extended facility for creating SWP/STP standing instructions for mutual fund units held in demat form, and update their processes/systems accordingly to support this facility.

## Important Dates

- SEBI circular date: July 17, 2026
- NSE circular issue date: July 24, 2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/MFSS/75381, Circular Ref. No: 0272/2026)

## Impact Assessment

This is an operational/procedural circular that enhances investor convenience by extending SWP/STP standing instruction facilities to mutual fund units held in demat form. It does not directly impact trading of listed securities but affects mutual fund participants, RTAs, and depository participants who will need to support the extended facility. Investors holding mutual fund units in demat form benefit from added flexibility for systematic withdrawal and transfer plans.
