## Summary

NSE has issued Circular Ref. No. 158/2026 (Download Ref No: NSE/SURV/73077) dated March 02, 2026, notifying that Ansal Properties & Infrastructure Limited (Symbol: ANSALAPI, ISIN: INE436A01026) will be moved to **Stage I of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM)** with effect from **March 04, 2026**. This action continues the exchange's ongoing GSM surveillance framework established under previous circulars dating back to February 2017.

## Key Points

- **Security affected:** ANSAL PROPERTIES & INFRASTRUCTURE LIMITED (ANSALAPI), ISIN: INE436A01026
- **Action:** Movement to Stage I of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM)
- **Effective date:** March 04, 2026
- **Stage I conditions:** 100% applicable margin rate and a price band of 5% or lower as applicable
- Members are urged to take adequate precautions while trading in ANSALAPI
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued in continuation of the GSM framework established through prior exchange circulars:
- NSE/SURV/34262 (February 23, 2017)
- NSE/SURV/38389 (July 20, 2018)
- NSE/SURV/42790 (November 29, 2019)
- NSE/SURV/59425 (November 17, 2023)
- NSE/SURV/64066 (September 20, 2024)
- NSE/SURV/70168 (September 12, 2025)

The GSM framework places securities under escalating surveillance stages (I through IV) based on identified risk criteria, with each stage imposing progressively stricter trading conditions.

## Compliance Requirements

- **All NSE Members** must apply a **100% margin rate** on trades in ANSALAPI from March 04, 2026
- Members must enforce the applicable **price band of 5% or lower** for ANSALAPI
- Members are required to take adequate precautions while facilitating client trades in the affected security
- Members should review the GSM framework FAQs at https://www.nseindia.com/regulations/graded-surveillance-measure for detailed stage-wise action guidance

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** March 02, 2026
- **GSM Stage I Effective Date:** March 04, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**For Traders and Investors:** The 100% margin requirement means traders must deposit the full value of their position as margin, substantially increasing the capital required to trade ANSALAPI. Combined with the 5% price band, this significantly constrains intraday price movement and leveraged positions.

**For NSE Members/Brokers:** Members must immediately update risk and margin systems to reflect the new requirements for ANSALAPI before market open on March 04, 2026. Non-compliance with margin collection norms could attract regulatory action.

**GSM Stage Overview (for reference):**
| Stage | Key Restriction |
|---|---|
| I | 100% margin, 5% price band |
| II | Trade-for-Trade, 5% price band, 50% ASD by buyers |
| III | Trade-for-Trade, weekly trading (Mondays), 100% ASD by buyers |
| IV | Trade-for-Trade, weekly trading (Mondays), 100% ASD, no upward movement |

ANSALAPI enters at Stage I, the least restrictive GSM tier, but further non-compliance or continued surveillance triggers could escalate it to higher stages.
