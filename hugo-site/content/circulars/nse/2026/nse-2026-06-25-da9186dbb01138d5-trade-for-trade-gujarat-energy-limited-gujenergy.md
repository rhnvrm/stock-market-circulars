## Summary

NSE has directed that Gujarat Energy Limited (symbol: GUJENERGY), formerly known as Gujarat Gas Limited, will trade in the Trade for Trade segment (Series: BE) with effect from July 2, 2026 (Thursday). This change is pursuant to a Scheme of Arrangement and will include participation in the call auction during the pre-open session.

## Key Points

- GUJENERGY (formerly Gujarat Gas Limited) moves to Trade for Trade (Series: BE) from July 2, 2026
- Change is pursuant to a Scheme of Arrangement
- Stock will participate in call auction during pre-open session as per SEBI Circular No. CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012
- Circular issued by NSE Listing Department (Download Ref No: NSE/CML/74908, Circular Ref. No: 1031/2026)

## Regulatory Changes

GUJENERGY is being moved from its current trading series to the Trade for Trade (BE) series. In the BE series, each trade results in compulsory delivery — intraday netting is not permitted, and positions cannot be squared off intraday. This is a significant change in trading mechanics governed by the Scheme of Arrangement and applicable SEBI guidelines.

## Compliance Requirements

- All members must ensure clients are aware of the shift to Trade for Trade (BE series) for GUJENERGY from July 2, 2026
- Members must ensure full delivery obligations are met for all trades in GUJENERGY — no intraday netting allowed
- Pre-open call auction participation rules under SEBI Circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 (January 20, 2012) apply

## Important Dates

- **July 2, 2026 (Thursday):** GUJENERGY moves to Trade for Trade segment (Series: BE) and participates in pre-open call auction

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding or intending to trade GUJENERGY will face mandatory delivery obligations from July 2, 2026. Intraday trading strategies (buy and sell on the same day to square off) will not be available. This is particularly impactful for high-frequency and intraday traders. The move is a direct consequence of the Scheme of Arrangement (demerger/restructuring of the former Gujarat Gas Limited into Gujarat Energy Limited), and is consistent with NSE's standard practice of placing newly restructured entities in the BE segment for an initial period to ensure price discovery and delivery-based settlement.
