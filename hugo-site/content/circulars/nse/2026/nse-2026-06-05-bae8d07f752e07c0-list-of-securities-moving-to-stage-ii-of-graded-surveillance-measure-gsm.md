## Summary

NSE has directed that WINSOME YARNS LIMITED (WINSOME) and BLUE CHIP INDIA LIMITED (BLUECHIP) shall be moved to Stage II of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective June 8, 2026. Trading in these securities will be subject to Trade for Trade settlement with a 5% price band and an Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers.

## Key Points

- Two securities are being escalated to Stage II of GSM: WINSOME YARNS LIMITED (ISIN: INE784B01035) and BLUE CHIP INDIA LIMITED (ISIN: INE657B01025)
- BLUE CHIP INDIA LIMITED will also undergo a series change from EQ to BE, as per BSE
- Effective date for Stage II GSM applicability: June 8, 2026
- Buyers must deposit ASD equal to 50% of trade value
- Price band restricted to 5% or lower as applicable
- Settlement will be on Trade for Trade (TFT) basis
- Members advised to exercise adequate caution while trading in these securities

## Regulatory Changes

These securities are being moved up from their prior GSM stage to Stage II under the NSE GSM framework. This escalation is in continuation of earlier circulars including NSE/SURV/34262, NSE/SURV/38389, NSE/SURV/42790, NSE/SURV/59425, NSE/SURV/64066, NSE/SURV/73167, and NSE/SURV/74254. The series change for BLUE CHIP INDIA LIMITED from EQ to BE is aligned with BSE.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must ensure buyers of WINSOME and BLUECHIP securities deposit ASD of 50% of the trade value
- All trades must be settled on a Trade for Trade basis — no netting of positions
- Members must observe the applicable price band of 5% or lower
- Members are encouraged to direct queries to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- **June 5, 2026**: Circular issued
- **June 8, 2026**: Stage II GSM restrictions come into effect for WINSOME and BLUECHIP

## Impact Assessment

Moving to Stage II GSM significantly tightens trading conditions for WINSOME YARNS LIMITED and BLUE CHIP INDIA LIMITED. The Trade for Trade settlement eliminates intraday netting, reducing liquidity. The mandatory 50% ASD requirement increases the cost of buying these securities, likely dampening demand. The 5% price band further limits price discovery. Investors and traders holding or planning to trade these securities should reassess their positions and strategies ahead of the June 8, 2026 effective date. The series change for BLUE CHIP INDIA LIMITED (EQ to BE) may also affect how the stock appears and is traded across exchanges.
