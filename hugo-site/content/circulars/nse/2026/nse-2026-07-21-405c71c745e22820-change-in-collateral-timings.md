## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has revised the cut-off timings for collateral addition and release in the Currency Derivatives Segment, based on feedback received from members. This updates the timings previously notified vide circular ref. no. 018/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CD/73998 dated April 30, 2026). The revised timings apply to Cash and FDR addition/release as well as the release schedule for pledged securities.

## Key Points

- Cash and FDR Addition cut-off extended from 7:30 PM to 9:00 PM.
- Cash and FDR Release (Intraday) cut-off extended from 8:00 PM to 9:00 PM.
- Cash and FDR Release (End of Day) cut-off remains unchanged at 8:00 PM.
- Cash and FDR Release (Allocation) cut-off extended from 8:00 PM to 11:30 PM.
- Pledge Securities release now follows 16 defined batch timings from 8:30 AM through 8:00 PM, plus an End of Day (EOD) batch at 9:00 PM.
- Members must request release of repledged securities for pay-in requirements one day prior to the pay-in date, not on the pay-in date itself.

## Regulatory Changes

The circular amends the collateral timing framework established under circular ref. no. 018/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CD/73998 dated April 30, 2026), extending several cut-off times for cash/FDR collateral transactions and introducing a granular 16-batch release schedule for pledged securities.

## Compliance Requirements

All Members, Custodians, and Professional Clearing Members (PCMs) in the Currency Derivatives Segment must align their collateral submission and release processes with the new timings. Requests for release of repledged securities needed for pay-in must be submitted one day prior to the pay-in date.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 22, 2026
- Prior circular reference: April 30, 2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CD/73998)

## Impact Assessment

This is an operational change affecting back-office and treasury processes for clearing members, custodians, and PCMs dealing in currency derivatives. The extended cut-off times provide members greater flexibility for end-of-day collateral management, potentially reducing funding pressure near market close. There is no direct impact on listed equities or trading activity for retail investors.
