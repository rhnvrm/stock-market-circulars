## Summary

NSE has notified members that, based on communication received from Bank of India Mutual Fund, there is a change in the minimum redemption amount in certain schemes of Bank of India Mutual Fund on the NSE MF Invest platform, effective July 17, 2026. The circular also encloses a Notice-cum-Addendum (No. 18/2026-27) issued by Bank of India Mutual Fund detailing revised exit load structures for several of its schemes, amending the respective Scheme Information Documents (SID) and Key Information Memoranda (KIM).

## Key Points

- Change in minimum redemption amount for certain Bank of India Mutual Fund schemes on the NSE MF Invest platform, effective July 17, 2026.
- Notice-cum-Addendum No. 18/2026-27 revises exit load structures for multiple schemes, including Bank of India Large Cap Fund, Bank of India Banking & Financial Services Fund, Bank of India Large & Mid Cap Fund, and Bank of India Manufacturing Fund (partially listed in the extracted content).
- Exit load slabs and holding periods have been revised for the affected schemes (e.g., changes to percentage thresholds of initial units allotted and applicable holding periods before redemption/switch-out).
- The notice amends the SID and KIM of the affected schemes.

## Regulatory Changes

Bank of India Mutual Fund has revised the exit load applicable to redemptions/switch-outs across several of its equity schemes, changing both the percentage of exit load charged and the holding period thresholds that determine when exit load applies. These changes amend the existing SID and KIM disclosures for the affected schemes.

## Compliance Requirements

NSE members dealing on the NSE MF Invest platform must update their systems and investor communications to reflect the revised minimum redemption amounts and exit load structures for the affected Bank of India Mutual Fund schemes ahead of the effective date.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 17, 2026
- Notice-cum-Addendum Date: July 10, 2026
- Circular Issued: July 16, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has limited market-wide impact, as it pertains to operational and procedural changes (minimum redemption amount and exit load) within specific mutual fund schemes distributed via the NSE MF Invest platform. Impact is confined to investors and distributors dealing in the affected Bank of India Mutual Fund schemes, with no direct bearing on listed equities or overall market trading activity.
