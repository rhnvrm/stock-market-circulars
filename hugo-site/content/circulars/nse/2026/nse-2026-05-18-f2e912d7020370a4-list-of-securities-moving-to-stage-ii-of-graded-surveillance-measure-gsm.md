## Summary

NSE has moved two securities — Ansal Properties & Infrastructure Limited (ANSALAPI) and Morarjee Textiles Limited (MORARJEE) — to Stage II of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective May 19, 2026. Under Stage II, trading will be restricted to Trade for Trade (T2T) settlement with a 5% price band and an Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value payable by buyers.

## Key Points

- ANSALAPI (INE436A01026) and MORARJEE (INE161G01027) are elevated to GSM Stage II from May 19, 2026.
- Trading will be conducted on a Trade for Trade basis — no netting off of positions is permitted.
- Price band restricted to 5% or lower as applicable.
- Buyers must deposit an Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) equal to 50% of trade value.
- Settlement is strictly trade-to-trade; intraday netting is not allowed.
- Circular reference: NSE/SURV/74276, Circular Ref. No. 372/2026.

## Regulatory Changes

Both securities are being escalated within the GSM framework, moving from their prior stage to Stage II. This is issued in continuation of earlier GSM circulars dating back to February 2017 (NSE/SURV/34262) through the most recent March 2026 circular (NSE/SURV/73167). Stage II imposes stricter surveillance conditions compared to Stage I (100% margin, 5% price band) by additionally requiring Trade for Trade settlement and a 50% ASD from buyers.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must ensure all trades in ANSALAPI and MORARJEE are settled on a Trade for Trade basis with effect from May 19, 2026.
- Buyers in these securities must deposit ASD of 50% of trade value at the time of trading.
- No netting off of buy and sell positions is permitted; each trade must be settled individually.
- Members are advised to take adequate precautions and inform their clients of the revised trading conditions.
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in.

## Important Dates

- **May 18, 2026**: Circular issued (NSE/SURV/74276).
- **May 19, 2026**: Stage II GSM restrictions come into effect for ANSALAPI and MORARJEE.

## Impact Assessment

Elevation to GSM Stage II significantly restricts trading liquidity for both securities. The mandatory Trade for Trade settlement eliminates intraday speculation and prevents position netting, increasing the capital burden on traders. The 50% ASD requirement effectively doubles the upfront capital needed for buyers, likely reducing trading volumes and widening bid-ask spreads. Existing holders may face difficulty exiting positions due to reduced market depth. The 5% price band further limits daily price movement. Overall, this action signals heightened regulatory concern about price manipulation or abnormal trading patterns in these two stocks and serves as a strong caution to members and investors.
