## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of further issues of equity shares for 17 companies on the Capital Market segment, effective June 9, 2026. The circular is issued under Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A, specifying designated security codes and lot sizes.

## Key Points

- 17 companies have been admitted for further dealings on the NSE Capital Market segment effective June 9, 2026
- Allotments include shares issued under ESOP schemes and conversion of warrants on preferential basis
- All securities carry a market lot size of 1 and are pari passu with existing equity shares
- Apollo Micro Systems shares (13,000) have a lock-in expiry of December 10, 2026
- Trading must use designated security codes as specified in the annexure

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. The circular is issued in pursuance of existing Regulation 3.1.1 (admission to dealings) and Regulation 2.5.5 (lot sizes) of NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use the designated security codes for trading in these securities
- Trading in the listed securities must be in the lot sizes specified in the annexure
- Apollo Micro Systems allotted shares are subject to lock-in until December 10, 2026

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** June 9, 2026 — securities admitted to dealings on NSE
- **Aditya Birla Capital (ESOP):** Date of allotment — June 2, 2026; 221,982 shares (INE674K01013)
- **Apollo Micro Systems (Warrant Conversion):** Date of allotment — May 12, 2026; 13,000 shares at Rs. 114 (INE713T01028); Lock-in expiry — December 10, 2026
- **Axita Cotton (ESOP):** Date of allotment — May 28, 2026; 178,200 shares (INE02EZ01022)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing notification with moderate market impact. The addition of ESOP shares for Aditya Birla Capital (221,982 shares) and Axita Cotton (178,200 shares) represents modest dilution. Apollo Micro Systems' 13,000 warrant-conversion shares are subject to lock-in until December 2026, limiting near-term supply. The inclusion of high-profile names such as ICICI Bank, Meesho, Star Health, and Cholamandalam Finance signals ongoing capital activity across diverse sectors. No material disruption to trading is expected.
