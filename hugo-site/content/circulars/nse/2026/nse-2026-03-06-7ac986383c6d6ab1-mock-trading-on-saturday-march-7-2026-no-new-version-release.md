## Summary

NSE will conduct a mock trading session in the Capital Market Segment on Saturday, March 7, 2026. The session covers both Primary Site (Trading Session-1) and Disaster Recovery (DR) Site (Trading Session-2). No new NEAT+ version is being released; members will use NEAT+ 7.8.7. Trades executed during the mock session carry no financial obligations.

## Key Points

- Mock trading scheduled for Saturday, March 7, 2026 in the Capital Market Segment
- No new version release; NEAT+ version 7.8.7 applies (download path: `/common/NEATPlus787`)
- Two trading sessions: Session-1 from Primary Site, Session-2 from DR Site
- All outstanding orders will be cleared before each session
- Members using NNF software must manually clear orders in their systems
- UCC Validation will be skipped during contingency time for order entry
- Only NOTIS trade download will be accessible during the mock session
- Members should re-login to the live environment after mock session to ensure smooth connectivity for Monday, March 9, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. Members may use either Mock Trading or Simulated Environment to meet SEBI regulatory requirements as per circular NSE/MSD/46441 and SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must participate using all trading software during the mock session
- Members must re-login to the live environment post-session to ensure connectivity for the next trading day (Monday, March 9, 2026)
- Only valid UCC/PAN uploaded and approved before cutoff will be permitted; queries to be directed to uci@nse.co.in
- Refer to circular NSE/MSD/67674 dated April 24, 2025 for interactive connectivity details

## Important Dates

- **Mock Trading Date:** Saturday, March 7, 2026
- **Next Live Trading Day:** Monday, March 9, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational mock exercise with no market or financial impact. It allows members to test connectivity, trading software, and DR site failover procedures. No trades will result in fund pay-in or pay-out. The primary impact is operational: members are expected to dedicate time on Saturday for testing to ensure readiness for live trading on Monday, March 9, 2026.
