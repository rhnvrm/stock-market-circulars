## Summary

NSE has issued a final extension for members to migrate their self-service connectivity links from the existing Sify POP at Kohinoor–Kurla, Mumbai to the new Sify POP at Parle, Mumbai. The new deadline is end of day June 15, 2026. NSE has explicitly stated that no further extensions will be granted beyond this date.

## Key Points

- Final extension granted for migration of self-service connectivity links to new Sify POP location
- Existing POP at Kohinoor–Kurla, Mumbai will be discontinued after June 15, 2026 (EOD)
- New POP location is Parle, Mumbai (operated by Sify)
- Original migration announcement was made on August 05, 2025 via circular NSE/MSD/69495
- Previous deadline of June 08, 2026 (per circular NSE/MSD/74468 dated May 29, 2026) has now been extended
- Substantial progress has been made on managed connectivity link migrations; this extension applies to self-service links only
- Members with pending migrations risk disruption to Exchange connectivity for trading

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory rule changes. This circular updates the operational timeline for infrastructure migration previously notified under circular NSE/MSD/69495 (August 05, 2025) and subsequent circulars.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members subscribed to self-service connectivity links under the existing Sify POP at Kohinoor–Kurla must complete migration to the new Parle POP by EOD June 15, 2026
- Members should contact the Telecom Network Integrator (TNI) — Sify Technologies Limited — using contact details in Annexure 1 (Sify Handbook) for queries and coordination
- Contact details: Toll Free No: 1800-266-0050 | Email: msm@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Original migration announcement | August 05, 2025 |
| Previous deadline (Lease Line) | June 08, 2026 |
| **Final deadline for self-service link migration** | **June 15, 2026 (EOD)** |
| Existing POP discontinuation (Final) | June 15, 2026 |

## Impact Assessment

Members using self-service connectivity links at the Kohinoor–Kurla Sify POP who have not yet completed migration face the risk of trading connectivity disruption after June 15, 2026. NSE has emphasized this is the absolute final extension with no further deadline relaxation. The impact is operationally significant for affected members but is limited in market-wide scope since this is an infrastructure migration rather than a trading rule change. Managed connectivity link migrations have already been substantially completed.
