## Summary

NSE has admitted a fresh batch of privately placed commercial papers (CPs) to dealings on the Debt Market Segment with effect from May 26, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The issuers include major financial institutions and corporates such as HDFC Securities, HSBC Investdirect Financial Services, Kotak Securities, Godrej Industries, ICICI Securities, Tata Capital, Tata Capital Housing Finance, Indus Infra Trust, and Hero FinCorp.

## Key Points

- All securities are fresh issue Commercial Papers (CP) allotted on May 25, 2026 and listed on May 26, 2026
- Face value for all CPs: ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Maturities range from short-term (19 June 2026) to medium-term (28 September 2026)
- Total issuance spans 10+ tranches across multiple issuers
- Each CP carries a unique ISIN assigned for trading on the debt segment

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. The circular is issued in pursuance of existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

Members of NSE are notified of the new security codes (as per the Annexure) to be used for trading these CPs on the Debt Market Segment. No additional compliance action is required from members beyond acknowledging the new listings.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with low market impact. The listing of these commercial papers provides short-term funding instruments for well-known financial institutions and corporates on the NSE debt segment. Investors in the debt market can trade these CPs using the designated security codes. The issuance reflects normal treasury and liquidity management activities by the respective issuers. No equity market impact is expected.
