## Summary

NSE has directed that TARAPUR TRANSFORMERS LIMITED (Symbol: TARAPUR, ISIN: INE747K01017) shall be moved to Stage I of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective March 24, 2026. This imposes a mandatory 100% margin rate and a price band of 5% or lower on all trades in this security.

## Key Points

- TARAPUR TRANSFORMERS LIMITED (TARAPUR) is being moved to Stage I of GSM effective March 24, 2026
- ISIN: INE747K01017 (as per BSE)
- Applicable margin rate will be 100%
- Price band restricted to 5% or lower as applicable
- Members are advised to exercise adequate caution while trading in this security
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Regulatory Changes

The security is being placed under Stage I of the GSM framework, which is the first level of enhanced surveillance. This is in continuation of earlier circulars NSE/SURV/34262, NSE/SURV/38389, NSE/SURV/42790, NSE/SURV/59425, NSE/SURV/64066, and NSE/SURV/73167 establishing and updating the GSM framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must ensure 100% margin is collected for trades in TARAPUR
- Price band of 5% or lower must be enforced
- Members should take adequate precaution while trading in this security

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** March 23, 2026
- **Effective Date:** March 24, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Placement in Stage I GSM significantly restricts trading in TARAPUR TRANSFORMERS LIMITED. The 100% margin requirement means buyers must deposit the full trade value as margin, substantially increasing the cost of taking positions. The 5% price band further limits daily price movement. These restrictions reduce liquidity and increase trading costs, typically leading to reduced trading volumes. If surveillance concerns persist, the stock could be escalated to higher GSM stages (II through IV), which carry progressively stricter restrictions including Trade-for-Trade settlement, weekly trading limits, and Additional Surveillance Deposits.
