## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has introduced a new category — **Margin Violation** — for digital representation of penalty review requests through the member portal. This expands the existing digital penalty review system established in February 2024, allowing clearing members to raise margin violation penalty reviews online in addition to previously supported penalty types.

## Key Points

- Margin Violation penalties can now be contested digitally via the NCL member portal.
- This is an extension of the digital penalty review framework introduced under NCL/CMPL/60651 (February 12, 2024) and updated via NCL/CMPL/72710 (February 09, 2026).
- Penalty review requests must be submitted within **7 working days** from the date the penalty is made available to members.
- Delays in submission must be accompanied by written justification and are subject to Member Committee discretion.
- Appeals against Member Committee decisions must be filed within **45 calendar days** of NCL's intimation, per NCL/CMPL/69557 (August 07, 2025).
- Late appeals must also include justification and are handled by the Appeal Panel.

## Regulatory Changes

NCL has expanded the scope of digital penalty review to cover Margin Violation penalties, previously only available for other penalty categories. No change to the underlying timelines or appeal procedures — those remain governed by NCL/CMPL/58063 and NCL/CMPL/69557.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing members must use the NCL member portal to submit Margin Violation penalty review requests digitally.
- Review requests must be raised within 7 working days of the penalty being made available.
- Any delayed review request must be accompanied by a written justification.
- Appeals against Member Committee decisions must be filed within 45 calendar days of NCL's decision intimation.
- Delayed appeals must also include justification for the delay.
- Compliance is required with immediate effect.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** June 23, 2026 (immediate effect)
- **Penalty Review Deadline:** Within 7 working days from penalty availability
- **Appeal Deadline:** Within 45 calendar days from Member Committee decision intimation

## Impact Assessment

This update operationally benefits clearing members by enabling them to contest Margin Violation penalties digitally, streamlining a process that may previously have required manual or physical submissions. The impact is moderate and primarily procedural — no change to penalty amounts, trading rules, or market structure. Members should update their compliance workflows to account for the new category in the portal and ensure staff are aware of applicable deadlines.
