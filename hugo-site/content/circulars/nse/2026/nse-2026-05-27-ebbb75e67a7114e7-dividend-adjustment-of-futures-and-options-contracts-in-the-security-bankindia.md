## Summary

NSE has announced the adjustment of Futures and Options contracts for BANKINDIA (Bank of India) pursuant to a dividend corporate action. The dividend of Rs 4.65/- per share (face value Rs 10/-) will result in revised option strike prices effective from the ex-date of May 29, 2026.

## Key Points

- Symbol: BANKINDIA (Bank of India)
- Corporate action type: Dividend
- Face value: Rs 10/-
- Dividend amount: Rs 4.65/- per share
- Ex-date and effective date: May 29, 2026
- All OPTSTK contracts expiring June 30, 2026 are subject to strike price revision
- Strike prices are reduced by approximately Rs 4.65/- to reflect the dividend adjustment
- Revised strike prices and lot sizes may appear in decimal places; rounded to nearest tick size and nearest integer respectively

## Regulatory Changes

In pursuance of SEBI guidelines for adjustments to futures and options contracts on announcement of corporate actions, option strike prices for all BANKINDIA contracts expiring June 30, 2026 have been revised downward by the dividend amount.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must load the updated contract.gz / MII contract file (`NSE_FO_contract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz`) and spread file (`NSE_FO_spdcontract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz`) on their trading applications **before trading on May 29, 2026**.
- Files are available from the directory `faoftp/faocommon` on the Extranet server and also on the NSE website under all-reports-derivatives.
- Position adjustment methodology will be separately communicated by the respective Clearing Corporation.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 27, 2026
- **Ex-date / Effective date:** May 29, 2026
- **Affected expiry:** June 30, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Traders holding open positions in BANKINDIA options contracts expiring June 30, 2026 will see their strike prices revised downward by approximately Rs 4.65/-. For example, the 115.00 strike becomes 110.35, and the 120.00 strike becomes 115.35. This is a standard mechanical adjustment that preserves economic equivalence of existing positions; no action is required by option holders to maintain position value. Members using automated trading systems must update contract files before the ex-date to avoid order entry errors.
