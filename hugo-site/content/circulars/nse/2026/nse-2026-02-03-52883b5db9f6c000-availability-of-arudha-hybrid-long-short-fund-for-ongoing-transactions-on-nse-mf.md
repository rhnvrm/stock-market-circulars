## Summary

The National Stock Exchange of India has announced that the Arudha Hybrid Long-Short Fund of Bandhan Mutual Fund SIF will be reopened for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions on the NSE MF Invest Platform effective February 04, 2026. The fund will be available with SIP (Systematic Investment Plan), STP (Systematic Transfer Plan), and SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) facilities.

## Key Points

- Arudha Hybrid Long-Short Fund will reopen for ongoing transactions from February 04, 2026
- Available on NSE MF Invest Platform for subscriptions and redemptions
- SIP, STP, and SWP facilities will be enabled
- Fund offered by Bandhan Mutual Fund SIF
- 30 different plan options available (Regular and Direct plans with various IDCW options)
- All plans categorized under Equity category
- Both Growth and IDCW (Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal) options available
- IDCW frequencies include: Daily, Weekly, Fortnightly, Monthly, Quarterly, Half-yearly, Annual, and Periodic

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced. This is an operational update regarding fund availability.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance requirements for members. This is an informational circular notifying members about the availability of the fund schemes on the platform.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: February 04, 2026 - Arudha Hybrid Long-Short Fund reopens for ongoing transactions
- **Circular Date**: February 03, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a fund-specific operational announcement with no broader market implications.

**Investor Impact**: Positive for investors interested in hybrid long-short equity strategies. The fund provides access to a specialized investment strategy with flexible investment and withdrawal options through SIP, STP, and SWP facilities.

**Operational Impact**: NSE members and participants can now facilitate transactions in this fund for their clients through the NSE MF Invest Platform with comprehensive plan options including both regular and direct plans.
