## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of privately placed debt securities of Hero FinCorp Limited and NHPC Limited to dealings on the National Stock Exchange Capital Market segment, effective June 02, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.

## Key Points

- Seven debt securities across two issuers are being listed as a fresh issue on NSE's capital market segment.
- Hero FinCorp Limited is listing one debenture series (HFL 8.75% 2028 Sr 78) with 12,500 securities at face value ₹1,00,000 each.
- NHPC Limited is listing six pass-through (PT) bond series (STRPP A, C, D, E, G, H) all at 7.67% coupon, with 20,000 securities each at face value ₹1,00,000.
- All securities were allotted on May 29, 2026.
- Trading shall use designated security codes as specified in the Annexure; lot sizes apply per Regulation 2.5.5.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is issued under existing Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use the designated security codes for all trading in these securities.
- Trading in each security must conform to the lot sizes specified in the Annexure.

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date:** May 29, 2026 (all securities)
- **Effective Date for Trading:** June 02, 2026
- **Hero FinCorp HFL 8.75% 2028 Sr 78 Redemption:** May 26, 2028
- **NHPC STRPP A Redemption:** May 29, 2032
- **NHPC STRPP C Redemption:** May 29, 2034
- **NHPC STRPP D Redemption:** May 29, 2035
- **NHPC STRPP E Redemption:** May 29, 2036
- **NHPC STRPP G Redemption:** May 29, 2038
- **NHPC STRPP H Redemption:** May 27, 2039

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine debt securities listing with no material impact on equity markets. Institutional and fixed-income investors gain exchange-traded access to Hero FinCorp's 8.75% debenture and NHPC's 7.67% structured pass-through bonds across maturities from 2032 to 2039. The large face value (₹1,00,000 per unit) limits retail participation; impact is primarily relevant to debt market participants.
