## Summary

SEBI has passed a fresh order against Mr. Nishaan Singh, Proprietor of Sanbun Investments and Sanbun Capital Hedge Fund (PAN: HCCPS6377H), following a remand by the Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT). The SAT, in its order dated December 18, 2024, upheld SEBI's earlier findings of violation but directed SEBI to reassess the quantum of money received towards portfolio management services (PMS) and pass a fresh order on refund obligations and the period of debarment.

## Key Points

- SEBI's original order was dated January 30, 2024, finding Mr. Nishaan Singh in violation of Section 12(1) of the SEBI Act read with Regulation 3 of the SEBI (Portfolio Manager) Regulations, 2020, for operating unregistered PMS.
- SAT (Appeal No. 434 of 2024) upheld the violations but remitted the matter for reassessment of the money quantum.
- Total credits in the two HDFC bank accounts of the Noticee amounted to Rs. 16,19,83,314.61.
- The Noticee categorised credits into: (i) Course Fees, (ii) PMS activity money, and (iii) money neither course fees nor PMS.
- The Noticee admitted to collecting Rs. 49,76,127 as money received towards PMS activity.
- Course fee receipts totalled approximately Rs. 11,88,22,317.30, collected via UPI, cash, Instamojo payment gateway, and Paytm.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced. The order applies existing provisions of:
- Sections 11(1), 11(4), and 11B(1) of the SEBI Act, 1992
- SEBI (Portfolio Manager) Regulations, 2020 — Regulation 3 (registration requirement)

## Compliance Requirements

- Mr. Nishaan Singh is required to refund money received towards unregistered PMS activity to investors as directed in the fresh order.
- The period of debarment from securities market activity will be determined based on the reassessed quantum.
- Any entity or individual operating portfolio management services must hold valid SEBI registration under the PMS Regulations, 2020.

## Important Dates

- January 30, 2024: Original SEBI order against Mr. Nishaan Singh.
- December 18, 2024: SAT order (Appeal No. 434 of 2024) upholding violations and remanding the matter.
- April 19, 2025: Noticee submitted representation to SEBI post SAT remand.
- 2026-05-27: Fresh SEBI order issued pursuant to SAT remand.

## Impact Assessment

This order is primarily an enforcement action against a single individual operating unregistered portfolio management services. The broader market impact is limited, but it reinforces SEBI's stance on unregistered intermediaries. Investors who entrusted funds to Sanbun Investments for PMS may be entitled to refunds. The case also highlights the risk of entities offering financial courses alongside unregistered investment management, where the classification of receipts can become contested in enforcement proceedings.
