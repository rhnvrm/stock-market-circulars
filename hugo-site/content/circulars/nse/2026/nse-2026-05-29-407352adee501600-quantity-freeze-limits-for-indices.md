## Summary

NSE has updated the quantity freeze limits for futures and options contracts on five major indices as part of the computation methodology outlined in F&O consolidated circular NSE/FAOP/73928 dated April 28, 2026. The revised limits are effective from June 1, 2026.

## Key Points

- Quantity freeze limits revised for five index derivatives: BANKNIFTY, NIFTY, FINNIFTY, MIDCPNIFTY, and NIFTYNXT50.
- BANKNIFTY freeze limit set at 900 units.
- NIFTY freeze limit set at 1800 units.
- FINNIFTY freeze limit set at 1800 units.
- MIDCPNIFTY freeze limit set at 2800 units.
- NIFTYNXT50 freeze limit set at 600 units.
- Members must load updated contract files (`contract.gz` and `NSE_FO_contract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz`) before trading on June 1, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

The updated quantity freeze limits supersede the previously applicable limits under chapter 1.9 of the F&O consolidated circular (NSE/FAOP/73928, April 28, 2026). The computation methodology for determining freeze limits remains unchanged; only the resultant limit values are being updated.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must download and load the updated `contract.gz` and `NSE_FO_contract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz` files into their trading applications **before trading begins on June 1, 2026**.
- Updated contract files are available from the directory `faoftp/faocommon` on the Extranet server.
- Files are also accessible via the NSE website at the all-reports-derivatives section.
- Members can verify per-underlying freeze quantity details on the NSE equity derivatives contract information page.

## Important Dates

- **May 29, 2026**: Circular issued.
- **June 1, 2026**: New quantity freeze limits become effective; updated contract files must be loaded before trading commences.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational update affecting all F&O segment members trading index derivatives. Failure to update contract files before the effective date may result in trading disruptions or rejections for orders exceeding the new freeze thresholds. The changes affect the five most actively traded index derivative contracts on NSE, so timely compliance is important for members with significant index F&O activity.
