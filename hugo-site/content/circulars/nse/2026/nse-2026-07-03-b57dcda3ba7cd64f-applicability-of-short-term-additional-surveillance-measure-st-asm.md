## Summary

NSE has issued a circular under its Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework, placing 8 securities under Short-Term ASM (ST-ASM) Stage I effective July 07, 2026. No securities have been added to ST-ASM Stage II, and none are moving from Stage I to Stage II under this circular.

## Key Points

- 8 securities shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage I: AMCL (ANB Metal Cast Limited), BHARATSE (Bharat Seats Limited), HEXAGON (Hexagon Nutrition Limited), MACPOWER (Macpower CNC Machines Limited), SRIVASAVI (Srivasavi Adhesive Tapes Limited), UTKAL (Utkal Speciality Industries India Limited), VOGL (Vedanta Oil and Gas Limited), and WALPAR (Walpar Nutritions Limited).
- No securities were shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage II in this circular.
- No securities are moving from Stage I to Stage II under this circular.
- This action is purely a market surveillance measure and should not be construed as an adverse action against the concerned companies.
- ASM measures apply in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance frameworks.
- Price bands for scrips exiting the framework will revert to their pre-shortlisting levels, unless subject to another surveillance measure.

## Regulatory Changes

None; this circular applies the existing ST-ASM framework established under earlier circulars (NSE/SURV/39265, NSE/SURV/46557, NSE/SURV/52144, NSE/SURV/58558, NSE/SURV/64066) to newly identified securities meeting the inclusion criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must apply the applicable margin rate of 50% (or the existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at 100%) on all open positions in the Stage I securities as of July 06, 2026, and on new positions created from July 07, 2026 onward.

## Important Dates

- July 06, 2026: Reference date for open positions in shortlisted securities.
- July 07, 2026: Effective date for the revised margin requirements.

## Impact Assessment

The increased margin requirement (50%, capped at 100%) will raise the cost of holding and creating positions in the 8 listed securities, likely reducing trading volumes and liquidity in these stocks. The impact is contained to the specific securities named and does not affect the broader market or trading infrastructure.
