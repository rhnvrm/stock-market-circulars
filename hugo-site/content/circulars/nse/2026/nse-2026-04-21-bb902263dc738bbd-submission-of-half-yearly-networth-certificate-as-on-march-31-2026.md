## Summary

NSE has issued a detailed user manual circular requiring all stock broker members to submit their Half Yearly Networth Certificate as on March 31, 2026, through the ENIT (Exchange Network for Information & Technology) portal. The process involves collaboration between the member and their registered auditor, with the auditor entering networth details, digitally signing the certificate, and the member verifying and submitting to the Exchange.

## Key Points

- Members must register their auditor on the ENIT Member Portal before submission can begin
- Auditors must possess a valid Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) as a prerequisite
- The auditor logs into ENIT, fills in networth details, downloads the system-generated certificate template, applies their digital signature, and uploads it
- The auditor submits the request to the member for verification; an automated email notification is sent to the member
- The member must thoroughly verify the auditor's submission before final submission to the Exchange
- The circular covers three methods: Chapter X of SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations 2026 method, Auditor method, and RBI Method

## Regulatory Changes

This circular references compliance under Chapter X of SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 2026. The submission process is governed by the updated SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations framework and requires adherence to the ENIT portal workflow for half-yearly networth certification.

## Compliance Requirements

- Stock broker members must create an auditor login on the ENIT Member Portal (https://enit.nseindia.com/MemberPortal/)
- Auditor must be registered and granted access to the 'New Half Yearly Networth Submission' module under ENIT-Compliance
- Auditor must fill in all required networth details, e-sign the system-generated certificate template, and submit to the member for review
- Member must verify all details submitted by the auditor and submit the final declaration to the Exchange
- Both member and auditor must have valid login credentials and the auditor must have a Digital Signature

## Important Dates

- Reference date for networth certificate: **March 31, 2026**
- Submission deadline: Not explicitly stated in the circular content provided; members should refer to the Exchange's compliance calendar for the exact due date

## Impact Assessment

This circular impacts all NSE registered stock broker members who are required to submit half-yearly networth certificates. The procedural change introduces a structured ENIT portal-based workflow that digitizes and formalizes the submission process. Members need to ensure their auditors are pre-registered on the portal with valid digital signatures to avoid delays. No direct market or trading impact; operational compliance burden is moderate, primarily affecting back-office and compliance teams of broking firms.
