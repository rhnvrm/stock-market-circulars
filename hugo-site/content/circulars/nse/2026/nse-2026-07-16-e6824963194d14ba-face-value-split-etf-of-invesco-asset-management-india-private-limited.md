## Summary

NSE has notified a face value split for the Invesco India Nifty 50 Exchange Traded Fund (ETF), traded under the symbol IVZINNIFTY. The face value and paid-up value of the ETF will be reduced from Rs. 10 to Re. 1 per unit, effective July 31, 2026.

## Key Points

- The circular pertains to Invesco Asset Management (India) Private Limited's ETF scheme.
- Symbol: IVZINNIFTY (Invesco India Nifty 50 Exchange Traded Fund).
- Existing face value and paid-up value: Rs. 10 per unit.
- New face value and paid-up value: Re. 1 per unit.
- The change is effectively a 10:1 unit split, increasing the number of units held by investors proportionately while maintaining the same overall investment value.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory or policy change; this is an administrative face value adjustment for the ETF as approved and communicated by NSE Listing Department.

## Compliance Requirements

Members and market participants must update their systems to reflect the revised face value and paid-up value of IVZINNIFTY units effective from the specified date. No action is required from unit holders as the adjustment is automatic.

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: July 16, 2026
- Effective Date of Face Value Split: July 31, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The face value split does not affect the total investment value held by unitholders; it only changes the per-unit face value and correspondingly the number of units outstanding. Trading systems, contract notes, and price references for IVZINNIFTY will need to reflect the adjusted face value from the effective date. This is a routine administrative action with minimal market impact, primarily affecting record-keeping and unit pricing conventions for this specific ETF.
