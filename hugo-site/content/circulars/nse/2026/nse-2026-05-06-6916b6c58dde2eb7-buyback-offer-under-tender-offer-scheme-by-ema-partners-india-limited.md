## Summary

NSE has announced that EMA Partners India Limited will conduct a buyback offer under the Tender Offer Scheme using NSE's web-based bidding platform. The buyback covers up to 7,25,000 equity shares at a fixed price of Rs. 100 per share, with the offer open from May 7 to May 13, 2026. All Capital Market segment trading members are eligible to participate.

## Key Points

- EMA Partners India Limited is buying back up to 7,25,000 equity shares at Rs. 100 per share (face value Rs. 5)
- The offer is a fixed-price buyback conducted via NSE's web-based IPO bidding platform (https://eipo.nseindia.com)
- Security symbol: EMAPARTNER, Series: BB, ISIN: INE0ZOL01023
- Market lot for trading is 1,000 shares; acceptance under buyback follows entitlement ratios (shareholders may be left with odd lots)
- Members already registered on the IPO bidding platform can use existing credentials; others must use the admin terminal (login: Adminxxxxx where xxxxx is the Member Code)
- Merchant Banker: Indorient Financial Services Limited; Registrar: Bigshare Services Private Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. The offer is conducted in accordance with the existing Tender Offer Scheme framework per NSE circular NSE/CMTR/52776 dated June 29, 2022.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members wishing to participate must ensure access to the web-based bidding platform before the offer opens
- Members without existing access must log in to the admin terminal and create branch and user accounts prior to bidding
- Bidding must be submitted within the specified daily window (9:15 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.); custodial confirmation on issue closure day extends to 4:00 p.m.
- Eight investor categories are eligible: FII, MF, IC, FI, Other QIBs, Bodies Corporate, Individuals, and Other Non-Institutional Investors

## Important Dates

- **Issue Period:** May 7, 2026 to May 13, 2026
- **Daily Bidding Window:** 9:15 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.
- **Custodial Confirmation on Closure Day:** 9:15 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate and company-specific impact. Shareholders of EMA Partners India Limited have an opportunity to tender shares at Rs. 100, which may represent a premium over the prevailing market price. Trading members should note the odd-lot risk: since the market lot is 1,000 shares but acceptance is entitlement-based, shareholders may end up holding odd lots post-buyback. The offer size of 7,25,000 shares is relatively small in scale, limiting broader market impact.
