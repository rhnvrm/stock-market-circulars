## Summary

NSE has announced that Morarjee Textiles Limited (symbol: MORARJEE, ISIN: INE161G01027) will move to Stage II of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective July 14, 2026. This follows a series of prior circulars dating back to February 2017 that established and updated the GSM framework for this and other securities under surveillance.

## Key Points

- Morarjee Textiles Limited (MORARJEE, ISIN INE161G01027) moves to GSM Stage II effective July 14, 2026
- Trading will be restricted to Trade for Trade basis
- Price band capped at 5% or lower as applicable
- Buyers must deposit an Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value
- Action taken in continuation of prior circulars: NSE/SURV/34262, NSE/SURV/38389, NSE/SURV/42790, NSE/SURV/59425, NSE/SURV/64066, NSE/SURV/74254, and NSE/SURV/74795

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this circular applies the existing GSM framework (Stages I-IV) to reclassify MORARJEE from its prior stage to Stage II, which imposes trade-for-trade settlement, a capped price band, and a 50% ASD requirement on buyers.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must ensure trades in MORARJEE are executed only on a trade-for-trade basis from July 14, 2026
- Members must collect Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value from buyers before executing trades
- Members must apply the applicable price band of 5% or lower
- Members should exercise adequate precaution while trading in this security
- Queries can be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 14, 2026 — MORARJEE moves to GSM Stage II
- Circular Date: July 13, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This action significantly reduces liquidity and trading flexibility for MORARJEE by imposing trade-for-trade settlement (eliminating intraday trading), a tighter price band, and a substantial 50% cash deposit requirement for buyers. Investors holding or considering this security face higher transaction costs and reduced ability to exit positions quickly. The impact is confined to this single security and does not have broader market-wide implications, but is a high-severity development for stakeholders of this specific stock.
