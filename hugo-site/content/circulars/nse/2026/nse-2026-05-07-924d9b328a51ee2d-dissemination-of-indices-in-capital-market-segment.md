## Summary

NSE Indices Limited has disseminated the factsheet for the **Nifty Cement Index** as part of its Capital Market Segment index publications. The index tracks stocks from the Nifty Total Market belonging to the 'Cement & Cement Products' industry, with a maximum of 20 stocks selected based on 6-month average free-float market capitalization. The index was launched on February 16, 2026, with a base date of April 1, 2005 and base value of 1000.

## Key Points

- Index selects up to 20 stocks from the Nifty Total Market in the cement & cement products industry; currently has **16 constituents**
- Methodology: **Periodic Capped Free-Float**, with individual stock weight capped at **15%**
- F&O-eligible stocks are prioritized in selection; non-F&O stocks fill remaining slots
- Calculation frequency: **End of Day**; rebalancing: **Semi-Annually**
- Index variant available: **Nifty Cement Total Returns Index**
- Suitable for benchmarking fund portfolios, launching index funds, ETFs, and structured products

## Impact Assessment

The Nifty Cement Index provides a dedicated benchmark for the Indian cement sector. With the index currently showing negative 1-year returns (-7.76% price return), it reflects near-term headwinds in the cement sector. The top 5 constituents (Grasim, UltraTech, Shree Cement, Ambuja, J.K. Cement) account for over 70% of the index weight, indicating concentration risk. The index's beta above 1.0 over 1 year suggests higher volatility relative to the broader Nifty 50, relevant for risk-aware fund construction. This dissemination enables product creation (ETFs, index funds) tracking the cement sector.
