## Summary

NSE has withdrawn two earlier circulars that implemented Quality Control Orders (QCOs) for commodity futures contracts. The Ministry of Mines, Government of India, issued notifications withdrawing QCOs for Aluminium, Copper, Primary Lead, Refined Zinc, and Refined Nickel, prompting NSE to revert to the original quality specifications and additional deliverable grades that were in place prior to the QCO implementation. The change is effective from June 1, 2026 for contracts expiring in June 2026 and onwards.

## Key Points

- NSE withdraws circular NSE/COM/71308 (dated November 14, 2025) covering Lead Futures, Lead Mini Futures, Nickel Futures, Zinc Futures, and Zinc Mini Futures.
- NSE withdraws circular NSE/COM/68091 (dated May 20, 2025) covering Aluminium Futures, Alumini Futures, and Copper Futures.
- The withdrawal follows Ministry of Mines notifications rescinding QCOs for Aluminium, Copper, Primary Lead, Refined Zinc, and Refined Nickel.
- Original quality specifications and additional deliverable grades (pre-QCO) are restored for all eight affected contracts.
- For Lead Futures and Lead Mini Futures, the IS 27:2023 Standard Mark requirement (Bureau of Indian Standards license) is removed; only LME-approved brands with minimum 99.97% purity will be required.

## Regulatory Changes

The QCO-mandated requirement that goods conform to IS 27:2023 and bear the Standard Mark under a Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) license is withdrawn for Lead and Lead Mini Futures. Quality assessment reverts to reliance on the Certificate of Analysis (CoA) issued by the producer for LME-approved brands. Similar reversions apply to Nickel, Zinc, Zinc Mini, Aluminium, Alumini, and Copper contracts as per the Annexure enclosed with the circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- Warehouse Service Providers (WSPs) must revert to pre-QCO quality assessment procedures using CoA from producers for LME-approved brands.
- Members should update their operational and compliance procedures to reflect the restored original quality specifications for all eight affected contracts.
- No BIS Standard Mark certification will be required for deliveries in June 2026 contracts and beyond.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 21, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 1, 2026 (applicable from contracts expiring in June 2026 and onwards)

## Impact Assessment

This change primarily affects participants engaged in physical delivery of commodity futures. The removal of the IS 27:2023 / BIS Standard Mark requirement reduces the compliance burden for producers and WSPs, potentially widening the pool of deliverable goods. Traders and hedgers in Lead, Lead Mini, Nickel, Zinc, Zinc Mini, Aluminium, Alumini, and Copper futures should review updated quality specifications in the Annexure to ensure their stocks remain deliverable-grade compliant under the reverted standards. The change has no direct impact on financial settlement participants.
