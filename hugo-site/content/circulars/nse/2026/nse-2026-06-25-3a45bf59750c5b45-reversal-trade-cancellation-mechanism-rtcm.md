## Summary

NSE Surveillance has published the updated list of symbols subject to the Reversal Trade Cancellation Mechanism (RTCM) for the month of July 2026. The mechanism applies to the EQ, BE, BZ, SM, ST, and SZ series for approximately 170 listed symbols, as enumerated in the file `CM_RTCM_jul2026.csv` available on the NSE extranet.

## Key Points

- RTCM for July 2026 is applicable to all series (EQ, BE, BZ, SM, ST & SZ) for symbols listed in `CM_RTCM_jul2026.csv`.
- The symbol list excludes: (1) securities on the Approved Securities List published by NSE Clearing Ltd, (2) constituents of the Nifty 500 index, and (3) ETFs.
- The file `CM_RTCM_jul2026.csv` is available on the NSE extranet at path: `/common/Surveillance/RTCM`.
- RTCM does not absolve Trading Members of their obligations under circular NSE/INVG/46662 dated December 16, 2020.
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This circular is a periodic update to the existing RTCM framework established under NSE/SURV/62493 dated June 18, 2024. The update refreshes the symbol list applicable for July 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note the updated RTCM symbol list for July 2026 and ensure their systems reflect the applicable symbols and series.
- Members remain obligated to comply with circular NSE/INVG/46662 (December 16, 2020) on prevention of reversal trades, independently of the RTCM facility.
- Members should download the file `CM_RTCM_jul2026.csv` from the NSE extranet path `/common/Surveillance/RTCM` for the complete symbol list.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Period:** July 2026
- **Circular Date:** June 25, 2026
- **Reference Circular:** NSE/SURV/62493 dated June 18, 2024 (original RTCM framework)
- **Compliance Circular:** NSE/INVG/46662 dated December 16, 2020

## Impact Assessment

Approximately 170 small and mid-cap scrips across the cash market segment are subject to the RTCM for July 2026. The mechanism automatically cancels reversal trades between two PANs when certain thresholds are breached, reducing the risk of coordinated wash trading and price manipulation in less-liquid securities. Market participants holding or trading these symbols should be aware that reversal trades meeting the threshold criteria will be cancelled by the exchange without further notice. The exclusion of Nifty 500 constituents, ETFs, and NSE Clearing-approved securities limits the scope to higher-risk, lower-liquidity scrips.
