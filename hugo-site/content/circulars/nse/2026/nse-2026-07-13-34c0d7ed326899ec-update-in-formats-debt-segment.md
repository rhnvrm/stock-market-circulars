## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued updated formats for Bank Guarantees to be furnished by Clearing Members towards margin deposits in the Debt Segment. The circular provides revised templates, including Annexure 1(A) — Format of Bank Guarantee for Margin Deposit (Fungible) — to be used by banks approved by NCL when issuing guarantees on behalf of Clearing Members (individuals, partnership/LLP firms, or companies).

## Key Points

- The circular releases updated Bank Guarantee formats applicable to the Debt Segment, including the Fungible Margin Deposit format (Annexure 1(A)).
- The Bank Guarantee is to be issued by an NCL-approved bank in favour of NSE Clearing Limited, on behalf of a Clearing Member (proprietorship, partnership/LLP, or company).
- The guarantee is governed by the terms of the Master Agreement executed between the issuing bank and NCL.
- The guarantee format specifies that the bank's liability shall not exceed the guaranteed amount stated in the instrument.
- Clearing Members maintain margin deposits with NCL, which may be furnished partly or wholly through such bank guarantees.

## Regulatory Changes

NCL has revised/standardized the bank guarantee documentation format used for margin deposits in the Debt Segment, ensuring uniformity in the legal language, applicant details (proprietorship/partnership/company), and liability terms across guarantees submitted by approved banks.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing Members in the Debt Segment furnishing margin via bank guarantee must ensure their bank uses the updated Annexure format.
- NCL-approved banks must issue guarantees per the revised format and in accordance with the Master Agreement executed with NCL.
- Existing or renewed bank guarantees should conform to the new template to remain valid and acceptable to NCL.

## Important Dates

No specific effective date or deadline is stated in the extracted content; the circular appears effective from the date of issue (2026-07-13).

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact, operational/administrative circular affecting only Clearing Members and their banks in the Debt Segment. It standardizes documentation for margin deposits and does not affect trading, listed securities, or broader market participants.
