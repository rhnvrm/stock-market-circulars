## Summary

NSE has informed members of a revision to the Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) framework used for authorizing mutual fund transactions and systematic registrations/cancellations executed through the NSE MF Invest Platform. Under the revised process, the registered Email ID and Mobile Number maintained in the respective RTA (Registrar and Transfer Agent) records against the Folio Number and PAN will be used for 2FA authorization, effective from July 4, 2026. If no response is received from the RTA, UCC (Unique Client Code) records will be used instead.

## Key Points

- The revised 2FA process applies to: Additional Purchase, Redemption, Switch, SIP/XSIP Registration and Cancellation, STP Registration and Cancellation, and SWP Registration and Cancellation.
- The existing process for placing transactions and registering/cancelling systematic transactions remains unchanged.
- After transaction placement or registration/cancellation, investors will receive SMS and/or Email notifications based on RTA folio records, falling back to UCC records if RTA data is unavailable.
- A new dropdown option titled "Select Email/Mobile for Authorization" will let investors choose "All" (showing all pending records) or a specific Email ID/Mobile number (showing only matching records).
- Investors select the required orders/registrations/cancellations and choose the authorization mode (Email, Mobile, or Both).
- Masked Mobile Number and/or Email ID will be displayed based on the selected option, and OTP will be triggered via SMS and/or Email upon clicking "Send OTP".
- If the RTA-recorded mobile number is not exactly 10 digits, only the Email option will be available for 2FA authorization.
- Detailed operational guidelines are provided in the attached Process Note (Annexure-I).

## Regulatory Changes

The circular introduces a shift in the source of contact details used for 2FA authorization — from UCC-based records to RTA-based records (matched via Folio Number and PAN) as the primary source, with UCC as fallback. This aligns authorization credentials more closely with the actual folio holder's registered details at the RTA level.

## Compliance Requirements

Members must ensure their systems and processes account for the new 2FA authorization flow, including the updated dropdown selection mechanism and OTP triggering logic. Members should communicate the process changes to relevant operations teams and ensure investor support processes reflect the RTA-based contact detail sourcing.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 4, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is an operational/procedural change affecting the mutual fund transaction authorization workflow on the NSE MF Invest Platform. It does not directly impact trading of listed securities or specific stocks but affects mutual fund distributors, RTAs, and investors using the platform for SIP, STP, SWP, and other systematic transactions. The impact is primarily on operational processes and investor experience rather than market pricing or trading activity.
