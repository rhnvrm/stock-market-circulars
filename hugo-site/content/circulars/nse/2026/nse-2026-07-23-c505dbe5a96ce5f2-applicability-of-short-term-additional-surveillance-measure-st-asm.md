## Summary

NSE has issued a circular under its Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework, placing six securities under Short-Term ASM (ST-ASM) Stage I effective July 24, 2026. No securities were added to ST-ASM Stage II, and no securities moved between Stage I and Stage II in this cycle. The circular references prior ASM circulars dated October 27, 2018, December 4, 2020, April 28, 2022, September 25, 2023, and September 20, 2024.

## Key Points

- Six securities have been shortlisted for ST-ASM Stage I: Cyient DLM Limited (CYIENTDLM), Dar Credit & Capital Limited (DCCL), Giriraj Civil Developers Limited (GIRIRAJ), Huhtamaki India Limited (HUHTAMAKI), Marc Technocrats Limited (MARC), and MPS Limited (MPSLTD).
- No securities were shortlisted for ST-ASM Stage II in this cycle.
- No securities moved from Stage I to Stage II or from Stage II to Stage I.
- The ASM framework operates alongside all other prevailing surveillance measures imposed by exchanges.
- Inclusion under ASM is purely a market surveillance action and should not be viewed as an adverse judgment on the company.

## Regulatory Changes

No changes to the ASM framework itself; this circular applies the existing Short-Term ASM Stage I criteria to the listed securities based on periodic market surveillance review.

## Compliance Requirements

Members must apply the mandated margin rate of 50% (or existing margin, whichever is higher, capped at 100%) on all open positions in the listed securities as of July 24, 2026, and on new positions created from July 27, 2026 onward. Members should monitor client positions in these securities to ensure compliance with the enhanced margin requirement.

## Important Dates

- July 24, 2026: Effective date for inclusion of securities under ST-ASM Stage I; reference date for open positions subject to the margin requirement.
- July 27, 2026: Date from which the 50% (or higher) margin rate applies to all open and new positions in the listed securities.

## Impact Assessment

The increased margin requirement (50% or existing margin, whichever is higher) will raise the cost of trading and carrying positions in CYIENTDLM, DCCL, GIRIRAJ, HUHTAMAKI, MARC, and MPSLTD, likely reducing speculative trading volumes and leverage in these securities. The impact is company-specific and moderate in scope, as it affects only six securities and does not represent a market-wide policy shift. Market participants holding positions in these stocks should reassess margin funding and risk exposure ahead of the July 27, 2026 effective date.
