## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed Commercial Papers (CPs) from 11 companies to dealings on its Debt Market Segment effective June 08, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All securities were allotted on June 05, 2026, with redemption dates ranging from July to September 2026.

## Key Points

- 11 fresh Commercial Paper issuances across major corporates and financial institutions listed on NSE Debt Market Segment
- All CPs allotted on June 05, 2026; effective for trading from June 08, 2026
- Face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit across all issuances
- Issue prices range from ₹4,88,134.50 (IIFL Finance) to ₹4,95,536 (Tata Steel 23/07/26), reflecting varying tenors and credit profiles
- Largest issuance: NABARD CP 04/09/26 — 50,000 units (₹2,500 crore face value)
- Signed off by Bansri Gosalia, Senior Manager, NSE Listing Department

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. Members are notified of the designated security codes as specified in the Annexure for trading purposes.

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment:** June 05, 2026 (all securities)
- **Effective Trading Date:** June 08, 2026
- **Redemption Dates:**
  - Tata Steel CP 23/07/26 (INE081A14HB8): July 23, 2026
  - Tata Steel CP 03/09/26 (INE081A14HA0): September 03, 2026
  - TATA Projects CP 03/09/26 (INE725H14EC0): September 03, 2026
  - All remaining CPs: September 04, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a standard debt market listing notice with no material market-wide impact. The listings provide liquidity access on NSE for these short-term debt instruments. Notable issuances include NABARD (₹2,500 crore), ICICI Securities (₹1,060 crore), and Tata Steel combined (₹2,000 crore). Impact is confined to debt market participants and institutional investors trading in these specific CPs.
