## Summary

NSE has notified the suspension of trading in 8 HDFC Mutual Fund Fixed Maturity Plan (FMP) unit schemes effective June 24, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A. The suspension is on account of redemption, with the last day of trading being June 23, 2026 (closing hours).

## Key Points

- 8 HDFC FMP unit schemes suspended from trading effective June 24, 2026
- Reason for suspension: MF Redemption
- Last trading day: June 23, 2026 (closing hours)
- Schemes belong to HDFC FMP Sr 46 series, covering 1359D (Sep 2022) and 1406D (Aug 2022) tenors
- Circular reference: NSE/CML/74688 | Circular Ref. No: 0955/2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular implements an existing provision under Regulation 3.1.2 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A, which mandates trading suspension for mutual fund units approaching redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure no trading is executed in the listed schemes from June 24, 2026 onwards
- Investors holding units in these schemes should note that redemption proceeds will be settled as per the respective scheme's terms

## Important Dates

- **June 23, 2026**: Last day of trading (closing hours) for all 8 schemes
- **June 24, 2026**: Suspension of trading effective date

## Impact Assessment

Impact is limited and routine. The affected schemes are closed-ended HDFC Fixed Maturity Plans maturing around this period. Existing unit holders will receive redemption proceeds as per scheme terms. No impact on broader equity or debt markets. The suspension is a standard procedural step ahead of FMP maturity.
