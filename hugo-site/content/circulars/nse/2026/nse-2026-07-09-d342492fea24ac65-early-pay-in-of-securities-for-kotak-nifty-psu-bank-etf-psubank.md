## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a circular advising members, custodians, and PCMs on the process for early pay-in (EPI) of securities for the Kotak Nifty PSU Bank ETF (PSUBANK), in connection with a face value split (sub-division) of the ETF's units from Rs 10 per share to Re 1 per share. The record date and ex-date for the corporate action are both July 10, 2026. This circular supplements the consolidated circular on early pay-in of securities for margin exemption (Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/73996, dated April 30, 2026).

## Key Points

- The circular pertains to PSUBANK (Kotak Nifty PSU Bank ETF), ISIN INF373I01023.
- The corporate action is a face value split (sub-division) from Rs 10 per share to Re 1 per share.
- Record date and ex-date are both July 10, 2026.
- Members undertaking EPI on July 10, 2026 (settlement number 2026128) and July 13, 2026 (settlement numbers 2026128 & 2026129) must use the old/existing ISIN for early pay-in, without adjusting for the split, for the applicable settlement type on the trade date.
- Example given: for a sale quantity of 10 shares, the early pay-in in the depository should be made in the old ISIN for a quantity of 1 share (reflecting the 10:1 split ratio).
- Market participants/custodians must adhere to NCL circular NCL/CMPT/53386 dated August 22, 2022, regarding the SEBI circular on Block Mechanism in demat accounts for clients undertaking sale transactions.
- In case of early pay-in through block mechanism, the client-wise early pay-in allocation file should not be uploaded.
- Custodians providing early pay-in through the pool account must upload client-wise early pay-in allocation details based on actual traded quantity to pass on the benefit to clients.
- After the record date, market participants/custodians must execute pay-in/early pay-in instructions in the New ISIN in the depositories.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory rule is introduced; this circular operationalizes existing early pay-in and block mechanism guidelines (per NCL/CMPT/73996 and NCL/CMPT/53386) in the context of a specific corporate action (face value split) affecting PSUBANK.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and custodians must correctly identify and use the old ISIN for early pay-in on July 10 and July 13, 2026 for the specified settlement numbers, applying the correct quantity conversion per the 10:1 split ratio.
- Custodians using the pool account route must upload accurate client-wise early pay-in allocation files based on actual traded quantities.
- Entities using block mechanism must not upload client-wise allocation files, per existing SEBI/NCL guidance.
- Post record date, all pay-in and early pay-in instructions must shift to the New ISIN.

## Important Dates

- Record Date: July 10, 2026
- Ex-Date: July 10, 2026
- EPI in old ISIN: July 10, 2026 (Settlement No. 2026128) and July 13, 2026 (Settlement Nos. 2026128 & 2026129)

## Impact Assessment

The impact is operational and limited to NSE Clearing members, custodians, and PCMs handling early pay-in for PSUBANK around its face value split. Incorrect ISIN usage or quantity conversion during the transition window could lead to settlement mismatches or failed pay-in, but the corporate action itself (face value split) does not alter total investment value for unit holders, only the per-unit denomination and quantity.
