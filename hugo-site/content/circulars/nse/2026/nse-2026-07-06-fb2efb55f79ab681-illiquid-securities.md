## Summary

NSE has issued its periodic review circular concerning illiquid securities subject to the periodic call auction mechanism, in continuance of Exchange circular NSE/CMTR/25320 dated December 20, 2013, and pursuant to SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/38/2013 dated December 19, 2013. Based on trading activity analysis for the period January 2026 to June 2026, the Exchange has determined that no securities are being newly classified as illiquid, and no securities are moving out of the periodic call auction mechanism, effective July 09, 2026 (Thursday).

## Key Points

- Annexure I (securities newly subject to periodic call auction effective July 09, 2026): Nil
- Annexure II (securities moving out of periodic call auction mechanism effective July 09, 2026): Nil
- The classification review is based on trading activity data from January 2026 to June 2026
- Members are advised to exercise additional due diligence while trading in illiquid securities, whether on own account or on behalf of clients
- Queries can be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this circular; it is a routine periodic update under the existing framework established by SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/38/2013 and NSE circular NSE/CMTR/25320.

## Compliance Requirements

Members should continue to exercise additional due diligence when trading in securities currently classified as illiquid and subject to periodic call auction, both for proprietary trades and client transactions. No new action is required as a result of this specific circular since both annexures are Nil.

## Important Dates

- Effective Date: July 09, 2026 (Thursday)
- Review period basis: January 2026 - June 2026 trading activity
- Circular Date: July 06, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market impact as no securities are being added to or removed from the illiquid securities list this cycle. The periodic call auction mechanism for existing illiquid securities remains unchanged, and no shift in trading behavior or liquidity classification is expected as a result of this specific update.
