## Summary

NSE's Listing Department (Circular Ref. No. 0812/2026, Download Ref. No. NSE/CML/74314) notifies that further issues of equity shares for 17 companies have been admitted to dealings on the NSE Capital Market segment with effect from May 21, 2026. The admissions cover shares allotted under Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP) and preferential allotments by various listed entities.

## Key Points

- Effective date for trading of all newly listed securities: **May 21, 2026**
- Issued under Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A
- All 17 securities admitted to the Capital Market (EQ) segment with lot size of 1
- Securities are identified solely by their designated NSE codes for trading purposes
- Several issuances carry lock-in periods of approximately one year from allotment date

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. The circular operates under existing NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations (Regulation 3.1.1 for admission to dealings and Regulation 2.5.5 for lot sizes).

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use designated security codes for trading in these newly listed securities
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot sizes (Market Lot: 1 for all securities)
- Lock-in restrictions must be observed where applicable (e.g., ARMANFIN lock-in until 11-May-2027; ARSSBL lock-in until 13-Apr-2027)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative circular admitting further issues to trading. The primary impact is an incremental increase in the floating stock for each of the 17 companies. ESOP-based issuances are generally small relative to total paid-up capital and have minimal price impact. Notable companies in the list — HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, ICICI Prudential Life Insurance, and Polycab India — are large-caps where even significant ESOP tranches represent a negligible dilution. Companies with one-year lock-in periods (ARMANFIN, ARSSBL) will see no immediate supply-side pressure from those shares. Investors should note the full annexure details in the source PDF for complete distinctive number ranges and security descriptions for companies 6–17, which were not included in the available excerpt.
