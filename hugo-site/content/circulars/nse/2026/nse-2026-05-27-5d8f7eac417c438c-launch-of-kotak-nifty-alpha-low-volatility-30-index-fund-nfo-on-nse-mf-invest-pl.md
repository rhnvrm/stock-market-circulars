## Summary

Kotak Mutual Fund is launching the Kotak Nifty Alpha Low-Volatility 30 Index Fund as a New Fund Offer (NFO) on the NSE MF Invest Platform. The NFO subscription window runs from May 29, 2026 to June 12, 2026, with allotment scheduled for June 17, 2026. Six scheme variants are available across Regular and Direct plans with Growth, IDCW Reinvestment, and IDCW Payout options.

## Key Points

- NFO subscription period: May 29, 2026 to June 12, 2026
- Fund house: Kotak Mutual Fund
- Fund category: Equity (Index Fund tracking Nifty Alpha Low-Volatility 30)
- Six scheme variants available (Regular/Direct × Growth/IDCW Reinvestment/IDCW Payout)
- Allotment date: June 17, 2026
- Clear funds must be received in the designated Clearing Corporation account by June 16, 2026, 12:00 PM

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a product availability notification for exchange members regarding a new mutual fund NFO on the NSE MF Invest Platform.

## Compliance Requirements

Members transacting in this NFO must ensure that clear funds through any payment mode are credited to the designated Clearing Corporation account on or before June 16, 2026 by 12:00 PM.

## Important Dates

- **May 29, 2026**: NFO subscription opens on NSE MF Invest Platform
- **June 12, 2026**: NFO subscription closes
- **June 16, 2026, 12:00 PM**: Deadline for clear funds to reach the Clearing Corporation account
- **June 17, 2026**: Allotment date for all scheme variants

## Impact Assessment

This circular has limited broad market impact as it is a routine NFO launch notification. Exchange members using the NSE MF Invest Platform gain access to a new equity index fund product from Kotak Mutual Fund. Investors seeking exposure to the Nifty Alpha Low-Volatility 30 index — which blends alpha and low-volatility factor strategies — can subscribe through the platform during the NFO window. The six scheme variants (ISINs: INF174KA1A52, INF174KA1A78, INF174KA1A29, INF174KA1A45, INF174KA1A60, INF174KA1A37) cater to different investor preferences on plan type and dividend treatment.
