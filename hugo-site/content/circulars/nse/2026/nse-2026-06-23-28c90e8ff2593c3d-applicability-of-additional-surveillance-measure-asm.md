## Summary

NSE has shortlisted four securities for inclusion under the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (Long Term ASM) Stage I, effective June 24, 2026. A 100% margin requirement will apply on all open and new positions from June 29, 2026. No securities have been moved to Stage IV, and no inter-stage movements (Stage I→II or Stage II→III) are applicable this cycle.

## Key Points

- Four securities added to Long Term ASM Stage I effective June 24, 2026
- NIIT Limited (NIITLTD) is being moved from Short Term ASM (STASM) to Long Term ASM (LTASM) framework
- 100% margin applicable on all open positions as on June 25, 2026 and new positions from June 29, 2026
- No securities shortlisted under Stage IV; hence no shift from Rolling Settlement (EQ) to Trade-for-Trade (BE) this cycle
- ASM framework operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures

## Regulatory Changes

Securities meeting the Long Term ASM criteria are subject to enhanced surveillance actions per Exchange Circulars NSE/SURV/39265, NSE/SURV/45111, NSE/SURV/46557, NSE/SURV/48506, NSE/SURV/52090, NSE/SURV/63362, and NSE/SURV/64066. Shortlisting is based on market surveillance criteria and does not constitute an adverse action against the concerned company.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must apply 100% margin on open positions in the four listed securities as on June 25, 2026
- 100% margin must be collected on all new positions in these securities from June 29, 2026 onwards
- Members should communicate applicable margin changes to their clients

## Important Dates

- **June 24, 2026**: Effective date of Long Term ASM Stage I inclusion for all four securities
- **June 25, 2026**: Reference date for open positions subject to 100% margin
- **June 29, 2026**: 100% margin requirement comes into effect for open and new positions

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding or planning positions in NIITLTD, UCAL, VERTEXPLUS, and VIPULLTD will face a 100% margin requirement from June 29, 2026, significantly increasing the capital required to hold these positions. Since no securities are placed in Stage IV, there is no segment shift to Trade-for-Trade (BE series) in this cycle. The measure is intended to enhance market surveillance and reduce speculative activity in these securities.
