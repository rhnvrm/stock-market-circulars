## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has announced revised timings for collateral-related operations on July 3, 2026. The collateral allocation window has been extended to 10:00 pm, while the up-streaming and down-streaming window for cash and fixed deposit receipts (FDR) will remain open until 8:00 pm. This is issued under the Futures and Options Segment in reference to SEBI's circular on settlement of running accounts of client funds.

## Key Points

- Collateral up-streaming and down-streaming window (cash and FDR) extended to **8:00 pm** on July 3, 2026
- Collateral allocation window extended to **10:00 pm** on July 3, 2026
- NSE Clearing will endeavor to release Cash/FDR/BG within **one hour** of receiving a request from the clearing member, subject to available collateral post margin checks
- This change applies specifically to **July 3, 2026** and is related to the settlement of running accounts of client funds

## Regulatory Changes

This circular references SEBI circular no. SEBI/HO/MIRSD/DOP/P/CIR/2022/101 dated July 27, 2022, regarding the Settlement of Running Account of Client's Funds lying with Trading Members. The revised timings are an operational adjustment tied to that regulatory framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing members must take note of the revised collateral operation timings for July 3, 2026
- Members should plan their collateral release requests accordingly to allow NSE Clearing sufficient time to process within the one-hour turnaround commitment

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 25, 2026
- **Effective Date:** July 3, 2026 (revised timings apply only on this date)
- Collateral up/down-streaming window closes: **8:00 pm** on July 3, 2026
- Collateral allocation window closes: **10:00 pm** on July 3, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is an operational change with moderate impact on clearing members in the Futures and Options Segment. Members who manage client collateral (cash and FDR) will have extended windows on July 3, 2026, allowing more time for collateral operations. The one-hour release commitment from NSE Clearing provides operational clarity. No impact on listed securities or market trading hours.
