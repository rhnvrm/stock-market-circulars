## Summary

NSE has extended the Final CTCL mismatch reporting for the Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) segment to a daily basis, now available on the ENIT platform. Trading members must ensure all 12-digit CTCL/IBT/STWT/DMA/API terminal details are reported to the Exchange before routing orders through those terminals to avoid mismatches and associated penalties.

## Key Points

- Final CTCL mismatches for the EGR segment are now available on a daily basis via ENIT.
- Reporting path: ENIT-NEW-TRADE > Trade > Trade Compliance (Post Trade) > Final CTCL Mismatch (Report).
- All 12-digit terminal IDs (CTCL/IBT/STWT/DMA/API) must be reported to NSE prior to order routing.
- Failure to upload or uploading incorrect details will result in CTCL ID mismatches.
- Continuation of earlier circulars: NSE/MSD/73956 (April 29, 2026), NSE/MSD/74023 (April 30, 2026), and NSE/MSD/74581 (June 05, 2026).

## Regulatory Changes

Daily availability of Final CTCL mismatch reports for the EGR segment has been introduced on ENIT, expanding the compliance monitoring framework previously established for this segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must register all 12-digit terminal IDs (CTCL/IBT/STWT/DMA/API) with NSE via ENIT before routing any EGR segment orders.
- Members must monitor the daily Final CTCL Mismatch report on ENIT and rectify discrepancies promptly.
- Non-compliance may attract financial penalties and/or disciplinary action by the relevant authority.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** Immediately (June 24, 2026).

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on trading members active in the EGR segment. The shift to daily mismatch reporting increases the frequency of compliance checks, requiring members to maintain up-to-date terminal registrations on ENIT. Failure to comply risks penalties, making timely and accurate terminal uploads critical for members trading in Electronic Gold Receipts.
