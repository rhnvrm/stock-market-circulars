## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a circular revising the Market Wide Position Limits (MWPL), Trading Member (TM)-wise limits, FII/FPI (Category I & II), and Mutual Fund position limits for TRENT LIMITED in the Futures & Options segment. This is a partial modification of earlier circulars and takes effect from the trade date June 04, 2026.

## Key Points

- Circular Ref. No: 069/2026 (NCL/CMPT/74535), issued June 03, 2026
- Supersedes/partially modifies circular 050/2026 (NCL/CMPT/73997) dated April 30, 2026 and circular 034/2026 (NCL/CMPT/73404) dated March 20, 2026
- Revised position limits apply exclusively to TRENT LIMITED (Symbol: TRENT)
- Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL): 50,385,730 shares
- Overall Stock Limit for Client positions: 5,038,500 shares
- Overall Stock Limit for Proprietary positions: 10,077,000 shares
- Overall Stock Limit for TM (Client + Proprietary combined): 15,115,650 shares
- FPI Category I & Mutual Fund limit: 10,077,000 shares
- FPI Category II (individuals, family offices, and corporates): 5,038,500 shares

## Regulatory Changes

The position limits for TRENT in the F&O segment have been revised upward across all participant categories. These changes are issued under the authority of NSE Clearing Limited as part of ongoing monitoring and adjustment of derivative position limits in line with changes in the underlying stock's market capitalisation or free float.

## Compliance Requirements

- All trading members must note the revised limits and ensure client and proprietary positions in TRENT derivatives do not breach the updated thresholds from June 04, 2026 onwards
- FPIs (Category I & II) and Mutual Funds must adhere to their respective revised limits
- Risk and compliance teams should update internal position monitoring systems to reflect the new MWPL and participant-level limits

## Important Dates

- Circular date: June 03, 2026
- Effective trade date: **June 04, 2026**

## Impact Assessment

The revision increases position limits for TRENT across all categories, which may allow higher open interest build-up in TRENT futures and options. Traders and institutional participants with existing large positions should verify compliance with the new limits. The change is operationally routine but directly relevant to any member actively trading or hedging TRENT derivatives.
