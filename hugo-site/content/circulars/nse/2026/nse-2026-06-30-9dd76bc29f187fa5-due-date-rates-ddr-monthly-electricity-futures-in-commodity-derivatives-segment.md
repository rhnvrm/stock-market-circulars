## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has announced the Due Date Rate (DDR) for the monthly electricity futures contract (ELECMBL) that expired on June 29, 2026. The DDR has been fixed at Rs. 3899.00 per MWh. This rate will be used for final settlement of all open positions in the expired contract.

## Key Points

- DDR for ELECMBL contract expiring June 29, 2026 fixed at **Rs. 3899.00 per MWh**
- Base quantity: 1 MWh
- Issued by NSE Clearing Limited under the Commodity Derivatives Segment
- References prior circulars NCL/COM/69054 (July 10, 2025) and NCL/COM/69390 (July 30, 2025) governing the DDR procedure for monthly electricity futures

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational announcement of the final settlement rate for an expired contract, consistent with the existing DDR procedure established under earlier circulars.

## Compliance Requirements

All members with open positions in the ELECMBL monthly electricity futures contract that expired on June 29, 2026 must take note of the DDR of Rs. 3899.00 per MWh for final settlement purposes.

## Important Dates

- **June 29, 2026**: Expiry date of the ELECMBL monthly electricity futures contract
- **June 30, 2026**: Date of DDR announcement (Circular Ref. No. 227/2026)

## Impact Assessment

Low market impact. This is a routine post-expiry administrative notification affecting only members who held open positions in the June 2026 ELECMBL monthly electricity futures contract. The DDR of Rs. 3899.00 per MWh serves as the reference rate for computing final settlement obligations and gains/losses for those positions.
