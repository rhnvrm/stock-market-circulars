## Summary

NSE has issued an update to the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework. Three securities — Bharat Gears Limited (BHARATGEAR), OnMobile Global Limited (ONMOBILE), and Samay Project Services Limited (SAMAY) — have been newly shortlisted under ESM Stage I effective July 06, 2026, and will shift from the Rolling Settlement segment (EQ/SM) to the Trade-for-Trade segment (BE/ST) effective July 07, 2026. Viji Finance Limited (VIJIFIN) has been moved from ESM Stage I to Stage II. Five securities — Gaudium IVF and Women Health Limited (GAUDIUMIVF), Globesecure Technologies Limited (GSTL), Kirloskar Electric Company Limited (KECL), Mercury Ev-Tech Limited (MERCURYEV), and Setco (SETCO, list truncated in source) — have moved from Stage II back to Stage I. A consolidated list of all securities currently under the ESM framework is provided in Annexure III of the circular.

## Key Points

- New additions to ESM Stage I: BHARATGEAR, ONMOBILE, SAMAY — effective July 06, 2026, shifting from EQ/SM to BE/ST w.e.f. July 07, 2026.
- VIJIFIN moves from ESM Stage I to Stage II effective July 06, 2026.
- Securities moving from Stage II to Stage I effective July 06, 2026: GAUDIUMIVF, GSTL, KECL, MERCURYEV, SETCO (and possibly others per the full annexure).
- Securities newly shortlisted or moving to Stage I attract a minimum 100% margin on all open positions as of July 06, 2026, and on new positions created from July 07, 2026.
- Securities shifting to Stage II are placed under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction, effective July 06, 2026.
- ESM measures apply in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures.
- Price bands for securities moving out of the ESM framework revert to the pre-ESM applicable band, unless the security remains under another surveillance framework.
- Inclusion under ESM is purely a market surveillance measure and is not to be construed as an adverse action against the company.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular updates the list of securities under the ESM framework pursuant to prior NSE circulars (NSE/SURV/56948, 57609, 63361, 64066, 64400, and 69315). No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is a periodic review and reclassification of securities within the existing ESM Stage I/Stage II structure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure a minimum 100% margin is collected on all open and new positions in securities shortlisted under ESM Stage I/II as specified.
- Members must route trading in newly shortlisted/Stage-I securities through the Trade-for-Trade (BE/ST) segment from July 07, 2026.
- Members must apply the 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction for securities moved to Stage II from July 06, 2026.
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in.

## Important Dates

- July 06, 2026: ESM Stage I/Stage II reclassifications take effect; 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction for Stage II securities effective.
- July 07, 2026: Newly shortlisted/Stage I securities shift from EQ/SM to BE/ST segment; 100% margin applies to open positions as of July 06, 2026, and new positions from this date.

## Impact Assessment

The move to Trade-for-Trade with 100% margin requirements significantly reduces intraday liquidity and increases the cost of holding positions in BHARATGEAR, ONMOBILE, and SAMAY, likely triggering price and volume impact. VIJIFIN's move to Stage II tightens trading conditions further with a restrictive 2% price band. Conversely, securities exiting Stage II to Stage I (GAUDIUMIVF, GSTL, KECL, MERCURYEV, SETCO) see some easing of restrictions, though they remain under enhanced surveillance. Investors and members holding these securities should reassess margin requirements and trading strategies ahead of the effective dates.
