## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued the settlement calendar for the Offer for Sale (OFS) of shares of Bosch Home Comfort India Limited. This circular (NCL/CMPT/74755, Circular Ref. No: 0212/2026) references the earlier circular NCL/CMPT/73996 dated April 30, 2026, and confirms that OFS settlements will follow existing secondary market transaction rules.

## Key Points

- OFS order dates are June 18, 2026 (non-retail) and June 19, 2026 (retail and non-retail)
- Settlement dates are June 19, 2026 and June 22, 2026 depending on the category
- Non-retail (IS) category requires 100% margin for H-type settlements; 0% margin for T-type settlements
- Retail (RS) category has 100% margin with settlement on June 22, 2026
- Custodial confirmation deadline for non-retail orders on June 18 is 3:30 PM on June 18, 2026
- Custodial confirmation deadline for non-retail orders on June 19 is 3:30 PM on June 19, 2026
- T-type settlements have custodial confirmation at 7:30 AM on the following business day
- Applicable STT and stamp duty will be collected on the respective settlement day

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. The settlement is conducted per existing secondary market transaction rules as outlined in the reference circular NCL/CMPT/73996 dated April 30, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members, custodians, and PCMs must adhere to the specified custodial confirmation windows
- Funds pay-in must be completed by T+1 day 11:00 AM
- Securities and funds pay-out will occur by T+1 day 1:30 PM
- STT and stamp duty obligations must be met on the respective settlement day
- For queries, members should contact the help desk at 1800 266 0050 (Option 2) or ofs_ncl@nsccl.co.in

## Impact Assessment

This circular is operationally significant for members, custodians, and PCMs participating in the OFS of Bosch Home Comfort India Limited. It provides the precise settlement schedule required to manage funds and securities obligations. The two-day OFS window (June 18–19) with settlements on June 19 and June 22 is standard practice. Participants must ensure timely custodial confirmations and funds pay-in to avoid settlement failures. Broader market impact is limited as this pertains to a single company's OFS.
