## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (Circular Ref. No. 062/2026, Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/74400) has issued a revised list of Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs) eligible for cross margining in the Futures & Options segment. This update supersedes the previous circular no. 0046/2026 dated April 27, 2026, and takes effect from May 27, 2026.

## Key Points

- The revised list contains 21 ETFs eligible for cross margin benefit in the F&O segment.
- Each ETF has a specified minimum quantity required (and multiples thereof) for cross margin eligibility.
- ETFs span multiple asset classes including broad market indices, sectoral indices (IT, banking, pharma, auto), mid-cap, PSU banks, and CPSE.
- Multiple fund houses are represented: Nippon India, SBI, ICICI Prudential, Mirae Asset, Kotak Mahindra, HDFC, and CPSE.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular revises the eligible ETF list previously communicated under circular no. 0046/2026 (NCL/CMPT/73924) dated April 27, 2026. The revised list replaces the earlier list in its entirety.

## Compliance Requirements

All members of NSE Clearing Limited must update their cross-margin calculations and systems to reflect the revised eligible ETF list and the specified minimum quantities. Cross margin benefits can only be availed for ETF positions meeting or exceeding the minimum quantity thresholds listed.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 26, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 27, 2026
- **Supersedes:** Circular no. 0046/2026 dated April 27, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This revision affects F&O segment members who utilise ETF positions for cross-margin benefits. Members must ensure their risk management systems reflect the updated eligible ETF list and minimum quantity requirements from May 27, 2026. ETFs covering broad market, banking, IT, pharma, auto, mid-cap, PSU banks, and CPSE sectors continue to be eligible, providing diverse collateral options for cross-margin participants.
