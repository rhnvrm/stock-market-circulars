## Summary

NSE has announced that Q-Line Biotech Limited (Symbol: QLINE), listed under the SME IPO segment, will be transferred from the trade-for-trade segment (Series: ST) to the rolling segment (Series: SM) with effect from June 12, 2026. This follows the earlier Exchange Circular NSE/CML/74449 dated May 27, 2026 and is pursuant to SEBI guidelines under circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012.

## Key Points

- QLINE (Q-Line Biotech Limited) will move from Series ST (trade-for-trade) to Series SM (rolling segment) effective June 12, 2026.
- The transfer is in line with SEBI's mandatory trade-for-trade period requirements for newly listed SME IPO securities.
- This is a continuation/update to NSE circular NSE/CML/74449 dated May 27, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

The transfer is governed by SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012, which mandates that SME IPO securities trade in the trade-for-trade segment for a specified period post-listing before transitioning to the normal rolling settlement mechanism.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members (brokers) must ensure trading in QLINE is handled under Series SM (rolling segment) from June 12, 2026 onwards.
- Any pending obligations in the trade-for-trade segment (Series: ST) must be settled prior to the transition date.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 29, 2026
- **Effective Date of Transfer:** June 12, 2026
- **Reference Circular:** NSE/CML/74449 dated May 27, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The transition from trade-for-trade (ST) to rolling settlement (SM) is a routine milestone in the lifecycle of SME IPO stocks. Once in the rolling segment, QLINE shares will be eligible for normal intraday and delivery-based trading without the settlement-level restrictions of trade-for-trade. This change increases liquidity and trading flexibility for market participants holding or trading QLINE shares. Impact is limited to this single scrip and is a positive development for retail and institutional investors seeking normal market access.
