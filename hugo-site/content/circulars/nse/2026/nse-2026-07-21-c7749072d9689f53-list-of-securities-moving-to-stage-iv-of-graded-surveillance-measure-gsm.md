## Summary

NSE has issued a circular moving Morarjee Textiles Limited (Symbol: MORARJEE, ISIN: INE161G01027) to Stage IV of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, effective July 22, 2026. This is a continuation of prior surveillance actions dated February 23, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, November 17, 2023, September 20, 2024, May 15, 2026, and June 19, 2026.

## Key Points

- Morarjee Textiles Limited (MORARJEE) moves to GSM Stage IV effective July 22, 2026.
- Stage IV is the most restrictive stage under the GSM framework.
- Trading will be permitted only on a trade-for-trade basis with no netting off allowed at settlement.
- Price band capped at 5% or lower as applicable.
- Trading permitted only once a week (every Monday, or the first trading day of the week).
- Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 100% of trade value required from buyers.
- No upward price movement is permitted for the security.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular does not introduce new regulatory rules but applies the existing GSM framework's Stage IV surveillance actions to a specific security, per the escalation criteria established under earlier NSE surveillance circulars (NSE/SURV/34262, 38389, 42790, 59425, 64066, 74254, and 74795).

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must ensure trading in MORARJEE is conducted only on a trade-for-trade basis from July 22, 2026.
- Buyers must deposit 100% of trade value as Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD).
- Members must apply the 5% or lower price band and restrict trading to once weekly (Monday/first trading day).
- No upward price movement in the security should be facilitated.
- Queries can be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in.

## Important Dates

- **July 21, 2026**: Circular issued (Ref No: NSE/SURV/75309, Circular Ref. No: 547/2026).
- **July 22, 2026**: Effective date for MORARJEE moving to GSM Stage IV.

## Impact Assessment

This action significantly restricts liquidity and trading flexibility for Morarjee Textiles Limited shareholders and prospective buyers, as trading is limited to once a week with no upward price movement permitted and a mandatory 100% ASD requirement. Existing shareholders may face difficulty exiting positions, while the trade-for-trade mechanism with no netting increases settlement friction. The move signals heightened regulatory concern regarding unusual price/volume activity or other surveillance triggers associated with the stock, and market participants should exercise caution when considering exposure to this security.
