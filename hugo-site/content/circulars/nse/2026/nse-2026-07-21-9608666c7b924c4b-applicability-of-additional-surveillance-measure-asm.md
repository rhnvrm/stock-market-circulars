## Summary

NSE has issued a circular identifying securities that meet the criteria for inclusion under the Long-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (Long Term - ASM) framework. Two securities, D. P. Abhushan Limited (DPABHUSHAN) and Kck Industries Limited (KCK), have been shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Stage I effective July 22, 2026. Additionally, Twamev Construction and Infrastructure Limited (TICL) has been shortlisted to move from Stage I to Stage IV under Criteria VII, resulting in its shift from the Rolling Settlement segment (Series: EQ) to the Trade-for-Trade segment (Series: BE).

## Key Points

- DPABHUSHAN (D. P. Abhushan Limited, ISIN: INE266Y01019) moved from Short-Term ASM (STASM) to Long-Term ASM (LTASM) Stage I.
- KCK (Kck Industries Limited, ISIN: INE0J1E01027) newly shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Stage I.
- No securities were shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Stage IV (fresh), Stage I to Stage II, or Stage II to Stage III transitions in this circular.
- TICL (Twamev Construction and Infrastructure Limited, ISIN: INE388G01026) shortlisted to move from Stage I to Stage IV under Criteria VII, triggering a shift from EQ (Rolling Settlement) to BE (Trade-for-Trade) series.
- Applicable margin rate for securities under Long-Term ASM shall be 100% on all open positions as on July 23, 2026, and new positions created from July 24, 2026 onwards.
- ASM framework operates in conjunction with other prevailing surveillance measures.
- Inclusion under ASM is a surveillance action only and should not be construed as an adverse action against the company.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this circular applies the existing Long-Term ASM framework (as detailed in prior circulars NSE/SURV/39265, NSE/SURV/45111, NSE/SURV/46557, NSE/SURV/48506, NSE/SURV/52090, NSE/SURV/63362, and NSE/SURV/64066) to newly identified securities based on updated surveillance criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE trading members must apply 100% margin on all open and new positions in DPABHUSHAN and KCK from the effective dates.
- Members must ensure TICL trades are processed in the Trade-for-Trade (BE) series rather than Rolling Settlement (EQ) from July 24, 2026 (T+3 basis from circular issuance).
- Members should route queries to surveillance@nse.co.in and refer to the published ASM FAQs for procedural guidance.

## Important Dates

- July 22, 2026: Shortlisting date for securities under the Long-Term ASM framework per the Annexure.
- July 23, 2026: Reference date for existing open positions subject to 100% margin.
- July 24, 2026: Effective date for 100% margin on open/new positions and for TICL's shift to Trade-for-Trade (BE) segment.

## Impact Assessment

The 100% margin requirement will substantially increase the cost of holding or creating positions in DPABHUSHAN and KCK, likely reducing trading volumes and liquidity in these counters. TICL's shift to Trade-for-Trade will eliminate intraday trading and require delivery-based settlement, significantly curbing speculative activity in the stock. These measures are targeted at specific securities showing surveillance-triggering price/volume patterns and do not represent systemic market risk, but affected shareholders and traders should adjust position sizing and settlement expectations accordingly.
