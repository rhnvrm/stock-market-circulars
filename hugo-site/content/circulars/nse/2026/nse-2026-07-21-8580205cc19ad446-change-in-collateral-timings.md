## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has revised the cut-off timings for collateral addition and release under the Securities Lending & Borrowing Scheme (SLBS), superseding the timings notified earlier in circular ref. no. 046/2026 (dated April 30, 2026). The revised timings, effective July 22, 2026, extend several cut-offs to later in the evening and introduce a detailed batch-wise release schedule for pledged securities.

## Key Points

- Cash and FDR Addition cut-off extended from 7:30 PM to 9:00 PM.
- Cash and FDR Intraday Release cut-off extended from 8:00 PM to 9:00 PM.
- Cash and FDR End of Day Release cut-off remains unchanged at 8:00 PM.
- Cash and FDR Allocation cut-off extended from 8:00 PM to 11:30 PM.
- Pledge securities release now follows 16 defined batches from 8:30 AM through 8:00 PM, plus an End of Day (EOD) batch at 9:00 PM.
- Requests for release of re-pledged securities towards pay-in requirements must be made one day prior to the pay-in date, not on the pay-in date itself.

## Regulatory Changes

The circular revises timings previously set out in NCL/CMPT/73999 (Circular Ref. No. 046/2026, dated April 30, 2026), based on feedback received from members. The changes apply to all Participants and Custodians under the SLBS framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Participants and Custodians must align their internal processes with the new cut-off times for collateral addition, release, and allocation.
- Clearing members must submit release requests for re-pledged securities required for pay-in at least one day before the pay-in date.
- Queries can be directed to collaterals_ops@nsccl.co.in or via IVR Option 2 at 1800 266 0050.

## Important Dates

- Circular date: July 17, 2026
- Revised timings effective: July 22, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The revised timings provide participants and custodians additional time in the evening to complete collateral addition, release, and allocation activities, potentially easing end-of-day operational bottlenecks. The structured batch-wise release schedule for pledged securities improves predictability for back-office and treasury functions. Impact is operational in nature and confined to SLBS participants and custodians, with no direct effect on listed securities or market pricing.
