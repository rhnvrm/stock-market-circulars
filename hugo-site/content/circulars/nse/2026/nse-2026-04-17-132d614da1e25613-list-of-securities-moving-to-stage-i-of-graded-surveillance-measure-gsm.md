## Summary

NSE has placed SUPREME ENGINEERING LIMITED (Symbol: SUPREMEENG, ISIN: INE319Z01021) under Stage I of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, effective April 20, 2026. Trading in this security will be subject to a 100% applicable margin rate and a price band of 5% or lower.

## Key Points

- SUPREMEENG (SUPREME ENGINEERING LIMITED, ISIN: INE319Z01021) moves to GSM Stage I from April 20, 2026
- Applicable margin rate will be 100%
- Price band restricted to 5% or lower
- Members are advised to exercise adequate caution while trading in this security
- Circular reference: NSE/SURV/73784, Circular Ref. No. 277/2026

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued in continuation of prior NSE surveillance circulars (NSE/SURV/34262, NSE/SURV/38389, NSE/SURV/42790, NSE/SURV/59425, NSE/SURV/64066, NSE/SURV/73167) governing the GSM framework. The GSM framework has four stages with progressively stricter trading restrictions:

- **Stage I:** 100% margin rate, price band of 5% or lower
- **Stage II:** Trade-for-Trade, 5% price band, Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) of 50% of trade value by buyers
- **Stage III:** Trade-for-Trade, 5% price band, weekly trading (every Monday), ASD of 100% of trade value by buyers
- **Stage IV:** Trade-for-Trade, 5% price band, weekly trading (every Monday), ASD of 100% of trade value by buyers, no upward movement permitted

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must ensure trades in SUPREMEENG are executed with 100% margin collected from clients effective April 20, 2026
- Members must adhere to the 5% (or lower) price band restriction
- Members are urged to take adequate precaution and inform clients accordingly
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** April 17, 2026
- **Effective Date:** April 20, 2026

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors holding or intending to trade SUPREME ENGINEERING LIMITED face significantly increased capital requirements due to the 100% margin rate. The 5% price band limits daily price movement, reducing liquidity and potentially increasing volatility around the band limits. This placement signals regulatory concern about the security's trading patterns and may affect investor sentiment. Members and their clients should reassess exposure and risk management strategies for this security.
