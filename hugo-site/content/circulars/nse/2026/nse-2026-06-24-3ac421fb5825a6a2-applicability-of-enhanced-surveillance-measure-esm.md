## Summary

NSE has published its Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) update effective June 25, 2026. Ten securities have been newly added to ESM Stage I, three securities are being escalated from ESM Stage I to Stage II, and no securities are being moved down or excluded from the ESM framework. Newly added ESM Stage I securities will move from the EQ/SM series to the BE/ST (trade-for-trade) settlement series effective June 29, 2026.

## Key Points

- 10 securities added to ESM Stage I w.e.f. June 25, 2026
- 3 securities escalated from ESM Stage I to Stage II w.e.f. June 25, 2026
- No securities moved from ESM Stage II to Stage I
- No securities excluded from ESM framework
- Newly shortlisted Stage I securities will shift from EQ/SM to BE/ST settlement w.e.f. June 29, 2026
- BLACKROSE inclusion is as per BSE data

## Regulatory Changes

ESM is a surveillance framework applied to securities exhibiting abnormal price or volume movements. Placement in ESM restricts trading to trade-for-trade (BE/ST) settlement, removing the benefit of netting and intraday trading, and may impose additional margin requirements.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must update their systems to reflect the settlement series change (EQ/SM → BE/ST) for newly added Stage I securities from June 29, 2026
- Clients holding positions in affected securities should be informed of the change in settlement type
- No action required for securities remaining in their existing ESM stage

## Important Dates

- **June 25, 2026**: ESM stage changes effective (new Stage I additions, Stage I → Stage II escalations)
- **June 29, 2026**: Settlement series change from EQ/SM to BE/ST for newly added ESM Stage I securities
