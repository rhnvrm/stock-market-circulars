## Summary

NSE has informed its members that, pursuant to a communication from PGIM Mutual Fund, subscriptions via Existing Daily and Weekly Systematic Transfer Plan (STP) instalments into certain designated PGIM India schemes will not be accepted on the NSE MF Invest Platform with effect from the cut-off time of June 10, 2026. Monthly and Quarterly STP instalments remain operational. The restriction is driven by SEBI's overseas investment headroom limits.

## Key Points

- Daily and Weekly STP instalments into the three designated PGIM India overseas fund-of-fund schemes will be blocked after the cut-off time of June 10, 2026.
- Monthly and Quarterly STP instalments will continue without interruption.
- The affected schemes are: PGIM India Global Equity Opportunities Fund of Fund, PGIM India Emerging Markets Equity Fund of Fund, and PGIM India Global Select Real Estate Securities Fund of Fund.
- The revised subscription limits take effect from June 11, 2026.
- Processing of blocked Daily/Weekly STPs will be managed by the AMC/RTA.
- This is a revision to earlier addenda dated March 09, 2026, May 15, 2026, and June 04, 2026 regarding the same designated schemes.

## Regulatory Changes

SEBI letter no. SEBI/HO/OW/IMD-II/DoF3/P/25095/2022 dated June 17, 2022 permits Mutual Funds to accept subscriptions in overseas funds/securities only up to the headroom available without breaching overseas investment limits as of EOD February 1, 2022 at the Mutual Fund level. PGIM India AMC is revising subscription limits in compliance with this constraint.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must note that Daily and Weekly STP instalments into the designated schemes will not be processed on the NSE MF Invest Platform from the cut-off time of June 10, 2026 onward.
- Members should communicate this change to their clients who have active Daily or Weekly STPs into the affected schemes.
- No action is required for clients with Monthly or Quarterly STPs, as these continue normally.
- Unit holders are advised to update PAN, KYC, email address, mobile number, and nominee details with the AMC, and to link PAN with Aadhaar.

## Important Dates

- **June 10, 2026**: Cut-off time after which Daily and Weekly STP instalments will not be accepted.
- **June 11, 2026**: Effective date for revised subscription limits in designated schemes.
- **March 09, 2026**: Date of original Notice Cum Addendum on temporary suspension of subscriptions.
- **May 15, 2026 and June 04, 2026**: Dates of prior addenda on reopening and revision of subscription limits.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to investors who have active Daily or Weekly STP arrangements transferring into the three PGIM India overseas fund-of-fund schemes. Monthly and Quarterly STP investors are unaffected. The restriction is a consequence of SEBI's overseas investment limit framework and is consistent with similar actions taken by other AMCs managing overseas funds when headroom is constrained. Members on the NSE MF Invest Platform should proactively notify affected clients and help them consider alternative investment arrangements if needed.
