## Summary

NSE has admitted 10 privately placed commercial papers (CPs) to dealings on its Debt Market Segment effective May 22, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All securities were allotted on May 21, 2026.

## Key Points

- 10 commercial papers from 10 issuers admitted to NSE Debt Market Segment
- All CPs allotted on May 21, 2026; effective for trading from May 22, 2026
- Face value of all CPs: ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Redemption periods range from approximately 1 month (TIAPL) to 1 year (ICICI Securities)
- Largest issuance by quantity: Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited (20,000 units)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine admission notification under existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

All members of the NSE Debt Market Segment are notified of the new security codes assigned per the Annexure. No additional compliance action required beyond standard debt market trading procedures.

## Impact Assessment

Routine operational notice with no material market impact. The admission of these CPs expands liquidity options in the short-term debt market. HPCL's issuance is the largest at 20,000 units (₹10,000 crore face value), reflecting significant short-term funding activity by a public sector undertaking. ICICI Securities has the longest tenor CP maturing May 21, 2027 (approximately 1 year), while most others mature within 3 months.
