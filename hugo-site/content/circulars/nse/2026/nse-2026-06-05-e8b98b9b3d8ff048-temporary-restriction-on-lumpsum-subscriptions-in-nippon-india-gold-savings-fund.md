## Summary

Nippon India Mutual Fund has announced temporary subscription restrictions on two gold-linked schemes — Nippon India ETF Gold BeES (GOLDBEES) and Nippon India Gold Savings Fund — effective June 08, 2026. The restrictions are driven by prevailing market conditions and are temporary in nature. Transactions time-stamped on or before 3:00 PM on June 05, 2026 will be processed at applicable NAV without restriction.

## Key Points

- **Nippon India ETF Gold BeES (GOLDBEES):** Direct fresh subscriptions with the AMC for Large Investors are discontinued from June 08, 2026. Authorized Participants and Market Makers are exempt from this restriction.
- **Nippon India Gold Savings Fund:** Fresh/additional lumpsum subscriptions, switch-ins, and new lumpsum registrations are capped at Rs. 10 lakh per PAN per month.
- SIP, STP, and other special products in Nippon India Gold Savings Fund continue with a limit of Rs. 50,000 per PAN per day.
- Redemptions, switch-outs, and secondary market transactions on the exchange for GOLDBEES remain unaffected.
- Existing SIPs and STPs in Nippon India Gold Savings Fund will continue as per Scheme Information Document (SID) terms.
- Regulatory investment mandates (e.g., alignment of interest requirements for Designated Employees) are exempt from these restrictions.

## Regulatory Changes

This addendum modifies the subscription terms in the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM) of both schemes. The changes are:
1. Discontinuation of direct AMC subscription for Large Investors in Nippon India ETF Gold BeES.
2. Introduction of a monthly lumpsum cap of Rs. 10 lakh per PAN for Nippon India Gold Savings Fund.
3. Introduction of a daily SIP/STP cap of Rs. 50,000 per PAN for Nippon India Gold Savings Fund.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE member firms and distributors on the NSE MF Invest Platform must enforce the lumpsum subscription cap of Rs. 10 lakh per PAN per month for Nippon India Gold Savings Fund.
- No new lumpsum registrations exceeding the prescribed limits should be accepted from June 08, 2026.
- Large Investor direct subscription requests for Nippon India ETF Gold BeES must be rejected after the effective date; only Authorized Participants and Market Makers may create units directly with the AMC.

## Important Dates

- **June 05, 2026, 3:00 PM:** Cutoff for transactions to be accepted and processed without restriction at applicable NAV.
- **June 08, 2026:** Effective date for all subscription restrictions on both schemes.
- Restrictions remain in place until further notice.

## Impact Assessment

The restrictions primarily affect large retail and institutional investors seeking direct or lumpsum exposure to gold via these Nippon India schemes. Secondary market trading of GOLDBEES ETF units on the exchange is unaffected, providing an alternative channel for gold investment. Existing SIP and STP investors in Nippon India Gold Savings Fund face no disruption. The measure is temporary and reflects AMC-level risk management in response to market conditions, with limited systemic impact on broader market operations.
