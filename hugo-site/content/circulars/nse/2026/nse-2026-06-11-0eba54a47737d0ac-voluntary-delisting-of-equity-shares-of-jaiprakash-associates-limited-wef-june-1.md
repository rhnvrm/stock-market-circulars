## Summary

NSE's Listing Department has issued a circular (Ref No: NSE/CML/74660, Circular Ref: 2026/0939) notifying the withdrawal of admission to dealings — i.e., delisting — of equity shares of Jaiprakash Associates Limited (symbol: JPASSOCIAT) with effect from June 18, 2026. The delisting is consequent to a Resolution Plan approved by the Hon'ble National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT), Allahabad Bench, Prayagraj, under Sections 30(6) and 31 of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016.

## Key Points

- Jaiprakash Associates Limited (NSE symbol: JPASSOCIAT) will be delisted from NSE effective **June 18, 2026**.
- The delisting is classified as voluntary but is driven by an NCLT-approved Resolution Plan under the IBC, 2016.
- The legal basis is Sections 30(6) and 31 of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016, as adjudicated by the NCLT Allahabad Bench at Prayagraj.
- The circular is addressed to all NSE members.

## Regulatory Changes

Admission to dealings in the equity shares of Jaiprakash Associates Limited on the National Stock Exchange of India is being formally withdrawn. This is pursuant to the insolvency resolution process completed under the IBC framework, with NCLT approval serving as the regulatory trigger for the delisting action.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must cease trading in JPASSOCIAT equity shares on and after June 18, 2026.
- Members should inform their clients holding JPASSOCIAT shares about the impending delisting and the consequent illiquidity of the securities post-delisting.
- No new positions or orders in JPASSOCIAT should be accepted after the effective delisting date.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 11, 2026
- **Delisting Effective Date:** June 18, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The delisting of Jaiprakash Associates Limited represents a significant market event for retail and institutional investors holding the company's equity shares. Post-delisting, the shares will no longer be tradable on NSE, resulting in a complete loss of exchange-based liquidity. Investors will need to explore off-market options or any exit opportunity provided under the IBC resolution plan. This event underscores the risk associated with companies undergoing insolvency proceedings, where the resolution plan may result in the elimination or restructuring of existing equity. Brokers and depository participants should update client portfolios to reflect the delisted status of JPASSOCIAT from June 18, 2026.
