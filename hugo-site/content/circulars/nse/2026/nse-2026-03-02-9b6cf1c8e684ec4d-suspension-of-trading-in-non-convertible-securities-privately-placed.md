## Summary

NSE's Listing Department has issued a circular (Ref No: NSE/CML/73092, Circular Ref. No: 0361/2026) notifying the suspension of trading in two privately placed non-convertible securities effective March 4, 2026. The suspension is triggered by the scheduled redemption of these instruments, in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A.

## Key Points

- Two privately placed non-convertible securities are being suspended from trading effective March 4, 2026.
- Indian Railway Finance Corporation Limited (ISIN: INE053F09HN1) — suspended due to redemption.
- IIFL Finance Limited (ISIN: INE530B07435) — suspended due to redemption.
- The suspension is mandated under Regulation 3.1.2 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A.
- Circular issued by Vishakha Kine, Manager, NSE.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. The suspension is a standard procedural action under the existing NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A, Regulation 3.1.2, which governs the suspension of trading in debt securities upon maturity or redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members are notified to cease trading in the specified ISINs from the suspension date.
- No action required from equity investors; this applies only to debt market participants trading these specific non-convertible securities.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** March 2, 2026
- **Suspension Effective Date:** March 4, 2026 (for both securities)

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited and routine. The suspension of these two privately placed non-convertible securities is a standard lifecycle event triggered by scheduled redemptions. Holders of these instruments should expect the redemption proceeds to be settled as per the terms of the respective securities. There is no broader market disruption expected. Equity trading in IRFC and IIFL Finance shares on the capital market segment remains unaffected.
