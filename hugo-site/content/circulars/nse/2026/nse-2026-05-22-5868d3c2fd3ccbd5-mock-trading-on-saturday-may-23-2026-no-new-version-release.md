## Summary

NSE is conducting a mock trading session in the Currency Derivatives (CD) Segment on Saturday, May 23, 2026. No new NEAT version is being released for this session; members should continue using NEAT version 3.5.4.

## Key Points

- Mock trading is scheduled for Saturday, May 23, 2026 in the Currency Derivatives Segment
- No new NEAT version release; current version is NEAT 3.5.4 (download path: `/cdsftp/cdscommon/NEATCDS354`)
- Two trading sessions: Session-1 from Primary Site and Session-2 from DR Site
- Members should use NEAT Adapter version 1.0.27 (Windows and Linux) with GR PORT 10879
- All outstanding orders will be cleared before each session
- Trades during mock sessions carry no financial obligation (no fund pay-in or pay-out)
- Members are encouraged to re-login into the live environment after the mock session to ensure smooth connectivity for Monday, May 25, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational mock trading notice.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use NEAT Adapter version 1.0.27 with the applicable GR PORT (10879) for the Currency Derivatives segment
- Members using NNF software must manually clear outstanding orders in their systems before each session
- Only valid and compliant UCC/PAN uploaded and approved before cutoff will be permitted for order entry
- Software testing must comply with circulars NSE/MSD/46441 and SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234
- Refer to NSE/MSD/73993 dated April 30, 2026 for interactive connectivity details
- Refer to NSE/MSD/73674 dated April 09, 2026 for the latest NEAT Adapter Exe application

## Important Dates

- **Mock Trading Date:** May 23, 2026 (Saturday)
- **Session-1 (Primary Site):** Normal Market open 09:00, close 10:05
- **Session-2 (DR Site):** Normal Market open 10:45, close 11:30
- **Trade Modification End Time:** 11:40
- **Live Re-login Window:** 13:00 – 13:30
- **Live Market Resumption:** Monday, May 25, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has no impact on live markets or financial positions. It is a routine mock/disaster-recovery drill for Currency Derivatives Segment members. Participation helps ensure operational readiness and connectivity for the following trading week. No stocks, indices, or market participants are financially affected.
