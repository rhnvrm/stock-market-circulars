## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed commercial papers (CPs) from 10 issuers to dealings on its Debt Market Segment effective June 09, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All securities were allotted on June 08, 2026, with most maturing on September 07, 2026.

## Key Points

- 10 fresh commercial paper issuances admitted to NSE Debt Market Segment effective June 09, 2026
- All CPs allotted on June 08, 2026; majority redeem on September 07, 2026
- Face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit across all issuances
- Largest issuance: Export Import Bank of India (EXIM) with 60,000 units; NABARD with 49,000 units
- Issue prices range from ₹4,88,193.50 (IIFL Finance) to ₹4,94,261 (Aditya Birla Money Limited – short-tenor CP)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. Listing is in pursuance of existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. Members are notified of the designated security codes in the Annexure for trading purposes.

## Impact Assessment

Routine operational circular with no material impact on equity markets. Debt market participants and institutional investors holding or trading these CPs should note the designated NSE security codes for settlement. The issuances represent short-term borrowing (approximately 90 days) by well-known financial institutions and corporates. No action required by equity investors or retail market participants.
