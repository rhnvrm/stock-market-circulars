## Summary

NSE has communicated to all members an update from SEBI regarding amendments to the United Nations Security Council (UNSC) 1988 (2011) sanctions list. The UNSC Committee has removed 7 entries from the list, as notified via SC/16365 dated May 21, 2026. Members are required to take necessary compliance actions under Section 51A of the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act (UAPA), 1967 and SEBI's AML/CFT guidelines.

## Key Points

- UNSC Committee (resolution 1988/2011) removed 7 entries from its sanctions list via Note SC/16365 dated May 21, 2026.
- The updated ISIL (Da'esh) & Al-Qaida sanctions list (resolution 1989/2011) is available at www.un.org/securitycouncil/sanctions/1267/aq_sanctions_list.
- The updated Taliban sanctions list (resolution 1988/2011) is available at https://www.un.org/securitycouncil/sanctions/1988/materials.
- All members must scan existing and future accounts against the updated list.
- Delisting requests should be forwarded electronically to Joint Secretary (CTCR), MHA at jsctcr-mha@gov.in with a copy to MEA Joint Secretary (UNP) at jsunp@mea.gov.in.
- Individuals seeking removal from the ISIL/Al-Qaida Sanctions List may apply via the UN Ombudsperson at https://www.un.org/securitycouncil/ombudsperson/application.
- SEBI website maintains updated UNSC Sanctions Committee List under "Media and Notifications".

## Regulatory Changes

- 7 entries removed from the UNSC 1988 (2011) sanctions list effective May 21, 2026.
- No new entities added in this update; this is a removal-only amendment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must follow the procedure outlined in the Central Government Order dated February 02, 2021 for implementation of Section 51A of UAPA, 1967.
- Scan all existing accounts to ensure no account is held by or linked to any sanctioned entity or individual.
- Apply the same scrutiny to all future accounts.
- Comply with Clause 54 of SEBI Master Circular on AML Standards and CFT Obligations dated June 06, 2024.
- Regularly visit the SEBI website for ongoing updates to the UNSC Sanctions Committee List.
- Forward any received delisting requests to the designated government authorities.

## Important Dates

- **May 21, 2026**: UNSC Note SC/16365 issued; 7 entries removed from the 1988 (2011) sanctions list.
- **May 25, 2026**: NSE circular issued (NSE/ISC/74395, Circular Ref. No. 29/2026).
- **February 02, 2021**: Central Government Order on Section 51A UAPA implementation (reference procedure).
- **June 06, 2024**: SEBI Master Circular on AML/CFT obligations (reference guideline).

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine compliance update affecting all NSE members and registered intermediaries. The removal of 7 entries from the sanctions list reduces the universe of restricted individuals/entities. Members must update their screening processes to reflect these removals and ensure no flagged accounts remain incorrectly blocked. There is no direct market or trading impact, but non-compliance with AML/CFT obligations can attract regulatory action. Members are advised to maintain ongoing vigilance and regularly check the SEBI and UN websites for future updates.
