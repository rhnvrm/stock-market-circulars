## Summary

NSE has notified the suspension of trading in five privately placed non-convertible securities effective June 15, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A. The suspensions are triggered by redemption or exercise of a call option.

## Key Points

- Five NCD ISINs across four issuers will be suspended from trading on June 15, 2026.
- Suspensions are due to scheduled redemption or call option exercise.
- Circular issued under NSE Ref No: NSE/CML/74671, Circular Ref No: 0947/2026.
- Regulation cited: Regulation 3.1.2 of NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular applies existing Regulation 3.1.2 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A, which governs the suspension of non-convertible securities upon redemption or maturity.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members are informed that the listed ISINs will not be available for trading from June 15, 2026.
- No action required from members beyond noting the suspension dates for the affected securities.

## Important Dates

- **June 12, 2026**: Circular issuance date.
- **June 15, 2026**: Effective suspension date for all five securities.

| Sr. No. | Company | ISIN | Reason |
|---------|---------|------|--------|
| 1 | Tata Capital Housing Finance Limited | INE033L07EY9 | Redemption |
| 2 | Power Finance Corporation Limited | INE134E08LG0 | Redemption |
| 3 | REC Limited | INE020B08EM0 | Redemption |
| 4 | REC Limited | INE020B08ED9 | Redemption |
| 5 | Tata Capital Limited | INE306N08185 | Redemption for exercising call option |

## Impact Assessment

Impact is limited and routine. These are privately placed debt instruments reaching maturity or call option exercise dates. Existing holders will receive redemption proceeds; no further trading is possible after June 14, 2026. There is no broader equity market impact. Affected issuers — Tata Capital Housing Finance Limited, Power Finance Corporation Limited, REC Limited, and Tata Capital Limited — are large, well-rated entities for whom this represents normal lifecycle management of their debt instruments.
