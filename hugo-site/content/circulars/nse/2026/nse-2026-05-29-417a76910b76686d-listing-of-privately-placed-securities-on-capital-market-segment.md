## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of two privately placed debt securities to dealings on the National Stock Exchange Capital Market segment effective June 01, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A. Both securities carry a market lot size of 1 unit.

## Key Points

- Two privately placed securities admitted to NSE capital market segment from June 01, 2026
- Muthoot Finance Limited: T-bill linked NCD (Symbol: MFL29, Series: N0, ISIN: INE414G07JZ7), maturing July 26, 2029
- Cholamandalam Investment and Finance Company Limited: Market-linked NCD (Symbol: CIFCL29, Series: N0, ISIN: INE121A07SU3), maturing February 27, 2029
- Market lot size for both securities is 1
- Securities shall be identified solely by their designated codes for trading purposes

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular is issued under the existing framework of Regulation 3.1.1 (admission to dealings) and Regulation 2.5.5 (lot sizes) of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must use designated security codes (MFL29, CIFCL29) when trading these securities
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot sizes (1 unit per lot)
- Members should update their trading systems to recognize the new symbols and series codes effective June 01, 2026

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 29, 2026
- **Effective Date for Trading:** June 01, 2026
- **Maturity - MFL29 (Muthoot Finance):** July 26, 2029
- **Maturity - CIFCL29 (Cholamandalam):** February 27, 2029

## Impact Assessment

Limited market impact. This is a routine listing notification for two privately placed debt instruments. Muthoot Finance's MFL29 is a T-bill linked security, meaning its coupon is benchmarked to the prevailing T-bill rate, providing floating-rate exposure. Cholamandalam's CIFCL29 is a market-linked NCD. Both mature in 2029 and carry market lot sizes of 1, making them accessible in standard trading increments. The listing improves secondary market liquidity for existing holders of these privately placed instruments.
