## Summary

NSE has implemented a temporary restriction on lumpsum subscriptions in Aditya Birla Sun Life Gold Fund on the NSE MF Invest Platform, effective June 9, 2026. This follows an addendum (No. 33/2026) issued by Aditya Birla Sun Life AMC Limited citing prevailing market conditions. A parallel restriction also applies to Aditya Birla Sun Life Gold ETF for large investors.

## Key Points

- Lumpsum subscriptions and switch-in applications in **ABSL Gold Fund** are capped at an aggregate of **INR 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month**
- Applications exceeding this limit submitted after the cut-off time of **3:00 PM on June 08, 2026** will not be accepted
- **ABSL Gold ETF** restricts direct transactions from large investors investing more than **Rs. 25 crores** with Aditya Birla Sun Life AMC Ltd.
- NSE has applied these changes on the NSE MF Invest Platform with immediate effect
- All other features and terms & conditions of the SID and KIM remain unchanged

## Regulatory Changes

- Maximum amount limit introduced for lumpsum purchase and switch-in in ABSL Gold Fund: INR 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month
- Large investor (>Rs. 25 crore) direct transactions in ABSL Gold ETF suspended
- Changes enacted via Addendum No. 33/2026 to the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM)

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members and distributors must ensure lumpsum subscription orders for ABSL Gold Fund do not exceed INR 10 lakh per PAN per calendar month on the NSE MF Invest Platform
- Orders received post the 3:00 PM cut-off on June 08, 2026 that breach the aggregate limit must not be processed
- Members should communicate these restrictions to their clients investing in ABSL Gold ETF and ABSL Gold Fund

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** June 09, 2026 (Tuesday)
- **Cut-off for existing applications:** 3:00 PM, June 08, 2026 (applications received after this time exceeding the limit will not be accepted)
- **Addendum Date:** June 08, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This restriction moderately impacts retail and HNI investors seeking large lumpsum exposure to gold via the ABSL Gold Fund on NSE's platform. Investors wishing to invest more than INR 10 lakh per month in this fund will need to consider alternative gold fund options or ETF routes (subject to the >Rs. 25 crore cap for ABSL Gold ETF). SIP investments are not mentioned as restricted and are likely unaffected. The restriction is temporary and driven by prevailing market conditions, suggesting it may be lifted once conditions normalize.
