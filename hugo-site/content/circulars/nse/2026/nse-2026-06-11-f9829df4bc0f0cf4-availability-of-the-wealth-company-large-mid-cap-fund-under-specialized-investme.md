## Summary

NSE has informed all members that The Wealth Company Large & Mid Cap Fund, offered under the Specialized Investment Fund (SIF) framework of The Wealth Company Mutual Fund, will be reopened for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions on the NSE MF Invest Platform with effect from June 12, 2026. The platform will also support SIP (Systematic Investment Plan), STP (Systematic Transfer Plan), and SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) facilities for these schemes.

## Key Points

- Six scheme variants of The Wealth Company Large & Mid Cap Fund will be available on NSE MF Invest Platform from June 12, 2026
- The fund is categorized under the Specialized Investment Fund (SIF) structure
- All six variants fall under the Equity category
- Subscription, redemption, SIP, STP, and SWP transactions will be enabled
- The fund house is The Wealth Company Mutual Fund

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced by this circular. It is an operational notification regarding the reopening of an existing SIF scheme on the NSE MF Invest Platform.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance actions are required from members. Members who wish to transact in these schemes may do so through the NSE MF Invest Platform from June 12, 2026 onwards.

## Important Dates

- **June 12, 2026**: Effective date for reopening of The Wealth Company Large & Mid Cap Fund schemes on NSE MF Invest Platform for ongoing subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, STP, and SWP.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with low market impact. The reopening enables investors and distributors to transact in The Wealth Company Large & Mid Cap Fund variants through NSE's MF platform. The six scheme variants cover all standard options (Direct/Regular plans, IDCW Payout, IDCW Reinvestment, and Growth), providing full transaction flexibility. Members using the NSE MF Invest Platform should note the availability date to facilitate client transactions.
