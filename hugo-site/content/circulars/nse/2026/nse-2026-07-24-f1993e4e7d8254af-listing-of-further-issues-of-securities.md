## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of further issues of securities of two SME EMERGE-listed companies: Committed Cargo Care Limited and C P S Shapers Limited. These further issues will be admitted to dealings on the Capital Market segment (SME EMERGE) effective July 27, 2026, under Regulations 3.1.1 and 2.5.5 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.

## Key Points

- Committed Cargo Care Limited (Symbol: COMMITTED, ISIN: INE597Z01014) has 95,000 equity shares of Rs. 10/- each admitted, allotted pursuant to conversion of warrants issued on a preferential basis at an issue price of Rs. 81, with a market lot of 800 shares.
- C P S Shapers Limited (Symbol: CPS, ISIN: INE0QBU01012) has 31,275 equity shares of Rs. 10/- each admitted, allotted on a preferential basis at an issue price of Rs. 1,088, with a market lot of 150 shares.
- Both securities trade in the SM series on the SME EMERGE platform and rank pari passu with existing equity shares.
- Shares are currently credited under a temporary ISIN pending activation, per SEBI circulars dated August 2, 2012 and September 11, 2012.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes; this is a routine admission of further issues to trading under existing NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

Members must recognize the new distinctive number ranges and lock-in expiry dates for these securities and trade only in the specified lot sizes (800 for COMMITTED, 150 for CPS).

## Important Dates

- Date of Allotment (COMMITTED): May 27, 2026
- Date of Allotment (CPS): June 26, 2026
- Lock-in Expiry (COMMITTED): January 25, 2027
- Lock-in Expiry (CPS): 1,380 shares locked-in until February 28, 2028; 29,895 shares locked-in until February 27, 2027
- Effective Date of Listing: July 27, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has limited market-wide impact, being relevant primarily to existing shareholders, warrant holders, and traders of COMMITTED and CPS on the SME EMERGE platform. It increases the tradable share count for both companies following preferential allotments and warrant conversions.
