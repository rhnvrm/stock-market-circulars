## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has issued a revision to the Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL) and the Participant, Institutional Client, and Non-Institutional Client limits applicable to GOLDIAM and PSUBANK under the Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) Scheme for July 2026. This follows and partially modifies earlier circulars dated April 30, 2026 and June 30, 2026. The revised limits take effect from July 13, 2026.

## Key Points

- Circular Ref. No: 070/2026, Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/75160, dated July 10, 2026.
- Revises limits set out in circular NCL/CMPT/73999 (April 30, 2026) and NCL/CMPT/74959 (June 30, 2026).
- Applies to two symbols: GOLDIAM and PSUBANK.
- GOLDIAM: MWPL increased from 4,684,578 to 6,246,102 shares; Institutional Client Limit increased from 468,457 to 624,610 shares; Participant Limit increased from 468,457 to 624,610 shares; Non-Institutional Client Limit increased from 46,845 to 62,461 shares.
- PSUBANK: MWPL increased from 3,032,396 to 30,323,967 shares; Institutional Client Limit increased from 303,239 to 3,032,396 shares; Participant Limit increased from 303,239 to 3,032,396 shares; Non-Institutional Client Limit increased from 30,323 to 303,239 shares.

## Regulatory Changes

The Market Wide Position Limit and associated client-category limits (Participant, Institutional Client, Non-Institutional Client) for GOLDIAM and PSUBANK under the SLB Scheme have been revised upward for July 2026, superseding the limits communicated in the June 30, 2026 circular.

## Compliance Requirements

All Participants and Custodians dealing in SLB contracts for GOLDIAM and PSUBANK must apply the revised limits with effect from July 13, 2026 and ensure positions remain within the new thresholds.

## Important Dates

- Circular issued: July 10, 2026
- Revised limits effective: July 13, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The increased MWPL and client limits expand the permissible SLB positions in GOLDIAM and PSUBANK, allowing greater lending/borrowing activity and position-taking capacity for participants in these two securities. The impact is confined to SLB market participants trading these specific symbols and does not affect the broader market or other securities.
