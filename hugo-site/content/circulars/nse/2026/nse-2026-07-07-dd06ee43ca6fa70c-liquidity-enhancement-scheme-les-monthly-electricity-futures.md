## Summary

NSE has extended the Liquidity Enhancement Scheme (LES) applicable to Monthly Electricity Futures Contracts for a further period of 3 months. The extension will be effective from July 22, 2026 till October 21, 2026. All provisions under the earlier circulars governing this scheme continue to apply.

## Key Points

- The LES on Monthly Electricity Futures Contracts is extended for 3 months.
- Extension period: July 22, 2026 to October 21, 2026.
- This is a continuation of the scheme first introduced via Circular No. NSE/COM/68807 dated June 27, 2025, and subsequently modified/extended through multiple circulars (68867, 68885, 68968, 68969, 69072, and 72023).
- All other terms and conditions from the earlier circulars remain unchanged and continue to apply.
- The Exchange reserves the right to amend or discontinue the scheme at any time, with 15 days' advance notice.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this circular simply extends the validity period of the existing LES scheme for Monthly Electricity Futures under the Commodity Derivatives segment.

## Compliance Requirements

Members participating in the LES for Monthly Electricity Futures should note the revised validity period and continue to comply with the eligibility and participation criteria set out in the referenced prior circulars (NSE/COM/68807, 68867, 68885, 68968, 68969, 69072, and 72023).

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: July 07, 2026
- LES Extension Effective From: July 22, 2026
- LES Extension Valid Till: October 21, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative extension of an existing liquidity enhancement scheme in the commodity derivatives (electricity futures) segment. It has no direct bearing on listed equities or broader market operations, and is primarily relevant to market makers and members participating in the LES for electricity futures contracts. Impact on the general market and investors is minimal.
