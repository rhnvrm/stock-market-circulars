## Summary

NSE circular NSE/CML/74318 (Ref No. 0814/2026) notifies the admission of 6 government securities to dealings on the NSE Capital Market Segment effective May 22, 2026. The securities comprise 3 State Development Loans (SDLs) from Punjab and Rajasthan, and 3 Government of India Treasury Bills with tenors of 91 days, 182 days, and 364 days. All securities carry a standard lot size of 100 units.

## Key Points

- 6 new government securities admitted to NSE Capital Market Segment from May 22, 2026
- 3 SDLs: Punjab (7.91% 2041, 7.92% 2044) and Rajasthan (7.87% 2041)
- 3 GOI T-Bills: 91-day (matures Aug 20, 2026), 182-day (matures Nov 19, 2026), 364-day (matures May 20, 2027)
- All securities carry a uniform lot size of 100 units
- Securities must be traded using their designated symbol codes on the system
- Issued under Regulation 3.1.1 and Regulation 2.5.5 of NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular operates under existing Regulation 3.1.1 (admission of securities to dealings) and Regulation 2.5.5 (lot sizes) of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must use designated security codes for trading these instruments
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot sizes of 100 units per security
- Members dealing in government securities on the capital market segment should update their systems with the new ISINs and symbol codes prior to May 22, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine periodic listing of sovereign debt instruments. Impact is limited to debt market participants — primarily gilt funds, insurance companies, banks, and institutional investors active in the government securities segment. The SDLs from Punjab (coupon rates 7.91% and 7.92%) and Rajasthan (7.87%) offer medium-to-long-term state government debt exposure maturing in 2041 and 2044 respectively. The three T-Bill listings provide short-duration sovereign instruments for liquidity management. No impact on equity markets or equity investors anticipated.
