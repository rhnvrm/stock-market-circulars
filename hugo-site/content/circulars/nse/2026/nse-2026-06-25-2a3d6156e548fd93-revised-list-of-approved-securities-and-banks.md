## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a revised list of approved securities and empaneled banks for the Currency Derivatives (CD) segment, effective July 2026. This circular (Ref. No. 026/2026) supersedes the earlier circular 024/2026 dated May 22, 2026. The revision covers five annexures detailing equity shares, ETFs, government securities, open-ended mutual funds, and approved banks for Bank Guarantees (BGs) and Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDRs).

## Key Points

- **Annexure 1**: Updated list of equity shares forming the non-cash component of liquid assets, with market-wide limits, member-specific limits, and VAR-based haircut rates for the CD segment.
- **Annexure 2**: Updated list of Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs) forming the cash component of liquid assets, with applicable limits and haircut rates.
- **Annexure 3**: List of Sovereign Gold Bonds and GOI Securities (G-Sec/T-Bills) forming the cash component of liquid assets, with applicable haircut rates.
- **Annexure 4**: List of open-ended mutual funds acceptable as collateral, with market-wide applicable limits and haircut rates.
- **Annexure 5**: List of banks empaneled for issuance of Bank Guarantees (BGs) and Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDRs).
- A prudential value-based norm applies: exclusion of a pledged security must not result in a margin shortfall exceeding 25% at a Clearing Member level.
- G-Sec/T-Bills carry a shut period of two days; members must release these instruments at least two days before maturity.
- Members who are also banks must ensure G-Sec provided as collateral is not reckoned for SLR purposes and not used for trading.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular updates the approved securities list in the Currency Derivatives segment as part of a regular monthly revision cycle. The effective list replaces the prior approved list issued under circular 024/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CD/74375) dated May 22, 2026. Haircut rates continue to be VAR-based for equity shares.

## Compliance Requirements

- **All Members and Custodians/PCMs** must refer to the updated annexures for permitted quantities and applicable haircut rates when pledging securities as collateral in the CD segment.
- Members must not pledge G-Sec/T-Bills within two days of their maturity date; securities should be released to NCL at least two days prior.
- Member-banks must separately ensure that G-Sec submitted as collateral is excluded from their SLR calculations and not used for proprietary trading.
- Collateral portfolios must comply with the 25% margin shortfall prudential cap at the Clearing Member level.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: June 25, 2026
- **Effective Date**: July 2026 (entire month)
- **Previous Circular Superseded**: Circular 024/2026 dated May 22, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine monthly operational update affecting clearing members, custodians, and professional clearing members (PCMs) in the Currency Derivatives segment. The revision may alter which specific equity shares, ETFs, mutual funds, or G-Secs are permissible as collateral and may change applicable haircut rates or quantity limits, directly impacting margin planning and collateral substitution strategies. Members should review all five annexures to identify any additions, removals, or limit changes relative to the prior month's approved list. No structural changes to the collateral framework are introduced.
