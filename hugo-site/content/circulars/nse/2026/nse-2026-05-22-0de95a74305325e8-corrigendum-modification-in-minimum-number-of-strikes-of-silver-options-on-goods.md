## Summary

NSE issued a corrigendum to circular NSE/COM/74161 (dated May 12, 2026) revising the minimum number of strikes for Silver Options on Goods (OPTBLN). The minimum strike count is nearly quadrupling — from 51 strikes (25 ITM, 25 OTM, 1 NTM) to 199 strikes (99 ITM, 99 OTM, 1 NTM) per each of CE and PE — to better align with Silver's current price levels and volatility.

## Key Points

- Symbol affected: SILVER (Instrument Type: Options on Goods / OPTBLN)
- Existing minimum strikes: 25 In-the-money + 25 Out-of-the-money + 1 Near-the-money = 51 CE and 51 PE
- Revised minimum strikes: 99 In-the-money + 99 Out-of-the-money + 1 Near-the-money = 199 CE and 199 PE
- The Exchange retains discretion to introduce additional strikes beyond the minimum as required
- All other terms of the original circular NSE/COM/74161 remain unchanged

## Regulatory Changes

The minimum strikes parameter for Silver Options on Goods contracts is being increased nearly fourfold. This is a modification to contract specifications under the Commodity Derivatives segment. The change is issued as a corrigendum, superseding the corresponding parameter in the original circular NSE/COM/74161 dated May 12, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- No direct compliance action required from members beyond awareness of the revised contract specifications
- Members should update their systems and risk models to account for the expanded strike range available in Silver Options on Goods from May 25, 2026

## Important Dates

- **Reference Circular:** NSE/COM/74161 dated May 12, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 25, 2026
- **Applicable Contracts:** June 2026 contracts and onwards

## Impact Assessment

The near-quadrupling of minimum available strikes improves market depth and provides participants with a wider and more meaningful range of hedging and trading options for Silver. This is particularly relevant given Silver's current price levels and volatility. Broader strike coverage benefits both hedgers seeking precise risk management and traders looking for specific payoff structures. Systems generating or consuming options chain data for Silver will see a significantly larger set of contracts from May 25, 2026 onwards.
