## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a revised list of approved securities and banks for collateral purposes, updating its earlier circular no. 010/2026 dated Jun 25, 2026. The circular specifies the list of GOI securities/T-Bills and Sovereign Gold Bonds (cash component), Corporate Bonds (non-cash component), and empaneled banks for issuance of Bank Guarantees (BGs) and Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDRs), along with applicable haircuts and market-wide limits. This circular is effective for the month of August 2026.

## Key Points

- Annexure 1 lists approved GOI Securities (G-Sec)/T-Bills and Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs) accepted as cash component of liquid assets, along with applicable haircut rates.
- All SGBs/G-Secs/T-Bills issued by RBI during the month are automatically accepted from their date of issue.
- Sovereign Gold Bonds listed carry a uniform applicable haircut of 0.1 (10%).
- Annexure 2 lists approved Corporate Bonds (non-cash component of liquid assets) with market-wide applicable limits and haircut rates.
- Annexure 3 lists banks empaneled for issuance of BGs and FDRs.
- G-Sec/T-Bills carry a shut period of two days; members must release G-Sec/T-Bills at least two days before maturity.
- Member-banks are reminded that G-Secs provided as collateral should not be reckoned towards SLR requirements and must not be used for trading.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine monthly update superseding the list issued vide circular no. 010/2026 (download ref. NCL/DS/74875) dated Jun 25, 2026. No structural or policy changes are introduced; only the underlying list of eligible securities, banks, haircuts, and limits has been revised.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members, Custodians, and Professional Clearing Members (PCMs) must ensure collateral submitted for margin/liquid asset purposes conforms to the revised annexures.
- Members must observe the two-day shut period for G-Sec/T-Bills and release them at least two days prior to maturity.
- Member-banks must exclude G-Secs pledged as collateral from SLR computations and refrain from using them for trading.

## Important Dates

- Circular issued: Jul 22, 2026
- Effective period: Month of August 2026
- Supersedes circular no. 010/2026 (NCL/DS/74875) dated Jun 25, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine, operational circular primarily affecting NSE Clearing members, custodians, and PCMs regarding acceptable collateral composition and valuation (haircuts) for margin purposes. It does not directly affect listed company stock prices or trading activity for the broader market, but is relevant for clearing members' risk and collateral management processes.
