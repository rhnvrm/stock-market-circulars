## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has launched an automated portal for Clearing Members to claim Tax Deduction at Source (TDS) refunds. Previously, members submitted challans and TDS certificates manually via email. The new portal, accessible through the NCL Member Portal under Menu – NCL_TDS, digitises the entire process. The system is applicable for invoices raised by NCL from June 1, 2026 onwards.

## Key Points

- Clearing Members, Self Clearing Members, and Professional Clearing Members can now claim TDS refunds via an automated portal instead of manual email submissions.
- Individual challans must be submitted for NCL payments; common challans will not be accepted and claims based on them will be rejected.
- NCL's PAN number is AAACN2642L.
- The portal is available on the ENIT platform under Menu – TDS.
- TDS refunds for periods prior to June 1, 2026 must still be claimed by submitting TDS certificates to dl-tdsrefund-fa-ncl@nseclearing.co.in.
- The portal provides five functions: TDS Credit Member Master, Deduction Details, TDS Credit Status, Submit TDS Certificate, and TDS Certificate Status.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory mandate introduced. This is a process digitisation initiative by NCL replacing the existing manual email-based TDS refund submission process with an automated self-service portal.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use existing admin user IDs in the NCL portal to create Sub-users with NCL-TDS role access.
- Individual challans must be submitted per NCL payment; common/consolidated challans are not accepted.
- TDS certificates must be digitally signed before upload.
- Members must enter PAN details and TAN numbers (multiple TANs supported via 'Add TAN' feature) in the TDS Credit Member Master section upon first login.
- Two contact person details are mandatory for status update notifications.
- For pre-June 2026 TDS refunds, members must continue to submit TDS certificates via email to dl-tdsrefund-fa-ncl@nseclearing.co.in.

## Important Dates

- **June 1, 2026**: Effective date — the new automated portal applies to invoices raised by NCL from this date onwards.
- **June 25, 2026**: Circular issuance date; portal is live and available for use.

## Impact Assessment

This change streamlines the TDS refund process for all Clearing Members dealing with NCL, reducing turnaround times and eliminating dependency on manual email submissions. The self-service portal provides real-time status tracking. Members must set up Sub-users with appropriate NCL-TDS roles before they can use the portal. The restriction on common challans is a key operational constraint members must adhere to, or risk rejection of their claims. Overall operational impact is moderate — members need to adapt internal processes and ensure proper sub-user configuration.
