## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued updated risk management guidelines for the Closing Auction Session (CAS) in the Equity Cash Segment. The circular clarifies how margin validation will apply to orders carried over from the Continuous Trading Session (CTS) as well as orders modified or newly placed during CAS. These rules build upon the earlier introduction of CAS via NCL Circular Ref. No. 0030/2026 dated January 19, 2026.

## Key Points

- Orders carried over from the Continuous Trading Session (CTS) to the Closing Auction Session (CAS) will **not** be validated for margin sufficiency at the order level.
- If a carried-over CTS order is **modified** during CAS, it is treated as a new CAS order and will be subject to margin sufficiency validation.
- All orders placed in CAS—including modified carried-over CTS orders—must pass margin sufficiency checks against available capital before acceptance.
- If a member's available capital is insufficient to cover the margin requirement of a CAS order, that order will be rejected.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular amends and clarifies the risk management framework introduced for CAS under NCL Circular Ref. No. 0030/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/72373, dated January 19, 2026). The key regulatory distinction introduced is the differential treatment of carried-over CTS orders versus modified or new orders during CAS with respect to margin validation.

## Compliance Requirements

- All trading members must ensure sufficient available capital to cover margin requirements for any orders placed or modified during CAS.
- Members should review their systems to correctly distinguish between unmodified carried-over CTS orders (no margin check at order level) and modified/new CAS orders (margin check required).
- Risk and operations teams should update internal controls and order management systems to comply with these rules ahead of the effective date.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** August 3, 2026 — all provisions of this circular come into force.
- **Reference Circular:** NCL/CMPT/72373, dated January 19, 2026 (CAS introduction).

## Impact Assessment

This circular has high operational impact for all members active in the equity cash segment. The differentiated margin treatment for carried-over versus modified CAS orders requires members to update their risk systems and order validation logic. Members with constrained capital buffers are particularly affected, as modified orders during CAS could be rejected if capital is insufficient. The rules are designed to prevent excessive risk accumulation during the auction window while maintaining continuity for existing CTS orders.
