## Summary

NSE has issued a circular announcing that the equity shares of Alpine Texworld Limited, which recently listed via IPO, will be transferred from the trade for trade segment (Series: BE) to the normal rolling settlement segment (Series: EQ) with effect from August 4, 2026. This follows an earlier Exchange circular (NSE/CML/75290 dated July 20, 2026) and is issued pursuant to SEBI guidelines under circular reference CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012.

## Key Points

- Symbol ALPINETEX (Alpine Texworld Limited) will move from trade-for-trade (BE) series to rolling settlement (EQ) series.
- The change takes effect from August 04, 2026.
- The move follows the standard SEBI-mandated process applied to newly listed IPO securities after an initial trade-for-trade period.
- Issued under Circular Ref. No. 1185/2026 by NSE's Listing Department.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this circular applies existing SEBI guidelines (CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012) governing the transition of newly listed securities from trade-for-trade to normal rolling settlement.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members must update their systems to reflect the change in series for ALPINETEX from BE to EQ effective August 4, 2026, and ensure trades are settled under the applicable rolling settlement rules from that date.

## Important Dates

- **July 20, 2026**: Prior Exchange circular NSE/CML/75290 referenced.
- **July 21, 2026**: Current circular issued.
- **August 4, 2026**: Effective date for transfer of ALPINETEX from trade-for-trade (BE) to rolling settlement (EQ) segment.

## Impact Assessment

The shift from trade-for-trade to rolling settlement allows intraday trading and squaring off of positions in ALPINETEX, which was previously restricted under the T2T mechanism designed to curb speculative trading in newly listed securities. This is a routine, procedural change signaling normalization of trading activity for the stock, and is generally viewed as a positive development for liquidity and trading flexibility, though it may also increase volatility as intraday speculation becomes possible.
