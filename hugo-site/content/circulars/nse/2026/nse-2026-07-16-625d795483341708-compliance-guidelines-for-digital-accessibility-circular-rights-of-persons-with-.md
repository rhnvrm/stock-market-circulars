## Summary

NSE has issued a clarificatory circular (Ref. No. 66/2026, dated July 16, 2026) on the Compliance Guidelines for the Digital Accessibility Circular, which mandates compliance with the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 and rules made thereunder by all Regulated Entities. This follows SEBI Circular No. SEBI/HO/ITD-1/ITD_VIAP/P/CIR/2025/131 dated September 25, 2025, and NSE's earlier circular ref. no. NSE/COMP/70464 dated September 26, 2025. Based on representations from Trading Members (TMs), NSE has clarified the process for accessibility audits of digital platforms in consultation with SEBI.

## Key Points

- All Digital Platforms of Trading Members must undergo an Accessibility Audit conducted by an IAAP (International Association of Accessibility Professionals)-certified accessibility professional.
- For standard applications/platforms used across multiple TMs without customisation affecting accessibility (e.g., front-office CTCL solutions, back-office access, e-KYC products, mobile/internet trading applications), TMs may coordinate with vendors to obtain the accessibility audit report/certificate.
- Vendors must provide a covering letter to the TM detailing the platform version and the IAAP-certified professional who conducted the audit, along with a copy of the Accessibility Audit Report.
- A checkpoint will be incorporated into the Standardised Terms of Reference (TOR) of System Audit for validation that the vendor-provided IAAP certificate pertains to the standard, non-customised version of the platform.
- For TMs not required to submit a System Audit report, an alternative mechanism will be prescribed subsequently.
- For digital platforms/applications unique or customised to a specific TM (including websites, mobile/web apps, back-office/KYC applications, or in-house systems), the TM must independently undertake an accessibility audit through an IAAP-certified professional.
- The circular clarifies that vendors are not regulatorily obligated to obtain or provide an Accessibility Audit Report; any such arrangement is governed by contractual terms between the vendor and TM. TMs may, at their discretion, choose not to rely on vendor-provided audits.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular does not introduce new regulatory requirements but clarifies implementation mechanics of the existing SEBI mandate on digital accessibility compliance under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016. It introduces a bifurcated compliance pathway: (1) vendor-facilitated audits for standard, non-customised platforms shared across TMs, and (2) independent audits for TM-specific/customised platforms.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must ensure all digital platforms undergo IAAP-certified accessibility audits.
- For standard/shared platforms, TMs should coordinate with vendors to obtain audit reports, covering letters, and closure status of audit findings.
- System Auditors must validate, via the Standardised TOR checkpoint, that vendor-provided IAAP certificates correspond to the unmodified standard version of the platform.
- For customised or in-house platforms, TMs must arrange independent IAAP-certified audits directly.
- TMs relying on vendor certificates should ensure vendors provide requisite documentation; TMs may opt out of relying on vendor audits at their discretion.

## Important Dates

- September 25, 2025: SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/ITD-1/ITD_VIAP/P/CIR/2025/131 issued.
- September 26, 2025: NSE circular ref. no. NSE/COMP/70464 issued.
- July 16, 2026: Current clarificatory circular (Ref. No. 66/2026) issued.
- Alternative mechanism for TMs not requiring System Audit reports to be prescribed subsequently (no specific date given).

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on all Trading Members, as it streamlines compliance by allowing reliance on vendor-conducted audits for standardised platforms, reducing duplicative audit efforts across TMs using common vendor solutions. However, TMs with customised or proprietary digital platforms bear continued independent audit obligations. The clarification reduces compliance ambiguity and cost for smaller TMs using shared vendor platforms, while reinforcing accountability through the System Audit TOR checkpoint. No direct market or stock-specific impact is expected.
