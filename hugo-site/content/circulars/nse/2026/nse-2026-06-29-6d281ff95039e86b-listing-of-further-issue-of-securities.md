## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of further issues of securities for 16 companies effective June 30, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A. The new securities arise from various corporate actions including ESOP allotments, warrant conversions, and schemes of arrangement. Trading lot sizes are also specified under Regulation 2.5.5.

## Key Points

- Securities of 16 companies admitted to dealings on NSE Capital Market segment from June 30, 2026
- Ambuja Cements: 12,993,708 equity shares (Rs. 2 face value) allotted on April 10, 2026 pursuant to Scheme of Arrangement; ISIN INE079A01024; distinctive range 2622493599–2635487306
- Bonlon Industries: 2,200,000 equity shares (Rs. 10 face value) allotted on March 30, 2026 via conversion of warrants on preferential basis; issue price Rs. 33; lock-in expiry December 31, 2027
- Clean Max Enviro Energy Solutions: 41,420 equity shares (Re. 1 face value) allotted on June 26, 2026 under ESOP; ISIN INE647U01026; no lock-in
- CMS Info Systems and 12 other companies also have further issues listed (full annexure details in the PDF)
- All securities identified by designated NSE codes; trading in specified lot sizes (market lot 1 for disclosed entries)
- Circular signed by Srishti Soni, Manager, NSE Listing Department

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is an operational notification under existing NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations (Regulation 3.1.1 for admission to dealings and Regulation 2.5.5 for lot sizes).

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use designated security codes for trading in all newly listed securities
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot sizes as per the Annexure
- No separate compliance action required from listed companies beyond the allotment already completed

## Important Dates

- **June 29, 2026**: Circular issued
- **June 30, 2026**: Effective date — all further issues admitted to dealings on NSE
- **April 10, 2026**: Allotment date for Ambuja Cements shares (Scheme of Arrangement)
- **March 30, 2026**: Allotment date for Bonlon Industries warrants conversion
- **June 26, 2026**: Allotment date for Clean Max Enviro ESOP shares
- **December 31, 2027**: Lock-in expiry for Bonlon Industries preferential allotment shares

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational circular with low individual stock impact. The addition of further shares increases the floating stock for each company marginally. Notable inclusions are large-cap names (HDFC Bank, Infosys, ICICI Lombard, Ambuja Cements) alongside mid and small caps. The Ambuja Cements allotment of ~13 million shares under a Scheme of Arrangement is the largest disclosed tranche and could have a marginal dilutive effect. Bonlon Industries shares carry a lock-in until end-2027, limiting near-term tradeable supply. ESOP allotments (Clean Max) are typically small and have negligible market impact.
