## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of eight Commercial Paper (CP) issuances — six fresh issues and two re-issues — to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective June 04, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All securities were allotted on June 03, 2026.

## Key Points

- Six fresh CP issuances admitted: Kotak Securities Limited (Sr 69), Tata Capital Limited (Sr 9), Godrej Industries Limited (Sr 20), Reliance Retail Ventures Limited, Motilal Oswal Financial Services Limited, and IIFL Finance Limited.
- Two re-issuances admitted: Kotak Securities Limited (Sr 68) and ICICI Securities Limited.
- All CPs have a face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit; issue prices range from ₹4,58,819.50 to ₹4,91,038.50, reflecting varying tenors and issuer credit profiles.
- IIFL Finance CP (INE530B14HQ0) has the longest tenor, maturing June 02, 2027; all others mature in August–September 2026.
- Reliance Retail Ventures has the largest issuance by quantity: 25,000 units.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard operational notification under existing NSE Debt Market Trading Regulations (Regulation 3.1.1).

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance obligations for members. Exchange members dealing in debt securities should update their systems with the designated ISIN codes and security codes listed in the Annexure.

## Impact Assessment

Routine debt market operations with no material impact on equity markets. Institutional and high-net-worth investors participating in the NSE debt segment can now trade these CPs with their assigned security codes. The aggregate issuance represents normal short-term funding activity by established financial and corporate entities. IIFL Finance's longer-dated CP (12-month tenor) at a notably lower issue price (₹4,58,819.50) may attract attention from investors seeking duration exposure in the debt segment.
