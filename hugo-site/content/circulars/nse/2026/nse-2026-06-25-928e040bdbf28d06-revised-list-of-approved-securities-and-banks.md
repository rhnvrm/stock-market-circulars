## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a revised list of approved securities and empaneled banks applicable for the month of **July 2026**. This circular supersedes the previous circular no. 0183/2026 (NCL/CMPT/74376) dated May 22, 2026. The update covers equity shares, ETFs, GOI securities/T-Bills, Sovereign Gold Bonds, open-ended mutual funds, and corporate bonds eligible as collateral, along with market-wide limits, member-specific limits, and applicable haircut rates.

## Key Points

- Effective month: **July 2026**
- Supersedes Circular No. 0183/2026 dated May 22, 2026
- Seven annexures cover all collateral categories: equity shares, ETFs (non-cash and cash components), G-Sec/T-Bills, open-ended mutual funds, corporate bonds, and empaneled banks
- Haircut rates are VAR-based for equity shares in Annexure 1
- Value-based prudential norm applies: exclusion of a pledged security must not result in margin shortfall exceeding 25% at a Clearing Member level
- G-Sec/T-Bills carry a **shut period of two days**; members must release them at least two days before maturity
- Member-banks must not reckon G-Sec provided as collateral for SLR purposes or use them for trading

## Regulatory Changes

- Updated market-wide applicable limits and member-security-specific limits for all approved securities
- Revised haircut rates applicable to each security category for July 2026
- Refreshed list of banks empaneled for issuance of Bank Guarantees (BGs) and Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDRs)

## Compliance Requirements

- **All Members/Custodians/PCMs**: Apply the updated approved security lists and haircut rates when pledging collateral from July 2026 onwards
- **Members holding G-Sec/T-Bills**: Release securities at least **two days before maturity** to account for the two-day shut period
- **Member-banks**: G-Sec submitted as collateral must not be used for SLR computation or trading purposes
- Ensure pledged securities do not cause margin shortfall exceeding 25% at Clearing Member level upon exclusion

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: June 25, 2026
- **Effective From**: July 2026
- **Previous Circular**: NCL/CMPT/74376 dated May 22, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine monthly update impacting all capital market segment participants who use securities as non-cash or cash components of liquid assets. Members must review the updated annexures to ensure their collateral portfolios remain compliant with revised limits and haircut rates. The inclusion or exclusion of specific securities and changes in market-wide/member-specific limits may require rebalancing of collateral baskets. Empaneled bank list changes (Annexure 7) could affect BG/FDR acceptance. No extraordinary structural changes are indicated; operational impact is standard for a monthly collateral revision.
