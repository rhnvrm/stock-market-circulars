## Summary

NSE has revised the networth criteria for Market Makers participating in the SME Emerge platform, following consultation with ISF & Exchanges and guidance from SEBI. A tiered networth structure now governs the maximum number of SME companies a Market Maker may serve, ranging from INR 3 crores for up to 5 companies to INR 25 crores or more for unlimited participation. Existing market makers have a 6-month transition period ending November 21, 2026.

## Key Points

- Minimum networth of INR 3 crores required to act as Market Maker for up to 5 SME companies.
- For every additional INR 1 crore of networth, market making in 5 more SME companies is permitted.
- Market Makers with networth of INR 25 crores or above face no cap on the number of SME companies.
- A Market Maker cannot cover more than 15% of total SME companies in which market making is in progress, or 50 SME companies, whichever is higher (per exchange).
- No more than 5 Market Makers are permitted for any single SME company, per SEBI Master Circular SEBI/HO/MRD-PoD2/CIR/P/2024/00181 dated December 30, 2024.
- Compliance with networth criteria must be verified at the time of appointment by the Book Running Lead Manager (BRLM) and the SME Company.

## Regulatory Changes

The revised networth grid supersedes earlier circulars NSE/MSD/64530 (October 14, 2024) and NSE/MSD/58186 (August 31, 2023). The updated tiered structure is:

## Compliance Requirements

- **New registrations**: Revised networth criteria apply immediately from May 22, 2026.
- **Existing market makers**: May continue under existing networth criteria for current scrips for up to 6 months; must comply with revised criteria by November 21, 2026.
- A Market Maker failing to meet networth criteria must stop taking on any new SME companies but may continue obligations for existing ones.
- NSE will notify BRLMs and SME Companies if a Market Maker fails to meet the networth criteria, enabling them to reconsider market-making agreements.
- Requests for user ID activation/deactivation for market making must follow the procedure in NSE consolidated circular NSE/CMTR/73927 dated April 28, 2026.

## Important Dates

- **May 22, 2026**: Revised networth criteria effective immediately for new SME market maker registrations.
- **November 21, 2026**: Revised networth criteria become applicable to all existing SME market makers.

## Impact Assessment

This circular primarily impacts member brokers registered or seeking registration as Market Makers on NSE's SME Emerge platform. The tiered networth structure incentivises well-capitalised brokers and constrains smaller brokers from overextending across too many SME listings. The 6-month grace period for existing market makers provides adequate runway for compliance without disrupting ongoing market-making obligations. The cap of 5 Market Makers per SME company and the 15%/50-company per-exchange limit aim to ensure adequate competition and prevent market-making concentration risks in the SME segment.
