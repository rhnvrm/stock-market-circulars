## Summary

NSE's Inspection Department has issued a reminder to all trading members to submit their System Audit Report (SAR) for the financial year ending March 31, 2026. Submission must be completed electronically through the ENIT Member Portal on or before June 30, 2026. Failure to comply may result in penalty or disciplinary action as outlined in earlier NSE circulars.

## Key Points

- Submission of the System Audit Report is mandatory for all trading members meeting the applicability criteria specified in Annexure A of circular NSE/INSP/73850 dated April 22, 2026.
- The deadline for submission is June 30, 2026.
- Submission must be made electronically via the ENIT Member Portal.
- Penalties and disciplinary actions for non-submission are governed by NSE circular NSE/INSP/73792 dated April 17, 2026.
- Regional offices and a central help desk are available for clarifications.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This circular is a reminder reiterating obligations established under:
- NSE/INSP/73850 dated April 22, 2026 (original SAR submission requirement and applicability criteria)
- NSE/INSP/73792 dated April 17, 2026 (penalty and disciplinary action framework)

## Compliance Requirements

- All trading members falling under the applicability criteria in Annexure A of circular NSE/INSP/73850 must submit their System Audit Report for the period ended March 31, 2026.
- Submission must be made through the ENIT Member Portal before the due date.
- Trading members should review the applicability criteria to confirm whether they are required to submit.

## Important Dates

- **Audit period:** April 1, 2025 – March 31, 2026
- **Submission deadline:** June 30, 2026
- **Reference circular (original requirement):** NSE/INSP/73850 dated April 22, 2026
- **Reference circular (penalty framework):** NSE/INSP/73792 dated April 17, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular affects all trading members required to conduct a system audit under NSE's framework. The impact is operational — members must ensure their audit process is complete and reports are filed via the ENIT portal before month-end. Late or non-submission risks financial penalties and disciplinary proceedings. No market-wide or investor-facing impact is expected from this circular.
