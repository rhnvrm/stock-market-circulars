## Summary

NSE's Listing Department (Circular Ref. No. 0710/2026, Download Ref No. NSE/CML/73965) notifies members that privately placed securities from multiple issuers have been admitted to dealings on the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange with effect from April 29, 2026. The admission is in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Key Points

- Eight commercial papers (CPs) and one other debt security admitted to the NSE Debt Market Segment effective April 29, 2026.
- Seven CPs are fresh issues; one CP (Aditya Birla Capital Limited - ABFL) is a re-issue.
- All CPs carry a face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit; the debt security (Aseem Infrastructure Finance) carries a face value of ₹1,00,000 per unit.
- Allotment dates are April 27–29, 2026; redemption dates range from June 2026 to March 2027.
- Security codes (ISINs) are specified in the Annexure and are effective immediately.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is a standard listing notification issued under the existing framework of Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must update their systems with the new security codes (ISINs) as specified in the Annexure.
- Trading in these instruments on the Debt Market Segment is permitted from April 29, 2026 onwards.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing circular with low market impact. The admitted securities are short-to-medium term debt instruments (commercial papers maturing in 2–11 months) and one longer-dated debt security maturing in June 2029. Issuers include well-known corporates such as Hindustan Petroleum Corporation, Aditya Birla Capital, Cholamandalam Investment and Finance, TVS Credit Services, and IndoStar Capital Finance. The aggregate issuance represents normal short-term funding activity by these entities and does not signal any material change in their financial standing. Debt market participants and institutional investors trading on the NSE Debt Market Segment should take note of the new ISINs for order entry and settlement purposes.
