## Summary

NSE has announced the exclusion of Futures and Options contracts for two securities — Exide Industries Limited (EXIDEIND) and Nuvama Wealth Management Limited (NUVAMA) — pursuant to SEBI's eligibility criteria circular dated August 30, 2024. No new expiry month contracts will be introduced for these securities once the existing contract months expire.

## Key Points

- Contracts for new expiry months in EXIDEIND and NUVAMA will **not** be issued after existing contract months expire.
- Existing unexpired contracts for **May 2026, June 2026, and July 2026** will continue to be available for trading until their respective expiry dates.
- New strikes will also be introduced within the existing contract months as needed.
- With effect from **July 29, 2026**, no F&O contracts will be available for trading in these two securities.

## Regulatory Changes

This action is taken in reference to SEBI Circular Ref. No. SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-2/P/CIR/2024/116 dated August 30, 2024, which established revised eligibility criteria for stocks in the F&O segment. EXIDEIND and NUVAMA no longer meet those criteria, triggering their exclusion from the F&O segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must note that no fresh positions can be opened in new expiry months for EXIDEIND and NUVAMA.
- All outstanding positions in May 2026, June 2026, and July 2026 contracts must be closed or allowed to expire by their respective expiry dates.
- No rollover into subsequent months will be possible after the July 2026 expiry.

## Important Dates

- **May 29, 2026**: May 2026 contracts expire.
- **June 26, 2026**: June 2026 contracts expire.
- **July 29, 2026**: July 2026 contracts expire — last trading day for F&O contracts on EXIDEIND and NUVAMA; no contracts available thereafter.

## Impact Assessment

Traders and investors with open positions in EXIDEIND or NUVAMA F&O contracts must plan an orderly exit strategy before July 29, 2026. The removal eliminates the ability to hedge or speculate using derivatives on these stocks, which may increase cash market volatility around expiry dates. Market makers and arbitrageurs active in these counters will also need to unwind positions. Institutional participants using these contracts for portfolio hedging should seek alternative instruments.
