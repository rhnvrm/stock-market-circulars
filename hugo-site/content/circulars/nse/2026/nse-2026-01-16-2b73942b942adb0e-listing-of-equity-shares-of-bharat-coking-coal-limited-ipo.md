## Summary

Bharat Coking Coal Limited has been listed on NSE following its Initial Public Offering (IPO). The shareholding pattern filed under Regulation 31(1)(a) as of January 15, 2026 shows total equity shares of 4,657,000,000 (465.7 crore shares) held by 321,202 shareholders. The company has a clean capital structure with no partly paid shares, convertible securities, warrants, ESOPs, depository receipts, locked-in shares, pledged shares, or differential voting rights.

## Key Points

- Total issued equity shares: 4,657,000,000 (465.7 crore)
- Total number of shareholders: 321,202
- Promoter & Promoter Group holding: 4,191,300,000 shares (90.00%)
- Public shareholding: 465,700,000 shares (10.00%)
- Public shareholders: 321,201 investors
- All shares are fully paid-up equity shares
- Entire shareholding held in dematerialized form
- No encumbrances on promoter shares (no pledges, NDU, or other charges)

## Regulatory Changes

Not applicable - this is a new listing disclosure.

## Compliance Requirements

- Shareholding pattern filed under Regulation 31(1)(a) of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015
- Company has declared no partly paid shares, convertible securities, warrants, ESOPs, depository receipts, locked-in shares, pledged shares, or shares with differential voting rights
- Post-issue shareholding pattern disclosed as per prescribed format

## Important Dates

- Post-issue shareholding pattern date: January 15, 2026
- Circular date: January 16, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The listing of Bharat Coking Coal Limited adds a significant public sector coal company to the stock exchange with a substantial market capitalization. With 90% promoter holding and 10% public float, the stock provides retail and institutional investors access to the coking coal sector. The large public shareholding of 465.7 crore shares ensures reasonable liquidity for trading. The absence of any encumbrances, convertible securities, or complex capital structures indicates a straightforward investment opportunity in the government-owned coal mining sector.
