## Summary

NSE has announced, via Circular Ref. No. 0960/2026 (Download Ref No: NSE/CML/74703) dated June 15, 2026, that the equity shares of Vedanta Aluminium Metal Limited (Symbol: VAML) will be transferred from the Trade-for-Trade segment (Series: BE) to the Rolling segment (Series: EQ) with effect from June 30, 2026. This follows the earlier Exchange Circular NSE/CML/74659 dated June 11, 2026, and is in pursuance of SEBI guidelines under circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012.

## Key Points

- Vedanta Aluminium Metal Limited (Symbol: VAML) is being moved from the Trade-for-Trade (BE series) to the Rolling segment (EQ series).
- The effective date for the segment transfer is June 30, 2026.
- This circular follows the previous NSE circular NSE/CML/74659 dated June 11, 2026.
- The action is taken pursuant to SEBI circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012.

## Regulatory Changes

Pursuant to SEBI guidelines (circular CIR/MRD/DP/02/2012 dated January 20, 2012), the securities of VAML are being reclassified from the compulsory trade-for-trade (T+1 gross settlement) segment to the normal rolling settlement segment, easing trading conditions for the stock.

## Compliance Requirements

- All trading members are advised to take note of the segment change for VAML effective June 30, 2026.
- Members should update their systems and inform clients accordingly regarding the change in trading series from BE to EQ.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 15, 2026
- **Preceding Circular:** NSE/CML/74659 dated June 11, 2026
- **Effective Date of Transfer:** June 30, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The transfer of VAML from the trade-for-trade (BE) segment to the rolling (EQ) segment is a positive development for market participants. In the trade-for-trade segment, trades must result in delivery and cannot be netted off, limiting intraday and speculative trading. The move to the EQ (rolling) segment allows normal netting of buy and sell positions, enabling intraday trading and improving liquidity for the stock. This typically signals reduced surveillance concerns for the scrip.
