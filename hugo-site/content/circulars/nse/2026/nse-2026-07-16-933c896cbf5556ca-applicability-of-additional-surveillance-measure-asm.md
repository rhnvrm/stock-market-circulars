## Summary

NSE has issued a circular under its surveillance framework shortlisting B & A Limited (Symbol: BNALTD, ISIN: INE489D01011) for inclusion in the Long-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (Long Term - ASM) Stage I, effective July 17, 2026. As a result, a 100% margin will apply on all open and new positions in the security from July 21, 2026. No securities were shortlisted for Stage IV (Trade-for-Trade shift), nor for movement between existing ASM stages (I to II, II to III, I to IV, or II to IV) in this circular.

## Key Points

- B & A Limited (BNALTD) has been shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Framework Stage I effective July 17, 2026.
- No securities met criteria for Stage IV inclusion (which would trigger a shift from Rolling Settlement/EQ series to Trade-for-Trade/BE series) in this cycle.
- No securities were identified for movement between ASM stages (I→II, II→III, I→IV, II→IV) in this circular.
- ASM classification is purely a surveillance measure and is not to be construed as an adverse action against the company.
- The ASM framework operates in conjunction with other prevailing exchange surveillance measures.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued under NSE's existing ASM framework (referencing prior circulars NSE/SURV/39265, 45111, 46557, 48506, 52090, 63362 and 64066) and does not introduce new rules, but applies existing Long-Term ASM criteria to newly qualifying securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must apply a 100% margin requirement on all open positions in BNALTD as of July 20, 2026, and on new positions created from July 21, 2026 onwards.
- Members should monitor the security for any further stage escalation in future circulars.

## Important Dates

- July 17, 2026: BNALTD shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Stage I.
- July 20, 2026: Reference date for existing open positions subject to margin.
- July 21, 2026: 100% margin becomes effective on all open and new positions.

## Impact Assessment

The inclusion under Long-Term ASM Stage I significantly raises the cost of trading BNALTD due to the 100% margin requirement, likely reducing speculative activity and leverage in the stock. The impact is confined to this specific security and its market participants rather than the broader market.
