## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of a further issue of units of NDR INVIT Trust (Symbol: NDRINVIT) on the Capital Market segment, effective June 01, 2026. A total of 62,026,829 units allotted on preferential basis at an issue price of Rs. 135.80 per unit have been admitted to dealings under Series IV (ISIN: INE0Q7Q23015).

## Key Points

- **Symbol:** NDRINVIT | **Series:** IV | **ISIN:** INE0Q7Q23015
- **Issue Price:** Rs. 135.80 per unit
- **Date of Allotment:** May 26, 2026
- **Total Units Listed:** 62,026,829
- **Distinctive Number Range:** 396012215 to 458039043
- **Pari Passu:** Yes — new units rank equally with existing units
- Units were allotted on a **Preferential Issue** basis
- Lock-in periods apply to all allotted units as per Annexure I

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular is issued in pursuance of:
- Regulation 3.1.1 of NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A — admission of securities to dealings
- Regulation 2.5.5 of NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A — lot sizes for trading

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use designated security codes for NDRINVIT (Series IV) for all trades on the system
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot sizes as per Annexure
- Lock-in restrictions on the allotted units must be observed until the respective expiry dates listed in Annexure I

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment:** May 26, 2026
- **Effective Date for Trading:** June 01, 2026
- **Lock-in Expiry:** May 30, 2027 or May 31, 2027 (varies by tranche as per Annexure I)

Multiple smaller tranches (ranging from 75,000 to 725,000 units each) are locked in until either May 30 or May 31, 2027.

## Impact Assessment

This is a standard further issue listing for NDR INVIT Trust, an Infrastructure Investment Trust. The addition of over 62 million units at Rs. 135.80 expands the float available for trading from June 01, 2026. However, the lock-in provisions on all allotted units until May 2027 limit near-term selling pressure from preferential allottees. Market impact is expected to be moderate, primarily affecting existing unitholders through dilution and potentially influencing unit price discovery.
