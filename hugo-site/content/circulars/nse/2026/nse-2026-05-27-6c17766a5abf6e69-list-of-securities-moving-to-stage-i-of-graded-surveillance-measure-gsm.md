## Summary

NSE has moved Winsome Yarns Limited (Symbol: WINSOME, ISIN: INE784B01035) to Stage I of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework, effective May 29, 2026. Under Stage I, trading will be subject to a 100% applicable margin rate and a price band of 5% or lower.

## Key Points

- Winsome Yarns Limited (WINSOME) placed under Stage I of GSM effective May 29, 2026
- Applicable margin rate of 100% will be imposed on trades in this security
- Price band restricted to 5% or lower as applicable
- Members advised to exercise adequate caution while trading in the security
- Queries to be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued in continuation of earlier NSE surveillance circulars (NSE/SURV/34262, NSE/SURV/38389, NSE/SURV/42790, NSE/SURV/59425, NSE/SURV/64066, and NSE/SURV/73167) establishing and updating the GSM framework. The GSM framework comprises four progressive stages with increasingly stringent trading restrictions.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must apply a 100% margin rate for all trades in WINSOME
- Price band of 5% or lower must be enforced as applicable
- Members are required to take adequate precaution while trading in the above securities
- Members should inform clients of the changed trading conditions for WINSOME

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** May 27, 2026
- **Effective Date:** May 29, 2026 (Stage I GSM restrictions come into force)

## Impact Assessment

The placement of Winsome Yarns Limited under Stage I GSM significantly restricts trading activity in the stock. The 100% margin requirement means buyers must fund the full trade value upfront, substantially reducing leverage and likely suppressing trading volumes. The 5% price band limits daily price movement. Investors and traders holding or intending to trade WINSOME should be aware of these constraints. If the security progresses to higher GSM stages, restrictions intensify further — Stage II adds Trade-for-Trade settlement and a 50% Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD), while Stages III and IV restrict trading to once a week with 100% ASD requirements.
