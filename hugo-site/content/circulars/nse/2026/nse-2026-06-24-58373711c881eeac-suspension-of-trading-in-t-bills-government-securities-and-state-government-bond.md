## Summary

NSE has issued a circular under Regulation 3.1.2 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A, notifying the suspension of trading in 18 debt securities — comprising 91-day, 182-day, and 364-day Treasury Bills, one Government Security (G-Sec), and three State Government Bonds (SDLs) — effective from their respective maturity dates between June 30, 2026 and July 28, 2026.

## Key Points

- 18 securities across TB (T-Bill), GS (Government Security), and SG (State Government Bond) series are being suspended from trading on maturity.
- Suspensions are staggered from June 30, 2026 through July 28, 2026.
- The circular is issued under Regulation 3.1.2 of NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.
- Signed by Ruchi Jaiswal, Manager, NSE Listing Department (Ref No: NSE/CML/74841, Circular Ref No: 1006/2026).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notice issued pursuant to existing NSE trading regulations for suspending securities that have reached maturity.

## Compliance Requirements

All NSE members are notified to cease trading in the listed securities from their respective suspension dates. No specific action is required beyond ensuring no trades are placed in these securities on or after the stated suspension dates.

## Impact Assessment

Impact is minimal and confined to the debt market segment. These are routine suspensions of short-term government securities (T-Bills) and state/central government bonds upon reaching their maturity dates. Equity markets are unaffected. Institutional investors and primary dealers holding these instruments would have already factored in the maturity dates for redemption planning. No residual market risk arises from these suspensions.
