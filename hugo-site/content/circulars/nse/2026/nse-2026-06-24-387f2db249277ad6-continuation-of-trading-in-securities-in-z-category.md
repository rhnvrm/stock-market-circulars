## Summary

NSE has identified Rajvir Industries Limited as non-compliant under Regulation 27(2) of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015 for failure to submit Corporate Governance Reports for two consecutive quarters ending December 31, 2025 and March 31, 2026. Since the company's securities are already trading in Z Category pursuant to prior exchange circulars, the Z Category status will continue with effect from July 06, 2026, and trades will be settled on a Trade for Trade basis.

## Key Points

- Rajvir Industries Limited is non-compliant under SEBI LODR Regulation 27(2) (Corporate Governance Report submission) for Q3 FY2026 (December 31, 2025) and Q4 FY2026 (March 31, 2026)
- The company was already in Z Category under previous circular NSE/CML/49924
- Z Category (BZ Series) trading continuation is effective from July 06, 2026
- All trades in Z Category securities will continue to be settled on Trade for Trade Basis
- Reference SEBI Master Circular No. HO/49/14/14(7)2025-CFD-POD2/I/3762/2026, originally issued July 11, 2023 and last updated January 30, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular reaffirms the application of the existing Z Category framework under the SEBI Master Circular to Rajvir Industries Limited for continued non-compliance with corporate governance reporting requirements.

## Compliance Requirements

- Rajvir Industries Limited must submit outstanding Corporate Governance Reports under Regulation 27(2) of SEBI LODR Regulations, 2015 to exit Z Category
- Members of the Exchange are informed to ensure all trades in the company's securities are conducted under Trade for Trade settlement rules

## Important Dates

- **Non-compliance periods:** Quarter ending December 31, 2025 and quarter ending March 31, 2026
- **Z Category continuation effective date:** July 06, 2026
- **Circular date:** June 24, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to existing holders and traders of Rajvir Industries Limited securities. Since the company is already in Z Category, no change in trading mode occurs for current participants. However, the formal continuation order from July 06, 2026 reaffirms that intraday trading remains prohibited and all transactions must be settled on a delivery basis (Trade for Trade), reducing liquidity. Investors should be aware of heightened settlement risk and the company's persistent corporate governance non-compliance.
