## Summary

NSE circular NSE/CML/73654 (Ref: 0593/2026) dated April 08, 2026 notifies the admission of privately placed securities to the Debt Market Segment under Regulation 3.1.1 of NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. A total of ten Commercial Paper instruments across five issuers were admitted to dealings effective April 08, 2026, with allotment dates of April 07, 2026.

## Key Points

- Effective date for dealings: April 08, 2026
- All listed instruments are Commercial Papers (CP) under Fresh Issue category
- Five issuers: Kotak Securities Limited, Tata Steel Limited, IIFL Capital Services Limited, Aditya Birla Capital Limited (3 instruments), and Godrej Properties Limited (4 instruments)
- Face value for all CPs: ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Allotment date for all instruments: April 07, 2026
- Circular also references Re-Issue instruments (content truncated in source)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. Listing is pursuant to existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for members. Members are informed of the new security codes (as per Annexure) for trading purposes on the Debt Market Segment.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational listing circular with no material market impact. The admission of these CPs provides short-term funding instruments for five large corporates on NSE's Debt Market Segment. Godrej Properties is the most active issuer with four CP tranches totalling ₹350 crore face value. Aditya Birla Capital follows with three tranches. All instruments carry maturities ranging from one month to approximately four months, consistent with typical commercial paper tenors. Debt market participants and fixed-income traders should update their instrument databases with the new ISINs and security codes.
