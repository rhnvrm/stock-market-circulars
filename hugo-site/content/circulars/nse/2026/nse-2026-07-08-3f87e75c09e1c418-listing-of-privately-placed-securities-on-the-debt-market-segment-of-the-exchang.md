## Summary

NSE has notified, under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations, that a set of privately placed securities have been admitted to dealings on the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange effective July 08, 2026. The listed instruments comprise fresh commercial paper (CP) issuances from five issuers and one non-convertible debenture (NCD) issuance from Tata Capital Limited.

## Key Points

- Commercial papers listed: ICICI Securities Limited (ISIN INE763G14L86), IIFL Finance Limited (two series, ISINs INE530B14HZ1 and INE530B14IA2), Alembic Pharmaceuticals Limited (ISIN INE901L14CM2), Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited (ISIN INE257A14AI4), and Godrej Agrovet Limited (ISIN INE850D14VP1).
- Debenture listed: Tata Capital Limited's 7.88% Series D debentures due 2031 (ISIN INE976I07DG4), with a face/paid-up value of ₹100,000 and multiple issue prices ranging from ₹100,000 to ₹100,050 across different allotment tranches, totaling 275,000 securities.
- All commercial papers carry a face value of ₹500,000 per unit, issued at a discount to face value reflecting the applicable yield.
- Designated security codes for each instrument are specified in the circular's Annexure.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is a routine notification under existing NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations for admitting privately placed securities to trading.

## Compliance Requirements

No action required from market participants beyond awareness of the new security codes and ISINs now available for trading on the Debt Market Segment.

## Important Dates

- Date of Allotment: July 07, 2026 (ICICI Securities CP, IIFL Finance CPs, Alembic Pharmaceuticals CP, Tata Capital NCD) and July 08, 2026 (BHEL CP, Godrej Agrovet CP)
- Effective Date of Listing: July 08, 2026
- Redemption Dates: range from September 23, 2026 to October 26, 2026 for the commercial papers; July 07, 2031 for the Tata Capital debentures

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market-wide impact as it is a routine administrative listing of privately placed debt instruments. It is primarily relevant to institutional debt market participants, custodians, and clearing members who need to recognize the new ISINs and security codes for settlement and trading purposes on NSE's Debt Market Segment.
