## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed securities to dealings on the Debt Market Segment effective May 29, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The listing covers commercial papers issued by eight companies allotted on May 27–29, 2026, with redemption dates ranging from June 2026 to May 2027.

## Key Points

- Nine commercial paper (CP) instruments from eight issuers admitted to the NSE Debt Market Segment
- All securities are privately placed and issued at a discount to face value of ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Allotment dates: May 27, 2026 (most) and May 29, 2026 (Kotak Securities KSL CP 28/08/26 Sr 59)
- Redemption tenors range from approximately 1 month (EXIM CP 24/06/26) to 12 months (IIFL Capital Services ISL CP 26/05/27)
- Total quantity across all instruments: ~1,24,000 units

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations confirming admission of privately placed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Designated security codes assigned as per Annexure
- Members to note new ISINs and security codes for trading on the Debt Market Segment

## Impact Assessment

Routine operational listing notice with minimal market impact. Commercial papers from established financial institutions (EXIM Bank, L&T Finance, Kotak Securities, IIFL, Axis Securities) are being made tradeable on the NSE debt segment. The largest issuance is EXIM Bank's 54,500 units at ₹4,97,614 issue price, representing approximately ₹271 crore. These are short-to-medium term money market instruments with no impact on equity markets.
