## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has published the settlement calendar for the Offer for Sale (OFS) of shares of Central Bank of India. This circular references the earlier circular NCL/CMPT/73996 dated April 30, 2026 and clarifies that OFS settlements will follow existing rules for secondary market transactions.

## Key Points

- Two OFS order dates are covered: May 22, 2026 (non-retail / IS category) and May 25, 2026 (retail RS and non-retail IS categories)
- H-type settlements carry 100% margin; T-type settlements carry 0% margin
- Settlement dates are May 25, 2026 (for May 22 orders) and May 26, 2026 (for May 25 orders)
- Custodial confirmation deadline for non-retail IS category on May 22 is 3:30 PM; on May 25 the deadline is 3:30 PM same day
- T-type (non-retail) custodial confirmation is required by 7:30 AM on the settlement date
- STT and stamp duty will be collected on the respective settlement day

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. The circular reaffirms that OFS settlement follows existing secondary market transaction rules as per circular NCL/CMPT/73996 (April 30, 2026).

## Compliance Requirements

- Members, custodians, and PCMs must adhere to the published settlement calendar and confirmation timelines
- Funds Pay-in must be completed by T+1 day at 11:00 AM
- Securities and Funds Pay-out will occur by T+1 day at 1:30 PM
- Applicable STT and stamp duty must be settled on the respective settlement day
- For queries, contact the help desk at 1800 266 0050 (Option 2) or ofs_ncl@nsccl.co.in

## Impact Assessment

This circular is primarily operational, providing scheduling details for members, custodians, and PCMs participating in the Central Bank of India OFS. The two-day OFS window (May 22 for non-retail, May 25 for retail and non-retail) with T+1 settlements on May 25 and May 26 is consistent with standard OFS procedures. The 100% margin requirement for H-type settlements ensures full collateral backing for higher-risk participants, while the 0% margin for T-type reflects custodian-backed institutional trades. No disruption to regular secondary market trading is expected.
