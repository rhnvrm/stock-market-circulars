## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of privately placed commercial papers (CPs) from six issuers to dealings on the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange, effective July 24, 2026, under Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Key Points

- Trust Investment Advisors Private Limited: TIAPL CP 23/09/26, ISIN INE723X14NW1, 600 units, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,92,677.5, allotted 23-Jul-2026, redemption 23-Sep-2026
- ICICI Securities Limited: ISEC CP 24/12/26, ISIN INE763G14M28, 1,000 units, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,84,762.5, allotted 23-Jul-2026, redemption 24-Dec-2026
- CEAT Limited: CL CP 09/10/26, ISIN INE482A14GV8, 1,000 units, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,92,838, allotted 23-Jul-2026, redemption 09-Oct-2026
- CEAT Limited: CL CP 13/10/26, ISIN INE482A14GX4, 1,000 units, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,92,237, allotted 23-Jul-2026, redemption 13-Oct-2026
- Astec LifeSciences Limited: ALSL CP 20/07/27 Sr 199, ISIN INE563J14DR1, 500 units, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,62,183.5, allotted 23-Jul-2026, redemption 20-Jul-2027
- Tata Communications Limited: TCM CP 19/01/27, ISIN INE151A14321, 15,000 units, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,82,855.5, allotted 23-Jul-2026, redemption 19-Jan-2027
- Hdfc Securities Limited: HSL CP 23/10/2026, ISIN INE700G14VB1, 500 units, face value ₹5,00,000, issue price ₹4,90,954.5, allotted 24-Jul-2026, redemption 23-Oct-2026

## Regulatory Changes

None; this is a routine notification under existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations for admission of privately placed securities.

## Compliance Requirements

No action required from market participants beyond awareness of the new security codes for trading/settlement purposes on the Debt Market Segment.

## Important Dates

- Effective/listing date: July 24, 2026
- Various redemption dates ranging from September 2026 to July 2027 as detailed above

## Impact Assessment

This is an administrative listing notification for privately placed commercial papers on the debt segment. It has no direct impact on equity markets, retail investors, or broader trading operations, and is relevant primarily to debt market participants and institutional investors tracking these specific issuances.
