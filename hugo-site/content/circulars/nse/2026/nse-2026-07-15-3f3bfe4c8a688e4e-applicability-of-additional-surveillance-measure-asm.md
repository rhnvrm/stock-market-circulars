## Summary

NSE has issued a circular shortlisting Elitecon International Limited (Symbol: ELITECON, ISIN: INE669R01026) under the Long-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) Framework - Stage I, effective July 16, 2026. No securities were shortlisted for Stage IV or for movement between stages (II, III, IV) in this cycle - all such categories are listed as 'Nil'.

## Key Points

- Elitecon International Limited (ELITECON) has been shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Stage I effective July 16, 2026.
- No securities were newly shortlisted under Stage IV (Trade-for-Trade shift criteria).
- No securities are moving from Stage I to Stage II, Stage II to Stage III, Stage I to Stage IV, or Stage II to Stage IV in this cycle.
- This is a continuation of the ASM framework established through prior circulars (NSE/SURV/39265, 45111, 46557, 48506, 52090, 63362, 64066).
- ASM classification is purely a market surveillance measure and does not represent an adverse view on the company.

## Regulatory Changes

No changes to the ASM framework itself; this circular applies existing Long-Term ASM criteria to newly identified securities per the periodic review process.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must apply 100% margin on all open and new positions in ELITECON from the effective date.
- Members must ensure client-level compliance with the elevated margin requirement and monitor for any further surveillance actions.
- No Trade-for-Trade (Series: BE) shift is applicable in this cycle since no securities qualified under Criteria VII (Stage IV).

## Important Dates

- July 16, 2026: ELITECON added to Long-Term ASM Stage I.
- July 17, 2026: Reference date for open positions subject to margin.
- July 20, 2026: 100% margin becomes applicable on all open positions as of July 17, 2026, and on new positions created from this date onward.

## Impact Assessment

The 100% margin requirement significantly increases the cost of carrying or creating positions in ELITECON, likely reducing trading volumes and liquidity in the stock. The impact is confined to this single security and its market participants; there is no broader market-wide effect since no other stocks moved stages or were added to Stage IV in this circular.
