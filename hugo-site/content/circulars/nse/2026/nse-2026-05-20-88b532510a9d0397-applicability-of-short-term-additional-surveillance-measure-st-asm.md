## Summary

NSE has issued a circular under the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework, effective May 21, 2026. Seven securities have been newly shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage-I, while eleven securities have been excluded from the ASM framework. No securities are being moved between Stage-I and Stage-II in this update.

## Key Points

- 7 securities added to ST-ASM Stage-I effective May 21, 2026
- No securities added to ST-ASM Stage-II
- No inter-stage movements (Stage-I ↔ Stage-II)
- 11 securities excluded from the ASM framework effective May 21, 2026
- Consolidated ASM list continues to include multiple securities at Stage-I

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants (brokers, traders, investors) must note enhanced surveillance conditions for newly added ST-ASM Stage-I securities from May 21, 2026
- Securities under ASM Stage-I are subject to additional margin requirements and trade-for-trade settlement
- Members must update their risk management systems to reflect the revised ASM list
- Clients holding or intending to trade in newly added securities should be informed of the changed surveillance status

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** May 21, 2026 — all changes (additions and exclusions) come into force
- **Circular Date:** May 20, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**For newly added ST-ASM Stage-I securities (DSSL, EMBDL, FCL, IEML, JAINREC, LAMOSAIC, SPEB):** Traders will face higher margin requirements and trade-for-trade (T+1/T+0) settlement obligations, limiting leverage and potentially reducing liquidity. These stocks may see increased volatility and price pressure due to restricted participation.

**For excluded securities (ADDICTIVE, AEQUS, FRONTSP, KDL, PARAMOUNT, PURPLEUTED, RAIN, SONUINFRA, STLNETWORK, SYNOPTICS, UTLSOLAR):** Normal trading conditions are restored, which may improve liquidity and reduce margin burden for participants. RAIN (Rain Industries) is the largest-cap stock being removed, which may see a positive market reaction.

**Broader market impact:** The net addition of 7 securities to surveillance versus 11 exclusions indicates a modest net relaxation in the overall ASM scope for this cycle.
