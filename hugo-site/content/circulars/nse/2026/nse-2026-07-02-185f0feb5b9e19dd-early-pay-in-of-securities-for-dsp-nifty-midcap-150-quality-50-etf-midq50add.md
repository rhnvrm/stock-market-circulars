## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a circular referencing its consolidated circular on early pay-in of securities for margin exemption, advising members, custodians, and PCMs on the process for early pay-in (EPI) of DSP Nifty Midcap 150 Quality 50 ETF (MIDQ50ADD) units. This is on account of a face value split (sub-division) of the ETF from Rs 10 per share to Re 1 per share, with both record date and ex-date falling on July 3, 2026.

## Key Points

- Security: DSP Nifty Midcap 150 Quality 50 ETF, Symbol: MIDQ50ADD
- Existing/Old ISIN: INF740KA1QL0
- Corporate action: Face Value Split (Sub-Division) from Rs 10/- per share to Re 1/- per share
- Members doing EPI must use the existing/old ISIN without adjusting for the conversion ratio on the trade date
- Example: For a sale quantity of 10 shares (old face value), early pay-in should reflect 1 share equivalent in the old ISIN per the split ratio described
- Custodians providing early pay-in through pool accounts must upload client-wise early pay-in allocation details based on actual traded quantity
- In case of early pay-in via block mechanism, client-wise early pay-in allocation file should not be uploaded, per NCL circular NCL/CMPT/53386 dated August 22, 2022
- Post record date, members/custodians must execute pay-in/early pay-in instructions in the New ISIN

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory change; this is an operational/procedural circular implementing the previously issued consolidated circular (NCL/CMPT/73996 dated April 30, 2026) on early pay-in for margin exemption, applied specifically to the MIDQ50ADD face value split corporate action.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members and custodians must execute early pay-in instructions in the Old/Existing ISIN (INF740KA1QL0) for the specified EPI dates and settlement numbers, without adjusting for the face value conversion
- Adherence to SEBI's Block Mechanism circular (NCL/CMPT/53386) is required for early pay-in through block mechanism in client demat accounts
- Custodians using pool accounts must upload accurate client-wise allocation details reflecting actual traded quantities
- After the record date, all pay-in/early pay-in instructions must shift to the New ISIN

## Important Dates

- Record Date: July 3, 2026
- Ex Date: July 3, 2026
- EPI Date and Settlement Numbers: July 3, 2026 (Settlement No. 2026123) and July 6, 2026 (Settlement Nos. 2026123 & 2026124)

## Impact Assessment

This circular has limited direct market impact and primarily affects clearing members, custodians, and PCMs handling settlement operations for the MIDQ50ADD ETF around its face value split. Failure to follow the correct ISIN and quantity conventions during the transition period could result in settlement errors or mismatches, making operational compliance important for affected participants, though the broader market and retail investors are largely unaffected.
