## Summary

NSE will conduct a mock trading session in the Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, April 18, 2026. The session runs from 11:00 to 13:00 hours from the Primary Site. NEAT version 1.5.5 is mandatory for participation. There is no new version release associated with this mock session.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Saturday, April 18, 2026 in the Commodity Derivatives segment
- Normal Market open time: 11:00 hrs; Normal Market close time: 13:00 hrs
- Position Limit/Collateral value setup cutoff and Trade Modification end time: 13:10 hrs
- Re-login timings: Live Re-login start 16:00 hrs, close 16:30 hrs
- Mandatory NEAT version: 1.5.5 (download path: /comtftp/comtcommon/NEATCO155 on NSE Extranet)
- Old NEAT Adapter (v1.0.20) uses GR PORT 10857; New NEAT Adapter (v1.0.27) uses GR PORT 10859
- All outstanding orders will be cleared before each session
- Members using NNF software must manually clear orders in their systems

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. Members may use Mock Trading or Simulated Environment to meet SEBI regulatory requirements per SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 and NSE circular NSE/MSD/46441.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must upgrade to NEAT version 1.5.5 before participating
- Only valid and compliant UCC/PAN uploaded and approved before cutoff will be allowed to trade
- UCC Validation will be skipped during contingency time for order entry
- Members should participate using all trading software and re-login into the live environment to ensure smooth connectivity for Monday, April 20, 2026
- Refer to NSE/MSD/73674 dated April 09, 2025 for NEAT Adapter Exe application guidance
- Connectivity details available in NSE/MSD/67674 dated April 24, 2025 and NSE/COM/71599 dated December 2, 2025

## Important Dates

- April 18, 2026: Mock trading session (11:00–13:00 hrs)
- April 20, 2026: Live trading resumes on Monday; members must ensure connectivity

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational mock session with no financial obligations — trades will not result in any fund pay-in or pay-out. The session primarily impacts Commodity Derivatives segment members who need to verify connectivity, test software, and ensure readiness for live trading on April 20, 2026. No market disruption is expected.
