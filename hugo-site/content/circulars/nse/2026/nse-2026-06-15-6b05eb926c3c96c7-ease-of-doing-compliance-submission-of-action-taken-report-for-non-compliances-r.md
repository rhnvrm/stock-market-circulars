## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has prescribed a new framework for closure of non-compliances reported in half-yearly internal audit reports. Clearing members must now submit an Action Taken Report (ATR) certified by their empanelled internal auditor within 2 months of the due date for internal audit report submission. This framework is applicable from the half-year ended March 31, 2026 onwards.

## Key Points

- Clearing members must submit an Action Taken Report (ATR) for all non-compliances identified in internal audit reports.
- ATR must be certified by the empanelled internal auditor confirming compliance status, covering at least one month of sample verification.
- Detailed submission procedure for ATRs will be communicated via a subsequent circular.
- Critical non-compliances (e.g., misutilisation of client collaterals, net worth issues) will attract immediate enforcement action without waiting for ATR submission.
- Members must also adhere to SEBI norms on appointment and rotation of internal auditors per SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MIRSD/MIRSD2/CIR/P/2016/95 dated September 26, 2016.

## Regulatory Changes

NCL has introduced a structured ATR framework as an "Ease of Doing Compliance" initiative, aligning with similar frameworks issued by stock exchanges. Previously, enforcement actions were directly initiated for audit non-compliances per NCL/CMPL/59930 dated December 26, 2023. The new framework creates an intermediate step — ATR submission — before enforcement is triggered, provided timely closure is achieved.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing Members must submit ATRs for all internal audit non-compliances within the prescribed timeline.
- ATR must be certified by the empanelled internal auditor with at least one month of sample verification.
- Members should communicate the provisions of this circular to their respective internal auditors.
- Clearing Members must comply with SEBI norms on appointment and rotation of internal auditors, including disclosure of auditor independence declaration and registration details.
- Failure to close observations or submit ATR by the due date will result in enforcement actions per NCL/CMPL/59930 or subsequent penalty circulars.

## Important Dates

- **Effective from:** Half-year ended March 31, 2026 onwards.
- **ATR due date for period ended March 31:** July 31.
- **ATR due date for period ended September 30:** January 31.
- All non-compliances must be closed within 2 months from the end of the due date of internal audit report submission.

## Impact Assessment

This circular moderately impacts NSE Clearing Members by formalising a compliance closure mechanism that provides an opportunity to remediate audit findings before enforcement penalties are applied. The ATR framework reduces the risk of automatic penalties for minor non-compliances while maintaining strict timelines. Critical violations remain subject to immediate action. Members with empanelled auditors already in place should be minimally affected; the primary burden is ensuring timely ATR preparation, certification, and submission.
