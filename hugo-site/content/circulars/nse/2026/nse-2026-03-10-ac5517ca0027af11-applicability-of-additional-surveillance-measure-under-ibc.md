## Summary

NSE has issued an update on the applicability of Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) for companies undergoing proceedings under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), effective March 11, 2026. Neueon Corporation Limited (NEUEON) is newly shortlisted under ASM Stage I. No securities are moving from Stage I to Stage II or vice versa, and no securities are being excluded from ASM in this update.

## Key Points

- **New Addition to ASM IBC Stage I (w.e.f. March 11, 2026):** NEUEON (Neueon Corporation Limited, ISIN: INE333I01044)
- No securities have been shortlisted under "Receipt of corporate announcement / disclosure" category in this update (Nil)
- No securities are transitioning from Stage I to Stage II (Nil)
- No securities are transitioning from Stage II to Stage I (Nil)
- No securities are being excluded from ASM IBC in this update (Nil)
- The consolidated ASM (IBC) list now contains 18+ securities across Stage I and the "Receipt of corporate announcement / disclosure" category

## Regulatory Changes

ASM under IBC is applied by NSE to securities of companies that are undergoing insolvency and bankruptcy proceedings. The framework has two stages:
- **Stage I:** Enhanced margin requirements and surveillance
- **Stage II:** Further restrictions on trading
- **Receipt of corporate announcement / disclosure:** Monitoring category for companies that have made relevant IBC-related disclosures

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and investors must note the revised ASM applicability for affected securities
- Enhanced margin requirements apply to securities under ASM Stage I and Stage II
- Market participants dealing in the listed securities should review their exposure and margin obligations accordingly
- Securities marked with an asterisk (\*) indicate newly added or recently changed status

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** March 11, 2026 — All changes to ASM IBC applicability come into force

- NEUEON (Neueon Corporation Limited) — Traders and investors in this stock will face higher margin requirements and enhanced surveillance from March 11, 2026.
