## Summary

NSE has notified the admission of various privately placed securities, primarily commercial papers (CPs), to dealings on the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange with effect from July 01, 2026, under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The circular specifies the designated security codes and details for each instrument in the accompanying annexure.

## Key Points

- Fresh issue commercial papers listed from multiple issuers: Toyota Financial Services India Limited, HDFC Securities Limited, Aditya Birla Capital Limited (two separate CPs), IIFL Finance Limited, Aseem Infrastructure Finance Limited, CEAT Limited (two separate CPs), PNB Housing Finance Limited, and Kisetsu Saison Finance (India) Private Limited.
- Each commercial paper has a face value of Rs. 500,000 per unit, with issue prices reflecting the applicable discount.
- Date of allotment for all listed instruments is 30-Jun-2026.
- Redemption dates range from 05-Aug-2026 to 22-Feb-2027 depending on the instrument.
- Unique ISINs and security codes have been assigned to each instrument for trading identification purposes.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is a routine notification under existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations for admitting privately placed securities to the Debt Market Segment.

## Compliance Requirements

Members dealing in the Debt Market Segment should update their systems to recognize the new ISINs and security codes for the listed commercial papers to facilitate trading, clearing, and settlement.

## Important Dates

- Effective date of listing: July 01, 2026
- Date of allotment (all instruments): 30-Jun-2026
- Redemption dates: 05-Aug-2026 (Kisetsu Saison Finance), 25-Sep-2026 (Aditya Birla Capital, CEAT), 28-Sep-2026 (IIFL Finance, CEAT), 29-Sep-2026 (HDFC Securities, Aditya Birla Capital), 15-Jan-2027 (Aseem Infrastructure Finance), 22-Feb-2027 (PNB Housing Finance), 21-Jun-2027 (Toyota Financial Services)

## Impact Assessment

This circular has minimal market impact as it is a procedural listing notification for privately placed debt instruments (commercial papers) on the debt segment. It primarily affects institutional debt market participants and members trading in these specific instruments, with no direct implications for equity markets or retail investors.
