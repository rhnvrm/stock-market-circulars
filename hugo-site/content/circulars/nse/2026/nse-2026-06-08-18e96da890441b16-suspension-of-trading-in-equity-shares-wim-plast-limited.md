## Summary

NSE has notified the suspension of trading in equity shares of Wim Plast Limited (Symbol: WIMPLAST, Series: EQ) with effect from June 09, 2026 (i.e., from the closing hours of trading on June 08, 2026). The suspension is pursuant to Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A and is triggered by a Composite Scheme of Arrangement involving the company.

## Key Points

- Trading in WIMPLAST EQ shares will be suspended effective June 09, 2026
- The suspension takes effect from the closing hours of trading on June 08, 2026
- Reason for suspension: Composite Scheme of Arrangement
- Circular issued under Regulation 3.1.2 of NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A
- Circular Ref. No: NSE/CML/74603 | 0914/2026

## Regulatory Changes

Pursuant to Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A, NSE is exercising its authority to suspend the trading of a security undergoing a Composite Scheme of Arrangement. This is a standard regulatory mechanism to protect market integrity during corporate restructuring events.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must cease trading in WIMPLAST EQ from the closing hours of June 08, 2026
- Members should inform their clients about the suspension and prevent any orders being placed for this security post the suspension effective time
- Members should monitor further circulars from NSE regarding reinstatement of trading or any new listing post-scheme completion

## Important Dates

- **June 08, 2026**: Last day of trading; suspension effective from closing hours
- **June 09, 2026**: Trading suspension comes into full effect

## Impact Assessment

This suspension directly impacts all investors and traders holding or intending to trade in Wim Plast Limited equity shares on NSE. A Composite Scheme of Arrangement typically involves mergers, demergers, or restructuring activities that require trading to be paused to facilitate accurate price discovery and settlement post-scheme. Investors should watch for subsequent circulars detailing the outcome of the scheme, any new ISIN or symbol assignments, and the date when trading may resume or when new securities are listed.
