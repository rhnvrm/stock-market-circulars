## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed Commercial Paper (CP) securities from 10 companies to the Debt Market Segment of the Exchange effective March 16, 2026, pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. All instruments were allotted on March 13, 2026.

## Key Points

- 10 companies have listed Commercial Paper instruments on NSE's Debt Market Segment
- All CPs were allotted on March 13, 2026 and listed effective March 16, 2026
- Face value for all CPs is ₹5,00,000 per unit
- Maturities range from approximately 45 days (CEAT) to approximately 1 year (Sundaram Finance, LIC Housing Finance)
- Circular Ref No: 0451/2026, Download Ref No: NSE/CML/73302

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine listing notification under Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for members or investors. Members dealing in debt instruments should note the new security codes as specified in the annexure for trading purposes.

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment (all securities):** March 13, 2026
- **Listing Effective Date:** March 16, 2026
- **Redemption Dates by issuer:**
  - CEAT Limited (CL CP 28/04/26): April 28, 2026
  - Tata Steel Limited (TSL CP 11/06/26): June 11, 2026
  - Blue Star Limited (BSL CP 11/06/26 Sr 41): June 11, 2026
  - Kotak Securities Limited (KSL CP 15/06/26 Sr 343): June 15, 2026
  - Sundaram Finance Limited (SFL CP 12/03/27): March 12, 2027 (truncated in source)
  - ICICI Securities Limited (ISEC CP 12/06/26): June 12, 2026
  - Godrej Industries Limited (GIL CP 12/06/26 Sr 176): June 12, 2026
  - Axis Securities Limited (ASL CP 12/06/26 Sr 78): June 12, 2026
  - Mahindra & Mahindra Financial Services Limited (MMFSL CP 12/06/26 Sr 886): June 12, 2026
  - LIC Housing Finance Limited (LHFL CP 23/02/27 Sr 4): February 23, 2027

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative circular with minimal market impact. The listing of these Commercial Paper instruments provides short-term debt financing visibility for the issuing companies. The instruments span blue-chip corporates and financial institutions, reflecting normal treasury/working capital activity. Equity investors in these companies are not directly affected. Debt market participants can now trade these CPs on NSE's Debt Market Segment using the designated security codes. Total issuance across all listed CPs is significant in aggregate (e.g., LIC Housing Finance alone issued 19,000 units × ₹5,00,000 = ₹950 crore notional).
