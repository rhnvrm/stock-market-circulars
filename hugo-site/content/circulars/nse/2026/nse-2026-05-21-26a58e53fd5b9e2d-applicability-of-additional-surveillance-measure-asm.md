## Summary

NSE has issued an update to the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework, effective May 22, 2026. One security (KHFM) has been newly shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Stage I, while two securities (FMNL and NEPHROCARE) have been excluded from the ASM framework and moved to the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework.

## Key Points

- KHFM (Khfm Hospitality And Facility Management Services Limited, ISIN: INE00UG01014) added to Long-Term ASM Framework Stage I effective May 22, 2026.
- FMNL (Future Market Networks Limited, ISIN: INE360L01017) excluded from ASM and moved to ESM framework.
- NEPHROCARE (Nephro Care India Limited, ISIN: INE0SUN01013) excluded from ASM and moved to ESM framework.
- No securities are moving between ASM stages (Stage I→II, II→III, III→IV, IV→III, III→II, II→I are all Nil).
- No new securities shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Stage IV.

## Regulatory Changes

The circular operationalises stage-level changes within NSE's Long-Term ASM Framework. Securities classified under Stage IV (Criteria VII) are subject to migration from Rolling Settlement (Series: EQ) to Trade for Trade settlement (Series: BE) on a T+3 basis, where T is the date of circular issuance.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note revised settlement series for affected securities.
- For securities moved to ESM (FMNL and NEPHROCARE), applicable ESM-framework trading restrictions apply from the effective date.
- Investors holding KHFM should be aware of the heightened surveillance and any associated margin/trading restrictions under ASM Stage I.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** May 22, 2026 — all ASM additions, exclusions, and stage movements take effect.

## Impact Assessment

The update has a targeted impact on three securities. KHFM faces increased scrutiny under ASM Stage I, which typically involves additional surveillance and may include margin requirements or trading restrictions. FMNL and NEPHROCARE face stricter oversight under the ESM framework, which generally imposes more stringent trading conditions than ASM. The consolidated ASM list continues to include a broad set of securities across multiple stages, indicating ongoing surveillance activity across the market.
