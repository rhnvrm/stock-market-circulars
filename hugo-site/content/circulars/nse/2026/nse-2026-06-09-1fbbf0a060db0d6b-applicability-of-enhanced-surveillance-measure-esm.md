## Summary

NSE's Surveillance department has updated the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework effective June 10–11, 2026. Six new securities have been added to ESM Stage I, and one security (DCMSIL) moves from Stage I to Stage II. No securities are being excluded from ESM in this update.

## Key Points

- 6 securities added to ESM Stage I w.e.f. June 10, 2026, attracting 100% margin from June 11, 2026
- All Stage I additions shift from Rolling Settlement (EQ/SM series) to Trade-for-Trade segment (BE/ST series) w.e.f. June 11, 2026
- DCMSIL (DCM Shriram International Limited) moves from ESM Stage I to Stage II w.e.f. June 10, 2026
- Stage II securities trade under Trade-for-Trade with a 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction
- No securities are being excluded from the ESM framework in this circular
- ESM operates in conjunction with all other prevailing exchange surveillance measures

## Regulatory Changes

The circular references and updates the ESM framework originally established through circulars NSE/SURV/56948, NSE/SURV/57609, NSE/SURV/63361, NSE/SURV/64066, NSE/SURV/64400, and NSE/SURV/69315 (issued between June 2023 and July 2025). Securities qualifying under ESM are moved from the Rolling Settlement segment to the Trade-for-Trade segment, with Stage II additionally subject to Periodic Call Auction with a 2% price band.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must apply a minimum 100% margin on all open positions in newly added ESM Stage I securities as on June 10, 2026, and on all new positions from June 11, 2026
- Trading in affected securities must be handled under the Trade-for-Trade (BE/ST series) segment from June 11, 2026
- For DCMSIL (Stage II), trading must occur under Trade-for-Trade with 2% price band via Periodic Call Auction from June 10, 2026
- Members may contact NSE at surveillance@nse.co.in for further queries

## Important Dates

- **June 10, 2026**: ESM Stage changes take effect (Stage I to Stage II for DCMSIL; Stage II price band/PCA applies)
- **June 10, 2026**: 100% margin applies on open positions in new Stage I securities
- **June 11, 2026**: New Stage I securities shift to Trade-for-Trade (BE/ST series); 100% margin applies on all new positions

The 100% margin requirement and mandatory Trade-for-Trade settlement significantly reduce liquidity and increase capital requirements for market participants holding or trading these securities. Stage II classification for DCMSIL further restricts trading through Periodic Call Auction with a narrow 2% price band, limiting intraday price movement. NSE notes this classification is purely for market surveillance purposes and should not be construed as adverse action against the companies.
