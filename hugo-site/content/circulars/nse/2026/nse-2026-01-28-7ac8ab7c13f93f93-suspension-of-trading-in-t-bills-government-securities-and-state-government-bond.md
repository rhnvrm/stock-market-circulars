## Summary

NSE has announced the suspension of trading in 20 debt securities including Treasury Bills (T-Bills), Government Securities (GS), and State Government Bonds (SG) due to their scheduled redemption/maturity. The suspensions will take effect on various dates ranging from January 30, 2026 to February 25, 2026. This action is taken in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A.

## Key Points

- 20 debt securities to be suspended from trading
- Includes 12 Treasury Bills, 2 Government Securities, and 6 State Government Bonds
- Suspension dates range from January 30, 2026 to February 25, 2026
- All suspensions are due to maturity/redemption of the securities
- Issued under NSE Circular Ref. No: 0140/2026, Download Ref No: NSE/CML/72506

## Regulatory Framework

This suspension is implemented pursuant to Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A, which governs the suspension of securities from trading.

## Important Dates

- **January 28, 2026**: Circular issued
- **January 30, 2026**: First suspension (62RJ26)
- **February 3-25, 2026**: Staggered suspension dates for remaining securities
- **Effective Date**: Each security suspended from its respective suspension date as listed

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Minimal. This is a routine administrative action for debt securities reaching maturity. Investors holding these securities will receive redemption proceeds as per normal maturity procedures.

**Investor Impact:** Holders of these securities should be prepared for redemption and will receive principal and any applicable interest payments upon maturity. No trading will be possible from the suspension date onward.

**Operational Impact:** Standard operational procedure with no disruption to overall market functioning. The debt market segment will continue normal operations for all other listed securities.
