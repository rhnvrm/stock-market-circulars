## Summary

NSE has issued a corrigendum to its earlier circular (NSE/CML/74461, May 29, 2026) regarding the migration of Viviana Power Tech Limited from the SME Emerge platform to the Capital Market Segment (Main Board). The correction specifies that the trading series for the scrip should be read as **'BE'** instead of **'EQ'** in the Annexure, owing to the applicability of the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) as per NSE/SURV/74472 dated May 29, 2026. The scrip will continue to trade in the Trade-for-Trade segment until further notice.

## Key Points

- Trading series corrected from **'EQ'** to **'BE'** for Viviana Power Tech Limited post-migration to Main Board.
- The correction is necessitated by ESM applicability as per NSE circular NSE/SURV/74472 (May 29, 2026).
- The scrip remains in the **Trade-for-Trade** segment until further notice.
- This circular amends Annexure details in NSE/CML/74461 dated May 29, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

The series designation in the Annexure of NSE/CML/74461 is amended from 'EQ' to 'BE'. The 'BE' series is used for scrips under Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM), which imposes stricter trading conditions compared to the standard 'EQ' series.

## Compliance Requirements

- All members must update their systems and references to reflect the trading series as **'BE'** for Viviana Power Tech Limited.
- Members should note that the scrip is subject to ESM norms and Trade-for-Trade settlement obligations.
- Refer to NSE/SURV/74472 (May 29, 2026) for full ESM applicability conditions.

## Important Dates

- **May 29, 2026**: Original listing circular (NSE/CML/74461) and ESM circular (NSE/SURV/74472) issued.
- **June 01, 2026**: Corrigendum issued correcting series to 'BE'.
- Trade-for-Trade status continues **until further notice**.

## Impact Assessment

The placement in the 'BE' series under ESM and the Trade-for-Trade segment restricts intraday trading — positions cannot be squared off intraday and must result in delivery. This increases settlement risk and reduces liquidity compared to a standard 'EQ' series listing. Investors and members must account for mandatory delivery obligations. The ESM designation signals elevated regulatory scrutiny on the scrip post-migration from SME Emerge.
