## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of further equity shares for 20 companies on the Capital Market segment, effective June 25, 2026. The shares were allotted under Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOP/ESOS) and are admitted to dealings pursuant to Regulation 3.1.1 and 2.5.5 of the NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.

## Key Points

- 20 companies have new equity shares admitted to NSE Capital Market segment from June 25, 2026
- All securities are allotted under ESOP/ESOS schemes
- All newly listed shares carry Market Lot of 1 and are Pari Passu with existing equity shares
- No lock-in applicable for any of the securities listed
- Security identification is by designated codes; trading is in specified lot sizes

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is a routine notification under existing regulations:
- Regulation 3.1.1: NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A (admission to dealings)
- Regulation 2.5.5: NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A (lot sizes)

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must use the designated security codes for trading in these securities
- Trading must be conducted in the specified lot sizes as per the Annexure
- No additional compliance action required from members beyond standard trading procedures

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 24, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 25, 2026
- Key allotment dates per company:
  - 360 ONE WAM LIMITED (360ONE): June 18, 2026 — 198,421 shares (ISIN: INE466L01038)
  - Bandhan Bank Limited (BANDHANBNK): June 12, 2026 — 60,044 shares (ISIN: INE545U01014)
  - CARE Ratings Limited (CARERATING): June 10, 2026 — 11,250 shares (ISIN: INE752H01013)
  - Avenue Supermarts Limited (DMART): allotment date not fully disclosed in excerpt (ISIN: INE192R01011)
  - Additional companies: EID Parry India, Fedbank Financial Services, Brainbees Solutions, ICICI Prudential Life Insurance, IndoStar Capital Finance, Marico, Nippon Life India Asset Management, Nuvama Wealth Management, PG Electroplast, PB Fintech, Premier Energies, SBI Life Insurance, Shriram Finance, Thejo Engineering, Trent, Windsor Machines

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact. These are routine ESOP allotments resulting in marginal dilution across 20 large and mid-cap companies. The share counts are small relative to total outstanding equity for each issuer. No change in trading rules, price bands, or market microstructure. Existing shareholders may see negligible dilution. No action required by retail investors or market participants.
