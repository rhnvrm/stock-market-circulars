## Summary

NSE has notified the listing of further issued securities for 12 companies on the Capital Market segment, effective June 10, 2026. The securities were allotted through ESOP schemes, conversion of warrants on a preferential basis, and other corporate mechanisms. Companies covered include Borosil Renewables Limited, Clean Max Enviro Energy Solutions Limited, Cyient Limited, Lloyds Metals and Energy Limited, One 97 Communications Limited, Rishabh Instruments Limited, S.J.S. Enterprises Limited, TATA Consumer Products Limited, Tejas Networks Limited, VISA Chrome Limited, Vardhman Textiles Limited, and WeWork India Management Limited.

## Key Points

- Securities admitted to dealings on NSE Capital Market segment effective June 10, 2026
- Governed under Regulation 3.1.1 and 2.5.5 of NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A
- All newly listed securities carry a market lot size of 1
- All new securities rank pari passu with existing equity shares
- Borosil Renewables: 94,338 equity shares allotted via conversion of warrants on preferential basis (issued at Rs. 530), allotted May 8, 2026, with lock-in until December 21, 2026
- Clean Max Enviro Energy Solutions: 13,326 equity shares allotted under ESOP on June 5, 2026; no lock-in
- Cyient Limited: 12,476 equity shares of Rs. 5 each allotted under ESOP on May 29, 2026; no lock-in
- Lloyds Metals and Energy Limited: equity shares allotted at issue price of Rs. 740 (details partially disclosed)
- Circular ref: NSE/CML/74631 | Circular Ref. No: 0926/2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular is issued in pursuance of existing Regulation 3.1.1 (admission to dealings) and Regulation 2.5.5 (lot size notification) of NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must identify all newly listed securities by their designated NSE codes only
- All trades in these securities must be executed in the specified lot sizes (1 for all listed securities)
- Lock-in restrictions apply to Borosil Renewables shares until December 21, 2026; trading members must ensure compliance

## Important Dates

- **June 9, 2026**: Circular issued
- **June 10, 2026**: Effective date — all securities admitted to dealings on NSE Capital Market segment
- **May 8, 2026**: Date of allotment for Borosil Renewables warrant conversion shares
- **June 5, 2026**: Date of allotment for Clean Max ESOP shares
- **May 29, 2026**: Date of allotment for Cyient ESOP shares
- **December 21, 2026**: Lock-in expiry for Borosil Renewables preferential allotment shares

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine further listing notification with limited broad market impact. Each allotment represents incremental equity dilution for existing shareholders of the respective companies. ESOP allotments (Clean Max, Cyient) reflect employee compensation mechanisms and are typically small relative to total share capital. The Borosil Renewables preferential warrant conversion (94,338 shares at Rs. 530, locked-in until Dec 2026) has a slightly higher significance as it involves external investors but remains modest in scale. Lloyds Metals allotment at Rs. 740 issue price is notable given the stock's market profile. Overall market impact is low; individual stock investors in these 12 companies should note the dilution effect.
