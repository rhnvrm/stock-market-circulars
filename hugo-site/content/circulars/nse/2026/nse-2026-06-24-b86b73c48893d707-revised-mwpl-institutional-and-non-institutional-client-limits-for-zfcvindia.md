## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has revised the Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL) and the participant, institutional client, and non-institutional client position limits for the symbol **ZFCVINDIA** under the Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) Scheme. The revision, issued via Circular Ref. No. 061/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/74855), is effective from **June 25, 2026** and supersedes earlier limits set under NCL/CMPT/73999 (April 30, 2026) as partially modified by NCL/CMPT/74480 (May 29, 2026).

## Key Points

- MWPL for ZFCVINDIA in SLB increases from **7,58,703** to **45,52,219** shares — approximately a six-fold increase.
- Institutional client limit increases from **75,870** to **4,55,221** shares.
- Participant limit increases from **75,870** to **4,55,221** shares.
- Non-institutional client limit increases from **7,587** to **45,522** shares.
- All new limits are effective from June 25, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

The revised limits partially modify the earlier NCL circular NCL/CMPT/74480 dated May 29, 2026, which itself was issued in pursuance of NCL/CMPT/73999 dated April 30, 2026. The new limits represent a significant upward revision across all participant categories for ZFCVINDIA in the SLB segment.

## Compliance Requirements

All Participants and Custodians are advised to take note of the revised position limits for ZFCVINDIA effective June 25, 2026, and ensure their SLB exposure remains within the newly prescribed limits. No specific action is required beyond awareness and adherence.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 24, 2026
- **Effective Date:** June 25, 2026
- **Reference Circulars:** NCL/CMPT/73999 (April 30, 2026); NCL/CMPT/74480 (May 29, 2026)

## Impact Assessment

The approximately six-fold increase in MWPL and all client-level limits for ZFCVINDIA in the SLB scheme allows significantly higher lending and borrowing activity in this security. This may improve liquidity in the SLB segment for ZFCVINDIA and benefit participants seeking to take or hedge positions through securities lending. The change is administrative and does not impose new obligations but expands permissible exposure thresholds.
