## Summary

NSE has admitted eight privately placed debt securities to its Debt Market Segment effective May 04, 2026, under Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. The securities include five fresh commercial paper (CP) issuances, two fresh debt bond issuances, and one re-issued commercial paper across seven issuers.

## Key Points

- Seven issuers across two instrument types (Commercial Paper and Other Debt Securities) admitted to NSE Debt Market Segment.
- Five fresh CP issuances totalling ₹7,150 crore face value from HDFC Securities, Axis Securities, Sundaram Finance, IIFL Capital Services, and IndoStar Capital Finance.
- Two bond issuances: SIDBI at 7.61% coupon (302,500 units, FV ₹1,00,000) and Aditya Birla Capital at 8.0668% coupon (12,500 units, FV ₹1,00,000).
- One re-issued CP from Aditya Birla Capital (4,000 units) with redemption on 28-Jul-2026.
- All allotments dated 30-Apr-2026 except IndoStar Capital (04-May-2026).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. Listing is in routine pursuance of Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance actions required for exchange members. Members may trade these securities on the Debt Market Segment using the designated security codes specified in the annexure effective May 04, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing notification with no market-wide impact. Investors and traders in the NSE Debt Market Segment gain access to eight new tradeable securities. The SIDBI bond (₹3,025 crore face value) and the Aditya Birla Capital 10-year bond are the largest issuances by value. Short-term CP instruments from NBFC and financial sector entities reflect continued activity in the short-term debt market.
