## Summary

NSE has announced the adjustment of Futures and Options contracts for Indian Bank (symbol: INDIANB) pursuant to SEBI guidelines on corporate action adjustments. Indian Bank has declared a dividend of Rs 18.25 per share (face value Rs 10), with an ex-date of June 10, 2026. All affected option strike prices will be revised downward by Rs 18.25 effective from the ex-date.

## Key Points

- Symbol: INDIANB (Indian Bank)
- Corporate action: Dividend of Rs 18.25/- per share (face value Rs 10/-)
- Ex-date / Effective date: June 10, 2026
- All OPTSTK contracts expiring June 30, 2026 will have strike prices revised down by Rs 18.25
- Members must load updated contract.gz and spread files before trading on the ex-date
- Revised strike/futures base prices and lot sizes may appear in decimal places, rounded to nearest tick size and nearest integer respectively

## Regulatory Changes

Strike price adjustments are made in pursuance of SEBI guidelines for adjustments to futures and options contracts upon announcement of corporate actions. The adjustment methodology follows the prescribed framework; details are available at the NSE equity derivatives corporate actions adjustments page.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must load the updated contract.gz / MII contract file (`NSE_FO_contract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz`) and spread file (`NSE_FO_spdcontract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz`) on their trading applications **before trading on June 10, 2026**.
- Files are available from the directory `faoftp/faocommon` on the Extranet server, and also on the NSE website under all-reports-derivatives.
- Position adjustment methodology will be separately intimated by the respective Clearing Corporation.

## Important Dates

- **June 09, 2026**: Circular issued
- **June 10, 2026**: Ex-date and effective date for revised strike prices

## Impact Assessment

This is a standard corporate action adjustment affecting INDIANB derivatives traders. All OPTSTK contracts on INDIANB expiring June 30, 2026 will see strike prices reduced by Rs 18.25 (the dividend amount). For example, the 660 strike becomes 641.75, the 700 strike becomes 681.75, and so on across the full strike ladder. Traders holding open positions in INDIANB options must account for these revised strike prices and ensure their trading systems are updated with the new contract files before the market opens on June 10, 2026. There is no direct impact on equity holders beyond the standard ex-dividend price adjustment.
