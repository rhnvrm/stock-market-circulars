## Summary

NSE has disabled Fresh/Additional Purchases through lump sum mode and Switch-In transactions for Franklin India Asian Equity Fund (FAEF) and Franklin U.S. Opportunities Equity Active Fund of Funds (FUSOF) on the NSE MF Invest Platform, effective June 8, 2026. This action follows a communication and addendum from Franklin Templeton Mutual Fund, aimed at preventing a breach of industry-wide overseas investment limits set by SEBI.

## Key Points

- Fresh/Additional Purchases via lump sum mode are suspended for Franklin India Asian Equity Fund and Franklin U.S. Opportunities Equity Active Fund of Funds effective June 8, 2026.
- Switch-In transactions into these two schemes are also suspended from the same date.
- Fresh SIP and STP registrations will continue but are capped at ₹50,000 per PAN level per month (first holder/Guardian).
- Existing SIP, STP, and other special product registrations made prior to June 8, 2026 are not affected.
- Unitholders under the IDCW Reinvestment Option are also unaffected.
- The suspension is to comply with SEBI's Para 13.11 of the Master Circular for Mutual Funds dated March 20, 2026.
- Total overseas investment across all Franklin Templeton Mutual Fund schemes is capped at the level as of February 1, 2022.

## Regulatory Changes

The suspension is implemented pursuant to SEBI's overseas investment limits as outlined in Para 13.11 of the SEBI Master Circular for Mutual Funds dated March 20, 2026. Franklin Templeton Trustees hold the right under the Scheme Information Document (SID) to discontinue subscriptions for a specified period or until further notice.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members dealing in mutual fund transactions must note that lump sum purchases and switch-ins into FAEF and FUSOF are disabled on the NSE MF Invest Platform from June 8, 2026.
- Fresh SIP/STP registrations for these schemes must be processed within the ₹50,000 per PAN per month limit.
- Members should inform their clients about the restrictions and direct affected investors to alternative schemes if needed.

## Important Dates

- **June 5, 2026**: NSE circular issued; Franklin Templeton addendum dated.
- **June 8, 2026**: Effective date for suspension of lump sum purchases, switch-ins, and the ₹50,000/month SIP/STP cap for new registrations.
- **February 1, 2022**: Reference date for the cap on total overseas investment across all FTMF schemes.
- **May 15, 2026**: Date of earlier Franklin Templeton addendum referenced in this notice.

## Impact Assessment

Investors seeking to make new lump sum investments or switch into these two overseas fund-of-funds schemes will be unable to do so from June 8, 2026. This restricts capital inflows into both FAEF and FUSOF but preserves continuity for existing SIP/STP investors. New SIP/STP registrations remain possible but are subject to a ₹50,000 per PAN per month ceiling. The restriction is driven by regulatory compliance with SEBI's overseas investment limits and does not affect redemptions or the management of existing holdings. Impact is moderate and primarily affects retail and HNI investors planning fresh allocations to these overseas-focused schemes.
