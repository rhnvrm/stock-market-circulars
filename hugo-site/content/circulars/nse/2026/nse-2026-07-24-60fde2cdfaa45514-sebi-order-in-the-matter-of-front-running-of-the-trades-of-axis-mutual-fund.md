## Summary

NSE has circulated SEBI's final order dated July 24, 2026 (Order No. QJA/BS/ISD/ISD-SEC-7/32504/2026-27) in the matter of front-running the trades of Axis Mutual Fund. This follows the earlier interim order (WTM/SM/ISD/ISD-SEC-3/24180/2022-23 dated February 28, 2023) referenced in NSE Circular No. NSE/INVG/55814, under which the same set of noticees were restrained from accessing the securities market pending investigation. SEBI has now passed a final order restraining 21 noticees from buying, selling, or otherwise dealing in securities, and from being associated with the securities market in any manner, for periods ranging from 3 to 7 years.

## Key Points

- 21 noticees have been debarred from the securities market for periods of 3, 5, or 7 years depending on their role in the front-running scheme.
- Noticees debarred for 7 years include Viresh Joshi, Prijesh Kurani, Dharini Kurani, Rekha Kurani, Bharti Navnit Godaya, M K B Bespoke Audio General, and Bindesh Kurani.
- Noticees debarred for 5 years include Sumit Desai and Pranav Vora.
- Noticees debarred for 3 years include Vaibhav Pandya, Nishil Surendra Marfatia, Olga Trading Pvt. Ltd., Krunal Khamar, Kamlesh Arjundas Dhanrajani, Bhavin Shah, Rupal Bhavin Shah, Visa Capital Partners, Suresh K Jajoo, Vimla S Jajoo, Ankit Jajoo, and Sheepra Sumeet Kabra.
- The period already undergone under the 2023 interim order will be set off against the new debarment period.
- Noticees with open derivative positions may close out or square off such positions within 3 months from the date of the order, or at contract expiry, whichever is earlier.
- Noticees are permitted to settle pay-in and pay-out obligations for transactions entered into prior to the order.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulation is introduced; this is an enforcement order under SEBI's existing powers to restrain market access in cases of fraudulent and unfair trade practices (front-running).

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must ensure none of the listed noticees are permitted to buy, sell, or deal in securities, or otherwise access the securities market, directly or indirectly, during their respective debarment periods.
- Any open derivative positions held by the noticees must be closed out or squared off within 3 months of the order date or at contract expiry, whichever is earlier.
- Members must facilitate settlement of pay-in/pay-out obligations for pre-existing transactions of the noticees as permitted.

## Important Dates

- February 28, 2023: Original SEBI interim order (WTM/SM/ISD/ISD-SEC-3/24180/2022-23) restraining the noticees.
- July 24, 2026: SEBI final order (QJA/BS/ISD/ISD-SEC-7/32504/2026-27) issued, imposing final debarment periods with set-off for time already served.
- 3 months from July 24, 2026: Deadline for noticees to close out/square off open derivative positions.

## Impact Assessment

The order has high regulatory severity for the named individuals and entities, who face multi-year bans from the securities market. However, since this action targets specific individuals/entities involved in front-running Axis Mutual Fund trades rather than a listed company or its securities, the direct impact on market operations, listed stocks, or broader trading activity is low. The circular primarily serves as a compliance notice for NSE members to ensure enforcement of the restraint order.
