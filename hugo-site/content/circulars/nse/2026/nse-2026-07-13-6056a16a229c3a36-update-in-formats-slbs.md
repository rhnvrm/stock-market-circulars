## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a revised format of the Bank Guarantee (Annexure 1A) that Participants in the Securities Lending and Borrowing Scheme (SLBS) must use when submitting a bank guarantee as collateral. The circular updates the standardized documentation that approved commercial banks must use to issue guarantees on behalf of SLBS Participants in favour of NSE Clearing Ltd.

## Key Points

- The circular provides an updated Bank Guarantee format (Annexure 1A) for use by SLBS Participants.
- The guarantee is to be issued by an approved commercial bank in favour of NSE Clearing Ltd, Exchange Plaza, Bandra Kurla Complex, Mumbai.
- The format applies to individuals, partnership firms/LLPs, and companies participating in the SLBS as Participants.
- Participation in SLBS requires maintaining collateral with NCL as specified under the SLBS Circulars issued from time to time.
- The bank guarantee is accepted towards collateral for a specified equivalent rupee amount, at the request of the Participant.

## Regulatory Changes

The circular formalizes an updated/standardized Bank Guarantee format (Annexure 1A) that must be used by SLBS Participants and their guarantor banks going forward, ensuring uniformity in the legal language and structure of guarantees submitted as collateral.

## Compliance Requirements

- SLBS Participants (individuals, partnership firms, LLPs, or companies) seeking to submit collateral via bank guarantee must use the revised Annexure 1A format.
- The guarantee must be issued by an approved commercial bank and must correctly identify the Participant's legal status (individual, partnership/LLP, or company) along with complete registered/office address details.
- Participants and their banks should ensure the guarantee document conforms to the exact wording and structure prescribed in the updated annexure to avoid rejection.

## Important Dates

Specific effective date was not available in the extracted content; Participants should refer to the full circular and annexure document for the applicable effective date and any transition period for existing guarantees.

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact, operational/administrative circular primarily affecting SLBS Participants and their banks in terms of documentation compliance. It has no direct bearing on listed securities, trading activity, or stock prices, but non-compliance with the updated format could affect a Participant's ability to have their collateral bank guarantee accepted by NSE Clearing Ltd.
