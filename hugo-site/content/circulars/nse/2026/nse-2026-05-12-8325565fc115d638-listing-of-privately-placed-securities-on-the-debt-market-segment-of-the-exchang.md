## Summary

NSE Circular 0764/2026 (Ref: NSE/CML/74167), issued May 12, 2026 by the Listing Department, notifies the admission of privately placed securities to dealings on the Debt Market Segment under Regulation 3.1.1 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations. Nine securities across two categories — Commercial Paper (CP) and Other Debt Securities — have been listed effective May 12, 2026.

## Key Points

- Three commercial papers admitted: NABARD (INE261F14PG7), Sundaram Finance (INE660A14ZB1), and Trust Investment Advisors (INE723X14NB5)
- Six debt securities admitted from Topaz 04 2026 (ABS/structured), Ambium Finserve, Lloyds Metals and Energy, and Tauros 2026
- All allotments occurred between May 7–12, 2026
- Security codes as specified in the Annexure are now operative for trading
- Circular issued by Kruti Doshi, Manager, NSE Listing Department

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. The listing is effected in pursuance of existing Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for members or issuers beyond standard debt market trading obligations. Members may now trade these securities using the designated security codes on the NSE Debt Market Segment.

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing notification with no material impact on equity markets. The securities admitted are privately placed debt instruments — commercial papers (short-term) and debentures/structured debt (medium-to-long term) — aimed at institutional investors in the NSE debt segment. Lloyds Metals and Energy Limited (LLOYDSME) and Sundaram Finance (SUNDARMFIN) are the only issuers with NSE-listed equity; their respective debt listings are standard capital-raising activity and do not signal unusual corporate events. Coupon rates range from 7.50% (Topaz structured securities) to 12.95% (Tauros), reflecting varying credit risk profiles across the issuer pool.
