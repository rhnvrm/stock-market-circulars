## Summary

NSE Circular NSE/SURV/75059 (Circular Ref. No. 505/2026), dated July 06, 2026, notifies market participants about securities shortlisted under the Long-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) framework. As per the Annexure, Ganga Forging Limited (Symbol: GANGAFORGE, ISIN: INE691Z01023) has been shortlisted under Long-Term ASM Stage I effective July 07, 2026. No securities were shortlisted under Stage IV (which triggers a shift to Trade-for-Trade), and no securities moved between existing ASM stages (I to II, II to III, I to IV, or II to IV) in this cycle.

## Key Points

- Ganga Forging Limited (GANGAFORGE) added to Long-Term ASM Framework Stage I w.e.f. July 07, 2026
- No securities shortlisted under Stage IV (Nil)
- No securities moved between ASM stages in this circular
- Applicable margin rate of 100% on all open and new positions w.e.f. July 09, 2026
- ASM framework operates alongside all other prevailing surveillance measures
- Inclusion under ASM is a market surveillance measure, not an adverse action against the company

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes; this circular is a routine periodic update applying existing Long-Term ASM criteria (per earlier circulars NSE/SURV/39265, NSE/SURV/45111, NSE/SURV/46557, NSE/SURV/48506, NSE/SURV/52090, NSE/SURV/63362 and NSE/SURV/64066) to newly qualifying securities.

## Compliance Requirements

Members must apply a 100% margin rate on all open positions in GANGAFORGE as on July 08, 2026, and on all new positions created from July 09, 2026 onwards. No Trade-for-Trade (Series: BE) shift is required in this cycle as no securities qualified under Stage IV criteria.

## Important Dates

- July 07, 2026: GANGAFORGE included under Long-Term ASM Stage I
- July 08, 2026: Reference date for existing open positions
- July 09, 2026: 100% margin rate becomes applicable on open and new positions

## Impact Assessment

The 100% margin requirement will significantly increase the cost of trading GANGAFORGE, likely reducing speculative activity and liquidity in the stock. The impact is confined to this single security and does not signal a broader market-wide surveillance escalation, as no other stocks moved stages or were added to Stage IV in this cycle.
