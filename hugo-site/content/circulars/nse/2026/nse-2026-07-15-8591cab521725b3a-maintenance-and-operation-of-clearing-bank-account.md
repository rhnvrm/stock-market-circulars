## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a circular referencing its consolidated circular dated April 30, 2026 (Download Ref No. NCL/CMPT/73996, Part B, Item 5 on 'Procedure of funds pay-in and pay-out'). The circular directs Members, Custodians, and Professional Clearing Members (PCMs) to use prescribed letter formats when submitting documentation to or from their respective clearing banks for the maintenance and operation of clearing bank accounts.

## Key Points

- The circular prescribes specific annexures to be used depending on the purpose of the account action.
- New Member Enablement / New Settlement Account requires Annexures 1 and 2.
- Change in Settlement Account requires Annexures 1, 2, 3, and 4.
- Closure of Settlement Account requires Annexure 5.
- Name Change in Settlement Account requires Annexure 6.
- Annexure 1 is a format of an authorisation letter to be submitted by the clearing member to the clearing bank, authorizing the bank to debit/credit the clearing account per NCL instructions and to report account balances/information to NCL.
- Annexure 2 is an account confirmation letter format from the clearing bank to NCL, certifying the member's bank account details (account name, number, opening date, status, branch).
- Members must ensure account names follow a standard naming convention: Primary account as '<Member name> <Segment> NCL Settlement Account' and Secondary account as '<Member name> <Segment> NCL Secondary Settlement Account'.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory rule is introduced; this circular operationalizes and standardizes documentation procedures already referenced under the April 30, 2026 consolidated circular on funds pay-in and pay-out.

## Compliance Requirements

- Clearing Members, Custodians, and PCMs must use the specified annexure formats (Annexures 1–6) for the relevant account action (new account, change, closure, or name change).
- Clearing bank account names must strictly follow the prescribed naming convention for both primary and secondary settlement accounts.
- The authorisation letter (Annexure 1) must be submitted on the clearing member's letterhead, irrevocably authorizing the clearing bank to debit/credit the account per NCL instructions, including debits for dues payable to the Exchange, SEBI, or other regulators.
- The clearing bank must submit a confirmation letter (Annexure 2) on its letterhead certifying the account details to NCL.

## Important Dates

Circular issued and effective July 15, 2026 (Circular Ref. No. 0251/2026, Download Ref No. NCL/CMPT/75211). No separate compliance deadline specified; format applies to account actions going forward.

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact administrative/operational circular affecting only the internal documentation procedures of clearing members, custodians, PCMs, and clearing banks. It does not affect trading, listing, or investor-facing market activity and has no direct implication for stock prices or specific securities.
