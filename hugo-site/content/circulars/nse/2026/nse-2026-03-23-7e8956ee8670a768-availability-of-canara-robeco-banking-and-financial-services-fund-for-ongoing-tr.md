## Summary

NSE has announced that Canara Robeco Banking and Financial Services Fund (Canara Robeco Mutual Fund) will be reopened for ongoing subscriptions and redemptions, along with SIP (Systematic Investment Plan), STP (Systematic Transfer Plan), and SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) facilities on the NSE MF Invest Platform, effective March 24, 2026.

## Key Points

- Six scheme variants of Canara Robeco Banking and Financial Services Fund will be available for ongoing transactions from March 24, 2026
- Transactions supported: subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, STP, and SWP
- All six schemes are classified under the Equity category
- Both Regular and Direct plan options are available across Growth and IDCW (Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal) variants

| 2 | Canara Robeco Banking and Financial Services Fund Direct Growth | INF760K01LN9 | Equity |

| 4 | Canara Robeco Banking and Financial Services Fund Direct IDCW Payout | INF760K01LP4 | Equity |
| 5 | Canara Robeco Banking and Financial Services Fund Regular IDCW Reinvestment | INF760K01LQ2 | Equity |
| 6 | Canara Robeco Banking and Financial Services Fund Direct IDCW Reinvestment | INF760K01LR0 | Equity |

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine operational notification regarding fund availability on the NSE MF Invest Platform.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance requirements for members beyond awareness. Members are informed to facilitate ongoing transactions for investors in these schemes from the effective date.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** March 24, 2026 — Fund reopens for ongoing subscriptions, redemptions, SIP, STP, and SWP on NSE MF Invest Platform

## Impact Assessment

Low market impact. This is a routine operational update enabling retail and institutional investors to transact in Canara Robeco Banking and Financial Services Fund via the NSE MF Invest Platform. Investors interested in banking and financial services sector equity funds gain access to this fund through the platform. No disruption to existing market operations or trading activities is expected.
