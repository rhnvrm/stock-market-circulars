## Summary

National Stock Exchange of India (NSE) has issued a circular (Download Ref No: NSE/NMFTM/75143, Circular Ref. No: 1485/2026) informing members that ITI Mutual Fund has changed the names of two of its schemes on the NSE MF Invest platform, effective immediately. The change follows a Notice cum Addendum (No. 44/2026) issued by ITI Asset Management Limited on June 18, 2026, pursuant to SEBI Circular HO/24/13/11(1)2026-IMD-POD-1/I/7602/2026 dated March 20, 2026.

## Key Points

- ITI Mutual Fund has renamed two of its debt schemes as per SEBI's scheme nomenclature guidelines.
- "ITI Ultra Short Duration Fund" has been renamed to "ITI Dynamic Bond Fund."
- "ITI Ultra Short Term Fund" has been renamed to "ITI Dynamic Term Fund."
- The name change applies wherever the scheme names appear in the Scheme Information Document (SID), Key Information Memorandum (KIM), and Statement of Additional Information (SAI).
- All other terms and conditions of the SID, KIM, and SAI remain unchanged.
- The change is reflected on the NSE MF Invest platform with immediate effect.

## Regulatory Changes

The renaming stems from SEBI Circular HO/24/13/11(1)2026-IMD-POD-1/I/7602/2026 dated March 20, 2026, which mandated changes to scheme nomenclature. ITI Asset Management Limited finalized the revised names via Notice cum Addendum No. 44/2026 dated June 18, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must update their systems and records to reflect the revised scheme names on the NSE MF Invest platform.
- Distributors and members should ensure investors are informed of the new scheme names to avoid confusion during transactions (purchase, redemption, SIP, switch).
- No other changes to fund structure, investment objective, or terms are required to be actioned.

## Important Dates

- June 18, 2026: Notice cum Addendum No. 44/2026 issued by ITI Asset Management Limited.
- July 10, 2026: Effective date of the scheme name change on the NSE MF Invest platform (per NSE circular); addendum states effective date as the same.

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact administrative circular affecting only the nomenclature of two ITI Mutual Fund debt schemes, with no change to investment objectives, structure, NAV computation, or investor rights. Impact is limited to NSE members, distributors, and RTAs needing to update scheme names in their systems and investor communications to ensure continuity of transactions on the NSE MF Invest platform.
