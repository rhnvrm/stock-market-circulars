## Summary

NSE has revised the quantity freeze limits for futures and options contracts on five major indices pursuant to Chapter 1.9 of the F&O Consolidated Circular (NSE/FAOP/73928 dated April 28, 2026). The updated limits are effective from July 1, 2026, and members must update their trading applications with the new contract files before trading commences on that date.

## Key Points

- Quantity freeze limits updated for five index derivatives: BANKNIFTY (600), NIFTY (1800), FINNIFTY (1800), MIDCPNIFTY (2800), and NIFTYNXT50 (600).
- Members must load the updated `contract.gz` and `NSE_FO_contract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz` files into their trading applications before trading on July 1, 2026.
- Contract files are available on the Extranet server under `faoftp/faocommon` and on the NSE website at the derivatives reports section.
- Underlying-wise quantity freeze details are also published on the NSE website.

## Regulatory Changes

The quantity freeze limits are computed as per the methodology outlined in F&O Consolidated Circular NSE/FAOP/73928 dated April 28, 2026 (Chapter 1.9). The new limits replace previously applicable limits and are as follows:

| Index Symbol | Quantity Freeze Limit |
|---|---|
| BANKNIFTY | 600 |
| NIFTY | 1800 |
| FINNIFTY | 1800 |
| MIDCPNIFTY | 2800 |
| NIFTYNXT50 | 600 |

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must download and load the updated `contract.gz` and `NSE_FO_contract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz` files from the Extranet server (`faoftp/faocommon`) into their trading systems before the market opens on July 1, 2026.
- Members can alternatively access the contract file via the NSE website's all-reports-derivatives section.
- Failure to update contract files may result in trading application mismatches with exchange-enforced freeze limits.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 30, 2026
- **Effective Date:** July 1, 2026 — new quantity freeze limits come into force

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational update with moderate impact on F&O trading members. The revised freeze limits define the maximum order quantity permissible without triggering a freeze for index derivative contracts. Members trading large quantities in NIFTY, FINNIFTY, or MIDCPNIFTY contracts should review their order management systems to ensure compliance with the new thresholds. MIDCPNIFTY has the highest freeze limit at 2800 units, while BANKNIFTY and NIFTYNXT50 remain at the lowest threshold of 600 units. Retail traders are unlikely to be affected; institutional and high-volume traders should verify their systems are updated prior to July 1, 2026.
