## Summary

NSE has issued a circular on the applicability of the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective May 27, 2026. Fifteen securities have been shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage I, while six securities have been excluded from the ASM framework. No securities have been shortlisted for Stage II, nor are any moving between stages.

## Key Points

- 15 securities added to ST-ASM Stage I effective May 27, 2026
- No securities shortlisted for ST-ASM Stage II
- No securities moving from Stage I to Stage II or vice versa
- 6 securities excluded from the ASM framework effective May 27, 2026
- Gujarat Raffia Industries Limited (GUJRAFFIA) was shortlisted based on criteria satisfied at BSE
- Of the excluded securities, 2 (DSSL, UFBL) are moved to the Long-Term ASM (LTASM) framework and 3 (INSPIRISYS, NEOCHEM, SPEB) are moved to the Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) framework

## Regulatory Changes

The ST-ASM framework applies enhanced surveillance conditions to securities exhibiting abnormal price or volume movements. Securities placed under ST-ASM Stage I are subject to additional margin requirements and trade-for-trade settlement, restricting leveraged/intraday trading and requiring full upfront funds or securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must ensure compliance with revised margin and settlement requirements for newly added ST-ASM securities from May 27, 2026
- Clients holding positions in affected securities should be informed of changed trading conditions
- Securities moved from STASM to LTASM (DSSL, UFBL) will now be subject to LTASM-specific conditions
- Securities moved from STASM to ESM (INSPIRISYS, NEOCHEM, SPEB) will now be subject to ESM-specific conditions

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** May 27, 2026 — All additions, exclusions, and framework changes take effect
