## Summary

NSE has communicated to its members that PGIM India Mutual Fund has revised subscription limits for its designated overseas fund-of-fund schemes effective June 05, 2026. The maximum amount for fresh Systematic Investment Plans (SIP) has been capped at INR 50,000 per day per investor per scheme (at primary holder PAN level). Additionally, fresh Systematic Transfer Plans (STP) into designated schemes are suspended immediately, while existing SIP and STP instalments will continue to be processed normally.

## Key Points

- Maximum fresh SIP amount revised to INR 50,000 per day, per investor, per scheme (at primary holder PAN level), effective June 05, 2026.
- Fresh STP registrations into designated schemes are not accepted with effect from cut-off timing of June 04, 2026.
- Existing SIP and STP instalments registered as on June 04, 2026 will continue to be processed without interruption.
- The change applies to three designated schemes: PGIM India Global Equity Opportunities Fund of Fund, PGIM India Emerging Markets Equity Fund of Fund, and PGIM India Global Select Real Estate Securities Fund of Fund.
- This revision follows an earlier Notice Cum Addendum dated May 15, 2026 that reopened subscriptions in these designated schemes after a temporary suspension announced on March 09, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

SEBI vide letter no. SEBI/HO/OW/IMD-II/DoF3/P/25095/2022 dated June 17, 2022 permits mutual funds to accept subscriptions in overseas funds/securities up to the headroom available without breaching the overseas investment limits as of EOD February 1, 2022 at the mutual fund level. In compliance with this framework, PGIM India AMC is limiting fresh SIP registrations and suspending fresh STP registrations to stay within applicable overseas investment limits.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members on the MF Invest Platform must ensure fresh SIP registrations for the designated PGIM India schemes do not exceed INR 50,000 per day per investor per scheme (at primary holder PAN level).
- No fresh STP registrations into the three designated schemes should be accepted on or after the cut-off timing of June 04, 2026.
- Existing SIP and STP instalments must continue to be processed as scheduled.

## Important Dates

- **June 04, 2026 (cut-off time):** Last date/time for accepting fresh STP registrations into designated schemes.
- **June 05, 2026:** Effective date for the revised INR 50,000 per day SIP maximum limit.
- **March 09, 2026:** Date of original Notice Cum Addendum temporarily suspending subscriptions in designated schemes.
- **May 15, 2026:** Date of Notice Cum Addendum reopening subscriptions in designated schemes.

## Impact Assessment

The change has a moderate operational impact on investors and distributors using the NSE MF Invest Platform who were investing in PGIM India's three overseas fund-of-fund schemes. Investors wishing to invest more than INR 50,000 per day via SIP in any of the designated schemes will now be restricted. The complete suspension of fresh STP into these schemes may affect investors who use STPs as a systematic allocation strategy from domestic funds into these overseas offerings. Existing investors with registered SIP/STP instalments are unaffected. The restriction is driven by SEBI's overseas investment headroom limits, making this a regulatory compliance-driven change rather than a fund performance or solvency issue.
