## Summary

NSE has announced changes to the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective March 11, 2026. Six securities have been newly added to ST-ASM Stage I, no securities have been moved between stages, and four securities have been excluded from the ASM framework entirely.

## Key Points

- 6 securities newly shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage I effective March 11, 2026
- No securities moved to ST-ASM Stage II (Nil)
- No securities moved from Stage II to Stage I (Nil)
- 4 securities excluded from the ASM framework effective March 11, 2026
- Consolidated ASM list includes 27+ securities across Stage I and Stage II

## Regulatory Changes

The ST-ASM framework is a surveillance mechanism used by NSE to monitor securities exhibiting unusual price or volume movements. Inclusion in the framework results in enhanced margin requirements and trading restrictions to protect market integrity.

## Compliance Requirements

- Brokers and trading members must apply enhanced surveillance margins for ST-ASM listed securities
- Investors holding or trading in newly added securities should be aware of increased margin requirements effective March 11, 2026
- Members must update their risk management systems to reflect the new ST-ASM list

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date:** March 11, 2026 — All additions and exclusions come into effect
