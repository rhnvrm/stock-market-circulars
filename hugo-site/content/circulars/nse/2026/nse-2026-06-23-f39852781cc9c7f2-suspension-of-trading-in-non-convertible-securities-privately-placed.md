## Summary

NSE has notified the suspension of trading in two privately placed non-convertible securities issued by Sammaan Capital Limited, effective June 24, 2026. The suspension is triggered by the redemption of these instruments under Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A.

## Key Points

- Two NCDs of Sammaan Capital Limited are being suspended from trading on the NSE debt market segment.
- Suspension takes effect from June 24, 2026.
- Reason for suspension is redemption of the securities.
- ISINs affected: INE148I07FG5 and INE148I08215.
- Circular issued under authority of NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A, Regulation 3.1.2.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular is a standard operational notification issued in pursuance of existing NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A, Regulation 3.1.2, which governs the suspension of trading in debt securities upon redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure no trades are executed in INE148I07FG5 and INE148I08215 on or after June 24, 2026.
- No action required by retail investors beyond awareness that these securities will no longer be tradeable post-suspension.

## Important Dates

- **June 23, 2026**: Circular issued by NSE.
- **June 24, 2026**: Effective date of trading suspension for both securities (INE148I07FG5 and INE148I08215).

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited and routine. Both securities are being suspended solely due to scheduled redemption, meaning the instruments have matured and proceeds will be returned to holders. This has no broader market or systemic implications. Trading members dealing in NSE debt market instruments should update their systems to reflect the suspension of these two ISINs from June 24, 2026.
