## Summary

NSE has enabled its web-based bidding platform for the buyback offer of Patel Integrated Logistics Limited, conducted under the Tender Offer Scheme. The live bidding session runs from July 6 to July 10, 2026, and the circular sets out the operational details, security parameters, and login procedures for trading members participating in the offer.

## Key Points

- Patel Integrated Logistics Limited has approached NSE to use the web-based bidding platform for its buyback offer.
- The offer follows the Tender Offer Scheme per NSE circular NSE/CMTR/52776 dated June 29, 2022.
- Issue period: July 6, 2026 to July 10, 2026.
- Bidding and custodial confirmation window: 9:15 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.; custodial confirmation on issue closure extends to 4:00 p.m.
- Live bidding URL for members: https://eipo.nseindia.com.
- All Capital Market segment trading members are eligible to participate; members already using the IPO bidding platform can use existing login credentials.
- Members without platform access can use the admin terminal to create branches and users, with login ID format Adminxxxxx (xxxxx = Member Code).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this circular operationalizes the existing Tender Offer Scheme framework (NSE/CMTR/52776, June 29, 2022) for a specific issuer's buyback.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure proper login access (existing IPO platform credentials or admin terminal setup) ahead of the bidding window.
- Members must adhere to the specified bidding and custodial confirmation timings.
- Bids must be tagged to the correct investor category codes (FII, MF, IC, FI, OTH, CO, IND, NOH) as listed in Annexure 1.

## Important Dates

- Issue/Bidding Period: July 6, 2026 to July 10, 2026
- Daily bidding window: 9:15 a.m. – 3:30 p.m.
- Custodial confirmation on issue closure: until 4:00 p.m.

## Impact Assessment

The circular has a direct but narrow impact, primarily affecting Patel Integrated Logistics Limited (PATINTLOG) shareholders eligible to tender shares and trading members facilitating the buyback bids. Buyback offer details: fixed price of Rs. 20 per share (face value Rs. 10) for up to 54,00,000 equity shares. There is no broader market-wide effect; the circular is largely procedural, ensuring smooth operational execution of the buyback via the exchange's bidding infrastructure.
