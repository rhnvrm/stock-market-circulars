## Summary

NSE has notified all members of adjustments to Futures and Options contracts in MPHASIS LIMITED (Symbol: MPHASIS) pursuant to SEBI guidelines on corporate action adjustments. The adjustment is triggered by a dividend of Rs 62.00 per share (face value Rs 10) with an ex-date and effective date of July 8, 2026.

## Key Points

- Symbol: MPHASIS (MPHASIS LIMITED)
- Corporate action type: Dividend
- Face value: Rs 10/-
- Dividend amount: Rs 62.00 per share
- Ex-date / Effective date: July 8, 2026
- Revised option strike prices will be provided one day prior to the ex-date
- Revised strike/futures base prices and lot sizes may appear in decimal places and will be rounded to the nearest tick size and nearest integer respectively

## Regulatory Changes

In accordance with SEBI guidelines for F&O contract adjustments on corporate actions, option strike prices, futures base prices, lot sizes, and quantity freeze limits for MPHASIS contracts will be revised effective from the ex-date (July 8, 2026).

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must load the updated contract.gz / MII contract file (`NSE_FO_contract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz`) and spread file (`NSE_FO_spdcontract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz`) on their trading applications before trading commences on the ex-date.
- Updated files are available from the `faoftp/faocommon` directory on the Extranet server and also on the NSE website at https://www.nseindia.com/all-reports-derivatives.
- Position adjustment methodology will be separately communicated by the respective Clearing Corporation.

## Important Dates

- **Ex-date / Effective date:** July 8, 2026
- **Revised strike prices availability:** One day prior to ex-date (July 7, 2026)

## Impact Assessment

Holders of MPHASIS futures and options contracts will see adjusted strike prices and base prices effective July 8, 2026, reflecting the Rs 62.00 dividend. Members must ensure their trading systems are updated with the revised contract files before the market opens on the ex-date to avoid pricing discrepancies. The adjustment is a standard regulatory process and does not indicate any unusual market risk beyond normal dividend-related F&O repricing.
