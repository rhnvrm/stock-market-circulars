## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited (NCL) has issued a revised list of approved securities and banks for the Securities Lending & Borrowing Scheme (SLBS), superseding the earlier circular no. 062/2026 (Download Ref No: NCL/CMPT/74878) dated Jun 25, 2026. The circular provides updated annexures covering equity shares, ETFs, Sovereign Gold Bonds, G-Sec/T-Bills, open-ended mutual funds, and empanelled banks, along with applicable market-wide limits, member-specific limits, and haircut rates. This circular is addressed to all Participants and Custodians and is effective for the month of August 2026.

## Key Points

- Annexure 1: List of equity shares (non-cash component of liquid assets) with market-wide limits, member-specific limits, and applicable VAR-based haircut rates.
- Annexure 2: List of Exchange Traded Funds (non-cash component of liquid assets) with applicable limits and haircut rates.
- Annexure 3: List of Exchange Traded Funds (cash component of liquid assets) with applicable limits and haircut rates.
- Annexure 4: List of Sovereign Gold Bonds and GOI Securities (G-Sec)/T-Bills (cash component of liquid assets) with applicable haircut rates.
- Annexure 5: List of Open Ended Mutual Funds acceptable as collateral, with market-wide limits and haircut rates.
- Annexure 6: List of banks empanelled for issuance of Bank Guarantees (BGs) and Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDRs).
- G-Sec/T-Bills carry a shut period of two days; members must release G-Sec/T-Bills at least two days before maturity.
- Member banks must note that G-Sec provided as collateral should not be reckoned for SLR purposes and must not be used for trading.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a periodic (monthly) revision of the approved collateral list under the SLBS framework, updating security-wise permitted quantities and haircut rates rather than introducing new regulatory requirements.

## Compliance Requirements

Participants and Custodians must apply the revised security lists, limits, and haircut rates for collateral purposes starting August 2026. Members must ensure G-Sec/T-Bills are released at least two days ahead of maturity due to the shut period, and member banks must exclude SLB-pledged G-Sec from SLR computation and trading use.

## Important Dates

- Circular date: Jul 22, 2026
- Effective period: August 2026
- Supersedes circular 062/2026 dated Jun 25, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine operational update affecting clearing members' and custodians' collateral management under the SLB scheme. It does not directly affect trading in any specific security but ensures collateral valuations and eligible security lists remain current for risk management purposes.
