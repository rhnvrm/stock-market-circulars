## Summary

NSE has announced the applicability of Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM) effective May 27–29, 2026. Nine new securities are being included under ESM Stage I, one security (GAUDIUMIVF) is being moved from Stage I to Stage II, and no securities are moving from Stage II to Stage I. All newly included Stage I securities will be shifted from Rolling Settlement (EQ/SM series) to Trade-for-Trade (BE/ST series) effective May 29, 2026, with a minimum 100% margin requirement.

## Key Points

- 9 securities added to ESM Stage I effective May 27, 2026: ALLETEC, CLEDUCATE, CUDML, INSPIRISYS, NEOCHEM, SPEB, VERTOZ, VICTORYEV, and WELINV
- GAUDIUMIVF (Gaudium IVF and Women Health Limited) moves from ESM Stage I to Stage II effective May 27, 2026
- No securities moving from Stage II back to Stage I
- All newly included ESM securities shifted from Rolling Settlement (EQ/SM) to Trade-for-Trade segment (BE/ST) effective May 29, 2026
- Minimum 100% margin applies on all open positions as of May 27, 2026 and new positions from May 29, 2026
- Stage II securities placed under Trade-for-Trade with 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction effective May 27, 2026
- ESM operates in conjunction with all other prevailing surveillance measures
- Shortlisting under ESM is purely for market surveillance purposes and not an adverse action against the company

## Regulatory Changes

The circular references and builds upon previous ESM circulars: NSE/SURV/56948 (June 02, 2023), NSE/SURV/57609 (July 18, 2023), NSE/SURV/63361 (August 09, 2024), NSE/SURV/64066 (September 20, 2024), NSE/SURV/64400 (October 04, 2024), and NSE/SURV/69315 (July 25, 2025). The current circular updates the list of securities under the ESM framework per the established criteria.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must ensure minimum 100% margin is collected on open positions in the listed ESM Stage I securities as on May 27, 2026
- Members must collect 100% margin on all new positions in affected securities created from May 29, 2026
- Trading in affected securities must be conducted in the BE/ST (Trade-for-Trade) series from May 29, 2026
- GAUDIUMIVF trades must comply with Stage II restrictions: Trade-for-Trade with 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction from May 27, 2026
- Members may refer to FAQs on the NSE website for more details on the ESM framework
- Queries may be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Important Dates

- **May 27, 2026**: ESM Stage I inclusion effective date for all 9 new securities; GAUDIUMIVF moves to Stage II; 2% price band under Periodic Call Auction for Stage II securities begins
- **May 27, 2026**: Open position snapshot date for 100% margin applicability
- **May 29, 2026**: Shift from Rolling Settlement (EQ/SM) to Trade-for-Trade (BE/ST) for newly included ESM Stage I securities; 100% margin on new positions effective

## Impact Assessment

The inclusion of 9 securities under ESM Stage I significantly restricts their tradability. The mandatory shift to the Trade-for-Trade segment eliminates the ability to net off intraday positions, requiring full delivery settlement for all trades. The 100% margin requirement substantially increases the capital needed to hold positions, likely causing reduced liquidity and increased volatility in the affected securities. GAUDIUMIVF faces even stricter conditions under Stage II, with a tight 2% price band and Periodic Call Auction mechanism severely limiting price discovery and trade frequency. Market participants holding existing positions in these securities should be prepared for higher margin calls and reduced exit flexibility. The affected securities span diverse sectors including technology (ALLETEC, INSPIRISYS, VERTOZ), education (CLEDUCATE), chemicals (NEOCHEM), electric vehicles (VICTORYEV), and others.
