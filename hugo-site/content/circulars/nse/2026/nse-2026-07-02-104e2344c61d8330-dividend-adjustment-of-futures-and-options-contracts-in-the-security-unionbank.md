## Summary

NSE has issued a circular adjusting Futures and Options contracts in UNIONBANK on account of a dividend declared by Union Bank of India. The dividend of Rs 5.00 per share (face value Rs 10/-) will result in revised option strike prices effective from the ex-date/effective date of July 3, 2026.

## Key Points

- Symbol: UNIONBANK; Company: Union Bank of India
- Corporate action type: Dividend
- Dividend amount: Rs 5.00 per share; Face value: Rs 10/-
- Ex date & effective date: 03-July-2026
- Revised strike prices apply to OPTSTK contracts expiring 28-JUL-2026, detailed in Annexure 1
- Adjustment methodology available at NSE's equity derivatives corporate actions adjustments page

## Regulatory Changes

Adjustments are made in pursuance of SEBI guidelines for adjustment of futures and options contracts upon announcement of corporate actions. Strike prices for UNIONBANK options have been revised downward by Rs 5.00 to reflect the dividend payout (e.g., 140.00 to 135.00, 145.00 to 140.00, and so on across the strike ladder).

## Compliance Requirements

- Members must load the updated contract.gz / MII contract file (NSE_FO_contract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz) and spread file (NSE_FO_spdcontract_ddmmyyyy.csv.gz) on their trading applications before trading on the ex-date/effective date.
- These files are available from the faoftp/faocommon directory on the Extranet server, and MII contract and spread files are also available on the NSE website's all-reports-derivatives page.
- Position adjustment methodology will be separately communicated by the respective Clearing Corporation.

## Important Dates

- Ex-date & effective date: July 3, 2026
- Affected contract expiry referenced in Annexure 1: 28-JUL-2026

## Impact Assessment

The adjustment is a routine, mechanical correction to option strike prices and futures base prices necessitated by the dividend, ensuring contract values remain economically neutral post ex-date. Impact is limited to market participants holding or trading UNIONBANK F&O contracts; no broader market-wide effect is expected. Members must ensure timely system updates to avoid mispriced trades or settlement discrepancies.
