## Summary

NSE has informed members that, per communication from Edelweiss Mutual Fund, fresh systematic registrations (new monthly SIP and STP) have been suspended in seven designated overseas-focused schemes on the NSE MF Invest Platform, effective from the close of business hours on July 10, 2026. This follows an earlier notice dated October 13, 2025, which had capped fresh systematic subscriptions in these schemes at INR 5,000 per PAN per day due to overseas investment limits set by SEBI. The AMC's available headroom for overseas investment under the February 1, 2022 mutual fund-level limit is now nearing exhaustion, prompting this suspension.

## Key Points

- Fresh (new) monthly SIP and STP registrations suspended in seven Edelweiss Mutual Fund schemes effective close of business hours July 10, 2026.
- No restrictions on redemptions, Systematic Withdrawal Plans (SWP), switch-out, or STP-out transactions.
- Existing SIPs and STPs already registered will continue to operate normally.
- Suspension applies specifically to the NSE MF Invest Platform.
- This follows a prior October 13, 2025 notice that had capped fresh systematic subscriptions at INR 5,000 per PAN per day in these schemes.

## Regulatory Changes

The suspension stems from SEBI letter no. SEBI/HO/OW/IMD-II/DOF3/25095/2022 dated June 17, 2022, which permitted mutual funds to resume overseas investments up to the headroom available without breaching overseas investment limits set at the mutual fund level as of February 1, 2022. As Edelweiss Mutual Fund's available headroom nears its threshold, the AMC has decided to suspend fresh systematic subscriptions in the affected schemes. Corresponding changes will be made to the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM) of the designated schemes.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members must stop accepting fresh SIP/STP registration requests for the seven designated schemes with effect from close of business hours July 10, 2026.
- Members should continue processing redemptions, SWPs, switch-outs, and STP-outs without restriction.
- Existing systematic transactions (SIPs/STPs) already registered prior to the effective date should continue uninterrupted.
- The notice-cum-addendum forms an integral part of the SID/KIM/SAI of the affected schemes and overrides conflicting provisions.

## Important Dates

- October 13, 2025: Original notice capping fresh systematic subscriptions at INR 5,000 per PAN per day.
- July 10, 2026 (close of business hours): Effective date for suspension of fresh SIP/STP registrations in the designated schemes.

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to investors seeking to newly register SIPs or STPs in the seven affected overseas-focused Edelweiss Mutual Fund schemes: Edelweiss ASEAN Equity Off-shore Fund, Edelweiss Greater China Equity Off-shore Fund, Edelweiss US Technology Equity Fund of Fund, Edelweiss Emerging Markets Opportunities Equity Offshore Fund, Edelweiss Europe Dynamic Equity Offshore Fund, Edelweiss US Value Equity Off-shore Fund, and Edelweiss MSCI India Domestic & World Healthcare 45 Index Fund. Existing investors and systematic transactions are unaffected, and redemption/switch-out flexibility is preserved, minimizing disruption. The restriction reflects broader constraints on Indian mutual funds' overseas investment headroom under SEBI's regulatory framework rather than any scheme-specific performance or risk issue.
