## Summary

NSE's Listing Department has notified that the face value and paid-up value of two ETFs managed by DSP Asset Managers Private Limited will be reduced from Rs. 10 to Re. 1 per unit, effective July 03, 2026. The affected ETFs are DSP Nifty Midcap 150 Quality 50 ETF (MIDQ50ADD) and DSP Nifty Healthcare ETF (HEALTHADD).

## Key Points

- Face value and paid-up value for both ETFs will change from Rs. 10 to Re. 1
- Two ETFs affected: DSP Nifty Midcap 150 Quality 50 ETF (MIDQ50ADD) and DSP Nifty Healthcare ETF (HEALTHADD)
- The split ratio is effectively 10:1 (Rs. 10 → Re. 1)
- Circular issued by NSE Listing Department (Ref No: NSE/CML/74765, Circular Ref. No: 0985/2026)

## Regulatory Changes

The face value and paid-up value of MIDQ50ADD and HEALTHADD will be revised downward from Rs. 10 to Re. 1 per unit as per NSE's notification under its Listing Department circular.

## Compliance Requirements

All NSE members are required to note the revised face value for both ETFs and update their trading, settlement, and reporting systems accordingly prior to July 03, 2026. Unit-related calculations (lot sizes, margins, bhavcopy reporting) should reflect the new face value from the effective date.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 18, 2026
- **Effective Date:** July 03, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The face value split from Rs. 10 to Re. 1 is an administrative/structural change for unit holders of MIDQ50ADD and HEALTHADD. Existing investors will see their unit count increase proportionately while the total portfolio value remains unchanged. Trading systems, back-office software, and margin calculation frameworks that rely on face value data for these symbols must be updated before the effective date to avoid discrepancies in settlement and reporting.
