## Summary

NSE has issued a circular under Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A, notifying the suspension of trading in three privately placed Non-Convertible Securities (NCS) effective June 12, 2026. The suspensions are due to redemption of the respective instruments issued by Power Grid Corporation of India Limited and LIC Housing Finance Limited.

## Key Points

- Three NCS ISINs are suspended from trading on NSE effective June 12, 2026.
- All suspensions are due to redemption of the underlying debt instruments.
- Affected issuers: Power Grid Corporation of India Limited (1 ISIN) and LIC Housing Finance Limited (2 ISINs).
- Circular issued by the Listing Department under Ref No. NSE/CML/74650, Circular Ref. No. 0935/2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. The suspension is executed in pursuance of Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A, which governs the suspension of debt securities upon scheduled events such as redemption.

## Compliance Requirements

- Members are advised to note the suspension dates and ensure no trading takes place in the affected ISINs from the suspension date onwards.
- No specific compliance action is required from members beyond awareness of the suspended securities.

## Important Dates

- **June 11, 2026**: Circular issued.
- **June 12, 2026**: Suspension effective date for all three ISINs.

| Sr. No. | Company Name | ISIN | Suspension Date | Reason |
|---------|-------------|------|-----------------|--------|
| 1 | Power Grid Corporation of India Limited | INE752E07JZ5 | 12-June-2026 | Redemption |
| 2 | LIC Housing Finance Limited | INE115A07JT6 | 12-June-2026 | Redemption |
| 3 | LIC Housing Finance Limited | INE115A07JS8 | 12-June-2026 | Redemption |

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited and routine. Redemption-driven suspensions are standard lifecycle events for debt securities and indicate the instruments have reached their maturity. Holders of these NCS instruments will receive redemption proceeds as per the terms of issuance. There is no adverse market impact; the suspension simply removes the securities from the tradable universe on NSE's debt market segment.
