## Summary

NSE has notified the suspension of trading in five privately placed Non-Convertible Securities (NCS) with effect from July 9, 2026, in accordance with Regulation 3.1.2 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A. The suspensions are due to redemption of the securities or exercise of call options by the respective issuers.

## Key Points

- Trading will be suspended in NCS of 5 issuers effective July 9, 2026
- Aditya Birla Sun Life Insurance Company Limited (ISIN: INE951F08028) — suspended due to redemption for exercising call option
- Nomura Capital (India) Private Limited (ISIN: INE357L07432) — suspended due to redemption
- Aditya Birla Capital Limited (ISIN: INE860H07HN9) — suspended due to redemption
- IDFC First Bank Limited (ISIN: INE688I08160) — suspended due to redemption
- Aditya Birla Housing Finance Limited (ISIN: INE831R08043) — suspended due to redemption

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an implementation of existing Regulation 3.1.2 of the NSE Debt Market (Trading) Regulations Part A governing suspension of trading upon redemption or call option exercise of privately placed debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

Members must cease trading in the specified ISINs from the suspension date. No further action is required from investors beyond awareness of the suspension for settlement and holding purposes.

## Important Dates

- Circular date: July 8, 2026
- Suspension effective date: July 9, 2026 (for all five securities)

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to holders and traders of these specific privately placed NCS ISINs on NSE's debt market segment. This is a routine, procedural suspension tied to redemption or call option exercise and does not indicate any credit or compliance issue with the issuers. No broader market impact is expected.
