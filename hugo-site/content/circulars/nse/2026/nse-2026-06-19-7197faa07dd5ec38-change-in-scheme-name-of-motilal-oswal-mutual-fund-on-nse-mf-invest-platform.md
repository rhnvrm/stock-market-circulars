## Summary

NSE has informed its members that Motilal Oswal Asset Allocation Passive Fund of Fund – Aggressive will be renamed to **Motilal Oswal Multi Asset Passive Fund of Fund** with effect from **June 22, 2026** on the NSE MF Invest platform. This change is part of a broader recategorization of the scheme in compliance with SEBI's framework for Fund of Fund (FoF) schemes.

## Key Points

- Existing scheme name: **Motilal Oswal Asset Allocation Passive Fund of Fund – Aggressive**
- Revised scheme name: **Motilal Oswal Multi Asset Passive Fund of Fund**
- Effective date of change: **June 22, 2026**
- Scheme sub-category changes from "Fund of Fund" to **Multi Asset Allocation FoF**
- Scheme category changes from "Other Schemes" to **Other Schemes: Hybrid FOF (Domestic)**
- The scheme type description is updated to reflect investing in units of equity, debt, gold and international index funds/ETFs
- Investment objective revised to reflect a multi-asset portfolio approach including commodity ETFs/Index Funds

## Regulatory Changes

The recategorization is in accordance with SEBI's letter no. SEBI/HO/IMD/IMD-RAC-1/P/OW/2025/4102/1 dated February 6, 2025, which introduced a "Framework for launching of Fund of Fund (FOFs) Schemes with multiple underlying funds." Motilal Oswal Trustee Company Limited, as Trustee to Motilal Oswal Mutual Fund, has approved the changes to align the scheme with this framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- NSE members dealing in Motilal Oswal Mutual Fund schemes on the NSE MF Invest platform should update their records to reflect the new scheme name from June 22, 2026.
- Distributors and investment advisers should communicate the scheme name and category change to affected unitholders as required.
- Unitholders and members should refer to the Notice cum Addendum issued by Motilal Oswal Mutual Fund (enclosed with the circular) for complete details of all revised scheme features.

## Important Dates

- **June 19, 2026** – Circular issued by NSE
- **June 22, 2026** – Effective date for scheme name change and recategorization on NSE MF Invest platform

## Impact Assessment

This is an administrative and regulatory change with limited market impact. Existing unitholders of the scheme will see their holdings reflected under the new scheme name post June 22, 2026. The change aligns the fund with SEBI's updated FoF framework and broadens its multi-asset mandate to include commodity ETFs alongside equity, debt, and gold/international index funds. No redemption or subscription disruption is indicated by the circular.
