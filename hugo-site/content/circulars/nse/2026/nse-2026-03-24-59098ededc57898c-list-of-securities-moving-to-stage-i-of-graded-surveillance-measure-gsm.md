## Summary

NSE has moved ORTEL COMMUNICATIONS LIMITED (Symbol: ORTEL, ISIN: INE849L01019) to Stage I of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework effective March 25, 2026. This imposes a 100% margin requirement and a price band of 5% or lower on the security.

## Key Points

- ORTEL COMMUNICATIONS LIMITED (ORTEL) is being placed under Stage I of GSM effective March 25, 2026
- ISIN: INE849L01019
- Applicable margin rate: 100%
- Price band: 5% or lower as applicable
- Members are advised to take adequate precaution while trading in this security
- Queries can be directed to surveillance@nse.co.in

## Regulatory Changes

This circular is issued in continuation of earlier GSM circulars: NSE/SURV/34262, NSE/SURV/38389, NSE/SURV/42790, NSE/SURV/59425, NSE/SURV/64066, and NSE/SURV/73167 dated February 23, 2017, July 20, 2018, November 29, 2019, November 17, 2023, September 20, 2024, and March 06, 2026 respectively. The GSM framework provides a staged surveillance mechanism with progressively stricter trading restrictions.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must apply 100% margin rate when trading ORTEL
- Price band of 5% or lower must be enforced
- Members must exercise adequate precaution while trading in this security

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: March 24, 2026
- Effective Date: March 25, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The placement of ORTEL COMMUNICATIONS LIMITED under Stage I GSM significantly restricts trading activity. The 100% margin requirement means buyers must deposit the full trade value as margin, substantially increasing the cost of taking positions. The 5% price band limits daily price movement, reducing volatility but also constraining price discovery. Traders and investors holding or planning to trade ORTEL will face higher capital requirements. This is the least restrictive stage of GSM; further non-compliance or adverse price behavior could escalate to higher stages with even stricter restrictions including Trade-for-Trade settlement and weekly trading limits.
