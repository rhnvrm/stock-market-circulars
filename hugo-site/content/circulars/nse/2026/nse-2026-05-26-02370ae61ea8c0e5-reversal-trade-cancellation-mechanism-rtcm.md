## Summary

NSE has published the updated list of securities in the Capital Market (CM) segment subject to the Reversal Trade Cancellation Mechanism (RTCM) for June 2026. The list comprises approximately 280 stocks across various sectors. Under RTCM, intra-day reversal trades (where a client buys and subsequently sells, or sells and subsequently buys, the same security on the same day) in listed securities may be identified and cancelled by the exchange as a surveillance measure.

## Key Points

- The RTCM list for June 2026 contains approximately 280 securities in the NSE Capital Market segment.
- Reversal trades (same client, same security, same day buy-sell or sell-buy) in these securities are subject to cancellation.
- The mechanism is intended to deter manipulative or suspicious intra-day trading patterns.
- Securities span small-cap and mid-cap segments across diverse industries.
- The updated list supersedes the previous month's RTCM list and takes effect for June 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

The RTCM list is refreshed monthly by NSE under its surveillance framework. No structural change to the mechanism is indicated; this circular represents the routine update of in-scope securities for the June 2026 period.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members and their clients must be aware that reversal trades in any of the listed securities during June 2026 are liable to be cancelled by NSE.
- Brokers should notify clients with positions in these securities about the applicable RTCM restrictions.
- Algorithmic and high-frequency trading strategies that involve intra-day reversal patterns in these securities should be reviewed and adjusted accordingly.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Period:** June 2026
- **Circular Date:** 2026-05-26

## Impact Assessment

The RTCM applies to a large number of primarily small and mid-cap stocks. Traders and investors engaged in intra-day strategies involving these securities face the risk of trade cancellation, which could result in unintended open positions. Market participants, particularly proprietary desks and retail intra-day traders, should review their exposure to the listed securities for June 2026. The breadth of the list (280+ symbols) suggests continued surveillance focus on potential reversal-trade manipulation in the broader small-cap universe.
