## Summary

NSE has released its periodic update to the Short-Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework, effective July 14, 2026. The circular lists securities newly shortlisted under ST-ASM Stage I and Stage II, securities moving between stages, and securities excluded from the ASM framework altogether. A consolidated list of all securities currently under ASM (Stage I and II) is also provided.

## Key Points

- Two securities newly placed under ST-ASM Stage I: BUILDPRO (Shankara Buildpro Limited) and TAURIAN (Taurian MPS Limited).
- No securities newly placed under ST-ASM Stage II.
- One security moved from Stage I to Stage II: UTKAL (Utkal Speciality Industries India Limited).
- No securities moved from Stage II to Stage I.
- Seven securities excluded from the ASM framework entirely: AMCL (ANB Metal Cast Limited), BHARATSE (Bharat Seats Limited), CARTRADE (Cartrade Tech Limited), GRMOVER (GRM Overseas Limited), NFPSAMPOOR (NFP Sampoorna Foods Limited), VMARCIND (V Marc India Limited), VOGL (Vedanta Oil and Gas Limited).
- A consolidated list of all securities currently under ASM Stage I/II is included in the annexure (over 30 securities, mostly Stage I).

## Regulatory Changes

No change to the ST-ASM framework rules themselves; this is a routine periodic update to the list of securities shortlisted, migrated between stages, or excluded under the existing surveillance framework.

## Compliance Requirements

Trading members and market participants must apply the enhanced surveillance requirements (e.g., higher margins, price band restrictions) to newly added ST-ASM securities and may resume normal trading treatment for excluded securities, effective from the stated date.

## Important Dates

All changes are effective July 14, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

Securities newly added to or moved to a higher ST-ASM stage will face stricter trading conditions (increased margin requirements, potential price band restrictions), which may reduce liquidity and increase trading costs for these stocks. Securities excluded from ASM will see normalized trading conditions, potentially improving liquidity. Overall market impact is limited to the specific securities listed, as this is a routine surveillance update rather than a broad regulatory change.
