## Summary

SEBI has issued a Notice of Attachment of Demat Accounts dated November 17, 2025, under Recovery Certificate No. 8929 of 2025 against Prafulla Subhash Chandra Bhat (PAN: AMEPB0155F). This enforcement action is in relation to the matter of Prakash Constrowell Limited (now known as Setubandhan Infrastructure Limited). The notice authorizes attachment of demat accounts to recover outstanding dues.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. 8929 of 2025 issued against Prafulla Subhash Chandra Bhat
- Notice dated November 17, 2025
- PAN of noticee: AMEPB0155F
- Related to matter of Prakash Constrowell Limited (now Setubandhan Infrastructure Limited)
- Enforcement action involves attachment of demat accounts
- Legal action under SEBI recovery proceedings

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is an enforcement action under existing SEBI recovery mechanisms for outstanding dues.

## Compliance Requirements

- Depositories and depository participants must comply with the attachment notice
- Demat accounts of Prafulla Subhash Chandra Bhat must be frozen/attached as per the recovery certificate
- No transactions to be permitted in the attached demat accounts until further orders
- Entities holding demat accounts for the noticee must comply with SEBI's attachment directions

## Important Dates

- **November 17, 2025**: Date of Notice of Attachment
- **Recovery Certificate No. 8929 of 2025**: Issued in 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Individual Impact**: High - The attachment of demat accounts directly affects Prafulla Subhash Chandra Bhat's ability to trade or transfer securities, effectively freezing holdings until the recovery proceedings are resolved.

**Market Impact**: Low to Medium - While this is a significant enforcement action, it affects a specific individual in relation to a particular company matter. The impact is primarily on the noticee and related parties rather than broader market operations.

**Company Impact**: Medium - The matter relates to Prakash Constrowell Limited (now Setubandhan Infrastructure Limited), suggesting historical regulatory issues with the company that may affect investor perception.

**Precedent**: This action demonstrates SEBI's active use of recovery mechanisms and attachment powers to enforce regulatory compliance and recover dues from violators.
