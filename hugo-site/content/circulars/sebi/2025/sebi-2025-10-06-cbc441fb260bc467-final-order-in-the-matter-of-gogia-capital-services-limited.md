## Summary

SEBI issued a final order under Section 12(3) of the SEBI Act, 1992 read with Regulation 27 of SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008 against Gogia Capital Services Limited (Registration No. INZ000202733, PAN: AAACG2906L), a registered Stock Broker. The order follows a thematic inspection conducted by SEBI along with BSE, NSE, and MCX for the period April 01, 2022 to July 31, 2023, which revealed multiple violations of securities regulations.

## Key Points

- Gogia Capital Services Limited violated multiple provisions of SCRR, Stock Brokers Regulations, and various SEBI circulars
- Inspection period covered: April 01, 2022 to July 31, 2023
- Show cause notice issued on March 28, 2024 by the Designated Authority
- Three major categories of violations identified: net worth shortfall/improper books of accounts, engaging in unauthorized business activities, and non-settlement of clients' funds and securities
- Enquiry proceedings initiated under SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This order enforces existing provisions:
- Section 12(1) of SEBI Act, 1992
- Rule 8(3)(f) of SCRR
- Regulation 9(g) and Schedule VI of Stock Brokers Regulations
- Multiple SEBI circulars from 1997 to 2022 regarding broker compliance

## Compliance Requirements

**For Stock Brokers:**
- Maintain adequate net worth as per regulatory requirements
- Properly maintain books of accounts in accordance with Schedule VI of Stock Brokers Regulations
- Not engage as principal or employee in any business other than securities involving personal financial liability
- Timely settlement of clients' funds and securities as per prescribed timelines
- Comply with all provisions of SEBI Master Circular and related circulars on client fund management

## Important Dates

- **Inspection Period:** April 01, 2022 to July 31, 2023
- **Inspection Findings Communicated:** November 30, 2023
- **Noticee Response:** December 05, 2023
- **Designated Authority Appointed:** March 06, 2024
- **Show Cause Notice Issued:** March 28, 2024

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Limited direct market impact as this concerns a specific intermediary.

**Regulatory Impact:** Reinforces SEBI's strict enforcement of intermediary compliance requirements, particularly regarding:
- Net worth maintenance standards
- Segregation and timely settlement of client funds
- Restrictions on brokers engaging in other businesses
- Proper accounting and record-keeping practices

**Operational Impact:** 
- Stock brokers must ensure robust internal controls and compliance systems
- Enhanced scrutiny expected on net worth calculations and business activities
- Intermediaries should review their client fund settlement processes to ensure regulatory compliance

**Investor Protection:** The violations related to non-settlement of client funds and securities highlight critical investor protection concerns that SEBI is addressing through this enforcement action.
