## Summary

SEBI has issued a completion order for Recovery Certificate No. 7976 of 2024 dated November 21, 2024, against Raisuddin Enterprise (PAN: ACKPU8773A) in connection with violations related to dealing in illiquid stock options at the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE). This order confirms the completion of recovery proceedings against the entity for regulatory non-compliance.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. 7976 of 2024 issued against Raisuddin Enterprise
- Case relates to dealing in illiquid stock options at BSE
- Entity identified by PAN: ACKPU8773A
- Completion order confirms conclusion of recovery proceedings
- Enforcement action taken under SEBI's regulatory framework

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This order represents enforcement of existing regulations governing trading in illiquid stock options and market manipulation prevention.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must avoid engaging in trading practices involving illiquid stock options that violate SEBI regulations
- Entities subject to recovery certificates must comply with payment obligations
- Brokers and trading members should ensure client compliance with regulations on illiquid securities trading
- Enhanced due diligence required for transactions in illiquid stock options

## Important Dates

- **November 21, 2024**: Recovery Certificate No. 7976 of 2024 issued
- **November 13, 2025**: Completion order published

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Limited direct market impact as the action targets a specific entity for past violations. The order serves as a deterrent for similar regulatory violations.

**Operational Impact**: Entities engaged in options trading, particularly in illiquid securities, should review their compliance frameworks. The completion of recovery proceedings demonstrates SEBI's commitment to enforcing penalties and recovering dues from violators.

**Regulatory Significance**: Reinforces SEBI's enforcement mechanism for market manipulation and improper trading practices in derivatives markets, particularly concerning illiquid instruments where manipulation risks are higher.
