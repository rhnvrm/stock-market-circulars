## Summary

SEBI's Appellate Authority dismissed Appeal No. 6612 of 2025 filed by Mr. Talada Sandeep regarding non-availability of his SEBI Grade A Phase II Examination 2024 score card for Electrical Stream. The appellant sought his score cards for both General Stream and Electrical Stream examinations held on August 31, 2024. The CPIO informed that the information is available on SEBI's website under the "Careers" section and can be accessed using credentials from the Electrical Stream admit card. The Appellate Authority upheld the CPIO's response, citing that providing a link to publicly available information constitutes adequate compliance under the RTI Act.

## Key Points

- Appellant appeared for SEBI Grade A Phase II Examination 2024 under both General Stream and Electrical Stream
- Score card for General Stream was available but Electrical Stream score card was not displayed on portal
- CPIO responded that information is available on SEBI website under "Careers" section
- Appellant can access Electrical Stream score card using credentials from admit card
- Appeal filed on grounds of incomplete, misleading or false information
- Appellate Authority found no deficiency in CPIO's response
- Information available on website is considered in public domain

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is an administrative order disposing of an RTI appeal.

## Compliance Requirements

Not applicable. This order relates to individual RTI appeal regarding examination score card access.

## Important Dates

- **October 09, 2025**: RTI application filed by appellant
- **November 03, 2025**: CPIO response to RTI application
- **November 06, 2025**: Appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/25/00290)
- **Examination Date**: August 31, 2024 (SEBI Grade A Phase II Examination)

## Impact Assessment

This order has no market impact or regulatory significance. It is an administrative decision on an individual RTI appeal concerning access to examination score cards. The order reaffirms the principle that information available on public websites is in the public domain and providing a link to such information constitutes adequate compliance under the RTI Act, 2005. The decision has no implications for market participants, listed entities, or investors.
