## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer issued Adjudication Order No. Order/MS/RG/2026-27/32458 in respect of Late Sudha V Thakkar (PAN: AEUPT7605Q) concerning alleged dealing in Illiquid Stock Options (ISO) at BSE. The matter arose from a SEBI investigation into large-scale reversal of trades in illiquid stock options on BSE during the investigation period from April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015, which created artificial volume. Investigation found that 2,91,744 trades (81.41% of all trades executed in BSE's stock options segment) involved reversal of buy and sell positions between clients and counterparties, lacking genuine trading rationale. Of 14,720 entities found to have executed such non-genuine trades, the Noticee was identified as one of the participants. A Show Cause Notice dated August 02, 2022 was issued alleging violations of regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1) and 4(2)(a) of the SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices) Regulations, 2003.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated reversal trades in illiquid stock options on BSE for the period April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015.
- 2,91,744 trades (81.41% of all BSE stock options trades) during this period were identified as non-genuine reversal trades creating artificial volume.
- 14,720 entities, including the Noticee Sudha V Thakkar, were found to have executed such reversal trades.
- Alleged violations: regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1) and 4(2)(a) of the PFUTP Regulations, 2003.
- Show Cause Notice issued on August 02, 2022 under Rule 4(1) of the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995.
- A new Adjudicating Officer was appointed for the matter via communique dated June 01, 2026, following transfer from the erstwhile AO.
- The Noticee is referred to as deceased ("Late") in the order title.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an individual enforcement/adjudication action under existing PFUTP Regulations, 2003 and the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Compliance Requirements

This order pertains to a closed enforcement proceeding against an individual entity for historical trading conduct; it does not impose new compliance obligations on market participants generally.

## Important Dates

- Investigation Period: April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015
- Show Cause Notice issued: August 02, 2022
- New AO appointed: June 01, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The order has limited market-wide impact as it concerns a single individual's historical trading conduct in illiquid stock options at BSE. It is part of SEBI's broader, long-running enforcement effort against non-genuine reversal trading in illiquid stock options that affected thousands of entities, reinforcing regulatory scrutiny of artificial volume creation in derivatives markets.
