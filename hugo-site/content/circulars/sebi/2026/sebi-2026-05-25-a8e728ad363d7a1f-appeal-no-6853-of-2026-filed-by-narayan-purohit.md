## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority dismissed Appeal No. 6853 of 2026 filed by Narayan Purohit against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought a copy of the Draft Red Herring Prospectus (DRHP) filed by Shlokka Dyes Ltd (CIN U24299GJ2021PLC124004) on the BSE SME Platform on January 16, 2025, which was subsequently withdrawn due to non-receipt of NOC from the lender bank (State Bank of India, Ahmedabad). The Authority upheld the respondent's response, finding no deficiency as the DRHP is available in the public domain.

## Key Points

- Appellant Narayan Purohit filed RTI application dated April 07, 2026 seeking DRHP of Shlokka Dyes Ltd filed on BSE SME Platform
- SEBI CPIO responded on April 28, 2026, informing that the DRHP is available in the public domain and providing a link to access it
- Appellant filed appeal on the ground that information provided was incomplete, misleading, or false
- Appellate Authority found the DRHP filed by Shlokka Dyes Ltd dated January 16, 2025 is accessible from the BSE website
- Appeal dismissed; no interference with the respondent's decision warranted

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine RTI appellate order with no new directives.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements arising from this order. The order reaffirms the established principle that information available in the public domain need not be separately provided under the RTI Act, citing:
- Delhi High Court: Registrar of Companies & ors. Vs. Dharmendra Kumar Garg & anr.
- Central Information Commission: Shri K Lall vs. Shri M K Bagri (CIC/AT/A/2007/00112, April 12, 2007)

## Important Dates

- April 07, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant
- January 16, 2025: DRHP filed by Shlokka Dyes Ltd on BSE SME Platform
- April 28, 2026: CPIO response and appellant's appeal filing (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00155)
- May 25, 2026: Appellate Authority order issued dismissing the appeal

## Impact Assessment

No market or operational impact. This is an administrative RTI appellate order concerning access to a publicly available DRHP. Shlokka Dyes Ltd had withdrawn its SME IPO filing from BSE due to non-receipt of NOC from State Bank of India (Ahmedabad). The ruling has no bearing on listed securities, trading, or broader market participants.
