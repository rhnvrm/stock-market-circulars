## Summary

SEBI has released a Consultation Paper (PR No. 36/2026) proposing a Common Advertisement Code (CAC) to replace the existing fragmented, entity-specific advertisement frameworks applicable to Stock Brokers, Depository Participants, Investment Advisers, Research Analysts, Online Bond Platform Providers, Portfolio Managers, and Mutual Funds/AMCs. The CAC is proposed to be embedded in the SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008. Public comments are invited until July 14, 2026.

## Key Points

- Prior approval requirement for advertisements proposed to be replaced by post-issuance reporting within 24 hours
- Celebrity endorsements to be permitted for brand-level/entity-level promotion subject to prescribed conditions and prior approval
- Single unified CAC to replace all existing entity-specific and exchange-specific advertisement codes
- Regulated entities permitted to advertise ratings and rankings assigned by PaRRVA (Past Risk and Return Verification Agency) under prescribed conditions
- Revised definition of "advertisement" to clearly distinguish promotional communications from routine investor-service communications
- Illustrative list of communications not regarded as advertisements to be provided
- Supervisory Bodies to develop a Common Reporting Portal (digital platform) for advertisement reporting

## Regulatory Changes

- Transition from mandatory prior approval to post-issuance reporting within 24 hours for advertisements
- CAC to be embedded in SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008
- Unified framework to eliminate regulatory complexity and reduce compliance burden across all regulated entities
- Revised and clearer definition of "advertisement" to remove ambiguity
- Recognition of PaRRVA ratings and rankings in advertising subject to safeguards
- Celebrity endorsements permitted for brand/entity-level promotion (not product-level) with prior approval

## Compliance Requirements

- Once implemented, regulated entities must report advertisements post-issuance within 24 hours (replacing prior approval)
- Celebrity endorsements require prior approval and compliance with prescribed conditions
- Advertisement of PaRRVA ratings/rankings subject to prescribed conditions
- All advertisement reporting to be done via the Common Reporting Portal developed by Supervisory Bodies
- Entities must distinguish promotional communications from routine factual/investor-service communications per the revised definition

## Important Dates

- **June 23, 2026**: Consultation paper released by SEBI
- **July 14, 2026**: Last date for submission of public comments on the consultation paper

## Impact Assessment

This proposal affects a wide range of SEBI-regulated intermediaries including Stock Brokers, Depository Participants, Investment Advisers, Research Analysts, Online Bond Platform Providers, Portfolio Managers, and AMCs/Mutual Funds. The shift from prior approval to post-issuance reporting is expected to reduce compliance burden and improve ease of doing business. Permitting celebrity endorsements at the brand level opens new marketing avenues for intermediaries. The Common Reporting Portal will streamline regulatory oversight. The overall impact is medium — while the scope is broad, the changes are largely operational and compliance-focused, and the framework is still in consultation stage with no immediate effect.
