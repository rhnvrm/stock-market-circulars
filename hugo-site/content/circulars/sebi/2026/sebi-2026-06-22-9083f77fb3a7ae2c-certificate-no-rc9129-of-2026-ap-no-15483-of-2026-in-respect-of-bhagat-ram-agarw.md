## Summary

SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9129 of 2026 in Adjudication Proceeding No. AP 15483 of 2026 against Bhagat Ram Agarwal HUF (PAN: AAGHB3924E). The certificate pertains to SEBI's ongoing enforcement actions in the matter of illiquid stock options, where entities were found to have engaged in non-genuine trades in the illiquid stock options segment.

## Key Points

- **Recovery Certificate No.:** RC9129 of 2026
- **Adjudication Proceeding No.:** AP 15483 of 2026
- **Entity:** Bhagat Ram Agarwal HUF (Hindu Undivided Family)
- **PAN:** AAGHB3924E
- **Matter:** Illiquid Stock Options — non-genuine/manipulative trading
- A Recovery Certificate is issued when an adjudicated penalty remains unpaid, enabling SEBI to recover dues as arrears of land revenue

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is an enforcement action under existing SEBI regulations pertaining to prevention of fraudulent and unfair trade practices in the illiquid stock options segment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Bhagat Ram Agarwal HUF is required to pay the penalty amount as specified in the adjudication order under AP 15483 of 2026.
- Failure to comply with the Recovery Certificate may result in coercive recovery proceedings under the applicable provisions of law.

## Important Dates

- **Certificate Issued:** June 2026
- Specific payment deadline and original adjudication order date are contained in the full certificate document.

## Impact Assessment

This enforcement action is specific to Bhagat Ram Agarwal HUF and has no direct impact on the broader market or other market participants. It is part of SEBI's sustained crackdown on illiquid stock options manipulation, where entities engaged in reversal trades to book fictitious losses or gains. The issuance of a Recovery Certificate indicates the penalty was not paid voluntarily following the adjudication order, prompting SEBI to initiate compulsory recovery proceedings.
