## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority has dismissed Appeal No. 6854 of 2026 filed by Mayur Balaji Mamidwar against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought information regarding SEBI's action on a complaint filed by JPPL against Garware Synthetics Limited on August 14, 2025, including the company's reply to SEBI and the current status of the complaint. SEBI denied the information, and the Appellate Authority upheld the denial under Section 8(1)(h) of the RTI Act.

## Key Points

- Appellant Mayur Balaji Mamidwar filed an RTI application on March 16, 2026 seeking details of SEBI's action on a complaint filed by JPPL against Garware Synthetics Limited
- Information sought: (1) action taken by SEBI against Garware Synthetics Limited; (2) copy of Garware Synthetics Limited's reply/representation to SEBI; (3) current status of the JPPL complaint
- SEBI's CPIO responded on April 13, 2026 stating that examinations and investigations are conducted confidentially and SEBI will neither confirm nor deny their existence
- Appellant filed the appeal on April 28, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00156) challenging the refusal of information
- Appellate Authority upheld the denial citing Section 8(1)(h) of the RTI Act (exemption for information that would impede investigation or prosecution)

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This order reaffirms SEBI's established position on confidentiality of examination and investigation proceedings.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. The order is specific to the RTI appeal filed by the appellant.

## Important Dates

- August 14, 2025: Original complaint filed by JPPL before SEBI against Garware Synthetics Limited
- March 16, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant
- April 13, 2026: CPIO response to RTI application
- April 28, 2026: Appeal filed by appellant (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00156)

## Impact Assessment

This order has minimal market impact. It is a routine RTI appellate decision confirming SEBI's standard practice of maintaining confidentiality over ongoing examinations and investigations. The Appellate Authority relied on precedent from the Central Information Commission (CIC) in Manju Devi v CPIO, SEBI (Order dated April 29, 2025). Any enforcement actions against Garware Synthetics Limited, if and when taken, would be published as Orders on the SEBI website in the public domain.
