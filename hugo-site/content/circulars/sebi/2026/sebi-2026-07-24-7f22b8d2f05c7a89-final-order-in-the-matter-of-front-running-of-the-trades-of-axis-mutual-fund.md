## Summary

SEBI has passed a final order under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1) and 11B(2) of the SEBI Act, 1992 in the matter of front running of the trades of Axis Mutual Fund. The order follows an earlier interim order cum show cause notice (SCN) issued ex-parte, after which SEBI conducted inspections, allowed cross-examination, and received replies and hearings from 21 noticees. The noticees include Mr. Viresh Joshi (alleged to be a dealer/trader with access to Axis Mutual Fund's trade information), his associates and family members, and multiple connected individuals and entities (including M K B Bespoke Audio General, Olga Trading Pvt. Ltd., and Visa Capital Partners) alleged to have traded ahead of the mutual fund's large orders to profit from the anticipated price impact.

## Key Points

- Final order passed against 21 noticees for front running trades of Axis Mutual Fund.
- Proceeding originated from an ex-parte interim order cum show cause notice.
- Noticees raised preliminary objections including alleged violation of principles of natural justice, lack of urgency for the ex-parte interim order, denial of request to revoke interim directions despite deposit of alleged illegal gains, deficiencies in the investigation report, non-supply of trade logs, extraterritorial jurisdiction issues, non-impleadment of a necessary party, and questions on the evidentiary value of Mr. Viresh Joshi's statements.
- SEBI examined and ruled on these preliminary issues before proceeding to findings on the substantive front running allegations.
- The order covers individuals, family members, and corporate/proprietary entities allegedly used as conduits for the front running trades.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement order applying existing SEBI Act provisions (Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1), 11B(2)) and SEBI's PFUTP (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices) framework to the specific facts of this front running case.

## Compliance Requirements

- Noticees found in violation are subject to directions under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1) and 11B(2), which typically include disgorgement of illegal gains with interest, restraint from accessing the securities market, and monetary penalties.
- Intermediaries and mutual fund houses should reinforce internal controls, access restrictions on trade information, and surveillance systems to prevent similar front running by dealers/employees with knowledge of large pending orders.
- Market participants and mutual fund AMCs should review employee dealing and information-barrier policies in light of this order.

## Important Dates

- Order type: Final Order (follows a prior ex-parte Interim Order cum Show Cause Notice); specific dates of the interim order, hearings, and this final order are detailed within the full 146-page document but not fully captured in the excerpted content.

## Impact Assessment

This order is significant for market integrity as it addresses front running of a large mutual fund's trades, a practice that can erode investor trust in fund houses and their empanelled brokers. The directions likely include monetary penalties and market access restrictions for the 21 noticees, which could affect their ability to trade or operate in the securities market. For Axis Mutual Fund and other AMCs, the order underscores the need for stronger information barriers and surveillance around dealers and brokers with access to large pending trade information, and may prompt broader industry scrutiny of similar front running risks.
