## Summary

SEBI has passed a final order against First Global Finance Private Limited (FGF), a SEBI-registered Portfolio Manager (Reg. No. INP000006697), along with its principals Ms. Devina Mehra and Mr. Neeraj Khanna. The action follows an on-site inspection in December 2022 and a subsequent forensic audit (April 2021–December 2023) by Chokshi and Chokshi LLP, which identified two major categories of violations: illegal outsourcing of core portfolio management activities and dissemination of misleading marketing and performance claims.

## Key Points

- SEBI conducted an on-site inspection of FGF during December 05–09, 2022, revealing possible outsourcing of core portfolio management to Algo One AI Private Limited
- A forensic audit was approved and Chokshi and Chokshi LLP was appointed as forensic auditor via letter dated January 30, 2024; the Forensic Audit Report (FAR) was submitted on August 21, 2024
- The inspection period covered April 01, 2021 to December 31, 2023
- Three noticees: (1) First Global Finance Private Limited (PAN: AAACF0660J), (2) Ms. Devina Mehra (PAN: AAHPM4465E), (3) Mr. Neeraj Khanna (PAN: AAEPK5719J)
- A Show Cause Notice was issued on January 28, 2026
- The final order is passed under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1), and 11B(2) of the SEBI Act, 1992

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This order enforces existing provisions under:
- Regulation 24(10) of SEBI (Portfolio Managers) Regulations, 2020 — prohibition on outsourcing core investment activities
- SEBI Circular dated December 15, 2011 on outsourcing of activities by intermediaries
- Clauses 1, 3, and 13(a) of Schedule III (Code of Conduct) read with Regulation 21 of PMS Regulations
- Clause 2.1 read with Clauses 1.1–1.8 of Annexure 2A of the SEBI Master Circular for Portfolio Managers dated June 07, 2024
- Regulation 22(10) of PMS Regulations and Clauses 4.5.2.1, 4.5.3.4, and 5.6.1 of the SEBI Master Circular for Portfolio Managers
- Regulations 4(2)(k) and 4(2)(s) of the PFUTP Regulations

## Compliance Requirements

- FGF directed to cease and desist from outsourcing core investment-related activities to any other entity or person
- FGF directed to remove all exaggerated and misleading advertisements from the public domain and marketing materials
- FGF directed to restrict acceptance of new business and additional contributions from existing clients (per SCN directions)
- Portfolio managers must ensure all core investment and portfolio management functions are performed in-house by qualified and registered personnel
- Performance disclosures must follow consistent methodologies as prescribed under the SEBI Master Circular for Portfolio Managers

## Important Dates

- December 05–09, 2022: SEBI on-site inspection of FGF
- January 30, 2024: Chokshi and Chokshi LLP appointed as forensic auditor
- August 21, 2024: Forensic Audit Report submitted
- Inspection Period: April 01, 2021 – December 31, 2023
- January 28, 2026: Show Cause Notice issued to noticees

## Impact Assessment

This final order carries significant implications for the portfolio management industry. The findings that FGF delegated core investment decisions to Algo One AI Private Limited (and its personnel, particularly Mr. Achin Agarwal) represent a fundamental breach of fiduciary and regulatory obligations owed to clients. Investors in FGF's portfolio management schemes may have been exposed to undisclosed third-party influence over their funds. The finding of misleading performance disclosures further erodes investor confidence. The order signals SEBI's increased scrutiny of PMS firms for both operational compliance (outsourcing norms) and transparency in performance reporting. Other portfolio managers should review their outsourcing arrangements and marketing materials for compliance with PMS Regulations and SEBI's Master Circular.
