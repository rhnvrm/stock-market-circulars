## Summary

SEBI has issued a compliance order (Part II / follow-up notice) directing the attachment of the demat account and mutual fund folio(s) held by Narendrabhai Amin. The action is taken in exercise of recovery powers under Recovery Certificate No. 8484 of 2024, issued in connection with enforcement proceedings involving M/s Austral Coke and Projects Limited. The order requires depositories, depository participants, and mutual fund RTAs/AMCs to attach and restrain any dealing in the specified demat account and mutual fund folio(s) held by the named individual until the outstanding recovery dues are satisfied.

## Key Points

- Order issued under Recovery Certificate No. 8484 of 2024.
- Respondent/defaulter named: Narendrabhai Amin.
- Matter relates to enforcement action concerning M/s Austral Coke and Projects Limited.
- This is a second/supplementary notice ("-II") in the same recovery matter, indicating attachment coverage is being extended or reiterated to additional accounts/folios.
- Directs attachment of demat account(s) and mutual fund folio(s) held by the individual as a recovery mechanism.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulation is introduced. This is an enforcement/recovery action applying SEBI's existing powers (typically exercised via a Recovery Officer under the Second Schedule to the Income-tax Act, 1961 as made applicable to SEBI recovery proceedings) to attach specified financial assets belonging to a defaulter.

## Compliance Requirements

- Depositories and Depository Participants must place a freeze/attachment on the demat account(s) held by Narendrabhai Amin, restraining transfer, pledge, or any dealing in securities held therein.
- Mutual Fund AMCs/RTAs must similarly attach the identified mutual fund folio(s), restraining redemption, switch, or transfer of units.
- Intermediaries must report compliance/confirmation of attachment to SEBI as directed in the order.
- No further debit transactions should be permitted in the attached account/folio without SEBI's or the Recovery Officer's explicit permission.

## Important Dates

Order dated: 2026-07-07 (per SEBI publication date). Specific compliance/reporting deadlines, if any, are set out in the full order text, which should be referred to directly for exact timelines.

## Impact Assessment

The order has no direct impact on listed securities, trading activity, or the broader market — it is a targeted enforcement/recovery measure against an individual associated with a recovery matter involving M/s Austral Coke and Projects Limited. Its relevance is primarily for the depositories, DPs, and mutual fund intermediaries who must comply with the attachment, and for compliance teams tracking SEBI enforcement and recovery actions.
