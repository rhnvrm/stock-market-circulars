## Summary

SEBI has passed an order under Sections 11(1) and 11(2)(h) of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with Regulation 11(5) of the SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011 ("SAST Regulations"), in the matter of a proposed acquisition of shares and voting rights in Waaree Energies Limited ("Target Company") by C.T. Doshi Family Trust ("Acquirer Trust"). The Acquirer Trust had filed an application dated January 21, 2026 (revised April 20, 2026), along with subsequent correspondence, seeking exemption from the applicability of Regulations 3 and 5 read with Regulation 4 of the SAST Regulations, 2011, in connection with the proposed direct and indirect acquisition of shares and voting rights in the Target Company.

## Key Points

- Target Company: Waaree Energies Limited, incorporated December 18, 1990, registered office in Borivali (E), Mumbai; equity shares listed on BSE and NSE.
- Proposed Acquirer: C.T. Doshi Family Trust.
- The Acquirer Trust sought exemption from open offer obligations under Regulations 3 and 5 read with Regulation 4 of the SAST Regulations, 2011, for a proposed direct and indirect acquisition of shares/voting rights.
- As per the application, issued, subscribed and paid-up equity share capital of the Target Company is INR 2,87,64,09,050 divided into 28,76,40,905 equity shares of face value INR 10 each.
- Promoter and promoter group shareholding as on the date of application totaled 64.22% (18,47,32,113 shares), including Chimanlal Tribhuvandas Doshi (45.88%) and Waaree Sustainable Finance Private Limited, formerly Mahavir Thermoequip Private Limited (18.34%).
- Public shareholding stood at 35.78% (10,29,08,792 shares).
- The application and subsequent clarifications were submitted over several months (January, February, March and April 2026), indicating a detailed review process by SEBI.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is a specific exemption order addressing an individual application under the existing SAST Regulations, 2011 framework, particularly the exemption provisions under Regulation 11(5).

## Compliance Requirements

The order pertains specifically to the Acquirer Trust's compliance with (or exemption from) open offer triggers under Regulations 3, 4 and 5 of the SAST Regulations, 2011, in relation to the proposed acquisition within the promoter group of Waaree Energies Limited. Other market participants have no direct compliance obligations arising from this order.

## Important Dates

- January 21, 2026: Original application submitted.
- February 07, 2026, March 14, 2026, April 10, 2026, April 20, 2026, April 23, 2026, April 27, 2026: Supplementary clarifications/emails.
- April 20, 2026: Revised application submitted.

## Impact Assessment

The order relates to an intra-promoter-group restructuring/acquisition involving the C.T. Doshi Family Trust and does not appear to involve a change of control or a new external acquirer. Depending on SEBI's final determination (grant or rejection of exemption), the outcome could affect whether an open offer to public shareholders is triggered, which would have direct implications for minority shareholders and the stock's trading activity. Market impact is assessed as medium given the promoter-group nature of the transaction and the company's status as a listed entity on BSE and NSE.
