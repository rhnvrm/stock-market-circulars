## Summary

SEBI has issued a prohibitory order against MJVVD Prakash in connection with Recovery Certificates No. 2804, 2806, 2807, 2808, and 2809 of 2020. The order arises from SEBI enforcement proceedings in the matter of Ranklin Solutions Ltd and Vaishnavi Gold Ltd. A prohibitory order restrains the defaulter from transferring, alienating, or disposing of assets until outstanding dues are recovered by SEBI.

## Key Points

- Prohibitory order issued against MJVVD Prakash by SEBI's Recovery Officer
- Covers five Recovery Certificates: No. 2804, 2806, 2807, 2808, and 2809 of 2020
- Relates to SEBI enforcement matters involving Ranklin Solutions Ltd and Vaishnavi Gold Ltd
- Order prohibits the defaulter from disposing of or encumbering assets pending recovery of dues
- Issued under SEBI's recovery proceedings powers under the SEBI Act

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is an enforcement/recovery action under existing SEBI recovery proceedings framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- MJVVD Prakash is required to comply with the prohibitory order and must not transfer, alienate, or dispose of any assets without SEBI's permission
- Banks and financial institutions holding assets of the defaulter may be directed to freeze/hold such assets
- Third parties dealing with the defaulter's assets are on notice of the prohibitory order

## Important Dates

- Order date: March 16, 2026
- Recovery Certificates issued: 2020 (Certificates No. 2804, 2806, 2807, 2808, 2809)

## Impact Assessment

This order has no broad market impact. It is a targeted enforcement action against a specific individual (MJVVD Prakash) to facilitate recovery of SEBI-imposed dues arising from violations related to Ranklin Solutions Ltd and Vaishnavi Gold Ltd. The prohibitory order is a standard SEBI recovery tool to prevent asset dissipation by defaulters. Impact is limited to the named individual and any connected entities.
