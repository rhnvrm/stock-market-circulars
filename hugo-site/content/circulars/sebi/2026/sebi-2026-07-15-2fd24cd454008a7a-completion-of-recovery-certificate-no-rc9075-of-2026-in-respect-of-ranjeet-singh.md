## Summary

SEBI has issued an order marking the completion of recovery proceedings under Recovery Certificate No. RC9075 of 2026, issued against Ranjeet Singh Baid HUF (PAN: AAPHR3293F). The recovery certificate was originally issued in connection with enforcement action taken by SEBI relating to trading in illiquid stock options, a practice SEBI has targeted for creating artificial volumes and non-genuine trades in the options segment. This order closes out the recovery process for the outstanding dues/penalty associated with this specific certificate.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9075 of 2026 was issued against Ranjeet Singh Baid HUF (PAN: AAPHR3293F).
- The underlying matter pertains to trading in illiquid stock options, an area SEBI has actively pursued for enforcement due to concerns over artificial liquidity and non-genuine trading patterns.
- SEBI's Recovery Officer has now issued an order confirming completion of the recovery process under this certificate, typically indicating that the amount due has been recovered or the recovery proceedings have otherwise been concluded.
- This is an individual enforcement action and does not apply broadly to market participants or listed entities.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is a case-specific enforcement/recovery action rather than a new rule, circular, or policy change affecting market participants generally.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance obligations arise for market participants, listed companies, or intermediaries from this order. It concerns closure of recovery proceedings against a specific noticee (Ranjeet Singh Baid HUF) under powers exercised by SEBI's Recovery Officer, consistent with SEBI's recovery mechanism under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Important Dates

The completion order was published on the SEBI website on 2026-07-15. No further deadlines or compliance dates apply to other parties.

## Impact Assessment

The impact of this order is limited to the specific noticee named in the recovery certificate. It does not affect any listed company's securities, trading activity, or broader market operations. Such completion orders are largely administrative in nature, confirming the conclusion of SEBI's recovery process for previously adjudicated dues, and carry low significance for the broader investing public or market participants.
