## Summary

SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9141 of 2026 constituting a formal Notice of Demand against Asan Steels Private Limited (PAN: AAHCA8637P). This notice arises from SEBI's broader enforcement action in the matter of Illiquid Stock Options (ISO) trading on BSE, where numerous entities were found to have engaged in reversal and synchronized trades that created artificial volumes and facilitated tax evasion.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9141 of 2026 issued by SEBI against Asan Steels Private Limited (PAN: AAHCA8637P)
- Notice of Demand relates to outstanding dues (penalty and/or disgorgement) ordered in the Illiquid Stock Options matter
- SEBI is empowered under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992 to recover amounts as arrears of land revenue
- Non-payment of dues allows SEBI to initiate coercive recovery through the Recovery Officer
- Entity is directed to pay the demanded amount within the stipulated timeframe to avoid further recovery proceedings

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this document. This is an enforcement and recovery action pursuant to existing SEBI adjudicatory orders in the Illiquid Stock Options matter.

## Compliance Requirements

- Asan Steels Private Limited must pay the outstanding dues specified in the Recovery Certificate within the timeframe mentioned in the notice
- Failure to comply will result in coercive recovery proceedings initiated by SEBI's Recovery Officer, which may include attachment and sale of assets
- The entity must respond to the Notice of Demand and either pay dues or represent its case before SEBI as applicable

## Important Dates

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9141 issued: 2026
- Notice of Demand date: June 2026
- Payment deadline: As specified in the demand notice (typically within 15–30 days of receipt)

## Impact Assessment

This enforcement action is limited to Asan Steels Private Limited and has no direct impact on broader markets or investors. It is part of SEBI's ongoing recovery campaign against entities penalised in the large-scale Illiquid Stock Options manipulation case on BSE, which involved synchronised and reversal trades. The action reinforces SEBI's commitment to enforcing adjudicatory orders and recovering dues through statutory recovery mechanisms under Section 28A of the SEBI Act.
