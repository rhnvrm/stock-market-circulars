## Summary

SEBI has notified the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Issue and Listing of Securitised Debt Instruments and Security Receipts) (Amendment) Regulations, 2026, amending the SEBI (Issue and Listing of Securitised Debt Instruments and Security Receipts) Regulations, 2008. The amendment was published in the Gazette of India (Extraordinary) dated July 1, 2026, and takes effect from the date of publication in the Official Gazette. The changes primarily relate to the composition of trustee boards for special purpose distinct entities (SPEs), restrictions on SPEs acquiring debt or receivables from group-linked originators, replacement of 'originator' with 'servicer' in certain provisions, and modifications to periodic reporting requirements.

## Key Points

- Regulation 9(9) is amended to revise the composition norms for the board of trustees of a special purpose distinct entity (SPE): nominees of the sponsor or originator, or persons connected in any manner with the sponsor/originator or with a company having common management with the sponsor/originator, shall not constitute more than half of the SPE's board of trustees.
- A new proviso is inserted in Regulation 9(9): where the originator is an entity regulated by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), it shall not have more than one representative on the SPE's board of trustees, and that representative shall not have veto power.
- Regulation 10(3) is substituted: an SPE shall not acquire any debt or receivable from an originator that is part of the same group as the trustee, or that is under common control with the trustee (the explanation to this sub-regulation remains unchanged).
- Regulation 10A(1) is amended to replace the word 'Originator' with 'Servicer'.
- Regulation 10A(2) is amended: references to the securitisation trust created by the 'originator' are replaced with references to the 'securitisation trust', and the word 'servicer' is added in connection with related obligations.
- Regulation 11(3) is amended (clause (e) and related provisions) concerning periodic reports on the performance of the underlying asset pool, previously required from the originator on at least a quarterly basis.

## Regulatory Changes

The amendment reworks trustee-board eligibility and independence requirements for SPEs under the securitisation framework, caps RBI-regulated originators to a single, non-veto board representative on the SPE, prohibits SPEs from acquiring debt/receivables from group-linked or commonly-controlled originators, and shifts certain reporting and definitional references from 'originator' to 'servicer' to better reflect the party actually responsible for servicing functions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trustees, sponsors, originators and servicers involved in securitisation transactions under the SEBI (Issue and Listing of Securitised Debt Instruments and Security Receipts) Regulations, 2008 must review and align SPE board composition with the revised Regulation 9(9), including the new cap on RBI-regulated originator representation and veto power restrictions.
- Entities must ensure SPEs do not acquire debt/receivables from originators within the same group as, or under common control with, the trustee, per revised Regulation 10(3).
- Reporting and disclosure processes referencing 'originator' obligations under Regulations 10A and 11(3) should be updated to reflect the 'servicer' terminology and revised reporting responsibilities.

## Important Dates

- Gazette notification date: July 1, 2026 (published July 6, 2026, per CG-MH-E-06072026-274200).
- Effective date: The regulations come into force from the date of their publication in the Official Gazette.

## Impact Assessment

The amendment affects entities engaged in securitisation of debt instruments and security receipts, including trustees, originators/servicers, and special purpose distinct entities (SPEs). While it does not have a direct market-price impact on listed stocks, it strengthens governance and independence norms for SPE trustee boards and tightens restrictions on related-party debt acquisition, which is relevant for banks, NBFCs, and other RBI-regulated originators active in the securitisation market, as well as trustees and servicers who must update governance and compliance processes accordingly.
