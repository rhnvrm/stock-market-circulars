## Summary

This order was passed by the Appellate Authority under the RTI Act, 2005, at SEBI, disposing of Appeal No. 6915 of 2026 filed by Daniel Francis against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought clarifications on whether SEBI regulations permit intermediaries (stockbrokers, Depository Participants, RTAs, AMCs) to insist on affidavits or NOCs from legal heirs even after a probated Will has been submitted for transmission of securities and mutual fund units, and requested copies of related circulars. The CPIO had responded that most queries sought clarification/opinion rather than 'information' under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act, and pointed to the SEBI Master Circular for RTAs dated February 06, 2026 for the query on applicable circulars. The Appellate Authority upheld this position and dismissed the appeal.

## Key Points

- Appellant sought confirmation on whether probate of a Will is conclusive proof for transmission of securities without additional legal heir affidavits.
- Appellant referenced SEBI Circular dated 15.09.2016 (Standardization and Simplification of Procedures for Transmission of Securities) and SEBI Circular dated 04.01.2019 (Acceptance of Probate of Will for Transmission of Securities held in dematerialized mode), asking if these remain valid.
- CPIO held that queries 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, and 7 sought clarification/opinion, not 'information' as defined under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act, and thus could not be answered under RTI.
- For query 2 (copies of applicable circulars), the appellant was directed to the SEBI Master Circular for Registrars to an Issue and Share Transfer Agents dated February 06, 2026.
- The Appellate Authority agreed with the CPIO's characterization and found no grounds to fault the response as incomplete, misleading, or false.
- The appeal was disposed of/dismissed on this basis.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes were introduced by this order. It is an administrative RTI appellate ruling reaffirming existing scope limitations of the RTI Act (queries seeking opinion/clarification are outside its ambit) and pointing to the existing SEBI Master Circular for RTAs (February 06, 2026) as the consolidated source for transmission-related procedures.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements are imposed on market participants, brokers, DPs, RTAs, or AMCs by this order. Entities handling transmission of securities/mutual fund units based on probated Wills should continue to refer to the SEBI Master Circular for RTAs (dated February 06, 2026) and the 2016/2019 circulars referenced by the appellant for existing procedural requirements.

## Important Dates

- May 12, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant.
- June 01, 2026: CPIO's response letter.
- June 04, 2026: Appeal filed by appellant (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00206).
- February 06, 2026: Date of SEBI Master Circular for RTAs referenced in the response.

## Impact Assessment

This order has minimal market or operational impact as it is limited to disposing of an individual RTI appeal on procedural grounds without altering or clarifying substantive SEBI policy on transmission of securities via probated Wills. Investors and intermediaries seeking clarity on documentation requirements for probate-based transmissions should independently consult the SEBI Master Circular for RTAs and the specific 2016/2019 circulars, as this order does not resolve the underlying substantive question of whether additional affidavits can be demanded despite probate submission.
