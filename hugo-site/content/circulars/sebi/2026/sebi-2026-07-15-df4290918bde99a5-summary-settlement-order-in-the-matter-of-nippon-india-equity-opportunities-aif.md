## Summary

SEBI issued a Summary Settlement Order (Settlement Application No. SummSettlement/2026-27/1000) in the matter of Nippon India Equity Opportunities AIF - Scheme 9, a scheme managed by Nippon Life India AIF Management Limited under the Nippon India Event Opportunities Trust. The examination of the scheme's Quarterly Activity Reports for the quarters ended March 2024, June 2024, and September 2024 revealed that the Manager and sponsor failed to maintain their continuing interest in the scheme pro-rata to the net funds raised from other investors, as mandated under AIF Regulations.

## Key Points

- SEBI examined Quarterly Activity Reports of Nippon India Equity Opportunities AIF - Scheme 9 for the quarters ended March 2024, June 2024, and September 2024.
- For the quarter ended June 2024, the fund drew down ₹93.05 crores from other investors against a total commitment of ₹215.36 crores (43.2% drawdown rate), while the Manager/sponsor drew down only ₹3 crores against a ₹10 crore commitment (30% drawdown), failing to maintain pro-rata continuing interest.
- Similar shortfalls were observed for March 2024 (30% Manager/sponsor drawdown vs. 38.99% from other investors) and September 2024 (47.50% Manager/sponsor drawdown vs. 51.76% from other investors).
- The applicants in the settlement include Nippon India Event Opportunities Trust, Nippon Life India AIF Management Limited, and ten individuals: Ashish Moti Chhugani, Aashwin Dugal, Rahul Kishor Veera, Prashant Kutty, Aashray Vasa, Jai Vijay Daxini, Alok Surendranath Singh, Vivek Doshi, and Shruti Parekh.
- The non-compliance was found to violate regulation 10(d) of the SEBI (Alternative Investment Funds) Regulations, 2012, and provisions of the Master Circular for Alternative Investment Funds dated May 7, 2024.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes were introduced; this is an enforcement/settlement action applying existing AIF Regulations, specifically regulation 10(d) concerning the Manager/sponsor's obligation to maintain continuing interest in a scheme pro-rata to funds raised from other investors.

## Compliance Requirements

AIF Managers and sponsors must ensure their capital drawdowns keep pace proportionally with drawdowns from other investors in a scheme, maintaining continuing interest as required under regulation 10(d) of the AIF Regulations and the applicable Master Circular. Failure to do so may result in enforcement action, including settlement proceedings under SEBI's summary settlement framework.

## Important Dates

- Quarters examined: March 2024, June 2024, and September 2024
- Master Circular for Alternative Investment Funds referenced: May 7, 2024
- Settlement Application Number: SummSettlement/2026-27/1000

## Impact Assessment

This settlement order primarily affects the named AIF manager, sponsor, and associated individuals from a compliance and reputational standpoint. It does not directly impact listed securities or broader market trading, but serves as a reminder to AIF managers and sponsors of the regulatory requirement to maintain pro-rata continuing interest, reinforcing SEBI's ongoing surveillance of AIF drawdown practices.
