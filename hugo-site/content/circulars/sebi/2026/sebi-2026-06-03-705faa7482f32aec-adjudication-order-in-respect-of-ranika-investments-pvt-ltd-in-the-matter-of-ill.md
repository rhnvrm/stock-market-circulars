## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer has issued an adjudication order against Ranika Investments Pvt Ltd (PAN: AAACR5548K) for alleged manipulation in the illiquid stock options segment of BSE. The entity is accused of executing non-genuine reversal trades during the investigation period (April 1, 2014 – September 30, 2015), contributing to artificial trading volumes on the exchange.

## Key Points

- Ranika Investments Pvt Ltd executed 45 non-genuine trades across 12 stock options contracts on BSE during the investigation period.
- These trades resulted in artificial volume of 22,08,000 units in the illiquid stock options segment.
- SEBI's broader investigation found 2,91,744 alleged non-genuine trades comprising 81.40% of all trades in BSE's stock options segment during the period.
- Alleged violations cover SEBI PFUTP Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1), and 4(2)(a) — provisions prohibiting fraudulent and manipulative trade practices.
- Penalty proceedings are under Section 15HA of the SEBI Act, 1992.
- The original Adjudicating Officer was Ms. Asha Shetty; the case was subsequently transferred to the undersigned AO via order dated April 3, 2025.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This order applies existing PFUTP Regulations and SEBI Act penalty provisions to historical manipulative trading conduct.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must not engage in reversal trades or any trades designed to create artificial volumes in stock options or any other segment.
- Entities involved in illiquid stock options manipulation during April 2014 – September 2015 remain subject to ongoing adjudication proceedings by SEBI.
- Noticees in similar proceedings must respond to Show Cause Notices and participate in hearings as directed by the Adjudicating Officer.

## Important Dates

- **Investigation Period:** April 1, 2014 – September 30, 2015
- **Show Cause Notice issued:** August 1, 2022
- **SCN served (affixture):** March 28, 2023
- **Post SCN Intimation (PSI):** March 6, 2024
- **AO transfer order:** April 3, 2025
- **Hearing date:** May 12 (year not specified in available text)

## Impact Assessment

This order is part of SEBI's large-scale enforcement action targeting entities that manipulated illiquid stock options on BSE between 2014–2015. The direct market impact is limited given the historical nature of the trades, but the order reinforces SEBI's sustained commitment to prosecuting market manipulation years after the fact. Entities involved in similar reversal trading schemes face penalties under Section 15HA of the SEBI Act. The case also highlights SEBI's use of administrative proceedings to address systemic manipulation that artificially inflated traded volumes in thinly traded derivatives contracts.
