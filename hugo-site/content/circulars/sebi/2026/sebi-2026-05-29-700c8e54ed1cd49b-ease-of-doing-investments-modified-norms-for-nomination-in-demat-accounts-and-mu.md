## Summary

SEBI has issued modified norms for nomination in demat accounts and mutual fund folios via circular SEBI/HO/OIAE/OIAE_IAD-3/P/CIR/2026/12676 dated May 29, 2026. The revision addresses operational challenges raised by stakeholders following the original January 10, 2025 circular, simplifying the nomination process to prevent unclaimed assets and ease investor on-boarding.

## Key Points

- Nomination is mandatory for all new single demat accounts and mutual fund folios opened on or after the implementation date, unless an opt-out declaration is submitted.
- Nomination is optional for jointly held accounts and folios.
- All joint-holders must consent for providing or changing nominees, regardless of the mode of operation.
- Investors may designate up to 3 nominees per account or folio.
- Upon the investor's demise, nominees may continue in the same account/folio or open separate ones for their respective holdings.
- Nomination can be submitted online or offline (physical/wet signature); witness signature is not required for wet-signed forms unless a thumb impression is used.
- Mandatory information: nominee name and relationship; date of birth if the nominee is a minor.
- Optional information: nominee contact details, percentage share, KYC/identifier, and guardian details for minor nominees.
- Where percentage shares are not specified, assets are split equally; any odd lot goes to the first nominee listed.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular modifies the earlier SEBI circular on 'Revise and Revamp Nomination Facilities in the Indian Securities Market' dated January 10, 2025. Key changes include:

- Simplified mandatory information requirements (name and relationship only; date of birth required only for minor nominees).
- Contact details, percentage share, KYC identifiers, and guardian details are now optional rather than mandatory.
- Witness signature removed as a requirement for physically signed nomination forms (required only when a thumb impression is used, necessitating two witnesses).
- Online nomination may be validated via Digital Signature Certificate, Aadhaar-based e-sign, or Two Factor Authentication (OTP to registered mobile and email).
- Nomination form format standardised per Annexure-A of the circular.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Asset Management Companies (AMCs) and RTAs**: Must update nomination forms and systems to reflect the revised format in Annexure-A; must make nomination mandatory for new single folios; must offer both online and offline submission options.
- **Recognised Depositories**: Must implement revised nomination norms for demat accounts; must enable online nomination with the specified validation methods.
- **Depository Participants (DPs)**: Must ensure nomination is collected at account opening for single accounts and that opt-out declarations are properly processed.
- All regulated entities must facilitate investors who choose to fill in all, some, or none of the optional fields.
- Equal apportionment logic and odd-lot transfer to the first nominee must be implemented in back-end systems.

## Important Dates

- **May 29, 2026**: Circular issued.
- **Effective Date**: On or after the date of implementation of this circular (specific implementation date to be confirmed by regulated entities per SEBI guidance).
- **Reference Circular**: January 10, 2025 — original nomination circular (now partially superseded).

## Impact Assessment

This circular has moderate operational impact on AMCs, RTAs, depositories, and DPs who will need to update investor on-boarding workflows, nomination forms, and back-end systems. The simplification of mandatory fields and removal of witness requirements reduces friction for retail investors, potentially increasing nomination uptake and reducing future unclaimed asset cases. No direct impact on stock prices or market trading. Entities should review and update digital and physical nomination form templates, system validation logic, and investor communication materials before the implementation date.
