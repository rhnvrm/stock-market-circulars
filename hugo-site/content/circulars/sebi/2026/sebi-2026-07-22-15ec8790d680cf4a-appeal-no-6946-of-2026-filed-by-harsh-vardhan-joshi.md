## Summary

The Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005, has disposed of Appeal No. 6946 of 2026 filed by Harsh Vardhan Joshi against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought information relating to SEBI's Second-Level Review process under the SCORES (SEBI Complaints Redress System) framework and the basis on which his complaint was disposed of on May 21, 2026. The Appellate Authority upheld the CPIO's response on two of the three disputed queries but found the response inadequate on the third, remitting that query back to the CPIO for fresh consideration.

## Key Points

- Appellant Harsh Vardhan Joshi filed an RTI application dated June 01, 2026, which the CPIO responded to on June 22, 2026.
- The appellant challenged the CPIO's response to query nos. 8, 9, and 11 via an appeal dated June 23, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00232).
- Query nos. 8 and 9 sought circulars/SOPs and prescribed timelines governing Second-Level Reviews under SCORES; the CPIO pointed to publicly available SEBI Circular No. SEBI/HO/OIAE/IGRD/CIR/P/2023/156 dated September 20, 2023.
- The Appellate Authority held that since the circular is publicly available on the SEBI website, there is no obligation under the RTI Act to separately furnish it, citing Delhi High Court precedent (Registrar of Companies vs. Dharmendra Kumar Garg) and a CIC order (K Lall vs. M K Bagri).
- Query no. 11, seeking the approval/order/note based on which the appellant's complaint was disposed of on May 21, 2026, was not answered at all by the CPIO.
- The Appellate Authority found this non-response inadequate and remitted query no. 11 back to the CPIO for de novo consideration and a fresh response to the appellant.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an adjudicatory order under the RTI Act addressing a specific information request.

## Compliance Requirements

The CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai is required to reconsider query no. 11 of the appellant's RTI application afresh and issue an appropriate response, in line with the directions in the order.

## Important Dates

- June 01, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- June 22, 2026: CPIO's response to the application.
- June 23, 2026: Appeal filed by appellant (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00232).
- May 21, 2026: Date on which the appellant's underlying SCORES complaint was disposed of (subject of query no. 11).

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct market, listing, or trading impact. It is procedural in nature, pertaining to SEBI's internal RTI grievance redressal mechanism and transparency around its SCORES complaint disposal process. Relevance is limited to RTI practitioners and those tracking SEBI's investor grievance handling procedures.
