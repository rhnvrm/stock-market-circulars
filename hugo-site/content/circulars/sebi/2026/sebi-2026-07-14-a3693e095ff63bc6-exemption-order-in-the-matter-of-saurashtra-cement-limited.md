## Summary

SEBI has passed an order under Section 11(1) and Section 11(2)(h) of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with Regulation 11(5) of the SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011 ("SAST Regulations"), in the matter of a proposed indirect acquisition of shares and voting rights in Saurashtra Cement Limited ("Target Company") by the Mehta Family Trust ("Acquirer Trust"). The application, dated January 19, 2026, was filed by Mr. Jay Mahendra Mehta in his capacity as trustee of the Mehta Family Trust, seeking exemption from the open offer obligations under Regulation 3(2) read with Regulation 5 of the SAST Regulations, 2011, which would otherwise be triggered by the proposed acquisition.

Saurashtra Cement Limited is listed on both the BSE Ltd. and the National Stock Exchange of India Ltd. As per the shareholding pattern for the quarter ended March 2026, the company's issued and paid-up share capital comprises 11,12,87,280 equity shares of INR 10 each. The promoter and promoter group collectively hold a fragmented set of shareholdings, including individual family members (Arjun Jay Mehta, Jahnavi Jay Mehta, Jay Mahendra Mehta and other family members) as well as corporate/LLP entities such as Galaxy Technologies Private Limited (14.57%), Omna Enterprises LLP (9.46%), and Gujarat Industrial Investment Corporation (4.60%).

## Key Points

- Application filed by Jay Mahendra Mehta, trustee of the Mehta Family Trust, seeking exemption from open offer requirements for a proposed indirect acquisition of shares/voting rights in Saurashtra Cement Limited.
- The exemption sought is from Regulation 3(2) read with Regulation 5 of the SAST Regulations, 2011.
- Target Company: Saurashtra Cement Limited, incorporated June 11, 1956, registered office in Ranavav, Porbandar, Gujarat.
- Target Company shares are listed on BSE and NSE.
- Issued and paid-up share capital: INR 1,11,28,72,800, comprising 11,12,87,280 equity shares of INR 10 each.
- Promoter and promoter group shareholders include numerous individual Mehta family members and entities such as Galaxy Technologies Private Limited, Omna Enterprises LLP, and Gujarat Industrial Investment Corporation.
- The order is issued by SEBI's Whole Time Member (reference WTM/KCV/CFD/06/2026-27).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is a case-specific exemption order under existing SAST Regulations, 2011 provisions (Regulation 11(5)) that allow SEBI to grant exemptions from open offer obligations in appropriate cases, such as internal reorganizations within a promoter/family group that do not result in a change in effective control.

## Compliance Requirements

- The Mehta Family Trust and its trustee(s) must comply with any conditions attached to the exemption order (full order not shown in extracted content).
- The Target Company and its promoter group must ensure post-transaction shareholding pattern disclosures reflect the approved reorganization accurately.
- Any deviation from the exemption's conditions could subject the parties to open offer obligations under Regulation 3(2) and 5 of the SAST Regulations.

## Important Dates

- Application date: January 19, 2026
- Shareholding pattern reference date: Quarter ended March 2026
- Order reference: WTM/KCV/CFD/06/2026-27

## Impact Assessment

This order primarily affects the internal ownership structure of the promoter group of Saurashtra Cement Limited via the Mehta Family Trust and does not appear to alter public shareholding or trigger a mandatory open offer, given the exemption sought. Impact on minority/public shareholders is limited, though the order is relevant for tracking promoter group reorganization and SAST compliance in the cement sector. Market participants tracking Saurashtra Cement Limited should note the change in the nature of promoter holdings (from direct family ownership to trust-based ownership) as it may affect future control assessments and disclosure requirements.
