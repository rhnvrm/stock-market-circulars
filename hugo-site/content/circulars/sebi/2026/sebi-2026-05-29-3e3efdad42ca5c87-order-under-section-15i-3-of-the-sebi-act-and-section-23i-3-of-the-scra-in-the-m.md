## Summary

SEBI has issued an adjudication order under Section 15-I(3) of the SEBI Act, 1992 and Section 23-I(3) of the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956 against Suzlon Energy Limited (SEL) and four of its officials — Vinod R. Tanti, Girish R. Tanti, Kirti J. Vagadia, and Amit Agarwal — for alleged misrepresentation and misstatement in financial statements and violations of PFUTP Regulations and LODR Regulations. The investigation, triggered by an anonymous complaint in December 2019, covered FY 2014-15 through the first three quarters of FY 2020-21.

## Key Points

- SEBI received an anonymous complaint on December 12, 2019 alleging irregularities in SEL's dealings with its subsidiaries and associates.
- The matter was referred to NSE for preliminary examination; issues identified included investments, loans, impairment, and related party disclosures.
- SEBI conducted a full investigation and appointed forensic auditor Sarath & Associates to examine SEL's financial statements.
- Five noticees: Suzlon Energy Limited (PAN: AADCS0472N), Vinod R. Tanti (PAN: AARPT6367N), Girish R. Tanti (PAN: ABFPT3310E), Kirti J. Vagadia (PAN: AAKPV2224M), and Amit Agarwal (PAN: AADPA3646H).
- An Adjudicating Officer (AO) issued a Show Cause Notice (SCN) on November 09, 2022 alleging violations of SEBI Act, PFUTP Regulations, LODR Regulations, and clauses of the listing agreement.
- This order under Section 15-I(3)/23-I(3) represents the Whole Time Member's review/revision of the AO's findings.
- The order addresses preliminary objections from the noticees, their submissions on merits, and issues of consideration across 96 pages.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this order. The order applies existing provisions of:
- SEBI Act, 1992
- Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956
- SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices Relating to Securities Market) Regulations, 2003 (PFUTP Regulations)
- SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015 (LODR Regulations)
- Clauses of the Listing Agreement

## Compliance Requirements

- Listed companies must ensure accurate and truthful financial statements, including proper disclosure of investments, loans, impairments, and related party transactions.
- Key managerial personnel and directors are personally accountable for the integrity of financial disclosures under LODR Regulations and PFUTP Regulations.
- Companies and their officials must cooperate with SEBI investigations and respond to SCNs within stipulated timelines.

## Important Dates

- December 12, 2019: Anonymous complaint received by SEBI alleging irregularities in SEL.
- Investigation period: FY 2014-15 to FY 2019-20 and first three quarters of FY 2020-21.
- November 09, 2022: SCN issued by the Adjudicating Officer to the noticees.
- May 2026: Final order issued by SEBI Whole Time Member under Section 15-I(3)/23-I(3).

## Impact Assessment

This is a significant enforcement action against one of India's large renewable energy companies. The order carries implications for:
- **Suzlon Energy (SUZLON):** Subject to penalties and reputational impact; the findings on financial misrepresentation could affect investor confidence and the stock.
- **Senior Management Accountability:** Personal liability of promoter-directors (Tanti family members) and CFO-level officials signals SEBI's willingness to pierce corporate veil in accounting fraud cases.
- **Market Precedent:** The use of forensic auditors and multi-year investigations reinforces SEBI's capacity to pursue complex financial fraud cases over extended periods.
- **PFUTP Enforcement:** Reinforces that misstatements in financial disclosures qualify as fraudulent and unfair trade practices, widening the enforcement perimeter for accounting irregularities.
