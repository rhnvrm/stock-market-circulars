## Summary

SEBI's Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 has dismissed Appeal No. 6932 of 2026 filed by Jashan Gupta, who had sought detailed information on the allocation, custody, and utilization of funds collected under the Additional Surveillance Deposit (ASD) mechanism. The appellant's original RTI application (dated May 20, 2026) asked SEBI to explain the complete framework governing ASD funds, including where they are deposited, how interest/income earned on them is treated, whether such income is shared with investors, the criteria for levying ASD, and when the deposit is released back to investors. The CPIO responded that these queries sought clarification/opinion rather than 'information' as defined under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act, and noted that ASM/GSM surveillance measures, including ASD levies, are administered by Stock Exchanges in coordination with Clearing Corporations, with relevant circulars publicly available on exchange websites. The Appellate Authority upheld this position, citing precedent (Azad Singh vs. CPIO, Oriental Insurance Company Limited) that queries seeking explanation, opinion, advice, confirmation, or clarification fall outside the scope of RTI disclosure obligations.

## Key Points

- Appellant sought disclosure of the complete ASD framework: fund deposit location, utilization, treatment of interest/income earned, and criteria for levying the deposit.
- SEBI's CPIO held that these queries were in the nature of seeking clarification/opinion, not 'information' under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act.
- ASM, GSM, and ASD-related surveillance activities are implemented by Stock Exchanges in coordination with Clearing Corporations, not directly by SEBI.
- Relevant circulars on ASD/ASM/GSM are stated to be publicly available on Stock Exchange websites.
- The Appellate Authority relied on the CIC precedent in Azad Singh vs. Oriental Insurance Company Limited (order dated March 23, 2021) to dismiss the appeal.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an adjudicatory RTI appeal order and does not introduce new rules, frameworks, or amendments to the ASD/ASM/GSM surveillance regime.

## Impact Assessment

The order has minimal direct market impact. It does not alter how ASD is levied, held, or released, nor does it affect any listed securities or trading operations. Its relevance is limited to clarifying that detailed queries about internal fund-handling mechanics of surveillance deposits are not obtainable via RTI from SEBI, and that such information, if available, would need to be sought from Stock Exchanges or Clearing Corporations directly.
