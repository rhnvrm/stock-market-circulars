## Summary

SEBI has passed a fresh order in respect of Shri. Siddhant Suresh Chandan (PAN: BQWPC6396L) under Sections 11(1), 11(4) and 11B of the SEBI Act, 1992, in the matter of unregistered investment advisory services. The order follows a remand by the Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT), which on March 27, 2026 upheld SEBI's original findings from its March 15, 2023 order but set aside the quantum of fee collected and the refund direction. SAT directed SEBI's CGM to re-compute the fee liable to be refunded after granting the Noticee a fresh opportunity to explain bank statement entries.

## Key Points

- Original SEBI order dated March 15, 2023 found the Noticee in violation of Section 12(1) of the SEBI Act read with Regulation 3(1) of the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013, for rendering unregistered investment advisory services.
- The Noticee appealed to SAT (Appeal No. 150 of 2025); SAT upheld the core findings but remitted the matter back to SEBI solely for re-computation of the refundable fee amount.
- SAT directed the Noticee to appear before SEBI with a professional (preferably a Chartered Accountant) and directed SEBI to provide a hearing to explain bank statement entries before passing a fresh order.
- The Noticee appeared before SEBI on April 21, 2026 along with an accountant and claimed that bank account credits also included receipts from an online business, family/friend loans, salary, and brokerage — unrelated to the unregistered advisory activity.
- The Noticee failed to produce supporting documents at the hearing and was granted multiple extensions (15 days, then further rescheduling to May 11, May 22, and June 2, 2026) to submit written submissions with evidence.
- Despite repeated extensions, the available content indicates the Noticee continued to seek additional time without fully complying with document submission requirements.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an individual enforcement order arising out of a SAT remand for re-computation of a refund amount in a prior unregistered investment advisory case.

## Compliance Requirements

The order primarily concerns the Noticee's individual obligation to substantiate fund sources and comply with SEBI's directions; it does not impose new compliance requirements on market intermediaries or listed entities at large. Persons offering investment advice must remain registered under the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013.

## Important Dates

- March 15, 2023: Original SEBI order issued.
- March 27, 2026: SAT order upholding findings, remanding fee quantum for re-computation.
- April 21, 2026: Personal hearing before SEBI (CGM).
- May 7, 2026: Noticee requested one month's time to categorize bank transactions.
- May 11, May 22, and June 2, 2026: Rescheduled personal hearings.
- May 29, 2026: Deadline set for Noticee to file reply with supporting documents.

## Impact Assessment

This order has limited market-wide impact, being directed at a single individual noticee for unregistered investment advisory activity. It serves as a continued reminder of SEBI's enforcement focus on unregistered investment advisers and the procedural rigor (including SAT remand compliance) applied in such matters. No listed companies or broader trading operations are affected.
