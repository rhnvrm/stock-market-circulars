## Summary

This is an order by the Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005, concerning Appeal No. 6879 of 2026 filed by Dinesh Kumar against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought information on government and regulatory policies related to gold and silver trading, international futures markets, and their impact on India's current account deficit and household savings.

## Key Points

- Appellant Dinesh Kumar filed an RTI application originally with the Department of Economic Affairs, which was transferred to SEBI on May 8, 2026.
- SEBI (respondent) responded on May 12, 2026; the appellant filed this appeal on May 13, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00176).
- The RTI queries covered 8 topics relating to gold and silver trading policy, import data, international price linkages, and inter-agency consultations.
- SEBI transferred queries 1, 2, and 7 (impact on current account deficit, reports, and savings diversion policy) to the Reserve Bank of India via RTI MIS portal on March 25, 2026.
- Queries 3 and 8 (year-wise import values and responsible department) were transferred to the Ministry of Commerce under Section 6(3) of the RTI Act.
- Query 4 (on decoupling domestic prices from international futures markets) was rejected as seeking a "clarification" rather than "information" as defined under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act.
- Queries 5 and 6 (role of international futures in price volatility and inter-agency consultations) were denied on grounds that the information is not available with SEBI.
- The appellant filed the appeal on the ground of refused access to information.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an administrative appellate order under the RTI Act.

## Compliance Requirements

No compliance requirements for market participants. This order concerns SEBI's handling of an RTI application and the appropriate routing of queries to RBI and the Ministry of Commerce.

## Important Dates

- **May 8, 2026:** RTI application transferred from Department of Economic Affairs to SEBI.
- **March 25, 2026:** SEBI partially transferred relevant queries to RBI and Ministry of Commerce via RTI MIS portal.
- **April 17, 2026:** SEBI issued response letter no. CPIO/KS/2346/2026-27.
- **May 12, 2026:** Respondent replied to appellant's application.
- **May 13, 2026:** Appellant filed this appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00176).

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct market or operational impact. It is a routine RTI appellate proceeding. The underlying subject matter — gold and silver trading policy, international futures market linkages, and their macroeconomic effects — is of general public interest but does not result in any new regulatory directive or market action from this order.
