## Summary

SEBI has passed an order under Section 12(3) of the SEBI Act, 1992 read with Regulation 30A of the SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008 against ISF Special Opportunity Fund, an Alternative Investment Fund (AIF) registered with SEBI (Registration No. IN/AIF1/18-19/0575). The order arises from the Noticee's failure to file Quarterly Activity Reports (QARs) for the quarters ending March 2025, June 2025, September 2025 and December 2025, as mandated under Regulation 28 of the AIF Regulations, 2012 read with the SEBI Master Circular dated May 07, 2024.

## Key Points

- ISF Special Opportunity Fund is registered with SEBI as a Category I AIF under the SEBI (Alternative Investment Fund) Regulations, 2012.
- AIFs are required to file QARs within 15 calendar days from the end of each quarter, in the format hosted by industry associations IVCA and Equalifi, via the SEBI Intermediary (SI) Portal.
- The Noticee failed to file QARs for four consecutive quarters: March 2025, June 2025, September 2025 and December 2025.
- SEBI initiated Summary Proceedings under Regulation 30A of the Intermediaries Regulations, 2008 read with Section 12(3) of the SEBI Act, 1992.
- A Show Cause Notice (SCN) dated April 24, 2026 was served via Registered Post and email, asking why the AIF registration certificate should not be cancelled.
- The Noticee sought and was granted extension of time; it responded citing delayed login issues as the reason for non-filing and stated it was in the process of completing pending Quarterly Compliance Reports (QCRs) after filing its Annual Activity Report (AAR).
- Records show a similar past instance of delayed/non-filing of QARs flagged by SEBI in December 2023, for which the Noticee had also cited technical/login issues.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement order applying existing Regulation 28 of the AIF Regulations, 2012 and Regulation 30A of the Intermediaries Regulations, 2008 to a specific non-compliant entity.

## Compliance Requirements

- Registered AIFs must file QARs on the SI Portal within 15 calendar days of each quarter-end, in the industry-association-prescribed format.
- Failure to comply can trigger Summary Proceedings that may result in cancellation of AIF registration under Section 12(3) of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Important Dates

- Missed filings: Quarters ending March 2025, June 2025, September 2025, December 2025.
- Show Cause Notice issued: April 24, 2026.
- Noticee's response/extension request: May 18, 2026 (extension granted till June 02, 2026).
- Further submissions by Noticee: June 02, 2026 and June 10, 2026.
- Prior related SEBI communication: December 05, 2023 and December 06, 2023.

## Impact Assessment

The order primarily affects the Noticee AIF and its investors, with potential cancellation of its SEBI registration if non-compliance is not remedied. It has no direct bearing on listed securities, trading activity, or the broader market, and serves mainly as a compliance reminder to other registered AIFs regarding timely QAR filings.
