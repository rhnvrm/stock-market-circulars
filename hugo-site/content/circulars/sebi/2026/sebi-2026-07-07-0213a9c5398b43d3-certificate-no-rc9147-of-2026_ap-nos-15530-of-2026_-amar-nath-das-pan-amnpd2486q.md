## Summary

SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9147 of 2026 along with Attachment Proceedings (A.P.) Nos. 15530 of 2026 against Amar Nath Das (PAN: AMNPD2486Q) and Rajesh Kumar Agarwal (PAN: AFFPA4486E). The proceedings relate to the recovery of outstanding monetary dues arising from findings in the illiquid stock options matter, where these entities were previously found to have engaged in non-genuine trades in illiquid stock options contracts on the exchange.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9147 of 2026 has been drawn up against the named defaulters.
- Attachment Proceedings bear reference number A.P. Nos. 15530 of 2026.
- Two individuals are named: Amar Nath Das (PAN: AMNPD2486Q) and Rajesh Kumar Agarwal (PAN: AFFPA4486E).
- The matter pertains to the long-running SEBI enforcement action concerning reversal/non-genuine trades in illiquid stock options on the BSE.
- This is a recovery/attachment notice, issued under the recovery mechanism applicable to unpaid SEBI dues (typically invoking Income Tax Act recovery provisions as applied by the SEBI Act).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is an enforcement/recovery action applying existing SEBI recovery proceedings framework to specific defaulters identified in the illiquid stock options case.

## Compliance Requirements

The named individuals are required to clear the outstanding dues specified in the recovery certificate. Failure to comply may result in further coercive recovery measures, including attachment of bank accounts, demat accounts, or other assets held by the defaulters, as permitted under the applicable recovery procedure.

## Important Dates

The certificate and attachment proceedings are dated 2026-07-07 (per SEBI's publication). Specific compliance deadlines, if any, are set out in the full order and are not detailed in the available summary.

## Impact Assessment

This action has no direct impact on listed securities, trading, or market operations. Its effect is confined to the named individuals against whom SEBI is enforcing recovery of dues. It reflects SEBI's continued enforcement follow-through on its illiquid stock options crackdown and serves as a reminder to market participants of the consequences of adverse SEBI findings that go unpaid.
