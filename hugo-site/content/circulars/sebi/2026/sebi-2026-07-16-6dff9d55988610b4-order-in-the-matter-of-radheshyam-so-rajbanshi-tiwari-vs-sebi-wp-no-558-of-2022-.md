## Summary

The High Court of Judicature at Bombay, Nagpur Bench, disposed of Writ Petition No. 558 of 2022 filed by Radheshyam S/o Rajbanshi Tiwari challenging a Show Cause Notice (SCN) dated 22/10/2021 issued by SEBI's Deputy General Manager (Investigation Department). The petitioner had alleged violations of the Companies Act, 1956 and ICDR Regulations, and was called upon to show cause under Sections 11B(1), 11(4), 11(1), 11B(2) and 11(4A) read with Section 15HB of the SEBI Act, 1992. The petitioner argued that since the investigation under Section 11-C of the SEBI Act was initiated by the same officer who issued the SCN, this violated the principle that no person can be a judge in their own cause, raising apprehension of unfair consideration of any reply. SEBI's counsel clarified that the SCN was solely to determine whether an inquiry should be held under Section 11-B(2), not to impose a penalty. The Court accepted this submission, declined to interfere with the SCN at this stage, and disposed of the petition while granting the petitioner liberty to approach the Court afresh if warranted, keeping all points open.

## Key Points

- Writ Petition No. 558 of 2022 filed by Radheshyam S/o Rajbanshi Tiwari against SEBI was disposed of by the Nagpur Bench of the Bombay High Court on 16 July 2026.
- The petition challenged a Show Cause Notice dated 22/10/2021 issued under Section 11-C investigation proceedings by SEBI's Investigation Department.
- Petitioner's ground: the same official who conducted the Section 11-C investigation also issued the SCN, allegedly violating the principle against being a judge in one's own cause.
- SEBI clarified the SCN pertains only to whether an inquiry should be held under Section 11-B(2), not to impose penalty or take direct action against the petitioner.
- The Court declined to interfere with the SCN, disposed of the petition, and granted liberty to the petitioner to approach the Court again if needed, keeping all points open.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is a judicial order pertaining to a specific individual's writ petition and does not introduce new SEBI regulations or amend existing ones.

## Compliance Requirements

The petitioner (Notice No. 1 among 11 noticees) retains the obligation to respond to the original SCN dated 22/10/2021, raising any objections (including the bias argument) as part of that reply, which SEBI's Investigation Department must consider before deciding whether to hold a formal inquiry under Section 11-B(2) of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Important Dates

- SCN issued: 22 October 2021
- Court order date: 16 July 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order has minimal market-wide impact as it concerns a single petitioner among 11 noticees in an underlying SEBI investigation and only addresses a procedural challenge to the SCN's validity. The case remains open for further proceedings, with SEBI's investigation department still required to evaluate the SCN reply before any inquiry or penalty determination. No immediate action is required from listed companies or market participants generally, though it may serve as a procedural precedent for challenges to SEBI SCNs on grounds of investigator-adjudicator overlap.
