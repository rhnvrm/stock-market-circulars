## Summary

SEBI issued Adjudication Order No. Order/JS/VC/2026-27/32414 against Catalyst Trusteeship Limited (PAN: AACCG4147R, SEBI Reg. No. IND000000034) following a thematic inspection of debenture trustees covering the period July 31, 2022 to July 31, 2024. The inspection assessed compliance with SEBI (Debenture Trustees) Regulations, 1993 and related Master Circulars, specifically examining actions taken by debenture trustees upon issuer defaults. A Show Cause Notice dated January 5, 2026 was issued, and adjudication proceedings were initiated under Section 15HB of the SEBI Act.

## Key Points

- Catalyst Trusteeship Limited was inspected as part of SEBI's thematic review of debenture trustees' actions in response to issuer defaults.
- The inspection period covered July 31, 2022 to July 31, 2024.
- Four categories of regulatory violations were alleged in the Show Cause Notice dated January 5, 2026.
- A specific instance involved issuer Srivatsa Encivil Pvt. Ltd. defaulting on payments on June 30, 2024; Catalyst issued the default notice to the issuer on July 12, 2024, only after receiving specific instructions and draft notice from debenture holders on July 2, 2024.
- Violations included delays in issuing notices to debenture holders and failure to maintain proof of delivery of such notices.
- ISINs involved: INE0K8T07024 and INE0K8T07032.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this order. The order enforces existing provisions:
- Regulation 15(1)(d) of SEBI (Debenture Trustees) Regulations, 1993.
- Clause 3.3.1 of Chapter X of Master Circular for Debenture Trustees dated July 06, 2023 and May 16, 2024.
- Clauses 2.2 and 3.1.2 of Chapter VIII of Master Circular for Debenture Trustees dated May 16, 2024.
- Clause 4 of Chapter VIII of Master Circular for Debenture Trustees dated May 16, 2024.
- Chapter XII of Master Circular for Debenture Trustees dated May 16, 2024.

## Compliance Requirements

- Debenture trustees must promptly issue notices to debenture holders upon learning of an issuer default, without waiting for holder-specific instructions or draft notices from the holders themselves.
- Debenture trustees must maintain documented proof of delivery for all notices issued to debenture holders and issuers.
- Trustees must independently monitor for issuer defaults and take timely prescribed actions in accordance with DT Regulations and the applicable Master Circulars.
- Compliance with all chapters of the Master Circular for Debenture Trustees (particularly Chapters VIII, X, and XII) is mandatory.

## Important Dates

- Inspection Period: July 31, 2022 to July 31, 2024.
- Appointment of Adjudicating Officer: December 12, 2025.
- Show Cause Notice issued: January 5, 2026 (Ref. No. DIS/1995-1996/JS/2026).
- Issuer (Srivatsa Encivil Pvt. Ltd.) default date: June 30, 2024.
- Debenture holder complaint letters to Catalyst: July 2, 2024.
- Default notice sent by Catalyst to issuer: July 12, 2024.
- Order Number: Order/JS/VC/2026-27/32414.

## Impact Assessment

This order has limited direct market-wide impact but carries significant implications for the debenture trustee industry. It signals SEBI's heightened scrutiny of how debenture trustees respond to issuer defaults, particularly regarding timeliness and documentation. Debenture trustees across India should review their internal processes for monitoring issuer default events, issuing mandatory notices, and retaining proof of delivery. The order reinforces that trustees cannot defer protective actions pending debenture holder instructions when the regulatory framework already mandates proactive steps. Investors in non-convertible debentures and similar fixed-income instruments benefit from strengthened trustee accountability.
