## Summary

This is an order by SEBI's Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005, deciding Appeal No. 6930 of 2026 filed by Vikash Gupta. The appellant sought information on the status of an investigation/examination into transactions involving Max Financial Services Ltd. (MFSL), Max Life Insurance Co. Ltd. (MLIC), and Axis Group entities (Axis Bank Ltd., Axis Capital Ltd., Axis Securities Ltd.), which SEBI had earlier told the Delhi High Court (in W.P.(C) 2223/2024, judgment dated August 12, 2024) was at an "advanced stage of completion." The CPIO had denied the information citing exemptions under Sections 8(1)(e) and 8(1)(h) of the RTI Act, on grounds of fiduciary capacity and ongoing quasi-judicial proceedings. The appellant challenged this, alleging incomplete, misleading, or false information was provided.

## Key Points

- The appellant's RTI application dated May 20, 2026 sought six categories of information regarding the status, findings, enforcement actions, and internal records of SEBI's examination into Max Financial Services, Max Life Insurance, and Axis Group entities.
- The examination stems from a matter referenced in the Delhi High Court's judgment dated August 12, 2024 in W.P.(C) 2223/2024, where SEBI stated the probe was at an "advanced stage of completion."
- The CPIO's response dated June 11, 2026 denied disclosure, citing Section 8(1)(e) (fiduciary information with no larger public interest) and Section 8(1)(h) (information that would impede investigation or quasi-judicial proceedings) of the RTI Act.
- The respondent confirmed the matter remains under investigation and that quasi-judicial proceedings are underway and not yet concluded.
- The appellant's ground of appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00219, filed June 14, 2026) is that the information provided was incomplete, misleading, or false.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual RTI appellate order, not a regulatory or policy circular.

## Compliance Requirements

No direct compliance obligations arise for market participants from this order. It pertains solely to the RTI disclosure dispute between the appellant and SEBI's CPIO.

## Important Dates

- May 20, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- June 11, 2026: CPIO's response to the RTI application.
- June 14, 2026: Appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00219).
- August 12, 2024: Delhi High Court judgment in W.P.(C) 2223/2024 referencing the underlying examination.

## Impact Assessment

The order itself has no direct market impact, as it concerns procedural RTI disclosure rather than substantive enforcement. However, it confirms that a SEBI examination into transactions involving Max Financial Services Ltd., Max Life Insurance Co. Ltd., and Axis Group entities remains active and unresolved nearly two years after being described as being at an "advanced stage of completion." Investors in these entities should monitor for future SEBI enforcement actions, show-cause notices, or adjudication orders that could materialize once the examination concludes.
