## Summary

SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9075 of 2026 under Application/Appeal No. 15496 of 2026 against Ranjeet Singh Baid HUF (PAN: AAPHR3293F). This certificate pertains to the recovery of outstanding dues in the matter of Illiquid Stock Options, a segment where SEBI has previously identified widespread manipulation and imposed penalties on numerous entities.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9075 of 2026 has been issued against Ranjeet Singh Baid HUF.
- PAN of the entity: AAPHR3293F.
- The matter relates to trading in Illiquid Stock Options, which SEBI has identified as a manipulated segment.
- Application/Appeal reference: A.P No. 15496 of 2026.
- The recovery certificate is a formal mechanism used by SEBI to recover unpaid penalties or disgorgement amounts from defaulting entities.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this document. It is an enforcement action instrument — a recovery certificate — issued under SEBI's existing powers to recover dues from entities that have failed to comply with earlier penalty or disgorgement orders.

## Compliance Requirements

- Ranjeet Singh Baid HUF is required to pay the outstanding dues as specified in the recovery certificate.
- Non-compliance with a recovery certificate may lead to further enforcement actions, including attachment of assets and prosecution under applicable securities laws.
- The concerned recovery officer or designated authority will take steps to enforce recovery as per SEBI's recovery proceedings framework.

## Important Dates

- **Date of Issuance:** 2026-06-24
- Further deadlines for payment or compliance would be specified within the recovery certificate itself.

## Impact Assessment

This recovery certificate has a limited market-wide impact as it targets a single named entity (Ranjeet Singh Baid HUF). However, it signals SEBI's continued and active pursuit of enforcement actions in the Illiquid Stock Options matter, where the regulator has historically taken action against hundreds of entities involved in suspected manipulation. Market participants previously penalised in this matter who have not yet paid dues should treat this as a reminder of SEBI's ongoing recovery proceedings.
