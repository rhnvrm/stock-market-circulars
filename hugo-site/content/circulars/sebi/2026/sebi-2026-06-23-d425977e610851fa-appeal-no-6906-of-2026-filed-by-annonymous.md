## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority dismissed Appeal No. 6906 of 2026 filed by an anonymous appellant against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought details of the Appellate Authority and/or Adjudicating Officer for BRSR (Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting) reporting irregularities, along with their official contact details. The Appellate Authority upheld the respondent's position that these queries seek clarification/opinion and do not constitute 'information' as defined under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed RTI application on April 30, 2026 seeking details of the adjudicating/appellate authority for BRSR-related grievances and their contact information.
- CPIO responded on May 26, 2026 stating the queries were unclear, not specific, and in the nature of seeking clarification/opinion.
- Appellant filed appeal on May 28, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00201), alleging incomplete, misleading, or false information was provided.
- Appellate Authority found the queries indeed seek clarification/opinion and cannot be construed as 'information' under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act.
- CPIO additionally clarified that Adjudicating Officers are appointed only when enforcement actions are initiated after examination of a matter.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a procedural RTI appeal order clarifying the scope of information obligations under the RTI Act in the context of BRSR-related queries.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. The order reaffirms that queries seeking opinion, advice, or clarification from SEBI do not fall within the definition of 'information' under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act and SEBI has no obligation to respond to such queries under the RTI framework.

## Important Dates

- April 30, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant.
- May 26, 2026: CPIO response provided.
- May 28, 2026: Appeal filed by appellant (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00201).

## Impact Assessment

This order has minimal market or operational impact. It is a routine RTI appellate decision that reinforces established legal precedent (citing Azad Singh vs. CPIO, Oriental Insurance Company Limited, CIC order dated March 23, 2021) that requests for explanation, opinion, advice, or clarification do not constitute 'information' under the RTI Act. Entities and investors seeking redress for BRSR reporting irregularities should note that RTI is not the appropriate mechanism for obtaining regulatory opinions or identifying the relevant authority; they should instead use SEBI's SCORES platform or other designated grievance channels.
