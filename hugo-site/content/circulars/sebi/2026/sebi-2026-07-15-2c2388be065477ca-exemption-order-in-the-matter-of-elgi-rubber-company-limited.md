## Summary

SEBI has passed an order under Section 11(1) and Section 11(2)(h) of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with Regulation 11(5) of the SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011 ("SAST Regulations"), on an application filed by Mr. Sudarsan Varadaraj, in his capacity as trustee of Varnam Securities Trust ("Acquirer Trust"), seeking exemption from the open offer obligations under Regulation 3(1) read with Regulations 4 and 5 of the SAST Regulations. The exemption relates to the proposed direct and indirect acquisition of shares and voting rights of Elgi Rubber Company Limited ("Target Company") by the Acquirer Trust. The application, dated May 19, 2026, along with submissions dated June 3, 2026, was considered by SEBI. Mr. Sudarsan Varadaraj is already the largest promoter shareholder of the Target Company, holding 47.09% of its equity, and the proposed transaction appears to involve consolidation of promoter/promoter-group holdings into the trust structure that he manages.

## Key Points

- Target Company: Elgi Rubber Company Limited, incorporated October 16, 2006, registered office in Coimbatore, equity shares listed on NSE.
- Proposed Acquirer: Varnam Securities Trust, represented by Managing Trustee Sudarsan Varadaraj.
- Total paid-up capital of the Target Company: INR 5,00,50,000 divided into 5,00,50,000 equity shares of INR 1 each.
- As per the shareholding pattern for the quarter ended March 2026, promoters and promoter group collectively hold 65.03% (3,25,49,741 shares), and public shareholders hold 34.97% (1,75,00,259 shares).
- Sudarsan Varadaraj individually holds 47.09% (2,35,67,500 shares) and is also the Managing Trustee of Varnam Securities Trust, which currently holds no shares in the Target Company (per the disclosed table).
- Other promoter/promoter group members named include Harsha Varadaraj (4.50%), Varshini Varadaraj (4.50%), Jairam Varadaraj (0.28%), Sumanth Ramamurthi (0.02%), Anvar Jay Varadaraj, Maya Jay Varadaraj, and Varun Jay Varadaraj (0.01% each), LRG Technologies Limited (7.08%), and Elgi Equipments Limited (1.53%).
- The application sought exemption from the requirement to make an open offer that would otherwise be triggered under Regulations 3(1), 4, and 5 of the SAST Regulations, 2011, as a result of the proposed acquisition of shares/voting rights by the Acquirer Trust.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this order; it is a case-specific exemption determination by SEBI under its powers in Section 11(1) and Section 11(2)(h) of the SEBI Act, 1992 and Regulation 11(5) of the SAST Regulations, 2011, which allows SEBI to grant relief from open offer obligations in appropriate cases (e.g., internal reorganizations that do not alter effective control).

## Compliance Requirements

- The Acquirer Trust and related promoter entities must comply with any conditions attached to the exemption order (full conditions not included in the extracted excerpt).
- The Target Company and Acquirer Trust are expected to make requisite disclosures under SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011 and SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015 regarding any change in shareholding pursuant to the exemption.

## Important Dates

- May 19, 2026: Original application filed by the Acquirer Trust.
- June 3, 2026: Supplementary submissions filed.
- July 15, 2026: SEBI order (WTM/KCV/CFD/07/2026-27) issued/published.

## Impact Assessment

The transaction appears to be an internal restructuring within the existing promoter group of Elgi Rubber Company Limited, with the largest promoter shareholder consolidating holdings via a trust vehicle he manages. As no change in ultimate control or promoter identity is evident from the disclosed shareholding pattern, the market impact is expected to be limited. The order is primarily relevant for compliance and corporate governance tracking of the Target Company's promoter group structure rather than for trading or valuation implications.
