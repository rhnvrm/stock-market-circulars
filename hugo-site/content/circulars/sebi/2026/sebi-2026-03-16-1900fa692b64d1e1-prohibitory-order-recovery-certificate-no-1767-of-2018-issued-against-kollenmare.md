## Summary

SEBI has issued a prohibitory order under Recovery Certificate No. 1767 of 2018 against Kollenmareth Oomman Isaac in connection with the matter of ABL Biotechnologies Ltd. The order directs the attachment and freezing of the individual's assets to facilitate recovery of outstanding dues payable to SEBI.

## Key Points

- Prohibitory order issued under Recovery Certificate No. 1767 of 2018
- Order is directed against Kollenmareth Oomman Isaac
- Related to the matter of ABL Biotechnologies Ltd
- Order instructs banks and financial institutions to prohibit transactions on the defaulter's accounts and assets
- Part of SEBI's enforcement mechanism to recover unpaid penalties and disgorgement amounts
- Recovery Certificate was originally issued in 2018; this order represents a continued enforcement action

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is an enforcement action under existing SEBI recovery and enforcement provisions under the SEBI Act, 1992 and SEBI (Recovery of Amounts) Rules.

## Compliance Requirements

- Banks and financial institutions receiving this order must immediately freeze/prohibit transactions on accounts held by Kollenmareth Oomman Isaac
- Any entity holding assets of the named individual must comply with the prohibitory direction
- Non-compliance with a SEBI prohibitory order may attract further legal action

## Important Dates

- Recovery Certificate No. 1767 originally issued: 2018
- Prohibitory order date: March 16, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order has no broader market impact. It is an individual enforcement action targeting a specific person associated with ABL Biotechnologies Ltd for non-payment of SEBI-imposed dues. The action demonstrates SEBI's continued pursuit of long-pending recovery certificates and its commitment to enforcing penalty collection. Market participants and investors are not affected unless they are the named individual or hold assets on their behalf.
