## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer issued an order in respect of Sonia Chadha (PAN: AACPC8962H) concerning alleged trading in illiquid stock options at BSE. The case stems from a SEBI investigation covering April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015, which found that 2,91,744 trades (81.40% of all stock options trades at BSE during that period) were allegedly non-genuine, creating artificial trading volume. The Noticee is alleged to have executed 8 non-genuine reversal trades across 4 stock options contracts, resulting in artificial volume of 88,000 units. Proceedings were initiated for alleged violation of Regulations 3(a),(b),(c),(d), 4(1) and 4(2)(a) of the SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices Relating to Securities Market) Regulations, 2003 (PFUTP Regulations).

## Key Points

- Investigation covers the period April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015 (Investigation Period).
- SEBI identified large-scale reversal trades in the BSE stock options segment, allegedly creating artificial trading volumes.
- The Noticee allegedly executed 8 non-genuine trades in 4 stock options contracts, generating artificial volume of 88,000 units.
- A Show Cause Notice dated August 11, 2022 was issued under Rule 4(1) of the SEBI Adjudication Rules.
- The Noticee was informed of the SEBI ISO Settlement Scheme 2022 and provided a dedicated helpline for queries.
- Multiple Adjudicating Officers were appointed over time (Amit Kapoor, Shashi Kumar Valsakumar, and finally the current AO effective June 1, 2026) due to case transfers.
- A corrigendum to the order has also been issued and linked alongside the main order.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement/adjudication action applying existing PFUTP Regulations, 2003 to alleged past conduct.

## Compliance Requirements

The order pertains specifically to the Noticee's individual liability for alleged fraudulent/unfair trade practices. No new compliance obligations are imposed on market participants generally; entities engaged in options trading should continue to avoid non-genuine reversal trades that create artificial volume, as such conduct is treated as a violation of PFUTP Regulations.

## Important Dates

- Investigation Period: April 1, 2014 – September 30, 2015
- Show Cause Notice issued: August 11, 2022
- SEBI ISO Settlement Scheme 2022 details communicated: November 16, 2022
- Adjudicating Officer transfer: April 4, 2025 (Amit Kapoor); January 18, 2024 (Shashi Kumar Valsakumar, per order text)
- Current Adjudicating Officer appointed: June 1, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order has limited direct market impact as it concerns an individual's past trading conduct rather than a listed company or broad market policy. Its significance lies in reinforcing SEBI's continued enforcement against artificial volume creation via reversal trades in illiquid stock options, serving as a deterrent to similar market participants engaging in non-genuine trading practices.
