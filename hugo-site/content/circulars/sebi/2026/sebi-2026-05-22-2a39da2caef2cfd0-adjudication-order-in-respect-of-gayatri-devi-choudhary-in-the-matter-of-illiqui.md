## Summary

SEBI has issued an adjudication order (Order No. Order/AK/DS/2026-27/32413) against Gayatri Devi Choudhary (PAN: ACCPC0370P) for alleged participation in non-genuine reversal trades in the illiquid stock options segment of BSE during the investigation period (IP) of April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015. The noticee is alleged to have executed 2 non-genuine trades in 1 stock options contract, creating artificial volume of 96,000 units.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated large-scale reversal trades in BSE's stock options segment that created artificial volumes during April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015.
- A total of 2,91,744 trades — comprising 81.40% of all trades in BSE's stock options segment during the IP — were allegedly non-genuine.
- Gayatri Devi Choudhary executed 2 non-genuine trades in 1 stock options contract, resulting in artificial volume of 96,000 units.
- Alleged violations: SEBI PFUTP Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1), and 4(2)(a).
- Show Cause Notice (SCN) dated March 14, 2022 was issued; the noticee did not respond.
- Post Show Cause Intimation (PSI) dated August 11, 2022 was also issued with no response received.
- The current Adjudicating Officer was appointed via order dated April 03, 2025, following transfer of the case.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is an enforcement action under existing SEBI PFUTP Regulations, 2003 and Section 15HA of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must not execute reversal or non-genuine trades that create artificial volumes in any segment.
- Entities must respond to SEBI Show Cause Notices and participate in adjudication proceedings.
- Violations of PFUTP Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1), and 4(2)(a) are subject to monetary penalties under Section 15HA of the SEBI Act.

## Important Dates

- **Investigation Period:** April 1, 2014 – September 30, 2015
- **Original AO Appointment:** September 20, 2021
- **Show Cause Notice Issued:** March 14, 2022
- **Post Show Cause Intimation:** August 11, 2022
- **Current AO Appointment:** April 3, 2025
- **Order Date:** May 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is an individual enforcement action with limited broader market impact. It is part of SEBI's sustained campaign to penalize participants involved in the widespread illiquid stock options manipulation at BSE during 2014–2015. The case highlights SEBI's continued pursuit of enforcement actions even many years after the investigation period, signaling long-term accountability for market manipulation. The noticee's non-participation in proceedings may result in an ex-parte penalty order.
