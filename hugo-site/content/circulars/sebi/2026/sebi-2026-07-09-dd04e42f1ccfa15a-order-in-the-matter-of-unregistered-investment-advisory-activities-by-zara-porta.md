## Summary

SEBI issued an order under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1) and 11B(2) of the SEBI Act, 1992 against Zara Portal, Proprietor Mr. Anurag Jaiswal (PAN: BGEPJ2695F), in connection with unregistered investment advisory activities. The matter originated from complaints against a SEBI-registered investment adviser, Sai Proficient Research Investment Advisory (SPRIA), Proprietor Ms. Meeshika Vishwakarma, who was separately penalized via an order dated February 23, 2026. During examination of SPRIA's conduct, SEBI found that a complainant, Mr. Arun Agarwal, had received solicitation emails from an ID associated with SPRIA promising high returns (up to 85% accuracy, profits of ₹9 lakh on a ₹3,75,000 'package') from stock, commodity, futures and options trading advice. However, payment receipts (via Easebuzz) showed the actual payee/merchant of record was Zara Portal, not SPRIA, indicating funds collected under SPRIA's advisory branding were actually routed to Zara Portal.

## Key Points

- The order concerns Zara Portal, Proprietor Mr. Anurag Jaiswal, who is not a SEBI-registered investment adviser.
- The case emerged from a SCORES complaint filed by Mr. Arun Agarwal on February 5, 2020 against SPRIA.
- Emails from saiproficient05@gmail.com promised assured/high returns (up to 85% accuracy) via panel-based advisory packages costing ₹3,75,000 for a 6-month service.
- Mr. Arun paid ₹3,27,000 pursuant to these solicitations, but payment records showed the actual recipient/merchant was Zara Portal, not SPRIA.
- SPRIA was already penalized in a separate proceeding via order dated February 23, 2026, but is not a noticee in this specific order.
- The order examines potential violations of the SEBI Act, PFUTP Regulations, 2003, and IA Regulations, 2013 by Zara Portal for facilitating or benefiting from unregistered advisory activity and fund collection.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement order applying existing provisions of the SEBI Act, PFUTP Regulations, and IA Regulations against a specific unregistered entity.

## Compliance Requirements

Entities and individuals providing investment advice or collecting payments related to securities market advisory services must be registered with SEBI as Investment Advisers under the IA Regulations, 2013. Entities found to be routing or receiving payments for unregistered advisory services may face directions, penalties, or restraint under Sections 11 and 11B of the SEBI Act.

## Important Dates

- February 5, 2020: Complaint filed by Mr. Arun Agarwal on SCORES portal.
- September 27, 2018: First solicitation email from SPRIA-linked ID.
- October 12, 2018: Invoice issued for ₹3,75,000 payment.
- November 22, 2018: Follow-up solicitation email referencing SEBI registration and ISO certification claims.
- February 23, 2026: Order passed against SPRIA in a related but separate proceeding.

## Impact Assessment

This order has limited direct market impact as it targets an individual/proprietorship entity rather than a listed company or systemic market infrastructure. However, it reinforces SEBI's continued crackdown on unregistered investment advisory operations and fraudulent solicitation practices that mislead retail investors with assured-return claims, serving as a cautionary precedent for similar unregistered entities and reaffirming investor protection priorities under SEBI's regulatory framework.
