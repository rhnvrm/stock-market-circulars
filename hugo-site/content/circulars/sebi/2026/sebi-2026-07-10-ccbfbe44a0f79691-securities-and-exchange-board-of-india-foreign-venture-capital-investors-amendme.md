## Summary

SEBI has notified the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Foreign Venture Capital Investors) (Amendment) Regulations, 2026, amending the SEBI (Foreign Venture Capital Investors) Regulations, 2000. The amendment replaces all fee amounts previously denominated in US Dollars with equivalent amounts in Indian Rupees (or an acceptable equivalent in any other currency), and revises the process and timelines for Designated Depository Participants (DDPs) to remit fees collected from FVCIs to SEBI. The regulations will come into force 180 days from the date of their publication in the Official Gazette (published July 3, 2026).

## Key Points

- Regulation 3(3) has been amended to remove the reference to fees being charged as per the Second Schedule at that stage.
- All fees in the Second Schedule have been converted from USD to INR equivalents:
  - Registration fee: USD 2,500 → Rs. 2,30,000 (or equivalent in any acceptable currency)
  - Renewal fee: USD 100 → Rs. 9,000 (termed as fee for renewal/extension of registration period)
  - Fee under clause (5)(a): USD 5 → Rs. 500
  - Maximum fee under clause (5)(b): USD 150 → Rs. 15,000 (maximum)
- The timing of registration fee payment has changed: previously payable at the time of submitting the form to the DDP; now payable before the certificate of registration is granted, to the Designated Depository Participant.
- Clause (6) of the Second Schedule has been substituted with a new provision prescribing how DDPs must deposit fees collected from FVCIs with SEBI in Indian Rupees.

## Regulatory Changes

The amendment substitutes Clause (6) of the Second Schedule with new requirements:

1. For first-time registration: the DDP must deposit the fee with SEBI within 5 working days from the date the certificate of registration is granted to the FVCI, along with details in the format specified by SEBI from time to time.
2. For fees payable for subsequent blocks (renewal/extension of registration) or for delayed fee payment: the DDP must deposit the fee within 5 working days from the date the DDP receives the fee from the FVCI, along with details in the prescribed format.

## Compliance Requirements

- Designated Depository Participants must update internal processes to collect the revised INR-denominated fees from FVCIs at the appropriate stages (before grant of registration certificate, and at renewal/extension or delayed payment).
- DDPs must remit collected fees to SEBI within 5 working days of the relevant trigger event (grant of certificate or receipt of fee) along with prescribed details.
- FVCIs and DDPs should align documentation and payment processes to the new INR fee schedule rather than the erstwhile USD amounts.

## Important Dates

- Gazette notification date: July 3, 2026 (Notification No. SEBI/LAD-NRO/GN/309)
- Effective date: 180 days from the date of publication in the Official Gazette (i.e., on or around December 30, 2026)

## Impact Assessment

This amendment is primarily an operational and compliance change affecting Foreign Venture Capital Investors and Designated Depository Participants. It standardizes fee collection in Indian Rupees, removing currency conversion ambiguity, and tightens the timeline for DDPs to remit fees to SEBI. The change has no direct bearing on listed securities, trading activity, or broader market operations, and is expected to have limited market impact, being confined to FVCI registration and renewal administration.
