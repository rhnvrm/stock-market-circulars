## Summary

This is an appellate order under the Right to Information Act, 2005 (Appeal No. 6901 of 2026) filed by Umesh Kumar Chaturvedi against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant, an investor in PACL Ltd, sought information about land purportedly allotted against his PACL certificates (No. U262079xxx and U26205xxxx), whose refund claim was rejected on the PACL refund portal on grounds that land had already been allotted. The Appellate Authority found the respondent's reply unclear on whether the requested information is held by SEBI.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed RTI application on May 16, 2026 seeking details of land allegedly allotted against two PACL certificates
- CPIO, SEBI responded on May 20, 2026, directing appellant to the Justice (Retd.) R. M. Lodha Committee for refund-related queries
- Appellant filed appeal on May 26, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00197) citing incomplete, misleading, or false information
- Appellate Authority found it unclear from the respondent's reply whether the requested information is available with SEBI or not
- Appellant claims to have never received any land possession letter, allotment letter, registry, or sale deed from PACL Ltd

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an individual RTI appellate proceeding.

## Compliance Requirements

The Appellate Authority noted that SEBI's (CPIO's) response did not clearly confirm whether the information requested by the appellant is held by SEBI. SEBI is required to clarify the availability of the information or provide a definitive response in accordance with the RTI Act, 2005.

## Important Dates

- **May 16, 2026**: Original RTI application filed by appellant
- **May 20, 2026**: CPIO, SEBI responded to the RTI application
- **May 26, 2026**: Appellant filed the first appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00197)
- **June 25, 2026**: Appellate order issued

## Impact Assessment

This order has no broad market or operational impact. It concerns an individual investor dispute over PACL refund claim status. The Lodha Committee, constituted by the Supreme Court, handles PACL refund matters, and SEBI's role is limited to providing available records. The order signals that SEBI must be more definitive in RTI responses about whether requested PACL-related information is held by SEBI or exclusively with the Lodha Committee.
