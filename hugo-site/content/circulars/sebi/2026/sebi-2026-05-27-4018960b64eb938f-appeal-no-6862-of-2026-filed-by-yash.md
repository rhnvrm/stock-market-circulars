## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority issued an order in Appeal No. 6862 of 2026, filed by appellant Yash against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appeal arose from an RTI application dated April 09, 2026 seeking information on SEBI's service bond policies for employees joining from Central Government and grade-wise staffing details. The respondent partially exempted information and partially provided staffing data.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed RTI application on April 09, 2026 via the RTI MIS portal; respondent replied on April 28, 2026.
- Appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00161) was filed on May 01, 2026.
- Queries 1 and 3 (service bond rules and policy on fresh bond execution) were denied as exempt under Section 8(1)(a) of the RTI Act, as they pertain to SEBI's internal functioning.
- Query 2 (employee-wise data on service bonds) was denied under Section 7(9) of the RTI Act due to disproportionate diversion of resources required to collate the data.
- Query 4 on staffing was partially answered: SEBI's sanctioned strength (Grade A to F) as on March 31, 2026 is 1,166; persons in position is 1,028. Sanctioned strength includes 135 vacancies for an ongoing Grade A recruitment exercise.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an appellate order in an individual RTI matter and does not amend any SEBI regulation or circular.

## Compliance Requirements

No compliance requirements applicable to market participants. The order is confined to the disposal of the RTI appeal between the individual appellant and SEBI's CPIO.

## Important Dates

- April 09, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- April 28, 2026: Respondent's reply to RTI application.
- May 01, 2026: Appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00161).
- March 31, 2026: Reference date for SEBI staffing data disclosed in the response.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no impact on market participants, listed companies, intermediaries, or trading operations. It is an administrative RTI matter confined to SEBI's internal HR policies regarding service bonds and staffing. The partial disclosure of staffing numbers (sanctioned strength 1,166; persons in position 1,028) provides limited public insight into SEBI's workforce but carries no regulatory or market significance.
