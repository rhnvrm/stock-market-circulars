## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005, dismissed Appeal No. 6911 of 2026 filed by Surajsinh Vakhatsinh Rathod. The appellant sought information regarding refunds in the PACL matter. The CPIO had responded that the requested information is not available with SEBI, and the Appellate Authority found no deficiency in that response.

## Key Points

- Appellant Surajsinh Vakhatsinh Rathod filed an RTI application (received April 20, 2026) seeking information about refunds in the PACL matter.
- CPIO responded on May 05, 2026, stating the requested information is not available with SEBI, but noted that PACL-related public notices, press releases, status reports, and FAQs are available on SEBI's website.
- The appellant filed an appeal dated May 25, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/P/26/00031), received by the Appellate Authority on June 01, 2026.
- The Appellate Authority upheld the CPIO's response, citing CIC precedent (Sh. Pattipati Rama Murthy vs. CPIO, SEBI, July 8, 2013) that a public authority cannot invent information it does not possess.
- The appellant's request to transfer the application to the concerned public authority was rejected: the Justice (Retd.) R. M. Lodha Committee, which handles PACL property disposal and investor repayment, has been held not to be a public authority under Section 2(h) of the RTI Act (CIC decision in M Shanmugam v CPIO, Pearls Agrotech Corporation Ltd., dated March 14, 2024).
- The appeal was dismissed.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an administrative appellate order under the RTI Act.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. The order reaffirms established RTI jurisprudence that public authorities cannot be compelled to provide information they do not hold.

## Important Dates

- April 20, 2026: RTI application received by SEBI.
- May 05, 2026: CPIO response to the RTI application.
- May 25, 2026: Appellant filed the first appeal.
- June 01, 2026: Appeal received by the Appellate Authority.
- June 25, 2026: Appeal dismissed by the Appellate Authority (Ruchi Chojer), Mumbai.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no market or operational impact. It is a routine administrative disposal of an individual RTI appeal concerning the PACL investor refund matter. The order reinforces that SEBI does not hold information on PACL refunds, and that the Justice Lodha Committee — which manages PACL investor repayment under a Supreme Court mandate dating to February 2, 2016 — is not subject to RTI as it is not a public authority under Section 2(h) of the RTI Act.
