## Summary

The Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 adjudicated Appeal No. 6866 of 2026 filed by Sunil Kumar against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought information related to SEBI regulations governing stock exchange obligations for data accuracy, corporate action updates, and investor complaint statistics. The respondent rejected the queries as vague and not specific, and the appellate authority concurred.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed RTI application on April 12, 2026 seeking six categories of information regarding stock exchange data accuracy obligations
- CPIO responded on May 04, 2026, rejecting queries 1–5 as vague and not specific under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act
- Query no. 6 (investor complaints count) was rejected as information not maintained in the requested format
- Appellant filed appeal on May 05, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00164) alleging incomplete, misleading or false information
- Appellate Authority upheld the CPIO's response, finding the queries insufficiently clear and specific

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a procedural RTI appellate order with no amendments to existing SEBI rules, regulations, or circulars.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements arise from this order. The order reaffirms the established legal principle that RTI requests must be clear, specific, and seek information actually available in the records of the public authority.

## Important Dates

- April 12, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant
- May 04, 2026: CPIO response issued
- May 05, 2026: First appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00164)

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct market or operational impact. It is a routine appellate decision on an individual RTI matter. The order reinforces that broad or vague RTI queries seeking policy-level information already in the public domain will not be treated as actionable information requests under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act.
