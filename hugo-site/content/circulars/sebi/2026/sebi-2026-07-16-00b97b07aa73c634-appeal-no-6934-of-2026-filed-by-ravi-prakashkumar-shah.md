## Summary

The Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 at SEBI has disposed of Appeal No. 6934 of 2026 filed by Ravi Prakashkumar Shah. The appellant had sought certified copies of correspondence, surveillance alerts, exception reports, concentration monitoring data, and regulatory advisories exchanged between SEBI and Multi Commodity Exchange of India Ltd. (MCX) concerning MCX Gold Futures contracts during the period January 19-22, 2026. The CPIO had denied the information citing exemption under Section 8(1)(a) of the RTI Act, and the Appellate Authority concurred with this denial, holding that such surveillance and supervisory communications are strategic and confidential in nature.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed an RTI application dated May 23, 2026 seeking records on SEBI-MCX communications regarding Gold Futures contracts for January 19-22, 2026.
- Five categories of information were sought: (1) correspondence/internal notings between SEBI and MCX on surveillance, volatility and risk management; (2) surveillance alerts and exception reports; (3) open interest concentration and trading volume data; (4) regulatory advisories/directions issued to MCX during January 2026; (5) a categorical statement if no such records exist.
- The CPIO responded on June 17, 2026, denying all queries on grounds that the information pertains to SEBI's internal functioning and systems, and is strategic in nature, exempt under Section 8(1)(a) of the RTI Act.
- For query no. 4, the respondent additionally referenced complaints dated March 3 and March 13, 2026 filed by the appellant with SEBI.
- The appellant appealed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00223) on the ground that access to information was refused.
- The Appellate Authority concurred with the CPIO, finding that surveillance alerts and supervisory communications sought are strategic and confidential, given SEBI's role as regulator.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an adjudicatory order on an RTI appeal and does not introduce any new regulation, circular, or policy change.

## Compliance Requirements

Not applicable to market participants; this order pertains solely to SEBI's internal RTI disclosure process and does not impose obligations on listed entities, exchanges, or intermediaries.

## Important Dates

- May 23, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- June 17, 2026: CPIO response and appellant's appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00223).
- Period covered by the information request: January 19-22, 2026 (MCX Gold Futures surveillance).
- March 3 and March 13, 2026: Related complaints filed by appellant referenced in the order.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct market, trading, or stock-price impact. It confirms SEBI's practice of withholding internal surveillance and supervisory communications with exchanges (here, MCX) from public disclosure under the RTI Act's strategic/confidential information exemption. It is relevant primarily as a transparency and regulatory-process data point, indicating SEBI's continued reluctance to disclose specifics of its market surveillance interactions with exchanges, even in response to targeted RTI requests about a specific volatility episode in Gold Futures.
