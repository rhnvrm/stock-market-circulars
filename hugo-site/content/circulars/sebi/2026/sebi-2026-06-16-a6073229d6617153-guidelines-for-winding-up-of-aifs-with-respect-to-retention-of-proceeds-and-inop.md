## Summary

SEBI has issued guidelines specifying conditions under which AIFs and VCFs may retain liquidation proceeds beyond their permissible fund life, and introduces the concept of 'Inoperative Fund' status. The circular operationalises amendments to the AIF Regulations notified on April 18, 2026, covering Regulation 29 provisions on winding up, proceed retention, and surrender of registration.

## Key Points

- AIFs may retain proceeds beyond the liquidation/dissolution period if at least one of three specified conditions is met
- Retention on account of anticipated litigation requires consent from at least 75% of investors by value
- Retention for residual operational expenses is capped at three years from end of permissible fund life
- All retained monies must be invested as per Regulation 15(1)(f) of AIF Regulations
- AIFs with retained monies may apply for 'Inoperative Fund' status under Regulation 29(10A)
- Upon satisfaction of all liabilities and distribution of retained monies, the scheme must be formally wound up and registration surrendered to SEBI

## Regulatory Changes

The SEBI (Alternative Investment Funds) Regulations, 2012 were amended and notified on April 18, 2026, introducing:
- Regulation 29(7): Assets to be liquidated and proceeds distributed after satisfying liabilities, subject to SEBI-specified conditions
- Regulation 29(10A): New provision enabling AIFs to be tagged as 'Inoperative Fund' in SEBI-specified manner
- Regulation 29(11): Certificate of registration to be surrendered upon winding up

This circular specifies the conditions and modalities for: (i) retaining proceeds beyond permissible fund life, (ii) tagging as 'Inoperative Fund', and (iii) surrender of registration.

## Compliance Requirements

**Conditions for retaining proceeds beyond permissible fund life (at least one must be met):**
1. Demonstrable receipt of litigation notice or demand — including communications from tax authorities, regulatory bodies, law enforcement, courts, investors, or counterparties indicating potential tax, regulatory, or legal liability (covers show-cause notices, re-assessment notices, investigation summons, and similar communications)
2. Consent from at least 75% of investors by value of investment in the scheme, for retention on account of possible/probable litigation or tax demand — manager must disclose the amount retained and estimated retention period when seeking consent
3. Substantiation of amounts retained for residual winding up operational expenses via invoices, supporting documents, or records of comparable prior-year expenses

**Additional obligations:**
- Manager must disclose amount and estimated retention period to investors when seeking consent under condition 2
- Retention for operational expenses (condition 3) must not exceed three years from end of permissible fund life
- Implementation standards for operational heads under which monies may be retained to be formulated by Standard Setting Forum of AIFs (SFA) in consultation with SEBI
- All retained monies must be invested per Regulation 15(1)(f) of AIF Regulations
- AIFs intending to apply for 'Inoperative Fund' status must have one or more schemes with retained monies as specified

## Important Dates

- **April 18, 2026**: AIF Regulations amended and notified, introducing Regulation 29(10A) and related provisions
- **June 16, 2026**: This circular issued, specifying conditions and modalities for retain of proceeds and 'Inoperative Fund' status
- **Three-year cap**: Retention for residual operational expenses must not extend beyond three years from end of permissible fund life

## Impact Assessment

This circular directly impacts all AIFs and VCFs registered with SEBI. It provides a structured framework for funds approaching or past their wind-down stage, addressing practical challenges around unsettled liabilities and litigation. Key impacts include:

- **Fund managers**: Must ensure retention of proceeds complies with one of the three specified conditions; must obtain investor consent (75% threshold) and make disclosures where retention is for anticipated liabilities
- **Investors in AIFs**: Greater transparency required from managers on retained amounts and timelines; 75% investor consent threshold gives meaningful control over retention for anticipated liabilities
- **Operational**: SFA to formulate implementation standards for permissible operational expense heads, adding a layer of industry standardisation
- **'Inoperative Fund' designation**: Provides a formal regulatory status for funds in extended wind-down, reducing regulatory ambiguity for such vehicles
- No direct impact on listed equities or broader capital markets
