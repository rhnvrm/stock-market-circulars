## Summary

SEBI issued a General Remittance Order dated April 21, 2026 under Recovery Certificate No. 8711 of 2025 against Securekloud Technologies Ltd (formerly known as 8Kmiles Software Services Ltd, PAN: AABCP6266D). The order directs the recovery of outstanding dues owed to SEBI in the matter of Securekloud Technologies Limited.

## Key Points

- General Remittance Order issued by SEBI on April 21, 2026
- Recovery Certificate number: RC No. 8711 of 2025
- Subject entity: Securekloud Technologies Ltd (formerly 8Kmiles Software Services Ltd)
- PAN of the entity: AABCP6266D
- Order pertains to recovery of dues arising from prior SEBI proceedings against the company
- SEBI is exercising its recovery powers under the relevant provisions of the SEBI Act

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is an enforcement/recovery order implementing an earlier SEBI direction or penalty against Securekloud Technologies Ltd.

## Compliance Requirements

- Securekloud Technologies Ltd is required to remit the outstanding dues as specified in the Recovery Certificate No. 8711 of 2025
- Non-compliance with a General Remittance Order may invite further enforcement action including attachment of assets or bank accounts
- The company's bankers, depositories, or other financial intermediaries may be directed to facilitate the remittance

## Important Dates

- **April 21, 2026**: Date of issuance of the General Remittance Order
- **RC No. 8711 of 2025**: Underlying Recovery Certificate (issued in 2025)

## Impact Assessment

This order is targeted at Securekloud Technologies Ltd (formerly 8Kmiles Software Services Ltd) and represents SEBI's enforcement mechanism to recover dues from the company. The action signals ongoing regulatory scrutiny of the entity. Investors in the company should be aware of the company's outstanding liabilities to SEBI. The broader market impact is limited, as this is a company-specific enforcement action rather than a sector-wide regulatory change.
