## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has issued a Notice of Attachment dated July 22, 2026, under Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 9177 of 2026, drawn against Vidhi Nikunj Shah. The recovery proceeding arises in the matter concerning trading activities of certain entities in the scrip of DU Digital Technologies Limited, which has since been renamed DU Digital Global Limited. The notice is part of SEBI's enforcement mechanism to recover outstanding dues (such as monetary penalties, disgorgement amounts, or other sums) confirmed against the noticee pursuant to an earlier SEBI order.

## Key Points

- Notice of Attachment issued on July 22, 2026 by SEBI's Recovery Officer.
- Issued under Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 9177 of 2026.
- Directed against defaulter Vidhi Nikunj Shah.
- Arises from proceedings relating to trading activities of certain entities in the scrip of DU Digital Technologies Limited (now DU Digital Global Limited).
- Attachment notices are typically issued under the recovery provisions applicable to SEBI (akin to the Second Schedule of the Income Tax Act, 1961, as made applicable to SEBI recovery proceedings) to attach movable/immovable property, bank accounts, or other assets of the defaulter for non-payment of confirmed dues.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement/recovery action pursuant to an existing SEBI order against the named individual.

## Compliance Requirements

- The noticee (Vidhi Nikunj Shah) is required to clear the outstanding dues under RC No. 9177 of 2026 to avoid attachment/sale of the attached assets.
- Banks, depositories, and other intermediaries holding assets/accounts of the noticee may be required to comply with the attachment directions and report compliance to SEBI's Recovery Officer.

## Important Dates

- Notice of Attachment date: July 22, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

The notice has limited direct impact on the securities market or on DU Digital Global Limited as a listed entity, since it targets an individual defaulter's assets for recovery of dues rather than imposing obligations on the company itself. It is relevant primarily for regulatory and enforcement tracking, and for market participants monitoring SEBI's recovery actions related to past trading irregularities in the scrip.
