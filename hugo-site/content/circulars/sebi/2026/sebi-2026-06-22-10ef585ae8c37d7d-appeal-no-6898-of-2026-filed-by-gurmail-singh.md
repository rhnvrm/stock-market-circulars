## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 has disposed of Appeal No. 6898 of 2026 filed by Gurmail Singh against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought information related to the status of his refund application in the PACL matter. The appeal was found to be time-barred as it was filed after the 30-day statutory limit, and the respondent had already informed that the requested information is not available with SEBI.

## Key Points

- Appellant Gurmail Singh filed an RTI application dated April 09, 2026 seeking information about his PACL refund application and land allotment status against PACL Certificate No. U312005xxx.
- SEBI (respondent) responded on April 21, 2026 stating the requested information is not available with SEBI, and directed the appellant to SEBI's website for PACL-related public notices, press releases, and FAQs.
- The appellant filed the first appeal on May 25, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00194), which was beyond the 30-day limit from receipt of the CPIO's response dated April 21, 2026.
- No request for condonation of delay or explanation for the delay was submitted by the appellant.
- The Appellate Authority considered the appeal on merit notwithstanding the time-bar finding.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine appellate order under the Right to Information Act, 2005.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. The order reaffirms the established principle that a public authority (SEBI) is not obligated to provide information it does not hold. RTI appellants must file first appeals within 30 days of receiving the CPIO's response under Section 19(1) of the RTI Act.

## Important Dates

- April 09, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant
- April 21, 2026: CPIO/respondent's response to the RTI application
- May 25, 2026: First appeal received by SEBI (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00194) — filed beyond the 30-day statutory limit

## Impact Assessment

This order has no market or operational impact. It is an individual RTI appellate matter concerning a single investor's query about the PACL refund process. The decision reiterates that SEBI does not hold individualised PACL claimant data and directs investors to SEBI's public website resources for PACL-related information. No action is required by market participants.
