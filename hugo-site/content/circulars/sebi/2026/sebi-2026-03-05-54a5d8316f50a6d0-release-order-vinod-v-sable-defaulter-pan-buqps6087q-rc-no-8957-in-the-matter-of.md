## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has issued an order releasing all attached bank accounts, lockers, demat accounts, and mutual fund folios of Shri Vinod V. Sable (PAN: BUQPS6087Q), a defaulter in the matter of Svarnim Trade Udyog Ltd. The release follows full and final recovery of the outstanding dues under Recovery Certificate No. 8957 of 2025.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. 8957 of 2025 was drawn on November 25, 2025 for recovery of Rs. 58,51,000 (plus interest/costs/charges) from Vinod V. Sable.
- Notices of Attachment dated December 17, 2025 had attached the defaulter's bank accounts/lockers, demat accounts, and mutual fund folios under Attachment Proceedings Nos. 15068 & 15069 of 2025.
- SEBI recovered Rs. 60,03,000 (including further interest/costs/expenses), constituting full and final settlement of the demand.
- All banks, mutual funds, NSDL, and CDSL are directed to release the attached assets of the defaulter.
- Entities where the defaulter holds no accounts or folios need not inform SEBI.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an administrative closure order under SEBI's recovery proceedings.

## Compliance Requirements

- All banks in India, mutual funds, NSDL, and CDSL must release any attached accounts, lockers, demat accounts, or mutual fund folios held in the name of Vinod V. Sable (PAN: BUQPS6087Q) pursuant to the earlier attachment notices.
- Release must be made under intimation to SEBI.
- Entities with no accounts/folios for the defaulter are not required to respond.

## Important Dates

- November 25, 2025: Recovery Certificate No. 8957 of 2025 issued.
- December 17, 2025: Notices of Attachment issued for bank accounts, demat accounts, and mutual fund folios.
- March 5, 2026: Release order issued after full recovery of Rs. 60,03,000.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no broader market impact. It is an individual-level administrative action concluding SEBI's recovery proceedings against Vinod V. Sable in the matter of Svarnim Trade Udyog Ltd. The matter is fully resolved with complete recovery of dues.
