## Summary

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) has notified the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Issue and Listing of Municipal Debt Securities) (Amendment) Regulations, 2026, amending the SEBI (Issue and Listing of Municipal Debt Securities) Regulations, 2015. The notification (No. SEBI/LAD-NRO/GN/2026/305) was published in the Gazette of India on July 1, 2026, and the amended regulations take effect from the date of publication in the Official Gazette.

The amendment introduces new definitions for "Retail Individual Investor" and "Working Day," adds a new regulatory framework for issuance and listing of ESG debt securities under the municipal debt securities regime, introduces conditions governing transfer of funds raised through Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) set up under the Government of India's Pooled Finance Development Fund Scheme, and makes consequential changes to Regulation 6 regarding applicable schedules.

## Key Points

- New definition of "Retail Individual Investor" inserted in Regulation 2(1): an investor who applies or bids for municipal debt securities up to a value of Rs. 2,00,000 (two lakh rupees).
- New definition of "Working Day" inserted in Regulation 2(1), with two distinct explanations:
  - For the purpose of the bid/issue period, "working day" means all days (excluding Saturdays, Sundays, and public holidays) on which commercial banks are open for business in the city mentioned in the offer document.
  - For the purpose of the period between the bid/issue closing date and the date of listing of non-convertible securities on stock exchanges, "working day" means all days (excluding Saturdays, Sundays, and bank holidays) as determined by the Board, on which trading occurs in non-convertible securities on stock exchanges.
- New Regulation 4F inserted (after Regulation 4E): Issuers wishing to issue and list ESG debt securities must comply with the conditions prescribed for such securities under the SEBI (Issue and Listing of Non-Convertible Securities) Regulations, 2021, and circulars issued thereunder.
- New Regulation 5A inserted (between Regulations 5 and 6) governing fund transfer through a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV): where the issuer is an SPV formed under the Government of India's Pooled Finance Development Fund Scheme, the concerned municipalities must enter into an agreement with the issuer before funds are disbursed, and this arrangement must be disclosed in the offer document. Such an SPV must be structured either as a trust or as a company.
- Regulation 6 amended to add a reference to "Schedule IB (whichever is applicable)" alongside the existing reference to Schedule IA.
- Amendments also made to Regulation 9(1) (details truncated in the source extract available).

## Regulatory Changes

The amendment modifies the SEBI (Issue and Listing of Municipal Debt Securities) Regulations, 2015 by:

1. Adding definitional clarity for retail individual investors and working days, aligning municipal debt security issuances with terminology used elsewhere in SEBI's non-convertible securities framework.
2. Extending the ESG debt securities framework (applicable under the Non-Convertible Securities Regulations, 2021) to municipal debt securities issuers.
3. Formalizing governance requirements for SPVs created under the Pooled Finance Development Fund Scheme, requiring a binding agreement with participating municipalities prior to fund transfer and mandatory disclosure in offer documents.
4. Updating cross-references to schedules under Regulation 6 to account for the applicable schedule format.

## Compliance Requirements

- Municipal debt security issuers proposing to issue ESG-labeled debt securities must comply with the conditions specified under the SEBI (Issue and Listing of Non-Convertible Securities) Regulations, 2021, and related circulars.
- Issuers structured as SPVs under the Pooled Finance Development Fund Scheme must execute agreements with participating municipal bodies before transferring proceeds, and disclose these agreements in offer documents.
- Such SPVs must be constituted as either a trust or a company.
- Offer documents must specify the city used to determine "working day" for bid/issue period purposes.
- Issuers must apply the updated Schedule IA/IB reference under Regulation 6, as applicable to their issuance.

## Important Dates

- Notification date: July 1, 2026 (Ashadha 10, 1948 Saka).
- Effective date: The amendment regulations come into force from the date of their publication in the Official Gazette (July 1, 2026).

## Impact Assessment

This amendment primarily affects municipal bodies, SPVs formed under the Pooled Finance Development Fund Scheme, and merchant bankers/intermediaries involved in structuring and listing municipal debt securities in India. The introduction of an ESG debt securities framework could open a new fundraising avenue for municipalities seeking to finance green/sustainable urban infrastructure projects, potentially increasing market participation in this segment. The SPV fund-transfer safeguards add a layer of governance and disclosure aimed at protecting bondholders and ensuring accountability in the use of proceeds. As the Indian municipal bond market remains relatively small compared to corporate and government debt markets, the near-term market impact is limited but the changes are procedurally significant for entities operating in or considering this space, and do not directly affect specific listed equities.
