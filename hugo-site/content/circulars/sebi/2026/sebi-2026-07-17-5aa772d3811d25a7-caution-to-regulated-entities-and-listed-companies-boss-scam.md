## Summary

SEBI has issued Press Release No. 40/2026 cautioning regulated entities and listed companies about an emerging cybercrime trend known as the "Boss Scam" or CEO/MD impersonation fraud. The alert follows intimation from the Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C), which has observed fraudsters impersonating CEOs or high-ranking officials to trick finance officers into transferring funds to mule accounts, using either deepfake technology or malware-laden files that hijack WhatsApp Web sessions.

## Key Points

- The "Boss Scam" involves fraudsters impersonating CEOs/MDs via email, WhatsApp, Microsoft Teams, or other social media platforms to instruct subordinates to transfer funds.
- Strategy A: Impersonation through deepfake methods such as voice cloning, AI-manipulated video calls, and fake social media profiles/groups of senior officials.
- Strategy B: Sending a malicious .zip archive containing an executable (.exe) and a Dynamic Link Library (.dll) file; when opened by a finance officer, it installs a Trojan dropper that hijacks the victim's active WhatsApp Web session.
- Once WhatsApp Web is hijacked, fraudsters contact finance/accounts staff directly, instructing immediate payments to specified mule bank accounts.
- In some cases, fraudsters achieve full device takeover and alter contact names to impersonate the CEO/MD using a secondary phone number.
- Fraudulent instructions are sometimes accompanied by claims that the transaction is Unpublished Price Sensitive Information (UPSI) to discourage victims from verifying or disclosing it.

## Regulatory Changes

This is a press release/advisory and does not introduce new regulations, amendments, or compliance mandates. It serves as a cautionary notice based on inputs from I4C.

## Compliance Requirements

Regulated entities and listed companies are advised to:

- Remain cautious and cross-check fund transfer requests received via WhatsApp, email, or social media by directly calling seniors to verify authenticity.
- Avoid transferring funds solely based on instructions received through social media platforms.
- Refrain from installing executables without verifying the sender's identity, even if the file appears to come from a known contact, verifying via a direct call.
- Log out of any WhatsApp Web sessions that are not actively in use.
- Report any fraudulent activity or scam incidents immediately to 1930 or via www.cybercrime.gov.in.

## Important Dates

Press release issued: July 17, 2026 (Mumbai). No compliance deadlines specified.

## Impact Assessment

The advisory has no direct market or trading impact but carries operational and reputational risk implications for listed companies and regulated entities, particularly finance and accounts departments. Failure to heed the caution could result in significant financial losses through fraudulent fund transfers. Companies are expected to strengthen internal verification protocols and cybersecurity awareness among finance personnel to mitigate this risk.
