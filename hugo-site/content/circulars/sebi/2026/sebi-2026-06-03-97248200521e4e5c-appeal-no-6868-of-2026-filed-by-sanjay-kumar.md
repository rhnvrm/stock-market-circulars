## Summary

SEBI's Appellate Authority is adjudicating Appeal No. 6868 of 2026 filed by Sanjay Kumar against the Central Public Information Officer (CPIO), SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought information under the RTI Act regarding SEBI's handling of his complaint about alleged illegal fund mobilisation by Bahubala Private Limited. The CPIO denied the information citing statutory exemptions, and the appellant has challenged that denial.

## Key Points

- Appellant Sanjay Kumar had filed a complaint and subsequent appeal before SEBI (Appeal No. SEBII/E/A/26/0000078) regarding alleged illegal fund mobilisation by Bahubala Private Limited
- Related grievance was also filed via PMOPG portal (PMOPG/E/2026/0029270 dated 19-02-2026)
- RTI application dated April 06, 2026 sought copies of file notings, internal correspondence, communications to Bahubala Private Limited, inquiry/inspection reports, communications with PMO, disposal orders, and names of officials who processed the complaint
- CPIO denied information on queries 2–7 citing Section 8(1)(a) (strategic/internal functioning, may hamper SEBI's regulatory decision-making)
- CPIO denied information on query 8 (names/designations of officials) citing Section 8(1)(j) (personal information that may endanger physical safety)
- The Appellate Authority concurs with CPIO's response on query no. 2

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes arise from this order. It is an administrative adjudication of an individual RTI appeal.

## Compliance Requirements

No compliance requirements for market participants. This order pertains solely to the RTI information request by an individual appellant.

## Important Dates

- April 06, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant
- May 05, 2026: CPIO response to RTI application
- May 05, 2026: First appeal filed by appellant (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00166)

## Impact Assessment

This order has no impact on market participants, listed companies, or trading operations. It is a routine RTI appellate proceeding concerning an individual investor's grievance about alleged illegal fund mobilisation by a private limited company. The order reaffirms SEBI's standard practice of exempting internal file notings and deliberative documents from RTI disclosure under Section 8(1)(a), and official contact details under Section 8(1)(j).
