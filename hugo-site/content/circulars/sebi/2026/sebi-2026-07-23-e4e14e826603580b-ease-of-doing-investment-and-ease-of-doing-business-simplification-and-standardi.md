## Summary

SEBI has issued a circular revising the framework for transmission of securities, aimed at enhancing ease of doing investment and ease of doing business. The revision, made under Regulation 40(7) of the SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015 (as amended vide Gazette Notification SEBI/LAD-NRO/GN/2026/312 dated July 10, 2026), introduces a harmonised, standardised and risk-based process for transmission of securities across listed companies, RTAs, depositories, DPs and AMCs.

## Key Points

- Introduction of a new low-value claims category called Quick Transmission Processing (QTP) for small investors.
- Revised monetary thresholds for transmission under the simplified documentation framework:
  - Physical mode securities: QTP threshold of ₹10,000; simplified documentation threshold of ₹3 lakh per listed entity/MF/SIF units in SOA form per AMC (listed entities may raise this threshold at their discretion).
  - Dematerialised mode securities: QTP threshold of ₹30,000; simplified documentation threshold of ₹30 lakh per beneficial owner.
- Removal of the mandatory requirement of Probate of Will, aligned with recent amendments to succession laws.
- Introduction of a combined affidavit-cum-No Objection Certificate (NOC), replacing separate affidavit and NOC documents.
- Acceptance of death certificates with QR Code as an eligible document, in addition to original/attested copies.
- For foreign-issued death certificates, additional verification modes permitted via overseas branches of Indian banks or foreign banks with correspondent banking relationships.
- Processing entities must continue applying the revised framework's benefits to pending requests submitted before the effective date, without requiring re-submission of already-submitted documents in new formats.

## Regulatory Changes

The circular prescribes a revised transmission framework (detailed in a 38-page Annexure with model forms) that replaces the earlier process followed by listed companies, RTAs, depositories, DPs and AMCs for effecting transmission of securities. Key regulatory changes include the new QTP category, revised claim thresholds, removal of mandatory Probate of Will, combined affidavit-cum-NOC, and expanded acceptance of death certificate formats/verification modes.

## Compliance Requirements

- All listed companies, RTAs, depositories, DPs, AMCs and AMFI must adopt the revised transmission framework and model forms specified in the Annexure.
- Processing entities must apply the simplified procedure to transmission requests received before the effective date wherever feasible, and must not seek re-submission of documents already provided in old formats.
- Processing entities are required to submit monthly reports to SEBI (at rta@sebi.gov.in) on transmission request processing (cases received, approved, rejected, pending, and cases where additional documents were sought) for a period of 6 months.

## Important Dates

- July 10, 2026: Gazette Notification SEBI/LAD-NRO/GN/2026/312 amending Regulation 40(7) of LODR Regulations.
- July 23, 2026: Date of issuance of this circular.
- Effective date: 30 days from the date of issuance of this circular (i.e., on or around August 22, 2026).
- Monthly reporting to SEBI required for 6 months following implementation.

## Impact Assessment

This circular primarily affects operational and compliance processes for listed companies, RTAs, depositories, DPs, AMCs and investors' associations rather than trading or stock prices directly. It should meaningfully improve ease of doing investment for retail investors and legal heirs by reducing documentation burden and processing time for transmission of securities, particularly benefiting small investors through the new QTP category. Processing entities will need to update internal SOPs, forms, and reporting mechanisms ahead of the effective date and during the 6-month monthly reporting period to SEBI.
