## Summary

SEBI has passed an order under Sections 11(1) and 11(2)(h) of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with Regulation 11(5) of the SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011 (SAST Regulations), in the matter of a proposed acquisition of shares and voting rights in Vadilal Industries Limited (Target Company) by IVG Trust (Acquirer Trust). The order pertains to an application dated August 7, 2025 seeking exemption from the applicability of Regulation 3 and Regulation 5 read with Regulation 4 of the SAST Regulations, 2011, in connection with a proposed direct and indirect acquisition of shares in the Target Company.

## Key Points

- Vadilal Industries Limited is listed on both BSE and NSE, with a paid-up equity capital of INR 7,18,78,300 divided into 71,87,830 equity shares of INR 10 each.
- IVG Trust applied for exemption from open offer trigger requirements under Regulation 3 and Regulation 5 read with Regulation 4 of the SAST Regulations, 2011, in relation to a proposed reorganization of promoter/promoter group shareholding.
- The originally contemplated transaction involved partition of an HUF entity, which was later dropped; the Acquirer Trust submitted a revised application reflecting the updated transaction structure on November 9, 2025.
- SEBI identified discrepancies in the names of the settlor, trustees, and beneficiaries across KYC documents, the Trust Deed, and the shareholding pattern filed with the exchanges; the Acquirer Trust was directed to rectify these.
- Amended Trust Deed and related undertakings were submitted on May 8, 2026, followed by further clarifications sought by SEBI on May 19, 2026, with the Acquirer Trust's final response received on June 3, 2026.
- The shareholding pattern as of March 31, 2026 shows multiple promoter/promoter group members and entities (including Gandhi Virendra Ramchandra HUF, Rajesh R. Gandhi HUF, and individual family members) holding stakes in the Target Company, indicating the transaction relates to intra-family/promoter group consolidation via a trust structure.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is a case-specific exemption order under the existing SAST Regulations, 2011 framework, addressing whether the proposed acquisition of shares/voting rights by IVG Trust triggers open offer obligations under Regulations 3, 4, and 5.

## Compliance Requirements

The Acquirer Trust was required to rectify discrepancies between KYC documentation, the Trust Deed, and the shareholding pattern disclosed to exchanges, and to submit an amended Trust Deed along with undertakings before SEBI could finalize its examination of the exemption application.

## Important Dates

- August 7, 2025: Original exemption application received by SEBI.
- November 9, 2025: Updated application reflecting revised transaction structure received.
- May 8, 2026: Amended Trust Deed and undertakings submitted.
- May 19, 2026: Final clarifications sought by SEBI.
- June 3, 2026: Final reply received from the Acquirer Trust.

## Impact Assessment

The order primarily concerns an internal promoter/promoter-group restructuring of shareholding in Vadilal Industries Limited through a trust vehicle (IVG Trust), and SEBI's determination on whether this triggers mandatory open offer requirements under the SAST Regulations. While this does not directly alter public shareholding or trading activity, it is relevant for investors monitoring promoter group structuring and governance at Vadilal Industries Limited, and sets precedent on SEBI's treatment of intra-family trust-based shareholding consolidations under the takeover code.
