## Summary

SEBI's Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 has condoned the delay in filing and admitted the first appeal (Appeal No. 6904 of 2026) by M Ananttharam against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought detailed information about SCORES Complaint No. SEBIE/TN25/KANC/053207/1, which was filed on December 26, 2025 against ICICI Bank.

## Key Points

- Appellant M Ananttharam filed an RTI application dated March 23, 2026 seeking information related to a SCORES complaint against ICICI Bank.
- CPIO, SEBI responded by letter dated April 16, 2026.
- The first appeal was filed on May 27, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00198), beyond the permissible 30-day window under Section 19(1) of the RTI Act.
- The Appellate Authority condoned the delay, accepting the appellant's submission that the SCORES workflow history enclosed by respondent required careful examination and that the delay was not deliberate.
- The appeal is being considered on merit based on available records.
- The appellant sought 12 categories of information including certified copies of file records, internal note sheets, ICICI Bank's Action Taken Report, SEBI-ICICI Bank correspondence, complaint workflow records, closure notes, and SOPs for SCORES complaint processing.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an individual RTI appeal order disposing a procedural delay condonation request.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. The order is limited to directing that the RTI appeal filed by M Ananttharam be considered on merit.

## Important Dates

- December 26, 2025: Original SCORES complaint (No. SEBIE/TN25/KANC/053207/1) filed against ICICI Bank.
- March 23, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- April 16, 2026: CPIO, SEBI responded to the RTI application.
- May 27, 2026: First appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00198) — beyond the 30-day statutory deadline.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no market-wide impact. It is an individual RTI appellate proceeding concerning information access about a SCORES investor grievance complaint against ICICI Bank. The significance is limited to the appellant and does not affect trading, listing, or regulatory obligations for any broader class of market participants.
