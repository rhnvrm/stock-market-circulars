## Summary

SEBI has announced a comprehensive review of the Master Circular for Stock Exchanges & Clearing Corporations and the Master Circular for Commodity Derivatives as part of its 'optimal regulation' initiative. The exercise aims to simplify content, remove redundancies, delegate responsibilities, and rationalize periodic filings. Four consultation papers have been issued on ease of doing business for Stock Exchanges, with the fourth — on Trading Software and Technology — currently open for public comments until July 13, 2026.

## Key Points

- Consolidation into a single Master Circular covering both Stock Exchanges and Commodity Derivatives Exchanges
- Separate Master Circular to be issued for Clearing Corporations
- Separate consolidated circular for Market Infrastructure Institutions (MIIs) on common IT provisions
- Approximately 50% reduction in the number of pages of the Master Circular for Exchanges
- Discontinuation of several reports submitted to SEBI, replaced by delegation to MII Committees or website disclosures
- Discontinuation of Registration of Investment Managers with SEBI for Direct Market Access (DMA) facility
- Introduction of single window registration for brokers to provide Smart Order Routing facility
- Review of System and Network Audit Framework for MIIs
- Discontinuation of Close To Money (CTM) norms for Options in Goods
- Delineation of responsibilities in monitoring of position limits across products
- Review of Client Code Modification framework
- Introduction of liberalized norms for Liquidity Enhancement Scheme
- Merger of Investor Protection Fund for equity and commodity segments

## Regulatory Changes

The review proposes structural reorganization of the regulatory framework governing MIIs:
- A single Master Circular will replace separate circulars for Stock Exchanges and Commodity Derivatives Exchanges
- A dedicated Master Circular for Clearing Corporations will be issued separately after consultation
- A standalone consolidated circular for IT-related provisions applicable to all MIIs will be introduced
- Registration requirements for Investment Managers providing DMA will be discontinued
- CTM norms for Options in Goods will be discontinued
- Liberalized norms will be introduced for the Liquidity Enhancement Scheme
- Investor Protection Funds for equity and commodity segments will be merged

## Compliance Requirements

- **Stock Exchanges and Clearing Corporations**: Await issuance of revised Master Circulars after public consultation concludes; prepare for revised reporting and delegation structures
- **Brokers**: Single window registration for Smart Order Routing will replace current multi-step process
- **Investment Managers**: DMA registration with SEBI will no longer be required
- **Market Participants**: Review and submit comments on the fourth consultation paper (Trading Software and Technology for Exchanges) by July 13, 2026
- **MIIs**: Prepare for delegation of certain reporting responsibilities to internal committees or public disclosures on websites

## Important Dates

- **June 22, 2026**: PR No. 35/2026 issued; comprehensive review announced
- **July 13, 2026**: Deadline for public comments on the fourth consultation paper on Trading Software and Technology for Exchanges
- **TBD**: Master Circular for Exchanges to be issued after review of public comments on all four consultation papers
- **TBD**: Master Circular for Clearing Corporations to be issued after separate consultation

## Impact Assessment

This initiative represents one of the most significant structural overhauls of the regulatory framework for Market Infrastructure Institutions in recent years. The impact is broad and high:

- **Stock Exchanges and Commodity Exchanges**: Substantially reduced compliance burden (~50% fewer pages), streamlined reporting to SEBI, and delegated governance to internal committees
- **Clearing Corporations**: Will operate under a dedicated Master Circular tailored to their specific functions
- **Brokers and Intermediaries**: Simplified registration processes for DMA and Smart Order Routing; reduced regulatory friction
- **Derivatives Market**: Removal of CTM norms for Options in Goods and clearer position limit monitoring responsibilities
- **Investor Protection**: Merger of equity and commodity Investor Protection Funds creates a unified corpus
- **Technology and IT**: Consolidated IT circular for MIIs will standardize system and network audit frameworks

The changes are expected to lower cost of compliance, reduce redundant filings, and modernize the regulatory architecture governing Indian exchanges and clearing corporations.
