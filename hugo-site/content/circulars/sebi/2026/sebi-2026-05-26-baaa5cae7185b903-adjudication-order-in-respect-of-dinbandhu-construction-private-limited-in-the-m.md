## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer issued an order against Dinbandhu Construction Private Limited (PAN: AACCD8172B) for participating in large-scale reversal trades in the illiquid stock options segment of BSE during the investigation period (IP) of April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015. The noticee's trades contributed to the creation of artificial volumes and false appearance of trading activity, in violation of SEBI's PFUTP Regulations, 2003.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated trading in illiquid stock options at BSE for the period April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015
- Out of all trades in the BSE stock options segment during the IP, 2,91,744 trades (81.40%) were found to be non-genuine
- Dinbandhu Construction Private Limited was identified as one of the entities executing reversal trades that created artificial volumes
- The trades were deemed manipulative and deceptive in nature
- Adjudication Order No. Order/AK/DS/2026-27/32417 was issued under Section 15-I of the SEBI Act, 1992
- Ms. Asha Shetty was originally appointed as Adjudicating Officer (July 27, 2022); the undersigned was appointed following her transfer (April 03, 2025)
- Show Cause Notice was issued on August 04, 2022

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is an enforcement action under existing regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Entities must refrain from executing reversal trades or any trades that create artificial volumes or false appearance of trading activity in stock options or any other segment
- Compliance with Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1), and 4(2)(a) of SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices) Regulations, 2003 is mandatory
- Penalties under Section 15HA of the SEBI Act, 1992 are applicable for violations of PFUTP Regulations

## Important Dates

- Investigation Period (IP): April 1, 2014 – September 30, 2015
- Adjudicating Officer appointed: July 27, 2022
- Show Cause Notice issued: August 04, 2022
- Reassignment of Adjudicating Officer: April 03, 2025
- Order issued: May 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order has limited direct market impact as it pertains to historical trading activity from 2014-2015. The enforcement action reinforces SEBI's continued pursuit of entities involved in illiquid stock options manipulation at BSE. It serves as a deterrent and precedent for similar cases. No specific penalty quantum is disclosed in the available excerpt. The affected entity is a private construction company with no significant ongoing market presence.
