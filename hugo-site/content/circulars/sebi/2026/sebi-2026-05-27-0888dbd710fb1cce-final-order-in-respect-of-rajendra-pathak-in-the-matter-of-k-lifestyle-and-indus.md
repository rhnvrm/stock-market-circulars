## Summary

SEBI has issued a final order against Rajendra Pathak (PAN: AQBPP0893P), former CFO of K-Lifestyle & Industries Limited (KLIL), in a matter that was remanded by the Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT) via order dated July 10, 2025. The remand was ordered because SEBI's original final order dated December 21, 2021 — which imposed a penalty of INR 6 Lakh — was found to have been passed in violation of principles of natural justice. SEBI is now re-adjudicating the matter after providing Pathak a proper hearing.

## Key Points

- Rajendra Pathak served as CFO of K-Lifestyle & Industries Limited (KLIL) and approved financial results for FY 2015-16, 2016-17, and 2017-18.
- SEBI's interim order dated November 21, 2017 directed stock exchanges to appoint an independent forensic auditor for KLIL.
- BSE appointed a forensic auditor; the Forensic Audit Report (FAR) covering April 1, 2015 to December 31, 2017 was submitted to SEBI on June 3, 2019.
- The FAR revealed misrepresentation of KLIL's financials and misuse of funds/books of accounts.
- A Show Cause Notice (SCN) dated August 18, 2020 was issued against KLIL, its directors, and Pathak alleging non-genuine transactions and misrepresentation of financial statements.
- SEBI's original final order (December 21, 2021) held Pathak in violation of Regulation 33(2)(a) of SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015 and imposed a monetary penalty of INR 6 Lakh under Section 15HB of the SEBI Act, 1992.
- SAT in Appeal No. 158 of 2025, vide order dated July 10, 2025, allowed Pathak's appeal and remanded the matter back to SEBI for a fresh hearing.
- The present order constitutes SEBI's re-adjudication after granting the Noticee a proper opportunity to be heard.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This order applies existing provisions of:
- Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1), 11B(2), and 15I of the SEBI Act, 1992
- Rule 5 of the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995
- Sections 12A(1), 12A(2), and 23I of the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956
- Rule 5 of the Securities Contracts (Regulation) (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 2005
- Regulation 33(2)(a) of the SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015

## Compliance Requirements

- CFOs and directors approving financial results of listed entities are personally accountable for the accuracy and fairness of disclosures under Regulation 33(2)(a) of LODR Regulations.
- Listed companies must ensure financial statements are true, fair, and free of misrepresentation.
- Entities must not execute non-genuine transactions that misuse company funds or misrepresent books of accounts.

## Important Dates

- **November 21, 2017**: SEBI interim order directing forensic audit of KLIL.
- **April 1, 2015 – December 31, 2017**: Investigation period covered by the Forensic Audit Report.
- **June 3, 2019**: Forensic Audit Report submitted to SEBI by BSE.
- **August 18, 2020**: Show Cause Notice issued to KLIL, directors, and Rajendra Pathak.
- **December 21, 2021**: SEBI's original final order imposing INR 6 Lakh penalty on Pathak.

## Impact Assessment

This order reinforces SEBI's position on CFO accountability for approving misrepresented financial statements of listed entities. The SAT remand for violation of natural justice principles underscores that enforcement orders must comply with procedural fairness. The matter is limited in direct market impact as it concerns historical conduct (FY 2015-18) of a small-cap listed company. However, it signals that SEBI will re-adjudicate remanded matters and persist in enforcement even after appellate setbacks. The case may influence how CFOs and finance personnel approach sign-off responsibilities on listed company financials.
