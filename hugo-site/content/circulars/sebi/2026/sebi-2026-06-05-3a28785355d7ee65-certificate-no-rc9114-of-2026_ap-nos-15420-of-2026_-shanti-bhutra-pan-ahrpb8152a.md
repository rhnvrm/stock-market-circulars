## Summary

SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9114 of 2026 under Appeal Proceeding No. 15420 of 2026 against Shanti Bhutra (PAN: AHRPB8152A) in the matter of illiquid stock options. The certificate authorises recovery of outstanding dues — comprising the penalty imposed by SEBI's adjudicating officer — as an arrear of land revenue through the Recovery Officer, as the respondent failed to pay the amount within the stipulated period.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9114 of 2026 issued by SEBI's Recovery Officer
- Proceeding reference: A.P. No. 15420 of 2026
- Respondent: Shanti Bhutra (PAN: AHRPB8152A)
- Matter relates to SEBI's enforcement action against manipulative trading in illiquid stock options
- Certificate enables coercive recovery of unpaid penalty as arrear of land revenue under the SEBI Act

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This document is an enforcement instrument under existing provisions of the SEBI Act, 1992 and the SEBI (Recovery of Amounts) Rules, 2013.

## Compliance Requirements

- Shanti Bhutra is required to discharge the outstanding penalty amount specified in the recovery certificate immediately
- Non-compliance may lead to coercive recovery action including attachment of assets by the Recovery Officer
- The respondent should contact SEBI's Recovery Officer to settle dues and avoid further enforcement measures

## Important Dates

- Certificate issued: June 2026
- Payment of dues: Immediately upon receipt of certificate

## Impact Assessment

This is an individual enforcement action with no direct impact on broader markets or other market participants. The recovery certificate is part of SEBI's ongoing enforcement drive against entities that engaged in manipulative trading in illiquid stock options — a series of actions taken across multiple respondents in 2025–2026. The matter carries no systemic market risk.
