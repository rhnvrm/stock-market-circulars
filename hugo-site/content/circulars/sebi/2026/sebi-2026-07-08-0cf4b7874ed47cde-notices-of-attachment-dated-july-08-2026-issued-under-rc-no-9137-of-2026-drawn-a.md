## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has issued a Notice of Attachment dated July 08, 2026 under Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 9137 of 2026, drawn against Mediaone Global Entertainment Limited (PAN: AAACR0405M), in the matter of Mediaone Global Entertainment Limited. The notice attaches the movable/immovable property or bank accounts of the defaulter to recover outstanding dues owed to SEBI, in accordance with the Second Schedule to the Income Tax Act, 1961, as applied to recovery proceedings under the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Key Points

- Notice of Attachment issued on July 08, 2026.
- Issued under Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 9137 of 2026.
- Drawn against Mediaone Global Entertainment Limited (PAN: AAACR0405M).
- Action taken in the matter of Mediaone Global Entertainment Limited for recovery of outstanding statutory dues/penalties.
- Attachment is a step in SEBI's recovery proceedings against defaulters who have failed to pay dues (penalties, disgorgement amounts, fees, or other statutory dues) within the stipulated time.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement/recovery action under existing SEBI recovery proceedings framework (Recovery of dues under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with the Income Tax Act, 1961 recovery provisions).

## Compliance Requirements

The attached entity/individual is restrained from dealing with, transferring, or encumbering the attached property until the dues are cleared or the attachment is lifted by the Recovery Officer. Any person holding money on behalf of the defaulter is prohibited from making payments to the defaulter without SEBI's clearance.

## Important Dates

- Notice date: July 08, 2026
- Recovery Certificate No.: 9137 of 2026

## Impact Assessment

This attachment notice reflects continued enforcement action by SEBI against Mediaone Global Entertainment Limited for recovery of unpaid dues. It signals financial/legal distress for the named entity and may affect its ability to deal with attached assets. Market impact is limited unless the entity's securities are actively traded, but stakeholders and counterparties should note the enforcement status when assessing counterparty risk.
