## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer issued Adjudication Order No. Order/MS/KS/2026-27/32482 in respect of (late) Ms. Anju Rani (PAN: AJCPR9744F) concerning her alleged involvement in non-genuine reversal trades in Illiquid Stock Options (ISO) on the BSE Ltd. platform. The order stems from a broader SEBI investigation into the stock options segment of BSE covering the period April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015 (the "Investigation Period"), during which SEBI found large-scale reversal of trades creating artificial trading volume. Of the 2,91,744 trades examined (81.41% of all trades executed in BSE's stock options segment during the period), a large number were alleged to be non-genuine reversal trades lacking bona fide trading rationale. SEBI identified 14,720 entities, including the Noticee, as having executed such reversal trades, alleging that these trades gave a false or misleading appearance of trading activity and were manipulative and deceptive in nature.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated trading activity in BSE's illiquid stock options segment for the period April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015.
- 2,91,744 trades (81.41% of all stock options trades on BSE during the period) involved reversal of buy/sell positions between the same counterparties, alleged to lack genuine trading rationale.
- 14,720 entities were identified as having executed such non-genuine reversal trades, including the Noticee, Ms. Anju Rani.
- A Show Cause Notice (SCN) dated August 31, 2021 was issued to the Noticee under Rule 4(1) of the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995.
- The Noticee was alleged to have violated regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1), and 4(2)(a) of the SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices) Regulations, 2003 (PFUTP Regulations).
- The current Adjudicating Officer was appointed to the matter via communique dated June 1, 2026, following transfer of the case from earlier AO(s).
- The Noticee is referenced as deceased ("Late Ms. Anju Rani") in the order title, indicating she passed away during the pendency of proceedings.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an individual enforcement/adjudication order applying existing provisions of the SEBI Act, 1992 (Section 15-I) and the PFUTP Regulations, 2003 to historical conduct.

## Compliance Requirements

This order pertains specifically to the Noticee and does not impose new compliance requirements on market participants generally. Any penalty determination or further action would apply to the legal representatives/estate of the deceased Noticee, subject to the AO's findings in the full order.

## Important Dates

- Investigation Period: April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015
- Show Cause Notice issued: August 31, 2021
- Current AO appointed: June 1, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order has minimal market-wide impact as it concerns an individual adjudication matter tied to historical (2014-15) trading conduct in illiquid stock options at BSE. It does not affect currently listed securities, ongoing market operations, or broader compliance obligations for market participants. Its relevance is limited to enforcement precedent regarding SEBI's continued action against reversal-trade patterns in illiquid derivatives, and to the noticee's legal representatives regarding potential penalty liability.
