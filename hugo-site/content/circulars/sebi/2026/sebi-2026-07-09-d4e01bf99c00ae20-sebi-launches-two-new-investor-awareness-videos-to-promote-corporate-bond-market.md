## Summary

SEBI, in collaboration with Market Infrastructure Institutions (MIIs) and other market intermediaries, organised a programme on July 9, 2026 in Mumbai aimed at deepening the corporate bond market through investor awareness, issuer outreach, and stakeholder engagement. During the event, Whole Time Members Shri Amarjeet Singh and Shri K. V. R. Murthy launched two new investor awareness videos explaining credit ratings and the distinction between 'dirty price' and 'clean price' concepts for corporate bonds.

## Key Points

- Two new investor awareness videos launched on corporate bonds, covering credit rating importance and dirty price vs clean price concepts.
- Launched by WTMs Shri Amarjeet Singh and Shri K. V. R. Murthy at a Mumbai programme.
- Part of SEBI's ongoing initiative to deepen the corporate bond market via investor education.
- Initiative history: 4 videos launched at the inaugural programme on February 4, 2026; 2 more videos launched during the Issuer Outreach Programme in Mumbai on June 12, 2026; now 2 additional videos bring the total to 8 videos.
- Videos are accessible on the SEBI Investor website under Video Based Learning Modules > Bonds.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an investor education and awareness initiative, not a regulatory or policy change.

## Compliance Requirements

None. No action is required from listed entities, intermediaries, or market participants.

## Important Dates

- February 4, 2026: Inaugural programme with 4 investor awareness videos launched.
- June 12, 2026: Issuer Outreach Programme in Mumbai with 2 additional videos launched.
- July 9, 2026: Two new videos launched, bringing the total to 8 videos.

## Impact Assessment

This is a low-impact investor education initiative with no direct bearing on trading, listing, or compliance obligations. It may modestly aid retail and institutional investor understanding of corporate bond pricing and credit risk over time, supporting SEBI's broader goal of deepening the corporate bond market, but has no immediate market or stock-specific effect.
