## Summary

SEBI's Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 dismissed Appeal No. 6929 of 2026 filed by Doyal Mukergee against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought a certified copy of an inspection report conducted by the Magadh Stock Exchange (MSE), Patna, on the physical investment and construction site of M/s Tatanagar Bricks Ltd, referencing SEBI letters dated May 23, 2000 and June 8, 2000, and MSE's report dated April 2, 2001. The CPIO denied the request citing exemptions under Sections 8(1)(d) and 8(1)(e) of the RTI Act, and the Appellate Authority upheld this decision.

## Key Points

- Appellant Doyal Mukergee filed an RTI application dated May 13, 2026 seeking a certified inspection report on Tatanagar Bricks Ltd conducted by the Magadh Stock Exchange, Patna.
- The CPIO responded on June 11, 2026, denying the request under Section 8(1)(d) (commercial confidence) and Section 8(1)(e) (fiduciary relationship) of the RTI Act.
- The appellant filed an appeal dated June 12, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00217) on the ground of refusal of access to information.
- The Appellate Authority held that inspection reports and third-party information obtained by SEBI in its regulatory and supervisory capacity are received in a fiduciary relationship and are exempt from disclosure.
- The order relies on the Supreme Court's ruling in Institute of Chartered Accountants of India vs. Shaunak H. Satya and the CIC's decision in Ashok Kumar Rajak vs. CPIO, SEBI, both supporting non-disclosure of such fiduciary/third-party information.
- The Appellate Authority found no need to interfere with the CPIO's decision and dismissed the appeal.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual adjudicatory order under the RTI Act and does not introduce or amend any regulatory framework.

## Compliance Requirements

None applicable to market participants or listed entities; this order pertains solely to the RTI request process between the appellant and SEBI's CPIO office.

## Important Dates

- May 13, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- June 11, 2026: CPIO's response denying the information.
- June 12, 2026: Appeal filed by appellant.
- July 8, 2026: Appellate Authority's order dismissing the appeal.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct or indirect impact on trading, listing, or market operations. It concerns a historical inspection report (from 2000-2001) on a single entity, Tatanagar Bricks Ltd, and reaffirms SEBI's established policy of withholding third-party fiduciary and commercially confidential information under RTI exemptions. The decision is procedural in nature and relevant only to RTI practitioners or parties interested in SEBI's information disclosure policy.
