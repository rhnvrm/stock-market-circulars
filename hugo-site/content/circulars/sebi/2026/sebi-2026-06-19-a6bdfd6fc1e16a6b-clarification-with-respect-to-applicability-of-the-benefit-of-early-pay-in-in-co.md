## Summary

SEBI has issued a clarification revising paragraph 11.3.1 of Chapter 11 of the SEBI Master Circular for Commodity Derivatives Segment (dated August 4, 2023). The revision clarifies that while Clearing Corporations may exempt all types of margins for positions with early pay-in of certified goods (based on risk perception), mark-to-market (MTM) margins must still be collected from such participants.

## Key Points

- Clearing Corporations must provide early pay-in facility allowing market participants to deposit certified goods against relevant derivatives contracts.
- For positions where early pay-in has been made, Clearing Corporations may — based on risk perception — exempt imposition of **all types of margins**.
- However, **mark-to-market (MTM) margins** are explicitly excluded from this exemption and must continue to be collected.
- The revision was prompted by stakeholder representations and deliberations by the Working Group on "Review of current regulatory framework of delivery and settlement applicable to Agricultural Commodity Derivatives Segment" and the Commodity Derivatives Advisory Committee (CDAC).

## Regulatory Changes

Paragraph 11.3.1 of the SEBI Master Circular SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-1/P/CIR/2023/136 for Commodity Derivatives Segment is revised to read:

> "Clearing Corporations shall provide early pay-in facility to market participants permitting them to deposit certified goods to the Clearing Corporation accredited warehouse against relevant derivatives contracts. For such positions against which early pay-in has been made, based on risk perception, Clearing Corporations may exempt imposition of all types of margins. However, Clearing Corporations shall continue to collect mark to market margins from such market participants against such positions."

The key change is the explicit requirement to continue collecting MTM margins even when other margin types are exempted.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Stock Exchanges and Clearing Corporations** must update their systems to implement the revised early pay-in margin framework.
- They must communicate the provisions of this circular to their members.
- They must disseminate the circular on their websites.
- Clearing Corporations must ensure MTM margins are collected from all participants who have utilized early pay-in, regardless of any other margin exemptions granted.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date:** June 19, 2026
- **Effective Date:** September 21, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This circular has a moderate operational impact on Clearing Corporations operating Commodity Derivatives Segments. It clarifies a previously ambiguous aspect of margin collection under the early pay-in facility — ensuring that risk management through MTM margin collection is maintained even when other margins are waived. For market participants (particularly those dealing in agricultural commodity derivatives), this provides regulatory certainty that early pay-in of certified goods can still reduce their overall margin burden, while Clearing Corporations retain a minimum risk safeguard via MTM collection. The change is procedural/clarificatory in nature and does not introduce new products or alter trading mechanics.
