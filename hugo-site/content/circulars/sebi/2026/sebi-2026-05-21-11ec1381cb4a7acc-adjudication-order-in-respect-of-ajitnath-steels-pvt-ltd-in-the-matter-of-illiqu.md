## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer has issued an adjudication order (Order/AK/GN/2026-27/32411) against Ajitnath Steels Pvt Ltd (PAN: AACCA7201N) for alleged participation in non-genuine reversal trades in illiquid stock options on BSE during the investigation period of April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015. The company allegedly executed 4 non-genuine trades in 1 stock options contract, resulting in artificial volume of 9,60,000 units.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated large-scale reversal trades in BSE's stock options segment that created artificial volumes
- During the investigation period, 2,91,744 trades (81.40% of all BSE stock options trades) were found to be allegedly non-genuine
- Ajitnath Steels Pvt Ltd executed 4 non-genuine trades in 1 stock options contract, generating artificial volume of 9,60,000 units
- Show Cause Notice was issued on March 31, 2022; post-SCN intimation dated August 10, 2022 was returned undelivered
- The current Adjudicating Officer was appointed vide order dated April 3, 2025 following case transfer
- Violations alleged under PFUTP Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1), and 4(2)(a)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is an enforcement action applying existing SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices Relating to Securities Market) Regulations, 2003 (PFUTP Regulations) and Section 15HA of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must not engage in reversal trades or non-genuine transactions in stock options that create artificial volumes
- Entities involved in illiquid stock options trading during 2014–2015 should be aware that SEBI enforcement actions are ongoing
- Compliance with PFUTP Regulations 3 and 4 is mandatory; violations attract penalties under Section 15HA of the SEBI Act

## Important Dates

- Investigation Period: April 1, 2014 – September 30, 2015
- Show Cause Notice issued: March 31, 2022
- Post-SCN Intimation issued: August 10, 2022 (returned undelivered)
- Current AO appointed: April 3, 2025
- Adjudication Order issued: May 2026

## Impact Assessment

The direct market impact of this order is minimal as it concerns a private company's historical trades in illiquid stock options over a decade ago. However, it signals continued SEBI enforcement vigilance against BSE illiquid stock options manipulation — a pattern that affected 81.40% of all options trades during the investigation period. The order is part of a larger wave of similar adjudication actions taken by SEBI against numerous entities involved in the same scheme, reinforcing deterrence against market manipulation through artificial volume creation.
