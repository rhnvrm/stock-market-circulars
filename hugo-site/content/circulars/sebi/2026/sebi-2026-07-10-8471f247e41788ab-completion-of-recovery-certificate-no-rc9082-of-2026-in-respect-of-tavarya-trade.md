## Summary

SEBI has issued a notice confirming the completion of Recovery Certificate No. RC9082 of 2026, which was issued against Tavarya Trade-Link Private Limited (PAN: AACCT5851H). The recovery proceeding was initiated in connection with the illiquid stock options case, where entities were found to have facilitated or engaged in non-genuine trading in illiquid stock options contracts on the exchanges. With this notice, the recovery action under this specific certificate stands closed/completed.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9082 of 2026 pertains to Tavarya Trade-Link Private Limited, identified by PAN AACCT5851H.
- The certificate was issued as part of SEBI's ongoing recovery proceedings against entities involved in the illiquid stock options matter.
- SEBI has confirmed that the recovery process under this certificate has now been completed.
- This action forms part of a broader, long-running enforcement exercise by SEBI against numerous entities implicated in creating artificial volume/non-genuine trades in illiquid stock options.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this notice. It is an administrative/enforcement update confirming closure of a specific recovery certificate previously issued under SEBI's recovery proceedings framework (typically under the Second Schedule to the Income Tax Act, 1961, as applied to SEBI recoveries).

## Compliance Requirements

- No fresh compliance obligations arise for market participants generally from this specific notice.
- Tavarya Trade-Link Private Limited's recovery matter under RC9082 of 2026 is now closed, implying the outstanding dues/penalty amount covered under this certificate have been recovered or the proceeding otherwise concluded.

## Important Dates

- Notice/completion reported: July 2026 (as per PDF publication timestamp).
- Specific recovery certificate reference: RC9082 of 2026.

This circular has minimal direct market impact as it concerns the closure of an individual enforcement recovery action against a single entity rather than a rule change or broad market directive.
