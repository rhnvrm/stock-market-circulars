## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority under the RTI Act, 2005 has passed an order in Appeal No. 6938 of 2026, filed by appellant Suresh Kumar Vishnoi against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought certified information relating to his SCORES complaint against broker Angel One concerning alleged abnormal execution/slippage in a SENSEX 83100 CE options trade executed on February 3, 2026. The information sought included order audit trails, tick-by-tick market data, latency/technical anomaly records, documents submitted by Angel One to SEBI, and internal examination notes.

## Key Points

- Appellant's original RTI application was dated May 12, 2026; CPIO responded on June 01, 2026; appeal was filed on June 20, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00226).
- The underlying dispute involves a SENSEX 83100 CE options trade: 5,220 quantity bought at an average price of 524 on February 1, 2026, and sold at 668 on February 3, 2026, with the disputed execution window being 09:15:00 AM to 09:15:40 AM on February 3, 2026.
- The SCORES complaint reference cited is SEBIE/RJ26/JALO/015513/1.
- For queries on order audit trail, tick-by-tick data, and technical anomaly records (queries 1-3), the respondent stated the information is not available with SEBI.
- For queries on documents submitted by Angel One and the examining authority (queries 4 and 8), the respondent pointed the appellant to the complaint status and Action Taken Report available on the SCORES website via login.
- For query 5 on internal examination notes and communications, the respondent stated the SCORES complaint handling process is electronic and the ATR contains all relevant information.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an adjudicatory order under the RTI Act disposing of an information-access appeal; it does not introduce new rules or amend existing regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

Not applicable to market participants generally. The order pertains only to SEBI's CPIO's obligations to respond to and disclose information under the RTI Act in relation to a specific investor grievance.

## Important Dates

- May 12, 2026: Original RTI application filed.
- June 01, 2026: CPIO's response to the application.
- June 20, 2026: Appeal filed by the appellant.
- February 1-3, 2026: Dates of the underlying disputed options trade.

## Impact Assessment

This order has minimal to no market-wide impact. It is a procedural disposition of an individual's RTI appeal regarding a personal trading dispute with a broker and does not set a broad precedent, alter disclosure norms, or affect listed entities' obligations. Its relevance is confined to the specific appellant and the handling of RTI requests concerning SCORES-based investor complaints.
