## Summary

SEBI issued an ex-parte interim order under Sections 11(1), 11(4), and 11B(1) of the SEBI Act, 1992 against Osiajee Texfab Limited (OTL) and 19 Noticees, including the company's directors, key managerial personnel, and connected individuals/entities. The order follows a complaint alleging that OTL's directors and KMPs manipulated the company's share price with the help of stock brokers, and that the company's revenue was fictitious with dummy demand created to inflate share prices. SEBI's preliminary examination found the stock price surged from Rs. 50.40 on January 30, 2025 to Rs. 474.80 on January 27, 2026 (a rise of over 840% in under a year), despite the company having a low investor base of only 2,872 shareholders and thin daily trading activity.

## Key Points

- OTL is a Punjab-based textile company listed on BSE's main board, incorporated in 1995.
- SEBI examined allegations of price manipulation, misleading corporate announcements, dummy revenue, and coordinated trading among 19 connected Noticees.
- The order covers extensive analysis: share price movement and LTP contribution, misleading announcements/statements, site visit observations, and interconnections among Noticees in an alleged fraudulent scheme.
- SEBI's prima-facie findings conclude violations of the PFUTP Regulations, 2003, warranting urgent interim directions to prevent continued harm to investors.
- Noticees include individuals (e.g., Reema Saroya, Akashdeep Jain, Neeraj Kumar Singla) and corporate entities (Shreni Shares Limited, Navraav Electro Limited).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework is introduced; this is an enforcement action applying existing PFUTP Regulations, 2003 and SEBI Act provisions against specific violators.

## Compliance Requirements

The 19 Noticees are subject to directions issued by SEBI (details of specific restraints, such as market access bans and impounding of proceeds, are set out in the Directions section of the full 53-page order). Noticees must comply with SEBI's interim directions pending further investigation and are entitled to seek a hearing to contest the ex-parte order.

## Important Dates

- January 30, 2025: OTL share price at Rs. 50.40 (baseline for manipulation analysis).
- January 27, 2026: OTL share price peaked at Rs. 474.80.
- May 18, 2026: OTL shareholder count recorded at 2,872.
- Order issued: July 2026 (per PDF URL dating).

## Impact Assessment

This order has high impact on OTL shares and its associated Noticees, likely resulting in trading restrictions, market access bans, and reputational damage. It signals SEBI's continued crackdown on small-cap price manipulation schemes involving low-float, thinly-traded stocks, and may prompt increased scrutiny of similar companies with anomalous price runs and low institutional participation. Investors holding OTL shares face elevated risk given the fraudulent findings and potential trading suspension.
