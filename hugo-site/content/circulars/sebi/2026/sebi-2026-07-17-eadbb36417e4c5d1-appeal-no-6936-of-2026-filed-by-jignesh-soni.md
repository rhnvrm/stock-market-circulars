## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority disposed of Appeal No. 6936 of 2026 filed by Jignesh R Soni against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai under the Right to Information Act, 2005. The appellant, claiming his demat account may have been used by unauthorized third parties, had sought detailed transaction-wise records, pledge/POA details, DIS booklet usage, account modification history, and details of any SEBI/NSDL investigation into his account for the period May 10, 2016 to date. The CPIO responded that the information sought under queries 1 to 8 is not maintained by SEBI in the normal course of regulating the securities market, and that query 9 sought clarification/opinion rather than "information" as defined under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act. The Appellate Authority upheld this response.

## Key Points

- Appellant sought nine categories of information about his demat account, including transaction history, pledge/encumbrance details, POA registrations, off-market debit details, DIS booklet issuance, profile modification history, and any SEBI/NSDL investigation or account freeze.
- CPIO's response (dated June 08, 2026) stated queries 1-8 relate to records not maintained by SEBI in its normal regulatory course.
- Query 9 (on complaints/investigations/inspections) was held to be a request for clarification/opinion, not "information" under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act.
- Appellate Authority reviewed the application, response, and appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00224) and found the matter decidable on record, upholding the CPIO's position on queries 1-8.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual adjudication under the RTI Act and does not introduce any new regulatory requirement or policy change.

## Compliance Requirements

Not applicable to market participants or listed entities; this order pertains solely to the disposal of an individual RTI appeal.

## Important Dates

- May 28, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- June 08, 2026: CPIO's response to the RTI application.
- June 18, 2026: Appeal filed by appellant (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00224).

## Impact Assessment

This order has no material impact on the securities market, listed companies, or trading operations. It is relevant only to the appellant and illustrates that SEBI does not maintain granular demat account transaction records (such records are maintained by depositories/DPs), and that RTI queries seeking opinions or clarifications rather than factual information are not maintainable under the Act.
