## Summary

SEBI has issued a Remittance Advice against Anjali Agarwal (PAN: AFOPA0585K), who has been declared a defaulter, in the matter of dealing in Illiquid Stock Options on BSE. This action is taken under Recovery Certificate No. 9118 of 2026, as part of SEBI's ongoing recovery proceedings to recover dues from entities found to have violated securities market regulations.

## Key Points

- The remittance advice pertains to defaulter Anjali Agarwal, identified by PAN AFOPA0585K.
- The underlying matter relates to dealing in illiquid stock options on the BSE platform, a practice SEBI monitors closely for market manipulation and unauthorized trading concerns.
- The action is issued under Recovery Certificate No. 9118 of 2026, indicating a formal recovery process is underway against the defaulter.
- This is part of SEBI's enforcement mechanism to recover penalties, dues, or disgorgement amounts from non-compliant entities.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement action executing a previously issued recovery certificate against a specific defaulter.

## Compliance Requirements

The named defaulter is required to comply with the recovery certificate and remit the outstanding dues as directed by SEBI. No compliance obligations are imposed on other market participants, listed companies, or the general investor community.

## Important Dates

The remittance advice was issued in July 2026, corresponding to Recovery Certificate No. 9118 of 2026. No further deadlines beyond compliance with the existing recovery certificate are specified in the notice.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has minimal market-wide impact as it targets an individual defaulter in a specific illiquid stock options case rather than a broader regulatory or listed-company action. It reflects SEBI's continued enforcement against illiquid derivatives trading and unauthorized market practices but does not affect trading rules, listed securities, or the general investing public.
