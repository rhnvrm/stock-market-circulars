## Summary

This is an order by the SEBI Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005, in Appeal No. 6877 of 2026 filed by Samar Imran against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appeal arises from an RTI application dated April 20, 2026, seeking detailed information about the use of 'Legal Heirship Certificate (or its equivalent certificate)' in SEBI's Master Circular dated February 6, 2026, and related RTA compliance on transmission of securities.

## Key Points

- Appellant Samar Imran filed an RTI application on April 20, 2026 with 8 detailed queries regarding transmission of securities provisions.
- The application sought information on SEBI Master Circular (dated 06/02/2026) provisions on legal heirship certificates, internal file notings, RTA guidelines, and compliance records.
- SEBI's CPIO responded on May 11, 2026; the appellant then filed this appeal on May 12, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00174).
- For Query 1 (certified copy of Master Circular page), CPIO directed the appellant to the publicly available SEBI website.
- For Query 2 (internal file notings on the phrase 'Legal Heirship Certificate'), CPIO denied disclosure citing Section 8(1)(a) of the RTI Act (information relating to internal systems and procedures of SEBI).

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced by this order. It is a procedural appellate ruling on access to information under the RTI Act.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. The order pertains to SEBI's own obligations under the RTI Act regarding disclosure of internal records related to transmission of securities guidelines.

## Important Dates

- April 20, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant.
- May 11, 2026: CPIO response to the RTI application.
- May 12, 2026: Appeal filed by Samar Imran (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00174).
- February 6, 2026: Date of the SEBI Master Circular that is the subject of the information queries.

## Impact Assessment

This order has minimal direct market or operational impact. It is an administrative RTI appellate proceeding and does not alter any trading, listing, or compliance rules. However, it highlights investor interest in understanding RTA practices for transmission of securities, specifically around the acceptance of Legal Heirship Certificates and equivalent documents — an area of concern for retail investors dealing with inheritance of securities.
