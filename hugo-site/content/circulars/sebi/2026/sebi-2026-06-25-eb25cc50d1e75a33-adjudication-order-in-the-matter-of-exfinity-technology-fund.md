## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer issued Order No. Order/JS/DP/2026-27/32450-32456 against Exfinity Technology Fund (an Alternative Investment Fund) and six associated noticees for violating SEBI (Alternative Investment Fund) Regulations, 2012. The fund's Series I Scheme had an original tenure ending August 2019, extended to August 2021, and was required to be wound up by August 14, 2022 under Regulation 29(1)(a) read with Regulation 29(7). SEBI found the scheme was not wound up within this mandatory period, triggering adjudication proceedings under Section 15EA of the SEBI Act.

## Key Points

- Exfinity Technology Fund Series I Scheme had a tenure of 5 years (extendable by 1 year each, twice) from final closing date of August 14, 2014
- The scheme's end date was August 2019; extension end date was August 2021
- Under AIF Regulations, mandatory winding up was required by August 14, 2022 (one year after expiry of extended tenure)
- SEBI initiated proceedings against 7 noticees: the fund (Exfinity Technology Fund), its manager (Exfinity Venture Partners LLP), four individual key persons (V Balakrishnan, Shailesh Ghorpada, Chinnu Senthilkumar, Nihar Ranjan), and trustee (Vistra ITCL India Limited)
- Adjudicating Officer was appointed on August 29, 2025 under Section 15I of the SEBI Act
- Show Cause Notice was issued on November 21, 2025
- Penalties are being considered under Section 15EA of the SEBI Act

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This order enforces existing provisions of SEBI (Alternative Investment Fund) Regulations, 2012, specifically Regulation 29(1)(a) and Regulation 29(7) relating to mandatory winding up of AIF schemes upon expiry of their tenure.

## Compliance Requirements

- AIF schemes must be wound up within one year of the expiry of their extended tenure, as mandated by Regulation 29(1)(a) read with Regulation 29(7) of AIF Regulations
- Fund managers and trustees are jointly responsible for ensuring timely winding up
- AIFs must accurately disclose scheme tenure end dates and extension dates in quarterly filings to SEBI
- Key personnel of AIF managers and trustees may be held individually liable for regulatory violations

## Important Dates

- August 14, 2014: Final closing date of Exfinity Technology Fund Series I Scheme
- August 2019: Original end date of scheme tenure
- August 2021: End date of extended tenure
- August 14, 2022: Deadline by which scheme was required to be wound up
- August 29, 2025: Appointment of Adjudicating Officer
- November 21, 2025: Show Cause Notice issued to all noticees
- September 2022 (quarterly filing period): SEBI identified the non-compliance through quarterly reports

## Impact Assessment

This adjudication order is primarily relevant to the AIF industry, particularly Category I and Category II venture capital and private equity funds with fixed tenures. It signals SEBI's active monitoring of AIF quarterly filings for tenure compliance. Fund managers, trustees, and key managerial personnel face personal liability for winding-up failures. The order reinforces the importance of timely scheme closure processes and accurate regulatory disclosures. Market-wide impact is limited, but the enforcement action sets a clear precedent that the AIF ecosystem must adhere strictly to wind-up timelines and that both institutional and individual noticees will be held accountable.
