## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has issued a Notice of Demand under Recovery Certificate No. 9199 of 2026, dated 07.07.2026, against Rajesh Pandey (PAN: BFKPP2415Q) in connection with the matter of Retro Green Revolution Limited. The notice calls upon the noticee to pay the outstanding dues specified in the underlying recovery certificate, failing which SEBI may initiate coercive recovery proceedings under the modes of recovery prescribed in the Second and Third Schedules to the Income Tax Act, 1961, as made applicable to SEBI recovery proceedings under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. 9199 of 2026 was issued on 07.07.2026 against Rajesh Pandey (PAN: BFKPP2415Q).
- The proceeding arises from dues connected to Retro Green Revolution Limited.
- The notice of demand formally directs the noticee to clear the outstanding amount within the stipulated period.
- Non-compliance can lead to coercive recovery measures such as attachment and sale of movable/immovable property, attachment of bank accounts, and other modes available under the Income Tax Act recovery framework as applied to SEBI proceedings.

## Regulatory Changes

This is an individual enforcement notice rather than a policy or regulatory change; no amendments to rules, regulations, or master circulars are involved.

## Compliance Requirements

The noticee, Rajesh Pandey, is required to pay the amount specified in Recovery Certificate No. 9199 of 2026 within the period stated in the notice of demand. Failure to comply may result in SEBI's Recovery Officer proceeding with attachment and sale of assets, garnishee proceedings on bank accounts, or other recovery modes permitted under law.

## Important Dates

- Recovery Certificate / Notice of Demand date: 07.07.2026

## Impact Assessment

The notice is targeted at a specific individual in relation to a specific recovery matter and does not have implications for the securities market, listed companies, or the investing public at large. Its impact is confined to the noticee and does not affect trading activity, market structure, or investor obligations.
