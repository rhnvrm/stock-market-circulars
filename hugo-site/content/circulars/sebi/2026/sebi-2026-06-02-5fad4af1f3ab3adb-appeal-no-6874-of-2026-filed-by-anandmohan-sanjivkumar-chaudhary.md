## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority has disposed of Appeal No. 6874 of 2026 filed by Anandmohan Sanjivkumar Chaudhary against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought information under the RTI Act regarding his PACL refund claim registration numbers and the land allotment status recorded against them. SEBI's CPIO responded that the requested information is not available with SEBI, and the Appellate Authority upheld this response, finding no deficiency.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed an RTI application on April 25, 2026 seeking details of PACL refund claim registrations (U314037xxx and U314040xxx) and associated land allotment status
- CPIO responded on May 05, 2026 stating the requested information is not held by SEBI
- Appellant filed this appeal on May 10, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00171) alleging the response was incomplete, misleading, or false
- The Appellate Authority found no deficiency in the CPIO's response, citing CIC precedent that a CPIO cannot invent information it does not possess
- Responsibility for PACL property disposal and investor repayment lies with the Justice (Retd.) R. M. Lodha Committee, constituted pursuant to a February 2, 2016 order

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an administrative order disposing of an individual RTI appeal.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. PACL-affected investors seeking information on land allotment status or refund claims should approach the Justice (Retd.) R. M. Lodha Committee directly, not SEBI. SEBI's website contains Public Notices, Press Releases, Status Reports, and FAQs related to PACL matters.

## Important Dates

- April 25, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant
- May 05, 2026: CPIO responded to the RTI application
- May 10, 2026: Appellant filed this appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00171)
- February 2, 2016: Original order constituting the Justice Lodha Committee for PACL matters

## Impact Assessment

No market or operational impact. This order affects only the individual appellant and similarly situated PACL investors who may mistakenly seek land allotment or claim records from SEBI. It reaffirms that PACL investor redressal is channeled through the Justice Lodha Committee, not SEBI directly.
