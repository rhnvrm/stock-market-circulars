## Summary

This is an appeal filed by Himanshu Soni (Appeal No. 6859 of 2026) before SEBI's Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005. The appeal challenges the response of the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai to an RTI application dated April 02, 2026, which sought information about unauthorized IPO fund collection practices and SEBI's enforcement powers.

## Key Points

- The appellant filed an RTI application on April 02, 2026 seeking information on legality of personal bank account fund collection for IPOs, WhatsApp-based IPO groups, and SEBI enforcement actions.
- The respondent replied on April 29, 2026; the appellant filed this appeal on April 30, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00158).
- SEBI CPIO held that queries seeking clarification on legal permissibility do not qualify as 'information' under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act.
- For queries on IPO application methods, the respondent directed the appellant to SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018 and the SEBI Master Circular dated February 09, 2026 on ASBA Facility.
- For enforcement-related queries, the respondent stated that appropriate action is taken whenever violations are established under the SEBI Act, 1992 and related regulations.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This order pertains to the handling of an RTI application and does not amend any existing regulation.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. The order reiterates existing guidance:
- IPO applications must be processed through the SEBI-approved ASBA system.
- Collecting funds in personal bank accounts or operating WhatsApp-based IPO groups promising assured returns is not authorized under SEBI regulations.
- Relevant rules are available in SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018 and the SEBI ASBA Master Circular (February 09, 2026).

## Important Dates

- RTI Application filed: April 02, 2026
- CPIO Response: April 29, 2026
- Appeal filed: April 30, 2026
- Appeal Registration No.: SEBIH/A/E/26/00158

## Impact Assessment

No direct market or operational impact. The case has informational value for retail investors seeking clarity on SEBI's stance against unauthorized IPO schemes and WhatsApp-based investment groups. It reinforces that SEBI does not recognize or approve any IPO-related scheme promising assured/guaranteed returns outside the regulated ASBA framework.
