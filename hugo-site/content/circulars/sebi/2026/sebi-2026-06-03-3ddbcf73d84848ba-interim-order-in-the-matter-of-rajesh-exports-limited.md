## Summary

SEBI has issued an interim order against Rajesh Exports Limited (REL) — a gold refiner and manufacturer listed on BSE (531500) and NSE (RAJESHEXPO) — and its promoter Rajesh Mehta, citing multiple prima facie findings of financial fraud. The investigation covers the period April 1, 2020 to March 31, 2024, triggered by a shareholder complaint in March 2024 alleging misrepresentation related to large outstanding trade receivables. The order is passed under Sections 11(1), 11(4), and 11B of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Key Points

- **Noticees**: Rajesh Exports Limited (PAN: AAACR8642N) and Rajesh Mehta (PAN: ADKPM0172C)
- **Investigation Period**: April 1, 2020 – March 31, 2024
- **Trigger**: Shareholder complaint dated March 11, 2024 alleging financial misrepresentation related to large trade receivables outstanding for more than two years
- **Investigating Authority appointed**: October 23, 2024
- **Company profile**: Gold refiner, manufacturer, and retailer (brand: 'SHUBH Jewellers'); listed on BSE and NSE; market cap INR 3,210 crore; share price INR 108.70 (as of June 3, 2026)
- **Non-cooperation**: Noticees failed to cooperate with SEBI's investigation
- **Financial misrepresentation (consolidated)**: Non-disclosure of material consolidated financial information and misrepresentation of consolidated financial statements
- **Financial misrepresentation (standalone)**: Misrepresentation of standalone financial statements
- **Incorrect consolidation**: Financial statements not consolidated correctly
- **Dubious Africa gold mine claim**: Misrepresentation related to claimed "Investment in Gold Mines in Africa"
- **Trade receivables manipulation**: Incorrect adjustment of trade receivables against trade payables
- **Fund misutilization**: Prima facie findings of misutilization of company funds
- **Market impact**: Examination of dealing in securities and price-volume impact included in the order

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory framework changes introduced. The order is an enforcement action applying existing provisions of the SEBI Act, 1992 (Sections 11(1), 11(4), and 11B) and applicable disclosure and listing obligations regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- The Noticees (REL and Rajesh Mehta) are subject to interim directions as specified in Section F of the order
- All market participants and listed companies are reminded of obligations under SEBI's disclosure and financial reporting norms
- Companies must ensure accurate and complete consolidation of financial statements and proper disclosure of material financial information
- Trade receivables and payables must not be incorrectly netted or adjusted to misrepresent financial position
- Full cooperation with SEBI investigations is mandatory

## Important Dates

- **March 11, 2024**: Shareholder complaint received by SEBI
- **October 23, 2024**: SEBI appointed Investigating Authority
- **April 1, 2020 – March 31, 2024**: Investigation period
- **June 3, 2026**: Reference date for market cap and share price cited in the order
- **June 4, 2026**: Date of interim order

## Impact Assessment

This is a high-impact enforcement action against a mid-cap listed company with INR 3,210 crore market capitalization. The breadth of prima facie findings — spanning consolidated and standalone financial misrepresentation, incorrect consolidation, fabricated investment claims (Africa gold mines), trade receivable manipulation, and fund misutilization — suggests systemic financial reporting failures. Interim directions likely restrict trading and/or impose asset freezes on the Noticees. Investors in RAJESHEXPO / BSE 531500 face significant uncertainty pending the outcome of proceedings. The case may also prompt broader scrutiny of gold sector companies with large overseas receivables.
