## Summary

The Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 disposed of two identical appeals (Nos. 6860 & 6861 of 2026) filed by Sadhana Mishra against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought information regarding eligibility criteria for Registered Investment Adviser (RIA) and Research Analyst (RA) registration, including questions about educational qualifications, colour blindness as a disqualification, medical fitness requirements, and restrictions on dual registration as a Mutual Fund Distributor. The Appellate Authority upheld the respondent's position that most queries sought clarifications or opinions rather than factual information.

## Key Points

- Two identical RTI applications dated April 23, 2026 were filed and received a common response dated April 29, 2026.
- Appeals were registered as SEBIH/A/E/26/00159 and SEBIH/A/E/26/00160, filed April 30, 2026.
- Queries on BTech (EEE) eligibility for RIA/RA, colour blindness disqualification, medical fitness requirements, and RIA/RA-cum-MFD restrictions were held to seek clarification/opinion — not 'information' under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act.
- For educational qualification requirements (Query 4) and relevant regulation extracts (Query 6), appellant was directed to Regulation 7 of SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013 and SEBI (Research Analysts) Regulations, 2014, as well as the January 8, 2025 Guidelines on both.
- Appellate Authority concurred fully with the respondent's responses.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an appellate order under the RTI Act and does not alter any SEBI regulation.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. Entities or individuals seeking eligibility criteria for RIA or RA registration should refer to:
- Regulation 7, SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013
- Regulation 7, SEBI (Research Analysts) Regulations, 2014
- Guidelines on Investment Advisers dated January 8, 2025
- Guidelines on Research Analysts dated January 8, 2025

## Important Dates

- April 23, 2026: RTI applications filed by appellant
- April 29, 2026: CPIO response issued
- April 30, 2026: Appeals filed by appellant

## Impact Assessment

No market or operational impact. The order is an administrative disposal of RTI appeals and has no bearing on trading, listed entities, or regulated intermediaries. It reaffirms the established principle that queries seeking legal interpretations, clarifications, or opinions are outside the scope of the RTI Act.
