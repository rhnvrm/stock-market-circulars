## Summary

SEBI has issued a circular modifying the norms governing utilization of interest or income earned from investments made out of the Investor Protection Fund (IPF) of Depositories. Previously, Paragraph 4.46.1.1(C)(i)(2) of the SEBI Master Circular for Depositories dated December 03, 2024 mandated that 100% of interest or income from IPF investments be treated as corpus of IPF. Following representations from Depositories and deliberations by the Secondary Market Advisory Committee (SMAC) along with public consultation, SEBI has revised this requirement to at least 95% ploughback to corpus, while allowing up to 5% to be utilized for IPF Trust administrative and statutory expenses.

## Key Points

- Clause 4.46.1.1(B)(i)(c) revised: Depository contribution to IPF now requires at least 95% (previously 100%) of interest/income from IPF investments to be contributed to the fund.
- Clause 4.46.1.1(C)(i)(2) revised: At least 95% of interest or income from IPF investments received each year shall be ploughed back to IPF.
- Up to a maximum of 5% of such interest/income may be utilized during the financial year to meet expenses related to dedicated IPF Trust employees, other administrative and statutory expenses (e.g., applicable taxes, audit fees, charity commissioner's fee).
- If expenses exceed the 5% cap, the excess must be borne by the depository itself, not the IPF.
- Any unutilized portion of the 5% allocation in a given financial year must be ploughed back to the IPF.
- The change follows recommendations of SEBI's Secondary Market Advisory Committee (SMAC) and public consultation feedback, aimed at bringing uniformity and consistency in IPF fund utilization provisions across depositories and stock exchanges.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular amends Section 4 (Paragraphs 4.46.1.1(B)(i)(c) and 4.46.1.1(C)(i)(2)) of SEBI Master Circular No. SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-1/P/CIR/2024/168 for Depositories dated December 03, 2024. The amendment reduces the mandatory corpus contribution rate from 100% to a minimum of 95% of interest/income earned on IPF investments, while formally permitting up to 5% to be used for defined administrative and statutory expenses of the IPF Trust.

## Compliance Requirements

All Depositories (NSDL and CDSL) are directed to:

1. Take necessary steps and put in place systems to implement the revised utilization norms.
2. Make necessary amendments to relevant bye-laws, rules, and regulations wherever applicable.
3. Bring the provisions of this circular to the notice of market participants, including investors, and disseminate the circular on their websites.

## Important Dates

- Circular issued: July 07, 2026
- Effective date of applicability: September 01, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The impact is limited to internal fund management and administrative operations of Depositories (NSDL and CDSL) and does not directly affect listed securities, trading operations, or investors' day-to-day activities. The change provides depositories with modest additional flexibility (up to 5% of investment income) to cover legitimate administrative and statutory costs of running the Investor Protection Fund Trust, while still ensuring the vast majority (at least 95%) of income continues to strengthen the IPF corpus, which ultimately protects investors in case of depository-related defaults.
