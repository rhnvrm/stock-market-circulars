## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority, acting under the Right to Information Act, 2005, disposed of Appeal No. 6939 of 2026 filed by Sudhir Agarwal against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought certified copies of NEAT system records, KYC information, contract notes, trade logs, and authorisation records concerning his trading and demat account maintained with BMA Wealth Creators Ltd., in connection with a dispute involving trades allegedly executed through Mr. Dinesh Kumar Singh.

## Key Points

- The appellant filed an RTI application dated May 31, 2026, seeking seven categories of certified records relating to his trading account (DP ID 12046300) with BMA Wealth Creators Ltd.
- Information sought included: NEAT system-generated KYC/mobile/email details at account opening (Account Opening Form dated 07.02.2007); client registration records for UCC Code 20000022; contract note issuance/delivery records used in IGRP/Arbitration proceedings; trade logs involving Mr. Dinesh Kumar Singh; documents on alleged authorisation of Mr. Singh to trade on the appellant's behalf; and records on Mr. Singh's registration status and terminal-operating authorisation at the broker's Nehru Place office.
- The CPIO responded to the application by letter dated June 15, 2026.
- The appellant filed the present appeal on June 21, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00227) against the CPIO's response.
- The Appellate Authority reviewed the application, the CPIO's response, and the appeal, and determined the matter could be decided on the material on record.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual RTI appellate order and does not introduce any regulatory or policy change.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance obligations are imposed on market participants by this order.

- May 31, 2026: RTI application filed by the appellant.

## Impact Assessment

This order relates to a single investor's RTI request concerning historical trading records and a dispute with a broker (BMA Wealth Creators Ltd.) over alleged unauthorised trading. It carries no direct impact on listed companies, market operations, or broader regulatory policy, and is of interest primarily as a transparency/grievance-redressal precedent for individual investors pursuing RTI-based document requests in trading disputes.
