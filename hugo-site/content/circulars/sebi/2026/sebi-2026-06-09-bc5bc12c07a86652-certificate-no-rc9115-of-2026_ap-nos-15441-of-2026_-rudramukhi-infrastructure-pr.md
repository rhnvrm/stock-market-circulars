## Summary

SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9115 of 2026 in the matter of Adjudication Proceeding (A.P.) No. 15441 of 2026 against Rudramukhi Infrastructure Private Limited (PAN: AAGCR0172D). The certificate pertains to recovery of monetary penalty imposed on the entity for its role in fraudulent and manipulative trading in illiquid stock options on the BSE platform.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9115 of 2026 issued by SEBI's Recovery Officer
- Relates to Adjudication Proceeding No. 15441 of 2026
- Entity: Rudramukhi Infrastructure Private Limited (PAN: AAGCR0172D)
- Matter involves manipulation of illiquid stock options at BSE
- Certificate authorises recovery of outstanding penalty amount from the defaulting entity
- Part of SEBI's broader enforcement action against entities involved in illiquid stock options fraud

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is an enforcement action under existing SEBI (Recovery of Money) Regulations and the SEBI Act, 1992, following an adjudication order that imposed a monetary penalty on the entity.

## Compliance Requirements

- Rudramukhi Infrastructure Private Limited is required to pay the outstanding penalty amount as specified in the recovery certificate
- Failure to comply may result in attachment of bank accounts, immovable property, and other assets
- Any banks, depositories, or financial institutions holding assets of the entity may be directed to freeze/hand over such assets to SEBI

## Important Dates

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9115 issued: 2026 (exact date as per the certificate)
- A.P. No. 15441 of 2026: Underlying adjudication proceeding reference year 2026
- Payment/compliance deadline: As stipulated in the recovery certificate

## Impact Assessment

This recovery certificate has limited broader market impact as it targets a single private entity (Rudramukhi Infrastructure Private Limited) for penalty recovery. It forms part of SEBI's sustained enforcement campaign against participants found guilty of creating artificial volumes and fictitious trades in illiquid stock options on the BSE, a matter that has seen numerous adjudication orders and recovery actions over recent years. The action reinforces SEBI's commitment to market integrity and deterrence of manipulative trading practices.
