## Summary

This is an appellate order under the Right to Information Act, 2005, in Appeal No. 6850 of 2026 filed by Shashi against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought extensive information from SEBI regarding its handling of the Karvy Stock Broking case, including records related to Supreme Court Civil Appeal Nos. 388-392 of 2024, investor relief progress, legal representation details, and legal expenses. SEBI's respondent partially denied disclosure citing sub-judice status and other RTI exemptions.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed RTI application dated March 26, 2026 seeking information on SEBI's role in the Karvy Stock Broking Supreme Court proceedings
- Information sought covered eight broad categories: Supreme Court appeal records, post-SAT order actions, investor impact reports, pending investor claims, legal representation details, legal expense records, accountability mechanisms, and review meeting minutes
- SEBI responded on April 21, 2026; appellant filed this appeal on April 26, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00152)
- SEBI denied disclosure of records related to query nos. 1a and 2a (file notings and internal approvals for Supreme Court filing) under Section 8(1)(a) of the RTI Act, citing sub-judice and confidential strategic information
- Query no. 2b (correspondence with exchanges, depositories, IEPF post-SAT order) was denied as vague and not constituting "information" under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act
- Query no. 3a (investor impact and restitution records) was partially addressed, with SEBI noting examinations are conducted confidentially

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a quasi-judicial RTI appellate proceeding with no amendments to regulations or market rules.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. The order pertains solely to SEBI's internal information disclosure obligations under the RTI Act.

## Important Dates

- March 26, 2026: Appellant's original RTI application filed
- April 21, 2026: SEBI (respondent) replied to the RTI application
- April 26, 2026: Appellant filed this appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00152)

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct market or investor impact. It is an administrative RTI proceeding related to transparency around SEBI's conduct in the Karvy Stock Broking matter before the Supreme Court. The denial of certain information under Section 8(1)(a) reflects standard protection of sub-judice and strategically sensitive legal information. Investors affected by the Karvy case continue to be governed by ongoing Supreme Court proceedings and SEBI's regulatory actions, not by this RTI appeal.
