## Summary

SEBI has published an updated, consolidated version of the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Custodian) Regulations, 1996, reflecting all amendments up to July 08, 2026. The regulations govern the registration, conduct, obligations, inspection, and enforcement framework applicable to entities registered as Custodians of Securities in India. This is a reference/compilation document rather than a new standalone circular, intended to provide market participants and custodians with the current consolidated text of the regulations.

## Key Points

- The regulations are structured into six chapters: Preliminary; Registration of Custodians; General Obligations and Responsibilities; Inspection and Audit; Procedure for Action in Case of Default; and Power to Relax Strict Enforcement.
- Chapter II sets out the registration process for custodians, including application requirements, capital requirements, and grant/refusal of certificates.
- Chapter III covers general obligations including code of conduct, segregation of activities, monitoring and internal controls, separate custody accounts, client agreements, dispute resolution, and compliance officer appointment.
- Chapter IV establishes SEBI's inspection and audit powers over custodians, including auditor appointment and cost recovery provisions.
- Chapter V outlines procedures for regulatory action in cases of default (several original sections have been omitted following the 2002 Enquiry Officer regulations).
- Chapter VI provides SEBI power to grant exemptions from strict enforcement in special cases.
- Includes First Schedule (application and registration certificate forms), Second Schedule (fee structure), and Third Schedule (code of conduct).
- Definition of "change in control" has been amended, differentiating treatment for listed versus unlisted body corporates.

## Regulatory Changes

The document reflects amendments consolidated as of July 08, 2026, including revised definitions (e.g., "change in control" per Regulation 2(ba)) and provisions such as Dispute Resolution (Regulation 17A), Compliance Officer appointment (Regulation 19A) and associated obligations (19B), and Auditor appointment/cost recovery (Regulations 25A-25B) — indicating custodian regulations have been progressively strengthened with governance and dispute resolution mechanisms over time.

## Compliance Requirements

- Entities seeking registration as custodians must comply with capital adequacy, application, and certification requirements under Chapter II.
- Registered custodians must maintain segregation of activities, internal controls, separate custody accounts, and proper record-keeping.
- Custodians must appoint a Compliance Officer and adhere to the prescribed Code of Conduct (Third Schedule).
- Custodians are subject to SEBI inspection and audit, including cooperation with appointed auditors and potential cost recovery by the Board.
- Entities must maintain updated dispute resolution mechanisms per Regulation 17A.

## Important Dates

- Amendments consolidated as of July 08, 2026.
- Original regulations notified under S.O.344(E) pursuant to Section 30 of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Impact Assessment

This is a consolidated regulatory reference document rather than a new operative directive, so it carries low direct market impact and does not pertain to specific listed securities. However, it holds ongoing compliance relevance for entities registered or seeking registration as custodians of securities, who should review the updated consolidated regulations to ensure continued adherence to registration, conduct, and reporting obligations.
