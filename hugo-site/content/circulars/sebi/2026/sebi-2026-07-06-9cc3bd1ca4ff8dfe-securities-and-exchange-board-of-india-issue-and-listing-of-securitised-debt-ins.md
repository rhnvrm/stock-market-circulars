## Summary

SEBI has published an updated, consolidated version of the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Issue and Listing of Securitised Debt Instruments and Security Receipts) Regulations, 2008, reflecting amendments up to July 6, 2026. The regulations, originally notified under the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956 and the SEBI Act, 1992, govern the issuance and listing of securitised debt instruments and security receipts by special purpose distinct entities. The consolidated text incorporates historical amendments including the 2018 renaming from "Public Offer and Listing" to "Issue and Listing" regulations, and the 2025 amendments effective May 5, 2025, which introduced new definitions for terms such as "advertisement," "asset reconstruction company," and "control."

## Key Points

- The regulations are formally titled the SEBI (Issue and Listing of Securitised Debt Instruments and Security Receipts) Regulations, 2008, following a 2018 name change from the earlier "Public Offer and Listing" title.
- Key definitions in Regulation 2 cover terms such as "asset pool," "asset reconstruction company," "certificate," "clean-up call option," "credit enhancement," and "debt/receivables."
- "Advertisement" is now defined by cross-reference to the SEBI (Issue and Listing of Non-Convertible Securities) Regulations, 2021, per the 2025 amendment.
- "Control" is defined by cross-reference to the SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011.
- "Debt" or "receivables" includes mortgage debt and financial assets originated by originators regulated by the Reserve Bank of India, subject to conditions restricting such originators from undertaking securitisation activities or assuming securitisation exposures beyond specified limits.
- "Certificate" refers to a certificate of registration granted to a trustee under the SEBI (Debenture Trustees) Regulations, 1993.

## Regulatory Changes

The document reflects the cumulative effect of multiple amendments over time, most notably the SEBI (Issue and Listing of Securitised Debt Instruments and Security Receipts) (Amendment) Regulations, 2025 (effective May 5, 2025), which inserted new definitions for "advertisement," "asset reconstruction company," and "control," and updated references from "these regulations" to specifically cite the SEBI (Debenture Trustees) Regulations, 1993 for trustee certification.

## Compliance Requirements

Special purpose distinct entities, trustees, originators (including RBI-regulated originators), and asset reconstruction companies involved in securitised debt instruments and security receipts must ensure their operations, disclosures, and advertisements align with the updated definitions and cross-referenced regulations, including the SEBI (Issue and Listing of Non-Convertible Securities) Regulations, 2021 and the SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011.

## Important Dates

- Original regulations came into force upon publication in the Official Gazette in 2008.
- Name change to current title effective June 26, 2018.
- Most recent amendments incorporated effective May 5, 2025.
- Consolidated text updated as of July 6, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This is a consolidated regulatory reference document rather than a new operative rule change, primarily serving compliance and legal reference purposes for entities engaged in securitisation of debt instruments and security receipts, such as asset reconstruction companies, debenture trustees, and special purpose distinct entities. No direct or immediate market impact on listed securities is expected from this publication.
