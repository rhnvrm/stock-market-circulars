## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority (under the RTI Act, 2005) is adjudicating Appeal No. 6908 of 2026 filed by Jagadish Vital against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant seeks certified copies of recovery certificate files and related records arising from the SMS Techsoft/MNCL adjudication. SEBI's respondent denied the broader disclosure, citing exemptions under sections 8(1)(a) and 8(1)(e) of the RTI Act on grounds of internal functioning and fiduciary/commercial confidentiality.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed original RTI application on February 24, 2026; respondent replied on March 25, 2026.
- First appeal (Appeal No. 6812 of 2026, FAA Order dated April 23, 2026) directed SEBI to reconsider query nos. A1, A2, I(13), and B4.
- SEBI issued revised response on May 11, 2026, providing limited records (notices of demand, attachment notices, release order) but denied remaining information.
- Appellant filed fresh appeal in May 2026 alleging non-compliance with the April 23, 2026 FAA Order.
- Queries relate to: full file copies of RC-2786/2020 and RC-4469/2022; auction/sale records for Coimbatore ancestral property; and a certified list of all Recovery Certificates linked to SMS Techsoft/MNCL adjudication.
- SEBI denied broader disclosure under RTI Act sections 8(1)(a) (sovereignty/security/public order) and 8(1)(e) (information in fiduciary capacity and commercial confidentiality).

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes applicable to market participants. This is a case-specific RTI appellate proceeding.

## Compliance Requirements

No compliance requirements for market participants or intermediaries arise from this order.

## Important Dates

- February 24, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant.
- March 25, 2026: SEBI respondent's initial reply; appellant's first appeal filed.
- April 23, 2026: FAA Order in Appeal No. 6812 of 2026 directing reconsideration of specific queries.
- May 11, 2026: SEBI's revised response issued.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no impact on broader market operations, trading, or regulatory compliance for market participants. It is an internal enforcement and RTI matter confined to the individual appellant's dispute over access to SEBI recovery certificate records related to the SMS Techsoft/MNCL adjudication enforcement action.
