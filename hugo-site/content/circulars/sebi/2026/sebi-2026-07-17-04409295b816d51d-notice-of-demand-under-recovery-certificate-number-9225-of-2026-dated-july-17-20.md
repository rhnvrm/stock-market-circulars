## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has issued a Notice of Demand under Recovery Certificate No. 9225 of 2026, dated July 17, 2026, to Riju Rajpal HUF (PAN: AALHR9308B), who has been declared a defaulter in the matter of dealing in illiquid Stock Options on BSE. The notice formally demands payment of the outstanding dues specified in the recovery certificate, which was issued for recovery of an amount confirmed against the defaulter arising from SEBI enforcement proceedings.

## Key Points

- Notice of Demand issued under Recovery Certificate No. 9225 of 2026, dated July 17, 2026.
- Defaulter: Riju Rajpal HUF, PAN AALHR9308B.
- Underlying matter: dealing in illiquid Stock Options on BSE, a category SEBI has flagged in multiple enforcement actions for creating artificial trading volumes through reversal trades.
- The notice is issued by SEBI's Recovery Officer under powers derived from Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with the Second and Third Schedules to the Income Tax Act, 1961, and the Income Tax (Certificate Proceedings) Rules, 1962, as applicable to recovery of amounts due to SEBI.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an individual enforcement/recovery action under SEBI's existing recovery framework for defaulters who have failed to pay amounts due pursuant to SEBI orders (e.g., penalties, disgorgement, or other monetary directions).

## Compliance Requirements

- The defaulter, Riju Rajpal HUF, is required to pay the amount specified in the Recovery Certificate within the period stipulated in the notice (typically 15 days from the date of service).
- Failure to comply may result in recovery proceedings against the defaulter's movable and immovable property, including attachment and sale of assets, appointment of a receiver, and other coercive measures available under the Income Tax Act's recovery provisions as applied to SEBI recoveries.

## Important Dates

- Notice/Recovery Certificate date: July 17, 2026.
- Payment is due within the timeframe specified in the notice (generally 15 days from service), failing which further recovery action will be initiated.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has a narrow, individual-level impact confined to the named defaulter and does not affect listed securities, market infrastructure, or trading participants at large. It is part of SEBI's ongoing enforcement drive against entities involved in illiquid stock options trading on BSE and serves as a reminder of SEBI's continued recovery efforts against confirmed defaulters.
