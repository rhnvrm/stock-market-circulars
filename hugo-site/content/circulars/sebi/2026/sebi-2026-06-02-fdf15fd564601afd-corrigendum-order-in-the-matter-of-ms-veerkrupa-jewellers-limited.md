## Summary

SEBI issued a corrigendum dated June 2, 2026 to its earlier quasi-judicial order dated May 29, 2026 (reference number QJA/SS/IVD-2/ID13/32429/2026-27) in the matter of M/s Veerkrupa Jewellers Limited. The corrigendum makes two specific corrections to the original order and directs that the original order shall always be read together with this corrigendum.

## Key Points

- Corrigendum reference number: QJA/SS/IVD-2/ID13/32430/2026-27
- Original order date: May 29, 2026 (ref. QJA/SS/IVD-2/ID13/32429/2026-27)
- Two corrections made: a heading correction on page 99 and a full replacement of Para 96
- Issued by Santosh Shukla, Quasi-Judicial Authority, SEBI, Mumbai
- The original order and this corrigendum must be read together

## Regulatory Changes

**Correction 1 — Heading (Page 99):**
The heading "VII. Conclusion." is corrected to read "VII. Conclusion and Factors under Section 15J of the SEBI Act."

**Correction 2 — Para 96 (Full Replacement):**
The corrected Para 96 clarifies the nature of Noticee No. 9's business (digital marketing, social media marketing, share market advice, IPO marketing, public relations, SEO) and establishes a link between Noticee No. 9 and Noticee No. 10 through a shared mobile number (XXXXXX7777) belonging to Mr. Rajesh Shah, a director of Noticee No. 9, which also appeared in the trading/demat account details of Noticee No. 10 with brokers including Marfatia Stock Broking Pvt Ltd, Pramodkumar Jain Securities Pvt Ltd, Red Ribbon Stock Broking Pvt Ltd, and Bhansali Value Creations Pvt Ltd.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance obligations are imposed by this corrigendum. All parties to the original order (QJA/SS/IVD-2/ID13/32429/2026-27) must read that order in conjunction with this corrigendum.

## Important Dates

- **May 29, 2026:** Original order passed against M/s Veerkrupa Jewellers Limited
- **June 2, 2026:** Corrigendum issued from Mumbai

## Impact Assessment

This is a purely administrative correction with no new market, trading, or compliance impact. The substantive effect is a clarification of the section heading to include reference to Section 15J of the SEBI Act (which governs factors to be considered while adjudging quantum of penalty), and a more precise description of the connection between two noticees in the enforcement matter. No entities beyond the original noticees are affected.
