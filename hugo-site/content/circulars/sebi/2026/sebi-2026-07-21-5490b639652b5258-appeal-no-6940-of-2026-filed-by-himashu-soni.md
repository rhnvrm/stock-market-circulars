## Summary

This order is issued by the Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 (RTI Act), in Appeal No. 6940 of 2026 filed by Himanshu Soni against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had filed an RTI application dated May 28, 2026, seeking a wide range of information relating to the recognized process for applying to IPOs (including ASBA and UPI mechanisms), norms governing collection of investor funds by stock brokers and authorised persons, circulars/guidelines on prohibited practices such as collection of client money into personal bank accounts, offering assured returns, and use of informal communication channels (e.g., WhatsApp groups) for solicitation. The appellant also sought investor alerts on IPO-related frauds, enforcement/adjudication records over the last ten years against intermediaries for unauthorized fund collection or assured-return schemes, documents on brokers' supervisory obligations over authorised persons, and grievance redressal mechanisms for investors affected by unauthorized fund collection or misrepresentation. The CPIO responded via letter dated June 22, 2026, following which the appellant filed the present appeal on the same date (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00229).

## Key Points

- Appellant Himanshu Soni sought information under the RTI Act on IPO application processes, approved fund-collection mechanisms (ASBA, UPI), and broker/authorised-person obligations.
- Request covered circulars/guidelines prohibiting collection of investor funds into personal bank accounts, assured-return offers, and use of unapproved communication channels for solicitation.
- Appellant also sought copies of investor alerts on IPO frauds, unauthorised investment schemes, and misuse of broker/intermediary names.
- Request included ten years of enforcement/adjudication records against intermediaries for unauthorized fund collection, assured returns, and unauthorised IPO solicitation.
- Appellant sought clarity on brokers' supervisory responsibilities over authorised persons and available investor grievance redressal remedies.
- The Appellate Authority reviewed the application, CPIO's response, and the appeal, and found the matter could be decided on the material on record.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an RTI appellate order disposing of an information request and does not introduce or amend any regulatory provisions.

## Compliance Requirements

Not applicable to market participants; this order pertains solely to SEBI's obligations under the RTI Act to provide or clarify the availability of specific information/records to the appellant.

## Important Dates

- May 28, 2026: RTI application filed by the appellant.
- June 22, 2026: CPIO's response letter issued.
- June 22, 2026: Appeal filed by the appellant (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00229).

## Impact Assessment

This order has negligible direct market or operational impact. It is an administrative/procedural RTI disposal concerning transparency of SEBI's records on IPO fund-collection norms and enforcement history against intermediaries. Its indirect relevance lies in highlighting recurring investor-protection themes—unauthorized collection of funds by brokers/authorised persons, assured-return schemes, and informal solicitation channels—areas market participants and investors should remain vigilant about, though no new compliance obligations arise from this order itself.
