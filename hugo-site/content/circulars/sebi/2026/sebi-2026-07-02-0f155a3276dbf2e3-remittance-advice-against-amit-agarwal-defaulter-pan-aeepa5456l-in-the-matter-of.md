## Summary

SEBI has issued a Remittance Advice against Amit Agarwal (PAN: AEEPA5456L), who has been declared a defaulter, in connection with a case relating to dealing in illiquid stock options at BSE. The advice is issued pursuant to Recovery Certificate No. 9106 of 2026, as part of SEBI's ongoing recovery proceedings against the individual for dues arising from the case.

## Key Points

- Defaulter identified: Amit Agarwal (PAN: AEEPA5456L)
- Underlying matter: Dealing in illiquid stock options at BSE
- Recovery Certificate No. 9106 of 2026 forms the basis for this remittance advice
- The notice is part of SEBI's recovery/enforcement process against defaulters in illiquid options trading cases

## Regulatory Changes

This is not a policy or regulatory change; it is an individual enforcement/recovery action issued under existing SEBI recovery proceedings.

## Compliance Requirements

The remittance advice is directed at the recovery process concerning the named defaulter. Entities involved in executing or complying with the recovery certificate (e.g., recovery officers, banks, or authorities handling remittance) should process the advice in accordance with SEBI's recovery proceedings framework.

## Important Dates

- Recovery Certificate No. 9106 of 2026 (year 2026)
- Document issued/published: July 2, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This notice pertains to an individual defaulter and does not affect any listed company, stock, or broader market segment. Its impact is confined to the enforcement and recovery process against the named individual and has no direct bearing on trading, listing, or market operations.
