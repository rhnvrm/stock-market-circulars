## Summary

SEBI's Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 has disposed of Appeal No. 6943 of 2026 filed by appellant Kishan Lal against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appeal, filed on June 16, 2026 and received by the Appellate Authority on June 22, 2026, alleged non-receipt of a response to an RTI application dated May 19, 2026. The Appellate Authority found that the respondent had in fact issued a response on June 16, 2026, sent via Speed Post and uploaded to the RTI portal, and that the appeal was filed prematurely, before expiry of the statutory 30-day period.

## Key Points

- Appellant Kishan Lal filed RTI application on May 19, 2026, received by SEBI on May 21, 2026.
- CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai issued a response on June 16, 2026, via Speed Post and the RTI portal.
- Appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/P/26/00038) was filed on June 16, 2026 and received by the Appellate Authority on June 22, 2026.
- Appellate Authority Ruchi Chojer found the appeal premature, as it was filed before the 30-day statutory response window had lapsed.
- The respondent was found to have replied within the time stipulated under the RTI Act.
- Appeal disposed of as infructuous; appellant retains the right to file a fresh appeal if dissatisfied with the reply received.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual case order under the RTI Act, 2005 and does not introduce or amend any regulatory provisions.

## Compliance Requirements

No compliance action required from market participants or listed entities. The order pertains solely to procedural handling of an RTI request between the appellant and SEBI's CPIO office.

## Important Dates

- May 19, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- May 21, 2026: Application received by SEBI.
- June 16, 2026: CPIO response issued; appeal filed by appellant.
- June 22, 2026: Appeal received by Appellate Authority.
- July 20, 2026: Order passed disposing of the appeal.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no market or operational impact. It is a routine administrative disposal of an individual RTI appeal, confirming that SEBI's CPIO complied with statutory response timelines. No implications for listed companies, intermediaries, or investors at large.
