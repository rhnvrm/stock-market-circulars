## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority is examining Appeal No. 6869 of 2026 filed by Saket Dewangan against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought information about the status of multiple PACL refund applications, procedures for correcting land allotment errors, and bank account update mechanisms. The CPIO responded that the requested information is not available with SEBI.

## Key Points

- Appellant Saket Dewangan filed an RTI application on April 27, 2026 seeking details on PACL refund applications (Registration Nos. N152xxxx series)
- Queries covered: refund application status, correction of land allotment errors (YES/NO), expected timelines, copies of guidelines, land ownership documents, and bank account update due to Dena Bank–Bank of Baroda merger
- CPIO responded on May 5, 2026, stating the requested information is not available with SEBI
- Appellant filed the appeal on May 6, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00167), alleging incomplete, misleading, or false information was provided
- SEBI directed the appellant to SEBI website for public notices, press releases, status reports, and FAQs on PACL matters

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced by this order. The matter is an individual RTI appeal adjudication under the Right to Information Act, 2005.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. The order reaffirms the established principle (citing CIC decision in Sh. Pattipati Rama Murthy vs. CPIO, SEBI, July 8, 2013) that a CPIO cannot be compelled to provide information it does not possess.

## Important Dates

- April 27, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant
- May 5, 2026: CPIO, SEBI responded to the application
- May 6, 2026: Appellant filed the first appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00167)

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct market or operational impact. It concerns an individual's RTI appeal regarding personal PACL refund application queries. PACL investors seeking similar information are reminded that granular application-level data is not maintained by SEBI; public information on the PACL refund scheme is available on the SEBI website. Investors affected by the Dena Bank–Bank of Baroda merger should follow the committee's prescribed process for updating bank account details in PACL refund applications.
