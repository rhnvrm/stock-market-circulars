## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer issued an adjudication order (Order No. Order/AK/GN/2026-27/32410) against LCI India Limited (PAN: AAACL5741Q) for alleged participation in non-genuine reversal trades in illiquid stock options on BSE. The investigation covered the period April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015, during which LCI India Limited allegedly executed 12 non-genuine trades across 5 stock options contracts, generating artificial volume of 4,57,000 units.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated large-scale reversal trades in BSE's stock options segment for the period April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015.
- A total of 2,91,744 trades, comprising 81.40% of all BSE stock options trades during the investigation period, were found to be allegedly non-genuine.
- LCI India Limited was identified as one of the entities involved, executing 12 non-genuine trades in 5 stock options contracts.
- These trades resulted in artificial volume of 4,57,000 units, creating a false or misleading appearance of trading activity.
- Violations alleged under Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1) and 4(2)(a) of SEBI (PFUTP) Regulations, 2003.
- Show Cause Notice was issued on August 04, 2021 and duly delivered via SPAD and email.
- Post SCN Intimation was issued on August 10, 2022 (email) and August 30, 2022 (SPAD).
- The undersigned AO was appointed via Order dated April 03, 2025, following a case transfer.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this order. It is an enforcement action applying existing PFUTP Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1), and 4(2)(a), and Section 15HA of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants are reminded that executing reversal trades or non-genuine trades in any segment — including illiquid stock options — constitutes a violation of PFUTP Regulations.
- Entities must not engage in trades that create artificial volume or a false/misleading appearance of trading activity.
- Timely response to SCNs and regulatory communications (SPAD and email) is required from all regulated entities.

## Important Dates

- **Investigation Period:** April 1, 2014 – September 30, 2015
- **Show Cause Notice Issued:** August 04, 2021
- **Post SCN Intimation (Email):** August 10, 2022
- **Post SCN Intimation (SPAD):** August 30, 2022
- **Current AO Appointed:** April 03, 2025
- **Order Reference:** Order/AK/GN/2026-27/32410

## Impact Assessment

This order has limited current market impact as the violations pertain to a historical period (2014–2015). However, it is part of SEBI's broader enforcement sweep against entities involved in illiquid stock options manipulation on BSE, which collectively involved over 2.91 lakh non-genuine trades. The order signals continued regulatory pursuit of market manipulation cases regardless of the time elapsed, reinforcing deterrence against artificial volume creation in options markets.
