## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority has disposed of Appeal No. 6907 of 2026 filed by Hitesh Gaur, remitting the original RTI application to the CPIO for fresh consideration. The appellant had sought information regarding the PACL refund status of investor Sharwan Kumar Sharma, whose claim was flagged as unprocessable due to alleged land allotment by PACL Ltd. The respondent's earlier reply was found inadequate as it did not clarify whether the requested information was available with SEBI.

## Key Points

- Appellant Hitesh Gaur filed an RTI application on May 17, 2026 seeking information about a PACL refund status showing 'land allotted' against a specific PACL registration number.
- The application sought certified copies of land registry deeds, plot possession letters, or investor consent forms held by SEBI or the Justice R.M. Lodha Committee.
- The CPIO responded on May 20, 2026, directing the appellant to approach the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee, without clarifying whether the information was available with SEBI.
- Appellant filed the appeal on May 29, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00202) citing incomplete, misleading, or false information in the response.
- Appellate Authority Ruchi Chojer found the CPIO's reply inadequate for not clarifying SEBI's possession of the requested information.
- The application is remitted for de novo consideration with a directive to respond within 15 working days.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an administrative order on an individual RTI appeal.

## Compliance Requirements

- The CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai must reconsider the RTI application de novo and provide an appropriate response to the appellant within 15 working days from the date of receipt of this order.
- The response must clarify whether the requested information (land registry deeds, possession letters, or investor consent forms related to PACL refunds) is available with SEBI.

## Important Dates

- May 17, 2026: Original RTI application filed by Hitesh Gaur.
- May 20, 2026: CPIO's initial response to the RTI application.
- May 29, 2026: Appeal filed by the appellant (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00202).
- June 24, 2026: Appellate Authority order passed by Ruchi Chojer.
- Within 15 working days from receipt of order: Deadline for CPIO to provide fresh response.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no broader market or regulatory impact. It is limited to a single investor's RTI query regarding the PACL refund process. The matter concerns the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee's administration of PACL investor refunds, and the outcome will only affect the specific investor (Sharwan Kumar Sharma) and appellant (Hitesh Gaur) involved.
