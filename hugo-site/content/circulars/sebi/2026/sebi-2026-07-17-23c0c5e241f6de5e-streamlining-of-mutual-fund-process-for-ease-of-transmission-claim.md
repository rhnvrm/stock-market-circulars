## Summary

SEBI has advised AMFI (Association of Mutual Funds in India) to further simplify the standards on the "Procedure to Claim Units/Proceeds upon death of a unit holder" to ease operational challenges faced by kin of deceased investors during the mutual fund transmission process. AMFI has accordingly amended these standards as part of SEBI's ongoing investor-friendly initiatives.

## Key Points

- SEBI directed AMFI to simplify transmission claim procedures for mutual fund units upon death of a unit holder.
- Address mismatches: AMCs may rely on the latest available address details of the deceased unit holder, provided supporting documents are furnished.
- Name/signature mismatch: AMCs may adopt a framework aligned with guidelines prescribed for RTAs under the SEBI Master Circular for Registrars to an Issue and Share Transfer Agents dated February 06, 2026.
- In case of name mismatch, investors/claimants may submit self-certified documents such as Aadhaar or Passport.
- In case of signature mismatch, RTAs may follow appropriate procedures based on the nature of the mismatch.
- AMFI has been advised to train relevant entities to ensure aligned practices across AMCs.

## Regulatory Changes

AMFI has amended the standards on "Procedure to Claim Units/Proceeds upon death of a unit holder" to incorporate: (i) reliance on latest available address details for resolving address mismatches, and (ii) a harmonized name/signature mismatch framework aligned with Annexure-10 of the SEBI Master Circular for RTAs dated February 06, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- AMCs must update internal transmission-claim processes to accept the latest available address details supported by relevant documents in case of address mismatch.
- AMCs must adopt the harmonized name/signature mismatch verification framework consistent with RTA guidelines, including acceptance of self-certified documents (Aadhaar, Passport) for name mismatches.
- AMFI is required to conduct training for AMCs and other relevant entities on the revised transmission process to ensure consistent implementation.

## Important Dates

Circular issued: July 17, 2026 (Mumbai). Reference: SEBI Master Circular for Registrars to an Issue and Share Transfer Agents dated February 06, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This is a procedural/operational reform aimed at easing the mutual fund transmission process for legal heirs of deceased investors, reducing documentation friction and processing delays. It does not directly affect listed securities or trading activity but improves investor experience and operational consistency across AMCs and RTAs, supporting SEBI's broader investor protection mandate.
