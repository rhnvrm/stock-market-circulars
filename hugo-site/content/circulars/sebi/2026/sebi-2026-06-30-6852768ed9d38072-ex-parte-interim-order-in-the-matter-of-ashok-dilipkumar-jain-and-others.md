## Summary

SEBI issued an ex-parte interim order under Sections 11(1), 11(4), and 11B(1) of the SEBI Act, 1992, in the matter of Ashok Dilipkumar Jain and nine other Noticees. The order arose from an investigation into prima facie suspected price and volume manipulation in the scrip of Darjeeling Industries Limited (DIL), formerly known as Darjeeling Ropeway Company Limited. SEBI's examination uncovered evidence of a coordinated scheme involving preferential allotment of warrants, connections amongst the Noticees and other related entities, funding of preferential allottees by Ashok Dilipkumar Jain, and prima facie mis-utilisation of funds received from the preferential warrant allotment by DIL. The order also covers a site visit conducted at DIL's registered office addresses, trading analysis in the scrip, consolidated shareholding patterns, and corporate announcements/social media activity linked to the alleged manipulation.

## Key Points

- The matter involves 10 Noticees: Ashok Dilipkumar Jain, Dilip Keshrimal Sanklecha, Viha Ashok Jain, Sonali Abhaykumar Parmar, Abhishek Prakash Jain, Kirti Ravi Kothari, Kalidas Vijay Magar, Pradeep Sutodiya, Joy Banerjee, and Punyah Sachin Jain.
- SEBI found prima facie evidence of a scheme/arrangement related to the preferential issue of warrants by DIL, including similar schemes in other scrips.
- Ashok Dilipkumar Jain allegedly funded the preferential allottees of warrants.
- Funds raised from the preferential allotment of warrants by DIL were prima facie mis-utilised.
- SEBI conducted a site visit at DIL's registered office addresses as part of the investigation.
- Trading analysis revealed suspicious patterns in the scrip, including movement of the stock price following Ashok Dilipkumar Jain's statement recording.
- Corporate announcements, news, and social media activities by DIL were examined as part of the evidentiary findings.
- The order is ex-parte and interim in nature, indicating SEBI found urgent need to act without prior hearing due to potential investor harm.

## Regulatory Changes

This is an enforcement action rather than a regulatory/policy change. No new rules are introduced; SEBI is exercising its existing powers under Sections 11(1), 11(4), and 11B(1) of the SEBI Act, 1992 to issue interim directions against the Noticees.

## Compliance Requirements

The Noticees named in the order are subject to directions issued by SEBI (detailed in the Directions section of the order, beginning page 61), which typically include restraint from accessing the securities market, prohibition from buying, selling, or dealing in securities, and freezing of related bank/demat accounts pending further investigation. Market participants and intermediaries dealing with DIL or the named Noticees should ensure compliance with any restraint orders issued.

## Important Dates

The order was published in June 2026. Specific compliance deadlines and the date for the Noticees to respond/seek a hearing would be detailed within the full order document (pages 59-62, sections E and F).

## Impact Assessment

This order has high impact for investors and market participants dealing in Darjeeling Industries Limited (DIL) shares, given the prima facie findings of price/volume manipulation and fund mis-utilisation tied to a preferential warrant allotment scheme. The action signals heightened SEBI scrutiny of preferential allotment-based market manipulation schemes and serves as a deterrent for similar coordinated schemes in other listed scrips. Trading in DIL shares may see volatility or restrictions as a result of this order, and entities connected to the Noticees should assess their exposure and compliance obligations.
