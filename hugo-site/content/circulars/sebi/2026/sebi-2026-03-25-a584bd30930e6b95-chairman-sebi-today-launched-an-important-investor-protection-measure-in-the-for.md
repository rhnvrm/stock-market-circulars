## Summary

SEBI Chairman launched a first-of-its-kind 'Verified' label initiative on Google Play Store for stock trading apps offered by SEBI-registered entities. This public-private partnership with Google aims to protect retail investors from fraudulent trading apps that impersonate legitimate platforms. The verified badge will initially cover stock trading apps of SEBI-registered brokers, with plans to extend it to other regulated intermediaries.

## Key Points

- SEBI has partnered with Google to implement a 'Verified' badge on Google Play Store for all stock trading apps of SEBI-registered entities
- The initiative addresses the proliferation of fraudulent trading apps that deceive retail investors by impersonating genuine platforms
- SEBI Chairman advised investors to follow 'Pehle Pechaan Kare, Fir Nivesh Kare!' (First Identify, Then Invest)
- SEBI introduced a 'CVV' framework: Check (bank accounts via SEBI Check in Saa₹thi app), Validate (UPI ID handles with 'Valid' word via SEBI Check), and Verify (Verified Labels on Google Play)
- The Verified label will subsequently be extended to apps of other regulated intermediaries beyond stock brokers
- Google Play MD Aditya Swamy confirmed Google's intent to further collaborate on investor protection initiatives

## Regulatory Changes

No formal regulatory changes or circulars issued. This is a voluntary public-private partnership initiative between SEBI and Google to enhance investor protection through a trust verification mechanism on the Google Play Store platform.

## Compliance Requirements

- SEBI-registered brokers offering stock trading apps on Google Play Store should ensure their apps are enrolled for and display the 'Verified' label
- Investors are advised to check for the 'Verified' label before downloading any stock trading app from Google Play Store
- Investors should use the SEBI 'CVV' framework: use SEBI Check in the Saa₹thi app to verify bank accounts and UPI IDs, and look for Verified Labels on trading apps

## Important Dates

- **March 25, 2026**: SEBI Chairman launched the Verified Label initiative at an event in Mumbai
- No specific deadline mentioned for broker app enrollment; rollout appears to be immediate for SEBI-registered stock brokers

## Impact Assessment

This initiative has significant positive impact on retail investor protection in India. The proliferation of fake trading apps has been a growing menace, causing financial losses to unsuspecting investors. By providing a visible 'Verified' badge on Google Play Store, investors can now easily distinguish legitimate broker apps from fraudulent ones. The SEBI CVV framework consolidates multiple verification tools into a simple investor advisory. The initiative is expected to reduce fraud incidents linked to fake trading apps, enhance investor confidence in digital trading platforms, and set a precedent for similar collaborations with other app distribution platforms.
