## Summary

SEBI's Appellate Authority passed an order disposing of RTI Appeal No. 6924 of 2026 filed by Krishna Munnalal Malu against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought detailed information on inactive and unclaimed mutual fund folios in India, including total value, PAN-wise and non-PAN details, year-wise and AMC-wise breakdowns, ageing analysis, unclaimed redemption/dividend amounts, claim settlement statistics, and details of SEBI's regulatory measures to trace and return such investments.

## Key Points

- The appellant filed an RTI application dated May 02, 2026 seeking 14 categories of data related to inactive and unclaimed mutual fund folios.
- The CPIO responded via a letter dated May 22, 2026.
- The appellant, dissatisfied with the response, filed an appeal dated June 10, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00215).
- Queries covered: total inactive folios and value, PAN-based and non-PAN-based investor details, year-wise trend since inception, AMC-wise breakdown, ageing analysis (less than 1 year, 1-3 years, 3-5 years, more than 5 years), unclaimed redemption/dividend amounts, claims filed/processed/rejected with average processing time, success ratio of claims, SEBI's regulatory initiatives, expected timelines for resolution, and any internal studies on economic impact.
- The Appellate Authority reviewed the application, CPIO's response, and the appeal, and determined the matter could be decided on the material available on record.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes were introduced; this is an adjudicatory order under the RTI Act, 2005 disposing of an information request.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements are imposed on market participants, AMCs, or RTAs through this order.

## Important Dates

- May 02, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- May 22, 2026: CPIO's response to the RTI application.
- June 10, 2026: Appeal filed by appellant (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00215).

## Impact Assessment

The order has no direct market or operational impact on listed companies or trading activity. It reflects ongoing public interest and regulatory scrutiny around inactive and unclaimed mutual fund investments, an area where SEBI and AMCs may face continued pressure to improve investor tracing and fund reclamation mechanisms. Asset management companies and RTAs may see increased future RTI and regulatory attention on unclaimed folio data and investor protection processes.
