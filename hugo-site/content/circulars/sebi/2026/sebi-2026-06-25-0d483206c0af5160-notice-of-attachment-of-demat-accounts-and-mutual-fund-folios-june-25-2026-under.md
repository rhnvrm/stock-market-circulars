## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has issued a notice dated June 25, 2026, attaching the demat accounts and mutual fund folios held by Suryaraj Kumar (PAN: AAFPK9296D). The attachment has been ordered under Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 9109 of 2026, issued in connection with recovery proceedings arising out of the matter of Mediaone Global Entertainment Limited. The notice directs depositories, depository participants, and mutual funds/RTAs to freeze the specified accounts and folios and restrict any debit, transfer, or redemption pending recovery of the outstanding dues.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. 9109 of 2026 has been issued against Suryaraj Kumar (PAN – AAFPK9296D).
- The notice pertains to recovery proceedings connected with Mediaone Global Entertainment Limited.
- All demat accounts and mutual fund folios held by the defaulter, whether singly or jointly, are attached.
- Depositories, depository participants, mutual funds, and registrars/transfer agents are directed to mark a lien/freeze on the identified accounts and folios.
- No further sale, transfer, creation of charge, or redemption of the attached securities/units is permitted without SEBI's prior written approval.

## Regulatory Changes

This is an enforcement action under SEBI's recovery proceedings framework (Second Schedule to the Income Tax Act, 1961, read with the SEBI Act, 1992) rather than a new regulatory change. It exercises the Recovery Officer's powers to attach movable property, including securities held in dematerialised form and mutual fund units, to recover amounts due from the defaulter.

## Compliance Requirements

- Depositories and depository participants holding the demat accounts of Suryaraj Kumar must mark the accounts as attached/frozen and report compliance to SEBI.
- Mutual funds/RTAs administering folios in the name of Suryaraj Kumar must similarly freeze the folios against redemption, switch, or transfer.
- Any deviation or unauthorized debit from the attached accounts/folios must be reported to SEBI's Recovery Officer immediately.
- The defaulter is barred from dealing with the attached accounts and folios until the recovery certificate dues are cleared or the attachment is lifted by SEBI.

## Important Dates

- **June 25, 2026** – Date of the notice of attachment of demat accounts and mutual fund folios.
- Recovery Certificate No. 9109 of 2026 remains in force until the outstanding dues are recovered or the attachment is otherwise vacated by SEBI.

## Impact Assessment

The direct impact of this notice is confined to the named individual, Suryaraj Kumar, whose demat accounts and mutual fund holdings are now frozen and cannot be operated pending recovery of dues. There is no direct impact on listed companies, market infrastructure, or other investors. The notice reinforces SEBI's ongoing enforcement of recovery certificates against defaulters linked to securities market violations, and depositories/mutual funds involved must ensure timely compliance to avoid facilitating further dissipation of the defaulter's assets.
