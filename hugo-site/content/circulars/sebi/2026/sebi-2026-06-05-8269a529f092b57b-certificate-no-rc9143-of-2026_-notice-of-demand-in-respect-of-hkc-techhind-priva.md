## Summary

SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9143 of 2026 and a Notice of Demand against HKC Techhind Private Limited (PAN: AAACH6407P) in the matter of Illiquid Stock Options. This notice is issued under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992, requiring the entity to pay the outstanding penalty amount that was imposed through an earlier adjudication order and has remained unpaid.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9143 of 2026 issued against HKC Techhind Private Limited
- PAN of the noticee: AAACH6407P
- Action is in the matter of Illiquid Stock Options (BSE)
- Notice of Demand requires payment of outstanding penalty amount within the stipulated time
- Non-compliance may lead to coercive recovery measures under SEBI Act

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is an enforcement/recovery action pursuant to an earlier adjudication order issued in the Illiquid Stock Options matter.

## Compliance Requirements

- HKC Techhind Private Limited is required to pay the full demanded amount as specified in the Notice of Demand within the stipulated timeframe
- Failure to comply may result in attachment of assets, bank accounts, or other coercive recovery measures under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992
- The entity may approach SEBI or the Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT) if contesting the demand

## Important Dates

- Certificate issued: June 2026
- Payment must be made within the timeframe specified in the Notice of Demand (typically 15–30 days from receipt)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine recovery proceeding in SEBI's ongoing enforcement series against entities found to have traded in illiquid stock options on BSE in a non-genuine manner. The action is limited to HKC Techhind Private Limited and has no direct impact on broader market operations or other market participants. It is part of a large batch of recovery proceedings SEBI has been pursuing in 2025–2026 across hundreds of entities in the Illiquid Stock Options matter.
