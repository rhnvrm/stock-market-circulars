## Summary

SEBI issued a Final Order (WTM/AS/ISD/ISD-SEC-4/32507/2026-27) against Madhav Stock Vision Pvt. Ltd. (MSVPL), a registered stock broker, and five individuals for front running the trades of a "Big Client" identified as Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC). The order follows an Interim Order cum Show Cause Notice dated April 23, 2025, based on an investigation covering the period April 1, 2020 to December 1, 2023. SEBI's internal alert system flagged that trades placed by LIC through its empanelled brokers were being front run by MSVPL, leading to search and seizure operations at multiple locations on December 4, 2023.

## Key Points

- Six Noticees named: Madhav Stock Vision Pvt. Ltd. (broker), Jyotiswaroop Nandkishore Purohit, Pankit Bhagwati Jhaveri, Rajesh Bhagwati Jhaveri, Ajay Sampatraj Jain, and Rajkumar Prabhu Damani.
- Noticees were categorized into three groups: the Front Runner/Profit Maker (MSVPL), Information Carriers (Noticees 2 and 3, dealers at empanelled brokers), and other associated individuals.
- Noticees 2 and 3 (Jyotiswaroop Purohit and Pankit Jhaveri) were dealers/employees at Bhagwandas Gordhandas Financial Pvt. Ltd. (BGFPL), one of the Big Client's empanelled brokers, and allegedly passed non-public information (NPI) about impending LIC orders to MSVPL.
- Evidence gathered included Call Data Records (CDRs) linking brokers of the Big Client to dealers/directors of MSVPL, proximity of information carriers to dealing desks, trading pattern comparisons, KYC and bank statement analysis, and statements recorded under oath.
- Investigation also examined profit-sharing arrangements and fund transfers among the Noticees and related entities.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an enforcement order under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1) and 11B(2) of the SEBI Act, 1992, applying existing front running and market abuse provisions to the specific Noticees.

## Compliance Requirements

The Noticees are subject to directions issued by SEBI as part of this Final Order (specific directions, such as monetary penalties, disgorgement, or market restrictions, are detailed in the full order document). Stock brokers and their employees are reminded of obligations to prevent misuse of non-public client order information and maintain adequate information barriers between dealing desks and other market participants.

## Important Dates

- Investigation period: April 1, 2020 to December 1, 2023
- Search and seizure operations: December 4, 2023
- Interim Order cum Show Cause Notice: April 23, 2025
- Final Order issued: July 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order underscores SEBI's continued enforcement focus on front running by registered intermediaries, particularly involving large institutional clients such as LIC. While the direct market impact is confined to the named broker and individuals, the case reinforces surveillance and compliance expectations for stock brokers regarding information barriers and staff conduct, and may prompt other brokers to review internal controls around order handling for large institutional clients.
