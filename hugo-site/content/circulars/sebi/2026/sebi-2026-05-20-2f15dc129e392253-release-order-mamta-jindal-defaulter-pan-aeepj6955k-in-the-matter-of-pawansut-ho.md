## Summary

SEBI has issued a Release Order against Mamta Jindal (Defaulter, PAN: AEEPJ6955K) in the matter of Pawansut Holdings Limited, directing the release of attached bank accounts, demat accounts, and mutual fund accounts. This order follows the full recovery of outstanding dues under Recovery Certificate No. 2752 of 2020, originally issued on January 13, 2020.

## Key Points

- Release order issued in respect of Mamta Jindal (PAN: AEEPJ6955K), classified as a defaulter
- Pertains to Recovery Certificate No. 2752 of 2020 dated January 13, 2020
- The underlying matter relates to enforcement proceedings involving Pawansut Holdings Limited
- Order directs banks, mutual funds, NSDL, and CDSL to release all attached accounts
- Issued following full or sufficient recovery of outstanding dues under the certificate

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is an administrative enforcement order concluding recovery proceedings against an individual defaulter.

## Compliance Requirements

- All banks in India must release any attached bank accounts and lockers of Mamta Jindal (PAN: AEEPJ6955K)
- All mutual funds in India must release any attached mutual fund accounts
- NSDL and CDSL must release any attached demat accounts
- Release to be effected pursuant to attachment notices issued under Recovery Certificate No. 2752 of 2020

## Important Dates

- January 13, 2020: Recovery Certificate No. 2752 of 2020 originally issued
- May 20, 2026: Release Order issued following completion of recovery

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine administrative enforcement matter with minimal market impact. The release order concludes long-standing recovery proceedings against Mamta Jindal in connection with Pawansut Holdings Limited, spanning over six years since the original 2020 certificate. This is consistent with SEBI's enforcement pattern for older recovery certificates where account attachments are lifted once dues are fully recovered. No broader market or regulatory implications arise from this individual enforcement closure.
