## Summary

SEBI has issued a Settlement Order (No. SO/MS/RG/2026-27/8564-8565) disposing of both adjudication and enquiry proceedings initiated against Angel One Limited (PAN: AAACM6094R), one of India's largest retail stock brokers. The proceedings alleged multiple violations of SEBI's stock broker regulations, master circulars on broker conduct, and NSE compliance circulars related to client management, running accounts, and related obligations.

## Key Points

- Two settlement applications (Nos. 8564/2025 and 8565/2025) were filed by Angel One Limited to settle adjudication and enquiry proceedings respectively.
- The alleged violations span SEBI's 2009 broker conduct circular, the 2024 Master Circular for Stock Brokers (SEBI/HO/MIRSD/MIRSD-PoD-1/P/CIR/2024/110 dated August 09, 2024), and NSE circulars from 2022 and 2023.
- Specific provisions alleged to be violated include clauses on client funds/running accounts (Clauses 5(c), 7(e), 7(f), 7(g) and 32.5.1(c), 32.7.5, 32.7.6, 32.7.7), and NSE compliance obligations under NSE/COMP/56947 and NSE/COMP/53164.
- Show Cause Notices for both adjudication and enquiry were issued on May 14, 2025 by the erstwhile Adjudicating Officer.
- The case was handled by three successive Adjudicating Officers/Designated Authorities due to transfers: Shri Amar Navlani (appointed March 18, 2025), Shri Sudeep Mishra (appointed September 19, 2025), and the current officer (appointed June 08–09, 2026).
- Settlement Order number SO/MS/RG/2026-27/8564-8565 consolidates both proceedings.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this order. The order applies existing provisions under:
- Section 15-I and Section 15HB of the SEBI Act, 1992
- Regulation 27 of SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 1992
- Chapter V and VI of SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008
- SEBI Master Circular for Stock Brokers (August 09, 2024)
- NSE circulars NSE/COMP/56947 (June 02, 2023) and NSE/COMP/53164 (August 01, 2022)

## Compliance Requirements

- Stock brokers must comply with client fund management norms under SEBI's master circulars, including proper running account settlement, client fund segregation, and reporting obligations.
- Brokers are required to adhere to NSE compliance circulars on client onboarding, unique client codes, and related operational standards.
- Schedule II, Clause A(2) and A(5) obligations under SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 1992 must be followed regarding the code of conduct for brokers.
- Settlement of regulatory proceedings via the SEBI settlement mechanism is available to intermediaries facing adjudication or enquiry, subject to payment of settlement amount and compliance undertakings.

## Important Dates

- **May 14, 2025**: Show Cause Notices issued for both adjudication and enquiry proceedings.
- **March 18, 2025**: Initial appointment of Adjudicating Officer (Shri Amar Navlani).
- **September 19, 2025**: Appointment of successor AO/DA (Shri Sudeep Mishra).
- **June 08–09, 2026**: Appointment of current AO and DA (undersigned officer).
- **2026-27**: Settlement Order issued in financial year 2026-27.

## Impact Assessment

This settlement order is significant given Angel One Limited's position as one of the largest retail stock brokers in India with millions of active clients. The violations relate to client fund management and compliance with NSE and SEBI broker conduct norms—areas of ongoing regulatory scrutiny for the brokerage industry. The settlement mechanism resolves both adjudication (penalty proceedings) and enquiry (registration/licence proceedings) simultaneously, providing regulatory finality for the entity. For the broader market, this order reinforces SEBI's active enforcement stance against large intermediaries and signals continued focus on running account settlement practices, client fund segregation, and NSE compliance obligations across all registered stock brokers.
