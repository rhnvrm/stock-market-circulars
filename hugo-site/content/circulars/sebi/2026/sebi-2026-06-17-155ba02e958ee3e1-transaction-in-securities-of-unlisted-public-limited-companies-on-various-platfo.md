## Summary

SEBI has issued Press Release No. 32/2026 cautioning investors against conducting transactions or sharing sensitive personal information on unauthorized electronic platforms and websites that facilitate trading in unlisted securities of public limited companies. SEBI reiterates that only recognized stock exchanges are authorized to provide platforms for fund raising and trading in securities.

## Key Points

- Electronic platforms facilitating transactions in unlisted public company securities are neither authorized nor recognized by SEBI.
- This is a reiteration of earlier warnings issued via Press Releases dated December 9, 2024 and August 30, 2016.
- Prior advisories also covered unauthorized virtual trading platforms (paper trading/fantasy games) and unregistered online platforms offering unlisted debt securities.
- Investors on such platforms have no access to SEBI/Exchange investor protection benefits.
- Investor grievance redressal mechanisms (including smartodr.in) are not available for disputes arising from participation on such platforms.
- A list of SEBI-recognized stock exchanges is available on the SEBI website (www.sebi.gov.in).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This press release reaffirms the existing legal position that only SEBI-recognized stock exchanges are authorized to provide platforms for securities transactions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Investors should avoid conducting any transactions on unauthorized electronic platforms dealing in unlisted securities.
- Investors should refrain from sharing sensitive personal details on such platforms.
- Entities operating unauthorized platforms remain in violation of SEBI regulations.

## Important Dates

- Press Release issued: June 17, 2026
- Prior advisory: December 9, 2024
- Original advisory: August 30, 2016

## Impact Assessment

This is a reiterative investor advisory with no direct market or operational impact on listed securities or registered intermediaries. The circular targets retail investors who may be misled by unauthorized platforms trading in unlisted securities. No specific companies or securities are named. The absence of SEBI/Exchange jurisdiction over such platforms means investors have no recourse through standard grievance mechanisms, underlining the risk highlighted.
