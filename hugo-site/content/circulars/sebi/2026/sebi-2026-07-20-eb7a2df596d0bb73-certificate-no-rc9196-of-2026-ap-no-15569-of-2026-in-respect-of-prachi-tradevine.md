## Summary

SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9196 of 2026 (Attachment Proceeding No. 15569 of 2026) against Prachi Tradevine Pvt. Ltd. (PAN: AAFCP7889L) in connection with the long-running "Illiquid Stock Options" enforcement matter. The certificate initiates recovery proceedings for outstanding dues (typically penalty and/or disgorgement amounts along with interest) confirmed against the entity in earlier SEBI adjudication/enforcement orders relating to reversal trades executed in illiquid stock options on the equity derivatives segment.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No.: RC9196 of 2026
- Attachment Proceeding (A.P.) No.: 15569 of 2026
- Noticee/Defaulter: Prachi Tradevine Pvt. Ltd.
- PAN: AAFCP7889L
- Underlying matter: Illiquid Stock Options (reversal/non-genuine trading pattern cases)
- Certificate issued by SEBI's Recovery Officer to enforce recovery of confirmed dues

## Regulatory Changes

This is not a new regulatory change but an enforcement/recovery action under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with the Second and Third Schedules to the Income Tax Act, 1961 and the Income Tax (Certificate Proceedings) Rules, 1962, as applicable to recovery of amounts due to SEBI.

## Compliance Requirements

- Prachi Tradevine Pvt. Ltd. is required to pay the outstanding dues specified in the underlying order(s) failing which SEBI's Recovery Officer may proceed with attachment and sale of movable/immovable property, bank accounts, and other recovery modes available under the Income Tax recovery framework as applied to SEBI proceedings.
- Market intermediaries and depositories may be required to act on any attachment directions issued in relation to the entity's assets/holdings.

## Important Dates

Specific payment deadlines and dates of the underlying original order are set out in the full certificate document; readers should refer to the source PDF for exact dates.

## Impact Assessment

This action is specific to Prachi Tradevine Pvt. Ltd. and does not have direct market-wide trading or listing implications. It is relevant primarily from a compliance and enforcement-tracking perspective, reflecting SEBI's continued follow-through on recovery of dues from entities found to have engaged in non-genuine/reversal trading in illiquid stock options.
