## Summary

SEBI has issued an Amended Notice of Demand dated June 19, 2026 under Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 8446 of 2024 in the matter of Citrus Check Inns Limited. This notice represents an amendment to a prior demand for recovery of monetary dues arising from a SEBI enforcement order against the company.

## Key Points

- SEBI issued an Amended Notice of Demand under RC No. 8446 of 2024 against Citrus Check Inns Limited.
- The notice is dated June 19, 2026 and constitutes an amendment to the original notice of demand.
- A Recovery Certificate (RC) is issued by SEBI to facilitate enforcement and collection of outstanding dues, penalties, or disgorgement amounts ordered against an entity.
- The amendment may reflect revised demand amounts, updated interest calculations, or changes in recovery particulars.

## Regulatory Changes

No broader regulatory changes are introduced by this notice. It is a specific enforcement action under SEBI's recovery powers, likely under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992, which empowers SEBI to recover amounts due as arrears of land revenue.

## Compliance Requirements

- Citrus Check Inns Limited and/or the named noticees are required to pay the demanded amount within the stipulated timeline mentioned in the notice.
- Failure to comply may result in further enforcement action including attachment of assets or bank accounts.
- The noticees should review the amended demand carefully for any changes in the amount or payment instructions compared to the original notice.

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date:** June 19, 2026
- **Recovery Certificate:** RC No. 8446 of 2024 (issued in 2024)
- Payment deadline: As specified within the notice document.

## Impact Assessment

This notice is specific to Citrus Check Inns Limited and does not have broader market impact. It reflects SEBI's ongoing enforcement of recovery of dues from entities that have failed to comply with earlier penalty/disgorgement orders. Investors and market participants should note this as an indicator of regulatory non-compliance by the company. The amendment suggests the recovery proceedings are ongoing and that SEBI is actively pursuing collection.
