## Summary

SEBI has issued a Remittance Advice concerning recovery proceedings against Money Mishra Financial Services, identified as a defaulter (PAN: AAZFM1357R), in connection with a case involving unregistered portfolio management services allegedly provided by Allied Financial Services Private Limited. The recovery action is being executed under Recovery Certificate No. 9073 of 2026.

## Key Points

- The notice pertains to recovery of dues from Money Mishra Financial Services, named as a defaulter in SEBI's records.
- The underlying matter relates to unregistered portfolio management services provided without SEBI registration by Allied Financial Services Private Limited.
- Recovery is being pursued under Recovery Certificate No. 9073 of 2026, indicating that formal recovery proceedings (e.g., under the SEBI Act recovery mechanism) have been initiated or concluded.
- The document is a Remittance Advice, suggesting funds recovered from the defaulter are being processed/remitted as part of the enforcement action.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement/administrative action under SEBI's existing recovery framework for defaulters and unregistered intermediaries.

## Compliance Requirements

- Entities offering portfolio management services must be duly registered with SEBI under the SEBI (Portfolio Managers) Regulations.
- The named defaulter and associated entity should comply with the recovery certificate and any related directions issued by SEBI or the Recovery Officer.
- Investors and market participants dealing with unregistered intermediaries face heightened compliance and financial risk.
