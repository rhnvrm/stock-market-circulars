## Summary

SEBI has issued a Remittance Advice against Ashok Kumar Gupta & Sons HUF (PAN: AAOHA5520P), identified as a defaulter, in connection with proceedings arising from dealings in illiquid stock options on the BSE. The advice is issued under Recovery Certificate No. 9107 of 2026, pursuant to which the Recovery Officer directs the party holding funds or assets belonging to the defaulter to remit the amount due towards satisfaction of the outstanding recovery.

## Key Points

- The defaulter named is Ashok Kumar Gupta & Sons HUF, PAN AAOHA5520P.
- The matter relates to dealings in illiquid stock options on the BSE, a category SEBI has historically scrutinized for potentially manipulative or non-genuine trading activity.
- Recovery is being enforced under Recovery Certificate No. 9107 of 2026, issued by SEBI's Recovery Officer under the powers vested by the Second Schedule to the Income-tax Act, 1961, read with the SEBI Act, 1992.
- The remittance advice directs the recipient (typically a bank or entity holding the defaulter's funds/securities) to remit the sums due from the defaulter's account(s) to SEBI in satisfaction of the recovery certificate.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory or policy changes are introduced by this notice; it is an individual enforcement action under SEBI's existing recovery mechanism for unpaid penalties/dues.

## Compliance Requirements

- The addressee (bank/custodian/other entity holding the defaulter's assets) must comply with the remittance directive and transfer the specified amount to SEBI as instructed.
- Non-compliance by the addressee can expose it to being treated as a defaulter itself under recovery proceedings.
- The named defaulter remains liable for the underlying dues until the recovery certificate is fully satisfied.

## Important Dates

The circular/document was issued on 2026-07-06. Specific payment deadlines and recovery timelines are set out in the underlying Recovery Certificate No. 9107 of 2026, details of which are not fully extractable from the scanned source document.

## Impact Assessment

This notice has narrow, targeted impact limited to the named defaulter and the specific entity directed to remit funds. It does not affect listed companies, trading in exchange-listed securities, or broader market participants, and carries no systemic or sector-wide implications. It reflects SEBI's ongoing enforcement of penalties related to illiquid stock options trading on BSE.
