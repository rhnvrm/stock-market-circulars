## Summary

This is an appellate order by SEBI's Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005, in Appeal No. 6878 of 2026 filed by Samar Imran against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought detailed information regarding SEBI's prescribed documentary requirements for shareholder name changes, share transmission, dematerialisation where name mismatches exist, and the regulatory framework (or absence thereof) for legacy investor cases predating widespread identity documentation (pre-1960).

## Key Points

- Appellant filed an RTI application dated April 28, 2026, received via the RTI MIS portal.
- CPIO, SEBI responded on May 11, 2026; appellant filed this appeal on May 12, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00175).
- Queries span seven broad categories: prescribed documentary requirements, mandatory nature of specific documents (marriage certificate, gazette notification, PAN, Voter ID), legacy cases (pre-1960 records), acceptance of alternative evidence (affidavits, indemnity bonds, bank verification), limits on RTA/company discretion, historical non-existence of documents, and absence of a prescribed resolution mechanism.
- The Appellate Authority reviewed the application, CPIO's response, and the appeal to decide the matter on record.
- The appeal raises broader concerns about whether RTAs and companies are insisting on documents not mandated under SEBI regulations.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this order. The matter pertains to the interpretation and availability of existing SEBI circulars, master circulars, and regulations governing share-related transactions and investor documentation.

## Compliance Requirements

- No new compliance obligations arise from this RTI appellate order.
- The queries highlight areas where investors and market intermediaries (RTAs, companies) should be aware of SEBI's existing prescribed document lists for name changes, share transmission, and dematerialisation.
- RTAs and companies should not insist on documents beyond those prescribed under SEBI regulations.

## Important Dates

- RTI Application filed: April 28, 2026
- CPIO Response: May 11, 2026
- Appeal filed: May 12, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00175)
- Appeal order date: June 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is an individual RTI matter with no direct market or operational impact. However, the queries reflect systemic investor concerns about inconsistent documentary demands by RTAs and companies, particularly for legacy cases and name-change/transmission scenarios. If the appellate order results in clarifications or confirmations about SEBI's regulatory stance, it could inform investor grievance handling practices more broadly. No stocks, funds, or market segments are directly affected.
