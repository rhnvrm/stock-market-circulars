## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority dismissed Appeal No. 6910 of 2026 filed by Anirudh Prasad Kewat against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought information pertaining to refunds in the PACL matter via an RTI application dated March 16, 2026. The appeal was found time-barred and, on merit, dismissed as SEBI does not hold the requested information.

## Key Points

- Appeal filed by Anirudh Prasad Kewat against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai regarding PACL refund information
- RTI application was originally filed on March 16, 2026; CPIO responded on March 24, 2026
- First appeal was received on June 01, 2026, well beyond the 30-day statutory deadline under Section 19(1) of the RTI Act
- Appellant neither requested condonation of delay nor provided reasons for the delay
- Appeal dismissed as time-barred
- On merits, the respondent confirmed that the requested information is not available with SEBI
- Appellate Authority cited CIC precedent (Sh. Pattipati Rama Murthy vs. CPIO, SEBI, July 8, 2013): CPIO cannot invent information that does not exist
- Responsibility for PACL property disposal and investor repayment lies with the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee, constituted per Supreme Court order dated February 2, 2016

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a procedural appellate order under the Right to Information Act, 2005.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. RTI appellants must file first appeals within 30 days of receiving the CPIO's response as mandated under Section 19(1) of the RTI Act. Delay condonation requests must be explicitly made with valid reasons.

## Important Dates

- March 16, 2026: Appellant's original RTI application
- March 24, 2026: CPIO response to RTI application
- May 26, 2026: Appellant filed first appeal (registration date)
- June 01, 2026: First appeal received by Office of Appellate Authority (beyond 30-day deadline)

## Impact Assessment

No market or trading impact. This is a routine RTI appellate order affecting only the individual appellant. Investors seeking PACL refund-related information are directed to SEBI's website for public notices, press releases, status reports, and FAQs published by the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee.
