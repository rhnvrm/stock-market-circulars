## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer (AO) issued an Adjudication Order (No. Order/JS/YK/2026-27/32502) against Eastern Financiers Limited, a stock broker registered with SEBI (Registration No. INZ000193733). The order follows a joint SEBI-BSE inspection of the broker's Authorized Persons (APs) covering the period April 1, 2024 to April 30, 2025. The inspection findings were communicated to the Noticee on July 28, 2025, and after considering the broker's replies, a final Inspection Report alleged violations of the SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 1992, along with SEBI and BSE circulars. A Show Cause Notice (Ref. No. DIS/15259/2026) was issued on June 11, 2026 under Section 15-I of the SEBI Act read with Rule 4 of the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995, proposing penalty under Section 15HB of the SEBI Act.

## Key Points

- Eastern Financiers Limited (PAN: AAACE7613A) is registered with SEBI as a stock broker under registration number INZ000193733.
- SEBI and BSE jointly inspected five Authorized Persons of the broker: M/s. Finwell Global Services, Mr. Hitesh P Sanghvi, Ms. Jonaki Biswas, Mr. Lakshman Kumar Bihani, and Mr. Rohit Kumar Jain, with inspections conducted between June 3-10, 2025.
- A key alleged violation was failure to maintain pre-order trade confirmations: APs Ms. Jonaki Biswas, Mr. Rohit Kumar Jain and Mr. Hitesh P Sanghvi told inspectors that orders were received via telephone or Internet Based Trading (IBT), but the APs/Noticee could not provide call recordings to substantiate pre-order confirmations.
- Findings were communicated to the Noticee on July 28, 2025, and the broker responded via letters/emails dated August 6, August 25, and August 29, 2025.
- A Show Cause Notice was issued on June 11, 2026, requiring the broker to explain why an inquiry should not be held and why penalty should not be imposed under Section 15HB of the SEBI Act.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement action applying existing provisions of the SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 1992, Section 15-I and 15HB of the SEBI Act, and Rule 4/5 of the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995.

## Compliance Requirements

- Stock brokers must ensure that Authorized Persons maintain records of pre-order trade confirmations, including call recordings, for orders received telephonically or through Internet Based Trading.
- Brokers must be able to produce such records on demand during SEBI/exchange inspections.
- Brokers should ensure ongoing supervision and compliance monitoring of their network of Authorized Persons.

## Important Dates

- Inspection period: April 1, 2024 – April 30, 2025
- AP inspections conducted: June 3–10, 2025
- Findings communicated to Noticee: July 28, 2025
- Noticee replies: August 6, August 25, and August 29, 2025
- AO appointed: June 8, 2026
- Show Cause Notice issued: June 11, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The order pertains to an individual stock broker's internal compliance and supervisory practices regarding its Authorized Persons and does not directly affect listed companies, market-wide trading, or investors broadly. It serves as a compliance reminder for stock brokers regarding recordkeeping obligations for orders routed through APs, particularly the need to maintain verifiable pre-order trade confirmations.
