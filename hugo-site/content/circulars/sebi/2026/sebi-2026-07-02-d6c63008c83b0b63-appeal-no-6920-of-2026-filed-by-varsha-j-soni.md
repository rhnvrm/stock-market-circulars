## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority disposed of RTI Appeal No. 6920 of 2026 filed by Varsha J Soni against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought detailed information about her demat account held with Sharekhan Limited, including account opening details, all transaction records since April 2018, complaints registered against the account, pledge/margin pledge/collateral/hypothecation details, and any records of regulatory action, investor grievance, or unauthorized transfer of securities. The CPIO had responded that such information is not maintained by SEBI in the normal course of market regulation and advised the appellant to approach her depository participant directly. The Appellate Authority upheld this response, citing the Supreme Court's ruling in CBSE & Anr. vs. Aditya Bandopadhyay & Ors. that the RTI Act only provides access to information that is actually available and existing with the public authority, not information a public authority is not required to maintain.

## Key Points

- Appellant Varsha J Soni filed an RTI application dated May 23, 2026 seeking demat account details from SEBI, including transaction history since April 2018, complaints, pledge/collateral details, and unauthorized transfer records.
- The account in question is held with Sharekhan Limited as the Depository Participant.
- CPIO responded on June 01, 2026 stating the requested information is not maintained by SEBI and advised the appellant to approach her depository participant.
- Appellant filed an appeal dated June 08, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00211) on the ground of being refused access to information.
- Appellate Authority relied on the Supreme Court judgment in CBSE & Anr. vs. Aditya Bandopadhyay & Ors. (August 9, 2011), which held that the RTI Act only covers information that is part of the record of a public authority and required to be maintained under law.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual appellate order under the RTI Act and does not introduce any regulatory or policy change.

## Compliance Requirements

None applicable to market participants generally. The order clarifies that detailed demat account transaction and pledge records are maintained by depositories/depository participants, not by SEBI, and investors seeking such records should approach their DP directly.

## Important Dates

- May 23, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- June 01, 2026: CPIO response letter.
- June 08, 2026: Appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00211).

## Impact Assessment

This order has no market-wide impact and pertains solely to an individual investor's RTI request. It reaffirms the established principle that SEBI is not the custodian of individual demat account transaction records and that such information must be sought from the depository participant. No action is required from listed companies, brokers, or other market intermediaries as a result of this order, though it serves as a reference point for similarly situated investors on the appropriate channel for such requests.
