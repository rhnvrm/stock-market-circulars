## Summary

The Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 dismissed Appeal No. 6935 of 2026 filed by Sanjay Ramdas Tingare against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought information regarding the role of Octate Developers LLP in certain property sale transactions related to Survey No. 10/1 and 10/2 at Dhanori, Pune, including any Supreme Court orders permitting its inclusion as a "Consenting Party," details of amounts remitted to the Sahara-SEBI Refund Account from such sales, and records of litigation settlements. The CPIO responded that no information had been received from Saharas regarding the sale of the referenced properties, and therefore the queries could not be verified or answered. The Appellate Authority found the CPIO's response adequate and dismissed the appeal.

## Key Points

- Appellant Sanjay Ramdas Tingare filed an RTI application dated April 28, 2026, received by SEBI the same day.
- The application sought five categories of information concerning Octate Developers LLP's role in property sale transactions at Dhanori, Pune, and related remittances to the Sahara-SEBI Refund Account.
- The CPIO responded on May 22, 2026, stating no information had been received from Saharas on the sale of the specified properties.
- The appellant filed an appeal dated June 15, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/P/26/00035), received by the Office of the Appellate Authority on June 17, 2026.
- The Appellate Authority, Ruchi Chojer, reviewed the application, CPIO response, and appeal, and found no deficiency in the CPIO's reply.
- The appeal was dismissed on July 16, 2026, in Mumbai.
