## Summary

SEBI has issued a Notice of Demand under Recovery Certificate No. RC9224 of 2026 against Rose Securities Limited (PAN: AADCR1312E). The notice relates to outstanding dues arising from SEBI's enforcement proceedings in the illiquid stock options matter, where entities were found to have executed reversal trades in illiquid stock options contracts creating an appearance of trading volume/liquidity without any genuine change in beneficial ownership, in violation of SEBI regulations. The Recovery Officer has issued this certificate demanding payment of the amount due, likely comprising penalty and/or disgorgement amounts along with applicable interest, within the stipulated time frame under the Second Schedule to the Income Tax Act, 1961 (as applied to SEBI recovery proceedings under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an individual enforcement/recovery action taken under SEBI's existing recovery proceedings framework (Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992 read with the Second Schedule and Third Schedule to the Income Tax Act, 1961).

## Compliance Requirements

Rose Securities Limited is required to pay the demanded amount, along with any interest accrued, within the period specified in the notice. Failure to comply may result in coercive recovery measures such as attachment and sale of movable/immovable property, garnishee proceedings, or arrest and detention of the defaulter as per the recovery rules.
