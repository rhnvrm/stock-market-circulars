## Summary

SEBI has issued a prohibitory order against M Satish Kumar pursuant to Recovery Certificate Nos. 2851 of 2020 and 2852 of 2020 in the matter of Ranklin Solutions Ltd. The order directs the attachment and freezing of the noticee's assets, including bank accounts and demat holdings, to facilitate recovery of outstanding dues owed to SEBI.

## Key Points

- Prohibitory order issued against M Satish Kumar under Recovery Certificate Nos. 2851 and 2852 of 2020.
- The matter pertains to Ranklin Solutions Ltd., subject to prior SEBI enforcement proceedings.
- Order directs banks, depositories, and other financial institutions to freeze/attach assets held by the noticee.
- Recovery certificates were originally issued in 2020; this order represents continued enforcement action.
- Part of a broader batch of SEBI recovery proceedings issued in March 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is an enforcement action under existing SEBI recovery proceedings provisions (Schedule II of SEBI Act, 1992, read with the Second Schedule to the Income Tax Act, 1961 as applicable to recovery of SEBI dues).

## Compliance Requirements

- **Banks and financial institutions**: Must freeze bank accounts held by M Satish Kumar upon receipt of this order and report compliance to SEBI's Recovery Officer.
- **Depositories/DPs**: Must freeze demat accounts/securities held by M Satish Kumar and report status to SEBI.
- **M Satish Kumar**: Must not transfer, alienate, or dispose of any assets until further orders from SEBI.
- All concerned parties must acknowledge receipt and confirm compliance within the stipulated timeframe.

## Important Dates

- **Recovery Certificates Issued**: 2020 (RC Nos. 2851 and 2852 of 2020)
- **Prohibitory Order Date**: March 6, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct impact on broader markets or trading participants. It is a targeted enforcement action against one individual (M Satish Kumar) in connection with historic SEBI proceedings involving Ranklin Solutions Ltd. The practical effect is restricted to freezing the individual's financial assets pending recovery of dues. Market participants and investors are not directly affected.
