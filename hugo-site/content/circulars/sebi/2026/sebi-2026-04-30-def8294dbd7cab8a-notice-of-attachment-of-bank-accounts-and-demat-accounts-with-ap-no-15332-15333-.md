## Summary

SEBI has issued a Notice of Attachment of Bank Accounts and Demat Accounts (Attachment Proceedings No. 15332 & 15333 of 2026) dated April 30, 2026 against I Zone Academy Private Limited, designated as a defaulter, in connection with trading in Illiquid Stock Options on BSE. The action is taken under Recovery Certificate No. 9078 of 2026 to recover dues owed to SEBI.

## Key Points

- Two attachment orders issued: AP No. 15332 and AP No. 15333 of 2026
- Defaulter entity: I Zone Academy Private Limited
- Action taken under Recovery Certificate No. 9078 of 2026
- Violation relates to trading in Illiquid Stock Options on BSE
- Both bank accounts and demat accounts are subject to attachment
- Order dated April 30, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is an enforcement/recovery action under existing SEBI recovery proceedings framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- Banks and depositories holding accounts of I Zone Academy Private Limited are directed to freeze/attach the specified accounts
- The defaulter (I Zone Academy Private Limited) is prohibited from operating the attached bank and demat accounts
- Concerned financial institutions must comply with the attachment notice immediately upon receipt
- Any transactions contrary to the attachment order may be treated as violation of SEBI directions

## Important Dates

- **April 30, 2026**: Date of issuance of attachment orders AP No. 15332 & 15333 of 2026
- Recovery Certificate No. 9078 of 2026 governs the underlying recovery proceedings

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Low to negligible broader market impact. This is a targeted enforcement action against a single defaulter entity.

**Entity Impact**: High impact on I Zone Academy Private Limited — bank and demat accounts are attached, effectively restricting the entity's financial operations.

**Regulatory Significance**: Medium — demonstrates SEBI's continued enforcement of penalties arising from illiquid stock options manipulation on BSE, a segment that has seen sustained regulatory scrutiny. Recovery certificate proceedings indicate prior adjudication orders have already been passed and the entity has failed to comply, triggering coercive recovery.
