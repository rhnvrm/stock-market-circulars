## Summary

SEBI issued an adjudication order (Order No. Order/AK/GN/2026-27/32409) against Ararti Karmakar (PAN: BYKPK3038D) for alleged participation in large-scale reversal trades in the illiquid stock options segment of BSE during the investigation period of April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015. The noticee allegedly executed 4 non-genuine trades in 2 stock options contracts, creating artificial volume of 2,71,750 units.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated large-scale reversal trades in the BSE stock options segment for the period April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015
- A total of 2,91,744 trades (81.40% of all trades in BSE stock options) during the investigation period were found to be allegedly non-genuine
- Ararti Karmakar executed 4 non-genuine trades in 2 stock options contracts, resulting in artificial volume of 2,71,750 units
- Alleged violations include PFUTP Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1) and 4(2)(a)
- Show Cause Notice was originally issued on February 23, 2022; service attempts via SPAD returned undelivered
- Post SCN Intimation (PSI) dated August 10, 2022 also returned undelivered
- Mr. Suresh B. Menon was initially appointed as Adjudicating Officer; the case was later transferred to the undersigned AO vide Order dated April 3, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This order applies existing SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices Relating to Securities Market) Regulations, 2003 (PFUTP Regulations) and Section 15HA of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must not engage in reversal trades or any non-genuine trades that create artificial volumes in stock options or any securities segment
- Entities must ensure all trades are genuine and not manipulative or deceptive in nature
- Compliance with PFUTP Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1), and 4(2)(a) is mandatory for all market participants

## Important Dates

- **Investigation Period:** April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015
- **Show Cause Notice Date:** February 23, 2022
- **Post SCN Intimation Date:** August 10, 2022
- **AO Transfer Order Date:** April 3, 2025
- **Adjudication Order Number:** Order/AK/GN/2026-27/32409

## Impact Assessment

This enforcement action is part of SEBI's broader crackdown on manipulation in illiquid stock options on BSE, targeting entities that participated in reversal trades between 2014 and 2015. The case has limited direct market impact given its historical nature and involves a single individual. However, it reinforces SEBI's continued pursuit of market manipulation cases and signals that enforcement actions can follow many years after the violation period. The penalty, if imposed under Section 15HA of the SEBI Act, could be significant given the fraudulent and manipulative nature of the alleged trades.
