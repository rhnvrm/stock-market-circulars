## Summary

This is an appellate order under the Right to Information Act, 2005, in Appeal No. 6870 of 2026, filed by Samar Imran against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought SCORES complaint records and KFin Technologies correspondence related to two complaints (Ref No. SEBIE/AP25/VISA/000344/1 and SEBIE/AP26/VISA/000957/1) concerning a disputed stance on whether a Family Member Certificate/Legal Heir Certificate was sufficient or if a succession certificate was required.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed RTI application on April 20, 2026 seeking complete SCORES case records including all attachments, replies, and documents uploaded by KFin Technologies
- Respondent (CPIO, SEBI) replied on May 05, 2026 stating that SCORES is an electronic platform with no separate file noting maintained outside the system
- Appellant filed appeal on May 07, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00168) on grounds that the information provided was incomplete, misleading, or false
- The core grievance concerns KFin Technologies allegedly taking contradictory positions: first accepting a Family Member Certificate/Legal Heir Certificate as sufficient, then subsequently requiring a succession certificate in the same matter
- SEBI's SCORES system records all actions, observations, clarifications, and responses electronically, accessible via credentials

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is an individual RTI appellate proceeding.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. The matter is limited to the CPIO's obligation to provide information under the RTI Act.

## Important Dates

- April 20, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant
- May 05, 2026: CPIO, SEBI responded to the RTI application
- May 07, 2026: Appellant filed first appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00168)

## Impact Assessment

This order has no market-wide or operational impact. It is an individual RTI appeal concerning access to SCORES complaint records. The underlying dispute involves KFin Technologies' handling of a succession/inheritance matter for an investor, which is a narrow grievance with no systemic implications for broader market participants.
