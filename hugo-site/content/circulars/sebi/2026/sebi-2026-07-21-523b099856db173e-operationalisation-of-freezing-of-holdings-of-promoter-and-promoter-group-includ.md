## Summary

SEBI has issued a circular operationalising Regulation 24(i)(ea) of the SEBI (Buy-back of Securities) Regulations, 2018, which was inserted via a notification dated July 01, 2026. The regulation requires that shares or other specified securities held by promoters and promoter group (including their associates) be frozen at the ISIN level from the date the board resolution or special resolution for buy-back is passed until the closing of the buy-back offer. The freeze permits two exceptions: tendering of shares in a tender-offer route buy-back, and invocation of encumbrances created prior to commencement of the buy-back period. Depositories are directed to establish the operational framework and system enhancements to implement this requirement before August 01, 2026.

## Key Points

- Regulation 24(i)(ea) mandates ISIN-level freezing of promoter and promoter group (including associates) holdings during buy-back periods.
- Freeze period runs from the date of the board/special resolution approving the buy-back until the closing of the offer.
- Two permitted exceptions during the freeze: (a) tendering shares in a tender-offer route buy-back, and (b) invocation of encumbrances created before the buy-back period began.
- Depositories must build an operational framework, including system enhancements, and issue operational guidelines/communiqués.
- The framework must cover: format for companies to issue freeze instructions, operational modalities for the ISIN-level freeze, modalities for tendering in tender-offer buybacks, modalities for invocation/release of pre-existing encumbrances (with freeze continuing on invoked/released securities), and any other necessary operational procedures.
- Circular applies to Listed Companies, Recognised Stock Exchanges, Depositories, Registered Merchant Bankers, RTAs, and Share Transfer Agents.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular operationalises the newly inserted Regulation 24(i)(ea) under the SEBI (Buy-back of Securities) Regulations, 2018 (amended via notification dated July 01, 2026). It does not itself amend regulations but directs Depositories to build the systems and procedures needed to implement the ISIN-level freeze requirement on promoter holdings during buy-back periods.

## Compliance Requirements

- Depositories: Must put in place the operational framework and system enhancements, and issue operational guidelines/communiqués before August 01, 2026.
- Listed Companies: Must issue freeze instructions in the format prescribed by the depositories' operational framework and ensure compliance.
- Recognised Stock Exchanges, Merchant Bankers, RTAs, and Share Transfer Agents: Must ensure compliance with the circular and the depositories' operational framework.
- Issued under Section 11(1) of the SEBI Act, 1992 read with Section 26(3) of the Depositories Act, 1996.

## Important Dates

- July 01, 2026: SEBI notification amending Buy-back Regulations to insert Regulation 24(i)(ea).
- July 21, 2026: Date of this circular; comes into force with immediate effect.
- August 01, 2026: Deadline for Depositories to implement the operational framework and system enhancements.

## Impact Assessment

The circular has a moderate operational impact on depositories, listed companies undertaking buy-backs, and intermediaries such as RTAs and merchant bankers, who must adapt processes to comply with ISIN-level freezing of promoter holdings during buy-back periods. It strengthens investor protection by preventing promoters from trading frozen shares outside permitted exceptions during buy-backs, enhancing the integrity of buy-back processes. Direct market/trading impact on specific stocks is minimal since this is a procedural/systemic implementation circular rather than a company-specific action.
