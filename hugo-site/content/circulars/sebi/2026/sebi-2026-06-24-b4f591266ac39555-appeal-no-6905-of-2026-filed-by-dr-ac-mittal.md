## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority (under the Right to Information Act, 2005) dismissed Appeal No. 6905 of 2026 filed by Dr. A.C. Mittal against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought information on SEBI regulations governing the transfer of physical share certificates under specific conditions, including transfers without original certificates and transfers with missing distinctive/certificate numbers. The appeal was dismissed on the grounds that all SEBI regulations, circulars, and guidelines are publicly available on the SEBI website.

## Key Points

- Appellant Dr. A.C. Mittal filed an RTI application dated April 07, 2026 seeking information on SEBI rules for physical share transfers
- The application sought regulations covering: (1) transfer of equity shares in physical form without original share certificates; (2) transfers where certificate/distinctive numbers are missing from transfer deeds; (3) acceptance of blank indemnity bonds for duplicate share issuance; (4) special procedures/relaxations for R&T Agents in absence of primary identifiers
- Respondent (CPIO, SEBI) replied on April 28, 2026, directing the appellant to the SEBI Master Circular for Registrars to an Issue and Share Transfer Agents dated February 06, 2026 and Regulation 7 of the SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015
- Appellant filed the appeal on May 27, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00200) alleging the response was incomplete, misleading, or false
- The Appellate Authority upheld the respondent's reply, noting all SEBI regulations and circulars are publicly accessible on the SEBI website

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This order reaffirms the existing legal position that publicly available information on the SEBI website does not impose an obligation on SEBI to separately furnish such information under the RTI Act, citing:
- Delhi High Court: *Registrar of Companies & ors. vs. Dharmendra Kumar Garg & anr.*
- CIC: *Shri K Lall vs. Shri M K Bagri* (CIC/AT/A/2007/00112, April 12, 2007)

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements arising from this order. Entities seeking information on physical share transfer procedures should refer to:
- SEBI Master Circular for Registrars to an Issue and Share Transfer Agents (February 06, 2026)
- Regulation 7 of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015

## Important Dates

- April 07, 2026: Original RTI application filed by Dr. A.C. Mittal
- April 28, 2026: CPIO, SEBI responded to the application
- May 27, 2026: Appellant filed the appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00200)
- June 2026: Appellate Authority dismissed the appeal

## Impact Assessment

No market or operational impact. This is a routine administrative RTI appellate order with no new directives or regulatory changes. It confirms that SEBI's public disclosure of regulations on its website satisfies information access requirements under the RTI Act. Market participants, RTAs, and listed companies are unaffected.
