## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority under the RTI Act, 2005 dismissed Appeal No. 6888 of 2026 filed by Saurabh Shrivastava against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought information on the PACL refund process, including steps for refund, total amounts recovered and refunded, policy for higher-value claims, plot allotment alternatives, interest/compensation on refunds, and participation in PACL property auctions. The CPIO had responded that the requested information was not available with SEBI, and the Appellate Authority upheld this response.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed RTI application on May 3, 2026 seeking details on PACL refund procedures and property auctions.
- CPIO responded on May 5, 2026 stating the requested information is not available with SEBI and directed the appellant to SEBI's website for public notices, press releases, and FAQs related to PACL.
- Appellant appealed on May 17, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00185) alleging incomplete, misleading, or false information was provided.
- Appellate Authority upheld CPIO's response, citing the CIC precedent (Sh. Pattipati Rama Murthy vs. CPIO, SEBI, July 8, 2013): a CPIO cannot invent information it does not possess.
- Responsibility for PACL property disposal and investor repayment lies with the Justice (Retd.) R. M. Lodha Committee, constituted under the Supreme Court order dated February 2, 2016.
- Appeal dismissed by Appellate Authority Ruchi Chojer on June 15, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a procedural RTI appellate order with no new rules or amendments.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. Investors seeking information on PACL refunds are directed to public resources available on the SEBI website, including Status Reports, FAQs, Press Releases, and Public Notices pertaining to PACL Ltd.

## Important Dates

- May 3, 2026: Appellant's RTI application filed.
- May 5, 2026: CPIO's response issued.
- May 17, 2026: Appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00185).
- June 15, 2026: Appeal dismissed by Appellate Authority.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no broader market or operational impact. It is limited to the individual appellant's RTI query regarding PACL-related refunds. Investors with similar queries are reminded that PACL investor repayment and property disposal are managed exclusively by the Justice Lodha Committee under Supreme Court supervision, and relevant public information is available on the SEBI website.
