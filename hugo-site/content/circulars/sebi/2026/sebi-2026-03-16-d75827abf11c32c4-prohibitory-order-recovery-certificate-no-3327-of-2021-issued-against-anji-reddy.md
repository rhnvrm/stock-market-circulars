## Summary

SEBI issued a prohibitory order under Recovery Certificate No. 3327 of 2021 against Anji Reddy Vanga in the matter of Synergy Bizcon. The order directs the attachment of assets held by the defaulter to facilitate recovery of outstanding dues pursuant to earlier SEBI adjudication/enforcement proceedings.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. 3327 of 2021 issued against Anji Reddy Vanga
- Matter pertains to Synergy Bizcon
- SEBI issued prohibitory order directing attachment of movable/immovable assets
- Action taken under SEBI Act and Securities and Exchange Board of India (Recovery of Money) Regulations
- Order aimed at recovering dues payable by the defaulter to SEBI

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is an enforcement action under existing SEBI recovery regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Banks, depositories, and other financial institutions holding assets of Anji Reddy Vanga are directed not to allow any debit or transfer of the attached assets
- Any person in possession of assets of the defaulter must comply with the prohibitory directions
- Non-compliance with the order may attract further legal action under applicable laws

## Important Dates

- Recovery Certificate No. 3327 of 2021: Original certificate date in 2021
- Order Date: March 16, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is an individual enforcement action with no broader market impact. The prohibitory order restricts the movement of assets belonging to Anji Reddy Vanga to enable recovery of dues in the Synergy Bizcon matter. Impact is confined to the specific defaulter and entities holding their assets.
