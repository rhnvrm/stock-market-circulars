## Summary

SEBI has issued a Remittance Advice in connection with recovery proceedings initiated against Vikash Gupta (PAN: CUCPG7495R), identified as a defaulter and Portfolio Manager of M/s. Algo System. The matter also involves Ms. Pooja Patidar, an employee of an Investment Research Advisor (IRA). The recovery action is being carried out under Recovery Certificate No. 7571 of 2024, issued as part of SEBI's enforcement mechanism to recover dues or amounts payable pursuant to a prior regulatory order.

## Key Points

- Remittance advice issued against defaulter Vikash Gupta, PAN: CUCPG7495R.
- Vikash Gupta is identified as the Portfolio Manager of M/s. Algo System.
- The matter also names Ms. Pooja Patidar, an employee of an Investment Research Advisor (IRA), indicating a connected or related recovery proceeding.
- Recovery is being executed under Recovery Certificate No. 7571 of 2024.
- The document facilitates remittance of recovered amounts as part of SEBI's debt recovery process.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this document. It is an administrative/enforcement communication relating to execution of an existing Recovery Certificate issued against the named defaulter.

## Compliance Requirements

- The defaulter, Vikash Gupta, and associated parties are required to comply with the recovery certificate and remit the specified dues.
- Recovery Officers and associated banking/financial intermediaries handling the remittance must process the amount in accordance with SEBI's recovery procedures under the applicable recovery certificate.

## Important Dates

Specific payment deadlines and the date of issuance of Recovery Certificate No. 7571 of 2024 are referenced in the source document; the certificate pertains to the 2024 recovery cycle, with the remittance advice being processed in 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This is a targeted enforcement action against an individual portfolio manager and does not directly affect listed securities, market operations, or broader investor trading activity. It underscores SEBI's ongoing efforts to enforce recovery of dues from market intermediaries and individuals found liable under prior regulatory or adjudicatory orders, reinforcing investor protection and deterrence against non-compliance among portfolio managers and investment research advisors.
