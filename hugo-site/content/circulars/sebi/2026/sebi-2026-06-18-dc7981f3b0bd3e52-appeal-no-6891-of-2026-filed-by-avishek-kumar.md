## Summary

This is an RTI appellate order (Appeal No. 6891 of 2026) filed by Avishek Kumar against the Central Public Information Officer (CPIO), SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought detailed period-wise data from the SCORES grievance platform on complaints under the 'Listed Company-IPO/Prelisting/Offer document (shares)' category, with a specific focus on the participation and remarks entered by the Association of Investment Bankers of India (AIBI) in the complaint workflow.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed an RTI application dated April 20, 2026 via the RTI MIS portal; SEBI responded on May 19, 2026
- Appeal registered as SEBIH/A/E/26/00188, filed on May 20, 2026
- Information sought covers FY 2023-24, FY 2024-25, FY 2025-26, and from April 1, 2026 onwards
- Appellant requested period-wise counts of SCORES complaints under the IPO/Prelisting/Offer document category
- Specific queries targeted how AIBI appeared in complaint workflows (as designated body, assigned to, action taken by, etc.)
- Appellant sought counts of complaints where AIBI entered specific standardized remarks deflecting investors to the MI Portal
- If compiled data unavailable, appellant requested complaint-wise extracts with fields including registration number, category, action taken, remarks, and closure status

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes arising from this order. This is an appellate proceeding under the Right to Information Act, 2005.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. The order concerns SEBI's internal obligation to respond to RTI requests regarding SCORES complaint data.

## Important Dates

- RTI Application filed: April 20, 2026
- SEBI (Respondent) response: May 19, 2026
- Appeal filed: May 20, 2026
- Appeal registration: SEBIH/A/E/26/00188

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct market or operational impact. It is a transparency and accountability matter under the RTI Act, examining SEBI's disclosure of aggregated complaint statistics from the SCORES platform. The case may have indirect significance for investors seeking to understand how intermediary bodies like AIBI handle grievances in the IPO/prelisting segment, but does not affect trading, listing, or compliance obligations for market participants.
