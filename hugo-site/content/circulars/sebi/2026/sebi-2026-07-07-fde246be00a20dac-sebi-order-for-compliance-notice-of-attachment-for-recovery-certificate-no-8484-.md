## Summary

SEBI, exercising powers as Recovery Officer under the Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992 read with the Second Schedule to the Income-tax Act, 1961 (as applicable to recovery of sums due), has issued a Notice of Attachment against Narendrabhai Amin pursuant to Recovery Certificate No. 8484 of 2024 issued in the matter of M/s Austral Coke and Projects Limited. The order directs attachment of the named defaulter's assets, including demat holdings and related financial interests, to secure recovery of amounts certified as due and payable.

## Key Points

- The order is issued under Recovery Certificate No. 8484 of 2024, identified as "-II" indicating a further/follow-up attachment notice in the same recovery matter.
- The defaulter named in the order is Narendrabhai Amin, proceeded against in connection with dues arising in the matter of M/s Austral Coke and Projects Limited.
- The notice directs attachment of the defaulter's assets (including demat account holdings and/or mutual fund folios, where applicable) to prevent further transfer or disposal pending recovery.
- Depositories, depository participants, and/or asset management companies holding the defaulter's securities or units are required to comply with the attachment directions and report compliance to SEBI.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement action applying existing recovery provisions under the SEBI Act and the Income-tax Act's Second Schedule (recovery of arrears as land revenue) to a specific defaulter.

## Compliance Requirements

- The defaulter, Narendrabhai Amin, and any intermediaries holding his assets (depositories, DPs, RTAs, AMCs) must not permit any transfer, sale, or disposal of the attached assets without SEBI's prior permission.
- Intermediaries in receipt of this order are required to confirm compliance and furnish requisite details of the attached holdings to SEBI's Recovery Officer within the timeline specified in the order.
- Any dealing in the attached demat account or mutual fund folios in contravention of the attachment notice may attract further legal consequences.

## Important Dates

Specific compliance deadlines and the date of the underlying Recovery Certificate (No. 8484 of 2024) are set out in the original order; the notice was published by SEBI in July 2026.

## Impact Assessment

The order has no direct impact on listed securities, trading activity, or market-wide operations. Its effect is confined to the named individual defaulter and the intermediaries holding his attached assets, who must ensure compliance with the attachment to avoid regulatory action. The action reflects SEBI's continued use of recovery proceedings to enforce outstanding dues linked to the Austral Coke and Projects Limited matter.
