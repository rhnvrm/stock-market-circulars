## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer (AO) issued an adjudication order against Bhavin Sureshbhai Thakkar in connection with trading activities in the scrip of Evexia Lifecare Limited (formerly Kavit Industries Ltd., and originally incorporated as Jal Hi Power Petrochem Ltd. in 1990, listed on BSE since March 21, 1994). The order arises from a SEBI investigation triggered by a Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) from the Financial Intelligence Unit - India (FIU-IND) flagging unusual trading concentration by the entity Ethan Constructions Pvt. Ltd. in a few scrips, including Evexia, with trading turnover disproportionate to its income range. Following a preliminary analysis by BSE recommending further investigation, SEBI examined trading by certain entities, including the Noticee, in the scrip of Evexia for the period August 11, 2016 to November 11, 2016 (the Investigation Period).

## Key Points

- SEBI initiated adjudication proceedings against Bhavin Sureshbhai Thakkar (PAN: AFCPT8628L) for alleged violations of Section 12A(a), (b) and (c) of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d) and 4(1), 4(2)(a) and 4(2)(e) of the SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices Relating to Securities Market) Regulations, 2003 (PFUTP Regulations).
- The case stems from a BSE investigation report recommending scrutiny of trading in Evexia's scrip, prompted by an FIU-IND suspicious transaction report regarding Ethan Constructions Pvt. Ltd.
- The Investigation Period covered was August 11, 2016 to November 11, 2016.
- An AO was first appointed in the matter on November 20, 2023, with the current AO appointed via reallocation on April 2, 2025.
- A Show Cause Notice (Reference No. SEBI/HO/EAD-8/SKV/VC/50753/2023) was issued to the Noticee on December 18, 2023.
- The adjudication order is numbered Order/JS/VC/2026-27/32459.

## Regulatory Changes

This is an individual enforcement order and does not introduce new regulatory provisions. It applies existing provisions under Section 15-I of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with Rule 5 of the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995, and assesses alleged violations of Section 12A and the PFUTP Regulations, 2003.

## Compliance Requirements

The order pertains specifically to the Noticee, Bhavin Sureshbhai Thakkar, who was required to respond to the Show Cause Notice and participate in the adjudication proceedings under Section 15HA of the SEBI Act regarding penalty for fraudulent and unfair trade practices. No broader compliance requirements are imposed on market participants generally.

## Important Dates

- Investigation Period: August 11, 2016 to November 11, 2016
- Initial AO appointment: November 20, 2023
- Show Cause Notice issued: December 18, 2023
- Reallocation/current AO appointment: April 2, 2025

## Impact Assessment

The order is limited to an individual enforcement matter concerning alleged fraudulent trading practices by one noticee in the scrip of Evexia Lifecare Limited. While it reinforces SEBI's continued scrutiny of suspicious trading patterns flagged via STRs and exchange surveillance, it does not have direct market-wide or sectoral implications. It serves as a reminder to market participants of the regulatory risk associated with coordinated or suspicious trading activity and SEBI's ongoing enforcement of PFUTP Regulations.
