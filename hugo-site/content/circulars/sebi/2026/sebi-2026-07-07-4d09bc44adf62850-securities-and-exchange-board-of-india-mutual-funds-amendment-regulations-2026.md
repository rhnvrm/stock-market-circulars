## Summary

SEBI has notified the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Mutual Funds) (Amendment) Regulations, 2026, amending Regulation 42(2) of the SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026 (originally notified on January 15, 2026). The amendment clarifies that mutual funds are permitted to undertake intraday borrowing to address timing mismatches between the outflow and inflow of funds in a scheme's account, subject to conditions specified by SEBI. The amendment was published in the Gazette of India on July 3, 2026 and takes effect from the date of publication.

## Key Points

- Regulation 42(2) of the SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026 has been substituted with a new provision.
- The new provision permits mutual funds to undertake intraday borrowing specifically to bridge timing mismatches between scheme outflows and inflows.
- Such intraday borrowing is subject to conditions to be specified by SEBI.
- The amendment was issued under powers conferred by Section 30 read with Section 11(2)(c) of the SEBI Act, 1992.
- Notification signed by Amit Pradhan, Executive Director, SEBI.

## Regulatory Changes

Regulation 42(2) previously restricted certain borrowing activities by mutual fund schemes. The amended provision now explicitly states that nothing in sub-regulation (1) shall restrict mutual funds from intraday borrowing to address timing mismatches between outflows and inflows of a scheme, provided such borrowing complies with conditions specified by the Board.

## Compliance Requirements

Asset Management Companies (AMCs) and mutual fund trustees should:

- Review internal treasury and cash management policies to incorporate the intraday borrowing flexibility.
- Await and adhere to any specific conditions SEBI may separately prescribe governing such intraday borrowing.
- Ensure scheme-level documentation and risk management frameworks reflect this regulatory clarification.

## Important Dates

- **January 15, 2026**: Original SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026 published (Notification No. SEBI/LAD-NRO/GN/2026/294).
- **July 3, 2026**: Amendment Regulations notified and published in the Official Gazette; effective from this date.

## Impact Assessment

The amendment provides operational flexibility to mutual fund schemes by explicitly permitting intraday borrowing to manage short-term cash flow timing mismatches, which can help ensure smoother scheme operations and reduce liquidity-related friction. The impact is primarily operational and compliance-related for AMCs rather than having direct market or trading implications for listed securities.
