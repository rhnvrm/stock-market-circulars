## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority, under the Right to Information Act, 2005, has disposed of Appeal No. 6909 of 2026 filed by Nikhil Goyal against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The authority found that the respondent's reply did not clearly establish whether the requested information was available with SEBI, and accordingly remitted the application for fresh (de novo) consideration with directions to provide an appropriate response within 15 working days.

## Key Points

- Appellant Nikhil Goyal filed an RTI application dated May 23, 2026 seeking information about PACL certificate land allotment data
- The appellant sought: (1) data provided by PACL Ltd claiming land was allotted to a specific PACL Certificate No., and (2) details and documents of the land supposedly allotted
- CPIO responded on June 01, 2026, directing the appellant to the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee for refund-related communications
- Appellant filed the appeal on June 01, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00203) alleging incomplete, misleading or false information
- Appellate Authority found the CPIO's reply inadequate as it did not clarify whether the requested information exists with SEBI

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an administrative RTI appellate order directing reconsideration of an individual information request.

## Compliance Requirements

- CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai must conduct a de novo (fresh) review of the original RTI application
- CPIO must send an appropriate response to the appellant in terms of the RTI Act within **15 working days** from the date of receipt of this order

## Important Dates

- RTI Application filed: May 23, 2026
- CPIO Response: June 01, 2026
- Appeal filed: June 01, 2026
- Appellate Authority Order: June 24, 2026
- CPIO response deadline: 15 working days from receipt of this order

## Impact Assessment

This order has no broader market or regulatory impact. It is limited to a single RTI matter concerning an individual's query about PACL certificate land allotment data. The SEBI PACL refund process continues to be overseen by the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee, and SEBI has been directed only to clarify whether the specific requested information is held by it.
