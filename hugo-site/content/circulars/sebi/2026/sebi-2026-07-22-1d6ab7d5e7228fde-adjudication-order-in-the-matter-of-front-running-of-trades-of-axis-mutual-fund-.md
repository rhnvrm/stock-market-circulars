## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer passed an order against Pace Stock Broking Services Private Limited (PAN: AACCP8410G), a registered stock broker, in connection with front-running of trades of Axis Mutual Fund by certain clients of the Noticee. The investigation covered the period April 01, 2020 to March 31, 2022. SEBI alleged violations of regulations 9(b) and 9(f) of the SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 1992, read with clauses A(1), A(2), and A(5) of the Code of Conduct for Stock Brokers, section 11C(3) of the SEBI Act, 1992, and various SEBI/NSE circulars concerning IP address record-keeping and User ID requests. A key finding was that Pace Stock Broking Services, despite being a Type III category stock broker required to maintain and preserve IP address data of originating systems for algorithmic trading, could not produce complete IP address records for the investigation period.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated trades routed through Pace Stock Broking Services Private Limited suspected of front-running Axis Mutual Fund's trades between April 01, 2020 and March 31, 2022.
- A Show Cause Notice dated February 23, 2026 was issued alleging violations of regulations 9(b) and 9(f) of the SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 1992 and related codes of conduct.
- The Noticee, a Type III stock broker, failed to maintain and produce complete records of IP addresses of originating systems as mandated under SEBI Circular dated November 06, 2013.
- The Adjudicating Officer was appointed on February 10, 2026 under section 15-I of the SEBI Act and Rule 3 of the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995.
- Proceedings were initiated under sections 15A(a), 15A(c) and 15HB of the SEBI Act for the alleged violations.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes were introduced; this is an enforcement/adjudication order applying existing provisions of the SEBI (Stock Brokers) Regulations, 1992, the Code of Conduct for Stock Brokers, and SEBI/NSE circulars on IP address and User ID record-keeping for algorithmic trading.

## Compliance Requirements

Stock brokers, particularly Type III category brokers engaged in algorithmic trading, must maintain and preserve complete system logs, IP addresses of originating systems, user ID mappings, and control parameter records for orders and trades as mandated by SEBI Circular No. CIR/MRD/DMS/34/2013 and related NSE circulars. Failure to do so may result in adjudication proceedings and monetary penalties under sections 15A(a), 15A(c) and 15HB of the SEBI Act.

## Important Dates

- Investigation period: April 01, 2020 to March 31, 2022
- Adjudicating Officer appointed: February 10, 2026
- Show Cause Notice issued: February 23, 2026
- Order number: Order/JS/RJ/2026-27/32501

## Impact Assessment

The order underscores SEBI's continued crackdown on front-running schemes involving mutual fund trades and reinforces the regulatory expectation that stock brokers maintain robust IP address and system log records to prevent misuse of trading infrastructure. For Pace Stock Broking Services Private Limited, the order carries reputational and potential monetary penalty consequences. For the broader market, it signals heightened scrutiny of Type III algorithmic trading brokers' record-keeping practices and serves as a deterrent against enabling front-running of institutional investor trades, though the direct market impact on listed securities is limited.
