## Summary

The Securities and Exchange Board of India has notified the SEBI (Employees' Service) (Amendment) Regulations, 2026, amending the SEBI (Employees' Service) Regulations, 2001. The amendment, notified via Gazette No. SEBI/LAD-NRO/GN/2026/311 dated July 7, 2026, revises the definitions of 'dependent' and 'family members' for medical and LFC (Leave Fare Concession) benefit purposes, and introduces new defined terms relating to employees' personal investments in the financial sector, including 'restricted investment,' 'permitted investment,' and the Office of Ethical Conduct (OEC).

## Key Points

- Redefines 'dependent' to include 'dependent children' (entitled to medical/LFC benefits) and 'substantially dependent members' (family members added to the employee's list for SEBI medical/LFC benefits), excluding persons covered under a separate group mediclaim policy for whom the employee pays a separate premium.
- Redefines 'family members' to include: spouse; dependent children (including step-children and adopted children); any person for whom the employee is the legal guardian and who is substantially dependent; and any other blood relative of the employee or spouse, or relative by marriage, who is substantially dependent.
- Introduces a new definition of 'investment in financial sector' — investment made in, or deposits placed with, assets linked to the financial sector.
- Introduces 'restricted investment' — investments an employee cannot make while in SEBI's service, covering: (i) investment in equity; (ii) investment in instruments convertible into equity; and (iii) investment in or trading of equity/commodity derivatives.
- Clarifies exclusions from 'restricted investment': investments through pooled investment vehicles managed by a professional entity regulated by a financial sector regulator, and investments in units of InvITs and REITs.
- Introduces 'OEC' as SEBI's Office of Ethical Conduct.
- Introduces 'permitted investment' — investment in financial-sector-linked assets that does not fall within the scope of 'restricted investment.'

## Regulatory Changes

Amendments are made to Regulation 3(1) of the SEBI (Employees' Service) Regulations, 2001:
- Clause (f) (dependents) substituted with an expanded definition distinguishing dependent children and substantially dependent members.
- Clause (j) (family members) substituted with a broadened definition covering spouse, dependent children, legal wards, and dependent blood/marital relatives.
- New clause after (j): definition of 'investment in financial sector.'
- New clauses after (z, per Hindi text 'झ'): definitions of 'restricted investment' and 'OEC.'
- New clause after 'ञ': definition of 'permitted investment.'
- Additional clause after 'ट' referenced but truncated in the source text.

## Compliance Requirements

SEBI employees will need to align their personal investment activities and dependent/family declarations (for medical and LFC benefit purposes) with the newly defined terms. Employees must avoid 'restricted investments' (direct equity, convertible instruments, and equity/commodity derivatives) during their tenure with SEBI, except through permitted pooled vehicles regulated by a financial sector regulator, or InvIT/REIT units. The Office of Ethical Conduct (OEC) appears positioned as the body overseeing compliance with these investment restrictions.

## Important Dates

- Notified: July 7, 2026 (Gazette No. 438, New Delhi)
- Effective: From the date of publication in the Official Gazette (July 7, 2026)

## Impact Assessment

This amendment is an internal governance and human-resources matter affecting SEBI's own employees, not listed companies, market intermediaries, or trading activity. It has no direct impact on stock prices, listings, or market operations. Its significance lies in strengthening SEBI's internal ethical-conduct framework by tightening restrictions on employee personal trading in equities and equity-linked/derivative instruments, which may indirectly reinforce market confidence in the regulator's independence and integrity, but does not create compliance obligations for market participants.
