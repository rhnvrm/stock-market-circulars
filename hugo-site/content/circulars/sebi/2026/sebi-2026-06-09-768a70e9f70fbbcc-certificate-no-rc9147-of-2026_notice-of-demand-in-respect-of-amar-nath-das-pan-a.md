## Summary

SEBI has issued a Notice of Demand under Recovery Certificate No. RC9147 of 2026 against Amar Nath Das (PAN: AMNPD2486Q) and Rajesh Kumar Agarwal (PAN: AFFPA4486E), who are held jointly and severally liable. The notice pertains to the long-running enforcement action in the matter of Illiquid Stock Options, in which SEBI found widespread artificial-volume creation through circular trading in illiquid BSE stock option contracts.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9147 of 2026 issued against Amar Nath Das (PAN: AMNPD2486Q) and Rajesh Kumar Agarwal (PAN: AFFPA4486E) jointly and severally.
- The demand arises from SEBI's enforcement proceedings in the Illiquid Stock Options matter.
- The parties are required to pay the demanded amount within the stipulated timeframe or face further recovery action.
- Joint-and-several liability means SEBI may recover the full amount from either party.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is an enforcement/recovery document under existing SEBI (Recovery of Amounts) Rules.

## Compliance Requirements

- Amar Nath Das and Rajesh Kumar Agarwal must pay the amount specified in the Recovery Certificate within the deadline stated in the notice.
- Failure to comply may result in attachment of assets, freezing of bank accounts, or other coercive recovery measures under the SEBI Act.

## Important Dates

- Recovery Certificate No.: RC9147 of 2026
- Notice issued: June 2026
- Payment deadline: As specified in the notice (refer to the original PDF for the exact date).

## Impact Assessment

This notice has no direct market-wide or listed-company impact. It is an individual enforcement recovery action targeting specific persons found to have participated in manipulation of illiquid stock options on BSE. The broader Illiquid Stock Options enforcement programme has involved hundreds of entities and is part of SEBI's sustained effort to deter market manipulation in low-liquidity derivatives segments.
