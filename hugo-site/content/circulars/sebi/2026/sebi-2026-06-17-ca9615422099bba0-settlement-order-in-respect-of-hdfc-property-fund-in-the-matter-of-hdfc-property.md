## Summary

SEBI has passed a settlement order in respect of HDFC Property Fund and its investment manager HDFC Capital Advisors Limited for violations of regulation 24(2) of the SEBI (Venture Capital Funds) Regulations, 1996. The violation pertained to significant delays in winding up two venture capital fund schemes. The applicants settled the proceedings suo motu by paying ₹26,01,000 jointly, without admitting or denying the findings.

## Key Points

- Settlement applications 8629/2025 (HDFC Property Fund) and 8630/2025 (HDFC Capital Advisors Limited) were filed suo motu under the SEBI (Settlement Proceedings) Regulations, 2018.
- Two schemes were involved: HDFC India Real Estate Fund (HIREF) and HDFC IT Corridor Fund (HITCF), both originally constituted with a 7-year tenure starting July 2005.
- HIREF's extended tenure ended June 17, 2014, but asset liquidation and proceeds distribution were completed only on March 31, 2021 — a delay of approximately 7 years. A residual amount of ₹5.33 crore held for contingent liabilities was distributed to investors on March 25, 2025.
- HITCF's extended tenure ended June 28, 2014, and asset liquidation was completed on March 28, 2014, but a residual amount of ₹0.89 crore held for contingent liabilities was distributed to investors on March 25, 2025.
- HDFC Capital Advisors Limited assumed the role of investment manager of HDFC Property Fund effective May 2, 2023, due to internal restructuring within the HDFC group.
- The Internal Committee (IC) of SEBI met with the applicants' representatives on February 10, 2026 to deliberate on the settlement terms.
- The IC recommended a settlement amount of ₹26,01,000 (Rupees Twenty-six lakh one thousand only), payable jointly and severally.
- The applicants accepted and confirmed the settlement amount via letter dated February 17, 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This order applies existing provisions of regulation 24(2) of the SEBI (Venture Capital Funds) Regulations, 1996, which govern the timely winding up of VCF schemes upon completion of their tenure.

## Compliance Requirements

- The settlement is specific to HDFC Property Fund and HDFC Capital Advisors Limited; no broader compliance action is required of other market participants.
- Venture Capital Funds and their investment managers should ensure schemes are wound up and proceeds fully distributed within the stipulated tenure (including permissible extensions) to avoid similar enforcement action.
- Settlement amount of ₹26,01,000 is to be paid jointly by the applicants as per the agreed terms.

## Important Dates

- July 18, 2005: HIREF commencement date
- July 29, 2005: HITCF commencement date
- June 17, 2014: End of HIREF extended tenure
- June 28, 2014: End of HITCF extended tenure
- March 28, 2014: HITCF asset liquidation and distribution completed
- March 31, 2021: HIREF asset liquidation and distribution completed
- May 2, 2023: HDFC Capital Advisors Limited assumed investment manager role
- March 25, 2025: Residual amounts (₹5.33 crore for HIREF; ₹0.89 crore for HITCF) distributed to investors
- February 10, 2026: IC meeting with applicants' representatives
- February 17, 2026: Applicants filed revised settlement terms confirming ₹26,01,000 payment

## Impact Assessment

The impact of this order is limited and historical in nature. Both schemes have been fully wound up and all investor proceeds, including residual contingent liability reserves, have been distributed. The settlement resolves legacy enforcement risk for HDFC Property Fund and HDFC Capital Advisors Limited without admission of guilt. There is no direct impact on listed equity markets, mutual funds, or current investors. The order serves as a precedent reinforcing SEBI's expectation that VCF schemes must be wound up within their approved tenures, and that delays of multiple years will attract regulatory action even when eventual distribution is completed.
