## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority dismissed Appeal No. 6947 of 2026 filed by T Sriramaiah under the Right to Information Act, 2005. The appellant had sought clarification on the circular number governing futures trading in equity stocks using pledged shares as collateral to avoid interest, and whether brokers can charge margin interest on Futures and Options trades even when SEBI-approved stocks are pledged as collateral. The CPIO had declined to respond, stating the queries sought clarification/opinion rather than recorded "information" under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act. The Appellate Authority upheld this position, citing precedent from Azad Singh vs. CPIO, Oriental Insurance Company Limited, and found no deficiency in the respondent's original response.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed an RTI application dated May 31, 2026 seeking information on margin interest rules for F&O trading involving pledged collateral.
- CPIO responded on June 22, 2026, declining to answer on grounds that the queries sought clarification/opinion, not recorded information.
- Appellant filed appeal dated June 25, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00233) on the ground of being refused access to information.
- Appellate Authority relied on the Central Information Commission's ruling in Azad Singh vs. Oriental Insurance Company Limited (March 23, 2021), which held that CPIOs are not obligated to create, interpret, or compile information, or provide opinions/advice not already on record.
- Appeal dismissed; no deficiency found in the CPIO's original response.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an adjudicatory order under the RTI Act and does not introduce or amend any SEBI regulation, circular, or market policy.

## Compliance Requirements

Not applicable to market participants, brokers, or listed entities. This order pertains solely to the appellant's individual RTI request and SEBI's internal RTI response process.

## Important Dates

- May 31, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- June 22, 2026: CPIO response issued.
- June 25, 2026: Appeal filed by appellant.
- July 2026: Appellate Authority order issued dismissing the appeal.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no bearing on listed companies, market operations, or trading practices. It affirms existing RTI Act jurisprudence that public authorities are not required to provide opinions or interpretations beyond information already on record, and does not alter or clarify SEBI's actual rules on broker margin charges or collateral-based interest exemptions.
