## Summary

SEBI has issued an order under Section 12(3) of the SEBI Act, 1992 read with Regulation 30A of the SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008 against 34 SEBI-registered Investment Advisers (Noticees No. 1 to 34). The order arises from these entities' failure to comply with the requirement under Clause 3 of the Second Schedule to the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013, which mandates that every registered Investment Adviser pay a renewal fee every five years from the date of grant of registration in order to keep the registration in force.

## Key Points

- 34 Investment Advisers, individually and corporately registered with SEBI under Section 12(1) of the SEBI Act and the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013, are named as Noticees in the order.
- Noticees include individuals and entities such as Aishva Financial Services Private Limited, Ajay Chouhan, Algo Systems, Capitalvia Global Research Ltd., Prosper Research and Investment Advisors, Tradecure Financial Research, and others, each identified by their SEBI registration number and PAN.
- The order is grounded in the failure of these Noticees to pay the mandatory five-year renewal fee required to keep their Investment Adviser registration in force.
- Action has been initiated under Section 12(3) of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with Regulation 30A of the SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008, which empowers SEBI to suspend or cancel a certificate of registration for non-compliance.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. The order enforces existing requirements under Clause 3 of the Second Schedule to the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013, concerning periodic renewal fee payment.

## Compliance Requirements

- Registered Investment Advisers must pay the prescribed renewal fee every five years from the date of grant of registration to maintain their SEBI registration.
- Failure to pay the renewal fee exposes an Investment Adviser to regulatory action under Section 12(3) of the SEBI Act read with Regulation 30A of the SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008, which may include suspension or cancellation of the certificate of registration.

## Important Dates

The order does not specify individual renewal due dates for each of the 34 Noticees; renewal fee obligations are tied to each Noticee's respective five-year registration anniversary date.

## Impact Assessment

The order primarily affects the 34 named Investment Advisers, whose SEBI registration status is now subject to regulatory action for non-payment of renewal fees. Clients and investors engaged with these advisers should verify the current registration status of their adviser. The order has no direct impact on listed securities, trading activity, or broader market operations, but underscores SEBI's continued enforcement of compliance requirements among registered intermediaries.
