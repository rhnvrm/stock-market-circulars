## Summary

SEBI has issued a General Remittance Order (GRO) dated June 01, 2026 under Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 8756 of 2025 against Golden Syndicate Ventures Pvt. Ltd. The order arises from unregistered investment advisory activities conducted under the brand 'Baap of Chart'. The GRO directs banks and/or depositories to debit and remit outstanding dues payable to SEBI from the accounts of the defaulter entity.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. 8756 of 2025 issued against Golden Syndicate Ventures Pvt. Ltd
- Entity operated as an unregistered investment adviser under the 'Baap of Chart' brand
- General Remittance Order dated June 01, 2026 directs compulsory recovery of SEBI-imposed dues
- Banks/depositories are instructed to debit accounts of the defaulter and remit funds to SEBI
- Action falls under SEBI's enforcement and recovery proceedings framework

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This order is an enforcement step under the existing SEBI (Recovery of Amounts) Rules and relevant provisions of the SEBI Act, 1992, which empower SEBI to issue recovery certificates and remittance orders against defaulters who fail to pay adjudicated penalties or disgorgement amounts.

## Compliance Requirements

- Banks holding accounts of Golden Syndicate Ventures Pvt. Ltd are required to comply with the remittance direction and debit the specified amount
- Depositories may be directed to attach and remit securities/funds held in demat accounts of the defaulter
- Non-compliance with a SEBI General Remittance Order by a bank or depository can attract regulatory action
- The defaulter entity must ensure no further investment advisory activity is conducted without obtaining SEBI registration

## Important Dates

- Recovery Certificate Date: 2025 (RC No. 8756 of 2025)
- General Remittance Order Date: June 01, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order has limited direct market impact but is significant from an investor protection standpoint. 'Baap of Chart' was an unregistered entity providing investment advice, likely through social media platforms, exposing retail investors to unregulated guidance. SEBI's enforcement through the GRO mechanism signals continued regulatory vigilance against finfluencers and unregistered advisers. The action reinforces SEBI's broader crackdown on unregistered investment advisory activity in the digital/social media space. Retail investors who followed advice from such unregistered entities should be aware of the associated risks and verify adviser registration on the SEBI SCORES/intermediary portal.
