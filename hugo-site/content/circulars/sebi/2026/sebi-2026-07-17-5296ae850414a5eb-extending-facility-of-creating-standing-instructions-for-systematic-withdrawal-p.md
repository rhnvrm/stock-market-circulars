## Summary

SEBI has issued a circular extending the facility of creating standing instructions for Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) and Systematic Transfer Plan (STP) to Mutual Fund units held in demat form. Previously, this facility was available only for units held in non-demat/statement-of-account form through Mutual Funds or RTAs. Based on representations from Depositories, recommendations of a SEBI-constituted Working Group, and the Secondary Market Advisory Committee, SEBI has decided to extend this facility to demat-held units to improve ease of doing business for investors.

## Key Points

- SWP allows investors to redeem a specified number of units or amount periodically via standing instructions with a Mutual Fund or its RTA.
- STP allows investors to transfer investments from one scheme to another scheme of the same Mutual Fund via periodic redemption and subscription.
- This facility will now be extended to units held in demat form, which was previously unavailable.
- Implementation will occur in two phases: Phase-I for "Unit-based SWP/STP" (fixed number of units) and Phase-II for "Amount-based SWP/STP" (fixed amount payout or purchase).
- Depositories are designated as the nodal facilitators for implementing this framework.

## Regulatory Changes

- Depositories, Stock Exchanges, RTAs, Depository Participants, Mutual Funds/AMCs, and AMFI are all addressed as stakeholders in this circular.
- Depositories must jointly publish a standard operating framework on their website.
- Depositories must amend relevant bye-laws, rules, and regulations as necessary to implement the framework.
- System changes must be carried out by Depositories to support the new facility.
- Issued under Section 11(1) of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with Section 26(3) of the Depositories Act, 1996, and Regulation 97 of the SEBI (Depositories and Participants) Regulations, 2018.

## Compliance Requirements

- Depositories must publish the standard operational framework for SWP/STP standing instructions on their websites by October 31, 2026.
- Depositories must implement Phase-I (Unit-based SWP/STP) by January 31, 2027.
- Depositories must implement Phase-II (Amount-based SWP/STP) by April 30, 2027.
- Depositories must disseminate the provisions of this circular on their websites and make necessary bye-law/rule amendments and system changes.

## Important Dates

- October 31, 2026: Deadline for Depositories to jointly publish the standard operational framework.
- January 31, 2027: Deadline for implementation of Phase-I (Unit-based SWP/STP).
- April 30, 2027: Deadline for implementation of Phase-II (Amount-based SWP/STP).
- Circular effective immediately from date of issue (July 17, 2026).

## Impact Assessment

This circular is primarily an operational/process enhancement rather than a market-moving regulatory change. It benefits mutual fund investors who hold units in demat form by giving them the same convenience of automated withdrawal/transfer instructions previously available only to non-demat unit holders. Depositories, RTAs, DPs, and AMCs will need to undertake system and process changes to comply with the phased timeline, but there is no direct impact on listed company stock prices or trading activity.
