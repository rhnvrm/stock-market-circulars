## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9147 of 2026 and initiated Attachment Proceedings (A.P.) No. 15531 of 2026 against two individuals, Amar Nath Das (PAN: AMNPD2486Q) and Rajesh Kumar Agarwal (PAN: AFFPA4486E), in connection with the illiquid stock options matter. The document is a scanned recovery/attachment notice issued under SEBI's powers of recovery (typically under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, read with the Second and Third Schedules to the Income Tax Act, 1961, and the Income Tax (Certificate Proceedings) Rules, 1962), used to recover monetary penalties or dues that noticees have failed to pay following adjudication in cases involving non-genuine/manipulative trading in illiquid stock options on the exchanges.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9147 of 2026 has been issued against the named noticees.
- Attachment Proceedings No. 15531 of 2026 have been initiated to recover outstanding dues.
- Noticees: Amar Nath Das (PAN: AMNPD2486Q) and Rajesh Kumar Agarwal (PAN: AFFPA4486E).
- The underlying case relates to trading in illiquid stock options, a category SEBI has repeatedly flagged for reversal/synchronized trades used to generate artificial profits or losses.
- The source document is a scanned image-based PDF; granular figures (penalty amount, attached assets/bank accounts, prior order references) could not be extracted via OCR and should be verified against the original PDF.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual enforcement action, not a rule or policy change.

## Compliance Requirements

The noticees are required to settle the outstanding recovery amount specified in the certificate; failure to comply can lead to attachment of bank accounts, movable/immovable property, or other recovery measures available to the Recovery Officer under the applicable rules.

The action is limited to the two named individuals and does not directly affect any listed company, trading in specific securities, or broader market operations. Its relevance lies in ongoing SEBI enforcement against illiquid stock options manipulation, serving as a deterrent signal to market participants engaged in similar conduct.
