## Summary

SEBI has issued an ex-parte interim order under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11B(1) and 11D of the SEBI Act, 1992 against Surabhi Chauhan, Zahin Ismail Jessani, Stark Investments, Starkblue Ventures LLP and Shakuntala Davendra Singh. The order follows examination of 16 unique complaints alleging that these entities were carrying out unregistered Investment Advisory (UIA) and unregistered Portfolio Management Services (UPMS) activities without the mandatory SEBI registration required under the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013 and the SEBI (Portfolio Managers) Regulations, 2020.

## Key Points

- Stark Investments is a partnership firm of Zahin Ismail Jessani and Shakuntala Davendra Singh (mother of Surabhi Chauhan), each holding 50% controlling interest.
- Surabhi Chauhan and Zahin Ismail Jessani are designated partners and authorised signatories of Starkblue Ventures LLP.
- Investors were solicited through Investment Advisory Agreements executed via Stark Investments, while unregistered portfolio management activities were allegedly conducted directly by the individuals and/or through Stark Investments and Starkblue Ventures LLP.
- Investors were reportedly lured with claims of proprietary quantitative models, algorithm-based trading systems, stringent risk-management protocols and assured downside protection.
- Quarterly performance reports claiming returns superior to benchmarks such as the Nifty-50 were allegedly shared with investors.
- Investment recommendations were communicated through WhatsApp chats, Excel spreadsheets and performance reports, creating an impression of a formalised advisory business.
- SEBI is examining potential violations of the SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices Relating to Securities Market) Regulations, 2003 (PFUTP Regulations) in addition to the IA and PMS Regulations.

## Regulatory Changes

This is an enforcement action rather than a new regulation. SEBI has exercised its emergency/interim powers under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11B(1) and 11D of the SEBI Act to restrain the named entities from continuing unregistered advisory and portfolio management activities pending full investigation.

## Compliance Requirements

Entities offering investment advice or portfolio management services to the public must obtain and maintain valid SEBI registration under the IA Regulations, 2013 or the PMS Regulations, 2020, as applicable. Any entity found soliciting client funds or providing securities recommendations for a fee without such registration is liable to enforcement action, including ex-parte interim orders restraining market access and further activity.

## Important Dates

The order is dated based on the reference number WTM/AS/WRO/WRO-DIV-3/32505/2026-27, issued in July 2026. Specific compliance deadlines or hearing dates for the named entities were not detailed in the excerpted content.

## Impact Assessment

The order directly affects the named individuals and entities (Stark Investments, Starkblue Ventures LLP, and associated individuals) rather than any listed company or broader market segment, so direct market impact is limited. However, it reinforces SEBI's continued crackdown on unregistered investment advisory and portfolio management schemes operating via informal channels like WhatsApp and social platforms, signaling heightened enforcement risk for similar unregistered operators and reminding investors to verify SEBI registration before engaging advisory or PMS services.
