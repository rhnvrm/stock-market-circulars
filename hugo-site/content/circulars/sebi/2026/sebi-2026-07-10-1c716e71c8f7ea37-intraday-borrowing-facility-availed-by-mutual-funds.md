## Summary

SEBI, through Circular HO/(92)2026-IMD-POD-2/I/16006/2026 dated July 10, 2026, has permitted mutual funds to avail intraday borrowings to address liquidity mismatches arising from differences in market settlement timings. This follows a Gazette Notification (CG-MH-E-07072026-274229 dated July 3, 2026) amending the SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026. The circular supersedes the borrowing guidelines in clause 5.9.1 of the SEBI Master Circular for Mutual Funds dated March 20, 2026, and the earlier circular dated March 25, 2026, and lays down specific conditions governing the purpose, quantum, responsibility, and cost of such intraday borrowings.

## Key Points

- Mutual Funds may avail intraday borrowings for: unitholder pay-outs (redemptions, IDCW pay-outs, interest, etc.), pay-in for scheme investments, MTM obligations and forex settlements, and repayment of existing borrowings.
- Quantum of intraday borrowing is limited to guaranteed receivables (e.g., inflows from RBI, Clearing Corporations, subscription inflows) and non-guaranteed receivables expected by end of day (e.g., maturity proceeds, secondary market settlements from NCDs, CPs, CDs, OTC Swaps).
- AMCs may avail additional intraday borrowing solely for meeting redemption and other pay-outs to unitholders under Regulation 42(1) of SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026.
- AMCs are responsible for ensuring intraday borrowings are repaid by end of day, and that any conversion to overnight borrowings stays within regulatory limits and permitted purposes.
- Boards of AMCs and Trustees must approve a policy for use of the intraday borrowing facility, covering approval processes and monitoring mechanisms; the policy must be published on the AMC website.
- AMCs must maintain scheme-wise records detailing the underlying liquidity mismatch and expected source of repayment for each intraday borrowing.
- AMCs must ensure compliance with clauses 6 and 7 of the Fourth Schedule of SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026, and para 17.7 of the Master Circular.
- Cost of intraday borrowing, and any loss/cost from unforeseen events or delays in receiving guaranteed/non-guaranteed receivables, shall be borne by the AMC (not the scheme).

## Regulatory Changes

This circular implements amendments to the SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026 (via Gazette Notification no. CG-MH-E-07072026-274229 dated July 3, 2026) that permit intraday borrowings by mutual funds. It supersedes the borrowing guidelines in clause 5.9.1 of the SEBI Master Circular for Mutual Funds (March 20, 2026) and SEBI Circular no. HO/(92)2026-IMD-POD-2/I/7885/2026 (March 25, 2026). The circular is issued under Section 11(1) of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with Regulation 42(2) and Regulation 84 of the SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026.

## Compliance Requirements

- AMC Boards and Trustees must formally approve an intraday borrowing policy and publish it on the AMC website.
- AMCs must maintain scheme-wise documentation of liquidity mismatches and expected repayment sources for each intraday borrowing instance.
- AMCs must ensure repayment of intraday borrowings by end of day and adherence to regulatory limits if converted to overnight borrowings.
- AMCs must comply with Fourth Schedule clauses 6 and 7 and para 17.7 of the Master Circular.
- AMCs must absorb all costs and unforeseen losses related to intraday borrowing rather than passing them to the scheme/unitholders.

## Important Dates

- July 3, 2026: Gazette Notification amending SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 2026 to permit intraday borrowings.
- July 10, 2026: Date of this circular.
- September 1, 2026: Effective date of the circular.

## Impact Assessment

The circular provides mutual funds with a formal, regulated mechanism to manage short-term liquidity mismatches caused by differing settlement timings, potentially reducing forced asset sales or delayed payouts to unitholders. Operationally, AMCs will need to establish board/trustee-approved policies, monitoring frameworks, and scheme-wise record-keeping ahead of the September 1, 2026 effective date. Since borrowing costs and related losses must be borne by the AMC rather than the scheme, this protects unitholder returns but adds a cost/compliance burden on AMCs. The impact is industry-wide across all mutual funds and AMCs rather than specific to individual listed stocks.
