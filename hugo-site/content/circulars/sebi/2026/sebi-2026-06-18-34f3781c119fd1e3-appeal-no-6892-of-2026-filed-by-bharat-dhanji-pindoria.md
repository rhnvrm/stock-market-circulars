## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 disposed of Appeal No. 6892 of 2026 filed by Bharat Dhanji Pindoria against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought details on PACL policy statuses and refund information. The Appellate Authority upheld the CPIO's response, finding no deficiency, as the requested information is not available with SEBI.

## Key Points

- Appellant Bharat Dhanji Pindoria filed an RTI application on May 15, 2026 seeking information about specific PACL policy numbers, portal remarks, and refund circulars issued by the R.M. Lodha Committee.
- SEBI's CPIO responded on May 20, 2026, stating that all communication regarding PACL refund status is handled by Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee and directed the appellant to approach the Committee.
- The appellant filed the appeal on May 21, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00191) on grounds of refusal of access to information.
- The Appellate Authority found no deficiency in the CPIO's response, citing CIC precedent (Sh. Pattipati Rama Murthy vs. CPIO, SEBI, July 8, 2013) that SEBI cannot provide information it does not possess.
- Responsibility for PACL property disposal and investor repayment lies with the R.M. Lodha Committee, not SEBI.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an administrative RTI appellate order.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. The appellant is advised to approach the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee directly for PACL refund-related queries. SEBI's website contains relevant PACL public notices, press releases, status reports, and FAQs.

## Important Dates

- **May 15, 2026**: RTI application filed by appellant.
- **May 20, 2026**: CPIO, SEBI responded to the RTI application.
- **May 21, 2026**: Appellant filed the first appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00191).

## Impact Assessment

No market or operational impact. This is a routine RTI appellate order affecting only the individual appellant. The order reaffirms SEBI's consistent position that PACL refund administration is the exclusive domain of the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee and that SEBI does not hold the specific operational data sought by PACL claimants via RTI.
