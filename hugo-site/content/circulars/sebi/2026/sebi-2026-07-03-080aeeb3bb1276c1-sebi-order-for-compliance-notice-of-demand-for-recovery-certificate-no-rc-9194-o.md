## Summary

SEBI has issued a Notice of Demand under Recovery Certificate No. RC 9194 of 2026 in the matter of Trdez Investment Private Limited. The notice, issued by SEBI's Recovery Officer, formally demands payment of outstanding dues owed by the entity pursuant to an underlying SEBI order (such as a monetary penalty, disgorgement direction, or other recoverable amount), and initiates the recovery process under the applicable provisions of the SEBI Act, 1992 read with the Income Tax Act, 1961 (as applied to SEBI recovery proceedings) and the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules.

## Key Points

- SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC 9194 of 2026 against Trdez Investment Private Limited.
- The notice of demand formally requires the entity to pay the outstanding amount within the period specified in the notice.
- Non-compliance with the demand may result in coercive recovery measures, including attachment and sale of movable/immovable property, attachment of bank accounts, and other recovery mechanisms available to the Recovery Officer.
- This action stems from a prior SEBI order/direction against the entity that remained unpaid, prompting formal recovery proceedings.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement/recovery action against a specific, previously adjudicated matter involving Trdez Investment Private Limited.

## Compliance Requirements

Trdez Investment Private Limited is required to pay the demanded amount under Recovery Certificate No. RC 9194 of 2026 within the timeline specified in the notice, failing which SEBI's Recovery Officer may proceed with attachment and sale of assets, garnishee proceedings, or other recovery measures under law.

## Important Dates

Specific payment deadlines and underlying order dates are set out in the recovery certificate and notice document; readers should refer to the source PDF for exact dates.

## Impact Assessment

The direct impact is limited to Trdez Investment Private Limited and any parties connected to the recovery proceedings. As this does not concern a listed company's securities or broader market structure, the impact on the wider securities market is minimal. It serves as a reminder of SEBI's ongoing enforcement of recovery against entities with unpaid dues from earlier regulatory orders.
