## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority dismissed Appeal No. 6890 of 2026 filed by Sanjay Kumar Gupta against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought information on whether trading of shares of Mahanivesh (India) Limited (ISIN: INE641D01017) was suspended before March 31, 2013, and the reasons for such suspension. The respondent declined to provide the information, and the Appellate Authority upheld this decision.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed an RTI application dated April 24, 2026 seeking information on trading suspension of Mahanivesh (India) Limited (ISIN: INE641D01017) before March 31, 2013.
- Respondent CPIO responded on May 19, 2026, stating the queries seek clarification/opinion rather than "information" as defined under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act.
- Appellant filed an appeal on May 20, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00187) challenging the refusal.
- Appellate Authority found the queries to be in the nature of seeking clarification/confirmation, not "information" under the RTI Act.
- Reliance placed on CIC order in Azad Singh vs. CPIO, Oriental Insurance Company Limited (March 23, 2021), which held that CPIO cannot create, interpret, or compile information as per appellant's desire.
- Appeal dismissed with no deficiency found in the respondent's response.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a procedural RTI appeal order with no amendments to existing regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. The order reaffirms the existing position that CPIO is not obligated to provide clarifications, opinions, or confirmations under the RTI Act — only information available on record.

## Important Dates

- April 24, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant.
- May 19, 2026: Respondent CPIO replied to the application.
- May 20, 2026: Appellant filed the appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00187).
- June 18, 2026: Appellate Authority order dismissing the appeal.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no market or operational impact. It is a routine dismissal of an individual RTI appeal concerning historical trading suspension information for Mahanivesh (India) Limited. The order reinforces the established legal position that CPIO is only a communicator of existing records and cannot be compelled to provide analysis, clarification, or opinion under the RTI Act.
