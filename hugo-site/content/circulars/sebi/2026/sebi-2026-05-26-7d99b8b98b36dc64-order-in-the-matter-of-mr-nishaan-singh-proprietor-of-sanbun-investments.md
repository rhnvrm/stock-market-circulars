## Summary

SEBI has passed a fresh order under Sections 11(1), 11(4), and 11B(1) of the SEBI Act, 1992 against Mr. Nishaan Singh (PAN: HCCPS6377H), Proprietor of Sanbun Investments and Sanbun Capital Hedge Fund, for providing unregistered Portfolio Management Services (PMS). The order follows a remand by the Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT), which upheld SEBI's original findings but directed SEBI to reassess the quantum of money received towards PMS activity before passing a fresh order on refund and debarment period.

## Key Points

- SEBI's original order dated January 30, 2024 found Mr. Nishaan Singh guilty of violating Section 12(1) of the SEBI Act read with Regulation 3 of SEBI (Portfolio Manager) Regulations, 2020 for operating unregistered PMS.
- SAT upheld SEBI's findings vide order dated December 18, 2024 (Appeal No. 434 of 2024), but remanded the matter for reassessment of the quantum of PMS money collected.
- Total credits in the noticee's HDFC bank accounts (Sanbun Investments and personal) amounted to Rs. 16,19,83,314.61/-.
- The noticee categorised credits into: (i) Course Fees, (ii) PMS activity fees, and (iii) Money neither course fees nor PMS.
- The noticee admitted to collecting Rs. 49,76,127/- specifically as money received towards PMS activity.
- Course fees totalled approximately Rs. 11,88,22,317.30/-, collected via UPI, cash deposits, Instamojo payment gateway, and Paytm.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This order reaffirms existing requirements under Section 12(1) of the SEBI Act and Regulation 3 of SEBI (Portfolio Manager) Regulations, 2020 that any person providing portfolio management services must obtain registration from SEBI prior to commencing operations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Any individual or entity providing portfolio management services must be registered with SEBI under the PMS Regulations, 2020.
- Collecting fees or managing client funds without PMS registration constitutes a violation subject to refund orders and debarment.
- Mr. Nishaan Singh is directed to refund the PMS fees collected (Rs. 49,76,127/- as admitted) to clients along with applicable interest.
- The noticee is subject to a debarment period from securities markets as determined in the fresh order.

## Important Dates

- **January 30, 2024**: Original SEBI order against Mr. Nishaan Singh.
- **2024**: Appeal No. 434 of 2024 filed by the noticee before SAT.
- **December 18, 2024**: SAT order upholding SEBI's findings and remanding for reassessment.
- **April 19, 2025**: Noticee submitted representation to SEBI post SAT remand.
- **May 2026**: Fresh SEBI order passed pursuant to SAT remand.

## Impact Assessment

The order has limited broader market impact as it concerns a single unregistered individual operator. However, it reinforces SEBI's enforcement posture against unregistered intermediaries offering PMS-like services, particularly those attempting to disguise PMS income as educational/course fees. Investors who paid fees to Sanbun Investments or Sanbun Capital Hedge Fund for portfolio management may be eligible for refund. The debarment prevents Mr. Nishaan Singh from participating in the securities market for the specified period.
