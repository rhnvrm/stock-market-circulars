## Summary

SEBI has issued a General Remittance Order dated July 08, 2026 under Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 9101 of 2026, drawn against Nilesh Pramodbhai Shah (HUF). The order arises from enforcement/recovery proceedings related to the entity's dealing in illiquid stock options on the BSE and directs the remittance of amounts recovered towards the outstanding dues under the recovery certificate.

## Key Points

- The order pertains to Recovery Certificate No. 9101 of 2026 issued against Nilesh Pramodbhai Shah (HUF).
- The underlying matter relates to dealing in illiquid stock options at BSE, for which the entity was previously held liable (e.g., for penalty, disgorgement, or other monetary dues).
- A General Remittance Order directs the recovery officer, bank, or other holder of attached funds/assets to remit the recovered amount towards the outstanding demand under the RC.
- This is part of SEBI's ongoing recovery proceedings process for enforcement of monetary liabilities arising from earlier orders.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is an enforcement/recovery action executing an existing recovery certificate against a specific noticee.

## Compliance Requirements

- The entity/HUF (Nilesh Pramodbhai Shah) and any intermediary holding funds/assets on its behalf (banks, depositories, etc.) subject to the remittance order must comply with the directions to remit the specified amount to SEBI/recovery officer.
- No action is required from other market participants, listed companies, or the general investing public.

## Important Dates

- Order date: July 08, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order has a narrow, entity-specific impact limited to Nilesh Pramodbhai Shah (HUF) and does not affect listed securities, trading, or the broader market. It reflects SEBI's continued enforcement of recovery certificates against individuals/entities found liable in past illiquid stock options cases at BSE.
