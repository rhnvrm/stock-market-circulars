## Summary

SEBI has issued a prohibitory order against K.S. Chakravarthi in connection with Recovery Certificate Nos. 2826 of 2020 and 2827 of 2020 arising from the matter of Ranklin Solutions Ltd. The order restrains the noticee from alienating, transferring, or disposing of assets pending recovery of outstanding dues owed to SEBI.

## Key Points

- Prohibitory order issued against K.S. Chakravarthi under two recovery certificates (Nos. 2826 and 2827 of 2020)
- The order pertains to the enforcement matter involving Ranklin Solutions Ltd.
- The noticee is prohibited from disposing of, transferring, or creating encumbrance on assets
- Action taken under SEBI's recovery proceedings to enforce outstanding dues
- Part of a series of similar prohibitory orders issued by SEBI in March 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is an enforcement/recovery action under existing SEBI recovery certificate mechanisms and applicable provisions of the SEBI Act.

## Compliance Requirements

- K.S. Chakravarthi must not alienate, transfer, or dispose of any movable or immovable assets
- No encumbrance to be created on assets without prior SEBI approval
- Banks and financial institutions holding accounts/assets of the noticee are directed to freeze such assets as applicable
- Any third party in possession of assets belonging to the noticee is put on notice of this order

## Important Dates

- Recovery Certificates issued: 2020 (Nos. 2826 and 2827 of 2020)
- Prohibitory Order issued: March 6, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order has limited broader market impact as it targets a specific individual in connection with an enforcement matter. It reflects SEBI's continued efforts to recover dues from defaulters through prohibitory and attachment proceedings. Market participants and entities associated with K.S. Chakravarthi or Ranklin Solutions Ltd. should take note of the freezing of assets. No systemic or sector-wide impact is anticipated.
