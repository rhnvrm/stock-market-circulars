## Summary

SEBI issued a Summary Settlement Order (SO/2026-27/PSD/13) against Everstone Capital Advisors Private Limited, the Investment Manager of Kshitij Venture Capital Fund (VCF), for violating regulation 23(1)(a) read with regulation 24(2) of the SEBI (Venture Capital Funds) Regulations, 1996. The violation pertained to the delayed winding-up of a VCF scheme by approximately 4 years and 8 months beyond the permissible deadline. The matter was resolved through a settlement payment of ₹10,87,500.

## Key Points

- Everstone Capital Advisors Private Limited (PAN: AACCN7541C) acted as Investment Manager of Kshitij Venture Capital Fund
- The VCF scheme had a 7-year tenure (June 30, 2005 to June 30, 2012), extendable by 1 year on Trustee/Investment Advisor recommendation
- The scheme was lawfully extended to June 30, 2013, but was further and impermissibly extended to June 30, 2014
- SEBI regulations required winding up within 3 months of June 30, 2013, i.e., by September 30, 2013
- The last scheme asset was liquidated only in May 2018, creating a delay of 4 years and 8 months
- Final distribution to investors was made on February 23, 2024, after resolution of tax litigation in the Bombay High Court
- SEBI issued a Notice of Summary Settlement on March 17, 2026
- The Applicant filed a settlement application on April 16, 2026 and remitted ₹10,87,500 on April 10, 2026
- SEBI confirmed receipt of the settlement amount and formally settled the proceedings

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This order applies existing provisions of SEBI (Venture Capital Funds) Regulations, 1996 and SEBI (Settlement Proceedings) Regulations, 2018.

## Compliance Requirements

- Investment managers of VCFs must wind up schemes within 3 months of scheme tenure expiry, including any permitted extensions
- Unauthorised extensions of scheme tenure beyond what VCF Regulations permit constitute violations
- Investment managers bear responsibility for day-to-day management and administration, including timely winding-up compliance

## Important Dates

- June 30, 2005: Scheme commencement date
- June 30, 2012: Original scheme tenure end date
- June 30, 2013: Extended tenure end date (permissible extension)
- September 30, 2013: Deadline by which scheme should have been wound up
- June 30, 2014: Impermissible further extension granted by Investment Manager
- May 2018: Last scheme asset liquidated (approximately 4 years 8 months late)
- February 23, 2024: Final distribution to investors (post tax litigation settlement)
- March 17, 2026: SEBI Notice of Summary Settlement issued
- April 10, 2026: Settlement amount of ₹10,87,500 remitted by Applicant
- April 16, 2026: Formal settlement application filed

## Impact Assessment

This settlement has no broader market or systemic impact. It concerns a historical compliance lapse in a single VCF scheme that has since been fully wound up and distributed. The settlement closes enforcement proceedings definitively against Everstone Capital Advisors for this matter. The relatively modest settlement amount (under ₹11 lakh) reflects the limited market harm given that investors did ultimately receive their distributions, albeit with a significant delay partly attributable to tax litigation.
