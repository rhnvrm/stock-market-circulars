## Summary

SEBI issued an order (WTM/AS/WRO/32472/2026-27) under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1) and 11B(2) of the SEBI Act, 1992 against Shree Sai Proficient Financial Services, Proprietor Ms. Madhuri Garg (PAN: CMSPG1119C), for engaging in unregistered investment advisory activities. The matter originated from complaints against a SEBI-registered investment advisor, Sai Proficient Research Investment Advisory (SPRIA), Proprietor Ms. Meeshika Vishwakarma, who is not a Noticee in these proceedings. During examination of a complaint filed by Mr. Shyamsundar Virmani, who paid ₹5 lakhs based on promises of assured returns, SEBI traced the payments (via e-payment provider Easebuzz) to an account belonging to the Noticee, Shree Sai Proficient Financial Services, rather than to SPRIA. SEBI found the Noticee had been sending emails to prospective clients using SPRIA's registration number and offering assured returns, despite not being a registered investment adviser itself.

## Key Points

- Complaint filed on SCORES Portal (January 7, 2019) by Mr. Shyamsundar Virmani alleging non-receipt of assured returns after paying ₹5 lakhs.
- Payments were made for an "equity commitment service" promising 90-95% accuracy and approximately ₹8 lakh profit over 90 trading sessions.
- Payment trail via Easebuzz (e-payment service provider) linked the account to Shree Sai Proficient Financial Services, Proprietor Ms. Madhuri Garg, not to the registered advisor SPRIA.
- The Noticee allegedly used SPRIA's SEBI registration number in communications to prospective clients while conducting advisory activities without its own registration.
- Show Cause Notice dated June 5, 2025 was issued alleging the Noticee provided investment advisory services without obtaining the requisite registration under the IA Regulations.
- Alleged violations span the SEBI Act, PFUTP Regulations, 2003, and SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an enforcement order applying existing provisions of the SEBI Act, PFUTP Regulations, and IA Regulations against a specific entity.

## Compliance Requirements

Entities offering investment advice or portfolio-related services for consideration must obtain SEBI registration under the IA Regulations, 2013. Misrepresenting affiliation with or use of a registered entity's credentials to solicit investors constitutes a violation of the SEBI Act and PFUTP Regulations. Registered investment advisers and the public should verify advisor registration status directly via SEBI's official registry before making payments.

## Important Dates

- January 7, 2019: Complaint filed by Mr. Shyamsundar Virmani on SCORES Portal.
- February 14, 2020: Easebuzz provided KYC and account details of the Noticee to SEBI.
- February 28, 2020: Mr. Shyam submitted payment receipts to SEBI.
- June 5, 2025: Show Cause Notice issued to the Noticee.

## Impact Assessment

The order has limited direct market impact given it concerns a small, unregistered proprietary advisory entity rather than a listed company or major intermediary. However, it serves as an investor caution regarding assured-return schemes and impersonation of registered advisors, and reinforces SEBI's continued enforcement focus on unregistered investment advisory activities and misuse of legitimate registration credentials to defraud investors.
