## Summary

The Appellate Authority under the RTI Act at SEBI disposed of Appeal No. 6928 of 2026 filed by Sanjeev Goyal, who had sought certified documents and details regarding a "land allotted" status shown against his PACL Ltd. refund registration. Finding the CPIO's original response inadequate and unclear as to whether the requested information was even available with SEBI, the Appellate Authority remitted the matter back to the respondent (CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai) for de novo (fresh) consideration and directed a proper response within 15 working days.

## Key Points

- Appellant Sanjeev Goyal filed an RTI application dated June 09, 2026 seeking: (1) certified copy of the document/record basis which PACL Ltd. claimed land was allotted against his registration number; (2) specific land details (plot no., survey/khasra no., location, state); (3) whether SEBI or the Lodha Committee holds any registry, sale deed, or allotment letter signed by him; and (4) the procedure to reverse a wrongly recorded "land allotted" status and claim a cash refund instead.
- The CPIO responded on June 12, 2026, stating that refund-status communications are handled by the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee and advised the appellant to approach that Committee, while noting that PACL-related public notices, press releases, status reports and FAQs are available on the SEBI website.
- The appellant, dissatisfied, filed an appeal dated June 12, 2026 (Registration No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00218).
- The Appellate Authority (Ruchi Chojer) found the CPIO's reply did not clarify whether the specifically requested information (document basis for land allotment, land details, signed registry/deed) is actually held by SEBI, and held the queries had not been adequately addressed.
- The application was remitted to the respondent for de novo consideration and a fresh, appropriate response to the appellant.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes; this is an individual case-specific order under the RTI Act, 2005 directing procedural compliance by the CPIO.

## Compliance Requirements

The CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai is required to reconsider the appellant's RTI application afresh and provide an appropriate, RTI Act-compliant response within 15 working days from the date of receipt of this order (July 07, 2026).

## Important Dates

- June 09, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant
- June 12, 2026: CPIO response issued
- June 12, 2026: Appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00218)
- July 07, 2026: Appellate order issued; CPIO to respond within 15 working days from receipt

## Impact Assessment

This order has no bearing on listed companies, market operations, or broader investor base; it addresses a single investor's individual RTI request concerning the disputed PACL land-allotment refund status. Its significance is limited to RTI procedural compliance and transparency practices at SEBI regarding PACL refund matters, which remain an ongoing area of investor grievance given the large number of PACL depositors awaiting refunds.
