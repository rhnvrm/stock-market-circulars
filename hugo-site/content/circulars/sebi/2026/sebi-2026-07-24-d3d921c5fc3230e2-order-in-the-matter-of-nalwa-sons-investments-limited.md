## Summary

SEBI has passed an order in the matter of Nalwa Sons Investments Limited (NSIL) under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1), 11B(2), 15A(b), and 15HA of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with Section 23A(a) of the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956 and applicable Rules of 1995 and 2005. The order arises pursuant to a Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT) direction dated December 16, 2025 in Appeal No. 291 of 2025, wherein SAT disposed of an appeal filed by the Noticees challenging SEBI communications dated April 29, 2025 and May 29, 2025, and directed SEBI to pass a reasoned order disposing of an application dated February 10, 2025 within eight weeks. The core issue examined pertains to the realignment and reorganisation of investments held by NSIL in Jindal Holdings Limited (JHL) and Jindal Steel and Alloys Limited.

## Key Points

- The order covers 15 Noticees, including Nalwa Sons Investments Limited and members of the Jindal family (Sajjan Jindal, Ratan Jindal, Naveen Jindal, Prithvi Raj Jindal, Arti Jindal, Shallu Jindal, and others), along with related corporate entities (Siddeshwari Tradex Private Limited, Sahyog Holdings Private Limited, Virtuous Tradecorp Private Limited, OPJ Trading Private Limited).
- SAT had disposed of Appeal No. 291 of 2025 on December 16, 2025 by consent of both parties, directing SEBI to consider and pass a reasoned order on a pending application dated February 10, 2025 within an outer limit of eight weeks, while keeping all contentions of both parties open, including the question of maintainability.
- SAT clarified that its order was passed in the peculiar facts of the case at the request of Senior Advocates for both sides and is not to be treated as precedent.
- The matter concerns examination, investigation, and re-examination of the realignment and reorganisation of investments held by NSIL in Jindal Holdings Limited and Jindal Steel and Alloys Limited.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement/adjudicatory order addressing a specific matter involving NSIL and related Noticees pursuant to SAT's directions.

## Compliance Requirements

The Noticees are subject to the findings and directions contained in this SEBI order, which was required to be passed within eight weeks of SAT's December 16, 2025 order (i.e., by mid-February 2026). Specific compliance obligations depend on the detailed findings and directions in the full order.

## Important Dates

- April 29, 2025 and May 29, 2025: Original SEBI communications challenged by the Noticees.
- February 10, 2025: Application filed that SEBI was directed to dispose of via a reasoned order.
- December 16, 2025: SAT order disposing of Appeal No. 291 of 2025, directing SEBI to pass a reasoned order within eight weeks.
- December 18, 2025: Matter assigned within SEBI pursuant to the SAT order.

## Impact Assessment

This order has significant implications for Nalwa Sons Investments Limited and the associated Jindal family members and group entities, given the scale and prominence of the parties involved, including entities linked to JSW Group and Jindal Steel & Power. While the order primarily addresses a specific investment realignment matter within the group's corporate structure, it may carry reputational and governance implications for the involved entities and individuals. Investors and market participants tracking Jindal group companies should monitor for any follow-on regulatory or corporate actions stemming from this order.
