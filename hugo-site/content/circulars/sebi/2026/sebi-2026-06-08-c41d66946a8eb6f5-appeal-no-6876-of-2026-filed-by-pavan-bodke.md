## Summary

The Appellate Authority under the RTI Act, SEBI, dismissed Appeal No. 6876 of 2026 filed by Pavan Bodke against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought information about SEBI Helpline call records from a specific mobile number, but the respondent stated the information was not available. The Appellate Authority found no deficiency in the respondent's reply and dismissed the appeal.

## Key Points

- Appellant Pavan Bodke filed an RTI application on April 15, 2026 seeking SEBI Helpline call logs and call records for a specific mobile number
- The application also sought information on the retention period for SEBI Helpline call records
- CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai responded on May 11, 2026, stating the requested information is not available
- Appellant filed the appeal on May 11, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00173) on the ground of refusal of access
- Appellate Authority Ruchi Chojer dismissed the appeal on June 8, 2026

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an administrative appellate order under the Right to Information Act, 2005.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. The order reaffirms the established legal principle that public authorities are only obligated to provide information that exists in their records and are not required to collect or collate non-available information.

## Important Dates

- **April 15, 2026**: RTI application filed by appellant
- **May 11, 2026**: Respondent's reply and appellant's appeal filed
- **June 08, 2026**: Appeal dismissed by Appellate Authority

## Impact Assessment

No market or operational impact. This is a routine RTI appellate order with no implications for market participants, listed entities, or intermediaries. The order cites established Supreme Court and Central Information Commission precedents confirming that SEBI cannot be compelled to furnish information that does not exist in its records.
