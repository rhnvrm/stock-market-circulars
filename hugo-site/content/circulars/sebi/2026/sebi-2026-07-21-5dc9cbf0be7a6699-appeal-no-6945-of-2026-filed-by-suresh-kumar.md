## Summary

The RTI Appellate Authority at SEBI has disposed of Appeal No. 6945 of 2026, filed by appellant Suresh Kumar against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought detailed information regarding land allegedly allotted against his PACL certificates, including plot/survey/khasra numbers, allotment records, possession records, and ownership documents. The CPIO's original response merely directed the appellant to the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee and SEBI's website for general PACL information, without clarifying whether the specific requested records were held by SEBI. Finding this response inadequate, the Appellate Authority remitted the matter back to the CPIO for de novo (fresh) consideration.

## Key Points

- Appellant Suresh Kumar filed an RTI application dated June 07, 2026 seeking detailed records on land purportedly allotted against his PACL certificates (Nos. U087326xxxx, U087326xxx, U087326xxxx).
- The CPIO responded on June 12, 2026, directing the appellant to the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee, which handles PACL refund-related communication, and to publicly available PACL notices/FAQs on the SEBI website.
- The appellant filed an appeal dated June 23, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00231), alleging the information provided was incomplete, misleading, or false.
- The Appellate Authority found the CPIO's response did not clarify whether the requested information was actually held by SEBI, and thus was inadequate.
- The application has been remitted to the CPIO for de novo consideration and a fresh, appropriate response.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual case-specific order under the RTI Act, 2005, and does not introduce any new regulatory requirements or amendments.

## Compliance Requirements

The CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai, is required to reconsider the original RTI application afresh and send an appropriate, adequately reasoned response to the appellant in terms of the RTI Act.

## Important Dates

- June 07, 2026: RTI application filed.
- June 12, 2026: CPIO's original response.
- June 23, 2026: Appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00231).
- July 21, 2026: Appellate order issued; CPIO must respond within 15 working days from receipt of this order.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct impact on securities markets, listed companies, or trading activity. It is relevant only to the individual appellant and reflects SEBI's internal RTI grievance redressal process concerning PACL-related investor queries.
