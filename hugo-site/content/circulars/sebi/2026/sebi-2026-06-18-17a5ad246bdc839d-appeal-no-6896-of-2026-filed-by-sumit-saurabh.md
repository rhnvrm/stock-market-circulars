## Summary

This is an order by the Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 at SEBI, concerning Appeal No. 6896 of 2026 filed by Sumit Saurabh against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appeal arises from an RTI application dated April 2, 2026 in which the appellant sought information about alleged irregularities involving Omkara Assets Reconstruction Company (ARC), the BSE listing of a reportedly non-operational sugar mill, and related auditor and regulatory accountability.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed RTI application on April 2, 2026; SEBI's CPIO responded on April 30, 2026; appeal filed on May 21, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00190).
- Query 1: Whether SEBI investigated Omkara ARC's claim of ₹3,198 crore in insolvency proceedings against an original loan of approximately ₹21.25 crore, representing ~150x inflation and alleged capture of 99.15% voting power.
- Query 2: Whether SEBI investigated alleged discriminatory treatment — EPFO dues (₹12.58 crore) and Sugarcane Department dues (₹44.52 crore) from the ESIL lease period were rejected by the Resolution Professional, while Omkara ARC's ₹3,198 crore claim from the same period was accepted.
- Query 3: Whether SEBI verified the Information Memorandum used for BSE direct listing in 2014 for a mill allegedly non-operational since ~2015 or earlier, and whether false claims of capacity expansion were made.
- Query 4: Whether SEBI acted on a Motihari district administrative order (December 12, 2025, Memo 3752) that removed 149 acres of disputed land from restricted status and opened it for private builders, raising concerns about asset leakage.
- Query 5: Whether SEBI conducted a forensic audit of accounts certified by M/s BDS & Co. (appointed as statutory auditors on June 13, 2022) given alleged zero operations alongside large financial claims.
- Query 6: Identity of the SEBI/BSE officer who approved the allegedly false Information Memorandum for the 2014 listing based on what physical verification.
- Query 7: Whether SEBI was aware at the time of listing of ₹51.39 crore electricity dues to NBPDCL (power disconnected in 2004–05) and, if so, why this liability was not disclosed to investors.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this order. It is a procedural RTI appellate determination based on the material on record.

## Compliance Requirements

No direct compliance requirements are imposed on market participants by this order. The order pertains to the appellant's right to receive information from SEBI under the RTI Act, 2005.

## Important Dates

- April 2, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant.
- April 30, 2026: CPIO, SEBI responded to the RTI application.
- May 21, 2026: Appellant filed the first appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00190).
- June 13, 2022: M/s BDS & Co. appointed as statutory auditors of the subject company.
- December 12, 2025: Motihari district administrative order (Memo 3752) cited in the application.
- 2014: BSE direct listing of the allegedly non-operational mill.
- 2004–05: NBPDCL disconnected power supply due to ₹51.39 crore unpaid dues.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct market impact as it is limited to an RTI appellate proceeding. However, the underlying queries raise serious concerns about alleged fraud in an insolvency resolution process — including a ~150x inflation of an ARC claim and the listing of a reportedly non-operational mill on BSE. If substantiated through any subsequent SEBI investigation, the issues could have implications for IBC process integrity, ARC regulatory oversight, and BSE listing standards. Investors in the subject company (if still listed) may face risk from potential asset leakage and unresolved liabilities.
