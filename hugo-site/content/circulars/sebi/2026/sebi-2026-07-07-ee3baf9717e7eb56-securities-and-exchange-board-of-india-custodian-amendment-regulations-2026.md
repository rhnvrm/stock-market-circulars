## Summary

SEBI has notified the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Custodian) (Amendment) Regulations, 2026, vide notification No. SEBI/LAD-NRO/GN/2026/308, published in the Gazette of India on July 3, 2026. The amendment replaces the existing annual fee regime for custodians with a monthly fee regime under the SEBI (Custodian) Regulations, 1996. The amended regulations come into force on October 1, 2026.

## Key Points

- Regulation 9(d) is amended to require custodians to pay the monthly fee specified in Part A of the Second Schedule, in the manner specified in Part B of that Schedule, replacing the earlier requirement to pay an annual fee.
- Regulation 26(e) is amended to replace the word "annual" with "monthly" in relation to fee obligations.
- The Second Schedule, Part A, clause (iii): the earlier annual fee of Rs. 10,00,000/- or 0.0005% of Assets Under Custody (AUC), whichever is higher, is replaced with a monthly fee of Rs. 85,000/- or 0.0000416% of AUC, whichever is higher.
- The Second Schedule, Part B, clause (II) is revised to reflect "payment of monthly fee" instead of "payment of annual fee," with corresponding changes to sub-clauses (1), (2), and (3) governing payment timelines and transition arrangements.

## Regulatory Changes

1. **Fee structure**: The annual custodian fee (Rs. 10,00,000 or 0.0005% of AUC, whichever higher) is replaced with a monthly fee (Rs. 85,000 or 0.0000416% of AUC, whichever higher).
2. **Payment cycle**: From the effective date of the amendment, the monthly fee for each month must be paid within 15 days of the end of that month.
3. **New registrants**: A custodian granted registration on or after the effective date must pay a proportionate monthly fee for the month of registration (calculated on a pro-rata/daily basis), and thereafter the full monthly fee for every month it remains registered.
4. **Existing registrants**: A custodian registered before the effective date must pay the annual fee for the current financial year on a pro-rata basis up to the effective date, payable within 15 days of the effective date, and thereafter pay the full monthly fee for every subsequent month of registration.
5. **Adjustment for advance payments**: If a custodian has already paid an annual fee covering a period that falls partly or wholly after the effective date, the amount already paid for that overlapping period will be adjusted against (deducted from) the fee payable under the new provisions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Custodians must recalibrate internal fee accrual and payment processes to a monthly cycle instead of an annual cycle.
- Fee payments must be accompanied by a statement of Assets Under Custody in the format specified by SEBI, along with certification of accuracy (details continue beyond the excerpted text).
- Custodians should ensure timely monthly payment within 15 days of month-end to remain compliant post-effective date.
- Existing custodians need to compute and remit the pro-rata annual fee for the transition period before the effective date.

## Important Dates

- **Gazette notification date**: July 3, 2026
- **Effective date of amended regulations**: October 1, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This amendment primarily affects SEBI-registered custodians' regulatory compliance and cash-flow management, shifting fee remittance from an annual lump-sum to a monthly recurring payment. While it does not directly affect listed companies, trading activity, or investors, custodians will need to update internal financial and compliance systems to handle monthly fee computation, reporting, and payment within the prescribed 15-day window. The impact on the broader market is low, but it is a medium-priority operational and compliance change for custodian entities regulated under the SEBI (Custodian) Regulations, 1996.
