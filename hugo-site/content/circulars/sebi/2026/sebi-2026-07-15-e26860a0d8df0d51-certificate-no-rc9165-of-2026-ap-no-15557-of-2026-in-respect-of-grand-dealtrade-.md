## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9165 of 2026, registered as A.P. No. 15557 of 2026, against Grand Dealtrade Private Limited (PAN: AAECG1630P) for recovery of an outstanding monetary penalty. The underlying default relates to the entity's involvement in the long-running illiquid stock options trading matter at BSE, where SEBI had earlier imposed a penalty under the SEBI Act for reversal/non-genuine trades in stock options. Since the penalty was not paid within the stipulated period, SEBI has invoked the statutory recovery mechanism to recover the dues as if they were arrears of land revenue.

## Key Points

- The Recovery Certificate is issued under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with the Second and Third Schedules to the Income-tax Act, 1961, and the Income Tax (Certificate Proceedings) Rules, 1962.
- It certifies the amount due from Grand Dealtrade Private Limited as a defaulter for failure to pay a penalty imposed in the illiquid stock options case.
- Issuance of the certificate empowers the Recovery/Tax Recovery Officer to proceed with coercive recovery measures, including attachment of bank accounts, demat accounts, and other movable/immovable property of the defaulter.
- This is a procedural enforcement step and does not itself alter trading status, listing status, or rights of any listed security.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an enforcement action under existing recovery provisions of the SEBI Act and the Income-tax Act; no new regulation, rule, or policy has been introduced.

## Compliance Requirements

- Grand Dealtrade Private Limited is required to clear the certified outstanding dues to avoid further recovery action such as attachment of bank/demat accounts and other assets.
- Banks, depositories, and other intermediaries holding assets of the entity may be required to comply with any subsequent attachment or garnishee directions issued pursuant to this certificate.

## Important Dates

The certificate is dated in July 2026 (A.P. No. 15557 of 2026); the source document does not specify a separate compliance deadline beyond the standard recovery proceeding timelines under the Income-tax Act rules.

## Impact Assessment

The action is entity-specific and part of routine enforcement follow-up on a legacy illiquid stock options penalty. It does not affect listed companies, market operations, or investors at large, and carries low market-wide significance beyond signaling continued SEBI enforcement against unpaid penalties in the illiquid stock options matter.
