## Summary

SEBI issued a settlement order in the matter of I3E Trust (as trustee of the Infuse Capital scheme) and CIIE Initiatives (as investment manager), settling proceedings related to a delay of about two years in winding up the Infuse Capital venture capital fund scheme and distributing proceeds to investors. The applicants filed suo-motu settlement applications, and the matter was resolved through payment of a joint settlement amount without admitting or denying the findings.

## Key Points

- I3E Trust launched the "Infuse Capital" scheme with an initial eight-year tenure starting May 21, 2013, extendable by two years, making the extended end date May 21, 2023.
- Liquidation of assets and distribution of proceeds was completed only on March 31, 2025 — a delay of approximately two years beyond the permitted tenure.
- This delay violated regulation 23(1)(a) read with regulations 23(3) and 24(2) of the SEBI (Venture Capital Funds) Regulations, 1996.
- I3E Trust and CIIE Initiatives filed suo-motu settlement applications (Nos. 8749/2025 and 8750/2025) under the SEBI (Settlement Proceedings) Regulations.
- SEBI's Internal Committee recommended a settlement amount of Rs. 10,87,500, computed under Schedule II read with regulation 10 of the Settlement Regulations, payable jointly and severally.
- The applicants proposed to pay the amount jointly, which was accepted by the High Powered Advisory Committee and approved by the Panel of Whole Time Members on June 1, 2026.
- The applicants confirmed remittance of the settlement amount, and SEBI confirmed receipt of the payment.
- SEBI has agreed not to initiate any enforcement action against the applicants for the violations covered by this settlement.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is a case-specific settlement order under existing SEBI (Venture Capital Funds) Regulations, 1996 and SEBI (Settlement Proceedings) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

Venture capital funds and their trustees/investment managers must adhere to the prescribed tenure (including any permitted extension) for winding up schemes and completing liquidation and distribution of proceeds under regulation 23(1)(a) read with regulations 23(3) and 24(2) of the VCF Regulations. Delays in winding up can expose funds and managers to enforcement action, which may be resolved via the settlement process.

## Important Dates

- May 21, 2013: Infuse Capital scheme launched (initial 8-year tenure).
- May 21, 2023: End of extended tenure (after 2-year extension).
- March 31, 2025: Actual completion of liquidation and distribution of proceeds (approx. 2-year delay).
- March 11, 2026: Internal Committee meeting with applicants' representatives.
- March 16, 2026: Applicants filed revised settlement terms.
- May 6, 2026: High Powered Advisory Committee recommended settlement.
- June 1, 2026: Panel of Whole Time Members approved settlement terms.
- June 11, 2026: Applicants confirmed remittance of settlement amount.

## Impact Assessment

The order has limited market-wide impact as it pertains to a specific, relatively small venture capital fund scheme and does not involve listed securities or broader trading operations. It serves as a compliance reminder to venture capital fund trustees and investment managers regarding strict adherence to scheme tenure and timely winding-up obligations, with settlement offering a route to resolve regulatory violations without admission of guilt.
