## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer initiated proceedings against Jitendra Kumar Nahta HUF (PAN: AAEHJ2046M) in connection with an investigation into large-scale reversal trades in the stock options segment of BSE during the investigation period April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015. SEBI's investigation found that 2,91,744 trades, comprising 81.40% of all trades executed in BSE's stock options segment during this period, were allegedly non-genuine and created artificial trading volumes. The Noticee was identified as one of the entities involved in such reversal trades, allegedly executing 6 non-genuine trades in 3 stock options contracts resulting in artificial volume of 58,000 units.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated large-scale trade reversals in BSE's illiquid stock options segment for the period April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015.
- 2,91,744 trades (81.40% of total stock options trades on BSE during the period) were alleged to be non-genuine, creating artificial volumes.
- The Noticee allegedly executed 6 non-genuine reversal trades across 3 stock options contracts, resulting in artificial volume of 58,000 units.
- SEBI alleged violations of Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1) and 4(2)(a) of the SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices Relating to Securities Market) Regulations, 2003 (PFUTP Regulations).
- A Show Cause Notice (SCN) dated August 4, 2022 was issued to the Noticee under Rule 4(1) of the SEBI Adjudication Rules.
- The case saw multiple transfers of the Adjudicating Officer role, with the current AO appointed via order dated June 1, 2026.
- The SCN also referenced the SEBI ISO Settlement Scheme 2022.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes were introduced; this is an enforcement/adjudication action under existing PFUTP Regulations, 2003 and SEBI Adjudication Rules, 1995.

## Compliance Requirements

The Noticee was required to respond to the Show Cause Notice explaining why an inquiry should not be initiated and why penalty under Section 15HA of the SEBI Act should not be imposed for the alleged fraudulent and unfair trade practices.

## Important Dates

- Investigation Period: April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015
- SCN issued: August 4, 2022
- AO appointment (current): June 1, 2026
- Prior AO transfer: August 10, 2023
- Prior AO transfer: September 10, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This order primarily affects the individual Noticee (Jitendra Kumar Nahta HUF) rather than the broader market, given the small scale of the alleged trades (6 trades, 58,000 units) relative to the overall investigation. It serves as a continued signal of SEBI's enforcement focus on non-genuine reversal trades in illiquid stock options at BSE, relevant to market participants engaged in similar options trading activity, but carries limited systemic market impact.
