## Summary

SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9062 of 2026 in connection with A.P. Nos. 15317 & 15318 of 2026 against Dwitiya Trading Limited (PAN: AABCB4470B) in the matter of dealings in illiquid stock options. This action is part of SEBI's ongoing enforcement drive to recover penalties and dues from entities found to have engaged in manipulative or non-genuine trading in illiquid stock options on stock exchanges.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9062 of 2026 has been issued against Dwitiya Trading Limited (PAN: AABCB4470B).
- The proceedings are referenced under A.P. Nos. 15317 & 15318 of 2026.
- The matter relates to SEBI's investigation into illiquid stock options trading, where entities were found to have engaged in non-genuine, manipulative trades.
- Dwitiya Trading Limited is directed to pay the outstanding dues/penalties within the stipulated period.
- Failure to comply may result in further enforcement actions including attachment of assets and bank accounts.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This document is an enforcement/recovery instrument issued under the SEBI Act and the SEBI (Recovery of Penalties) Rules pursuant to earlier adjudication orders against Dwitiya Trading Limited.

## Compliance Requirements

- Dwitiya Trading Limited must satisfy the demand raised under Recovery Certificate No. RC9062 of 2026.
- Payment of the outstanding penalty/dues must be made to SEBI within the period specified in the accompanying notice of demand.
- The entity must respond to the notice and provide proof of payment or contest the demand through appropriate legal channels if disputed.

## Important Dates

- **Certificate Issuance Date:** April 2026
- **Due Date for Payment:** As specified in the Notice of Demand accompanying RC9062 of 2026 (refer to the original document for the exact deadline).

## Impact Assessment

This action is limited in scope to Dwitiya Trading Limited and has no direct impact on broader markets or other market participants. It represents a continuation of SEBI's enforcement actions in the illiquid stock options segment, signaling sustained regulatory vigilance in recovering penalties from entities found guilty of market manipulation. For investors and market participants at large, there is no operational or trading impact.
