## Summary

SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9082 of 2026 in the matter of Adjudication Proceeding (A.P.) No. 15460 of 2026 against Tavarya Trade-Link Private Limited (PAN: AACCT5851H). This certificate is issued in connection with violations related to illiquid stock options trading on Indian exchanges. The recovery certificate authorises SEBI to recover the penalty amount imposed through adjudication proceedings as arrears of land revenue.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9082 of 2026 issued against Tavarya Trade-Link Private Limited
- PAN of the noticee: AACCT5851H
- Adjudication Proceeding reference: A.P. No. 15460 of 2026
- Matter pertains to trading in illiquid stock options
- Certificate enables coercive recovery of adjudication penalty as per SEBI Act provisions
- Part of SEBI's ongoing enforcement actions against entities involved in illiquid F&O segment manipulations

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This document is an enforcement instrument—a recovery certificate—issued under the existing provisions of the SEBI Act, 1992, enabling recovery of dues from a penalised entity.

## Compliance Requirements

- Tavarya Trade-Link Private Limited is required to pay the penalty amount specified in the recovery certificate forthwith.
- Failure to comply enables SEBI to recover the amount as arrears of land revenue through the designated authority.
- Other market participants trading in illiquid options segments should ensure their transactions are bona fide and not structured to manipulate prices or create artificial volumes.

## Important Dates

- Certificate Date: June 15, 2026
- A.P. No. 15460 of 2026 (adjudication proceeding reference year: 2026)

## Impact Assessment

This recovery certificate has limited direct market impact as it targets a single private limited company. However, it is part of SEBI's sustained enforcement drive against illiquid stock options manipulation, which has historically been used for tax evasion and circular trading schemes. The broader series of such enforcement actions reinforces regulatory deterrence in the F&O segment, particularly for illiquid contracts. Market participants and brokers facilitating trading in illiquid options should review their client activity to avoid regulatory scrutiny.
