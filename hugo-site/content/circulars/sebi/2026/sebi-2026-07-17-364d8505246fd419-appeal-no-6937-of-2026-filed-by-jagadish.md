## Summary

This order is issued by the Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 in Appeal No. 6937 of 2026, filed by an appellant named Jagadish against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The underlying RTI application (transferred from the Department of Economic Affairs to SEBI on May 25, 2026) raised questions about SEBI's enforcement and recovery proceedings in the matter of SMS Techsoft (India) Ltd, including allegations involving MNCL, Kotak Mahindra Bank, and a disputed 'legal heir' entry attributed to the appellant in SEBI's records concerning Late Mahesh Chunnilal Shah. The respondent replied to the application on June 19, 2026, and the appellant filed the present appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00225) the same day. The Appellate Authority states the matter can be decided on the basis of material already on record.

## Key Points

- The appellant's RTI application posed three main queries: (1) why no fines were imposed on MNCL despite alleged real culpability; (2) details of a Kotak Mahindra Bank account (No. 67301002xxxx) allegedly opened via forged identity documents, including who opened/operated the account and why no RBI/SEBI action was taken against the bank for KYC failure; and (3) the source document/basis for SEBI recording the appellant as the 'legal heir' of Late Mahesh Chunnilal Shah.
- The appellant referenced a prior Appellate Authority order (AA Order 6812/2026 dated April 23, 2026) that reportedly remitted the parentage/legal-heir query, on the basis that SEBI's earlier refusal to disclose was unjustified.
- The appellant sought wide-ranging relief beyond the scope of the RTI Act, including: an immediate stay on recovery/auction proceedings; refund of Rs. 26.5 lakh allegedly extracted under void orders; return of a Rs. 50 crore property belonging to a non-party (Akil K.J.); penalties against MNCL, named individuals (Anish Shah, Rajesh Ranka, Biren Dhave), and Kotak Mahindra Bank (Bodakdev Branch); expungement of the 'sole mastermind' attribution to the late Mahesh C. Shah; disclosure of the investigation/probe file citing T. Takano v. SEBI (SC 2022) and Siddharth Shankar v. SEBI (Delhi HC, Dec 2025); and compensation of Rs. 900 crore.
- The order text provided is incomplete (truncates at the start of the respondent's reply summary), so the Appellate Authority's final determination and directions are not fully captured in this excerpt.
