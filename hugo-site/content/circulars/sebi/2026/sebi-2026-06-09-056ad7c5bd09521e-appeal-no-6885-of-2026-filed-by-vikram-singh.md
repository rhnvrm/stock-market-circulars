## Summary

SEBI's Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 has disposed of Appeal No. 6885 of 2026 filed by Vikram Singh against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought details about land allotted against PACL Certificate No. U199140471 in his name. SEBI's CPIO responded that the requested information is not available with SEBI, and the Appellate Authority upheld this response, finding no deficiency.

## Key Points

- Appellant Vikram Singh had filed an RTI application on April 10, 2026 seeking information about PACL land allotment details linked to Certificate No. U199140471.
- Information sought included plot details, allotment letter copies, possession/registry status, and certified copies of allotment-related documents.
- CPIO, SEBI responded on May 13, 2026 that the requested information is not available with SEBI.
- Appellant filed the appeal on May 16, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00182) alleging the response was incomplete, misleading, or false.
- The Appellate Authority found no deficiency in the CPIO's response, citing CIC precedent that a CPIO cannot invent information it does not possess.
- Appellant's request to transfer the application under Section 6(3) of the RTI Act was noted; the Authority clarified that PACL property disposal and investor repayment is handled by the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee, constituted pursuant to the Supreme Court order of February 2, 2016.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a disposal order on an individual RTI appeal.

## Compliance Requirements

No compliance requirements for market participants. The order is specific to this RTI appeal and reaffirms that PACL-related refund and property matters are under the jurisdiction of the Justice R.M. Lodha Committee, not SEBI directly.

## Important Dates

- April 10, 2026: Original RTI application filed by Vikram Singh.
- May 13, 2026: CPIO, SEBI responded to the RTI application.
- May 16, 2026: Appellant filed the first appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00182).

## Impact Assessment

No market or operational impact. This is a routine RTI appellate proceeding affecting only the individual appellant. Investors with PACL-related queries are directed to the Justice R.M. Lodha Committee, which has been entrusted with disposal of PACL properties and repayment to investors under Supreme Court oversight.
