## Summary

The Appellate Authority under the RTI Act at SEBI dismissed Appeal No. 6902 of 2026 filed by Rahul against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought information regarding refunds in the matter of PACL Ltd., but SEBI's CPIO responded that the requested information is not available with SEBI. The Appellate Authority found no deficiency in the respondent's reply and dismissed the appeal.

## Key Points

- Appellant Rahul filed an RTI application (received May 02, 2026) seeking information on refunds in the PACL Ltd. matter.
- CPIO, SEBI responded on May 13, 2026, stating the information sought is not available with SEBI.
- The appellant filed an appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/P/26/0028) received by the Appellate Authority on May 26, 2026.
- The Appellate Authority cited the CIC precedent (Sh. Pattipati Rama Murthy vs. CPIO, SEBI, July 8, 2013): a CPIO cannot invent information that does not exist.
- The responsibility for disposal of PACL properties and repayment to investors rests with the Justice (Retd.) R. M. Lodha Committee, constituted under the Supreme Court order dated February 2, 2016.
- The respondent had already provided links to PACL-related Status Reports, FAQs, Press Releases, and Public Notices available in the public domain.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a disposal order on an individual RTI appeal.

## Compliance Requirements

No compliance requirements for market participants arise from this order.

## Important Dates

- May 02, 2026: RTI application received by SEBI.
- May 13, 2026: CPIO responded to the application.
- May 26, 2026: Appeal received by the Office of Appellate Authority.
- June 22, 2026: Appeal dismissed by Appellate Authority Ruchi Chojer.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no market or operational impact. It is a routine administrative disposal of an individual RTI appeal concerning PACL Ltd. refund information. Investors seeking PACL-related updates are directed to public notices, press releases, status reports, and FAQs available on the SEBI website, as well as the Justice R. M. Lodha Committee overseeing repayments.
