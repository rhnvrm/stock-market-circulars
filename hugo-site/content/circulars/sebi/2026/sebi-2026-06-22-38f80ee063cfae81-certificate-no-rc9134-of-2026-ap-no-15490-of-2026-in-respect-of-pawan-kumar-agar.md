## Summary

SEBI has issued Recovery Certificate No. RC9134 of 2026 under Adjudication Proceeding No. 15490 of 2026 against Pawan Kumar Agarwal HUF (PAN: AAJHP1925N) for recovery of monetary penalty imposed in connection with trading in illiquid stock options on BSE.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. RC9134 of 2026 issued by SEBI's Recovery Officer.
- Pertains to Adjudication Proceeding (A.P.) No. 15490 of 2026.
- The defaulter is Pawan Kumar Agarwal HUF with PAN AAJHP1925N.
- The matter relates to alleged manipulation or unfair trade practices in the illiquid stock options segment of BSE.
- The certificate authorises recovery of the outstanding penalty amount as arrears of land revenue under the SEBI Act.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is an enforcement action under existing SEBI (Recovery of Amounts) Rules and the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Compliance Requirements

- Pawan Kumar Agarwal HUF is required to pay the penalty amount specified in the adjudication order.
- Failure to comply with the recovery certificate may result in attachment and sale of assets or other coercive recovery measures under applicable law.
- Banks and financial institutions may be directed to freeze accounts of the defaulter in furtherance of recovery.

## Important Dates

- Certificate issued: June 2026
- Adjudication Proceeding No. 15490 of 2026 concluded prior to issuance of this certificate.

## Impact Assessment

This recovery certificate has limited broader market impact as it targets a single entity — Pawan Kumar Agarwal HUF — for past misconduct in the illiquid stock options segment. It is part of SEBI's ongoing enforcement drive against entities found to have engaged in non-genuine trades in BSE's illiquid stock options segment. Market participants and intermediaries should note SEBI's continued focus on this segment and ensure compliance with fair trading norms.
