## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer issued an adjudication order against Saachi Overseas (PAN: ABQFS9871C) for alleged participation in large-scale reversal trading in illiquid stock options on BSE during April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015. The entity executed 4 non-genuine trades in 1 stock options contract, generating artificial volume of 4,36,000 units, in violation of SEBI's PFUTP Regulations.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated large-scale reversal trades in BSE's stock options segment for the period April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015
- Of all trades in the BSE stock options segment during the investigation period, 2,91,744 trades (81.40%) were found to be allegedly non-genuine
- Saachi Overseas specifically executed 4 non-genuine trades in 1 stock options contract, creating artificial volume of 4,36,000 units
- A Show Cause Notice was issued on August 10, 2022 (ref: EAD-8/AS/AK/42275/1/2022) and served via SPAD
- The case was initially assigned to Smt. Asha Shetty as Adjudicating Officer, then transferred to the current AO on April 3, 2025
- The Noticee was also informed about the SEBI Settlement Scheme, 2022

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This order applies existing provisions:
- SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices Relating to Securities Market) Regulations, 2003 — Regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1), and 4(2)(a)
- Section 15HA of the SEBI Act, 1992 (penalty for fraudulent and unfair trade practices)
- Section 15-I of the SEBI Act, 1992 read with Rule 5 of SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must not engage in reversal trades or any strategy that creates artificial volumes in stock options or any securities segment
- Entities must not execute trades designed to be non-genuine or that result in a false or misleading appearance of trading activity
- Compliance with PFUTP Regulations is mandatory for all market participants trading on BSE and other exchanges

## Important Dates

- **April 1, 2014 – September 30, 2015**: Investigation period for illiquid stock options trading on BSE
- **August 10, 2022**: Show Cause Notice issued to Saachi Overseas
- **April 3, 2025**: Current Adjudicating Officer appointed after case transfer
- **Order No.**: Order/AK/RK/2026-27/32426

## Impact Assessment

This order has limited direct market impact as it concerns historical trading activity from 2014–2015. However, it reflects SEBI's sustained enforcement posture against illiquid stock options manipulation on BSE — a broad investigation that found over 81% of segment trades to be non-genuine. The action reinforces that SEBI will pursue enforcement proceedings against even relatively small participants (4 trades) in manipulation schemes. Market participants involved in similar historical schemes should note that SEBI continues to actively adjudicate these legacy cases.
