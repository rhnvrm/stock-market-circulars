## Summary

This is an order by SEBI's Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005, in Appeal No. 6865 of 2026 filed by Rajeev Sahani against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appeal arises from an RTI application dated April 06, 2026, seeking information about SEBI's awareness of and response to a WhatsApp-based pre-IPO investment fraud that impersonated IIFL Wealth Management Limited and its CEO Yatin Shah between December 2025 and January 2026.

## Key Points

- Appellant Rajeev Sahani filed an RTI application on April 06, 2026 seeking information about SEBI's handling of a WhatsApp pre-IPO investment fraud involving impersonation of IIFL Wealth Management and CEO Yatin Shah.
- The fraud involved a WhatsApp group of approximately 115 members and was reported in national media (New Indian Express) as early as January 6, 2026.
- The appellant had also filed a separate SEBI SCORES complaint about the same fraud.
- SEBI's CPIO responded on April 28, 2026; the appellant filed this appeal on May 03, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00163).
- The RTI queries covered: complaints received by SEBI from various sources (complainant, IIFL, police, I4C/MHA), IIFL's reporting obligations, SEBI's public advisories on WhatsApp-based fraud, investigations under Section 11C of the SEBI Act, proceedings against unregistered investment advisers, and SEBI's SOP for brand impersonation incidents.
- SEBI's CPIO stated that responses to queries 1 and 2 seek clarification/opinion, not 'information' under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act, and that complaints received are examined confidentially.

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced by this order. It is a procedural adjudication of an RTI appeal.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants arise from this order. The order is limited to determining whether the CPIO's RTI response was adequate under the RTI Act, 2005.

## Important Dates

- **April 06, 2026**: RTI application filed by Rajeev Sahani.
- **April 28, 2026**: CPIO, SEBI responded to the RTI application.
- **May 03, 2026**: Appellant filed Appeal No. 6865 of 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00163).
- **January 6, 2026**: Fraud pattern publicly reported in New Indian Express.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct market or operational impact. It is an administrative RTI matter. However, it highlights a broader investor protection concern: WhatsApp-based groups impersonating SEBI-registered entities (such as IIFL Wealth Management) to solicit pre-IPO investments. Investors and registered entities should be aware of such impersonation fraud patterns. SEBI's stated position—that complaints are handled confidentially and that action taken is published on its website—reflects standard RTI practice rather than any new policy shift.
