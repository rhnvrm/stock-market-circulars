## Summary

The Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005, at SEBI dismissed Appeal No. 6941 of 2026 filed by Vikram Singh, who had sought information regarding refunds to investors in the PACL matter. The Authority upheld the CPIO's response that such information is not available with SEBI, noting that the responsibility for disposal of PACL's properties and repayment to investors rests with the Justice (Retd.) R. M. Lodha Committee, constituted pursuant to a Supreme Court order dated February 2, 2016.

## Key Points

- The appellant's RTI application (received by SEBI on April 22, 2026) sought information on refunds to investors in the PACL matter.
- The CPIO responded on May 05, 2026, stating the information sought is not available with SEBI, while pointing to publicly available PACL-related public notices, press releases, status reports, and FAQs on the SEBI website.
- The appellant filed an appeal dated June 06, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/P/26/00037), received by the Office of Appellate Authority on June 22, 2026.
- The Appellate Authority cited the CIC decision in Sh. Pattipati Rama Murthy vs. CPIO, SEBI (July 8, 2013), holding that a public authority cannot invent information it does not possess.
- The appellant's request to transfer the application under Section 6(3) of the RTI Act was rejected, since the Justice Lodha Committee has been held by the CIC (in M Shanmugam v CPIO, Pearls Agrotech Corporation Ltd., decided March 14, 2024) to not be a "public authority" under Section 2(h) of the RTI Act.
- The appeal was dismissed, with no deficiency found in the CPIO's original response.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an adjudicatory order under the RTI Act disposing of an individual appeal and does not introduce or modify any regulatory requirement.

## Compliance Requirements

Not applicable. No new obligations are imposed on market participants, listed entities, or intermediaries.

## Important Dates

- April 22, 2026: RTI application received by SEBI.

- June 06, 2026: Appeal filed by Vikram Singh.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no direct market or operational impact. It confirms that SEBI does not hold specific data on investor-wise refunds in the PACL matter, and that queries on repayment status should be directed to the Justice Lodha Committee or the publicly available PACL disclosures on SEBI's website. It is relevant primarily to PACL investors seeking refund-related information rather than to listed companies or trading activity.
