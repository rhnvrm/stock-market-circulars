## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer has passed an order in the matter of a malware attack on Central Depository Services (India) Limited (CDSL) that occurred on November 18, 2022. The attack rendered several servers and end-user computers inaccessible, forcing CDSL to isolate affected systems, disconnect its network, and rebuild operations on a clean virtual local area network (VLAN). Settlements originally scheduled for November 18, 2022 were completed only on November 20, 2022. Following submission of interim and final Root Cause Analysis (RCA) reports by CDSL, SEBI examined the incident and CDSL's compliance with the mandated cybersecurity framework, resulting in adjudication proceedings against CDSL, its then Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) Mr. Rajesh Nadkarni, and its then Chief Technology Officer (CTO) Mr. Amit Mahajan.

## Key Points

- Malware attack detected on November 18, 2022 at 03:00 hours IST, post End of Day (EOD) operations, affecting servers and end-user computers of CDSL.
- CDSL isolated affected systems and disconnected its network to contain the spread of the malware.
- Recovery involved building a separate clean VLAN with scanned desktops and servers, completed by November 19, 2022.
- Settlements due on November 18, 2022 were delayed and completed on November 20, 2022.
- CDSL submitted interim and final Root Cause Analysis (RCA) reports to SEBI prepared by an external forensic agency.
- SEBI's examination alleged CDSL violated multiple provisions of the SEBI (Depositories and Participants) Regulations, 2018, along with several SEBI circulars and advisories on cybersecurity, remote access, and telecommuting.
- The then CISO (Noticee No. 2) and CTO (Noticee No. 3) were alleged to have violated provisions of the Third Schedule read with regulation 27(2) of the DP Regulations relating to individual responsibilities.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this order; it is an enforcement action applying existing cybersecurity and depository compliance requirements, including:
- Clauses 1, 5 and 9 of Part-D of Third Schedule read with regulation 17 of the DP Regulations.
- SEBI's Advisory dated May 18, 2020 on remote access and telecommuting.
- SEBI Circular dated July 06, 2015 (as modified by Circular dated May 20, 2022) on cybersecurity and cyber resilience framework.
- SEBI Circular dated March 22, 2021.
- SEBI Circular dated December 07, 2018.
- Clauses iii(e) and iii(f) of Part-C of Third Schedule read with regulation 27(2) of the DP Regulations (applicable to the CISO and CTO).

## Compliance Requirements

CDSL, as a depository, and its designated cybersecurity officers are required to maintain robust systems, controls, and processes as per SEBI's cybersecurity and cyber resilience framework, including incident detection, containment, remote access controls, and timely reporting/root cause analysis following any cybersecurity incident. This order underscores the need for market infrastructure institutions to strictly adhere to these mandated frameworks and for designated officers to fulfil their individual compliance responsibilities.

## Important Dates

- November 18, 2022: Malware attack detected on CDSL systems (03:00 hours IST).
- November 19, 2022: Recovery on clean VLAN completed.
- November 20, 2022: Delayed settlements for November 18, 2022 completed.
- Order Ref. No. Order/JS/RJ/2026-27/32498-32500 (current adjudication order).

## Impact Assessment

This adjudication order highlights significant operational and compliance risk for CDSL, a critical depository in India's securities market infrastructure, stemming from a 2022 cybersecurity incident that disrupted settlement operations. The order and any resulting penalties could have reputational implications for CDSL and set a precedent regarding individual accountability of CISOs and CTOs at regulated market infrastructure institutions for cybersecurity lapses. Market participants and other depositories/exchanges should note the heightened regulatory scrutiny on cybersecurity compliance and incident response frameworks.
