## Summary

SEBI issued an Adjudication Order (No. Order/MS/BK/2026-27/32477) in respect of Late Padma Singhwani (PAN: BEPPS2291B) concerning alleged dealing in Illiquid Stock Options (ISO) at BSE. The matter stems from a SEBI investigation into large-scale reversal of trades in illiquid stock options on BSE between April 1, 2014 and September 30, 2015 (the "Investigation Period"), which allegedly created artificial volume and false or misleading appearance of trading. During this period, 2,91,744 trades (81.41% of all trades executed in BSE's stock options segment) were found to involve reversal of buy and sell positions between clients and counterparties, and 14,720 entities, including the Noticee, were identified as having executed such non-genuine trades.

## Key Points

- SEBI investigated illiquid stock options trading on BSE from April 1, 2014 to September 30, 2015.
- 2,91,744 trades (81.41% of total stock options segment trades) were identified as non-genuine reversal trades.
- 14,720 entities, including Late Padma Singhwani, were found to have executed such reversal trades.
- A Show Cause Notice dated January 24, 2022 was issued to the Noticee under Rule 4(1) of the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995.
- The Noticee was alleged to have violated regulations 3(a), (b), (c), (d), 4(1) and 4(2)(a) of the SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices) Regulations, 2003.
- A new Adjudicating Officer was appointed on June 1, 2026, following transfer of the case from earlier AO(s).

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes; this is an enforcement action under existing provisions of the SEBI Act, 1992 (Section 15-I) and the PFUTP Regulations, 2003.

## Compliance Requirements

Not broadly applicable to market participants; relates specifically to adjudication proceedings against the named individual (now deceased) for alleged past violations of fraudulent and unfair trade practice regulations.

## Important Dates

- Investigation Period: April 1, 2014 – September 30, 2015
- Show Cause Notice issued: January 24, 2022
- New Adjudicating Officer appointed: June 1, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order has minimal direct market impact as it pertains to a historical case involving an individual entity (now deceased) in the illiquid stock options segment at BSE. It serves as a continuing example of SEBI's enforcement stance against non-genuine reversal trading practices that create artificial market volumes, reinforcing compliance expectations around genuine trading conduct in derivatives markets.
