## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has issued a Notice of Attachment dated July 22, 2026, under Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 9178 of 2026, drawn against Madhu Kumari Bairwa. The recovery proceeding arises out of the matter concerning trading activities of certain entities in the scrip of DU Digital Technologies Limited (now renamed DU Digital Global Limited). The notice is one of several similar attachment notices issued on the same date under different RC numbers against different individuals named as defaulters in the same matter.

Note: The source PDF is a scanned image document without an extractable text layer, so granular details such as the underlying penalty/disgorgement amount, the property or accounts being attached, and specific statutory provisions invoked could not be extracted verbatim. The summary below is based on the notice title and SEBI's standard recovery/attachment procedure.

## Key Points

- The notice is issued under RC No. 9178 of 2026 against defaulter Madhu Kumari Bairwa.
- It relates to a SEBI enforcement matter involving trading activities of certain entities in the scrip of DU Digital Technologies Limited (now DU Digital Global Limited).
- Notices of Attachment under a Recovery Certificate are typically issued when a noticee has failed to pay an amount (penalty, disgorgement, or other dues) confirmed by an earlier SEBI order within the stipulated period.
- The attachment is generally directed at movable/immovable property, bank accounts, or demat holdings of the defaulter to enforce recovery of the outstanding dues.
- Multiple related notices dated the same day (under separate RC numbers) were issued against other individuals in connection with the same underlying matter.

## Regulatory Changes

This is an enforcement/recovery action rather than a new regulatory change. It is issued by SEBI's Recovery Officer under the recovery mechanism available to SEBI (Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with the Second and Third Schedules to the Income-tax Act, 1961 and the Income-tax (Certificate Proceedings) Rules, 1962), used to enforce recovery of unpaid SEBI-ordered dues.

## Compliance Requirements

- The named defaulter (Madhu Kumari Bairwa) is required to clear the outstanding recovery amount to avoid further coercive recovery action, including attachment and possible sale of attached assets.
- Banks, depositories, or other intermediaries holding assets/accounts of the defaulter may be required to act on the attachment notice and restrict operations on the attached property/accounts as directed by the Recovery Officer.
- No compliance action is required from listed entities, market intermediaries, or the general investing public as a result of this specific notice.
