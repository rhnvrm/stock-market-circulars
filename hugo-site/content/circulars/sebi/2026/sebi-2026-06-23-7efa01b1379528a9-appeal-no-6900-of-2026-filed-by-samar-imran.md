## Summary

The Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 disposed of Appeal No. 6900 of 2026 filed by Samar Imran against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought information regarding Hindustan Unilever Limited's (HUL) compliance with the SEBI Master Circular dated February 6, 2026 on transmission of securities, specifically concerning the acceptance of Legal Heirship Certificates. The Appellate Authority upheld the respondent's position that the queries were vague and not specific enough to be disclosable under the RTI Act.

## Key Points

- Appeal filed by Samar Imran (Appeal Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00196) against CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai
- Original RTI application dated April 20, 2026; SEBI responded on May 18, 2026; appeal filed May 26, 2026
- Appellant sought six categories of information related to HUL's compliance with SEBI Master Circular dated February 6, 2026 (Page 168, Point 8) on transmission of securities
- Information sought covered communications between SEBI and HUL, compliance reviews, complaints, and monitoring records regarding Legal Heirship Certificate acceptance
- SEBI (respondent) denied queries 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 as vague and non-specific, not qualifying as "information" under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act
- For query 5 (complaints), SEBI noted complaints are handled confidentially and enforcement actions are published on the SEBI website
- Appellate Authority concurred with SEBI's response on all counts

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a procedural RTI appellate order.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements. The order reaffirms the established legal principle that RTI requests must seek clear, specific information available in the records of the public authority to be disclosable under the RTI Act.

## Important Dates

- April 20, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant
- May 18, 2026: SEBI responded to the RTI application
- May 26, 2026: Appellant filed the appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00196)
- June 2026: Appellate Authority order issued

## Impact Assessment

This order has minimal market or operational impact. It is a routine RTI appellate disposal reaffirming that vague and non-specific RTI queries cannot be entertained. The underlying subject — HUL's compliance with SEBI Master Circular provisions on transmission of securities and acceptance of Legal Heirship Certificates — remains a matter of investor interest but is not addressed substantively in this order.
