## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has issued a Notice of Demand under Recovery Certificate No. 9221 of 2026, dated July 15, 2026, against Oriental Trimex Limited (PAN: AAACO1556G), which has been classified as a defaulter. The notice initiates formal recovery proceedings against the entity for outstanding dues owed to SEBI, likely arising from unpaid penalties, fees, or other monetary directions issued in a prior SEBI order.

## Key Points

- A Recovery Certificate (No. 9221 of 2026) has been issued against Oriental Trimex Limited, identified by PAN AAACO1556G.
- The entity has been formally designated a "Defaulter" in SEBI's recovery proceedings.
- The Notice of Demand is issued pursuant to recovery mechanisms available to SEBI under Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with the Income Tax Act's Second Schedule provisions as applied to SEBI recovery actions.
- The notice requires the defaulter to pay the outstanding demand within the stipulated period, failing which further coercive recovery measures (such as attachment and sale of property, or other recovery officer powers) may be invoked.

## Regulatory Changes

This is an enforcement action rather than a new regulatory change. It reflects continued use of SEBI's statutory recovery machinery to collect unpaid dues from entities that have defaulted on payment obligations arising from earlier SEBI orders or directions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Oriental Trimex Limited is required to settle the demand specified in Recovery Certificate No. 9221 of 2026 within the timeframe prescribed in the notice.
- Non-compliance may result in escalated recovery action by the Recovery Officer, including attachment of bank accounts, movable/immovable property, or other assets belonging to the defaulter.

## Important Dates

- Notice/Recovery Certificate date: July 15, 2026.

## Impact Assessment

The notice primarily affects Oriental Trimex Limited and its financial standing, as it signals active enforcement of a monetary recovery action by SEBI. Market-wide impact is limited, as this is an entity-specific enforcement measure rather than a policy or market-structure change. Investors and counterparties dealing with Oriental Trimex Limited should note the ongoing regulatory recovery action and associated default status.
