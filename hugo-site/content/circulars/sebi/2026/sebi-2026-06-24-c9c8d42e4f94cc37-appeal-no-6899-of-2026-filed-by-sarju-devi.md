## Summary

This is an RTI appellate order (Appeal No. 6899 of 2026) before SEBI's Appellate Authority. The appellant, Sarju Devi, filed an RTI application on May 21, 2026 seeking information about two PACL refund applications that could not be processed because the portal showed land had been allotted. The appellant disputes this, asserting she never received any land, allotment letter, possession letter, or registry/sale deed.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed RTI application dated May 21, 2026 regarding two PACL certificate numbers (U174245xxxx and U17422xxxx)
- Both refund applications were blocked on the PACL refund portal citing land allotment to the appellant
- Appellant denies ever receiving land, allotment documentation, or physical possession
- CPIO, SEBI responded by letter dated May 25, 2026
- First appeal filed May 26, 2026 with registration number SEBIH/A/E/26/00195
- The Appellate Authority is deciding the matter based on available records

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is an adjudicatory order in an individual RTI appeal matter.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements for market participants. The order pertains to SEBI's obligation under the RTI Act, 2005 to provide information held in its records relating to PACL refund processing.

## Important Dates

- May 21, 2026: Original RTI application filed by appellant
- May 25, 2026: CPIO, SEBI responded to RTI application
- May 26, 2026: First appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00195)
- June 2026: Appellate Authority order issued

## Impact Assessment

This order has no broad market or regulatory impact. It is an individual RTI appeal concerning PACL refund processing for a single investor. The case highlights the ongoing challenges faced by PACL certificate holders whose refund applications are stalled due to disputed land allotment records. Relevant only to other PACL investors in similar situations where portal status shows land allotment despite no physical allotment being received.
