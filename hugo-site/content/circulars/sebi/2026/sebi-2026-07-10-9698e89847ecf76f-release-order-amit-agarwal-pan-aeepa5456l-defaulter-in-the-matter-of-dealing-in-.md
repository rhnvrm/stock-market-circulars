## Summary

SEBI has issued a Release Order in the matter of Amit Agarwal (PAN: AEEPA5456L), who had been declared a defaulter in connection with dealing in illiquid stock options on the BSE. The order relates to Recovery Certificate No. 9106 of 2026 and formally releases/closes the recovery proceedings initiated against the individual.

## Key Points

- The order pertains to an individual identified as Amit Agarwal (PAN: AEEPA5456L), previously classified as a defaulter by SEBI.
- The underlying matter concerns dealing in illiquid stock options on the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE), a category SEBI monitors closely for market manipulation risk.
- The release order is issued under Recovery Certificate No. 9106 of 2026, indicating recovery proceedings (such as attachment of assets/bank accounts) initiated earlier are now being withdrawn or concluded.
- This is an enforcement/recovery action document rather than a market-wide regulatory circular.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual enforcement order and does not introduce any new rule, regulation, or market-wide change.

## Compliance Requirements

No compliance obligations arise for market participants generally. The order is specific to the named individual and the recovery officer/authority administering Certificate No. 9106 of 2026.

## Important Dates

Order dated July 2026 (as per document); specific certificate reference is No. 9106 of 2026.

## Impact Assessment

This release order has no discernible impact on listed companies, trading activity, or the broader market, as it concerns the closure of an individual recovery/enforcement action related to a past illiquid stock options case. It is relevant primarily for compliance and enforcement tracking purposes.
