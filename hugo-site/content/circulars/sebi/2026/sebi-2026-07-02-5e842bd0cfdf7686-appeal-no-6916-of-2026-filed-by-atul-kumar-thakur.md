## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 has disposed of Appeal No. 6916 of 2026 filed by Atul Kumar Thakur against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant sought information regarding grievance redressal for a complaint concerning non-convertible redeemable debentures purchased by his late father from Weird Infrastructure Limited in 2012. The appeal was found to be time-barred, having been filed well beyond the 30-day statutory limit, but was nonetheless examined on merits and disposed of based on the respondent's original reply detailing recovery proceedings against the defaulting company.

## Key Points

- Appellant Atul Kumar Thakur filed an RTI application dated February 20, 2026 seeking status of grievance redressal for complaint number UTT25100882 regarding NCDs purchased by his father from Weird Infrastructure Limited in July 2012.
- The CPIO responded on February 27, 2026; the first appeal was filed only on June 06, 2026, exceeding the 30-day limit under Section 19(1) of the RTI Act, with no request for condonation of delay.
- The Appellate Authority held the appeal time-barred and liable to be dismissed on that ground, but proceeded to examine it on merits.
- The respondent's original reply stated that recovery proceedings were initiated against Weird Infrastructure Corporation Ltd. and its promoters/directors via Recovery Certificate No. 826 of 2015.
- Bank accounts, demat accounts, and mutual fund portfolios of the defaulters were attached under attachment proceedings no. 2371 and 2372 of 2015 dated December 4, 2015.
- As of the response date, Rs. 5,60,206/- had been recovered pursuant to remittance advice issued to banks.
- Prohibitory orders dated August 4, 2016 and February 10, 2017 were issued restraining defaulters from disposing of, transferring, or alienating properties.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual case order under the RTI Act and does not introduce any regulatory changes.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance requirements are imposed on market participants. The order reiterates the CPIO's existing obligations to respond to RTI applications within the statutory framework, and appellants' obligation to file first appeals within 30 days of receiving a response, absent a valid request for condonation of delay.

## Important Dates

- February 20, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- February 27, 2026: CPIO's response to the application.
- June 06, 2026: First appeal filed (beyond the 30-day statutory window).
- December 4, 2015: Attachment proceedings (no. 2371 and 2372 of 2015) against Weird Infrastructure Corporation Ltd.
- August 4, 2016 and February 10, 2017: Prohibitory orders issued against defaulters.
- July 21, 2017: SEBI notification/order (SEBI/NRO/RD/Weird Infra/78/2017) directing refund with interest to investors.

## Impact Assessment

This order has negligible market or operational impact. It pertains to an individual investor's RTI grievance regarding recovery of funds from a long-defaulted NCD issuer (Weird Infrastructure Limited), for which SEBI recovery proceedings have been ongoing since 2015. The order confirms that only a partial amount (Rs. 5.6 lakh) has been recovered against the defaulters to date, which may be of interest to other affected NCD holders tracking recovery status, but it carries no implications for listed securities, trading, or broader regulatory policy.
