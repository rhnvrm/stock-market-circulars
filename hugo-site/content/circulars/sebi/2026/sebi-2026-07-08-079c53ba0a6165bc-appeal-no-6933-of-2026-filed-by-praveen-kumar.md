## Summary

The SEBI Appellate Authority dismissed Appeal No. 6933 of 2026 filed by Praveen Kumar against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai under the RTI Act, 2005. The appellant had sought urgent SEBI intervention, inspection, and investigation into Shares Bazaar Pvt Ltd, an entity alleged to have collected investor money on promised returns while falsely claiming SEBI registration, and which has since stopped payments to investors. The Appellate Authority upheld the CPIO's position that such requests for action/investigation do not constitute "information" as defined under Section 2(f) of the RTI Act, and therefore cannot be compelled through an RTI request.

## Key Points

- Appellant Praveen Kumar filed an RTI application dated May 27, 2026 alleging that Shares Bazaar Pvt Ltd collected money from investors while misrepresenting itself as SEBI-registered, and has since stopped payments.
- The appellant stated that a second-level review request filed in SCORES on May 10, 2026 was still pending, and that hundreds of investors and large sums of money were at risk.
- The appellant requested SEBI to: (i) urgently investigate Shares Bazaar Pvt Ltd, (ii) inspect its Hyderabad office and verify investor/payment records, (iii) collect details of all affected investors and amounts collected, and (iv) take immediate regulatory/legal action.
- The CPIO responded on June 15, 2026 stating the request sought "action" from SEBI rather than "information," and thus fell outside the scope of Section 2(f) of the RTI Act, while still providing guidance on lodging and tracking grievances via SCORES.
- The appellant appealed on June 16, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00222) on the ground that access to information was refused.
- The Appellate Authority concurred with the CPIO, citing CIC precedents (Mohan Kumar Sharma vs. CPIO, PMO, 2019; Surendra Vishwakarma vs. President Secretariat, 2016) holding that the RTI Act cannot compel an authority to take or initiate action.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual appellate order under the RTI Act and does not introduce new rules, regulations, or market-wide requirements.

## Compliance Requirements

No new compliance obligations are created for market participants, intermediaries, or listed companies. The order reaffirms existing SCORES-based grievance redressal as the appropriate channel for investor complaints against entities like Shares Bazaar Pvt Ltd, rather than RTI applications.

## Important Dates

- May 27, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- May 10, 2026: Second-level review requested in SCORES (referenced as still pending).
- June 15, 2026: CPIO response to RTI application.
- June 16, 2026: RTI appeal filed (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00222).

## Impact Assessment

The order has no direct impact on listed securities, trading, or market operations. Its significance is limited to clarifying the boundaries of the RTI Act relative to SEBI's investor grievance processes (SCORES). It indirectly highlights an ongoing investor complaint against Shares Bazaar Pvt Ltd (SCORES Complaint No. SEBIM/TG26/HYDE/018161/1) involving alleged default on investor payments and misrepresentation of SEBI registration, which may be of interest for tracking unauthorized market practices, though no regulatory action against the entity is confirmed in this order.
