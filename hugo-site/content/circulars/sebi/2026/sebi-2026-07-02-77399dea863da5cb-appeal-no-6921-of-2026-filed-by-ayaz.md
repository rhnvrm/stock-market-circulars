## Summary

The Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 at SEBI has disposed of Appeal No. 6921 of 2026 filed by appellant Ayaz against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought detailed information regarding a PACL Certificate (No. U00717xxxxx) held in the name of Smt. Tabssum, specifically concerning land purportedly allotted as per PACL's refund portal data, including allotment letters, plot/khasra details, possession records, and eligibility status for refund. The CPIO's response directed the appellant to the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee, which handles PACL refund communications, and pointed to publicly available PACL materials on the SEBI website. Finding this response unclear as to whether the requested information was actually held by SEBI, the Appellate Authority remitted the matter back to the respondent for de novo (fresh) consideration and directed a proper response within 15 working days.

## Key Points

- Appellant Ayaz filed an RTI application dated June 01, 2026 seeking details on a specific PACL certificate's land allotment status.
- The CPIO's June 08, 2026 response referred the appellant to the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee and general PACL public materials on SEBI's website.
- The appellant filed an appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00213) dated June 09, 2026 alleging incomplete, misleading, or false information was provided.
- The Appellate Authority found the CPIO's response did not clarify whether SEBI actually possesses the specific requested records.
- The appeal was disposed of by remitting the application back to the CPIO for de novo consideration.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual adjudicatory order under the RTI Act and does not introduce any new regulation, circular, or policy change.

## Compliance Requirements

The CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai is directed to reconsider the original RTI application afresh and provide an appropriate, clear response to the appellant in terms of the RTI Act.

## Important Dates

- June 01, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant.
- June 08, 2026: CPIO's response to the application.
- June 09, 2026: Appeal filed by appellant.
- Within 15 working days from receipt of this order: Deadline for CPIO to issue a fresh, de novo response.

## Impact Assessment

This order has no bearing on listed companies, market operations, or broader investor classes beyond the individual appellant's PACL refund case. It reflects SEBI's internal RTI grievance-redressal process regarding the long-running PACL land allotment/refund matter overseen by the Lodha Committee, and its impact is confined to procedural compliance by the CPIO's office.
