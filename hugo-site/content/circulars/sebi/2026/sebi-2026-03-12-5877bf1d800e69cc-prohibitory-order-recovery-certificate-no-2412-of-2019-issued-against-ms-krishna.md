## Summary

SEBI has issued a prohibitory order against M/s Krishna Enterprises (Sole Proprietor: K Rajendran) pursuant to Recovery Certificate No. 2412 of 2019. This order arises from proceedings in the matter of Edserv Softsystems Ltd. and is aimed at preventing the disposal or transfer of assets by the defaulter pending recovery of outstanding dues owed to SEBI.

## Key Points

- Prohibitory order issued against M/s Krishna Enterprises, a sole proprietorship run by K Rajendran.
- Recovery Certificate No. 2412 of 2019 forms the basis of this enforcement action.
- The underlying matter pertains to Edserv Softsystems Ltd., likely involving irregular dealings in illiquid stock options or related violations.
- The order prohibits the named entity from alienating, transferring, or disposing of any assets without prior SEBI approval.
- Action is part of SEBI's ongoing enforcement and recovery proceedings against defaulters in the Edserv Softsystems case.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is an enforcement order under existing SEBI recovery and attachment powers pursuant to the SEBI Act and the SEBI (Recovery of Amounts) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- M/s Krishna Enterprises (K Rajendran) must not transfer, alienate, or dispose of any movable or immovable assets, bank accounts, or securities without SEBI's written permission.
- Any bank, financial institution, or depository holding assets of the named entity must comply with the prohibitory directions upon receipt of this order.
- Third parties dealing with the named entity in asset transactions are put on notice of this restriction.

## Important Dates

- **Order Date:** March 12, 2026
- **Recovery Certificate:** No. 2412 of 2019 (originally issued in 2019; enforcement ongoing)

## Impact Assessment

This order has no broad market impact. It is a targeted enforcement action affecting only M/s Krishna Enterprises and K Rajendran personally. The Edserv Softsystems matter is a legacy case involving illiquid stock option manipulation; SEBI continues to pursue recovery from entities implicated in such cases. Investors and market participants at large are unaffected. The order signals continued regulatory diligence in pursuing outstanding recovery certificates.
