## Summary

SEBI has passed a final order in the matter of Hexa Tradex Limited (HTL), following an eight-week directive from the Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT) dated December 16, 2025, arising from Appeal No. 290 of 2025. The case concerns the realignment and reorganisation of investments held by HTL (through its subsidiary Hexa Securities and Finance Company Limited, HSFCL) into promoter group private companies — Danta Enterprises Private Limited (since merged into Siddeshwari Tradex Private Limited), Virtuous Tradecorp Private Limited, OPJ Trading Private Limited, and Sahyog Holdings Private Limited (formerly Sahyog Tradecorp Private Limited) — during FY 2013-14. SEBI's show cause notice alleged this divestment was carried out without following due legal procedure, resulting in HTL's financials not presenting a true and fair view to the detriment of investors, in violation of the SEBI Act, SCRA, PFUTP Regulations, LODR Regulations, and PIT Regulations.

## Key Points

- Order passed under Sections 11(1), 11(4), 11(4A), 11B(1), 11B(2), 15A(b), and 15HA of the SEBI Act, 1992, along with Section 23A(a) of SCRA read with the relevant Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties Rules (1995 and 2005).
- 15 noticees named, including Hexa Tradex Limited and prominent Jindal family members: Prithvi Raj Jindal, Sminu Jindal, Sajjan Jindal, Naveen Jindal, Ratan Jindal, Arti Jindal, Shallu Jindal, along with executives Pravesh Srivastava, Raj Kamal Aggarwal, Kuldip Bhargava, and promoter-group entities Virtuous Tradecorp Private Limited, OPJ Trading Private Limited, Sahyog Holdings Private Limited, and Siddeshwari Tradex Private Limited.
- Matter originated from SAT's disposal of an appeal challenging SEBI communications dated April 29, 2025 and May 29, 2025, with SEBI directed to pass a reasoned order on the noticees' application dated April 24, 2025 within eight weeks.
- SAT clarified its order was passed in the peculiar facts of the case (by consent of both parties) and is not to be treated as precedent; all contentions of both sides, including maintainability, were kept open.
- Core allegation: investments held by HTL through its subsidiary HSFCL were divested to related private companies during FY 2013-14 without following due procedures, misrepresenting HTL's financial position.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement order concluding a long-running investigation and SAT-directed reconsideration.

## Compliance Requirements

The order is directed at the named noticees; specific directions, penalties, or remedial actions (if any) would be detailed in the full order which extends to 75 pages. Noticees should review the complete order for applicable compliance or penalty obligations.

## Important Dates

- December 16, 2025: SAT order disposing of Appeal No. 290 of 2025.
- December 18, 2025: Matter assigned to SEBI following SAT's order.
- April 24, 2025: Noticees' application that SEBI was directed to reconsider.
- April 29, 2025 and May 29, 2025: SEBI communications originally challenged before SAT.
- FY 2013-14: Period during which the alleged investment realignment took place.

## Impact Assessment

This order concludes a prolonged regulatory and legal process concerning HTL and its promoter group, including senior members of the Jindal family. As it involves enforcement provisions covering violations of SEBI Act, SCRA, PFUTP, LODR and PIT Regulations, it may result in penalties, directions, or other regulatory consequences for HTL and the named individuals/entities. Market impact is likely limited to HTL and closely associated group entities, but the order carries reputational significance given the prominence of the noticees involved.
