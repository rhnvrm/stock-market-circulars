## Summary

SEBI's Appellate Authority (Ruchi Chojer) issued an order dismissing RTI Appeal No. 6942 of 2026 filed by Amol Prabhakar Khandave. The appellant had sought information regarding Octate Developers LLP's role as a "Consenting Party" in certain sale transactions of property at Survey No. 10/1 and 10/2, Dhanori, Pune, and details of any amounts remitted from such sales into the Sahara-SEBI Refund Account. The CPIO had earlier responded that no information had been received from Saharas regarding the sale of this property, and therefore the queries could not be verified. The Appellate Authority found the CPIO's response adequate and dismissed the appeal.

## Key Points

- Appellant filed an RTI application dated April 28, 2026, seeking five categories of information related to Octate Developers LLP's involvement in property sale transactions at Dhanori, Pune, and related remittances to the Sahara-SEBI Refund Account.
- CPIO responded on May 22, 2026, stating no information had been received from Saharas regarding the sale of the specified property, so the queries could not be ascertained or verified.
- Appellant filed an appeal dated June 12, 2026 (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/P/26/00036), received by the Appellate Authority on June 22, 2026, expressing dissatisfaction with the CPIO's reply.
- The Appellate Authority reviewed the application, CPIO response, and appeal, and concluded the respondent had adequately addressed the queries with available information.
- The appeal was dismissed with no directive for further disclosure.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an adjudicatory order under the RTI Act, 2005 concerning a specific information request; it does not introduce or amend any regulatory framework.
