## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has passed a general remittance order dated July 13, 2026, under Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 4690 of 2022, issued against Ravi Swarajya Bahrathi in connection with the matter of Venus Power Ventures Ltd. The order pertains to the recovery of outstanding dues (such as penalties, disgorgement amounts, or other sums directed to be recovered) from the noticee and directs the remittance of recovered funds pursuant to SEBI's recovery proceedings.

## Key Points

- The order relates to Recovery Certificate No. 4690 of 2022, issued against Ravi Swarajya Bahrathi.
- The matter arises out of proceedings connected with Venus Power Ventures Ltd.
- The order is a "general remittance order," typically directing that amounts recovered (through attachment of bank accounts, property, or other means) be remitted to SEBI's designated account.
- This forms part of SEBI's ongoing recovery enforcement process against defaulters who have failed to comply with earlier SEBI orders or pay amounts due.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced by this order. It is an enforcement/recovery action taken under the existing framework of the SEBI Act, 1992 (including provisions relating to recovery of amounts under Section 28A, read with the Second Schedule to the Income Tax Act, 1961, as applicable to SEBI recovery proceedings).

## Compliance Requirements

- The noticee, Ravi Swarajya Bahrathi, and any intermediaries (such as banks or custodians holding attached funds/assets) are required to comply with the remittance directions contained in the order.
- Entities holding attached funds or property related to this recovery proceeding must remit the specified amounts as directed by SEBI's Recovery Officer.

## Important Dates

- Order date: July 13, 2026
- Underlying Recovery Certificate: RC No. 4690 of 2022

## Impact Assessment

This order has limited direct market impact as it concerns an individual recovery proceeding rather than a listed company or broad market policy. Its significance is primarily for the specific noticee and any entities holding attached assets connected to the case. It reflects SEBI's continued enforcement of recovery mechanisms against defaulters and reinforces investor protection objectives, but does not affect trading, listing, or disclosure norms for the broader market.
