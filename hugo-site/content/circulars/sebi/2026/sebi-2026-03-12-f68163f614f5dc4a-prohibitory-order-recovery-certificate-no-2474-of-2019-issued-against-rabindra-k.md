## Summary

SEBI has issued a prohibitory order against **Rabindra Kumar Jena** pursuant to Recovery Certificate No. 2474 of 2019, arising from proceedings in the matter of **Rightmax Technotrade International Ltd.** The order prohibits the respondent from transferring, alienating, or disposing of any assets or properties until outstanding dues owed to SEBI are recovered in full.

## Key Points

- Prohibitory order issued against Rabindra Kumar Jena under Recovery Certificate No. 2474 of 2019
- The recovery proceedings relate to the matter of Rightmax Technotrade International Ltd.
- The order restrains the respondent from transferring, alienating, or otherwise disposing of movable and/or immovable assets
- Issued by SEBI's Recovery Officer under the SEBI Act and applicable recovery rules
- Part of SEBI's ongoing enforcement action related to illiquid stock options or related violations

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This is an enforcement/recovery action under existing SEBI recovery proceedings framework.

## Compliance Requirements

- **Rabindra Kumar Jena** must not transfer, alienate, encumber, or dispose of any assets or properties without prior permission from SEBI
- Banks and financial institutions holding accounts of the respondent may be directed to freeze such accounts
- Any third party with knowledge of this order must comply with its directions

## Important Dates

- **Recovery Certificate No.:** 2474 of 2019 (original certificate issued in 2019)
- **Order Date:** March 12, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This order has no broader market impact. It is a targeted individual enforcement action to recover dues from Rabindra Kumar Jena in connection with the Rightmax Technotrade International Ltd. matter. The prohibitory order is a standard step in SEBI's recovery proceedings to prevent dissipation of assets before dues are collected. Impact is limited solely to the respondent named in the order.
