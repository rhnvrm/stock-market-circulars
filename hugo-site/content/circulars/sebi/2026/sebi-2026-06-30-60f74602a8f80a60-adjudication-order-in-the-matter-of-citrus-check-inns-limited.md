## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer has issued an adjudication order against Citrus Check Inns Limited (Citrus) and four of its directors — Omprakash Basantlal Goenka, Prakash Ganpat Utekar, Venkatraman Natarajan, and Narayan Shivram Kotnis — for operating an unregistered collective investment scheme (CIS) in violation of Section 11AA, Section 12(1B) of the SEBI Act, 1992, and Regulation 3 of the SEBI (Collective Investment Schemes) Regulations, 1999. The order follows a prolonged investigation and litigation history spanning SEBI's interim and confirmatory orders, an appeal before the Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT), and a Supreme Court directive to complete the investigation.

## Key Points

- SEBI received a complaint in January 2014 alleging Citrus was running a "Ponzi Scheme" and mis-selling investment products without refunding investor money.
- SEBI's preliminary examination found Citrus was mobilising funds constituting a collective investment scheme without the mandatory certificate of registration.
- An ad interim ex-parte order was passed on June 3, 2015 against Citrus and its four directors, followed by a confirmatory order on August 24, 2015.
- The Hon'ble SAT (February 3, 2016) upheld SEBI's prima facie view that Citrus's business was a CIS but set aside the interim/confirmatory directions, instead directing Citrus to seek CIS registration.
- The Supreme Court (November 9, 2016) set aside the SAT order and directed SEBI to complete its investigation and conclusively determine whether Citrus's business constituted a CIS.
- SEBI completed its investigation on May 4, 2017 covering FY 2011-12 to FY 2015-16, approving Section 11B and adjudication proceedings.
- The National Company Law Tribunal initiated Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP) against Citrus on May 2, 2017, imposing a moratorium under Section 14 of the IBC.
- The Supreme Court stayed the IBC proceedings (January 8, 2018), impleaded SEBI as a party, and constituted a Sale cum Monitoring Committee (SMC), later headed by Justice J.P. Devadhar, to oversee the sale of Citrus's properties.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this order applies existing provisions of the SEBI Act and CIS Regulations to Citrus's conduct and imposes penalties under Section 15-I of the SEBI Act read with the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995.

## Compliance Requirements

Citrus Check Inns Limited and its named directors are subject to the findings and penalties of this adjudication order. Entities operating fund mobilisation schemes from the public must ensure registration as a CIS under Regulation 3 of the CIS Regulations before soliciting investments, failing which they risk SEBI enforcement action, including ex-parte interim orders and adjudication penalties.

## Impact Assessment

This order primarily affects Citrus Check Inns Limited, an unlisted company already under insolvency proceedings, and its individual directors who may face monetary penalties. Direct impact on listed equity markets is minimal since Citrus is not a publicly traded company on major exchanges. However, the order reinforces SEBI's continued enforcement focus on unregistered collective investment schemes, serving as a cautionary precedent for entities and promoters engaged in similar fund-mobilisation structures without regulatory registration.
