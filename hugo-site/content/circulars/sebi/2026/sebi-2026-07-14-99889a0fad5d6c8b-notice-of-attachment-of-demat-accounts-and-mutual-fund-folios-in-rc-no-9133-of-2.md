## Summary

SEBI, acting through its Recovery Officer under the recovery proceedings provisions applicable to the Board (Section 28A of the SEBI Act read with the Second and Third Schedules to the Income Tax Act, 1961, and the Income Tax (Certificate Proceedings) Rules, 1962), has issued a notice of attachment against Pragnesh Bhupendrabhai Thakkar (PAN: AEVPT7357L) in relation to Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 9133 of 2026. The certificate was issued for recovery of an outstanding amount arising from proceedings concerning trading in illiquid stock options at BSE. The notice directs attachment of the defaulter's demat (depository) accounts and mutual fund folios/units so that they cannot be transferred, dealt with, or dissipated pending recovery.

## Key Points

- Recovery Certificate No. 9133 of 2026 has been issued against Pragnesh Bhupendrabhai Thakkar (PAN: AEVPT7357L).
- The underlying default relates to a matter of trading in illiquid stock options at BSE.
- SEBI's Recovery Officer has attached the individual's demat accounts held with depositories/depository participants.
- Mutual fund folios/units held in the name of the defaulter have also been attached.
- Depositories, depository participants, mutual funds, and RTAs are directed to mark a lien/freeze on the attached accounts and folios and not permit any debit, transfer, redemption, or dealing without SEBI's Recovery Officer's approval.

## Regulatory Changes

This is not a policy or regulatory change; it is an individual enforcement/recovery action taken under existing recovery proceedings framework applicable to SEBI (Section 28A of the SEBI Act, 1992, read with the Income Tax Act's Second and Third Schedules and the Income Tax (Certificate Proceedings) Rules, 1962).

## Compliance Requirements

- Depositories/depository participants must ensure the named individual's demat account(s) are attached and no transfer or dealing is permitted.
- Mutual fund AMCs/RTAs must mark the specified folios as attached and stop processing redemptions, switches, or transfers on those folios.
- Any dealing with the attached accounts/folios without written permission from the SEBI Recovery Officer would be treated as void and may attract further legal consequences.
- The defaulter is expected to clear the outstanding recovery amount or approach the Recovery Officer as per the process laid out in the notice.

## Important Dates

- Notice published: July 14, 2026.
- Specific payment deadlines and hearing dates, if any, are set out in the full notice on SEBI's website and are not detailed in the extractable summary.

## Impact Assessment

The action is narrowly targeted at recovering dues from a single individual found liable in connection with illiquid stock options trading at BSE and has no direct bearing on listed companies, market indices, or trading in specific securities. Its significance is limited to demonstrating SEBI's continued enforcement of recovery certificates against individuals who have not honored earlier orders/penalties, and it serves as a deterrent signal to other entities with pending SEBI dues. Market-wide impact is negligible.
