## Summary

SEBI's Appellate Authority under the Right to Information Act, 2005 issued an order dismissing Appeal No. 6926 of 2026 filed by Mukul Singh against the CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai. The appellant had sought information regarding refunds to investors in the PACL matter. The appeal was dismissed both on the ground of being time-barred and on merits, as the respondent had already clarified that the specific information sought was not available with SEBI, though related public information on the PACL matter is available on SEBI's website.

## Key Points

- The appellant filed an RTI application dated April 02, 2026 (received by SEBI on April 10, 2026) seeking information on refunds to investors in the PACL matter.
- The CPIO responded on April 21, 2026, stating the specific information sought was not available with SEBI, but pointed to public notices, press releases, status reports, and FAQs on the PACL matter available on the SEBI website.
- The appellant's first appeal, dated June 02, 2026, was filed beyond the 30-day limitation period under Section 19(1) of the RTI Act, with no request for condonation of delay or explanation for the delay.
- The Appellate Authority held the appeal time-barred but also considered it on merits.
- Citing the CIC decision in Sh. Pattipati Rama Murthy vs. CPIO, SEBI (July 8, 2013), the Authority held that SEBI cannot invent information it does not possess.
- The Authority noted that responsibility for disposal of PACL properties and repayment to investors rests with the Justice (Retd.) R.M. Lodha Committee, not directly with SEBI.

## Regulatory Changes

None. This is an individual adjudicatory order under the RTI Act and does not introduce any new regulatory requirement.

## Compliance Requirements

Not applicable to market participants or listed entities; this order pertains solely to an individual RTI appellant and SEBI's CPIO/Appellate Authority process.

## Important Dates

- April 02, 2026: RTI application filed by appellant
- April 10, 2026: Application received by SEBI
- April 21, 2026: CPIO response issued
- June 02, 2026: First appeal filed (beyond the 30-day limitation)
- June 10, 2026: Appeal received by the Office of First Appellate Authority

## Impact Assessment

This order has no bearing on listed companies, trading, or market operations. It is relevant only to investors seeking information on the long-pending PACL refund process, confirming that refund disbursement is managed by the Justice R.M. Lodha Committee rather than SEBI directly, and that further updates should be sought from that Committee or SEBI's public PACL disclosures rather than through individual RTI requests to SEBI.
