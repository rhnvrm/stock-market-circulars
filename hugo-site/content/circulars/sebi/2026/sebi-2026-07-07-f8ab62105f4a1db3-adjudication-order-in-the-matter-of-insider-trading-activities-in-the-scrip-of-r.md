## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer has passed an order against Mr. Raj Kumar Agarwal, former promoter and director of Hi-Tech Chemicals Limited, for alleged insider trading in the scrip of RHI Magnesita India Ltd. (RHI). RHI had acquired the refractory business of Hi-Tech Chemicals Limited via a Business Transfer Agreement (BTA) dated October 18, 2022, which was publicly disclosed on October 19, 2022. SEBI's investigation covered the period July 08, 2022 to December 19, 2022, and alleged that the Noticee, due to his connection with RHI during implementation of the BTA, had access to Unpublished Price Sensitive Information (UPSI) regarding RHI's proposed acquisition of Dalmia OCL Limited (a subsidiary of Dalmia Bharat Refractories Limited), and traded in RHI shares while in possession of this UPSI, violating Regulation 4(1) of the SEBI (Prohibition of Insider Trading) Regulations, 2015.

## Key Points

- RHI Magnesita India Ltd. is listed on both BSE and NSE.
- SEBI conducted an internal-alert-based investigation into insider trading in RHI's scrip covering July 08, 2022 to December 19, 2022.
- The Noticee, Mr. Raj Kumar Agarwal, was promoter/director of Hi-Tech Chemicals Limited, whose refractory business was acquired by RHI under a BTA dated October 18, 2022 (disclosed October 19, 2022).
- The alleged UPSI pertained to RHI's proposed acquisition of Dalmia OCL Limited (DOCL), a wholly owned subsidiary of Dalmia Bharat Refractories Limited (DBRL).
- On November 19, 2022, RHI disclosed to NSE its intent to execute a Share Swap Agreement (SSA) with DBRL to acquire 100% of DOCL's equity (8,24,83,642 shares) in exchange for 2,70,00,000 new RHI shares via preferential allotment.
- The Noticee is alleged to have traded in RHI shares during the UPSI period while in possession of such UPSI, violating Regulation 4(1) of the PIT Regulations.
- An Adjudicating Officer was appointed on April 02, 2026 under Section 15-I of the SEBI Act read with Rule 3 of the SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995.
- A Show Cause Notice (Ref. No. SEBI/HO/EAD-2/JS/YK/DIS/10104/2026) was issued on April 10, 2026 under Rule 4 of the Rules and Section 15-I of the SEBI Act, seeking explanation as to why penalty under Section 15G(i) should not be imposed.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement action applying existing Regulation 4(1) of the SEBI (Prohibition of Insider Trading) Regulations, 2015 and Section 15G(i) of the SEBI Act, 1992.

## Compliance Requirements

- Persons connected to listed companies through business transfer agreements or similar transactions must treat information about related material corporate actions (such as subsequent acquisitions) as UPSI and refrain from trading in the company's securities until such information is publicly disclosed.
- Entities and individuals with access to UPSI due to their business relationships with listed companies are subject to PIT Regulations obligations, including trading restrictions and disclosure requirements.

## Important Dates

- July 08, 2022 – December 19, 2022: SEBI's investigation period.
- October 18, 2022: Business Transfer Agreement between RHI and Hi-Tech Chemicals Limited executed.
- October 19, 2022: BTA publicly disclosed to stock exchanges.
- November 19, 2022 (17:57:39 IST): RHI's disclosure to NSE regarding proposed Share Swap Agreement with DBRL for acquisition of Dalmia OCL Limited.
- April 02, 2026: Adjudicating Officer appointed.
- April 10, 2026: Show Cause Notice issued to the Noticee.

## Impact Assessment

The order underscores SEBI's continued enforcement focus on insider trading arising from business transactions involving listed companies and their counterparties. While the direct financial and operational impact on RHI Magnesita India Ltd. is limited since the company itself is not the subject of penalty, the case highlights UPSI-related trading risks for individuals connected to a listed company via M&A or business transfer transactions, and serves as a compliance reminder for market participants regarding insider trading controls during deal execution.
