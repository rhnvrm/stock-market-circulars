## Summary

SEBI has revised the Monthly Cumulative Report (MCR) format applicable to all Mutual Funds, AMCs, Trustee Companies, and AMFI. The revision is necessitated by the introduction of new scheme categories under the February 26, 2026 circular on Categorisation and Rationalisation of Mutual Fund Schemes (now consolidated as clause 3.7 of the Master Circular). The updated MCR format is effective from June 2026 onwards.

## Key Points

- The MCR format prescribed under clause 6.20 of the SEBI Master Circular for Mutual Funds (March 20, 2026) has been revised.
- New scheme categories introduced under the Categorisation and Rationalisation circular (February 26, 2026) are now reflected in the updated MCR format.
- Revised MCR format is enclosed as Annexure A; the MCR SIF (Scheme Information File) format is enclosed as Annexure B.
- All other conditions under the relevant Master Circular clause remain unchanged.
- Issued under Section 11(1) of the SEBI Act, 1992 read with SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations 2026.

## Regulatory Changes

The MCR format has been expanded to accommodate new and renamed scheme categories across the following broad groupings:
- **Debt Schemes**: Overnight Fund, Liquid Fund, Ultra Short Term Fund, Ultra Short to Short Term Fund, Money Market Fund, Short Term Fund, Medium Term Fund, Medium to Long Term Fund, Long Term Fund, Dynamic Term Fund, Corporate Bond Fund, Credit Risk Fund, Banking and PSU Debt Fund, Gilt Fund, 10-year Constant Maturity Gilt Fund, Floating Interest Rates Fund, Sectoral Fund.
- **Equity Schemes**: Multi Cap Fund, Large Cap Fund, Large & Mid Cap Fund, Mid Cap Fund, Small Cap Fund, Flexi Cap Fund, Dividend Yield Fund, Value Fund, Contra Fund, Focused Fund, Sectoral Fund, Thematic Fund, ELSS-Tax Saver Fund.
- **Hybrid Schemes**: Conservative Hybrid Fund, Balanced Hybrid Fund, Aggressive Hybrid Fund, Balanced Advantage Fund/Dynamic Asset Allocation, Multi Asset Allocation Fund, Arbitrage Fund, Equity Savings Fund.
- **Life Cycle Funds**: Maturity tenors of 5, 10, 15, and 20 years (and others).

Columns retained in the revised format include: number of schemes, folios, funds mobilised (cumulative from April 1), redemptions, net inflow/outflow, AUM, average AUM, segregated portfolio details, and SIP-related metrics (inflows, existing/new/matured/prematurely terminated/closing SIP accounts).

## Compliance Requirements

- All Mutual Funds, AMCs, Trustee Companies/Boards of Trustees, and AMFI must adopt the revised MCR format (Annexure A) and MCR SIF format (Annexure B) starting from the June 2026 MCR reporting cycle.
- Existing reporting obligations and all other conditions under clause 6.20 of the Master Circular remain in force.

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: May 19, 2026
- **Effective From**: June 2026 MCR onwards

## Impact Assessment

This is an operational and administrative change with medium impact on AMCs and RTAs who will need to update their MCR reporting systems to reflect the new scheme category structure. There is no direct market or investor-level impact. Fund houses should ensure their internal reporting pipelines and data feeds are aligned with the revised format before the June 2026 reporting deadline.
