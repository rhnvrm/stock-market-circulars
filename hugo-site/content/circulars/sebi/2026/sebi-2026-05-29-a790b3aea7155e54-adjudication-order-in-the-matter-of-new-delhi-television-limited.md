## Summary

SEBI's Adjudicating Officer has issued Order No. Order/JS/DP/2026-27/32428 against New Delhi Television Limited (NDTV, PAN: AAACN0865D) under Section 23-I of the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956. The proceedings concern alleged failures by NDTV to make timely and adequate disclosures to stock exchanges regarding material events connected to the indirect acquisition of control by Vishvapradhan Commercial Private Limited (VCPL) through a 2009 loan and call option agreement with NDTV's promoters.

## Key Points

- SEBI's June 26, 2018 order had concluded that VCPL indirectly acquired control of NDTV via a loan and call option agreement dated July 21, 2009 with promoters Dr. Prannoy Roy and Mrs. Radhika Roy (through RRPR Holding Private Limited).
- Despite the 2018 SEBI order, NDTV made disclosures to BSE and NSE on June 27, 2018 and July 2, 2018 reiterating that promoters continued to own 61.45% and control NDTV, and that no undisclosed price-sensitive information existed.
- SEBI investigated the period October 14, 2008 to September 30, 2010 following a complaint by Quantum Securities Pvt. Ltd. (shareholder) alleging violations of securities laws by NDTV promoters.
- Adjudication proceedings were initiated under Section 23E of SCRA for alleged violations of Regulations 30(1), 30(3), 30(4), and 30(6) read with Clause 8 of Para B of Part A of Schedule III of the LODR Regulations.
- Additional alleged violation of Clause 8 of Para B of Annexure-I to SEBI Circular No. CIR/CFD/CMD/4/2015 dated September 09, 2015.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This order applies existing disclosure obligations under LODR Regulations and the SCRA to NDTV's conduct during the investigation period.

## Compliance Requirements

- Listed companies must promptly disclose to stock exchanges any material events, including changes in control or significant agreements (such as loan and call option agreements) that may affect shareholding or management control.
- Disclosure obligations under Regulation 30 of LODR Regulations are mandatory and cover all events that could have a bearing on share price or investor decisions.
- Companies must ensure their communications to exchanges do not omit or misrepresent material facts relating to control arrangements.

## Important Dates

- July 21, 2009: Date of the 2009 Loan Agreement and call option agreement between VCPL and NDTV promoters.
- October 14, 2008 – September 30, 2010: Investigation period examined by SEBI.
- September 9, 2015: Date of SEBI Circular No. CIR/CFD/CMD/4/2015 referenced in the alleged violation.
- June 26, 2018: SEBI's original order directing VCPL to make a public announcement.
- June 27, 2018 and July 2, 2018: NDTV's disclosure letters to stock exchanges post the 2018 SEBI order.
- Order No. Order/JS/DP/2026-27/32428: Issued in FY 2026-27.

## Impact Assessment

The order is significant for NDTV and its shareholders as it formalises SEBI's adjudication of disclosure lapses in a long-running regulatory matter involving one of India's most prominent media companies. A penalty, if imposed, would be under the SCRA framework. The case reinforces SEBI's stance on strict compliance with LODR disclosure norms, particularly for control-change events. Broader market impact is limited, but the precedent underscores that listed entities must proactively disclose control-related agreements even when promoters dispute regulatory findings.
