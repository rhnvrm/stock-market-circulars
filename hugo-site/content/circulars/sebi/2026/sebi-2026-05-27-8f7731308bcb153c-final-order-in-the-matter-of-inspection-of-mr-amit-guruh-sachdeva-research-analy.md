## Summary

SEBI issued a Final Order under Section 12(3) of the SEBI Act, 1992 read with Regulation 27 of the SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008 against Mr. Amit Guruh Sachdeva (SEBI Registration No. INH100005190), a registered Research Analyst. The order follows an enquiry initiated after a surprise onsite inspection in March 2024, which found violations of the RA Regulations, PFUTP Regulations, and SEBI's April 2023 Circular.

## Key Points

- Mr. Amit Guruh Sachdeva is a registered Research Analyst (INH100005190, PAN: BIJPS0406C).
- SEBI conducted a surprise onsite inspection on March 14–15, 2024, covering the period April 1, 2022 to February 29, 2024.
- Two primary violations were established by the Designated Authority (DA):
  1. The Noticee misled clients by providing assurances of fixed returns.
  2. The Noticee failed to cooperate with SEBI's inspection by causing undue delays and deleting data from his email account.
- Inspection findings were communicated to the Noticee on June 27, 2024; he responded on June 29, 2024.
- A Pre-Enquiry Show Cause Notice was issued on October 31, 2024; the Noticee replied on November 20, 2024.
- Personal hearing was granted and availed on May 26, 2025 through an authorized representative.
- The DA submitted the Enquiry Report on August 29, 2025, finding violations of RA Regulations and PFUTP Regulations established.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This order applies existing provisions:
- SEBI (Research Analyst) Regulations, 2014
- SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices relating to Securities Market) Regulations, 2003 (PFUTP Regulations)
- SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008 — Regulations 23, 25, 26, 27, and 35
- SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/MIRSD/MIRSD-PoD2/P/CIR/2023/52 dated April 6, 2023

## Compliance Requirements

- Research Analysts must not provide assurances or guarantees of fixed returns to clients, as this constitutes misleading conduct under PFUTP Regulations.
- Research Analysts are required to fully cooperate with SEBI inspections, including promptly providing data and records, and must not delete or destroy information relevant to an ongoing inspection.
- Registered intermediaries must comply with all applicable RA Regulations and SEBI circulars governing conduct and disclosure.

## Impact Assessment

This order has limited broader market impact as it pertains to a single individual Research Analyst. However, it signals SEBI's continued enforcement focus on:
1. **Investor protection**: Cracking down on RAs who mislead retail clients with promises of fixed or guaranteed returns, a common mis-selling practice.
2. **Regulatory cooperation**: Taking strict action against intermediaries who obstruct inspections by deleting records, reinforcing the integrity of SEBI's oversight mechanism.

For other registered Research Analysts, this case serves as a reminder of obligations under RA Regulations and the consequences of non-cooperation with regulatory inspections. The final order likely entails suspension or cancellation of the Noticee's SEBI registration as a Research Analyst.
