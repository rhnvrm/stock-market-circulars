## Summary

SEBI's Recovery Officer has issued a Notice of Demand dated July 15, 2026 under Recovery Certificate (RC) No. 9222, drawn against Ankit Ajitbhai Panchal. The notice stems from an earlier SEBI enforcement order concerning trading activities of certain entities in the scrip of DU Digital Technologies Limited (now renamed DU Digital Global Limited). Having failed to comply with the underlying directions (such as disgorgement of unlawful gains and/or payment of a monetary penalty), the noticee is now being pursued for recovery of the outstanding dues through formal demand proceedings.

## Key Points

- The Notice of Demand is issued under Recovery Certificate No. 9222, a formal instrument used by SEBI's Recovery Officer to enforce recovery of unpaid dues.
- The action is connected to a prior SEBI order examining trading activities of certain entities in the scrip of DU Digital Technologies Limited (now DU Digital Global Limited).
- Ankit Ajitbhai Panchal is named as the defaulter/noticee from whom recovery is being sought.
- This is a follow-on enforcement step, indicating that amounts due under the original order remain unpaid.

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced; this is an enforcement/recovery action under SEBI's existing powers (Section 28A of the SEBI Act, read with the Income Tax Act's Second and Third Schedules for recovery of sums due).

## Compliance Requirements

The noticee, Ankit Ajitbhai Panchal, is required to clear the outstanding dues specified in the demand notice within the stipulated period, failing which SEBI's Recovery Officer may proceed with further coercive recovery measures such as attachment of bank accounts, movable/immovable property, or other assets.

## Important Dates

- Notice of Demand date: July 15, 2026

## Impact Assessment

The impact of this notice is largely confined to the individual noticee and does not represent a market-wide regulatory change. It reinforces SEBI's continued enforcement follow-through on prior orders relating to irregular trading activity in DU Digital Technologies Limited (now DU Digital Global Limited), signaling to market participants that non-compliance with SEBI orders leads to escalated recovery proceedings.
