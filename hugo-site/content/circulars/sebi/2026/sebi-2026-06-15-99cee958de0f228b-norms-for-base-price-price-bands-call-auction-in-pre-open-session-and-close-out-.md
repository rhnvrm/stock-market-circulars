## Summary

SEBI has issued revised norms for Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs) covering base price determination, price bands, call auction in the pre-open session, and close-out procedures. The circular addresses the lag issue with the existing T-2 NAV-based base price and the mismatch between fixed price bands and underlying asset price ranges. Changes are based on recommendations from a Working Group of Stock Exchanges, the Secondary Market Advisory Committee (SMAC), public consultation, and stakeholder discussions.

## Key Points

- Base price for ETFs changes from T-2 day NAV to T-1 day closing price (last 30 minutes VWAP)
- Fallback base price hierarchy: T-1 VWAP → Last Traded Price (LTP) → latest available closing NAV
- Base price to be adjusted for corporate actions
- Fixed price band of ±20% replaced with dynamic price bands starting at ±10% for Equity and Debt ETFs (excluding Overnight and Liquid ETFs)
- Dynamic bands can flex up to ±20% in increments of 5%, after a cooling-off period
- Cooling-off period: 15 minutes when trades execute at or above 9.90% band level; 5 minutes if this occurs in the last 30 minutes of trading
- Band flexing is unidirectional — no sliding adjustment on the opposite side
- Flexing at one exchange is applicable across all exchanges
- Stock Exchanges and AMCs must jointly implement T-1 day closing NAV as base price by April 1, 2027

## Regulatory Changes

- **Base Price:** Shifted from T-2 NAV to T-1 closing VWAP (last 30 minutes) for all ETFs. Full migration to T-1 closing NAV deferred to April 1, 2027 due to operational challenges.
- **Price Bands:** Dynamic price bands with initial band of ±10% replacing the erstwhile fixed ±20% band for Equity ETFs and Debt ETFs (other than Overnight and Liquid ETFs). Maximum flex cap remains at ±20%.
- **Cooling-off Period:** Introduced at 15 minutes (standard) and 5 minutes (when breach occurs in last 30 minutes of session).
- **Band Flexing Rules:** Maximum two instances of 5% flex allowed in one direction per session; flexing at one exchange triggers same at all exchanges.
- **Overnight ETFs:** Price band remains at ±5% on the revised base price.
- These norms supersede the relevant provisions in Chapter 1 (Paragraphs 2.3.1 and 2.5) of the SEBI Master Circular for Stock Exchanges and Clearing Corporations dated December 30, 2024.

## Compliance Requirements

- **All Recognized Stock Exchanges:** Implement revised base price (T-1 VWAP) and dynamic price band framework immediately; coordinate with AMCs to implement T-1 closing NAV base price by April 1, 2027; ensure cross-exchange synchronization of band flexing.
- **All Recognized Clearing Corporations:** Align close-out procedures per revised norms.
- **Asset Management Companies (AMCs) of Mutual Funds:** Jointly address operational challenges with stock exchanges to enable T-1 closing NAV-based base price implementation by April 1, 2027.
- **AMFI:** Disseminate circular to member AMCs and ensure awareness.
- All entities to note that prior provisions in the Master Circular (December 30, 2024) for ETF price bands stand modified by this circular.

## Important Dates

- **June 15, 2026:** Circular issuance date; revised base price (T-1 VWAP) and dynamic price band norms come into effect
- **April 1, 2027:** Deadline for Stock Exchanges and AMCs to implement T-1 day closing NAV as the base price for ETFs

## Impact Assessment

This circular has high market and operational impact. The shift to dynamic price bands (from a fixed ±20%) better aligns ETF trading ranges with underlying asset volatility, reducing the risk of artificial price dislocations. The cooling-off mechanism adds a circuit-breaker layer that was previously absent for ETFs. The use of T-1 VWAP (instead of T-2 NAV) as base price reduces the one-day lag, improving price discovery relevance. The mandatory cross-exchange band synchronization prevents regulatory arbitrage between exchanges. AMCs and exchanges face a defined 10-month runway to implement the more operationally complex T-1 NAV base price, making near-term disruption limited but requiring coordinated systems development. All ETF products listed on Indian exchanges are affected, making this a broad-impact regulatory update for passive investing infrastructure.
