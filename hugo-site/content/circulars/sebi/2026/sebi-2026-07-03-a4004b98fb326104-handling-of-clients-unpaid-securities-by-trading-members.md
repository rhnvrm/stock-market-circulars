## Summary

SEBI has revised Paragraph 46 of the Master Circular for Stock Brokers dated June 17, 2025, governing the handling of client's unpaid securities by Trading Members (TMs). The revision follows representations from the Brokers' Industry Standards Forum (ISF) seeking alignment of the existing framework with regulatory developments such as mandatory pay-out of securities directly to clients' demat accounts, as well as changes in market practices since the original provisions were introduced in 2019 and 2022.

## Key Points

- For trades not covered under the Margin Trading Facility (MTF), pay-out of unpaid securities must now be made directly to the client's demat account.
- Following pay-out, an auto-pledge (without specific client instruction) is created in favor of a new dedicated account called the Client Unpaid Securities Pledgee Account (CUSPA), to be opened by the TM.
- TMs must notify clients via email/SMS about their funds obligation and the TM's right to sell the pledged securities if the obligation is not met.
- TMs must formulate and communicate a policy (standalone or part of the Risk Management Policy) covering the process, reasons, manner, and timing for invocation/release of pledge and liquidation of unpaid securities.
- The policy must specify a maximum period, not exceeding five trading days from the pay-out date, within which clients must meet their payment obligation.
- Securities pledged to CUSPA may be considered for reporting client margin collection to the Clearing Corporation, but TMs cannot allow trading exposure to clients based on such pledged securities.
- TMs must determine, on a daily basis, the maximum value of securities that may remain pledged, based on client ledger balance, overall margin obligations, or other factors per exchange operational guidelines.
- If pledged securities exceed the determined maximum pledge value, the TM must release the pledge on the excess securities.

## Regulatory Changes

This circular amends Paragraph 46 of the Master Circular for Stock Brokers dated June 17, 2025, replacing earlier provisions introduced via CIR/HO/MIRSD/DOP/CIR/P/2019/75 (June 20, 2019) and SEBI/HO/MIRSD/MIRSD-PoD-1/P/CIR/2022/153 (November 11, 2022). The key structural change is the introduction of a mandatory auto-pledge mechanism via a new CUSPA account, replacing prior direct retention/handling arrangements for unpaid securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must open and operationalize a Client Unpaid Securities Pledgee Account (CUSPA).
- TMs must implement auto-pledge processes for unpaid securities pay-outs on non-MTF trades.
- TMs must send client communications (email/SMS) regarding funds obligations and sale rights.
- TMs must draft, maintain, and communicate a formal policy on handling unpaid securities, including invocation/release of pledge and liquidation procedures.
- TMs must perform daily assessments of maximum permissible pledge value and release excess pledged securities accordingly.
- Stock Exchanges and Depositories must support operational guidelines and infrastructure for CUSPA and related pledge mechanisms.

## Important Dates

- Circular issued: July 03, 2026.
- Underlying Master Circular being amended: dated June 17, 2025.
- Maximum client payment obligation period: up to five trading days from the pay-out date (per TM policy).
- Specific effective/implementation date for the amended provisions is not stated in the extracted text; TMs should refer to the full circular and any exchange operational guidelines for implementation timelines.

## Impact Assessment

The revised framework enhances investor protection by ensuring unpaid securities are transferred directly to clients' demat accounts rather than retained elsewhere, while still safeguarding TMs' interests through the auto-pledge mechanism. This is largely an operational and compliance-driven change affecting stock brokers, exchanges, and depositories, requiring system and process updates (CUSPA account setup, daily pledge value monitoring, client communication workflows). It does not directly impact specific listed securities but affects the ease of doing business for brokers and improves transparency and risk management for client unpaid securities handling across the market.
