## Summary

SEBI has issued Settlement Order No. SO/AK/GN/2026-27/8631-8632 in the matter of Cupid Limited, settling adjudication proceedings against Unistone Capital Private Limited (Applicant 1) and Mr. Jitendra Sanghavi (Applicant 2). The proceedings arose from alleged violations of the SEBI (Prohibition of Insider Trading) Regulations, 2015 (PIT Regulations), specifically relating to contra trades executed within six months and trades conducted without obtaining mandatory pre-clearance in the scrip of Cupid Limited.

## Key Points

- Applicant 1 (Unistone Capital Private Limited) alleged to have violated Para B(5) of Unistone Code of Conduct and Para 8, Schedule C read with Regulation 9(1) and (2) of PIT Regulations, 2015
- Applicant 2 (Mr. Jitendra Sanghavi) alleged to have violated Para B(1) of Unistone Code of Conduct and Para 4, Schedule C read with Regulation 9(1) and (2) of PIT Regulations, 2015
- Both applicants entered contra trades in Cupid Ltd where buy and sell legs were executed within six months
- Both applicants also executed buy/sell trades in Cupid Ltd during the investigation period without obtaining pre-clearance
- Settlement applications filed on July 16, 2025 under SEBI (Settlement Proceedings) Regulations, 2018
- HPAC recommended settlement on March 25, 2026 after multiple IC meetings and revised proposals
- Settlement accepted without admission or denial of facts and conclusions of law

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This order applies existing provisions of:
- SEBI Act, 1992 – Section 15HB (penalty)
- SEBI (Prohibition of Insider Trading) Regulations, 2015 – Regulation 9(1) and (2), Schedule C
- SEBI (Settlement Proceedings) Regulations, 2018
- SEBI (Procedure for Holding Inquiry and Imposing Penalties) Rules, 1995

## Compliance Requirements

- Designated persons and insiders must ensure contra trades (buy and sell within six months) are avoided in company scrip as required under applicable Codes of Conduct
- Pre-clearance must be obtained before executing trades in company scrip during designated windows and investigation periods
- Entities covered under PIT Regulations must maintain and enforce internal Codes of Conduct aligned with Schedule C requirements

## Important Dates

- May 28, 2025: SEBI appointed Adjudicating Officer
- June 13, 2025: Show Cause Notice issued to applicants
- July 16, 2025: Settlement applications filed by both applicants
- September 18, 2025: First Internal Committee (IC) meeting
- November 06, 2025: Second IC meeting
- February 26, 2026: Third IC meeting; revised settlement terms proposed
- March 10, 2026: Further revised settlement terms submitted
- March 25, 2026: HPAC meeting recommending settlement terms

## Impact Assessment

This settlement order has limited direct market impact but carries significant compliance signalling value. The combined settlement amount of ₹67,34,000 (₹34,84,000 from Applicant 1 and ₹32,50,000 from Applicant 2) underscores SEBI's continued focus on enforcing PIT Regulations, particularly around contra trade restrictions and pre-clearance norms. Market participants, especially designated persons at listed companies and their connected entities, should review their internal compliance frameworks to ensure adherence to Code of Conduct requirements and pre-clearance procedures to avoid similar adjudication proceedings.
