## Summary

SEBI has issued a Notice of Attachment of Demat Accounts (No. 15390 of 2026) dated June 01, 2026, against AD Advertising Private Limited, a defaulter with PAN AAJCA3551C, in connection with the matter of Fortis Healthcare Limited. The attachment is executed under Recovery Certificate No. 8808 of 2025, directing depositories and depository participants to freeze the demat accounts held by the defaulter.

## Key Points

- Attachment notice number: 15390 of 2026
- Issued on: June 01, 2026
- Defaulter: AD Advertising Private Limited, PAN: AAJCA3551C
- Underlying matter: Fortis Healthcare Limited
- Recovery Certificate No.: 8808 of 2025
- Action: Attachment/freezing of demat accounts held by the defaulter entity
- Directed to: Depositories and relevant Depository Participants (DPs)

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is an enforcement order under SEBI's existing recovery and attachment powers pursuant to applicable securities law and the recovery certificate mechanism.

## Compliance Requirements

- Depositories (NSDL/CDSL) are required to immediately freeze/attach all demat accounts held in the name of AD Advertising Private Limited (PAN: AAJCA3551C).
- Depository Participants maintaining such accounts must ensure no debit transactions are processed from the attached accounts.
- Any credits to the attached accounts must be reported to SEBI as per applicable procedures.
- DPs must acknowledge receipt of the attachment notice and confirm compliance.

## Important Dates

- Recovery Certificate No. 8808 of 2025: Underlying certificate date (2025)
- Attachment Notice Date: June 01, 2026
- Effective immediately upon receipt by depositories and DPs

## Impact Assessment

The impact of this circular is narrow and targeted. It affects only the demat accounts of AD Advertising Private Limited, a defaulter in the Fortis Healthcare Limited matter. There is no direct impact on Fortis Healthcare Limited as a listed company or on its shareholders. Broader market participants are unaffected. This is a standard SEBI enforcement measure to recover dues from a party that has failed to comply with prior orders or pay assessed amounts under Recovery Certificate No. 8808 of 2025.
